   Senator Jim Webb   Virginia                                                                                        Press Releases                   October 4  2007 Warner   Webb  FY2008 Defense Appropriations Reflect Virginia s Contributions to National Security  Troop Welfare                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Washington   DCSenator John W  Warner  R VA  and Senator Jim Webb  D VA    both members of the Senate Committee on Armed Services  announced today that  provisions in the Department of Defense Appropriations Act for fiscal year 2008   H R  3222   reflect Virginias significant contributions to national security  and the welfare of our nations servicemembers   I am proud of Virginias contribution to our national  defense  and I am pleased to see such important and substantive measures  included in this legislation  said Senator Warner  These programs will  benefit those members of the armed forces currently serving overseas  as well as  help secure Americas military dominance for the  future   Once again  I was pleased to work with Senator Warner  in a bipartisan spirit to ensure that our nations critical defense programs are  funded in the most responsible way  said Senator Webb  The bills 3 5 percent  across the board pay raise for military personnel reflects our common commitment  to see to the welfare of our men and women in uniform and their families  The  bills provisions also support many of the important contributions Virginians  are making to our nations defense requirements    The bill  appropriates funding for the following  projects    Radford   Radford Army Ammunition  Plant  The Senate appropriated  10 million  in  addition to the  32 million appropriated in Fiscal Year 2007  to address a  backlog of critical infrastructure modernization projects at Radford Army  Ammunition Plant  This funding will help modernize Radfords World War II era  infrastructure in an environmentally responsible manner by addressing the  plants inefficient solvent recovery and acid storage and containment  systems  The Radford  Army Ammunition Plant is the sole U S  source for a wide range of military  propellants used by all branches of the U S  armed forces  It is a major  employer in the New  River Valley    Hampton Roads   USS Gerald R  Ford  CVN 78   Construction  The Senate provided  2 7 billion for  funding for construction of the next nuclear powered aircraft carrier  Gerald R   Ford  CVN 78      Virginia Class Submarine  Construction  The Senate appropriated  470 million to  begin advanced procurement of materials necessary to begin construction of two  nuclear powered Virginia class submarines per year in 2010  rather than 2012 as  the Administration had recommended     Free Electron Laser for Naval  Applications  The Senate added  2 million for continued  research in directed energy and lasers at the Thomas Jefferson National  Laboratory in Newport  News    Danville   Military  Tires  The Senate agreed to a provision mandating  the Defense Logistics Agency take measures to ensure all tire manufacturers be  on an equal footing in the competitive market and new tire types are subject to  fair competition among manufacturers  including Goodyear  This measure affects  more than 2 200 workers in the Danville region who work for Goodyear and  manufacture tires for the United States Armed  Services   Additional Provisions   F 35 Lightning II Joint Strike  Fighter Alternate Engine  The Senate provided  480 million to  continue development and testing of the F136 alternate jet engine  This program  will ensure full and fair competition among suppliers so that the F 35 flies  with a reliable and best performing engine     Commonwealth Wide Military Programs    In addition  the Senate appropriated in  excess of  19 million for military research and development programs and more  than  12 million for operations  maintenance  and procurement activities in the  Commonwealth  In addition to numerous Virginia  companies  these programs will be executed by George Mason University  Norfolk State University  the College of William and Mary  and Virginia Tech in  conjunction with the Department of Defense
