                                                        June 8  2007 McCaskill Moves To Protect Patients Against Abuse                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Bipartisan legislation would establish national system to screen employees at long term care facilities WASHINGTON  D C   Today  U S  Senator Claire McCaskill stood with Senators Herb Kohl  D WI   Pete Domenici  R NM   and four other colleagues to co sponsor bipartisan legislation aimed at protecting patients of long term care facilities from employee abuse  The Patient Safety and Abuse Prevention Act of 2007 would establish a coordinated nationwide system to screen employees of long term care facilities  The system would also facilitate the sharing of information between states in order to prevent disqualified workers from moving from state to state   As a member of the Senate Aging Committee  McCaskill participated in a hearing last month on this issue  At the hearing  the committee heard testimony on ways the federal government can work to prevent individuals from preying on the elderly and other vulnerable populations  Currently  some states do not have adequate background check systems in place and there is no system in place to share this information nationwide   I am proud to stand with my colleagues on both sides of the aisle to introduce legislation that will establish stronger safety measures to protect our loved ones living under the care of others  said McCaskill  While the vast majority of these employees do admirable and invaluable work  we must make sure that those who do not have the patients best interests in mind are being kept out of these facilities   The program created by the bill is designed to stop individuals who have a history of substantiated abuse or a serious criminal record from working in long term care facilities  It would require coordinated background checks that utilize state and national registries  as well as the FBI Integrated Automated Fingerprint Identification System  Applicants that fail the background check are stopped from moving between facilities and states   The bill  which received the endorsement of the AARP  would require all states to participate in the coordinated nationwide screening system by 2011  Missouri stands to receive up to  3 million federal dollars to ensure the safety of patients in long term care facilities around the state                     Questions or comments        Text Size
