SENS. SARBANES, MIKULSKI ANNOUNCE $40,000 FEDERAL GRANT FOR HAGERSTOWN COMMUNITY COLLEGE
  Sens. Paul Sarbanes (D-MD) and Barbara A. Mikulski (D-MD) today announced that the Appalachian Regional Commission (ARC) has approved a grant of $40,000 for Hagerstown Community College's Job Training Institute.  
 The college will provide an additional $94,163 in matching funds in order meet the project's total cost of $134,163. 
   The combined funding will be used to increase the understanding of the new Job Training Institute and its services, as well as improving the connections between program graduates and employers. The project will develop and distribute marketing materials targeting potential and current students, community agencies and area employers. Various print and non-print media, as well as multi-paneled brochures and CDs will also be used to support the Job Training Institute's message.
   "The creation of jobs and the strengthening of local economies are crucial to Western Maryland's continued growth and development," said Sarbanes. "This project will help get the word out and further strengthen ties between students and potential local employers.
   "Maryland Community Colleges are leaders in developing a world class workforce," said Senator Mikulski. "That's why I'm so proud that Hagerstown Community College will be receiving this funding to get the word out about their Job Training Institute." 
   The Appalachian Regional Commission is a federal-state partnership that works with the people of Appalachia to create opportunities for self-sustaining economic development and improved quality of life.
