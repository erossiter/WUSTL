SENS. SARBANES, MIKULSKI ANNOUNCE OVER $1 MILLION IN FEDERAL FUNDING FOR TWO WESTERN MARYLAND AIRPORTS
  Sens. Paul S. Sarbanes (D-MD) and Barbara A. Mikulski (D-MD) announced today that two Western Maryland airports are being awarded a total of over $1 million in aviation grants from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), under the U.S. Department of Transportation, for improvements at their facilities. 
   The awards announced today are for:
   * Hagerstown Regional Airport will receive $979,053 to complete Phase III of the airports goal of improving its public terminal area. 
   * Fredrick Municipal Airport will use $86,070 to design and install fencing to secure the airport's perimeter.
   "Improving our state's transportation infrastructure is one of my top priorities," said Sarbanes. "These funds will provide for improvements for a growing passenger population and will enhance the safety of the passengers and pilots using these facilities." 
   "I fight every year to make sure that Maryland's transportation needs are a priority in the federal checkbook," said Senator Mikulski. "That's why I am proud to announce this federal investment in keeping Western Maryland on the move."
