SENS. ISAKSON, CHAMBLISS ANNOUNCE FUNDING FOR COASTAL HERITAGE SOCIETY
  Sens. Johnny Isakson (R-Ga.) and Saxby Chambliss (R-Ga.) today announced that the Coastal Heritage Society will receive a $471,200 grant for construction of the Savannah Battlefield Interpretive Center in Savannah. 
   The grant will be distributed through the Department of Housing and Urban Development. 
   "I am pleased to announce this grant for the Coastal Heritage Society," said Isakson. "This historical society has worked for many years to preserve sites and promote greater awareness and understanding of Georgia history." 
   "This important grant will help the Coastal Heritage Society in their important efforts to preserve history and it will also help provide a boost for local tourism," said Chambliss. 
   The Coastal Heritage Society is a non-profit organization devoted to preserving the history and culture of the coastal Georgia region. The organization provides a number of services for the Savannah community. The society currently manages three significant historical sites, Old Fort Jackson, the Savannah History Museum, and the Roundhouse Railroad Museum.
   Housing and community revitalization are at the heart of HUD's mission. This grant is part of the Department's plan to meet local needs and expand opportunities for citizens of Georgia and the nation.
