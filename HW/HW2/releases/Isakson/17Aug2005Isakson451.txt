SENS. ISAKSON, CHAMBLISS ANNOUNCE FUNDING FOR KENNESAW STATE UNIVERSITY
  Sens. Johnny Isakson (R-Ga.) and Saxby Chambliss (R-Ga.) today announced that Kennesaw State University will receive $217,617 from the U.S. Department of Education for the GEAR-UP Program, designed to increase the number of low income students who are prepared to enter and succeed in post-secondary education.  
   Working with 625 seventh graders over six years, the Marietta-Kennesaw Partnership, which includes Marietta City Schools, Chattahoochee Technical College, Kennesaw State University, Cobb Chamber of Commerce and InfoMart, will provide a variety of strategies and programs to increase academic performance, graduation rates, and student and parent knowledge of postsecondary education to improve the number of low-income students who succeed in college. 
   "I am pleased to announce this grant for Kennesaw State University," said Isakson. "This funding will help all students to succeed in rigorous academic courses and that means more of them will graduate and go on to college."
   "The biggest investment that we can make as a nation is in our youth," said Chambliss. "I commend Kennesaw State Universityfor recognizing this fact and for working hard to see that these Georgia children receive the valuable support they need."
