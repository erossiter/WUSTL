SENS. ISAKSON, CHAMBLISS ANNOUNCE FUNDING FOR CARROLL COUNTY ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT FOUNDATION
  Sens. Johnny Isakson (R-Ga.) and Saxby Chambliss (R-Ga.) today announced that the Carroll County Economic Development Foundation (Carroll Tomorrow) will receive a $184,118 grant for a new center that will help small businesses get started.  
   The grant, distributed by the Appalachian Regional Commission, will allow the Burson Center for Business Development to begin operation in 2006 to provide services both to startup firms housed within the facility and off-premise affiliate clients.
   "I am pleased to announce this grant for the Carroll County Economic Development Foundation," said Isakson. "This grant will enhance the economic development opportunities for Carroll County."
   "These funds for the Carroll County Economic Development Foundation will continue to aid Carroll's businesses and create new jobs," said Chambliss. "It is important that we support Georgia usinesses as the area continues to thrive." 
   The Appalachian Regional Commission is a federal-state partnership that works with the people of Appalachia to create opportunities for self-sustaining economic development and improved quality of life.
