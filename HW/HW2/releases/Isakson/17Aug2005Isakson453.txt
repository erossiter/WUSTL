SENS. ISAKSON, CHAMBLISS ANNOUNCE OVER $2.5 MILLION IN HUD FUNDING FOR ATHENS-CLARKE COUNTY
  Sens. Johnny Isakson (R-GA) and Saxby Chambliss (R-GA) today announced that Athens-Clarke County will receive $2,621,891 in grants from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development to help meet housing needs and expand economic opportunities for low-income families.  
   The grants are being distributed in the following categories: Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) and HOME programs. 
   "I am pleased to announce these important funds for Athens-Clarke County," said Isakson. "These valuable grants will help the community aid those who need it most."
   "These important programs fund a wide range of community initiatives, such as housing and support services, public improvements and economic development projects in Athens and across Clarke County," said Chambliss. " I will continue to work with my colleagues in support of the programs that mean so much to so many Georgia communities." 
   The CDBG program will receive a grant of $953,797 to develop viable urban communities by providing decent housing and a suitable living environment, and by expanding economic opportunities, principally for low- and moderate-income persons. 
   The HOME program will receive a grant of $953,797 to expand the supply of decent, affordable housing to low- and very low-income families to fund housing programs that meet local needs and priorities.
