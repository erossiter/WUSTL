SENS. ISAKSON, CHAMBLISS ANNOUNCE FUNDING FOR TOCCOA DOWNTOWN REVITALIZATION PROJECT
  Sens. Johnny Isakson (R-Ga.) and Saxby Chambliss (R-Ga.) today announced that the City of Toccoa will receive a $300,000 grant to revitalize its historic downtown business district.  
   The grant, distributed by the Appalachian Regional Commission, will assist the town's efforts to increase commercial activity in its downtown, enhance entrepreneurial activity, promote tourism and strengthen the region's economic health. It is projected that the project will support the creation of 20 new jobs, retain 57 existing jobs and leverage over $700,000 in private investments. 
   "I am pleased to announce this grant for Toccoa," said Isakson. "This grant will enhance the downtown area as well as economic development opportunities for the city." 
   "This important funding will go a long way to strengthen the rapidly growing downtown," said Chambliss. "I am proud to support the City of Toccoa as the area continues to thrive." 
   The Appalachian Regional Commission is a federal-state partnership that works with the people of Appalachia to create opportunities for self-sustaining economic development and improved quality of life.
