                                      Press Releases             Senate Passes  8 million for Military Robotics Barrasso Secures Money for UW  Wyoming National Guard  DoD Program November 9  2007 WASHINGTON  U S  Sen  John Barrasso  R Wyo   announced the Senate has approved the 2008 Department of Defense Appropriations conference report  which includes  8 million for the Joint Robotics Program at     Camp    Guernsey   The bill now awaits President Bushs signature   Im pleased the conference report passed by the Senate last night includes  8 million for robotics research at UW and the unique training facility at     Camp    Guernsey   Barrasso said  This is a major victory for UW and the Wyoming National Guard      Wyoming s military bases must remain an integral part of our nations military training needs  Barrasso added  I am proud to have played a major role in supporting our troops and our militarys innovative technologies to help soldiers in the field   UW is set to receive  4 million under the bill  The remaining  4 million goes to     Camp    Guernsey    Unmanned vehicles have become the new eyes and ears of our military allowing them to better control the battlefield  The Department of Defense has placed significant emphasis on   Wyoming as one of the critical places needed to test and evaluate these vehicles   U S  Sen     Craig Thomas started the funding initiative for Joint Robotics and provided more than  7 million for the program over the last three fiscal years    The Fiscal Year 2008 funding will provide for activities designed to make the Joint Training and Experimentation Center  JTEC  at     Camp    Guernsey an extremely effective testing site for the Department of Defenses Joint Robotics Program   The Wyoming Robotics initiative is a joint project between the University of Wyoming  UW   the Wyoming National Guard  and the Joint Robotics Program of the Office of the Secretary of Defense    The    University of    Wyoming has established a robotics work area within the     College of    Engineering   The money will be used for a wide range of activities to support the needs of the Joint Robotics Program and continued development of the Joint Urban Training and     Experimentation    Center   The center will be an integrated joint training environment for the testing and evaluation of these platforms   Half of the funds go toward the Joint Training and    Experimentation    Center at     Camp    Guernsey   In addition to testing of a variety of new technology and training techniques with live troops  the program has created a mobile urban training facility for use by all units  The other half of money specifically funds two projects at UW  Fly eye optical technology and anti jitter hexapod stabilizer technology                                              November 2007 Press Releases
