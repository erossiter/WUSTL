SEN. VOINOVICH SECURES OVER $1 MILLION FOR SOUTHWEST OHIO REGIONAL TRANSIT AUTHORITY
  Sen. George V. Voinovich (R-OH) today announced that the Southwest Ohio Regional Transit Authority has been awarded $1,016,009 from the Federal Transit Administration. The funds, which will be used for a state-of-the-art fare collection system, were secured by Sen. Voinovich through the appropriations process.  
   "Due to our current energy crisis, it is critical that we continue to improve our public transportation systems," Sen. Voinovich said. "We must promote usage and make our public transportation systems more efficient, affordable and accessible." 
   The fare collection system will provide passenger information to improve service on the Southwest Ohio Regional Transit Authority's transit system, which is based in Cincinnati.
