         WASHINGTON  D C    Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced that the University of Iowa will receive almost  200 000 to reduce violent crimes against women on campus  These funds were made available through the U S  Department of Justice  DOJ   Harkin is a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee              I am pleased that the University of Iowa is receiving funds to support women who have been the targets of violence and to prevent future crimes from occurring     Harkin said     Violence against women remains a major problem on college campuses across the country  However  with this survivor centered approach  we can help these women get the support they need to overcome these terrible acts             The University of Iowa will use this funding to establish a comprehensive anti violence educational program to inform the university community about sexual assault  dating violence  and stalking  The university will also create the Mentors in Violence Prevention Program to encourage men to actively speak out against violence against women and develop a six week support group for survivors that will be offered three times per year  These funds will also be used to organize and develop an anti violence campus coalition to improve victims   experiences with the legal system  review campus protocols and policies  and research and secure support for implementing a web based anti violence course as a condition of enrollment                                Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
