        Given the limited production capacities for antivirals and other medications and the level of investment other countries are making  this funding is urgently needed     Harkin said     We must secure the quantities of antivirals and other medications needed to protect a significant percentage of Americans in the event of a pandemic             On April 20  Harkin and Specter  along with Senator Orrin Hatch  R UT   wrote to Health and Human Services  HHS  Secretary Michael Leavitt strongly urging him to identify and purchase appropriate antidotes to a potential influenza pandemic  In response to reports that the U S  has fallen behind other countries in stockpiling the only known antidote to an outbreak of pandemic flu  including the Asian flu  the Senators recommended that HHS place a high priority on developing and implementing the influenza response strategy and identifying and purchasing anti influenza medical products  such as antivirals and vaccines          On April 12  2005  CDC Director Dr  Julie Gerberding testified before the House Appropriations Committee that the U S  stockpile includes treatments for less than two percent of our population  It has been reported that worldwide production capacity of an antiviral  which is effective against many strains of the influenza including avian flu  is quickly being purchased by other countries        Influenza viruses cause 20 000 to 40 000 deaths in the U S  each year   Many of these could be prevented with increased use of the influenza vaccine   Even more alarming is the fact that no currently available vaccine is effective against avian flu  which experts agree could be the basis for an influenza pandemic   Experts estimate that 90 000 to 300 000 Americans and up to 50 million people worldwide could die if left unprotected during an influenza pandemic                                   Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
