         WASHINGTON  D C    Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced that the Drake University will receive a  150 000 grant from the U S  Department of Education  The university will use these funds to provide a M S  degree in Rehabilitation Counseling and a specialization in assistive technology to students who work for the Iowa Vocational Rehabilitation Services  IVRS  or the Iowa Department for the Blind  Harkin secured the funds as the ranking Democrat on the appropriations subcommittee that funds education initiatives                  Qualified rehabilitation counselors and assistive technology specialists help open the doors of opportunity for persons with disabilities     Harkin said     By training rehabilitation counselors and assistive technology specialists  Drake University will be training counselors that can help people achieve their professional and personal goals                This project will build upon Drake  s success at improving students   job development and job placement skills through advanced training in assistive technology  This new project will also strive to identify and recruit people from underrepresented populations to fill positions around the state               The Iowa state VR agencies  Division of Deaf Services  the Region VII RCEP  past IVRS consumers  and Community Rehabilitation Programs were involved in developing this proposal  They will help to recruit students  provide fieldwork placements  and assist in teaching classes            Harkin was the chief sponsor of the Americans with Disabilities Act  ADA   landmark legislation which seeks equality of opportunity  full participation  independent living  and economic self sufficiency for millions of Americans with physical and mental disabilities  Harkin also co authored the Assistive Technology Act of 2004  which improves and extends federal programs that help individuals with disabilities access services and devices                             Originating
