 WASHINGTON  D C   Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced that the Judicial Branch of the State of Iowa will receive  2 5 million over the next five years to implement community based approaches that successfully support families struggling with substance abuse  These funds were made available through the Department of Health and Human Services  Harkin is Chairman of the Senate panel that funds health initiatives   It is a heartbreaking fact that meth has wrecked havoc in a number of Iowa families  Harkin said  I hope that these funds help Iowa identify effective ways to help families overcome substance abuse and put their lives back together  By utilizing resources effectively  I am confident that Iowa can continue to make gains in the fight against meth and develop ways to ensure all Iowa kids can grow up in a safe  healthy home   At five pilot sites across the state  the initiative will investigate how state policy and procedural barriers prevent effective substance abuse treatment  The project will also establish five family drug courts  serving more than 200 families across the state  Additionally  a plan will be developed to replicate the successful elements of this grant throughout the state   Harkin has led the fight in Congress to ensure Iowa receives the support it needs to fight the meth epidemic  He recently introduced the Methamphetamine Abuse Prevention and Treatment Act that would invest in preventing meth use and treating individuals and families that have been devastated by the drug  Harkin also secured funding to lock every anhydrous ammonia tank in Iowa  making it impossible for meth cooks to gain access to this key ingredient for producing the drug       Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
