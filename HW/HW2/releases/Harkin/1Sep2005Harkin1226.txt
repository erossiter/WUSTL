     CEDAR RAPIDS    The deadline for Iowa students applying for acceptance at the United States military academies is fast approaching  according to Iowa Senator Tom Harkin  D   Young men and women hoping to apply for a nomination to the classes entering service academies in summer of 2006 must have all of their application materials submitted to Senator Harkin s Cedar Rapids office by Friday  September 30  2005              Senator Harkin will nominate up to ten Iowans for every opening he has at the academies  with final selection for admission being made by the individual academy                  Each year I look forward to recommending so many outstanding young Iowans to the service academies   It is a genuine honor     Harkin said    It is truly inspiring to see that sohow many young Iowans are eager to serve our nation by entering the military               Our nation s military academies offer outstanding educational opportunities for young Iowans interested in military service   Students can specialize in a variety of fields at the U S  Military Academy at West Point  the U S  Naval Academy at Annapolis  the Air Force Academy at Colorado Springs and the Merchant Marine Academy at Kings Point            Harkin enjoyed a successful military career in the Navy and served as a jet pilot after graduating from Iowa State University in 1962            Most state high school guidance counselors have information about application procedures prospective candidates must follow   Senator Harkin uses a competitive method to screen applicants  including the evaluation of ACT and or SAT scores  high school grade point average  leadership abilities  and physical aptitude   Harkin has also appointed an academy selection committee that conducts personal interviews with academy finalists            Interested individuals should contact Sen  Harkin s Cedar Rapids office immediately to obtain an application packet   Questions regarding the academy
