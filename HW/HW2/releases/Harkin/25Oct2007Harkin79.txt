 WASHINGTON  D C   As part of his ongoing efforts to improve early child development  Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced that Drake University received  2 306 363 to continue Head Start Full Year  Part Day Handicapped Programs  As Chairman of the Senate panel that funds education initiatives  Harkin secured a  200 million increase in funding for Head Start programs in this year s Senate appropriations bill   Each year  Head Start helps thousands of Iowa children develop basic learning skills that will help them thrive in school  Harkin said  Making investments in education initiatives like Head Start is key to helping Iowa kids build a strong foundation for future success   Since 1965  Head Start has served more than 20 million low income children and their families  According to studies  at risk kids who were enrolled in quality early childhood programs like Head Start are 25 percent less likely to be retained a grade  They are less likely to end up on welfare or in jail  Additionally  Head Start graduates are also one third more likely to graduate from high school       Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
