 WASHINGTON  D C   Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  announced today that Waterloo  Des Moines  Marion County and Sioux City received  2 072 083 in grants from the U S  Department of Justice  DOJ  Office of Community Oriented Policing Services  COPS   The funds were awarded under COPS Methamphetamine Initiative and the COPS Methamphetamine Training and Technical Assistance programs  These programs focus on training and technical assistance  equipment purchasing  officer overtime  community outreach  and other approved costs of local law enforcement agencies   Meth continues to plague America destroying lives  families and entire communities  said Harkin  These funds will help Iowas narcotics officers obtain the tools they need to fight meth across the state   The COPS program provides law enforcement agencies with an array of community policing training and technical assistance resources  Many police chiefs  sheriffs and criminal justice experts have credited community policing  a strategy that uses partnerships between law enforcement officers and the people they serve  with the reduction in violent crime over the past several years   Harkin has been a long time supporter of the COPS Program  He has led the fight in Congress to ensure Iowa receives the support it needs to fight the meth epidemic   COPS Methamphetamine Initiative  Used to help targeted areas combat the manufacture  use  and distribution of meth  as well as to collaborate with other entities in the prevention and treatment of meth abuse   Waterloo  369 366  Des Moines  406 484  Marion County  406 852  Sioux City  390 784  COPS Methamphetamine Training and Technical Assistance Program  Used to increase the capacity of training and technical assistance providers to develop a more informed and engaged community with regards to meth use and abuse   Sioux City  498 597      Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
