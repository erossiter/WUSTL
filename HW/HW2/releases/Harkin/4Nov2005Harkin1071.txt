      WASHINGTON  D C     Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced the University of Iowa will receive funds for a new Senator Paul D  Wellstone Muscular Dystrophy Cooperative Research Center from the National Institutes of Health  NIH   Harkin sponsored legislation naming Muscular Dystrophy  MD  research centers after Senator Paul D  Wellstone  D MN   a leader in the fight against MD  after his death in 2002  Harkin secured the funds for these centers as the ranking Democrat on the subcommittee that funds NIH                I am please that these funds will help the University of Iowa continue leading the fight against muscular dystrophy     Harkin said    Senator Wellstone  s passionate leadership in the fight against muscular dystrophy was truly inspirational  and it is deeply gratifying to see research centers around the country carry on his legacy                  The University of Iowa  s research center will explore therapeutic strategies for different muscular dystrophies  The center will research muscle membrane maintenance and repair  as well as people with Fukutin Related Protein MD and the development of a mouse model for further investigation of MD  The center  s cores will serve as a national resource for human muscle biopsies  fibroblast cell cultures and embryonic stem cells for MD researchers and will provide advanced services for diagnosing the different dystrophies                An additional two Wellstone research centers will be started at the University of Pennsylvania and Washington  D C  s Children s National Medical Center  creating a total of six centers nationwide  The centers spring from the Muscular Dystrophy Community Assistance  Research and Education  MD CARE  Act passed by Congress in 2001  The centers work individually and collaboratively  and they are required to make core resources or services available to the national muscular dystrophy research community                Muscular Dystrophy is characterized by progressive weakness and degeneration of the skeletal or voluntary muscles which control movement  Researchers at the Senator Paul D  Wellstone Centers study various forms of MD  including Duchenne Becker Muscular Dystrophy  Myotonic Dystrophy  Facioscapulohumeral Dystrophy  Limb Girdle Muscular Dystrophy  and others  MD can affect people of all ages  Although some forms first become apparent in infancy or childhood  others may not appear until middle age or later                                  Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
