    WASHINGTON  D C     As part of his continuing support for rural Iowa  Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  announced today that the Iowa Department of Natural Resources will receive  163 662 in grants from the United States Department of Agriculture  s National Park Service   Harkin is the Ranking Democrat on the Senate Agriculture  Nutrition  and Forestry Committee            These funds will help communities make improvements to local recreational facilities     said Harkin      Supporting parks and recreational facilities increases local tourism and provides excellent venues for people to exercise and enjoy themselves                The grants will be given to the Iowa Department of Natural Resources  DNR  for two state parks                Backbone State Park in Delaware County     66 662 grant to assist in the replacement and installation of new sewer lines                Jester Park in Dallas County     97 000 grant to assist in the development of a Natural Playscape and trail enhancements  The grant will be administered by the Polk County Conservation Board           The funds are made available through the National Park Service  s Land and Water Conservation Fund Program   The Land and Water Conservation Fund Program provides matching grants to states to develop public outdoor recreational areas and facilities                           Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
