        The Byrne grants are critical to Iowa  s neighborhoods and communities     said Harkin      By targeting illegal drug activity  these funds give law enforcement officials in Waterloo and Cedar Falls additional resources to strike at one of the major factors behind crime in Iowa and across America             These funds will benefit the Tri County Drug Task Force  a multi jurisdictional task force responsible for the investigation and prosecution of drug related crimes   The tri county area ranks third in Iowa in methamphetamine lab investigations   In 2004 the task force investigated 332 cases  made 257 arrests  and seized  77 724 in cash and an estimated  998 015 in drugs                   The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime   Grant funds can be used for state and local initiatives for any of the following purpose areas   1  law enforcement programs  2  prosecution and court programs  3  prevention and education programs  4  corrections and community corrections programs  5  drug treatment programs  and 6  planning  evaluation  and technology improvement programs             Last year Iowa received  5 3 million from the Byrne grant program   Unfortunately  the President  s 2006 law enforcement budget proposal includes a 100  cut for the program   Harkin  a leader in the fight to preserve the Byrne grant program  criticized the President  s law enforcement budget  saying that proposed cuts would slash jobs and undermine law enforcement  s efforts to fight crime and drug problems such as methamphetamine throughout Iowa                               Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
