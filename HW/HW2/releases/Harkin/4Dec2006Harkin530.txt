      WASHINGTON D C      Senator Tom Harkin  D IA   a longtime advocate of curbing certain marketing practices targeting children  today released the following statement on the American Academy of Pediatrics   revised policy position    Children  Adolescents  and Advertising               I commend the American Academy of Pediatrics for taking a strong stance on the indisputable public health impacts that marketing is having on our children  AAP  s new recommendations represent an important step in recognizing that we must take action now to curb the growing obesity epidemic among kids  to improve nutrition and eating habits  and do more to discourage alcohol and tobacco use             Harkin has introduced legislation that would restore the Federal Trade Commission  s  FTC  authority to regulate marketing directed at children  This measure would give the FTC the power to restrict the advertising of junk food to children under age 18  The bill would also address the onslaught of junk food marketing to children in schools  giving the U S Secretary of Agriculture the authority to prohibit all junk food advertising in schools where parents are not present           Harkin is the Senate leader in federal efforts to combat the obesity epidemic by promoting wellness and healthy habits among adults and children  In addition to his efforts on food marketing  Harkin has introduced the Healthy Lifestyles and Prevention  HELP  Act  which takes a comprehensive approach to promoting healthy lifestyles and preventing chronic disease like obesity                           Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
