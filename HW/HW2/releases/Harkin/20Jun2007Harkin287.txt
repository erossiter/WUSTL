 WASHINGTON  D C   Senator Tom Harkin   D IA   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee   today announced he has secured  250 000 for  the Driftless Area National Wildlife Refuge as part of the FY 2008  Interior Appropriations Bill  The  bill is expected to be approved by the full Senate Appropriations Committee on Thursday   It will then need to be approved by both the House and Senate before it becomes law   By working to preserve endangered species  the  Driftless Area National Wildlife Refuge helps to safeguard Iowas biodiversity   Harkin said  I am pleased that I  am able to help secure this assistance as a senior member of the Senate  Appropriations Committee   The purposes and goals of the Driftless Area National  Wildlife Refuge  DANWR  are directly tied to recovery plans  which describe the  steps needed to recover and conserve two endangered species  the Northern  monkshood and the Iowa Pleistocene snail  Because of the fragile nature of their  habitat and the low number of populations for each of these species  the primary  recovery goal for both species is protecting and conserving the majority of  remaining populations and their habitats  Without some form of protection   populations of these species could be lost from one event       Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
