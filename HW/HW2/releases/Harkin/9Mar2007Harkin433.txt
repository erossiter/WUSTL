              WASHINGTON  DC   Senator Tom Harkin  D IA   Chairman of the Senate Committee on Agriculture  Nutrition and Forestry  met this week with leaders of major national foundations to generate ideas and improve government nonprofit coordination in rural development   In the new farm bill  Harkin is working to develop a Rural Collaborative Investment Program  which would provide assistance to rural areas                             Creating job equality and improving the quality of life for rural America will be a priority in the new farm bill   said Harkin    We can accomplish this goal by devising rural development strategies that combine the financial resources and creativity of the nonprofit sector in partnership with USDA and other government programs useful in rural areas   We had a productive meeting and I look forward to putting our plans into action                                Harkin is working to create a Rural Collaborative Investment Program   a framework to address the very significant and ongoing rural disadvantage in federal funding commitments to community resources and economic development   Communities in self selected geographical areas would develop local partnerships among government  business and nonprofit organizations to develop and implement comprehensive plans to improve their areas with the help of federal assistance                              Representatives from the Council on Foundations  the Northwest Area Foundation  the Annie E  Casey Foundation and others attended the meeting   Senator Debbie Stabenow of Michigan  the Chair of the Committee s Rural Revitalization  Conservation  Forestry and Credit Subcommittee  joined Chairman Harkin in holding the meeting                                       Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
