         WASHINGTON  D C     As part continuing support for Iowa education  Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  announced today that BCLUM Community School District in Conrad received  157  976 and Fairfield Community School District received  239 140 to initiate  expand  or improve physical education programs  These funds were made available through the U S  Department of Education  s Carol M  White Physical Education Program  Harkin is the ranking Democrat on the Senate panel that funds education initiatives            Physical education should be a key component of Iowa kids   education     Harkin said    Teaching kids how to exercise when they are young will help them to live longer and healthier lives             The Carol M  White Physical Education Program aims to aid local education agencies and community based organizations in securing equipment  support  and teacher and staff training that will help them meet their states   standards for physical education  In order to receive funding  applicants must design a physical education program that meets state standards and incorporates additional elements which help to improve children  s physical well being and development          Harkin has been a long time advocate for initiatives that help children lead healthier lives  As part of the 2002 farm bill  Harkin created the Harkin Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Program which provides free fresh fruits and vegetables to children in schools across the country  including 25 schools in Iowa  Additionally  in 2005  28 public and private organizations in Iowa received nearly  3 million in Harkin wellness grants to develop community approaches to promote prevention and wellness  Harkin also sponsored the 2005 Healthy Lifestyles and Prevention  HeLP  America Act  comprehensive legislation that seeks to combat obesity and other preventable chronic conditions by providing all sectors   child care centers  schools  workplaces  and communities    with the tools and incentives
