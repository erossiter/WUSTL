      WASHINGTON  D C     As part of his continuing support for Iowa  s Community Health Centers  Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced that Community Health Center of Fort Dodge received  643 500 from the U S  Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  to support its health services  Harkin is a senior member of the Senate panel that funds health programs                  Community health centers improve access to health care for Iowans who lack adequate health insurance     said Harkin    These funds will help to ensure that all Iowans get the quality  affordable healthcare they need                Senator Harkin was instrumental in encouraging the community of Ft  Dodge to apply to become a community health center and in obtaining a  30 000 grant to help them open the center  The Community Health Center of Fort Dodge plans to serve approximately 5 000 patients  Fifty five percent of the patient visits will be dedicated to serving the uninsured   When the Center first opens  it will provide the following services  primary medical care  family planning  immunizations  and nutrition counseling                As ranking Democrat of the subcommittee that funds health programs  Harkin has been an ardent supporter of Iowa  s 12 federally funded community health centers   Harkin has obtained funds for the facilities   construction  equipment purchases  and dental expansions through his work on the Labor  HHS and Education Appropriations subcommittee  In FY 2005  Harkin also secured funds for a statewide Health IT system that will bring cutting edge electronic health records and e prescribing technology to Iowa  s community health centers                                Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
