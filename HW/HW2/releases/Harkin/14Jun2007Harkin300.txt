 WASHINGTON  D C    Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today released the following statement in response to an announcement by Kelloggs that it plans to drastically change the products and methods used in marketing to children under age 12  The companys decisions will be guided by a nutrition criteria that will determine which products will be marketed to children on television  print  radio and the Internet   Todays announcement by the Kellogg Company has the potential to be a defining moment in our nations fight against childhood obesity  said Harkin  Americas young people make up the most overweight generation in history  and risk having a shorter lifespan than their parents  Every one of us has a role to play in this public health crisis  parents  schools  government and the private sector  I applaud Kelloggs for raising the bar and acting responsibly on behalf of our youngest citizens  I hope that other food companies follow suit   Harkin recently joined Senator Sam Brownback  R KS  and others to launch the Task Force on Media and Childhood Obesity  an innovative public private partnership aimed at combating the clear and present health dangers stemming from poor nutrition and childhood obesity   Harkin is the Senate leader in federal efforts to combat the obesity epidemic by promoting wellness and healthy habits among adults and children  He has authored the Healthy Lifestyles and Prevention  HeLP  America Act  which takes a comprehensive approach to addressing the challenges posed by chronic disease and focuses on the power of prevention   In addition to his efforts on food marketing  Harkin is the architect of the Harkin Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Program    now operating in eight states and on three Indian reservations    which provides free fresh fruits and vegetables to children in schools       Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
