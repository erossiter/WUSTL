   WASHINGTON  D C     Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  announced today that the Iowa Department of Public Health has received a  1 183 972 grant from the U S  Department of Justice  DOJ  to continue implementing a jail based substance abuse treatment program in Polk  Scott  and Woodbury counties             It is crucial that we do everything we can to ensure that Iowans receive the treatment they need to overcome their addiction to meth and other drugs     said Harkin     By helping inmates live drug free  this treatment program gives them the chance to start a new life              Senator Harkin has been a long time supporter of the Iowa Department of Health  s drug fighting efforts  obtaining  2 5 million for the program over the past 4 years   The program aims to increase offenders   knowledge of substance abuse and to reduce anti social thinking  criminal behavior  and the negative effects of substance abuse  It also aims to reduce the number of offenders who violate their supervision status by returning to substance abuse             This money and this program are greatly needed     Polk County Sheriff Dennis Anderson said    People get treatment that would not have received it in the past  This is one way to fight the war on drugs  It just makes sense and it works              Thus far  the Iowa Department of Public Health  s jail based substance abuse treatment program in Polk County has achieved impressive results  Out of the 64 people who successfully completed the program  78 percent were drug free  95 percent had no further arrests  and 57 percent were employed full time after six months                   Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
