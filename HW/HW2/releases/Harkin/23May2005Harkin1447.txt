        The Byrne grants are critical to Iowa  s neighborhoods and communities     said Harkin      By targeting illegal drug activity  these funds give law enforcement officials in Keokuk additional resources to strike at one of the major factors behind crime in Iowa and across America             These funds will benefit the Lee County Narcotics Task Force   The Lee County Narcotics Task Force is a multi jurisdictional task force comprised of the Keokuk Police Department  Lee County Sheriff  s Department  and the Fort Madison Police Department   The task force was established almost 12 years ago and continues to serve the citizens of Lee County by concentrating its efforts on the control of narcotics trafficking               The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime   Grant funds can be used for state and local initiatives for any of the following purpose areas   law enforcement programs  prosecution and court programs  prevention and education programs  corrections and community corrections programs  drug treatment programs  and planning  evaluation  and technology improvement programs                 Last year Iowa received  5 3 million from the Byrne grant program   Unfortunately the President  s 2006 law enforcement budget proposal eliminates the program   Harkin  a leader in the fight to preserve the Byrne grant program  criticized the President  s law enforcement budget  saying that proposed cuts would slash jobs and undermine law enforcement  s efforts to fight crime and drug problems such as methamphetamine throughout Iowa                                    Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
