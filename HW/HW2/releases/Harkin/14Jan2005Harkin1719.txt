        America  s veterans stood strong and defended our nation in a difficult time of war    for this we owe them a tremendous debt  Veterans should not be forced to wait years to receive commendations they earned in their courageous service to our country  One of the greatest things about my job as senator is that I am able to assist Iowa veterans who have not received the recognition they deserve     Harkin said          Leo J  Goetzinger of Cedar Rapids      As a Corporal with the U S  Army  Groetzinger was a truck driver with the Headquarters Company 36th Tank Battalion in Europe during World War II and with the 34th Ordinance Depot Company in Korea   Goetzinger will be presented with the Good Conduct Medal  American Campaign Medal  European African Middle Eastern Campaign Medal  World War II Victory Medal  Army of Occupation Medal with German Clasp  National Defense Service Medal  Korean Service Medal  United National Service Medal  World War II Honorable Service Lapel Button  and Marksman Badge with Rifle Bar           Armond Scheiner of Vinton      Scheiner was a Lieutenant with the U S  Navy during World War II   As executive officer for his squadron in the Pacific Theater  Scheiner served aboard the USS Louisville   He will be awarded the Navy Air medal  Navy Honorable Discharge Button  World War II Honorable Service Lapel Pin  Asiatic Pacific Campaign Medal with one Silver Star and three Bronze Stars  American Campaign Medal  and the World War II Victory Medal          Gerald Lee Gardner of Cedar Rapids  deceased        Gardner served as a Private First Class with the U S  Marine Corps in the Vietnam War from 1969 to 1970   He was killed in action on February 2  1970 at the age of twenty when an enemy rocket hit his camp at Quang Nam Province in South Vietnam   A Purple Heart  National Defense Service Medal  Vietnam Service Medal with One Bronze Star  Combat Action Ribbon  and Gold Star Lapel Button will be presented to Gardner  s family          Harold Green of Oelwein  deceased       Green served in the U S  Navy during the Vietnam War from 1965 to 1968   During his time aboard the USS Okinawa  Green served as an electrician  motion picture operator  fireman  and petty officer   Green passed away in June of 2004    A National Defense Service Medal  Vietnam Service Medal with One Bronze Campaign Star  Republic of Vietnam Meritorious Unit Citation  and Republic of Vietnam Campaign Medal with Device will be presented to Green  s family          Harkin  who is a Navy veteran  has been a leader in the Senate in calling on the Department of Defense to improve the process for issuing military medals  In 1999 Harkin added an amendment to the Defense Authorization bill which directed the Secretary of Defense to eliminate the backlog in decoration requests  Since then  delays in receiving medals have been greatly reduced           For help in obtaining military medals contact any of Senator Harkin  s Iowa
