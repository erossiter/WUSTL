     WASHINGTON  D C    Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced that the Iowa Coalition Against Domestic Violence is receiving  892 475 from the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  Office of Violence Against Women  The funds will help strengthen services for victims of domestic violence  Harkin is a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee            I am pleased that Iowa will receive funds to support victims of domestic violence     Harkin said    Violence against women and children remains a major problem  but with this survivor centered approach we can help these individuals get the support they need to overcome these terrible acts              The Iowa Coalition Against Domestic Violence  ICADV   the University of Northern Iowa Interactive Services Project  the Domestic Violence Intervention Program  the Seeds of Hope  the Domestic Sexual Assault Outreach Center  the Waypoint Services for Women Children and Families  and the Crisis Intervention Services will collaborate to continue to address the unmet needs of Battered Substance Abusing Women  BSAW  within thirteen Iowa counties   They will work together to  establish new and enhance existing partnerships between domestic violence programs and substance abuse treatment agencies  develop and promote a strong coordinated community response to address domestic violence services and unmet needs  and develop a curriculum and facilitate a bi weekly group for BSAW          The Rural Domestic Violence and Child Victimization Enforcement Grant Programs aims to strengthen the safety of victims of domestic violence  dating violence and child abuse living in rural areas by creating and enhancing collaborative partnerships between criminal justice agencies  victim service providers and community organizations                                    Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
