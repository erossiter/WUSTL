                  WASHINGTON D C       Senator Tom Harkin  D IA   a longtime supporter of Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grants and co sponsor of the legislation restoring the funding authorization level for the program  praised its passage out of the Senate Judiciary Committee today  The legislation would authorize  1 095 billion in Byrne funding nationally for the upcoming year                            Byrne grants are a critical tool for our law enforcement officers in the fight to combat illegal drug activity   Harkin said   It is imperative we give local police the resources they need to fight crime and drug activity in their local communities                            Nationally  from 2003 2006  local law enforcement funding for the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program has been slashed by almost sixty percent   Funding plunged from  1 billion dollars in 2003 to just  416 million in 2006   In the past year  Iowa sustained a  2 million cut  a full 42 percent of Byrne funding   At the same time  violent crime in the United States increased 2 5 percent in 2005  and an additional 3 7 percent in the first half of 2006  the largest increase in fifteen years   The increase was much more severe in the meth plagued Midwest  with violent crime up 5 7 percent                         It is critical that we stop this rise in meth driven crime   The way to do that is to get drug task force officers back on the streets and get regional drug task forces back up and running after three years of sustained cuts   This bill is a first step in making that happen   Harkin said                                        Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
