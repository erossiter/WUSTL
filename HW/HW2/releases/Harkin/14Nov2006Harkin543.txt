      WASHINGTON  D C     As part of his continuing effort to improve Iowans   access to affordable housing  Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced that Heartland Hill Habitat for Humanity near Waverly received  198 000 from the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD   These funds will be used to rehabilitate old  abandoned military houses that will eventually be sold to low income families  Harkin secured these funds in the FY 2006 Transportation  Treasury  Judiciary  Housing and Urban Development Appropriations Bill  Harkin is a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee            These funds will help Habitat for Humanity rehabilitate houses  providing affordable homes for Iowa families and helping to revitalize the surrounding area     Harkin said     I have always believed that quality and affordable housing is an important part of the American dream             Habitat for Humanity will fix up about 20 houses  renovating one house at a time  The money from the sale of each house will be used to support the rehabilitation of the next house  therefore helping to accelerate the development process                           Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
