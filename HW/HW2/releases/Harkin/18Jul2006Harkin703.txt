   WASHINGTON  D C     Senator Tom Harkin  D IA   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations committee  today announced over  6 million to support statewide  transportation  economic revitalization  and housing initiatives were included in the FY 2007 Transportation  Treasury  Judiciary  Housing and Urban Development  TTHUD  Appropriations measure   Both the House and full Senate must approve the bill before it becomes law            Providing investments in transportation  economic development  and affordable housing will give a needed boost to Iowa  s economy     Harkin said    I am pleased to have secured these essential funds to continue improving the quality of life for all Iowans             The bill provides  4 2 billion nationwide for the Community Development Block Grant  CDBG  program  CDBG funds help provide housing  a suitable living environment  and increased economic opportunities primarily for persons of low and moderate income  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee that funds HUD  Harkin worked to restore funds for the CDBG program in the 2007 Appropriations bill after the President proposed that the program be sharply reduced   The President also called for changes in how funds are awarded to states that would have been harmful to Iowa   No such formula changes were agreed to in the appropriations bill          Statewide Funding            Main Street Iowa Program   800 000  These funds will help restore historic downtown areas and develop affordable housing above downtown storefronts  Grants are competitively awarded to small communities by the Iowa Department of Economic Development  Harkin has secured a total of  2 5 million in four prior years for the Main Street Iowa program          Assisted Living facilities   300 000   These funds will be used by the Iowa Finance Authority to leverage the construction of additional assisted living facilities around the state   These facilities help older Iowans maintain the maximum level of independence they can  avoiding the use of nursing homes            5 million to replace buses in Iowa  s urban and rural transit systems  including those that follow a fixed route and those that provide point to point service  often for people with disabilities  Over sixty percent of Iowa  s bus fleet has currently exceeded their expected life span         Iowa Air Services  Burlington  Fort Dodge and Mason City will continue to receive assistance for their local commercial air services   Funding for these local air services were restored in the spending bill approved today and blocked an Administration proposal that would have slashed those funds                      Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
