already been waiting almost five years  it is simply unfathomable that NIOSH and the Advisory Board could not complete and transmit a simple letter in less than a month             As a result of endless delays in the dose reconstruction process  last year Congress determined that the Secretary of HHS would only have 30 days to act upon a recommendation of the advisory board  and that failure to act would result in the creation of a cohort   However  this 30 day clock does not begin ticking until the recommendation reaches the Secretary  s desk   Following that  Congress has 30 days to act   If no actions are taken to block the board  s recommendation  the cohort will take effect making IAAP workers from 1949 to 1974 that have one of 22 specified cancers automatically eligible to receive  150 000            Harkin  s office coordinated the first ever petition requesting that workers be added to the Special Exposure Cohort  SEC  and presented it to NIOSH on June 14  2004   Along with Mallikrodt in St  Louis  IAAP was the first facility to be considered for immediate compensation by the advisory board             In order to quickly move the IAAP petition  Harkin successfully added an amendment in June 2004 to the Defense Appropriations bill that speeds up efforts to administratively add workers to the cohort if recommended by HHS   Harkin also introduced an amendment in June 2004 that would allow IAAP workers to be automatically eligible for compensation   However  Republicans in Congress stripped the amendment from the Defense Authorization Bill            IAAP was the   forgotten    nuclear weapons plant until Harkin brought it to the government  s attention after being contacted by Robert Anderson  a constituent and former IAAP worker who has non Hodgkins lymphoma  in 1997   Anderson presented the petition to the board today    Harkin was a Senate sponsor of the original Energy Employees Occupational Illness Compensation Act  EEOICPA  in 2000 and of a bill to amend it last fall  and he has obtained government funding for health screening of workers at the plant          A copy of the letter  1 page  follows Director Howard and Chairman Ziemer                      As you both know  on February 9  2005 in St  Louis the NIOSH Advisory Board on Radiation and Worker Health voted 7 to 0 to recommend to Health and Human Services Secretary Michael Leavitt that former nuclear weapons workers at the Iowa Army Ammunition Plant who have developed cancer be placed in a Special Exposure Cohort  SEC    Inclusion in an SEC will entitle the workers to receive automatic compensation due to an inability to accurately reconstruct the radiation doses they received            Inexplicably  more than a month after the action of the NIOSH advisory board  the letter of recommendation has not yet reached Secretary Leavitt  s desk   I am sure you are aware that as a result of  endless delays in the dose reconstruction process  last year Congress determined that the Secretary of HHS would only have 30 days to act upon a recommendation of the advisory board  and that failure to act would result in the creation of a cohort   However  this 30 day clock does not begin ticking until the recommendation reaches the Secretary  s desk          I hereby request that you formally notify Secretary Leavitt of the decision of the advisory board without further delay   Iowa workers have already been waiting almost five years   It is simply unfathomable that NIOSH and the Advisory Board could not complete and transmit a
