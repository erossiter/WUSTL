   WASHINGTON  D C   Senator Tom Harkin  D IA   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations committee and its joint conference committee with the House  announced this morning that it has approved  1 75 million for transportation and economic development projects in Sioux City  The funding will be included in the conference report of the 2008 Transportation  Housing and Urban Development  THUD  Appropriations and will now seek final approval from the U S  House and the Senate before making its way to the Presidents desk  the President has threatened to block this bill     These funds will help to prevent an increase in Sioux City residents property taxes and ensure that the travelers who rely on the I 29 thoroughfare have a safe  efficient roadway for years to come  said Harkin  We are also assisting with the long overdue demolition of the KD meatpacking facility  an unsafe structure that should be cleared from the Sioux City landscape  I look forward to seeing the impacts this funding will have on Northwest Iowa     Statewide  the bill provides more than  15 million for specific Iowa initiatives  including  4 69 million to replace buses in Iowas urban and rural transit system   536 000 for Harkins Main Street project to help restore historic downtown areas  and  201 000 for Homeward  a rural electric cooperative focused on providing housing construction to rural areas     A breakdown of Sioux Citys funds include      1 05 million for improvements to I 29 in Sioux City  Specifically  these funds will be used to cover part of the cost of moving utilities  Without this funding  the cost of moving these utilities would fall upon Sioux City taxpayers      700 000 to demolish KD Station in Sioux City  This large  meatpacking facility was converted for retail use in recent years and has now been abandoned  Sioux City has condemned the structure because it is extremely unsafe       Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
