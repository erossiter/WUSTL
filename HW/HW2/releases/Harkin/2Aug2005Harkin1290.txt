        The Byrne grants are critical to Iowa  s neighborhoods and communities     said Harkin      By targeting illegal drug activity  these funds give law enforcement officials in Dubuque additional resources to strike at one of the major factors behind crime in Iowa and across America             The City of Burlington and Des Moines County will share the funds  with  22 305 going to the County and  13 127 for city use   Des Moines County will use its funds to support salaries and benefits for three members of the Southeast Iowa Narcotics Task Force  thus enabling to the task force to continue its progressive approach to combating narcotics use  Burlington will purchase upgraded in car computers for the patrol division to increase officers   time in the community          The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime   Grant funds can be used for state and local initiatives for any of the following purpose areas   1  law enforcement programs  2  prosecution and court programs  3  prevention and education programs  4  corrections and community corrections programs  5  drug treatment programs  and 6  planning  evaluation  and technology improvement programs             Last year Iowa received  5 3 million from the Byrne grant program   Unfortunately  the President  s 2006 law enforcement budget proposal eliminates the program   Harkin  a leader in the fight to preserve the Byrne grant program criticized the President  s law enforcement budget  saying these cuts would undermine law enforcement efforts to fight crime and drug problems like methamphetamine throughout Iowa                                        Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
