   WASHINGTON  D C     Senator Tom Harkin  D IA   senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that the Department of Transportation is awarding three grants totaling  277 608 for the Loess Hills National Scenic Byway to boost marketing of the byway  implement a corridor management plan  and create a welcome center interpretive project     This funding will be used to boost the profile of one of Iowas most beautiful thoroughfares and will help draw new visitors to our state  Harkin said  Investments like this are key to further developing tourism in Iowa     The Iowa Department of Transportation received a  200 000 grant for projects at the Harrison County Historical Village and Interpretive Center  This funding will be used for the development of a multimedia presentation on Loess Hills  the design and construction of a 1 mile Americans with Disabilities Act  ADA  accessible trail  and to design and contract an elevated ADA accessible observation deck overlooking the Loess Hills National Scenic Byway     I am especially pleased that some of this funding will make it easier for every American to enjoy the beauty of the Loess Hills Scenic Byway  said Harkin      27 588 will be used to formulate strategic planning  coordinate volunteer activities  and update a byway brochure  Another grant of  50 020 will fund the development and distribution of promotional and educational materials about the Loess Hills National Scenic Byway     The Federal Highway Administrations scenic byways grants recognize  preserve and enhance America s scenic roads and highways throughout the United States       Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
