   WASHINGTON  D C    Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  announced today that the city of Des Moines has received a  211 816 grant from the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  to fight crime                Byrne grants are critical to Iowa  s neighborhoods and communities     said Harkin      By targeting illegal drug activity  these funds give law enforcement officials in Des Moines additional resources to strike at one of the major factors behind crime in Iowa and across America               The Des Moines police department will use these funds to purchase equipment for officer safety  evidence collection and investigation  and to upgrade its indoor target system  The department will also purchase an all terrain vehicle  ATV  to improve police services in the downtown riverwalk area  The Polk County Sheriff  who will receive 15 percent of the JAG funds  will use the funds to support a new computer aided dispatch system            The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime   Grant funds can be used for state and local initiatives for any of the following purpose areas   1  law enforcement programs  2  prosecution and court programs  3  prevention and education programs  4  corrections and community corrections programs  5  drug treatment programs  and 6  planning  evaluation  and technology improvement programs               Last year  Iowa received  5 3 million from the Byrne grant program   Unfortunately  the President  s 2006 law enforcement budget proposal eliminates the program  Harkin has worked in the Senate to restore over  600 million to the Byrne program for the upcoming year   Harkin has criticized the President  s law enforcement budget  saying these cuts would undermine law enforcement efforts to fight crime and drug problems like methamphetamine throughout Iowa
