                  WASHINGTON  D C    Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced that the Iowa Center for Research on Botanical Dietary Supplements at Iowa State University will receive approximately  4 2 million over three years to study botanicals used as ingredients in dietary supplements  These funds were made available through the National Institutes of Health s  NIH  Office of Dietary Supplements  Harkin is the Chairman of the Senate Appropriations panel that funds NIH                               Funding medical research is the first step toward the technological and scientific breakthroughs that provide hope for many Americans   Harkin said    I am proud that Iowa State University has received this funding to study the composition and potential health benefits of certain dietary supplements  components  Hopefully  this research will lead to discoveries that can help many Americans                            Specifically  this study will investigate the anti viral and anti inflammatory properties of Hypericum  St  John s wort   Prunella  Self heal  and several types of Echinacea  Echinacea and Hypericum are two of the nation s top selling herbal dietary supplements  Before the Iowa Center of Research on Botanical Dietary Supplements was established  however  there were few scientific studies to understand the plants and their effect on human health                            Harkin has been a long time supporter of the National Institutes of Health  the federal agency responsible for medical research  Beginning in 1998  Harkin successfully led the effort to double the agency s funding over five years  This year  Harkin has already held three hearings in which he asked NIH institute and center directors about the funding they need to continue aggressively pursuing medical research                                                      Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
