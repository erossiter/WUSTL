                        WASHINGTON  D C    A group of bipartisan Senators led by Senate Committee on Agriculture  Nutrition and Forestry Chairman Tom Harkin  D IA  today urged the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  to expedite the rulemaking process to carry out the Livestock Mandatory Reporting Act of 1999   Congress passed the bill reauthorizing the law last year  which requires the USDA to issue a final rule   Until that rule is final  price reporting by packers will remain only on a voluntary basis                            The Livestock Mandatory Reporting Act adopted by Congress in 1999 requires packers  processors  and importers to provide critical price  contracting  supply and demand information to USDA  which uses the information to create price reports for livestock producers                        In a letter to USDA Secretary Mike Johanns  the lawmakers also pressed the agency to implement recommendations from the Government Accountability Office  GAO  to improve transparency  operation and function of the reporting program   Senator Harkin commissioned the GAO report  and released it in December 2005                               Mandatory price reporting is critical to providing much needed transparency in livestock markets and helping to level the playing field in the access to information for livestock producers across the country   wrote the lawmakers    The Agricultural Marketing Service  AMS  is writing a rule  but until an interim final rule or final rule is published  the mandatory reporting system will remain a voluntary program    We ask that you expedite this process and publish an interim final rule without delay                            The full text of the letter follows               May 14  2007                 The Honorable Mike Johanns                 Secretary of Agriculture                      200 A Jamie L  Whitten Building                Washington  D C  20250                    Dear Secretary Johanns                          Mandatory price reporting is critical to providing much needed transparency in livestock markets and helping to level the playing field in the access to information for livestock producers across the country   Under the Livestock Mandatory Reporting Act  packers  processors and importers are required to report critical market data to USDA  which is then calculated  published and made available to the industry   The reported prices and other information have become an integral part of setting prices paid for livestock in the United States  both under contracts and in the open market                       Last year Congress passed the Livestock Mandatory Reporting Act  which was signed into law by President Bush on October 5  2006   This reauthorization reinstated requirements enacted in 1999 with certain changes   The Agricultural Marketing Service  AMS  is writing a rule  but until an interim final rule or final rule is published  the mandatory reporting system will remain a voluntary program    We ask that you expedite this process and publish an interim final rule without delay                        In response to letters dated September 20 and October 5  2006 written by some members of the Senate Committee on Agriculture  Nutrition and Forestry  USDA detailed plans to implement recommendations from the Government Accountability Office  GAO  through administrative procedures   GAO recommendations will improve transparency and the operation and function of the Livestock Mandatory Reporting program    We ask that USDA fully carry out those recommendations and additional suggested changes                            We appreciate your attention to these matters and ask that you expedite the rulemaking process to carry out the LMR Act   Thank you for your cooperation and assistance                     Sincerely yours                    Tom Harkin                                                                                 Chuck
