      WASHINGTON  D C     Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today praised passage of an amendment to the Budget Resolution to provide critical dollars for the fight against meth   The amendment provides  99 million in new funding for the Meth Hot Spots program authorized in the Combat Meth bill that passed the Senate last week   The Combat Meth law established national restrictions on the sale of pseudoephedrine   Harkin co sponsored the amendment along with Senators Dianne Feinstein  D CA  and Jim Talent  R MO             President Bush  s FY07 Budget proposal cut resources for the Meth Hot Spot program by  23 million nationwide and restricted to lab clean up   Since the state of Iowa  s law limiting the sale of pseudoephedrine is even stricter than the federal law  the practical effect of the President  s proposal would be the elimination of the Meth Hot Spots program in the state            I am pleased that all the members of the Seante who worked to pass the Combat Meth Act have also come through to provide the critical dollars to pay for our continuing battle against meth   We cannot afford to slash funds which have been crucial to the fight against methamphetamine     said Harkin           Senator Harkin led the fight in Congress to ensure Iowa receives the support it needs to fight the Meth epidemic  Harkin continues to fight for additional resources to support Iowa law enforcement  s pursuit against this deadly drug                               Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
