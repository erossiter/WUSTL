   WASHINGTON  D C    Senator Tom Harkin  D IA   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  today announced that the Commerce  Justice  and Science  CJS  Appropriations Subcommittee approved  250 000 for Barnabas Microloan Program  an effort of Lutheran Services of Iowa and other charitable groups   The funds will be administered by the Institute for Social and Economic Development Ventures  The bill is expected to be approved by the full Senate Appropriations Committee on Thursday  It will then need to be approved by both the House and Senate before it becomes law              Teaching people of modest means how to run a small business and providing them with small loans to get started can help them achieve self sufficiency     Harkin said    I hope that this program will provide people with knowledge and financial help that will enable them to secure their economic future          The Barnabas Program is a faith based initiative that encourages people to achieve self sufficiency   Faith based and economic development leaders are working together to advance this initiative in rural Iowa communities  providing micro enterprise development training classes  Earned Income Tax Credit tax services  Individual Development Accounts and other types of assistance  Churches have provided space for classes and business development  but individuals will be eligible for services regardless of their religious affiliation                  Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
