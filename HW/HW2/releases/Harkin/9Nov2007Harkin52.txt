   WASHINGTON  D C   Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced that the South Slope Cooperative Telephone Company  which serves Linn  Johnson and Benton counties  received a  53 604 000 loan to improve telecommunications in rural areas  These funds were made available through the United State Department of Agricultures  USDA  Telecommunications Loan program  Harkin has long worked to improve Iowas communications infrastructure  As Chairman of the Senate Agriculture  Nutrition and Forestry Committee  the committee charged with crafting the new farm bill  Harkin is again working to help rural communities can stay connected to the world     Improving access to quality telephone and broadband service is key to ensuring Iowans can run a business and go about their daily lives in all corners of the state  Harkin said  Investing in our communications infrastructure now will lay the groundwork needed to help communities grow in the years to come     Specifically  these funds will be used to connect 632 new subscribers  deploy 102 new route miles of fiber and copper cable  install 10 Gigabit Ethernet transport toll and transport electronics  upgrade existing Nortel DMS switches  fortify the Central Office building  and purchase vehicles and work equipment       Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
