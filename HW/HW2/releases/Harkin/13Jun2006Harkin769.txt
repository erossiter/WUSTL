       WASHINGTON  D C     Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced that three Iowa colleges and universities received over  438 000 to fund scholarships for disadvantaged students pursuing medical careers  These funds were awarded by the U S  Department of Health and Human Services  HHS   Harkin is the ranking Democrat on the Senate Appropriations panel that funds health and education initiatives            I hope these funds will help talented Iowans pursue careers in medicine  regardless of their personal finances     Harkin said    The qualified medical professionals trained at Iowa  s colleges and universities play a crucial role in providing Iowans with quality health care             The Scholarships for Disadvantaged Students program provides scholarships to full time students who demonstrate financial need and are enrolled in health professional and nursing programs  Participating schools select scholarship recipients and determine their financial need  Students pursing careers in medicine  osteopathic medicine  dentistry  optometry  pharmacy  podiatric medicine  veterinary medicine  nursing  public health  chiropractic  allied health  clinical social work  and several other medical fields are eligible for scholarships          The individual grant awards are as follows                     Allen College in Waterloo     14 902                    Indian Hills Community College in Ottumwa     138 016                    University of Iowa in Iowa City     240 241                                Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
