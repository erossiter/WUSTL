 WASHINGTON  D C   Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today announced rural development funding from the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  for three Iowa communities  the Cities of Lenox and Meriden and Lakeside Lutheran Home  Harkin is Chairman of the Senate Agriculture  Nutrition  and Forestry Committee   Residents rely on local community facilities as meeting places or centers of public health  Rural development funding provides the financial resources that allow our community facilities to thrive  said Harkin  I congratulate all recipients on receiving this competitive funding and I look forward to the improvements it will generate    The City of Lenox received a grant of  15 250 to purchase furnishings and equipment for the new community center  The funding was administered through the Community Facility Grant Program   The City of Meriden received a grant of  13 700 to finance the replacement of the community center roof  The funding was administered through the Community Facility Grant Program   Lakeside Lutheran Home secured a conditional commitment for a guaranteed loan of  36 700 and a grant of  19 700 to purchase medical records automation equipment and software to maintain the medical records of the residents  They will also purchase a backup electrical generator to assure essential medical equipment can continue to operate in case of a power outage  The funding was secured through the Community Facility Guaranteed Loan and Grant Program       Originating Site  Number  Site Filter  Search
