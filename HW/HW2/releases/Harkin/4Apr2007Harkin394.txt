                        WASHINGTON  D C       Senator Tom Harkin  D IA  today released the following statement in response to the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation announcing a  500 million commitment to reverse the rate of childhood obesity by 2015                                  I applaud the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation s ambitious pledge to create real change in the health and well being of America s youngsters   said Harkin   Childhood obesity is a problem that affects families of every race and income level  and contributes to rising healthcare costs for all Americans  Today s youth make up the most overweight generation in history  and risk having a shorter lifespan than their parents  We must halt this public health crisis and bring together communities  schools  and industry to develop innovative solutions   These new resources will help us empower more children and ensure future generations have every opportunity to live healthy lives                                Harkin is the Senate leader in federal efforts to combat the obesity epidemic by promoting wellness and healthy habits among adults and children   He has authored the Healthy Lifestyles and Prevention  HeLP  America Act  which takes a comprehensive approach to addressing the challenges posed by chronic disease and focuses on the power of prevention   Promoting healthy behaviors such as eating nutritious foods  being physically active  avoiding tobacco use  and ensuring access to mental heath services can prevent or control the devastating effects of many chronic diseases           Harkin recently joined Senator Sam Brownback  R KS  and others to launch the Task Force on Media and Childhood Obesity  an innovative public private partnership aimed at combating the clear and present health dangers stemming from poor nutrition and childhood obesity                               In addition to his efforts on food marketing  Harkin is the architect of the Harkin Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Program    now operating in eight states and on three Indian reservations    which provides free fresh fruits and vegetables to children in schools  He has also proposed legislation to give communities funding for more bike paths and sidewalks and tax credits for employers who implement wellness programs for their employees                            More information about the Robert
