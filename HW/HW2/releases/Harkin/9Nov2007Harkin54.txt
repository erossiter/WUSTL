   WASHINGTON  D C   Senator Tom Harkin  D IA   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations committee and its joint conference committee with the House  announced this morning that it has approved  4 7 million for transportation and economic development projects in Central Iowa  The funding will be included in the conference report of the 2008 Transportation  Housing and Urban Development  THUD  Appropriations and will now seek final approval from the U S  House and the Senate before making its way to the Presidents desk  the President has threatened to block this bill     These funds go to providing much needed affordable housing in Ames and Marshalltown  two communities that offer an outstanding quality of life and wonderful neighborhoods to raise families  said Harkin  We have also approved funding to continue construction on the MLK Southeast Connector so Des Moines area residents can quickly navigate the city  I look forward to seeing the improvements this funding will generate for the Des Moines area and the Central Iowa region     Statewide  the bill provides more than  15 million for specific Iowa initiatives  including  4 69 million to replace buses in Iowas urban and rural transit system   536 000 for Harkins Main Street project to help restore historic downtown areas  and  201 000 for Homeward  a rural electric cooperative focused on providing housing construction to rural areas     A breakdown of Central Iowas funds include     DES MOINES    MLK  Southeast Connector   2 3 million  These funds will be used to continue construction of the MLK
