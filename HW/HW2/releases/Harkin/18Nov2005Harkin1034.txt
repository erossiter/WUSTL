      WASHINGTON  D C     Senator Tom Harkin  D IA   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations committee  today announced that he secured  2 4 million to support Iowa transportation  economic revitalization  and housing initiatives in the final FY 2006 Transportation  Treasury  Judiciary  Housing and Urban Development  TTHUD Appropriations Bill   The House and Senate are expected to approve the final bill later this week   The bill must be signed by the President before it becomes law            Providing investments in transportation  economic development  and affordable housing will give a needed boost to Iowa  s economy     Harkin said    I am pleased to have secured these essential funds to continue improving the quality of life for all Iowans             The bill also provided over  3 7 billion nationwide for the Community Development Block Grant  CDBG  program  CDBG funds develop viable communities by providing housing  a suitable living environment  and increased economic opportunities primarily for persons of low and moderate income   As a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee that funds HUD  Harkin worked to restore funds for the CDBG program in the 2006 Appropriations bill after the President proposed that the program be cut             Main Street Iowa Program   1 million   These funds will help restore historic downtown areas and develop affordable housing above downtown storefronts  Grants are competitively awarded to small communities by the Iowa Department of Economic Development  Harkin secured a total of  1 5 million in three prior years for the Main Street Iowa program           1 4 million to replace buses in Iowa  s urban and rural transit systems including those that follow a fixed route and those that provide point to point service  often for people with disabilities  Sixty two percent of Iowa  s bus fleet has currently exceeded their expected life span                           Originating Site  Number  Site
