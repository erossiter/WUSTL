SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE COMMITTEE APPROVAL OF PENNSYLVANIA WATER PROJECTS
  Sens. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, announced that the Appropriations Committee has approved federal funding for water projects in Pennsylvania. The funding is included in the Environmental Protection Agency title of the Fiscal Year 2006 Interior Appropriations bill.  
   "This funding will provide the resources to restructure water treatment systems and make improvements to the existing water and sewer infrastructure in Pennsylvania," Senator Santorum stated. "These are necessary upgrades that will ensure safe and environmentally responsible practices throughout the Commonwealth." 
   "I am pleased my colleagues have approved funding for these vital improvements to Pennsylvania's infrastructure," Senator Specter said. "These projects will ensure that residents in these areas have safe and sanitary water supplies." 
   The Fiscal Year 2006 Appropriations Bill is pending further action by the full Senate. 
   Funding has been approved for the following Pennsylvania projects: 
   * $1.75 million for the Three Rivers Wet Weather Demonstration Program in Allegheny County to fix municipal sewers, protect our nation's streams and augment the investment of local governments in compliance with the Clean Water Act; 
   * $800,000 for the Plum Creek and Allegheny County combined sewer overflow and flood protection project; 
   * $750,000 for the Borough of Archbald for a sewer improvement project; 
   * $500,000 for the City of Lancaster for water infrastructure improvements; 
   * $500,000 to Penn State University for the Small Public Water System Technology Center to foster technology evaluation and training for operators, trainers, engineers, owners and managers of small water systems; 
   * $250,000 for Millcreek Township for storm sewer pipe construction; 
   * $250,000 for Menallen Township for public sewer service extension; 
   * $250,000 for Tulpehocken Township for central sewer collection and treatment replacement; 
   * $250,000 for the Borough of Pottstown for a stormwater infrastructure improvement project; 
   * $200,000 for Penn Hills for an interceptor improvement project in Penn Hills, Pennsylvania.
