SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE FEDERAL FUNDING FOR EDUCATION PROJECTS IN CENTRAL PENNSYLVANIA
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor, Health and Human Services, and Education, announced today federal funding for projects in central Pennsylvania, contained in the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill. "This funding will help to maintain and increase the high standards set in education throughout central Pennsylvania," said Sen. Santorum. "The world of education is moving forward at a rapid pace with new technology being introduced into classrooms virtually everyday. With funding like this, the goal is to provide all Pennsylvanians with a modern, quality education."  
   "I am pleased my colleagues have approved funding for these important central Pennsylvania projects," Senator Specter said. "Education is the single greatest capital investment we can make in our nation's future, and this funding will provide resources that will empower students to become more productive members of society, live happier lives and make incredible contributions to our country." The Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations Bill was passed by the Senate on November 20, 2004 and is pending final approval by the House of Representatives, followed by the President's signature. 
   Projects to receive funding include: 
   * $1 million for Harrisburg University of Science and Technology in Harrisburg for curriculum development, laboratory equipment and technology to create a comprehensive university that will integrate an affiliated high school level preparatory school and business incubator. 
   * $900,000 for Lock Haven University in Lock Haven for professional development partnerships and related services. 
   * $420,000 for Bucknell University in Lewisburg for campus-wide technology infrastructure upgrades, including wireless technology. 
   * $200,000 for Centre County to implement, in coordination with Clinton County, educational programming targeting the prevention of drug use by students. As part of this initiative, high school students focusing on drug prevention will develop Project Coffee House, which will present theatrical productions. 
   * $200,000 for Shippensburg University in Shippensburg for equipment, technology and curriculum development for a Performing Arts Center. The Center will provide space for academic facilities, public outreach programming, and artistic performances. The facility will house the university's music department and offer music and performing arts laboratory space. 
   * $175,000 for Military Heritage Foundation in Carlisle Barracks for the United Sates Army Heritage and Education Center for education materials and programs on military and social history. This funding will be used to support the ongoing development and distribution of K-12 educational outreach programs based on the "Building a History Curriculum: Guidelines for Teaching History in Schools." 
   * $100,000 for the Crispus Attucks Charter School in York to serve at-risk youth. 
   * $100,000 for Messiah College in Grantham to support initiatives to improve educational opportunities for children in grades K-12. This funding will be used to develop the Institute for Community Research and Collaborative Partnerships to increase hands-on learning opportunities for students, to enhance faculty teaching and scholarship, and to collaborate with community liaisons in the greater Harrisb.
