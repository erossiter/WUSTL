SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE SENATE APPROVAL OF DEFENSE PROJECTS IN LEHIGH VALLEY
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of Senate Appropriations Committee, announced today that the U.S. Senate has approved funding for several defense related projects in the Lehigh Valley. The funding is contained in the Fiscal Year 2006 Defense Appropriations bill.  
   "Pennsylvania plays a strong role in our national defense and I am proud of the people and organizations that help to make our state a top competitor for defense projects that create jobs and strengthen our economy," said Senator Santorum. "This funding will enable these important defense projects in the Lehigh Valley to move forward in the effort to achieve a more secure nation." 
   "A number of Pennsylvania companies are enhancing the way our Armed Forces operate," Senator Specter said. "These projects will go far in supporting the brave men and women who serve our country. I am pleased my colleagues in the Senate have recognized the valuable contribution these projects will make to our nation's defense." 
   Projects which have been approved to receive funding in the Lehigh Valley include: 
   * $5 million for Night Vision Devices in Lehigh Valley for the Miniature Hand-Held Thermal Imager to equip select personnel of the XVIII Airborne Corps with miniature hand-held thermal imagers that will improve situational awareness and provide early warning of the presence of hostile forces; 
   * $4.8 million for Air Products, Inc. in Lehigh County for the Solid Electrolyte Oxygen Separator, to continue the development of an electrically driven Solid Electrolyte Oxygen Separator (SEOS) generator capable of delivering high purity, therapeutic-grade oxygen; 
   * $4.1 million for Lehigh University in Lehigh County to research the development of novel materials for optical technologies, innovative methods of packaging optoelectronic devices, and non-linear materials for broadband communication and display technologies.
