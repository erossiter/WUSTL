SEN. SANTORUM SPONSORS SENATE RESOLUTION TO COMMEMORATE HOLOCAUST REMEMBRANCE DAY
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, today introduced a Senate resolution to commemorate Holocaust Remembrance Day. The resolution, S. Res. 445, passed today by unanimous consent.  
   "Each year on Holocaust Remembrance Day, we recognize the historic significance of the Holocaust, and we remember and honor the millions of victims of this tragedy in our world's history. I am proud to have sponsored this Senate resolution commemorating Holocaust Remembrance Day," said Senator Santorum. 
   The following Senators cosponsored the bipartisan resolution: Joe Liebermen (D-CT), Bill Nelson (D-FL), George Voinovich (R-OH), James Inhofe (R-OK), John Ensign (R-NV), Olympia Snowe (R-ME), Sam Brownback (R-KS), George Allen (R-VA), Dianne Feinstein (D-CA), Mike DeWine (R-OH), Carl Levin (D-MI), Lincoln Chafee (R-RI), Bill Frist (R-TN), Frank Lautenberg (D-NJ), Conrad Burns (R-MT), Arlen Specter (R-PA), Craig Thomas (R-WY), John Warner (R-VA), Ted Stevens (R-AK), John Sununu (R-NV), Jim Talent (R-MO) and Mike Crapo (R-ID).
