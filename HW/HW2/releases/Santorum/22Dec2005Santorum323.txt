SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE SENATE APPROVAL OF DEFENSE PROJECTS IN SOUTHEASTERN PENNSYLVANIA
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of Senate Appropriations Committee, announced today that the U.S. Senate has approved funding for defense-related projects in southeastern Pennsylvania. The funding is contained in the Fiscal Year 2006 Defense Appropriations bill. 
   "Pennsylvania plays a strong role in our national defense and I am proud of the people and organizations that help to make our state a top competitor for defense projects that create jobs and strengthen our economy," said Senator Santorum. "This funding will enable these important defense projects in southeastern Pennsylvania to move forward in the effort to achieve a more secure nation."  
   "A number of Pennsylvania companies are enhancing the way our Armed Forces operate," Senator Specter said. "These projects will go far in supporting the brave men and women who serve our country. I am pleased my colleagues in the Senate have recognized the valuable contribution these projects will make to our nation's defense." 
   Projects which have been approved to receive funding in southeastern Pennsylvania include: 
   * $6.5 million to Drexel University in Philadelphia County for the Applied Communications and Information Networking (ACIN) Program. ACIN will continue to adapt and equip the Department of Defense with state-of-the-art communications and networking technology to facilitate command and control, cooperative engagement, precision strikes, intelligence dissemination, logistics, and daily operations; 
   * $5 million to Gestalt in Montgomery County for the Distributed Mission Interoperability Toolkit (DMIT) program. DMIT is a suite of tools that provides for the efficient translation of air mission tasks from C41 systems into a format that is compatible with mission simulators; 
   * $4 million to Analytical Graphics in Chester County for a Satellite Assets for Joint Navigation Warfare project. The ability to jam GPS guided weapons is becoming increasingly available. Funding for this project will aid in the development of tools, which can accurately model the threats faced by U.S. GPS guided weapons, allowing for better testing, more thorough test analysis, and more accurate planning for the development of GPS guided weapons; 
   * $3.5 million to Piasecki Aircraft in Delaware County for a VTDP Compound Helicopter Technology Flight Demonstration project. This funding will demonstrate the Vectored Thrust Ducted Propeller (VTDP) technology's potential to increase speed, range, survivability, and reduced life cycle costs; 
   * $3.5 million to Vision Technologies in Philadelphia County for a Machinery Control Surveillance System. This project will utilize a video system to augment current Damage Control Quarters (DCQ) capabilities and monitor mission-critical spaces aboard gas turbine ships; 
   * $3 million to Villanova University for Applied Research in Computing Enterprise Services (ARCES). This program will focus academic and industry research on unique military issues not addressed by the commercial arena. This research will provide increases in the efficiency of military operations, improve intelligence gathering and sharing and lower the cost of developing future command and control systems; 
   * $2.8 million to Edmond Optics in Montgomery County for Manufacturing of Precision Optics. This project will provide for a secure U.S. base for low cost precision aspheric optics and optical subassemblies, benefiting surveillance, targeting, and fire control systems used on a wide range of weapon systems; 
   * $2.55 million to Morophotek in Chester County for Mismatch Repair. This project will develop fully human, high-affinity antibodies that can serve as biosensors for the early detection of pathogenic agents as well as be clinically developed as drugs for the treatment of those exposed to biowarfare agents; 
   * $2.5 million for Bentley Systems in Chester County to engage in the Pentagon Integrated Campus Pilot Program. Since the September 11th attacks, the Pentagon Washington Headquarters Service (WHS) and Pentagon Force Protection Service (PFPS) have been tasked with managing critical infrastructure protection at the Pentagon. Funding will be used to accelerate a pilot program at the Pentagon to capture, organize, and integrate facilities information for the WHS and improve the delivery of Pentagon building infrastructure information to the Pentagon Force Protection Service; 
   * $2.5 million to Rajant Corporation in Delaware County for a Portable and Mobile Emergency Broadband System. This system will provide a portable wireless broadband communication system that allows for the rapid deployment of secure, self-powered, high bandwidth applications in remote sites or disaster areas where existing internet communications have been disrupted. Funding will provide for the development of technology, which will enhance security, integrate the system with other military and homeland security networks, and increase the system's range and capacity; 
   * $2.5 million to the Power and Energy Corporation in Bucks County for a Pure Hydrogen Supply from Logistics Fuel project. Fuel cells harness the chemical energy of hydrogen to generate electricity without combustion or pollution. The Navy is conducting research to develop fuel cell technology for shipboard use, which extracts hydrogen fuel from logistic fuels. Funding will be used to develop technology which will allow the extraction of hydrogen from logistics fuels and eliminates the contaminants limiting fuel cell use; 
   * $2.5 million for a Center for Rotorcraft Innovation in Delaware County. The Center for Rotorcraft Innovation will be a state of the-art research facility that will serve to coordinate rotorcraft research by working directly with private industry, academic centers of excellence, and the government; 
   * $2 million to Drexel University in Philadelphia County for the National Bioterrorism Civilian Medical Response Center (CIMERC). In 2000, Drexel University received a Congressional designation as the National Bioterrorism Civilian Medical Response Center (CIMERC). Since its inception, CIMERC has been working with the US Army to develop a demonstration test-bed for new technologies related to bioterrorism which are linked to bioterrorism civilian preparedness and crisis response; 
   * $1.8 million to Johnson Matthey Fuel Cells North America in Chester County for an Affordable Lighweight Power Supply Development project. This program will provide the Air Force with a durable and cost effective high temperature Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM), the single most critical component preventing the widespread introduction of light weight fuel cells as a power source; 
   * $1.7 million to SAP in Lebanon and Dauphin Counties for Enterprise Resource Planning for Installations and Deployment. This project will modernize Army National Guard (ARNG) installation and facilities management information systems by linking each system, thus allowing detailed and total visibility of all material inventories. This program will improve ARNG capabilities to mobilize and deploy in response to the Global War on Terrorism and domestic emergencies; 
   * $1.5 million to SAP in Lebanon and Dauphin Counties for an Army Medical Department Enterprise Resource Planning project in Cumberland County. The U.S. Army Medical Material Department (AMEDD) is charged with providing medical supplies to the Army worldwide. Funding will upgrade the AMEDD's medical support to combat forces by transitioning operationally critical medical logistics capabilities from the legacy Tactical Army Medical Material Information System (TAMMIS) into a newer Enterprise Resource Planning system. This transition will lead to quicker medical supply replenishment, lighter supply chain burdens, and greater responsiveness in treating wounded soldiers in the field; 
   * $1.5 million to InterDigital in Montgomery County for Advanced Antenna Technologies. This funding will provide for advanced research and development of smart antennas for use in battlefield wireless local area networks, mobile wideband code division multiple access (WCDMA) technologies, and systems necessary to ensure successful Joint Force operations as defined by Joint Operations Concepts; 
   * $1.5 million to BAE Systems in Montgomery County for Low Band Transmitter EA-6B Support Jamming Upgrade. Transmitters used aboard EA-6B aircraft will be upgraded to provide radar-jamming support. The EA-6B is the sole U.S. military support jamming aircraft, and is currently using transmitters, which are over 30 years old. The upgrade will provide expanded war fighting capability against modern integrated air defense systems, and a significant decrease in lifetime support costs; 
   * $1 million to Oto Malara in Delaware County for 76mm Super Rapid Medium Caliber Gun for Littoral Combat Ships research. This research will explore the possibility of placing a 76mm deck gun on the Navy's next-generation Littoral Combat Ships; 
   * $1 million for Navy Logistics Readiness Research Center (NLRRC) in Philadelphia County. This center will focus government, academic, and industry expertise on developing and instituting Readiness Based Sparing (RBS) tools and processes, in order to resolve Navy logistics issues including spare parts shortfalls, configuration issues and the Navy's ability to meet surge requirements, and to integrate RBS with the Navy's Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems; 
   * $1 million to Franklin Fuel Cells in Chester County for Copper-Ceramic Solid Oxide Fuel Cell Technology research. Deployment of fuel cells has the potential to greatly reduce military fuel costs and distribution logistics. However, current fuel cell technologies must use either hydrogen or zero/low sulfur fuels which makes them impractical for military deployment. This program will design, fabricate, and demonstrate a 2kW copper-ceramic solid oxide fuel cell (SOFC) auxiliary power unit prototype to be delivered to the Navy no later than July 2007; 
   * $1 million to 3e Technologies in Indiana and Philadelphia counties for a Security Video Distribution System for Shipboard Force Protection. This funding will provide for the research and design of a system capable of viewing and controlling both live and stored video from any computer, handheld PC, or Portable Digital Assistant (PDA) on a Navy ship using the shipboard network. Current systems do not have this ability. This system will dramatically increase situational awareness ship-wide; 
   * $1 million to Navmar Applied Sciences in Delaware County for CURTIS Upgrades to the P3C BMUP Aircraft. This project will increase intelligence-gathering capabilities of Naval P-3C Aircraft, while also improving the aircraft's ability to relay gathered intelligence to operational commanders and ground personnel.
