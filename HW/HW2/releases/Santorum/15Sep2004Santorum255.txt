SEN. SANTORUM ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR RENEWABLE ENERGY DEVELOPMENT IN RURAL PENNSYLVANIA
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, announced that the Department of Agriculture has awarded funding for renewable energy development in rural areas of Pennsylvania through the Rural Development program.  
   This funding will be used to assist farmers and rural small businesses in developing renewable energy systems, and in making energy-efficient improvements to their operations. 
   "I am committed to advancing renewable energy ventures in rural Pennsylvania," Santorum stated. "Advancements made with renewable technologies are not only consistent with the goal of a healthy and sustainable environment, but allow us to expand our domestic resources and reduce our dependency on foreign energy sources." 
   Grants have been approved for the following rural areas in Pennsylvania: 
   * $196,978 for the Four Winds Farm in Potter County, Pennsylvania.
  * $249,435 for the Turbotville Development Corporation in Northumberland County, Pennsylvania.
  * $203,725 for the Penn England Farm in Blair County, Pennsylvania. 
  * $177,975 for the Custom Fuels, Inc. dba Soy Energy in Adams County, Pennsylvania.
