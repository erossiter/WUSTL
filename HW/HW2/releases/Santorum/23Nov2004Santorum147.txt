SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE FEDERAL FUNDING FOR CENTRAL PENNSYLVANIA PROJECTS
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of the U.S. Senate Appropriations Committee, announced today federal funding for projects in central Pennsylvania, contained in the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill. "I applaud my colleagues in the Senate for approving the Health and Human Services funding for projects in central Pennsylvania," Sen. Santorum said. "The funding will allow the communities in central Pennsylvania to purchase new equipment, construct new health care facilities, and provide the most advanced health care modernizations which will aid in the health and wellness of the citizens of central Pennsylvania."  
   "I am pleased my colleagues have approved this important funding for central Pennsylvania," Senator Specter said. "These projects are vital to ensuring quality primary and emergency care, as well as advanced preventive care measures, for the area's residents." 
   The Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations Bill was passed by the Senate on November 20, 2004 and is pending final approval by the House of Representatives, followed by the President's signature. 
   Projects to receive funding include: 
   * $1 million for Pennsylvania State University in Hershey to establish a program to apply clinical procedures to everyday practical applications. The program will design training programs for clinical investigators, foster a multidisciplinary approach to clinical research, develop animal models of human disease, develop new clinical research biostatistical tools and establish regional and national resources. 
   * $400,000 for Franklin and Marshall College in Lancaster to construct a state-of-the-art interdisciplinary Life Sciences building. 
   * $250,000 for Keystone Health Center in Chambersburg to construct a new building that will consolidate the remote services within a more economical, efficient and convenient facility. This new building will stand adjacent to the existing Keystone Health Center Family Practice Building. 
   * $250,000 for Fulton County Medical Center in McConnellsburg to construct a new facility to meet community health care needs as well as provide space for future growth. 
   * $250,000 for Good Samaritan Hospital in Lebanon to purchase a CAT Scan Machine for the Emergency Center. 
   * $250,000 for J.C. Blair Memorial in Huntingdon to purchase a Hyperbaric Oxygen Chamber to treat people with chronic wounds, a CT scanner, and a digital telemetry system. 
   * $250,000 for Jersey Shore Hospital in Jersey Shore to replace radiology equipment, which will allow Jersey Shore Hospital to expand telemedicine efforts and implement the Susquehanna Valley Rural Health Partnership's goal of a region-wide, lifetime clinical record for patients. 
   * $250,000 for Lancaster General Hospital, College of Nursing & Health Sciences, in Lancaster to purchase a human patient simulator for physician education. 
   * $250,000 for Philipsburg Area Hospital in Philipsburg to improve computer network infrastructure, nursing care equipment, and renovate the facility. 
   * $250,000 for Pinnacle Health System in Harrisburg to continue implementation of a Computerized Physician Order Entry and Medication Administration Checking. This will enable Pinnacle Health System to use bar coding and web technology to enhance patient care. 
   * $200,000 for Soldiers and Sailors Memorial Hospital in Wellsboro to purchase new x-ray equipment. 
   * $100,000 for Susquehanna Health System in Williamsport to add a computerized physician order entry system. In this system, physicians would enter medication orders and directions through a computer. The system bar-codes medications and physician orders to ensure correct doses are administered safely and in a timely manner.
