SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER APPLAUD WESTERN PENNSYLVANIA DISTRICT JUDGE NOMINATION
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Judiciary Committee, today applauded the President for nominating Nora Barry Fischer to be a United States District Judge for the Western District of Pennsylvania.  
   "I am extremely pleased the President has nominated Nora Barry Fischer to fill this vacancy," said Senator Santorum. "I believe Nora to be well suited for a federal judgeship position in the Commonwealth, and I extend my best wishes to her and her family. This judgeship has been open since February 2004, and I hope that we can move forward to quickly confirm this nominee." 
   "I am pleased to report that Nora Barry Fischer has been nominated by the President to be a judge for the Western District of Pennsylvania," said Senator Specter. "She has an excellent academic record, has been a distinguished lawyer and will make an outstanding federal judge." 
   Nora Barry Fischer is a native of Homestead, Pennsylvania. She graduated from St. Mary's College in 1973, and went on to earn her J.D. degree from the University of Notre Dame Law School in 1976. Currently, Fischer is an Equity Partner at the well respected firm of Pietragallo Bosick & Gordon LLP in Pittsburgh.
