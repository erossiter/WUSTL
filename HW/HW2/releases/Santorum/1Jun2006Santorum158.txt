SEN. SANTORUM, COMMERCE SECRETARY GUTIERREZ ANNOUNCE $1.2 MILLION INVESTMENT IN JOHNSTOWN, PA.
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and U.S. Commerce Secretary Carlos M. Gutierrez today announced a $1.2 million Economic Development Administration (EDA) investment to the Johnstown Industrial Development Corporation, Richland Township Supervisors, Highland Water and Sewer Authority and Forest Hills Municipal Authority, Johnstown, Penn. The investment will help expand the Johnstown Business Park. The project is expected to create 500 jobs and generate $58 million in private investment. Of the $1.2 million, $200,000 was provided as a supplement from the U.S. Appalachian Regional Commission (ARC).  
   "I am pleased that EDA has made this commitment to Pennsylvania's continued economic growth," said U.S. Senator Rick Santorum (R-PA). "Investing in the people of Johnstown is an excellent decision that will surely reap economic benefits for the community." 
   "President Bush and I are committed to partnering with communities to promote innovation and competitiveness. Through economic development grants like these, the Commerce Department will continue to encourage investments in local areas, grow the economy and create jobs," said Gutierrez. 
   EDA serves as a venture capital resource to meet the economic development needs of distressed communities throughout the United States. EDA's mission is to lead the federal economic development agenda by promoting innovation and competitiveness, preparing American regions for growth and success in the worldwide economy.
