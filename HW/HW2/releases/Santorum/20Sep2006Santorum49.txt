SEN. SANTORUM ANNOUNCES MORE THAN $1 MILLION IN FEDERAL FUNDING TO HELP ENSURE FAIR HOUSING PRACTICES IN PENNSYLVANIA
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, today announced that the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) Office of Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity (FHEO) has approved federal funding for Pennsylvania.  
   "I am pleased Pennsylvania has received more than $1 million in federal funding to help ensure that fair housing laws are enforced and that Pennsylvanians can enjoy access to fair and equal housing without fear of discrimination," said Senator Santorum. 
   Pennsylvania is the recipient of the following Private Enforcement Initiative (PEI) Grants: 
   * $270,000 to the Fair Housing Council of Montgomery County in Glenside, Montgomery County. 
   * $275,000 to the Fair Housing Council of Suburban Philadelphia, Inc. in Swarthmore, Delaware County. 
   * $275,000 to the Fair Housing Partnership of Greater Pittsburgh, Inc. in Pittsburgh, Allegheny County 
   Pennsylvania is the recipient of the following Education and Outreach Initiative (EOI) Grants: 
   * $99,863 to the St. Martin Center, Inc. in Erie, Erie County 
   * $51,489 to the Three Rivers Center for Independent Living, Inc. in Wilkinsburg, Allegheny County. 
   * $50,000 to the Community Impact Legal Services in Coatesville, Chester County. 
   Private Enforcement Initiative (PEI) grants assist private, tax-exempt fair housing enforcement organizations in the investigation and enforcement of alleged violations of the Fair Housing Act and substantially equivalent fair housing laws. Grantees are Fair Housing Enforcement Organizations with at least one year of experience in enforcement related activities and Qualified Fair Housing Enforcement Organizations with at least two years of experience in enforcement related activities. 
   Education and Outreach Initiative (EOI) assists organizations that inform the public about their rights and obligations under the Fair Housing Act and substantially equivalent fair housing laws. Grantees are public or private for profit or not for profit institutions or other public or private entities that carry out programs to prevent or eliminate discriminatory housing practices.
