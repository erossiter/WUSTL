SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE RELEASE OF FUNDING FOR TRANSPORTATION PROJECTS IN PENNSYLVANIA
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen.  
 Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of the Senate Transportation Appropriations Subcommittee, announced today that the Department of Transportation has released federal funding for projects in Pennsylvania. Senators Specter and Santorum have worked to secure this funding in recent appropriations bills. 
   "This significant funding provides new buses and structural improvements which will increase commuter comfort and usability," said Senator Santorum. "It is important that we continue to upgrade and expand mass transportation facilities so that more people are encouraged to use public services." 
   "I am pleased the Department of Transportation has provided this important funding to improve bus facilities throughout Pennsylvania," said Senator Specter. "This funding is an investment in Pennsylvania's future because public transportation is vital to the growth and quality of life in urban areas." 
   Funding announced today includes: 
   * $3,436,175 to the Southeastern Pennsylvania Transportation Authority (SEPTA) for the engineering, design and construction of an intermodal facility in Montgomery County; 
   * $1,671,837 to Capital Area Transit (CAT) in Harrisburg for the purchase of buses; 
   * $1,555,618 to the York County Transportation Authority for the purchase of eight replacement buses; 
   * $1,495,015 to the Lehigh and Northampton Transportation Authority (LANTA) for the expansion of the Easton bus facility and the leasing of the Allentown Intermodal Facility.
