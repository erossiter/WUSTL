SEN. SANTORUM ANNOUNCES COMMITTEE APPROVAL OF PENN HILLS PROJECT
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, announced that the Appropriations Committee has approved $200,000 for the Beulah Road Interceptor project in Penn Hills.  
 The funding is included in the Environmental Protection Agency title of the Fiscal Year 2006 Interior Appropriations bill. 
   "I am pleased that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for the modernization and improvement of the current sewer system for my home town, Penn Hills," Senator Santorum stated. "These are necessary upgrades that will ensure safe and environmentally responsible practices throughout the Penn Hills community. Residents can rest assured that the problems associated with the outdated system are being addressed." 
   The funding will be used to replace a portion of the sanitary sewer line that is in poor condition, as well as other improvements and replacements to the sewer system throughout Penn Hills. To date Senator Santorum has secured $750,000 to help rehabilitate the Penn Hills water and sewer infrastructure.
