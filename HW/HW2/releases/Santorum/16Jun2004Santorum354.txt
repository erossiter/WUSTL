SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE SENATE CONFIRMATION OF JUDGE LARRY STENGEL
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen.  
 Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of the Senate Judiciary Committee, announced today that the U.S. Senate has confirmed Lawrence Stengel to serve on the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania. 
   "I am confident that when Larry Stengel takes his seat on the federal bench, he will conduct himself with the same fair judgment and integrity as he did serving Lancaster County," said Senator Santorum. "Larry is an outstanding candidate and I am pleased that my colleagues agree in confirming his nomination to the Eastern District Court." 
   "Judge Stengel was nominated for this position because of his outstanding academic and professional credentials," Senator Specter said. "He has served on the Court of Common Pleas with great distinction and will make an outstanding contribution to our judicial system." 
   Lawrence Stengel is currently a judge for the Lancaster County Court of Common Pleas and has been nominated to serve on the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania. Judge Stengel was nominated by the President on November 6, 2003, his confirmation hearing was held on February 5, 2004, and was passed out of committee on March 11, 2004.
