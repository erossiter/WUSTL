SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE COMMITTEE APPROVAL OF FUNDING FOR REVITALIZATION PROJECTS IN PENNSYLVANIA
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, announced today that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved federal funding for projects in Pennsylvania. The funding is included in the Economic Development Initiative portion of the Departments of Veterans Affairs and Housing and Urban Development, and Independent Agencies FY2005 Appropriations bill.  
   "I am pleased that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved federal funding for important Pennsylvania projects to receive the necessary resources to make improvements and advances in communities throughout the Commonwealth," Santorum stated. "The rehabilitation of several historic buildings, houses, and parks along with the construction of new community buildings provides necessary upgrades to several projects in Pennsylvania as well as the preservation of Pennsylvania's historic, environmental and architectural character." 
   "I am pleased my colleagues on the Senate Appropriations Committee have recognized the importance of these programs," Senator Specter said. "These projects will revitalize and improve cities and towns across the Commonwealth, and are vital to Pennsylvania's future economic and cultural growth." 
   The Senate Appropriations Committee approved funding for the following Pennsylvania projects: 
   * $300,000 for Urban Redevelopment Authority in Pittsburgh for the redevelopment of South Side Works; 
   * $300,000 for the City of Scranton for the Cedar Avenue Revitalization; 
   * $300,000 for Bucknell University in Lewisburg for the rehabilitation of the Lewisburg Downtown Theater; 
   * $250,000 for the City of Erie for site preparation and redevelopment of the vacant and blighted Koehler Brewery Building; 
   * $250,000 for the City of Greensburg for construction of a Center for the Arts; 
   * $250,000 for Our City Reading in Reading for the rehabilitation of abandoned houses and parks to provide quality home ownership opportunities to low-income families; 
   * $250,000 for the Greater Wilkes-Barre Chamber of Business and Industry in Wilkes-Barre for the acquisition and redevelopment of the historic Irem Temple, which will be converted into a cultural center; 
   * $250,000 for the City of Lancaster for the rehabilitation and renovation of the Lancaster Central Market; 
   * $250,000 for Eagles Mere Village, Inc, in Eagles Mere for the acquisition and rehabilitation of downtown buildings, as part of the town center revitalization initiative; 
   * $250,000 for the Allegheny County Department of Community and Economic Development in Pittsburgh for the planning, design, and construction of Schenley Plaza; 
   * $250,000 for the Greene County Department of Planning and Development in Franklin Township for the construction a multi-tenant facility at EverGreene Technology Park; 
   * $200,000 for Universal Community Homes in Philadelphia for the conversion of land into for-sale units to low- and moderate-income families; 
   * $200,000 for the Borough of Lewistown for the rehabilitation and renovation of the Lewistown Municipal Building; 
   * $200,000 for the Darby Borough Community Development Corporation in Darby for a Main Street revitalization initiative including acquisition, renovation, and demolition of downtown buildings, as part of an urban redevelopment plan; 
   * $200,000 for the Chester County Industrial Development Authority in East Whiteland and Tredyffrin Townships for the redevelopment of the Atwater Brownfields site; 
   * $200,000 for the Inglis Foundation in Philadelphia for the planning, design, and construction of housing for individuals with disabilities; 
   * $250,000 for the Allegheny West Foundation in Philadelphia for the Budd Plant rehabilitation project; 
   * $250,000 for the Indiana County Development Corporation in Indiana for the Indiana Springs development project.
