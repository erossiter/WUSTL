SEN. SANTORUM AWARDED FOR EFFORTS TO PROVIDE HEALTHCARE ACCESS TO UNINSURED
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, today was presented with the "Healthcare that Leaves No One Behind" leadership award for his work to expand healthcare access for the uninsured, his commitment to protect the sanctity of life, and his advocacy on behalf of religious affiliated healthcare centers.  
   "I am honored to receive this award today from Ascension Health. Religious affiliated healthcare providers such as Ascension Health are committed to protecting the most vulnerable patients in our society. Government should work with these valuable partners to increase access to quality healthcare for all Americans," Senator Santorum said. "In addition, I appreciate the work that Ascension Health does to protect the sanctity of life." 
   Anthony Tersigni, CEO and President of Ascension Health, presented Senator Santorum with the award at Ascension Health's CEO Council meeting in Washington, D.C. Peter Bergman, President and CEO of Good Samaritan Regional Medical Center in Pottsville, Pennsylvania, which is a member of Ascension Health, attended the awards ceremony. The award is given annually to a Member of Congress who has shown outstanding leadership in expanding access for the uninsured. 
   Ascension Health is the nation's largest Catholic health system and the nation's largest nonprofit health system, serving patients through a network of hospitals and related health facilities. Ascension Health provides acute care services, long-term care, community health services, psychiatric care, rehabilitation, and residential care.
