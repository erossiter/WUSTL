SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE FEDERAL FUNDING FOR EDUCATION PROJECTS IN LEHIGH VALLEY
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor, Health and Human Services, and Education, announced today federal funding for projects in the Lehigh Valley region, contained in the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill. "This funding will help to maintain and increase the high standards set in education throughout the Lehigh Valley region," said Senator Santorum. "The world of education is moving forward at a rapid pace with new technology being introduced into classrooms virtually everyday. With funding like this, the goal is to provide all Pennsylvanians with a modern, quality education."  
   "I am pleased my colleagues have approved funding for these important Allentown area projects," Senator Specter said. "Education is the single greatest capital investment we can make in our nation's future, and this funding will provide resources that will empower students to become more productive members of society, live happier lives and make incredible contributions to our country." The Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations Bill was passed by the Senate on November 20, 2004 and is pending final approval by the House of Representatives, followed by the President's signature. 
   Projects to receive funding include: 
   * $200,000 for the Center for Advancing Partnerships in Education (CAPE) in Allentown for distance learning programs. This funding will be used to provide Pennsylvania school districts with the capacity to utilize a highly, specialized Internet connection through a mobile broadband satellite. This technology will enable students to interface with institutions worldwide through digital instruction. 
   * $200,000 for DeSales University in Center Valley for computer wiring and technology upgrades. This funding will be used to expand the Digital Campus project, which transforms traditional classrooms into centers for distance learning. The Digital Campus Master Plan includes smart classrooms, and campus-wide technology upgrades. 
   * $175,000 for Muhlenberg College in Allentown to develop a program for advanced discovery in the exploration of the physical and life sciences at the secondary and post-secondary grade levels. This funding will be used to develop and expand a program for advanced discovery in the exploration of the physical and life sciences at the secondary and post-secondary grade levels, and will complement the college's science exploration initiatives. 
   * $100,000 for the Allentown Public Library in Allentown to provide an on-line service, which will allow the library to teach research skills to students. 
   * $100,000 for the Discovery Center of Science and Technology in Bethlehem for educational programs and exhibits that will demonstrate best practices for teaching science. This funding will be used to develop programs that provide hands-on science programs in area K-8 classrooms. 
   * $100,000 for Lafayette College in Easton for technology updates to the David Bishop Skillman Library. This funding will be used for technology improvements including computer workstations, digital multi-media studio, classrooms, and instructional technology-consulting lab. The library will serve as a multi-purpose digital learning center. 
   * $100,000 for Lehigh University in Bethlehem for research to improve the lives of disabled individuals at the Center for Promoting Healthy Development for Individuals with Disabilities. The primary objective of the Center is to create a living laboratory that establishes partnerships with schools, parents, and community service providers to enhance the lives of individuals with disabilities. 
    * $100,000 for Moravian College in Bethlehem for equipment, technology, and curriculum development for a science initiative. The goal of the initiative is to increase the quality of graduates in math, computer science, physics, biochemistry, chemistry, and biology. 
   * $100,000 for the Rodale Institute in Kutztown for development and dissemination of a health and environmental education website. The purpose of the website is to inspire children to make nutritional food choices while learning of the connection between healthy soil, healthy environment, and healthy food. 
   * $75,000 for the Allentown Art Museum in Allentown to provide educational programming to 25 school districts in seven Pennsylvania counties.
