SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE COMMITTEE APPROVAL OF REVITALIZATION PROJECTS IN PENNSYLVANIA
  Sens. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of the U.S. Senate Appropriations Committee, announced today that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved federal funding for projects in Pennsylvania. The funding is included in the Economic Development Initiative account of the Departments of Transportation, Treasury, the Judiciary, Housing and Urban Development, and Related Agencies Fiscal Year 2006 Appropriations bill.  
   "I am pleased that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved federal funding for important Pennsylvania projects to make improvements and advances in communities throughout the Commonwealth," Senator Santorum stated. "The rehabilitation and construction of community assets promotes economic development in Pennsylvania, and complements ongoing efforts by the private sector." 
   "I am pleased my colleagues on the Senate Appropriations Committee have recognized the importance of these programs," Senator Specter said. "These projects will revitalize and improve cities and towns across the Commonwealth, and are vital to Pennsylvania's future economic and cultural growth." 
   The Senate Appropriations Committee approved funding for the following Pennsylvania projects: 
   * $300,000 for the Allegheny County Airport Authority for the construction of an infrastructure for cargo distribution, aircraft maintenance and related amenities; 
   * $200,000 for the City of Lancaster for the rehabilitation and renovation of the Lancaster Square; 
   * $200,000 for Our City Reading in Reading for the rehabilitation of abandoned houses to provide quality home ownership opportunities to low-income families; 
   * $200,000 for Universal Community Homes in Philadelphia for the conversion of land into for-sale units to moderate- and low-income families; 
   * $200,000 for the Allentown Art Museum in Allentown for the expansion and modernization of its facilities; 
   * $200,000 for the Greene County Department of Planning and Development in Franklin Township for the construction of a multi-phased business park on the grounds of the Greene County Airport; 
   * $200,000 for Impact Services Corporation in Philadelphia for the renovation, redevelopment, and conversion of an existing building into housing units for homeless veterans; 
   * $200,000 for Shippensburg University Foundation for the construction of Phase III of the Shippensburg Regional Conference Center, which will provide accommodations for business and career development; 
   * $200,000 for the Pittsburgh Zoo in Pittsburgh for the development of the zoo's expansion project; 
   * $200,000 for Gaudenzia, Inc. in Norristown for the renovation and expansion of its residential facilities for low-income families; 
   * $200,000 for the Greater Wilkes-Barre Chamber of Business and Industry in Wilkes-Barre for the acquisition and redevelopment of the historic Irem Temple, which will be converted into a cultural center; 
   * $200,000 for Partnership CDC in Philadelphia for the acquisition and renovation of affordable housing for moderate- and low-income families. 
   * $200,000 for the City of Carbondale, Pennsylvania for the South Main Street Economic Development Initiative which is designed to reduce blight along the City's Main Street Corridor. 
   * $200,000 for the Redevelopment Authority of the City of Corry to acquire a brownfield site in downtown Corry, Pennsylvania. 
   * $200,000 for Weatherly Borough, Pennsylvania to acquire and redevelop the Lehigh Valley Railroad Shops and Weatherly Steel Plant complex in the heart of Weatherly, PA. 
   * $200,000 for Indiana County, Pennsylvania to acquire the Wayne Avenue Property in Indiana. 
   * $200,000 for Armstrong County, Pennsylvania for remediation and infrastructure development on a 14.2 acre of brownfield property in Apollo Borough. 
   * $200,000 for Perry County, Pennsylvania to support the County's efforts to develop an industrial park in New Bloomfield. 
   * $200,000 for People for People, Inc. for planning and project development efforts for the Triangle redevelopment project in North Philadelphia. 
   * $200,000 for the Southwestern Pennsylvania Commission, to develop the Alta Vista Business Park, a mixed-use business park on a former strip mine site adjacent to I-70, in Washington County, Pennsylvania. 
   * $200,000 to the City of Scranton, Pennsylvania for the North Main Avenue redevelopment project. 
   * $200,000 for Oxford Mainstreet, Inc, Oxford, PA to revitalize the downtown commercial district. 
   The FY06 Appropriations Bill is pending further action by the full Senate and the House of Representatives.
