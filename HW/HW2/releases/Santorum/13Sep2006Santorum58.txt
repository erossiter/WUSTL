SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER COMMENT ON THIRD CIRCUIT COURT OF APPEALS NOMINEE
  Sens. Rick Santorum, Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Arlen Specter, Chairman of the Senate Judiciary Committee, commented on President Bush's nomination of Judge Thomas M. Hardiman to serve on the United States Third Circuit Court of Appeals.  
   "In 2003, I enthusiastically supported Tom's nomination to the U.S. District Court," said Senator Santorum. "I believe Tom will make an excellent addition to the Third Circuit and I am pleased that President Bush nominated such an outstanding candidate to fill this vacancy." 
   "I am pleased that Judge Thomas Hardiman has been nominated to serve on the United States Third Circuit Court of Appeals," said Senator Specter. "Thomas Hardiman is an accomplished judge with excellent academic credentials. He has served on the U.S. District Court for the Western District of Pennsylvania with great distinction, and I think he will make an outstanding addition to the Third Circuit." 
   The President nominated Judge Hardiman to be a Third Circuit Judge on September 13, 2006. 
   Judge Hardiman is currently a judge on the U.S. District Court for the Western District of Pennsylvania, where he has served since November 2003. He is a graduate of the University of Notre Dame, where he earned a Bachelor of Arts with honors in 1987. He earned his J.D. with honors from Georgetown University Law Center in 1990. 
   Following graduation, Judge Hardiman joined the Washington, D.C. office of Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meager & Flom. In 1992 he moved to Pittsburgh and joined the firm of Cindrich & Titus. In 1999, Judge Hardiman joined Reed Smith LLP as a partner in the litigation department until he took the bench in 2003. 
   Judge Hardiman is actively involved with Big Brothers Big Sisters of Greater Pittsburgh, Inc., where he has been a Director since 1995, served as President from 1999-2000, and presently chairs the Board of Excellence Committee.
