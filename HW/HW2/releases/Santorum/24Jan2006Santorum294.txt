SEN. SANTORUM ANNOUNCES $2.7 MILLION RURAL DEVELOPMENT LOAN FOR BOROUGH OF NORTH BELLE VERNON, WESTMORELAND COUNTY
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, today announced the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Office of Rural Development has approved a Water and Waste Disposal Direct Loan for the Borough of North Belle Vernon, Westmoreland County in the amount of $2,790,000.  
   Rural Development funds will be used to complete the wastewater collection system. The Municipal Authority of Belle Vernon Borough will provide wastewater disposal and treatment. The system will serve approximately 1,014 users. 
   "This loan hopefully will allow the borough of North Belle Vernon to complete the water and sewage separation project. By treating sewage waste and storm run-off water separately, the treatment facility will function at higher efficiency and save residents money," said Senator Santorum. 
   For more information on programs offered by USDA Rural Development, contact the Pennsylvania State Office at (717) 237-2299 or visit its website at www.rurdev.usda.gov/pa. 
   "USDA Rural Development programs are an important to keeping Pennsylvania clean and maintaining a safe and healthy environment. As Pennsylvania's first Senator to serve on the Agriculture Committee since 1904, I am committed to working with USDA Rural Development for the benefit of the Commonwealth," said Senator Santorum.
