SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE FEDERAL FUNDING FOR SOUTHWESTERN PENNSYLVANIA EDUCATION PROJECTS
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor, Health and Human Services, and Education, announced today federal funding for projects in southwestern Pennsylvania, contained in the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill. "This funding will help to maintain and increase the high standards set in education throughout southwestern Pennsylvania," said Senator Santorum. "The world of education is moving forward at a rapid pace with new technology being introduced into classrooms virtually everyday. With funding like this, the goal is to provide all Pennsylvanians with a modern, quality education."  
   "I am pleased my colleagues have approved funding for these important southwestern Pennsylvania projects," Senator Specter said. "Education is the single greatest capital investment we can make in our nation's future, and this funding will provide resources that will empower students to become more productive members of society, live happier lives and make incredible contributions to our country." 
   The Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations Bill was passed by the Senate on November 20, 2004 and is pending final approval by the House of Representatives, followed by the President's signature. 
   Projects to receive funding include: 
   * $250,000 to the Pittsburgh Tissue Engineering Initiative to increase access to teacher education programs which offer continuing education and professional development opportunities focusing on tissue engineering for underserved learners. 
   * $200,000 to Beaver County to educate K-12 students about the dangers of online predators and pedophiles. In collaboration with the Community College of Beaver County, the County will provide speakers, computer training classes, and awareness classes for students. 
   * $200,000 to the Regional Learning Alliance, in Marshall Township, in partnership with Slippery Rock University, which will provide continuing education and training to meet the needs of business and industry within the Western Pennsylvania region. The Regional Learning Alliance will operate a full-service training facility which will provide career services, conference space, and manufacturing development services. 
   * $200,000 to Pittsburgh Children's Museum to arts and expand after-school outreach programs to at-risk children. The museum allows children the opportunity to participate in age-appropriate activities with a variety of media that help build developmental and creative skills. 
   * $150,000 to the Pittsburgh Digital Greenhouse to sustain and enhance its Continuing Education Short Course initiative, and to develop a Collaborative Curriculum between Carnegie Mellon University, PSU, and the University of Pittsburgh. The Continuing Education project addresses short-term workforce needs, while the Curriculum project addresses long-term workforce development by improving the quality of MS and BS degree programs. 
   * $100,000 for National Character Education Foundation in Zelienople, to conduct outreach programs in character education in PA schools. 
   * $100,000 for Robert Morris University in Moon Township, to grow and expand their new Department of Nursing. 
   * $100,000 for National Foundation for Teaching Entrepreneurship in Pittsburgh, to provide technology-based, entrepreneurial training program for students. 
   * $100,000 to Duquesne University to develop a supercomputing facility that will strengthen its interdisciplinary research and educational programs in the area of computational sciences. This facility will support the Center for Computational Sciences at Duquesne University, a nationally recognized center in the fields of biotechnology, nanotechnology and in the energy/fuel sciences. 
   * $100,000 to the Pittsburgh School District to launch a Reserve Teacher Corps initiative which addresses teacher quality by developing a model for the deployment of highly qualified substitute teachers in public schools. The District will develop a master of arts in teaching curriculum and provide mentoring and evaluation of teachers in the program. 
   * $100,000 to the Community College of Beaver County to develop educational programs, including the acquisition of technology. The technology will provide tower simulations to enhance the training quality and increase the number of students participating in the program. 
   * $100,000 to Saint Vincent College in Latrobe to support the Fred M. Rogers Center for Learning and Children's Media which will promote early learning, the primacy of caring relationships, and responsible uses of media. Saint Vincent College will establish degree, certificate, and continuing education programs in early learning, create an on-site early learning center to demonstrate best practices for undergraduates and graduates, and expand access to early childhood materials. 
   * $75,000 to Carnegie Science Center to develop the Final Frontier exhibit. The exhibit will involve interactive technology, planetarium programming and educational activities.
