SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE FUNDING FOR BUS SECURITY FOR PENNSYLVANIA
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen.  
 Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of the Senate Homeland Security Appropriations Subcommittee, announced today that the Department of Homeland Security (DHS), Office of State and Local Government Coordination and Preparedness (SLGCP) has approved funding to improve security for operators and passengers by providing bus security enhancements and training to bus companies nationwide. 
   "Safety and security are top priorities for Pennsylvania residents and it is necessary to continue to improve security on the buses across the Commonwealth," said Senator Santorum. "This funding will help to ensure the safety of all operators and passengers of both private and public bus companies." 
   "As we recognize the anniversary of September 11th, we must also establish priorities to protect our homeland," Senator Specter said. "Enhancing security throughout our transportation system is essential to fighting terrorism, and I commend the Department of Homeland Security for recognizing this as a priority." This funding is part of the SLGCP's Fiscal Year 2004 Intercity Bus Security Grant Program, which provides private and public bus operations with funding to promote security awareness and enhance facility and vehicle security. The following bus companies in Pennsylvania will receive funding: 
   * $138,776 for Carl R. Bieber, Inc in Berks County for Monitoring, Tracking and Communication Technologies. 
   * $132,076 for the Capitol Bus Company in Dauphin County for Facility Security and Vehicle Security Enhancements. 
   * $33,759 for MGR Travel, Ltd. DBA Elite Coach in Lancaster County for Monitoring, Tracking and Communication Technologies. 
   * $16,295 for A.C. Coach Operations Inc. in Mercer County for Facility Security Enhancements.
