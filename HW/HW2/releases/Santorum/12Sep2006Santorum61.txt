SEN. SANTORUM APPLAUDS JUDICIARY COMMITTEE FOR TAKING UP WESTERN PENNSYLVANIA DISTRICT JUDGE NOMINATION
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, today applauded the Senate Judiciary Committee for considering the nomination of Nora Barry Fischer to be a United States District Judge for the Western District of Pennsylvania.  
   "I am extremely pleased the Senate Judiciary Committee has taken up the nomination of Judge Fischer, and I urge them to work together to send her nomination to the floor as soon as possible," said Senator Santorum. "I believe Nora to be well-suited for a federal judgeship position in the Commonwealth, and the American Bar Association has agreed, unanimously finding her to be well-qualified. This judgeship has been open since February 2004, and I hope that we can move forward to quickly confirm this nominee." 
   Nora Barry Fischer is a native of Homestead, Pennsylvania. She graduated from St. Mary's College in 1973, and went on to earn her J.D. degree from the University of Notre Dame Law School in 1976. Currently, Fischer is a partner at the well-respected firm of Pietragallo Bosick & Gordon LLP in Pittsburgh.
