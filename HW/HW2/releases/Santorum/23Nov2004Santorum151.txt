SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE FEDERAL FUNDING FOR REVITALIZATION PROJECTS IN PENNSYLVANIA
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced federal funding for revitalization projects in Pennsylvania. The funding is included in the Economic Development Initiative portion of the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill.  
   "I am pleased to help provide the resources necessary that Pennsylvania projects have received the necessary resources to make improvements and advances in communities throughout the Commonwealth," Senator Santorum stated. "The rehabilitation of several historic buildings, houses, and parks along with the construction of new community buildings provides necessary upgrades to several projects in Pennsylvania as well as the preservation of Pennsylvania's historic; environmental and architectural character." 
   "I am pleased my colleagues have recognized the importance of these programs," Senator Specter said. "These projects will revitalize and improve cities and towns across the Commonwealth, and are vital to Pennsylvania's future economic and cultural growth." 
   The Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations Bill was passed by the Senate on November 20, 2004 and is pending final approval by the House of Representatives, followed by the President's signature. 
   Funding has been approved for the following Pennsylvania projects: 
   * $300,000 for the Urban Redevelopment Authority of Pittsburgh for the redevelopment of South Side Works; 
   * $300,000 for the City of Scranton for the revitalization of Cedar Avenue; 
   * $300,000 for Bucknell University in Lewisburg for the rehabilitation of the Lewisburg Downtown Theatre; 
   * $250,000 for the Allegheny West Foundation in Philadelphia for the Budd Plant rehabilitation project; 
   * $250,000 for the Indiana County Development Corporation in Indiana for the Indiana Springs development project; 
   * $250,000 for the City of Erie for site preparation and redevelopment of the vacant and blighted Koehler Brewery Building; 
   * $250,000 for the City of Greensburg for construction of a Center for the Arts; 
   * $250,000 for Our City Reading in Reading for the rehabilitation of abandoned houses and parks to provide home ownership to low-income families; 
   * $250,000 for the Greater Wilkes-Barre Chamber of Business and Industry in Wilkes-Barre for the acquisition and redevelopment of the historic Irem Temple, which will be converted into a cultural center; 
   * $250,000 for the City of Lancaster for the rehabilitation and renovation of the Lancaster Central Market; 
   * $250,000 for Eagles Mere Village, Inc., in Eagles Mere for the acquisition and rehabilitation of downtown buildings, as part of the town center revitalization initiative; 
   * $250,000 for the Allegheny County Department of Community and Economic Development, in Pittsburgh for the planning, design, and construction of Schenley Plaza; 
   * $250,000 for the Greene County Department of Planning and Development in Franklin Township for the construction a multi-tenant facility at EverGreene Technology Park; 
   * $200,000 for Universal Community Homes in Philadelphia for the conversion of land into for-sale units to low- and moderate-income families; 
   * $200,000 for the Borough of Lewistown for the rehabilitation and renovation of the Lewistown Municipal Building; 
   * $200,000 for the Darby Borough Community Development Corporation in Darby for a Main Street revitalization initiative including acquisition, renovation, and demolition of downtown buildings, as part of an urban redevelopment plan; 
   * $200,000 for the Chester County Industrial Development Authority in East Whiteland and Tredyffrin Townships for the redevelopment of the Atwater Brownfields site; and 
   * $200,000 for the Inglis Foundation in Philadelphia for the planning, design, and construction of housing for individuals with disabilities.
