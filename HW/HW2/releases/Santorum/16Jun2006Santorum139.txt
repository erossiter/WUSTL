SEN. SANTORUM ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR INDIANA COUNTY MUNICIPAL SERVICES AUTHORITY
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference and member of the Senate Agriculture Committee, today announced that the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Office of Rural Development has approved a grant for $58,000 for the Indiana County Municipal Services Authority.  
   The funding will be used to assist with the costs to repair and make flood protection improvements at the Plumville sewage treatment plant and Shelocta main pump station. 
   "I am pleased to have played a role in helping Indiana County obtain these important funds which will improve the sewage collection system," said Senator Santorum. "USDA Rural Development programs are important to keeping Pennsylvania clean and maintaining a safe and healthy environment. As Pennsylvania's first Senator to serve on the Agriculture Committee since 1904, I am committed to working with USDA Rural Development for the benefit of the Commonwealth." 
   For more information on programs offered by USDA Rural Development, contact the Pennsylvania State Office at (717) 237-2299 or visit its website at www.rurdev.usda.gov/pa.
