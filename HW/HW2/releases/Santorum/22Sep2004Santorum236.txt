SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE COMMITTEE APPROVAL OF FUNDING FOR WATER TREATMENT IMPROVEMENT PROJECTS IN PENNSYLVANIA
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, announced today that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved federal funding for projects in Pennsylvania. The funding is included in the Environmental Protection Agency portion of the Departments of Veterans Affairs and Housing and Urban Development, and Independent Agencies FY2005 Appropriations bill.  
   "This funding will provide the resources to restructure water treatment technologies and make improvements to the existing water and sewer treatment facilities in Pennsylvania," Santorum stated. "These necessary upgrades will ensure safe and environmentally responsible practices throughout the Commonwealth." 
   "I am pleased my colleagues on the Senate Appropriations Committee have approved funding for these vital improvements to Pennsylvania's infrastructure," Senator Specter said. "These projects will ensure that residents in these areas have safe and sanitary water supplies." 
   The Senate Appropriations Committee approved funding for the following Pennsylvania projects: 
   * $1,500,000 for the Three Rivers Wet Weather Demonstration Program in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania to develop innovative, cost-effective solutions to assist municipalities in eliminating sewer overflows; 
   * $500,000 to Penn State University for the Small Public Water System Technology Center in Dauphin County to help provide small public water system operators with important hands-on training, demonstration of new treatment technologies and use of innovative distance training techniques; 
   * $250,000 for the Derry Township Municipal Authority in Hershey for wastewater treatment plant upgrades; 
   * $250,000 for the Mercer County Sanitary Sewer and Water Treatment project in the City of Hermitage, City of Sharon, and Borough of Sharpsville; 
   * $250,000 for the City of Lancaster for water infrastructure improvements; 
   * $250,000 for the Newport Borough Sewer Authority for storm and sewer water separation; 
   * $250,000 for the York City Sewer Authority for wastewater collection system improvements; 
   * $250,000 for Pocono Township in Tannersville for the Route 611 Corridor sewer line construction; 
   * $200,000 for the Municipality of Penn Hills for the Madison Avenue Storm Sewer Project; 
   * $200,000 for the Nesquehoning Borough Authority in Carbon County for a water main replacement; 
   * $200,000 for the Mercer County Regional Council of Governments for the Shenango Valley Sewer/Water Improvement Project; 
   * $200,000 for the Berwick Industrial Development Association in Berwick for the sanitary storm water system; 
   * $200,000 for the City of Johnstown for water and sewer improvements at the Point Stadium multi-use facility.
