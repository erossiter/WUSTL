SENS. SANTORUM, SPECTER ANNOUNCE FEDERAL FUNDING FOR PENNSYLVANIA INTERIOR PROJECTS
  Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA), Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, and Sen. Arlen Specter (R-PA), a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, announced federal funding for Pennsylvania projects. This funding is contained in the Interior portion of the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill.  
   "Cultural preservation and environmental conservation is of importance to Pennsylvanians as they continue to experience and enjoy our Commonwealth's natural and historical treasures," Santorum stated. "I am pleased that these projects received funding that will assist programs in Pennsylvania in their efforts to preserve and protect environmental and historical sites." 
   "I am pleased my colleagues have recognized the importance of these Pennsylvania projects," Senator Specter said. "With this funding, Pennsylvania programs will have the resources to conserve and revamp vital land spaces, and enrich future generations by preserving Pennsylvania's rich industrial, cultural and historical heritage." 
   The Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations Bill was passed by the Senate on November 20, 2004 and is pending final approval by the House of Representatives, followed by the President's signature. 
   Funding has been approved for the following Pennsylvania projects: 
   * $5.75 million for Gettysburg National Military Park (NMP) Wills House in Gettysburg for restoration to this historical house where President Abraham Lincoln spent the night prior to delivering the Gettysburg Address. 
   * $1.5 million for Valley Forge National Military Park to purchase 197 acres adjacent to St. Gabriel's reform school to prevent development of the area. 
   * $1.056 million for Flight 93 National Memorial in Somerset, Pennsylvania to build starter public facilities and services at the Flight 93 memorial site and for administration of the site. 
   * $900,000 for Rivers of Steel Heritage Area to conserve the industrial and cultural heritage of the steel industry in southwestern Pennsylvania. 
   * $800,000 for Delaware and Lehigh National Heritage Corridor to assist the Delaware and Lehigh National Heritage Corridor Commission in planning and implementing an integrated strategy for protecting and promoting cultural, historical, and natural resources in this 150-mile long region. 
   * $700,000 for Pocono Environmental Education Center (PEEC) for improvement to cabins, the existing visitor, education, and administration building and wastewater treatment system. 
   * $550,000 for the Lackawanna Heritage Valley Authority to implement projects that develop the historic, cultural, natural, and economic resources of the Lackawanna Valley through the preservation, education, and promotion of the area's anthracite coal mining heritage. 
   * $500,000 for Schuylkill River Valley National Heritage Area to conserve, interpret, and develop the historical, cultural, natural, and recreational resources related to the industrial and cultural heritage of the Schuylkill River Valley, extending from Schuylkill County south through Philadelphia County. 
   * $250,000 for Benjamin Franklin Tercentenary Commission to develop a fitting and proper tribute in celebration of the 300th anniversary of Benjamin Franklin's birth. 
   * $150,000 for Moravian College Brethren House for renovation and preservation of this 18th Century landmark.
