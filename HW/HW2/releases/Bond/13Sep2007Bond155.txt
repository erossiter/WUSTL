
WASHINGTON, D.C. - U.S. Senator Kit Bond announced that he secured more than $6 million in federal funds for critical transportation projects in Southwest Missouri.   These dollars will mean safer and more efficient roads and bridges and healthier communities in Southwest Missouri, said Bond. As this states senior Senator one of my top priorities is to ensure our state receives the resources needed for critical projects that will improve the lives of Missourians.  As the ranking member of the Senate Transportation, Housing and Urban Development Appropriations Subcommittee, Bond secured more than $6 million in federal funds for Southwest Missouri transportation projects in the subcommittees fiscal year 2008 spending bill. The bill passed the Senate yesterday by a vote of 88-7. Before being signed into the law the bill must be reconciled with the House of Representatives version.   Bond secured funds for the following critical transportation projects:   $500,000 for Highway 13 at Broadmoor Intersection in Springfield. Funds will provide for the repair and expansion of ingress and egress lanes at the intersection.   $3,000,000 for Route 266  I-44 Interchange in Greene County. Funds will upgrade Interstate 44 and Route 266 interchange to serve the Springfield-Branson National Airport Relocated Midfield Terminal.   $750,000 for the U.S. 60 Corridor west of Republic. Funds will provide for the design, construction of lanes, safety and congestion improvements.   $2,000,000 for the City Utilities of Springfield Intermodal Transfer Facility in Springfield. Funds will construct a new intermodal transfer facility for the city's public transit system.   $500,000 for Camp Barnabas in Purdy. Funds will provide for the construction of new handicap accessible cabins for children with special needs.  Bond also secured funds for the following statewide projects:   $2,000,000 for the Missouri Statewide Bus  Bus Facilities.  # # #
