SEN. DORGAN BECOMES CHAIRMAN OF INTERSTATE COMMERCE, TRADE & TOURISM SUBCOMMITTEE
  Sen. Daniel Inouye (D-HI), chairman of the Commerce, Science & Transportation Committee, announced today that Sen. Byron Dorgan (D-ND) is the new chairman of the Interstate Commerce, Trade & Tourism Subcommittee. Dorgan will now be in a key position to hold hearings and advance legislation on commerce, trade and tourism issues.  
   "As chairman of the Interstate Commerce, Trade & Tourism Subcommittee, I will push for policies that look out for consumers and American workers," said Dorgan. "We need to ensure American competitiveness and work towards trade practices that are fair to American businesses and their workers." 
   The Interstate Commerce, Trade & Tourism Subcommittee has jurisdiction over trade and competition oversight, Olympics and professional sports, and tourism. In addition, the subcommittee oversees the Federal Trade Commission and consumer issues, such as spam, ID theft and do-not-call lists. 
   As a member of the Commerce, Science & Transportation Committee, Dorgan will also serve on the Aviation Operations, Safety and Security Subcommittee, the Science, Technology and Innovation Subcommittee, the Space, Aeronautics and Related Sciences Subcommittee, and the Surface Transportation and Merchant Marine Infrastructure, Safety and Security Subcommittee. These assignments position him to play a central role in policy making that could affect North Dakota air passenger service and federal highways.
