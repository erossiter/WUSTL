SEN. DORGAN MEETS WITH FORMER GOV. SCHAFER, PROMISES SUPPORT
  U.S. Senator Byron Dorgan (D-ND) met Wednesday with former North Dakota Governor Ed Schafer, who has been nominated to be President Bush's next Secretary of Agriculture. The meeting at Dorgan's Capitol Hill office was part of a series of visits Schafer will make to Senate offices in the run-up to the confirmation process. Dorgan told Schafer he will support his nomination to the post. 
   A statement from Senator Dorgan on the meeting is below.
   TEXT OF STATEMENT BY SENATOR BYRON DORGAN
   November 14, 2007
   "I met with Governor Schafer this afternoon. I told him it's an honor for a North Dakotan to be nominated to a Cabinet post, especially the position of Secretary of Agriculture because it's so important to our state. 
   "We have thousands of family farmers whose lives depend on good farm policy, and I look forward to working with him on those issues.
   "I told Governor Schafer I support his nomination and will work with him through this nomination process."
