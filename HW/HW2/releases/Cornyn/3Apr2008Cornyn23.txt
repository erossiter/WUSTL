SEN. CORNYN HOSTS MEETING WITH MAYOR LEPPERT, DALLAS LEADERS TO DISCUSS SHARED GOALS FOR ECONOMIC GROWTH, PUBLIC SAFETY
  U.S. Sen. John Cornyn, R-Texas, today hosted a meeting with Dallas Mayor Tom Leppert and several other Dallas-area elected officials, to discuss several shared priorities for the safety and continued growth of Dallas residents, businesses and infrastructure. 
   "I was pleased to welcome Mayor Leppert and several Dallas leaders to Washington. The Mayor and I have already had the opportunity to work together on several initiatives important to Dallas families, including the important fight to curb cheese heroin abuse among Dallas youth," Sen. Cornyn said.
   Sen. Cornyn added: "I pledged my continued support for Mayor Leppert's priorities to enhance safety and economic growth in Dallas. A top priority for both of us is the fruition of the Trinity River Corridor Project to boost flood protection for thousands of Dallas residents, improve infrastructure to keep pace with Dallas's rapidly growing population, and generate jobs and economic growth to benefit the community. Specifically, I reiterated my support for the Dallas Floodway Extension to reinforce the city's levee system and prepare and protect major buildings, businesses and residences from future flooding."
   Sen. Cornyn serves on the Armed Services, Judiciary and Budget Committees. In addition, he is Vice Chairman of the Senate Select Committee on Ethics. He serves as the top Republican on the Judiciary Committee's Immigration, Border Security and Refugees subcommittee and the Armed Services Committee's Airland subcommittee. He served previously as Texas Attorney General, Texas Supreme Court Justice, and Bexar County District Judge.
