SEN. CORNYN ISSUES STATEMENT ON MARGARET SPELLINGS NOMINATION TO BE SECRETARY OF EDUCATION
  Sen. John Cornyn (R-Texas) made the following statement Wednesday regarding President Bush's announcement that he will nominate Margaret Spellings of Texas to succeed Rod Paige as Secretary of Education:  
   "I am encouraged to learn that the President has chosen Margaret Spellings as his nominee for Secretary of Education. Margaret is someone who I count as a friend and I have enormous respect for her wise and compassionate counsel on a wide number of domestic policy issues.
   "As Secretary of Education, I am confident that she will provide the country with a strong and knowledgeable voice on critical education issues as she furthers the No Child Left Behind policy goals over the next four years."
   Margaret Spellings currently serves as Assistant to the President for Domestic Policy. Prior to her White House appointment, she worked for six years as then-Gov. George W. Bush's Senior Advisor, with responsibility for developing and implementing education policy. She also worked as Associate Executive Director of the Texas Association of School Boards. Other previous experience includes work for Texas Governor William P. Clements and for members of the Texas House of Representatives. Spellings is a graduate of the University of Houston.
   Sen. Cornyn chairs the subcommittee on the Constitution, Civil Rights and Property Rights, and is the only former judge on the Judiciary Committee. He served previously as Texas Attorney General, Texas Supreme Court Justice and Bexar County District Judge.
