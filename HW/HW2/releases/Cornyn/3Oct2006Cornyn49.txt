SEN. CORNYN APPLAUDS $36 MILLION FEDERAL GRANT FOR UNIVERSITY OF TEXAS HEALTH SCIENCE CENTER AT HOUSTON
  Sen. John Cornyn today made the following statement regarding a $36 million, five-year grant that was awarded to the University of Texas Health Science Center at Houston today by the National Institutes of Health. This important federal funding will allow UT-Houston to build the Center for Clinical and Translational Science, which will be the first of its kind in the State of Texas. 
   "This is federal funding is great news for news for UT-Houston and it is a true credit to the hard work, dedication and determination of its world-class scientists and physicians. They have been true leaders in the fields and this grant will ensure that the City of Houston remains a leader in the advancement of scientific and medical breakthroughs. I congratulate everyone involved with this critical program and look forward to seeing the tremendous benefits that this Center will ultimately yield for the City of Houston and its citizens."
