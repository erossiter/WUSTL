SENATE HONORS 60TH ANNIVERSARY OF MODERN STATE OF ISRAEL
  U.S. Sen. John Cornyn, R-Texas, Vice Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference, made the following statement Wednesday regarding unanimous Senate passage of a resolution, S. Res. 522, recognizing the 60th anniversary of the founding of the modern State of Israel. The bipartisan resolution, co-sponsored by Sen. Cornyn, also reaffirms the bonds of close friendship and cooperation between the United States and Israel. It passed late Tuesday. Click here for the full text. 
   "Israel and America have a strong friendship that spans oceans, and is based on shared values. This resolution reaffirms our commitment to preserving and strengthening that alliance.
   "In the face of common threats, our relationship today is as important as ever. We have mutual goals of defeating radical Islamic terrorism, fostering Middle East stability and promoting freedom.
   "Israel has shown an unwavering belief in democracy, justice, security and peace. They deserve not only our friendship and support, but our admiration. I extend my warmest congratulations to the State of Israel and the Israeli people for their upcoming anniversary."
   Sen. Cornyn serves on the Armed Services, Judiciary and Budget Committees. In addition, he is Vice Chairman of the Senate Select Committee on Ethics. He serves as the top Republican on the Judiciary Committee's Immigration, Border Security and Refugees subcommittee and the Armed Services Committee's Airland subcommittee. He served previously as Texas Attorney General, Texas Supreme Court Justice, and Bexar County District Judge.
