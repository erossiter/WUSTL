                                                                             Corker Votes for Higher Ed Reauthorization                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Increases Pell Grant  Simplifies FAFSA  Expands Grants for Teacher Training and Mentoring                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Tuesday  July 24  2007   WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  today voted for S 1642  the Higher Education Authorization Bill  which authorizes increases in the Pell Grant program  simplifies FAFSA  the Free Application For Student Aid   and expands grants for teacher preparation  retention and mentoring     By increasing the Pell Grant program and simplifying the FAFSA from eight pages to two  I believe this legislation will extend the opportunity of education to more young people across our country  said Corker  As mayor of Chattanooga  I saw firsthand the importance of having high quality teachers in the classroom  so Im delighted this bill expands grant programs to better prepare  mentor and retain Americas teachers     This bill also helps ensure that our young people are prepared for an increasingly competitive world marketplace by increasing grants in math and science education     The Higher Education Authorization Bill  which passed unanimously by a vote of 95 to 0  includes provisions to     Authorize increases in the Pell Grant program  a  1 990 increase over current levels by 2011 2012   5 400 in 2008 2009   5 700 in 2009 2010   6 000 in 2010 2011  and  6 300 in 2011 2012    Expand grant programs for teacher preparation  retention and mentoring programs    Simplify the Free Application For Student Aid  FAFSA  and reduce it from an eight page  100 question form to a two page form    Expand Academic Competitiveness Grants and National Science and Mathematics Access to Retain Talent Grants    Reform ethics requirements for lenders and institutions by requiring colleges and universities to establish a code of conduct governing relationships with lenders  prohibiting financial aid officers from takings gifts from lenders  and requiring universities to have at least three unaffiliated lenders on their preferred lender list    Last Thursday  July 19  Corker voted for the Higher Education Access Act of 2007 which passed the Senate by a vote of 78 18  The bill increased the maximum amount available to Pell Grant eligible students to  5 400  eliminated the three year limitation on deferments for members of the military  granted loan forgiveness to those serving in the public sector  and established a grant program to help states further increase college access for low income students  The measure fell within the budget allocation for higher education programs and did not result in deficit spending
