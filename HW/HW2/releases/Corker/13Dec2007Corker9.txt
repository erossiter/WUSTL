                                                                             Corker Applauds Passage of Broad Energy Security Legislation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Bill Strengthens and Reforms Fuel Economy Standards for the First Time In More Than 30 Years from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Thursday  December 13  2007  WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  a member of the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee  today voted for Senate passage of H R  6  broad energy legislation that will promote the swift and continued development of biofuel alternatives to Middle Eastern oil  conserve energy by improving energy efficiency standards  and reduce gasoline consumption by increasing fuel economy standards  The bill passed the Senate by a vote of 86 to 8 late Thursday  It must now be passed by the House before it can be sent to the President to be signed into law   Although I was concerned with the process by which versions of this bill were presented to the Senate this week and last  I am pleased that we were able to reached a compromise that has strong bipartisan support and will increase our countrys energy security  said Corker  The bill strengthens and reforms fuel economy standards for the first time in more than 30 years  which is one of the most significant ways America can reduce its oil consumption  promotes swift and continued development of biofuel alternatives to foreign oil  like cellulosic ethanol which has so much potential in Tennessee  and improves energy efficiency standards   We improved this bill by removing a prescriptive  one size fits all electricity standard that would harm consumers in the Southeast  as well as a regressive tax package that would increase energy prices at a time when Americans can least afford it   I thank the bill managers  Senators Jeff Bingaman and Pete Domenici  for their tremendous work in forging a bipartisan consensus on this important legislation  Members on both sides of the aisle had to make tough decisions in order for us to pass an energy bill this year  and I appreciate all of their efforts  The legislation we passed today is good for our country and good for Tennessee
