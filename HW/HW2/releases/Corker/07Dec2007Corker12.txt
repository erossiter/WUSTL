                                                                             Corker Votes against Cloture on House Energy Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Bill benefits certain regions of the country over others  delays passage of comprehensive energy legislation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Friday  December 07  2007  WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Senator Bob Corker  R Tenn   a member of the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee  today voted against cloture on H R 6  the energy bill passed yesterday in the House of Representatives  The Senate rejected the motion to invoke cloture by a vote of 53 to 42  Sixty votes were necessary in order for the Senate to proceed to the legislation     The Senate voted against moving forward on H R 6 because it veered from bipartisan energy legislation that the Senate passed in June  The House bill included a renewable electricity standard that picks winners and losers among clean energy sources  hurting areas of the country that lack certain resources     I have concerns with the way in which this bill was negotiated and believe that todays cloture vote was not a serious attempt at completing comprehensive energy legislation this year  Bipartisan negotiations were occurring that  if honored  could have produced a strong  meaningful piece of legislation  Corker said     I hope we will be able to consider an energy bill like the one passed by the Senate earlier this year  which would have strengthened and reformed fuel economy standards for the first time in 30 years  promoted swift and continued development of biofuel alternatives to foreign oil  and improved energy efficiency standards  Unfortunately  the energy bill that passed the House yesterday is flawed and includes a prescriptive  one size fits all electricity standard that would harm consumers in the Southeast     I look forward to working with my colleagues to find a solution that contains the increased fuel economy standards  one of the most significant ways America can reduce its oil consumption  and strong incentives for the development of advanced biofuels  such as cellulosic ethanol  which has so much potential in Tennessee
