                                                                             Corker Votes for Bill to Expand Stem Cell Research Without Breaking Ethical Divide                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Votes Against Using Federal Dollars to Incentivize Destruction of Human Embryos                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Wednesday  April 11  2007  WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  today voted and expressed strong support for S  30  the Hope Offered through Principled and Ethical Stem Cell Research  HOPE  Act  sponsored by Sens  Johnny Isakson  R GA  and Norm Coleman  R MN   which Corker says   allows additional research to take place on stem cells without creating an ethical divide          Corker voted against S  5  the Stem Cell Research Enhancement Act  restating his belief that  it is wrong to provide federal funding for stem cell research that destroys human embryos           Corker stated   Few of us are untouched by the many illnesses that plague Americans  My own family has been touched by Alzheimer s disease because my father has it  I have spent a tremendous amount of time looking into this issue  reading white papers  talking to researchers all across America  and visiting an embryonic adoption center         I have learned that honorable people   who truly want to see cures take place in America and for people all across the world   can disagree as it relates to embryonic stem cell research          I have also learned that tremendous breakthroughs are taking place with adult stem cells  cord blood stem cells  and stem cells derived from amniotic fluids         I believe science is going to outpace our ability to deal with this issue         And finally  I believe that science will allow us to continue to achieve cures for Americans and for people all across this world without creating this ethical divide of destroying human embryos          I strongly support and applaud Senator Isakson and Senator Coleman for putting forth the Hope Act  which allows additional research to take place on stem cells without creating an ethical divide  And I oppose S  5  which uses federal dollars to incentivize the destruction of human embryos         S  30  which passed the Senate by a vote of 70 to 28  would permit federal funding of scientific research that does not harm embryos  but could produce pluripotent stem cells from other sources such as amniotic fluid and from embryos that have died naturally                               S  5  which passed the Senate by a vote of 63 to 34  would permit federal funding of embryonic stem cell research that allows for the potential destruction of viable embryos  It is similar to legislation that passed Congress last year but was later vetoed by President Bush
