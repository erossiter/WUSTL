                                                                             Corker Votes for Passage of the Water Resources Development Act                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Is Disappointed the Bill Doesn t Include Reforms to Set Project Priorities                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Wednesday  May 16  2007  WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  made the following statement today after voting for H R  1495  the Water Resources Development Act  WRDA   which authorizes funding for the U S  Army Corps of Engineers         Maintaining and improving water infrastructure is important for public safety  economic development and quality of life   said Corker   This bill includes a number of important projects that will improve water quality and safety for communities and citizens across our state         Corker also voted for an amendment to the bill  which failed  that would have established a commission to analyze authorized water projects to classify them within a tiered system of priority  This prioritized list would have been provided to Congress and made available to the public          Although I voted for the bill   which is long overdue   I m disappointed that it does not include reforms to the Corps process that would have helped Congress set priorities   Corker added   The current system of authorizing projects is inefficient  We end up providing too little funding for too many projects  which holds up progress and completion dates and keeps the most critical projects from being finished in a timely fashion          The Water Resources Development Act passed by a vote of 91 to 4
