                                                                             Corker Votes for Head Start Reauthorization Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Wednesday  November 14  2007  WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  today voted to reauthorize the Head Start program which provides comprehensive development services for low income children to help them better prepare for kindergarten  The bill  which passed the Senate by a vote of 95 to 0  raises the income eligibility to 130 percent of the federal poverty level  requires that at least half of the teachers in programs have a bachelors degree in early childhood education  and forces programs to reapply for funds at least every five years     As a former mayor  Ive seen firsthand how the Head Start program provides children who have the greatest needs with a strong foundation for a successful future  said Corker  The bill we passed today renews  strengthens and expands Head Start so we can improve this experience for the nearly 20 000 Tennessee children currently participating and expands Head Starts reach to more children in need  Weve also added safeguards to help ensure that only the most deserving programs continue to receive funds  enhancing accountability and protecting taxpayer dollars     The conference report to accompany H R  1429  the Improving Head Start Readiness Act of 2007  must now go to the President for signature
