                                                                             Alexander  Corker Urge DOE to Plan for Oak Ridge Cleanup Efforts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Thursday  June 07  2007  WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senators Lamar Alexander  R TN  and Bob Corker  R TN  have written a letter urging the U S  Department of Energy  DOE  to complete its long term plan for conducting much needed nuclear cleanup work at Oak Ridge     There are roughly 150 contaminated structures  storage tanks  and other facilities at Oak Ridge National Lab and Y 12 that need to be addressed  said Alexander  Delaying the cleanup threatens the safety and effectiveness of these institutions that are so important to keeping our nation safe and competitive in the global economy     Continuing with the cleanup efforts at Oak Ridge would advance the science mission at the Lab and the national security mission at Y 12  said Corker  It is necessary that funding be available for additional sites so that as the current cleanup winds down  the transition to these other sites can begin seamlessly     In a letter dated June 4  the Senators wrote to DOE Deputy Secretary Clay Sell urging the Department to include additional sites  contaminated structures  storage tanks  a scrap yard  and source materials at the Oak Ridge National Laboratory  ORNL  and the Y 12 Weapons Site  in the long term plan for the Environmental Management  EM  program  which is the federal program for cleaning up contaminated DOE sites  The Senators stress that adding these sites to the EM program is essential to Oak Ridge meeting the requirements under the Federal Facilities Agreement with the State of Tennessee     Proceeding with the cleanup would be beneficial to the science mission at ORNL and the national security mission at Y 12  they write  Moreover  it is necessary that funding for cleanup be provided in the same time frame as the completion of the East Tennessee Technology Park  ETTP  so that as the ETTP work ramps down to closure  the IFDP  Integrated Facilities Disposition Plan  work can ramp up to utilize the existing workforce skills in the region      A copy of the letter is attached    June 4  2007    The Honorable Clay Sell   Deputy Secretary   U S  Department of Energy   1000 Independence Avenue  SW   Washington  DC 20585    Dear Mr  Sell     We are writing to request that you review the Critical Decision 0  CD 0  package for the remaining cleanup scope at Oak Ridge  labeled as the Integrated Facilities Disposition Plan  IFDP   as soon as possible     We are concerned that the reduced funding request for the Environmental Management  EM  program in the Presidents Fiscal Year 200S budget jeopardizes progress on the much needed nuclear cleanup work at Oak Ridge  We are particularly concerned that no specific funding was included for the IFDP     Folded within the WDP are approximately 150 contaminated structures  storage tanks  a scrap yard and source materials at the Oak Ridge National Laboratory  ORNL  and the Y 1 2 Weapons Site that we believe should be addressed to better support the Departments science and nuclear security missions  The condition of the aging facilities is increasing the difficulty to safely decontaminate and decommission  DD  the structures in Oak Ridge     The Critical Decision 0 package for the IFDP would establish a mission need for this scope of work to be added into the EM baseline  The work scope that is contained in this package is essential to finishing the cleanup in Oak Ridge and proceeding with the groundwater and soil remediation requirements under the Federal Facilities Agreement with the State of Tennessee    Proceeding with the cleanup would be beneficial to the science mission at ORNL and the national security mission at Y 12  Moreover  it is necessary that funding for cleanup be provided in the same time frame as the completion of the East Tennessee Technology Park  ETTP  so that as the ETTP work ramps down to closure  the IFDP work can ramp up to utilize the existing work force skills in the region     The Oak Ridge Reservation provides necessary capabilities and resources for DOE operations  The State of Tennessee and DOE have enjoyed mutual benefits from both the ORNL science programs and the Y  12 Complex national security programs    Thank you for your attention to our request     Sincerely    Lamar Alexander   United States Senator
