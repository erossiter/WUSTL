                                                                             Corker Votes to Reauthorize State Children s Health Insurance Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Hopes SCHIP Debate Leads to Meaningful Discussion of Health Care Reform                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Friday  August 03  2007  WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  late Thursday voted to reauthorize the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  SCHIP  and expressed his desire that Congress use the SCHIP debate as a starting point for discussing broader health care reforms  such as his Every American Insured Health Act  that provides a comprehensive solution to give all Americans access to health insurance     I voted to reauthorize and fund the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  because I believe it has been an important and successful program  While this reauthorization bill is far from perfect  it does ensure that low income children across America will have health insurance  said Corker  I think all of us agree that this is a short term solution  and that we need to move beyond solutions that only nibble at the edges of a much larger crisis facing our country  the 45 million Americans that lack basic health insurance  I will continue to work with my colleagues on both sides of the aisle to develop broader proposals to ensure that all Americans have access to affordable  high quality private health insurance     Last week  Senator Corker  along with Senator Richard Burr  R NC   introduced the Every American Insured Health Act  S  1886  which provides a revenue neutral proposal to ensure that all Americans have access to affordable health care coverage that they can take from job to job  The bill is cosponsored by Senators Coburn  R OK   Martinez  R FL   Alexander  R TN  and Dole  R NC   Specifically  the bill   Provides refundable  advanceable flat tax credits   2 160 for individuals and  5 400 for families  in a revenue neutral manner  meaning it does not add to the federal deficit        Equalizes the tax treatment of those who have employer provided health insurance and those who do not  Gives Americans the right to choose the health insurance that meets their needs  Eliminates the current cost shifting in the health care market that drives up costs for everyone  and Provides Americans with the ability to save for future health care costs by putting all remaining tax credit money into a health care spending account   The proposal Senator Burr and I have offered with our colleagues is meant to be the starting point of a debate  We need more ideas and more discussion  devoid of political labels and the dead on arrival mindset that often dominates the discussion of both parties when new proposals are made  This Congress owes the American public our attention to a crisis that is affecting so many individuals and their families  I believe this is our moral obligation
