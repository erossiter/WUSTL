                                                                             Corker Applauds USDA s Decision to Make Farmers in All 95 Counties Eligible for Emergency Loans                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  USDA Declares State  Natural Disaster Area  As a Result of April Freeze from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Monday  June 18  2007  WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  today applauded the U S  Department of Agricultures  USDA  decision to designate the entire state of Tennessee as a primary natural disaster area  making farmers in all 95 Tennessee counties who suffered losses as a result of the April freeze eligible to apply for low interest emergency loans from the Farm Service Agency  FSA      Corker joined other members of the Tennessee Congressional delegation in an April 17  2007 letter to U S  Agriculture Secretary Mike Johanns urging him to approve the State of Tennessee s request for an agricultural disaster declaration for all Tennessee counties  The record low temperatures that began on April 4  2007 and continued through April 11  2007 caused extensive crop damage across Tennessee     This is good news for Tennessee farmers who suffered tremendous losses as a result of the record low temperatures we had in April  said Corker  I was proud to support the states request for this disaster declaration  and I hope the designation and subsequent loans will provide some help to farmers across our state as they work to recover their crops and their livelihoods     In considering loan applications  the FSA will be taking into account the extent of losses  security available  and repayment ability     Local FSA offices can provide farmers with further information     Attached below is the delegations April 17 letter to theUSDA April 17  2007    The Honorable Mike Johanns   Secretary   United States Department of Agriculture   1400 Independence Avenue  SW   Washington  D C  20250    Dear Mr  Secretary     We are writing to urge you to approve the State of Tennessees request for an agricultural disaster declaration for all 95 counties across Tennessee as a result of the effects of an early freeze beginning April 4 and continuing until recently  Governor Phil Bredesen is requesting the declaration so that qualifying farmers would be able to receive low interest emergency loans from the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA   along with any other assistance that might be approved by Congress     Across Tennessee  record low temperatures and deep freeze have devastated much of our fruit  vegetable and winter wheat crops  among others  Early estimates indicate 50 to 100 percent losses for these crops  Exact damages will not be known for some time  but tremendous losses for affected farmers are imminent and many of them will require a full season to recover     Thank you for your consideration of Tennessees request for assistance  Please feel free to contact our offices should any additional information be needed     Sincerely     Lamar Alexander   United States Senator    Bob Corker   United States Senator    Steve Cohen   Member of Congress    David Davis   Member of Congress    John Duncan   Member of Congress    John Tanner   Member of Congress    Marsha Blackburn   Member of Congress    Lincoln Davis   Member of Congress    Bart Gordon   Member of Congress
