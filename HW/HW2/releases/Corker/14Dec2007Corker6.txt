                                                                             Corker Votes for Farm Bill to Aid Tennessee Farmers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Bill Supports Commodity Producers and Provides New Incentives for Alternative Energy Production                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Friday  December 14  2007   WASHINGTON  D C   U  S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  made the following comments today after voting for the Farm Bill  H R  2419  the Food and Energy Security Act of 2007  The bill passed the Senate by a vote of 79 to 14  and now differences between this measure and the bill passed by the House in July must be reconciled in a conference committee    The Farm Bill is tremendously important to Tennessee  It provides our farmers and ranchers with an adequate safety net  and at the same time  contains reforms that will improve various programs  said Corker It is my hope that the Farm Bill moves swiftly through conference and is signed into law in time for the upcoming planting season   The Senate passed Farm Bill maintains support for our commodity producers and provides new incentives for the production and expansion of alternative energy  including cellulosic ethanol  continued Corker  Tennessees climate and terrain make us ideally suited to produce the kinds of feedstocks used in the biofuels industry  and our farmers are helping lead the way in expanding this industry
