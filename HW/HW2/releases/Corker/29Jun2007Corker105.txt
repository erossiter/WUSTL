                                                                             Alexander  Corker Announce  2 Million For Memphis Law Enforcement Project                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Friday  June 29  2007  WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Lamar Alexander  R TN  and Bob Corker  R TN  today announced that the Fiscal Year  FY  2008 Commerce  Justice and Science Appropriations bill  which was approved by the Senate Appropriations Committee yesterday  includes  2 million in funding for the Memphis Regional Law Enforcement Project     The Memphis community is vital to the economic future and stability of Tennessee  said Alexander  Making sure that it is a safe  prosperous place to live and work requires coordination between all levels of government     Last month I met with law enforcement officials in Memphis to discuss their efforts to fight violent and gang related crime in their community  said Corker  As a former mayor  I know how important their work is to local citizens  and I hope these funds will go a long way toward helping solve this problem     The Memphis Regional Law Enforcement Project is a University of Memphis led effort combining law enforcement from the City of Memphis  Shelby County  the State District Attorney General and the US District Attorney to address the growing gang problem in the Memphis region  Recently the Department of Justice released statistics showing Memphis as one of the fastest growing cities for violent crime in the United States  This initiative will combine all the regional resources to provide an integrated approach to combat violet crime and gang activity     The Senate version of the FY08 Commerce  Justice and Science Appropriations bill must now be considered by the full United States Senate
