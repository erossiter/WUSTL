                                                                             Alexander  Corker Applaud Dr  Benjamin Hooks  Presidential Medal of Freedom Recipient                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Monday  October 29  2007  Washington  D C   U S  Senators Lamar Alexander and Bob Corker made the following statements today following President Bushs announcement that Dr  Benjamin L  Hooks  of Memphis  is a recipient of the Presidential Medal of Freedom  the nations highest civil award     Ben Hooks deserves the presidential medal for his courage  for his eloquence and for his early and persistent commitment to equal opportunity for every American  said Alexander     Ben Hooks has served our state and country with great honor for more than a half century  As a judge  minister  member of the Federal Communications Commission  and executive director of the NAACP  Dr  Hooks has been a pioneer in the Civil Rights Movement  said Corker  I am tremendously proud to call Dr  Hooks a friend  a Tennessean and now a Medal of Freedom recipient  He has worked tirelessly to improve the lives of all Americans     The announcement from the White House reads  Benjamin L  Hooks has dedicated his life to equality  opportunity  and justice  He is a pioneer of the Civil Rights movement  and his efforts to extend the full promise of America to all its citizens have helped bring our Nation closer to its founding ideals     Established by Executive Order 11085 in 1963  the Presidential Medal of Freedom may be awarded by the President to any person who has made an especially meritorious contribution to  1  the security or national interests of the United States  or  2  world peace  or  3  cultural or other significant public or private endeavors     President Bush will honor Dr  Hooks and the other recipients at a White House ceremony on Monday  November 5  2007
