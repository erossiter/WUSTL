                                                                             Corker Cosponsors Amendment to Improve Women s Access to OB GYNs in Rural Areas                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Wednesday  December 12  2007  WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  delivered floor remarks today voicing his support and co sponsorship of an amendment to expand access to obstetrics care in rural areas  In various states like Tennessee  OB GYNs are leaving their practices due to the extraordinarily high cost of medical malpractice insurance  The Healthy Mothers and Healthy Babies Rural Access to Care amendment  offered to the Farm Bill by Senator Judd Gregg  R NH   would place caps on non economic damages for OB GYNs facing lawsuits in rural counties across the country  Ninety one of Tennessees 95 counties are classified as rural areas  and according to the Tennessee Medical Association  the number of obstetricians in these 91 rural counties dropped from 179 in 1997 to 103 in 2003     More than 30 of our states 95 counties have inadequate access to obstetrical care  and 15 counties have no obstetrical access at all  The outrageous cost of medical malpractice insurance has driven many doctors out of this field  Our mothers and babies deserve better  said Corker  Sen  Greggs amendment addresses this problem in a thoughtful way  and I was proud to cosponsor and vote for it to improve rural access to care
