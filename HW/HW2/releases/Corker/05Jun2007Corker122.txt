                                                                             Corker Urges Administration to Provide Sufficient Funding for Processing Claims of Cold War Veterans and Their Families                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Tuesday  June 05  2007  WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  has written to U S  Health and Human Services  HHS  Secretary Michael Leavitt and U S  Labor Secretary Elaine Chao asking that the administration provide sufficient funding to process the claims of Cold War Veterans and their families who are included under Part B of the Energy Employees Occupational Illness Compensation Program  EEOICPA      Cold War veterans and their families made great sacrifices to serve our country  and they should be treated with respect  said Corker  I will continue working with Senator Alexander and our colleagues to ensure that they receive the compensation and medical benefits they deserve as quickly as possible     Congress created the Energy Employees Occupational Illness Compensation Program in 2000 to provide compensation and medical benefits to workers who contracted radiation induced cancers  beryllium diseases or silicosis during the course of their employment with the Department of Energy  DOE  or its contractors  The EEOICPA is responsible for identifying the workers who are eligible for compensation     The letter  also signed by U S  Sen  Lamar Alexander  R TN  and 15 other senators  states     It has come to our attention that critical components of EEOICPA face a serious funding shortfall and program offices have already taken steps to cut back on claims processing  In particular  we understand that the Office of Compensation Analysis and Support  OCAS  within the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health  NIOSH  which conducts activities to assist claimants and support the role of the Secretary of Health and Human Services under the EEOICPA Programis not receiving the funds necessary to perform dose reconstruction activities and process Special Exposure Cohort petitions at full capacity  and it is our hope that your own Departments will work together to solve this problem     This news is extremely troubling to us  In establishing the EEOICPA program  Congress intended our Cold War Heroes and their families to be compensated as quickly as possible  Delays resulting form insufficient programmatic funding are unacceptable  The NIOSH funding shortfall must be quickly corrected     According to the Department of Labor  Tennessee has more than 23 000 health claims from more than 9 000 individual workers under the EEOICPA  Nearly 7 000 Tennessee claims are still waiting for a final decision under this system
