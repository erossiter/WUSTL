                                                                             Corker Says Energy Bill is Good for Tennessee                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Thursday  June 21  2007  WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN   a member of the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee  today voted for a procedural motion to continue moving the energy bill forward  The bill promotes swift and continued development of biofuel alternatives to petroleum and is the first major piece of national energy legislation since the Energy Policy Act of 2005     I believe this legislation will increase our countrys energy security by taking steps to reduce our gasoline consumption and dependence on foreign oil  said Corker  This bill is particularly good for the many Tennesseans who are playing a significant role in creating biofuel alternatives to petroleum     Tennessees climate and terrain make us ideally suited to produce the kinds of feedstocks used in the development and production of biodiesel and cellulosic ethanol  I visited the University of Tennessee  Knoxville a few weeks ago for an update on the Tennessee Biofuels Initiative  a program developed by UT to position our state as a leader in the nation s efforts toward reduced dependence on petroleum  They have set a goal of working with Tennessee farmers to produce one billion gallons of Grassoline annually  This level of production would replace approximately 30 percent of Tennessee s present petroleum consumption     Im proud of the role our state is playing in helping our country become more energy secure  and I will continue to look for ways we can create even greater opportunities for innovation in Tennessee and across the country     While I wish this bill was broader in its scope as it relates to many other energy sources  I believe it is an important step in the right direction     Corkers vote was to invoke cloture on the Reid substitute amendment  1502 to H R  6  the CLEAN Energy Act of 2007  which passed by a vote of 61 to 32
