                                                                             Corker Statement on Iraq                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         from the Office of Senator Bob Corker  Thursday  September 13  2007  WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Bob Corker  R TN  made the following statement tonight following President Bushs remarks to the nation  Earlier this week Corker  a member of both the Senate Armed Services and Senate Foreign Relations Committees  attended over nine hours of hearings as Commanding General David Petraeus and U S  Ambassador to Iraq Ryan Crocker presented their Iraq assessments and recommendations to the Senate  Im glad the President has decided the Petraeus Plan is the best way forward in Iraq  said Corker  Under General Petraeus leadership  our military is  province by province  changing the mission on the ground in Iraq and more and more  Iraqi sheiks are viewing us as enablers rather than occupiers  Based on the military gains this summer  the Petraeus Plan is redeploying troops over the next year  bringing approximately 30 000 troops home by the end of this summer  and transitioning the role of our troops to greater involvement in counterinsurgency efforts  in focusing on al Qaeda  and in border security  In my mind  this isnt very different from the proposals other senators on both sides of the aisle have offered in recent months  I hope my Senate colleagues will come together around a proposal that supports the plan our General on the ground has recommended  and put aside the one upsmanship and message votes that seek only to paint the other side in a negative light  We have an opportunity to unite around a strategy that builds on our successes and implements gradual troop withdrawals so that over time we are supporting the operations of the Iraqi people as they continue to move themselves towards a more secure Iraq
