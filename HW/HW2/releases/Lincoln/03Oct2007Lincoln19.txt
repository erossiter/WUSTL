                    Lincoln Condemns Bush Veto of Children s Health Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Both                                                    Lincoln Condemns Bush Veto of Children s Health Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  October 03  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington  U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today released the following statement in response to President Bushs veto of legislation renewing the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  SCHIP   the federal state partnership program that provides funding for ARKids First    I am extremely disappointed by the Presidents decision to jeopardize health care coverage for the 10 million low income  uninsured children who would be covered by Congress bipartisan proposal  This fiscally responsible bill has the support of a majority of Americans and 43 Governors who know the value of the program to the working families of their states    The bipartisan childrens health bill would extend coverage for the 6 6 million children currently receiving health care through the program and allow us to cover 3 8 million new children  The compromise would allow Arkansas to reach 45 000 additional uninsured kids  ensuring they have access to the doctors visits they need to develop into healthy adults  Additionally  the bill would strengthen dental and mental health coverage for low income children  The  35 billion that Congress  in a bipartisan effort  proposed to add to the childrens health program for a five year period doesnt even add up to what we spend in Iraq in a four month period    This legislation is about priorities  What could be more important than the health of our nations most precious resource  our children  Today  the President failed our children and Americas working families
