                    Lincoln Homeowner Tax Relief Provision Passes Senate                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Both                                                    Lincoln Homeowner Tax Relief Provision Passes Senate                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  December 18  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843  Washington  U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today announced Senate passage of her provision to bring relief to homeowners   Lincolns provision encourages home ownership by allowing mortgage holders with family incomes not exceeding  110 000 to deduct premiums paid for private mortgage insurance  Families who do not meet certain down payment standards are required to buy private mortgage insurance  which until this year  was not tax deductible   The deductibility of mortgage insurance for low  to middle income families will provide valuable assistance to many who would otherwise never realize the American dream of owning their own home  Lincoln said  Last year  Lincoln secured the new deduction of private mortgage insurance for homeowners  The deduction was set to expire December 31  2007  Lincolns provision extends the deduction for three years until December 31  2010   Lincolns mortgage insurance deduction proposal is part of a larger housing tax relief package unanimously approved by the Senate  The House of Representatives is expected to consider the legislation this week
