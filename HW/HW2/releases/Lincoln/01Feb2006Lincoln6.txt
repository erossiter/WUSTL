                                                                                                                                                                 FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  February 01  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   said today that although Arkansas has thus far experienced milder temperatures this winter  low income households across the state are still financially strapped due to record high energy costs           At a Capitol Hill press conference today  Lincoln said that in order to help relieve these economic stresses  President Bush should boost funding for the federal safety net known as the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP           Federal energy assistance programs play an important role each year in helping low income Arkansans meet their heating and cooling needs   Senator Blanche Lincoln said   Unfortunately the economic relief we might have had from milder temperatures this winter was cancelled out by record high energy costs  This is strapping our working families who are already feeling the pinch of higher gas prices  stagnant wages and ever increasing health care costs         LIHEAP is the nation   s primary source of direct energy assistance for low income families  seniors and people with disabilities   Last year  the President funded LIHEAP at  2 1 billion nationally  a figure well below its capacity   Arkansas received  12 million in assistance from the program which went to approximately 56 000 households to help with their energy costs       According to recent statistics  on average  families will spend  270 more this winter than last year on home energy costs   Lincoln said today that even if the President fails to once again fully fund LIHEAP  he should at least account for this dramatic increase and boost the program   s funding                 High energy costs make it more difficult for families on fixed incomes to meet their basic household expenses and that   s why it is so important that the President boost funding for this essential program     Lincoln said     With the added demand placed on our resources by the addition of thousands of hurricane evacuees  it   s even more important that President Bush recognize this need and boost funding for LIHEAP
