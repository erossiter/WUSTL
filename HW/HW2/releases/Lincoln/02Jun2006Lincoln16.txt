Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor joined with Representative Vic Snyder  AR 02  today to announce a major federal investment in the teachers and students of the Little Rock School District  LRSD      The  979 057 grant from the U S  Department of Education will support the    Teaching American History    project   The initiative aims to help the district   s American history teachers develop a deeper understanding and appreciation of traditional American history with the goal to build upon their teaching skills and raise student achievement          The investment we make today for our children   s education will serve to strengthen our nation as they grow into tomorrow   s leaders     Senator Lincoln said      Our teachers play a critical role in the development of our young people  and we should give them every resource available to help children succeed in the classroom            Empowering teachers with new and innovative tools will help educate and inspire students to learn more about America   s unique place in history     Senator Pryor said      The value of this investment for classrooms in Little Rock should not be understated   We must teach our children to embrace lessons from the past  which will better equip them to lead our nation s future            We can t move America forward without knowing where we ve been     said Congressman Snyder      These funds are a great investment in teaching history        The    Teaching American History    project   s goals include  increasing content knowledge and teaching practices for all LRSD American history teachers  developing an expert group of American history teachers within the district to provide ongoing support  ensuring administrative leadership support of the project  enhancing student participation and achievement  and sharing results with other educational networks     The three year grant extends from the beginning of the 2006 2007 school year until the end of the 2008 2009 school year   Over the grant period  teachers will have the opportunity to complete 210 hours of in depth study   The LRSD is the largest public school district in Arkansas  serving over 26 000 students with 123 American history teachers in grades 5  8  and 11     The LRSD has partnered with the University of Arkansas at Little Rock Department of History  the Clinton Presidential Library  the Central High School Historic National Park Site  National History Day  Inc  and the Gilder Lehrman Institute of American History to develop this comprehensive professional development initiative
