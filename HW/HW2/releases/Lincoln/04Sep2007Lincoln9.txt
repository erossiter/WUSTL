                    Lincoln  Pryor  Boozman Announce Grant to Combat Domestic Violence in 3rd District                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Both                                                    Lincoln  Pryor  Boozman Announce Grant to Combat Domestic Violence in 3rd District                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 04  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 WASHINGTON  U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor  D AR  and U S  Representative John Boozman  R AR  today announced a major grant to assist women and children who are victims of domestic abuse in the Third District  The Crisis Center for Battered Women  Inc   in Fort Smith will receive  479 364 as part of the Rural Domestic Violence  Dating Violence  Sexual Assault  Stalking  and Child Victimization Assistance Grants program  The money  from the Department of Justices Office on Violence Against Women  will go toward funding an outreach coordinator and four outreach advocates to travel to Third District communities  meet with victims and assist them with safety planning  shelter services  and other crisis intervention services  The Crisis Center will also develop curriculum to prevent dating violence and sexual assault for local schools and summer youth groups  Additionally  materials on domestic violence and sexual assault awareness will be developed for adult audiences  Ive visited with victims of domestic abuse and have seen first hand the positive impact that counseling and treatment programs have  Lincoln said  Families facing serious  life threatening situations deserve quality services to help them in their time of need  The Crisis Center provides the resources that many families tragically require and now  because of this grant  the center will be able to offer a helping hand to even more individuals  Countless children and families are alive today as a direct result of the support they receive from the Crisis Center  said Pryor  I m pleased this federal funding will expand and strengthen the Center s outreach and educational services  It s simply a smart and necessary investment into the community  Keeping children and families safe from the threat of abuse is of paramount importance  Boozman said  The Crisis Center has shown tremendous leadership and compassion to assist the victims of domestic abuse and work to develop ways to prevent it from happening again  This money is important for the safety of children and families in the Fort Smith community  and I am pleased to see it go to such an important organization  The Rural Domestic Violence  Dating Violence  Sexual Assault  Stalking  and Child Abuse Enforcement Assistance Program implements certain provisions of the Violence Against Women Act first enacted in 1994 and reauthorized in 2005  The primary purpose of the program enhances victim safety in cases of domestic violence  dating violence  sexual assault  and child sexual abuse living in rural areas by encouraging collaborative partnerships between criminal justice agencies  victim service providers and community organizations to respond to these crimes
