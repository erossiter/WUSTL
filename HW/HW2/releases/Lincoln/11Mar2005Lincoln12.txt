                                                                                                                                                           FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  March 11  2005Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   said that legislation approved by the Senate Thursday will curtail manipulation of bankruptcy laws by making it more difficult for higher income individuals to completely eliminate debt by filing for bankruptcy        There was a time when the stigma attached to bankruptcy was bad enough that people went out of their way to avoid it     Senator Blanche Lincoln said     Today  the evidence shows that bankruptcy proceedings are being manipulated by some to avoid debts even when they have the ability to pay   That   s just wrong   Unfortunately  lenders have to be protected by laws when there is no honor        The Bankruptcy Abuse Prevention and Consumer Protection Act of 2005 passed the Senate by a vote of 74 25   If enacted  the legislation would create a formula to determine a debtor   s eligibility for bankruptcy relief based on the individual   s ability to repay debt   The bill would make it more difficult for an individual to file for Chapter 7 bankruptcy  which excuses significant portions of debt known as    dischargeable debt      The bill also would redefine dischargeable and non dischargeable debt  and it would require payments such as child support to be repaid  which now are dischargeable under current law           Lincoln said that a change in bankruptcy laws will improve the ability of lenders to extend credit to higher risk and low wage workers because lenders    exposure to abusive borrowing practices will be reduced    Moreover  Lincoln noted that law abiding lenders and borrowers pay the costs when the system is misused        When we allow people to cast off debts that they have the ability to pay  we are shifting that cost on to honest lenders and other honest borrowers     Lincoln said      When lenders face higher risks  they are going to charge higher interest   That is just the way it works   Honest borrowers and business people shouldn   t have to pay higher rates because of the dishonesty of others        Lincoln has been an advocate for financial literacy programs and noted this bill requires credit card companies to issue warnings that consumers who only pay the minimum amount on their bills will see an increase in the amount of interest they owe   The legislation also requires credit card companies to show the time period it would take consumers to repay the balance of their statements
