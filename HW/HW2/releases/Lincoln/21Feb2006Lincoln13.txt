and Women Guest Column by U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  February 21  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 As we close out the month of February  I   d like to pay tribute to the numerous African American Arkansans who have given to our nation through honorable military service   From the Revolutionary War to Iraq and Afghanistan today  African Americans have made tremendous sacrifices throughout our nation   s history and have played a major role in the shaping of our democracy       Many  many African Americans are now serving in the Middle East   Many come from small towns like Luxora or Monticello or DeQueen or Fayetteville   They may come from different corners of the state but they all share a love for this country and a willingness to defend it   We not only should honor their sacrifice but also the willingness of their families to support them as they go to distant and dangerous lands      Their sacrifice and the sacrifice of their families are the latest examples of a long history of African American   s commitment to the defense of this nation   African Americans have made numerous contributions in the defense of our ideals and freedoms  and it is this service that we owe our utmost appreciation and gratitude       Another example of the history of African American military service is the legendary and famed Tuskegee Airmen   The airmen were instrumental in changing the perception that most Americans had about African American fighting men       Commissioned during WWII  the Tuskegee Airmen were the first African American fighter pilots in the U S  Military   Of all the fighting units in our nation   s history  no group of African Americans is more celebrated and renowned  than this elite group of military airmen   Because of their dedication and determination  the Tuskegee Airmen broke the color barrier at this critical time in our nation   s history  and they hailed from every region of the country  including right here in Arkansas      The eight Arkansans who served as Tuskegee Airmen were Dr  Richard Caesar  Dr  Granville C  Coggs  Lt Colonel Woodrow W  Crockett  Jerry T Hodges  Herbert E  Clark  James Ewing  William T  Mattison and Marsille P  Reed   Each one of these brave  devoted men possessed a strong personal desire to serve the United States of America to the best of his ability  and for that we owe each of them a great deal of gratitude        Not only should we honor the service of the Arkansans who are currently serving in the Middle East  but we should also remember those Arkansans like the Tuskegee Airmen who made our modern military possible    The selfless service of our present and past military heros instills   in future generations of Americans a national spirit of patriotism for them to follow   And for that I   m deeply humbled  proud and forever indebted to them for their service
