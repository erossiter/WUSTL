                    Lincoln Calls For Two Step Approach To Reform Broken Immigration System                                                                                                Both                                                    Lincoln Calls For Two Step Approach To Reform Broken Immigration System                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  March 30  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   said today that reforming the nation   s broken immigration system will require securing and strengthening our porous borders while putting the approximately 11 million undocumented workers already in this country on a rigorous path to permanent residency       Our immigration system is broken  and we need comprehensive reform to fix it   Lincoln said   We need to secure our borders  crack down on employers who knowingly hire illegally  and bring undocumented workers out of the shadows  We must know who is crossing our borders and living and working in our country  Illegal immigration is a national security issue and it   s jeopardizing our ability to provide services to our taxpaying citizens      During the Senate   s current debate on immigration reform  Lincoln said that the most realistic course is improving border security  while putting in place a system to identify the millions of undocumented workers already living illegally in this country  She cautioned the Senate against extending blanket amnesty to the nation   s illegal alien population  and said that stricter enforcement alone or mass deportations were also non workable solutions      Instead  Lincoln said that the Senate   s approach should resemble legislation introduced by Senator John McCain which she has cosponsored  Lincoln said that her legislation would provide incentives for undocumented immigrants to step out of the shadows  but in no way extend a free pass or send them to the front of the line  She said that undocumented workers should only apply for permanent residence after working for several years  paying a monetary penalty  undergoing a rigorous background and security check  learning English and American civics  paying taxes  and going to the back of the line to begin a long path to citizenship      Immigration is a national security issue  and therefore we must do a better job of enforcing our immigration laws and securing our borders   Lincoln said   Doing this while at the same time forcing those already here to adhere to a strict path towards permanent residency would allow us to focus more time and resources on all those who have entered this country for the wrong reasons  such as terrorists and criminals
