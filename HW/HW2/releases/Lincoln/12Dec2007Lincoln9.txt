                                                                                               Both                                                    Lincoln Fights Cuts to Southern Agriculture                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  December 12  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington  U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today vigorously fought an amendment to the farm bill that would reduce critical support payments to southern row crop farmers  ultimately hindering their ability to compete in a global marketplace and threatening our domestic source of food and fiber  We need a fair and balanced farm bill that recognizes and values the contributions of all regions of the country  Lincoln said  The Dorgan Grassley amendment specifically targets our southern rice and cotton producers  while farmers outside the South face far less input costs by growing less capital intensive crops   In a speech on the Senate floor  Lincoln spoke against the payment limitation amendment offered by Senators Byron Dorgan  D N D   and Charles Grassley  R Iowa   The amendment would create a devastating hole in the current safety net relied upon by southern farmers  Lincoln said   This amendment  if included in the final farm bill  would make producers of stable commodities such as rice and cotton less competitive in the global marketplace  If we put these growers out of business  theyre not going to go to another area of our country  That production will move overseas to places like Vietnam  Thailand  Brazil  and China  and we will have severely damaged our domestic supply of food and fiber  As news reports continue to highlight cases of dangerous imported food  why would we weaken our support of our U S  farmers who produce the safest and most abundant and affordable food around the world  We didnt become dependent on foreign oil overnight  but thats certainly where we are today  The last thing we want is to be dependent on foreign food  Lincoln  a senior member of the Senate Agriculture Committee  hails from a seventh generation Arkansas farm family  She was a key negotiator during the writing of the 2002 farm bill and has continued in her role as advocate for farm families during the 2007 debate
