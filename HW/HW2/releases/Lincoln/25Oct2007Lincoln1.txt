 Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington  U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark  issteppingup her efforts to help small business employees and self employed individuals obtain quality health insurance at an affordable price   Today Lincoln took part ina Senate Finance Committee hearing  whichexplored opportunities and challenges in expanding health care coverage for employees of small businesses  In August  Lincoln successfully passed a bipartisan resolution through the Senate that calls for legislation to improve the access of small business employees and self employed individuals to affordable and meaningful health insurance coverage  Lincoln is continuing to work on that legislation  which she hopes to introduce by the end of this year   Small businesses face significant challenges in providing healthcare coverage to their employees  said Lincoln  I am committed to helping small businesses provide adequate and affordable health insurance not only because it will contribute to their employees well being and peace of mind  but also because it will allow these businesses to be more competitive in ourglobaleconomy   At todays hearing  Lincoln highlighted the substantial disadvantages small business employers face  ascompared to largercompanies  becausethey have fewer resources available to provide health benefits to their employees  Lincoln also pointed to the importance of the federal government and states working together in a collaborative effort to successfully enact wide reaching reform   Under current law  employees who receive health insurance through their employer do not have to pay income taxes on health insurance premiums  Workers without employer provided health benefits and self employed individuals must obtain health insurance on the more expensive individual market  and they do not receive the same favorable tax treatment  A recent study demonstrates that larger businesses are more likely to offer health insurance benefits to their workers  Over 95 percent of businesses with 50 or more employees offer health insurance  while only 45 percent of businesses with less than ten workers are able to offer it
