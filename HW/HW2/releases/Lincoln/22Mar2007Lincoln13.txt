                    Lincoln Warns Traveling Arkansans of Passport Delays                                                                              Both                                                    Lincoln Warns Traveling Arkansans of Passport Delays  FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  March 22  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington   U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today alerted Arkansans traveling outside the country to expect delays in receiving a passport          Last week  the U S  Department of State issued a warning that the demand for passports has hit a record high   The State Department advises travelers to allow 10 to 12 weeks for routine passport processing  up from the previous recommendation of six weeks  and to allow four weeks  instead of two  for expedited processing            Americans traveling by air outside the country  including Canada  Mexico  and the Caribbean  are required to have a passport   The passport requirement does not apply to U S  citizens traveling to or returning directly from a U S  territory  as those travelers are not considered to have left the country   U S  territories include  Guam  Puerto Rico  the U S  Virgin Islands  American Samoa  Swains Island  and the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands          Lincoln warned Arkansans traveling abroad during the busy spring and summer months to plan accordingly and encouraged those who need assistance to contact her State Office in Little Rock at 1 800 352 9364            To obtain or renew a passport  or check the status of your passport application online  travelers should visit the State Department website  www travel state gov  or call the National Passport Information Center  1 877 487 2778  during the hours of 6 00 a m    11 00 p m  central time  Monday   Friday  excluding federal holidays
