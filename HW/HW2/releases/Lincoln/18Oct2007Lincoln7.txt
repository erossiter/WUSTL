Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington  U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today released the following statement after the Presidents allies in the U S  House of Representatives sustained a veto of legislation to reauthorize the successful State Childrens Health Insurance Program  SCHIP   known in Arkansas as ARKids First  Those standing in lock step with the President have failed our children and Americas working families by supporting his veto of bipartisan legislation to cover 10 million low income uninsured children nationally  While Congressional Democrats and Republicans came together and negotiated a compromise in good faith to give uninsured children access to the doctors visits they need  the President drew a line in the sand and perpetuated myths about the bipartisan bill  The fact is that the Presidents plan would drop coverage for 1 4 million children already enrolled in the program  That is simply unacceptable  Congresss bipartisan compromise would ensure coverage for the 6 6 million children currently enrolled and would extend coverage to 3 3 million children who would otherwise be without health insurance  The agreement does not expand eligibility to children in families with higher income levels  nor does it cover illegal immigrants  as critics have claimed  The Presidents veto  sustained by his allies in the House  is a blow to our childrens health  our countrys future  and the bipartisan spirit in which the agreement was created  In producing this compromise  lawmakers checked their politics at the door  This cooperation should be recognized by the President  but unfortunately  hes the one who failed to move beyond politics  The issue is our childrenwhat could be more important  The President clearly has not made childrens health his priority  I will continue to fight on behalf of the 110 000 children in Arkansas and the 10 million children across this country whose health care coverage has been jeopardized by his decision
