                                                                                                                                                       FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 25  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 WASHINGTON  DC   Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor  along with Representatives Vic Snyder  AR 02  and John Boozman  AR 03  today announced that the National Science Foundation  NSF  has awarded more than a million dollars for science programs for Arkansas universities   The members of Arkansas congressional delegation announced the University of Arkansas Little Rock will receive an NSF award of  579 175 for the Little Rock Science Scholars  USS  program  The project is designed to increase the number of scientifically trained employees for the state of Arkansas and the country  The funding will enable UALR to create a scholarship program that will encourage college bound disadvantaged students to enroll in biology  chemistry and earth science  Recruitment efforts include web based advertisements  brochures  posters and personal contact  Academic  social and personal support will be provided for students in the form of a first year class  research opportunities and a career planning course   In addition  qualified graduate and undergraduate students at the University of Arkansas will be eligible for scholarships for studies in nanotechnology  The program will receive an NSF award for  599 770 to cover the costs of the program  UofA has established educational programs in Microelectronics Photonics to meet the need for professionals with expertise in emerging areas of nanotechnologies  This field is increasingly important to advance information technology  telecommunications  health care  manufacturing and several areas of national defense    Educational opportunities inscience and technologyare critical for our future as a state and as nation  said Lincoln  The scholarships funded by thesefederal grants will help our universities continue toattractquality students whocanbecome leadersin these importantfields   Science holds the key to many of todays challenges  including disease and lack of energy resources  and its up to todays students to unlock the solutions  Pryor said  Im proud of Arkansas universities who are leading the way through advanced science programs and reaching out to attract as many students as possible into these exciting fields    This funding will provide a gateway for more Arkansan students to further scientific research in America  said Congressman Snyder   I am pleased to see the University of Arkansas to continue to take the lead in cutting edge technologies  Boozman said  This money will directly benefit its work in maintaining its reputation as a leading research university as it brings more and more students to scientific fields which will lead the State of Arkansas into the future
