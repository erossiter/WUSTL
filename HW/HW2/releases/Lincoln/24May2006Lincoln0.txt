                                                                                                                                                            FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  May 24  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today called upon the federal government to use every resource at its disposal to find those individuals responsible for stealing sensitive personal information of about 26 5 million veterans     Lincoln also questioned why Department of Veterans Affairs  VA  officials waited three weeks after the security breach to inform veterans they were susceptible to identify theft         Our nation   s veterans deserve the best service we can provide them after their great sacrifice on our behalf  and the least we can do is safeguard their personal information     Lincoln said      These veterans have been put at risk and they deserve better treatment from their government        For weeks  veterans have unknowingly been vulnerable to identity fraud while the VA waited to inform them   By waiting  the Administration has given these thieves ample opportunity to destroy the lives of these veterans        Lincoln has heard from veterans in Arkansas and across the country who are outraged that their privacy has been jeopardized   She encourages any veteran with questions to call the VA   s special hotline at 1 800 FED INFO or visit www va gov opa and www firstgov gov     The Senate Committee on Veterans Affairs will hold an emergency hearing Thursday in which VA Secretary Jim Nicholson is expected to testify   Lincoln looks forwards to answers for the millions of veterans left vulnerable by this unacceptable failure to keep our veterans safe
