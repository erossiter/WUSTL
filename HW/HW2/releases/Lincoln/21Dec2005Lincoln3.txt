                                                                                                                                                       FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  December 21  2005Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Sen  Blanche L  Lincoln  D Ark   today said that funding support for American troops and hurricane victims is a national priority and Congress should remain in session until it is complete   Lincoln also noted that Republican tactics have delayed the defense bill to the 11th hour of the current session of Congress        This is a case of misplaced priorities     Lincoln said      The House finished its work on the defense bill last June and the Senate adopted our bill with my support in October   Why are we just now seeing the conference report a few days before Christmas and then as part of a high stakes political maneuver   What message does that send the troops        Lincoln spoke following failure of a procedural move that would have permitted a vote on drilling rights in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge  ANWR  as part of the defense spending bill for FY2006        Drilling rights in Alaska has been debated and defeated in this Congress for many years  and I am disappointed that Alaska Senators have used our troops as pawns to try to win passage of an economic development project for their region     Lincoln said      This extraordinary attempt to insert it into a bill that funds our troops and provides for their safety at a time of war is inappropriate        The defense spending bill provides  50 billion for operations in Iraq and Afghanistan   1 billion of which is designated for National Guard and Reserve equipment   The legislation fully funds military pay  benefits and medical programs
