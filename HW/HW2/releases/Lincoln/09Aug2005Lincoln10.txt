                                                                                                                                                               FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 09  2005Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Little Rock     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   Monday received the American Health Care Association   s    2005 Leading Light of Long Term Care Award    for her longstanding leadership and dedication on long term care issues   Lincoln was presented the award by the Arkansas Health Care Association      As a member of the Senate Finance Committee and the Senate Special Committee on Aging  I have the unique opportunity to protect and strengthen programs like Medicare and Medicaid for the thousands of seniors and individuals with disabilities in Arkansas who depend on them every day   Lincoln said   I am honored to receive this award  and I will continue to work with the Arkansas Health Care Association to ensure that quality long term care services remain accessible in Arkansas         Senator Lincoln is a true leader on health care issues in Washington  particularly regarding long term care and ensuring our seniors receive the quality care they need and deserve     said Randy Wyatt  Executive Vice President of the Arkansas Health Care Association     Her efforts on seniors    behalf help the more than 21 000 Arkansas residents in nursing homes receive quality care  and assure that the approximately 229 nursing homes in the state receive our government   s support        Wyatt added that such a commitment is making a positive impact in Arkansas  where providers are dedicated to raising staff salaries  hiring additional staff  improving facilities and making additional investments in direct care and care related resources in order to meet the state   s growing long term care needs         We are grateful to have Senator Lincoln serving as a voice for the residents of Arkansas   s nursing homes  as well as the people they employ     Wyatt said     The Leading Light Award is presented to members of Congress who have through their votes and legislative initiatives in Congress distinguished themselves as champions of quality nursing home care for seniors and the disabled
