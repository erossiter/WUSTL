Grant for Winrock International                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 18  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 WASHINGTON  DC   Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor and Representative Vic Snyder  AR 02  today announced that Winrock International has been awarded a  199 932grant through the Department of Agricultures Rural Development Program  This funding will allow the Arkansas Rural Enterprise Center  AREC  to continue to provide technical assistance and training to cooperatives    Agriculture is the backbone of our state s economy  said Lincoln  The work that Winrock International does with small acreage and disadvantaged farmersstrengthens rural economies throughout our stateand enhancesthe qualityof life forsmall growers   Ensuring that Arkansass newly formed cooperative networks have the training and resources they need is essential to their success  I commend Winrock International for their leadership and dedication to helping farmers in rural areas  said Pryor  In turn  farmers increase production  create more jobs and the improve quality of life for their entire community   Winrock International is a phenomenal organization and I am proud of the great services it offers Arkansas and the world  said Congressman Snyder  I am happy they received this development grant  as this funding will go towards projects to further enhance rural agriculture in Central Arkansas   This grant allows Winrock to continue working with underserved farmers  specifically immigrant poultry farmers to diversify farm income  Annett Pagan  Director of U S  Programs at Winrock said  It fits in very well with Winrock s sustainable food systems projects  which promote locally grown food   In 1992  Winrock International established the AREC to work in rural Arkansas in expanding jobs and income  Since then  the AREC has worked on cooperative development projects dealing with Arkansas  secondary wood products manufacturers  small sawmills  poultry producers  and a network of business owners and managers and educators  AREC has provided these industries with services that include technical assistance in marketing research  business planning  new product development  plant expansion  and insurance
