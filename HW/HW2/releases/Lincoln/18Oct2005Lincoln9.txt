                    Lincoln  Delay in FSA Closures Right Decision                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Both                                                    Lincoln  Delay in FSA Closures Right Decision                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  October 18  2005Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   said today that U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  Secretary Mike Johanns    decision to delay the planned closure of 713 Farm Service Agency  FSA  offices is the right decision       I   m extremely pleased that the USDA and Secretary Johanns have heard the voice of the American farmer and chosen to take some time to fully review the impacts of closing or consolidating many of our county FSA offices   Senator Blanche Lincoln said   I agree with the USDA that our FSA offices should operate as effectively as possible  but at a time when our farmers are experiencing extremely high operating costs due to drought conditions earlier in the year combined with high fuel costs  now is simply not the time to make such hasty  ill advised move       In September  the USDA and Secretary Johanns announced a decision to close 713 of the nation   s 2 351 county FSA offices  At that time  USDA said that due to cost constraints and greater efficiency needs  such a move was necessary  Upon learning of the proposal  Lincoln  a senior member of the Senate Agriculture Committee  led efforts among other farm state Senators to oppose the decision until a more thorough review was conducted       As I made perfectly clear last month when the USDA proposed such an idea  now is the worst time to reduce farmer access to USDA assistance programs   Lincoln said   Our farmers depend on their county FSA offices and this would have forced an added hardship upon them at the worst possible time this growing season  I appreciate Secretary Johanns taking the time to listen to my concerns  as this delay will allow USDA to take another approach toward the end goal of eliminating government waste and improving government efficiency
