                    Lincoln Seeks to Boost Funding to Address Record High Heating Costs this Winter                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Both                                                    Lincoln Seeks to Boost Funding to Address Record High Heating Costs this Winter                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  October 25  2005Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   said she plans to vote to boost funding for an important federal safety net to help working families with expected record high heating costs this winter     Lincoln said that she will vote in the Senate to increase funding for the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP   The vote is expected to take place as early as today       At a time when Americans are already suffering from high gas prices  stagnant wages  and ever increasing health care costs  it   s important that we do all we can to support efforts such as LIHEAP to help our working families cope with yet another expected economic strain   Lincoln said   Federal energy assistance programs play an important role in helping low income families meet their heating and cooling needs  With natural gas prices already at record levels  the cost of electricity and heating a home will likely increase for Arkansans  Now is the time to pass this vital assistance      Lincoln has consistently supported fully funding the LIHEAP program  the nation   s primary source of direct energy assistance for low income families  Currently  Arkansas is scheduled to receive approximately  12 million in LIHEAP assistance this year  Lincoln said she plans to again support an initiative to give the President the ability to fully fund LIHEAP for Fiscal Year 2006  If the President were to fully fund the program  Arkansas would receive up to  61 7 million in LIHEAP assistance
