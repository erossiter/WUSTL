                     Lincoln  Pryor  Boozman Announce Grant to Combat Domestic Violence    FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 12  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor  along with Representative John Boozman today announced the Fayetteville based Arkansas Coalition Against Sexual Assault will receive  83 401 to combat violence against women through the Grants to State Sexual Assault and Domestic Violence Coalitions Program     With this funding  the Coalition plans to further outreach into the rural areas of Northwest Arkansas  It plans awareness seminars to provide women in rural areas with tools to recognize and protect themselves from domestic violence  and how to report any incidents of violence to proper authorities     As we approach National Domestic Violence Awareness Month in October  we are reminded how critical it is to work with community leaders  law enforcement agencies  and victim assistance organizations to educate communities across our state about domestic violence   said Lincoln   I applaud the Arkansas Coalition Against Sexual Assault for taking the opportunity to access these federal dollars to help people in Northwest Arkansas  particularly those living in rural areas  who may not have access to such assistance          It is absolutely unacceptable that 1 out of every 3 American women will be sexually assaulted in her lifetime     said Pryor      However the good work of ACASA has proven critical to the well being and safety of women and children throughout Arkansas   This grant will go a long way in furthering ACASA   s mission to ultimately eliminate sexual violence           As the father of three daughters  I am very proud ACASA sought out this grant to further its mission of protecting women in Northwest Arkansas     Boozman said     Only 16  of rapes are ever reported to the police  and I applaud the Coalition   s plans to spread out to the rural areas of the Third District and tell women it is right to report domestic violence and sexual assault        Authorized by the Violence Against Women Act 9  VAWA  of 2000  the grant program supports state coalition efforts to coordinate victim services within their state as well as collaborate with other federal  state  and local entities to respond to violence against women issues affecting their jurisdictions      State coalitions continue to play a critical role in advancing the goals of VAWA  serving as a collective voice to end violence against women  More specifically  state coalitions work on many levels to address violence against women while also serving as a resource to their various state agencies and local programs
