                    Arkansas Senators Launch Online Petition to Preserve Disaster Relief                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Both                                                    Arkansas Senators Launch Online Petition to Preserve Disaster Relief                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  May 17  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor today are asking Arkansans to support local farmers by persuading President Bush to back down on his threat to veto an emergency appropriations bill if it includes any disaster relief for our farmers   They want Arkansans to sign an online petition on their websites  http   lincoln senate gov and http   pryor senate gov  which they will deliver to the President and to the Speaker of the House  who declared the disaster package    dead on arrival          Lincoln and Pryor said the Senate passed long awaited emergency disaster assistance in April to help farmers recover from higher input costs as part of the Fiscal Year 2006 emergency supplemental bill  but the House of Representatives did not include similar language in its version of the legislation   A Senate House conference committee will negotiate the final bill  and the Arkansas Senators are concerned the disaster relief provisions will not survive with a looming veto threat from the President and opposition to disaster relief from certain House members         The threats by this President and the Republican led House to cut assistance to our farmers who have suffered severe economic loss due to factors outside their control are simply inexcusable     Lincoln said     It is clear that our agricultural producers are not being heard by this administration  and this online petition is yet another tool for rural America to send a strong message to those who don t think emergency agriculture assistance is necessary   I plan to keep fighting to make certain the President gets the message that our farmers in Arkansas and across the country need help  and I strongly encourage every Arkansan to tell this administration it cannot turn its back on rural America            The President   s veto threat tells me he either isn   t hearing the plight of our farmers or he doesn   t care about their livelihood  That   s why I   m asking Arkansans to join me in sending a very loud and clear message that he needs to support our farmers in their time of need     Pryor said     With quick and widespread participation  this online petition will serve as a megaphone for our farmers  and we will break the perception in the Administration and House of Representatives that disaster relief is unnecessary          The Senators added a number of other Senators have also posted the online petition on their websites  including Senators Byron Dorgan  D N D    Ken Salazar  D Colo   and Mark Dayton  D Minn
