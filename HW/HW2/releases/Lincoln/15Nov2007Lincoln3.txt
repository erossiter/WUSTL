                    Lincoln  Pryor  Ross Announce Grants for Volunteer Fire Departments                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Both                                                    Lincoln  Pryor  Ross Announce Grants for Volunteer Fire Departments                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  November 15  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington   U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor along with Representative Mike Ross  AR 04  announced Thursday that the McCaskill Volunteer Fire Department  the Hatfield Fire and Rescue Department  and the Norphlet Volunteer Fire Department have been awarded grants through the Department of Homeland Security s  DHS  Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program   The McCaskill Volunteer Fire Department was awarded  166 250 to help purchase a new vehicle to assist the department  The Hatfield Fire and Rescue Department has been awarded  41 491 and the Norphlet Volunteer Fire Department was awarded  10 355 to help the departments enhance current operations and improve firefighter safety  As first responders  our local firefighters play an integral role in keeping our communities safe  Lincoln said These federals dollars will help our fire departments provide necessary equipment and training to those in the line of duty so thatthey can continue to provide the best possible service to the citizens of their communities  Im pleased to see taxpayer dollars invested back into the community through vital resources that protect families and homes  said Pryor These funds will also go a long way to improve current operations and ensure our firefighters are safe in the line of duty  Our rural communities rely heavily on their volunteer fire departments and I am proud to help ensure that they have the necessary equipment and resources to fight fires  Ross said  I am pleased to join with my colleagues today to announce these federal funds so that the families and residents of these communities will know that their fire departments have the resources to keep them safe  The purpose of the grant program is to provide first responders with additional training and equipment which will enhance their abilities to protect the health and safety of the public and protect firefighters from job related hazards  The DHS Office of State and Local Government Coordination and Preparedness administers the grant program in cooperation with the U S  Fire Administration  The Assistance to Firefighters Grant  AFG  Program has been providing funding to fire departments and other first responders since 2001  To date  a total of  2 2 billion in grants through the AFG program has been awarded to first responder organizations to purchase response equipment  personal protective equipment  and vehicles
