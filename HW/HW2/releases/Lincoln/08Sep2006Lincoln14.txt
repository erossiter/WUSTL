                    Lincoln  Pryor and Boozman Announce COPS Grants for Boone County                                                                              Both                                                    Lincoln  Pryor and Boozman Announce COPS Grants for Boone County  FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 08  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington      U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor  along with Representative John Boozman today announced that the Boone County Sheriff   s Department will receive  47 403  in a  law enforcement grant through the Community Oriented Policing Services  COPS  Secure Our Schools program   The program supports local efforts to improve security in schools     With this funding  the Sheriff   s Department plans to install a camera surveillance system in the Valley Springs schools  purchase radio communications equipment for the Bergman schools  and obtain a case management reporting system for the Sheriff   s Department       It   s important that the federal government continue its work to ensure that our schools can provide a safe environment for students and teachers     Lincoln said      These funds enable law enforcement agencies in Arkansas to combat school violence in partnership with educators and community leaders         Students learn better in an environment where they feel safe  and I think our parents  teachers and schoolchildren would agree that our neighborhoods and schools are safer as a result of the COPS program   Pryor said   I commend Boone County officials for seeking this funding  and I will continue to fight for resources that enhance school safety and benefit Arkansas    schools         The security of our schools is of paramount importance     Boozman said     I am proud we are able to assist local law enforcement form a partnership with local schools to protect students and educators  Children should not have to be worried about violence when they go to school everyday  This program will allow them to focus on learning  and preparing themselves for the future        In addition to funding school safety projects  COPS supports innovative local community policing initiatives  funds the purchase of crime fighting technology  provides training and technical assistance resources  and funds the hiring of local law enforcement officers     For additional information about the COPS Secure Our Schools program  or to learn which law enforcement agencies received grants  visit the COPS website at www cops usdoj gov
