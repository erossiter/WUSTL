Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington   U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor along with Representative Mike Ross  AR 04  announced Thursday that rural fire departments in Arkansas have been awarded  194 294 in grants from the Department of Homeland Security  DHS  Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  This award will help the Antoine and Center Point Volunteer Fire Departments enhance current operations and improve firefighter safety  It will help the Perrytown Volunteer Fire Department purchase a new vehicle to assist the department  As first responders  our local firefighters play an integral role in keeping our communities safe  Lincoln said  This federalfunding willhelptheseSouthwest Arkansas fire departments continue toprovide the best possible service to their communities  We must make every effort to ensure our brave firefighters have the resources they need to keep our families and communities safe  said Pryor These grants are a life saving investment and will better equip our volunteer fire departments for future fires and natural disasters  Our rural communities rely heavily on their volunteer fire departments and I am proud to help ensure that they have the necessary equipment and resources to effectively do their jobs  Ross said  I am pleased to join with my colleagues today to announce these federal funds so that the families and residents of these communities will know that their fire departments have the resources to keep them safe  This program supports fire departments by providing the tools and resources necessary to ensure the safety of both department personnel and the general public     The following is a list of grant recipients in Arkansass Fourth Congressional District and the amount awarded to each recipient  DHS Assistance
