                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  October 02  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington  U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor and Representative Mike Ross announced today that the city of McGehee will receive a  25 838 Rural Development grant through the United States Department of Agriculture  USDA   The funds will be used to purchase two new police cars   Cities throughout Arkansas depend on federal dollars in order to protect and improve their communities  said Lincoln  I applaud the citizens of McGehee for seeking these funds to purchase new equipment that will help first responders keep local families safe   These two new patrol cars will enable McGehee law enforcement officers to build on their commitment to protect the community and respond to emergencies as efficiently and effectively as possible  said Pryor  As a member of the Senates Homeland Security Committee  I will continue to identify and support programs that ensure local law enforcement officials have the resources and equipment to protect the communities they serve   Our local law enforcement officers are the front line of security to keep our neighborhoods and communities safe  and these federal resources will allow them to acquire the needed tools to help perform their duties for the people of McGehee  Ross said   USDA Rural Development grants support public facilities and services  promote economic development  and offer technical assistance to help address the diverse and unique needs of rural communities
