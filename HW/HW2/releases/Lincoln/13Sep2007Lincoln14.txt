Children s Health Insurance                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 13  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Little Rock  U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   began her two day  statewide tour through Arkansas today calling for the renewal of the successful State Childrens Health Insurance Program  SCHIP   Lincoln kicked off her SCHIP tour at Arkansas Childrens Hospital  ACH  with Governor Mike Beebe and ACH CEO Dr  Jonathan Bates    Since SCHIP began in 1997  we have been able to reduce the number of uninsured children nationwide by one third from 11 3 million in 1997 to 8 9 million in 2004  Lincoln said  As a result of ARKids First  which is funded by SCHIP  and other initiatives  Arkansas has been a national leader in reducing its ranks of uninsured children  from over twenty percent in 1997 to ten percent today  The President has threatened to veto Congress work to preserve coverage for children currently enrolled and to reach millions more children  Providing health insurance for our children is a moral obligation  not a political fight   Arkansas children need access to high quality health care  and for ten years our state has increased access to that care through the ARKids First program  Beebe said  In order to continue this progress and ensure that our low income children get the coverage they need  President Bush must take action to re authorize the SCHIP program   Renewal of the SCHIP legislation is critical because we as parents  lawmakers  physicians  Americans  have a responsibility to these children to do everything we can to ensure they have quality health insurance  We cannot turn our backs on their medical needs  They are our future and by not providing for them  were also hurting ourselves  said Jonathan Bates  M D   president and CEO  Arkansas Childrens Hospital   The SCHIP reauthorization package  which the U S  Senate passed with overwhelming bipartisan support  would provide an additional  35 billion of funding over five years   60 billion total  to preserve health insurance coverage for children currently enrolled in states programs while reaching an additional 3 2 million uninsured  low income children  The Presidents proposal  to increase funding by  5 billion over five years  falls well short of the funding needed to simply maintain coverage for those currently enrolled    Lincolns statewide SCHIP tour continues on Friday with open forums in Fort Smith  Hamburg  and West Memphis
