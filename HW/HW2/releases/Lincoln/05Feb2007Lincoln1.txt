Lincoln to Chair Agriculture Subcommittee   FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  February 05  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today was named Chair of the Senate Agriculture Committee   s Subcommittee on Production  Income Protection and Price Support allowing her to strengthen her role as an advocate for agricultural producers and rural Americans      As Chair of the Subcommittee on Production  Income Protection and Price Support  I will have the opportunity to focus on issues that are critical for farm families and rural communities who depend on a strong agricultural economy   Lincoln said   I will work to ensure that the 2007 Farm Bill provides the necessary safety net for our agricultural producers in Arkansas and across the country and makes further investments in rural America      The Subcommittee on Production  Income Protection  and Price Support has jurisdiction over legislation and oversight involving the production of agricultural crops  commodities and products  The subcommittee also oversees farm and ranch income protection and assistance  insurance and risk protection  fresh water food production  and commodity price support programs       In addition  Lincoln will serve as a member of the Subcommittee on Rural Revitalization  Conservation  Forestry and Credit as well as the Subcommittee on Nutrition and Food Assistance  Sustainable and Organic Agriculture and General Legislation  Through these subcommittees  Lincoln will have the opportunity to address issues that affect the nation   s forests  rural communities  agriculture and rural credit  nutrition and hunger prevention  food production  trading of futures  and pesticides      Lincoln is the fifth highest ranking Democrat on the Senate Agriculture Committee which has responsibility over the country   s farm policy  Lincoln has served on the Agriculture Committee since entering the Senate in 1999 and played a key role in the 2002 farm bill debate  Lincoln   s seat on the Agriculture Committee will ensure that Arkansas    agricultural producers will be heard during the farm bill reauthorization process this year
