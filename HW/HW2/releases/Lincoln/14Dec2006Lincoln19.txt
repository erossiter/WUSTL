                    Midland Fire Grant Announced                                                                              Both                                                    Midland Fire Grant Announced  FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  December 14  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor  D AR  and Representative John Boozman  R AR  today announced a  151 783 grant for the Midland Fire Department          The money  according to Chief Pete Daffron  will go to the purchase of turnout gear  which is the protective equipment firefighters wear when combating a blaze           As first responders  local firefighters play an integral role in keeping our communities safe   Lincoln said   With these federal dollars  the Midland Fire Department will be able to purchase safety equipment to protect those in the line of duty so they can provide the best possible service to the citizens of Midland              Our firefighters put their lives on the line to keep us safe  and it s important that we provide them with the equipment they need to protect themselves and our communities   said Pryor   This grant is well deserved                I am proud to see the Midland Fire Department take this important step to protect those who protect us from fire     Boozman said     I encourage all of our rural fire departments to apply for these federal grants to make all of our communities safer             The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program is a part of the Preparedness Directorate s Office of Grants and Training in the U S  Department of Homeland Security  Grants are given to fire departments to enhance their ability to protect the public and fire service personnel from fire and related hazards  Three types of grants are available  Assistance to Firefighters Grant  AFG   Fire Prevention and Safety  FP   and Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response  SAFER           The primary goal of the Assistance to Firefighters Grants  AFG  is to meet the firefighting and emergency response needs of fire departments and nonaffiliated emergency medical services organizations
