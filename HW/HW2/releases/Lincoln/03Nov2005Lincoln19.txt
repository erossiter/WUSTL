                                                                                                                                                      FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  November 03  2005Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   fought successfully today in the Senate against an effort to reduce critical support payments to southern row crop farmers        I am pleased that my colleagues took to heart what I have been saying all along  that now is neither the time nor the place to break the promise of support our government has made to our farmers by rewriting the Farm Bill in an annual budget debate     Lincoln said      If this amendment had passed  it would have been devastating to southern agriculture   Moreover  it would have broken the firm promise the federal government made to American farmers and ranchers half way through the deal        Lincoln  a senior member of the Senate Agriculture Committee  spoke on the floor of the Senate yesterday against an amendment offered by Senator Charles Grassley  R Iowa  to the Senate budget reconciliation bill   Lincoln said that if such an effort were to pass  it would create a gaping hole in the current safety net relied upon by southern farmers   After Lincoln   s strong plea to her colleagues  the Grassley amendment failed to pass the Senate today  in a vote of 46 53     Senator Grassley   s amendment would have severely altered the nation   s current agriculture policy as established in the 2002 Farm Bill  which Lincoln helped write and eventually pass   In that comprehensive law  which dictates U S  agricultural policy for a six year period  Lincoln fought to establish payment limitations that didn   t disproportionately impact southern farmers  whose crops require a more costly capital investment to grow than those produced in other regions  like the Midwest     Lincoln said such a change in current law would have imposed unreasonable payment limitations and drastically reduced the level of support promised to farmers in that 2002 law   Since the Farm Bill is not scheduled to be reauthorized until the end of 2007  Lincoln said that Senator Grassley   s amendment would have broken a firm promise the federal government has made to American farmers and ranchers
