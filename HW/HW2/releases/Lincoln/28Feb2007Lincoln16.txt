      Lincoln Introduces Bill to Protect Endangered Species  FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  February 28  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington   U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today introduced the Endangered Species Recovery Act of 2007  ESRA   legislation which provides important financial incentives for those who take steps to help endangered or threatened species on the properties they own       As a farmer s daughter from the Arkansas Delta  I know firsthand the deep respect our producers have for the land and its inhabitants   Lincoln said   Often  our private landowners are in the best position to address the needs of a particular species  and this bill encourages them to be a part of the process  Greater involvement of private landowners will almost certainly have a significant impact on recovery of species  I am proud of the bipartisan effort to make these tax incentives a reality       ESRA provides  400 million annually in new tax credits  plus additional deductions and exclusions  for private landowners who take steps to help endangered or threatened species on the properties they own  Specifically  ESRA provides new tax credits for landowners who agree to put easements on their property or agree to restore  enhance  or manage endangered species habitat on their land  It expands tax deductions for any landowner who takes part in the recovery plans specified under the Endangered Species Act  The legislation also allows landowners to exclude from taxable income certain federal payments under cost share conservation programs     In 2005  Lincoln partnered with Senator Mike Crapo  R Idaho  to form a bipartisan working group to address the need to update and strengthen the Endangered Species Act  Lincoln and Crapo first introduced the ESRA in 2006 to provide tax incentives for private landowners      The bipartisan ESRA has the support of Finance Committee Chairman Max Baucus  D Mont   and Ranking Member Charles Grassley  R Iowa   The legislation also enjoys broad support from a variety of interest groups  including the National Wildlife Federation  Environmental Defense  the Defenders of Wildlife  the National Endangered Species Act Reform Coalition  Farm Bureau  and the Congressional Sportsmen s Foundation
