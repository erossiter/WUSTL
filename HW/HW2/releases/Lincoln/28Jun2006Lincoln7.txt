                    Lincoln Measure to Extend Tax Fairness to Disabled Veterans One Step Closer to Reality                                                                                                Both                                                    Lincoln Measure to Extend Tax Fairness to Disabled Veterans One Step Closer to Reality                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 28  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today announced that her legislation to protect disabled veterans from being unfairly taxed on disability benefits that are awarded retroactively has moved one step closer to becoming a realization      This much needed legislation protects our disabled veterans from being unfairly taxed on their entitled benefits  simply because their disability claims were not processed in a timely manner   Lincoln said   Providing this relief is the least we can do for those who have sacrificed so greatly on our behalf       Lincoln   s legislation   The Disabled Veterans Tax Fairness Act of 2006   was approved by the Senate Finance Committee Tuesday as part of a  Good Government  tax package     While the Department of Veterans Affairs  VA  resolves most of its disability claims in less than a year  there are also instances of lost paperwork  administrative errors  and appeals of rejected claims that often delay thousands of disability awards for years  When this occurs  disability compensation is awarded retroactively and a disabled veteran   s previous military retiree pay  on which the veteran has already paid federal income taxes  is re designated as nontaxable disability compensation  Thereby  the disabled veteran is entitled to a refund of taxes paid and must file an amended tax return for each applicable year      Unfortunately  under current law the IRS Code bars the filing of amended returns beyond the last three tax years  As a result  many disabled veterans are denied the opportunity to file a claim for repayment of additional years of back taxes already paid even though the IRS owes them a refund for those years     Lincoln   s legislation would add an exception to the IRS Code for amending returns  This exception would allow disabled military retirees whose disability claims have been pending for more than three years to receive refunds on previous taxes paid for up to five years that their claim was pending     Lincoln introduced her bipartisan legislation with Senator Craig Thomas  R Wyo    Lincoln   s legislation is supported by The Military Coalition  a coalition of 36 military  veterans  and uniformed services organizations  including the Military Officers Association of America  the Air Force Sergeants Association  the Veterans of Foreign Wars  and the National Guard Association of the United States  representing more than 5 5 million members of the uniformed services and their families
