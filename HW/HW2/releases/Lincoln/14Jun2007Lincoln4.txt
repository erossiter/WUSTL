          Lincoln Provision Advances in Military Tax Relief Package  FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 14  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington  U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today announced that her proposal to protect our nations disabled veterans from being unfairly taxed was included in a Senate Finance Committee tax package aimed at providing relief to American servicemen and women and veterans  Lincoln helped craft the Defenders of Freedom Tax Relief Act of 2007 introduced Tuesday by Committee Chairman Max Baucus  D Mont   and Ranking Member Charles Grassley  R Iowa    Those who have worn our nations military uniforms in the past and those who are currently serving deserve all the help they can get from a grateful nation  Lincoln said  This tax package will provide relief to thoseto whom we owe so much  and I am particularly proud to have worked with Chairman Baucus and Senator Grassley to ensure that our veterans and their families are not unjustly penalized for delays beyond their control   Lincolns provision would protect our nations disabled veterans from being unfairly taxed on their entitled benefits that have been awarded retroactively    Lincoln called her proposal  which was first introduced in 2006  a necessary fix because  while the Department of Veterans Affairs  VA  resolves most of its disability claims in less than a year  there are also instances of lost paperwork  administrative errors  and appeals of rejected claims that often delay thousands of disability awards for years  When this occurs  disability compensation is awarded retroactively and a disabled veterans previous military retiree pay  on which the veteran has already paid federal income taxes  is re designated as nontaxable disability compensation  The disabled veteran is then entitled to a refund of taxes paid and must file an amended tax return for each applicable year    Under current law the IRS Code bars the filing of amended returns beyond the last three tax years  As a result  many disabled veterans are denied the opportunity to file a claim for repayment of additional years of back taxes already paid even though the IRS owes them a refund for those years   Lincolns provision would add an exception to the IRS Code for amending returns  This exception would allow disabled military retirees whose disability claims have been pending for more than three years to receive refunds on previous taxes paid for up to five years that their claim was pending  Once the VA has determined a veteran is owed his or her disability refund  the veteran has one year to file an amended tax return   In addition to the veterans tax fairness provision  the Defenders of Freedom Tax Relief Act includes several provisions Lincoln has championed  These provisions would do the following    Expand the qualified mortgage bond program for veterans  Permanently extend the election to include combat pay as earned income for the earned income tax credit  EITC   Allow differential military pay to be treated as a payment of wages  Provide a tax credit for small employers of Reservists and National Guardsmen called for active duty  Institute penalty free withdrawals from retirement plans for Reservists called to active duty  Allow for a roll over of military death gratuities for survivors to tax favored accounts  Exclude gain from sale of principal residence for intelligence service employees  and Treat state payments to service members as nontaxable gifts
