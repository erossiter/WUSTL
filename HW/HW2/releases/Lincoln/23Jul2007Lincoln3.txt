                                     Lincoln  National Center for Missing and Exploited Children Critical to Child Safety  FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 23  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington  U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   recently helped introduce legislation to reauthorize the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children  NCMEC   a non profit organization that works to protect children from abduction and sexual exploitation  As parents and lawmakers  we must continuously look for ways to keep our children safe and punish those that do them harm  said Lincoln  Since 1984  the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children has assisted law enforcement with more than 130 300 missing child cases  resulting in the recovery of more than 112 900 children nationwide  It is critical that Congress continue to support the National Center in its mission to protect our nations most valuable resource  our children   The Protect Our Children First Act of 2007 would reauthorize the NCMEC through 2013 and also improve and expand the grants and programs available through NCMEC  Lincoln is the co founder of the Senate Missing  Exploited  and Runaway Childrens Caucus  which aims to promote  educate  and develop effective policies for the protection of Americas youth  Since its creation in 2003  the Caucus has successfully worked to support and pass significant pieces of legislation such as the Sex Offender Notification Act and the Adam Walsh Child Protection Act that improve access to sex offender registries and increases support for law enforcement efforts to track and capture those who prey on children  Earlier this year  Lincoln met with British Minister of Parliament Gerry Sutcliffe to discuss ways our two countries can work together to better protect children from sexual offenders in the United States and Britain  She also worked with NCMEC to help unveil NetSmartz411 org  a free and innovative resource designed to help parents keep their children safer on the Internet
