                    Lincoln  Pryor and Snyder Announce  62 000 Grant for the University of Central Arkansas                                                                                                Both                                                    Lincoln  Pryor and Snyder Announce  62 000 Grant for the University of Central Arkansas                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 04  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington   U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor along with U S  Representative Vic Snyder  AR 02  announced today that the University of Central Arkansas has been awarded a  62 000 grant through the U S  Department of Education s Fulbright Hays Group Project Abroad Program      The Fulbright Hays Group Projects Abroad Program supports overseas projects in modern foreign languages and area studies for groups of teachers  students  and faculty  Projects may include short term seminars  curriculum development  or group research or study  The University of Central Arkansas will use the grant to fund a short term seminar enabling teachers to travel to Petrozavodsk and Murmansk  two northern Russian cities that are rarely visited by American educators  The seminar is designed to enhance the educators  understanding of Russia as they observe  study and live in the two cities and travel to adjacent locations       In order to be successful in today s global community  we must have an understanding of the world around us   said Lincoln   With the help of these federal dollars  educators from the University of Central Arkansas will have an opportunity to experience and learn from another culture and will be able to pass the knowledge they gain on to their students in Arkansas       This funding provides an exciting opportunity for University of Central Arkansas teachers   Their first hand knowledge of the business  cultural and political environment in Russia will enhance their students understanding of world affairs   said Pryor      The U S  and Russia have a linked past  but the future of the relationship will best be shaped by the understanding that comes from these kinds of exchanges   said Congressman Vic Snyder   These programs are vital  and I m pleased UCA received this grant
