                                                                                                                                                               FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 28  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     Several Arkansas projects were included in the Agriculture Appropriations Bill recently passed by the Senate Appropriations Committee  U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor announced today      Lincoln and Pryor preserved funding for several Arkansas projects despite spending constraints this fiscal year   After approval by the Senate  the Fiscal Year 2007 legislation will go to a House Senate conference committee to negotiate differences between the two chambers          Agriculture drives Arkansas    economy  and federal funding for agricultural research is critical to sustaining Arkansas    role in the global marketplace     Lincoln said      Our communities in Arkansas deserve their fair share of federal dollars  and I will continue my fight alongside Senator Pryor to ensure that these important Arkansas projects are included in the final agriculture spending bill            Federal support for local agriculture research and development initiatives is critical to the future of farming  but also to improving the quality of life in rural Arkansas     Pryor said      These projects are smart investments  and I look forward to working with Senator Lincoln to ensure their funding stays intact as the legislative process advances        The FY 2007 Agriculture spending bill passed by the Senate Appropriations Committee includes funding for the following Arkansas projects          1 million to include the state of Arkansas in the USDA Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Pilot Program   The Committee has expanded the program from twelve to fifteen states  including Arkansas   The program enables 25 schools in Arkansas to provide students with fresh fruit and vegetable snacks during the school day   Schools may choose how to implement the program and may select the mix and quantities of fruits and vegetables to offer students            41 273 million for the National Center for Toxicological Research in Jefferson County  more than  7 million over the President   s request of  34 240 million         An additional  500 000 for the Arkansas Children   s Nutrition Center in Little Rock for expanded research on children   s nutrition and health from conception through adolescence   Also  continued funding for Sorghum Research and the Diet and Immune Function at Arkansas Children   s Nutrition Center  Total funding level estimated at  5 6 million           2 5 million for the Delta Regional Authority   The Authority provides long term coordination among federal  state  and local entities committed to economic development in the Lower Mississippi Delta region   These funds are contained in the Rural Community Advancement Program for grants           1 418 million for the Animal Science Food Safety Consortium for food safety research and education in Arkansas  Illinois  and Kansas   In Arkansas  the University of Arkansas Division of Agriculture  the University of Arkansas for Medical Science and Arkansas Children   s Hospital will participate          456 000 for the Center for Integrated Forest Management Strategies at the University of Arkansas at Monticello for forestry research and education activities          1 108 million for the Institute for Food Science and Engineering at the University of Arkansas at Fayetteville for food science and nutrition research and education          178 000 for the Beef Producers Improvement program at the University of Arkansas at Fayetteville for research and education on management techniques for the cattle industry          1 784 million for Geographic Information Systems   Arkansas is one of eight states that participate in this project          2 5 million to the Appropriate Technology Transfer for Rural Areas  ATTRA  program at the University of Arkansas at Fayetteville   The ATTRA program   s headquarters are located in Montana   Its national mission is to collect and provide cutting edge research and production information and technical assistance to commercial producers  county extension agents  and others involved in information dissemination on organic and sustainable agriculture practices that are environmentally friendly and offer lower production costs          223 000 for the Ozark Nutrient Management project         124 000 for the UAPB Small Farm Outreach and Water Management Center         203 000 for the University of Arkansas at Pine Bluff for aquaculture research and education activities          330 000 for an animal disposal unit in the state of Arkansas  based at the University of Arkansas         The Poultry Production and Product Safety Research Unit at the University of Arkansas at Fayetteville  Total funding level estimated at  1 58 million          The National Center for Agricultural Law Research and Information at the Leflar School of Law at the University of Arkansas at Fayetteville  Total funding level estimated at  502 000            Dale
