                    Arkansas Congressional Delegation Announces  1 77 Million for Six Arkansas Colleges  Universities Million for Six Arkansas Colleges  Universities                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 06  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington  U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor and U S  Representatives Marion Berry  Vic Snyder  and John Boozman Thursday announced that six Arkansas colleges and universities have been awarded Upward Bound grants totaling  1 773 937    Arkansas Tech University  Lyon College  Philander Smith College  the University of Arkansas  Fayetteville  and the University of Arkansas  Fort Smith will receive funding from the Upward Bound Math and Science Program  This initiative is designed to prepare high school students for postsecondary education programs that lead to careers in math and science  The program includes such services as summer programs with intensive math and science training  year round counseling  and exposure to faculty members or graduate students serving as mentors  Two hundred fifty eight students are expected to participate in Arkansas    Additionally  Pulaski Technical College and the University of Arkansas  Fayetteville will receive funding from the Upward Bound Veterans Program designed to assist veterans in preparing for postsecondary education plans  The program provides academic instruction  tutoring  counseling  and work study programs  Approximately 240 veterans are expected to participate    Upward Bound is a valuable federal program that helps thousands of Arkansans and people across the country reach their full academic potential  Lincoln said  These grants will allow our colleges and universities to continue to expand educational opportunities to students and veterans across Arkansas   Upward Bound is an innovative program that understands the importance and value of higher education  Participants will be critical to the future of Arkansas workforce so that we remain competitive in todays growing global economy  said Pryor   The program at Lyon College goes beyond the normal summer program  said Berry  It provides disadvantaged students with opportunities beyond the classroom to cultivate their interest in the math and science fields  Any investment in children is an investment in our nation s future  which is why I m proud to have helped secure funding for such a worthwhile cause   Upward Bound is a valuable program  said Congressman Snyder  It provides young people the opportunity to fulfill their potential through higher education   Math and science education are key to Arkansas future as we become a larger player in the global economy  I am extremely proud that three Third District universities are taking such a leadership role in reaching out to the children of Arkansas  Boozman said  Additionally  as a member of the House Veterans Affairs Committee  I am glad to see my alma mater do their part in helping our military veterans prepare for their own educations  It is my hope that Arkansas veterans avail themselves of this important program   Upward Bound  an initiative of the U S  Department of Education  serves high school students from low income families  high school students from families in which neither parent holds a bachelors degree  and low income  first generation military veterans who are preparing to enter postsecondary education    Arkansas colleges and universities receiving Upward Bound grants include the following   Math and Science Program Arkansas Tech University  Russellville   250 000 Lyon College  Batesville
