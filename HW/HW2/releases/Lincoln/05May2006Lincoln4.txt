                                                                                                                                                      FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  May 05  2006Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today said that she joined her fellow Americans and our Mexican neighbors in celebrating Cinco de Mayo  which commemorates the historic victory of the Mexican people in defense of their freedom and sovereignty at the Battle of Puebla on May 5  1862          I   m delighted to join Mexican Americans in Arkansas and those across North America who celebrate Cinco de Mayo     Senator Blanche Lincoln said      While it is celebrated in Mexico with parades and a re enactment of the battle  here at home Cinco de Mayo has taken a new meaning among Mexican Americans who celebrate their heritage with music  food and pride for their culture   This holiday has become a wonderful celebration of the contributions that Mexican Americans have made to our country through their hard work  family values and patriotism        Cinco de Mayo began on May 5  1862  when Mexican troops defeated an invading French army that overpowered them in numbers and weapons in the city of Puebla  This battle became a symbol of patriotism  pride and self determination for the people of Mexico       This year  while we celebrate Cinco de Mayo  Lincoln pledged to continue fighting for much needed immigration reform to strengthen our borders and allow undocumented workers to step out of the shadows to place them on an earned path toward citizenship        I encourage all Americans to celebrate Cinco de Mayo by honoring the contributions that Mexican Americans have made and continue to make to our nation     Lincoln said      This is a day that reminds us to embrace our diversity  never forget where we came from  and know that if we work together  we can make America   s promise a reality for all
