                    Lincoln Offers Bill to Better Protect Elderly From Abuse                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Both                                                    Lincoln Offers Bill to Better Protect Elderly From Abuse                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  November 16  2005Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   Tuesday introduced a bill to put into place a national system to better address the growing problems of elder abuse  neglect and exploitation in this country          Elder abuse happens everywhere  at all levels of income and in all geographic areas     Lincoln said      With 77 million Baby Boomers on the brink of retirement  it   s even more important for Congress to look for solutions to ensure that Americans in the twilight of their lives are protected from abuse that threatens their productivity  safety and independence         Each year  between 500 000 and 5 million seniors in our country are abused  neglected  or exploited   An estimated 84  of all cases are never  reported  Lincoln introduced the    Elder Justice Act of 2005     a comprehensive bill that represents the culmination of 25 years of congressional hearings on the distressing effects of elder abuse   Lincoln said that her bill  which she introduced with Senator Orrin Hatch  R Utah   would confront the growing national trend of elder abuse by establishing a new system similar to the way our nation currently combats child abuse and violence against women                                                                                             Lincoln said her bill would increase collaboration between federal  state  local  and private entities  law enforcement  long term care facilities  consumer advocates  and families to prevent and treat elder abuse   In addition  it would fund important research  law enforcement and criminal background checks on employees in long term care facilities  public health initiatives and the development of future initiatives to combat elder abuse                                                                                             The Elder Justice Act of 2005 would establish offices in the Department of Health and Human Services and the Department of Justice to combat crimes against the elderly and create a national board to develop a strategic plan to advance elder justice   In addition  the bill would establish the nation   s first ever elder justice resource center to serve as a central repository and information database on perpetrators of such abuse and its victims                                                                                              The Elder Justice Act will take steps to help make our older American family members safer whether it be in their homes  assisted living facilities or in nursing homes     Lincoln said      This new set of federal initiatives will allow us to better detect instances of elder abuse and it will devote more resources to help seniors recover from abuse after it has taken place
