                    Lincoln  Pryor Introduce Bill to Mint Commemorative Coin to Mark Anniversary of Little Rock Central   s Desegregation Crisis                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Both                                                    Lincoln  Pryor Introduce Bill to Mint Commemorative Coin to Mark Anniversary of Little Rock Central   s Desegregation Crisis                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  March 09  2005Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 WASHINGTON  D C      Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor today introduced legislation that would create a commemorative silver dollar honoring the 50th Anniversary of the desegregation of Little Rock Central High School       Lincoln and Pryor said the commemorative coins would bring national and international attention to the lasting legacy of the desegregation of Little Rock Central High School and its contribution to the civil rights movement   They credited Congressman Vic Snyder for introducing identical legislation in January and garnering support from 299 House cosponsors     Under the legislation  500 000 coins will be minted by the Treasury and issued beginning January 1  2007  with a portion of the funds covering production costs and the rest slated for improvements and programs at the Central High Museum and Visitor Center           This bill is a small token of recognition of the gift that the Little Rock Nine and the entire Little Rock community has given to this nation   Lincoln said   Their principled stand helped to move the state and the nation forward as it marched toward greater equality for all  and I salute their courage and determination          Two commemorative coins are issued by Congress each year to reflect significant events in American history  The Little Rock Nine helped define the meaning of equal rights for our nation  and their determination to attend Central High shaped history     Pryor said     As the 50th anniversary of the desegregation crisis approaches  this coin is an opportunity to honor the important role these events played in our nation   s history            The desegregation of Little Rock Central High School was a monumental day in our state   s  and our nation   s history   Snyder said    A commemorative coin issued by the U S  Mint seems like an appropriate way to honor its 50th anniversary  I owe a sincere thank you to Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor for introducing identical legislation in the U S  Senate  and to Congressman John Boozman of the 3rd Congressional District for his help in pushing the bill along        In the 108th Congress  Pryor introduced legislation to leverage funding to build a new Visitor Center at the High School   Additionally  Pryor  Lincoln and Snyder worked alongside members of the Little Rock Nine and former Congressional Black Caucus Chairman Elijah Cummings to successfully secure  733 000 in design funding   As a result of this funding  the National Park Service has been able to move forward with the Visitor Center  awarding contracts to an architect and an exhibit designer   They plan to fight for the remaining construction funding this year
