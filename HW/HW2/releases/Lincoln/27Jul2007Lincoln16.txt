 Laning  202 224 4843        Washington  U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark    a senior member of the Senate Agriculture Committee and Chair of the Subcommittee on Production  Income Protection  and Price Support  released the following statement upon the House of Representatives passage of the Farm Bill       I would like to commend Chairman Peterson of the House Agriculture Committee for his hard work passing a Farm Bill that maintains the components of our farm programs  includes billions of dollars for conservation and nutrition programs  and makes a strong investment in renewable energy  The Senate Agriculture Committee will begin our work in September and my goal will be to ensure that we continue the investment in our farm programs and that Arkansas farmers are not disproportionately impacted by the new payment limitation provisions  I have had many productive talks with my fellow committee members on both sides of the aisle  and I have stressed the need to continue a policy that ensures our commitment to producing a safe domestic food and fiber supply   Unfortunately  the Presidents veto threat of the Farm Bill is another example of this Administrations failing commitment to farmers in Arkansas and communities across rural America  Our producers should know that I will continue to fight to ensure they have the safety net they need to compete in an uncertain global marketplace    Lincoln played an instrumental role in writing the 2002 Farm Bill
