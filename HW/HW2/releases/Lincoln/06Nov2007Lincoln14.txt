                                                                                                                                                          FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  November 06  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington  Upon returning from a three day trip to Colombia  U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today said that the South American country is making advances with respect to its government and economy and that the pending U S  Colombia trade agreement would be beneficial for American businesses and agricultural producers    Lincoln  Chair of the Senate Finance Committees Subcommittee on International Trade and Global Competitiveness  traveled to Colombia November 2 4 with U S  Trade Representative Susan C  Schwab and Acting U S  Secretary of Agriculture Charles Conner    During her visit  Lincoln met with Colombian President lvaro Uribe Vlez and other officials  including Minister of National Defense Juan Manuel Santos  Trade Minister Lus Guillermo Plata  and Colombian Ambassador to the U S  Carolina Barco  She also met with Colombian labor leaders viewed areas of economicdevelopmentin the city of Medelln  visited a farm outside the city  and toured the Catam Air Base in Bogot  the central command of Colombias anti drug operation    Colombia has made tremendous progress with respect to strengthening its democratic government and growing its economy  Lincoln said  From my meeting with President Uribe  I witnessed firsthand his commitment to protecting his citizens from violence and to combating his countrys well known drug problem   Lincoln added  Our pending trade agreement with Colombia is a win win for both countries  Colombia benefits from a trading relationship with the U S   and more importantly  our American businesses and agricultural producers are given access to Colombian markets  The agreement turnsa one way streetwherethe U S  market isopen to Colombian goodsinto a two way streetwhere U S  businesses can begin accessing the Colombia market  As a member of the Finance Committee and chair of the subcommittee of jurisdiction  Lincoln plays an important role in the pending free trade agreement with Colombia  The agreement currently awaits approval by the Senate and House
