 Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   won a significant legislative battle today by successfully restoring funding in the federal budget to combat the nation   s growing meth epidemic      This was a significant victory for law enforcement across this country who are now one step closer to having the necessary resources they depend on to combat this deadly drug that   s destroying our communities   Senator Blanche Lincoln said   I   m pleased that the majority of my Senate colleagues recognized the severity of our nation   s meth epidemic  and took the President   s cut seriously enough to make restoring this critical funding a national priority in this budget       During the debate on the federal budget in the U S  Senate late this afternoon  Lincoln offered her amendment to restore  115 million that was cut by President Bush from the COPS program   s Methamphetamine Enforcement and Clean Up initiative  also referred to as the COPS Hot Spots initiative      After a day of lobbying her Senate colleagues on both sides of the aisle  Lincoln   s amendment gained enough support and was accepted by the Senate  The legislation will now go to a conference committee of House and Senate negotiators to reconcile the differences between the Senate and House passed budgets      Earlier this week  Lincoln cosponsored a bipartisan effort which successfully boosted funding to fight meth in the Combat Meth Act to  99 million  The initiative is part of the PATRIOT Act and would provide funding to train law enforcement agencies to combat meth and reduce violent crime associated with the deadly drug
