                    Arkansas Delegation Announces  3 6 Million For Victims of Crime Assistance Funds                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Both                                                    Arkansas Delegation Announces  3 6 Million For Victims of Crime Assistance Funds                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 22  2007Contact  Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor  and U S  Representatives Marion Berry  Vic Snyder  John Boozman  and Mike Ross announced today that the U S  Department of Justice has awarded  3 670 000 in Victims of Crime Assistance  VOCA  funding for the state of Arkansas  The funding will be awarded by the State to local community based organizations to provide training and technical assistance to service providers  In addition  the funding will support crime victim assistance projects that expand and enhance direct services to victims of crime   Arkansans who have fallen victim to a crime deserve quality services to help them in their time of need  said Lincoln  These federal dollars will fund victim assistance programs that will support these individuals and help combat the negative impact crime can have on our communities  Many organizations in Arkansas successfully help lift up victims of abuse and other crimes so they can recover and resume fulfilling lives  This funding will allow such outreach programs to expand or enhance their services to members of our community  Pryor said  Criminals must pay for their crimes  and in this case they do  said Berry  This money comes from fines collected from criminals and is distributed to victims to help with expenses like medical bills and funeral costs  This funding is  in more ways than one  a lifesaver that will help victims rebuild their lives  Services for the victims of crime are important tools in the healing and recovery process  Boozman said  This money will benefit programs such as domestic violence shelters  and assist victims of sexual abuse and other crimes  It is important funding that I am happy to see Arkansas receive  I am proud to announce these funds which will assist victims of crime across Arkansas  Ross said  It is important to have a trained staff ready to aide victims in their time of need and help them get on with their lives after having experienced such a traumatic event  The Office for Victims of Crime program was established by the 1984 Victims of Crime Act  VOCA  and provides funding for a range of services including crisis intervention  counseling  guidance  legal advocacy  transportation to court or to shelters and referrals  Funding for OVCs programs are derived from criminal fines  forfeited bail bonds  penalties  and special assessments collected by Federal Courts  U S  Attorneys Offices  and the Federal Bureau of Prisons from offenders convicted of federal crimes
