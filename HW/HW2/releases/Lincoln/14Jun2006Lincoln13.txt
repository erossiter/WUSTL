Katie Laning  202 224 4843 Washington     U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor announced today that the Hattie W  Caraway Post Office in Jonesboro is one step closer to becoming a reality     A proposal to name a Jonesboro postal facility after Arkansas    first female Senator passed the Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs earlier today   The measure will now be considered before the full Senate        Hattie Caraway has been a tremendous role model for me as I have followed in her footsteps to become the second woman to serve Arkansas in the U S  Senate     Lincoln said      I am proud that we can honor her historical achievement and commitment to Arkansas with this facility in her hometown of Jonesboro           Hattie Caraway served Arkansas with unwavering dedication and public integrity  and designating this post office in Jonesboro in her name is a small step Congress can take to honor her legacy   I look forward to working with Senator Lincoln  and with my colleagues on both sides of the aisle  to gain the full Senate   s approval on this legislation     said Pryor     On January 12  1932  Caraway  a native of Jonesboro  became the first woman ever elected to the U S  Senate after winning a special election to fill the remaining months of her husband   s term after his death   Arkansans elected Caraway to the Senate two more times  serving until 1945   In 1933  Caraway became the first woman to chair a Senate committee and in 1943  she became the first woman to take up the gavel on the Senate floor as the Senate   s presiding officer      The new Hattie W  Caraway Post Office will be an expansion of the mail sorting facility built in 1998 at 2404 Race Street in Jonesboro   The 280 000 square feet facility will house post office boxes  clerk services  sorting capabilities and a carrier center to serve the growing Jonesboro area     U S  Representative Marion Berry  AR 01  introduced a companion bill that passed the House of Representatives on February 8
