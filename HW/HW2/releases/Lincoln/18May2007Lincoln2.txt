Laning  202 224 4843 Washington   U S  Senator Blanche Lincoln  D Ark   today announced the selection of summer interns for her offices in Washington and Little Rock  Lincoln s Summer Internship Program provides students the opportunity to learn about the federal government and the nation s legislative process       I m extremely honored to have the opportunity to host so many outstanding young Arkansans in my offices this summer   said Lincoln   As someone who s received great satisfaction from my public service  I hope to be able to provide these students a rewarding  educational experience       The response once again was overwhelming and the selection process extremely competitive   Lincoln said   I continue to be impressed by the quality of young people from Arkansas who are interested in public service      The following interns will participate in Lincoln s first summer session  which runs from May 29 through June 29  In Little Rock  the following students have been selected  Jason Arthur of Little Rock and Godfrey White of Osceola  The following students will work for the same period in Lincoln s Washington office  AJ Berna of Little Rock  Mark Daven of Little Rock  Meg Holden of Fort Smith  Susannah Kimmey of Atlanta  Ga   Collin Stewart of Dumas  and Sarah Tutt of Camden     For Lincoln s second summer session  which lasts from July 2 until August 3  the following students will work in Little Rock  Jason Arthur of Little Rock and Freddie Smith of Hope  Those selected to participate in Lincoln s Washington office are  Alex Bachari of Little Rock  Kate Evans of Little Rock  Kristen McCollum of Jonesboro  Katherine Peterson of Little Rock  Caitlin Quinn of Little Rock  and Will Wright of DeQueen     In addition  Lincoln will host a Spitze Public Policy Legislative Intern from the Dale Bumpers College of Agricultural  Food and Life Sciences at the University of Arkansas  Grant Hunt of Brinkley will serve as a Spitze intern in Lincoln s Washington office from May 29 through August 3     Lincoln s internship program gives students who have completed their sophomore year in college an opportunity to learn about the federal legislative process  improve their communications skills  and gain experience in a professional environment  Over the course of the five week program  interns will work closely with the legislative staff  the constituent services staff  and the communications office
