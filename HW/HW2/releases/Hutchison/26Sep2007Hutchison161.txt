SEN. HUTCHISON ANNOUNCES $2.7 MILLION IN GRANTS FOR HSIS
  Texas' senior Senator, Kay Bailey Hutchison (R-TX), today announced that five Hispanic Serving Institution (HSIs) in Texas have been awarded a total of $2,765,079 in grants by the U.S. Department of Education's Strengthening Institutions HSI Program. 
   "Education is a foundation of democracy and helps to guarantee our leadership in an increasingly global economy," Sen. Hutchison said. "I am pleased to announce these grants for our HSIs. These institutions are vital to ensuring that many Texans have the opportunity to advance their education and I will continue working in the Senate to increase their funding at the federal level."

   According to the U.S. Department of Education, their HSI program provided grants to help HSIs to expand educational opportunities for, and improve the academic attainment of, Hispanic students. The grants also enable HSIs to expand and enhance their academic offerings, program quality, and institution stability.
   Sen. Hutchison recently announced an over $500 million increase in federal HSI funding that she supported and helped secure in H.R. 2669, the College Cost Reduction Act of 2007, which the President will sign into law tomorrow. The conference report to the bill passed the Senate on September 7.
