SEN. HUTCHISON ANNOUNCES OVER $15 MILLION AWARDED TO TEXAS FOR HEAD START PROGRAMS
  Sen. Kay Bailey Hutchison (R-TX), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced that Texas communities have been awarded grants totaling $15,030,368 by the Department of Health and Human Services for Head Start early childhood education programs.  
   "Head Start has been instrumental in helping Texas children prepare for school," Sen. Hutchison said. "I am proud to announce that these communities will receive additional resources to strengthen their childhood education programs."
   Head Start is a comprehensive child development program that prepares young children for school. The following communities have been awarded grants:
   * $5,600,918 to the Hidalgo County Head Start Program
   * $3,670,470 to Neighborhood Centers, Inc. in Houston, Texas
   * $3,255,486 to Avance, Inc. Houston, Texas
   * $2,503,494 to the Harris County Department of Education.
