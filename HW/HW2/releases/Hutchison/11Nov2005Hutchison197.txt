SEN. HUTCHISON COMMENTS HONORING OUR NATION'S VETERANS
  Sen. Kay Bailey Hutchison (R-TX), Chairman of the Military Construction and Veterans Affairs Appropriations Subcommittee and a member of the Senate Veterans Affairs Committee, today released the following statement honoring the service of our nation's veterans. A list of Sen. Hutchison's activities on behalf of veterans is listed below: 
   "Veterans Day is a time for Americans to come together in honor of the brave men and women who served and sacrificed to protect our freedom and way of life. As our nation fights the War on Terror, today is also a time to show support for our troops currently serving overseas and their families back home. As we thank them for their service, we must continue our work in Congress to ensure our veterans - past, present and future - receive the proper care and benefits they have earned and deserve." 
   Senator Hutchison's Accomplishments for Veterans:
   * As Chairman of the Appropriations Subcommittee on Military Construction and Veterans Affairs, Sen. Hutchison:
   - Secured $1.5 billion in emergency supplemental medical services to make up for the shortfall in the budget. The VA would have run out of money and cut services if this had not been approved; 
   - Secured $23.3 billion for medical services in FY06; 
   - Secured $3.2 billion for medical facilities; 
   - Secured $412 million for medical research; 
   - Secured $1.5 billion for IT programs; 
   - Secured $815 million for construction; 
   - Secured $104 million for grants for the construction of state extended care facilities. 
   * Has pushed successfully to fund research and treatment for Gulf War Illness; 
   * Is pursuing efforts to name the Waco VA as a Mental Health Center of Excellence to provide the best treatment for Post-Traumatic Stress Syndrome and other related illnesses; 
   * Introduced legislation extending the time period Gulf War veterans suffering from chronic disabilities have to file with the Department of Veterans Affairs; 
   * Co-sponsored legislation to achieve parity with other surviving spouses the basic annuity that is provided under the Survivors Benefit Plan for surviving spouses who are at least 62 years of age; 
   * Co-sponsored legislation to establish a presumption of service connection for certain veterans with Hepatitis C; 
   * Co-sponsored legislation to increase burial benefits for veterans; 
   * Co-sponsored legislation to provide for a Korea Defense Service Medal to be issued to members of the Armed Forces who participated in operations in Korea after the end of the Korean War; 
   * Is working to save the Waco and Big Spring VA hospitals from closure.
