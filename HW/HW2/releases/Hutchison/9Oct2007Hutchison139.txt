SEN. HUTCHISON ANNOUNCES OVER $100,000 IN TEXAS READING GRANTS
  Texas' senior Senator Kay Bailey Hutchison (R-TX) today announced that nine Texas communities have been awarded a total of $103,500 in grants by the National Endowment for the Arts "Big Read" project to encourage children in all age groups to read more great literature. 
   "Our children should experience the great works of literature," Sen. Hutchison said. "This funding will help communities make the classics available to a new generation of readers."
   The following organizations will receive funding:
   City - Organization - Funding 
   San Antonio - San Antonio Public Library Foundation - $20,000 
   El Paso - El Paso Public Library - $20,000 
   Brownsville - UT Brownsville - $16,000 
   Dallas - OCA City of Dallas Latino Cultural Center - $10,000 
   Live Oak - Northwest Lakeview College - $10,000 
   Beaumont - Communities in Schools Southeast Texas - $7,500 
   Weatherford - Weatherford College - $7,500 
   Marfa - Friends of the Marfa Public Libraries - $7,500 
   Galveston - Rosenberg Library/Galveston County Reads - $5,000 
   Total - $103,500 
   According to the National Endowment for the Arts, the funding will be used to conduct local "Big Read" programs. Communities will also be provided with free support materials such as teachers guides, audio book introductions and a comprehensive Web site.
