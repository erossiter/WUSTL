SEN. HUTCHISON ANNOUNCES $496,000 FOR SOUTHERN METHODIST UNIVERSITY IS BEING RELEASED BY DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION
  Sen. Kay Bailey Hutchison (R-TX) announced today the Department of Education is releasing $496,000 from the Fund for the Improvement of Post-Secondary Education (FIPSE) for Southern Methodist University research. Sen.  
 Hutchison successfully included the funds for SMU in the Fiscal Year 2005 Labor, Health and Human Services appropriations bill.
   "Southern Methodist University is a proven leader in scientific research. More advanced equipment and laboratory resources will strengthen and improve their already strong research capabilities," Sen. Hutchison said.
   The FIPSE resources will be used for equipment upgrade and lab modernization at SMU's Opto-Electronics Laboratory to support technology research. The research will be used in developing a data transmission link for local area networks that will be 40 times faster than current industry standards.
