SEN. HUTCHISON HOSTS THIRD AFRICAN AMERICAN SUMMIT IN WASHINGTON
  This week more than 500 African American leaders met in Washington, D.C. for a two-day summit organized by Sen. Kay Bailey Hutchison (R-TX) to discuss the economy, education, health care and other issues with Bush Administration officials and senior members of Congress. 
   "More than 500 attendees from across the country have registered to attend the third African American Summit and I am honored to serve as its chair. It is an important opportunity for leaders from all levels to join together to discuss key issues affecting African Americans," Sen. Hutchison said. "I would like to thank the leaders in Washington who participated in the summit and the attendees who traveled to our nation's capital. They contributed to an informative and constructive dialogue that will help lead to progress on critical issues."
   Sen. Hutchison and event co-host Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA) were joined by U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development Secretary Alphonso Jackson, Senator David Vitter (R-LA), Senator Norm Coleman (R-MN) and Senator James Talent (R-MO).
