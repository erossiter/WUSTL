SEN. HUTCHISON ANNOUNCES $54 MILLION AWARDED TO PROTECT TEXAS PORTS
  Sen. Kay Bailey Hutchison (R-TX), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Office of State and Local Government Coordination and Preparedness has awarded a total of $54,094,294 to Texas ports.  
   * H5 Beaumont ports received a total of $6,901,293 
   * 2 Freeport ports received a total of $2,657,000 
   * 24 Houston ports received a total of $35,325,116 
   * 5 Port Arthur ports received a total of $4,135,135 
   * 3 Texas City ports received a total of $5,885,750 
   "This funding will strengthen security efforts in these active Texas ports," Sen. Hutchison said. "We have identified nation's ports as a source of vulnerability and we must continue to provide this critical funding to ensure our homeland is secure.\" 
   The funding, which will be made available September 30, 2005, will help improve security for operators and passengers through physical security enhancements with a focus on a number of national priorities.
