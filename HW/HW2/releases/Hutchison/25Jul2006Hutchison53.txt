SEN. HUTCHISON RECEIVES ARMY ASSOCIATION 'OUTSTANDING LEGISLATOR' AWARD
  Sen. Kay Bailey Hutchison (R-TX), chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Military Construction and Veterans Affairs, today was awarded the Association of the U.S. Army's 2006 "Outstanding Legislator" award at a recognition ceremony in Washington, D.C.  
   "I am honored to receive this prestigious award," Sen. Hutchison said. "I am a proud advocate for America's troops and veterans. They have made great sacrifices to defend our freedom at home and overseas, and I will continue to ensure we fulfill our promise to support them."
   The Association of the U.S. Army (AUSA) is a non-profit, national education organization dedicated to advancing the interests of active and retired soldiers, guardsmen, reservists and their families. The Outstanding Legislator Award is given annually by the association and will be presented to Sen. Hutchison by former Army Chief of Staff and current AUSA President General Gordon Sullivan (Ret.).
