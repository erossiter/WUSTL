SEN. HUTCHISON ANNOUNCES $650,000 RESEARCH GRANT FOR TEXAS A&M-CORPUS CHRISTI
  Texas' senior Senator Kay Bailey Hutchison (R-TX) today announced that Texas A&M University-Corpus Christi will receive a $650,000 research grant from the National Science Foundation (NSF). 
   "Technology is increasingly important to a productive learning environment, and these NSF funds will help Texas A&M researchers develop new and innovative methods in this field," Sen. Hutchison said.
   The grant will support a project entitled, "CRI: IAD - Computing Infrastructure for Research-Based Learning (CIRBL)," under the direction of Dr. John Fernandez, Associate Professor of Computer Science.
