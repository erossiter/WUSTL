SEN. HUTCHISON MEETS WITH LUBBOCK CHAMBER OF COMMERCE
  Texas' senior Sen. Kay Bailey Hutchison, today met with members of the Lubbock Chamber of Commerce in her Washington, D.C. office to discuss local issues affecting the Hub City and South Plains. 
   "Agriculture, transportation and research are important priorities for the future of the Lubbock community," Sen. Hutchison said. "Hearing directly about the issues and concerns confronting their community helps me address those issues here in Washington."
   Lubbock Mayor David Miller and Chamber of Commerce President Eddie McBride attended the meeting with Sen. Hutchison held to discuss priority issues in the area including, reauthorization of the Farm Bill, research funding for Texas Tech University and the Ports to Plains corridor.
