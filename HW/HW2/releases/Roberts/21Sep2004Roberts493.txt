SEN. ROBERTS: SENATE INTELLIGENCE COMMITTEE FAVORABLY REPORTS OUT NOMINATION OF REP. GOSS TO BE DIRECTOR OF CENTRAL INTELLIGENCE
  Sen. Pat Roberts, Chairman of the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence today announced that the Committee favorably reported out the nomination of Rep.  
 Porter Goss (R-FL) to be the Director of Central Intelligence. 
   "I am pleased the Committee acted quickly and voted strongly in favor of the nomination of Porter Goss. His experience in both oversight and as an intelligence officer makes him uniquely qualified to lead the intelligence community as we debate its critical reforms." 
   The Committee voted 12 in favor, four against and one member gave no instructions. 
   The nomination now goes to the full Senate for debate and vote.
