SENATE INTELLIGENCE COMMITTEE FAVORABLY REPORTS OUT NOMINATIONS OF AMBASSADOR NEGROPONTE, LT. GEN. HAYDEN
  Sen. Pat Roberts, Chairman of the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence, today announced that the Committee favorably reported out the nomination of Ambassador John Negroponte to be the Director of National Intelligence and Lieutenant General Michael V. Hayden to be the Deputy Director of National Intelligence. 
   "I am pleased the Committee quickly approved the nomination of the nation's first National Intelligence Director," Senator Roberts said. "Ambassador Negroponte has a demonstrated record as an outstanding manager and leader and has a knowledgeable and capable deputy in General Hayden." 
   The nomination now goes to the full Senate for debate and vote.
