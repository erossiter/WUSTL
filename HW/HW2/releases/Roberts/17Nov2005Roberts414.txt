SEN. ROBERTS: SENATE PASSES STATE, JUSTICE, COMMERCE APPROPRIATIONS BILL; CONTAINS IMPORTANT FUNDS FOR KANSAS
  Sen. Pat Roberts today announced that late last night, the Senate passed the appropriations conference report for Science, the Departments of State, Justice, and Commerce and Related Agencies for Fiscal Year 2006 containing investments in Kansas programs. 
   Senator Roberts said, "I am pleased the Senate has acted quickly to make investments in law enforcement and education in Kansas."
   The following projects Senator Roberts personally worked to include in the legislation:
   * $223,000 for YouthFriends, of Greater Kansas City, a mentoring program for Kansas youth;
   * $200,000 for Neosho County to assist the Southeast Kansas regional in-car digital video project for law enforcement agencies in the region;
   * $1 million for the Dole Scholarship program. It is funded through NASA, administered through the Dole Institute at the University of Kansas and provides scholarships for Kansas students to attend Kansas colleges and universities.
   The bill now goes to the president to be signed into law.
