SEN. ROBERTS: DEFENSE AUTHORIZATION BILL APPROVED BY SENATE CONTAINS IMPORTANT KANSAS PROJECTS
  Sen. Pat Roberts, Chairman of the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence and a senior member of the Senate Armed Services Committee, is pleased to announce the Senate passed the Defense Authorization Bill with important Kansas projects. 
   "I am proud of the contributions that Kansas makes to our national defense," Senator Roberts said. "These projects reflect my focus on modernizing and transforming our forces, and giving our men and women in uniform the necessary training and equipment to confront future threats, be they conventional or unconventional. This bill continues our efforts to improve the quality of life for our men and women in uniform and their families."
   The bill now goes to a conference committee with the House version of the legislation. Among the projects that benefit Kansas, Senator Roberts helped secure the following funds:
   FORT LEAVENWORTH 
   * $3 million for the Battle Command Team Training Program at Fort Leavenworth. This program grants unit commanders the ability to properly train critical battle staff techniques in preparation for combat operations. 
   MCCONNELL AFB
   * $12.5 million for additional equipment for the 161st Intelligence Squadron at McConnell Air Force Base. The procurement of this equipment will expand the ability of the 161st Intelligence Squadron in the Kansas Air National Guard to staff, train, and equip for critical Unmanned Aerial Vehicle operations. 
   * $10 million for the KC-135 Global Air Traffic Management (GATM) program. These funds will be used to accelerate completion of the program. 
   KANSAS STATE
   * $2.5 million for Biosecurity Research for Soldier Food Safety. This is a collaborative project between U.S. Army Natick Soldier Systems Center and KSU's Biosecurity Research Institute that develops technologies that rapidly and reliably detect food contaminants, ultimately having a positive impact on soldier health, performance, and effectiveness. 
   * $2.9 million for Neutralizing Facility Threats with Novel Technology. This project funds operationally oriented research, education, and testing to develop novel technology, and technology applications, including reactive nanoparticle, acoustic and other means to neutralize facility threats. 
   * $5.5 million for the Marine Corps Urban Operations Environmental Laboratory. These funds will provide expanded assessment, analysis, neutralization, and development of capabilities to ensure minimum environmental and collateral damage with nontraditional and traditional capabilities used in urban and other missions and activities wherever environmental impact is a concern. 
   * $2.6 million for the Urban Operations Laboratory Weaponization program to provide expanded research, testing and evaluation. This includes research, testing and evaluation of concepts of development and employment, more powerful candidate systems for non-lethal and scalable responses, and capabilities suitable for scalable responses and for use in urban environments. 
   UNIVERSITY OF KANSAS
   * $12 million for SensorNet, a chemical and biological agent sensor network. SensorNet supports the real-time detection, identification, and assessment of chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear threats to the United States. The University of Kansas is contributing to the development of a rail test bed in Kansas City. 
   PITTSBURG STATE 
   * $2.5 million for Multifunctional Materials for Naval Structures at Pittsburg State University. These funds are used to study radar transparent, flame retardant, corrosion resistant, impact resistant and self-repairing advanced structural materials at Pittsburg State University.
   WICHITA STATE UNIVERSITY
   * $4 million for Aging Military Aircraft Fleet Support. This funds technical personnel, facilities and equipment to support and enhance the Department of Defense mission in maintaining the aging fleet of military aircraft. The Aging Aircraft Research Laboratory of the National Institute for Aviation Research at Wichita State University is involved in several US Air Force programs, assisting with investigations for maintaining the structural integrity and airworthiness of several aging aircraft fleets. 
   * $3 million for the Next Generation Ice Protection System for UAV's. These funds will address current UAV in-flight, ice protection shortfalls. Research contributing to this project is conducted at Wichita State University.
   WICHITA
   * $483.9 million for the Airborne Laser (ABL) Missile Defense System. Boeing conducts significant work on the ABL at their Wichita facility. 
   * $333.3 million for the Joint Primary Aircraft Training System (JPATS) for the Air Force. Raytheon Aircraft, Wichita, manufactures the JPATS aircraft (T-6A), utilized in the initial phase of military pilot training.
   * $964 million for the Multi-mission Maritime Aircraft (MMA). Boeing is an important partner in the MMA program. 
   * $562.16 million for UH-60 'Black Hawk' helicopters for the Army. Plastic Fabricating Company and Excel Manufacturing Company, Wichita, manufacture components for the UH-60 helicopter. 
   * $8Olathe.5 million for the procurement of GL-1800AP Truck Mounted Aircraft Deicers for the Air Force. The equipment is provided by Global Ground Support of Olathe.
   OTHER
   * $1 million for the Parents as Teachers program. The program provides assistance to military families by providing instructional assistance to parents of pre-school children. The program is currently in place at both Fort Riley and Fort Leavenworth.
