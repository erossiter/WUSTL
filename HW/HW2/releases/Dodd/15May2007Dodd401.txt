CHAIRMAN DODD ANNOUNCES BANKING COMMITTEE MARK UP THIS WEEK
  Sen. Chris Dodd (D-CT), Chairman of the Senate Committee on Banking, Housing and Urban Affairs, today announced that the Committee will meet in executive session this week to mark up six bills and vote on pending nominations. 
   The executive session will take place Wednesday, May 16, at 9:30 a.m., in Room SD-538 Dirksen Senate Office Building. There will be a press availability immediately following.
   The Committee will meet in executive session to mark up the following bills:
   * The Foreign Investment and National Security Act of 2007
   * The International Emergency Economic Powers Enforcement Act of 2007
   * H.R. 1675, the Preservation Approval Process Improvement Act of 2007
   * H.R. 1676, the Native American Home Ownership Opportunity Act of 2007
   * S. 254, a bill to award posthumously a Congressional gold medal to Constantino Brumidi
   * An original bill to make technical corrections to Title III of SAFETEA-LU
   The Committee will also vote on the nominations of Mr. David George Nason, of Rhode Island, to be Assistant Secretary of the Treasury for Financial Institutions; Mr. Mario Mancuso, of New York, to be Under Secretary of Commerce for Export Administration; Mr. Michael W. Tankersley, of Texas, to be Inspector General of the Export-Import Bank of the United States; Mr. Robert M. Couch, of Alabama, to be General Counsel of the Department of Housing and Urban Development; Ms. Janis Herschkowitz, of Pennsylvania, to be a Member of the Board of Directors of the National Consumer Cooperative Bank; Dr. Nguyen Van Hanh, of California, to be a Member of the Board of Directors of the National Consumer Cooperative Bank; and Mr. David George Nason, of Rhode Island, to be a Member of the Board of Directors of the National Consumer Cooperative Bank.
