                                                                         News Room News Releases          Language provides justice for Marine families                       Washington  D C    Monday  October 1  2007                       Today the United States Senate approved a provision  originally entitled the Justice for Victims of State Sponsored Terrorism Act  S 1944   as part of the Defense Department Authorization Bill of 2007  The provision  originally sponsored by Senators Arlen Specter  R Pa  and Frank Lautenberg  D N J    makes exceptions to the Foreign Sovereign Immunities Act to allow victims of state sponsored terrorism to bring a private suit against the sponsoring foreign terrorist government  This provision allows for state lawsuits and creates a federal action that can be used against state sponsors of terrorism     On October 23  2004  in Philadelphia  I was privileged to take part in a memorial service held in honor of the servicemen killed in the 1983 Beirut attack  and the family members comments about how they had been denied the ability to seek legal redress were both poignant and persuasive  Specter said  It is vitally important that these victims and their families have a day in court and the ability to recover damages from terrorist states that commit  direct  or materially support terrorist acts against American citizens or nationals    The passage of this legislation is of critical importance to the 18 Pennsylvania families of the 241 U S  Marines who were killed or injured in the 1983 barracks bombing in Beirut  Pennsylvania plaintiffs in the case of Peterson v  Islamic Republic of Iran will now be able to collect damages from Iran that they were previously not able to under state law
