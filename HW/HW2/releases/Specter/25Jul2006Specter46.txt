    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding Approved in the Fiscal Year 2007 LHHS Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Tuesday  July 25  2006     U S  Senators Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Arlen Specter  R PA   Chairman of the Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education  announced today the U S  Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for several southeastern Pennsylvania healthcare projects   The projects are contained in the Fiscal Year 2007 Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations bill     I applaud my colleagues in the Committee for approving the Health and Human Services funding for projects in southeastern Pennsylvania     Senator Santorum said     The funding will allow communities to purchase new equipment  construct new health care facilities  and provide the most advanced health care modernizations which will aid in the health and wellness of the citizens of southeastern Pennsylvania         I am pleased my colleagues have approved this important funding for southeastern Pennsylvania     Senator Specter said     These projects are vital to ensuring quality primary and emergency care  as well as advanced preventative care measures  for the area  s residents      The bill will now await action on the Senate floor   The projects in the bill include         800 000 for Albert Einstein Healthcare Network  AEHN  in Philadelphia County to expand genetic testing and education outreach for Jewish genetic diseases          800 000 for Temple University Health System in Philadelphia County for renovation and upgrades of Temple University s Hospital         800 000 for University of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia County to establish a National Center for Translational Therapeutics         600 000 for Fox Chase Cancer Center in Philadelphia County to purchase a NMR Spectrometer for cancer researchers to understand the function of proteins and nucleic acids at the atomic level         500 000 for Temple University  School of Medicine in Philadelphia to establish a Center for Minority Health         500 000 for Thomas Jefferson University in Philadelphia County for the purchase of a Fourier Transform Ion Cyclotron Resonance Mass Spectrometer  FTICR MS          500 000 for Wills Eye Hospital in Philadelphia County for creating  equipping  and staffing their Patient Clinic         450 000 for Thomas Jefferson University Hospital in Philadelphia County for development of the Jefferson Breast Care Center as a national model for       comprehensive multidisciplinary  and patient focused care consolidating all breast care services in one location         250 000 for Mercy Health System in Montgomery County to enhance the Picture Archiving Communication System  PACS  currently in operation at its acute care facilities         250 000 for Nazareth Hospital in Philadelphia County for stroke prevention programs in the African American community         250 000 for Holy Redeemer Health System in Philadelphia County for construction of a senior center in NE Philadelphia and Eastern Montgomery County         100 000 for Philadelphia Department of Public Health in Philadelphia County for pandemic flu preparedness         100 000 for National Nursing Centers Consortium in Philadelphia County for a Lead Safe Babies Program         100 000 for The Doe Fund  Inc  in Philadelphia County to provide
