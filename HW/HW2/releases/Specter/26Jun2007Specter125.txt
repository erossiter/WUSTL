    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases                               Washington  D C    Tuesday  June 26  2007                      Today  U S  Senator Arlen Specter  R Pa   ranking member of the Senate Judiciary Committee  spoke on the Senate floor regarding Employee Free Choice Act                In voting to impose cloture  to cut off debate  on the Card Check legislation  Senator Specter emphasized that his vote was on procedure  not the underlying substance or merits of the bill  in order to allow the Senate to consider serious deficiencies in existing labor law               In a twenty four page written statement  Senator Specter cited 18 specific cases showing improper practices by both labor and employers   Those cases illustrated the sharp infighting between labor and management on union elections  with the NLRB being totally ineffective  really dysfunctional  in failing to provide a forum to resolve those disputes with delays ranging from five to twelve years which made the issues moot by the time they were decided    Senator Specter urged the Senate to consider the Canadian experience where five of ten provinces have abandoned the secret ballot in favor of the card check   He noted that Canada has expedited procedures where elections were held in five to ten days of the filing of a petition               He called for bipartisanship  citing then Senator John F  Kennedy  s statement in 1959 on a labor bill  and noted that major labor legislation had been enacted in the past on voice votes
