    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding Approved in the Fiscal Year 2007 LHHS Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Tuesday  July 25  2006     U S  Senators Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Arlen Specter  R PA   Chairman of the Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education  announced today the U S  Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for several Allentown Reading area healthcare projects   The projects are contained in the Fiscal Year 2007 Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations bill     I applaud my colleagues in the Committee for approving the Health and Human Services funding for projects in the Lehigh Valley     Senator Santorum said     The funding will allow communities to purchase new equipment  construct new health care facilities  and provide the most advanced health care modernizations which will aid in the health and wellness of the citizens of the Lehigh Valley         I am pleased my colleagues have approved this important funding for the Lehigh Valley     Senator Specter said     These projects are vital to ensuring quality primary and emergency care  as well as advanced preventative care measures  for the area  s residents      The bill will now await action on the Senate floor   The projects in the bill include         450 000 for Sacred Heart Hospital in Lehigh County for a surgical procedures suite         450 000 for Pocono Medical Center in Monroe County to expand the Dale and Frances Hughes Cancer Center         300 000 for The Reading Hospital and Medical Center in Berks County to construct a new  sixty thousand square foot School of Health Sciences   The new three story  state of the art building will house instructional classrooms and a 300 seat auditorium  various science  skills and computer labs         250 000 for Good Shepherd Rehabilitation Hospital in Lehigh County for medical equipment in the inpatient Rehabilitation Hospital and outpatient Ambulatory Care Center  which will house three Centers of Excellence  Rehabilitation   Assistive Technology  Neurorehabilitation and Pain Management                                                                 Browse by
