                                                                         News Room News Releases          Grant will help Youthful Offenders Earn Degrees and Jobs                       Washington  D C    Thursday  November 1  2007                       U S  Senator Arlen Specter  a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that the Department of Labor has awarded a  1 million competitive grant to the School District of Philadelphia for their Open Door School Project  a program designed to help youthful offenders earn high school diplomas and connect with employers     I am pleased the Department of Labor has awarded this grant to the School District of Philadelphia  said Specter  These resources will enable the School District to address the issues of crime and dropout rates among students by providing youth who have been involved in the criminal justice system with the guidance and support to become productive and engaged members of society     We are grateful to Senator Arlen Specter and the U S  Department of Labor for providing the District with a crucial grant to launch the Districts new Open Door School Project  an accelerated academic program for students who are returning from out of home placement in juvenile detention  said Tom Brady  Interim CEO of the School District of Philadelphia  These students are a top priority as we address the dropout crisis in Philadelphia     The federal funding is part of the Department of Labors 2007 Youthful Offender Grants  a competitive grant program that seeks to advance the long term career prospects of juvenile offenders by improving both their educational achievement and employment outcomes  Today  the Department of Labor will announce  20 million in grant money to organizations across the nation  the School District of Philadelphia is the only Pennsylvania recipient     In June  Senator Specter secured  4 8 million in federal funding for the School District Philadelphia to reduce youth involvement in gangs as part of the Fiscal Year 2007 Appropriations Bill  Additionally  Senator Specter recently secured  1 million in federal funding in the Fiscal Year 2008 Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations bill for the recruitment  placement  and oversight of school based mentoring programs  This project is to be implemented by the United Way of Southeastern Pennsylvania and Big Brothers and Big Sisters of Southeastern Pennsylvania in collaboration with the Philadelphia Public Schools  The funding is pending final approval from Congress and the President
