    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases                               Washington  D C    Friday  December 8  2006     Senator Arlen Specter  a member of the Homeland Security Appropriations Subcommittee  announced today that the Department of Homeland Security has approved funding for fire companies in Pennsylvania       Pennsylvania fire services must be staffed at full capacity in order to adequately respond to an emergency situation in which people  s lives may be at risk     Senator Specter said     I applaud the Department of Homeland Security for providing this funding to help ensure that our Pennsylvania firefighting operations are functioning at their maximum potential      These grants are the tenth round of Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response  SAFER  grants from the Office of State and Local Government Coordination and Preparedness and the United States Fire Administration  s Assistance to Firefighter Grant Program     This program is designed to assist fire departments and volunteer firefighter interest organizations to increase the number of firefighters and enhance their ability to ensure that their communities have adequate protection from fire and fire related hazards   These five year awards will help fire departments attain 24 hour staffing and meet minimum firefighter standards   SAFER grants will fund the hiring of firefighters and the recruitment and retention of volunteer firefighters     Funding has been approved for the following company in Pennsylvania          156 000 for Upper Gwynedd Township Fire Department in Montgomery County        20 000 for Southern Manheim Township Fire Company in Lancaster County   Grant recipients can visit the AFG website link
