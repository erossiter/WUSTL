    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding is Part of the FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Friday  June 17  2005     Senator Arlen Specter  a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  and Senator Rick Santorum  Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  announced today that the Appropriations Committee has approved federal funding for Pennsylvania projects contained in the FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations bill    I am pleased my colleagues on the Appropriations Committee have provided this funding for important projects throughout Pennsylvania   Senator Specter said    These projects will enhance the lives of community residents in a variety of ways  including making the Port of Philadelphia competitive with other ports  protecting residents from floods and preserving our state landmarks     I commend the Appropriations Committee for recognizing the needs associated with these infrastructure projects in Pennsylvania   said Senator Santorum    This funding constitutes an investment toward environmental preservation and economic prosperity of the Commonwealth  including the Ports of Pittsburgh and Philadelphia    The FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill is pending further action by the full Senate and the House of Representatives  followed by the President s signature    Projects contained in today s announcement include    X                46 million for Locks and Dams 2  3  and 4  Monongahela River in Allegheny County for rehabilitation   The nearly century old locks and dams are the last of the old and undersized locks on the Monongahela River system and are structurally unstable   There is a substantial chance that important structural components of these facilities could fail at any time which could devastate water supplies to communities and vital industries  as well as stalling commerce through the Port of Pittsburgh    X  15 million for Emsworth Locks and Dams  Ohio River in Allegheny County for rehabilitation   The proposed project includes replacement of the dam gates  gate hoisting machinery  electrical power and distribution system and scour protection system      X  10 496 million for the Wyoming Valley Levee Raising Project  which has been ongoing since Tropical Storm Agnes caused widespread flooding in 1972   In January 1996  floods nearly breached the levee system  forcing emergency officials to evacuate more than 100 000 people  Hundreds of homes were destroyed and the community suffered tens of millions of dollars in damage     X  3 million for the Delaware River Main Channel Deepening Project   The existing 40 foot deep channel restricts the efficient movement of tankers  dry bulk carriers and container vessels   The 45 foot deepening project will result in transportation savings to commodities consisting of crude oil imports  iron ore imports  and scrap exports    X  2 55 million for the Upper Ohio River Navigation Study of Emsworth  Dashields  Montgomery Locks and Dams in Allegheny County to continue a long term feasibility study on the three aged structures    X  1 million for the Three Rivers Wet Weather Demonstration Program in Allegheny County to reroute streams from municipal sewers into restored natural channels in Sheraden Park in Pittsburgh and Stowe Township    X  820 000 for the Fairmount Dam Fishladder in Philadelphia County to increase efficiency by reconfiguring the structure to allow for passage of American Shad   The reconfiguration will provide access to a habitat that will spawn and rear Shad and other migratory fish on the Schuylkill River    X  620 000 for the Presque Isle Pennisula for maintenance of the beaches  periodic repair and replenishment of the beach sand    X  430 000 for the Upper Tioga River Watershed in Tioga County for a restoration report  which will identify the economic benefits of restoring the Upper Tioga River Watershed for local  state and national interests      X  300 000 for the Neshannock Creek Emergency Stream Bank Protection project in the City of New Castle  Pennsylvania to protect its main sewer line which is threatened by erosion   This work is needed to protect New Castle        s public infrastructure from damage caused by erosion    X  250 000 for the Ingham Spring Dame and Lake Reconstruction Project in Bucks County to begin environmental restoration  preservation and reconstruction of a 19th Century earthen dam and related
