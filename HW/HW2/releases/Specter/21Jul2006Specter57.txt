                                                                         News Room News Releases          Funding Approved in the FY07 TTHUD Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Friday  July 21  2006     U S  Senators Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Arlen Specter  R PA   a senior member of the U S  Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that U S  Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for several transportation projects in the Lehigh Valley   The projects are contained in the FY07 Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary  Housing and Urban Development and Related Agencies Appropriations bill     I am pleased that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding to expand and improve our roadways and mass transit infrastructure     Senator Santorum said    It is important that the Lehigh Valley continuously maintain and modernize transportation in order to ensure the safe travel of its citizens  alleviate congestion  create more efficient means for transportation  and sustain a robust economy        I am pleased that my colleagues on the Appropriations Committee have recognized the importance of these transportation projects in Pennsylvania     said Senator Specter     These projects are necessary advancements to the state  s infrastructure and an important force for economic growth in our local communities      The bill will now await action on the Senate floor   The projects in the bill include         1 million for the City of Reading Streetscape Improvements   Museum Road is an important gateway to the metropolitan area  while North Thirteenth Street is a busy street lined by Reading School District facilities ranging from kindergarten through high school  as well as Albright College  a private liberal arts college   These resurfacing projects will help complete efforts the City began in 2006         1 million for the Schuylkill Valley MetroRail   The Schuylkill Valley MetroRail  SVM  project is a 74 mile rail line between Philadelphia and Wyomissing Reading  Pennsylvania  with 34 proposed stations including 52nd Street  Conshohocken  Norristown  Valley Forge  King of Prussia  Phoenixville  Royersford  Pottstown and Douglassville         1 million for the City of Easton  s 13th Street Interstate 22 Ramp Repair and Safety   The well traveled exit serves as the primary entrance to and exit from the western portion of the community  and is utilized by both residential and commercial industrial users   Little more than minor repairs have occurred in the fifty years since the structure was built  and as surrounding land uses and travel demands have changed the interchange and adjoining intersection have become extremely dangerous         500 000 for the American Parkway Bridge in Allentown  which consists of a four lane urban arterial highway with a signature bridge over the Lehigh River  connecting Center City Allentown with U S  Route 22   This funding will be used for corridor improvements and design of the bridge structure                                                                 Browse by
