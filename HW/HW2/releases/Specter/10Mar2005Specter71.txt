    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases                               Washington  D C    Thursday  March 10  2005                      U S  Senator Arlen Specter  R PA   Chairman of the Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations Subcommittee  today introduced legislation that reaffirms guaranteed health benefits for coal miners and their families  a commitment stipulated in the Coal Act of 1992      I am very closely aware of the effect of this legislation on 14 000 retired coal miners and their dependents in Pennsylvania and over 60 000 individuals nationally     Senator Specter said     These health benefits form a central underpinning for the medical care structure of the coal field community   I urge my colleagues to support this legislation to ensure that the promise made to our miners is not revoked      The legislation  introduced today  clarifies the lack of authority the bankruptcy court has to modify or terminate statutory obligations under Section 9711 of the Coal Act  The Coal Act of 1992 applied to a fixed pool of coal miners in every state except Hawaii that was closed as of 1994   The Act mandated coal operators to fulfill their promise to provide their employees and families health benefits and stipulated that those obligations could not be modified     Senator Specter  s legislation was prompted by an August 2004 bankruptcy court decision in which the court concluded that Section 1114 trumped the Coal Act  exempting Horizon Natural Resources from paying benefits to over 3 000 miners  retirees  and their families     Senator Specter is the original cosponsor of legislation introduced in the 108th Congress that would have reinforced the statutory requirements of
