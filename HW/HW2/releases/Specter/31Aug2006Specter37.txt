    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases                               Washington  D C    Thursday  August 31  2006     U S  Senators Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Arlen Specter  R PA   a senior member of the U S  Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that the U S  Department of Education has selected two Pennsylvania organizations to receive federal funding under the Early Reading First Program   This funding is expected to benefit nearly 500 students in Pennsylvania   The following organizations have each been awarded a 3 year grant         1 887 754 for the Heritage Health Foundation of Braddock  PA       3 080 059 for the Lancaster Lebanon Intermediate Unit 13   Instructional Services   Community Education of East Petersburg  PA     The Early Reading First program helps to provide a solid educational foundation for Pennsylvania  s youth  which is why I believe this program is vitally important     said Senator Santorum     I am so pleased that these two organizations have received funding to help in their efforts to ensure that all preschool age children are taught the necessary skills to succeed in their early education         I am pleased to see that the Department of Education has awarded this important funding to Early Reading First programs in Pennsylvania     Senator Specter said     This money will help early education centers provide students  many of which come from low income families  with the foundation they need to succeed in school and become productive members of society      The Early Reading First program provides grants to support local efforts to ensure that all children enter kindergarten with the necessary language  cognitive  and early reading skills for continued success in school   The Early Reading First initiative is designed to transform existing early education programs into centers of excellence that provide high quality  early education to preschool age children  particularly those from low income families                                                                 Browse by
