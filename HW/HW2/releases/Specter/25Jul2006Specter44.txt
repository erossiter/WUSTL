    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding Approved in the Fiscal Year 2007 LHHS Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Tuesday  July 25  2006     U S  Senators Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Arlen Specter  R PA   Chairman of the Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education  announced today that U S  Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for several northeastern Pennsylvania education projects   The projects are contained in the FY07 Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations bill     This funding will help to maintain and increase the high standards set in education throughout Pennsylvania     said Senator Santorum     The world of education is moving forward at a rapid pace with new technology being introduced into classrooms virtually everyday   With this funding  the goal is to provide all Pennsylvanians with a modern  quality education        I am pleased my colleagues have approved funding for these important northeastern Pennsylvania projects     Senator Specter said     Education is the single greatest capital investment we can make in our nation  s future  and this funding will provide resources that will empower students to become more productive members of society  live happier lives and make incredible contributions to our country      The bill will now await action on the Senate floor   Education projects approved in the bill include          100 000 for King s College in Luzerne County to develop the Center for Community Engagement and Learning to facilitate community access to college resources  while offering students  faculty  and staff opportunities for service learning  King s College will partner with charitable organizations to promote a campus wide culture of community engagement         100 000 for Wilkes University in Luzerne County to support program and curriculum development and equipment acquisition related to the University s Institute for the Environment   The Institute will provide a research and mentoring based learning community for its students         100 000 for Lackawanna College in Lackawanna County to provide training to area residents in skills required to qualify as Computerized Numerical Controls Machinists         100 000 for Scranton School District in Lackawanna County to develop foreign language  arts and humanities curriculum aligned to academic standards and provide training for teachers to use this curriculum   The goal of this initiative is to ensure rigorous courses and consistency in teaching foreign languages  arts and humanities across the district         100 000 for University of Scranton in Lackawanna County to promote health education through the Center for Global Health  Education  and Rehabilitation
