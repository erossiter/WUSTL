    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Would Provide Relief to Millions of Middle Class Taxpayers                       Washington  D C     Thursday  August 2  2007       Related Resources     DOC  31 0 KBs          Today  Senator Arlen Specter  ranking member of the Senate Judiciary Committee  offered an amendment to the Small Business Tax Relief Bill  H R  976   that would provide permanent relief to the rising number of middle class taxpayers impacted by the Alternative Minimum Tax  AMT   The Specter amendment would repeal an AMT tax rate hike imposed in 1993  Combined with the continued extension of the AMT exemption  this amendment would restore the original intention of the AMT     This amendment would eliminate the 1993 alternative minimum tax rate increase  a remedial step which I suggest to my colleagues is long overdue  Specter said on the Senate floor     The amendment  which was defeated on budgetary point of order by a vote of 47 52  is the most recent of Senator Specters efforts to provide relief to taxpayers affected by the AMT  On March 1  2007  Senator Specter introduced the AMT Rate Reduction Act of 2007  which has been referred to the Senate Finance Committee     The number of taxpayers subject to AMT liability has risen sharply over recent years because of a failure to index for inflation and AMT rates being higher relative to the regular income tax system  Absent congressional action  it is estimated that the number of taxpayers subject to the AMT will jump from 3 5 million in 2006 to 23 million in 2007  with middle income taxpayers bearing the biggest brunt  In Pennsylvania alone  837 000 Pennsylvanians will be forced to file their returns under the AMT in 2007     The AMT was created in 1969 in response to a small number of high income individuals who paid little or no federal income taxes  However  as a result of inflation and bracket creep  average American families are now affected by the tax  For example  taxpayers filing joint returns with no dependents will be subject to the AMT starting at income levels of  75 386  and large families at income levels as low as  49 438    Attached is a copy of the Senators statement as entered into the record                                                                  Browse by
