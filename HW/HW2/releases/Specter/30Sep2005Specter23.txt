    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding is Part of the FY06 Defense Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Friday  September 30  2005     Senator Arlen Specter  a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  and Senator Rick Santorum  Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  announced today that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for defense related projects in Central Pennsylvania   The funding is contained in the Fiscal Year 2006 Defense Appropriations bill  which must be passed by both chambers of Congress and signed by the President    A number of Pennsylvania companies are enhancing the way our Armed Forces operate   Senator Specter said    These projects will go far in supporting the brave men and women who serve our country   I am pleased my colleagues in the Senate Appropriations Committee have recognized the valuable contribution these projects will make to our nation s defense     Pennsylvania plays a strong role in our national defense and I am proud of the people and organizations that help to make our state a top competitor for defense projects that create jobs and strengthen our economy   said Senator Santorum    These funds will allow Pennsylvania defense contractors to continue their efforts to achieve a more secure nation    Projects which have been approved to receive funding in Central Pennsylvania include      X  6 Million for Pennsylvania State University in Centre County for a Large Unmanned Undersea Vehicle  LUUV  Test Bed   The Navy needs unmanned systems to reduce the vulnerability of manned platforms and multiply their effectiveness   The LUUV will provide an off board  unmanned vehicle with a wider variety of capabilities that will be applicable to both guided submarines and littoral combat ships     X  6 Million for General Dynamics in York County for Mid Range Munition  MRM   CE  TM3   Funding will continue development of the Army  Mid Range Munition  MRM    The MRM will give the Army s current and future gun systems their first intelligent munition  providing accurate engagements against maneuvering and hidden targets  in all weather conditions   It is effective far beyond the range of today s conventional tank ammunition     X  5 million to JLG in Fulton County  for the All Terrain Lifter Army System  ATLAS  for the procurement of a self deployable rough terrain  manually operated forklift capable of operating efficiently in nuclear  biological  and chemical environments     X  4 million to the Pennsylvania National Guard for the Northeast Regional  Counterdrug Training Center   Established in 1998  the Northeast Counterdrug Training Center  NCTC  provides no cost counterdrug training and education to law enforcement and drug demand reduction specialists in 18 northeastern states     X  2 1 million to SAP for an Army Medical Department Enterprise Resource Planning project in Cumberland County   The U S  Army Medical Material Department  AMEDD  is charged with providing medical supplies to the Army worldwide   Funding will upgrade the AMEDD s medical support to combat forces by transitioning operationally critical medical logistics capabilities from the legacy Tactical Army Medical Material Information System  TAMMIS  into a newer Enterprise Resource Planning system   This transition will lead to quicker medical supply replenishment  lighter supply chain burdens  and greater responsiveness in treating wounded soldiers in the field
