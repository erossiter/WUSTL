    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding is Part of the Senate FY06 Defense Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Friday  September 30  2005     Senator Arlen Specter  a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  and Senator Rick Santorum  Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  announced today that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for defense related projects in Western Pennsylvania   The funding is contained in the Fiscal Year 2006 Defense Appropriations bill  which must be passed by both chambers of Congress and signed by the President     A number of Pennsylvania companies are enhancing the way our Armed Forces operate     Senator Specter said     These projects will go far in supporting the brave men and women who serve our country   I am pleased my colleagues in the Senate Appropriations Committee have recognized the valuable contribution these projects will make to our nation  s defense        Pennsylvania plays a strong role in our national defense and I am proud of the people and organizations that help to make our state a top competitor for defense projects that create jobs and strengthen our economy     said Senator Santorum     These funds will allow Pennsylvania defense contractors to continue their efforts to achieve a more secure nation      Projects which have been approved to receive funding in Western Pennsylvania include         5 million for L3 Brashear in Allegheny County for the XM25 Advanced Airburst Weapon System  which is capable of firing 25mm air bursting ammunition and will be part of the next generation of infantry rifle system  Modeling of the system under simulated combat conditions indicates a fivefold increase in lethality over the weapons in current use          3 5 million for National Tissue Engineering Center  NTEC  to fund the continuation of a broad based research agenda including cell based therapies  biomaterials  tissue engineering  medical devices  and artificial organs at the Pittsburgh Tissue Engineering Initiative          2 5 million to SiMMtec  Inc  for Solid State High Output Diode Arrays in Allison Park  PA  The SiMM diode array is a new device that demonstrates superior performance over previous diode array designs and addresses known shortcomings          2 million for the Soldier Treatment Regeneration Consortium  This consortium will employ regenerative medicine techniques toward the development of advanced treatments for military applications  It will unite the clinical and research facilities of the nation  s two leading regenerative medicine research institutes    the McGowan Institute for Regenerative Medicine  Pittsburgh  PA  and the Wake Forest University Regenerative Medicine Institute with the US Army  s Institute for Surgical Research based at Fort Sam Houston  the San Antonio based TRISAT trauma center collaborative  the Walter Reed Army Medical Center  and Tissue Genesis Inc   a developer of advanced bioreactor technology          2 million for Alcoa s Non line of Sight Cannon  NLOS C  and Mortar  NLOS M   The objective of the Alcoa Collaborative Development Approach for Non line of Sight Cannon  NLOS C  and Mortar  NLOS M  is to continue to develop  design  demonstrate  validate and implement a methodology that will enable incorporation of lightweight structural materials  optimized structural design and optimal integration of crew protection systems and structure  This program will cost effectively address DoD weight targets  performance requirements  and survivability criteria         1 5 million for Lord in Erie County for an In flight Propeller Balancing System  IPBS   which will develop and certify IPBS for the United States Air Force C 130 E and H series aircraft  Fleet implementation will decrease in flight vibration levels by simultaneously balancing the four propellers automatically  eliminate the maintenance cost of manual balancing by United States Air Force personnel  and provide increased aircraft availability and operational readiness          1 million for the University of Pittsburgh in Allegheny County for Vaccine Research in Biodefense and Emerging Infections Initiative  which will focus on the development of new vaccines to protect against biological agents which pose a health risk to the U S  population and are most easily weaponized for acts of bioterrorism          1 million for Trident Systems in Fayette County for Naval Special Warfare UUV Sensors and C2 STTR  This funding will facilitate the development of the system architecture for small Unmanned Undersea Vehicles  UUV  capable of air delivery into minced littoral waters          1 million for Eye See 360  Inc  in Pittsburgh for the Advanced Panoramic Sensor Systems to develop advanced visual imaging technologies and related sensor and computing technologies for unmanned aerial vehicles  UAVs  with the Office of Naval Research          1 million for ALung Technologies Intravenous Membrane Oxygenator for research  development and clinical studies of the IMO  The technology could provide a valuable instrument for treating battlefield casualties of chemical and biological warfare  including inhalation anthrax                                                                 Browse by
