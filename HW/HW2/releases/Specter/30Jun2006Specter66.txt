    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding is Part of the Fiscal Year 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill                         Friday  June 30  2006     U S  Senator Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and U S  Senator Arlen Specter  R PA   a Senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved federal funding for Pennsylvania projects contained in the FY 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations bill     Clean  efficient and reliable energy production is essential to strengthening our economy and fostering greater economic development in Pennsylvania     said Senator Santorum     This funding supports the innovation of Pennsylvania companies that are committed to finding and developing new and sustainable technologies to meet our energy demands well into the 21st century        I am pleased my colleagues have approved funding for these important Pennsylvania projects     Senator Specter said     The research these projects yield will result in cleaner and more efficient energy production  and will increase our productivity and competitiveness in the global market                        The Fiscal Year 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations bill will now await action by the full Senate   Funding has been approved for programs that will support the following projects  the funding for which would be allocated through the Department of Energy  DOE                           5 million for AEA Technologies in Allegheny County to provide technology to support the Memorandum of Understanding between the United States Department of Energy and the United Kingdom Department of Trade and Industry for cleanup in the energy and environmental arena  such as waste management  decommissioning  renewable energy  energy efficiency and remote handling                           2 million for Ion Transport Membrane  ITM  Syngas Project  Air Products and Chemicals  Inc   in Lehigh County to fund technologies to produce synthesis gas  hydrogen and carbon monoxide  from natural gas in a single step                              1 million for a Biodiesel Injection Blending Facilities Project at Independence Biofuels  in Dauphin County to develop  construct and operate biodiesel injection blending facilities across Pennsylvania that will increase the use of this clean  domestically produced  renewable fuel for vehicles and home heating                          1 million for Children  s Hospital of Philadelphia to help implement Magnetoencephalography  MEG  technology that provides 4 dimensional imaging of human brain function   CHOP is developing the world s first dedicated infant MEG system and one of only two MEG systems located in U S  pediatric hospitals                          750 000 for Siemens Westinghouse in Allegheny County for research and development of Solid Oxide Fuel Cells  Solid Oxide Fuel Cells are expected to play a major role the development of distributed energy generation systems in the future because of their low emissions  small size  high efficiencies and ease of siting                                                                 Browse by
