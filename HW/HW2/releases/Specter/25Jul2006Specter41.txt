    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding Approved in the Fiscal Year 2007 LHHS Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Tuesday  July 25  2006     U S  Senators Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Arlen Specter  R PA   Chairman of the Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education  announced today that U S  Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for several Allentown area education projects   The projects are contained in the FY07 Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations bill     This funding will help to maintain and increase the high standards set in education throughout Pennsylvania     said Senator Santorum      The world of education is moving forward at a rapid pace with new technology being introduced into classrooms virtually everyday   With this funding  the goal is to provide all Pennsylvanians with a modern  quality education        I am pleased my colleagues have approved funding for these important Lehigh Valley projects     Senator Specter  said     Education is the single greatest capital investment we can make in our nation  s future  and this funding will provide resources that will empower students to become more productive members of society  live happier lives and make incredible contributions to our country      The bill will now await action on the Senate floor   Education projects approved in the bill include         100 000 for Muhlenberg College in Lehigh County for a Public Health education program that will allow pre med students to assist in the dissemination of healthcare information and advise on healthcare issues         100 000 for Cedar Crest College in Lehigh County for continued development of a nursing education program  including support for clinical courses  Cedar Crest College will use this funding for faculty development and further enhancement of its baccalaureate and graduate nursing program         100 000 for Center for Advancing Partnerships in Education  CAPE  in Lehigh County to provide PA school districts with the capacity to utilize a highly specialized Internet connection  Internet 2  through a mobile broadband satellite   This technology will enable students to interface with institutions worldwide through digital instruction         100 000 for Da Vinci Discovery Center of Science and Technology in Lehigh County to create a professional development partnership for area teachers between the Center  Cedar Crest College  and the Allentown School District to increase K 12 student achievement in the sciences  with a focus on secondary science education         100 000 for Lehigh Carbon Community College in Lehigh County to develop a PA Academy of Arts  Sciences and Advanced Technologies  which will provide career education in science and technology to students   Students will achieve through involvement in hands on activities  exposure to undergraduate science and technology students  instruction by science and technology faculty and involvement with industry professionals           100 000 for Moravian College in Northampton County for equipment  technology  and curriculum development as part of a natural science initiative  The goal of the initiative is to increase the quality of graduates in math  computer science  physics  biochemistry  chemistry and biology         100 000 for East Stroudsburg University in Monroe County to develop the  Reaching Out for New Science and Mathematics Students Program   that will engage at risk and minority students and their teachers in standards based science and mathematics instruction  Students in grades 6 9 will be offered a campus based academic experience and expose them to university faculty instruction         100 000 for Roberto Clemente Charter School in Lehigh County to enable economically disadvantaged high school students to prepare for careers in nursing and other healthcare professions                                                                 Browse by
