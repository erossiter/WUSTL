    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding Approved in the Fiscal Year 2007 LHHS Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Tuesday  July 25  2006     U S  Senators Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Arlen Specter  R PA   Chairman of the Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education  announced today the U S  Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for several northeastern Pennsylvania healthcare projects   The projects are contained in the Fiscal Year 2007 Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations bill     I applaud my colleagues in the Committee for approving the Health and Human Services funding for projects in northeastern Pennsylvania     Senator Santorum said     The funding will allow communities to purchase new equipment  construct new health care facilities  and provide the most advanced health care modernizations which will aid in the health and wellness of the citizens of northeastern Pennsylvania         I am pleased my colleagues have approved this important funding for northeastern Pennsylvania     Senator Specter said     These projects are vital to ensuring quality primary and emergency care  as well as advanced preventative care measures  for the area  s residents      The bill will now await action on the Senate floor   Northeastern Pennsylvania healthcare projects in the bill include         300 000 for Bloomsburg Hospital in Columbia County for renovation that includes a redesign of the ER and patient areas to better maintain patient privacy and confidentiality during consultation  registration and treatment  and an information systems upgrade to significantly improve patient flow         300 000 for Barnes Kasson County Hospital in Susquehanna County for  Prenatal and Women  s Healthcare Education         250 000 for Allied Services Foundation in Lackawanna County for pediatric health programs         250 000 for Community Medical Center Healthcare System in Lackawanna County for improvements of information technology to assure patient safety and health care outcomes are maximized         250 000 for Mercy Health Partners in Lackawanna County to integrate evidence based knowledge for both nursing and interdisciplinary care teams into Mercy  s clinical information systems         250 000 for Moses Taylor Hospital in Lackawanna County to digitize its clinical health care information management process  providing clinicians with anytime  anywhere access to patient information           250 000 for Hazleton General Hospital in Luzerne County for a Bar Code Point of Care Bedside Verification System for all inpatient nursing floors that will improve the accuracy and efficiency of patient care         250 000 for Wyoming Valley Health Care System in Luzerne County for an Emergency Room Medical Record and Electronic Patient Tracking System         200 000 for College Misericordia in Luzerne County for equipment for Assistive Technology Research Institute within a new twenty first century Learning Center   This Center will add much needed assistive technology laboratory space  classroom  conference and seminar space supported by a full wireless infrastructure
