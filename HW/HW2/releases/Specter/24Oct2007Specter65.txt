    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases                               Washington  D C    Wednesday  October 24  2007                   Today  U S  Senator Arlen Specter  R Pa    ranking member of the Senate Judiciary Committee  commended the Senates approval of Judge Leslie Southwick appointment to the U S  Court of Appeals for the 5th Circuit  The Senate confirmed Judge Southwicks nomination by a vote of 59 38    Below is a copy of Senator Specters statement at a press conference following the successful vote     Our Leader  Senator McConnell  has said it all  but as the saying goes  that wont stop the rest of us from repeating it     It is a very significant vote  We had a very contentious time going back two decades to the last two years of President Reagans term  the last two of President George H W  Bush  During the Clinton administration  there was regrettable reciprocity and we came very close to destroying one of the fundamental principles of the Senate  unlimited debate  with a so called nuclear constitutional option and we worked through that     This is very significant  Its a real mark of bipartisanship to get 62 Senators to vote for cloture  that counts the 13 Democrats  I begin by thanking the 13 Democrats  especially Senator Dianne Feinstein who provided the critical vote  a real profile in courage in committee  Without Senator Feinsteins vote  we would not have gotten to the cloture vote     I think it is a very significant sign for the American people who are sick and tired of the bickering and the partisanship  This is a big step on a new page  This may be out of precedent without calling on the home state Senators but if I may with the concurrence of the home state Senators yield to the heroine  the lady of the day Senator Feinstein                                                                    Browse by
