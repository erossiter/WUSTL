    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases                               Washington  D C    Thursday  August 25  2005     U S  Senator Arlen Specter  a senior member of the Transportation Appropriations Subcommittee  and U S  Senator Rick Santorum  Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference announced today that the United States Department of Transportation has awarded two small Pennsylvania airports over  700 000 in federal funding to increase commercial air service in their areas   The Department of Transportation awarded Small Community Air Service Development Program  SCASDP  grants in the amount of  500 000 for Williamsport  PA and  220 000 for Bradford  PA       Increasing commercial air service at these two locations has the potential to yield tremendous economic benefits     Senator Specter said     I applaud the Department of Transportation for providing the Williamsport and Bradford airports with a promising opportunity to increase their services and ridership        I am pleased that Williamsport and Bradford have received this important funding from the Department of Transportation     said Senator Santorum     Not only will this funding expand commercial air service in these regions  but it will also have a significant economic impact as ridership and airport services increase      Williamsport will use the funding to recruit a second carrier to the community   Bradford will use the funding to institute a comprehensive marketing and promotion campaign to increase ridership in the air service provided by Colgan Air to Pittsburgh under the Essential Air Service program   The grants  appropriated a total of  20 million by Congress for fiscal year 2005  encourage commercial air service in some of America  s smaller communities   This year  s determination was especially competitive with 80 applicants  of which only 37 were chosen in the country
