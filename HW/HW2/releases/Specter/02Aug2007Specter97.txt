    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases                               Washington  D C    Thursday  August 2  2007     U S  Senator Arlen Specter  R Pa   ranking member of the Senate Judiciary Committee  today applauded the favorable committee action taken on the nomination of Judge Leslie Southwick to the U S  Court of Appeals for the 5th Circuit  Judge Southwick was voted out of committee on a 10 9 vote     Its a direct matter  the qualifications of the nominee were analyzed and evaluated and acted on  Specter said  Judge Southwick is really a poster board model of judicial confidence  and really  heroism  having gone to Iraq in his fifties  as a volunteer Judge Advocate General     Southwicks distinguished legal career includes 11 years as a Mississippi State appellate court judge  where he wrote nearly 1 000 opinions and participated in approximately 7 000 cases  In his forties  he sought an age waiver in order to join the Judge Advocate Generals Corps in the Army Reserve  In his fifties  he requested to be transferred to a line combat unit that anticipated deployment to Iraq  He took a leave from the bench to serve for a year and a half at forward operating bases in Iraq     The American Bar Association has rated Judge Southwick unanimously well qualified  and the Mississippi State Bar honored him in 2004 with its judicial excellence award  In 2006  the Senate Judiciary Committee unanimously recommended Judge Southwick to be a district court judge  but he did not receive a floor vote before the 109th congress adjourned  The President then nominated Judge Southwick to the appeals court in January of 2007     The nomination is now poised for a fair up or down vote on the floor
