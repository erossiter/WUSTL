    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding Is Included in the EPA Portion of the FY06 Interior Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Monday  August 1  2005     Senator Arlen Specter  a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  and Senator Rick Santorum  Chairman of the Republican Conference  announced that the Senate has approved federal funding for water projects in Pennsylvania   The funding is included in the Environmental Protection Agency portion of the FY2006 Interior Appropriations bill    I am pleased the Senate has approved funding for these vital improvements to Pennsylvania        s infrastructure   Senator Specter said    These projects ensure that residents in these areas have safe and sanitary water supplies     This funding provides the resources to restructure water treatment systems and make improvements to the existing water and sewer infrastructure in Pennsylvania   Senator Santorum stated    These are necessary upgrades that will ensure safe and environmentally responsible improvements are made to these systems  which frequently are contributing factors in flooding    The FY06 Interior Appropriations Bill was passed by the Senate Friday night    Funding has been approved for the following Pennsylvania projects     X  1 75 million for the Three Rivers Wet Weather Demonstration Program in Allegheny County to fix municipal sewers  protect our nation        s streams and augment the investment of local governments in compliance with the Clean Water Act     X  800 000 for the City of Plum Creek and Allegheny County for a combined sewer overflow project     X  750 000 for the Borough of Archbald for a sewer improvement project     X  695 000 for Philadelphia Southeastern Waterways Restoration Program for stormwater infrastructure improvements     X  500 000 for the City of Lancaster for water infrastructure improvements     X  500 000 to Penn State University for the Small Public Water System Technology Center in Harrisburg  Dauphin County to foster technology evaluation and training for operators  trainers  engineers  owners and managers of small water systems      X  250 000 for Millcreek Township in Erie County for storm sewer pipe construction     X  250 000 for Menallen Township in Fayette County for public sewer service extension     X  250 000 for Tulpehocken Township in Berks County for central sewer collection and treatment replacement      X  250 000 for the Borough of Pottstown for stormwater infrastructure improvements                                                                 Browse by
