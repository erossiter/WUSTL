                                                                         News Room News Releases          Funding is Part of the FY 2008 Defense Appropriations Conference Report                       Washington  D C    Friday  November 9  2007                       U S  Senators Arlen Specter  a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  and Bob Casey  the junior senator from Pennsylvania  announced today that the full U S  Congress has approved federal funding for several defense related projects in Pennsylvania as contained in the Fiscal Year 2008 Defense Appropriations Conference Report     I am pleased that my colleagues have recognized the valuable contribution these projects will make to our nations defense  said Specter  Pennsylvania is a top competitor for defense projects that create jobs and strengthen our economy and these funds will go far in supporting the brave men and women who serve our country     I am grateful Congress approved initiatives designed to strengthen our national defense and support our troops  said Casey  These projects will help protect and equip our men and women in uniform while helping to create jobs in Pennsylvania     The Conference Report must now be approved by President of the United States before funding is final    Funding has been approved for the following Pennsylvania defense projects      6 5 million for Letterkenny Army Depot  LEAD  in Franklin County to develop and test a safe recover  reuse and recycle technology to demilitarize munitions in an environmentally friendly manner  LEAD  the largest employer in Franklin County  would use the funding to develop state of the art procedures and demilitarize the munitions center s concentration of aging  multiple launched rocket systems  Currently  the only method available to do this is via open burn open detonation      4 million for the Pennsylvania National Guards Northeast Counter Drug Training Center in Lebanon County to provide training
