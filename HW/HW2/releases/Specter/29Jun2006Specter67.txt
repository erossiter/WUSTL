                                                                         News Room News Releases          In Response to 34 Counties Ravaged by Torrential Rainfall                      Washington  D C    Thursday  June 29  2006     Senator Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Senator Arlen Specter  R PA   the Senior Senator from Pennsylvania  today urged President Bush to provide federal assistance to Pennsylvania in response to severe flooding caused by torrential rainfall which has occurred over the past several days   The letter from the Senators today supplements Governor Rendell  s request that President Bush provide federal assistance for 34 of Pennsylvania  s sixty seven counties that suffered severe damage from the storm   By approving a Federal Disaster Declaration for Pennsylvania  President Bush enables affected residents to be eligible for federal assistance that can include grants to help pay for temporary housing  home repairs and other serious disaster related expenses   Low interest loans from the U S  Small Business Administration also will be available to cover residential and business losses not fully compensated by insurance   The Declaration would also make available funds for eligible public infrastructure  which can include damaged roads and bridges  water control facilities  parks and recreation areas and emergency services   A copy of the letter is attached   In addition  Senator Santorum today spoke to White House Chief of Staff Josh Bolten to emphasize the importance of providing this federal assistance to the affected areas in Pennsylvania    Senator Santorum visited Luzerne and Bucks Counties yesterday and is scheduled to tour flood damaged areas throughout Pennsylvania tomorrow and Saturday    Senator Specter is scheduled to tour flood damaged areas in Bucks County on Friday and flood damaged areas in Wilkes Barre next Thursday
