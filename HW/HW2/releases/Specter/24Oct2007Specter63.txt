                                                                         News Room News Releases          Encourages Agencies to Continue to Monitor and Study Affected Community                       Washington  D C    Wednesday  October 24  2007       Related Resources     PDF  204 5 KBs        U S  Senator Arlen Specter  the longest serving Senator from Pennsylvania  sent a letter to Dr  Howard Frumkin  Director of the Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry  ATSDR   and Dr  Calvin Johnson  Secretary of Pennsylvanias Department of Health  PA DOH   regarding the study on the incidence of polycythemia vera in Carbon  Luzerne and Schuylkill Counties     I am heartened by the studys findings that there are no environmental or occupational causes for the disease  however  the fact remains that there is a higher than usual number of cases for this community  Specter wrote in the October 22nd letter     Senator Specter participated in the October 2006 announcement of the study  a statistical analysis of regional health data  which sought to respond to concerns regarding the incidence of the rare blood cancer in the tri county region     Monday  upon learning of the studys results  Senator Specter urged the PA DOH and the ATSDR to continue to study and monitor the community  I encourage your agencies to partner with able academic institutions as necessary and know that I am committed to working to find adequate resources for further study    Text of the letter follows  October 22  2007   Dear Drs  Frumkin and Johnson     I appreciate the work that the Pennsylvania Department of Health  PA DOH  and the Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry  ATSDR  have done to review the incidence of polycythemia vera  PV  in the Carbon  Luzerne and Schuylkill tri county area  I am heartened by the studys findings that there are no environmental or occupational causes for the disease  however  the fact remains that there is a higher than usual number of cases for this community     As such  I urge the PA DOH and ATSDR to continue to study and monitor the cases that are reported  as well as to keep the residents and the medical community apprised of all findings so as to improve diagnosis and treatment  Additionally  I understand that this PV incidence in the tri county area presents an opportunity for additional academic study into the cause of the disease  which is currently unkown  I encourage your agencies to partner with able academic institutions as necessary and know that I am committed to working to find adequate resources for further study     The health of my constituents is of utmost importance and I would greatly appreciate your continued attention to this matter  Thank you for your assistance thus far     Sincerely     Arlen Specter                                                                  Browse by
