    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding is Part of the Senate FY06 Defense Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Friday  September 30  2005     Senator Arlen Specter  R PA   a senior member of the U S  Senate Appropriations Committee  and Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  announced today that the U S  Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for several defense related projects in the Lehigh Valley   The funding is contained in the Fiscal Year 2006 Defense Appropriations bill  which must be passed by both chambers of Congress and signed by the President      A number of Pennsylvania companies are enhancing the way our Armed Forces operate     Senator Specter said     These projects will go far in supporting the brave men and women who serve our country   I am pleased my colleagues in the Senate Appropriations Committee have recognized the valuable contribution these projects will make to our nation  s defense        Pennsylvania plays a strong role in our national defense and I am proud of the people and organizations that help to make our state a top competitor for defense projects that create jobs and strengthen our economy     said Senator Santorum     These funds will allow Pennsylvania defense contractors to continue their efforts to achieve a more secure nation      Projects which have been approved to receive funding in the Lehigh Valley include         6 9 million for Air Products  Inc  in Lehigh County for the Solid Electrolyte Oxygen Separator  to continue a U S  Air Force oxygen generation program          5 million for Night Vision Devices in Lehigh Valley for the Miniature Hand Held Thermal Imager will be used to equip select personnel of the XVIII Airborne Corps with miniature hand held thermal imagers that will improve dismounted soldiers   situational awareness and enhance combat identification          3 million for Lehigh University in Lehigh County to research the development of novel materials for optical technologies  innovative methods of packaging optoelectronic devices  and non linear materials for broadband communication and display technologies                                                                 Browse by
