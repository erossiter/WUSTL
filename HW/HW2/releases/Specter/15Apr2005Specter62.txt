                                                                         News Room News Releases          Simple Tax can be Filed on a Postcard and Completed in Minutes                      Washington  D C    Friday  April 15  2005                      Senator Arlen Specter  R PA   Chairman of the U S  Senate Judiciary Committee  has introduced a simpler tax proposal  which would scrap the 17 000 pages of current IRS tax code in favor of a 20  flat tax   The revenue neutral legislation would allow taxpayers to file returns on a postcard that could be completed in 15 minutes   The 10 line postcard tax form offered by Senator Specter would identify the taxpayer  specify the total compensation that the taxpayer received  and specify the allowance and number of dependents   The 20  flat tax plan would eliminate taxes on estates  dividends and capital gains  but would allow two important deductions  those on mortgage interest and on charitable contributions       The date of April 15th stabs fear  anxiety  and unease into the hearts of millions of Americans   Every year during   tax season     millions of Americans spend their evenings poring over page after page of IRS instructions  going through their records looking for information  and struggling to find and fill out all the appropriate forms on their federal tax returns     stated Senator Specter     My flat tax legislation would make filing a tax return a manageable chore  not a seemingly endless nightmare for most taxpayers      In addition to simplifying the filing process and eliminating double taxation  Senator Specter  s flat tax proposal also lowers the tax burden on the taxpayers in lower income brackets   Under the current tax code  a family of four which does not itemize deductions would pay taxes on all income over  22 100 that is personal exemptions of  12 400 and a standard deduction of  9 700   By contrast  under Senator Specter  s proposal  the same family would receive a personal exemption of  30 000  and would pay tax only on income over that amount   Senator Specter  s proposal was the first flat tax bill to be introduced in the Senate on March 2  1995   Since that year  Senator Specter has introduced the legislation in every Congress   On May 14  2003  Senator Specter offered an amendment to the Tax Reconciliation legislation which urged the Senate to hold hearings and consider legislation providing for a flat tax   The amendment was passed by the Senate by a vote of 70 to 30 on May 15  2003  Senator Specter then testified on this issue at a subsequent hearing held by the Joint Economic Committee on November 5  2003                                                                 Browse by
