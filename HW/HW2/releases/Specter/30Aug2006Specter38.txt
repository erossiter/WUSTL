                                                                         News Room News Releases                               Washington  D C    Wednesday  August 30  2006                      Senator Rick Santorum  Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Senator Arlen Specter  a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today  300 000 in federal funding for a new August Wilson Center for African American Culture to highlight the contributions of African Americans in the region     The new facility  projected to open in downtown Pittsburgh in early 2008  will feature art galleries  a 500 seat theater and multi purpose spaces for community use   The funding was secured in appropriations bills currently moving through the Senate   200 000 in the Fiscal Year 2007 Senate Transportation  Treasury and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill and  100 000 in the Fiscal Year 2007 Senate Labor  Health and Human Services and Education Appropriations bill   Additionally  Senator Santorum secured  100 000 for this project in the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill     The August Wilson Center for African American Culture is a not for profit organization that presents performing arts  visual arts and education programs that celebrate the contributions of African Americans within the region   The August Wilson Center is currently engaged in a capital campaign to raise  35 9 million for the new facility                                                                        Browse by
