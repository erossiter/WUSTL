    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases                               Washington  D C    Wednesday  April 6  2005     Senator Arlen Specter  R PA   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved an amendment  sponsored by Senators Specter and Robert Byrd  to extend the Abandoned Mine Reclamation Fund  presently set to expire on June 30  2005   The amendment accepted today will extend collection of reclamation fees at existing rates until September 30  2005   The fund was established in 1977 as a means of reclaiming abandoned mines  funded by collecting fees on current coal mining     By far  Pennsylvania has the most extensive problem of any of the States in the Union  where we have some 250 000 acres of abandoned surface mines which are in need of reclamation     said Senator Specter     Additionally  2 400 miles of streams are affected  polluting our water supply  because Pennsylvania has been so heavily mined   It is obviously a very important matter for the environment in Pennsylvania and the nation  and environmental protection is a high priority in our country      Abandoned mines are a tremendous risk for residents of the Commonwealth  with more than 1 6 million living within one mile of an abandoned mined   Pennsylvania has over 250 000 acres of abandoned surface mines and 2 400 miles of polluted streams caused by mine drainage    the largest source of water quality impairment  threatening human health and safety   In 2004  three individuals died in Pennsylvania alone due to abandoned mine hazards   In the 108th Congress  Senator Specter introduced legislation  S 2049  to extend the Abandoned Mine Reclamation Fund until 2018   In September of 2004  Senators Specter and Byrd successfully shepherded through the Senate a temporary extension of the program until June 30  2005   The extension is contained in the Senate FY05 Supplemental Appropriations bill  which still must receive approval from both chambers of Congress and the President                                                                 Browse by
