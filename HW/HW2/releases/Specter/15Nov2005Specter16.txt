    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Energy Funding Is a Part of the FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Tuesday  November 15  2005     U S  Senator Arlen Specter  R PA   a senior member of the U S  Senate Appropriations Committee  and U S  Senator Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  announced today that the Congress has approved federal funding for Department of Energy projects in Pennsylvania   The funding is part of the FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations bill       I am pleased my Congressional colleagues have approved funding for these important Pennsylvania projects     Senator Specter said     The research these projects yield will result in cleaner and more efficient energy production  and will increase our productivity and competitiveness in the global market          Clean  efficient and reliable energy production is essential to strengthening our economy and fostering greater economic development in Pennsylvania     said Senator Santorum     This funding supports the innovation of Pennsylvania companies that are committed to finding and developing new and sustainable technologies to meet our energy demands       The FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill passed the Senate on November 14  2005 with a vote of 84 4   The House of Representatives passed the bill on November 9  2005   The bill will now be sent to the President for his signature   Funding has been approved for the following projects  which would be allocated through the Department of Energy  DOE                         106 941 million for the National Energy Technology Lab in Allegheny County for operations and research of more than 700 research projects in all 50 States and 16 foreign countries  most involving the development of advanced coal  oil  and natural gas technologies                           5 million for AEA Technologies in Allegheny County to provide technology to support the Memorandum of Understanding between the United States Department of Energy and the United Kingdom Department of Trade and Industry for cleanup in the energy and environmental arena  such as waste management  decommissioning  renewable energy  energy efficiency and remote handling                           4 million for Siemens Westinghouse in Allegheny County for research and development of Solid Oxide Fuel Cells  Solid Oxide Fuel Cells are expected to play a major role the development of distributed energy generation systems in the future because of their low emissions  small size  high efficiencies and ease of siting                            2 million for Ion Transport Membrane  ITM  Syngas Project  Air Products and Chemicals  Inc   in Lehigh County to fund technologies to produce synthesis gas  hydrogen and carbon monoxide  from natural gas in a single step                                          1 5 million for the University City Science Park in Philadelphia County for laboratory expansions and technology upgrades                          1 million for the Green Oil Company in Bucks County for research and development of canola based automotive oil  which has potential environmental benefits as being ninety eight percent biodegradable and reducing United States dependence on foreign energy sources                            500 000 for Breen Energy Solutions in Allegheny County for a research project on coal waste slurry reburn as a renewable energy source
