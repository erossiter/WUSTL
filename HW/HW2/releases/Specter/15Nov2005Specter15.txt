    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding is Part of the FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Tuesday  November 15  2005     U S  Senator Arlen Specter  R PA   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  and U S  Senator Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  announced today that the Congress has approved federal funding for Pennsylvania projects contained in the FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations bill     I am pleased my Congressional colleagues have provided this funding for these important projects throughout Pennsylvania     Senator Specter said     These projects will enhance the lives of community residents in a variety of ways  including making the Port of Philadelphia competitive with other ports  protecting residents from floods and protecting our essential locks and dams system        Pennsylvania  s rivers provide transit that is essential to our local economies     said Senator Santorum     This funding provides for flood protection projects and maintenance of the waterway infrastructures  the failure of which would be devastating to flood prone communities and the industries that depend on Pennsylvania  s rivers      The FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill passed the Senate on November 14  2005 with a vote of 84 4   The House of Representatives passed the bill on November 9  2005   The bill will now be sent to the President for his signature    Projects contained in today  s announcement include     50 8 million for Locks and Dams 2  3  and 4  Monongahela River in Allegheny County for rehabilitation   The nearly century old locks and dams are the last of the old and undersized locks on the Monongahela River system and are structurally unstable   There is a substantial chance that important structural components of these facilities could fail at any time which could devastate water supplies to communities and vital industries  as well as stalling commerce through the Port of Pittsburgh     15 million for Emsworth Locks and Dams  Ohio River in Allegheny County for rehabilitation   The proposed project includes replacement of the dam gates  gate hoisting machinery  electrical power and distribution system and scour protection system      10 496 million for the Wyoming Valley Levee Raising Project  which has been ongoing since Tropical Storm Agnes caused widespread flooding in 1972   In January 1996  floods nearly breached the levee system  forcing emergency officials to evacuate more than 100 000 people  Hundreds of homes were destroyed and the community suffered tens of millions of dollars in damage     2 25 million for the Delaware River Main Channel Deepening Project   The existing 40 foot deep channel restricts the efficient movement of tankers  dry bulk carriers and container vessels   The 45 foot deepening project will result in transportation savings to commodities consisting of crude oil imports  iron ore imports  and scrap exports     1 275 million for the Upper Ohio River Navigation Study of Emsworth  Dashields  Montgomery Locks and Dams in Allegheny County to continue a long term feasibility study on the three aged structures     750 000 for the Three Rivers Wet Weather Demonstration Program in Allegheny County to reroute streams from municipal sewers into restored natural channels in Sheraden Park in Pittsburgh and Stowe Township     750 000 for the Fairmount Dam Fishladder in Philadelphia County to increase efficiency by reconfiguring the structure to allow for passage of American Shad   The reconfiguration will provide access to a habitat that will spawn and rear Shad and other migratory fish on the Schuylkill River     465 000 for the Presque Isle Pennisula for maintenance of the beaches  periodic repair and replenishment of the beach sand     430 000 for the Upper Tioga River Watershed in Tioga County for a restoration report  which will identify the economic benefits of restoring the Upper Tioga River Watershed for local  state and national interests
