    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding Approved in the Fiscal Year 2007 LHHS Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Tuesday  July 25  2006     U S  Senators Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Arlen Specter  R PA   Chairman of the Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education  announced today that U S  Senate Appropriations Committee has approved funding for several southeastern Pennsylvania education projects   The projects are contained in the FY07 Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations bill     This funding will help to maintain and increase the high standards set in education throughout Pennsylvania     said Senator Santorum     The world of education is moving forward at a rapid pace with new technology being introduced into classrooms virtually everyday   With this funding  the goal is to provide all Pennsylvanians with a modern  quality education        I am pleased my colleagues have approved funding for these important southeastern Pennsylvania projects     Senator Specter said     Education is the single greatest capital investment we can make in our nation  s future  and this funding will provide resources that will empower students to become more productive members of society  live happier lives and make incredible contributions to our country      The bill will now await action on the Senate floor   Education projects approved in the bill include         1 million for Philadelphia School District for its summer school initiative   This funding will be used to support the School District of Philadelphia s summer school program  designed to provide essential help and support to students having academic difficulties   The summer school program  serving over 60 000 students in over 120 locations for grades 1 11  provides additional reading and mathematics to students struggling academically         500 000 for the University of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia to support a global languages and culture program to serve area K 12 students  This funding will be used to establish the physical and programmatic infrastructure necessary to create  pilot and implement a model global languages and culture program for K 12 students in urban public schools   The program will also train teachers in critical languages and cultures         300 000 for People for People in Philadelphia for an after school program for at risk  low income children in the North Central neighborhood of Philadelphia   The program serves students in grades K 8 offering homework assistance  computer training  test preparation  and postsecondary education counseling         200 000 for City Year  Inc  in Philadelphia to reduce truancy and drop out rates in Philadelphia high schools         100 000 for Free Library of Philadelphia for technology upgrades and expansion of technology resources and installation of an HVAC system for preservation of Rare Book collection and other historical materials  Additional computers and a PC training facility which will provide the latest in multi media innovations  and additional computers will also be added to the library         100 000 for Chester County Intermediate Unit in Chester County for a vocational technical education program that will involve career training and instruction for grades 9 14   This program will contain high school  career and technical programs  college courses and dual enrollment initiatives that blend high school and for credit college courses         100 000 for Lincoln University in Chester County for campus wide technology upgrades to its instructional and administrative technology in student computer laboratories  computer classrooms and on line resources         100 000 for Project HOME in Philadelphia to support after school programs in Project HOME s Honickman Learning Center and Comcast Technology Labs  The after school programs focus on the integration of technology with art  education and enterprise  Students are provided educational opportunities
