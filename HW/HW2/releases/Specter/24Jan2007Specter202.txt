                                                                         News Room News Releases          Bill Would Help Protect Judges  Their Families Against Increased Threats of Violence                      Washington  D C    Wednesday  January 24  2007                      Two leading members of the Senate Judiciary Committee today joined with key Senators and House members in introducing a bipartisan  bicameral bill that would strengthen and expand protections for judges and their families    Senator Arlen Specter  R Pa  Ranking Member of the Judiciary Committe  and Senator Patrick Leahy  D Vt   Chairman of the Judiciary Committee  teamed up with Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid  D Nev   Senate Majority Whip  Dick Durbin  D Ill  as well as Senators Edward Kennedy  D Mass   Orrin Hatch  R Utah  Charles Schumer  D N Y   John Cornyn  R Texas  and Susan Collins  R Maine  to introduce the Court Security Improvement Act of 2007   Rep  John Conyers  D  Mich   the Chairman of the House Judiciary Committee introduced a companion bill in the House today  The House bill is also a bipartisan measure  sponsored by Chairman Conyers  Robert C    Bobby    Scott  D Va   and Louie Gohmert  R Texas     The ability of the judiciary to determine the rule of law without fear or favor is an indispensable prerequisite to our democratic society     stated Senator Specter     Our judges   personal security  along with their judicial independence  must be safeguarded at all costs  and I believe that this bill is an important step toward providing those safeguards       Key provisions of the bill include                  New criminal penalties for the misuse of restricted personal information to threaten to seriously harm judges  their families or other individuals performing official duties              Enhancing penalties for tampering with or retaliating against witnesses              New resources for state courts to improve security for state and local courts               Provisions extending life insurance benefits to bankruptcy  magistrate and territorial judges   Senators Specter and Leahy first joined forces with Senator Durbin and others to bolster security in courts around the country in 2005  in the wake of increasingly violent threats against judges and their families  including the tragic murder of the family of Judge Joan Lefkow of Chicago   During a Judiciary Committee hearing in May of that year  Judge Lefkow testified about the murder of her husband and her mother  who were shot to death in the basement of the judge s home in February 2005   The Chicago man responsible for the killings had been upset with Judge Lefkow for dismissing a malpractice suit he had filed  The man confessed to the shootings before committing suicide     The Senators introduced a similar court security bill at the time   That bill passed the Senate last Congress  but not the House
