                                                                         News Room News Releases          Legislation Establishes a Steel Industry National Historic Site in Southwestern PA                      Washington  D C    Wednesday  February 28  2007     Senators Arlen Specter and Bob Casey announced today legislation that would establish an affiliate of the national park system in southwest Pennsylvania devoted to the history of the steel industry in the United States     The Steel Heritage National Historic Site would be located at the former U S  Steel Homestead Works in Allegheny County  Pennsylvania   Specific sites would include the location of the Battle of the Homestead  the Carrie Furnace Complex  and the Hot Metal Bridge       It is appropriate that we honor the nation  s steel industry with a historic site in Pennsylvania     Senator Specter stated    Pennsylvania  s strong workforce and valuable natural resources have contributed to the industry  s rich heritage  and I will work for swift passage of legislation        The steel industry and the workers who fueled that industry are an integral part of Pennsylvania  s heritage  especially in the Southwest     said Senator Casey     Senator Specter has pursued this effort for a number of years   I am proud to join him      Representative Mike Doyle  a long standing champion of the preservation effort  has introduced companion legislation in the House of Representatives   Senator Specter introduced similar legislation in the 107th  108th  and 109th Congresses
