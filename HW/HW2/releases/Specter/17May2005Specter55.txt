    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding from Hope VI Public Housing Revitalization Program                      Washington  D C    Tuesday  May 17  2005     U S  Senator Arlen Specter  R PA   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  and U S  Senator Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  and Congressman Charlie Dent  R PA 15   announced today that the Allentown Housing Authority will receive  20 000 000 through the Department of Housing and Urban Development  s Home Ownership for People Everywhere  HOPE VI  program   The funding will enable the Allentown Housing Authority to revitalize the Hanover Acres and Riverview Terrace public housing development   The project calls for complete demolition of the current Hanover Acres and Riverview Terrace units and the construction of 322 new units  a 17 000 square foot community center  a greenway and a park   The project will also provide services such as youth programs  adult education  child care  and homeownership and money management education       The HOPE VI program has been very successful in providing low income families with better housing  employment  and educational opportunities     Senator Specter said     I am very pleased to see the City of Allentown receive the resources it needs to provide these opportunities to residents in need        The revitalization of the Hanover Acres and Riverview Terrace is an important initiative in the City of Allentown that will not only provide affordable housing but will incorporate community services  giving families more opportunities for improved education and job training and placement     said Senator Santorum     The HOPE VI program has been very successful in meeting the needs of low income families and providing opportunities for affordable housing   The HOPE VI program is an essential part of the effort to improve public housing within Pennsylvania       In Allentown  we have some of the country s oldest low income housing  with Hanover Acres and Riverview Terrace dating back to the 1930s   This money will help make needed upgrades to these communities   Congressman Dent said    This new community will enhance the East Side neighborhood of Allentown  a neighborhood that has at times not received the same level of investment as surrounding Allentown neighborhoods     The HOPE VI program provides funding for the demolition and reconstruction of obsolete  severely distressed public housing   HOPE VI enhances communities by decentralizing poverty and giving families an opportunity to live in mixed income neighborhoods with better educational and employment opportunities
