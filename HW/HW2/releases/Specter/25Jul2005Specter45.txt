    United States Senator Arlen Specter  Pennsylvania   News Room                                                                                                                                                                                                                 News Room News Releases          Funding Is Included in EDI Portion of the FY06 Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Monday  July 25  2005     Senator Arlen Specter  a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  and Senator Rick Santorum  Chairman of the Republican Conference  announced today that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved federal funding for projects in Pennsylvania   The funding is included in the Economic Development Initiative portion of the Departments of the Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary  Housing and Urban Development  and Related Agencies Fiscal Year 2006 Appropriations bill    I am pleased my colleagues on the Senate Appropriations Committee have recognized the importance of these programs   Senator Specter said    These projects will revitalize and improve cities and towns across the Commonwealth  and are vital to Pennsylvania        s future economic and cultural growth     I am pleased that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved federal funding for important Pennsylvania projects to make improvements and advances in communities throughout the Commonwealth   Senator Santorum stated    The rehabilitation and construction of community assets promotes economic development in Pennsylvania  and complements ongoing efforts by the private sector    The FY06 Appropriations Bill is pending further action by the full Senate and the House of Representatives   The Senate Appropriations Committee approved funding for the following Pennsylvania projects     X  300 000 for the Allegheny County Airport Authority for the construction of an infrastructure for cargo distribution  aircraft maintenance and related amenities     X  200 000 for the City of Lancaster for the rehabilitation and renovation of the Lancaster Square     X  200 000 for Our City Reading in Reading for the rehabilitation of abandoned houses to provide quality home ownership opportunities to low income families     X  200 000 for Universal Community Homes in Philadelphia for the conversion of land into for sale units to moderate  and low income families     X  200 000 for the Allentown Art Museum in Allentown for the expansion and modernization of its facilities     X  200 000 for the Greene County Department of Planning and Development in Franklin Township for the construction of a multi phased business park on the grounds of the Greene County Airport     X  200 000 for Impact Services Corporation in Philadelphia for the renovation  redevelopment  and conversion of an existing building into housing units for homeless veterans     X  200 000 for Shippensburg University Foundation for the construction of Phase III of the Shippensburg Regional Conference Center  which will provide accommodations for business and career development     X  200 000 for the Pittsburgh Zoo in Pittsburgh for the development of the zoo        s expansion project     X  200 000 for Gaudenzia  Inc  in Norristown for the renovation and expansion of its residential facilities for low income families     X  200 000 for the Greater Wilkes Barre Chamber of Business and Industry in Wilkes Barre for the acquisition and redevelopment of the historic Irem Temple  which will be converted into a cultural center     X  200 000 for Partnership CDC in Philadelphia for the acquisition and renovation of affordable housing for moderate  and low income families     X  200 000 for the City of Carbondale  Pennsylvania for the South Main Street Economic Development Initiative which is designed to reduce blight along the City s Main Street Corridor     X  200 000 for the Redevelopment Authority of the City of Corry to acquire a brownfield site in downtown Corry  Pennsylvania     X  200 000 for Weatherly Borough  Pennsylvania to acquire and redevelop the Lehigh Valley Railroad Shops and Weatherly Steel Plant complex in the heart of Weatherly  PA     X  200 000 for Indiana County  Pennsylvania to acquire the Wayne Avenue Property in Indiana     X  200 000 for Armstrong County  Pennsylvania for remediation and infrastructure development on a 14 2 acre of brownfield property in Apollo Borough     X  200 000 for Perry County  Pennsylvania to support the County s efforts to develop an industrial park in New Bloomfield     X  200 000 for People for People  Inc  for planning and project development efforts for the Triangle redevelopment project in North Philadelphia     X  200 000 for the Southwestern Pennsylvania Commission  to develop the Alta Vista Business Park  a mixed use business park on a former strip mine site adjacent to I 70  in Washington County  Pennsylvania     X  200 000 to the City of Scranton  Pennsylvania for the North Main Avenue redevelopment project     X  200 000 for Oxford Mainstreet  Inc  Oxford  PA to revitalize the downtown commercial district
