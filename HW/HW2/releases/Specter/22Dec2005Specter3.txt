                                                                         News Room News Releases          Funding is Part of the FY06 Defense Appropriations Bill                      Washington  D C    Thursday  December 22  2005     Senator Arlen Specter   a senior member of the U S  Senate Appropriations Committee  and Senator Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  announced today that the U S  Senate has approved funding for several defense related projects in the Lehigh Valley   The funding is contained in the Fiscal Year 2006 Defense Appropriations bill     A number of Pennsylvania companies are enhancing the way our Armed Forces operate     Senator Specter said     These projects will go far in supporting the brave men and women who serve our country   I am pleased my colleagues in the Senate have recognized the valuable contribution these projects will make to our nation  s defense        Pennsylvania plays a strong role in our national defense and I am proud of the people and organizations that help to make our state a top competitor for defense projects that create jobs and strengthen our economy     said Senator Santorum     This funding will enable these important defense projects in the Lehigh Valley to move forward in the effort to achieve a more secure nation       Projects which have been approved to receive funding in the Lehigh Valley include         5 million for Night Vision Devices in Lehigh County for the Miniature Hand Held Thermal Imager to equip select personnel of the XVIII Airborne Corps with miniature hand held thermal imagers that will improve situational awareness and provide early warning of the presence of hostile forces         4 8 million for Air Products  Inc  in Lehigh County for the Solid Electrolyte Oxygen Separator  to continue the development of an electrically driven Solid Electrolyte Oxygen Separator  SEOS  generator capable of delivering high purity  therapeutic grade oxygen          4 1 million for Lehigh University in Lehigh County to research the development of novel materials for optical technologies  innovative methods of packaging optoelectronic devices  and non linear materials for broadband communication and display technologies                                                                 Browse by                                                 Current record
