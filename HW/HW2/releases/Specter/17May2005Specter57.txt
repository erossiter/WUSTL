                                                                         News Room News Releases          Funding is part of the Federal Highway Administration  s Emergency Hurricane Relief Program                      Washington  D C    Tuesday  May 17  2005     U S  Senator Arlen Specter  R PA   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  and U S  Senator Rick Santorum  R PA   Chairman of the Senate Republican Conference  announced that  39 million has been awarded for Pennsylvania from the Federal Highway Administration  s Emergency Relief program for the repair and reconstruction of federal highways and roads throughout the Commonwealth   The effected roads suffered serious damage as a result of heavy rains and severe flooding from Hurricane Ivan in September 2004     The powerful remnants of Tropical Storm Ivan ravaged parts of Pennsylvania  s transportation infrastructure     said Senator Specter     This funding is vital for local governments to restore those roadways and ensure they remain safe        Hurricane Ivan caused devastating damage to Pennsylvania  s roadways  particularly the areas of western Pennsylvania     said Senator Santorum     I am pleased that the much needed funds provided by Congress are being awarded to the Commonwealth to alleviate the burden for repair and reconstruction of roadways in the hardest hit regions      Included in the Military Construction Appropriations and Emergency Hurricane Supplemental Appropriations Act of 2005  Congress provided these additional relief funds for emergency expenses resulting from the 2004 hurricanes   These funds reimburse the state for the repairs already completed and provide funding for the remaining repairs necessary to restore the highways to their pre disaster
