                                                                                                                                                                                    NEW JERSEY SENATORS RESPOND TO PRESIDENTIAL DISASTER DECLARATION FOLLOWING MASSIVE FLOODING                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Friday  July 7  2006      WASHINGTON    U S  Senators Robert Menendez  D NJ  and Frank R  Lautenberg  D NJ  released statements following the Federal Emergency Management Agency  s announcement that a Presidential Declaration of Disaster has been issued for New Jersey following the recent flooding  This declaration marks the federal government  s intention to provide resources to assist New Jerseyans most impacted by the floods      I m happy to see that the administration has responded to our early call for a disaster designation     Menendez said    We will continue to press FEMA to immediately assist those New Jerseyans affected by the floods to help get them back on their feet  This is the first of many steps to help those who lost their homes  their businesses  and their quality of life  I will continue to work here in the Senate to ensure that the federal government  through the Army Corps of Engineers  conducts the necessary research to better understand how to limit the devastating effects of future flooding         It  s critical that we get these funds to New Jersey quickly without unnecessary bureaucratic delays   The sooner people can get the funding to repair their homes and businesses  the sooner their lives can return to normal   People having to flee their homes in this area is becoming all too common an occurrence  so I am pleased to see federal funding available to reduce the risk of flooding     said Senator Lautenberg
