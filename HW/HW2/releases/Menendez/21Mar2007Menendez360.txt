                                                                                                                                                                                    MENENDEZ   IRAN SANCTIONS FRAMEWORK HAS TOO MANY LOOPHOLES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         We say we want to be tough on Iran  then we turn around and act like a pussycat                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Wednesday  March 21  2007      WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Robert Menendez  D NJ   a member of the Senate Banking and Foreign Relations committees  today denounced glaring limitations to the sanctions currently enforced against Iran  During a Banking Committee hearing on assessing the effectiveness of current U S  sanctions on Iran  Menendez highlighted the myriad loopholes present in the current U S  sanctions framework against Iran   loopholes the lawmaker said that undermine the very goal of imposing sanctions on recalcitrant states     Last year  Congress enacted legislation that reauthorized the Iran Libya Sanctions Act and I was happy that Congress acted before the sanctions expired   Menendez said    However  I said at the time that I would continue to advocate for closing the gaps in current law and tightening the sanctions framework  It is my belief that today s hearing must be the first step toward creating comprehensive legislation that not only strengthens the Iranian sanctions framework  but also closes the existing loopholes
