                                                                                                                                                                                    SEN  MENENDEZ APPLAUDS FINAL PASSAGE OF BILL TO BOOST HIGHER EDUCATION AID FOR STUDENTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           More than  430 million in grant aid will be available for NJ students over next 5 years                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Friday  September 7  2007       WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Robert Menendez  D NJ  applauded this mornings Senate passage of the final version of higher education legislation that marks a major new investment in student aid  including hundreds of millions of dollars for college students in New Jersey  The College Cost Reduction and Access Act  H R  2669  increases grant aid to New Jersey students by  434 million over the next five school years and also provides  114 8 million in additional loan aid for New Jersey students over that time period   The billions of grant aid from this bill will make a tremendous difference for students across in New Jersey and across the nation  students who are struggling to stay afloat because of the cost of college  students who are saving every last penny in the hopes that they can achieve their dreams of college  said Menendez  New Jersey students are no strangers to the skyrocketing costs of a college education  In fact  within the past five school years  the cost of attendance  including tuition  fees  room and board  at four year public colleges in New Jersey has increased by almost 50 percent while household incomes have not kept up   With this legislation  we are helping throw the doors to college open for all students who are determined to achieve  In the global economy we live in today  we clearly cannot afford as a nation to have our students priced out of a college education   What the College Cost Reduction and Access Acts means for New Jersey students    Provides more than  40 million in new grant aid for the 2008 2009 school year    Increases grant aid to New Jersey students by  434 million over the next five school years    Provides  114 8 million in additional loan aid for New Jersey students over next five school years   What the legislation means for students nationwide    Invests nearly  20 billion in new grant aid for students    Increases the maximum award for Pell Grant recipients by  500 next year and to  5 400 by 2012    Cuts interest rates on student loans in half  from 6 8 percent to 3 4 percent for undergraduate students with subsidized student loans    Caps monthly loan payments at 15 percent of a students discretionary income to help students manage loan payments    Provides loan forgiveness for borrowers who spend ten years in a public service occupation    Improves college preparation for underserved students by restoring funding for Upward Bound  a key college access program   Expanding access to college by creating College Access Challenge Grants to increase college outreach activities in every state    Creates new TEACH Grants that provide  4 000 annual scholarships for high achieving students who teach a high need subject in a high need school    Provides more than  500 million to support Historically Black Colleges and Universities  Hispanic Serving Institutions  and other colleges that serve minority students    For more information on the cost of college in New Jersey and some of the remedies passed by the Senate  see attached report compiled by the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee
