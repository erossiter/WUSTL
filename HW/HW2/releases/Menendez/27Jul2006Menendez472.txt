                                                                                                                                                                                    MENENDEZ   APPALLED   BY NAVY FAILURE TO SECURE NEW JERSEY RECRUITS   PERSONAL DATA                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Lawmaker calls for full accounting of laptop thefts  Navy plan to   rectify situation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Thursday  July 27  2006      WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Robert Menendez  D NJ  released a statement following reports that two Navy laptop computers    reportedly containing information on 31 000 Navy recruiters and applicants    were stolen from two different Navy offices in New Jersey  The thefts were reported to have occurred in early June and early July from Navy recruiting stations in Trenton and Jersey City      It is appalling and almost criminal that New Jerseyans who are answering their nation  s call to serve cannot count on their personal information to be properly secured  Sailors serve an integral role in our nation  s defense and in the global war on terror    the least we can do is protect the personal information they volunteer during the application process  The Secretary of the Navy needs to provide a full accounting of how this occurred  as well as how the Department of the Navy will rectify a situation that could impact thousands of New Jerseyans
