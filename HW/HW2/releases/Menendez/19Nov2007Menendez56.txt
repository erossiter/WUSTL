                                                                                                                                                                                    SENATOR MENENDEZ INTRODUCES BILL TO ENSURE REGULATORS DO THEIR PART TO PRESERVE AND EXPAND MINORITY OWNED BANKS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Monday  November 19  2007      Washington D C   Senator Robert Menendez  D NJ   a member of the Banking  Housing  and Urban Affairs Committee  last week introduced the Preserving and Expanding Minority Depository Institutions Act to hold federal regulators accountable for preserving and expanding minority owned banks    The bill would require additional federal regulators  such as the Federal Reserve and the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency  OCC   to carry out the goals of the Financial Institutions Reform  Recovery  and Enforcement Act of 1989  FIRREA   which include providing minority banks with technical assistance and working to promote minority owned institutions  It would also require regulators to report annually on the steps they are taking to ensure the needs of minority owned institutions are met    When only 2 percent of financial institutions in the wealthiest country in the world are minority owned  when we know that minority owned institutions are not always receiving the range of services that should be available to them  it is clear we can do more to help them succeed  said Menendez  This bill is a first  but critical  step toward making sure that we are giving this important sector the tools and the resources it needs to foster successful financial institutions    Recently  the Government Accountability Office  GAO  issued a report that
