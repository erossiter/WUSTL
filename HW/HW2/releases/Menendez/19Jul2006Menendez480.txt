                                                                                                                                                                                    MENENDEZ SPEAKS WITH SENIOR STATE DEPARTMENT OFFICIAL ABOUT NEW JERSEYANS IN LEBANON                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Lawmaker phones Under Secretary of State Burns to express urgency of evacuating Garden State residents                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Wednesday  July 19  2006      WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Robert Menendez  D NJ  late yesterday expressed to Under Secretary of State R  Nicholas Burns the immediacy of evacuating New Jerseyans who are still stranded in Lebanon  Menendez  s office has been in contact with family members of New Jersey residents stranded in the Lebanon region  The New Jersey lawmaker released a statement following his conversation      I have spoken with Under Secretary Burns and have conveyed to him in very explicit terms the urgency of locating and rescuing New Jerseyans facing perilous conditions in Lebanon   New Jerseyans are at risk and the Department of State must make immediate provisions to bring them to safety  I find it woefully irresponsible and reprehensible that the Bush administration began mobilizing resources to evacuate our citizens long after other countries  But  there will be a time and place to fully explore why our government was so slow to react to the increasing instability in Lebanon  Now is not the time  however  We must now be about the business of locating and rescuing our citizens from harm  s way  I will continue to pressure the Department of State and other government agencies until every single New Jerseyan is safe
