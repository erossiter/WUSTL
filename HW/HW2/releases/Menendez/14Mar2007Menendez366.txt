                                                                                                                                                                                    MENENDEZ BLASTS PREDATORY LENDERS   TIME HAS RUN OUT FOR INDUSTRY  WE NEED LEGISLATIVE SOLUTION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Wednesday  March 14  2007      WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Robert Menendez  D NJ   a member of the Senate Banking Committee  today blasted the deceitful practices of some subprime lenders whose nefarious tactics place extraordinary financial burdens on those at the bottom of the economic ladder  The Garden State lawmaker said the industry has not demonstrated a sincere enough effort to police itself from such unscrupulous practices and therefore requires legislative intervention from the Congress     Predatory lending is making a mockery of the homeownership dream for far too many individuals   Menendez said   Ironically  however  deceitful subprime lenders are living the dream   often making millions in profits   Last year  subprime loans totaled about  605 billion  which is a fifth of the total overall market for U S home loans   We simply cannot ignore this segment of the market  which serves some of our most vulnerable populations including women  seniors  and minorities     I want to assure the American people   those who are currently struggling to pay their mortgage and those who are looking to own a home   that I will not rest until they are protected against the claws of predatory lenders   Enough is enough   American consumers deserve safe and secure mortgage options and new protections against predatory lending
