                                                                                                                                                                                    SUBPRIME ACTION FROM CONGRESS STILL NECESSARY  SAYS SEN  MENENDEZ                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Banking Committee member releases statement reacting to Fed Chair s announcement                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Thursday  May 17  2007      WASHINGTON   After Fed Chairman Ben Bernanke said today that the Fed would work to curb abusive practices in subprime mortgage lending  U S  Senator Robert Menendez  D NJ   member of the Senate Banking Committee  released the following statement     I applaud and appreciate Chairman Bernanke s commitment to helping do away with many of the abusive practices that have contributed to the tsunami of foreclosures in this country  I look forward to working with him as we further develop solutions      I continue to believe that this commitment must also be met by Congressional action that would eliminate predatory lending and otherwise help strengthen the mortgage lending system so that we can prevent mass foreclosures in the long term  The force of law will most effectively and efficiently help solve the underlying problems  The Fed s action will take us a long way  but we need to go the extra mile to solidify the mortgage lending system
