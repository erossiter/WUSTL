Miller  Allyn Brooks LaSure  202 224 4744                                                                                                      Thursday  February 2  2006       WASHINGTON    The U S  Senate today passed   an amendment offered by U S  Senator Robert Menendez  D NJ    that will protect middle class families from a tax increase forced   by the alternative minimum tax  Without this measure  over 17   million middle class taxpayers would have faced an unexpected   tax increase  in many cases more than  1 000 this year        The Senate acted in America  s best interest today   by changing this law     Menendez said    The AMT    in its previous form  penalized families in a way never intended   by the original legislation  Today we spoke for the hard working   middle class families who didn  t deserve    and   couldn  t afford    a   stealth   tax bill   in the mail          Menendez offered his amendment to the tax reconciliation bill   currently before the Senate  Specifically  the amendment   replaced tax cuts on dividends and capital gains with alternative   minimum tax relief for middle class families           In the bill before us  we have a clear choice       Menendez said yesterday on the Senate floor    We can   stop a tax increase for 17 million middle class taxpayers      a tax that was never intended to penalize anyone   considered   middle class      or we can   continue to give people who need it the least a break on their   capital gains at the expense of middle class workers         We face a choice of values   Do we value ensuring   fairness for all hard working Americans  or would we rather give   a break to those who need it the least     Menendez said       The alternative minimum tax was originally designed to ensure   that people making more than  200 000 paid some income tax    but because it was never indexed for inflation  each year it   captures more and more middle class families unless it is   changed by Congress         The Senate  s tax reconciliation bill  passed last   November  protected middle class families from additional   taxation through the alternative minimum tax  The House bill   does not offer such protections
