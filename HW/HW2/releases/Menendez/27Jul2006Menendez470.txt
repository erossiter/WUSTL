                                                                                                                                                                                    MENENDEZ  LAUTENBERG CALL ON NAVY SECRETARY FOR ACCOUNTING OF LAPTOP THEFTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Lawmakers seek Navy plan to   rectify situation   that could impact thousands                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Thursday  July 27  2006      WASHINGTON    U S  Senators Robert Menendez  D NJ  and Frank R  Lautenberg  D NJ  today called upon Secretary of the Navy Donald Winter to fully account for how two Navy laptop computers    reportedly containing information on 31 000 Navy recruiters and applicants    were stolen from two different Navy offices in New Jersey  The senators requested the Navy  s plans to protect individuals whose information may have been compromised  as well as their plan to minimize the risk for future breaches and to require immediate reporting of such incidents      We find it deplorable that New Jerseyans who are answering their nation  s call to duty cannot count on their information to be properly secured     the lawmakers wrote     Sailors serve an integral role in our nation  s defense and in the global war on terror    the least we can do is protect the personal information they volunteer during the application process        The thefts were reported to have occurred in early June and early July from Navy recruiting stations in Trenton and Jersey City
