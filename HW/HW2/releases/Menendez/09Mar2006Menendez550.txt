Miller  Menendez  Allyn Brooks LaSure  202 224 4744                                                                                               Thursday  March 9  2006      WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Robert Menendez    D NJ  released the following statement in reaction to today  s   announcement from DP World          If DP World is going to completely divest the operations it   is acquiring at U S  ports  than that is a victory for the security of   our country   But divestiture must include ownership  control   and operations  so we need to see the details          It is an unacceptable risk to our national security to turn   control of operations at U S  ports over to a foreign government     The Bush administration was wrong to approve this sale in the   first place  and I am pleased that one of my first efforts in the   Senate was helping lead a bipartisan majority that stood up and   said so        But blocking this transaction is just the first step in   securing our ports   Congress needs to act to pass the   legislation I have introduced with Senator Clinton at the outset   of this debate to block any company owned by a foreign   government from controlling operations at U S  ports  so we   never again face the possibility of our ports being turned over to   a foreign government        We also need to invest more in securing our ports  where   only five percent of containers entering our country are currently   inspected   I will be offering an amendment to the budget next   week to add nearly a billion dollars to port security and greatly   increase container scanning and inspection   Unfortunately    Republicans defeated it in the Budget committee today  but now   that there is a bipartisan consensus to improve the security of   our ports  I hope the full Senate will reverse that decision
