UNIVERSAL STANDARDS   NEEDED FOR CHEMICAL  PLANT SECURITY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Contact  Allyn Brooks LaSure  202 224 4744                                                                                           Tuesday  March 21  2006      WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Robert Menendez    D NJ  released the following statement in reaction to Homeland   Security Secretary Michael Chertoff  s speech on chemical plant   security          While Secretary Chertoff  s remarks this morning indicate   some progress has been made on chemical plant security  we   are still far from our goal  In news accounts of the Secretary  s   speech  he fell short of endorsing universal security standards at   chemical plants and instead suggested that plants use a sliding   scale in how they secure their facilities         In his speech he denounced   micromanaging   and called for     performance standards   as opposed to specific safeguards  This   is unacceptable  Many of these plants are within fallout range of   more than one million Americans and news organizations have   documented gaping security holes at some of these facilities          The Department of Homeland Security must play a   proactive role in establishing and enforcing universal standards   for chemical plant facilities    standards that protect that families   and communities that dwell in the shadow of these facilities    Homeland Security should play a proactive role    working   alongside the chemical industry    to establish universal   standards  Anything less discounts the grave risk these facilities   represent in our communities
