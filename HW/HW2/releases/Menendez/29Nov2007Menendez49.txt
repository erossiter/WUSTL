                                                                                                                                                                                    SEN  MENENDEZ CRITICIZES HASTY SEC RULING LIMITING SHAREHOLDERS  RIGHTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Thursday  November 29  2007      WASHINGTON  Yesterday  the Securities and Exchange Commission adopted a rule that will curtail the ability of shareholders to select corporate board members  Earlier this month  in a Senate Banking Committee hearing on this issue  Senator Robert Menendez  D NJ  had urged SEC Chairman Cox not to rush into a ruling  The SEC is currently in transition  operating with four members instead of five  with another member set to depart  Senator Menendez released the following statement  Shareholders have been given the short end of the stick with this ruling  which was made at a time when the SEC is ill equipped to consider such a weighty issue  I believe this is the type of crucial issue that requires a thorough consideration and a commission operating at full strength  Chairman Cox has said that he will come back to this issue again next year  and I will hold him to his word  This issue deserves more healthy deliberation by a full commission
