                               MARTINEZ REMEMBERS FORMER NASCAR PRESIDENT BILL FRANCE  JR   June 6  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today gave the following remarks on the Senate floor in memory of former NASCAR president and Floridian  Bill France  Jr     Senator Martinez said     I rise today to pay tribute to a great Floridian  Bill France  Jr   a man who lived the American dream  a man who literally turned an idea and hard work into the multibillion dollar national exhibition we now today know as NASCAR  Bill France was a great Floridian in many other ways as well  He was someone who contributed greatly to his local community of Daytona Beach  Florida  as well as to the state of Florida at large  Mr  France left us at his home in Daytona Beach when he passed away earlier this week after a long and difficult battle with cancer     We in Florida know what the news reports confirmed immediately after his death   that Bill made NASCAR what it is today  the sold out races  and the coverage  and the regalia and this man s love of the sport  Bill France moved as a young man and his family  Bill France  Sr  and his mother  Anne  to Daytona  Florida  in 1935 to escape the Great Depression  With  100 in his pocket  Bill France started a new life setting up an auto repair shop and took a quick interest in racing  In 1938  he would set up the Daytona Beach Road Course and from there the rest is history     Mr  President  this course he set up back in those days was so unique and to see photographs of it is one of those things that one can only hearken back to the old Florida that is no more  But the races were essentially conducted on the strip of beach in Daytona Beach  they would circle around A1A  the strip of highway that was there at the time  and then circle around back onto the beach  And the spectators would sit there on the beach side and watch these cars as they raced on    literally on the beach     Bill Jr  spent his young life around the racetrack and working toward the legacy his father had begun to build  He worked on cars  helped out during races  and beginning in 1956  he worked every day of the week for more than a year on the construction of the Daytona International Speedway     In 1972  Bill Jr  took the reins of the racing organization that his father had helped to found in 1948 and took the risks and made the decisions that took NASCAR to a whole new level  The International Motorsports Hall of Fame describes it this way  Other than the founding of NASCAR itself  Bill Jr  s appointment to leadership is probably the most significant event in the history of the sanctioning body  As rule maker  promoter  ambassador  and salesman  France has set the standard by which all other motorsports are measured  He has taken it from a regional sport to a national sport and nurtured it on television which culminated with a record setting  2 4 billion contract  He served for a quarter century leading NASCAR to unbelievable heights and set the stage for what it has become today     I know I speak for the hundreds of thousands of fans  the drivers  the pit crews  and anyone and everyone who enjoys NASCAR as well as Floridians and Daytona Beach residents when I say a well deserved thank you to Bill France  Jr   for making our weekends a lot more exciting  more enjoyable  and a lot faster  Florida thanks you for your vision  Bill  We will miss you  but you leave behind a legacy that we will never forget                                                                    June 2007 News Releases
