                               MARTINEZ  RIVIERA BEACH FIRE RESCUE TO RECEIVE  1 242 000 DHS GRANT  October 18  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez today announced that the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS  has awarded  1 242 000 to the Riviera Beach Fire Rescue located in Riviera Beach  Florida  The funds are part of DHSs Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response  SAFER  grants program  a program to help departments increase their number of trained firefighters   The funds from DHS are critical to helping fire departments throughout the nation achieve greater preparedness for the important work they do  Riviera Beach Fire Rescue serves several critical roles in the community and these funds will help ensure they have the resources they need to achieve their missions  Martinez said  The award is testament to the quality and important role of Riviera Beach Fire Rescue   Riviera Beach Fire Rescue serves a community of more than 30 000 people  The department consists of four fire stations located throughout the city and employs 77 certified members who offer a variety of rescue and safety services to the community   Funds under DHSs SAFER program are intended to support the hiring of firefighters and the recruitment and retention of volunteer firefighters  The goal of any SAFER grant is to enhance the ability of grantees to attain and maintain 24 hour staffing and to assure that their communities have adequate protection from fire and fire related hazards                                                                    October
