                               ORLANDO VA HOSPITAL PROJECT ADVANCES   December 11  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that S  3421  the Veterans Affairs authorization bill  passed the Senate this weekend and will be sent to the White House where President Bush is expected to sign it  Included in the bill is an extension of authority for the new Orlando VA hospital allowing the project to proceed   The priority of building this hospital has never been in doubt but this extension underscores the commitment of Congress and the Administration to move this project forward  said Martinez  This hospital remains my highest priority for veterans in Florida and I look forward to the site selection announcement early next year   The VA remains committed to this project  they know the costs and are prepared to move forward  said Martinez who noted that he recently discussed the project with Secretary of Veterans Affairs Jim Nicholson   The Orlando VA Hospital is authorized for  377 7 Million  In addition  there are four other major VA projects for Florida  including   Construction of patient privacy deficiencies at the Department of Veterans Affairs Medical Center in Gainesville   85 2 Million    Construction of an Ambulatory Surgery   Outpatient Diagnostic Support Center in the Gulf South Submarket of Veterans Integrated Service Network  VISN  8 and completion of Phase I land purchase  Lee County   107 85 Million    Upgrade of essential electrical distribution systems at the Department of Veterans Affairs Medical Center  VAMC  in Tampa   49 Million   and   Expansion of the Spinal Cord Injury Center addition at VAMC  Tampa   7 1 Million                                                                    December 2006 News
