                               MARTINEZ APPLAUDS VA SITE SELECTION FOR NATIONAL CEMETERY SERVING NORTH FLORIDA   September 14  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today applauded the selection of a site for the new national cemetery serving North Florida  Senator Martinez was informed by the Secretary of Veterans Affairs  VA  that the site selected is the city owned property north of the Jacksonville International Airport     This is great news for the veterans of Jacksonville and the region  I am pleased the site selection is complete and the project will move forward  Martinez said  North Florida veterans are honored and will be well served by this site     Congressman Ander Crenshaw  R Jacksonville  also commented on the critical nature of moving forward with the project     Todays announcement represents the culmination of more than 3 years of work since I first proposed legislation which passed Congress  to locate a National Veterans Cemetery in Jacksonville  Jacksonville is home to one of the largest veterans populations in the nation  but the closest cemetery is over 100 miles away  Our veterans and their families deserve a site that is close to home   now they will have just that  said Crenshaw     The Secretary of Veterans Affairs will announce the site selection this afternoon  The property to be acquired is an approximately 568 acre tract of land which the city of Jacksonville owns  According to the VA  the cemetery will begin providing burials for veterans beginning in spring of 2008
