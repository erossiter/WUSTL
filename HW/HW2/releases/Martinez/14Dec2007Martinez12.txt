                               MARTINEZ  FHA MODERNIZATION PASSES SENATE Measure will help FHA combat subprime mortgage crisis December 14  2007   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today commended colleagues for voting to overhaul and modernize the Federal Housing Administration  FHA   In a floor speech this morning  Senator Martinez said updating the FHAs standards will provide it with the flexibility needed to combat the subprime mortgage crisis and support sound lending in the twenty first century  The reform bill was approved by the Senate by a vote of 93 to 1     Congress cannot simply sit back and ignore what is happening in our housing market  As another round of subprime loans prepares to reset and more and more families face the reality of foreclosure  we have the opportunity to use the resources of the federal government in a reasonable and responsible way to mitigate future losses  said Martinez  the former Secretary of the U S  Housing and Urban Development agency  The reforms in this bill will empower FHA to reach more families that need help  first time homebuyers  minorities  and those with low to moderate incomes     The reform includes broader underwriting standards so that current homeowners can refinance before they lose their homes     Over the past seventy two years  FHA has been a mortgage industry leader  helping over thirty four million Americans become homeowners at no cost to the taxpayers  said Martinez  With this legislation  weve built an even better program that compliments conventional mortgage products and allows FHA to continue to serve hardworking  creditworthy Americans                                                                   December 2007 News Releases             Browse by
