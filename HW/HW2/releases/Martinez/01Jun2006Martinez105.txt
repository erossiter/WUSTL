                               SENATOR MARTINEZ ENCOURAGES ALTERNATIVE ENERGY PRODUCTION IN FLORIDA  June 1  2006   TAMPA  FL   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today met with representatives of the alternative fuels community in Kinder Morgans facility at the Port of Tampa to discuss efforts of alternative energy production in Florida     Its critical that our country explore new ways to produce alternative forms of energy from our natural crops  and Florida ought to be at the forefront of this movement  Martinez said  Florida is a consumer state  and by developing and distributing ethanol and other bio fuels we can make a great contribution   Senator Martinez worked to include a provision in the 2005 Energy Policy Act that granted  36 million to sugar cane producing states as part of the Sugar Cane Ethanol Program    9 million was designated for Florida  The program provides funding to help support farmers in the research and development of sugarcane conversion into ethanol   Florida has the strategic capability to become an ethanol leader  The Sugar Cane Ethanol Program will allow farmers in Florida to become involved in the burgeoning alternative fuels market in the U S   ultimately improving energy independence   Easing supply issues is a national priority  and with the continued research and development of ethanol and bio fuels we will progressively move beyond the current crisis  Martinez said    Under the Energy Bills Renewable Fuels Standard  the U S  fuel blend must contain 7 5 billion gallons of ethanol by 2012
