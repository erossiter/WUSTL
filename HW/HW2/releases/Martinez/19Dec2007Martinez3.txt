                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              MARTINEZ  1 MILLION FLORIDIANS TO AVOID HIGHER IRS TAX RATE Alternative Minimum Tax measure passes House December 19  2007   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today commended his House colleagues for passing an Alternative Minimum Tax  AMT  patch  a measure that will help as many as 1 million Floridians avoid a substantial tax increase    With this patch  as many as 1 million hard working Floridians will avoid an excessive and unintended tax hike  Martinez said  No longer subject to an obscure and outdated tax rule  more Floridians can expect to keep more of their hard earned dollars     Under current law  individuals pay the higher of their AMT or regular tax  Introduced as part of the Tax Reform of 1969  the AMT was an effort to close tax shelters for the wealthiest Americans  Since the AMT was not indexed to inflation and recent tax cuts  more middle income taxpayers  about 70 percent of tax filers with incomes between  75 000 and  100 000  would have been subject to this tax     The measure now goes to the White House for the Presidents signature                                                                  December 2007 News Releases             Browse by                                                                          Current record
