                               MARTINEZ  FHA MODERNIZATION MOVES FORWARD  September 19  2007   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today voted in favor of overhauling the Federal Housing Administration  FHA   Senator Martinez said updating the FHAs standards will provide it with the flexibility needed to support sound lending in the twenty first century  The reform bill was approved by the Senate Banking Committee this morning by a vote of 20 to 1     Congress cannot simply sit back and ignore what is happening in our housing market  As another round of subprime loans prepare to reset and more and more families face the reality of foreclosure  we have the opportunity to use the resources of the federal government in a reasonable and responsible way to mitigate future losses  said Martinez  the former Secretary of the U S  Housing and Urban Development agency  The reforms in this bill will empower FHA to reach more families that need help  first time homebuyers  minorities  and those with low to moderate incomes     The reform includes broader underwriting standards so that current homeowners can refinance before they lose their homes     Over the past seventy two years  FHA has been a mortgage industry leader  helping over thirty four million Americans become homeowners at no cost to the taxpayers  said Martinez  With this legislation  weve built an even better program that compliments conventional mortgage products and allows FHA to continue to serve hardworking  creditworthy Americans     The FHA provides mortgage insurance on loans made by FHA approved lenders throughout the United States  FHA insures mortgages on single family  multifamily  manufactured homes and hospitals  It is the largest insurer of mortgages in the world insuring over 34 million properties since its inception in 1934     The bill now moves to the full Senate for consideration  A similar measure has passed the U S  House of Representatives
