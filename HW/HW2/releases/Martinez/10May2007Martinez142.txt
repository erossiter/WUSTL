                               MARTINEZ INTRODUCES SPACEPORT EQUALITY ACT  May 10  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today introduced the Spaceport Equality Act of 2007  a measure to promote the greater establishment of the space transportation industry by offering and expanding financial incentives  Introducing the bill with Senator Martinez as original cosponsors are senators Jeff Bingaman  D NM   Kay Bailey Hutchison  R TX   Pete Domenici  R NM   Dianne Feinstein  D CA   Elizabeth Dole  R NC   and Bill Nelson  D FL      This will give the space transportation industry the same financial incentives currently granted to airports  seaports  and rail  spurring private investment and modernizing our launch facilities  said Martinez  With global interest in space technology on the rise  spaceports are a vital component of the infrastructure needed to maintain the U S s competitive edge in the international space arena     Congressman Dave Weldon  R FL 15th  today introduced the House companion bill     By allowing spaceports to finance new construction 100 percent tax free  the Spaceport Equality Act will stimulate investment in building and modernizing our nations space launch facilities  said Weldon  This will not only enhance our national security  it will also create new jobs in places like Port Canaveral  where thousands of my constituents work     Most U S  space launch facilities are operated by NASA and the Air Force  where priority for launches at these facilities is given to government projects  Spaceports provide additional launch infrastructure  increasing commercial research and development of space technology     Florida stands to benefit from investment in this industry  Increased spaceport infrastructure and activity in the state could mean as much as  30 million in additional economic activity by the year 2015  To keep our competitive edge  we simply must invest in these facilities  Martinez concluded
