                               MARTINEZ  CRIMINALS BARRED FROM RECEIVING BENEFITS OF IMMIGRATION BILL  May 17  2006   WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today applauded the Senates passage of an amendment to the Hagel Martinez Immigration Reform bill  S 2611  that will bar criminals from receiving benefits of the Hagel Martinez Comprehensive Immigration Reform Act of 2006  The amendment would exclude immigrants with criminal records from being placed on a pathway to citizenship   This amendment underscores the bills language and intent that criminals will not have a path to citizenship  said Martinez  This bill is about security and opportunity and felons promote neither  Adding this provision to the overall bill strengthens Hagel Martinez and reinforces the fact that were serious about securing our nation   The amendment prohibits individuals convicted of a felony or three misdemeanors from participating in a program that places them on a pathway to citizenship  This amendment does not create any new offenses or criminalize illegal immigrants   Senator Martinez cosponsored the amendment authored by Senator Jon Kyl  R AZ   Other cosponsors include Senators John Cornyn  R TX   John McCain  R AZ   Bill Frist  R TN   George Allen  R VA   John Thune  R SD   Lindsey Graham  R SC   and Landrieu  D LA   The measure was approved by the Senate  99 0
