                               MARTINEZ  SENATE APPROVES  45 7 MILLION FOR CUBA DEMOCRACY ASSISTANCE   September 6  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today introduced an amendment to fund Cuba Democracy Assistance programs at  45 7 million dollars  Martinez introduced the amendment with Senators Bob Menendez  D NJ   John Ensign  R NV   and Bill Nelson  D FL   and the funding was approved by the Senate as an amendment to the Department of State and Foreign Operations Appropriations Act for fiscal year 2008     Cuba is living through a transitional moment  a historic moment  After half a century  this regime belies legitimacy or anything close to democratic rule  My hope is that by restoring these funds  we will help the Cuban people create the conditions similar to those created in Eastern Europe where through our assistance the forces of democracy and freedom prevailed  Martinez said     These funds are used to provide needed humanitarian assistance to Cuban civil society and the pro democracy movement     Last month  I talked with members of the opposition in Cuba  who hope and dream of the day when they will have the opportunity to speak freely  where human rights are observed  and where they have the opportunity to elect their own leaders  These people pleaded with us to assist them  Martinez said  What happens in Cuba is important to the United States  It is important to our national security  At a time when we see the opportunity for political change  this would be the wrong time to cut back and to diminish our commitment
