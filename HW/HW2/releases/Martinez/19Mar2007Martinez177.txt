                               MARTINEZ PRAISES MORE THAN  35 MILLION IN FEDERAL RELIEF AND RECOVERY ASSISTANCE FOR TORNADO VICTIMS   March 19  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today praised the approval of more than  35 million in federal assistance for tornado recovery in Florida  The U S  Department of Homeland Securitys Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  has committed  17 2 million to families and communities in disaster assistance  emergency service  debris removal and infrastructure repair  The Small Business Administration  SBA  has approved more than  18 million in low interest disaster loans for homeowners  renters and businesses     The tornados caused tremendous destruction throughout central Florida  I am pleased that FEMA and SBA are proving immediate and long term assistance  so Floridians affected by the disaster can continue rebuilding their lives  Martinez said     Numerous tornados swept through Central Florida in December and February  Residents of Lake  Seminole  Sumter and Volusia counties continue to be eligible to apply for a wide range of state and federal disaster recovery programs  including assistance to help pay for temporary housing  home repairs and other serious disaster related expenses  SBA low interest loans may be available to cover residential and business losses not compensated by insurance  The deadline to apply for assistance from the February tornados is April 4  2007  Those affected by the December tornadoes have until April 9  2007 to apply  For more information  residents can contact
