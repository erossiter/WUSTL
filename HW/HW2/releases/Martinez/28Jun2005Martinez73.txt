                               MARTINEZ  INVENTORY PROVISION TARNISHES ENERGY BILL  June 28  2005   WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today voted against the Senate version of the energy bill calling the overall package    tarnished    by the oil and natural gas inventory provision      This is a very good bill that unfortunately contains a very bad provision     said Senator Martinez     The Outer Continental Shelf inventory provision harms Florida  It sets the worst precedent  It harms coastal states that oppose offshore drilling     The Landrieu provision contained in the Senate version of the energy bill requires the Department of the Interior to conduct an inventory of all natural gas and oil resources contained in the Outer Continental Shelf of the United States  Preliminary estimates from the Minerals Management Service indicate the cost of doing such an inventory of United States coastlines  excluding Alaska  could exceed  1 billion      This inventory sends a message to states and oil companies that the federal moratorium on offshore drilling is meaningless     said Senator Martinez     This inventory provision tells the people of states like Florida that it   s only a matter of time     The measure that passed the Senate must still be reconciled with the House version  Senator Martinez remains hopeful the inventory provision can be stricken during the conference committee negotiations      I hold out hope that this bill will go to conference and come back a better bill so that I can support it     said Senator Martinez     My vote depends on whether the final bill respects Florida   s rights  and the rights of other states  to keep the protections that blanket our coastal waters
