                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              MARTINEZ STATEMENT ON US CUBA MIGRATION POLICY CHANGES  August 11  2006   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today expressed support for the Department of Homeland Securitys changes to US Cuba Migration policy  The policy will encourage legal and safe migration  allow medical professionals in third countries to seek admission into the United States and will prohibit entry to those in the regime that have committed human rights abuses    Senator Martinez said  The changes are very important during these tense moments of hope and uncertainty in Cuba  For too many years our migration policy has been under the de facto control of the Cuban regime  They have been the final arbiters of who would receive a U S  visa under the policy set out in the 1994 Migration Accords  That is unacceptable   Today the United States has made clear its intent to provide safe and legal means to promote family reunification without exposing Cubans to the risks of a dangerous voyage in the Florida straits   I support U S  policy changes that promote and encourage democratic change on the island  My hope is that there will soon come a day when all Cubans can be reunited in freedom and that their only means to express themselves openly will not require risking their lives                                                                   August 2006 News Releases             Browse by                                                                                                                                  Current record
