                               MARTINEZ ANNOUNCES MORE THAN  2 6 MILLION FOR FLORIDA INSTITUTIONS  August 9  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced three National Oceanic  Atmospheric Administration  NOAA  grants totaling  2 666 087 for Florida state and academic institutions  The Florida Department of Environmental Protection will receive  2 042 039  the University of Florida has been awarded  500 000  and the University of Miami will receive  124 048     NOAAs continued substantial investment in Florida speaks highly of the quality of our institutions  Martinez said  All of these projects will lead to a greater understanding of our oceans and atmosphere and how they react under certain environmental conditions     The grant awarded to the Florida Department of Environmental Protection will support building of the Apalachicola Natural Estuarine Research Reserve Coastal Education Center to increase visitation and improve work space  The center will include a visitor center space  multi purpose room  media room  amphitheatre  as well as office and laboratory space     The University of Floridas funding will be used for a Regional Storm Surge and Inundation Model Testbed  which will facilitate the transition from research and development of storm surge and inundation models to operations by federal  state and county agencies  The Testbed will validate  compare  and integrate existing model activities in the region and explore ways to provide potential enhancements to existing hazard maps     The University of Miami project will advance understanding of how noise  due to internal oceanic and atmospheric dynamics  impacts climate variability and predictability                                                                   August
