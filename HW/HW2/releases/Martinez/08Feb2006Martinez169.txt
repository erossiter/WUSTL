                               MARTINEZ  MMS PLAN LACKS PERMANENCY  February 8  2006   WASHINGTON  DC  U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today made comments in response to the Mineral Management Services Draft Proposed Outer Continental Shelf Oil and Gas Leasing Program for 2007  2012  Martinez said  Its not as good as we had hoped but not as bad as we feared  The main component missing is permanency for Florida  It does not provide any sort of permanent protection for Florida  So under this proposal  we would be dealing with the issue again in five years and losing yet more of the Gulf to further and further drilling  Senator Bill Nelson and I have been working toward protecting Floridas coastline permanently  Thats why last week it was so timely to introduce our legislation  the Permanent Protection for Florida Act of 2006  Our proposal is far superior for Florida and does provide permanent protection  We are quibbling over a substantial area of the ocean floor but these are not insurmountable differences  The main problem  both in the MMS proposal and in Senator Pete Domenicis plan is the lack of permanency for the state of Florida   I am pleased that in my conversation today with Interior Secretary Gail Norton  she underscored the commitment she made to me that the Stovepipe area off the coast of the Florida Panhandle will remain under presidential moratorium at least until the year 2012  The proposal also respects the military mission line  What the MMS proposal does not do is offer permanency and thats what I told Secretary Norton that we needed in order to have an agreement  Overall  this proposal shows how close we are in terms of where to open the 181 area but there remains a gulf of difference between the MMS proposal and permanent protection  At the end of the day this isnt about drilling  this isnt about oil companies  this is about doing what is best for Florida                                                                 February 2006 News
