                               MARTINEZ CALLS FOR SANCTIONS AGAINST SUDANESE GOVERNMENT  March 23  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL   along with 33 other Senators  sent an open letter to President Bush calling for him to pressure the United Nations Security Council  UNSC  to impose multilateral economic sanctions on the Sudanese government for their refusal to comply with an earlier request to have 20 000 peacekeepers enter the region  Since the UNSC passed the resolution passed last August  only 200 U N  personnel have been deployed to Darfur     The letter states     There are political risks to advancing this strategy  but we urge you to weigh those risks against the verdict of history if we fail to try  If the Security Council does not act  the United States should engage with our allies to create a coalition that will impose economic penalties on the Sudanese government  The United States has already implemented a number of unilateral sanctions  and we understand that you are considering still more  a development that we would applaud  However  the real key is to changing Khartoums behavior most likely lies in multilateral sanctions  especially those aimed at the Sudanese oil industry     We encourage you to put this matter before the United Nations Security Council as soon as possible  A threatened veto should not silence us     We know that you share our commitment tot this issue and we commend your courage in recognizing this genocide for what it is  We look forward to continuing our efforts until a timely solution to the crisis in Darfur is found                                                                   March 2007 News Releases             Browse by
