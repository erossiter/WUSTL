                               MARTINEZ ANNOUNCES  4 3 MILLION FOR HURRICANE WILMA RELIEF IN FLORIDA  November 3  2005   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced  4 3 million will be granted to the state of Florida for labor costs associated with Hurricane Wilma recovery efforts  U S  Secretary of Labor Elaine Chao shared the good news with Senator Martinez during a phone conversation earlier today    The  4 3 million designated in the National Emergency Grant will help to create nearly 600 new jobs to help with disaster relief efforts  Hurricane Wilma affected Floridians in 20 counties throughout the state  and people need help to be able to get back up on their feet  Martinez said    The emergency funds  granted to the Florida Agency for Workforce Innovation  will be used for temporary employment on projects that provide food  clothing  shelter  and other humanitarian assistance for disaster victims    Additionally  the project will also provide employment and training services to eligible dislocated workers in the following counties  Brevard  Broward  Charlotte  Collier  DeSoto  Glades  Hardee  Hendry  Highlands  Indian River  Lee  Martin  Miami Dade  Monroe  Okeechobee  Osceola  Palm Beach  Polk  St  Lucie  and Sarasota Counties   This grant will allow state and local officials to use the resources we have effectively  Martinez said  We want the whole world to know  Florida is open for business                                                                 November 2005 News Releases             Browse by
