                               MARTINEZ   100 MILLION COMING FOR FLORIDA FOR CANKER COMPENSATION  June 8  2006   WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today recognized the United States Department of Agricultures  USDA  announcement of  100 million in citrus canker compensation as beneficial for Floridas commercial citrus growers but less than adequate in the face of an interim rule that would quarantine Floridas fresh citrus shipments to other citrus producing states for the 2006 2007 season   I am pleased the USDA has taken action to release this compensation funding  This assistance is critical to Floridas citrus producers who have incurred tremendous expenses to comply with federally mandated citrus canker eradication efforts  These funds will help make producers whole for efforts already undertaken  said Martinez  While I am pleased with Secretary Johanns leadership on this issue and the announcement of the release of funds  there are still a number of compensation claims that remain outstanding  and I remain concerned about further costs imposed on this industry through additional federal mandates and restrictions  I look forward to working with the USDA to make sure Floridas citrus growers remain competitive   Senator Martinez yesterday asked USDA Secretary Mike Johanns to suspend further consideration of an interim rule that would prohibit Florida citrus shipments to other citrus producing states and territories including Louisiana  Alabama  Guam  Puerto Rico  and the Virgin Islands on top of Arizona  New Mexico  Texas  and California  The USDA informed Senator Martinez the interim rule could go into effect almost immediately but USDA will solicit public comments before a final rule is set in place
