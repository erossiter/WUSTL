                               MARTINEZ RECOGNIZES 20th ANNIVERSARY OF HOMELESS ASSISTANCE PROGRAM Senator Martinez co sponsored legislation to raise awareness through designated month July 19  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today marked the 20th anniversary of the Department of Housing and Urban Developments  HUD  McKinney Vento Homeless Assistance Program by calling for the programs continued support with expanded emphasis on eradicating the problem  Originally signed into law by President Ronald Reagan in 1987  this program began by providing the homeless with emergency relief provisions for shelter  food  mobile health care  and transitional housing     This is a bittersweet momentwhile great progress has been made through this program  chronic homelessness continues to plague our nation  Martinez said  We must continue our commitment to support and expand these types of programs that have helped tens of thousand of homeless people by providing safe havens and placing them in permanent housing  This investment has proven we are making serious inroads toward ending this challenging problem     In addition  Senator Martinez recently was the lead co sponsor of a resolution designating November as National Homeless Youth Awareness Month  The measure  which passed the Senate earlier this month  encourages additional work to help keep young people off the streets     This effort brings much needed attention to a critical issue  There are an estimated 39 000 homeless young people in Florida and thousands more nationwide  Sen  Martinez said  Im hopeful we can build awareness  promote prevention  and encourage good hearted organizations to continue their efforts to reduce the number of young people facing the nightmare of homelessness     According to the National Alliance to End Homelessness  there are between 1 3 million and 2 8 million homeless children across America  comprising nearly one third of the homeless population
