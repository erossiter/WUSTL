                               MARTINEZ  HEALTH IT BILL WILL HELP BRING HEALTHCARE INTO 21ST CENTURY  November 18  2005   WASHINGTON  DC  U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today said passage of S  1418  The Wired for Health Care Quality Act  would encourage better quality health care by helping local and regional health plans share medical information    The Senate passed S 1418 by unanimous consent late last evening  The legislation was originally introduced in July of this year by Senators Frist  Clinton  Enzi  Kennedy and Martinez    President Bush recently announced his goal to integrate healthcare networks into one central and secure system by 2015  Computer physician order entry  e prescribing  preventative reminders  and prescription bar code scanning are essential elements of the integration    A secure and integrated health information system will allow patients and health care providers universal access to the same set of current medical data  said Senator Martinez  By delivering the most accurate and up to date data in a timely manner  we will enable health care providers to make better and more informed medical decisions   The bill creates a National Coordinator of Health Information Technology  In doing so the legislation will help create a collaborative to adopt health IT standards  offers grant programs for individual providers to encourage linkage to a broader network and authorizes appropriations for the grant programs  Finally  The Wired for Health Care Quality Act establishes clear standards for quality measures   This bill encourages the development of interoperability standards The new legislation will enable the coordination and interaction of health IT systems    saving time  money  resources  and countless lives  said Martinez   This bill is essential for national coordination and supports Governor Bushs recent efforts to connect Floridas healthcare system  Combining his Health Information Advisory Board with federal support    Floridas doctors  hospitals  insurers and other providers can bring the healthcare system into the 21st century  Martinez concluded
