                               MARTINEZ  IRS EXTENDS FILING DEADLINE FOR RESIDENTS OF SOUTHEASTERN FLORIDA   September 2  2005   Orlando  FL  September 2  2005   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the Internal Revenue Service has approved a filing extension for residents of Broward  Miami Dade  and Monroe counties   In a letter to IRS Commissioner Mark Everson  Senator Martinez requested the extension in light of the power and communications disruptions related to Hurricane Katrina   As you are aware  Hurricane Katrina made landfall in Southeastern Florida  Thousands of residents have been severely affected by power outages  telephone service disruptions  and physical damage to their property and vehicles  Hurricane Katrina has not only disrupted the personal lives of the residents of Florida  it has also interrupted the normal conduct of business   I am asking that the Internal Revenue Service extend the September 15  2005  deadline to file tax returns and submit tax payments to October 31  2005   This afternoon the IRS announced it is giving residents of three Florida counties  Miami Dade  Broward  and Monroe  an extension until October 31  2005 to file tax returns and make payments  The IRS has also extended filing deadlines for residents in hurricane affected areas of Alabama  Mississippi  and Louisiana
