                               MARTINEZ ANNOUNCES MORE THAN  53 MILLION IN FUNDING FOR FORT PIERCE COURTHOUSE  March 15  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the Government Services Administration  GSA  will allocate over  53 million for the construction of a new courthouse in Fort Pierce  FL  The funds for this critical project were included in the FY 2007 continuing resolution  the measure funding the federal government through the rest of Fiscal Year 2007  which Senator Martinez supported     I am extremely pleased that the GSA has put the funds forward  allowing the construction of a new courthouse in Fort Pierce  a city that has experienced tremendous growth  For far too long  the Fort Pierce court system has been working out of sub standard  unsecured facilities throughout the city to meet the needs of its citizens  Martinez said  With this new courthouse  all of the court and related agencies will finally be under one roof     The courts and related agencies are currently located in four leased locations in downtown Fort Pierce  The GSA plan would construct a 123 400 sq  foot courthouse that would meet the 10 year space needs of the courts and accommodate the 30 year growth needs of the courts in Fort Pierce                                                                   March 2007 News Releases             Browse
