                               MARTINEZ  FLORIDA FIRST RESPONDERS TO RECEIVE MORE THAN  540 000  February 8  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS  has awarded more than  540 000 in grants to five Florida fire departments and rescue services  The grants are part of DHSs 2006 Assistance to Firefighters Grants  AFG  program  designed to help departments and emergency medical service organizations improve their ability to protect the public and first responder personnel from fire and related hazards     Equipping our fire departments with the best knowledge and technology available will help them provide the most advanced protection to our citizens  I thank the Department of Homeland Security for continuing their commitment to helping our departments protect our citizens  Martinez said      Since 2001  Assistance to Firefighters grants have enhanced the ability of first responders to protect the communities they serve from fire and fire related hazards  Funds under this program are intended to support first responder training  provide adequate firefighting EMS equipment  including rescue vehicles  protective gear  as well as support the wellness and fitness of firefighters     Floridas first responders receiving grants    Department  Pace Volunteer Fire Department   Amount   169 200
