                               MARTINEZ PRAISES USDA WAIVER OF DISASTER ASSISTANCE PENALTY  August 27  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today praised the U S  Department of Agricultures  USDA  decision to waive the repayment of Florida Disaster Assistance Program  FDP  funds provided to agriculture producers following the 2004 hurricane season     During the 2004 hurricane season  the USDA provided immediate and necessary disaster assistance to Floridas devastated farming industry  Martinez said  While I was grateful for this assistance for Florida  the recent notice of repayment created confusion among our growers and would have put them in financial distress  I commend the USDAs decision to give our farmers some flexibility in meeting the repayment requirements     Last week  Senators Martinez and Bill Nelson  D FL  joined Florida Representatives Adam Putnam  R Bartow   Vern Buchanan  R Sarasota   Tom Feeney  R Oviedo   Mario Diaz Balart  R Miami   and Tim Mahoney  D Palm Beach Gardens  in sending a letter to USDA Secretary Mike Johanns asking for this waiver     USDA states that repayment will be waived as long as producers provide evidence that they purchased 2008 Federal crop insurance or noninsured crop disaster assistance  NAP  on all crops for which a FDP payment was received  In the event that the 2008 sales closing date for crop insurance or NAP has passed  evidence for 2009 coverage will also be acceptable     Recently  3 326 producers received notice from USDA requiring recipients of FDP funds to prove they had crop insurance for the next year  or face the repayment of all disaster assistance plus interest  More than 2 000 recipients of this letter could not prove linkage  repayment would have totaled more than  24 million                                                                     August 2007 News Releases             Browse by
