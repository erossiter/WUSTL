                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              SENATOR MARTINEZ EXPRESSES DISAPPOINTMENT AT HUMAN RIGHTS COUNCIL DECISION  June 21  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today made the following statement regarding the United Nations Human Rights Councils decision to discontinue monitoring the human rights situation in Cuba     Martinez said     I am extremely disappointed with the decision this week by the UN Human Rights Council in Geneva to not extend the existing mandate on Cuba  I am also dismayed at the actions taken by the Mexican Representative at the Council  the procedural moves resulted in the silencing of countries such as Poland and the Czech Republic who were simply attempting to stand up for freedom  His actions discredit this institution  The new Human Rights Council begins with no credibility on this vital issue     To in any way infer by this decision that the human rights situation has improved or that abuses have subsided would be an insult the victims of abuse in Cuba  It is unfortunate that this new body has chosen to interfere with a process that could have proved effective  The fact that the Cuban Regime is hailing this as a victory while they still have prisoners of conscience sitting in its gulag makes clear how seriously flawed this decision has been                                                                   June 2007 News Releases             Browse by                                                                                                      Current record
