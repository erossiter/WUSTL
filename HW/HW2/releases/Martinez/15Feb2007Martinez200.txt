                               MARTINEZ  DHS ANNOUNCES SITE SELECTION FOR NEW ORLANDO IMMIGRATION FIELD OFFICE  February 15  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced the Department of Homeland Security has awarded the lease for a new immigration facility in Orlando  The 37 000 square foot Orlando Field Office will be constructed on a 5 86 acre site at Lee Vista Boulevard and Corporate Centre Boulevard and will house 82 employees     Central Florida has a broad and diverse immigrant community and this new facility will help handle the caseload of those seeking better and more efficient access to immigration services  said Martinez  I thank Director Gonzalez for working to locate this facility in Orlando and build it in such a way that it stands as a symbol of hope and freedom for those wishing to become part of the rich fabric of our nation     United States Citizenship and Immigration Services  USCIS  Director Emilio T  Gonzalez notified Senator Martinez of the decision this afternoon  The facility is estimated to be ready to occupy in late summer 2008     In 2005  the current Orlando field office handled an estimated 13 700 citizenship applications and 14 000 legalization requests     Oxford Development was the successful offeror with a team comprised of Leo Daly Architect   Engineer and W G  Mills as their Design Build General Contractor  Over the next 18 months this development team will work in conjunction with the General Services Administration and the USCIS Field Support Center Office of Facilities to bring this office to the greater Orlando area                                                                   February 2007 News Releases             Browse by
