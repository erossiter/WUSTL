                               MARTINEZ  SOUTH FLORIDA AWARDED MORE THAN  8 MILLION FOR HURRICANE PREPAREDNESS AND DISATER RELIEF  February 15  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the U S  Department of Homeland Securitys Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  has awarded more than  4 5 million to the Mount Sinai Medical Center and more than  2 million to Miami Dade County in Public Assistance grants  In addition  FEMA has awarded more than  1 5 million to the Doctors Hospital in Coral Gables in Hazard Mitigation grants     These awards are great news for the state of Florida and for the facilities who serve our constituents in times of need  This assistance is important for our states improvements in hurricane safety  preparedness  and the ability to rapidly respond during emergency situations  said Martinez     FEMAs Hazard Mitigation and Public Assistance grant programs are monetary federal assistance that enables states to initiate projects that will protect against injury and loss of life  In addition  these grants provide financial reimbursements for disaster relief  FEMAs Public Assistance grant program is set up to distribute federal funds to states  local governments  and private non profit organizations to aid in disaster relief     FEMAs Hazard Mitigation grant program is managed by the state and provides funds for projects that make preventative improvements to their facilities to protect life and property                                                                   February 2007 News Releases
