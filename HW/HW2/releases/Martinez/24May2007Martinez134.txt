                               MARTINEZ ANNOUNCES TWO NEW VA CLINICS DESIGNATED FOR PUTNAM AND JACKSON COUNTIES  May 24  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the Department of Veterans Affairs  VA  plans to open two new community based outpatient clinics in Jackson and Putnam counties  VA Secretary James Nicholson notified Senator Martinez of the decision this afternoon     Floridas veterans deserve the highest quality care and will be well served by these clinics  Martinez said  Often the biggest barrier to accessing quality care is the distance to a veterans facility  Locating clinics closer to the populations they serve is a great step toward advancing the health needs of those whove served our country  I commend Secretary Nicholson for helping meet the needs of our veterans     According to the VA  the Jackson County clinic will serve 3 600 existing patients and 310 new patients  the facility is anticipated to be open in 8 months  The Putnam County clinic will serve 12 600 existing patients and 1 500 new patients  the facility is anticipated to be open in 14 months     VA is committed to providing world class health care to the men and women who have served this nation  VA Secretary Jim Nicholson said  These new clinics will bring VAs top notch care closer to the veterans who have earned it     Services at both facilities will include comprehensive primary care  health promotion  maintenance  routine urgent care procedures and education  Mental Health Services will provide screening and prevention for mental disorders  and diagnostic evaluation for mental illness and substance abuse  psychotherapy and or psychosocial counseling for mental disorders  and referral for inpatient or residential care  direct care  or access to consultation for special emphasis and or complex problems                                                                  May 2007 News Releases             Browse by
