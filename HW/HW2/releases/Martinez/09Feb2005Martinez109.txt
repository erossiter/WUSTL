                               MARTINEZ  NOT THE TIME TO EASE AGRICULTURAL RESTRICTIONS ON CUBA  February 9  2005   Washington    U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today said loosening agricultural trade restrictions in Cuba would have no effect other than perpetuating Castro   s oppression of his citizens    most basic human rights   Martinez   s comments were made in response to legislation introduced this afternoon by Finance Committee members Senators Craig  Baucus  Roberts and Lugar which would ease restrictions on the sale of agricultural products to Cuba    Martinez said      Cuba remains one of the five remaining countries    along with Iran  Libya  North Korea and Syria     designated by the U S  Department of State as a state sponsor of international terrorism  It is a country whose regime provides safe haven to and cooperation with negative forces in Latin America and around the world      If that was not reason enough  when Castro continues to subject his own people to oppression and shows no signs of letting up on human rights abuses  it is not the right time to ease embargo restrictions whatsoever      Our focus needs to be on freeing dissidents and continuing to support the opposition movement within Cuba     not rewarding Castro and subsidizing and strengthening his totalitarian regime      We as lawmakers must remain steadfast in our commitment to freedom  democracy and human rights and to those individuals in Cuba who have shown their willingness to sacrifice their life for freedom
