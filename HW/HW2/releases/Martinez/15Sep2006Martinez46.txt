                               PANAMA CITY AIRPORT RELOCATION PROJECT RECEIVES FINAL FAA APPROVAL   September 15  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced the Panama City  Bay County International Airport relocation project has received final approval from the Federal Aviation Administration  FAA   The senator received word from FAA Administrator Marion Blakey that the Administration has favorably released their Record of Decision  ROD  allowing the airport relocation project to move forward with construction   This announcement means this project moves to the construction phase  This is great news for a project vital to the economic development of this region  said Martinez  The new airport will meet safety and expansion concerns and bring a component that is critical to this regions continued growth   After nearly three years of detailed analysis  the FAA approved the relocation of the Panama City  Bay County International Airport to the proposed site in West Bay  According to the ROD  This marks the completion of an extraordinarily thorough and objective analysis of the alternatives to address the deficiencies and constraints at the existing airport   After nearly a decade of extensive planning and public dialogue  the relocation of the airport will allow for the construction of a flexible  expandable  environmentally sensitive airport that meets all federal safety and design standards  Martinez concluded   This project has an estimated construction cost of  278 million  The project is expected to begin construction in early to mid
