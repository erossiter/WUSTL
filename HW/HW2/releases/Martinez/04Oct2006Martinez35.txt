                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              FRIST AND MARTINEZ VISIT BAGHDAD   October 4  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senate Majority Leader Bill Frist  M D    R Tenn   and Sen  Mel Martinez  R Fla   spent the last two days in Baghdad  Iraq  where they had the opportunity to meet with Iraqi President Jalal Talabani  Prime Minister Nuri al Maliki  Ambassador Zalmay Khalilzad and other Iraqi officials     Both senators also spent significant time with members of the United States armed forces and received multiple briefings from military personnel and government officials  Among their visits was Balad Airbase  one of the largest such facilities in Iraq  where branches of the military are working together in an impressive  coordinated effort to form a central nervous system for operations     Sen  Frist noted as a point of particular interest his meeting with Iraqi doctors  who discussed with him the difficulties of practicing their profession in the country and improvements they feel should be implemented to improve the nations healthcare system     Both Sens  Frist and Martinez had the opportunity to visit with troops from their respective home states  and each expressed his gratitude for the sacrifices they have made defending the cause of freedom     The final stop of the senators congressional delegation was Amman  Jordan  where they met with King Abdullah  They return to the United States tomorrow                                                                       October 2006 News Releases             Browse by                                                                                                                                  Current record
