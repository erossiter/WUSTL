                               MARTINEZ APPLAUDS SENATE AGREEMENT TO MOVE FORWARD ON WRDA  July 14  2006   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today commended Senate Majority Leader Bill Frist  R TN  and Senator James Inhofe  R OK  for scheduling floor consideration of S  728  the Water Resources Development Act of 2005  WRDA   Senator Frist announced WRDA will be up for debate and a likely vote on passage next week   Florida has a great number of priorities in this bill  Whether its Everglades restoration  shoring up the dike around Lake Okeechobee  or moving forward on port projects that are vital to our states commerce  we have to move forward  Martinez said  Ive impressed upon our leaders that we need to advance environmental restoration projects like the Indian River Lagoon and Picayune Strand  these are projects which will have a major impact on improving the overall health of the Everglades ecosystem   The last time WRDA was authorized was 2000  the reauthorization is approximately three years overdue  WRDA legislation provides the Army Corps of Engineers with the authority to study water resource problems  construct projects  and make major modifications to existing projects   The fact that this bill is coming to the floor is the product of bipartisan cooperation among members of the authorizing committee and I congratulate them on their work  Martinez added  Seeing this bill come to the floor is very good news for Florida and the nation and Im hopeful it means well see its completion before years end
