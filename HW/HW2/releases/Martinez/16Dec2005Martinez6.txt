                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              MARTINEZ DISAPPOINTED BY INACTION ON PATRIOT ACT REAUTHORIZATION   December 16  2005   Washington  DC  U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today expressed disappointment that the Senate has failed to move forward on reauthorizing the expiring provisions of the Patriot Act  Earlier this week the U S  House of Representatives passed the Patriot Act Reauthorization by a vote of 251 to 174  As a cabinet member during the 9 11 terrorist attacks  I am acutely aware of the challenges and hurdles our federal law enforcement officials face in protecting Americans from terrorists  These are tools our agencies need  said Martinez  This reauthorization allows law enforcement and intelligence agencies to share information that saves lives while protecting civil liberties  I strongly urge those of my Senate colleagues who are holding up the process to reconsider and act quickly on this measure because our national security demands it  Sixteen of the Patriot Acts provisions are set to expire at years end                                                                  December 2005 News Releases             Browse by                                                                                                                    Current record
