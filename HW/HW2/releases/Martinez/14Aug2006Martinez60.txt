                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              MARTINEZ  USDA TO PROVIDE RELOCATION ASSISTANCE TO HAWTHORNE VILLAGE RESIDENTS  August 14  2006   ORLANDO  FL   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  applauded todays decision by the U S  Department of Agriculture to provide funds to assist in the relocation of Hawthorne Village residents     In July  Martinez urged the USDAs Rural Development office to provide immediate relocation assistance to the residents of Hawthorne Villages  which is scheduled to be demolished due to uninhabitable conditions     Residents were initially denied relocation assistance from USDA  but were given a letter of priority to use at subsidized housing complexes operated in other rural communities in Florida  However  in many instances this would take Hawthorne Village residents far from jobs  families and supportive services     USDAs decision will provide  30 000 to the Florida State Rural Development Office to be used specifically for moving expenses for the remaining residents of Hawthorne Village     Martinez said     I am very pleased that USDA has gone above and beyond in ensuring Hawthorne Village residents have the resources they need to relocate  This is already a difficult time for this community  and I applaud the USDA for making this transition easier and providing this much needed assistance                                                                    August 2006 News Releases             Browse by                                                                                                                    Current record
