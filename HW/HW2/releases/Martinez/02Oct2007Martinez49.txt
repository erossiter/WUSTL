                               MARTINEZ  SENATE APRROVES LIFTING CONSTRUCTION MORATORIUM AT FORT BUCHANAN  October 2  2007   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced another positive development in the effort to end the construction moratorium at Fort Buchanan  Puerto Rico  Language allowing construction was included in the fiscal year 2008 Defense Authorization bill  which passed the Senate this week by a vote of 92 to 3     Lifting this moratorium will give those at Fort Buchanan the ability to modernize their facilities  allowing Puerto Rico to continue to play an important role in our national defense  said Martinez  author of the language ending the moratorium  These long overdue construction projects are vital as the base transitions to its new function as an Army Reserve Installation  I am pleased that the military will soon be able to make the necessary adjustments to the base enabling them to better carry out their mission at Fort Buchanan     The Martinez amendment strikes the language included in the National Defense Authorization Act for FY 2001  Section 1507  Repealing the moratorium will allow Fort Buchanan to move ahead with pre approved construction projects including the construction of a permanent fuel point for government vehicles  a new Directorate of Emergency and Security Building  and the initiation of improvements to the family housing area  It will also allow for the construction of a Military Entry Processing Station facility for recruitment                                                                  October
