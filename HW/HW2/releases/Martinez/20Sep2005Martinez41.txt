                               MARTINEZ BILL TO EXEMPT DISASTER PAYMENTS FROM INCOME SIGNED INTO LAW  September 20  2005   WASHINGTON  DC  U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that President Bush has signed into law a bill that allows low income individuals to receive disaster relief payments without the fear of losing other government assistance  House Resolution 804  an amendment to the National Flood Insurance Program  NFIP   was introduced by Senator Martinez in the U S  Senate and Richard Baker  R LA  in the U S  House of Representatives    The timing of this could not be better  Martinez said  As victims from Katrina work to put their lives back together and as Floridians work to rebuild from six destructive hurricanes in the past year  this law allows people to take full advantage of disaster recovery dollars    Administered by the Federal Emergency Management Agency  payments made under the NFIP for flood mitigation activities are currently counted as income or resources when determining eligibility for any federal means tested program  Allowing mitigation payments to be exempt from income or resources would increase the number of persons eligible for certain means tested programs  including Food Stamps and Medicaid   Until now  many families have refused to accept disaster mitigation payments in fear of losing vital assistance including certain federal education  nutrition  and healthcare benefits  Martinez said  This law corrects that situation by excluding from gross income any FEMA disaster relief payment for those in desperate need of relief    The NFIP was created by Congress in 1968 to address the increasing costs of taxpayer funded disaster relief for flood victims and the increasing amount of damage caused by
