                               MARTINEZ     LET   S END THE DEBATE     February 16  2006   WASHINGTON  DC  U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today made the following comments regarding the Senate Energy  Natural Resources Committees hearing on the Domenici Bingaman bill  S  2253   Since last years debate on the Energy Policy Act of 2005  we have been discussing the need to open up new areas of the Outer Continental Shelf for oil and gas exploration  The Florida Congressional Delegation has strongly resisted any attempt  legislative or otherwise  to weaken the Federal moratoria that has prevented oil and gas rigs from creeping towards our coast  However  with growing pressures to drill in Lease 181 including a new five year plan by Interior and various legislative initiatives threatening Floridas protections  including the one we are considering today  I have joined with Senator Nelson in introducing S  2239  the Permanent Protection for Florida Act   The Permanent Protection for Florida Act actually opens more area for exploration than S  2253  Our bill would open over 1 3 million acres of Lease Sale 181 and roughly 5 million acres south of 181 that were previously under moratorium in the Eastern Planning Area  Unlike all other proposals  the Permanent Protection for Florida Act provides the assurances the people of Florida want and need to protect our tourism industry  our ecosystem and the military training done off our Gulf  We believe this bill is a good compromise  By allowing significant exploration of areas that were once off limits to oil and gas companies  all we as Floridians ask in return is permanent protections for those areas closest to our shores   The people of Florida are willing to compromise and open new areas of the Gulf of Mexico  but we need to know that we will not have to fight ever encroaching resource development closer and closer to Floridas shores every year
