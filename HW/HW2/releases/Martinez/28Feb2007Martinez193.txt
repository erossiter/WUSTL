                               U S  SENATE BANKING COMMITTEE TO HOLD HEARING ON CREATION OF NATIONAL CATASTROPHE FUND  February 28  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today praised a commitment from Senate Banking Committee Chairman Christopher Dodd  D CT  to hold a hearing on the potential creation of a National Catastrophe Fund     An insurance crisis is unfolding affecting Florida families and businesses and we need to do something  The economic distress wrought by disasters affects us all  In light of past disasters  and in expectation of future ones  weve got to find a better way to spread and finance risk  Martinez said at a meeting of the Senate Banking Committee  I support the concept of a national catastrophe fund in order to stabilize and strengthen the insurance market and encourage proper disaster mitigation     In response to the senators statements  Senator Dodd agreed with the importance of the issue and stated  We will have a hearing on the subject matter you have raised  Its clearly one deserving of this committees attention  So we are planning on scheduling a hearing to talk about that  the ideas and suggestions coming out of the states most directly affected by it     With a national catastrophe fund  we have an opportunity to minimize risk nationwide and ensure our economy is able to absorb losses from large scale disasters  I think its time we take a more serious look at this issue  Martinez concluded     Chairman Dodds commitment came during a Senate Banking  Housing  Urban Affairs Committee hearing on
