                               MARTINEZ STATEMENT FOR NOMINATION OF EMILIO GONZALEZ  October 18  2005   WASHINGTON  DC  U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today delivered the following introductory statement before the Senate Judiciary Committee at the nomination hearing of Florida resident Emilio Gonzalez  Gonzalez is President Bushs nominee for Director of the Bureau of U S  Citizenship and Immigration Services at the Department of Homeland Security   Martinez said   Mr  Chairman  it is a great honor for me to be here with you this afternoon to introduce my good friend Emilio Gonzalez   The president has chosen a great person to lead our nations citizenship and immigration services  Emilio Gonzalez will bring a great depth of international and domestic policy experience to this position  His dedication to our nation began many years ago when as a young man he enlisted in the military  eventually making the rank of colonel    As the Director for Western Hemisphere Affairs for President George W  Bush  he served as a key national security advisor to both President Bush and Dr  Condoleezza Rice  giving counsel and advice on foreign policy issues  I mention this to point out the fact that Mr  Gonzales  through his hard work  has already earned the trust of the president and his key advisors and will be able to hit the ground running in this very important position   As an immigrant to this country himself  Emilio recognizes the great democratic and economic opportunities the United States offers those looking for a better way of life for themselves and their families  He takes the knowledge his own experience has afforded him and balances it with a keen understating of the National Security requirements that go hand in hand with these policies   He will know how to balance this important responsibility with compassion and understanding  Emilio is a man of character and competence  he is also a man committed to his faith and his wonderful family    We are currently debating and discussing the very important issue of immigration reform  Our country is at a point where we need to address the immigration issue in a way that recognizes the economic contributions these immigrants are making while balancing the need for stronger borders and enforcement   I know that Emilio  a colleague during our time in the administration and a friend  will serve our country well in this position and urge my colleagues in the Senate to join me in a swift confirmation of Mr  Gonzalez                                                                   October 2005
