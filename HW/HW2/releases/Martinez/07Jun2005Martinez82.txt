                               MARTINEZ APPLAUDS PRESIDENT S AFRICAN RELIEF EFFORT  June 7  2005    WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today applauded President Bush   s plan to send an additional  674 million in humanitarian relief to African nations         I support the president   s action today and echo his call to the international community to increase support for emergency humanitarian needs in Africa     said Senator Martinez  the Chairman of the Subcommittee on African Affairs on the Senate Foreign Relations Committee     While working to improve the economic growth of these countries we must also support the humanitarian needs required today        Senator Martinez met with British Prime Minister Tony Blair this morning to discuss African aid and the goals of the upcoming G8 summit         Prime Minister Blair and I discussed Africa  the tragic cycle of poverty and disease over much of that continent  and ways in which industrialized nations can help     said Martinez     Prime Minister Blair recognized the United States as a leader in providing African aid  I told the Prime Minister that I am confident we will continue to make progress and I will use my subcommittee to advance those policies that can help bring stability to Africa   s developing nations        Senator Martinez will revisit the issue as he presides over his first nomination hearing as the Chairman of the African Subcommittee tomorrow  June 8 at 2 30pm in SD 419   The committee will consider ambassador nominations to Ghana  Liberia  Cape Verde  and Mali
