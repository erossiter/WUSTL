                               MARTINEZ   225 000 AWARDED TO THE UNIVERSITY OF MIAMI     COOPERATIVE INSTITUTE MARINE AND ATMOSPHERIC STUDIES  June 18  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today praised the U S  Department of Commerce National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration s  NOAA  decision to issue a  225 000 grant to the University of Miamis Cooperative Institute for Marine and Atmospheric Studies division  This grant will fund the Universitys continued studies of development of a regional hydrodynamic circulation simulation model for the Southwest Florida shelf  Florida Straits and Gulf Stream that encompasses Florida Bay  and the Florida Keys     I am pleased the University of Miami will be able to further enhance their important study of the circulation processes of our ocean  said Martinez  Studying the movement of these current patterns and monitoring water quality is extremely important to the health of the Florida Keys National Marine Sanctuary and the unique ecosystem of South Florida  and I thank NOAA for funding these efforts   With this grant  the University of Miami will continue to further develop the NOAA University of Miami regional hydrodynamic model in order to perform simulations that include all relevant coastal processes that affect circulation around the South Florida ecosystems with implications on nutrient transport
