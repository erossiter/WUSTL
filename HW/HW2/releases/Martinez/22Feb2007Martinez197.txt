                               MARTINEZ  EXPANDING HEALTH INSURANCE AFFORDABILITY TOP PRIORITY  February 22  2007   NAPLES  FL   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today spoke with medical professionals at the Neighborhood Health Clinic in Naples about his commitment to lower the cost and expand the availability of health insurance through the implementation of the Tax Equity and Affordability  TEA  act  Senator Martinez has introduced this legislation in Congress in the hopes of expanding health insurance opportunities to 4 million Floridians and 47 million Americans nationwide     Martinez said    Healthcare remains the publics top domestic issue  Americans cite rising healthcare costs as their most pressing financial problemmore than energy costs and taxes combined    This can be changed   We can transform our healthcare system to deliver more choices of greater quality at a lower cost to every American   It will take innovative ideas  the right priorities  bold leadership  and we must act now    That is why I have introduced the Tax Equity and Affordability Act  a bill to provide low income families  and all families outside the employer based health insurance system  a refundable tax credit    By making the money available now  the TEA Act ensures that those who cannot afford health insurance have the financial flexibility to buy health insurance   A deduction alone will not achieve the goal  low income families simply do not have the financial means to buy insurance    The TEA act  S  397  would amend the Internal Revenue Code to allow individual taxpayers a refundable tax credit for health insurance costs paid for the benefit of the taxpayer  the taxpayer s spouse  and dependents  direct the Secretary of the Treasury to make advance payments of health insurance tax credit amounts to health insurance providers  and limit the tax exclusion for employer provided health care coverage                                                                  February 2007 News
