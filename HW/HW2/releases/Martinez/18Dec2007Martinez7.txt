                               MARTINEZ  SENATE APPROVES    DOCTOR FIX    Measure fixes Medicare reimbursement rate for physicians December 18  2007   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today commended his colleagues for approving the so called doctor fix  a move to replace a scheduled 10 percent cut to the Medicare physician reimbursement rate in 2008 with a 0 5 percent increase through June 20  2008     Thousands of Floridians who rely on Medicare as their primary healthcare provider will now be spared being denied access to treatment  said Martinez  Leaving the Medicare cut in place would have put an even greater strain on doctors  who are already forced to be selective in determining which Medicare patients they can afford to treat     The Medicare reimbursement rate fix is only good until June of 2008     In Florida  the dilemma has reached a critical mass  with an increasing number of doctors leaving the program  said Martinez  We need a permanent fix that reduces Medicare bureaucracy and corrects the ongoing problem with the reimbursement rate     In addition to the Medicare reimbursement rate fix  the measure also extends the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  SCHIP  through March of 2009  The legislation passed the Senate by unanimous consent and must now be considered by the House of Representatives
