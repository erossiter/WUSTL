                               MARTINEZ   284 000 AWARDED TO THE FLORIDA FISH   WILDLIFE CONSERVATION COMMISSION     FISH   WILDLIFE RESEARCH INSTITUTE  July 12  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced a  284 637 grant for the Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission s Fish and Wildlife Research Institute in St  Petersburg  This grant will fund the Institute s survey of Florida s Atlantic coast to collect data for comprehensive stock assessments  The effort assists the Atlantic States Marine Fisheries Commission  ASMFC  in managing fisheries resources     This money will help the Fish  Wildlife Research Institute monitor Florida s waters  collecting the data necessary to maintain the health and supply of our commercial and recreational fisheries  said Martinez     With this grant  the Fish and Wildlife Research Institute will conduct biostatistical sampling  improve data collection needed for accurate stock assessments  and provide compliance report information  stock assessments  and their review  This data will be used to refine stock assessments for important species  such as bluefish  weakfish  red drum  Atlantic croaker  spiny dogfish  and Atlantic menhaden  Florida specific assessments will be done for spotted seatrout and red drum  This data will be made available to the ASMFC to include in their regional stock assessments
