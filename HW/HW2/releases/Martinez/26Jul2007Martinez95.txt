                               MARTINEZ ANNOUNCES PASSAGE OF THE    WOUNDED WARRIORS    ACT   July 26  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  this week announced the Senates passage of the Dignified Treatment of Wounded Warriors Act  The bill requires the Secretary of Defense and the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to develop a comprehensive policy by January 1  2008 on the care  management  and transition of severely wounded military personnel to the Department of Veterans Affairs or civilian life     This legislation is an important first step in making these two large departments work together better  in a way that will ensure our military men and women have a smooth transition after military duty  Martinez said  I will continue this work to ensure that our service men and women   our veterans   receive the absolute best care and support this nation has to offer     With better coordination  the departments of Defense and Veterans Affairs will be able to develop a more rational  standardized and common system of disability evaluations  The new policy addresses enhancing healthcare  expanding diagnosis and treatment of post traumatic stress disorder and traumatic brain injury  improving department response to disability  and rehabilitating patient housing     The Wounded Warriors Act will also require a National Academy of Sciences study of the physical and mental health and other readjustment needs of members who served in Operation Iraqi Freedom and Operation Enduring Freedom and their families
