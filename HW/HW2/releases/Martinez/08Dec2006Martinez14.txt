                               MARTINEZ  KIRKPATRICK WAS ARTICULATE VOICE FOR AMERICAN VALUES  December 8  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today issued the following statement after learning of Dr  Jeane Kirkpatricks passing     Martinez said     Dr  Jeane Kirkpatrick had a long and remarkable career of public service and we as a nation are better because of the work she did  As the first woman appointed to serve as permanent representative of the U S  to the United Nations and a member of President Ronald Reagans Cabinet and National Security Council  Ambassador Kirkpatrick was a strong advocate for our nations interests and the spread of freedom around the world  She was a key figure in implementing President Ronald Reagan s foreign policy during the Cold War  resulting in the demise of the U S S R     After retiring from government service  she continued to write extensively and speak of the need for serious reform at the U N  and a robust U S  response to the threat of terrorism  She was a true believer of progress and prosperity in nations through democracy and freedom  She recognized the great harm Castros dictatorship put upon the people of Cuba and she became one of the founding members of the Center for a Free Cuba  Freedom loving people around the world owe her a debt of gratitude     Dr  Kirkpatrick was an intelligent and articulate voice for American values  and with her loss  our nation loses a skillful diplomat and a great American  I thank her for her service and offer my deepest condolences to her family
