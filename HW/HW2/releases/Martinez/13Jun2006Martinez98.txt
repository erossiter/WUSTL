                               SENATOR MARTINEZ PROMOTES ENVIRONMENTAL AWARENESS THROUGH    NATIONAL CLEAN BEACHES WEEK     June 13  2006   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today introduced a Senate resolution designating June 28  2006 through July 5  2006  as National Clean Beaches Week  The designation is part of Senator Martinezs effort to recognize the considerable value and role of beaches in the economy and culture of the United States    Florida has world renowned beaches that draw more than 80 million tourists annually  nationally  the number and value of beaches is even greater  Its fitting that we draw attention to the value and importance of our sandy coastlines  said Martinez  This resolution encourages all citizens to work to keep beaches safe and clean for the continued enjoyment of all  I want to thank New Jersey Senator Frank Lautenberg for his leadership on this issue and for joining me in making this a bipartisan effort   The New Jersey Shore is a state treasure that enriches our lives and strengthens our economy  said Senator Lautenberg  We must increase awareness of the threats to our beautiful beaches  and Im proud to join my colleague Senator Martinez in sponsoring this resolution   According to a recent report by the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration  coastal areas produce 85 percent of all tourism revenue in the United States and beaches are the leading tourism destination in the country  The U S  Geological Survey notes that beaches play an important role in watersheds  inland areas  and coastal waters  Senator Martinezs resolution supports the federal governments development of science based policies based on those facts   Floridas estimated 825 miles of beaches draw more than 80 million tourists to the state every year  putting more than  57 billion dollars into Floridas economy                                                                 June 2006 News Releases             Browse by
