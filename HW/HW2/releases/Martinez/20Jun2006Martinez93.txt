                               MARTINEZ  ARBITRARY DEADLINES THREATEN TO HINDER PROGRESS IN IRAQ  June 20  2006   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today issued the following statement in regards to attempts by Senate Democrats to set arbitrary deadlines for U S  troop withdrawal  The senator said premature troop departure would be a major setback in the global war on terror    Martinez said    An attitude of defeatism does not constitute a strategy for victory  We can not and will not be defeated militarily  The only way we will be defeated is by our own lack of resolve  If we had listened to detractors who told us to cut and run  al Qaeda terrorist Abu Musab al Zarqawi would be alive and planning his next killing and the future of a radical caliphate in Iraq    Our enemies have a long term strategy  we must be equal to the challenge  The vacuum our premature departure would leave in Iraq would be a major setback in the global war on terror   While we all wish to see the end of the struggle and our troops safe return home  this must not be determined by an arbitrary deadline that signals retreat in defeat  The war on terror will not be easy or short  As Americans  we must be firm in our resolve to the task at hand  Our troops on the ground are making progress with steadfast determination  We must be equally determined to finish the job
