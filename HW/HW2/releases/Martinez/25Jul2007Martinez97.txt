                               MARTINEZ  SENATE ACTION REQUIRED ON BORDER SECURITY  July 25  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today joined several senators in introducing the Border Security First Act of 2007 as an amendment to the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations bill  The legislation provides  3 billion in emergency funding in the continued efforts to secure our nations borders  An effort to attach the bill to the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Act failed by a vote of 52 44     The American people deserve to have confidence in their government  confidence that their government will do what is says  the American people expect their government to have control over our borders  Martinez said  This act will provide an increase in resources to improve our security  by continuing to build the fence and physical barriers the Congress has authorized and increasing surveillance on the border     The Act contained provisions that require operational control over 100  of the U S  Mexico land border  23 000 Border Patrol agents hired  trained  and reporting for duty  4 Unmanned Aerial Vehicles  105 ground based radar and camera towers  300 miles of vehicle barriers  700 miles of border fencing and a permanent end to the catch and release policy with 45 000 detention beds
