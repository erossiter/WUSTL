                               MARTINEZ     BISCET A VOICE FOR FREEDOM       President Bush awards Presidential Freedom Award to Imprisoned Cuban  November 5  2007   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today called Dr  Oscar Elias Biscet a constant voice for freedom and democracy and praised President George Bushs selection of Dr  Biscet for the Presidential Medal of Freedom     Dr  Biscet has made his lifes work about fighting for the recognition and protection of the most basic human rights  He believes in life and freedom  and for his views he has been unjustly imprisoned by the Cuban regime  Even from his prison cell  Dr  Biscet continues his work to this day  serving as an inspiration to us all  I call for Dr  Biscets release and urge the international community to join me in this call     Dr  Biscet has been a constant voice for freedom and democracy for the People of Cuba  We must continue to encourage voices of freedom from within Cuba and I urge our friends and allies around the world to also support the pro democracy efforts currently underway     Dr  Biscet is the founder and president of the Lawton Foundation for Human Rights  an organization that peacefully promotes the defense of all human rights  He was arrested for his beliefs in 1999  Despite his poor health and imprisonment  he continues his work to this day     The Presidential Medal of Freedom is America s highest civilian award and an honor reserved for individuals the President deems to have made especially meritorious contributions to the security or national interests of the United States  to world peace  or to cultural or other significant public or private endeavors                                                                   November 2007 News Releases
