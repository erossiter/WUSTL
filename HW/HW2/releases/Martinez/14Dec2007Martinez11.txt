                               U S  SENATE HONORS JUDGE C  CLYDE ATKINS  December 14  2007   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the U S  Senate has agreed to honor the public service of former U S  District Court Judge C  Clyde Atkins  Judge Atkins served as judge in the Southern District of Florida from 1966 to 1999  and served as Chief Judge of the district from 1977 to 1982     Paying tribute to Judge Atkins public service  the Senate has agreed to designate the United States Courthouse in Miami as the C  Clyde Atkins United States Courthouse     Judge Atkins served our country and South Florida with distinction  This recognition is a tribute to his ability to fairly apply the rule of law to some of the most complex and controversial issues of his day  Martinez said  I join the members of the bar and bench in saluting his outstanding accomplishments    Judge C  Clyde Atkins was born on November 23  1914  in Washington  DC  In 1921  he moved to Miami  Florida  with his family  Judge Atkins attended Miami High School  and graduated from the University of Florida College of Law in 1936  He practiced law in private practice for more than 25 years  and was a partner in the law firm of Walton  Lantaff  Shroeder  Atkins  Carson and Wahl from 1941 to 1966     In 1966  President Lyndon B  Johnson nominated and the Senate confirmed Judge Atkins to serve as a U S  District Court Judge for the Southern District of Florida  He served as Chief Judge from 1977 to 1982 and assumed senior status on December 31  1982  Judge Atkins continued to serve until his death in 1999                                                                   December 2007 News Releases             Browse by
