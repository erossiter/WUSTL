                               MARTINEZ BACKS FEDERAL MARRIAGE AMENDMENT Vote on amendment blocked by procedural maneuver June 7  2006   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today voted in favor of moving toward amending the Constitution to declare that marriage in the United States shall consist only of the union of a man and a woman  Senator Martinez is an original cosponsor of the measure  Senate Joint Resolution 1  The measure was defeated procedurally by a vote of 49 to 48  The measure required 60 votes to move forward   Marriage between a man and a woman is a societal pillar and an undeniable positive force that promotes stability and family well being  I am committed to protecting values such as marriage that serve as the foundation of a healthy society  said Martinez  A number of states have already spoken on this matter through their elected officials  but because activist judges have interpreted both the Federal Constitution and state constitutions very broadly in order to overturn the will of the people regarding same sex marriage  we have to act   The constitutional amendment reads  Marriage in the United States shall consist only of the union of a man and a woman  Neither this Constitution  nor the constitution of any state  shall be construed to require that marriage or the legal incidents thereof be conferred upon any union other than the union of a man and a woman   The Constitution should not be interpreted by state and federal courts to impose same sex marriage on the people of the United States  Martinez stated  It is therefore proper for the people to continue to speak on this issue thru their elected officials by amending the U S  Constitution to ensure that the sanctity of marriage will be protected from activist courts
