                               MARTINEZ   85 2 MILLION AUTHORIZED FOR GAINESVILLE VA MEDICAL CENTER  December 11  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that S  3421  the Veterans Affairs authorization bill  passed the Senate this weekend and will be sent to the White House where President Bush is expected to sign it  Included in the bill is an authorization for  85 2 million in construction funds for the Department of Veterans Affairs Medical Center in Gainesville   Making certain our nation keeps its promises to veterans remains among my highest priorities  said Martinez  The addition of a new bed tower at the Gainesville VA hospital will greatly improve the amount and quality of care that facility can provide   In addition  four other major VA projects for Florida were authorized  including   An extension of authority for construction of a new Orlando VA Hospital   377 7 Million     Construction of an Ambulatory Surgery   Outpatient Diagnostic Support Center in the Gulf South Submarket of Veterans Integrated Service Network  VISN  8 and completion of Phase I land purchase  Lee County   107 85 Million    Upgrade of essential electrical distribution systems at the Department of Veterans Affairs Medical Center  VAMC  in Tampa   49 Million   and  Expansion of the Spinal Cord Injury Center addition at VAMC  Tampa   7 1 Million
