                               MARTINEZ ANNOUNCES  16 MILLION FOR THE GAINESVILLE JOB CORPS CENTER  April 26  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that Del Jen  Inc will receive more than  16 million in funds for their operation of the Gainesville Job Corps Center  Martinez was notified by the U S  Department of Labor  DOL   which works with Del Jen  Inc  to provide training services through the Labor Departments Job Corps program for at risk youth     These funds will help prepare Florida s students to successfully join America s workforce  said Martinez  Valuable academic and vocational training is essential to the career success of our youth  The Job Corps program provides an opportunity to those who might not otherwise have access to these services in preparing for their future     Del Jen  Inc  is in charge of the operation of the Gainesville Job Corps Center  which hopes to serve an estimated 525 male and female students per year  This award will fund the period of June 1  2007 through May 31  2009  with the possibility of funding for three additional years  Vocational training available at the Gainesville Job Corps Center includes  automotive technician  business office technologies  culinary arts  pre law  health occupations  painting  facilities maintenance  tile setting  carpentry  plastering  and advanced career training
