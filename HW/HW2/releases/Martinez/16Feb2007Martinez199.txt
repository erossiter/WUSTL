                               MARTINEZ  FLORIDA HOUSING AUTHORITIES TO RECEIVE MORE THAN  1 MILLION  February 16  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  has awarded more than  1 million in grants to four Florida Housing Authorities  The grants are part of HUDs 2006 Public and Indian Housing Resident Opportunity and Self Sufficiency  ROSS  program  designed to provide supportive services to public housing agencies and organizations that assist elderly and disabled residents in continuing to live independently     Floridas Housing Authorities are committed to providing supportive services to those who wish to live independently  With these grants  our Housing Authorities will be able to continue to provide valuable assistance to our elderly and disabled residents  said Martinez  These grants and the continued efforts of these organizations are examples of the kind of care and respect all of our Florida residents deserve     The ROSS program provides grants to public housing agencies  tribes tribally designated housing entities  Resident Associations  nonprofit organizations  including grassroots  faith based  and other community based organizations     Floridas Housing Authorities receiving grants    Department  Housing Authority of the City of Fort Myers   Amount   250 000   Location  Fort Myers  FL   Department  Housing Authority of the City of Orlando   Amount   345 081   Location  Orlando  FL   Department  Housing Authority of the City of Sarasota   Amount   150 000   Location  Sarasota  FL   Department  Jacksonville Housing Authority   Amount   350 000   Location  Jacksonville  FL                                                                  February 2007
