                               GULF COAST COMMUNITY COLLEGE AWARDED  1 5 MILLION   December 18  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the Department of Commerces Economic Development Administration has awarded a  1 5 million grant to the Gulf Coast Community College  GCCC  in Panama City  The grant is intended to help GCCC renovate and expand their George G  Tapper Health Sciences Building  maximizing the schools ability to support the workforce demand within the local healthcare industry     This expansion is essential for an improved quality of health care in this region  The ability to offer high quality health care and a well trained work force will also help our regions overall economy  said Martinez  This grant is a compliment to the excellent education and training that GCCC provides     In addition to the programs the Tapper Building has offered for the past twenty two years  the renovation and expansion will provide for the following new programs  Diagnostic medical sonography  magnetic resonance imaging  MRI   Surgical First Assistant lab  respiratory therapy sleep lab  advanced certifications for nurses  including diagnostic pathophysiology  and cardiology EKG complex interpretations  and expanded offerings specifically for paramedics and emergency medical technicians
