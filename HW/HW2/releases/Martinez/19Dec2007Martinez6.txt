                               MARTINEZ  U S  MARSHALS REGIONAL FUGITIVE TASK FORCE APPROVED FOR FLORIDA  December 19  2007   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced the U S  Senate has approved  2 82 million to expand the U S  Marshals Service s Regional Fugitive Task Force so that it can dedicate specific resources to Florida  The funds are included in the Commerce  Justice and Science division of the Consolidated Appropriations Act for fiscal year 2008  The funding will be used to assign a task force to cover the state  The bill passed the Senate by a vote of 76 to 17     As a father and grandfather  nothing concerns me more than the safety of our communities  Violent crime has a widespread detrimental impact on our quality of life  This task force will help  Martinez said  The U S  Marshals task force service is dedicated to getting the worst of the worst offenders off our streets  I am pleased that a greater presence of the task force in Florida is about to become a reality     The Regional Fugitive Task Force specifically targets and apprehends violent criminals  gang members  and child predators  Six task forces already exist around the U S  and have proven effective in tracking down the most dangerous fugitives  They work in concert with local  state and federal agencies and last year closed more than 22 000 felony warrants across the nation     With a proven record of success in other areas of our nation  I am pleased the Regional Fugitive Task Force is coming to Florida  Martinez said  By coordinating with state and local officials  the task force will work to track and apprehend violent offenders and fugitives  making Florida a safer place to live                                                                   December 2007 News
