                               MARTINEZ  ENERGY GRANT WILL ADVANCE FLORIDA   S RENEWABLE ENERGY INITIATIVES  February 28  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the U S  Department of Energy  DOE  has awarded ALICO  Inc  part of a  385 million grant to build a biorefinery plant in LaBelle  Florida  The company stands to receive up to  33 million over the next four years to facilitate the construction and operation of a plant that will use advanced technologies to convert agriculture waste and woody materials into cellulosic ethanol     Martinez said     I am extremely pleased that the DOE has selected ALICO to build a new refinery in LaBelle  Florida is ripe for investment and is ready to advance new energy initiatives     ALICOs cutting edge facility will bring Florida and our country one step closer to better use of renewable energy and energy efficient technologies to meet the growing need for clean affordable energy  In addition  this plant will create new jobs and economic development opportunities for the people of Hendry County  This is great news for the state of Florida     According to a study by the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services  Florida has the potential to be a national leader in the production of alternative fuels like cellulosic ethanol and biomass     ALICO  Inc  is a major agricultural company that will use an innovative thermochemical technology  developed by Bioengineering Resource Inc   that ferments synthetic gas  The proposed plant will produce 13 9 million gallons of ethanol a year and 6 255 kilowatts of electric power  as well as 8 8 tons of hydrogen and 50 tons of ammonia per day                                                                   February 2007 News Releases
