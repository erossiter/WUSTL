                               MARTINEZ CALLS FOR  RISK BASED DISBURSAL  OF HOMELAND SECURITY FUNDS    July 12  2005   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today spoke in favor of amending the fiscal year 2006 Department of Homeland Security appropriations bill so that it distributes grant funding based on risk assessment instead of static population formulae     The concept of this amendment is simple  to direct homeland security dollars to the areas where the threat of attack is greatest  As former Mayor of Orange County  I understand the concerns of regional managers who find that their major city on its own is not very populous    But if you include the interests of a region  be they tourist attractions  amusements parks or resorts  at any one time there can be millions of visitors  For instance  Orange County  Florida is one of the top vacation destinations in the world  In 2003 the region played host to over 45 million visitors    Florida is also home to 14 deepwater ports  many of which are nationally significant and critically important parts of our countrys shipping infrastructure  For example  at the Port of Miami nearly 4 million cruise passengers passed through the Port and over 9 million tons of cargo transited through the seaport  This combination of cruise and cargo activities supported approximately 98 000 jobs  and has an economic impact in Miami Dade County of over  12 billion    The whole state of Florida  in fact  now plays host to 77 million tourists a year  Thats on top of our 17 million person population  This amendment  the FeinsteinCornyn amendment  meets Secretary  Michael  Chertoffs desire and that is to require the Department of Homeland Security to allocate grants to states based primarily on threat assessment and vulnerability    The full text of Senator Martinezs speech as prepared for delivery is attached          Senator Mel Martinez   Floor Statement   Feinstein Cornyn Amendment   FY 2006 Department of Homeland Security Bill   Mr  President    I rise today in support of the Feinstein Cornyn amendment to the Fiscal Year 2006 Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Bill
