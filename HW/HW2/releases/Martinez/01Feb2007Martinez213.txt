                               MARTINEZ  FLORIDA FIRST RESPONDERS TO RECEIVE MORE THAN  850 000   February 1  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS  has awarded  856 801 in grants to eight Florida fire departments and emergency services  The awards include  185 940 to the Holley Navarre Fire Protection District   147 020 to the Chaires Capitola Volunteer Fire Rescue  and  136 040 to the Bristol Volunteer Fire Department  The grants are part of DHSs 2006 Assistance to Firefighters Grants  AFG  program  designed to help departments and emergency medical service organizations improve their ability to protect the public and first responder personnel from fire related hazards     Floridas fire departments and emergency services are essential to public safety  The funds will help departments  including those that depend heavily on volunteers  enhance their ability to serve Florida communities  said Martinez  The awards speak highly of each department and their commitment to the safety of our residents     The goal of Assistance to Firefighters grants is to enhance the ability of first responders to protect the communities they serve from fire and fire related hazards  Funds under this DHS program are intended to support first responder training  provide adequate firefighting EMS equipment  including rescue vehicles  as well as support the wellness and fitness of firefighters     Floridas first responders receiving grants     Department  Holley Navarre Fire
