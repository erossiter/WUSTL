                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              MARTINEZ  CLASS ACTION BILL NECESSARY TO CURB ABUSES  February 11  2005   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today voted in favor of S  5  the Class Action Fairness Act of 2005  which curbs venue shopping of class action lawsuits and worthless coupon settlements for plaintiffs   Martinez said      The Class Action Fairness Act is a bipartisan bill that will solve many of the current class action litigation abuses  As a lawyer with extensive courtroom experience  I believe strongly in the rights of harmed individuals to have their day in court   However  certain states have become havens for attorneys manipulating the current class action system for personal gain        The Class Action Fairness Act simply shifts many class action lawsuits from state courts to the federal courts   The purpose of the shift is to make sure that lawyers do not pick friendly states and friendly state judges to argue cases that on their merits do not deserve its day in court        Again  individuals who are harmed deserve their day in court and deserve to be compensated if they can prove harm   I do believe there are growing abuses in the class action system that this legislation addresses in a way that defends the interest of the victim                                                                  February 2005 News Releases             Browse by                                                                          Current record
