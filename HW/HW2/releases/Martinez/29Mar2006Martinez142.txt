                               SENATOR MARTINEZ ANNOUNCES  2 8 MILLION IN AID FOR HURRICANE KATRINA VICTIMS IN FLORIDA  March 29  2006   WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced more than  2 8 million in Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  funding for victims of Hurricane Katrina evacuees who sought refuge in Florida  This funding is part of the total  1 5 billion released by HHS last week that will be used to help offset medical costs associated with caring for the victims   Hurricane Katrina left thousands of people on the Gulf Coast with physical ailments and in need of assistance  said Senator Martinez  Many health care providers in Florida stepped in to provide emergency medical care to the victims of this horrific storm and deserve to be compensated    The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services  CMS   a division of HHS  granted special  emergency Medicaid eligibility to Hurricane Katrina victims in Florida and 31 other states following the disaster  The bulk of funds will be designated for state Medicaid programs  States that took in the largest numbers of refugees will receive additional funding to reimburse providers who cared for those victims that were unable to pay for their own medical care    I am pleased that Floridas programs and providers will receive fair compensation for the assistance they provided during this critical time  said Senator Martinez                                                                  March 2006 News Releases             Browse by
