                               MARTINEZ  FLORIDA UNIVERSITIES TO RECEIVE TRANSPORTATION RESEARCH FUNDS   June 30  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez today announced that the U S  Department of Transportation  DOT  has awarded  1 1 million to two universities in Florida for transportation research projects  The University of Central Florida and the University of South Florida represent two of the 20 institutions nationwide to receive grant funding    These awards are indicative of the commitment  leadership and foresight demonstrated by our universities to search for solutions to our nations transportation issues  Martinez said  Im proud of the work of these institutions and look forward to continuing to support their programs    The DOT announced that the University of Central Florida  UCF  will receive  860 000  the University of South Florida  USF  will receive  271 000    Most recently  Senator Martinez supported USFs transportation research center in the Safe  Accountable  Flexible  Efficient Transportation Equity Act  A Legacy for Users  SAFETEA LU  to study bus rapid transit   The U S  Department of Transportation  DOT  awarded grant funding this week to 20 transportation research centers located at universities across the country  The University Transportation Centers  UTC  program advances the nation s transportation system through education  research and technology transfer  and is administered by DOTs Research and Innovative Technology Administration  RITA                                                              June 2006
