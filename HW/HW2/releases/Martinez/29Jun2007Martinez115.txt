                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              MARTINEZ  GOVERNMENT OF VENEZUELA STIFLING CORE DEMOCRATIC PRINCIPLES   June 29  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today made the following comments after meeting with Julio Borges  president of Primero Justicia  Venezuelas second largest opposition party     Martinez said     This week  I heard first hand testimony of the deteriorating political  economic and social freedoms in Venezuela  Everyday it becomes more apparent that the one man rule of Venezuela is stifling core democratic principles we all uphold     The government shutdown last month of Radio Caracas Television    Venezuelas oldest TV station and one of few independent media outlets  is a clear example of how conditions are getting worse  not better     Additionally  this weeks announcement that two U S  oil companies would be withdrawing from Venezuela is another sign that the Venezuelan Government is taking steps to deter foreign investment and consolidate its power  instead of  using those investments to promote economic growth and stability which would benefit the Venezuelan people     These actions have resulted in further erosions of freedoms  I call on our friends and allies in the region to urge the Organization of American States to act  We have a responsibility to support the Venezuelan people                                                                   June 2007 News Releases             Browse by                                              Current record
