                               MARTINEZ  RE EQUIPPING NATIONAL GUARD MUST BE A PRIORITY   June 14  2006   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today called for priority re equipping of National Guard units returning from deployments in Iraq and Afghanistan  Senator Martinez is seeking to amend the fiscal year  FY  2007 Defense Authorization bill  Martinez calls re equipping critical to making certain National Guard units have the equipment they need to assist during natural disasters  including hurricanes   When guardsmen return home from Iraq and Afghanistan  most of their equipment stays behind  This is understandable  this is a way to meet vital war zone needs  But the result is a deficit of equipment stateside  Martinez said  My aim is to make certain that  especially during hurricane season  the National Guard in Florida and other disaster prone areas have what they need to carry out their mission   In a statement on the Senate floor  Senator Martinez pointed out that the Florida National Guard deployed 5 800 troops within the State of Florida and along the Gulf Coast during 4 major hurricanes last year  In support of Hurricane Katrina recovery efforts  the Florida Guard sent 2 500 troops to Mississippi as part of the emergency compact agreement that the states have with the Guard    The National Guard plays a crucial role in helping stabilize areas in the immediate hours and days following a disastrous hurricane  Martinez stated  During hurricane season and during the war on terror  we can not sustain the National Guard without prioritizing equipment replacement                                                                 June 2006 News Releases             Browse by
