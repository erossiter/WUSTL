                                MARTINEZ  CONGRESS MUST ACT ON COMMUNICATIONS REFORM    August 30  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today issued a statement regarding the Advanced Telecommunications and Opportunities Reform Act  The bill  sponsored by Senator Ted Stevens  R AK   reforms existing communications laws to promote competition  cost savings for consumers  and the speedy deployment of broadband services to all Americans    Senator Martinez said   This legislation is pro consumer  It reforms the cable franchising process to permit more and better competition in the delivery of cable television services  This will bring real competition and give consumers the ability to choose who provides their video programming  This is something consumers want and deserve   A recent Phoenix Center report estimates that each year Congress delays cable franchise reform  it costs American consumers  8 2 billion dollars  Florida consumers alone lose  626 million dollars each year  We have the power to change this and we should act on this bill now   The bill also allows small rural telecommunications carriers to offer video services and  where feasible  increases the availability of satellite services  In addition  the legislation encourages the expansion of wireless broadband and home networking services by freeing up unused broadcast spectrum                                                                    August 2006 News Releases             Browse by                                              Current record
