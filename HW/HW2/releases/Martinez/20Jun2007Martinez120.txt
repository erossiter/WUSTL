                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              MARTINEZ  TAXPAYERS SHOULD NOT BE FORCED TO FINANCE DESTRUCTION OF LIVE EMBRYOS  June 20  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today commended President Bushs decision to veto S 5  a bill allowing taxpayer dollars to finance the destruction of live embryos for embryonic stem cell research  Martinez also praised the Presidents signing of an executive order to strengthen our nations commitment to research on pluripotent stem cells     The U S  government already funds life saving stem cell research that doesnt breach ethical boundaries  We constantly see new evidence reinforcing the value of adult stem cells  cells that have a proven record of success  in both lab trials and real life applications  said Martinez  Adult stem cell research is non controversial because it doesnt engage in the destruction of human life  As a civil society  we have a responsibility to dedicate public resources to research that preserves and protects life  and not support research that destroys life     Earlier this year  Senator Martinez voted in favor of S  30  the HOPE Act  a bill to allow scientists to apply for federal funds to study embryos that have died naturally  S  30  the HOPE Act  passed by a vote of 70 to 28  The bill is currently awaiting the action of a House committee                                                                      June 2007 News Releases             Browse by                                                                                                                    Current record
