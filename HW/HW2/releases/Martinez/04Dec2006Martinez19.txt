                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              MARTINEZ STATEMENT ON BOLTON RESIGNATION   December 4  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today expressed disappointment that the acting U S  ambassador to the United Nations has announced his resignation  Ambassador John Bolton announced today he will step down from the post when his appointment expires in January   The U S  is losing a key diplomat  In his time at the United Nations  John Bolton has demonstrated himself to be effective  well qualified and well suited to the position  said Martinez  Its disappointing that a person of this caliber and skill will be unable to continue serving his country in this capacity  He clearly had the support of a majority of the U S  Senate  its a shame his nomination could not be brought to a vote   In his time as interim ambassador  Bolton led the U S  effort at the UN to demand that Iran suspend its nuclear program  he negotiated the condemnation of North Korea for its missile launches  he worked to end the conflict between Israel and Lebanon  he championed the creation of a UN peacekeeping force in Darfur  and he led the effort to address the deteriorating situation in Burma                                                                   December 2006 News Releases             Browse by                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Current record
