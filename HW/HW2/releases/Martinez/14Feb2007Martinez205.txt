                               MARTINEZ  FY    07 CONTINUING RESOLUTION DOESN   T MEET FLORIDA   S NEEDS  February 14  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today released the following statement in response to the passing of H J Res 20   the Continuing Resolution  CR   the  463 5 billion dollar measure funding the federal government through the rest of Fiscal Year 2007    It is unfortunate that the Continuing Resolution that passed in the Senate today significantly cuts funds for a number of programs that are critically important to Florida  Section 8 housing  NASA funding  and agricultural research programs  In addition  the Base Realignment and Closure program also faces tremendous cuts and will delay programs that were scheduled for this year  This bill passed today with the intentions and necessity to keep the federal government running but it does not meet Floridas needs     Earlier this week  I introduced five amendments in hopes to remedy these problems within the massive spending bill  I had hoped we could restore the  3 1 billion in funding that was cut to implement the militarys base realignment  Without this funding the military will be forced to use resources intended for active missions for programs scheduled for cancellation or realignment     My amendments were not allowed for consideration  nor were any amendments offered by other senators  due to the actions of the majority party  This bill could have been much better for Florida     I will continue working through the FY 08 budget process to make the affected programs
