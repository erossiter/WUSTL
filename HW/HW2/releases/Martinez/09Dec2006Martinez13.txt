                               MARTINEZ  HISTORIC GULF PROTECTIONS HEADED TO THE WHITE HOUSE  December 9  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today voted in favor of protecting Floridas portion of the Gulf of Mexico from offshore drilling  The measure containing the language of the Gulf of Mexico Energy Security Act of 2006 was contained in H R  6111 which was agreed to by the U S  Senate this morning  79 to 9  and will now go to the White House for President Bushs signature    Now is the time and this is the opportunity to protect the state of Florida  We sought protection and today we secured it  I feel good about this agreement and I know it is the best that Florida can do at this point in time  said Martinez  This has truly been a bipartisan effort  one that satisfies Floridas need to protect our fragile environment  while protecting our resilient and dynamic economy    Martinez continued  This compromise was difficult to forge  essentially two years in the making  but the protections it brings are critically important  Some argue more could have been done for Florida  Others protest that Florida is afforded far too many protections  The fact is that if nothing had passed  oil and gas leasing would open in the Gulf next year through the Department of Interiors Five Year Plan  with no additional protections for Florida    The agreement provides Florida at least 125 miles of protection through 2022  The agreement also ensures there shall be no drilling east of the Military Mission Line through 2022  meaning no drilling would take place within 234 miles from the coast of Tampa  The agreement also provides an option for existing leaseholders within the 125 mile buffer to swap their leases in other areas open for leasing
