                               MARTINEZ ANNOUNCES  383 000 IN GRANTS FOR FLORIDA FISH ORGANIZATIONS  July 19  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced grants awarded to two Florida fishery organizations  The Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission s Fish and Wildlife Research Institute in St  Petersburg will receive a grant of  206 096 dollars to fund the data collection of the goliath grouper  The Gulf and South Atlantic Fisheries Foundation in Tampa will receive a  177 207 dollar grant     These dollars will fund projects to improve the health and stability of our marine food source  Martinez said  It is vital that we continue to study and protect Florida s fishery resources and take steps to improve our understanding in these fields     The Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission s Fish and Wildlife Research Institute will receive funding to build a cooperative team of scientific  recreational  and commercial SCUBA divers to observe and tag goliath grouper in coastal and offshore waters of Florida s central west coast  This data will be used to develop better ways of support for the development of conservation for this living marine resource     The Gulf and South Atlantic Fisheries Foundation will receive funding to address the problems associated with the new bycatch reduction devices within the shrimp trawl fishery  Through this project  a more efficient and effective process can be accomplished within the fishing industry                                                                   July 2007 News
