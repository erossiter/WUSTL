                               MARTINEZ  ALTERNATIVE FUELS CRITICAL COMPONENT TO MEETING U S  ENERGY NEEDS  KEEPING DRILLING OFF FLORIDA   S COASTS   August 16  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today said to best protect Floridas Gulf from the threat of offshore drilling  Florida needs to successfully develop the use of alternative fuels     Senator Martinez participated in the University of Floridas Alternative Fuels Forum  Floridas Road to Energy Independence  The Promise of Alternative Fuels where he highlighted the need for the U S  to reduce reliance on foreign sources of oil while promoting Floridas role in developing renewable energy sources     Last month  Senator Martinez secured a compromise in the U S  Senate that would give Florida a 125 mile buffer from offshore oil drilling in Floridas Gulf until the year 2022  Martinez has urged that in the meantime  the U S  needs to find ways to decrease our dependence on fossil fuels and harness the promise of alternative fuels     Martinez said     The Florida delegation is committed to protecting our tourism economy  our ecosystem and our military training done in the Gulf     However  making the choice to protect our coast from offshore drilling means that Florida has to help find alternative ways to run our cars and cool our homes     We are a nation that is overly reliant on foreign sources of oil  We have an obligation to move toward more renewable sources of energy that will help us decrease our addiction to fossil fuels  Florida has a role to play in the development of alternative fuels and the state is uniquely positioned to be a leader in the market because of plentiful feedstocks and our strong agriculture industry  Additional investment now will pay great energy dividends in the future     Martinez was joined by more than 90 stakeholders from around the state to discuss the importance of developing alternative fuels  the challenges to overcome in distribution  and how incentives can aid in the development of the alternative energy market                                                                   August 2006 News Releases             Browse by
