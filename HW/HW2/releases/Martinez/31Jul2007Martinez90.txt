                               MARTINEZ PRAISES NEW VA NURSING ACADEMY AT THE UNIVERSITY OF FLORIDA  July 31  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced that the Department of Veterans Affairs  VA  will be partnering with the University of Florida as part of their new VA Nursing Academy  The program will increase the Universitys nursing school faculty and clinical resources to train nurses to look after the health care needs of the nations veterans     This partnership will expand the University of Floridas nursing program to enhance the treatment and care of Floridas veterans  Martinez said  This is a wonderful way to give participants top notch training  while assisting our veterans     The University of Florida is one of four VA nursing partnerships selected from among 42 applications  Additional VA nursing school partnerships will be selected in 2008 and 2009  for a total of 12 partnerships in the five year pilot program     The American Association of Colleges of Nursing has reported that more than 42 000 qualified applicants were turned away from nursing schools in 2006 because of insufficient numbers of faculty  clinical sites  classroom space and clinical mentors  VA currently provides clinical education for approximately 100 000 health professional trainees annually  including students from more than 600 schools of nursing
