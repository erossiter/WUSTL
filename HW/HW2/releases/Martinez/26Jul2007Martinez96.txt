                               MARTINEZ  BORDER SECURITY ACT APPROVED BY SENATE  July 26  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today praised the passage of the Border Security First Act of 2007 as an amendment to the fiscal year 2008 Department of Homeland Security Appropriations bill  The legislation  which Martinez helped to negotiate  provides  3 billion in emergency funding in the continued efforts to secure our nations borders  The amendment passed by a vote of 89 to 1     I am pleased that the Senate has voted to include this important legislation as part of the Homeland Security bill  We need to control our borders and this will help our government do just that  Martinez said  It provides an increase in resources to improve our security by continuing to build the fence and physical barriers and increasing surveillance on the border     The Act contains provisions requiring operational control over 100  of the U S  Mexico land border  23 000 Border Patrol agents hired  trained  and reporting for duty  4 Unmanned Aerial Vehicles  105 ground based radar and camera towers  300 miles of vehicle barriers  700 miles of border fencing  and a permanent end to the catch and release policy relating to illegal aliens apprehended at the border and those overstaying their visas                                                                   July 2007
