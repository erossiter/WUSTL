                               MARTINEZ  BILL BENEFITING EVERGLADES PASSES SENATE More than  2 Billion in Florida Projects Included in Act  May 16  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today voted in favor of the Water Resources Development Act of 2007  WRDA   The measure authorizes Everglades restoration  projects for the Picayune Strand and Indian River Lagoon  a study for the Lake Okeechobee Herbert Hoover Dike  and authorizes numerous Army Corps of Engineers projects vital to Florida  The bill passed the Senate by a vote of 91 to 4     This long overdue authorization is essential to the vitality of Floridas ecosystem and management of water quality issues  On Everglades restoration  this bill brings the federal government back to the table as an equal partner  said Martinez  No single bill Congress approves will have as much positive impact on Floridas environment than this     The last time WRDA was authorized was the year 2000  the reauthorization is now five years overdue  WRDA legislation provides the Army Corps of Engineers with the authority to study water resource problems  construct projects  and make major modifications to existing projects  Key projects in this bill include a  1 5 million study of the Herbert Hoover Dike system to determine the risk of flooding from Lake Okeechobee  more than  1 35 billion for the cleanup of the Indian River Lagoon   375 million for the restoration of Picayune Strand  and  95 million for Everglades restoration and water conservation  and over  80 million for the Site 1 Impoundment that will protect quality and improve water flows into the Everglades     The House of Representatives has already passed a version of WRDA  Differences must now be reconciled between the two bodies before a bill can be brought up for final passage     Florida Projects in WRDA Bill       1 5 million for study of Herbert Hoover Dike     1 35 billion for Indian River Lagoon     375 3 million for Picayune Strand
