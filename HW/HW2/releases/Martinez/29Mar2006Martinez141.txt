                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              SENATOR MARTINEZ ANNOUNCES  480 MILLION FOR HURRICANE DAMAGED HIGHWAYS AND TRAFFIC SIGNALS   March 29  2006    WASHINGTON  DC U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  announced  480 million in funding from the Department of Transportation for Floridas highways  roads and traffic signals damaged by Hurricanes Rita and Wilma    Water and wind damage from Hurricanes Rita and Wilma destroyed thousands of traffic signals  highway signs and left Florida roadways cluttered with downed trees  These funds will reimburse the state for replacing the traffic signals and signs  restoring damaged highways  and removing debris from road immediately following the storms   Repairing roads and providing safe highways were top priorities in Florida following Hurricanes Rita and Wilma because of the need to transport water and emergency medical supplies  said Senator Martinez  I am pleased Florida communities have received compensation for their swift response to the damage caused by these destructive storms   In all  Florida has received more than  523 million in funding from the Department of Transportation this year to repair and rebuild federally supported highways and bridges                                                                  March 2006 News Releases             Browse by                                                                          Current record
