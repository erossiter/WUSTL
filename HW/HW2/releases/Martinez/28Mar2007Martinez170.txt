                               MARTINEZ INTRODUCES    RAISE    ACT  March 28  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today introduced a bill to increase and widen the availability of after school tutoring funded by the No Child Left Behind Act  Senator Martinezs bill  the Raising Achievement through Improving Supplemental Education  RAISE  Act  is modeled after achievements already occurring in Florida schools where after school programs are helping students improve their academic performance     The RAISE Act complements No Child Left Behind by ensuring students who need after school tutoring have access to it and parents know the programs are available  Currently  the after school component is going largely unused  Martinez said  The RAISE Act will help students achieve academic success by utilizing innovative  after school programs  Overall  the RAISE act will help raise achievement scores in low performing schools     Martinez noted that only 23 percent of the 2 5 million eligible students participated in SES programs in the 2005 06 school year  Although enrollment in SES has increased somewhat since then  many students are still not receiving much needed tutoring  The act specifically targets the Supplemental Educational Services  SES  program  a program designed to help students in low performing schools increase their academic achievement     SES is a tutoring program  The RAISE Act clears the way for this program to become well known  widely available  and easily accessible to eligible students  said Martinez  Letting parents know what resources are available is critical to student achievement     It is estimated that only one quarter of the available SES funds were utilized in the 2004 2005 school year  Using this figure  it is expected that up to 1 7 million additional children could receive free tutoring if the RAISE Act were implemented                                                                   March 2007 News
