                               MARTINEZ   DRILLING IN EASTERN GULF NOT THE ANSWER   September 7  2005   Washington  DC  September 6  2005   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today made the following statement regarding the Senate Energy  Natural Resources Committees renewed focus on oil and natural gas exploration in the Gulf of Mexico   Todays hearing was a timely and critical opportunity to address the issue of rising energy prices  It is proper that the federal government help alleviate short term disruptions and price spikes such as those brought about by Hurricane Katrina  Unfortunately  several voices used this opportunity to focus on the prospect of opening the Eastern Gulf to offshore drilling  More drilling in the Gulf is not a solution  in this case it would have done nothing to affect fuel prices   Just last week Committee Chairman Pete Domenici raised the question of whether it was wise to concentrate our oil refining capacity in an area prone to hurricanes  The Chairman is on the right track when he raises concerns about concentration  I share his concern and I also stand firmly by my previous statements that Floridas portion of the Gulf must be protected from drilling  The area in question is critical to military training  its environment is part of a delicate and diverse ecosystem  and the Eastern Gulf is an integral part of Floridas  50 billion economy                                                                                September 2005 News Releases             Browse by
