                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              MARTINEZ OPPOSES TAXPAYER FINANCING OF EMBRYONIC STEM CELL RESEARCH Martinez votes in favor of alternative bill to expand ethical research opportunities April 11  2007   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today voted against S  5  a bill to allow taxpayer funds to finance embryonic stem cell research  The measure passed the Senate by a vote of 63 to 34  Senator Martinez voted in favor of S  30  the HOPE Act  a bill to allow scientists to apply for federal funds to study embryos that have died naturally  S  30  the HOPE Act  passed by a vote of 70 to 28     The government currently funds life saving stem cell research that doesnt breach ethical boundaries  Adult stem cells have a proven record of success  in both lab trials and real life applications  Adult stem cell research is non controversial because it doesnt engage in the destruction of human life  said Martinez  As a civil society  we have a responsibility to dedicate public resources to research that preserves and protects life  and not support research that destroys life     President Bush has vowed to veto S  5                                                                   April 2007 News Releases             Browse by                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Current record
