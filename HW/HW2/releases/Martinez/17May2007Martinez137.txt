                               MARTINEZ  BUDGET RESOLUTION HURTS THE AMERICAN TAXPAYER  May 17  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today opposed the biggest tax increase in American history by voting against the Budget Resolution for Fiscal Year 2008  The measure will increase taxes by a minimum of  736 billion dollars over the next five years  The resolution passed in the Senate by a vote of 52 to 40     It is unfortunate that an unreasonable budget resolution passed today  taking more money out of the pockets of the American people  said Martinez  The budget the President had introduced would have balanced the budget  while making tax cuts permanent  Instead  the Democrats have chosen to create a surplus by cutting into paychecks of citizens      The annual budget resolution  an agreement between the House and Senate  sets federal spending and tax levels for the next five fiscal years  It provides the level of discretionary federal spending that must be renewed annually  such as funding for the various departments and agencies in the federal government  The resolution provides the framework for subsequent legislative action on the annual appropriations bills  revenue measures  debt limit legislation  reconciliation legislation  and any other budgetary legislation                                                                     May 2007 News Releases
