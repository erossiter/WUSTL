                               SENATOR MARTINEZ COMMENTS ON PRESIDENT   S NEW COMPREHENSIVE IRAQ STRATEGY  January 10  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  tonight commented on the Presidents announcement of a new comprehensive Iraq strategy    Martinez said    When I was in Iraq this fall  I met with many of our troops  many who happened to be Floridians  and I saw high morale and sense of purpose among them  What I believed then  and what I believe now  is that we need a new and comprehensive strategy that will lead the Iraqis to take charge of their own country    I support the comprehensive approach laid out by President Bush  It has a reasonable chance to achieve the success we very much need in Iraq  And there is no alternative but success    I am encouraged that the President spoke of the need and the intent of the Iraqi Government to step up and do their part to combat the complex sectarian violence  the internal insurgency and the threat of al Qaeda elements in Iraq   By taking the lead role in the stability and defense of their own country and by putting a serious financial commitment into reconstruction and job creation in Iraq  the Iraqi government has made the type of commitment that must occur if this plan is to succeed     There is no doubt that the road ahead will continue to be difficult  Americans continue to make great sacrifices in the service of our country  But the price of failure in Iraq is what we have to weigh everything against   It would be a terrible mistake for our country and for the greater security in the Middle East to leave a vacuum for terrorism to fester in Iraq    It is vital that Congress examine the presidents plan and I hope that we can pull together  Republicans and Democrats to solve the most intractable and serious problem we face today  This is too important for politics                                                                  January 2007 News Releases             Browse by
