                               MARTINEZ  SENATE APPROVES FUNDING FOR U S  MARSHALL REGIONAL FUGITIVE TASK FORCE   October 16  2007   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced the U S  Senate has approved  5 million dollars to expand the U S  Marshal Service s Regional Fugitive Task Force  The funds are included in the Commerce  Justice and Science Appropriations Act of 2008   The bill passed the Senate by a vote of 75 to 19    Expanding the task force s presence in Florida will help get the worst of the worst offenders off the street  Martinez said  Fugitives often cross multiple jurisdictions in their flight from the law  This task force coordinates with local  state and federal resources to track and apprehend violent offenders  wherever they are  This funding will be the seed money for establishing a Regional Fugitive Task Force in Florida bringing us one more option in fighting violent crime    Regional Fugitive Task Forces are charged with targeting and apprehending violent criminals  gang members  and child predators  Six task forces already exist around the U S  and have proven effective in tracking down the most dangerous fugitives  They work in concert with local  state and federal agencies and last year closed more than 22 000 felony warrants across the nation    The Commerce  Justice  and Science Appropriation bill now must be reconciled
