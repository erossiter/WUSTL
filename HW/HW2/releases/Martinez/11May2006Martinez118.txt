                                                                                                                                                                 Filter by                                                              SENATOR MARTINEZ APPLAUDS SENATE ACTION ON TAX INCREASE PREVENTION ACT   May 11  2006   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today applauded the passage of H R  4297  the Tax Reconciliation Bill  The measure that will prevent  70 billion in tax increases passed the Senate  54   44  and now proceeds to the White House for the Presidents signature   Republican tax relief policies have helped to create a very strong economy with low unemployment  higher earnings  and a vibrant housing market  said Martinez  In addition to lower taxes  Americans have increased investment and the government is receiving record revenues   The Tax Increase Prevention and Reconciliation Act of 2005 extends and modifies certain provisions of the tax code to prevent  70 billion in tax increases from affecting Americas working families  The bill includes increased expensing for small business and reduced rates on capital gains and dividends   Under current law  capital gains and dividend income are taxed at a 15 percent rate  The rate will be reduced to zero in 2008 for taxpayers in the 10  and 15 percent tax brackets  Without action  these rates will increase after 2008  The provision extends these reduced rates through 2010                                                                  May 2006 News Releases             Browse by                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Current record
