                               MARTINEZ FIGHTS BILLBOARD PROVISION IN EMERGENCY WAR SUPPLEMENTAL  March 28  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today joined Tennessee Senator Lamar Alexander on legislation that would strike a provision from the Emergency War Supplemental bill that would permit the replacement or repair of billboards affected by natural disasters  The provision would exempt billboards in 13 states  including Florida  from removal standards enacted through the 1965 Highway Beautification Act  The result would allow aged and decrepit billboards expected to be removed in the next decade to be replaced and rebuilt anew     Martinez said     Reducing billboard clutter and regulating the size and placement of outdoor advertising is a difficult but important task for our communities  Senate Majority Leader Harry Reids measure weakens federal standards  allowing companies to disregard a federal requirement that prohibits structurally unsound billboards from being removed permanently  even if they violate zoning restrictions  The impact would be a huge number of billboards built using non conforming standards     As a matter of principle  we should not even be debating this issue as part of the Emergency War Supplemental  we should be concentrating on expediting funds so that our troops fighting the War on Terror continue to have the best available training and technology                                                                   March 2007 News Releases             Browse by
