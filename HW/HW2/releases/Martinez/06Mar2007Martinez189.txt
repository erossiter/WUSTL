                               MARTINEZ HONORS A CUBAN PATRIOT  March 6  2007   Washington  DC   WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today in a speech on the Senate floor honored Mario Chanes de Armas  the longest serving political prisoner of the Cuban dictatorship  Mr  Chanes passed away in late February     Martinez said     I rise today  first of all  to pay tribute to a Cuban patriot  His name is Mario Chanes de Armas     We speak of individuals who spend their lives fighting for the fundamental right of people to live in freedom  we often think of individuals like Nelson Mandela and Natan Sharansky     Today I want to share with you a story of Mario Chanes de Armas  who spent years in Castro s gulag  He was the longest serving political prisoner the world has ever known  30 years in prison for his political beliefs     Sadly  Mr  Chanes died last week at the age of 80 before his one true dream could be fulfilled  the freedom for the people of Cuba  I want to extend my condolences to the members of his family and his many friends     He was a man of great conviction and held a true love for humanity  Mr  Chanes was a wonderful freedom fighter in the truest sense of the world  Originally a labor leader  he demonstrated leadership and charisma and was an early ally of the then perceived reformer for Fidel Castro     They worked against the Batista dictatorship and he and Castro shared a cell until they were released     After his takeover  he began to see the true nature of the individual  of his former cell mate  He realized Castro did not care about human rights or democracy as he once claimed  but rather Castro became an irrational and devoted communist and enemy of freedom     For pointing out the danger of Castro s regime to the Cubans  Chanes was jailed for 30 years     Human Rights Watch points out they served months in cells sometimes without light or ventilation  They are often provided no beds  no mattresses  Their rations of food or water are barely enough to sustain life  Sanitation and medical conditions are so bad that inmates often leave prison with serious ailments if they are allowed to leave at all     Chanes de Armas suffered these conditions  For his opposition to dictatorship he was put in tapiadas  which were steel isolation cells  dark  and in gavetas  which were drawers so narrow he only had room to stand for days on end  feet swelling  and all sorts of other complications arising from hours upon hours of standing on your feet  For what  For refusing to reject communism  They never broke his spirit in spite of all of these punishments     Mr  President  today I want to take a moment to remember Mario Chanes de Armas for the ideals he tried so hard to bring to Cuba  democracy  rule of law  His dreams live on  and his legacy and his fight will continue and his legacy will light our way                                                                   March 2007 News Releases             Browse by
