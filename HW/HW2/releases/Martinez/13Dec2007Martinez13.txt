                               MARTINEZ  ENERGY BILL    POSITIVE STEP    TO INCREASING ENERGY INDEPENDENCE  December 13  2007   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today voted in favor of a national energy bill  a measure increasing national fuel efficiency standards and encouraging the use and development of renewable sources of energy and biofuels     This bill contains important priorities for securing future energy production  Martinez said  Its critically important that we increase fuel efficiency standards  encourage and expand the use of renewable fuels  and work toward greater energy independence  This bill moves us in that direction     The bill passed by the Senate will raise fuel economy standards to 35 miles per gallon for cars and light trucks by 2020  The current CAFE standard is 27 5 miles per gallon for cars and 22 5 miles per gallon for light trucks  a level which has been in place since 1985  Senator Martinez noted that increasing fuel efficiency standards will result in the saving of more than 1 5 million barrels of oil per day by the year 2030     The energy bill adopted by the Senate increases the Renewable Fuels Standard  RFS   which sets annual requirements for the amount of renewable fuels produced and used in motor vehicles  The expanded RFS requires 9 billion gallons of renewable fuels in 2008 and increases to a 36 billion gallon requirement by 2022     The energy bill also requires increased energy efficiency standards for consumer appliances  such as dishwashers  clothes washers  refrigerators and freezers  and requires the federal government to substitute energy efficient lighting for incandescent bulbs     Though this is historic and important legislation  we can still do more in the areas of energy production  Martinez said  Our nation faces serious energy challenges and the growing demand will have to be met by greater development of nuclear production  continued alternative fuels development  and streamlining of the process by which we develop energy     Martinez also noted his concern that the measure restricts the manner in which states like Florida can allocate highway dollars     There are still problems with this bill  It places limitations on how states use highway funds  Martinez said  I am hopeful we can fix that and other potentially problematic provisions
