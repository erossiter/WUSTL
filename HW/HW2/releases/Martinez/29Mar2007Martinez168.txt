                               MARTINEZ WINS BILLBOARD BATTLE     STRIKES PROVISION FROM EMERGENCY WAR SUPPLEMENTAL  March 29  2007   Washington   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today announced a provision that would permit the replacement or repair of billboards affected by natural disasters has been removed from the Emergency War Supplemental  The provision would have exempted billboards in 13 states  including Florida  from removal standards enacted through the 1965 Highway Beautification Act  If the provision had remained  it would have allowed aged and decrepit billboards expected to be removed in the next decade to be replaced and rebuilt anew  The effort  led by Senator Lamar Alexander  R TN   culminated in a brief floor debate where the provision was deemed non germane to the underlying war supplemental     Martinez said     Im pleased we won this battle  Reducing billboard clutter and regulating the size and placement of outdoor advertising is a difficult but important task for our communities  On the face of it  Senate Majority Leader Harry Reids measure is policy with which I disagree  as a matter of principle we should not even have had to debate this matter as part of an Emergency War Supplemental  Slipping billboard provisions into something thats supposed to fund our troops is an insult to those defending our nation
