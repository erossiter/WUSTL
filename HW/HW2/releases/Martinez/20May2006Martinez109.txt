                               MARTINEZ APPLAUDS VA SITE SELECTION FOR NATIONAL CEMETERY SERVING SARASOTA   May 20  2006   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today applauded the selection of a site for the new national cemetery serving the Sarasota region  Senator Martinez was informed by the Secretary of Veterans Affairs  VA  that the site selected is the Hawkins Ranch property  a parcel four miles east of Interstate 75  southeast of the city of Sarasota  Exit 205    This is great news for the veterans of Sarasota and the region  I am pleased this significant phase of the process is accomplished and the project can move forward  Martinez said   The Secretary of Veterans Affairs announced the selection this afternoon  The property to be acquired is an approximately 466 acre tract of pasture land which is part of a larger property known as the Hawkins Ranch  According to the VA  the cemetery will provide burials for veterans beyond the year 2040   Building this new cemetery is directed by The National Cemetery Expansion Act  Pub  L  No  108 109  which specifies that VA shall establish new national cemeteries in six areas of the country in greatest need for a veterans cemetery  Among those sites specified is the Sarasota County  Florida area   The Hawkins Ranch property has been selected as the VAs preferred alternative of the sites identified in the Environmental Assessment  Funds for purchase are in the FY 2006 VA appropriation                                                                 May 2006 News Releases
