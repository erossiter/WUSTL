                               MARTINEZ  SENATE APPROVES MINIMUM WAGE INCREASE  SMALL BUSINESS RELIEF   February 1  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today voted in favor of the Fair Minimum Wage Act of 2007  a bill to increase the federal minimum wage from  5 15 to  7 25 an hour in phased increments over a two year period  The measure also includes more than  8 billion dollars in tax relief provisions for small businesses most affected by the federally mandated increase in wages     I am pleased that we have completed a minimum wage increase  the first increase in almost a decade  I hope it will help many families in need  said Martinez  Coupled with the minimum wage increase are provisions to protect small businesses and their employees  Small businesses employ half of the private sector workforce and are most vulnerable to wage increases  their needs must be addressed     The following tax relief provisions were included in the legislation    Expensing  A one year extension of a provision allowing small businesses to combine as much as  112 000 in expenses into one annual tax deduction    Leasehold Improvements  An extension through March 31  2008  of the provisions that allow property owners to more quickly deduct the cost of making improvements to a restaurant or to leased property  Also  extends the provision to apply to improvements made to owner occupied retailer space and for the construction of new restaurants    Cash Method of Accounting  A permanent change to the tax code that will allow more businesses to simplify their bookkeeping by allowing them to use the cash method of accounting for tax purposes    S Corp Reforms  A modification to the standards that allow small businesses to qualify for or stay within the S Corp tax rules    Certified Professional Employer Organizations   Establishes a certificate program for Companies that provide and oversee employees for other corporations  The certification would require these companies to meet certain standards set by the IRS  Businesses that contract with certified professional employer organizations would be assured they would not be liable for those taxes already paid to the certified professional employer organization    Work Opportunity Tax Credit  A five year extension of the tax credit provided to employers who hire workers who have experienced barriers to entering the workforce  Also  a modification to the work opportunity tax credit to include a tax credit for employing veterans disabled after the September 11  2001
