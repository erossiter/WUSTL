                               MARTINEZ  WRDA PASSES SENATE  FLORIDA PROJECTS INTACT    July 19  2006   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Mel Martinez  R FL  today voted in favor of S  728  the Water Resources Development Act of 2005  WRDA   The measure authorizes numerous Army Corps of Engineers projects that are vital to Florida  The bill passed the Senate by voice vote    Florida has a great number of priorities in this bill including Everglades restoration  studying solutions for the dike at Lake Okeechobee  and port projects vital to our states commerce  Im pleased to see this bill move forward  Martinez said  The bill also contains long overdue authorizations for the Indian River Lagoon and Picayune Strand projects  These projects are essential to improving the overall health of the Everglades ecosystem   The last time WRDA was authorized was 2000  the reauthorization is approximately three years overdue  WRDA legislation provides the Army Corps of Engineers with the authority to study water resource problems  construct projects  and make major modifications to existing projects   I will continue working with my colleagues to require the Corps of Engineers to focus on the safety of the Herbert Hoover Dike  which protects communities in the Southern Everglades from flooding from Lake Okeechobee  said Martinez  Senator Bill Nelson and I are working to ensure this issue receives top priority   In late April  the South Florida Water Management District released a study highlighting concerns with the structural integrity of the Dike after the last two very serious hurricane seasons in Florida    The House of Representatives has already passed a version of WRDA  Differences must now be reconciled between the two bodies before a bill can be brought up for final
