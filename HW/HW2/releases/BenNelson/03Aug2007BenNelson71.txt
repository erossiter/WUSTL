                                                         Press Release    August 3  2007         NELSON   SENATE APPROVES CHILDREN S HEALTH CARE LEGISLATION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Nebraska s Senator s Small Business Health Care and Family Leave for Wounded Warriors Amendments Included                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          August 3  2007   The United States Senate yesterday approved legislation to reauthorize and expand the State Children s Health Insurance Program  S CHIP    Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson joined 67 of his colleagues in supporting the legislation          As governor of Nebraska  I worked with the legislature to create Kids Connection   This program expanded access to health care for thousands of children who would otherwise have done without   said Nelson    The Senate s actions will help Nebraska continue  and even improve  the Kids Connection program        S CHIP is a joint federal state program that is intended to provide health coverage for children whose families make too much to receive Medicaid  but either can t afford private coverage or do not receive it through their work         The reauthorization passed by the Senate yesterday devotes an additional  35 billion to the program over the next five years and provides states with flexibility and federal assistance to cover more children         The legislation also includes two amendments co sponsored by Senator Nelson   The first is a sense of the senate that affirms its intent to enact legislation this year to improve access to health care for employees of small businesses   Nelson has long pushed for such legislation  which is critically important in Nebraska as two out of three small businesses don t provide coverage for their employees       The other amendment expands Family Medical Leave to six months for the spouses  children  parents or next of kin of service members who suffer from a combat related injury or illness   This amendment is similar to legislation co sponsored by Nelson last week        These amendments cover two of my top priorities   better access to health care for employees of small businesses and better care for wounded warriors   said Nelson    As the chair of the Armed Services Personnel Subcommittee  I will continue to work to makes sure the men and women who serve this country receive the care they deserve
