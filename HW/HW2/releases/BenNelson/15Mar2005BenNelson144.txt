                     PRESS RELEASE  NELSON PUSHES FOR FIRST RESPONDERS IN BUDGET                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Press Release    March 15  2005         NELSON PUSHES FOR FIRST RESPONDERS IN BUDGET                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Nebraska  s Senator Wants to Restore Cuts to Fire Fighters  Police  and Homeland Security Grant Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             WASHINGTON  D C     The Senate is currently considering a budget resolution that includes drastic funding cuts for programs that provide training  equipment and resources to America  s firefighters  police and other first responders  Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson and Michigan Senator Debbie Stabenow today offered an amendment to the budget to restore those cuts and preserve a grant program that provides homeland security funds to states      Nelson  s amendment would restore  1 6 billion in cuts to first responder programs in the Fiscal Year 06  FY 06  budget resolution     Since coming to the Senate  Nelson has consistently fought for homeland security funding to help support local and state first responders  He has defended the COPS program  the federal Byrne grant program and other grant programs that support law enforcement and first responders from cuts      Nelson has championed the State Homeland Security Grant Program  SHSGP  which provides homeland security funds for States  The  SHSGP  provides funding to state governments to protect our hometowns   Under this program in 2003  Nebraska received  19 million for homeland security initiatives  Under the proposed changes to the program included in the budget  Nebraska would have received only  10 million       Nelson and Stabenow proposed closing tax loopholes to cover the cost of restoring the funds  Closing those loopholes would save the federal government  3 2 billion  of which  1 6 billion would be used to reduce the deficit and  1 6 billion would go toward the first responder programs  including           298 million to the State Homeland Security grant program          79 million to the Urban Area Security Initiative          226 million for firefighter assistance grants          485 million for the COPS program          537 million for the Byrne Justice Assistance grants       It  s irresponsible for Congress to impose unfunded mandates to states and local governments     said Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson     We must continue to take hometown security measures as seriously as we did immediately after 9 11 and remember that rural areas are vulnerable to terrorist attacks on the nation  s food supply and water resources  This amendment would have restored the much needed and depended upon funding that States like Nebraska need to ensure that our hometowns are as safe as they can be         The amendment failed on a 46 52 vote today  leaving the cuts to first responder programs intact        30
