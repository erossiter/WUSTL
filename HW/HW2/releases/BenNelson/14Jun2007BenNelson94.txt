                                                         Press Release    June 14  2007         NELSON  WOUNDED WARRIORS BILL CLEARS FIRST HURDLE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Nelson Amendment Adds Rehab and Vocational Care for Injured Troops                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 June 14  2007  Wounded active duty troops being treated at Veterans Affairs facilities would receive additional treatment they need through an amendment to the Wounded Warriors Act introduced by Nebraskas Senator Ben Nelson   The Senate Armed Services Committee today approved Nelsons amendment to authorize the Department of Defense  DoD  to pay the VA for the non medical services it provides to wounded active duty personnel  Currently  the DoD is only authorized to pay the VA for healthcare services it provides active duty service members and not for the vocational and rehabilitative treatment sometimes necessary for a soldiers recovery   When a soldier is severely injured while protecting our nation  we have every obligation to treat them to the fullest extent  said Nelson  This amendment ensures our wounded warriors will receive the non medical treatment they need to carry on with their lives and careers   The Dignified Treatment of Wounded Warriors Act  S  1606  is a comprehensive policy to improve health care for service members and comes in response to recent reports that have found care lacking The bill would require the Secretary of Defense and the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to develop a comprehensive policy by January 1  2008  on the care  management  and transition from the military to VA or civilian life of service members with combat related injuries or illnesses Other provisions of the bill include    Authorizing medically retired service membersto receive the active duty health care benefit for 3 years  Requiring the Secretary of Defense  in consultation with the Secretary of the Veterans Administration  to develop a comprehensive plan or plans on prevention  diagnosis  mitigation  and treatment of Traumatic Brain Injury  TBI  and Post Traumatic Stress Disorder  PSTD  The bill would also authorize  50 million for improved diagnosis  treatment  and rehabilitation of service members with TBI or PTSD  Establishing a DOD VA Interagency Program Office to develop and implement a joint electronic health record system  Requiring the military departments to use VA standards to make disability determinations  and prohibit deviation from VA standards except to give the service member a higher disability rating    The Senate Armed Services Committee approved the Wounded Warriors legislation  which is now ready for Senate floor debate  Senator Nelson is Chairman of the Senate Armed Services Personnel Subcommittee
