                                                         Press Release    March 2  2007         NELSON WANTS ANSWERS ON WALTER REED SCANDAL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Personnel Subcommittee Chairman Says Various Panels Must Report Quickly  Publicly   March 2  2007   The American people deserve clear and immediate answers to questions surrounding the management at Walter Reed Army Medical Center in Washington and assurances that the problems will be corrected and not repeated there or at other facilities  Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson said today        What the American people and the Congress want are answers  On Monday I will be seeking answers as to what steps are being taken to correct the problems at Walter Reed  I see this as a failure of the Army chain of command from both military and civilian leadership  The Pentagon and other the review panels announced by President Bush and Defense Secretary Gates must conduct immediate reviews of the decision process and oversight failures and make their reports public as soon as possible        The Washington Post has published a series of reports detailing unacceptable living conditions and breakdowns in services provided to wounded soldiers being treated at Walter Reed Army Medical Center in Washington  D C  Today  Defense Secretary Gates announced the resignation of the Secretary of the Army         Senator Nelson  Chairman of the Armed Services Personnel Subcommittee  will visit Walter Reed Hospital on Monday  March 5 to review facilities and talk to recuperating soldiers
