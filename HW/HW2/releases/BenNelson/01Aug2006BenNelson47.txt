                                                         Press Release    August 1  2006         NELSON  INCREASING NATURAL GAS SUPPLY BOOSTS US ENERGY SECURITY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  More Supply Could Lower Fertilizer Costs   Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson voted today to improve America  s energy security by expanding offshore drilling in the Gulf of Mexico  The energy boosting expansion will quickly increase the domestic production of natural gas  which is estimated to account for 70 to 90 percent of the cost of producing fertilizer  The bill passed the Senate today 71   25       Expanding the domestic production of natural gas in the Gulf not only holds hope for America  s energy security but also Nebraska  s agricultural producers     said Nelson        This increased production of natural gas is a tremendous relief for farmers and ranchers directly affecting their fertilizer prices and supply in the next few years  It is a much needed change for the first time in twenty years     said Bob Anderson  President of the Nebraska Agri Business Association  who lauded Nelson  s support of the issue     The Senate bill directs new oil and gas leasing in 8 3 million acres of the Gulf of Mexico and according to a preliminary CBO analysis will result in a net revenue gain of  926 million for the federal government  Drilling already exists in the region and the existing extraction and transportation infrastructure already in place could result in more immediate production than other proposals      Nelson cosponsored previous legislation  S  2253  expanding drilling in the region
