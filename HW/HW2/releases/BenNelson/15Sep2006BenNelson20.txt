                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                LINCOLN  NE    Expressing his disappointment that leaders of the Senate and House have prevented a vote on drought aid  Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson today invited top Congressional leaders to visit the National Drought Mitigation Center at the University of Nebraska Lincoln so they will learn first hand the urgent need for drought relief       I am very disappointed the Congressional leadership has worked so hard to prevent any farm relief legislation from being considered by this Congress  and I am deeply frustrated that this same leadership has utilized congressional procedures and technicalities to prevent an up or down vote on agriculture disaster relief     Nelson wrote in his letter     In letters to Senate Majority Leader William Frist  House Speaker Dennis Hastert  and Representative Bob Goodlatte  Nelson extended a personal invitation to each to visit the National Drought Mitigation Center at the University of Nebraska Lincoln       They need to come to Nebraska to see first hand the urgent need for drought aid     said Nelson     Maybe when they see the severe impact the multi year drought has had on our farmers and ranchers they will allow a vote on disaster relief for agriculture and treat our request for aid the same as they do aid for hurricanes and other natural disasters        Nelson also has written a letter to Nebraska Governor Dave Heineman asking that he get more involved in securing drought assistance by putting pressure on Congressional leadership and asking fellow governors to do the same
