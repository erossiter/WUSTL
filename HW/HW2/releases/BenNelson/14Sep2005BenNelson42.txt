                     PRESS RELEASE  SENATE ADOPTS GAS PRICE GOUGING INVESTIGATION AMENDMENT                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Press Release    September 14  2005         SENATE ADOPTS GAS PRICE GOUGING INVESTIGATION AMENDMENT                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson cosponsored Amendment Seeking FTC Probe                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              WASHINGTON D C     The Senate today unanimously approved an amendment  cosponsored by Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson and offered by Senator Mark Pryor of Arkansas  mandating the Federal Trade Commission immediately begin an investigation on nationwide gas prices in the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina  The amendment was accepted as part of the pending Commerce  Justice and Science  CJS  appropriations bill for Fiscal Year 2006         Consumers in Nebraska and across the nation deserve to know the root cause for high gas prices     said Senator Nelson    The Federal Trade Commission will look into this matter and report back to congress on the cause and economic impact of these record high prices for fuel         On September 6  Senator Nelson introduced a bill to require an FTC investigation into the rising cost of gas and possible price gouging  Nelson  s legislation requires the FTC to conduct a two week inquiry into the retail price of gasoline  The FTC would provide Congress a report on the results of the inquiry and Congress would conduct a public hearing on the FTC  s findings  The FTC would examine prices for both regular gasoline and ethanol blended gasoline  The amendment passed today is similar to Nelson  s bill        We must find out  not speculate  not accuse  not assume    but find out whether or not gas price gouging is occurring through the supply chain or distribution markets     Senator Pryor said    I believe this investigation will help us get to the root cause of exorbitant gas prices  and I believe it sends a strong signal to the American people that their concerns about skyrocketing gas prices are being heard          The provision requires the FTC to conduct a thorough and immediate investigation to determine if and where gas price gouging is occurring throughout the supply chain and distribution markets  Additionally  the investigation will include       A comparison and reasons for changes in profit levels over the past year for companies involved in the production  distribution  or sale of petroleum products   A summary of tax expenditures for companies involved in the production  distribution  or sale of petroleum products   The effects of increased gasoline prices and price gouging on economic activity in the United States   The overall cost of increased gas prices and price gouging to the economy  including the impact on consumers   purchasing power in both state and federal disaster areas  and other areas affected by disaster  and  Recommendations to Congress on legislative action to protect consumers from price gouging        The FTC will provide an initial report to Congress within 30 days  updates every 30 days afterwards  and a final report no later than 180 days after enactment       We need to ensure that price gouging isn  t taking place and that some entities are not trying to profiteer off a national tragedy     said Nelson    if evidence of wrongdoing is uncovered  Congress will take appropriate action
