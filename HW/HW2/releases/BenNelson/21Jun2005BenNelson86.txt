                     PRESS RELEASE  Merchant Mariners                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Press Release    June 21  2005         Merchant Mariners                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  WASHINGTON  D C  Thousands of World War II Merchant Mariners who have not been fairly compensated for their service will receive comparable treatment with other veterans under terms of a bill introduced Monday by Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson       Merchant Mariners transported troops  ammunition  food  gas  and other supplies that were necessary to win the war in open waters aboard slow moving cargo ships     said Nelson     They were a target of enemy forces which sank more than 800 of these Liberty Ships  as they were known  More than nine thousand Merchant Mariners were lost at sea   The casualty rate amounted to 1 out of every 26 which was the highest casualty rate of any branch of the service yet they have been denied fair treatment all these years because they were civilians        Nelson  s bill  S  1272  also known as   the Belated Thank you to the Merchant Mariners of World War II Act of 2005      would pay  1000 per month  tax free  to the merchant marines or their widows   It would also grant them   veteran    status which would increase their monthly credit by  160  thus increasing their monthly Social Security check       For more than 40 years the Merchant Marines were denied any G I  Bill of Rights benefits   In 1988  they were granted a watered down version of the G I  Bill of Rights but some portions of those benefits were never made available to them   My bill will finally grant these brave men their proper recognition as veterans    said Nelson     There are approximately 10 000 Merchant Marines and their widows who were old enough to serve in World War II still living   Their average age is 81   The cost of Nelson  s bill would total approximately  120 million the first year and drop by some  20 million per year after that as Merchant Mariners and their spouses pass on over the next 10 to 12 years     Nelson  s bill has been referred to the Senate Veterans Affairs Committee for further deliberation
