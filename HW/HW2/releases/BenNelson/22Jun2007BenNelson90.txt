                     PRESS RELEASE  NELSON OPPOSES STEM CELL PROVISION IN SPENDING BILL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Press Release    June 22  2007         NELSON OPPOSES STEM CELL PROVISION IN SPENDING BILL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Nebraska s Senator Also Questions Fiscal Responsibility of Certain Funding                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         June 21  2007   Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson today voted against a spending measure because it contained a controversial provision that would expand federal funding on embryonic research and raised concerns about inadequate and fiscally irresponsible funding for several Nebraska projects included in the bill        In particular  Nelson pointed to the inclusion of a provision that would allow an expansion of federal funding embryonic research   A similar  controversial measure was vetoed by President Bush yesterday       The Senate Appropriations Committee voted 26 3 to send the fiscal year 2008 Labor  Health and Human Services legislation to the full Senate committee for consideration  Nelson is a member of the Appropriations Committee        This spending bill contains funding for many worthwhile programs  such as Social Security and Medicare  K 12 education programs  higher education  Pell Grants and many other worthy programs   said Senator Nelson    The embryonic research provision raises concerns about a Presidential veto of the bill         Nelson also expressed concern about several Nebraska related projects that had been funded at an inadequate level   The level of funding provided will make it difficult if not impossible for the projects to be completed  In some cases  these projects  commonly referred to as  earmarks   were funded at less than 5 or 10 percent of the total cost of the programs  Nelson believes that the dramatic inadequate funding level for these projects could doom them to failure        I support each of these projects and I requested funding for many of them on behalf of Nebraska constituents   said Nelson    But inadequately funding these projects may result in the funds never being used or being used for something other than their originally intended purpose   We should either fund the projects at a level that gives them a chance for success or we should not fund them at all         Nelson plans to contact recipients of the funds before floor action on the bill to ensure that the funds allocated in the bill can be used for the intended projects and that other sources of funding can be accessed to complete the projects
