                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Press Release    March 29  2006         SENATE LOBBY REFORM BILL PASSES  INCLUDES TWO NELSON PROVISIONS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Nebraska  s Senator Supports New Rules Requiring Transparency  Disclosure                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           The U S  Senate today passed legislation governing the interaction of federal officials and lobbyists that will require more disclosure and transparency on a number of issues including earmarks and lobbying     The bill includes two provisions proposed by Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson including his proposal to establish an independent commission to make lobbying reform recommendations to Congress and his Sense of the Senate that any tougher rules adopted by Congress be placed on all branches of government        I am pleased that the new lobbying reform bill will require an independent commission to review these practices and make recommendations to Congress     said Senator Nelson  who earlier this year introduced legislation along with Senator Norm Coleman to establish the independent bipartisan commission    Taking steps to reform lobbying and increase transparency in government is important to begin to rebuild the trust of the American people in its government        The legislation includes provisions that would increase transparency with regard to Congressional earmarks  Each earmark must be accompanied by information including the name of the Member who sought earmark and an explanation for its justification  It will require conference reports to be available to the public and Members of Congress via the Internet at least twenty four hours before the vote  Also  a point of order procedure would be applied to any earmark that appears for the first time in a conference report  The lobby reform bill bans rescinds floor privileges to former members who are lobbyists  Lawmakers and their staff would be prohibited from accepting any gifts and meals paid for by lobbyists      The bill amends the Lobbying Disclosure Act to expand the definition of lobbyist and the activities that they engage in  including grass roots activities  Also it would require quarterly  instead of semiannual  filing of lobbying disclosure reports  and require electronic filing of lobbyist registrations and disclosure reports   Also it requires disclosure by registered lobbyists of all past executive branch and congressional employment
