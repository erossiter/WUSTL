                                                         Press Release    July 19  2007         NELSON  SENATE LIMITS COLLEGE AFFORDABILITY FOR AMERICA S FAMILIES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 July 19  2007   Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson today condemned the Senate s rejection of an amendment he offered to preserve college affordability options for America s families as a  disappointment for America s families        Nelson and Senator Richard Burr today offered an amendment to temper cuts in federal subsidies for the Federal Family Education Loan  FFEL  program  The Higher Education Reconciliation Bill  H R  2669  proposes cutting  18 5 billion over five years from the FFEL program  Nelson s amendment would maintain  15 65 billion in cuts and restore the difference  ensuring the program could continue to be an option for America s families and students seeking affordable college assistance        The Senate rejected the amendment by a 36 61 vote        Today s vote is a disappointment to all those American families seeking ways to afford higher education for their children   said Senator Nelson   Eliminating the FFEL program will make attending college more difficult and more expensive for thousands of students and their families  This bill reduces competition in lending and limits options for families seeking ways to make college more affordable        Eight of ten schools rely on the FFEL program to serve their students financial needs  Senator Nelson s floor
