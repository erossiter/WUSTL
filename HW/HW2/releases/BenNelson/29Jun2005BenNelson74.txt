                                                         Press Release    June 29  2005         NELSON STATEMENT ON ANNOUNCEMENT OF NEW ETHANOL PLANT IN ALBION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    WASHINGTON  D C     Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson today applauded Cargill  s decision to build a new ethanol plant in Albion  NE        This is great news for Nebraska corn producers   The prospects of passing energy legislation this year which includes a robust renewable fuels standard are great   Cargill  s announcement is timely and further demonstrates how Nebraska is positioned to take the lead in providing our nation with clean  domestically produced energy sources     said Senator Nelson     On June 28  the U S  Senate passed its version of a comprehensive energy bill  The bill establishes an aggressive Renewable Fuels Standard that requires oil refiners to blend 8 billion gallons of renewable fuels  mainly ethanol and biodiesel  into the nation  s transportation fuel supply by 2012     The price of gasoline at the pumps makes renewable fuels so important  The energy bill gives incentives for the installation of additional refueling stations and further extends production credits for biodiesel  another important and emerging source of domestic fuel      Soon after being elected Nebraska  s 37th Governor  Nelson formed and was the first Chair of the Governor  s Ethanol Coalition to promote increased ethanol use nationwide  The Coalition began with nine Midwest states and grew to 22 states  Puerto Rico and 2 foreign countries under his stewardship  More than 6000 Nebraskans are now employed directly or indirectly in Nebraska ethanol production  One of the first bills Nelson cosponsored as Senator was a bill to boost production of ethanol and other renewable fuels  which was the basis for the agreement in the Senate energy bill  Senator Nelson received the Renewable Energy Leadership Award in 2002 for his work promoting ethanol
