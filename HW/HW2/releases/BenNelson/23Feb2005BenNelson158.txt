                     PRESS RELEASE  MORSE BLUFF VOLUNTEER  CHAPMAN RURAL AND AURORA VOLUNTEER FIRE DEPARTMENTS RECEIVE FEMA FIREFIGHTING GRANTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Press Release    February 23  2005         MORSE BLUFF VOLUNTEER  CHAPMAN RURAL AND AURORA VOLUNTEER FIRE DEPARTMENTS RECEIVE FEMA FIREFIGHTING GRANTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        WASHINGTON  D C     Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson announced today that the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  and the U S  Fire Administration  USFA  has awarded Assistance to Firefighters Grants to the Morse Bluff Volunteer Fire Department  the Chapman Rural Fire Protection District and the Aurora Volunteer Fire Department     Designed to strengthen and enhance basic firefighting capabilities and other emergency services across the United States  the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program will give awards through four program areas   These areas include the Fire Operations and Firefighter Safety Program  the Fire Prevention Program  the Emergency Medical Services Program  and the Firefighting Vehicles Acquisition Program      The thirty sixth round of FEMA grants brings Fire Operations and Firefighter Safety funding to three rural Nebraska fire departments   The Morse Bluff Volunteer Fire Department will receive  85 500 for a firefighting vehicle  the Chapman Rural Fire Protection District will receive  71 931 for fire operations and fire safety  and the Aurora Volunteer Fire Department will receive  10 800 for fire operations and fire safety      The Fire Operations and Firefighter Safety Program provides funding for additional training  personal protective equipment  wellness and fitness  and modifications to fire stations and facilities   This grant also provides funding for new firefighting equipment   The Firefighting Vehicles Acquisition Program includes the purchase of  pumpers  brush trucks  tankers tenders  rescue vehicles  ambulances  quints  aerials  foam units and fire boats         With these funds  our fire departments can expand their efforts to continue to make Nebraska as safe as it can be  said Nelson        These grants will assist these rural fire departments in more effectively and resourcefully protecting Nebraska
