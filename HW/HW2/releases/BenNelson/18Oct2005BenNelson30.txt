                                                         Press Release    October 18  2005         NELSON  EXTEND PROGRAM THAT SUPPORTS NEBRASKA AIRPORTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Nebraska  s Senator Believes Smaller Airports Need More Time to Recover From 9 11 Attacks                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           WASHINGTON  DC   Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson and several Senate Colleagues today offered an amendment that would help small airports that are still feeling the negative impact of the 9 11 terrorist attacks on their ridership levels   The amendment was introduced on Monday by Senator Jeff Bingaman of New Mexico       The amendment would allow small airports to continue to receive the same level of funding through the Air Improvement Program  AIP  as they did prior to the terrorist attacks  It was offered to be considered during debate on the fiscal year 2006 transportation appropriations bill       Nelson  s amendment would give the virtual primary airports one more year to regain a level of 10 000 boardings        Three Nebraska airports    Scottsbluff  Kearney  and Grand Island    had enplanements above 10 000 in 2000 and have been considered virtual primary airports for the past two fiscal years  The FAA recently posted preliminary enplanement data for 2004  which will be used to determine the Fiscal Year  FY  2006 AIP entitlements  According to this preliminary data  Kearney  and Grand Island will fall short of the 10 000 threshold   While Scottsbluff  for the first time since 2001  will exceed 10 000 boardings        Smaller airports like those in Kearney  Grand Island and Scottsbluff are not only convenient for residents but  they provide a vital link for business and help ensure that those Nebraska Communities remain competitive in attracting new opportunities   said Nelson    Without the current level of federal funding  the future of these airports is very much in jeopardy          Joining Senators Bingaman and Nelson as cosponsors of this amendment are Senators Carl Levin and Debbie Stabenow  both of Michigan        In 2003 Congress passed VISION 100  the FAA reauthorization bill  which gave certain primary airports two years to regain a minimum of 10 000 boardings to retain their minimum AIP entitlement of  1 million   These airports are dubbed   virtual primary    airports   The two year   grace period    for the virtual primary airports expired September 30  2005   That provision was offered by Senator Nelson and was based on his Small Airport Releif Act  SARA  that protected funding levels based on ridership during the aftermath of September 11th
