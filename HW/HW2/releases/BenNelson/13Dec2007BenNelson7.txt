                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Press Release    December 13  2007         NELSON   ENERGY BILL WILL EXPAND PRODUCTION OF RENEWABLE FUELS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     December 13  2007  The United States Senate today approved legislation to diversify our nation s energy sources  promote conservation and reduce our dependence on foreign sources of fuel    Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson joined 85 of his colleagues in voting in favor of the legislation  Nelson issued the following statement    In a short period of time  ethanol has become a major industry in Nebraska and a major source of fuel across the nation  It is time to expand on that success    This new legislation will continue to allow for the growth of ethanol  while also promoting other biofuels  such as those derived from cellulosic material  The bill will raise the Renewable Fuel Standard to 36 billion gallons by 2022  more than half of which would have to come from advanced biofuels  It also provides resources for more biofuel research    This renewed focus on ethanol and other renewable fuels will reduce our dependence on foreign sources of oil  protecting the environment  and spurring economic development in rural communities    The legislation must now be passed by the House of Representatives and sent to the President for his approval
