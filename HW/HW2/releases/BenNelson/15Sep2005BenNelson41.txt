                     PRESS RELEASE  NELSON  CJS BILL INCLUDES ANTI METH MEASURES AND GAS PRICES INQUIRY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Press Release    September 15  2005         NELSON  CJS BILL INCLUDES ANTI METH MEASURES AND GAS PRICES INQUIRY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON D C     The United States Senate today completed work on an appropriations bill that includes three measures  cosponsored by Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson  that will assist local law enforcement in combating meth and require the Federal Trade Commission  FTC  to examine the cause of record high gas prices        The Senate passed the Fiscal Year 2006 Commerce  Justice  and Science  CJS  Appropriations bill 91 4 after accepting the three amendments cosponsored by Nelson  The bill will now go to a conference committee to iron out differences with the House of Representatives   version of the bill        The three amendments cosponsored by Nelson                    Examination of High Gas Prices by the FTC  On Wednesday  the Senate adopted an amendment that mandates the Federal Trade Commission to immediately begin an investigation on nationwide gas prices in the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina  Earlier this month  Nelson introduced a stand alone bill that would require a similar investigation into the root cause of high gas prices by the FTC                    Increase funding for the Byrne JAG program  On Wednesday  the U S  Senate adopted unanimously an amendment that restores funding for the Byrne Justice Assistance Grant  JAG  program to the Fiscal Year 2003 level   Nebraska has used the Byrne funds to develop and operate 9 multi jurisdictional drug task forces  training for law enforcement officers  implement and upgrade the State s Automated Fingerprint Identification System  AFIS   support for the operation of rural Drug Courts  develop and implement a comprehensive treatment program for drug offenders within the State Dept  of Corrections  train law enforcement officers  develop and operate a Drug Prosecution Unit in the Attorney General s office  and to develop and operate the computerized Nebraska Criminal Justice Information System                    Combat Meth Act  On Friday  the Senate adopted the Combat Meth Act as an amendment to the CJS bill  The Combat Meth Act will set a federal minimum for the treatment of pseudoephedrine products by placing them behind the counter  requiring a signature and identification for purchases  and setting a monthly limit of 7 5 grams per person  Senator Nelson sought to make changes that would create protections for rural consumers  The result is a provision that would allow the DEA and States to develop alternative procedures to continue to allow cold medicine to be sold at retail stores without pharmacies and in rural areas which meet appropriate security criteria  Additionally  the Combat Meth Act authorizes more funding to help fight meth  including enforcement  training and research dollars           Efforts to fight meth in Nebraska will receive an important boost by the provisions the Senate approved today     said Senator Nelson    And Consumers will soon have useful information regarding the high cost of gasoline and its economic impact  I am pleased the Senate has approved these measures
