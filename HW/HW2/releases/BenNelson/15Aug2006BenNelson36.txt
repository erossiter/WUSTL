                                                         Press Release    August 15  2006         NELSON  RENEWABLE FUEL SECURITY TO BE A PRIORITY FOR SENATE AG COMMITTEE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson continues to emphasize the importance of renewable fuels for our national energy security in advance of Wednesday  s Senate Agricultural Committee hearing in Grand Island   He has worked to get a farmer representative from the ethanol industry on the witness panel and he continues to work on cutting edge ethanol issues such as cellulosic ethanol that can be produced from biomass energy crops like switchgrass       I believe that American agriculture can lead the way in our search for alternative and renewable sources of fuel  moving the US toward energy security and eventually  energy independence     said Nelson    Our farmers are poised to provide the nation with an abundant source of clean  high quality energy that will reduce our destructive reliance on foreign oil    just as we are seeing in Nebraska        Currently  there are 12 Nebraska ethanol plants that produce well over 500 million gallons of ethanol each year while employing thousands of Nebraskans in quality jobs   Furthermore  researchers at the University of Nebraska continue to research and develop production of switchgrass and they are keeping Senator Nelson informed of their progress and findings       My hope for the new farm bill  which I  m calling the Food and Fuel Security Act  is that we will achieve what I know is possible  a safe and secure food supply and progress toward energy independence        Soon after being elected Nebraska  s 37th Governor  Nelson formed and was the first Chair of the Governor  s Ethanol Coalition to promote increased ethanol use nationwide  The Coalition began with nine Midwest states and grew to 22 states  Puerto Rico and 2 foreign countries under his stewardship   More than 6000 Nebraskans are now employed directly or indirectly in Nebraska ethanol production   One of the first bills Nelson cosponsored as Senator was a bill to boost production of ethanol and other renewable fuels  which was the basis for the agreement in the Senate energy bill   Senator Nelson received the Renewable Energy Leadership Award in 2002 for his work on that bill
