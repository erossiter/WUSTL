                     PRESS RELEASE  NELSON INTRODUCES LEGISLATION TO ASSIST RURAL NEBRASKA AIRPORTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Press Release    September 30  2005         NELSON INTRODUCES LEGISLATION TO ASSIST RURAL NEBRASKA AIRPORTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    WASHINGTON  DC   Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson is seeking to help small airports that are still feeling the negative impact of the 9 11 terrorist attacks on their ridership levels   He is a cosponsor of bipartisan legislation that will allow small airports to continue to receive the same level of funding through the Air Improvement Program  AIP  as they did prior to the terrorist attacks       In 2003 Congress passed VISION 100  the FAA reauthorization bill  which gave certain primary airports two years to regain a minimum of 10 000 boardings to retain their minimum AIP entitlement of  1 million   These airports are dubbed   virtual primary    airports   The two year   grace period    for the virtual primary airports expires today  September 30  2005    The bill would give the virtual primary airports one more year to regain a level of 10 000 boardings       Three Nebraska airports    Scottsbluff  Kearney  and Grand Island    had enplanements above 10 000 in 2000 and have been considered virtual primary airports for the past two fiscal years  The FAA recently posted preliminary enplanement data for 2004  which will be used to determine the Fiscal Year  FY  2006 AIP entitlements  According to this preliminary data  Kearney  and Grand Island will fall short of the 10 000 threshold   While Scottsbluff  for the first time since 2001  will exceed 10 000 boardings       The 9 11 attacks had a devastating impact on the aviation industry and small airports were no exception   Ridership levels have been slowly increasing at these airports and Senator Nelson believes it is appropriate to give these airports another year to recover and try to get their ridership back to pre 9 11 numbers         Rural Nebraska airports are not only convenient for residents but  they provide a vital link for business and help ensure that western Nebraska remains competitive in attracting new opportunities   said Nelson    Without the current level of federal funding  the future of these airports is very much in jeopardy          Other sponsors of Senate bill 1793 are Senator Jeff Bingaman New Mexico  Senator Arlen Specter of Pennsylvania  Senator Tom Harkin of Iowa and John Rockefeller of West Virginia        In 2003  Senator Nelson introduced the Small Airport Relief Act  SARA   a bill designed to protect small airports from facing federal funding cuts due to decreased ridership in the aftermath of the September 11th terrorist attacks  Nelson  s bill was adopted as part of VISION 100 the FAA Reauthorization Act in 2003  The legislation Nelson cosponsored would extend the provisions originally included in the SARA legislation
