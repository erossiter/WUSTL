                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Press Release    March 17  2005         STATEMENT OF NEBRASKA  S SENATOR BEN NELSON ON THE 2006 BUDGET RESOLUTION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           WASHINGTON  D C     The United States Senate today completed work on the 2006 Budget Resolution that sets the budget for next year at  2 6 trillion dollars and includes  2 8 billion in cuts over the next five years to agriculture  Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson issued the following statement       Even though some of the controversial cuts were reinstated  I voted against this budget because once again    as last year   it treats rural communities and farmers and ranchers unfairly  The budget includes steep cuts in agriculture programs and law enforcement programs that smaller and rural communities rely on to keep their citizens safe from crime and drugs       Agriculture spending is less than 1  of the total U S  budget  yet has been asked to shoulder a disproportionate share of the proposed budget cuts   Agriculture spending is not the cause of the federal deficit  and should not bear a disproportionate share of responsibility for eliminating it        This budget does not include a penny in anticipated cost for Social Security reform and it includes only a fraction of the cost of the war in Iraq        As a former Governor  I am accustomed to balanced budgeting  But  in Washington the budget process is like trying to bake a pie one piece at a time         30
