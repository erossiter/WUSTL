                     PRESS RELEASE  NELSON  AMENDMENT SECURES  470 MILLION FOR HUMANITARIAN FOOD ASSISTANCE AS PART OF EMERGENCY FUNDING BILL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Press Release    April 20  2005         NELSON  AMENDMENT SECURES  470 MILLION FOR HUMANITARIAN FOOD ASSISTANCE AS PART OF EMERGENCY FUNDING BILL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Bipartisan amendment restores funds for programs that fight hunger in Sudan  Ethiopia  Bangladesh   Africa                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON    A bipartisan proposal to devote  470 million to international humanitarian food assistance as part of the  80 6 billion emergency spending bill was passed unanimously by the Senate today   The amendment was authored by Wisconsin Senator Herb Kohl and Ohio Mike DeWine and was cosponsored by Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson     In the wake of the urgent need for food aid following the tsunami disaster  the U S  Agency for International Development  USAID  had to divert assistance from many existing crises in other parts of the world   The bipartisan amendment provides funding for ongoing USAID efforts in Sudan  Ethiopia  Bangladesh and Africa  The original FY05 Supplemental Appropriations Bill provided only  150 million  which was insufficient for USAID to meet its commitments         Even thought the media coverage of the tsunami disaster has receded  the need for humanitarian assistance remains and must be met     said Senator Ben Nelson    The unexpected and large scale tsunami disaster forced humanitarian organizations to redirect resources from other areas of need in Africa and around the globe  The need in those areas has not gone away and we must step up our commitment to fight starvation and disease in these vulnerable populations       The food aid amendment will allow the U S  to meet the estimated emergency food requirements in areas that have been identified by USAID for assistance this year  This includes Sudan  due to the conflict in Darfur  Ethiopia  due to drought  Bangladesh  due to flooding  western Africa  due to locusts  and the Democratic Republic of Congo  Chad  Rwanda and Uganda due to refugees and fighting  The amendment restores funding to developmental food aid programs that were diverted to meet emergencies in other parts of the world   These programs are vital to long term food security in those countries  The amendment also restores funds to the Food for Progress Program that were diverted to provide immediate assistance to victims of the Indian Ocean Tsunami        The proposal is supported by the Coalition for Food Aid  a group of international relief agencies   Their members include the American Red Cross  CARE  Catholic Relief Services  Save the Children and the amendment is also endorsed by the U S  Conference of Catholic Bishops   The amendment is also endorsed by many commodity organizations throughout Nebraska and the United States      30
