                                                         Press Release    July 31  2007         NELSON   SENATE SHOULD PASS BILL TO HONOR WWII MERCHANT MARINES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  House of Representatives Clears Merchant Marines Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              July 31  2007   The House of Representatives passed legislation yesterday to recognize the contributions of World War II era Merchant Marines   Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson  the senate sponsor of the legislation  praised the House s actions and called for the Senate to follow suit        Thousands of men served their nation honorably as merchant mariners during the Second World War   Yet they have never been appropriately recognized for their contribution to the war effort   said Nelson    The House just took a major step to rectify this situation and now it is time for the Senate to do the same        For more than 40 years the Merchant Mariners were denied any G I  Bill of Rights benefits   In 1988  they were granted a watered down version of the G I  Bill of Rights but some portions of those benefits were never made available to them         Nelson s bill  S  961   the Belated Thank you to the Merchant Mariners of World War II Act of 2007    would pay  1000 per month  tax free  to the merchant marines or their widows     The companion version of the bill  HR23  introduction in the House by Veterans Affairs Committee Chairman Bob Filner  passed yesterday by voice vote        The Senate version of this legislation has already garnered the support of 46 of my colleagues who have signed on as co sponsors   said Nelson    With the strong backing of these individuals  I will work with the leadership of both parties to do what is right for these merchant marines   pass this bill          There are approximately 9500 Merchant Mariners and their widows who were old enough to serve in World War II still living   Their average age is 83
