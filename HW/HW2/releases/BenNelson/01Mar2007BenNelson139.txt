                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Senator Responds to Findings of the Commission on the National Guard and Reserves                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  March 1  2007   The Commission on the National Guard and Reserves released a report today stating that our reserve military components do not receive sufficient funding or equipment and are often left out of important planning for national emergencies   Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson  a co sponsor of legislation to elevate the status of the Guard to Joint Chiefs level  issued the following statement on the report        For years  I have fought to make sure that the National Guard and Reserves receive equitable treatment when it comes to equipment and training   The role of the National Guard and Reserves has changed since September 11th from a strategic reserve to an operational force  yet the structure of our armed services has not kept pace with this transition   I m pleased that the Commission recognized the need to make institutional changes  and although I disagree with their belief that the chief of the National Guard doesn t need to be a member of the Joint Chiefs of Staff  I believe this report is an important stepping off point as Congress works to provide the Guard and Reserve with the resources and input they need to effectively do their jobs        Senator Nelson  the chairman of the Senate Armed Services Personnel Subcommittee  is an original co sponsor of the National Guard Empowerment Act   This legislation would give the National Guard a seat at the table in the Pentagon where deployment  strategic  economic and personnel decisions are made   It would enhance coordination among military leaders and Governors and local elected officials during emergencies
