                                                         Press Release    March 29  2006         CONGRESS APPROVES BILL TO HONOR TUSKEGEE AIRMEN                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Nelson    Nebraska is honored to count 4 of these living legends among its citizens                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   WASHINGTON  DC    Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson is applauding final approval of legislation that honors the Tuskegee Airmen with the Congressional Gold Medal       The Tuskegee Airmen are true American patriots in every sense of the word     said Nelson     Their bravery during wartime is a fascinating story by itself but what gives them   Living Legend    status is their much broader achievement that extends beyond the heroism during war   These are men who had to overcome prejudice and discrimination just so they could serve their country and in so doing lead the way toward integrating the armed forces  These are men who are to the military what Jackie Robinson was to baseball        Tuskegee Airmen is the term used to describe African American fighter pilots of the 99th Pursuit Squadron  later incorporated into the 332nd Fighter Group  who fought during World War II in the U S  Army Air Corps   There were 926 Tuskegee Airmen   66 died in combat   33 were shot down and became prisoners of war   Survivors stay in touch through Tuskegee Airmen groups around the country  During 200 missions escorting B 17  s  B 24  s and British bombers  the Tuskegee Airmen never lost a bomber they were escorting to enemy fighters   No other escort unit could claim such a record       Nelson was a co sponsor of S  392  which passed the Senate Tuesday and was sent to President Bush for his signature   The House had earlier approved similar legislation to honor the Tuskegee Airmen with a Congressional Gold Medal in recognition of their unique military record which inspired revolutionary civil rights reform in the Armed Forces     Of the approximately 18 original Tuskegee Airmen with ties to Nebraska by birth or having lived here  4 currently reside in Nebraska   They are  Lt  Col  Charles Lane  retired  of Omaha  Lt  Col  Harrison Tull  retired  of Bellevue  Robert Holts of Omaha and Lt  Colonel Paul Adams  retired  of Lincoln
