                                                         Press Release    April 17  2006         NELSON PROMOTES SMALL BUSINESS HEALTH INSURANCE PLAN TO SMALL BUSINESS ENTREPRENUERS IN OMAHA                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Nebraska  s Senator has a plan to lower costs  expand coverage for America  s small businesses                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Omaha  NE    America  s small businesses can look forward to more affordable health insurance coverage for their employees in the near future  Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson said today     Before more than a hundred small business owners  employees and business leaders at a forum in Omaha  Nelson said his legislation designed to empower small business to band together to purchase health insurance for their employees will drive down costs and increase coverage for those currently uninsured   Nelson has joined with Senator Mike Enzi  WY   the Chairman of the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee  in offering S  1955  the Health Insurance Marketplace Modernization and Affordability Act       Health care premiums are experiencing double digit growth annually and small businesses can  t keep up with the costs  Since 2000  group premiums for family coverage have grown nearly 60 percent     Senator Nelson told the audience    If we don  t do something to help small businesses cope with the costs of health care  soon we will have an entire workforce without health insurance coverage        Nelson  s legislation will address high costs for health insurance by allowing small business to band together to increase their purchasing power and better negotiate health plans and coverage for their employees  An independent study found that the Nelson Enzi small business health insurance bill will reduce costs by twelve  12  percent and could insure nearly one million currently uninsured employees     In Nebraska  there are at least 30 000 small businesses with fewer than 50 employees that would be directly impacted by this legislation  Currently  20 000 of these businesses don  t offer health benefits      Nelson spoke to small business entrepreneurs and employees at the Harry A  Koch Company Annual Meeting  The event was held today at the University of Nebraska at Omaha William H  Thompson Alumni Center
