                                                         Press Release    July 13  2007         NELSON  WOUNDED WARRIORS BILL ADDED TO DEFENSE AUTHORIZATION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Nelson Amendments Improve Respite Care  Travel Reimbursements                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      July 13  2007   Help is on the way for injured and ill veterans in the veterans health care system  Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson said today after the U S  Senate adopted a comprehensive military health system reform package as an amendment to a defense bill on Thursday  The Senate also approved on Thursday two Nelson authored amendments designed to improve health benefits for military personnel and their families        The Dignified Treatment of Wounded Warriors Act  S  1606  legislation Nelson has cosponsored  was added as an amendment to the must pass fiscal year 2008 Defense Authorization bill  HR 1585   Also included in the package were two amendments by Senator Nelson  an original co sponsor of the legislation and the chairman of the Senate Armed Services Personnel Subcommittee       One amendment would authorize respite care for family members serving as primary caregivers to seriously injured active duty service members   This care would ensure that the service member continues to receive appropriate medical care in the case that the family members are not able to do so   The other would authorize travel reimbursements for military personnel  who have retired for medical reasons  which can be used to reduce costs for veterans who travel to receive specialty health care for disabilities incurred during combat        Despite differences of opinion about the war  we are all in agreement that we have an obligation to provide the very best care for military personnel who are wounded or become ill in the line of duty   said Nelson   The Senate has now taken an important step toward improving our military health care system   Help is on the way        The Dignified Treatment of Wounded Warriors Act  S  1606  is a comprehensive policy to improve health care for service members and comes in response to recent reports that have found care lacking   The bill would require the Secretary of Defense and the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to develop a comprehensive policy by January 1  2008  on the care  management  and transition from the military to VA or civilian life of service members with combat related injuries or illnesses   Other provisions of the bill include         Authorizing medically retired service members to receive the active duty health care benefit for 3 years     Requiring the Secretary of Defense  in consultation with the Secretary of the Veterans Administration  to develop a comprehensive plan or plans on prevention  diagnosis  mitigation  and treatment of Traumatic Brain Injury  TBI  and Post Traumatic Stress Disorder  PSTD    The bill would also authorize  50 million for improved diagnosis  treatment  and rehabilitation of service members with TBI or PTSD    Establishing a DOD VA Interagency Program Office to develop and implement a joint electronic health record system    Requiring the military departments to use VA standards to make disability determinations  and prohibit deviation from VA standards except to give the service member a higher disability rating
