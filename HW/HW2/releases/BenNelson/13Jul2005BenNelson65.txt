                                                         Press Release    July 13  2005         NELSON STATEMENT ON NEW ESTIMATED   333 BILLION DEFICIT FOR 2005                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   WASHINGTON  D C     The White House Office of Management and Budget  OMB  today released new budget deficit projections that indicate the federal budget deficit will reach  333 Billion in 2005     Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson has worked in Congress to control unchecked spending  In 2001  he offered a   circuit breaker    that would have required immediate action by Congress to check spending if budget projections dipped into deficits  Nelson has also advocated   pay as you go    spending restrictions on the federal budget that would require all new budgetary revenue and spending adjustments to be offset   The Washington Congressional Leadership opposed and rejected both efforts     Nelson today issued the following statement regarding the new deficit projections       Today  s deficit projections present a bleak economic picture and one that Washington cannot continue to ignore  It is the height of irresponsibility to continue to spend the country into these record deficits that threaten our future economic security  That  s why I have proposed and supported responsible budgeting by pushing a pay as you go requirement that would force Congress to pay for any new spending through spending reductions in other programs  Unfortunately  the leadership in Washington rejected that approach in favor of continued unchecked spending
