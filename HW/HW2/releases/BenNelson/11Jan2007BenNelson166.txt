                                                         Press Release    January 11  2007         NELSON REACTS TO BUSH SPEECH ON IRAQ PLAN                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Following the President  s address to the nation regarding the future of Iraq  Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson released the following statement       Tonight the president outlined a new plan for the U S  mission in Iraq  I agree with his approach to establish benchmarks for progress    or conditions for staying    for the Iraqi government to meet  I also believe the push for job creation is prudent because it could accomplish much in the effort to reduce violence        I am trying to maintain an open mind  however  I cannot ignore the recommendations of the Iraq Study Group and our senior military leaders who have all but unanimously said that sending more troops to Iraq is not the answer   After two meetings at the White House in the last five days  I am not convinced that sending in an additional 21 000 troops will quell the sectarian violence or stabilize Baghdad       The President  s plan depends on many assumptions     It assumes an increase of 21 thousand troops is sustainable     And if the increase is sustainable  it assumes that 21 thousand troops are sufficient to achieve the objective of securing Baghdad     It assumes the National Guard and Reserve components will not be further stretched by this deployment     It assumes Iraqi troops will be trained  equipped  and capable and will show up and engage the enemy     It assumes Prime Minister al Maliki can deal with Moqtada al Sadr and his militia     It assumes the Shiia dominated government can achieve national reconciliation and put an end to the civil war       The failure of any one of these assumptions could undermine the entire plan        Senator Nelson met with Steve Hadley  Assistant to the President for National Security Affairs  in the White House today  Nelson discussed the plan for Iraq with the President at the White House Friday  He serves on the Senate Armed Services and Appropriations Committees
