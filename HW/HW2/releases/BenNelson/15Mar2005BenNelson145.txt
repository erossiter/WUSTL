                     PRESS RELEASE  NELSON SUPPORTS FIRST RESPONDERS IN BUDGET                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Press Release    March 15  2005         NELSON SUPPORTS FIRST RESPONDERS IN BUDGET                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON  D C     Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson announced today that he will cosponsor an amendment offered by Senator Debbie Stabenow of Michigan  to restore the  1 6 billion in first responder cuts to the Fiscal Year 06  FY 06  budget     Nelson has continuously fought for homeland security funding to help support local and state first responders   Nebraska received  19 million in 2003 from the State Homeland Security grant program  Under the proposed changes they would have received only  10 million       Of the  3 2 billion generated by closing the tax loopholes   1 6 billion would be used for deficit reduction and  1 6 billion would go toward the first responder programs including           298 million to the State Homeland Security grant program           79 million to the Urban Area Security Initiative           226 million for firefighter assistance grants           485 million for the COPS program          537 million for the Byrne Justice Assistance grants      It  s irresponsible for Congress to impose unfunded mandates to states and local governments     said Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson     We must continue to take hometown security measures as seriously as we did immediately after 9 11 and remember that rural areas are vulnerable to terrorist attacks on the nation  s food supply and water resources   This amendment will restore the much needed and depended upon funding that states like Nebraska need to ensure that our hometowns are as safe as they can be          30
