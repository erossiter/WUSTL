                                                         Press Release    February 9  2005         NELSON COSPONSORS BILL TO EASE RESTRICTIONS ON TRADE WITH CUBA                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Nebraska  s Senator Working to Clarify Payment Provisions to Facilitate Sales of Nebraska Products                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  WASHINGTON  D C     Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson is continuing his efforts to promote the sales of Nebraska agricultural commodities  food products  and agricultural inputs to improve the rural economy         Today  with Senator Max Baucus  D MT   Senator Larry Craig  R ID   and 17 additional cosponsors  Senator Nelson cosponsored a bill to facilitate the sale of agricultural products to Cuba as authorized by the Trade Sanctions Reform and Export Enhancement Act of 2000         Congress was pretty clear in its intent to loosen trade restrictions with Cuba when it passed this legislation     said Senator Nelson     A recent decision by the Treasury Department to interpret the law outside of the normal and customary rules of international trade is a kind of   de facto   restriction that creates a dangerous precedent         The bill is designed to bring clarity to provisions regarding the method of payment between the buyers and sellers of these products     I contend  as do a number of my colleagues on both sides of the aisle  that these transactions should simply require payment to be received by the seller prior to the shipment s release to the buyer     said Nelson      Cuba was ranked at the bottom of 226 agricultural export markets for United States companies in 2001  and now ranks in the top 25     It  s a very important source of revenue for U S  agriculture  including the manufacturers and distributors of grains and value added food products      Nelson added    Watching helplessly as Cuban importers make the economic decision to conduct business elsewhere in order to minimize the risk associated with the onerous rules on transfer of payments is not an acceptable situation             30
