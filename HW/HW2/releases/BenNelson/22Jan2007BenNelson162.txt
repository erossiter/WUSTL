                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Press Release    January 22  2007         NELSON PROPOSAL TO REFORM EXECUTIVE   JUDICIARY BRANCH ETHICS TO GET ANOTHER LOOK                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Following passage of an Ethics Reform bill aimed at  Congress  Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson announced plans for future legislation to reform Executive and Judiciary branch ethics       I am pleased that Congress has shown it is taking seriously its responsibility to the American people by tightening the rules and laws that govern members of the Senate  but many of the reforms we have approved should apply to all branches of government     Nelson said      Nelson laid the groundwork for future legislation by introducing an amendment to S 1  the Ethics Reform bill   Senator Joe Lieberman  Chairman of the Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee  has assured Nelson that the amendment warrants thorough evaluation and will be considered by his committee later this year     Last year the Senate expressed its desire to expand ethics reform to other branches of government when it approved a sense of the Senate amendment sponsored by Nelson     The amendment would limit gifts and travel paid by lobbyists and requires a waiting period after someone leaves a government job before they could begin lobbying activities
