                                                         Press Release    February 28  2007         NELSON ANNOUNCES VISIT TO WALTER REED MEDICAL CENTER                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Tour Will Come In Advance of Senate Armed Services Hearing on Conditions at Facility                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               February 28  2007   Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson announced today that he would tour Walter Reed Medical Center next Monday  March 5  to view conditions firsthand and talk with service members   Media reports have found that physical conditions in at least one of the outpatient facilities were severely degraded and that  generally  those recovering had to endure a bureaucratic maze to get treatment  disability benefits or information on their status in the armed services        What happened at Walter Reed was as national disgrace   The men and women being treated there have fought for our country overseas  yet now it seems that they had to live in deplorable conditions while fighting for even a basic level of information and services   said Nelson    I want to see firsthand what is going on there and speak to the men and woman who have so honorably serviced our nation        Next Tuesday  March 6  the Senate Armed Services Committee will hold hearings on the conditions at Walter Reed   Senator Nelson is a member of the committee and the chairman of the Personnel Subcommittee   Last week  he wrote to the Chairman of the Armed Services Committee asking for hearings on Walter Reed
