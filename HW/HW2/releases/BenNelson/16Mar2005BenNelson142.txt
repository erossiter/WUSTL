                     PRESS RELEASE  NELSON INTRODUCES THE SUPPORT OUR SCOUTS ACT OF 2005                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Press Release    March 16  2005         NELSON INTRODUCES THE SUPPORT OUR SCOUTS ACT OF 2005                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Legislation will protect Boy Scouts Ability to Meet on Military Bases and other Federal Property                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   WASHINGTON  D C    Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson today joined U S  Senate Majority Leader Bill Frist  M D   R TN  and Senate and House colleagues to introduce the  Support Our Scouts Act of 2005   This legislation will ensure that the Boy Scouts of America are treated fairly by guaranteeing their right to equal access to public facilities  forums  and programs       This legislation addresses attempts to exclude the Boy Scouts from such facilities at the federal  state  and local levels  and legal moves by groups to discontinue longstanding Defense Department support to the Scouts  Boy Scouts  like other non profit youth organizations  depends on the ability to use public facilities to allow their members to participate in their leadership development programs and forums        Since 1910  the Boy Scouts have helped mold America s future leaders   said Frist   The Boy Scouts have instilled in our youth values of honesty  integrity  and character  These unique experiences have empowered millions of America s youth with the skills they need to serve as our nation s future leaders         As an Eagle Scout  I know first hand about the kind of leadership development programs and learning opportunities the Boy Scouts provide to millions of young Americans   said Senator Nelson   I am concerned by the threat to the Boy Scouts ability to continue to provide these opportunities on our military bases and other federal installations        The  Support Our Scouts Act of 2005  allows military families who live on military installations to enjoy the same community benefits of families who don t live on bases by providing the opportunity to participate in a rewarding and enriching child development activity like the honored and storied Boy Scouts organization  The legislation guarantees the Boy Scouts  right to equal access by removing any doubt that Federal agencies may welcome Scouts to hold meetings and go camping on federal property  It ensures the Defense Department can continue providing support consistent with U S  law  The legislation also ensures state or local government entities do not deny Boy Scouts equal access to public facilities  programs  or forums  Finally  the act clarifies that federal agencies cannot provide less support to the Scouts than the agency has in the past       Senator Frist is the lead author of this legislation  and he is cosponsoring the bill along with Nebraska s Senator Ben Nelson  Senator Alexander  R TN   Senator Allen  R VA   Senator Burns  R MT   Senator Bunning  R KY   Senator Craig  R ID   Senator Ensign  R NV   Senator Enzi  R WY   Senator Nelson  D FL   Senator Sessions  R AL   Senator Smith  R OR   Senator Talent  R MO  and Senator Vitter  R LA        The Boy Scouts of America was founded in 1910  Its goal then and still today is to help young people grow into responsible  well rounded citizens  More than 100 million Americans have participated in programs offered by the Boy Scouts of America
