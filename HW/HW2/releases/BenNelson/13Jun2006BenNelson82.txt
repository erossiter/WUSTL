                                                         Press Release    June 13  2006         NELSON RENEWS EFFORT TO PROVIDE AGRICULTURE SECURITY TAX CREDIT                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Bill Helps Farmers and Agricultural Businesses Secure Chemicals                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    In response to the recent foiled Canadian terrorist plot  Nebraska  s Senator Ben Nelson amplified his efforts to provide farmers and agricultural businesses a tax credit for securing their chemical storage facilities from terrorists  Today  Senators Nelson and Pat Roberts  KS  circulated a dear colleague letter to garner support for legislation from their fellow Senators       Agricultural pesticides and fertilizers  while important to farmers  can also be used for illegal purposes  including the manufacture of explosives and meth     said Nelson    Unsecured fertilizer is a very real threat  one brought home to us by recent terrorist activity in Canada        Nelson and Roberts introduced the bipartisan Agricultural Business Security Tax Credit Act  S  2052  last November that would provide a tax credit of up to 30  for costs associated with securing fertilizer and other chemicals in rural areas  The program is voluntary  and eligible agricultural businesses would qualify for the 30  tax credit to offset security costs that can soar to  100 000 per site       As a member of the Senate Ag Committee  I know the important role of agricultural businesses in making our country the leading agricultural producer in the world     said Nelson    And as a member of the Senate Armed Service Committee I know the need to encourage proactive efforts that safeguard our national security by appropriately securing agricultural chemicals and pesticides that could be used for terrorist purposes
