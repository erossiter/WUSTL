SENS. MIKULSKI, CARDIN ANNOUNCE $1 MILLION FEDERAL GRANT TO ESTABLISH, PROTECT BALTIMORE COUNTY AGRICULTURAL CENTER
  Sens. Barbara A. Mikulski and Benjamin L. Cardin (both D-Md.) announced today that the National Park Service has awarded a $1 million Land and Water Conservation Grant to Baltimore County to acquire a129.3-acre site in the Worthington Valley for development of the Baltimore County Agricultural Center.  
   The Baltimore County Agricultural Center will include a working farm, demonstration projects of forestry and vineyards, educational programs, meeting rooms and offices for all Baltimore County agricultural programs. The site also will house the headquarters for the Maryland Breeders Association. 
   "I'm proud of this federal-state partnership to protect Maryland's heritage and land. This grant will help Baltimore County residents and visitors enjoy the natural beauty of our state," said Senator Mikulski. "Marylanders can count on me to continue to make protecting our environment a priority in the federal checkbook." 
   "This grant will make it possible for a significant site within Baltimore County to remain agricultural in nature. Open space is critical to good environmental planning, and I join Baltimore County and Maryland in its commitment to preserving agricultural land that can be accessible to the public," said Senator Cardin, a member of the Environment and Public Works Committee. 
   The Baltimore County Agricultural Center will be located at the gateway to the Worthington Valley on Shawan Road, across from Oregon Ridge Park. Baltimore County will provide a $697,540 match and the State of Maryland will provide a $2,164,210 match. The total cost of the project will be $3,926,750.
