CONGRESS APPROVES DEFENSE SPENDING BILL, INCLUDES $32.7 MILLION FOR HARFORD, CECIL COUNTY ARMED FORCES
  Sens. Barbara A. Mikulski and Paul Sarbanes (both D-Md.) announced today that both the U.S. House and Senate have given final approval and sent to President Bush for his signature the 2006 Defense spending bill, which includes an additional $150 million for Maryland defense projects. The legislation funds the Department of Defense and will go toward critical research, development, test and evaluation programs, as well as weapons and equipment required for the U.S. Armed Forces.  
   "We need to do everything we can to support our troops - not just with words, but with deeds. Together, we can do better. These much needed funds will help our troops fulfill their mission both at home and abroad," said Senator Mikulski. "I will keep fighting to put money in the federal checkbook for U.S. troops and their families." 
   "We continue to work hard to ensure that Maryland's military installations have the resources and capabilities necessary to carry out their critical missions and this funding helps in these endeavors," said Senator Sarbanes. "This bill also provides needed funding to enable our troops to have the most up to date equipment for them to successfully carry out their missions." 
   The spending bill includes more than $32.7 million in additional funding for Harford and Cecil County projects, including: 
   MARYLAND NATIONAL GUARD AND RESERVE 
   * $11.2 million for a M22 Automatic Chemical Agent Alarm (ACADA)
   * $2.8 million for Extended Cold Weather Clothing System (ECWCS) 
   U.S. MARINE CORPS 
   * $5.6 million for All Purpose Environmental Clothing System 
  ABERDEEN PROVING GROUND 
   * $4 million for a Rotorcraft Survivability Assessment Facility to better protect military helicopters 
   * $3 million for the Fluorescence Activated Sensing Technology Integrated Threat Management System to detect and identify biotech agents, chemicals and toxins 
   * $2 million to create the Knowledge Integration and Management Center of Excellence at Morgan State University in collaboration with the Army Research Lab to help turn data into useful knowledge for soldiers 
   * $2 million for Spinel Tactical Armor Manufacturing Technology - a transparent, lighter, better armor material for vehicles and aircraft 
   * $1.1 million for the Aberdeen Technology Transfer Initiative 
   * $1 million for a Laser Interrogation of Surface Agents - JCSD Solid State Laser chemical and biological detection system 
   Senator Mikulski is a member of the Appropriations Committee and the Subcommittee on Defense. Senator Sarbanes is the senior Democrat on the Banking Committee.
