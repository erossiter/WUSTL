                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi pleased with extension of border closure for Canadian beef  March 2  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi praised the recent delay in the opening of the U S  Canadian border to imported cattle     The U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  was set to open the border to Canadian cattle on March 7  In January R CALF United Stockgrowers of America sued the USDA in a federal Montana court citing the border opening would put consumers and ranchers at risk  Judge Richard Cebull granted a preliminary injunction on the border re opening until the lawsuit is heard     When I traveled the state last week  concerns about opening the U S  border to Canadian cattle was a worry among constituents  These concerns are valid  My colleagues and I are working to ensure proper steps are followed before opening the border and this delay gives us more time  said Enzi  There is currently no way for consumers to tell the difference between U S  and Canadian beef without country of origin labeling  With confirmed cases of mad cow in Canada  opening the border should not be taken lightly  Until country of origin labeling is implemented the border should stay closed    Three cases of mad cow disease have been confirmed in Canada  Enzi has worked to keep American consumers informed as well as bolster American agricultural markets with country of origin labeling implementation  Enzi wrote a letter on Jan  11  2005 urging the USDA to safeguard the nations beef supply by keeping the border closed to live cattle under 30 months old and certain other animals and beef products from Canada  Enzi has also introduced legislation with U S  Senator Tim Johnson  D S D   to keep the border closed until country of origin labeling is implemented  Mandatory country of origin labeling is scheduled to be implemented by September 2006                                                        March 2005 News Releases           Browse by
