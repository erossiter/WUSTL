                                                                                                                                                                                      Looking for solutions  not closed doors  September 4  2007 Washington  D C    U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   said the Park Service should keep its eye on benefitting the public as it decides the Sylvan Pass issue  The National Park Service recently issued a risk assessment concerning avalanche risks in Sylvan Pass and the Park Services proposed closing of the East Entrance of Yellowstone National Park  I have said all along and will continue to say that the Park Service needs to look for ways to keep the pass open and available for the public rather than looking for ways to justify closing it  Shutting out people and restricting access is not the answer to preserving our national parks  The citizens  business owners and outdoor enthusiasts in the area are working towards solutions  not justification  Enzi will review the assessment further and may make official comments with the other members of the Wyoming delegation on the importance of keeping Sylvan Pass open during winter months                                                      September 2007 News Releases           Browse by                                                      Current record
