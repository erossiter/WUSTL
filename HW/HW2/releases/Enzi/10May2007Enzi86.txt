                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi continues action to declare English as the nation  s official language  May 10  2007 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   co sponsored a bill recently that would make English the official language of the United States  Since the founding of this nation the Constitution and other important government documents have been written in English  Now its time for us to write English into the law  said Enzi  This bill is about promoting a common language that provides unity among citizens and fosters greater communication Establishing an official language would save the government the expensive and time consuming task of preparing documents in many languages  Along with declaring English as the official language of the United States the bill would require individuals to demonstrate sufficient understanding of English for usage in everyday life during the naturalization exam to attain citizenship   The bill  the Official English Act of 2007  S  1335  was introduced by Senator Jim Inhofe  R  Okla   and is estimated to save taxpayers  1 2 billion each year on funds currently used to provide federal forms and perform services in languages other than English  Enzi has supported similar legislation in the past   English is the official language of 27 states and 51 nations  Wyoming declared English to be the official language of the state in 1996
