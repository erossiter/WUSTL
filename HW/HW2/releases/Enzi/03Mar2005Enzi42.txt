                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi  hunting  fishing states   prerogative  March 3  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   is co sponsoring legislation that would ensure hunting and fishing in Wyoming stays regulated by the state  not the feds    The bill  S  339   was introduced in direct response to a recent court decision handed down by the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals  The court ruled that state hunting and fishing regulation negatively affects interstate commerce  By charging different prices for licenses for non residents and state residents and for limiting the issuance of tags for non residents  the court concluded that state regulation of recreational hunting could be unconstitutional because interstate commerce is under federal jurisdiction    Wyoming s wildlife is Wyoming s wildlife and it s worth our watching out for who manages it  said Enzi  It s the state that bears most of the cost of wildlife management even for species like the wolf and grizzly bear  This legislation represents a reality check for the Ninth Circuit  We are not going to give away control to the federal government of one of Wyoming s most important assets    The bill would protect the traditional authority of the states to regulate hunting and fishing and allow states to continue distinguishing between residents and non residents when issuing hunting and fishing licenses     The bill has been referred to the Senate Committee on the Judiciary  It is a bi partisan bill also sponsored by senators Harry Reid  D Nev   Max Baucus  D Mont   Ted Stevens  R Ala   John Ensign  R Nev   and Ben Nelson  D Neb
