                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi  s military anti fraud bill goes to President  September 21  2006 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   is pleased a bill he authored that will protect military personnel against individuals using deceitful practices to sell financial products is on its way to the President   The House passed Senator Enzis Military Personnel Financial Services Protection Act today by a vote of 418 3  President George W  Bush is expected to sign the bill  From the beginning drafts of this bill up to the accomplishment of sending it to the President I have kept in mind the military families who have been taken advantage of  said Enzi  member of the Senate Banking Committee  Keeping our military safe at home and overseas is not a partisan issue and this bipartisan bill is a testament to that   Some financial services companies have been disguising  marketing and selling high cost periodic payment plan certificates solely to military personnel on military bases  These plans  nonexistent in the civilian market  are similar to mutual funds except extremely high sales commissions rob investors of years of earnings and penalize them when they stop investing in the fund  Some insurance products also sold exclusively to the military provide very low death benefits while charging very high premiums  Salespersons have disguised the product to appear endorsed by the Department of Defense or the federal government  Many investors who fall prey to these types of sales become aware that they were scams too late  The bill would provide for state insurance regulator jurisdiction over the sale of insurance products on military bases  It would also require the Department of Defense and federal securities regulators to keep a list of individuals barred or banned from military bases  Finally  it would ban the sale of periodic payment plans  some of the worst types of financial scams perpetrated against our military personnel   Abuses have been taking place on military bases for decades and most recently brought to light by a series of New York Times articles  according to Enzi  The Senate passed the Military Personnel Financial Services Protection Act on July 20  2006   An audio
