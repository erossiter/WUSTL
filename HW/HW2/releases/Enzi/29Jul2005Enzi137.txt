                                                                                                                                                                                      Thomas  Enzi help protect Second Amendment   July 29  2005 Washington  D C   The Senate passed a bill co sponsored by U S  Senators Craig Thomas and Mike Enzi  both R Wyo   Friday which would prevent frivolous lawsuits that threaten Second Amendment freedoms     The Senate passed the Protection of Lawful Commerce in Arms Act  S  397  by a vote of 65 31  Thomas and Enzi said it would block politically motivated lawsuits against gun manufacturers and sellers  Over the past few years dozens of cities have brought lawsuits against gun manufacturers and others seeking damages for the unlawful use of their products  The bill would prevent litigation brought against gun manufacturers for the criminal use of their products  The bill would not affect suits brought because of legitimate product failure  While the legislation does not grant blanket immunity to manufacturers  it does allow legitimate victims their day in court for cases involving defective firearms     Holding a gun manufacturer responsible for the criminal use of their product simply doesnt make sense  But more disturbing than that is watching gun control advocates abuse the courts with lawsuits aimed at getting their way with public policy  Thomas said  While a significant number of the cases like this have been dismissed in court  these frivolous lawsuits impose heavy legal costs and threaten an industry thats important to Wyoming    This bill will not only protect the rights of Wyoming gun owners  it will prevent bogus litigation from bankrupting an industry that provides jobs  recreation and protection for millions of Americans  Gun manufacturers shouldnt be held responsible for the deliberate misuse of their products any more than knife or baseball bat makers should be for theirs  said Enzi     Thomas  Enzi and other supporters of the legislation point out that the bill would protect national security by keeping military gun contracts in the hands of American firms  Domestic companies  like the Beretta Corporation  say they risk bankruptcy without the types of reform contained in the bill     Senator Larry Craig  R Idaho  originally sponsored the legislation  introduced on February 16 of this year  A similar bill  H R  800  passed the House Judiciary Committee and is awaiting further action by the House of Representatives     end
