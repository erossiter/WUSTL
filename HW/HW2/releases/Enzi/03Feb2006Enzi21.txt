                                                                                                                                                                                      Thomas  Enzi push to keep Air and Army guard personnel  February 3  2006    Washington  D C   In a pre emptive shot across the bow         U S  Senators Craig Thomas and Mike Enzi  both R Wyo   warned the White        House that if its planning to cut the number of Army and Air Guard        troops it should consult with the states Adjutant Generals first    In a letter to Secretary of Defense Donald Rumsfeld  Thomas and Enzi along        with 73 other senators  urged the Department of Defense to keep the number        of personnel for the Air and Army Guard at its current numbers     The        National Guard is a dual purpose force that has demonstrated its inherent        value to the security of the nation in response to overseas threats and        domestic disasters  the senators wrote  The National Guards        response to hurricanes Katrina and Rita demonstrated the value of the National        Guards paramount mission to support civilian authorities  defend        the homeland  and respond to emergencies at home    Recent reports have indicated that the DOD may recommend personnel cuts        for both the Army and Air Guard in the Presidents fiscal year 2007        budget request which is expected to be released next week  In response to        the overwhelming support for the letter  Army officials announced plans        to reevaluate cutting National Guard personnel
