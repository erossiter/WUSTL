                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi pushes for clean troop  veteran funding bill Leadership should not meddle with military funding October 31  2007 Washington  D C    U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   rebuffed plans by Democrat congressional leadership this week to attach excessive and wasteful non military spending to a military spending bill intended for troops and veterans   I have voted against most of Congresss spending bills this year because they were laden with unnecessary spending and drastically bloated this nations already increasing deficit  Now leadership is looking to attach even more pork to a bill that should be strictly focused on veterans and providing our nations heroes with the resources needed to defend our country  Our military men and women should be equipped to battle our countrys enemies and our veterans should be taken care of  not put in the middle of the partisan political boxing ring in Washington  said Wyomings senior senator   The Senate passed the Military Construction and Veterans Affairs Appropriations Act  H R  2642  by a vote of 92 1 on Sept  6  The House of Representatives passed the bill on June 15  The differences between the two bills need to be worked out in a conference committee but the House of Representatives has yet to name conferees to the committee   Enough is enough  The president is ready to sign this bill and there is no excuse for a hold up  The American people support our past and present soldiers and wont accept more delays when a clean military funding bill could have and should have been passed weeks ago  said Enzi
