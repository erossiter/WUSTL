                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi  hunting  fishing will stay regulated by states  May 10  2005 Washington  D C   Wyoming will remain in control of hunting and fishing regulations once the President signs a bill Congress passed today  according to U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo    Wildlife is one of Wyomings most important assets and this bill reaffirms that Wyoming citizens  who bear most of the costs associated with managing wildlife  will remain in control  said Enzi     The Senate passed the supplemental appropriations bill conference report accompanying H R 1268 by a vote of 100 0  Enzi voted for the bill which included an amendment that would protect the traditional authority of the states to regulate hunting and fishing and would also allow states to continue distinguishing between residents and non residents when issuing hunting and fishing licenses     Language in a previous bill Enzi co sponsored  S  339  which would keep control of hunting and fishing regulations out of the hands of the feds  was included in the supplemental bill that will now be sent to the President for his signature    Enzi co sponsored the legislation after court rulings from the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals called into question the authority of states to distinguish between resident and non resident hunting and fishing licenses    The people of Wyoming know how to manage the wildlife in our state better than the Ninth Circuit Court or the federal government  This bill makes sure that Wyoming citizens  who have the most pride in area wildlife  are the people in charge  said Enzi    The House passed the conference report on
