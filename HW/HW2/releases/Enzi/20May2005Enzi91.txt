                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi works to terminate death tax   May 20  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   co sponsored legislation recently that would immediately put the federal estate tax six feet under     Eliminating the death tax would greatly benefit farmers  ranchers and small business owners who work their whole lives to provide for their families  Everyone is taxed enough on the money they earn when theyre alive  said Enzi  Congress needs to send the death tax to its grave so citizens will know that when they pass away they are passing everything they left to their families  not the government    Enzi co sponsored S  988  the Jobs Protection and Estate Tax Reform Act of 2005  introduced by Senator Jeff Sessions  R Ala  that would permanently repeal the death tax this year     Under the Economic Growth Tax and Relief Reconciliation Act passed in 2001  the death tax would gradually phase to nothing in 2010  but the bill included a sunset provision that would reinstate the tax in 2011  The death tax is now 45 percent on estates worth more than  1 5 million  The Jobs Protection and Estate Tax Reform bill would immediately and permanently repeal the tax     Enzi has voted several times in past Congresses to permanently repeal the death tax  He is also a co sponsor of  S  420  a bill similar to S  988 that would permanently repeal the death tax  The House passed a similar estate tax bill  H R  8  last month    The American Family Business Institute awarded Enzi with the Defending the American Dream award for his continuous efforts to repeal the death tax this month       end
