                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi committee moves forward on health bills  February 9  2005 Washington  D C    The Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee  HELP Committee   added to its list of accomplishments today by unanimously passing multiple healthcare measures that  among other benefits  would improve Americans access to healthcare insurance  according to the panel s chairman Mike Enzi  R Wyo     Enzi said the bills would encourage progress on two important fronts in health care policy  security of personal genetic information and access to health insurance for individuals with preexisting health conditions     The Genetic Nondiscrimination Act  S  306  would prohibit health insurers and employers from discriminating on the basis of genetic information  The State High Risk Pool Funding Extension Act  S  288  would extend seed money and federal grants that support state based programs to maintain health insurance pools for high risk individuals who  because of their health problems  cannot find coverage in the private insurance market     The committee gathered in a room just off the Senate floor after a vote and approved the bills in rapid fashion     Five bills  Three nominations  Four minutes  said Enzi  We are efficiently working our way through these important bipartisan measures  some of which we ve been working on for years  I m hopeful we will have floor time scheduled very soon on at least one of them  the Genetic Nondiscrimination Act  We ll work hard to keep our progressive momentum going and I thank Ranking Member  Ted  Kennedy and all committee members for their diligence     Other bills approved at today s mark up  include  S  265   reauthorization of the Trauma Care Systems Planning and Development Act  a bill to help states develop and implement trauma care systems  S 302   the Foundation for the National Institutes of Health Improvement Act  a bill making technical changes to improve how the Foundation operates  S 285   the Children s Hospitals Education Equity and Research Act  a bill to reauthorize support for the training of the next generation of pediatricians     The committee also approved two nominees for the National Museum and Library Sciences Board and the nominee to be Inspector General of the Corporation for National and Community Services     Enzi is hopeful the bills will be equally successful on
