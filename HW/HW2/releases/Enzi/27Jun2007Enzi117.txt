                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi works to ease restrictions on Cuba  June 27  2007 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   a staunch advocate for free trade and travel to Cuba  co sponsored legislation recently to ease trade and end travel restrictions between the U S  and Cuba  The U S  can be of greater help to the people of Cuba by allowing the trade of products and ideas than it can by trying to force a change in government through unilateral isolation  said Enzi   Senators Max Baucus  D Mont   and Mike Crapo  R Idaho  introduced the Promoting American Agricultural and Medical Exports to Cuba Act  which Enzi co sponsored  The bill would remove further barriers for exporting goods to Cuba by broadening markets for potatoes  wheat  peas  beans  barley  lentils  dairy  beef  pork  wood  medical supplies and other products  The bill would eliminate current restrictions on travel to Cuba that only allow Cuban Americans with nuclear family still on the island to return to Cuba once every three years  The bill  if passed  would be the second easement in trade restrictions on the country since the embargo was enacted in 1962 as protest to the communist regime  The bill  S  1673  has been referred to the Senate Finance Committee                                                       June 2007 News Releases           Browse by                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Current record
