                                                                                                                                                                                      Senate committee passes   national   designation of Jackson museum   July 25  2007 Washington  D C   The National Wildlife Art Museum in Jackson overcame another hurdle to officially be able to claim that title  according to U S  Senators Mike Enzi and John Barrasso  both R Wyo  House Concurrent Resolution 116  would officially designate the Jackson gallery as a national museum  The Senate Energy Committee cleared the bill today  It is now headed to the Senate floor for consideration by the full Senate   A national designation would recognize what Wyomingites already know   this museum is different than any other in the country  It embodies the sacred connection wildlife  nature and humans share  Enzi said  I hope the full Senate will consent to this resolution so we can pass it unanimously  I was very pleased to have voted for this bill in committee  said Barrasso  I commend Mike and Barbara for their hard work to ensure the National Wildlife Art Museum in Jackson receives the designation it so richly deserves  A national designation for the museum is to secure a national reputation and awareness of the museum  not an attempt to acquire federal funding  according to Enzi  The resolution would not require the Presidents signature in order to take effect   The House of Representatives passed House Concurrent Resolution 116  introduced by Representative Barbara Cubin  R Wyo   on June 6  2007  Enzi introduced similar legislation in the Senate and then took the lead on ushering through House Concurrent Resolution 116 following the unanimous House passage
