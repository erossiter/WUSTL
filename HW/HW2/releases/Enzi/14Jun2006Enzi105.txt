                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi says SOS spending reform bill is money   June 14  2006 Washington  D C   U S  Sen  Mike Enzi  R Wyo   along with Budget Committee Chairman Judd Gregg  R N H   Majority Leader Bill Frist  R Tenn   and other senators today unveiled the SOS  Stop Over Spending Act  The bill is a comprehensive plan to rein in spending  reduce the deficit and inject some reality into the federal budget process  Everyone loves the guy who picks up the tab  Uncle Sam has been doing it for years  but what the American people are beginning to realize is that hes been doing it with IOUs  These IOUs will have to be paid by our kids in the form of drastic cuts in services and taxes higher than weve ever seen  Its past time we pay our own way and put responsible spending controls in place  Thats just what Chairman Gregg  my colleagues and I are doing with this comprehensive bill  said Enzi who is a member of the Senate Budget Committee and a cosponsor of the legislation  This bill turns red into black and puts us on solid ground with four solid budget pillarsspending restraint  budget enforcement  fiscal responsibility and deficit reduction  The bill would define deficit reduction targets and put in place budget caps  It would require emergency spending to be counted against budget caps and establish across the board cuts if the caps were exceeded  Under the SOS bill  the President would have the ability to veto specific spending provisions within large appropriations bills  as well as mandatory spending  SOS would put Congress on a biennial budget time line similar to how the Wyoming Legislature works  Appropriations would include funding for two years instead of one to give more time for oversight of that spending                                                       June 2006 News Releases
