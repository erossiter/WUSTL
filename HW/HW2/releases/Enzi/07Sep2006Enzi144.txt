                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi backs transparency spending bill  September 7  2006 Washington  D C   Imagine a user friendly Web site that would allow visitors to track all federal earmarks  grants  contracts and loans  U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   and his Senate colleagues have envisioned such a website and have written legislation to make it possible for taxpayers to see how their money is being spent and hold the government accountable for excess spending   This bill would create a one stop shop to see if Congress is being good stewards of taxpayer dollars  If the public isnt aware of money being spent it is unable to hold Congress responsible for excess and extravagant spending  This database would shine light on a process that needs reform  said Enzi  member of the Senate Budget Committee  This bill would finally give the public an honest look at the ledgers and as an accountant I know the value of keeping tabs on expenditures     Enzi co sponsored the Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act  S  2590 that would require the Office of Management and Budget to maintain and establish a website that lists all entities receiving federal funds in amounts over  25 000  The information would include the name of each receiving entity  the amount of federal funds received annually by the program and the location of the entity  All information must be posted within 30 days of funding being awarded to an organization     The Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs passed the bi partisan bill introduced by Senators Tom Coburn  R Okla   and Barack Obama  D Ill   and is awaiting debate on the Senate floor  The House has passed similar legislation to create a database for federal grants    Around  300 billion in grants are approved by the federal government every year
