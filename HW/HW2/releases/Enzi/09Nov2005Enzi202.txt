                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi helping tribes build and fund affordable housing  November 9  2005 Washington  D C   Housing on the Wind River Reservation is set to improve with the passage of a bill that would help unemployed and out of school young adults build affordable housing in their communities while receiving education and leadership development  according to U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   a proponent of the legislation     Home ownership is one of the most important building blocks in a successful community  said Enzi  This bill recognizes the pride and sense of accomplishment that comes from owning and working on a home  Not only can young people be proud of this work  but they can also be proud of their membership in a community through such collaborative efforts as this grant offers     Under the Native American Housing Enhancement Act of 2005  young adults ages 16 to 24 in the Shoshone and Arapaho Indian tribes of Fremont County are eligible applicants for the Youthbuild program  This program provides valuable experience  education  and leadership development for unemployed individuals and those not currently enrolled in school  Youth involved in the program construct and rehabilitate affordable housing in their communities to be used permanently for low income housing    The Senate passed H R  797 unanimously last night which included language identical to a bill Enzi introduced earlier this year  Representative Rick Renzi  R Ariz   introduced H R  797 which is the House version of Enzis bill  The legislation clarifies that tribes and tribal entities can access certain grant income used for affordable housing activities  ultimately providing more flexibility in planning  The bills also make it clear that tribes can qualify for funding from the U S  Department of Agriculture while exercising tribal preference in contracting without violating the Civil Right Act  The House bill  H R  797  as passed by the Senate was slightly changed from the original version and must go back to the House for approval  Enzi is hopeful this will happen soon so the President can sign the bill into law    Last year Enzi cosponsored a similar bill  but the 108th Congress adjourned before the bill could be taken up  Since 1993 when the Youthbuild program was created  more than  300 million Youthbuild grants have been awarded to public and private non profit organizations  Over 8 351 young people have participated in the program and constructed or rehabilitated more than 4 935 affordable housing units in their communities  For more information in the
