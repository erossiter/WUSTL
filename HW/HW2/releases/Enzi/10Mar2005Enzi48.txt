                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi votes to protect legitimate bankruptcy filers  March 10  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   voted today in support of a bill that would protect legitimate bankruptcy filers and help to prevent unjust filings    This bill targets the abusers of the system  not those who legitimately need help  Many people who cant financially recover from a medical tragedy  one of the number one reasons for bankruptcy  or other severe misfortune will be protected by this bill  said Enzi  a member of the Senate Banking Committee  There are  however  people out there who abuse the system and use bankruptcy to get out of debt they can pay  We all absorb the costs of fraudulent bankruptcies by paying higher interest rates and fees    The Senate passed S  256 by a vote of 74 25 Thursday evening    Enzi said the wording of the bill has been painstakingly written over eight years in order to restore the responsibility and integrity of the bankruptcy system  The bill would     Establish a means test to distinguish between legitimate filers who need Chapter 7 protection and those who can pay a portion of their debts under Chapter 13 or those who do not need bankruptcy at all      Permanently extend Chapter 12  the farmer bankruptcy chapter to help farmers and ranchers keep their land and schedule flexible repayment plans if forced to declare bankruptcy due to severe financial difficulties because of drought     Help small business debtors comply with uniform bankruptcy standards and implement faster claim processes so businesses and their employees can get back to work     Enact safeguards for victims of abusive creditor practices and give priority claims to child support payments    My colleagues and I will also continue working to find solutions that get to the root causes of bankruptcy  Making health insurance and medical expenses affordable is one of my priorities as chairman of the Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee and if we can lower these costs it will have a positive effect on the number of bankruptcies filed each year  said Enzi                                                       March 2005 News Releases           Browse by
