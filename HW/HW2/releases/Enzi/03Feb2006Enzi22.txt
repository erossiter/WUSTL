                                                                                                                                                                                       Enzi  Senate send tax deduction bill to conference committee  February 3  2006    Washington  D C   The U S  Senate passed a bill yesterday that would        continue state and local sales tax deductions  aid students in paying college        tuition  help teachers pay for classroom supplies  and help the economy        grow  according to U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   who voted for the bill           The Senate passed the Tax Relief Extension Reconciliation Act of 2005  H R         4297  by a vote of 66 31 late last night     This bill extends        provisions in the tax code so that families  students  teachers and hard        working Americans can breathe a little easier during tax season and contribute        to our economy  said Enzi  member of the Senate Budget Committee         The U S  economy has added jobs as we work to make taxes reasonable         Thats no coincidence    Since Congress enacted tax relief in 2003  over 4 7 million net new jobs        have been added to the American economy    The bill would extend certain current tax provisions  including college        tuition deduction  and the state and local sales tax deductions were extended        until 2010  A new tax deduction was included in the bill for charitable        giving by taxpayers who do not itemize their taxes  The bill also extended        through 2006 tax incentives for business activities on Indian reservations         the tax credit for research and development as well as the work opportunity        tax credit     The bill also included a low income savers credit  charitable deductions        for contributions of food inventory  deductions for certain expenses of        elementary and secondary school teachers and small business expensing    Enzi highlighted the Bureau of Labor Statistics report issued today that        showed the U S  economy added 193 000 new net jobs in January and the unemployment        rate fell to 4 7 percent  its lowest level since July of 2001     The House of Representatives passed H R  4297 by a vote of 234 197 on Dec         8  2005  The two pieces of legislation will next go to a conference committee        to work out the differences between the two
