                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi pleased with Senate passage of anti discrimination bill  February 17  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   Chairman of the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions  HELP  Committee today hailed passage of a bill designed to prevent insurance companies and employers from discriminating against those they cover or employ because of a person s genetics     The Genetic Nondiscrimination Act of 2005  S  306  would establish clear guidance for health insurers and employers  as well as consumers about the use and disclosure of genetic information     Enzi said the bill  by prohibiting this type of discrimination  would encourage individuals to take advantage of genetic screening  counseling and testing     Advances in genetic science are transforming our fight against deadly inherited diseases  But fear of discrimination by health insurers and employers on the basis of predictive genetic information can deter individuals from taking advantage of these life saving genetic tests and therapies   Enzi said  This bill  by providing protection against the misuse of genetic information  will encourage the development of science that can improve the health of generations to come     Enzi said that after years of work on the bill  the Senate passed the legislation with a resounding 98 0 vote                                                       February 2005 News Releases
