                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi bill to increase amount of funds insured by feds  July 29  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo  introduced a bill in the Senate Friday calling for reform of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation  FDIC  that would better protect the retirement funds of Americans and help banks better insure deposits    The Safe and Fair Deposit Insurance Act of 2005 would change current FDIC policies to reflect inflation and the growing size of deposits by increasing the retirement fund size that the FDIC can insure from  100 000 under the current system to  250 000  The bill also calls for checking  savings and other deposit accounts to be adjusted for inflation in 2010 and every five years thereafter  Enzi has been working on this proposal for some time and is hopeful hell be able to make real progress on the legislation this session    The policies were using to insure our deposits were written 25 years ago when a movie ticket cost about  3  Now its over twice that much  It is time we reform these policies to reflect the needs and costs of today  said Enzi  a member of the Senate Banking Committee  This bill would properly insure retirement funds immediately while giving the FDIC power to adjust the size of insurable deposits to reflect rising inflation     The bill has been referred to the Senate Banking Committee  It was co sponsored by some of Enzis Banking Committee colleagues  Senators Chuck Hagel  R  Neb   Tim Johnson  D S D   and Wayne Allard  R Colo     end                                                       July 2005 News Releases
