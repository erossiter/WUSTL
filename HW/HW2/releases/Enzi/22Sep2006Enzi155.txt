                                                                                                                                                                                      Bill signed helps Wyoming young adults build housing for communities  September 22  2006 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   Chairman of the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions  HELP  Committee  recently praised the President for signing the S 3534 YouthBuild Transfer Act  This bill could help Wyoming young adults build housing for their communities while setting a firm foundation for their future  This program serves students in need of these education and training services  it enables them to serve their communities by building affordable housing  and assists them in transforming their own lives and roles in society  Enzi said  In a previous bill I sponsored that was signed into law  young adults ages 16 to 24 in the Eastern Shoshone and Northern Arapaho Indian tribes are eligible applicants for the YouthBuild program  I hope communities will seize this opportunity to open a chapter in their area for young adults   This law will transfer the YouthBuild program from the Department of Housing and Urban Development to the Department of Labor  DOL   Enzi said this is a positive because the program will be more closely aligned with and benefit from cooperation with the larger workforce system at the state and local levels  The transfer also expands the list of activities that are eligible for YouthBuild funds  The YouthBuild program was created in 1992 and is designed to provide valuable experience  education and leadership development for unemployed young adults and those who are not currently enrolled in school  Youth who are involved in this program construct and rehabilitate affordable housing in their communities to be used permanently for low income housing  while they study to earn their high school diploma or GED   Since the program was created  more than  300 million YouthBuild grants have been awarded to public and private non profit organizations  More than 8 351 youth have participated in the program and built or rehabilitated more than 4 935 affordable housing units in their communities  For more information on the YouthBuild program visit                                                       September 2006 News Releases
