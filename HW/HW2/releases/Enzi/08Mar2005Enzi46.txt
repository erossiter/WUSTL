                                                                                                                                                                                      Perkins education bill sails through Enzi committee  March 8  2005 Washington  D C   The Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions  HELP  Committee  today approved legislation that would address the needs of our changing workforce by targeting federal funds for academic instruction and job skills training  said U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   Chairman of the Committee    Enzi said the reauthorization of the Carl D  Perkins Career and Technical Education Improvement Act  will help close the gap that threatens Americas long term competitiveness    This legislation will require state agencies to work together to identify the needs of the workforce and design job and skills training programs to match those needs  More importantly  it will provide vital resources that are needed to prepare students of all ages for a lifetime of learning necessary to pursue high wage and high skilled occupations  Enzi said    S  250  introduced by Enzi on February 1  also emphasizes the needs of non traditional students and other lifelong learners  who are returning to school for the first time  or those who are seeking additional skill training     The American economy is in the midst of a skills revolution  making a quality education more important than ever  Enzi said  Some estimates suggest that 60 percent of the jobs created in the next decade will require skills that only 20 percent of workers today possess  The Perkins Act is a critical piece of a comprehensive effort to train American workers to fill the good jobs being created     S  250 improves programs authorized under the Perkins Act by requiring a more effective accountability system and stronger partnerships between schools and the business community    During Wednesdays mark up  the HELP Committee also approved three other bills that included S  544  the Patient Safety and Quality Improvement Act of 2005  a bill to permit caregivers to analyze the cause of medical errors   without fear of being sued and without compromising a patients legal rights  The committee also passed S  525  the Caring for Children Act of 2005  a bill to reauthorize the Child Care and Development Block Grant Program and assist states in improving the quality of child care available to families    The Perkins bill may be considered by the Senate as a whole as early as this week                                                      March 2005
