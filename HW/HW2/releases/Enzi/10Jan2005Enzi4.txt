                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi comments on Administration pension plan  January 10  2005 Washington  D C   Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pension Committee Chairman Mike Enzi  R Wyo   is pleased action is being proposed by Secretary of Labor Elaine Chao and the Bush Administration to reform our nation s private pension systems and to protect the retirement security of 34 million workers and retirees     We are at a crossroads with our nation s defined benefit pension systems and the only way to go is forward  We need to put in place major reforms that ensure the stability of our private  defined benefit pension plans  Enzi said  Hard working Americans rely upon their defined benefit pension plans with hopes that the plans will sustain them into retirement  Unfortunately  the defined benefit system is broken  We need to fix the problems now before the system deteriorates further and taxpayers end up footing the bill for a huge  painful bailout  Reforms are necessary to ensure that workers receive the pension benefits they are promised and to ensure the long term solvency of the Pension Benefit Guaranty Corporation  The Administration s proposal is a good starting point for addressing these concerns  I look forward to working closely with Secretary Chao and the Administration to protect this key component of retirement security     Chairman Enzi anticipates holding hearings on the Administration s proposal in the near future and working with the relevant Congressional Committees  the Administration and stakeholders to craft comprehensive pension reform legislation
