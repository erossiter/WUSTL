                                                                                                                                                                                      Banking bill signed  February 16  2006 Washington  D C   Enzi took another trip to the White House earlier in the week to witness President George W  Bush sign a bill that would protect retirement funds of Americans     Enzi  member of the Senate Banking Committee  has worked on the language of the Federal Deposit Insurance Reform Conforming Amendments Act of 2005  for the past five years and said the changes are important for the people who put their money in banks in rural areas    We were not only able to get this bill language included in the deficit reduction bill  which I watched the President sign last week  we were successful in passing the bill by itself  To make sure this happened  we passed the bill twice  putting the bill through a parallel process  Enzi said  Keeping money in local communities allows those communities to make additional economic progress     The bill changes current FDIC deposit insurance limits for the first time to reflect inflation and emphasize the need for Americans to save more for retirement  General and municipal deposit accounts will continue to be insured at the  100 000 limit  but allowed to be adjusted for inflation every five years  Retirement coverage will be immediately increased from  100 000 to  250 000 and will be adjusted for inflation the same as other accounts                                                       February
