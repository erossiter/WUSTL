                                                                                                                                                                                      Hudson firefighters to receive funding  January 31  2007 Washington  D C    The Hudson Fire Department is set to receivefundingwhich they plan to spend on newair packs for their firefighters  according to U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo    TheHudson Fire Department stationwill usethe  69 000 grant awarded to them from the Department of HomelandSecurityAssistance to Firefighters Grant Program to purchase new packs in addition to expanding other resources for protecting the health and safety of the Hudson community   Early last year  Enzi encouraged all Wyoming fire departments to take advantage of the millions of dollars in federal grants by attending local informational workshops to learn of the application process involved in obtaining the funds available to them   Local firefighters are there for a community when they need it the most  This funding is available to help make sure firefighters have the necessary equipment and training  said Enzi    To date  20 different grants totaling more than  2 million have been given to the state of Wyoming from the fiscal year 2005 funding    In 2006 alone  the Department of Homeland Security awarded approximately  485 million nationally to fire departments and EMS organizations through the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program AFGP   Grant money can be used by recipients for firefighter health and safety programs  enhancing emergency medical services  the purchase of additional personal protection equipment  or modifications to fire stations and facilities    A current list of award recipients and dates announced is posted at www usfa fema gov  A complete list of applications  financial information and state fire department data is available at     Fire departments with questions regarding the grant program can reach the fire
