Healthy funding leads to healthy forests  October 22  2007 Washington  D C    Forests in the Rocky Mountain Region experience a number of natural problems including a bark beetle epidemic  wildfire and drought  U S  Senators Mike Enzi  John Barrasso and Representative Barbara Cubin  all R Wyo   are working to ensure inadequate funding is not added to the list of hazards facing our national forests  Senators and representatives wrote a letter to Chief Gail Kimbell  head of the U S  Forest Service  USFS   recently expressing their preference that Wyoming  Colorado and South Dakota forests might receive adequate funds from the USFS 2008 budget to maintain state forest lands   We urge you to take all necessary steps to ensure that the national forests in the Rocky Mountain Region are funded to their full capabilities for hazardous fuels and forest management programs in the fiscal year 2008  the delegation wrote  At a minimum  given the regions performance and management needs  we would be very concerned by any reductions in funding or programs from FY 07 levels  The delegation noted that healthy forests impact the states tourism and timber industry as well as crucial wildlife
