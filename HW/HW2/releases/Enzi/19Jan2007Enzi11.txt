                                                                                                                                                                                      Good news for Wyoming soda ash producers  January 19  2007 Washington  D C   The U S  Export Import Bank will not be helping a locomotive company to finance the sale and refurbishment of locomotives for a soda ash facility in Kenya  according to U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo  Enzi  a senior member of the Senate Banking Committee and vocal opponent of the plan  said the Ex Im Bank reviewed the locomotive companys application last year and was considering it  Enzi met with Ex Im Bank Chairman Jim Lambright and urged him to oppose the proposal  explaining that helping Kenyan soda ash production would be done to the detriment of workers and companies with operations in Sweetwater County  Wyoming  Ex Ims mission is to assist in financing the export of U S  goods and services to international markets  but not if in so doing it hurts existing U S  markets  If these locomotives go to Kenya it will strengthen that countrys industry not just today  but for years in the future  We want to help American companies  but not if it costs Wyoming jobs  Enzi said  Thats one less thing people in the Wyoming soda ash business will have to worry about  The Ex Im Bank informed Enzi Tuesday that the locomotive company had officially withdrawn its application for financing                                                      January 2007 News Releases           Browse by
