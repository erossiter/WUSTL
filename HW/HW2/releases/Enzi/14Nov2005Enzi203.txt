                                                                                                                                                                                      Senate Passes Funding Measure for Wyoming   Senators Thomas  Enzi highlight funding for future projects  November 14  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Senators Craig Thomas and Mike Enzi  both R Wyo   announced the final passage of H R  2419  the Energy and Water Appropriations Bill for fiscal year 2006 by a vote of 84 4  Wyoming received funds for three projects  The bill passed the House last week and now heads to the President for his signature     Wyoming continues to lead the country in exploring our energy resources  The projects will continue to thrust Wyoming into the forefront of energy research  Energy exploration and research are key to our nations energy needs  Thomas said  RMOTC is a good example of a federal agency seizing an opportunity to do testing and exploration     This is a bill that is bursting with potential  said Enzi  The Wyoming delegation has been able to enhance this bill with specific projects designed to ensure Wyoming is a leader in energy development for tomorrow  The purpose of this bill is to provide appropriations for fiscal year 2006 for energy and water development and related purposes    The bill contains just over  31 2 billion in spending for fiscal year 2006    Enzi  Thomas joint requests      2 million approved for the Coal Bed Methane Research Center at the University of Wyoming      6 million approved for Cooperative Research through DOE  The Western Research Institute receives half of this funding   Thomas specific request      3 million approved for the Rocky Mountain Oilfield Testing Center  RMOTC
