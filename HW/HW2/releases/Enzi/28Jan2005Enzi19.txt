                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi calls mouse de listing a win for Wyoming  January 28  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   was pleased to hear the U S  Fish and Wildlife Service announce today its intention to send a rule to the Federal Register that will begin the delisting process for the Preble s meadow jumping mouse     The agency is basing its rule on genetic evidence that the Preble s meadow jumping mouse is likely the same subspecies as the Bear Lodge jumping mouse  which is not endangered     Farmers  ranchers  and other property owners in southeast Wyoming have had to live in fear of a mouse  They have had to worry about being held hostage by faulty science and the Endangered Species Act  Today s announcement has enabled people to breathe easier  said Enzi     In 1998 the Preble s meadow jumping mouse was listed as a threatened species under the Endangered Species Act  In 2001 farmers and ranchers were able to continue to operate without being prosecuted for the accidental killing of the mouse  In 2003 areas in Albany  Converse  Laramie and Platte counties were designated as critical habitat for the mouse  In 2004 the U S  Fish and Wildlife Service began the review process to remove the mouse from federal protection  Today s announcement is part of that process                                                       January 2005 News Releases           Browse by
