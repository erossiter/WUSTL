                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi votes to bring terrorists to justice  September 28  2006 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   is pleased to announce the Senate passage of legislation today that provides a framework to detain  question and prosecute terrorists  By creating a new court designed specifically to bring terrorists to justice and also set clear guidelines for interrogators seeking information from detainees  Enzi said American interests at home and overseas will be better protected   This bill acknowledges that we are fighting a war like none before  Terrorist organizations plot against us and do not adhere to the rule of law and must be treated differently than common criminals  said Enzi  This bill allows our military  intelligence and law enforcement communities to question  file charges against and then try terrorists and keep us safe while still treating them humanely   The Senate passed the Military Commission bill  S  3930  by a vote of 65 34 today  The House passed the bill by a vote of 253 168 on Sept  27  The President is expected to sign the legislation   Since World War II military and civilian tribunals have been set up to prosecute foreign country soldiers and civilians captured in a time of war  Nothing is formally set up for terrorist war criminals captured while engaging in hostilities against the U S  The bill would create five  or 12  member military commissions overseen by a military judge  This new system will allow terrorists to be brought to justice before the end of the conflict  said Enzi  Terrorists want to hurt us  Their lives are based on destroying ours  The only way they will stop is if we stop them first  This bill helps us do that  said Enzi   Under the bill  terrorists will be presumed innocent until proven guilty  have the right to military and civilian counsel  present evidence  request to exclude evidence and the right to appeal  Provisions in the bill also clarify U S  obligations under the Geneva Convention relative to the treatment of prisoners of war   The mastermind behind the Sept  11 attacks  Khalid Shaikh Mohammad  is currently being held at Guantanamo Bay  Enzi said that information obtained through interrogation of other detainees thwarted several plots Khalid Shaikh Mohammad was planning against the U S  after Sept  11                                                       September 2006 News Releases           Browse by
