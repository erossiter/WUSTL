                                                                                                                                                                                      HELP Committee Chairman Enzi issues invitation for Terri Schiavo to appear at Senate hearing  March 18  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R WY   Chairman of the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee  HELP Committee  sent a letter  dated March 17  2005  to Terri Schiavo and her husband requesting their presence at a hearing before his Committee on Monday  March 28  2005     The purpose of the hearing  Health Care Provided to Non ambulatory Persons  is to review health care policies and practices relevant to the care of non ambulatory persons such as Mrs  Schiavo  Enzi said  Friday     Although I understand this is an emotional and difficult situation for the Schiavo family  I believe we must keep in mind that a human life is at stake and immediate action is required to ensure that a decision in this matter is not reached in haste  Enzi said  Terri Schiavos very survival has become a national discussion that has focused attention on the urgent need for Congress to examine current health care practices used in the care of non ambulatory individuals  We must not allow her spirit to be extinguished     Non ambulatory individuals  including those with disabilities  require additional supports and services beyond what is provided to ambulatory individuals  These include support in non medical  non institutional settings and technologies to assist the patient and caregivers in providing appropriate care as well as other essential services such as transportation and housing     Federal criminal law protects witnesses called before official Congressional committee proceedings from anyone who may obstruct or impede a witness attendance or testimony  More specifically  the law protects a witness from anyone who    by threats  force  or by any threatening letter or communication   influences  obstructs  or impedes an inquiry or investigation by Congress                                                      March 2005 News Releases
