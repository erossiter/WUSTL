                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi  Disaster assistance can and should be planned for Supports Farm Bill disaster assistance program November 6  2007 Washington  D C    U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   using his accounting background for reference  added his support to a measure in the 2007 Farm Bill that would allow the federal government to plan ahead for weather related disasters to help producers  consumers and taxpayers   Enzi  along with 19 Senate colleagues  circulated a letter to fellow senators at the start of debate for the Farm Bill  urging support of the Supplemental Disaster Assistance Program  The program is in the Farm Bill language that passed the Senate Finance Committee and the authors of the letter are working to ensure the language remains in the legislation  While weather is uncertain  disaster assistance for our farmers should be more predictable  the senators wrote in a Nov  5 letter  By creating a dedicated source of funding  it serves to protect the budgetary resources for other agricultural priorities that have been raided in the past to provide offsets for disaster spending  Enzi likened the program to a family starting a rainy day savings account to ensure protection against future misfortunes  As any accountant will tell you  it is important to have a rainy day fund  Right now it is unacceptable and irresponsible that the federal government does not have a rainy day account for agriculture disasters or any other disasters for that matter  Almost every year some sort of disaster strikes our agriculture producers whether it be drought  hurricanes or fires  said Enzi  This program would provide fiscal responsibility and predictable assistance to agriculture producers while easing the minds of taxpayers  It should remain in the Farm Bill    Related Resources     62 0 KBs
