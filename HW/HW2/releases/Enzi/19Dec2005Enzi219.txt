                                                                                                                                                                                      House   Senate pass stem cell cord blood bill   New hope for treatment of devastating illness  Enzi says  December 19  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo  Chairman of the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions  HELP  Committee  said the House of Representatives approved H R  2520  the Stem Cell Therapeutic and Research Act of 2005  Saturday  The bill will expand access to life saving therapies for many Americans facing devastating illnesses  The House action followed the Senates unanimous approval of the measure Friday night    The goal of this bill is to ensure that individuals in need of transplants receive appropriately matched transplant materials  Enzi said  Cord blood and bone marrow are non controversial sources of stem cells  and they have proven track records as therapies    The initial costs of creating a network of cord blood units with an inventory appropriate for the entire nation are high  Enzi added  For that reason  and because of the important public health need that is currently unmet  it is appropriate for a government program to help start up this network     Stem cells in cord blood are able to replace many diseased tissues with healthy ones  They also are proven and effective therapies for diseases such as leukemia  sickle cell  Fanconis anemia  Krabbes disease and severe combined immunodeficiency  The bill  which passed the Senate late Friday  now awaits the Presidents signature    Enzi praised the steadfast efforts of Senator Orrin Hatch  R Utah  who serves on the HELP Committee  as key to developing the Senate legislation and helping ensure its passage on Friday  He also credited the support of Senators Richard Burr  R N C   John Ensign  R Nev   Chris Dodd  D Conn   and Jack Reed  D R I   whose efforts were also instrumental to ensuring Senate passage of the bill    H R  2520 will allow qualified cord blood stem cell banks to assist in establishing and maintaining a National Cord Blood Stem Cell Bank Network  which will     acquire tissue type  test  cryopreserve  and store donated units of human cord blood acquired with the informed consent of the donor      make cord blood units available to transplant centers for stem cell transplantations     make cord blood units that are not appropriate for clinical use available for peer reviewed research    The bill authorizes up to  34 million of support in Fiscal Year 2006  with another  53 million in each of the following years  up to FY 2010  The funding will not only cover the establishment of the national cord blood bank network but also continue the bone marrow registry program                                                       December 2005 News Releases           Browse by
