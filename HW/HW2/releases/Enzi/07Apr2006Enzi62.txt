                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi leads Congressional delegation trip to India  Italy  Kuwait and Sri Lanka American competitiveness is focus April 7  2006    Washington D C    U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   Chairman of the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions  HELP  Committee is leading a group of senators and officials from the Administration to India  Europe and the Middle East next week    The congressional delegation s primary focus will be learning more about foreign educational systems and the implications to U S  competitiveness in the emerging world market    I read Thomas Friedman s book   The World is Flat   very carefully  He wrote that our society has been one of dreams more than memories  but there is a danger of this changing and once memories exceed dreams then we are in trouble  I want America to remain the place where dreams come true  but we won t be able to do that in a vacuum  We need to learn all we can about the successes of our world neighbors and how we can benefit  Enzi said    Enzi  Education and Early Childhood Development Subcommittee Chairman Lamar Alexander  R Tenn   Employment and Workplace Safety Subcommittee Chairman Johnny Isakson  R Ga   and Secretary of Education Margaret Spellings comprise the group     Biopreparedness  disaster relief  landmines and foreign relations are other issues that the group will study  The group will meet with political leaders  students  diplomats  businesspeople  American officials and troops serving our country abroad  scientists  humanitarian workers and others while visiting India  Italy  Kuwait and Sri Lanka from April
