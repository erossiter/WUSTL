                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi encourages airports to apply for funds  January 28  2005 Washington  D C   U S  Sen  Mike Enzi  R Wyo   and the U S  Department of Transportation  DOT  are inviting communities to take advantage of grants designed to support small towns and cities working to improve their airline service     Under a program developed by the Small Community Air Service Development Program  approximately  20 million is available for up to 40 grants to help communities address their local air service problems  such as high fares and insufficient levels of service by using federal funds to support communities working to attract or improve air service     With the help of this generous grant  Wyoming air service would be able to compete with outside markets and help strengthen the state s economic stability  said Enzi     Enzi pointed out that past recipients have benefitted from the grants and have seen passenger numbers rise and an over all improvement within their systems  This is the fourth year the DOT has awarded grants under this program     The DOT will give priority to proposals from communities that have high airfares compared to other communities  contribute financially to the project from sources other than airport revenues  and have established or will establish public private partnerships to improve their air service  Proposals will also be judged on the likelihood that a broad segment of the public with limited access to the national transportation system will be helped and will use the assistance in a timely fashion     Applications for grants are due April 22  2005
