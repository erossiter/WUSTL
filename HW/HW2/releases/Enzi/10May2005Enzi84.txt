                                                                                                                                                                                      Wyoming seasonal small businesses get boost  May 10  2005 Washington  D C   Congress sent a bill to the President today that would aid Wyoming businesses in finding the help they need during busy tourist seasons  according to U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo     This bill is becoming law just in time for the summer tourist season and it will give a boost to many Wyoming businesses in need of workers  especially in Jackson and Cody  said Enzi  Parts of this bill are about businesses being able to operate at full capacity when no local workers fill the jobs     The Senate passed the supplemental appropriations bill conference report accompanying H R 1268 by a vote of 100 0 which included an amendment that makes changes to the H2B Visa program  Enzi voted in favor of the bill  The changes will allow small businesses to hire workers from outside the country for a limited length of time once the businesses have proven that no local workers are available to fill the job  The employer is held responsible for terminating the employment at the end of a specified time and the employee is required to return to their home country upon completion of the job     Many Wyoming businesses  especially in the service sector  have been in emergency situations for the last two summers with a lack of workers  This bill allows employers to contract outside help without giving those workers residency status  Enzi said     Enzi co sponsored an amendment in the original supplemental appropriations bill to change the H2B Visa program  The House passed the conference report on May 5 by a vote of 368 58
