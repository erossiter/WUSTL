                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi promotes preservation  co sponsors conservation credit  May 14  2007 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   is co sponsoring legislation that would help protect more wildlife habitat and open spaces in Wyoming while also helping tax payers who donate their land   Conservation easements have helped slow down urban sprawl and are an effective tool to permanently conserve land in the United States  said Enzi  Landowners who choose to use their land for conservation easements are helping the country  This tax credit should continue to be an option  If the land is protected the wildlife is protected and we can grow old knowing our children will live to see the land as we see it today   Enzi is co sponsoring S  469  which would continue conservation easement tax credits and boost the ability for landowners to utilize tax benefits while protecting wildlife habitat and open spaces  The bill would amend the Internal Revenue Code to make permanent the tax deduction for charitable contributions by individuals and corporations of real property interests for conservation purposes   The bill has been referred to the Senate Finance committee  Enzi has been a strong proponent of conservation easements and tax credits and was integral in making these tax credits available through Dec  31  2007  The credit extension was included in pension reform legislation he helped author  That legislation was signed into law by President Bush last year  This bill  S  469  would make those tax credits permanent   A conservation easement is a voluntary  legally binding agreement that limits certain types of uses or prevents development from taking place on a piece of property now and in the future  while protecting the propertys ecological or open space values  The bill is a bipartisan effort sponsored by Senators Max Baucus  D Mont   and Charles Grassley  R Iowa  along with nine other senators                                                        May 2007 News Releases
