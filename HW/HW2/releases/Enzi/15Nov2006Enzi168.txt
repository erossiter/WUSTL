                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi votes for military construction  veterans bill  November 15  2006 Washington  D C   U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   is pleased a bill approved by the Senate provides significant funding for the Wyoming Army and Air National Guard  veterans and military construction  Our military men and women deserve to have the best training and base facilities and this bill delivers the proper funding  Enzi said  This bill is a commitment to our troops and shows our respect and appreciation to our nations veterans  Following on the heels of Veterans Day I find it quite appropriate to pass this legislation and move forward as quickly as possible with the final negotiations in the conference report   Wyoming specific projects authorized in the Senate version of H R  5385  include     Air National Guard   Cheyenne  Replace Squadron Operations Facility   7 6 million   Air National Guard   Cheyenne  addition to the Fire Crash Rescue Station   4 2 million   Army National Guard   Camp Guernsey  Multi Purpose Machine Gun Range   1 796 million   Air Force   F E  Warren Air Force Base  Renovation of dormitory 320   11 million The Senate passed the fiscal year 2007 Military Construction Appropriations bill Tuesday evening by a voice vote  The bill provides about  16 billion for active and reserve military construction components  about  78 billion for the Department of Veterans Affairs and about  29 billion for the Veterans Medical Service   The House passed its version of the legislation by a vote of 395 0 on May 19  2006 and it is now headed to a conference committee where members of the House and Senate will work out the differences between the two versions  The Military Construction and Veterans Affairs appropriation bill provides funding for the planning  design  construction  alteration  and improvement of military facilities worldwide  both for Active and Reserve forces  It provides resources to the Department of Veterans Affairs for veterans benefits and care and other related
