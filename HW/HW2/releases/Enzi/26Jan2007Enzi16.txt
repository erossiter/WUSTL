                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi helps secure community funding  January 26  2007 Washington  D C    U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo   is pleased to announce that Fremont County residents will have more resources to plan  implement or expand projects that will benefit the educational  health and social service needs of the community  In a letter to Assistant Secretary of Education Michael Cohen  Enzi requested the Department of Education s support in awarding a 21st Century Community Learning Centers grant to Fremont County  A 21st Century grant would be tremendously beneficial in expanding and extending school based learning opportunities and increasing collaboration among schools and community based organizations  said Enzi  Fremont County was chosen to receive a 21st Century grant totaling  5 6 million over three years  A first year award of about  1 89 million was awarded to the Wind River consortium of schools through the Fremont County Board of Cooperative Educations Services  BOCES    BOCES will operate 12 centers to provide about 3 260 kindergarten through twelfth graders and about 2 350 community members with before school  after school  weekend and summer services   The 21st Century Community Learning Centers program awards grants to rural and inner city public schools  The program targets funds to high need rural and urban communities that lack the resources to establish after school centers and which have low rates of student achievement and high rates of juvenile crime  school violence and drug abuse  The program is directed toward increasing academic success and parental and
