                                                                                                                                                                                      Enzi praises passage of gun liability bill headed to President  October 20  2005 Washington  D C   The House finished up its work today on a Senate bill that would block politically motivated lawsuits against gun manufacturers and sellers  The bill would also prevent frivolous lawsuits that threaten Second Amendment freedoms  according to U S  Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyo     The House passed S  397 by a vote of 283 144 today  Enzi co sponsored the bill which the Senate passed by a vote of 65 31 in July  The bill is now on its way to the Presidents desk and he is expected to sign the bill into law     Regulation by litigation has to stop  It threatens the very health of an industry that sells a legal product  said Enzi  Gun violence is a problem with much deeper roots than the availability of firearms  We need to address the problem of crime at its basic level and not try to sue crime out of existence by targeting businesses in our country    Over the past few years dozens of cities have brought lawsuits against gun manufacturers and others seeking damages for the unlawful use of their products  Enzi likened the lawsuits to suing the maker of a baseball bat if it was used in an attack  The legislation does not grant blanket immunity to manufacturers  It does allow legitimate victims their day in court for cases involving defective firearms     The bill would also keep military gun contracts in the hands of American firms to help protect national security      end                                                       October 2005 News Releases           Browse by
