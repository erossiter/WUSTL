SEN. DEWINE ANNOUNCES FEDERAL FUNDING FOR SOUTHWEST OHIO PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, announced the Committee passage Thursday of the fiscal year 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations bill that includes funding for Southwest Ohio projects. 
   "As a member of the Appropriations Committee, I am proud to have helped secure funding for these worthy projects. I look forward to working with my colleagues to maintain funding in the final version of the bill," said Senator DeWine. "Many of these projects will help the Department of Energy cleanup former work sites in Ohio, as well as screen former workers for potential health problems."
   Senator DeWine secured funding for the following projects:
   Cincinnati Park Board
   * Cincinnati Central Riverfront Park - $1 million to construct a park that will link the Central Riverfront attractions to Downtown Cincinnati and other riverfront parks.
   Middletown Regional Health System
   * Imaging Systems - $510,000 for Positron Emission Tomography (PET) and Computerized Tomography (CT) imaging systems that allow physicians to better recommend cancer treatments.
   Miami Conservancy District
   * Holes Creek Flood Protection - $1.355 million for the construction of a floodwall to protect commercial and industrial properties, as well as for the purchase of three other properties in the flood plain.
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following projects:
   Department of Energy Site Closures
   * Fernald Closure Project - $268.789 million for the final stages of plant closure that includes final cleanup and legacy management.
   * Miamisburg Mound Closure Project - $46.069 million for the final stages of plant closure that includes final cleanup and legacy management.
   Medical Screening Programs
   * Former Department of Energy Worker Health Screenings - $14 million for health screening programs nationwide for former Department of Energy workers found to be at significant risk for health problems due to workplace exposures. This includes funding for the Portsmouth Gaseous Diffusion Plant Screening Program, Fernald Atomic Council Medical Screening Program, and Mound Workers Medical Screening Program.
   This bill now goes to the Senate floor for consideration.
