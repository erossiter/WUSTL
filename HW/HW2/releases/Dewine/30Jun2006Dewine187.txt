SEN. DEWINE ANNOUNCES FEDERAL FUNDING FOR NORTHEAST OHIO PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, announced the Committee passage of the fiscal year 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations bill that includes $2 million to help Northeast Ohio. 
   "As a member of the Appropriations Committee, I am proud to have helped secure funding for these worthy projects. I look forward to working with my colleagues to maintain funding in the final version of the bill," said Senator DeWine. "Many of these projects will not only help supply communities with healthy water but will fund energy projects that may help Ohio and its workers become leaders in alternative energy development."
   Senator DeWine secured funding for the following projects:
   Case Western Reserve University/The Cleveland Clinic/University Hospitals of Cleveland
   * National Center for Regenerative Medicine - $500,000 to acquire state-of-the-art imaging equipment for a project to research regeneration of tissue and organs in animals for future human application.
   Rolls-Royce Fuel Cell Systems
   *Emissions Reduction Technologies - $500,000 to modify existing fuel processing technologies to reduce emissions from gas turbines, microturbines and internal-combustion engines.
   Cuyahoga Community College
   *Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy Technologies Workforce Training Program - $500,000 to educate and train students on energy efficiency and renewable energy applications of hydrogen and fuel cell technologies.
   U.S. Army Corps of Engineers
   * Mahoning River Environmental Dredging Project - $500,000 to clean up more than 31 miles of the Mahoning River that has been contaminated by old steel factories.
   This bill now goes to the Senate floor for consideration.
