SEN. DEWINE SECURES FEDERAL FUNDING FOR SOUTHERN OHIO PROJECT
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH) today announced federal funding for Ohio economic development projects included in the fiscal year 2006 Transportation, Treasury, and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill.  
 The Senate Appropriations Committee passed the bill and it now goes to the full Senate for consideration. 
   "These worthy projects would improve economic and cultural opportunities in southern Ohio. Not only do these projects include an opportunity to improve the facilities of a favorite summer attraction that tells the important story of Ohio heritage," said Senator DeWine, a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee. "I look forward to working with my Senate colleagues to ensure funding for this project is included in the final version of the bill." 
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following regional project: 
   The Scioto Society, Incorporated 
   "Tecumseh!" Capital Improvement Project - $250,000 for improvement projects at the Sugarloaf Mountain Amphitheatre, home to the outdoor drama "Tecumseh!". This project includes updating the lighting system, repaving driveways and parking lots, and refurbishing housing for employees.
