SEN. DEWINE ANNOUNCES FEDERAL MONEY FOR MIAMI VALLEY ENERGY, WATER PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced the Senate Appropriations Committee Passage of the fiscal year 2006 Energy and Water bill, which includes funding for regional projects.  
   "Ensuring this site is safe for Ohio families and that former workers are receiving services they need are some of my priorities on the Senate Appropriations Committee," said Senator DeWine. "I look forward to working with my colleagues to ensure funding for these projects is included in the final bill." 
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following project: 
   Miamisburg Environmental Management Project 
   * Miamisburg Mound Cleanup - $75.5 million to complete the cleanup of the Maimisburg Mound for future commercial activities. 
   Senator DeWine secured money for the following national project: 
   Former Worker Medical Screening 
   * Former Worker Medical Screening Program - $16.5 million for the national program that includes $1 million for former workers employed after 1985 at the Miamisburg Mound site. This screening program is a proven program that saves lives. 
   The bill now goes before the full Senate for consideration.
