SEN. DEWINE ANNOUNCES FEDERAL MONEY FOR NORTHEAST OHIO AGRICULTURE PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced the Senate Appropriations Committee Passage of the fiscal year 2006 Agriculture Appropriations bill, which includes funding for regional projects.  
   "These worthy projects will help move Ohio's rich agricultural heritage into the 21st century while still protecting natural resources in the area," said Senator DeWine. "As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee I look forward to working with my colleagues to ensure funding for these projects is included in the final bill." 
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following projects: 
   The Ohio State University 
   * Ohio Aquaculture Research and Development Initiative - $800,000 for The College of Food, Agricultural and Environmental Sciences to improve broodstock, production techniques and recalculating aquaculture systems for fish using long and short-term strategies aimed to increase the productivity, innovation, and profitability of Ohio and Midwest aquaculture operations. Sites in Franklin, Pike, Wayne, and Wood counties. 
   * Emergency Detection and Response Research Program - $750,000 for The College of Food, Agricultural and Environmental Sciences to provide pragmatic support for the operation and maintenance of the proposed BSL-3 Ag facility to be built on the campus of the Ohio Agicultural Research and Development Center. This will support faculty and staff on both the Columbus and Wooster campuses. 
   The bill now goes before the full Senate for consideration.
