SEN. DEWINE ANNOUNCES FEDERAL MONEY FOR NORTHEAST OHIO ENERGY, WATER PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced Northeast Ohio projects included in the fiscal year 2006 Energy and Water Appropriations Conference Report, which should receive final passage from both the House and Senate this week.  
   "These projects will create new jobs while advancing medical and energy technology in Ohio," said Senator DeWine. "Patients and consumers in Ohio and across the country will be affected by the results of these projects, putting the state and Ohio's workers on the cutting edge of 21st century technological advancements." 
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following projects: 
   Case Western Reserve University/Cleveland Clinic Foundation/University Hospitals 
   - National Center for Regenerative Medicine - $1.5 million for equipment for the support laboratories and support facilities of the Center's clinical research units. The Center studies and utilizes non-embryonic adult and cord blood stem cells to regenerate new tissue within diseased organs. 
   Rolls-Royce Energy Systems 
   - Fuel Cell Systems - $3 million for Ohio based research and development of fuel processors for megawatt-scale solid oxide fuel cell systems for stationary power generation. This project will also help with the commercialization of cost effective fuel cell systems for Ohioans. 
   After passing the House and the Senate, the bill will go to the president for his signature.
