SEN. DEWINE SECURES FEDERAL FUNDING FOR OHIO TRANSPORTATION PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH) today announced the Senate Appropriations Committee allocated funding for a Southwest Ohio transit projects when it passed the fiscal year 2006 Transportation, Treasury, and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill.  
 The bill now goes to the Senate floor for consideration. 
   "Ensuring that Ohio's roads, railways, and airports are safe and efficient for families and visitors is essential to the overall quality of life in our state," said Senator DeWine, a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee. "Maintaining and updating Ohio's transportation infrastructure could not only save lives by making our communities safer, but make Ohio more attractive to businesses that rely on efficient transportation systems." 
   Senator DeWine secured funding for the following Southwest Ohio projects: 
   Cincinnati Zoo and Botanical Garden 
   * Uptown Crossings Joint Development Transit Project - $2 million for a park and ride facility to allow the 60,000 people working within the one-mile radius of Uptown Crossings to leave their cars and use transit busses and shuttles. The Uptown Crossings Project will encourage business growth, promote tourism, and provide for the needs of area residents. 
   Senator DeWine and Senator George Voinovich (R-OH) secured funding for the following statewide project: 
   Ohio Public Transit Association 
   * Bus Program - $12.58 million for the replacement of buses and other vehicles across the state to maintain existing levels of transit service and to provide services to people with disabilities.
