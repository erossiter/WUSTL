SEN. DEWINE SECURED FEDERAL FUNDING FOR MIAMI VALLEY PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced that funding for Miami Valley projects he secured was included in the fiscal year 2006 Transportation, Treasury, and Housing and Urban Development and Judiciary Appropriations Conference Report.  
   "The variety of programs funded in this bill will help troubled youth and improve the cultural opportunities in the Miami Valley," said Senator DeWine. "While the Daybreak Opportunity House will give hope to adolescents and teens; the Gateway to the Paul Laurence Dunbar Memorial and the "Tecumseh!' Capital Improvement Project will allow more Ohioans to enjoy the area's rich heritage." 
   Senator DeWine secured funding for the following regional projects: 
   Wright Dunbar, Inc. 
   * Gateway to Paul Laurence Dunbar Memorial - $200,000 for the construction of a plaza and memorial statue at the entrance to the Paul Laurence Dunbar Historic District. The project will complete the restoration of the streetscape and encourage heritage tourism in the area. 
   Daybreak, Inc. 
   * Daybreak Opportunity House - $200,000 to aid in the construction of a facility where troubled young people can receive the services and support they need for long-term success. Some of the programs Daybreak plans to operate in the facility are an emergency shelter, transitional housing, follow-up care, and educational and counseling services. 
   Senator DeWine secured funding for the following statewide project: 
   Ohio Public Transit Association 
   * Bus Program - $5.6 million for the replacement of buses and other vehicles across the state to maintain existing levels of transit service and to provide services to people with disabilities.
