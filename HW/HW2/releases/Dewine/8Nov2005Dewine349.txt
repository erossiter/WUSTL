SEN. DEWINE ANNOUNCES FEDERAL MONEY FOR CENTRAL OHIO ENERGY, WATER PROJECT
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced a Central Ohio project included in the fiscal year 2006 Energy and Water Appropriations Conference Report, which should receive final passage from both the House and Senate this week.  
   "This project makes Ohio's workers and Ohio companies more competitive in the 21st century," said Senator DeWine. "This project will enhance the state's technological capabilities and provide new high-tech, high-paying jobs." 
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following project: 
   Emerson Network Power 
   * Power Communications and Control Software and Hardware - $2 million for the software and hardware for research, development, and training as part of a three step plan to improve the nation's power grid. The proposed work would provide cutting-edge and high-paying jobs in Ohio benefiting Ohio's utilities and installed manufacturing base. 
   After passing the House and the Senate, the bill goes to the president for his signature.
