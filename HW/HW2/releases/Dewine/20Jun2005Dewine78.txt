SEN. DEWINE ANNOUNCES FEDERAL MONEY FOR CENTRAL OHIO ENERGY, WATER PROJECT
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced the Senate Appropriations Committee Passage of the fiscal year 2006 Energy and Water bill, which includes funding for a regional project.  
   "This worthy project will not only create much needed jobs in Ohio, but advance technological capabilities in the state, making Ohio more competitive in the 21st century," said Senator DeWine. "As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee I look forward to working with my colleagues to ensure funding for this project is included in the final bill." 
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following project: 
   Emerson Network Power 
   * Power Communications and Control Software and Hardware - $2.5 million for the software and hardware for research, development, and training as part of a three step plan to improve the nation's power grid. The proposed work would provide cutting-edge and high-paying jobs in Ohio benefiting Ohio's utilities and installed manufacturing base. 
   The bill now goes before the full Senate for consideration.
