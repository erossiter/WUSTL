SEN. DEWINE SECURES FUNDING FOR DEFENSE PROJECTS IN DAYTON REGION
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced that the Committee-passed fiscal year 2007 Defense Appropriations bill contains $35.5 million in funding for Dayton-area projects.
   "I am proud to help secure funding for these very worthy projects," said Senator DeWine. "As a member of the Appropriations Committee, I want to make sure the defense community in Ohio has proper funding to do their job to protect us at home and abroad. Given all the sacrifices that our service men and women make in defense of our freedoms, the least we can do is provide them with the proper tools. I look forward to working with my Senate colleagues to ensure funding is retained in the final version of the bill." 
   Senator DeWine secured funding for the following area projects:
   Wright State University
   * Advanced Sensor-Based Vigilance Technologies - $2 million for the establishment of a joint vigilance research facility and nucleus of expertise to anticipate and counter insurgent and terrorist threats in the Global War on Terror.
   Defense Research Associates, Inc.
   * Affordable Visible Missile Warning System - $3.5 million for the completion of the development of an affordable visible missile warning system and to demonstrate that system against live-fire missiles.
   Radiance Technology
   * Enhanced Space and Missiles Predictive Battlespace Awareness (PBA) - $3 million for the support of an integrated PBA analysis program and to improve the national capability to provide strategic warning of foreign ballistic missile developments.
   Ball Aerospace & Technologies Corporation
   * Phase 2 MASINT Distributed Exploitation Element for the Air Force - $6 million to develop the capability to meet documented warfighter needs for improved reporting of tactical Measurement and Signature Intelligence (MASINT) information into the Air Force's Distributed Common Ground System (DCGS).
   University of Dayton Research Institute
   * Multifunctional Nanostructured Materials for Future Combat Systems - $4 million to fund research to replace traditional metallic systems with lightweight, corrosion resistant materials at lower costs.
   Wright Brothers Institute
   * Capabilities Analysis Part II - $3 million to extend and expand a program created in FY05 that provides analysis of national defense capabilities for federal, state and local agencies.
   Wright Brothers Institute/University of Dayton Research Institute/ SAIC
   * Characterization of Airborne Environments for Tactical Lasers - $4 million to fund the development of technologies that will allow high-energy lasers to operate effectively on tactical fighter jets.
   Alien Technology Corporation
   * RFID Rapid Adoption Collaboration Initiative, Phase II - $9 million to speed the collaboration of government agencies, contractors and suppliers as they develop Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) system for the government. RFID is an automatic identification method that relies on radio waves.
   Sensor Technology Systems
   * Operational Enhancements for Night Vision Goggles - $1 million to enhance the capabilities of the AN-PVS 21 Low Profile Night Vision Goggles that include improved power supply and improved communications systems.
   Senator DeWine secured funding for the following national program:
   Navy Family Lines
   * COMPASS - $300,000 for a team mentoring program designed to educate new Navy spouses, enabling them to understand the military lifestyle and realistically meet the challenges that lie ahead.
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following national programs:
   Defense Health Program
   * Breast Cancer Research Program - $150 million for the breast cancer research program administered by the Army. This program responds quickly to cutting-edge scientific advances and is able to focus on research that is traditionally under funded.
   * Ovarian Cancer Research Program - $15 million for the ovarian cancer research program. This program responds quickly to cutting-edge scientific advances and is able to focus on research that is traditionally under funded.
   * Prostate Cancer Research Program - $80 million for the prostate cancer research program. This program responds quickly to cutting-edge scientific advances and is able to focus on research that is traditionally under funded.
   This bill now goes to the Senate floor for consideration.
