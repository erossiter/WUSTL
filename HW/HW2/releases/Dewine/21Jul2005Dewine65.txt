SEN. DEWINE SECURES FEDERAL FUNDING FOR SOUTHWEST OHIO PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH) today announced federal funding for Ohio economic development projects included in the fiscal year 2006 Transportation, Treasury, and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill.  
 The Senate Appropriations Committee passed the bill and it now goes to the full Senate for consideration. 
   "These worthy projects will encourage business development and consolidation Dayton, as well as support Ohio's cultural heritage," said Senator DeWine, a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee. "I look forward to working with my Senate colleagues to ensure funding for this project is included in the final version of the bill." 
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following regional projects: 
   Wright Dunbar, Inc. 
   * Gateway to Paul Laurence Dunbar Memorial - $200,000 for the construction of a plaza and memorial statue at the entrance to the Paul Laurence Dunbar Historic District. The project will complete the restoration of the streetscape and encourage heritage tourism in the area. 
   Development Projects, Inc. 
   * Building Construction for Job Expansion - $500,000 for the consolidation of NCR's operations at its world headquarters in Dayton. The project is expected to generate 300 new jobs in the short term, with the possibility of adding additional jobs in the future. This consolidation includes the construction of a new 30,000 - 40,000 square foot building with an expected completion date at the end of 2007.
