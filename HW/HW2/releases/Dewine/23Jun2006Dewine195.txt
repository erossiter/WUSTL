SEN. DEWINE SECURES FUNDING FOR NORTHWEST OHIO, STATEWIDE AGRICULTURE PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, announced the Committee passage of the fiscal year 2007 Agriculture Appropriations bill that includes $7.122 million for projects to help Northwest Ohio. 
   "As a member of the Appropriations Committee, I am proud to have helped these worthy projects receive funding," said Senator DeWine. "With the knowledge gained from agricultural research and policy initiatives we can help Ohio's agricultural community remain strong."
   Senator DeWine secured funding for the following regional projects:
   University of Toledo
   * Greenhouse Nurseries - $719,000 for the continuation of a University of Toledo research project to support the economic competitiveness of Ohio's greenhouse industry by reforming marketing and reducing costs.
   * Greenhouse and Hydroponics - $1.5 million for the continuation of a cooperative agreement between United States Agriculture Research Service and the University of Toledo on ways to optimize environmental conditions and enhance production and quality while reducing pests and disease problems in the greenhouse industry.
   * Phytoestrogen Research - $1.5 million for a collaboration between University of Toledo,Tulane University, and Xavier University to assess the health benefits of soy and ways to identify the most desirable components of the plant that may be formulated as a supplement.
   * Phytoremediation Plant Research - $771,000 for the development of approaches to remediation and correct contamination problems in Lake Eire.
   * Dietary Intervention - $1.237 million for the University of Toledo, The Ohio State University, and the Medical University of Ohio to research the health benefits of specific foods.
   * Agricultural Sewage Sludge Monitoring - $1.274 million for the assessment of health and environmental impacts of sewage sludge on agricultural fields in Northwest Ohio.
   Natural Resources Conservation Service and Ducks
   * Lake Erie Wetlands Conservation Corridors Project - $124,000 for accelerating wetland conservation along Ohio's Lake Erie Coastal zone. The goal of the program is to target conservation activities so they are linked at the landscape level to ensure high quality habitats and safe migration corridors.
   Senator DeWine secured funding for the following statewide project:
   The Ohio State University
   * The Ohio Center for Farmland Policy Innovation - $396,000 or The Ohio State University to develop a land policy laboratory that will work with local governments and area farmers to help preserve farmland.
   "This center would help Ohio's farmers work with local governments to develop farmland policy that meets the specific needs of the area," added Senator DeWine.
   The bill now goes to the full Senate for consideration.
