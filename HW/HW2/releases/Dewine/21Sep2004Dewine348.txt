SENS. DEWINE, VOINOVICH ANNOUNCE FEDERAL FUNDING FOR SOUTHEAST OHIO PROJECTS
  Sens. Mike DeWine (R-OH) and George Voinovich (R-OH) today announced the Senate Appropriations Committee passage of the fiscal year 2005 Veterans Affairs and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill, which includes $14.8 million for Ohio projects overall, and $775,000 in funding for Southeast Ohio projects.  
   "As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, it is one of my priorities to secure funding for worthy projects," said Senator DeWine. "These projects are important for development in Ohio and I look forward to working with my colleagues to make sure funding is retained in the final bill." 
   "These funds will help boost Ohio's recovering economy, and I look forward to working with my colleagues in the Senate to ensure that the funding is maintained in the final version of the bill," said Senator Voinovich. 
   The Veterans Affairs and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations Bill includes funding for Southeast Ohio projects in the following areas: 
   Village of Racine 
   * $500,000 to the Village of Racine in Meigs County for water treatment plant improvements. 
   Village of Corning 
   * $100,000 to the Village of Corning in Perry County for the Wastewater System Improvement Project. 
   Washington, Morgan and Noble Counties 
   * $175,000 to the Tri-County Rural Water and Sewer District in Washington, Morgan, and Noble Counties for the Tri-County/Noble County Water Interconnect Project.
