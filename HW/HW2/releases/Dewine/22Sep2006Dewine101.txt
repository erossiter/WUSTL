SEN. DEWINE ANNOUNCES $2.5 MILLION FOR CONSTRUCTION OF DAYTON CREATIVE TECHNOLOGY CENTER
  Sen. Mike DeWine today announced that Dayton has been awarded a $2.5 million grant from the Department of Commerce's Economic Development Administration (EDA). The investment will help build the Creative Technology center, a technology accelerator facility that will provide space for the next generation of entrepreneurs and provide opportunities to grow businesses in the fields of high-technology. The project is expected to create 157 jobs and generate $1,361,000 in private investment. 
   "This is good news for Dayton. This funding will help Dayton retain and attract high-tech businesses that have the potential to offer more jobs to area residents and spur continued economic development in the area," said Senator DeWine.
   The EDA provides capital resources to meet the economic development needs of distressed communities throughout the United States. EDA's mission is to lead the federal economic development agenda by promoting innovation and competitiveness, preparing American regions for growth and success in the worldwide economy.
