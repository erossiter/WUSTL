SENS. DEWINE, VOINOVICH ANNOUNCE FEDERAL FUNDING FOR NORTHEAST OHIO PROJECTS
  Sens. Mike DeWine (R-OH) and George Voinovich (R-OH) today announced the Senate Appropriations Committee passage of the fiscal year 2005 Veterans Affairs and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill, which includes $14.8 million for Ohio projects overall, and $11.275 million in funding for Northeast Ohio projects.  
   "As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, it is one of my priorities to secure funding for worthy projects," said Senator DeWine. "These projects are important for development in Ohio and I look forward to working with my colleagues to make sure funding is retained in the final bill." 
   "Securing funding for NASA Glenn is essential to help ensure that this important Northeast Ohio center for research and development continues to be a key contributor to our space program and the community," said Senator Voinovich. "These funds will help boost Ohio's recovering economy, and I look forward to working with my colleagues in the Senate to ensure that the funding is maintained in the final version of the bill." 
   The Veterans Affairs and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations Bill includes funding for Northeast Ohio projects in the following areas: 
   Youngstown
   * $250,000 to Youngstown Central Area Community Improvement Corporation for construction of the Advanced Technology Incubator for Market Ready Applications. 
   Cleveland 
   * $550,000 to the Cleveland Playhouse Square for IDEA Center. The IDEA Center, a state of the art digital center, will provide educational opportunities by advancing applied technology and arts education for underserved K-12, post-secondary and professional education. 
   * $300,000 to CAMP for the Cleveland Manufacturing Technology Complex. The new complex is expected to create technology-based jobs. 
   Akron
   * $400,000 to the City of Akron for combined sewer overflow improvements. 
   Parma 
   * $300,000 to the City of Parma for city sewer replacement project. 
   Carroll County 
   * $300,000 to the Muskingum Watershed Conservancy District for Atwood Conference Center Water Treatment Plant improvements in Carroll County. 
   Jefferson County 
   * $175,000 to the Jefferson County Water and Sewer District for the Crestview/Belevedere Sanitary Sewer Project 
   NASA 
   * $4,000,000 for NASA Glenn's Intelligent Propulsion Systems (Prop 21). 
   * $1,000,000 for NASA Glenn's National Center for Communications, Navigation, and Surveillance. 
   * $4,000,000 for the Commercial Technology Program at NASA Glenn. 
   The full Senate must consider the bill. Once the bill passes the full Senate, it goes to a conference committee where differences will be worked out with the House version. Then the final version goes to the President for his signature.
