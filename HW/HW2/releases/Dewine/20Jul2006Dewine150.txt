SEN. DEWINE ANNOUNCES FEDERAL FUNDING FOR NORTHEAST OHIO PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, announced the Committee passage of the fiscal year 2007 Labor, Health and Human Services Appropriations bill that includes $6.22 million to help Northeast Ohio.
   "As a member of the Appropriations Committee, I am proud to have helped many worthy projects receive funding," said Senator DeWine. "I am particularly glad that money will be going to so many programs that will help improve healthcare for the residents, and in particular, the children of Northeast Ohio. I look forward to working with my colleagues to ensure this program funding is included in the final version of the bill." 
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following regional project:
   MetroHealth System, Cleveland
   * Pediatric Intensive Care Unit (PICU) Renovation - $350,000 to expand and update the medical technology and treatment options available at the busiest PICU in Northeast Ohio.
   Community Health Access Project (CHAP)
   * Changing the Focus to Outcomes - $100,000 to fund Community Health Workers to reach out and identify those individuals most at-risk for health problems in the community. They provide basic health education, assist individuals in receiving services, and identify individuals before there is a catastrophic health problem. This early intervention saves lives and money.
   Rainbow Babies and Children's Hospital, University Hospitals Health System
   * Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU) Renovation - $700,000 for construction and upgrade of the hospital's NICU, incorporating cutting edge research to measure the impact of improved design on patient outcomes and staff efficiency.
   Kent State University and Oak Clinic
   * Partnership for Multiple Sclerosis and Neurodegenerative Disease Research - $475,000 to fund a research partnership between Kent State and Oak Clinic aimed at better understanding and treating Multiple Sclerosis and other neurodegenerative diseases.
   Case Western Reserve University
   * National Flight Nurse Academy and Flight Nursing Program- $200,000 to construct a flight nurse laboratory that simulates the interior of an air medical service helicopter. It will be used for training purposes.
   Children's Hospital Medical Center of Akron
   * Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU) Construction - $500,000 for construction and renovation of the Level III Regional NICU.
   Cleveland Clinic
   * Children's Institute for Learning and Development - $500,000 to purchase medical equipment that will be used to diagnose and treat diseases in the areas of neurology, developmental and rehabilitative pediatrics, psychology, and allied health services.
   Cleveland Sight Center
   * Low Cost Braille Display Technology - $300,000 to purchase technology that displays Braille at a 1/10th of the traditional cost, making Braille displays affordable to many more blind people.
   Summa Health System
   * Cancer Center - $500,000 to purchase equipment needed for chemotherapy, radiation, immunotherapy and other services.
   Forum Health
   * Tod Children's Hospital Pediatric Services Project - $700,000 to purchase materials and equipment needed for a major upgrade of the current facility which will provide state-of-the-art medical care for at-risk infants in the area.
   Welcome House
   * Eldercare/Mental Retardation Developmental Disabilities (MRDD) Project - $500,000 to create a unique residential living setting that combines the best of the eldercare service system (comfort-care and compassion) and continues to provide services and supports based on traditional MRDD services (teaching and habilitation).
   The Paula and Anthony Rich Center for the Study and Treatment of Autism
   * The Rich Center for Autism Technology Grant - $500,000 to fund a program that is designed to establish distance learning about autism, to provide professionals access to best practices for serving the individuals with autism, to provide new avenues for research with a living laboratory of human subjects and to serve the needs of families affected by autism.
   Humility of Mary Health Partners
   * Medical Records & Medication Administration Project - $500,000 to pay for the hardware components of the project including a server, and hand-held bar code scanners that nurses will use at the bedside to scan bar-coded medications and the patient armbands.
   Vocational Guidance Services
   * Power Sewing Facility Equipment Improvements - $300,000 to purchase equipment that will allow Vocational Guidance Services to expand their sewing contracts thereby creating approximately 45 new jobs for Ohioans with disabilities.
   St. Vincent Charity Hospital
   * Emergency Department Improvements - $100,000 for construction of a new Emergency Department, equipped to handle Bioterrorism and other disasters.
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following statewide project:
   Ohio Council of Urban Leagues
   * Mentoring Best Practices Model - $500,000 for a program that allows young people between the ages of 12-18 years of age to receive holistic mentoring, providing them with a well rounded set of life skills development. The project is a proven model that will be implemented by the Urban Leagues in the State of Ohio, including those in Akron, Cincinnati, Cleveland, Columbus, Dayton, Lorain County, Stark County, Toledo, Warren and Trumbull County, covering the entire state and touching the lives of over 500 young people.
   "All of our children need mentors and I am very pleased that this money will be used for a statewide program with a proven track record for helping our children develop the skills they need to succeed," said Senator DeWine.
   This bill now goes to the Senate floor for consideration.
