SEN. DEWINE ANNOUNCES FEDERAL FUNDING FOR SOUTHWEST OHIO PROJECTS
  Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, announced the final passage of the fiscal year 2006 Interior Appropriations Bill, which includes funding for Southwest Ohio projects.  
   "I am pleased these important projects will receive necessary funding," said Senator DeWine. "Not only will these programs will ensure Ohio residents can enjoy the state's rich history and abundant natural resources, but will provide access to better, cleaner drinking water for families." 
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following regional projects: 
   Village of Higginsport 
   * Higginsport Sanitary Sewer Project - $850,000 for a new sanitary sewer collection and treatment system. Currently, village residents rely on individual on-site systems to treat their sewage. This project allows the village to meet the requirements of the Clean Water Act and eliminates existing pollution from the village. 
   Greene Community Foundation 
   * Water projects - $150,000 for wastewater and drinking water improvement projects for the Glen Helen Outdoor Education Center. 
   Aviation Heritage Foundation 
   * National Aviation Heritage Area Operations - $200,000 for the National Aviation Heritage Area to facilitate collaboration among organizations to promote heritage tourism and education programs related to Miami Valley's aviation history. 
   Senator DeWine helped secure funding for the following state and national projects: 
   Great Lakes Legacy 
   * Great Lakes Legacy Program - $30 million for the Great Lakes Legacy Act, a bill authored by Senator DeWine and Senator Carl Levin (D-MI) that became law in 2002. 
   The Act expedites the cleanup of the Great Lakes. In Ohio there are four major areas of concern, the Maumee River, the Ashtabula River, the Black River, and the Cuyahoga River. 
   National Underground Railroad Network to Freedom 
   * National Underground Railroad Network to Freedom Grants - $375,000 for the Network to Freedom Grant Program, which provides technical and rehabilitation assistance to registered Underground Railroad Network to Freedom sites. 
   Historic Preservation Fund 
   * National Grant Program for State Offices - $36 million for State Historic Preservation offices. These grants will allow states to continue historic preservation activities. 
   * Save America's Treasures Program - $30 million to help protect historic structures, collections, works of art, maps and journals that document the history and culture of the United States. 
   The bill final version of the bill now goes to the president for his signature.
