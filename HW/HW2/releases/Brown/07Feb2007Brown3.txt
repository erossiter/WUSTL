            Statement Of  On The Public Transportation Terrorism Prevention Act Of 2007                                                                                                                               Press Release    Statement Of  On The Public Transportation Terrorism Prevention Act Of 2007 February 7  2007  WASHINGTON  United States   D OH  released the following statement today on the Public Transportation Terrorism Prevention Act of 2007 Mr  Chairman  thank you for your leadership in calling attention to the need to bolster the security of our public transit system Transit security is a vital concern for all of our communities  and Ohio is no exception  We have focused so much of our attention on aviation security that we run the risk of shortchanging other  equally critical security needs  A population equal to half our country rides public transit in a given year  and tens of thousands of Americans work in our public transit systems every day Ohio has the 10th largest number of public transit riders in the country  Hundreds of thousands of Ohioans will ride our public transit system today and every work day  They need to be able to go to work  run their errands  and go about their lives with the knowledge that we have done everything we can do to ensure their safety We have seen too many examples around the world of terrorists attraction to public transit not to take action here at home  This legislation will provide appropriate federal assistance  in assessing the risks  developing security guidelines and approaches  and providing some fund for transit systems the threats they face  The bill also creates local security training programs for transit workers who are critical to any effort Mr  Chairman  I applaud your diligence and that of the other veteran members of this committee  This is now the third Congress that this Committee will have acted on this legislation  Lets hope the third time is the charm  Thank you
