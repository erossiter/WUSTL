            Brown Speaks Out Against Bush Veto Threat                                                                                                                               Press Release    Brown Speaks Out Against Bush Veto Threat  August 8  2007  Toledo  OHIO  As the Childrens Health Insurance Program legislation works its way through Congress  U S    D OH  today continued his statewide campaign against the Presidents veto threat at the Columbus Childrens Hospital  In any given month  approximately 150 000 Ohio children are covered by this program   Tens of thousands more could gain access if this bill is signed into law The childrens health insurance program covers kids who have no other options  said Brown  In most cases  these are sons and daughters of parents with full time jobs  Every eligible child in Ohio and across the nation should be able to benefit from this program The CHIP legislation  which passed the Senate last week  would provide  35 billion in additional funding for the reauthorization of the Childrens Health Insurance Program  The additional funds would not only maintain coverage for the millions of children already enrolled in the program  but would also expand health care coverage to millions more children nationwide who are eligible for the program but not yet enrolled  The president has previously threatened to veto the bill One uninsured child is one too many  The president has an opportunity to do something positive for American children  Lets hope he doesnt squander it Brown  a member of the Senate Health  Education  Labor  and Pensions  HELP  Committee  worked with his colleagues on the Finance Committee to increase funding for the State Childrens Health Insurance Program in the federal budget   The Childrens Health Insurance Program  which originally passed in 1997  must be reauthorized by Congress every ten years  and is set to expire September 30  2007  Brown was joined today by Ohio Governor Ted Strickland  Dr  Steve Allen  CEO of Columbus Childrens Hospital  Margaret Demko  Columbus parent and Dr  William Cotton  President of the Ohio American Academy of Pediatrics
