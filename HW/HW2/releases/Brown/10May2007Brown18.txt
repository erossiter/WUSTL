            With Gas Prices On The Rise  Brown Urges Feds To Consider Options To Avoid An Energy Crisis This Summer                                                                                                                               Press Release    With Gas Prices On The Rise  Brown Urges Feds To Consider Options To Avoid An Energy Crisis This Summer  May 10  2007  WASHINGTON  With gas prices rising daily  United States   D OH  today urged the administration to avoid another energy crisis this summer by working proactively  The driving season is still several weeks away  yet gas prices are already more than  3 a gallon in Ohio  well above the national average  Brown is calling for a new direction in U S  energy policy  including energy independence  Oil companies are enjoying billions of dollars in tax subsidies  and once again  gas prices are on the rise  forcing middle class families to foot the bill  Brown said  Our government needs to stop subsidizing multinational oil companies and start investing in policies that will lower gas prices and lead our nation toward energy independence Brown wrote to Samuel Bodman  Secretary of the U S  Department of Energy  this week  asking him to outline for the American public exactly what steps the Administration is taking to prevent a repeat of last summers gas crisis Brown traveled throughout Ohio last year  and heard from middle class families about how far family budgets were stretched  while oil companies reported record profits  Despite U S  Energy Secretary Samuel Bodman admitting on Meet the Press last year to a national gas crisis  Brown noted the administration has not yet changed its energy policy According to AAA  gas prices are already up 5 percent nationally since this time last year  and its only May
