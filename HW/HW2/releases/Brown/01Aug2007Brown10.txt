            Brown Champions Effort To Give Pharmacies More Time To Comply With New Medicaid Rule                                                                                                                               Press Release    Brown Champions Effort To Give Pharmacies More Time To Comply With New Medicaid Rule  August 1  2007  WASHINGTON  DC  U S  Senators Sherrod Brown  D OH  and George Voinovich  R OH  offered an amendment yesterday to the Childrens Health Insurance Program  CHIP  legislation that would provide more time for Medicaid recipients and pharmacists to comply with a new rule effective October 1st  A bill earlier this year included a provision requiring all Medicaid prescriptions TO be written on tamper  proof paper to avoid fraud  Currently  most physicians do not use these pads and supplies are not readily available  especially in rural areas  The Brown Voinovich amendment would delay implementation of the rule for 18 months for most prescriptions  Tamper proof prescriptions would still be required for Schedule II  S2  narcotics  On October 1  patients across Ohio and across the country could be turned away from their local pharmacies and not receive the vital prescriptions they need  We are not going to let that happen  said Senator Brown  a member of the Health  Education  Labor  and Pensions  HELP  Committee The Brown Voinovich amendment would delay implementation of the tamper proof requirement for 98 percent of Medicaid prescriptions for 18 months  with the exception of Schedule II  S2  narcotic prescriptions   Anti fraud efforts have focused on these controlled substances  which are a lucrative target for prescription tampering activities  By attaching this amendment to the CHIP legislation  which is on the Senate floor this week  Brown and Voinovich hope to ease the fears of Medicaid participants who would be placed at risk by any delay in access to needed medications  In 2006  doctors wrote approximately 330 million prescriptions for Medicaid beneficiaries  11 percent of the nearly 3 1 billion total prescriptions written each year   The new requirement is especially hard on small  family owned pharmacies  The deadline leaves little time for federal and state Medicaid officials to educate doctors and pharmacists about the requirement
