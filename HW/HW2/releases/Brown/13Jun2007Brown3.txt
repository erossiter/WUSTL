                                     Press Release    Brown  Stabenow Blast Treasury Decision To Once Again Not Designate China As A Currency Manipulator June 13  2007  Washington  DC  U S  Senators Sherrod Brown  D OH  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  today expressed disappointment in Secretary Henry Paulson and the Department of the Treasury for failing to designate China as a currency manipulator in its semiannual report to Congress  Congress gave this authority to the Treasury Department under the Omnibus Trade and Competitiveness Act of 1988   EDITORS SEE LETTER ATTACHED In addition  Brown and Stabenow yesterday participated in a colloquy on trade on the Senate floor  Earlier this week  member of the U S  House of Representatives took to the House floor to discuss the implications of fair trade It is widely accepted that China manipulates its currency by pegging it to the dollar  Best estimates show that currency manipulation amounts to a 40 percent subsidy for China  The U S  ever expanding trade deficit has contributed to the loss of more than 2 million manufacturing jobs in the last six year  including more than 185 000 in Ohio  Bipartisan legislation that is WTO compliant was introduced yesterday in the Senate to address Chinas currency manipulation This summer  Congress is expected to debate proposed free trade agreements with Peru  Panama  Colombia  and South Korea  Last month  Democratic leadership negotiated an outline with the administration for future free trade agreements  However  concerns remain about enforceability and about rules in other areas of the agreements  including on services privatization and investment policy Fast Track expires June 30  and would allow for Congress to restore its constitutional authority over trade policy making for the
