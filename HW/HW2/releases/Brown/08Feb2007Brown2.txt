            Statement Of  From Today s HELP Committee Roundtable On No Child Left Behind Reauthorization                                                                                                                               Press Release    Statement Of  From Today s HELP Committee Roundtable On No Child Left Behind Reauthorization February 8  2007  WASHINGTON  United States   D OH  released the following statement from todays Senate Health  Education  Labor  and Pensions  HELP  Committee roundtable discussion on No Child Left Behind  NCLB  Reauthorization Five years ago Congress made a promise to teach every child in America and leave not one behind  The No Child Left Behind Act added important accountability provisions to Title I of ESEA and required states to set clear timelines for improving student achievement  It placed particular emphasis on closing achievement gaps and making sure all students were taught by qualified teachers   All of these are necessary steps to bring accountability and high performance to schools  but to some extent they will just be nice words unless we fully fund No Child Left Behind and make sure states have flexibility in meeting their assessment standards  The budget proposed by the president under funded the No Child Left Behind Act by  14 8 billion  This totals to a cumulative shortfall of  70 9 billion since the enactment of the law  This is simply not right  In my state of Ohio  there are almost 700 schools designated as being in need of improvement  States must be given assistance so they can properly conduct all the testing required by NCLB and implement corrective actions for their schools  The federal government must step up to the plate and fulfill its responsibility to every child     This roundtable is a significant step towards keeping the promise that every child will have a quality education and the chance to participate in the American dream  This is the simple dream of going to school  working hard  and making a life for themselves and their families  Today we have the opportunity to hear from administrators  principals and teachers in the field who are working hard to improve student learning  If we are going to make any progress we must learn what is working and what is not working in our schools  I thank the Chairman for holding this roundtable and look forward to working with the committee to keep our promise to the nation  provide a quality education for every child  and leave no child behind
