            After Statewide Veterans Tour Last Month  Brown Introduces New Legislation Offering Relief For Those Getting Called Up For Duty While In School                                                                                                                               Press Release    After Statewide Veterans Tour Last Month  Brown Introduces New Legislation Offering Relief For Those Getting Called Up For Duty While In School  June 27  2007  Washington  DC  United States   D OH   joined by Senator Ken Salazar  D CO   today introduced new legislation  the Veterans Education Tuition Support  VETS  Act  which would enable college students to keep their academic career on track after being called up to active military service When soldiers head off to war  they need to know that they will be given the time and the support they need  without falling unnecessarily behind academically or financially  when they return to their normal lives as college students  Brown said Last month  Brown held a series of roundtables with veterans from across Ohio in Canton  Youngstown  and Toledo  and in New Philadelphia  he led the first ever joint field hearing of the Senate and House Committees on Veterans Affairs  Brown discussed issues of importance to the more than 1 million veterans in Ohio  including mental health  homelessness  elder care  and veterans service improvements  Brown also met with newly returning veterans to discuss the GI Bill  employment services  and readjustment issues The VETS Act amends the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act  SCRA  and would require colleges and universities to refund tuition for servicemembers who deploy during the semester and restore their academic status when they return  It would also extend the period of time before returning service members must reenroll in school or face repaying their student loans and cap student loan interest rates at 6 percent during deployment  The VETS act would apply to all National Guard  reservists  and active duty military member enrolled in higher education institutions  Brown worked closely with the Iraq and Afghanistan Veterans of America  IAVA  in developing this legislation  Companion legislation will be introduced in the House today by Rep  Susan Davis  D CA  Brown also introduced legislation that would increase GI Bill funding for all veterans in the program pursuing degrees in science  technology  engineering  and math  STEM  by  2 000 a year  The funding would be available  prorated  at the beginning of each quarter or semester While the U S  has a shortage of students going into careers in math and science  our military are getting real world  high tech experience  This legislation capitalizes on the skills of our servicemembers by providing additional start up money for those pursuing degrees in math and science  Brown added  Earlier today  the Senate Veterans Affairs Committee  of which Brown is a member  approved several pieces of legislation  making a long term commitment to those serving in the military  Brown is a cosponsor of the four initiatives that came out of committee today     The Joshua Omvig Veterans Suicide Prevention Act  S  479  would direct the Secretary of Veterans Affairs to develop and implement a comprehensive program to reduce the incidence of suicide among veterans  Suicide among veterans suffering from post traumatic stress disorder  PTSD  has become a serious problem  and this legislation would develop and implement a comprehensive program to reduce the incidence of veteran suicide  including outreach and preemptive measures  treatment and recovery  organizational measures  and research     The Veterans Compensation Cost of Living Adjustment Act  COLA  of 2007  S  423  would increase the rates of compensation for veterans with service connected disabilities and the rates of dependency and indemnity compensation for the survivors of certain disabled veterans  Programs affected by this COLA bill include compensation  clothing allowance  dependency and indemnity compensation to surviving spouses  and dependency and indemnity compensation to children     The Blinded Veterans Paired Organ Act of 2007  S  1163  would improve compensation for veterans in certain cases of impairment of vision involving both eyes  and to provide for the use of the National Directory of New Hires for income verification purposes     The Veterans Traumatic Brain Injury and Other Health Programs Improvement Act of 2007  S  1233  would improve programs in the areas of traumatic brain injury and mental health  to enhance travel reimbursement for certain qualifying veterans  to expand outreach and access to care for rural and homeless veterans  to authorize funds for the construction and modernization of facilities  and for other purposes
