            Brown Announces Hardin And Seneca Counties Now Included For Federal Emergency Aid                                                                                                                               Press Release    Brown Announces Hardin And Seneca Counties Now Included For Federal Emergency Aid  September 4  2007  Washington  DC  U S    D OH  today announced that Hardin and Seneca Counties have been added to the major disaster area declaration by the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  and will now be eligible for individual aid   Families in Hardin and Seneca Counties are recovering from the recent flooding  Now federal assistance is available  Senator Brown said  Too many homes were ruined  too many businesses devastated  and too many farms washed out  In August  Brown toured the flooded region  visiting Shelby  Bucyrus  and Findlay  Brown met with Governor Ted Strickland  Secretary of Homeland Security Michael Chertoff  and Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  Director R  David Paulison to discuss federal aid for the region  On Friday  Senators Brown and George Voinovich urged the president to approve Stricklands request for disaster designation  In addition  Brown plans to work with the Small Business Administration and the Department of Agriculture to ensure local businesses and farms receive needed federal assistance  Individual assistance provides aid for those whose homes were damaged by a disaster  Such aid includes temporary housing  repair  and construction  The maximum aid amount is  28 200 per household for damages not covered by insurance   People affected by last months flooding can call 1 800 621 FEMA for FEMA assistance  If they need additional assistance  they can contact Senator Browns office at 440 242 4100
