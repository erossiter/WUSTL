            Freshman Senators Call For Meeting With Finance Committee Chairman To Discuss Transforming Current Trade Policy                                                                                                                               Press Release    Freshman Senators Call For Meeting With Finance Committee Chairman To Discuss Transforming Current Trade Policy March 12  2007  WASHINGTON Members of the United States Senate Freshman class  including Senators Sherrod Brown  D OH   Benjamin Cardin  D MD   Robert Casey  D PA   Amy Klobuchar  D MN   Bernard Sanders  D VT   James Webb  D VA   and Sheldon Whitehouse  D RI  called for a meeting with Finance Committee Chairman Max Baucus  D MT  to discuss problems and opportunities in US trade policy  In a letter sent Thursday  the newly elected Senators stressed the importance of trade in last Novembers elections In Ohio and in so many states across the nation  trade is a major topic on the minds of voters  Brown said  Years of job killing trade agreement are taking their toll on workers and small business owners alike  Trade polices we set in Washington  and negotiate across the globe  have a direct impact on places like Akron and Urbana    and Athens  Meeting with Senator Baucus will allow us to take what we heard last year and use it to help shape a new direction for trade In 2006  the U S  trade deficit reached a record  836 billion  the fifth straight year with a record  while over the past six years  the U S  lost 3 million manufacturing jobs  s emphasized the need to develop a new trade policy that will empower Congress to exercise its constitutional authority over trade agreements  An enforceable set of labor and other key standards in future trade agreements can address income inequality issues and help to ensure in the U S  and abroad  workers are able to share in the wealth rather than run in a race to the bottom   According to Fridays report  the U S  trade deficit was  59 1 billion in January  which is a slight reduction from Decembers  61 5 billion  Although down slightly from the record pace set last year  the trade deficit remains unsustainably large  The January trade deficit with China was 19 percent higher than it was a year ago  In Ohio  more than 40 000 jobs have been lost to China alone in the last decade Last weeks numbers underscore the need for a new direction for trade now  Revamping U S  trade policy is not just about taking better hold of our economic health  it is about establishing priorities in Washington that reflect family values at home and building strong relationships with trading partners abroad  While the administration continues to be out of touch with Main Street  I am pleased to say that in this Congress  there is a bipartisan fair trade effort under way  Brown said
