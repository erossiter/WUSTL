            In Wake Of 3rd Largest Meat Recall  Brown Emphasized Importance Of Recall Insurance                                                                                                                               Press Release    In Wake Of 3rd Largest Meat Recall  Brown Emphasized Importance Of Recall Insurance October 5  2007  Washington  DC  In the wake of the Topps Meat Company announcement that they are closing after the recall of 21 7 million pounds of beef last week  U S    D OH  today emphasized the importance of recall insurance to prevent similar situations  Brown has legislation that would require insurance for distributors to ensure they are able to cover the costs associated with both product recalls and all personal and property damages  The bill would also give the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  mandatory recall authority to speed up the removal of unsafe meats Eighty seven workers are out of a job because a company could not afford a large recall  recall insurance should not be an option  Thats eighty seven families with an uncertain future  Brown said  We need to ensure that consumers are protected from unsafe meat and companies and their workers are protected against a recall  We need more inspections  but we also need to ensure that distributors consider recalls as a factor when they make sourcing and supply decisions According to news reports last Tuesday  Topps Meat Company recalled a portion of frozen beef that allegedly sickened 25 people in Connecticut  Florida  Indiana  Maine  New Jersey  New York  Ohio  and Pennsylvania  Over the weekend  the recall of meat expanded to 21 7 million pounds  After the third largest meat recall in U S  history  Topps announced today they will be forced to close their doors after 67 years  The recalled meat was produced in late June and July  and the E  coli in the beef began sickening people in August  While the USDA said there were no recall delays  it took nearly six weeks before the first recall was issued The Food and Product Responsibility Act of 2007  S 2081  would require that distributors of food and consumer products demonstrate the financial capacity to cover risks associated with recalls and product safety  Specifically  U S  Customs and Border Protection  in conjunction with other agencies  would develop a program to ensure distributors are able to cover the costs associated with both product recalls and all personal and property damages that may occur as a result of a defective product  Distributors would demonstrate that they possess product recall and liability insurance or have sufficient financial resources to afford a recall and any subsequent damage claims  Products covered would include Auto parts Food  drugs  devices  and cosmetics Biological products Consumer products Meat  meat products  poultry  and poultry products  eggs and egg products  In addition  Browns legislation would grant the Secretary of Agriculture the authority to require mandatory recalls of meat  poultry  and egg products and grant the Food and Drug Administration  FDA  the authority to require recalls for fruits  vegetables  and other products it regulates
