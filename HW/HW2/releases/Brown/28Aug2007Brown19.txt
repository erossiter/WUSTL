            Brown  Federal Emergency Aid On Its Way To Ohio                                                                                                                               Press Release    Brown  Federal Emergency Aid On Its Way To Ohio  August 28  2007  Washington  DC  U S    D OH  announced that late yesterday  a major disaster area was declared by the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  for Allen  Crawford  Hancock  Putnam  Richland  and Wyandot Counties in Ohio  These counties will now be eligible for individual aid and hazard mitigation from FEMA   This announcement brings relief to thousands of Ohio families recovering from the recent flooding  Senator Brown said  Many homes were destroyed  businesses devastated  and farms washed out  Now we need to rebuild our communities and help people reestablish their lives  I am pleased Governor Strickland and the Bush Administration worked quickly to make federal assistance available  Last week  Brown toured the flooded region  visiting Shelby  Bucyrus  and Findlay  Brown met with Governor Ted Strickland  Secretary of Homeland Security Michael Chertoff  and Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  Director R  David Paulison to discuss federal aid for the region  On Friday  Senators Brown and George Voinovich urged the president to approve Stricklands request for disaster designation  In addition  Brown plans to work with the Small Business Administration and the Department of Agriculture to ensure local businesses and farms receive needed federal assistance  Individual assistance provides aid for those whose homes were damaged by a disaster  Such aid includes temporary housing  repair  and construction  The maximum aid amount is  28 200 per household for damages not covered by insurance   Counties will also be eligible for assistance through Hazard Mitigation  It provides aid to states and local governments to implement long term hazard mitigation measures aimed at reducing the devastating effects of natural disasters  People affected by last weeks flooding can call 1 800 321 3362 for FEMA assistance  If they need additional assistance  they can contact Senator Browns office at 440 242 4100
