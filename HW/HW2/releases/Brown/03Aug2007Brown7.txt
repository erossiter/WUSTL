            Brown Supports Strengthening America s Competitveness Globally                                                                                                                               Press Release    Brown Supports Strengthening America s Competitveness Globally August 3  2007  WASHINGTON  United States   D OH  today hailed Senate passage of the conference report on the America COMPETES Act that passed late last night  Brown is an original cosponsor of this legislation to make America more competitive in the global economy  The legislation will help the United States maintain a competitive edge in science and technology by investing in research and education  It also strengthens the commitment to the National Science Foundation  NASA and other science related federal agencies which help to advance that competitive edge Too few students are entering careers in math and science  and it is affecting our competitiveness in the global arena  Brown said  This legislation will strengthen schools and research centers in Ohio and across the country with funding for research and development  math and science education  and innovation infrastructure to regain Americas competitive edge in the world According to the National Science Board  the United States is falling behind other nations in math and science  One of the factors businesses look for when considering moving to an area is skilled labor needed  The U S  National Science Foundation cites that high tech industry employment grew by 50 percent between 1990 and 2002  and the Bureau of Labor Statistics projects that U S  demand for science and engineering workers will grow at least three times as fast as the overall economy in the next ten years
