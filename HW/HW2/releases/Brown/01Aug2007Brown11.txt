            Brown Urges Treasury Secretary Paulson To Protect Retirees  Interests                                                                                                                               Press Release    Brown Urges Treasury Secretary Paulson To Protect Retirees  Interests August 1  2007  Washington  DC  U S    D OH  today sent Treasury Secretary Henry Paulson a letter calling on him to respect the rights of retirees to recover the retirement benefits they are due  Brown was joined by Senator Edward M  Kennedy  chairman of the Senate Health  Education  Labor  and Pensions  HELP  Committee  and fellow committee members Jeff Bingaman  D NM   Patty Murray  D WA   Tom Harkin  D IA   and Barbara Mikulski  D MD    Their letter called on Treasury to be faithful to the letter and spirit of the recently enacted Pension Protection Act of 2006  PPA  American workers deserve better  We want to make sure companies keep their promises to retirees  said Brown  The new pension law changed the way lump sum distributions from cash balance plans are calculated in the future  but it did not excuse miscalculations of benefits that occurred in the past   Congress debated retroactivity at great length and decided that the provisions of the of the act dealing with cash balance pension plans were intended to apply solely on a prospective basis  and thus did not prevent retirees whose pensions were unlawfully calculated under prior law from recovering the full amounts they are due In May  a group of eight Republicans sent Paulson a letter urging him to interpret the laws effective date retroactively  which would block the courts from deciding what retirees are owed
