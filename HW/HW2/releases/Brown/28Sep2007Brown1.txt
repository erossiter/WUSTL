            Freshman Class Wartime Contracting Amendment Wins Approval                                                                                                                               Press Release    Freshman Class Wartime Contracting Amendment Wins Approval  September 28  2007  Washington  DC  A landmark provision cosponsored by U S    D OH  and members of the Senate Democratic freshman class won unanimous approval in the Senate late last night   The amendment would establish an independent  eight member Commission to investigate U S  wartime contracting in Iraq and Afghanistan Tens of billions of taxpayer dollars have gone to private contractors who operate outside the rules  Senator Brown said  The result has been chaos  projects never completed  dollars never accounted for  and benchmarks never met  We have wasted too much money on private contractors  and if they cant be trusted with our dollars  they shouldnt be doing business with our country The amendment would create a Commission to investigate the governments growing reliance on civilian contractors to perform wartime functions   The Commission on Wartime Contracting would work in partnership with the Special Inspector General of Iraq Reconstruction  SIGIR  to expand oversight to include investigations of wartime contracting in Iraq and Afghanistan  not just Iraq reconstruction  This expansion is in response to recent reports of contractor abuses  including the latest federal investigation of Blackwater  which has the largest contracts for private security in Iraq  Currently  the number of contractors  180 000  exceeds the number of American troops in Iraq and Afghanistan  156 247   The amendment would require a study and investigation into the impact of the governments growing reliance on civilian contractors to perform wartime functions   The commission would assess the extent of waste  fraud  abuse  and mismanagement of wartime contracts  and the extent to which those responsible have been held accountable  Congressmen John Tierney  D MA  introduced similar language in the House of Representatives yesterday as a stand alone bill  The amendment  sponsored by Senator Brown and Senators Jim Webb  D VA   Claire McCaskill  D MO   Benjamin L  Cardin  D MD   Bernie Sanders  I VT   Bob Casey  D PA   Amy Klobuchar  D MN   Sheldon Whitehouse  D RI   and Jon Tester  D MT   was debated during consideration of the Defense Authorization Act  which authorizes funding for the U S  Department of Defense for fiscal year 2008
