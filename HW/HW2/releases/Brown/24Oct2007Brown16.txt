            Brown  Ohioans Applying For Federal Disaster Aid Only Have Until The End Of The Week                                                                                                                               Press Release    Brown  Ohioans Applying For Federal Disaster Aid Only Have Until The End Of The Week October 24  2007  Washington  DC  U S    D OH  today alerted residents and businesses in Allen  Crawford  Hancock  Hardin  Putnam  Richland  Seneca  and Wyandot Counties that they must apply for federal assistance by close of business this Friday  October 26 in order to be eligible for aid  Ohioans who suffered damage from the flooding in August must register with the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  or apply for a low interest loan from the U S  Small Business Administration  SBA  by the end of this week In August  Brown toured the flooded region  visiting Shelby  Bucyrus  and Findlay  Brown met with Governor Ted Strickland  Secretary of Homeland Security Michael Chertoff  and Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  Director R  David Paulison to discuss federal aid for the region Individual assistance is available for those whose homes were damaged in the August floods  Such aid includes temporary housing  repair  and construction  The maximum aid amount is  28 200 per household for damages not covered by insurance  People affected by the August flooding can call 1 800 621 3362 for FEMA assistance  If they need additional assistance  they can contact Senator Browns office at 440 242 4100
