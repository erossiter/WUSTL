            Brown Announces Senate Passage Of The America Competes Act                                                                                                                               Press Release    Brown Announces Senate Passage Of The America Competes Act  April 25  2007  WASHINGTON  United States   D OH  today announced Senate passage of the bipartisan America COMPETES Act of 2007  Brown is an original cosponsor of this legislation to make America more competitive in the global economy  Too few students are entering careers in math and science  and it is affecting our competitiveness in the global arena  Brown said  This legislation gets to the root of the problem by enhancing educational opportunities in the math and sciences  According to the National Science Board  the United States is falling behind other nations in math and science  One of the factors businesses look for when considering moving to an area is skilled labor needed  The U S  National Science Foundation cites that high tech industry employment grew by 50 percent between 1990 and 2002  and the Bureau of Labor Statistics projects that U S  demand for science and engineering workers will grow at least three times as fast as the overall economy in the next ten years  This legislation will strengthen schools and research centers in Ohio and across the country with funding for research and development  math and science education  and innovation infrastructure to regain Americas competitive edge in the world  Brown said The America COMPETES Act  or the America Creating Opportunities to Meaningfully Promote Excellence in Technology  Education and Science Act  will focus on three primary areas of importance to maintaining and improving United States innovation      Strengthening education in the areas of math  science  technology  and engineering      Increasing research investment      Developing an innovation infrastructure
