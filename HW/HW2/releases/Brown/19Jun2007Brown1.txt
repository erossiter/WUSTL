            Brown Pushes For Senate Passage Of Employee Free Choice Act                                                                                                                               Press Release    Brown Pushes For Senate Passage Of Employee Free Choice Act  June 19  2007  Washington  DC  United States   D OH  today pushed for Senate passage of the Employee Free Choice Act  EFCA   which the Senate is expected to vote on this week  The House passed similar legislation in March with bipartisan support  Brown participated in a Capitol Hill rally attended by more than 2 000 people this afternoon  EFCA would give workers new rights to organize and fight for fair wages and benefits   In Ohio  median household income is down almost 10 percent  while the average CEO makes 411 times more than the average worker  said Brown  The Employee Free Choice Act goes to the heart of working families fight to reap the benefits of the productivity they helped create EFCA would give workers the ability to form unions and eliminate some of the barriers that employers throw in the path of unionization   Among its provisions  the bill would increase penalties on employers that obstruct unionizing efforts  establishing arbitration and mediation procedures  and simplifying the process by which employees choose to form unions This legislation would be instrumental in addressing income inequality by helping workers organize for increases in wages and benefits   There are people in our communities that work hard  play by the rules  do everything we ask  and would love to have a union representing them  This fight is for those 60 million workers in this country who want to join a union  Brown added
