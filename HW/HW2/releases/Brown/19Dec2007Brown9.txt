            Brown Expresses Concern About Recent China Food Safety Agreement                                                                                                                               Press Release    Brown Expresses Concern About Recent China Food Safety Agreement December 19  2007  Washington  DC  U S    D OH  sent a letter to Health and Human Services Secretary Michael Leavitt requesting the agency investigate a Chinese exporter currently exempt from U S  food safety inspection that was put on a Canadian import alert list Brown registered concerns about the recently signed Memorandum of Agreement  MOA  signed by Secretary Leavitt and Chinas General Administration of Quality Supervision  Inspection and Quarantine  AQSIQ  at last weeks Strategic Economic Dialogue in Beijing  Rather than ensuring certification of exporters of all products  a reading of the MOA suggests that our government is creating a special program to facilitate imported shipments from Chinese food exporters who have been caught repeatedly shipping contaminated food  The goal of this program would then be to facilitate more Chinese exports of high risk foods  Brown wrote Brown also cited a December 15 Wall Street Journal report about a Chinese seafood exporter exempted from U S  safety standards that is on a Canadian import alert list  This MOA seems to represent a reactive approach that relies on the Chinese government  when consumers and the food industry need a proactive one  said Brown  Import safety requires much more than responding to crisis
