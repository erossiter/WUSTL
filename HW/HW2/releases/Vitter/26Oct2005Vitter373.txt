SEN. VITTER ANNOUNCES $10.47 MILLION FEMA GRANT FOR HURRICANE KATRINA
  Sen. David Vitter announced today that the Orleans Parish Sewerage and Water Board will receive $10.47 million from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) to pay for costs associated with Hurricane Katrina recovery efforts. 
   "I appreciate the U.S. Department of Homeland Security awarding this money to Louisiana," said Vitter. "The Orleans Parish Sewerage and Water Board suffered damage to its equipment and facilities during Hurricane Katrina, and this $10.47 million federal grant will help cover some of these expenses." 
   The Orleans Parish Sewerage and Water Board experienced a disruption of its sewage collection network due to Hurricane Katrina. During the disruption, the storm caused damage to its facilities and equipment.
   "The federal government continues to provide resources to assist our local and state governments as we continue our recovery from Hurricane Katrina," said Vitter. "I will continue my efforts, in conjunction with the Louisiana delegation, to convince congressional leadership and the Bush Administration to bring additional resources to Louisiana to help in the recovery process."
