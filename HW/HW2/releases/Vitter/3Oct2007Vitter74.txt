REP. ALEXANDER, SEN. VITTER ANNOUNCE GRANT FUNDING FOR POINTE COUPEE PARISH
  U.S. Rep. Rodney Alexander and U.S. Sen. David Vitter today announced that the Hospital Service District No. 1 of Pointe Coupee, LA, Inc. will receive a USDA Rural Development Grant totaling $372,670. The funding, provided through the Distance Learning and Telemedicine Grant Program, will be used for a telemedicine project to enable medical professionals across Pointe Coupee Parish to provide care to rural patients in a timely manner 
   The project will include a HealthCare professional portal, which will allow for remote access to the current Hospital Information System. The system will allow providers access to CT scans, MRI, ultrasound, and traditional X-ray images.
   "As medical technology becomes more advanced, it is important to ensure that all people have access to top-notch health care, and this project will move Pointe Coupee forward in its efforts to provide citizens in rural areas with quality, timely medical care," Alexander said.
   "As our health care system continues to modernize, this federal funding helps advance the medical technology for Pointe Coupee parish to provide greater care for its rural patients," said Vitter.
