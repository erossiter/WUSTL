SENS. BAUCUS, TESTER ANNOUNCE DEVELOPMENT MONEY FOR INDIAN COUNTRY
  Montana Senators Max Baucus and Jon Tester today announced $80,000 in grants for two Montana Indian reservations as part of the Department of Commerce's Economic Development Council. 
   The Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes of the Flathead Nation and the Fort Belknap Indian Community will each receive a grant of $44,000 for 2008.
   The money will help the tribes continue their economic development efforts.
   "Helping the reservations build a strong economy is good for them and good for all Montana," Baucus said. "This funding will provide a much-needed boost to the tribes by providing more good-paying jobs."
   "I am proud of the efforts the Salish-Kootenai, Assiniboine, and Gros Ventre tribes," said Tester, a member of the Senate Indian Affairs Committee. "Economic development is a critical component to improving opportunity in Indian Country, and I look forward to seeing these dollars at work.".
