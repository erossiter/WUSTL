BOYER CHUTE NATIONAL WILDLIFE REFUGE HOSTING LEWIS, CLARK COMMEMORATIVE EVENT
  Sen. Chuck Hagel (R-NE) announced today that the Boyer Chute National Wildlife Refuge near Fort Calhoun has invited Nebraskans to view a 55-foot replica of the keelboat used by Lewis and Clark.  
 This event is part of the bicentennial commemoration celebration of Lewis and Clark's expedition. 
   "Lewis and Clark are important figures in Nebraska's history. I encourage Nebraskans to visit this beautiful area and learn about Lewis and Clark's contributions to the spirit of our state and nation," Hagel said. 
   Visitors can view the keelboat July 31-August 3 and enjoy trail walks, refreshments and educational presentations and materials. Free shuttle buses will be provided from Fort Atkinson State Historical Park in Fort Calhoun to the Refuge every 20 minutes, from 10 A.M.-6 P.M. July 31, noon-6 P.M. August 1, and 10 A.M.-4 P.M. August 2 and 3. For more information contact Boyer Chute National Wildlife Refuge at 402/468-4313.
