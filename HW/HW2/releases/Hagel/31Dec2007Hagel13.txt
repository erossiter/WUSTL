SEN. HAGEL MEETS WITH PAKISTANI AMBASSADOR TO U.S.
  U.S. Senator Chuck Hagel (R-NE) issued the following statement today after meeting with the Pakistani Ambassador to the United States, Mahmud Ali Durrani, at Pakistan's Embassy in Washington, D.C. Senator Hagel also signed a condolence book to the people of Pakistan, on behalf of himself and the people of Nebraska.  
   "On behalf of myself, and all Nebraskans, I express my deepest condolences to the people of Pakistan for the tragic assassination of former Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto. This is a moment of great challenge for Pakistan and the United States will remain a steadfast partner of a stable, secure Pakistan. 
   "When I spoke with Pakistani Ambassador Durrani, we discussed the importance of moving forward with the democratic process and parliamentary elections. Ambassador Durrani assured me that the Pakistani government was committed to the Pakistani elections going forward on schedule. He also said the Pakistani government is open to all international scenarios to investigate the assassination of former Prime Minister Bhutto. 
   "The United States and the international community should continue to help Pakistan, its people and its leaders, overcome this tragedy and emerge stronger as a country. Pakistan is a key ally of America in our efforts against Islamic extremist and terrorist, especially in Afghanistan."
