SENATE PASSES TRANSPORTATION-HUD APPROPRIATIONS BILL
 The United States Senate passed today the Transportation, Housing and Urban Development (HUD) Appropriations bill by a vote of 88-7. U.S. Senator Chuck Hagel (R-NE) voted for the legislation. The bill provides funding for the Departments of Transportation and Housing and Urban Development. The bill also includes several Nebraska road and civil projects supported by Hagel.  
   "This bill provides important funding to maintain and improve our nation's transportation system. It also provides needed funding for housing and economic development projects throughout Nebraska and the country. These are important, targeted projects that address significant needs in our state," Hagel said. 
   The bill includes funding for the following Nebraska projects supported by Hagel: 
   * $300,000 for the Antelope Valley Community Revitalization in Lincoln. 
   * $2,000,000 for the Saddle Creek Road Relocation in Omaha. 
   * $500,000 for the US-30 Viaduct in Columbus. 
   * $1,000,000 for the I-80 Westbound Bridge over Missouri River in Omaha. 
   * $1,000,000 for the Nebraska Innovation Center at UNL. 
   * $1,000,000 for the Willa Cather Foundation in Red Cloud. 
   The bill will now go to a House-Senate Conference.
