SENATE PASSES ENERGY, WATER APPROPRIATIONS BILL
  The U.S. Senate passed the Fiscal Year 2006 Energy and Water Appropriations bill today by a vote of 84 to 4. Sen.  
 Chuck Hagel (R-NE) requested funding for several Nebraska projects that were included in the bill. 
   "This bill provides funding for several important water and energy projects in Nebraska addressing flood control, environmental restoration and solar cell development. The targeted programs address significant needs in Nebraska communities," Hagel said. 
   The bill appropriates funding for the following projects requested by Hagel: 
   * $2.215 million for the Antelope Creek Flood Control Project in Lincoln; 
   * $800,000 to advance solar cell technology at the University of Nebraska-Kearney; 
   * $2.5 million for the Sand Creek Environmental Restoration Project in Wahoo; 
   * $194,000 for Lower Decatur Bend Environmental Improvement in Burt County; 
   * $1.5 million for the Clear Creek Flood Reduction Project in Western Sarpy County; 
   * $1.677 million for Harlan County Lake Operations and Maintenance; 
   * $186,000 for the Heron Haven Wetland Restoration Project near Papillon; 
   * $60,000 for Sarpy County Water and Wastewater Planning and 
   * $320,000 for the Harlan County Lake's Dam Safety Project. 
   The Energy & Water Appropriations bill passed the House last week by a vote of 399-17. The bill will now go to the President for his signature.
