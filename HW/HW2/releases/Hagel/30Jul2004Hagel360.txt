USDA AWARDS $589,700 TO NEBRASKA IN ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY INCENTIVES
  Sen. Chuck Hagel (R-NE) announced today that the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) will award Nebraska $589,700 in additional funds for the Environmental Quality Incentives Program (EQIP).  
 This funding is in addition to $750,000 Nebraska received earlier this month. 
   "This award is good news for Nebraska. The additional funds will assist producers with ground and surface water conservation, animal feeding operations, and general conservation practices. Nebraska's producers know the importance of practicing good stewardship, and these funds will allow them to continue that practice," Hagel said. 
   EQIP provides a voluntary conservation program for farmers and ranchers that promotes agricultural production and environmental quality. EQIP offers financial and technical help to assist eligible participants install or implement structural and management practices on eligible agricultural land. 
   The awards recognize states that have achieved the greatest efficiency in the program through cost-share dollars and other management practices. Other states receiving the incentive award include: Arkansas, Indiana, Iowa, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri, New York, North Carolina, Ohio, Pennsylvania, South Dakota and Wisconsin 
   Additional information can be found at www.nrcs.usda.gov.
