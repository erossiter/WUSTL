     Durbin Pushes for Action on Russian Arms Sales to Sudan                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          6 26 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Pushes for Action on Russian Arms Sales to Sudan                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Tuesday  June 26  2007       United States Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  sent a letter to the President late yesterday  urging him to confront Russian President  Vladimir Putin  about Russian arms sales to the government of Sudan when Putin visits Bush in Maine on July 1st  Russia  a permanent member of the UN Security Council is transferring arms to the Sudanese government in violation of the UN arms embargo currently in place  Joining Durbin in sending this bipartisan letter to the White House were Senators Brownback  Feingold and Smith   President Putin is promising to promote peace while profiting from violence said Durbin  In recent years  and in direct violation of the UN embargo  Russia exported over  34 million in equipment and arms sales to the Sudanese government used to launch attacks on Darfur  Next week  the President has not only an opportunity  but a responsibility  to call on Putin to immediately reverse his nations  actions and end arms sales to Sudan   Amnesty International recently issued a report on continued arms transfers to Sudan and singles out China and Russia as the two leading suppliers  According to the report  Russia has exported over  21 million in aircraft and equipment and over  13 million in helicopters and parts  to the Sudanese government which in turn uses those supplies to launch attacks in Darfur   The ongoing humanitarian crisis in Darfur has resulted in mass killings  rape  torture  and the torching of homes and entire villages  More than 200 000 people have been killed  2 5 million displaced  and a mounting refugee crisis is spilling into neighboring countries  Despite growing global attention and pressure to stem the violence  the Government of Sudan has been resistant to ending the crisis   This is the fourth letter Durbin has sent to the President this year regarding the genocide in Darfur  Durbin  Chairman of the Subcommittee on Human Rights and the Law  has stressed the need to do more to address the genocide in Darfur  Durbin speaks regularly on the floor in an effort to bring to light some of the atrocities coming from Sudan and has introduced three bills in this congress that will put added pressure on the Sudanese government to end the genocide
