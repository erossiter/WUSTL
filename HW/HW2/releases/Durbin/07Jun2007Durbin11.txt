     Durbin Co Sponsors Minority Health Improvement And Health Disparity Elimination Act Of 2007                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      6 7 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Co Sponsors Minority Health Improvement And Health Disparity Elimination Act Of 2007                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Thursday  June 7  2007   CHICAGO   IL   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today announced that he is co sponsoring the Minority Health Improvement and Health Disparity Act of 2007  The legislation would authorize roughly  500 million to improve the health and health care of racial and ethnic minority and other health disparity populations  The legislation  introduced today by Sen  Edward Kennedy  D MA  and Sen  Thad Cochran  R MS   is also co sponsored by Sen  Barack Obama  D IL   Sen  Hillary Clinton  D NY   Sen  Jeff Bingaman  D NM  and Sen  Sherrod Brown  D OH   We need to improve the way the federal government addresses health disparities and work to eliminate them  said Durbin  All Americans deserve access to quality health care  Racial and ethnic minorities make up approximately one third of the U S  population but disproportionately comprise 52 percent of the uninsured and suffer a greater burden of illness and death than the white population  The Minority Health Improvement and Health Disparity Elimination Act proposes a comprehensive approach to address health disparities  It provides grants to communities to increase public awareness about access to health care and disease prevention  The bill also reauthorizes the National Center on Minority Health and Health Disparities at the National Institutes of Health and strengthens its role in coordinating and planning minority health and health disparity research  Studies have shown that minority health professionals are more likely to care for patients from minority and disadvantaged backgrounds and to provide culturally sensitive care  so creating a diverse healthcare workforce is essential for a healthy America  The bill would help achieve that goal by reauthorizing the programs that help schools recruit and retain minority students and students from disadvantaged backgrounds like the Title VII health workforce diversity  Centers of Excellence and Health Careers Opportunity programs
