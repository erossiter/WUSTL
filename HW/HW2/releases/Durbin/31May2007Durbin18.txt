     Durbin Announces Nearly  5 5 Million in FEMA Grants for Illinois                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 5 31 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Announces Nearly  5 5 Million in FEMA Grants for Illinois                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Thursday  May 31  2007   WASHINGTON   DC   U S  Sen  Dick Durbin  D IL  announced today that the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA   part of the Department of Homeland Security  DHS   has awarded  5 464 291 in grants to counties in Southwestern and Central  Illinois  These grants were awarded in response to a severe winter storm involving freezing rain  heavy snow and high winds that caused extensive property damage and disrupted public services throughout Illinois in December of 2006  The severe winter storm caused a build up of ice on power lines and trees  which resulted in area power outages that affected customers and damaged power lines  power poles and transformers  According to FEMA  funding is obligated directly to the State for the projects  It is the States responsibility to ensure that eligible sub grantees receive these awards  Following the States review process and upon receipt of appropriate documentation  they will provide funds to the sub grantees on a reimbursable basis  The projects are as follows   Sangamon County   1 519 484 87 in funding for City Water  Light   Power  Workers had to replace approximately seven miles of power line  24 power poles  19 cross arms and 82 streetlights with the total project cost at  2 025 979 83    Dewitt  Logan  Macon  McLean  Piatt and Woodford Counties   1 346 758 82 in funding for Corn Belt Energy Cooperative  Damage included more than 2 000 line breaks  and workers had to replace approximately 15 000 linear feet of power line and 486 power poles with the total project cost at  1 795 678 42    Jersey  Macoupin and Montgomery Counties   1 329 297 74 in funding for MJM Electric Cooperative  Workers had to replace nearly 25 000 linear feet of power line and 189 power poles with total project cost at  1 772 396 98     Bond  Fayette  Macoupin  Madison and St  Clair Counties   1 268 749 88 in funding for Southwestern Electric Cooperative  Workers had to replace nearly 30 000 linear feet of power line  118 power poles and five transformers with the total project cost at  1 691 666 50
