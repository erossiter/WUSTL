     Durbin  Emanuel Continue Commitment To Protecting The Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                11 30 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin  Emanuel Continue Commitment To Protecting The Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Friday  November 30  2007       Continuing their commitment to protecting the environmental health of the Great Lakes  U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  and U S  Representative Rahm Emanuel  D IL  called on BP Products North America Inc  to explain the circumstances surrounding allegations that the company violated the Clean Air Act requirements at its Whiting  Indiana Refinery  Durbin  Emanuel and other members of the Illinois Congressional Delegation have been working with BP since July when they urged the company to reconsider its decision to dump more ammonia and sludge into Lake Michigan  We are writing today to continue the dialogue and inquire about what BP is doing to address clean air issues in the region  the Illinois members wrote  We are concerned that these alleged violations are at odds with your companys commitment to preserving the environment  and would like to better understand the circumstances surrounding the violations and what plans BP has to bring the Whiting refinery into compliance As we have previously discussed  economic development and environmental stewardship can and should go hand in hand    The following is the text of the letter  November 30  2007 Mr  Robert A  Malone  President  BP America  501 Westlake Park Blvd  Houston  TX 77079 2604 Dear Mr  Malone  In the past months we have begun a dialogue on the urgent need to improve Lake Michigans water quality  We appreciate your commitment to this important issue and the role that BP can play in improving the environmental health of this great treasure  We are writing today to continue the dialogue and inquire about what BP is doing to address clean air issues in the region  The EPA informed us yesterday of alleged Clean Air Act violations at the Whiting refinery that have led to significant increases in emissions of dangerous air pollutants  There is a clear link between clean air and clean water  We are concerned that these alleged violations are at odds with your companys commitment to preserving the environment  and would like to better understand the circumstances surrounding the violations and what plans BP has to bring the Whiting refinery into compliance  We all can agree that Lake Michigan and the rest of the Great Lakes need greater environmental stewardship  not an increase in pollution  As we have previously discussed  economic development and environmental stewardship can and should go hand in hand  In the past you have expressed your personal commitment  as well as the commitment of BP  to do all that you can to work to protect Lake Michigan  At our last meeting  you mentioned that you were going to need more time to look at the air quality issues  In light of the EPA objections  we would like to have answers to the questions posed above as soon as possible  We look forward to hearing from you and learning more about BPs efforts to address clean air issues at the Whiting facility  Sincerely  Richard J  Durbin  U S  Senator   Rahm Emanuel  Member of Congress
