     Durbin Addresses Gas Price Crisis And Call For Higher Fuel Economy Standards                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     6 13 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Addresses Gas Price Crisis And Call For Higher Fuel Economy Standards                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Wednesday  June 13  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today continued his drive for better fuel economy in cars and trucks  calling on Congress to support Energy legislation now under consideration by the Senate that includes a bipartisan proposal to raise the average fuel economy for all vehicles  The Ten in Ten Fuel Economy Act raises the average fuel economy standards for all vehicles  including SUVs and sedans  from 25 to 35 miles per gallon by 2017  If this bill were to become law  Durbin said the U S  would realize a huge fuel savings in just a few years  Two years ago  when the Senate last considered energy policy  I introduced legislation that called for higher fuel economy standards  said Durbin  The Senate wasnt ready for that bill  Since then  gasoline prices have risen 45   The Senate should be ready now  I know the American people are  Durbin said that the average fuel economy of our nations cars and trucks has steadily declined since 1985 when the last fuel efficiency requirements ended  In 1975  when American cars were averaging 14 miles a gallon  Congress passed the Corporate Average Fuel Economy  CAFE  law  which required manufacturers to double the fuel economy of their vehicles to nearly 28 miles a gallon in 10 years  The Ten in Ten Fuel Economy Act would require the National Highway Transportation and Safety Administration to increase CAFE standards for all cars  trucks and SUVs by 10 miles per gallon over 10 years  or from 25 to 35 miles per gallon by Model Year 2020  and an additional 4 percent increase per year until 2030  By 2025  if fully enacted  the bills fuel economy increases for cars and light duty trucks would  Save 2 1 million barrels of oil per day  nearly the amount of oil imported today from the Persian Gulf    Achieve an 18 percent reduction of carbon dioxide emissions from anticipated levels  or the equivalent of taking 60 million cars off the road in one year      Save consumers a net  70 billion in fuel costs  based on a  3 12 gas price
