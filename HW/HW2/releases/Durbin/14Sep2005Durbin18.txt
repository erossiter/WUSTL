    9 14 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN   KAISER STUDY SHOWS NO END IN SIGHT FOR RISING COST OF HEALTH CARE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Wednesday  September 14  2005    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today called the results of the Kaiser Family Foundation annual survey of health benefits provided by employers    a national tragedy      The survey released today shows that health insurance premiums increased by more than 9 percent in 2005     an increase that is more than three times the growth in worker   s earnings and two and a half times the rate of inflation   Since 2000  health insurance premiums have gone up 73 percent        Health care is a vitally important issue for businesses and working families across the nation     Durbin said      I hear stories of people struggling to pay for their health insurance every time I go home to Illinois   It is time for Congress to take action     it has been more than a decade since lawmakers have made any effort to lower the cost of health care           I urge my colleagues to work with me to not let another year pass without addressing this problem     said Durbin      We cannot escape the reality that health insurance is now prohibitively expensive for caring employers who want to offer health care coverage for their employers        Senator Durbin introduced legislation earlier this year to help small businesses provide quality  affordable health insurance to their employees   The Small Employers Health Benefits Act  S  637  is modeled after the successful federal employee health insurance program and has the endorsement of many major organizations including the American Medical Association and the National Association of Women Business Owners
