     DURBIN SEEKS DROUGHT RELIEF                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      9 8 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN SEEKS DROUGHT RELIEF                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Thursday  September 8  2005    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today introduced legislation to provide critical relief for farmers whose crops have suffered in one of the worst droughts in a century     The Durbin bill would extend last year   s disaster relief bill for this crop year  allowing farmers in the affected states to receive crop disaster payments  livestock compensation payments and orchard tree assistance payments from the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA    Senators Barack Obama  D IL   Kit Bond  R MO   and Jim Talent  R MO  are also cosponsors of the drought relief bill        This year  farmers across the Midwest have struggled with a terrible drought     said Durbin      In some parts of Illinois  this has been the driest season in 105 years   As of this month  67 percent of Illinois corn was in poor or very poor condition           The aftereffects of Hurricane Katrina now make a bad situation even worse     weakening the capacity of the Port of New Orleans and increasing shipping costs and energy costs for farmers nationwide     Durbin said        We must take action to ensure that farmers can stay in business     said Durbin     Most Illinois farms run by are families who depend on farming for their livelihood   This year they need our help   This simple  bipartisan legislation will extend last year   s emergency designation for farms affected by this year   s drought        The Durbin bill is a one year relief bill  extending several federal aid options to farmers and ranchers in the affected states   Crop disaster payments compensate farmers for lost crops due to natural disasters like drought  the livestock compensation program covers livestock loss and increased grazing costs  and the tree assistance program helps replant trees  bushes and vines that have been damaged or destroyed
