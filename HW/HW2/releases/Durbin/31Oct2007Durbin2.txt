     Durbin Calls on Federal Government to Take an Active Role in Reducing Community and Healthcare Associated Infections                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             10 31 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Calls on Federal Government to Take an Active Role in Reducing Community and Healthcare Associated Infections                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Wednesday  October 31  2007   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today introduced legislation to improve the prevention  detection and treatment of community and healthcare associated infectionsparticularly antibiotic resistant bacteria such as MRSA  Durbins initiative  the Community and Healthcare Associated Infections Reduction  CHAIR  Act of 2007  follows several confirmed cases of MRSA infections involving students in the Chicagoland area over the last few weeks  The bill is co sponsored by Senators Barack Obama  D IL  and Chuck Schumer  D NY   This legislation builds on what hospitals are already doing and what infectious disease experts and government agencies agree is critical to reducing the emergence of these infections  said Durbin  We have a national responsibility to improve the prevention  detection and treatment of community and healthcare associated infections  Durbins legislation addresses key needs with five specific initiatives  1  Need  Better Data  Durbin bill   Hospitals will be required to report infection rates    Right now reporting is voluntary for hospitals  so we just dont have very good data   2  Need  Comprehensive infection control programs Durbin bill   Calls for a report on best practices to be done by AHRQ and result in new infection control standards    Calls on CMS to ensure that plans hospitals submit adhere to the new infection control standards created by AHRQ    Part of each hospitals quality control plan will address the prevention and management of infectious disease  specifically drug resistant infections  Potentially  the infection control program could include     Point person or committee in the hospital that oversees infection control Hand washing regulations Standards for labwork Staff training and education on infection prevention and control System to track infected patients Protocol for staff that have been exposed Communication between healthcare facilities   3  Need  Help prevent infections by rewarding hospitals that succeed  Durbin bill   Calls for a feasibility study of a financial reward for hospitals that lower the rate of infections   4  Need  Greater coordination of and greater emphasis on research at the federal level  Durbin bill   Creates an interagency working group to discuss and coordinate a comprehensive federal effort to reduce and prevent infections  The interagency group will consist of representation from each federal agency that has jurisdiction over  or is affected by  infections   including CMS  CDC  HRSA  AHRQ  FDA and USDA    Authorizing funding at the National Institutes of Health for the establishment of new research programs  or enhancement of existing research programs  that focus on CHAI   5  Need  Public education on the issue Durbin bill   Creates a grant program for states who carry out a public awareness campaign to educate and increase awareness of reducing and preventing CHAI  especially in schools
