     Durbin  Senate Appropriations Committee Approves  25 8 Million to Prevent Spread of Emerald Ash Borer                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            7 19 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin  Senate Appropriations Committee Approves  25 8 Million to Prevent Spread of Emerald Ash Borer                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Thursday  July 19  2007    WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  said that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved  25 8 million   2 million for the state of Illinois  to combat and prevent the spread of the Emerald Ash Borer  which was recently found in DuPage County  This funding represents a significant increase from the  16 3 million    1 5 million for Illinois   approved by the Senate Appropriations Committee in last years Agricultural Appropriations bill    The discovery of the Emerald Ash Borer in DuPage County is troubling news  Illinois has more than 131 million ash trees  with about 20 percent of the trees in the Chicago area  said Durbin  The  2 million approved by the Senate committee will help Illinois officials develop a plan to meet this challenge and avert a potentially devastating situation  In the past few years  this insect has killed tens of millions of trees throughout the Midwest  and we need continue efforts to get ahead of this infestation    A destructive insect known as the Emerald Ash Borer  EAB  was recently discovered in DuPage County  Illinois  EAB is a bright green beetle that kills trees by burrowing into their bark and destroying the trees ability to bring water from the roots to upper branches  Infected trees usually begin to die within two to three years  Under todays bill  the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved  25 8 million nationwide to be set aside for activities related to EAB  Senator Durbin wrote a letter to the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Agriculture that garnered 24 cosigners in an effort to increase the funding over fiscal year 2007 levels    On June 27  Durbin introduced bipartisan legislation to help municipalities defray the costs of performing Emerald Ash Borer management activities normally performed by the federal government  The Emerald Ash Borer Municipality Assistance Act of 2007  cosponsored by Senator Barack Obama  D IL  and others will create a low interest revolving loan fund for communities for the purchase of capital equipment and replacement trees to perform duties on municipal government land within quarantine areas  Communities would have a 20 year window to repay the loan  In addition  the bill would allow states to contract with local units of government to perform EAB duties    On June 13th of 2006  immediately following the first sighting of the ash borer in Illinois  Durbin called on the USDA to develop a plan to curb the spread of this pest  Later that month  Durbin helped secure  1 5 million to combat and prevent the spread of the Emerald Ash Borer  Durbin also sent a letter late last summer urging that certain affected locations in Illinois be declared federal emergency quarantine areas so that they can apply for low interest emergency loans to help eradicate the Emerald Ash Borer  Recently  Durbin announced a  100 000 USDA Forest Service grant to the Illinois Emerald Ash Borer Wood Utilization Team    The Emerald Ash Borer was first sighted in 2002 in Michigan  Forestry experts estimate that the Emerald Ash Borer has been responsible for the devastation of than 12 million ash trees in Michigan  Ohio  and Indiana
