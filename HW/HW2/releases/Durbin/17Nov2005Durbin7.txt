     DURBIN SAYS SENATE MUST PLACE HEALTH OF CHILDREN AHEAD OF INCREASING WEALTH OF RICHEST AMERICANS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 11 17 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN SAYS SENATE MUST PLACE HEALTH OF CHILDREN AHEAD OF INCREASING WEALTH OF RICHEST AMERICANS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Thursday  November 17  2005           U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  said it is a moral priority for Congress to make health care accessible to the more than nine million uninsured children in America       Durbin introduced an amendment yesterday that called on the Senate to oppose any extension of the capital gains and dividend tax cuts until Congress has taken steps to ensure that all children in America have access to affordable  quality health insurance   The amendment to the Tax Reconciliation bill was defeated  but Durbin vowed to fight for its passage as an amendment on other bills          There are nine million children without health insurance  including more than 250 000 children in Illinois    Most of these children come from working class families who were forced to give up their health insurance when they couldn   t afford the monthly premiums  The Senate should insist that adequate health insurance for these kids is a higher priority than any more tax breaks for millionaires     Durbin said     It is time to put the health of children ahead of the wealth of the richest Americans        Durbin noted that the majority of the benefits of the capital gains and dividend tax cuts go to households with incomes over  1 million       Durbin applauded the state of Illinois for covering uninsured children   Governor Blagojevich signed a bill this week that created the All Kids program which will offer Illinois    uninsured children comprehensive health care that includes doctor   s visits  hospital stays  prescription drugs  vision care  dental care and medical devices like eyeglasses and asthma inhalers       Durbin   s amendment would have provided grants to states and community and non profit organizations to facilitate the enrollment of the 6 8 million children who are currently eligible for SCHIP but not enrolled
