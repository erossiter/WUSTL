     Durbin Statement on the Nomination of Chicago Judge Mark Filip for U S  Deputy Attorney General                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  11 15 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Statement on the Nomination of Chicago Judge Mark Filip for U S  Deputy Attorney General                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Thursday  November 15  2007    Washington  DC   The Senate Assistant Majority Leader Dick Durbin  D IL  today released the following statement on the upcoming nomination of U S  District Judge Mark Filip of Chicago who the White House has announced will be nominated as Deputy Attorney General at the U S  Department of Justice Mark Filip has earned an excellent reputation as a district court judge in the Northern District of Illinois  In a survey of district court judges that was released last year by a Chicago public interest bar association  Judge Filip earned top ratings for his temperament  independence  and legal ability  Those qualities will serve him well if he is confirmed to be the Deputy Attorney General  I look forward to learning more about him and hearing his views on significant issues such as safeguarding the fundamental rights of all Americans while fighting a smarter  more effective war on terrorism  Senator Durbin has served on the Senate Judiciary Committee for 8 years  The Judiciary Committee is charged with holding confirmation hearings for the top positions at the Department of Justice
