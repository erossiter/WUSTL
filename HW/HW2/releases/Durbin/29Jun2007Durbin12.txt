     Foreign Operations Programs Approved by Senate Appropriations Committee                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          6 29 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Foreign Operations Programs Approved by Senate Appropriations Committee                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Friday  June 29  2007   Washington  DC    U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee  of which he is a member  has approved funding and language for several international programs included in the Foreign Operations spending bill  The Fiscal Year  FY  2008 appropriations bill for Foreign Operations includes the following programs that Durbin requested  International Tuberculosis  TB  Control  including Drug Resistant TB   20 million increase from FY07 and the Presidents request  Especially in light of rising incidences of multi drug resistant  MDR  and extremely drug resistant  XDR  TB    African Health Capacity  Report language added to emphasize the importance of training and maintaining health care workers in developing countries  Senator Durbin has also introduced the African Health Capacity Investment Act of 2007 to further assist health care workers in sub Saharan Africa address HIV AIDS  maternal and child mortality  and other major diseases    HIV AIDS and Nutrition  Report language included to fulfill the intent clearly stated in the United States Leadership Against HIV AIDS  Tuberculosis and Malaria Act of 2003 regarding the importance of integrating food and nutritional support with HIV AIDS activities and to improve health outcomes    Senator Paul Simon Water for the Poor   100 million increase from FY07 for the Development Assistance Account for safe drinking water and sanitation supply projects  Also the inclusion of bill language strengthening adherence to the Water for the Poor Act of 2005 for clean water and water management assistance funds    Girls Empowerment  A  12 million set aside for the empowerment of girls in Afghanistan  To further programs which improve access of girls in developing countries to healthcare  education and training  and protection  including from child marriage  human trafficking  gender based violence  HIV AIDS  and other sexually transmitted diseases     GROWTH Incentive Fund  Report language included to support the establishment of the GROWTH Fund  which is intended to enhance economic opportunities for very poor  poor and low income women in developing countries  Programs supported by the Fund should increase women owned enterprise development  property rights for women  womens access to financial services  women in organizational leadership positions  womens ability to benefit from global trade  and improve womens employment benefits and conditions    International Conservation Global Environmental Facility  GEF    26 million increase from FY07 for natural resource management which is a critical step in the reduction of poverty for millions in developing countries  sustainable economic growth requires sustainable resources management    Microfinance Programs   50 million increase from FY07 and Presidents request for microfinance programs  Microfinance leader Mohamed Yunus won the Nobel Peace Prize last year  indicating the strong international support for such programs  Also report language was included to maximize assistance to poor clients    Maternal and Child Mortality   60 million increase to the top line to fund efforts to decrease maternal death rates  Postpartum hemorrhage  PPH  is the leading cause of maternal mortality  Increasing resources in this area  particularly in countries with large populations and high rates of maternal mortality could make a major difference in maternal death rates    Foreign Military Financing  FMF  Israel   2 4 billion in funding approved by committee for Foreign Military Financing which refers to congressionally appropriated grants given to foreign governments to finance the purchase of American made weapons  services and training    Jerusalem YMCA  The bill included language maintaining the ongoing support of the Jerusalem YMCA  Founded in 1878  the Jerusalem YMCA provides services to Jews  Muslims and Christians including bi lingual day care center and elder programs    International Trust Fund for Demining and Mine Victims Assistance  ITF    1 million increase in funding  The ITF was established by the Government of the Republic of Slovenia in March 1998 with the aim of helping Bosnia and Herzegovina in its mine clearance and mine victim assistance efforts
