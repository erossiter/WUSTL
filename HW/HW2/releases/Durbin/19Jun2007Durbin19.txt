     Durbin Bill to Name Post Office in Fairbury for Dr  Francis Townshend Clears Final Congressional Hurdle                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          6 19 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Bill to Name Post Office in Fairbury for Dr  Francis Townshend Clears Final Congressional Hurdle                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Tuesday  June 19  2007   WASHINGTON  DC   Legislation written by U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  and Representative Tim Johnson  D IL  to name the Post Office located at 127 East Locust Street in Fairbury  Illinois as the Dr  Francis Townsend Post Office Building was approved by the House of Representatives today  Dr  Townsend created the Townsend old age revolving pension plan and led a social movement advocating increased benefits for the elderly during the 1930s  Durbin s legislation passed the Senate on May 23  2007  Dr  Francis Townsend was an innovator and original thinker  said Durbin  His efforts to fight poverty set the stage for Roosevelts New Deal social security programs during the Great Depression  This year is the 150th anniversary of Fairbury  Illinois  Naming this post office after one of its most famous citizens is a fitting celebration of both Townsends contribution to the city and this important milestone  Francis Townsend grew up in Fairbury  Illinois  the son of a farmer  He became a physician and served in the Army Medical Corps during World War I  After retiring from medicine  Townsend developed an old age pension plan for seniors  The Townsend Plan created a federal pension of  200 a month paid to every citizen sixty and older on the condition that the pensioner spend the entire sum within 30 days in order to stimulate the economy  By 1935  his leadership in promoting his plan had created a social movement of over 2 million members in 7 000 clubs across the country  It is likely that Townsends efforts contributed to an expedited passage of President Franklin D  Roosevelts Social Security Act  a major New Deal initiative  Durbin was joined in his efforts by Senator Barack Obama  D IL   He also worked closely with the Office of Mayor Robert Walter  Jr  in Fairbury on the post office renaming measure  The bill now goes to the President for his signature
