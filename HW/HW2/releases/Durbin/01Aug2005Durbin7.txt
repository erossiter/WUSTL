    8 1 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN  OBAMA AND EVANS ANNOUNCE  25 MILLION IN FEDERAL FUNDING FOR ROUTE 51 EXPANSION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Monday  August 1  2005   Highway to be Expanded From Decatur to Centralia     MACON  IL      U S  Senators Dick Durbin  D IL  and Barack Obama  D IL  and U S  Representative Lane Evans today announced  25 million in federal funding for the expansion of Rt  51 into a complete four lane highway from Decatur to Centralia   The project  funded through the TEA 3 bill recently passed by Congress  is widely expected to foster economic growth throughout Central Illinois             We   ve been working toward this goal for a long  long while     Durbin said      The expansion of Route 51 is going to increase roadway capacity  improve safety and enhance economic development     and we might just please a few farmers along the way   This funding will mean economic growth for the communities along US 51   A modern highway design will divert truck traffic from residential areas  speed shipments from producers and manufacturers to final markets  and save time for area residents commuting to work             Securing this funding for the expansion of Route 51 is the result of the Illinois delegation working together in a bipartisan manner to put the interests of the people of Illinois first     Obama said     I am proud that I was able to help write this bill and work with my colleagues to make ensure that Illinois will continue to have safe and efficient roads  rails and bridges              The new Transportation Bill is a jobs bill     Evans said      It will provide an important boost to job creation and spur economic opportunities for our state and nation   The legislation will also help us address the problems of congestion  traffic safety and crumbling roads and bridges   It will make our roads and highways safer for the traveling public   I was pleased to work with area citizens  state officials  and the congressional delegation to increase the flow of transportation funds to Illinois          The Rt  51 project calls for the highway to be extended from Decatur to Centralia   The Decatur to Pana leg of the expressway corridor    where truck traffic is heavy and accidents are all too frequent    is especially critical to the region   Rt  51 currently extends over 400 miles from South Beloit  at the Illinois Wisconsin border  to Cairo in Southern Illinois   All but 75 miles of the highway  from Moweaqua to Centralia  is a four lane structure         Durbin has consistently supported the expansion project  securing  2 million in fiscal year 2004 for the widening of Rt  51 south from Mowequa to Assumption and  500 000 in fiscal year 2005 for improvements to Rt  51 in Christian and Shelby Counties
