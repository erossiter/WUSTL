     DURBIN RESPONSE TO STATE OF THE UNION ADDRESS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    1 31 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN RESPONSE TO STATE OF THE UNION ADDRESS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Tuesday  January 31  2006    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today released the following statement on this evening   s State of the Union Address        I think Americans were looking for a new direction and new ideas from the President  and sadly there was very little of either in the State of the Union Address tonight           While I did not agree with everything in the President   s speech tonight  I am glad that he addressed three key issues     health care  energy and education           The White House told us before the speech that the President was going to spend significant time addressing the problem of health care  Instead  the ideas we heard tonight were mostly recycled concepts like health savings accounts  Health savings accounts offer high costs  high risks and low benefits to consumers           The energy initiative the President put forward tonight is important  The President abandoned the energy bill which he signed just a months ago    and he should have  it was a bad bill  He has announced that the nation must move away from our dependence on foreign oil  That is a worthy bipartisan goal and I support it           I thought his education initiative putting more math and science teachers in the classroom was exactly the right thing to do           We need to work closely together to find common ground in these three areas
