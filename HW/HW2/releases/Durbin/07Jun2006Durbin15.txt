     DURBIN WORKS TO ENSURE ILLINOIS SENIORS ENROLLED IN MEDICARE PART D HAVE ACCESS TO THEIR LOCAL PHARMACIES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        6 7 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN WORKS TO ENSURE ILLINOIS SENIORS ENROLLED IN MEDICARE PART D HAVE ACCESS TO THEIR LOCAL PHARMACIES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Wednesday  June 7  2006   Washington  D C       U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  joined a bipartisan group of senators in calling on the U S  Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  to conduct a review to ensure that rural Medicare Part D beneficiaries in Illinois and across the country are assured access to local community pharmacies     In a bipartisan letter to the HHS Inspector General Daniel R  Levinson  Durbin and more than 30 senators wrote     Medicare beneficiaries in rural areas often have only one pharmacy available to them  In many instances  the next closest pharmacy is ten  if not twenty  miles away  We know that the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services  CMS  analyzed the pharmacy networks submitted by prescription drug plans  We are concerned  however  by reports that even though plans    networks meet the standards  some beneficiaries in rural areas still face difficulties in using their prescription drug benefit at a conveniently located pharmacy        Durbin said     Many seniors in Illinois have relied on their community pharmacists for years  but now they are forced to travel out of town or across the county to get their prescriptions filled   Seniors should not be expected to drop their local pharmacists as a price of enrollment in Medicare Part D   That is an unacceptable price to pay        With more than 25 000 independent community pharmacies across the nation  the senators also raised concerns about the level at which these local pharmacists are being reimbursed under Medicare Part D      Pharmacists have informed us that in many cases  reimbursements fall well below their costs  which will undermine the long term viability of local pharmacies and the MMA goal of ensuring beneficiaries    access to them     the senators wrote
