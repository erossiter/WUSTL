    2 6 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN MEETS WITH HEAD OF SMALL BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION  STEVEN PRESTON OF DOWNERS GROVE  IL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Tuesday  February 6  2007   WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today met with Steven Preston  Administrator of the Small Business Administration  SBA  and former Executive Vice President of Service Master  in Downers Grove  IL   Durbin requested today s meeting with Mr  Preston to discuss how SBA can improve loans for small business entrepreneurs and to discuss recent improvements to the disaster loan program     Dr  Muhammad Yunus won the Nobel Peace Prize last fall for providing the world with an outstanding model of microloaning  We can now use that model to help thousands of Americans who are trying to start small businesses   said Durbin    Today  I stressed to Mr  Preston that we need to fund microloan programs in the federal budget instead of placing the burden on those looking for financial assistance     In the budget presented to Congress yesterday  the President proposed cutting funding for the SBA Microloan Program by  13 million   The SBA Microloan Program provides small loans to new or growing businesses by funneling investment capital through nonprofit community based lenders   The administration has proposed funding the program through higher interest rates on intermediaries which in turn will increase interest rates on the loans themselves  In today s meeting  Durbin raised concerns that higher interest rates could drive potential borrowers away from the process    Durbin and Preston also discussed recent improvements to SBA s disaster loan program    The SBA recently implemented new processes that allow them to process loan requests more efficiently   Preston reported that the SBA is currently close to eliminating the backlog of requests from Hurricanes Katrina and Rita and are in a much better position to deal with future disasters    In 2006  SBA s Illinois District Office assisted 4 381 businesses in obtaining  688 million in general business loans   These loans were made through fixed asset loan programs  Working through nearly 200 local lenders  borrowers can obtain SBA guarantees on their loans  The maximum loan guarantee for one business or individual is  1 5 million  Proceeds can be used for working capital and to purchase machinery and equipment  real property  and inventory  Guaranteed loans may also be used to purchase an existing business  Terms vary depending upon the use of loan proceeds  Interest rates also vary depending upon the size and term of the loan
