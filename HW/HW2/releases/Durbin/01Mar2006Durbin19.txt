    3 1 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN PRAISES ILLINOIS LAWMAKERS FOR PASSING STATE RENEWABLE FUELS STANDARD                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Wednesday  March 1  2006    WASHINGTON  DC     U S  Sen  Dick Durbin  D IL  today praised Illinois legislators for approving a    tough and responsible    statewide standard for renewable fuels   The Illinois Renewable Fuels Standard bill  SB 2236   which was approved by the Illinois State Senate  will boost ethanol and biodiesel use and production while creating several new programs aimed to accelerate research  investment  and industry expansion          Illinois farmers stand ready and willing to contribute to America   s energy security and independence   Replacing Mideast oil with Midwest ethanol is a winner for everyone but the oil sheiks     said Durbin     When we can use our state s agricultural expertise to reduce our dependence on foreign suppliers  Illinois farmers win and the whole nation benefits          Durbin also praised the sponsors and key supporters in the State Senate  Senator Deanna Demuzio  D Carlinville  Senator John Sullivan  D Rushville  John O  Jones  R Chicago  and Todd Sieben  R Geneseo  saying that without their leadership the bill would never have made it through the Senate       The Illinois Renewable Fuels Standards bill  which now goes to the House for action  would provide  25 million annually over the next two years for everything from ethanol plant expansion and construction to research initiatives at local research institutes   The bill would also establish that by 2008  a minimum of 10 percent of all gasoline used in Illinois be ethanol  with a goal of expanding this to 15 percent by 2012   Finally  it would reduce the current State Sales Tax Incentive for ethanol from its current rate of 20  of the state sales tax to 10  in July and phase it out in 2015       Durbin noted that this legislative success comes on the heels of the recently passed U S  House Senate Energy bill which included the Renewable Fuels Standard  RFS   a provision that creates a nationwide standard for the use of domestically produced renewable fuels  like ethanol  and increases the use of those fuels to 7 5 billion gallons by 2012          Illinois farmers stand ready and willing to contribute to our nation   s energy security and independence  I strongly urge the Illinois House to adopt this measure     said Durbin
