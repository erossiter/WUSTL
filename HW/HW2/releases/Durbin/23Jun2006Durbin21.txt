     DURBIN   SENATE APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE APPROVES  1 5 MILLION TO PREVENT SPREAD OF EMERALD ASH BORER                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            6 23 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN   SENATE APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE APPROVES  1 5 MILLION TO PREVENT SPREAD OF EMERALD ASH BORER                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Friday  June 23  2006    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  said that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved  1 5 million to combat and prevent the spread of the Emerald Ash Borer  which was recently found outside of Chicago        The discovery of the Emerald Ash Borer in Kane County is troubling news   Illinois has more than 131 million ash trees  with about 20 percent of the trees in the Chicago area     said Durbin     The  1 5 million approved by the Senate committee will help Illinois officials develop a plan to meet this challenge and avert a potentially devastating situation   In the past few years  this insect has killed tens of millions of trees throughout the Midwest  and we need to get ahead of this infestation        A destructive insect known as the Emerald Ash Borer  EAB  was discovered in Kane County  Illinois last week   EAB is a bright green beetle that kills trees by burrowing into their bark and destroying the trees    ability to bring water from the roots to upper branches  Infected trees usually begin to die within two to three years     Last week  Senator Durbin called on the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  to develop a plan to curb the spread of this pest and report on their progress within fifteen days   In a letter Durbin sent to the head of USDA   s Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service  he wrote     As you know  time is of the essence   I would appreciate receiving a report within 15 days describing the specific plans APHIS will implement to combat EAB in Northern Illinois and the timelines that will guide the agency   s response   Specifically  I would like to know what resources APHIS will commit to this discovery of emerald ash borer        The Emerald Ash Borer was first sighted in 2002 in Michigan  Forestry experts estimate that the Emerald Ash Borer has been responsible for the devastation of than 12 million ash trees in Michigan  Ohio  and Indiana
