     Durbin Meets with French Ambassador to Urge Immediate Extradition of Hans Peterson                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               9 6 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Meets with French Ambassador to Urge Immediate Extradition of Hans Peterson                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Thursday  September 6  2007         U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today met with the newly appointed French Ambassador  Pierre Vimont  and the French Charg d Affaires  Franois Rivasseau  to urge France to reconsider its decision not to extradite Hans Peterson  an American citizen who reportedly confessed to the 2006 murder of Dr  David Cornbleet in Chicago     Dr  Cornbleet s family  the people of Illinois  and the United States criminal justice system deserve to see Hans Peterson face trial in the United States  said Durbin  The French government must exercise the discretion it has under our extradition treaty  Senator Obama and I will do everything in our power to prevent Peterson from using French law to escape justice in the US     Earlier this month  Peterson turned himself in to French authorities in St  Martin and reportedly confessed to the brutal murder of Dr  Cornbleet  a Chicago dermatologist  Peterson is currently in French custody in Guadeloupe  According to reports  Peterson  whose mother is French  fled to French territory after allegedly committing the murder in an effort to avoid criminal prosecution in the United States  The U S  Department of Justice Office of International Affairs has issued a formal request to the Government of France for Peterson s extradition     On August 23  France announced its decision not to extradite Peterson  While the extradition treaty between the United States and France says that there is no obligation upon the requested state to grant the extradition of a person who is a national of the requested state  this treaty does not preclude France from exercising its discretion to extradite one of its nationals to the U S     Last week  Durbin and Senator Barack Obama  D IL  sent a letter to the French Foreign Minister  Bernard Kouchner  urging France to reconsider its decision  On August 20  Durbin and Obama sent a letter to Charg d Affaires Franois Rivasseau at the French Embassy in Washington  DC  urging the Government of France to agree to the United States  request for Petersons extradition to face criminal prosecution for Dr  Cornbleets murder  The Illinois Senators also sent a letter to Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice  requesting that she immediately urge her French counterparts to examine the extradition request and to advise the U S  Embassy in Paris of the importance of this case
