     DURBIN INTRODUCES BIPARTISAN LEGISLATION CALLING FOR U S  POSTAGE STAMP TO PROMOTE PUBLIC AWARENESS OF DOWN SYNDROME                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             3 21 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN INTRODUCES BIPARTISAN LEGISLATION CALLING FOR U S  POSTAGE STAMP TO PROMOTE PUBLIC AWARENESS OF DOWN SYNDROME                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Wednesday  March 21  2007    WASHINGTON  DC    In honor of World Down Syndrome Day  U S  Sen  Dick Durbin  D IL  and Sen  Thad Cochran  R MS  today introduced bipartisan legislation calling on the the U S  Postal Service to issue a commemorative stamp that would promote public awareness of Down syndrome   Down Syndrome International has designated March 21st as World Down Syndrome Day to signify the relationship between Down syndrome and the 21st chromosome        About 350 000 people in the United States are affected with Down syndrome and the condition impacts millions more parents  siblings  and friends   Durbin said    The United States Postal Service has raised awareness for many worthy causes  We hope this legislation will move rapidly to a Senate vote  sending a strong message that the time has come to issue a stamp encouraging greater understanding and acceptance of people with this common genetic condition        Down syndrome is a genetic condition that causes delays in physical and intellectual development   Those who suffer from the condition have an extra portion of the 21st chromosome present in their cells  Chromosomes are thread like structures composed of DNA and other proteins  They are present in every cell of the body and carry the genetic information needed for that cell to develop  Human cells normally have 46 chromosomes arranged in 23 pairs       Durbin noted that Down syndrome affects people across all ethnic and economic levels and occurs in one out of every 733 live births   About 350 000 people in the United States have Down syndrome and it occurs in all ethnic groups and among all economic classes  Some experts predict that the number of people living with Down syndrome will double in the next decade  further increasing the need for public education and awareness       Durbin has consistently advocated greater awareness for Down syndrome   In October of 2003  Senator Durbin and bipartisan group of senators introduced legislation to commemorate Down syndrome Awareness Month   Last year  Senator Durbin co sponsored legislation to improve the support and information that health officials provide families that have received a diagnosis of Down syndrome or who are considering adopting a baby with Down syndrome       The legislation is endorsed by the National Down Syndrome Society  the Arc of the United States  the National Association for Down Syndrome  and the Central Illinois Down Syndrome Organization
