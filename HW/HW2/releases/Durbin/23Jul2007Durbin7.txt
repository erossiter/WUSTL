     Durbin Asks for Hold on BP Air Permit Variance                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   7 23 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Asks for Hold on BP Air Permit Variance                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Monday  July 23  2007       U S  Senator Dick Durbin today sent a letter to the Great Lakes Program Manager at the U S  Environmental Protection Agency  EPA   Region 5 asking for a hold to be placed on the air permit variance for the British Petroleum  BP  Whiting refinery until the public has a chance to weigh in  The air permit variance may allow the refinery to emit more tiny particles into the air than state law normally allows  The permit is scheduled to take effect today  Monday  July 23 before the public hearing on August 9th  which was scheduled to give attendees the opportunity to express their views     Today s actions follow Durbin s recent work with Congressman Rahm Emanuel  D IL  to keep BP from dumping into Lake Michigan  On July 20  Durbin sent at letter to the President of BP requesting a meeting to discuss the recently approved a National Pollution Discharge Elimination System  NPDES  permit for the BP Whiting refinery which would allow an increase in ammonia and sludge pollution discharges into Lake Michigan by 54 percent and 35 percent respectively  On July 18  Durbin and Emanuel sent a letter to Tony Hayward  Chief Executive Officer of BP calling on him to reconsider the decision to increase dumping into Lake Michigan     On July 16  Senator Durbin sent a letter to Benjamin Grumbles  Assistant Administrator for the Office of Water at the Environmental Protection Agency  EPA  to express his strong opposition to a permit that will allow BPs Whiting refinery in Indiana to discharge more pollution into Lake Michigan  Also on July 16  Durbin and Emanuel sent a letter to the EPA Administrator  Stephen L  Johnson expressing strong concern over the issuance of the permit and requesting for a hold to be placed on the permit until certain assurances have been met    The following is the text of the letter    July 23  2007   Mary A  Gade  Regional Administrator  Great Lakes National Program Manager US Environmental Protection Agency  Region 5  77 W  Jackson Boulevard       Dear Regional Administrator Gade    I write to request a hold on the air permit variance approved by the Indiana Department of Environmental Management  IDEM  for the BPs Whiting refinery facility  pending public input    The air permit variance approved by the IDEM Commissioners order takes effect Monday  July 23  despite the fact that a public hearing is scheduled for Thursday  August 9  The purpose of the August 9 forum is to hear the views of attendees on the proposed revisions to the implementation plan    The public should have a full understanding of BPs request for the variance and an opportunity to make an informed objection to the Commissioners order  should they wish to  To accommodate public input  I respectfully request that you require IDEM to extend the objection period to BPs air permit variance to 30 days after the public hearing scheduled for August 9     I look forward to hearing from you regarding your decision on this matter      Sincerely     Richard J  Durbin  United States Senator
