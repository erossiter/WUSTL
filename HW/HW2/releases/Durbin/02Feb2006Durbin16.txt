      2 2 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN   S EFFORTS PAYOFF FOR UNINSURED IN ILLNOIS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Thursday  February 2  2006    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  applauded the passage of the State High Risk Pool Funding Extension Act  H R  4519  which passed the Senate late last night   High risk pools provide health insurance options to individuals with medical conditions that make it impossible for them to qualify for health insurance coverage in the regular market    The bill now goes to the President for his signature     Durbin was successful in securing a compromise that guarantees Illinois will receive its fair share of federal grants for the operation of high risk pools   According to the compromise  Illinois will receive  5 4 million   Under the original bill  Illinois was scheduled to receive just  3 8 million in federal funds        This is a victory for uninsured Illinoisans who rely on this state federal program for their health insurance     Durbin said      I could not stand idly by when the earlier bill would have resulted in hundreds of Illinoisans losing their health insurance   The Senate took an important step forward last night by passing a formula that is more fair to Illinois and other large states        Durbin noted that he opposed the original bill because it    cut funding to states with large numbers of uninsured people  such as Illinois  Colorado  Indiana  Maryland and Oklahoma  and increased funding to states with a smaller number of uninsured Americans   There is no reason a state like Illinois  which has 1 8 million people lacking access to health insurance  should suffer a 60 percent cut in funding while a state like South Dakota with 90 000 uninsured people sees its funding more than double  The money should follow the people in need  not be determined by politics
