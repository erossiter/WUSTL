STATEMENT FOLLOWING PRESIDENT S VETO OF BILL TO EXPAND FEDERAL FUNDING FOR EMBRYONIC STEM CELL RESEARCH                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Wednesday  July 19  2006    Washington  D C       U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today issued the following statement after President Bush vetoed legislation to expand federal funding for embryonic stem cell research        President Bush may have vetoed the stem cell bill today  but he has not vetoed the hopes of millions of Americans   Those families who wake up every morning to face another day with a deadly disease or a disability will not forget this decision by the President to stand in the way of sound science and medical research           President Bush   s first veto makes him the first president in the history of the United States to restrict medical research and the efforts of science to reduce suffering from disease and disability    Many of us in Congress  along with the vast majority of American families  will continue to fight until the promise of stem cell research is fully realized
