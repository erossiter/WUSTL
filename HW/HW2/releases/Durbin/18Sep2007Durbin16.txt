     Durbin Announces Downstate Air Service to Begin November 4                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       9 18 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Announces Downstate Air Service to Begin November 4                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Tuesday  September 18  2007    WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today announced that Great Lakes Airlines plans to restart service to St  Louis on November 4  2007  Great Lakes Airlines was awarded the Essential Air Service contracts in Marion Herrin  Quincy and Decatur  Illinois after Regions Air  the previous regional airline  discontinued their service to these areas  Customers can begin booking flights on Great Lakes flights from Marion Herrin  Quincy and Decatur on Sunday  September 23  Todays announcement brings a sigh of relief from travelers  businesses and tourists in downstate Illinois who have had to deal with uncertain and disrupted air service for too long  said Durbin  Great Lakes Airlines  American Airlines and the Department of Transportation have worked together with community leaders to mobilize the resources and equipment needed to serve downstate Illinois  I am pleased to see that safe  efficient air service will be up and running again in these downstate communities  The Essential Air Service  EAS  contracts to Quincy  Marion Herrin and Decatur were held by Regions Air which abruptly halted service on March 8th  The two year contract consists of a  3 5 million per year federal subsidy to operate the EAS program that was established by Congress in 1978 to ensure communities with commercial air service before airline deregulation could continue scheduled flights  Without EAS  many rural communities would have no commercial air service at all  and residents of smaller cities would have to travel significant distances for flights  Durbin has worked with American Airlines  Great Lakes Airlines and the Department of Transportation asking that the necessary planes  personnel and equipment are in place so that service between downstate communities and St  Louis Lambert International Airport could resume  On March 19  2007  Durbin sent a letter to the President of American Eagle Airlines  Peter M  Bowler urging that the airline work with Great Lakes to begin service immediately  On April 11  2007  Durbin sent a letter to the CEO of Great Lakes Airline Chuck Howell encouraging Great Lakes Airlines to begin providing scheduled passenger air service as soon as possible  On June 12  2007  Durbin sent letters to the CEO of Great Lakes Airlines  Chuck Howell and the Chairman of the Board of Directors  Doug Voss asking again that the airline work to begin providing scheduled passenger air service from Decatur  Marion Herrin  Quincy  and Springfield  Illinois to St  Louis as soon as possible  On June 14  2007  Durbin met with Transportation Secretary Mary E  Peters urging the Department of Transportation to move forward on several Illinois transportation issues including downstate Illinois air service  OHare modernization  and expanded Amtrak service in Illinois  He followed up with this request in a meeting with the Transportation Secretary on September 11  2007
