AMENDMENT TO DELAY POTENTIAL CUTS IN MEDICAID PAYMENTS TO HOSPITALS NATIONWIDE SUCCESSFULLY ADDDED TO SUPPLEMENTAL SPENDING BILL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Thursday  March 22  2007    WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today successfully added an amendment to the supplemental spending bill that would put a two year moratorium on the Medicaid rule that threatens to take more than  623 million in federal and state funding out of Illinois  Medicaid program and could affect hospitals in as many as 42 states       Senator Durbin offered the amendment during today s Appropriations Committee mark up  where it was accepted with a vote of 18 11  and is now part of the supplemental spending bill that will go to the Senate floor for a debate on Monday   The vote by the full Senate on the supplemental appropriations bill is expected to take place later next week        Without this amendment  Cook County Hospital and other hospitals throughout the state of Illinois could face restricted payments as soon as September under Medicaid  a program that provides basic services to the uninsured and those with limited insurance   said Durbin       On January 18  CMS proposed a rule that would impact more than 57 million Americans and 2 million Illinoisans who rely on the Medicaid program  as well as health care providers who deliver essential services to them   This proposed rule  CMS 2258 P  imposes cost limits for public health care providers and alters the definition of  public  status   Specifically  the proposed rule would limit payments to government safety net providers by capping reimbursement payments  redefining eligible public providers  and imposing new restrictions on non federal funding sources       On  February 15th  Durbin sent a letter to Senate Finance Committee Chairman and Ranking Member  Senator Max Baucus  D MT  and Senator Chuck Grassley  R IA  urging them to help prevent Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services  CMS  from implementing their proposed plan to dramatically cut Medicaid funding   Senator Durbin was joined by 42 other senators from both political parties in sending the letter       The amendment Durbin offered today would be paid for by altering an existing Medicaid program that requires brand name drug makers to provide a 15  rebate to the federal government on any drug it sells through the Medicaid program   Durbin s amendment raises that to 20   a move that is estimated to generate  1 35 billion over 5 years
