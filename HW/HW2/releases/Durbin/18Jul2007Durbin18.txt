     Durbin Introduces Bipartisan Bill to Increase Investment in Biodiesel Education                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  7 18 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Introduces Bipartisan Bill to Increase Investment in Biodiesel Education                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Wednesday  July 18  2007    WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  joined his colleagues  Senators Amy Klobuchar  D MN   Mike Crapo  R ID  and Larry Craig  R ID   earlier this week in introducing a bill to extend funding for the Biodiesel Fuel Education Program for an additional five years  The 2002 Farm Bill provided the program with  1 million per year  this legislation would double funding and provide the program with  2 million annually   Since the The Biodiesel Education Program began in 2004  it has helped increase awareness throughout the industry and among consumers through media outreach  press events and article placement  said Durbin  By doubling our commitment to funding biodiesel eduacation  we can increase public understanding of biodiesel s benefits and make greater strides toward energy independence   The Biodiesel Fuel Education Program was established as part of the energy title of the 2002 Farm Bill  The United States Department of Agriculture would house the National Biodiesel Education Program  The purpose of this program is to increase the awareness and ultimately utilization of biodiesel  It provides educational funding to support increased fuel quality measures  increase acceptance of biodiesel by engine manufacturers  vehicle fleet operators  and the general public   Biodiesel is the only alternative fuel to have fully completed the health effects testing requirements of the Clean Air Act  The use of biodiesel in a conventional diesel engine results in a substantial reduction of unburned hydrocarbons and carbon monoxide  Research also documents the fact that the ozone forming potential of the hydrocarbon emissions of pure biodiesel is nearly 50  less than that of petroleum fuel  Pure biodiesel does not contain sulfur and therefore reduces sulfur dioxide exhaust from diesel engines to virtually zero   Senator Durbin has been a leader in promoting renewable fuels  On June 13  2007  Durbin introduced the Biodiesel Promotion and Quality Assurance Act that would increase biodiesel production nearly three fold over current levels  to 1 25 billion gallons in 2012  Additionally  the Administrator of the EPA would have the ability to set future targets  This proposal will effectively ensure that a portion of the renewable fuel that must be blended under the Renewable Fuel Standard is biodiesel
