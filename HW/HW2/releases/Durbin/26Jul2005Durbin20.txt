     DURBIN EARNS PERFECT SCORE FROM NATIONAL HISPANIC LEADERSHIP GROUP                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               7 26 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN EARNS PERFECT SCORE FROM NATIONAL HISPANIC LEADERSHIP GROUP                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Tuesday  July 26  2005    CHICAGO  IL      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  earned a perfect score of 100 percent on the National Hispanic Leadership Agenda Congressional Scorecard for the 108th Congress   The results  released today  laud Durbin for his support of issues such as education  health care  civil rights  economic mobility and worker   s rights       The group listed the following votes in their analysis      Durbin twice voted against legislation in the 108th Congress that would have severely cut funding for Hispanic education and other programs while voting for a measure to restore and expand funds for Hispanic education programs   Durbin supported an amendment to increase funding for child care assistance in the 108th Congress and he also voted against an amendment that would have taken away each state   s option to provide health care benefits to legal immigrant pregnant women and children   On technological issues  Durbin voted against an Internet access tax   With the Internet being available to just 32 percent of the Latino community compared to 70 percent of Whites  an access tax would have hindered necessary growth    While not included in today   s scorecard  Durbin co sponsored the Next Generation Hispanic Serving Institutions Act in the 109th Congress   The legislation would establish a grant program for HSIs that offer certification or degree programs beyond a bachelor   s degree   Durbin has also fought for funding for migrant Head Start and dropout prevention programs among other education initiatives       In the 108th Congress  Durbin introduced the DREAM Act with Sen  Orrin Hatch  R UT  and plans to reintroduce it in the 109th Congress   The DREAM Act would put undocumented students on the road to legal resident status if they met certain academic and civic standards       Thirteen groups composed the NHLA Congressional Scorecard Committee  including the U S  Hispanic Chamber of Commerce  National Council of LaRaza and National Hispanic Medical Association   The NHLA was founded in 1991 as a nonpartisan coalition of major Hispanic national organization and distinguished Hispanic leaders from across the nation       Democrats earned an average mark of 95 percent on the Congressional Scorecard while their Republican counterparts managed only a score of 34 percent
