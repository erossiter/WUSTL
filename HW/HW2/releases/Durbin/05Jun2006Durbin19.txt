     DURBIN ANNOUNCES  15 MILLION FOR MIDWAY AIRPORT TO IMPROVE RUNWAY SAFETY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         6 5 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN ANNOUNCES  15 MILLION FOR MIDWAY AIRPORT TO IMPROVE RUNWAY SAFETY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Monday  June 5  2006    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Sen  Dick Durbin  D IL  announced today that the Federal Aviation Administration  FAA  has awarded a  15 million grant to Chicago Midway International Airport for the improvement of runway safety   Durbin has urged the FAA and Midway to improve runway safety by installing a type of soft concrete known as Engineered Materials Arresting System  EMAS    EMAS is a mixture of water  foam and concrete that crushes in a controlled manner under the weight of an aircraft and gradually stops its forward momentum          The Midway runway accident that led to the death of 12 year old Joshua Woods last December is tragic     said Durbin      We can never make up for this terrible loss of life  but we must take every precaution  to ensure not only the safety of airline passengers  but that of Chicago area residents and commuters   Today  as a result of efforts by the City of Chicago and FAA  we have secured  15 million dollars that will be used to make significant improvements to runway safety at Midway airport        Last December  a Southwest Airlines plane overran the runway at Midway and killed a young boy  Joshua Woods  who was riding in a car on a neighboring street   In April  the City of Chicago Department of Aviation asked the FAA to support the City s proposal to install EMAS at Midway Airport and provide grant money to help fund it   Durbin stressed the need for this funding at a May 4th Transportation Appropriations Subcommittee hearing   At the hearing  the Senator brought up the fatal incident at Midway and pressed FAA Administrator Blakey to work with the City of Chicago and Midway Airport officials and provide funds for the installation of EMAS       On June 5  2006  the FAA released  15 million in runway safety area funds for installation of EMAS at the ends of the two runways which pose the greatest safety threat  This  15 million will be used to start construction on the end of one runway in 2006  and the ends of the other runway by December 2007       The EMAS will be installed at four locations      Northwest corner of the airport at 55th and Central  Southeast corner of 63rd and Cicero  Southwest corner of the airport at 63rd and Central  Northeast corner of the airport at 55th and Cicero
