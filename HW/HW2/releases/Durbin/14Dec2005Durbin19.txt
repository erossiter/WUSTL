LINCOLN PENNY BILL PASSES CONGRESS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Wednesday  December 14  2005    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL   co chair of the Abraham Lincoln Bicentennial Commission  today announced the passage of legislation which will issue a series of one cent coins to honor the 200th anniversary of the birth of Abraham Lincoln   The Lincoln Commission was established by Congress in 2001 to organize the national observance of the 200th anniversary of Lincoln   s birth and to recommend activities that will best pay tribute to Lincoln on the occasion     The redesigned pennies will be issued in 2009  the year of Lincoln   s bicentennial  and will feature four designs on the reverse side of the coins  each representing a different aspect of the life of Abraham Lincoln   The different designs would depict his birth and early childhood in Kentucky  his youth in Indiana  his professional life in Illinois and his Presidency in Washington  DC   The present image of Lincoln would stay on the front of the penny        Abraham Lincoln is a true American hero  and his life can provide inspiration to Americans of any age and background     Durbin said      From modest Midwestern roots  Lincoln rose to the Presidency through his intelligence  integrity and commitment to the nation that he loved   To honor the 200th anniversary of his birth  this special series of coins will pay tribute to his life and work        Issued in 1909 on the 100th anniversary of Lincoln   s birth the    Lincoln cent    featuring Victor David Brenner   s likeness of the 16th president is one of the most recognizable designs in American currency   In the nearly 100 years of production of the Lincoln cent there have been only two designs on the reverse side  the original featuring two wheat heads  which was phased out in 1958  and the current representation of the Lincoln Memorial in Washington  DC     The Durbin bill instructs the Secretary of the Treasury to issue four one cent coins during the year 2009   The designs will be chosen by the Secretary after consultation with the Abraham Lincoln Bicentennial Commission  the Commission of Fine Arts  and the Citizens Coinage Advisory Committee   After December 31  2009  the image on the reverse of the penny will be permanently changed to one that represents President Lincoln   s preservation of the United States as a single  united nation     The Abraham Lincoln Bicentennial One Cent Coin Redesign Act  S 341  was co sponsored by Barack Obama  D IL   Jim Bunning  R KY   Richard Lugar  R IN  and Evan Bayh  D IN    The Durbin bill was approved late last night by the U S  House of Representatives as part of a larger package of legislation
