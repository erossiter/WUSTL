     DURBIN  OBAMA  ANNOUNCE CONFIRMATION OF VIRGINIA M  KENDALL AS FEDERAL DISTRICT JUDGE IN NORTHERN DISTRICT OF ILLINOIS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           12 21 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN  OBAMA  ANNOUNCE CONFIRMATION OF VIRGINIA M  KENDALL AS FEDERAL DISTRICT JUDGE IN NORTHERN DISTRICT OF ILLINOIS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Wednesday  December 21  2005    Washington  DC      U S  Senators Dick Durbin  D IL  and Barack Obama  D IL  announced that Virginia M  Kendall was confirmed tonight to fill the federal district court vacancy in the Northern District of Illinois  In May  Durbin and Obama  in consultation with Speaker of the House Dennis Hastert  R IL  submitted Kendall   s name to President Bush for his consideration        Virginia Kendall is an extraordinary woman who will make a great contribution to the federal judiciary    said Durbin      Along with Senator Obama and Speaker Dennis Hastert  I strongly supported her nomination   On a bipartisan basis  we reviewed many fine applicants and found Virginia Kendall to be the best   She is a highly respected prosecutor with a stellar reputation for diligence  intelligence and integrity and will bring to the bench a great depth of experience and legal ability        Ms  Kendall has been a federal prosecutor in the U S  Attorney   s Office in Chicago since 1995   She received the FBI Director   s Letter of Recognition in 1998 for serving as lead counsel in the first Internet kidnapping case in the nation   She also received this award for her work in the United States v  Segal prosecution against a corporate president and his corporation who defrauded individuals of over  30 million   Ms  Kendall won the Chicago Crime Commission Star of Distinction Award in 2004 for her work on the Project Safe Neighborhoods initiative and the Coalition Against Child Exploitation   She has served as an Adjunct Professor at Loyola University of Chicago School of Law since 1993  and she worked as a law clerk to Judge George Marovich on the federal district court in Chicago   Ms  Kendall is a graduate of Northwestern University and Loyola University of Chicago School of Law  where she won the First Place Oralist award in the Jessup International Moot Court Competition  U S  Region    Ms  Kendall was born in Evanston  Illinois and now lives with her husband in Glenview  Illinois   They have three children     The President traditionally respects the wishes of a home state senator of his own party in making federal district court nominations   In cases where both U S  senators are of the opposite party from the President  he usually consults with the highest ranking elected official of his own party   The State of Illinois has a long tradition of seeking bipartisan cooperation in the recommendation of federal district court nominees for Presidential consideration
