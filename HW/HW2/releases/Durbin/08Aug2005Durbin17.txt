    8 8 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN VISITS CENTRAL ILLINOIS FARMS HIT BY DROUGHT                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Monday  August 8  2005    McLEAN  IL      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  visited two local farms on Thursday to see firsthand the effects of the drought on the crops in Central Illinois     Durbin told the farmers and agriculture leaders present that if the drought results in the crop loss many anticipate he is prepared to offer legislation when the Senate reconvenes to bring emergency funding to Illinois farmers   Several weeks ago  Durbin encouraged U S  Secretary of Agriculture Mike Johanns to declare Illinois an agricultural disaster area   The Secretary later declared 101 of Illinois    102 counties agriculture disaster areas     The period from March to June of 2005 was the third driest in Illinois since 1895   The National Agriculture Statistics Service recently rated 61 percent of Illinois    corn crop as very poor or poor   Soybeans aren   t faring much better with 41 percent of Illinois    crop rating very poor or poor        I   m worried about Illinois    farm families     Durbin said      Some corporate farms may be able to withstand a year like this   But for many farm families  the severe drought will devastate their crops and dry up profits this fall   While I can   t promise the rain needed to end this drought  I can promise to go back to Washington to bring federal help to those who need it most        Over the course of the last several months  Durbin has met with Illinois Director of Agriculture Chuck Hartke  the Illinois Farm Bureau  Illinois Farmers Union  Illinois Soybean Association and Illinois Corn Growers     The federal disaster declaration gives farmers the resources to recoup come of this year   s losses by allowing those who meet certain requirements to apply for low interest emergency loans from the Farm Service Agency   The USDA has also directed farmers to the Emergency Conservation Program  Federal Crop Insurance and the Noninsured Crop Disaster Assistance Program
