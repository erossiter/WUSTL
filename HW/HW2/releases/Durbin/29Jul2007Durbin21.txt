     Durbin  Senate Approves Funding Increase for Firefighter Assistance Grants     7 29 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin  Senate Approves Funding Increase for Firefighter Assistance Grants                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Sunday  July 29  2007   CHICAGO  IL   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today announced that the Senate has approved increased funding for Firefighter Assistance Grants in the FY 2008 Homeland Security Appropriations Bill  Durbin made the announcement during a news conference with Chicago Fire Department and local officials at a downtown firehouse    President Bush proposed drastic funding cuts in his FY 2008 budget  see chart below   The Senate would increase funding for both the Assistance to Firefighters Grantsor FIRE grantsprogram and the Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response Grantsor SAFER grantsprogram  The Presidents budget proposed a 45  funding cut for FIRE grants and the elimination of SAFER grants    Our first responders and emergency preparedness officials need to know that the Department of Homeland Security will provide them with the tools they need  Durbin said    FIRE grants help meet the firefighting and emergency response needs of fire departments and nonaffiliated emergency medical services organizations  Since 2001  FIRE grants have helped firefighters and other first responders obtain critically needed equipment  protective gear  emergency vehicles  training  and other resources needed to protect the public and emergency personnel from fire and related hazards  SAFER grants provide funding directly to fire departments and volunteer firefighter interest organizations in order to help them increase the number of trained  front line firefighters available in their communities    Illinois received 220 FIRE grant awards for more than  20 million in 2006  So far in 2007  Illinois has received 25 FIRE grant awards worth more than  2 million  Illinois was awarded 14 SAFER grants for more than  4 4 million in 2006  The application period for FY 2007 SAFER grants begins tomorrow    The FY 2008 Homeland Security Appropriations bill now goes to a House Senate Conference Committee which will send the final version to the President
