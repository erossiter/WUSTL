    3 7 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN MEETS WITH ILLINOIS FARM BUREAU LEADERS TO DISCUSS FARM BILL BUDGET  ETHANOL   WRDA                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Wednesday  March 7  2007    WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL   who serves on the Senate Agriculture Appropriations Subcommittee  today met with the leaders of the Illinois Farm Bureau  IFB  to discuss several issues regarding the 2007 Farm Bill budget  the potential role of cellulosic ethanol as a renewable energy resource  and the Water Resources Development Act  WRDA         Illinois farm land is among the most productive in the world   said Durbin    It is important to Illinoisans that we work together to pass a Farm Bill and ensure we have adequate shipping routes to get our agricultural products to market        At today s meeting  Illinois Farm Bureau members thanked Senator Durbin for writing to the Senate Budget Committee and urging them to adopt a budget resolution that will provide an adequate level of funding for the Farm Bill   Durbin noted that market forces and our current budget deficit will mean this Farm Bill may be tighter than in past years   The fiscal climate may make it difficult to write a bill that both provides a safety net to farmers and authorizes new funding for nutrition  conservation  and renewable energy proposals being supported by advocates and Members of Congress       Participants also stressed the need to move forward with research and development initiatives needed to make cellulosic ethanol a widespread commercial product   Durbin noted that cellulosic ethanol has the potential to help the country achieve energy security and independence  improve our air quality  and develop an industry that will create jobs in rural America  Currently  there are several hurdles that need to be overcome  including improving the efficiency of the enzymes that break down the cellulosic material  as well as learning how to grow  harvest  transport  and store bulky biomass more efficiently        Illinois farmers stand ready and willing to contribute to America s energy security and independence   said Durbin   When we use our state s agricultural products to create renewable fuels  we reduce our dependence on foreign suppliers while helping our farmers by increasing demand for their crops        Durbin also updated Farm Bureau leaders on the status of the lock and dam construction bill  WRDA  noting that at a recent field hearing in Louisiana  Environment and Public Works Committee Chairman  Senator Barbara Boxer  D CA  announced that she would push for the quick passage of WRDA    The legislation includes Congressional authorization for locks and dam modernization on the Upper Mississippi and Illinois Rivers  It also authorizes specific projects for navigation  ecosystem restoration  and flood and storm damage reduction        Passing WRDA will mean over 50 000 new jobs for the region  shorter shipping times  and improved business for Midwest producers along the River   said Durbin    The Mississippi River transports  12 billion worth of products every year  affecting agriculture  commercial  and labor interests across the state
