STATEMENT REGARDING ASSOCIATION HEALTH PLANS LEGISLATION IN U S  HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Tuesday  July 26  2005    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today made the following statement regarding legislation under consideration in the House of Representatives to establish Association Health Plans  AHPs      Democrats and Republicans agree that something must be done to help small businesses and their employees deal with rising health care costs  but the AHP proposal being debated in the House is a misguided effort         Congress tried the AHP approach in the 1970s   Because of the massive fraud and abuse that resulted from exempting these health plans from state oversight  consumers  physicians and hospitals were left with more than  124 million in unpaid medical bills   Congress was forced to repeal to the exemptions and allow state regulation of these plans         In the Senate  I have introduced with Senator Blanche Lincoln the Small Business Employers Health Benefits Program Act   Our bill is a common sense approach that will help small businesses provide insurance coverage to their employees   The House has an opportunity to pass similar legislation offered by Congressman Kind and Congressman Andrews   I urge Congress to reject the AHP proposal in favor of a bill to protect consumers while creating an affordable health care option for small business owners and their employees
