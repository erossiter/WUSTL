     Durbin Announces  122 Million in Homeland Security Grants for Illinois                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           7 19 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Announces  122 Million in Homeland Security Grants for Illinois                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Thursday  July 19  2007    WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Sen  Dick Durbin  D IL  announced today that the United States Department of Homeland Security has awarded  122 552 067 in grants to several areas throughout Illinois  Homeland Security grants enhance the ability of states  territories  and urban areas to prevent  protect against  respond to and recover from terrorist attacks and other disasters  Illinois will receive funding in the following areas   Public Safety Interoperable Communications  Statewide   36 414 263 in funding to help state and local first responders improve public safety communications and coordination during a natural or man made disaster   16 195 438 of the funding will go to the city of Chicago    Urban Area Security Initiative  UASI   Chicago   47 2 million funding for the UASI Program focuses on the unique planning  equipment  training and exercise needs of high threat  high density urban areas  It assists them in building sustainable capacity to prevent  respond  and recover from acts of terrorism    State Homeland Security Program  SHSP   Statewide   22 3 million in funding for building capabilities at the state and local levels through planning  equipment  training  and exercise activities  SHSP also supports the implementation of state homeland security strategies and key elements of the national preparedness architecture  including the National Preparedness Goal  the National Incident Management System  and the National Response Plan    Law Enforcement Terrorism Prevention Program  LETPP   Statewide   15 9 million in funding for the state to provide resources to law enforcement and public safety departments to support critical terrorism prevention activities  including establishing and enhancing intelligence fusion centers    Metropolitan Medical Response System Awards  MMRS   Chicago   258 145 in funding to establish and sustain local capabilities to respond to all hazards mass casualty incidents  including terrorism  epidemic disease outbreaks  natural disasters  and large scale hazardous materials incidents    Citizen Corps Program  Statewide   479 659 in funding for the state to enhance citizen and community involvement in emergency preparedness  planning  mitigation  response  and recovery
