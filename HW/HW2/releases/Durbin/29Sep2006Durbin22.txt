     DURBIN AMENDMENT HELPS INNOCENT AIRLINE PASSENGERS MISTAKENLY PLACED ON TERRORIST WATCH LIST                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     9 29 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN AMENDMENT HELPS INNOCENT AIRLINE PASSENGERS MISTAKENLY PLACED ON TERRORIST WATCH LIST                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Friday  September 29  2006   Washington  D C       U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today said the federal government will create a new process to help clear the names of thousands of innocent passengers who have been mistakenly placed on a terrorist watch lists  An amendment by Durbin was included in the Homeland Security Appropriations bill that was approved by the Senate and the House   The President is expected to sign the legislation          All Americans are willing to do their part to keep our country safe  but no American should have to pay a price for bureaucratic mix ups and red tape     Durbin said      The federal government will now be required to put in place a process to remove the names of innocent American passengers who have been singled out because their names appear on a watch list          Durbin said his amendment directs the Secretary of Homeland Security to develop new procedures to expeditiously clear the names of individuals who have been mistakenly placed on a terrorist database list or who have names identical to or substantially similar to names on these database lists within the next six months  The lists covered by Durbin s provision include the Transportation Security Administration   s  TSA  No Fly and Selectee watch list       Since the terrorist attacks of September 11  2001  the TSA and other federal agencies have maintained various lists that contain the names of individuals suspected of posing a risk of terrorism or threat to airlines or passenger safety       Durbin noted  however  that many innocent passengers have been mistakenly placed on such lists  and thousands more have names similar or identical to terror threats that are on these lists   As a result  innocent passengers face delays and are prevented from using the internet or electronic kiosks located at the airport   They must be cleared each time by airline personnel at the check in counter  often causing passengers to miss flights   Durbin said that current TSA procedures for clearing innocent travelers is a long process  finally resulting in a clearance letter that passengers must present to airline employees at the counter each time they fly
