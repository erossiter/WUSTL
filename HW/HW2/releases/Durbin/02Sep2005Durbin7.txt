    9 2 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN ANNOUNCES  6 MILLION FOR U S  67 BRIDGE IN BEARDSTOWN                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Friday  September 2  2005    BEARDSTOWN  IL      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today announced  6 million in federal funding for a U S  Route 67 bridge study   The funding is part of  215 million Durbin and U S  Senator Barack Obama  D IL  have secured for Illinois in the TEA 3 transportation bill recently passed in Congress        The current bridge is 50 years old and repair efforts have often led to crippling congestion and major headaches for commercial traffic     Durbin said      This study will help us determine the best location for this well traveled bridge   The preliminary study needs to be completed quickly so that by the time expansion of U S  67 gets to Beardstown  the bridge is already in place   Senator Obama and I have identified this project as one of our Downstate priorities        The study is expected to take two years to complete   The U S  Route 67 bridge is one of several projects along    Corridor 67    as officials look to aid economic development in Central Illinois     The TEA 3 transportation bill has a five year life span   Under the transportation bill passed in 1998  Illinois received an average of  927 million each year  Under the current Senate bill  Illinois will receive an average of  1 236 billion a year  or  309 million more per year  Over the five year life of the bill  that is  1 545 billion more for Illinois  an increase of 33 34 percent more each year than under TEA 21     Under this bill  Illinois transit will receive  2 1 billion in formula funds  In the previous Transportation bill  Illinois received only  1 9 billion over six years  Over a comparable year period  Illinois would receive  2 467 billion     128  over TEA 21   s level     Durbin said that for every  1 billion spent on transportation infrastructure  it is estimated that 24 000 new jobs are supported  By providing Illinois with  1 2 billion per year in transportation funding  this bill will support tens of thousands of new jobs for Illinois families
