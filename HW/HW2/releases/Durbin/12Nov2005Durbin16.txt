     DURBIN ANNOUNCES  2 MILLION FOR IMPROVEMENTS TO BRIGHTON BUNKER HILL ROAD                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        11 12 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN ANNOUNCES  2 MILLION FOR IMPROVEMENTS TO BRIGHTON BUNKER HILL ROAD                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Saturday  November 12  2005    RURAL BUNKER HILL  IL      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today announced  2 million in federal funding to begin construction on Brighton Bunker Hill Road in Macoupin County   The funding is part of  215 million Durbin and Obama have secured for Illinois in the TEA 3 transportation bill which was signed into law in August          Safety is our number one concern when it comes to this project     said Durbin      When you mix sharp curves  steep hills and an increase in traffic like we   ve seen in Macoupin County  it   s a recipe for trouble   Widening the road and alleviating structural problems will ensure Brighton Bunker Hill Road meets the needs of the growing Macoupin County population        The 10 mile stretch of Brighton Bunker Hill Road links U S  Highway 67 in Brighton to Illinois Highway 159 in Bunker Hill       The TEA 3 transportation bill has a five year life span   Under the transportation bill passed in 1998  Illinois received an average of  927 million each year  Under the current Senate bill  Illinois will receive an average of  1 236 billion a year  or  309 million more per year  Over the five year life of the bill  that is  1 545 billion more for Illinois  an increase of 33 34 percent more each year than under TEA 21       Under this bill  Illinois transit will receive  2 1 billion in formula funds  In the previous Transportation bill  Illinois received only  1 9 billion over six years  Over a comparable year period  Illinois would receive  2 467 billion     128  over TEA 21   s level       Durbin and Obama said that for every  1 billion spent on transportation infrastructure  it is estimated that 24 000 new jobs are supported  By providing Illinois with  1 2 billion per year in transportation funding  this bill will support tens of thousands of new jobs for Illinois families          This transportation bill makes a significant investment in highways and transit infrastructure that is so important to communities across the state     said Durbin     Senator Obama helped write this bill and made sure Illinois received its fair share of highway and transit funds  I was pleased to work with him and our Illinois colleagues in the House to provide the funding needed for our roads  bridges  and transit systems
