     DURBIN  OBAMA MEET WITH TRANSPORTATION SECRETARY TO DISCUSS ILLINOIS PRIORITIES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  7 12 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN  OBAMA MEET WITH TRANSPORTATION SECRETARY TO DISCUSS ILLINOIS PRIORITIES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Tuesday  July 12  2005    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senators Dick Durbin  D IL  and Barack Obama  D IL  today met with U S  Secretary of Transportation Norman Mineta to discuss a number of Illinois transportation priorities  including funding for Amtrak  the Northwest Chicagoland International Airport at Rockford  RFD   and modernization at Chicago   s O   Hare International Airport     President Bush released a budget in February that included zero funding for Amtrak service in Illinois   Last month  the House of Representatives added  550 million to boost Amtrak funding to  1 2 billion        Amtrak is vitally important to Illinois     said Durbin      Unfortunately  the President and his Administration are once again attempting to privatize and eventually eliminate Amtrak rail service   In Illinois alone  more than 3 million Amtrak passengers would be out on the highways and more than 2 000 employees would be out their jobs   Year after year  the President drives Amtrak to the brink of insolvency and Congress is forced to ride to the rescue   I stand ready to reject the Administration   s cuts and privatization plan when the Senate Appropriations Committee considers the transportation funding bill next week           It doesn   t make sense to provide millions of dollars to upgrade and maintain our roads  and then to turn around and eliminate funding for rail service     said Obama      Amtrak service is vital to Illinoisans all across the state  and I will fight any attempts by the Bush Administration to cut its budget        Durbin and Obama also urged Secretary Mineta to support the Greater Rockford Airport   s application for  3 million in Small Community Development Grant funds   The funds would promote passenger service and attract additional airlines to Rockford   s airport        Rockford plays an important role in Illinois    regional aviation system     Durbin said      The airport in Rockford is part of the solution to congestion around O   Hare and Midway Airports in Chicago   Securing this grant will benefit RFD   s customers  carriers and community           The Rockford airport is an important part of transportation and economic development of northern Illinois     said Obama      This grant will create jobs and help RFD continue to provide the people in northern Illinois with vital air service        The Senators asked Secretary Mineta to update them on the status of the Federal Aviation Administration   s review of the Environmental Impact Statement  EIS  of the O   Hare Modernization program   Once FAA approval is secured  the City of Chicago will be free to begin Phase One of the modernization project   Secretary Mineta indicated that the EIS review may be completed as early as the end of this month
