     Durbin Chairs Hearing on  Material Support  Bar                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  9 19 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Chairs Hearing on  Material Support  Bar                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Wednesday  September 19  2007    Washington  D C    United States Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today chaired a hearing examining the controversial provision of U S  immigration law known as the material support bar  The material support bar allows the government to deny asylum and refugee claims from anyone who has provided anything from small financial payments to meals and shelter to terrorist organizations  Unfortunately the bar often applies to victims of human rights abuses or to people forced or coerced into aiding these groups  Over the last 30 years  the United States has resettled over 2 6 million refugees from around the world  Durbin said  However  current law is barring some individuals from finding safe haven in America  Children kidnapped and forced to fight for militias  nurses made  at gun point  to provide medical support to rebels  and families forced to dig graves for paramilitaries all are being denied asylum for giving material support to terrorist organizations  We need to do better  The hearing  in the Judiciary Subcommittee on Human Rights and the Law  focused on the governments implementation of the material support bar  including the case of a Columbian nurse who was forced to provide medical aid to the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Columbia  or FARC guerrillas  The nurse has been denied asylum in the United States because the government considers medical care to be material support  The terms of the material support bar are defined very broadly and the Department of Homeland Security  DHS  has applied them in a sweeping manner  Pro democracy groups have been included  in addition to groups who have fought side by side with US forces in past conflicts  Testifying at the hearing were representatives for the Department of Homeland Security  Human Rights First  the U S  Conference of Catholic Bishops  and Mariana   the Columbian nurse forced to treat FARC forces  In 1997  I was giving a presentation with a doctor for a health campaign  Mariana testified  During the talk  an audience member collapsed  so the doctor and I attended to him while the other attendees left  But the fallen man suddenly began to laugh  and two other men came over  identified themselves as FARC members  kidnapped and physically assaulted while forcing me to treat guerrilla forces  This went on for months  Because of the risk to her and her family  Mariana  whose real name was disguised to protect her identity  testified from behind a screen  Senator Durbin became the Chairman of the first ever congressional committee dealing specifically with human rights this January  The Senate Subcommittee on Human Rights and the Law has jurisdiction over all human rights law and polices  including genocide  human trafficking  war crimes and torture  The Subcommittees first hearing  this February  was on the genocide in Darfur and it has also held hearings on human trafficking and child soldiers  The material support bar was raised in Aprils hearing on child soldiers  Because of the unique nature of this issue and the groups of people affected by the bar  Durbin agreed to hold a hearing devoted entirely to bar
