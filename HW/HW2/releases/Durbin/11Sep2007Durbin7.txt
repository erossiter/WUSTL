     Durbin Asks EPA for Answers on Lake Michigan Pollution                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           9 11 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Asks EPA for Answers on Lake Michigan Pollution                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Tuesday  September 11  2007   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin late yesterday sent a letter to the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency  Stephen Johnson  asking for data on the current levels of pollution in Lake Michigan  the sources of the pollution  and the health and environmental consequences  While stopping BP s plan to dump additional waste was progress  said Durbin  We have a lot of work ahead of us  Lake Michigan is not just another body of water  it is a treasure to be protected  In order to move forward  we need a comprehensive assessment of the current levels  sources and consequences of pollution in the Lake  Once we ve gathered this information  we will develop a strategy  Thirty Years after the passage of The Clean Water Act  there are still warnings about the safety of eating fish caught in Lake Michigan  Durbin said  We can do better  Yesterday  Durbin and Emanuel met again with the Chairman and President of BP America  Bob Malone to reaffirm BP Americas August 23 announcement that they have reversed their decision to add to the pollution in Lake Michigan by expanding their Indiana Whiting Refinery  Text of the letter appears below    The Honorable Stephen L  Johnson  Administrator  U S  Environmental Protection Agency  1200 Pennsylvania Ave NW  Washington  DC 20460   Dear Administrator Johnson  The recent outcry over the re permitting of the BP Whiting refinery that allows the facility to increase its discharges of ammonia and suspended solids has focused public attention on the health of Lake Michigan  a precious natural resource that provides drinking water for 10 million people  During these public discussions additional questions have arisen as to the current levels of pollution in Lake Michigan  the sources of the pollution  and the health and environmental consequences of the pollution  Access to better data will help policymakers as they work to ensure the wellbeing of the lake  I appreciate that these issues are technically complex  but improving the environmental health of the lake requires better information about the threats to its water quality  I have the following specific requests  1  Please provide me with the number of permits that are currently in effect for Lake Michigan discharges  2  Provide a list of the contaminants that EPA permits consider  3  List the levels of these contaminants in the lake today and the trends they have shown over the past 20 years  4  Identify the significant point sources for each of these pollutants  5  Identify which of these contaminants  at their current levels in the lake  present the greatest risk to human health and the environmental health of the lake  6  Compare the levels of regulated discharges to those from unregulated sources of lake pollution  Please respond to this request by October 10  2007  I look forward to your reply  Sincerely  Richard J  Durbin  United States Senator
