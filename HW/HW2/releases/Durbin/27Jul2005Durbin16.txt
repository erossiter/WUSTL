      7 27 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN STATEMENT REGARDING USDA   S DESIGNATION OF ILLINOIS COUNTIES AS AGRICULTURAL DISASTER AREAS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Wednesday  July 27  2005    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today made the following statement regarding the U S  Secretary of Agriculture  USDA  Mike Johanns    announcement that 101 Illinois counties have been designated agricultural disaster areas       The announcement by the USDA that 101 of 102 Illinois counties have been designated as agriculture disaster areas could not come at a more important time             Everybody in Illinois knows what the drought has done   It   s the third worst drought we   ve seen in more than a century   Many farmers across the state talk about losing about one third of their corn crop   In some parts of the state it is even worse     total devastation             This helping hand from the federal government will help many farm families get though one of the most difficult growing seasons in modern memory   I   ll do my best to make sure that money comes to Illinois in a timely basis and  if necessary  take action in Washington to add provisions to the Agriculture Appropriations bill to help even further             Tomorrow  I look forward to meeting with Secretary Johanns  Senator Obama and Illinois Director of Agriculture Chuck Hartke to discuss the drought  the full impact of this federal aid and what we need to do to help farm families
