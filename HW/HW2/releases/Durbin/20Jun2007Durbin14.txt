    6 20 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Meets with Incoming Commander at Rock Island Arsenal  Major General Robert Radin                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Wednesday  June 20  2007   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today met with the Major General Robert Radin who will be taking over command of the Army Sustainment Command that is headquartered at Rock Island Arsenal in Rock Island  Illinois   The Rock Island Arsenal is vital to our nations defense and the regions economy  Durbin said  Major General Radin is a respected commander who brings valuable experience to his new position  He and I discussed the importance of maintaining the Arsenal s role as a critical national security asset in our nations defense   Major General Robert Radin will assume the position as Commanding General of the Army Sustainment Command on July 25  2007  Major General Radin will also be the senior mission commander and will oversee all of activities at the Rock Island Arsenal  This position is currently held by Major General Jerome Johnson  Radin  a West Point graduate  is returning to Rock Island where he previously commanded the Joint Munitions Command from 2003 2005  He is currently the Chief of Staff for Operations at the Army Material Command at Ft  Belvoir   The Army Sustainment Command  tasked with improving logistical support to soldiers on the battlefield  opened its headquarters at Rock Island Arsenal in September 2006  It is part of the Army Material Command and continues to work on improving the speed of delivery and the efficiency of maintaining materials and equipment   Rock Island Arsenal is our only remaining Army foundry and metal manufacturing facility  When American soldiers began facing improvised explosive devices on the roads of Iraq  private industry could not keep up with the speed and innovation needed to produce protective armor  Rock Island did  producing steel armor kits for Humvees and other vehicles  In the Military Construction Appropriations bill that was recently approved by the Senate Appropriations Committee  Durbin secured  3 35 million in funding to both finish remodeling the existing Rock Island Arsenal Fire and Police Station and complete the remaining 4 000 square feet of the new addition  Durbin originally secured  7 4 million for this project in the 2006 Budget
