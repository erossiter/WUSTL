     DURBIN INTRODUCES LEGISLATION TO HELP COLLEGE STUDENTS MANAGE TEXTBOOK COSTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     4 2 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN INTRODUCES LEGISLATION TO HELP COLLEGE STUDENTS MANAGE TEXTBOOK COSTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Monday  April 2  2007    BLOOMINGTON  IL    U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  visited Illinois State University today to discuss legislation he recently introduced to ensure that college textbooks and supplemental materials are more available and affordable for college students   This bill  known as the College Textbook Affordability Act  would help students manage costs by requiring publishers and colleges receiving federal assistance to print the price of textbooks and supplemental materials in the course schedule so students could take that into account when selecting classes        The average college student spends nearly  900 on textbooks every year and the cost of textbooks is climbing at four times the rate of inflation   said Durbin    We know these books cost significantly less for students overseas  What we don t know is why publishers and their cooperating faculty are penalizing American students        Students face a number of challenges when trying to purchase required textbooks   New editions are expensive and often issued unnecessarily   This makes previous editions outdated and forces students to pay more for new books rather than being able to buy used books   Packaging supplemental materials such as CD ROMs and workbooks  with textbooks is called book bundling  Faculty members who make decisions on which textbooks are used in their classes often do not have complete information on the relative prices of different texts  Furthermore  information like the International Standard Book Number  ISBN  is often unavailable to students until just before a term commences  making it difficult for students to shop for the lowest priced book        Publishers use new editions and bundled material to drive up costs for students who are already having trouble paying for school   Durbin said    It is time to give them the information and options they need to make educated decisions about managing their finances in school        The College Textbook Affordability Act      Requires publishers to include the price of textbooks and supplemental material when providing information to faculty  as well as the history of revisions and whether the textbook and supplemental material are available in a potentially lower priced alternative format  Requires publishers who bundle course material to offer the textbooks and supplemental material in unbundled versions  Requires colleges receiving federal assistance to include the ISBN and retail price of textbooks and supplemental materials in the course schedule  Requires schools  when asked  to provide bookstores with access to the course schedule  ISBN numbers of course material  and the maximum and current course enrollment numbers
