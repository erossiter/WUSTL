      6 30 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN CALLS FOR A RAISE FOR AMERICA   S WORKERS BEFORE A RAISE FOR CONGRESS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Friday  June 30  2006    CHICAGO  IL      Continuing his fight to ensure the minimum wage is increased before Congress gets a raise  U S Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  called on Congress to put the needs of hard working men and women across the country ahead of their own wallets       While Illinois raised its minimum wage to  6 50 an hour  the federal minimum wage has not been raised in nine years   Last week the Senate yet again rejected increasing it  Approximately 330 000 people in Illinois would benefit from raising the minimum wage to  7 25 an hour   Minimum wage workers making  6 50 an hour and working 40 hours a week  52 weeks a year make  13 520 a year in Illinois   Over the last nine years  members of Congress have given themselves a  31 600 raise  At a time when they are struggling with high gas prices  high health care costs  and the high cost of college tuition  Durbin believes hard working Americans deserve fair pay          Every day  millions of Americans go to work some at two or three jobs and at the end of the month  still don t make enough money to provide decent day care for their kids  pay medical bills  and provide the food and utility bills that are part of every family s daily life     Durbin said       I believe that this Congress should not receive a cost of living adjustment until the minimum wage workers across America receive an increase in pay  They ve been waiting for nine years  That s long enough  Hard working Americans deserve a raise  and it is past time the Congress gave it to them          Last week  a majority of the Senate vote to raise the minimum wage to  7 25 an hour   eight Republicans crossed party lines to show their support  There s no reason why we shouldn t be able to get this done  No one who works for a living should have to live in poverty        Durbin said Democrats will be heading home over the July 4th Recess to talk to American people about their vision for a new direction
