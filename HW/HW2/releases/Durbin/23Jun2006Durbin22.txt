     DURBIN   SENATE APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE APPROVES FULL FUNDING FOR WETLANDS RESERVE PROGRAM                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      6 23 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN   SENATE APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE APPROVES FULL FUNDING FOR WETLANDS RESERVE PROGRAM                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Friday  June 23  2006    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  said the Appropriations Committee approved funding for the Wetlands Reserve Program  WRP   the nation   s leading wetland conservation program   The Committee   s action came one week after Durbin and Sen  Larry Craig  R ID  led a bipartisan effort to provide funding for all 250 000 acres of WRP after the House bill provided funding for only 144 000 acres   While an exact dollar amount for WRP was not specified in the bill  the program is estimated to cost approximately  400 million        Saving and restoring wetlands that clean our water  protect our watersheds  and provide wildlife habitat at modest costs under the WRP is a tremendous value     said Durbin      In Illinois  this type of restoration funding has successfully repaired more than 27 000 acres of wetlands since 1990   The decision by the Senate Appropriations Committee to provide the Wetlands Reserve Program with full funding will help us protect nature   s most productive ecosystem        The Wetlands Reserve Program is a voluntary conservation program that assists landowners interested in protecting and restoring wetlands by funding the purchase of long term agreements and easements and providing technical and financial assistance  Wetlands serve a critical role in mitigating the effects of natural disasters  limiting erosion  enhancing water quality  and fostering wildlife diversity     On June 16  2006 Durbin and Craig sent a letter to the Senate Agricultural Appropriations Subcommittee urging full funding of WRP   In the letter  the senators noted that applications for wetlands restoration projects far exceed available funding   In a two year period  the Natural Resource Conservation Service rejected nearly one million acres of wetland restoration projects as a result of funding shortfalls
