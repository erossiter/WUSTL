     Statement of Senator Durbin on Executive Order Increasing Sanctions in Burma     10 19 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Statement of Senator Durbin on Executive Order Increasing Sanctions in Burma                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Friday  October 19  2007       United States Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  released the following statement today  after President Bush announced an increase in sanctions against the ruling military junta in Burma    Im pleased with the Presidents announcement today and urge Congress to do what we can to support greater economic sanctions against the brutal dictatorship in Burma    Global condemnation of Burma has been loud and swift  No nation on earth should support or protect such a regime     The circumstances in Burma couldnt be more compelling  A Nobel Peace Prize winner has been held under house arrest for 12 of the last 18 years  a brutal and corrupt military government is pillaging the countrys economic wealth   and repeated attempts by the people through elections and peaceful demonstrations to bring about democratic change are extinguished    The dramatic images being shown on television and on the front pages of newspapers around the world show the bravery and dignity of these peaceful protesters  To the people of Burma  We are with you    Recently Senator Durbin met with the Chinese Ambassador regarding the situation in Burma  The Senator discussed the need for China in particular to play a more constructive role in ending the violence and pushing for democratic change     Senator Durbin is the Chairman of the first Congressional committee dealing solely with the issue of human rights  He is an outspoken leader in the Senate on some of the most egregious violations of human rights in the world  His committee has held hearings on the genocide in Darfur  child
