     Statement of Senator Durbin on President s Request for More Funds to Combat Global Aids                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          5 30 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Statement of Senator Durbin on President s Request for More Funds to Combat Global Aids                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Wednesday  May 30  2007         United States Senator Dick Durbin gave the following statement regarding the President s request  today  for  30 billion over 5 years to combat global AIDS through the President s Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief  PEPFAR  and other programs        I am encouraged that the President remains committed to his pledge to fight the spread of AIDS across the globe   The increase in funding that President Bush has requested will go a long way in combating this disease  especially in the 15 countries involved in PEPFAR  I look forward to working with the Senate to provide these funds  There is  however  more that must be done       In just five years  over  7 billion has been distributed to 136 countries through the Global Fund to Fight AIDS  Tuberculosis and Malaria  The Global Fund expands our ability to directly combat these devastating  yet treatable and preventable diseases  To date  every dollar committed by the United States to this program has been matched and doubled by contributions from other donors  Sadly  the Administration s budget proposal this year cuts the U S  contribution to the Global Fund from  742 million to a proposed  300 million  That must change       Today  along with 28 other members of the Senate  I am sending a bipartisan letter to the Chair of the Appropriations Subcommittee for State and Foreign Operations  asking for  1 3 billion to fund the Global Fund program in FY08  I urge the President to work with all of us to support an increase in US contributions to this program       Today there are nearly 40 million people throughout the world living with AIDS  Every year  3 million die and 5 million more will become infected with HIV  It is through programs like PEPFAR and the Global Fund that we are beginning to see a reversal of the terrible devastation caused by AIDS
