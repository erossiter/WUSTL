     DURBIN MEETS WITH ILLINOIS EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT AGENCY TO URGE EXPANDING STATEWIDE COMMUNICATIONS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                2 13 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN MEETS WITH ILLINOIS EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT AGENCY TO URGE EXPANDING STATEWIDE COMMUNICATIONS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Tuesday  February 13  2007   WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today met with the Director of the Illinois Emergency Management Agency  IEMA  William Burke to discuss Illinois homeland security issues including the need for an expanded statewide communications system   At the meeting  Durbin stressed the need for a larger statewide system to replace multiple aging and technically outdated radio systems  The State of Illinois has entered into a partnership with Motorola Corporation to develop a statewide communications system known as STARCOM 21   This new system allows for all government users and first responders across state of Illinois to be on one frequency   Our first responders and emergency officials have to be able to communicate in case of a natural disaster or terrorism attack  said Durbin   The new STARCOM radios will help firefighters  EMTs  police and other emergency officials communicate with each other no matter where a disaster strikes  It will save lives and help protect Illinoisans  The first phase of federal funding has equipped 2000 first responders with essential radios   Now we must move forward   Over the last few years  Senator Durbin has secured more than  27 million dollars for interoperable communications equipment  including  10 million to help Illinois purchase 1 700 STARCOM radios   Last December the system became operational in a limited number of areas across the state   In May 2007  the Illinois State Police will conduct the final test of the statewide radio system and support facility to make sure all radios are completely up and running   In a report released last June by the U S  Department of Homeland Security  Illinois ranked 11th in the nation in terrorism preparedness  In that evaluation  which graded 10 categories of emergency preparedness  Illinois received its lowest marks for its evacuation preparedness abilities    including communications capacity  These radios will help improve communications in case of an emergency   Today s meeting was attended by Jill Morgenthaler  the Deputy Chief of Staff for Public Safety  Bill Burke  the Director of the Illinois Emergency Management Agency  Steve Greene  IEMA Legislative Liason  Mike Chamness  Director of the Illinois Terrorism Task Force and Margaret Larson  Director of Governor Blagojevich s Washington D C  office
