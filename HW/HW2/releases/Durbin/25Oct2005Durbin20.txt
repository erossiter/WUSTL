     BIPARTISAN GROUP OF SENATORS CALL ON PRESIDENT BUSH TO ACCEPT SENATE VERSION OF PATRIOT ACT                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      10 25 2005                                                                                                                                                                            BIPARTISAN GROUP OF SENATORS CALL ON PRESIDENT BUSH TO ACCEPT SENATE VERSION OF PATRIOT ACT                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Tuesday  October 25  2005   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today joined a bipartisan group of Senators to call on President Bush to accept the Senate passed bill to reauthorize the PATRIOT Act   The foundation of the compromise  which was approved unanimously by the Senate three months ago  was the Security and Freedom Enhancement  SAFE  Act  a bill that Durbin introduced with Senators Larry Craig  R ID   Russ Feingold  D WI   and John Sununu  R NH    The key question we are facing is can we be both safe and free   The Senate passed bill strikes the right balance  The President has repeatedly called for Congress to reauthorize the PATRIOT Act   Now  he should call upon the House Republican leadership to accept the bipartisan bill passed by the Senate  Durbin said   Like the SAFE Act  the Senate passed bill retains all of the new powers created by the PATRIOT Act while placing reasonable checks and balances on these powers   Additionally  the Senate bill strengthens judicial oversight  protects privacy rights and enhances public and Congressional reporting on the use of controversial PATRIOT Act powers  like sneak and peek warrants   Durbin added that the House of Representatives passed a flawed bill that would reauthorize the PATRIOT Act without sufficiently protecting the constitutional rights of innocent Americans   The House bill also includes controversial extraneous provisions that were not part of the original PATRIOT Act  are unrelated to the war on terrorism  and unnecessarily expand the governments powers   The Senate passed compromise and the SAFE act have attracted support from across the political spectrum including organizations like the ACLU  American Conservative Union  the U S  Chamber of Commerce and the National Association of Manufacturers   The Senate bill shows that it is possible for Republicans and Democrats to come together to protect our fundamental constitutional rights and give the government the powers it needs   It shows that we can fight terrorism without changing the nature of our free and open society   It shows that we can be safe and free   Its time to get it signed into law  Durbin said
