     DURBIN MEETS WITH STEVEN PRESTON OF DOWNERS GROVE  IL  PRESIDENT   S NOMINEE TO HEAD SMALL BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 5 4 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN MEETS WITH STEVEN PRESTON OF DOWNERS GROVE  IL  PRESIDENT   S NOMINEE TO HEAD SMALL BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Thursday  May 4  2006    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today met with Steven Preston  former Executive Vice President of Service Master  in Downers Grove  IL  and the President   s nominee to be the new chief of the Small Business Administration  SBA    Durbin requested today   s meeting with Mr  Preston to discuss how SBA can further improve the processing of disaster recovery loan requests  legislation to make health insurance more affordable for Illinois    small businesses  as well as other business reforms beneficial to small businesses in Illinois          Steven Preston graduated from Northwestern University  went on to earn his MBA at University of Chicago  and served as both chief financial officer and executive vice president of a successful business in Downers Grove     said Durbin      He understands business and he understands Illinois  and I am hopeful he will bring that understanding to his work at the Small Business Administration          During their meeting  Durbin discussed improving coordination between SBA  the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA   and federal and state bureaucracy      Two years ago  the response to the tornado in Utica left residents feeling stranded     said Durbin      We need to improve upon the process so that Illinois families can rest assured that they will be taken care of   We are beginning to see the positive results of recent changes to SBA   s disaster recovery loan process   After this year   s tornado in my hometown of Springfield  businesses received the help they needed quickly and efficiently   I wanted to make it clear to Mr  Preston that I stand ready to work with him improve the system to ensure that Illinois businesses will have the resources they need when faced with future disasters        Durbin also talked with Preston about legislation to help small businesses afford health insurance    Durbin   s bill  The Small Employers Health Benefits Program  SEHBP   would allow small businesses across America to band together for lower health care prices by pooling their purchasing power and spreading their risk over a large number of participants   Employers would qualify for an annual tax credit to partially offset contributions on behalf of low income employees          There are 1 8 million people in Illinois with no health insurance and thousands more who are under insured     said Durbin      Contrary to popular belief  most of the people in Illinois who don   t have insurance are not out of work    in fact  they work full time in small businesses that cannot afford health insurance for their workers  Small business owners in Illinois and across America are trying to do their part to help provide their employees with health insurance  but they are struggling with annual double digit premium increases  This bill helps solve that problem and I would hope the SBA would lend its support in getting it signed into law
