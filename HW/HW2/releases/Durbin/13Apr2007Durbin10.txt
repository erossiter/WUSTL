                                                                                                                                                                                                  Friday  April 13  2007    CHICAGO  IL    U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today joined elected officials and community leaders to celebrate the official opening of Cima  a job training and business development center housed in the Humboldt Park Vocational Education Center at Wilbur Wright College   The project was formerly known as the Latino Development and Technology Accelerator Center       Durbin visited the center in 2004 and in the Fiscal Year 2005 Energy Appropriations bill  he and Congressman Luis Gutierrez secured  250 000 in federal funding for the Technology Center        Cima will help develop and cultivate the spirit of scientific learning and entrepreneurship at the heart of the Humboldt Park Community   Durbin said    This technology center is going to equip area residents with the computer and networking skills needed in today s digital age        The demand for technology jobs is high and Cima has committed to intensely training students to get into the workforce fully prepared and fully employed   Cima will also couple job training with business development  providing area businesses with the education and technological resources they need to grow their businesses       Durbin said the need for high tech skills in the Latino community is substantial  referencing a report released last month by the Pew Hispanic Center and Pew Internet and American Life Project which found      32 percent of Spanish dominant adults log onto the Internet compared to 76 percent of Latinos who are bilingual and 78 percent who are English dominant   29 percent of Latino adults have a broadband connection at home compared with 43 percent of white adults
