     Durbin  Every American Deserves Equal Access to Housing     6 29 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin  Every American Deserves Equal Access to Housing                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Friday  June 29  2007       U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  yesterday introduced legislation that nearly triples funding for fair housing programs including the Fair Housing Initiatives Program  FHIP  run by the Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD   Specific activities that will be funded under The Housing Fairness Act of 2007 include the processing of complaints  the investigation of cases of housing discrimination  and the development and operation of education and outreach programs to educate the general public of fair housing rights   Some may ask why take this action now  Every American deserves equal access to housing  yet millions of fair housing violations occur each year and the discrimination continues to worsen  said Durbin  The Fair Housing Initiatives Program plays a vital role in fighting housing discrimination  Without the significant increase in funding proposed in todays legislation  the program will not be able to operate effectively   Nearly four million fair housing violations occur annually against African Americans  Latinos  Native Americans and Asian Pacific Islanders alone  Millions of additional violations are committed against people in the protected classes under the Fair Housing Act  family status  disability  religion  color  race  sex and national origin  Additionally  recent investigations of real estate agents uncovered an 87  rate of racial steering and a 20  rate of denial for African American and Latino perspective homeowners   The FHIP funds fair housing organizations and other non profit groups to provide vital services to their communities  These services include education enforcement programs to assist consumers in recognizing and reporting housing discrimination and to conduct investigations of housing discrimination complaints   Specifically  the Housing Fairness Act of 2007 will   Authorize funding for HUD to conduct national testing and investigation of housing discrimination  The testing is modeled on previous HUD national housing discrimination reports but makes the testing an annual program     Increase the funding level for the FHIP  It also clarifies that fair housing centers should be able to receive funding for both education and enforcement grants     Authorize HUD to conduct a national media campaign to inform customers of their fair housing rights and encourages the Secretary to publicize regulations regarding communities fair housing requirements as recipients of federal housing funds  and    Create a competitive matching grant program for private nonprofit organizations to study the causes of housing discrimination and segregation and their effects on education  poverty  and economic development  It also implements pilot projects that test innovative solutions to these problems     On May 16  2007  Durbin and nine other Senators sent a letter to the Senate Appropriations Committee on Transportation  Housing and Urban Development requesting the inclusion of  26 million for FHIP in the 2008 Department of Housing and Urban Development appropriations bill  The legislation was cosponsored by Senators Charles Schumer  D NY   Debbie Stabenow  D MI   and Sherrod Brown  D OH    Similar legislation will be introduced in the House by Representative Al Green  D TX
