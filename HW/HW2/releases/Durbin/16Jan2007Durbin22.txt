     DURBIN CONVENES MEETING ON EMERALD ASH BORER IN ELGIN                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            1 16 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN CONVENES MEETING ON EMERALD ASH BORER IN ELGIN                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Tuesday  January 16  2007    ELGIN  IL    U S  Sen  Dick Durbin  D IL  met with local  state and federal officials today in Elgin to discuss the threat of the Emerald Ash Borer and the efforts to track and contain the invasive species     EAB is a bright green beetle that kills trees by burrowing into their bark and destroying the trees    ability to bring water from the roots to upper branches  Infected trees usually begin to die within two to three years   The Emerald Ash Borer was first sighted in 2002 in Michigan  Forestry experts estimate that the Emerald Ash Borer has been responsible for the devastation of than 20 million ash trees in Michigan  Ohio  and Indiana   To date  the EAB has been discovered in Wilmette  Winnetka  Evanston  Campton Township  the Elgin Rutland Township border  Hampshire Township  Plato Township and Blackberry Township        The battle against EAB begins in our backyards     Durbin said      Homeowners  business owners and local officials are on the front lines of this battle   We all need to pitch in   We need to get educated about this threat   We need to take the initiative and look for troubled ash trees on our property and then we need to promptly report any findings through the proper 800 numbers        Last year  Durbin requested and secured emergency U S  Department of Agriculture funding in the amount of  7 6 million to help fight EAB in Illinois   The funding has been used through a cooperative agreement between USDA     APHIS and Illinois Department of Agriculture to conduct bark peeling surveys in Kane County and Cook County   It has also been used to establish quarantines and a regulatory framework and to set up compliance agreements with qualified companies to remove ash trees and or move them outside of the regulated area   The funding has also been used to work closely with Michigan officials to learn more about the steps they have taken to contain EAB   Last  the funding has helped with outreach and education by Illinois Department of Agriculture of municipalities   Durbin has also held meetings in many of the towns where EAB has been discovered to help increase public awareness        Trees are an important resource   Our ash trees are important to the nursery industry  property values  aesthetic pleasure  wildlife habitat and eco diversity   Illinois is home to more than 110 million of these trees   Their benefits are substantial  but hard to quantify     said Durbin      I   ll continue to work on securing additional funding for Illinois    fight against EAB        For homeowners  the concern has been    what is the future of the ash tree planted in my front yard      for municipal and forestry officials it has been    how much is this going to cost my city and my department      for state officials    what is the effect on Illinois    economy and green spaces      Although the focus of our attention is different  what we all have in common is our concern that this infestation has the potential to cause significant damage     said Durbin        The tree loss we   re discovering today has been underway for more than 5 years   This is going to require a long term commitment and everyone needs to help   It   s going to take a coordinated strategy at all levels of government to address the EAB threat   Resources need to be gathered quickly so we can quickly and aggressively take action against EAB     said Durbin
