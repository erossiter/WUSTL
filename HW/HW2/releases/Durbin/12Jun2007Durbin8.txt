     Durbin  Levin Introduce Resolution to Recognize Juneteenth Independence Day                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      6 12 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin  Levin Introduce Resolution to Recognize Juneteenth Independence Day                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Tuesday  June 12  2007      U S  Senators Dick Durbin  D IL  and Carl Levin  D MI  today introduced legislation recognizing the significance of June 19  1865  commonly known as Juneteenth Independence Day  The day commemorates the arrival of Union soldiers in Galveston  Texas with news that the Civil War had ended  African Americans who had been slaves in the Southwest celebrated June 19 as the anniversary of their emancipation  The legislation is co sponsored by Senators Harry Reid  D NV   Barack Obama  D IL  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI   Representative Danny Davis  D IL  has introduced a similar resolution in the House  Two years after President Lincolns Emancipation Proclamation and months after the end of the Civil War  Union soldiers finally delivered the news of freedom  said Durbin  While news can travel quickly across the globe in todays world  it is important to continue the tradition of celebrating Juneteenth Independence Day  Cultures across the globe look to this historical event as a source of inspiration and an opportunity for celebration  Juneteenth Day marks the time when slavery finally came to an end in the United States over 140 years ago  said Levin  This anniversary of emancipation recognizes one of the most tragic periods of our nations history  While the suffering  degradation and brutality of slavery cannot be repaired  the memory can serve to ensure that no such inhumanity is ever perpetrated again on American soil  We honor Juneteenth Independence Day as a way to both remember the cruelty of slavery and celebrate freedom  said Reid  We solemnly mark the anniversary of the Southwests emancipation as a significant milestone in the abolition of slavery and we continue our work to ensure all Americans enjoy the liberties our nation promises  Juneteenth Independence Day was the day our nation finally reclaimed its dignity  said Senator Obama  More than two years after President Lincolns Emancipation Proclamation ending slavery  June 19th represents the sacrifices of generations past who showed us that ordinary people can do extraordinary things  While this resolution celebrates an incredible triumph over injustice  it also reminds us how far we must continue to go to ensure equality and fairness for all Americans  Juneteenth Independence Day is as important for what it symbolizes today as for what it meant in 1865  said Stabenow  Even though the Emancipation Proclamation had been made and the Civil War had ended  there were many people who had still not experienced the freedom they were entitled to  We must keep the lesson of Juneteenth close to our hearts and realize that ensuring freedom isnt just about passing laws  its about providing true equality for all in our communities  our states and our nation
