     DURBIN ASKS PRESIDENT BUSH TO APPOINT DUCKWORTH TO COMMISSION LOOKING INTO VETERANS CARE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         3 7 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN ASKS PRESIDENT BUSH TO APPOINT DUCKWORTH TO COMMISSION LOOKING INTO VETERANS CARE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Wednesday  March 7  2007    WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today sent a letter to President Bush asking that Iraqi war veteran Tammy Duckworth be appointed to the bipartisan Presidential Commission tasked with conducting a comprehensive review of the care the U S  Department of Defense and the Veterans Administration is providing wounded servicemen and women       In light of a number of articles that were printed earlier this month detailing the appalling conditions at Walter Reed Army Medical Center  the Bush Administration has been asked to name an independent commission to investigate all post combat medical facilities and recommend changes  Durbin commended the President for working to establish the Commission  saying veterans  health   both physical and mental  is of paramount importance        Durbin said Duckworth had insight and experiences that would add greatly to the work of the commission  Durbin said  Tammy Duckworth is uniquely qualified to serve on your Commission and would provide valuable  firsthand information regarding the care given soldiers at our country s military and veterans  hospitals       O n November 12  2004 as she was co piloting a Black Hawk helicopter north of Baghdad  a rocket propelled grenade struck the cockpit of her aircraft and exploded        she  spent months recovering at Walter Reed Army Medical Center  faced multiple surgeries  extensive rehab and continuous upgrades of her prostheses        Duckworth is currently serving as the Acting Director of the Illinois Department of Veteran s Affairs  She joined the ROTC in 1990 and was commissioned to the Army Reserve in 1992 in Illinois   In 2004  she was deployed to Iraq as a Major in the Illinois Army National Guard   After being wounded in Iraq she received care and therapy at Walter Reed Army Medical Center        In addition to her military experience and firsthand knowledge of our nation s military hospitals  Tammy Duckworth s education and civilian career would make her a valuable member of your Commission   Durbin wrote   I urge you to give the highest consideration to Ms  Tammy Duckworth as you work to appoint members to this important Presidential Commission
