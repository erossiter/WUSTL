     DURBIN  MORE THAN  4 MILLION FOR ILLINOIS PROJECTS APPROVED BY APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          7 14 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN  MORE THAN  4 MILLION FOR ILLINOIS PROJECTS APPROVED BY APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Friday  July 14  2006   Washington  DC    U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL   today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee  of which he is a member  has approved  4 4 million for projects in the state of Illinois in the Commerce Justice Science spending bill         The Fiscal Year  FY  2007 appropriations bill for the U S  Departments of Commerce  Justice and State includes funding for the following projects in Illinois      Department of Justice   Family Based Treatment for Meth   4 million for The Pregnant and Parenting Women Who Are Methamphetamine Offenders Program   This is the first time the new program  established in this year   s renewed version of the PATRIOT Act  has received any funding   The Department of Justice will offer competitive grants to states  which can use the money to provide mental health and substance abuse treatment for female offenders who are addicted to methamphetamine if these women are pregnant or have small children   This family based approach to treatment has proven to be effective at higher rates than more traditional substance abuse programs   The treatment dollars will complement drug control efforts in the fight against methamphetamine  Methamphetamine is a growing problem in the state of Illinois   The number of methamphetamine labs seized by law enforcement authorities in Illinois has more than doubled between 2000 and 2005   from 403 labs to nearly 1 000   Since 1997  the quantity of methamphetamines seized annually by the Illinois State Police has increased more than tenfold      Department of Commerce   Shedd Aquarium Center for the Great Lakes  Chicago   250 000 for Great Lakes conservation education   The Great Lakes initiative will target eight states and two Canadian provinces with awareness and education messages about the value and vulnerability of the Great Lakes and the need to protect against habitat loss  shoreline degradation  water diversion  invasive species and declining water quality   The initiative includes public advertising on Great Lakes preservation and planned exhibits at public festivals and events   Funding will also strengthen environmental preservation displays and programming at Shedd Aquarium and in the region        Small Business Incubator  McLean County     150 000 for a business incubator and university research park for small and medium size businesses in McLean County   The City of Bloomington  Town of Normal  Illinois State University and the Economic Development Council of the Bloomington Normal Area have identified the need for a business incubator as an integral part of the region   s economic growth strategy   The planned space will be supported with administrative assistance  access to capital and technical assistance   ISU will provide faculty and staff to ensure hands on support for research and applications for industry and to foster business applications for academic research   The facility will serve start up enterprises  medium sized businesses that would benefit from the technical and administrative assistance and academic research
