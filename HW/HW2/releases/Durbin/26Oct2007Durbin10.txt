     Durbin Bill To Help End Hunger Added To Farm Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                10 26 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Bill To Help End Hunger Added To Farm Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Friday  October 26  2007        United States Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today announced that the language of his bill  The Hunger Free Communities Act  was included in the 2007 Farm Bill  The Farm Bill was approved unanimously by the Senate Agriculture Committee and now heads to the full Senate for debate     Durbins proposal increases federal resources available to locally based organizations working to end hunger in their communities  Additionally  the legislation reaffirms the goal of cutting food insecurity and ending hunger in the United States and requires the Secretary of Agriculture to provide status reports on the progress toward that goal    Hunger is not a partisan issue  and we now have the opportunity to forge a new bipartisan partnership  committed to addressing hunger in communities all across our country  Durbin said  During the 1960s and 1970s  under both Democratic and Republican Administrations  our country put in place programs that substantially reduced the number of people who struggle to feed their families  Unfortunately  today hunger and food insecurity has been on the rise  That is why having the Hunger Free Communities Act included in the Farm Bill is so important    The bipartisan Hunger Free Communities bill was first introduced on April 19  2007 and is cosponsored by U S  Senators Pete Domenici  R NM   Russell Feingold  D WI   Edward Kennedy  D MA   Richard Lugar  R IN   Blanche Lincoln  D AR   Bill Nelson  D FL   Barack Obama  D IL   Jack Reed  D RI   Gordon Smith  R OR   Ron Wyden  D OR   The House version of the bill was introduced by Congressman Jim McGovern  D MA  and cosponsored by Congresswoman Jo Ann Emerson  R MO     According to the U S  Department of Agricultures most recent report on hunger and food insecurity  13 4 million households  representing 38 1 million people  nearly 14 million of whom are children  experienced hunger or the risk of hunger  This represents more than one in ten households in the United States     The Hunger Free Communities Act of 2007 directs the Secretary of the Department of Agriculture to report on national hunger and food security data  the progress toward the goals of cutting food insecurity and ending hunger  and the impact of hunger on the health of our communities  It also requires the Secretary to provide recommendations to further reduce hunger     The legislation also directs Congress to establish an anti hunger grant program  the first of its kind  with an emphasis on assessing hunger in individual communities and promoting cooperation and collaboration among local anti hunger groups  The bill will also create an additional grant program to expand the efforts and build the infrastructure of local anti hunger groups  especially those in underserved areas and areas where rates of food insecurity  hunger  poverty  or unemployment are higher than the national average  These public private partnerships will be funded through a  50 million a year  five year grant program to help communities reduce  and ultimately eliminate  hunger
