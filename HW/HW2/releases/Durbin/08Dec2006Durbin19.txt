                                                                                                                                                                                          Friday  December 8  2006    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  has been invited to be the special guest of Dr  Muhammad Yunus when he accepts the Nobel Peace Prize this weekend in Olso  Norway   While most of the Nobel Prizes are awarded in Stockholm  Sweden  the Peace Prize ceremony traditionally takes place in Olso on December 10th  the anniversary of the death of Alfred Nobel  founder of the Nobel Prize       The Nobel Peace Prize for 2006 is being awarded to Dr  Yunus and the Grameen Bank for their efforts to eliminate poverty in the world and promote economic and social development          Dr  Muhammad Yunus has given hope to many through his Grameen Bank     the people   s bank   He believed if you loaned the poorest people in the world a small sum of money that you could change their lives dramatically and they would repay the loan     said Durbin      Thirty years later  Dr  Yunus s theory of microenterprise is now a phenomenon which has touched 100 million people around the world   It is hard to think of any single idea in our lifetime which has changed the lives of so many people          Durbin first met Dr  Yunus in Bangladesh and has worked with him for more than ten years to advance microcredit and economic development in the world   s poorest nations  One of the greatest successes in recent years is microcredit programs targeted toward poor women   Most microcredit programs have repayment rates greater than 95 percent and have helped many women change their lives and those of their families          Dr  Muhammad Yunus believes overcoming poverty is not just a gesture of charity  it is an act of justice  It is the protection of a fundamental human right  the right to dignity and a decent life   He is truly deserving of the Nobel Peace Prize and I am honored to call him a friend     said Durbin
