     DURBIN SAYS SCIENCE AND POLITICS DON T MIX  SENATE APPROVES AMENDMENT TO ENSURE SCIENTIFIC INTEGRITY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             10 27 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN SAYS SCIENCE AND POLITICS DON T MIX  SENATE APPROVES AMENDMENT TO ENSURE SCIENTIFIC INTEGRITY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Thursday  October 27  2005           The United States Senate passed a provision by U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  to ensure that the information provided to the American people by the federal government is based on honest  accurate and objective scientific research  not political ideology       The Durbin amendment  which was included in the Labor  HHS  Education Appropriations bill  prohibits the use of federal funds to ask candidates for appointment to scientific advisory committees to disclose their voting history  their political affiliation  or their opinions on unrelated political topics          We are ill served when the federal government picks scientific and medical experts to serve our nation based on politics instead of qualifications   There is no room in America   s research labs for political cronies    Americans must have full faith and confidence in the scientific information that is being provided by federal agencies     Durbin said       Durbin pointed to an example where Dr  William R  Miller  a professor of psychology and psychiatry at the University of New Mexico  was denied a position on the National Advisory Council on Drug Abuse after he admitted that he had not voted for President Bush       Union of Concerned Scientists  UCS  voiced strong support for the Durbin measure   In a letter to Durbin  the organization   s Director for the Scientific Integrity Program wrote     On behalf of our more than 100 000 members and activists  UCS applauds your amendment to the Labor HHS Appropriations bill that bans political litmus tests for federal scientific advisory committee members and blocks the dissemination of deliberately false or misleading scientific information   This important amendment will ensure that the most objective and impartial science informs critical policies affecting the health and welfare of all Americans          When the federal government seeks expert technical advice  it should look for the best possible expertise  It shouldn   t limit itself to only those experts who voted for a particular candidate or who agree with the President   s policy agenda  That   s not how we in our personal lives would go about choosing our doctors  and it shouldn   t be the way our government seeks out expert scientific advice     Durbin said
