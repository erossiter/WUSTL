     DURBIN  SEHBP WOULD MAKE HEALTH INSURANCE AFFORDABLE FOR SMALL BUSINESSES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        4 6 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN  SEHBP WOULD MAKE HEALTH INSURANCE AFFORDABLE FOR SMALL BUSINESSES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Thursday  April 6  2006    WASHINGTON      At a U S  Senate Finance Committee hearing today  U S  Senators Dick Durbin  D IL  and Blanche Lincoln  D AR  said that a bill they   ve introduced would offer small businesses more affordable health care choices for their employees without sacrificing important benefits like diabetes supplies and mammograms        The high cost of health insurance is the top concern for small businesses in Illinois and across America     said Durbin      With today   s coverage options  small businesses are unfairly forced to make a choice between taking care of their employees and paying the bills that keep their doors open   Our Small Employers Health Benefits Program will give employees of small businesses the same kinds of health care choices Members of Congress already have  for less than they pay now  while making it affordable for owners to provide it           The crisis of ever escalating health care costs is not going away  and in fact  it   s getting worse     said Lincoln      Small Businesses know that offering health insurance helps them with recruitment  retention  employee performance  and the overall success of the business   This is something I firmly believe Congress should address right now   Our bill would help our small businesses  the true backbone of our communities  and it would allow us to begin to address the very real needs of the working uninsured        Both Democratic Senators said their bill titled the    The Small Employers Health Benefits Program Act of 2006      SEHBP  would allow small businesses with up to 100 employees to band together for lower health care prices by pooling their purchasing power and spreading their risk over a large number of participants   The two Senators have worked on their SEHBP proposal together since 2003 and said that their bill is modeled after the successful    Federal Employees Health Benefits Program    which has provided health care benefits to federal workers for over 40 years     Durbin and Lincoln touted their bill at a Finance Committee hearing today examining various approaches to small business health insurance   A competing plan would bypass state mandated coverage for many important health needs and allow insurance companies to define benefits packages     SEHBP  which combines the best of what the government can do with the best that the private sector can do  has been endorsed by and array of organizations including   the American Academy of Family Physicians  the American Medical Association  the American Osteopathic Association  the Consumers Union  the Federation of American Hospitals  the National Association of Community Health Centers  the National Association of Women Business Owners  the Small Business Majority  the American Diabetes Association  the American Cancer Society  the National Mental Health Association  the American Academy of Pediatrics  and the National Women   s Law Center
