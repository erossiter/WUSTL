     DURBIN HAILS RENEWAL OF VOTING RIGHTS ACT BY U S  SENATE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         7 20 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN HAILS RENEWAL OF VOTING RIGHTS ACT BY U S  SENATE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Thursday  July 20  2006          U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today issued the following statement after the Senate unanimously voted to renew the Voting Rights Act  which was set to expire in 2007        The Voting Rights Acts is not just another law   On the day that President Lyndon Johnson signed the original Voting Rights Act of 1965  he said it was one the most monumental laws in the history of American freedom   Today  our generation renewed its commitment to the same basic values and principles that guided this Congress to pass this historic bill in 1965           Some have argued that this debate and this bill were unnecessary because discrimination does not exist in America today   But in hearings in the Senate and House  witnesses testified that discrimination does exist   We need to step back as a nation and ask ourselves   Why is it that so many voting machines in cities where the poorest people live don   t work well   Why is it that those who are challenged time and time again turn out to be the poor  the dispossessed           Voter discrimination continues and it remains a threat to American democracy  The right to vote is the primary right in any democratic nation   Today  the Senate renewed its commitment to protecting that right in America
