     Durbin Introduces Bill to Apply Greater Economic Pressure on Sudan                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               6 7 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Introduces Bill to Apply Greater Economic Pressure on Sudan                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Thursday  June 7  2007       United States Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  introduced bipartisan legislation today that increases the pressure on the Government of Sudan to take action to end the humanitarian crisis in Darfur  The Sudan Disclosure and Enforcement Act will require US listed companies that operate in the Sudanese oil industry to file more detailed disclosures with the Securities and Exchange Commission  SEC   increase the civil and criminal penalties for violating American sanctions  and authorize greater resources to track Sudanese economic activity and to pursue those who violate sanctions  Senators Brownback  Dodd  Klobuchar  Mikulski  Nelson  FL   Obama and Wyden are among the cosponsors of the bill  This is the first time in history that we have called a genocide for what it is  But recognizing this crisis isnt enough  We must act  Durbin said  This bill can make a difference  It not only provides more transparency  making it easier for individuals to chose whether to support companies dealing in Sudan  but it dramatically increases penalties for violating American sanctions  Its time we show the world that we are no longer going tacitly support a regime engaged in genocide  Specifically  Durbin s bill would  Express the Sense of the Congress that the international community should put pressure on the Government of Sudan to convince that regime that the world will not allow this crisis to continue    Require more detailed SEC disclosures by U S  listed companies that operate in the Sudanese petroleum sector    Create a true deterrent by increasing penalties for violating economic sanctions from the current level of  10 000 to  250 000 for civil sanctions and up to  1 million for criminal sanctions    Require the Administration to report on the effectiveness of the current sanctions regime and recommend other steps that Congress could take to help end the crisis  and   Authorize greater resources for the Office of Foreign Assets Control to strengthen its capabilities in tracking Sudanese economic activity and pursuing sanctions violators   This is the fourth initiative Durbin has undertaken this year to bring an end to the killing in Darfur  This spring  the Senate passed another bipartisan bill introduced by Senator Durbin  The Genocide Accountability Act  which closed the legal loopholes that prevented the Justice Department from prosecuting those whove committed genocide outside the United States  In March  Durbin sent a letter to the President asking him to introduce a UN resolution that would impose multilateral economic sanctions of the Sudanese Government  Durbin followed with an additional letter in May and President Bush has since announced his intention to move forward with such a plan  Senator Durbin became the Chairman of the first ever congressional committee dealing specifically with human rights this January  The Senate Subcommittee on Human Rights and the Law has jurisdiction over all human rights law and polices  focusing intently on issues of genocide  human trafficking  war crimes and torture  The committees first hearing  this February  was on the genocide in Darfur  Senator Durbin is also the Chairman of the Senate Financial Services and General Government Appropriations Subcommittee which has jurisdiction over the Treasury Department and the Securities and Exchange Commission
