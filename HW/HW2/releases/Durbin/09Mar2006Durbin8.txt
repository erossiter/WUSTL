     DEMOCRATS VOW TO KEEP AMERICA COMPETITIVE IN THE GLOBAL ECONOMY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  3 9 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DEMOCRATS VOW TO KEEP AMERICA COMPETITIVE IN THE GLOBAL ECONOMY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Thursday  March 9  2006           Senate Democrats today vowed to keep America competitive in the global marketplace and called for a real investment by the Federal government in research funding  education  job training  renewable and clean energy sources and small business development       U S  Senators Dick Durbin  D IL   Byron Dorgan  D ND   Maria Cantwell  D WA  and Jeff Bingaman  D NM  said that the President   s budget does not match the public commitment he made to Americans during his State of the Union Address when he unveiled his American Competitiveness Initiative   The budget the President submitted to Congress fails to provide American researchers  entrepreneurs  and workers the tools necessary to keep America   s edge in scientific and technological innovations          We know the solutions that would put America on the right track and continue to keep our country the innovative and technological leader of the globe  but this Administration has refused to make them a national priority     said Senator Durbin      It is in our national interest to put more math and science teachers in our classrooms and to expand research that would end our dependence on foreign oil   We believe these necessary investments will make America more prosperous and secure in the 21st Century             At a time when American jobs are being shipped overseas and our trade deficit is at an all time record  it is unbelievable that President Bush offers up these misplaced priorities in this budget  Instead of making Americans more competitive in the global economy  this report makes clear that the Bush budget would do just the opposite by proposing cuts that would hurt workers  students and entrepreneurs     said Senator Dorgan   Senator Cantwell said     America needs an aggressive national strategy to create high quality jobs and keep our country on the cutting edge of innovation   Reliable  affordable energy sources are a must if we want jumpstart our economy and help America   s businesses grow   Businesses need relief from today   s sky high energy prices and are counting on us to make affordable  home grown alternative fuels a reality   We need to harness American ingenuity and expertise to break our dangerous over dependence on foreign oil meet the demands of an ever changing global economy             If we   re going to continue to create the jobs of the future and retain our competitive edge in the global economy  our nation needs to make a serious commitment to funding education initiatives as well as scientific research and development   Unfortunately  the budget released by the White House does neither     Senator Bingaman said      This is an issue of tremendous importance  and it   s my hope that Congress will provide the leadership that the administration has not
