     SENATE APPROVES DURBIN MEASURE TO CUT THROUGH RED TAPE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           9 15 2005                                                                                                                                                                            SENATE APPROVES DURBIN MEASURE TO CUT THROUGH RED TAPE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Thursday  September 15  2005    Washington  D C       The Senate today approved an amendment by U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  that will help put back to work medical professionals who fled Louisiana and Mississippi during Hurricane Katrina    The amendment will allow licensed medical professionals from Louisiana or Mississippi and who serve Medicare  Medicaid  SCHIP and Indian Health Service patients to practice for 90 days in their new state while seeking state licensure   The amendment was added to the State  Justice and Commerce Appropriations bill        Thousands of medical professionals who fled their homes in Louisiana and Mississippi are now settling in Illinois and other parts of the country  but it can take months to obtain a license to practice in their new state   We must get them back to work immediately     Durbin said     Durbin added     Facilities around the country  like Comprehensive Therapeutics in Glenview  Illinois  want to hire physical therapists  doctors  nurses  dentists and pharmacists who have fled Louisiana and Mississippi   This amendment cuts through the red tape and gets these doctors and nurses back seeing patients and healing people in Illinois and across the nation        To qualify for the 90 day waiver  medical professionals must have a valid license from Louisiana or Mississippi  be in good standing in the state  have applied for a license in their new state and are not barred from practicing in that State  The amendment is time limited and specific to Hurricane Katrina   The waiver expires in May of 2006 and no person can obtain more than one waiver  or receive a waiver of more than 90 days     Medical professionals who are unable to produce documentation of their licensure in Louisiana or Mississippi must sign an affidavit attesting to their licensure     Durbin   s measure is endorsed by the Acute Long term Hospital Association  the Alliance for Quality Nursing Home Care  the American Academy of Family Physicians  the American Association of Homes and Services for the Aging  the American Dental Association  the American Health Care Association  the American Nurses Association  the American Physical Therapy Association  Federation of State Medical Boards and the Long term Care Pharmacy Alliance
