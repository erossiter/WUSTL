                                                                                                                                                                                            Wednesday  June 27  2007   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin today met with a group of newly elected City of Chicago Aldermen and Park District Officials to discuss the Urban Park and Recreation Recovery Program  UPARR  and other city issues   The City of Chicago and the federal government have had a long  successful history of working together  said Durbin  I congratulated the newly elected Aldermen today and invited them to share their ideas on how to maintain this close partnership   UPARR is a program run by the National Park Service that gives grants to cities and metropolitan counties to help meet the communitys park and recreation needs  For over 30 years  this program helped cities and metropolitan counties to meet pressing park and recreation needs  in part  by providing matching funds  During that time  Illinois received nearly  16 million in federal grants under the UPARR program  The program has not received federal funding since 2002  Senator Durbin has been active in trying to reinstate funding for the program   The Aldermen that attended the meeting today include  Michelle Harris  8th Ward   Toni Foulkes  15th Ward   Willie Cochran  20th Ward   Scott Waguespack  32nd Ward   Sharon Denise Dixon  24th Ward   Bob Fioretti  2nd Ward   Pat Dowell  3rd Ward   Lona Lane  18th Ward   Carrie Austin  34th Ward  and Joann Thompson  16th Ward
