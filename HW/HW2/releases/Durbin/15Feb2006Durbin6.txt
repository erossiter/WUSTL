     DURBIN  DEMOCRATS OFFER PRESCRIPTION FOR CHANGE TO MEDICARE DRUG BILL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     2 15 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN  DEMOCRATS OFFER PRESCRIPTION FOR CHANGE TO MEDICARE DRUG BILL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Wednesday  February 15  2006           Calling it a    special interest fiasco     U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today joined U S  Senator Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and U S  Representative John Dingell  D MI  to offer a    Prescription for Change    to the unpopular and complex Medicare drug benefit        Illinois seniors wanted a Medicare benefit that would lower their drug prices     Durbin said      What they have today is a confusing benefit  a law that prohibits Medicare from negotiating with pharmaceutical companies and a plan that guarantees drug companies billions of dollars in profits    Medicare Part D is a reward for drug companies and a disaster for America   s seniors        Durbin noted that the Medicare drug benefit included a  10 billion    slush fund    for HMOs  and that at least 13 former Republican members of Congress and staffers who wrote the law now work or represent the pharmaceutical industry   Durbin also pointed to recent comments by a leading academic expert on the industry who forecasted that drug companies will make as much as  40 billion over the next ten years in windfall profits     At the same time  Durbin said  states like Illinois have had to take immediate action to help cover low income seniors  while hundreds of thousands of seniors across the country are being overcharged and discover that their plan does not cover their most important prescriptions        America   s seniors are paying a special interest surcharge for this fiasco     Durbin added     House and Senate Democrats  including Durbin  will hold a series of events next week on the controversial drug benefit program  and will offer seniors and persons with disabilities a series of legislative proposals to improve it     Durbin and others support providing the Department of Health and Human Services with the authority to negotiate for better prices from the pharmaceutical industry and to give seniors the opportunity to receive benefits directly from Medicare rather than through HMOs   A recent study found that drug prices offered by ten leading private drug plans under the Medicare drug program are more than 80 percent higher than the prices negotiated by the Veterans    Administration     Legislation is also pending before Congress to ensure that seniors and people with disabilities immediately receive the drugs they need at prices they can afford under the terms of coverage they have been promised    And the Medicare Informed Choice Act Legislation will give seniors and people with disabilities the time they need to navigate the confusing enrollment process and allow them to change plans once during this first chaotic year        With more than 130 different drug plans in Illinois  many seniors are frustrated and angry   We must change the benefit to make it fair  reliable and affordable     Durbin said
