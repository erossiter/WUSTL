     DURBIN PUSHES LEGISLATION TO AVERT ADDITIONAL SHORTAGE OF NURSES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 3 22 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN PUSHES LEGISLATION TO AVERT ADDITIONAL SHORTAGE OF NURSES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Thursday  March 22  2007    WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  along with Senator Ted Kennedy  D MA  and Senator Chris Dodd  D CT  today cosponsored legislation to amend the National Labor Relations Act  NLRA  to clarify a legal distinction between supervisors and employees       Under current law  supervisors cannot organize a union or collectively bargain   In October of 2006  the National Labor Relations Board issued a broad interpretation of the term supervisor  which will lead to more employees being deemed supervisors  The new rule will have a serious detrimental effect on workers in such occupations as manufacturing and nursing   The legislation introduced today is known as the Re empowerment of Skilled and Professional Employees and Construction Tradesworkers  RESPECT  Act        If Congress does not act  8 million workers in the United States will no longer have the right to organize   said Durbin    Illinois is one of many states suffering from nursing shortages   With 40 000 nurses in the state  many will be unable to join unions and collectively bargain  salary levels will be at risk and working conditions will be in question  driving more people to leave the profession and discouraging others from becoming nurses        In 2001  the Supreme Court ruled that the interpretation of the term supervisor would be left to the National Labor Relations Board to determine what it should mean in the context of the NLRA   Last year  several cases came before the Board to determine whether 1  nurses acting as supervisors in a hospital  2  nurses acting as supervisors in a long term care facility and 3  load men acting as supervisors in a manufacturing facility should be deemed supervisors   The ruling of the National Labor Relations Board expanded the term  supervisor        Durbin noted that the ruling of the National Labor Relations Board is contrary to the Congressional intent of the Taft Hartley Act of 1947 which defined the term  supervisor  more narrowly   Under the new ruling  over 8 million workers  including over 800 000 nurses nationwide  will be stripped of their right to join to join a union   Building and construction trades  broadcasting  energy  shipping and other health care industries will also be affected   In the current economic and labor structure  skilled mid level workers who make decisions and issue instructions are quite common and these employees should not be prohibited from their basic right to organize       Last July  Durbin signed onto a letter urging the National Labor Relations Board to hold oral arguments before ruling on the case   The letter  stressing the ramifications that a broad interpretation would have for millions of workers  was initiated by Senator Kennedy  D MA  and signed by several Senate Democrats   The National Labor Relations Board refused to hear oral arguments   Durbin said his legislation will replace the NLRB s flawed ruling and restore the original intent of the law
