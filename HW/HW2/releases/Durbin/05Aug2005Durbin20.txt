     DURBIN ANNOUNCES  5 2 MILLION FOR DES PLAINES RIVER FLOOD CONTROL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                8 5 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN ANNOUNCES  5 2 MILLION FOR DES PLAINES RIVER FLOOD CONTROL                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Friday  August 5  2005    MT  PROSPECT  IL      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today announced  5 2 million in federal funding has been approved by the Senate for flood damage reduction along the Des Plaines River   The funds were included in the Senate fiscal year 2006 appropriations bill for the U S  Department of Energy and the Army Corps of Engineers     Durbin said that  4 million will go toward Phase I of the Des Plaines River Flood Damage Reduction project to complete construction of the Palatine Road floodwall  initiate construction on remaining portions of Levee 37 and for the expansion of Big Bend Lake    1 2 million in Phase II funding will be used to conduct hydrology and hydraulic studies  environmental and economic studies and to formulate plans to control flooding in tributaries of the Des Plaines River        The Upper Des Plaines River Watershed project is one of the most important flood control initiatives in the State of Illinois     said Durbin  a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee      This funding will protect homes and businesses and allow expanded economic development in the region   I worked with Senator Obama to ensure that the U S  Army Corps of Engineers continues to fund this project  including Levee 37
