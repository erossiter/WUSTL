    8 3 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN AND OBAMA ANNOUNCE  3 MILLION FOR SOUTH ARSENAL ROAD IMPROVEMENTS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Wednesday  August 3  2005    JOLIET  IL      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  and Barack Obama  D IL  today announced  3 million in federal funding for improvements to South Arsenal Road in Island City Industrial Park   The funding is part of  215 million Durbin and Obama have secured for Illinois in the TEA 3 transportation bill recently passed in Congress        The South Arsenal Road was built for farm traffic     Durbin said      The road needs to be overhauled to handle commercial traffic in Island City Industrial Park     Durbin said      The funds we are bringing today will help assure Island City Industrial Park will be an engine of economic development in the Joliet Area           Securing this funding for South Arsenal Road is the result of the Illinois delegation working together in a bipartisan manner to put the interests of the people of Illinois first     Obama said     I am proud that I was able to help write this bill and work with my colleagues to make ensure that Illinois will continue to have safe and efficient roads  rails and bridges        The South Arsenal Development Authority is developing Island City Industrial Park on the southern parcel of the Joliet Arsenal   JADA has already committed  8 million to demolish buildings left from the arsenal land and extend water and sewer lines on the property     Island City Industrial Park is expected to create 1 500 permanent jobs in addition to 8 000 jobs generated at CenterPoint Intermodal Center  which borders on South Arsenal Road   Thousands of construction jobs are also expected to be created     Will County is the fastest growing county in Illinois and the tenth fastest growing county across the nation     The TEA 3 transportation bill has a five year life span   Under the transportation bill passed in 1998  Illinois received an average of  927 million each year  Under the current Senate bill  Illinois will receive an average of  1 236 billion a year  or  309 million more per year  Over the five year life of the bill  that is  1 545 billion more for Illinois  an increase of 33 34 percent more each year than under TEA 21      Under this bill  Illinois transit will receive  2 1 billion in formula funds  In the previous Transportation bill  Illinois received only  1 9 billion over six years  Over a comparable 6 year period  Illinois would receive  2 467 billion     128  over TEA 21   s level     Obama and Durbin said that for every  1 billion spent on transportation infrastructure  it is estimated that 24 000 new jobs are supported  By providing Illinois with  1 2 billion per year in transportation funding  this bill will support tens of thousands of new jobs for Illinois families        It   s a time to put people to work at improving our roads and our bridges and our public transportation so that Illinois can still be the crossroads of a 21st century America     said Obama of the bill     This bill will provide much needed and long overdue investments in transportation in Illinois           This transportation bill makes a significant investment in highways and transit infrastructure that is so important to communities across the state     said Durbin     Senator Obama helped write this bill and made sure Illinois received its fair share of highway and transit funds  I was pleased to work with him and our Illinois colleagues in the House to provide the funding needed for our roads  bridges  and transit systems
