     DURBIN CALLS ON DOD TO CHANGE WIND FARM POLICY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   6 16 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN CALLS ON DOD TO CHANGE WIND FARM POLICY                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Friday  June 16  2006    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today introduced a measure that would require the Department of Defense  DOD  to examine new wind farm facilities on a case by case basis  The amendment would reverse a recent DOD policy to halt the construction of wind farms within the line of site of a military radar installation   The measure would also require the DOD to submit a report by December 30th  2006 that would create a centralized process for evaluating wind farms and would outline possible mitigation techniques that would allow military radars and wind facilities to coexist   Two weeks ago  Durbin and 5 other Midwestern senators questioned the policy of halting wind farm development nationwide          The decision by the Bush Administration to issue a blanket order to halt windmill construction throughout the country is unnecessary and may have serious negative economic effects     said Durbin      If the Administration believes there is a legitimate concern  we should examine wind farms on a case by case basis and only take action against those that pose a real threat to radar installations          The President recently signed into law a measure which called on the DOD to submit a report to Congress on the effects of wind farms on military readiness  specifically investigating whether windmill facilities interfere with military radar   While the report is being completed  the FAA has issued    Notice of Presumed Hazard    letters to more than a dozen wind farms and facilities in Illinois  Wisconsin  North Dakota and South Dakota  thereby preventing these projects from moving forward          There are military installations  throughout the country  currently operating within range of wind farms without radar interference problems     said Durbin      We should not put an unnecessary hold on one of our country   s most valuable sources of clean alternative energy   DOD needs to respond quickly on how we can mitigate any interference with military radar so that we don   t stand in the way of wind energy expansion          On June 2  Senators Durbin and Feingold questioned the Administration s decision to delay wind farm facility construction and sent letters to the DOD and FAA expressing concern with the government order to contest installation of wind farm facilities across the country   In the letter  the senators stressed the harmful impact of the temporary halt on wind facility construction  noting that     p rohibiting  even temporarily  the development of wind energy facilities within those areas would be a considerable setback for efforts to increase our country   s energy independence          In addition to Durbin and Feingold  the letters were signed by four other senators including Herb Kohl  D WI   Kent Conrad  D ND   Byron Dorgan  D ND  and Barack Obama  D IL
