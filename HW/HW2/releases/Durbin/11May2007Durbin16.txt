     Durbin Proposes Legislation to Address Rising Gas Prices and Promote Energy Independence                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         5 11 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Proposes Legislation to Address Rising Gas Prices and Promote Energy Independence                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Friday  May 11  2007    CHICAGO  IL    With gas prices beginning to climb  U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  said Senate Democrats plan to schedule votes in the next few weeks on the National Energy and Environmental Security Act  legislation that expands the use of alternative fuels  increases the energy efficiency of both buildings and cars  and prevents price gouging  profiteering and market manipulation       Durbin also expressed optimism on the prospects of passing a bill that sets new fuel economy standards   Durbin has long supported setting a new ambitious and attainable fuel efficiency standard for cars and certain light trucks  including sport utility vehicles   The bill  often referred to as CAFE  Corporate Average Fuel Economy   is designed to get the United States back on track toward a cleaner  more efficient and less oil dependent energy future   A similar bill with the same goal received Senate Committee approval earlier this week and Durbin believes the legislation has a good chance to pass the full Senate this year  though the standard in the bill is not as strong as he would like        The most efficient way to lower prices is to reduce demand   said Durbin    Continued reliance on foreign sources of oil  in a world with increasing competition for those limited resources  is a dangerous future for America   We need to reduce our dependence  first by increasing the fuel economy of our cars   It s a solution I ve been advocating for years   It s the right thing to do and the momentum for real reform is building   said Durbin       On Thursday  Durbin introduced the Strategic Gasoline and Fuel Reserve Act of 2007   The legislation would require the Department of Energy to hold and manage 45 million barrels of unleaded gasoline  21 million barrels of diesel fuel and 7 5 million barrels of jet fuel to be used in times of supply shortages   shortages that adversely impact the U S  economy       The U S  currently maintains a Strategic Petroleum Reserve  but without refinery capacity  putting crude oil on the market does little to provide gasoline to consumers and alleviate high prices at pump  as gas prices are largely affected by tight supplies       This reserve  which represents about a 4 day supply based on current demand  would provide a price buffer when pipelines or refinery outages occur  helping to mitigate price spikes that bite consumers in the pocketbook       In the past  the Consumer Federation of America has urged Congress to create a national fuel reserve   They recognize that American households who own cars will consume over 100 billion gallons of gasoline this year   costing them over  200 billion at the pump
