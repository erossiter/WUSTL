     DURBIN AMENDMENT ADDS  50 MILLION FOR PEACEKEEPING OPERATIONS IN DARFUR                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          4 4 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN AMENDMENT ADDS  50 MILLION FOR PEACEKEEPING OPERATIONS IN DARFUR                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Tuesday  April 4  2006           Saying that the world must be committed to ending the genocide in Darfur  Sudan  U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  was successful today in increasing by  50 million the Administration   s request for peacekeeping operations in the region   The amendment was approved by a voice vote in the Senate Appropriations Committee        Estimates show that between 200 000 and 400 000 people have died in Darfur and two and a half million people are displaced     Durbin said      Women have been raped  and children have been brutalized in untold numbers   Approximately 200 000 people are in refugee camps in Chad  and the situation in that country is rapidly deteriorating           We have witnessed the genocide and have condemned it   Now  we must act to halt it before another innocent life is lost     Durbin added     Durbin noted that the President requested  123 million to help maintain the African Union  AU  mission  the only force on the ground in Darfur   Durbin said that while he supports the President   s request  he believes it is not sufficient to help the AU meet its goals  while it awaits the arrival of United Nations peacekeepers   That is why Durbin  joined by Senator Patrick Leahy  D VT   offered an amendment to the Supplemental Appropriations bill to add  50 million to the President   s request for peacekeeping operations  increasing the amount to  173 million        At last  the United Nations is moving toward action     Durbin said      The UN is planning and preparing for a peacekeeping force that is expected to be on the ground by the end of the year   In the meantime  we must ensure that the AU mission in Darfur has the resources it needs until it joins forces with the UN        Durbin   s amendment was included in the Supplemental Appropriations bill for emergency funding for the war in Iraq and Katrina recovery   The bill  which Durbin supported  was approved by the Senate Committee today   The House has already passed the Supplemental Appropriations bill  which included the additional  50 million in funding for Darfur as well        The AU has played an important role  but stopping genocide is not their responsibility alone  it   s the world   s   This amendment will provide the funds to support the transition to a UN mission with AU participation     Durbin concluded
