     Durbin Announces  100 000 Grant for Emerald Ash Borer Initiative                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 6 12 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Announces  100 000 Grant for Emerald Ash Borer Initiative                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Tuesday  June 12  2007        U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today commended the USDA Forest Services decision to award a Wood Education and Resource Center  WERC  grant to the Illinois Conservation Foundation  The grant will provide  100 000 over two years to help Illinois communities and industries combating the threat of the Emerald Ash Borer  The Emerald Ash Borer is a bright green beetle that kills trees by burrowing into their bark and destroying the trees ability to bring water from the roots to upper branches  Infected trees usually begin to die within two to three years  Illinois has more than 131 million ash trees  comprising about 20 percent of all of the trees in the Chicago area  said Durbin  In the past few years  this insect has killed tens of millions of trees throughout the Midwest forcing communities to spend large sums of money to dispose of the infected trees  The funding for the Ash Reclamation Project will help bring together businesses and industries in the Chicago area to maximize our ability to utilize urban ash trees  The grant will help fund the Ash Reclamation Project administered by the Illinois Conservation Foundation  The project will make connections among businesses and industries involved in urban forestry  sustainable development  economic development and manufacturing in the Chicagoland area  The negative impact of losing urban ash trees to Emerald Ash Borer can be mitigated by utilizing the substantial supply of valuable wood that is currently going to waste  The goal of the project is to create thegroundwork needed to develop a successful urban ash utilization initiative in Illinois  In February 2007  Senator Durbin wrote to Steve Milauskas  Director of the USDA Forest Service Wood Education and Resource Center in support of the grant submitted by the Illinois Conservation Foundation  Senator Barack Obama  D IL  cosigned the letter along with eight members of the Illinois Congressional Delegation including Representatives Donald Manzullo  R IL   Luis Gutierrez  D IL   Ray LaHood  R IL   Phil Hare  D IL   Danny Davis  D IL   Mark Kirk  R IL   Jan Schakowsky  D IL  and Tim Johnson  R IL   The Emerald Ash Borer was first sighted in 2002 in Michigan and first discovered in Illinois with a Kane County spotting in mid June 2006  On July 11  2006  the Illinois Department of Agriculture announced its second discovery of the ash borer in the Village of Wilmette in Cook County  Infected trees usually begin to die within two to three years  Since its initial discovery in Michigan  forestry experts estimate that the Emerald Ash Borer has been responsible for the devastation of more than 12 million ash trees in Michigan  Ohio  and Indiana  On June 13th  immediately following the first sighting of the ash borer in Illinois  Durbin called on the USDA to develop a plan to curb the spread of this pest Later that month  Durbinhelped secure 1 5 million to combat and prevent the spread of the Emerald Ash Borer Durbinalso sent a letter late last summerurging that certain affected locations in Illinois be declared federal emergency quarantine areas so that they canapply for low interest emergency loans to help eradicate the Emerald Ash Borer
