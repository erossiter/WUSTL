     Durbin Secures an Additional  40 Million to Fight AIDS  Tuberculosis and Malaria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 6 29 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Secures an Additional  40 Million to Fight AIDS  Tuberculosis and Malaria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Friday  June 29  2007  WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL   cofounder of the Congressional Global AIDS Emergency Task Force  successfully offered an amendment that adds an additional  40 million for the Global Fund to Fight AIDS  Tuberculosis and Malaria to the  550 million already included in the Foreign Operations Appropriations bill  The legislation was approved by the Senate Appropriations Committee yesterday    It is critical that the United States continue to be a leader in the global war against HIV AIDS  said Durbin  This amendment ensures that we commit our fair share to battle this worldwide problem that will challenge us for decades to come  As the worlds wealthiest and most powerful nation  our actions encourage other donor nations to step up and devote additional resources to fight the global AIDS pandemic    Started in 2002  the Global Fund is a critical tool in the fight against these three pandemics that together kill six million people each year  In just five short years  the Global Fund has committed  7 7 billion to 136 countries around the world  It has had notable successes  including 1 1 million people treated with lifesaving AIDS treatment  2 8 million treated for TB  and 30 million bed nets distributed to protect pregnant women and their children from malaria  Roughly 36 million people have died from HIV AIDS since the start of the epidemic and an estimated 74 million have been infected  Every 60 seconds  there are 5 more deaths from AIDS and 9 more infections  Over the next decade  an estimated 50 million more people will contract HIV    On average  every  100 million contribution to the Global Fund will generate the following results    630 000 bed nets to fight malaria  and  150 000 treatments for malaria  and   80 000 highly effective DOTS treatments for TB  and  370 000 people with HIV tests  and  11 000 people with life saving AIDS treatment    Senator Durbin has been a leader in the fight against HIV AIDS ever since making his first trip to Africa in January 2000  Senator Durbin  working with Republican colleagues across the aisle  has steadily helped increase funding for the Global Fund  Last year  the Senate passed an amendment offered by Durbin and former Senator Rick Santorum to increase the United States contribution to the Global Fund to Fight AIDS  Tuberculosis  and Malaria to  866 million in fiscal year 2007  Most recently  on June 8  2007  Durbin sent a letter to the White House this week  renewing his call for increased funding for the Global Fund
