     DURBIN JOINS BIPARTISAN GROUP OF SENATORS IN REAUTHORIZING AMTRAK FOR 6 YEARS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    1 16 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN JOINS BIPARTISAN GROUP OF SENATORS IN REAUTHORIZING AMTRAK FOR 6 YEARS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Tuesday  January 16  2007    WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today joined a bipartisan group of Senate colleagues in introducing The Passenger Rail Investment and Improvement Act of 2007   The legislation  introduced with Senators Frank Lautenberg  D NJ   Trent Lott  R MI   Daniel K  Inouye  D HI   and Ted Stevens  R AL   among others  reauthorizes Amtrak funding for 2007 through 2012        Today  we are introducing a reauthorization bill for one of the most valuable transportation programs for Illinois and the nation     said Durbin      Amtrak provides quick  cost effective  and reliable public ground transportation to 30 communities and more than 3 million people in Illinois   It   s now up to us  as lawmakers  to pass this reauthorization measure and subsequent appropriations bills to provide Amtrak with sufficient funds to keep things on track        Amtrak   s reauthorization expired in 2002   Since that time  no consensus has been reached on Amtrak policy  and Amtrak reform efforts and funding have stalled while Congress and the Bush Administration decide Amtrak   s fate   Passage of an Amtrak Reauthorization measure will help build a consensus around increased Amtrak funding while introducing needed reforms        More than 25 million people rode Amtrak in 2006  the fourth straight year of ridership increases  These record high ridership numbers on Amtrak show the general public feels that Amtrak is a vital element of our national transportation system     said Durbin      This bill shows that the Senate is committed to ensuring that Amtrak maintains and builds its system to meet the growing needs of the traveling public        Last week  Durbin spoke with Amtrak President and CEO Alex Kummant about funding for the three Illinois intrastate lines  as well as initiatives to fund new lines in Rockford  Peoria  and the Quad Cities    During Wednesday   s conversation  Kummant noted that the Amtrak feasibility study on extending Amtrak service to Rockford and the northwest region of Illinois will be completed shortly        The  24 million commitment from the State of Illinois and four new trains from Amtrak have put Illinois Amtrak expansion on the right track     said Durbin     It   s time to take advantage of this momentum  bring northern Illinois on board and Amtrak service to growing regions of our state        Durbin noted that Illinois benefits  both directly and indirectly  from Amtrak jobs and service   An average of 48 Amtrak trains run each day on more than 1 000 miles of track in Illinois   Amtrak employs more than 2 000 Illinois residents and annually spends  56 million for goods and services in the state   Illinois is the central rail hub of the North American continent  bringing 2 5 million passengers through Amtrak   s Midwestern hub in Chicago each year     Durbin recently helped secure a commitment from Amtrak to supply four new trains for additional round   trip service on the three Illinois routes   The 2007 Illinois budget committed to finance the service for these trains by doubling their Amtrak funding to  24 3 million   As result of this state and federal cooperation  new service on the Carbondale  Quincy and St  Louis lines began in October  2006  Durbin is a member of the Senate Transportation Appropriations Subcommittee
