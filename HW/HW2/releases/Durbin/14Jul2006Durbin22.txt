LATEST EMERALD ASH BORER SITE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Friday  July 14  2006    WILMETTE  IL      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  met with village and forestry officials in Wilmette today before touring the neighborhood where the second infestation of the Emerald Ash Borer was confirmed on Thursday by the Illinois Department of Agriculture     Earlier this month  Durbin met with federal  state  county and local officials in St  Charles to discuss their efforts to track and contain the Emerald Ash Borer in Kane County   Durbin  a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  told the officials he recently secured committee approval of  1 5 million to combat and prevent the spread of the Emerald Ash Borer in Illinois   The funding  which is included in the Agriculture Appropriations bill  now needs to be approved by the full Senate        The discovery of the Emerald Ash Borer in Wilmette is troubling news   Illinois has more than 131 million ash trees  with about 20 percent of the trees in the Chicago area     said Durbin     The  1 5 million approved by the Senate committee will help Illinois officials meet this challenge and avert a potentially devastating situation   In the past few years  this insect has killed tens of millions of trees throughout the Midwest  and we need to get ahead of this infestation        In late June  Senator Durbin called on the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  to develop a plan to curb the spread of this pest and report on their progress    In a letter Durbin sent to the head of USDA   s Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service  he wrote     As you know  time is of the essence   I would appreciate receiving a report within 15 days describing the specific plans APHIS will implement to combat EAB in Northern Illinois and the timelines that will guide the agency   s response   Specifically  I would like to know what resources APHIS will commit to this discovery of emerald ash borer        EAB is a bright green beetle that kills trees by burrowing into their bark and destroying the trees    ability to bring water from the roots to upper branches  Infected trees usually begin to die within two to three years   The Emerald Ash Borer was first sighted in 2002 in Michigan  Forestry experts estimate that the Emerald Ash Borer has been responsible for the devastation of than 12 million ash trees in Michigan  Ohio  and Indiana
