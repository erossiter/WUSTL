     DURBIN  MCGOVERN INTRODUCE HUNGER FREE COMMUNITIES ACT OF 2007                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   4 19 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN  MCGOVERN INTRODUCE HUNGER FREE COMMUNITIES ACT OF 2007                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Thursday  April 19  2007        U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  and Congressman Jim McGovern  D MA  today introduced the Hunger Free Communities Act of 2007  legislation to increase federal resources available to locally based organizations working to end hunger in their communities   Additionally  the Act reaffirms the goal of cutting food insecurity and ending hunger in the United States and requires the Secretary of Agriculture to provide status reports on the progress toward that goal   The bipartisan bill is cosponsored by U S  Senators Pete Domenici  R NM   Russell Feingold  D WI   Edward Kennedy  D MA   Richard Lugar  R IN   Blanche Lincoln  D AR   Bill Nelson  D FL   Barack Obama  D IL   Jack Reed  D RI   Gordon Smith  R OR   Ron Wyden  D OR  and Congresswoman Jo Ann Emerson  R MO        According to the U S  Department of Agriculture s most recent report on hunger and food insecurity  13 4 million households   representing 38 1 million people  nearly 14 million children   experienced hunger or the risk of hunger   This represents more than one in ten households in the United States        Hunger is not a partisan issue  and we now have the opportunity to forge a new bipartisan partnership  committed to addressing hunger in communities all across our country   Durbin said    During the 1960s and 1970s  under both Democratic and Republican Administrations  our country put in place programs that substantially reduced the number of people who struggle to feed their families   Unfortunately  today hunger and food insecurity has been on the rise   That is why we introduced The Hunger Free Communities Act         Hunger is a national problem but we must address it community by community   McGovern said    We have the resources and the ability to end hunger  what we need is the political will to do it   Far too many Americans go to bed hungry at night  and the federal government must be a partner in alleviating this crisis   I applaud Senator Durbin for his continuing leadership on this issue  and I look forward to working with him and my House colleagues on this legislation        Title I of the Hunger Free Communities Act of 2007 directs the Secretary of the Department of Agriculture to report on national hunger and food security data  the progress toward the goals of cutting food insecurity and ending hunger  and the impact of hunger on the health of our communities   It also requires the Secretary to provide recommendations to further reduce hunger       Under Title II of the Durbin bill  Congress will establish an anti hunger grant program  the first of its kind  with an emphasis on assessing hunger in individual communities and promoting cooperation and collaboration among local anti hunger groups   The bill will also create an additional grant program to expand the efforts and build the infrastructure of local anti hunger groups  especially those in underserved areas and areas where rates of food insecurity  hunger  poverty  or unemployment are higher than the national average   These public private partnerships will be funded through a  50 million a year  five year grant program to help communities reduce  and ultimately eliminate  hunger
