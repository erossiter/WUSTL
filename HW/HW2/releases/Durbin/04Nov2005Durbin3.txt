     DURBIN  BUDGET THREATENS TO HURT ILLIONIS FARMERS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                11 4 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN  BUDGET THREATENS TO HURT ILLIONIS FARMERS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Friday  November 4  2005    WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today warned that the Senate approved budget reconciliation bill threatens critical conservation programs in Illinois  Durbin said the proposal to cut funding for the Conservation Reserve Program  CRP  and the Conservation Security Program  CSP  could potentially impact Illinois farmers   Durbin voted against the legislation        I am sorry to say  this budget has the wrong priorities  It increases the burdens on seniors and farmers  cuts health care spending  reduces the availability of affordable housing  and cuts environmental protections   It rewards the special interests instead of standing up for American families    said Durbin     Durbin noted that in Illinois and the nation  the Conservation Reserve Program  CRP  is the single largest environmental restoration program  with contracts on 41 000 Illinois farms  setting aside fragile land to avoid erosion and chemical contamination of our water sources   The program adds  106 million annually to the Illinois farm economy through direct payments to producers   CRP also helps reduce farm commodity payments by taking marginal land out of production     The Conservation Security Program  CSP  provides financial and technical assistance to promote the conservation and improvement of soil  water  air  energy  plant and animal life   In this year   s budget proposal  the program faces a 30 percent reduction in funds over the next five years  totaling  822 million     This comes on the heels of over  3 billion in cuts made to the CSP over the last two years in other spending legislation  These cuts threaten to substantially scale back CSP   s expansion and prevent thousands of Illinois farmers from enrolling in the program        These conservation programs not only preserve state wildlife habitats  they bolster Illinois    farm economy  keep our drinking water clean and attract tourism     noted Durbin      Currently only 19  of Illinois    most environmentally significant cropland is enrolled in CRP  with a long waiting list of farmers hoping to take part   These cuts would make it impossible for new applicants to participate        Durbin has pledged to work with farm groups  policymakers and the Bush Administration to ensure that farmers and those who depend on the agricultural economy are not adversely affected by these budget cuts
