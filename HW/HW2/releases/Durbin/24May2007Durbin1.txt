     Durbin Announces Senate Passage Of Bill To Name Post Office In Fairbury For Dr  Francis Townsend                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 5 24 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin Announces Senate Passage Of Bill To Name Post Office In Fairbury For Dr  Francis Townsend                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Thursday  May 24  2007      WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today announced Senate passage of his legislation to name the Post Office located at 127 East Locust Street in Fairbury  Illinois as the  Dr  Francis Townsend Post Office Building    Dr  Townsend created the Townsend old age revolving pension plan and led a social movement advocating increased benefits for the elderly during the 1930s        Dr  Francis Townsend was an innovator and original thinker   said Durbin    His efforts to fight poverty set the stage for Roosevelt s New Deal social security programs during the Great Depression   This year is the 150th anniversary of Fairbury  Illinois   Naming this post office after one of its most famous citizens is a fitting celebration of both Townsend s contribution to the city and this important milestone        Francis Townsend grew up in Fairbury  Illinois  the son of a farmer   He became a physician and served in the Army Medical Corps during World War I   After retiring from medicine  Townsend developed an old age pension plan for seniors   The Townsend Plan created a federal pension of  200 a month paid to every citizen sixty and older on the condition that the pensioner spend the entire sum within 30 days which was included in order to stimulate the economy   By 1935  his leadership in promoting his plan had created a social movement of over 2 million members in 7 000 clubs across the country   It is likely that Townsend s efforts contributed to an expedited passage of President Franklin D  Roosevelt s Social Security Act  a major New Deal initiative       Durbin was joined in his efforts by Senator Barack Obama  D IL  and Representative Tim Johnson  R IL   who is planning to introduce similar legislation in the House   He also worked closely with the Office of Mayor Robert Walter  Jr  in Fairbury on the post office renaming measure
