     DURBIN ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR DECATUR PROJECT TO OFFER HOUSING TO CHRONICALLY HOMELESS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            11 28 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR DECATUR PROJECT TO OFFER HOUSING TO CHRONICALLY HOMELESS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Monday  November 28  2005    DECATUR  IL      U S  Sen  Dick Durbin  D IL  was in Decatur today to announce  200 000 in federal funding for a project to provide housing to the area   s homeless population   Durbin  a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee  secured the funding as part of a fiscal year 2006 compromise bill which funds transportation  housing and other projects   The bill was approved by the House and the Senate and sent to President Bush to be signed into law          Homelessness is a problem in every town  including Decatur   Some of these folks need help from their neighbors to get headed in the right direction again   We are their neighbors   A real roof over their head will give them a chance for a job and a better tomorrow     said Durbin       The funding secured by Durbin will be used for Single Room Occupancy Apartments  SRO s  for individuals who are capable of independent living but need more intensive services than those in the supportive living apartments  Approximately 14 units will be built when the project is fully funded       The funding will be dispersed through the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development and used in coordination with the Neighborhood Housing Development Corporation  the City of Decatur s Department of Economic and Urban Development  the Macon County Homeless Council  and the Corporation for Supportive Housing       Chronic homelessness  as defined by HUD  refers to an unaccompanied homeless individual with a disabling condition who has either been continuously homeless for a year or more or has had at least four episodes of homelessness in the past three years  To be considered chronically homeless  persons must have been sleeping in a place not meant for human habitation  e g   living on the streets  and or in an emergency shelter during that time       A recent study by the Neighborhood Housing Development Corporation indicates that the chronically homeless make up approximately 10 to 15 percent of the entire homeless population
