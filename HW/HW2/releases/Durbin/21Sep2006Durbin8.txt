     DURBIN REACTS TO COMMENTS BY USTR ON AGRICULTURE SAFETY NET                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      9 21 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN REACTS TO COMMENTS BY USTR ON AGRICULTURE SAFETY NET                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Thursday  September 21  2006   Washington  D C       U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today made the following statement after U S  Trade Representative Susan Schwab spoke in favor of a plan to reduce U S  agriculture payments in exchange for increased market access for U S  agricultural products   The so called Australian plan proposes a  5 billion reduction in U S  agriculture payments in exchange for a 5  decrease in European tariffs on U S  agricultural imports        When we passed the 2002 Farm Bill  we made a solid promise to our farmers and the rural businesses they support    the federal government will provide a safety net for you and your family during hard times   This Administration should keep to that promise           I am troubled by the comments made by U S  Trade Representative Susan Schwab to our trading partners in Australia   Negotiating away protections for American farmers for minimal market access is not a fair trade and it is not wise policy for our nation  American farm policy should be dictated by Congress and the Administration and not by the World Trade Organization           My state of Illinois is home to more than 73 000 farms   Our agriculture policy supports far more than these farmers   Our nation   s farmers deserve a guarantee from the Administration that it will not destroy their safety net        In response to similar comments made in June  Durbin joined 56 Senators in calling on the president to reject trade agreements that would put American farmers at a disadvantage
