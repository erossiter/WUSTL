     DURBIN INTRODUCES LEGISLATION DESIGNATING  ADOPT A SCHOOL LIBRARY MONTH                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          3 29 2007                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN INTRODUCES LEGISLATION DESIGNATING  ADOPT A SCHOOL LIBRARY MONTH                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Thursday  March 29  2007   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin today introduced legislation that would designate September 2007 as Adopt a School Library Month   This legislation reaffirms what studies have consistently shown  children are more likely to succeed when they have access to a high quality library at school   Senator Norm Coleman  R MN  is a co sponsor of this legislation        Some of my favorite childhood moments were spent losing myself in a book about faraway places  exciting adventures and historic figures   said Durbin    Sadly  today  many children do not have access to new books that could establish them as lifelong readers   In fact  some libraries in Illinois are unable to purchase new books and must rely on donations from the community   We can not expect our students to compete in today s global economy unless we provide them with the tools they need to succeed        Increasingly  school libraries are facing extraordinary obstacles in the effort to provide students with high quality resources   These circumstances are not due to lack of concern by librarians  school officials or members of the community   Rather  school leaders are working with limited budgets and unforgiving performance standards       There is  however  a strong link between school libraries and student achievement  both in the classroom and on standardized tests   When libraries are well funded  students score an average of 5 percent to 13 percent higher on their reading and writing test scores  The data shows that children are more likely to succeed when they have access to current  well funded libraries       Durbin s legislation will designate the month of September to raise public awareness about the important role that school libraries play in the academic success of every child in America   During the month  the federal government  states  local governments  schools  nonprofit organizations  and businesses will have an opportunity to highlight some of the great work that can be done on school libraries with ceremonies  programs and other activities  Durbin said this has the potential to jumpstart community efforts to replenishing book shelves  revitalizing technology and redecorating our libraries
