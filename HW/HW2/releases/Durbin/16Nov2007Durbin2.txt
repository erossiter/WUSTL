    11 16 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin  Illinois Delegation Members Ask For Explanation Of FAA Plans To Expand Capacity At O Hare                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Friday  November 16  2007   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  was joined by members of the Illinois Congressional Delegation today in sending a letter to Robert A  Sturgell  Acting Administrator at the Federal Aviation Administration asking him to explain the FAAs plans to expand the capacity at OHare airport  They also asked that the FAA work with Congress and the public to implement long term solutions that utilize the increased capacity and benefits expected from the OHare Modernization Program  OMP   The Illinois members wrote  We would like you to clarify the FAAs position regarding the caps and whether or not you intend to extend the caps when they expire next year  We also would like you to report back to us with a detailed explanation of your plans to expand capacity at OHare after the new runway opens in late 2008  Recent news reports have quoted an FAA official as saying that the hourly flight cap at OHare would not be lifted for the scheduled November 2008 opening of the new runway  This has caused Illinois members to express concern that a decision may have been made on flight capacity levels at OHare by the FAA without consulting the City of Chicago  the public or Congress  Illinois Congressional members signing onto todays letter in addition to Senator Durbin include  Senator Barack Obama  D IL   Representatives Judy Biggert  R IL   Jerry Costello  D IL   Danny Davis  D IL   Rahm Emanuel  D IL   Luis Gutierrez  D IL   Phil Hare  D IL   Tim Johnson  R IL   Mark Kirk  R IL   Ray LaHood  R IL   Dan Lipinski  D IL   Jan Schakowsky  D IL  and John Shimkus  R IL     Text of the letter appears below  November 15  2007 Robert A  Sturgell  Acting Administrator  Federal Aviation Administration  800 Independence Avenue SW  Washington  DC 20591 Dear Acting Administrator Sturgell  We are writing regarding the recent news reports concerning the future of the current flight cap in place at OHare International Airport  The Chicago Tribune quoted the FAAs chief operating officer as saying the hourly flight cap would not be lifted for the scheduled November 2008 opening of the first new runway at O Hare  Any decision regarding the current flight cap should not be made without public and Congressional input  The cap on flights currently placed on O Hare is only a short term solution to managing congestion and delays until enhancements from the O Hare Modernization Plan  OMP  begin to come on line  In order to mitigate congestion and expand capacity at O Hare  the FAA  working with the Congress and the public  should implement long term solutions that utilize the increased capacity and benefits expected from the OMP  Unfortunately  the news reports indicate that officials at the FAA have already made a decision concerning OMP without consulting the City of Chicago  the public  or Congress  We would like you to clarify the FAAs position regarding the caps and whether or not you intend to extend the caps when they expire next year  We also would like you to report back to us with a detailed explanation of your plans to expand capacity at OHare after the new runway opens in late 2008  We look forward to working together to ensure that the expansion of OHare will provide travelers with the most reliable  viable  and safe air transportation opportunities  Sincerely  Richard J  Durbin  United States Senator
