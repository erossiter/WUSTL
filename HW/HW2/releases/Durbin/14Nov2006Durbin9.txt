     DURBIN MEETS WITH CHICAGO BOARD OF TRADE AND MERCANTILE EXCHANGE LEADERS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         11 14 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN MEETS WITH CHICAGO BOARD OF TRADE AND MERCANTILE EXCHANGE LEADERS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Tuesday  November 14  2006    Washington  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin today met with the leadership of the Chicago Mercantile Exchange and the Chicago Board of Trade to discuss the recent merger announcement and to stress the importance of reauthorizing the Commodity Exchange Act          Two great exchanges are combining to create a new global market force     said Durbin      The new CME group will bring together 21st century technology and the creative entrepreneurial spirit that has made Chicago a business leader for generations  The strength of the Chicago financial services community will be increased even further by the combination of these two great Chicago companies  Chicago will be the home of the largest exchange in the world for futures contracts  and I m confident that this industry will continue to innovate and create even more good jobs for Illinois in the years ahead        In today   s meeting  Durbin stressed the need to reauthorize the Commodity Exchange Act which provides exchanges  such as the Chicago Mercantile Exchange and the Chicago Board of Trade  the assurances that regulations will not be altered after exchanges are completed   Without the assurances provided by the Commodity Exchange Act  businesses risk facing unforeseen regulations after having made significant investments in innovative products and services   Reauthorizing the Commodity Exchange Act would also protect trading participants from unscrupulous practices  and would increase criminal and civil penalties for unlawful trading      Durbin has been one of the Senate leaders writing legislation to reauthorize the Commodity Futures Trading Commission  CFTC   The CFTC oversees the activities of both the Merc and the CBOT and is charged with protecting market users and the public from fraud  manipulation  and abusive practices related to the sale of commodities  futures and options
