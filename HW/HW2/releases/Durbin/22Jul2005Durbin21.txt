     DURBIN TO PROPOSE LEGISLATION TO PROVIDE RELIEF TO FARMERS FACING DROUGHT CONDITIONS IN ILLINOIS     7 22 2005                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN TO PROPOSE LEGISLATION TO PROVIDE RELIEF TO FARMERS FACING DROUGHT CONDITIONS IN ILLINOIS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Friday  July 22  2005    CHICAGO  IL      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  said he will offer legislation to help provide relief to Illinois farmers grappling with the most serious drought in many years   Today  Durbin wrote to U S  Secretary of Agriculture Mike Johanns  asking the Secretary to join him in a visit to an Illinois farm to see the extreme drought conditions at his earliest convenience   Durbin also sent a letter to the Chairman and Ranking Member of the Senate Subcommittee on Agriculture and Rural Development  making them aware of his intention to offer an amendment to the Senate Fiscal Year 2006 Agriculture Appropriations bill  which is likely to be debated in the Senate after the August recess     In the letters to Sec  Johanns and Chairman Robert Bennett  R UT  and Ranking Member Herb Kohl  D WI   Durbin outlined his concerns about the extreme drought conditions        The period from March to June of this year was the third driest in Illinois since 1895   Precipitation has been almost 50 percent below average statewide rainfall   Combined with unusually high temperatures during the month of June  the lack of precipitation has caused serious harm to this year   s harvest   The National Agriculture Statistics Service recently rated 55 percent of the state   s corn as very poor or poor and 36 percent of the state   s soybeans as very poor to poor        Illinois is the second largest producer of corn and the leading producer of soybeans    Although we will not fully know the extent of the damage incurred by this year   s drought until harvest time  current data suggests that producers will face extreme hardship   Many predict that conditions will worsen as the season progresses  especially in North Eastern and North Central Illinois   These conditions are especially alarming because farmers are at a critical point in the growing season     Durbin wrote
