     Durbin and Illinois Colleagues Take Action to Prevent Spread of Hospital Infections                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              6 29 2007                                                                                                                                                                            Durbin and Illinois Colleagues Take Action to Prevent Spread of Hospital Infections                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Friday  June 29  2007       At todays Illinois Delegation Luncheon  United States Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  and fellow Illinois members signed a letter addressed to the Acting Administrator of the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services  CMS   Leslie Norwalk  urging CMS to reimburse hospitals for the costs of treating Methicillin resistant Staphylococcus Aureus  MRSA   MRSA is a preventable Hospital Acquired Infection  HAI  that is drug resistant and can result in lengthy  expensive hospital treatments and death  Illinois Delegation members Barack Obama  D IL   Judy Biggert  R IL   Mark Kirk  R IL   Bobby Rush  D IL   and Rahm Emanuel  D IL  signed the letter  which was sent today    Last year  MRSA resulted in thousands of deaths and  3 billion in hospital fees  Durbin said  If Medicare starts to cover MRSA costs  we can save lives while saving the healthcare system money    According to the results of a major new study  up to 1 2 million hospital patients are infected with drug resistant infections each year  and MRSA alone results in more than  3 billion a year in additional costs for the Medicare program  MRSA meets the criteria specified by Congress for inclusion in the Inpatient Prospective Payment System  IPPS  proposed rule  IPPS is the payment system with which Medicare reimburses hospitals for providing impatient care to beneficiaries  Healthcare providers are increasingly concerned about the high incidence of MRSA and recognize the need to combat the superbug aggressively by conducting proactive screening and isolating patients with infection prior to their admittance    In Illinois  lawmakers are working actively to address MRSA by toughening hospital regulations  The State Senate passed the MRSA Screening and Reporting Act  SB233  on June 22 and the bill is now awaiting Governor Rod Blagojevichs signature  The Act would require Illinois hospitals to screen for MRSA  monitor adherence to hand hygiene  and report all infections for publication on a web site
