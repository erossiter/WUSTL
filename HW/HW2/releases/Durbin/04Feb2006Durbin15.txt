     DURBIN APPLAUDS PASSAGE OF MINE SAFETY LEGISLATION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               2 4 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN APPLAUDS PASSAGE OF MINE SAFETY LEGISLATION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Saturday  February 4  2006    WASHINGTON  DC      U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  said he was encouraged by the passage of legislation which he cosponsored to help protect the lives of miners in Illinois   The Mine Safety Tax Relief Act of 2006  which was included as an amendment to the Tax Reconciliation Act  will provide tax credits and incentives to encourage coal companies to invest in mine safety equipment and training          The recent tragic events in West Virginia and Kentucky have captured the nation s attention and exposed the serious dangers our miners face every day     said Durbin      This bill gives coal companies strong incentives to invest in new technology and improved equipment to protect those miners  We have more than 3 500 miners in Illinois   We owe it to them and to their families to ensure all 25 mines in operation in Illinois are well equipped and adequately prepared   In addition to these economic incentives  we must make certain that our mines are carefully and regularly inspected          The legislation encourages mining companies  through tax incentives  to purchase safety equipment  including communications technology that allows operators above to communicate with miners below  in case of accidents  electronic tracking devices which allow operators to know where miners are  breathing devices  and mine atmospheric monitoring equipment   The legislation encourages these investments by providing mine operators with a tax deduction in the amount of 50  of the cost of the equipment purchased       The bill also encourages mine companies to form rescue teams where they don   t exist and increase the number of mine rescue team members trained where teams are already in place   For decades the coal mining industry had been shrinking and mines were being closed  resulting in a formation of fewer mine safety teams across the country   Coal mining in America and in Illinois has seen a recent resurgence in the past two years  causing new mines to open  and necessitating the creation of additional mine rescue teams   Mining companies would be provided with a tax credit  of up to  10 000 for each mine rescue team member trained   The credit would offset approximately 20  of the cost of training a miner to serve on a mine rescue team       Durbin noted that the legislation also provides incentives for mine operators to act now because the tax credit provisions will only be available for 3 years  starting with the 2006 tax year       Durbin has also written to state and federal agencies responsible for overseeing mine safety asking them for a review of mine safety records in Illinois and elsewhere  In his letters he asked the agencies to examine two issues     the impact funding cuts for mine safety inspections have had on mine safety  and a status report on safety conditions at all of Illinois    mines   President Bush has proposed cuts to the federal coal safety inspection budget in four of the five years he has been in office   Analysts say that those reduced budget numbers have accounted for the loss of 130 mine inspectors since 2000       The mine safety proposal  which was authored by Sen  John D  Rockefeller  D WV   was offered as an amendment to the tax reconciliation bill  and was approved by the Senate late Thursday night   The bill now goes to a conference committee where the Senate and House versions of the bill will be reconciled
