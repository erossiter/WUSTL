     DURBIN S HUNGER FREE COMMUNITIES ACT OF 2006 APPROVED BY SENATE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  12 8 2006                                                                                                                                                                            DURBIN S HUNGER FREE COMMUNITIES ACT OF 2006 APPROVED BY SENATE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Friday  December 8  2006           U S  Senator Dick Durbin  D IL  today applauded Senate passage of his Hunger Free Communities Act of 2006  S 1120   The legislation will increase the federal resources available to locally based organizations working to end hunger in their communities   The bipartisan bill is cosponsored by U S  Senators Richard Lugar  R IN   Blanche Lincoln  D AR   and Gordon Smith  R OR         It is shameful that in 21st Century America too many of our fellow citizens face hunger and food insecurity is on the rise     Durbin said      Hunger is not a partisan issue  and with the Hunger Free Communities Act of 2006  we can forge a new bipartisan partnership to help those who are struggling to feed their families        According to the U S  Department of Agriculture   s most recent report on hunger and food insecurity  35 1 million people     including nearly 12 4 million children     live in households that experienced hunger or the risk of hunger  This represents more than one in ten households in the United States  11 percent  and is an increase of nearly 1 9 million  from 33 2 million in 2000     Title I of the Hunger Free Communities Act of 2005 reaffirms Congress    commitment to cut U S  food insecurity in half and sets a new goal of ending hunger in the U S    Furthermore  it directs the Department of Agriculture to release two reports in five years on the status of efforts to eliminate hunger and to provide recommendations to further reduce hunger     Under Title II of the Durbin bill  Congress will establish an anti hunger grant program  the first of its kind  with an emphasis on assessing hunger in individual communities and promoting cooperation and collaboration among local anti hunger groups  This public private partnership will be funded through a  50 million a year  five year grant program to help communities reduce  and ultimately eliminate  hunger     The Hunger Free Communities Act of 2006 now goes to the House for consideration
