SEN. WARNER ANNOUNCES $465,542 FOR FIRE DEPARTMENTS IN VIRGINIA
  Today, Sen. John W. Warner, R-Va., announced that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has approved $465,542 in federal grants for fire departments in Virginia. 
   The fire departments in Bridgewater, Chilhowie, Ebony, and Clintwood will receive the funds under DHS's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program for Fiscal Year '04. 
   These grants are appropriated annually by Congress to DHS "to improve the effectiveness of firefighting operations, firefighter health and safety programs, and to establish or expand fire prevention programs throughout the United States." 
   The grants will be divided as follows: 
   * Ebony Volunteer Fire Department, Ebony, Virginia, $84,231 
   * Bridgewater Volunteer Fire Company, Inc, Bridgewater, Virginia, $106,589 
   * Chilhowie Fire Department, Chilhowie, Virginia, $187,242 
   * Clintwood Volunteer Fire Department, Clintwood, Virginia, $87,480 
   In announcing the grant, Senator Warner said, "Firefighters play a critical public safety role in communities across Virginia. This money will help these dedicated public servants keep their fellow Virginians safe."
