SEN. WARNER ANNOUNCES $100,008 IN GRANTS FOR FIRE DEPARTMENTS IN VIRGINIA
  Today, Sen. John W. Warner, R-Va., announced that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has approved $100,008 in federal grants for fire departments in Virginia. 
   The fire departments in Chatham and Rixeyville will receive the funds under DHS's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program for Fiscal Year '04.
   These grants are appropriated annually by Congress to DHS "to improve the effectiveness of firefighting operations, firefighter health and safety programs, and to establish or expand fire prevention programs throughout the United States." 
   The grants will be divided as follows:
   * Chatham Fire Department, Chatham, Virginia, $30,240
   * Little Fork Volunteer Fire and Rescue Company, Rixeyville, Virginia $69,768 
   In announcing the grant, Senator Warner said, "Firefighters play a critical public safety role in communities across Virginia. This money will help these dedicated public servants keep their fellow Virginians safe."
