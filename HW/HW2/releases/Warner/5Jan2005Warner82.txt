SEN. WARNER ANNOUNCES $225,000 GRANT FOR FIRE DEPARTMENT IN VIRGINIA
  Today Sen. John W. Warner, R-Va., announced that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has approved $225,000 in federal funds to a fire department in Virginia.  
   Blairs Fire and Rescue in Blairs, Virginia, will receive the funds under DHS's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program for Fiscal Year '05. 
   These grants are appropriated annually by Congress to DHS "to improve the effectiveness of firefighting operations, firefighter health and safety programs, and to establish or expand fire prevention programs throughout the United States." 
   In announcing the grant, Senator Warner said, "Firefighters play a critical public safety role in communities across Virginia. This money will help these dedicated public servants keep their fellow Virginians safe."
