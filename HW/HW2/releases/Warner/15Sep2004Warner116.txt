SEN. WARNER ANNOUNCES $268,380 IN GRANTS FOR VIRGINIA VOLUNTEER FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Today Sen. John W. Warner, R-Va., announced that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has approved $268,380 in federal grants for volunteer fire departments in Virginia.  
   Under DHS's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program for Fiscal Year '04: 
   * The Burkeville Volunteer Fire Department will receive $193,500 for a new firefighting vehicle. 
   * The Clinch Mountain Volunteer Fire Department will receive $74,880 for operations and firefighter safety. 
   These grants are appropriated annually by Congress to DHS "to improve the effectiveness of firefighting operations, firefighter health and safety programs, and to establish or expand fire prevention programs throughout the United States." 
   In announcing the grant, Senator Warner said, "Firefighters play a critical public safety role in communities across Virginia. This money will help these dedicated public servants keep their fellow Virginians safe."
