SEN. WARNER ANNOUNCES $184,294 IN GRANTS FOR FIRE DEPARTMENTS IN VIRGINIA
  Today, Sen. John W. Warner, R-Va., announced that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has approved $184,294 in federal grants for fire departments in Virginia.  
   The fire departments in Bowling Green, Dublin, and Wytheville will receive the funds under DHS's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program for Fiscal Year '04.
   These grants are appropriated annually by Congress to DHS "to improve the effectiveness of firefighting operations, firefighter health and safety programs, and to establish or expand fire prevention programs throughout the United States."
   The grants will be divided as follows:
   * Bowling Green Volunteer Fire Department, Bowling Green, Virginia, $66,240 
   * Little Creek Volunteer Fire Department, Dublin, Virginia, $21,618 
   * Wytheville Fire Department, Wytheville, Virginia, $96,436 
   In announcing the grant, Senator Warner said, "Firefighters play a critical public safety role in communities across Virginia. This money will help these dedicated public servants keep their fellow Virginians safe."
