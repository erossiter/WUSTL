SEN. WARNER ANNOUNCES $708,158 FOR FIRE DEPARTMENTS IN VIRGINIA
  Today Sen. John W. Warner, R-Va., announced that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has approved $708,158 in federal grants for fire departments in Virginia.  
   The fire departments will receive the funds under DHS' Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program for Fiscal Year '05. 
   These grants are appropriated annually by Congress to DHS "to improve the effectiveness of firefighting operations, firefighter health and safety programs, and to establish or expand fire prevention programs throughout the United States." 
   The grants will be distributed as follows: 
   * Prince George Volunteer Fire Department, Company 1, Prince George, Virginia, $182,108 
   * Reva Volunteer Fire and Rescue Company, Culpeper, Virginia, $247,500 
   * Toms Brook Volunteer Fire Department, Toms Brook, Virginia, $224,100 
   * Shenandoah Farms Volunteer Fire Department, Front Royal, Virginia, $54,450 
   In announcing the grant, Senator Warner said, "Firefighters play a critical public safety role in communities across Virginia. This money will help these dedicated public servants keep their fellow Virginians safe."
