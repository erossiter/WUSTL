SEN. WARNER ANNOUNCES FINAL FUNDING LEVELS FOR ENERGY, WATER PROJECTS IN VIRGINIA
  Today Sen. John Warner, R-Va., announced that the United States Senate voted to approve H.R.2419, the Energy and Water Development Appropriations Act for FY2006. From the programs funded by this bill, numerous projects in Virginia will receive funding.  
   As passed, the bill included the following projects in Virginia: 
   * $30 million authorization for Chesapeake Bay native oyster restoration. 
   * $65.97 million for the Appalachian Regional Commission, which promotes economic development activities in southwest Virginia. 
   * $500,000 to research submerged aquatic vegetation in the Chesapeake Bay. 
   * $273,000 to continue non-native oysters studies in the Chesapeake Bay. 
   * $9.5 million to continue the Grundy Flood Control and Redevelopment Project as scheduled with the construction of the ring wall, access bridge, and other central features. 
   * $40,000 to control shoreline erosion along the Chesapeake Bay in Mathews County. 
   * $300,000 for the Lynnhaven River Basin to improve oyster beds, increase submerged aquatic vegetation, prevent further silting, and restore wetlands. 
   * $200,000 to further study erosion problems at Willoughby Spit in Norfolk. 
   * $750,000 to continue the construction of the combined sewer overflow project in Richmond. 
   * $1.5 million to complete the removal and cleanup of the Embrey Dam in Fredericksburg. 
   * $3 million to replace the Goshen Dam at Lake Merriweather because it does not meet national dam safety regulations. 
   * $3.2 million to complete the 50-foot inbound channel element of the Norfolk Harbor and Channels Deepening Project. 
   * $5 million to continue construction of the flood control and channelization project along the Roanoke River in the downtown area of Roanoke. 
   * $3 million for beach nourishment of the Sandbridge area of Virginia Beach. 
   * $8.55 million to complete construction of the Virginia Beach Hurricane Protection Project. 
   * $450,000 to obtain permits, perform final engineering and design, and begin dredging the Appomattox River near Petersburg. 
   * $13.2 million to raise the dikes and expand the Craney Island disposal site in Norfolk. 
   * $1 million for the Center for Advanced Separation Technologies at Virginia Tech. 
   * $400,000 for the Institute for Advanced Learning Research in Danville. 
   * $1 million for the Massey Cancer Center at Virginia Commonwealth University. 
   * $370.7 million for nuclear physics research in DOE's Office of Science, of which funds will be used by the Thomas Jefferson National Lab in Newport News, most notably to upgrade their continuous electron beam accelerator. 
   Senator Warner said, "This funding will provide important resources to projects throughout Virginia, with particular emphasis on vital energy research and necessary water initiatives." 
   Now passed by the full House and Senate, the legislation needs only the President's signature to become law.
