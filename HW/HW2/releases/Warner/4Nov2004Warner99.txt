SEN. WARNER ANNOUNCES GRANT FOR BLACKSBURG AREA FIRE DEPARTMENT
  Today Sen. John W. Warner, R-Va., announced that the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has approved a federal grant for a fire department near Blacksburg, Virginia.  
   The Rich Creek Volunteer Fire Department, in Rich Creek, Virginia, will receive $67,500 under DHS's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program for Fiscal Year '05. 
   This grant is appropriated annually by Congress to DHS "to improve the effectiveness of firefighting operations, firefighter health and safety programs, and to establish or expand fire prevention programs throughout the United States." 
   In announcing the grant, Sen. Warner said, "Firefighters play a critical public safety role in communities across Virginia. This money will help these dedicated public servants keep their fellow Virginians safe."
