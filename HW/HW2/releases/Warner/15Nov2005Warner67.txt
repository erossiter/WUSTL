SEN. WARNER ANNOUNCES FINAL FUNDING LEVELS FOR COMMERCE, JUSTICE, STATE, SCIENCE PROJECTS IN VIRGINIA
  Today, Sen. John Warner, R-Va., announced that the United States Senate voted to approve, H.R. 2862, the Commerce, Justice, Science, State Appropriations Act for FY 2006. From the programs funded by this bill, numerous projects in Virginia will receive funding.  
   As passed, the bill included the following projects in Virginia: 
   * $550,000 to the Horseshoe Crab Research Center at Virginia Tech to advance the horseshoe crab population dynamics research for biomedical, fisheries, and ecological applications. 
   * $2,000,000 for the Virginia Institute of Marine Science at the College of William and Mary to monitor developments of oyster populations within the artificial reef formations in the Chesapeake Bay. 
   * $2,000,000 for to continue environmental studies of non-native oysters in the Chesapeake Bay. 
   * $500,000 for the continuation of the trawl survey. 
   * $550,000 to protect critical lands on the Eastern Shore of Virginia. 
   * $2,000,000 to acquire the Jamestown Campsite and Marina in order to maintain the integrity of the Jamestown National Historic Site. 
   * $2,000,000 for the continuation of the Wallops Ocean Observation Project, which has resulted in a greater understanding of ocean coastal environmental systems. 
   * $500,000 for interpretive buoys to mark the exploration of Chesapeake Bay by Captain John Smith. 
   * $912,000,000 for overall aeronautics research and development, a 7% increase over the President's budget request for FY 2006. This increased funding is vital for aeronautics facilities, such as NASA Langley in Hampton, Virginia. 
   * $1,500,000 for the establishment of a digital discovery lab at the NASA Langley Visitor Center. 
   * $4,000,000 for infrastructure upgrades at the Wallops Island Flight Facility to accommodate unmanned aerial vehicles. 
   * $3,000,000 to support Old Dominion University's operation of the NASA Langley Research Center's low-speed wind tunnel. 
   * $1,350,000 for ChildSafeNet and Fairfax County to help law enforcement protect children against child predators. 
   * $500,000 for An Achievable Dream an innovative school in Newport News, VA, that educates economically disadvantaged students and helps close the achievement gap. 
   * $250,000 for Children's Hospital of the King's Daughters for operation of its child abuse center. 
   * $500,000 for the Courtroom 21 Project at the College of William and Mary School of Law. 
   * $1,985,000 for Center for Criminal Justice Technology to assist law enforcement in crime related projects. 
   * $100,000 for the City of Norfolk's law enforcement technologies initiative. 
   * $150,000 for Regent University's juvenile delinquency prevention program. 
   * $660,000 for Virginia Community Policing Initiative to continue providing community policing, training, and technical assistance to Virginia's law enforcement agencies and communities. 
   * $100,000 for the City of Suffolk's Police Department to improve its law enforcement technology program. 
   * $200,000 for the City of Virginia Beach to enhance the police department's law enforcement capabilities by helping to fulfill vital technology and equipment needs. 
   * $1,000,000 for the Leadership Program for Emerging Democracies, an international exchange program involving a consortium of Virginia Military Institute, Christopher Newport University, the College of William and Mary, Shenandoah University, and James Madison University. 
   * $900,000 for the International Forum on Democracy as part of the Jamestown 2007 celebration. 
   * $6,500,000 for the Critical Infrastructure Protection Project, a collaborative research project by George Mason University and James Madison University. 
   * $1,000,000 for the City of Martinsville in partnership with the Industrial Development Authority of Halifax for research and development in the area of photo-luminescent material manufacturing. 
   * $500,000 for the Advanced and Applied Polymer Processing Institute for education and training programs. 
   * $2,000,000 for Virginia Community College System educational web portal project. 
   Senator Warner said, "This funding will provide important resources to projects throughout Virginia, with particular emphasis on key business, civic and educational programs. I am particularly pleased with the significant increase in funding for the NASA Langley Research Center as continued progress in this area remains a key to American competitiveness in the military and civilian aerospace markets." 
   Now passed by the full House and Senate, the legislation needs only the President's signature to become law.
