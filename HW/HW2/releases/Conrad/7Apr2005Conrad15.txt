Conrad Bill Aims to Boost Number of Tribal College Teachers Legislation Helps Provide a Quality Education                    April 7  2005  Washington  Concerned with the challenges tribal colleges have in recruiting and retaining qualified faculty  Senator Kent Conrad today introduced bipartisan legislation to forgive student loans to individuals who commit to teach at tribal colleges in North Dakota and across the nation  I am a strong supporter of tribal colleges because they bring hope and opportunity to America s Indian communities  Senator Conrad said  My bill will provide these institutions with extra help in attracting qualified teachers  and help deserving students receive a quality education  Senator Conrad s Tribal Colleges and Universities Teacher Loan Forgiveness Act will provide up to  15 000 in loan forgiveness to students who commit to teach for up to five years at a tribal college  The funding is to be used against a student s Perkins Loan  Federal Family Education Loan  or federal direct loan  Senator Conrad s bill drew bipartisan support and was co sponsored by Senator Byron Dorgan  of North Dakota  as well as Senators Conrad Burns  R MT   Tim Johnson  D SD   Herb Kohl  D WI   Pete Domenici  R NM   John Thune  R SD   and Jeff Bingaman  D NM   Senator Conrad  a senior member of the Senate Indian Affairs Committee  helped form a bipartisan Task Force on Tribal Colleges and Universities  The task force helps raise awareness of the difficulties tribal colleges and universities have in recruiting and retaining qualified teachers because of their geographic isolation and low faculty salaries  Today  tribal colleges serve more than 30 000 students who represent more than 250 tribes from across the United States  Mexico and Canada  Tribal colleges in North Dakota include Fort Berthold Community College  New Town  Cankdeska Cikana Community College  Fort Totten  Turtle Mountain Community College  Belcourt  Sitting Bull College  Fort Yates  and United Tribes Technical College  Bismarck  Tribal colleges and universities play an important role in their communities as educational  economic  and cultural centers  Senator Conrad said  We must help provide a quality education to help Native Americans of all ages reach their full potential
