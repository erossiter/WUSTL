Senators Call for Action on Ag Disaster Legislation Conrad  Dorgan Urge Senate Leaders Not to Adjourn Before Helping Farmers                    December 1  2005  Washington  Leading a bipartisan group of farm state lawmakers  Senators Kent Conrad and Byron Dorgan today called on Senate leaders to pass emergency legislation before the Senate adjourns to assist agricultural producers hurt by Hurricane Katrina and other natural disasters this year  This has been a tough year for many of our farmers in North Dakota and across the nation  Senators Conrad and Dorgan said in a joint statement  These producers need help and they need it now  In the letter to Senate Republican Leader Bill Frist and Senate Democratic Leader Harry Reid  authored by Senator Conrad  more than 20 bipartisan Senators urge the Senate leaders not to adjourn for the year before considering emergency disaster assistance for our nation s farmers and ranchers  As the Senate concludes its agenda for the current session  we hope you will honor the commitment that agricultural disaster assistance will receive appropriate attention before we adjourn  Senators Conrad and Dorgan write  Weather related disaster losses have affected more than half of the counties in the United States  In addition to the extensive loss of crops and livestock in the Gulf  the nation s farmers and ranchers have also faced drought in the Midwest  flooding in the Plains region  crop infestations and livestock diseases  The spike in energy prices has hit agricultural producers especially hard  Higher fuel prices mean it costs more to produce fertilizers  run tractors and operate farm machinery  Earlier this year  Senators Conrad and Dorgan introduced the Agriculture Disaster Assistance Act of 2005  The legislation would provide badly needed relief for agricultural producers who suffered losses due to the hurricanes and other smaller scale disasters across the nation  The legislation includes several provisions to provide relief  including  a crop disaster program  an extension of the Livestock Assistance Program  and an additional  100 million in Emergency Conservation Program funds  Twenty Senators joined in signing the Conrad Dorgan letter  including Senators Max Baucus  D MT   Norm Coleman  R MN   Jim Talent  R MO   Ben Nelson  D NE   Ken Salazar  D CO   Mary Landrieu  D LA   Debbie Stabenow  D MI   Maria Cantwell  D WA   Ron Wyden  D OR   Barack Obama  D IL   Dick Durbin  D IL   Jim Jeffords  I VT   Mark Dayton  D MN   Patty Murray  D WA   Chris Dodd  D CT   Carl Levin  D MI   Tim Johnson  D SD   Mark Pryor  D AR   Bill Nelson  D FL  and Pat Roberts  R KS   Attached below is the text of the letter sent to Senators Frist and Reid  Dear Senators Frist and Reid  As you develop the Senate schedule for the balance of this session  we urge that you not adjourn the Senate before it has the opportunity to consider a comprehensive  emergency economic and disaster assistance program for our nation s farmers and ranchers  Emergency assistance for farmers and ranchers is necessary because of the loss of crops and livestock due to weather related causes  including Hurricanes Katrina  Rita and Wilma as well as losses caused by drought  flood and disease in other parts of the nation  In addition  farmers and ranchers have suffered significant income losses this year due to rapidly escalating input and transportation surcharge costs associated with rising energy prices  As you know  our farmers cannot pass these added expenses on to their consumers due to the nature of agricultural markets  As you are aware  several bills have already been introduced in the Senate to provide this type of assistance to our agricultural producers  The supporters of these initiatives were requested to withhold offering them as amendments to the supplemental appropriations for hurricane relief as well as the annual agriculture appropriations bills in order to expedite the consideration and passage of those bills  Senators agreed to that request with the understanding that agricultural disaster assistance would be considered as part of other legislation that would come before the Senate this year  As the Senate concludes its agenda for the current session  we hope you will honor the commitment that agricultural disaster assistance will receive appropriate attention before we adjourn  Sincerely
