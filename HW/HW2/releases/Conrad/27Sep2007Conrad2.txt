Conrad  CHIP Passage a Big Victory for ND Kids Bipartisan Support Key to Expansion of Children s Health Care Program                   September 27  2007  Washington  The U S  Senate today overwhelmingly passed legislation to renew the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  an initiative Senator Conrad has long supported  The bipartisan vote in favor of CHIP will double the federal support to North Dakota  where more than 14 000 children are without health insurance    Too many children in North Dakota and across the country are deprived of the health care they need  Senator Conrad said  By renewing CHIP  we are demonstrating that the health of our children is both a legislative and moral priority of this Congress    The Senate voted 67 29 to expand the popular program by an additional  35 billion over five years  The renewal of CHIP  which currently covers more than 6 6 million children  will allow for an additional 3 1 million children to be enrolled nationwide    We see children without health insurance everyday  It s an unacceptable situation in need of a bold solution  said Bob Sanderson  President and CEO of Lutheran Social Services of North Dakota  We are very happy with the steps Congress has taken with regard to SCHIP and appreciate Senator Conrad s leadership and commitment to improving the health of our children    In addition to protecting our childrens health  enrolling children in a health insurance program also makes fiscal sense  Senator Conrad said  Many children without coverage use the emergency room for health care  which is the most expensive avenue to address their needs  Additionally  minor illnesses  if left unchecked  can end up costing more in the long run    Congress will not support a more worthy project for our nations children this year  Senator Conrad said  I strongly urge the President to join Congress in protecting our childrens health by signing this vital legislation    The House of Representatives passed the CHIP conference report by a vote of 265 159 Wednesday    The President has already declared his intent to veto the childrens health care bill
