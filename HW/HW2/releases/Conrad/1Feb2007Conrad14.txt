Conrad Introduces Tribal College Teacher Bill Legislation Helps Tribal Colleges and Universities Hire and Keep Faculty                   February 1  2007  Washington    Senator Kent Conrad introduced bipartisan legislation today to forgive student loans for teachers who commit to tribal colleges in North Dakota and across the nation   The legislation is an effort by Senator Conrad to help tribal colleges overcome problems recruiting and retaining qualified faculty  All students deserve a quality education taught by a quality teacher  Senator Conrad said   My bill will help tribal colleges attract additional teachers  and  in turn  help students get a good education  Senator Conrad s Tribal Colleges and Universities Faculty Loan Forgiveness Act will provide up to  15 000 in loan forgiveness to those who commit to teach for up to five years at a tribal college   Students with a Perkins Loan  Federal Family Education Loan  or federal direct loan would qualify for assistance  Senator Conrad s bill was co sponsored by Senator Byron Dorgan  of North Dakota  as well as Senators John McCain  R Ariz    Jeff Bingaman  D NM   Herb Kohl   D Wis   Pete Domenici  R NM  and John Thune  R SD   Data collected by the American Indian Higher Education Consortium reveals that in 2000 2001  56  of tribal college and university faculty served for 5 years or less  In addition  the most recent data reports the average annual salary of faculty at tribal colleges and universities is approximately  33 500  Today  tribal colleges serve more than 30 000 students who represent more than 250 tribes from across the United States  Mexico and Canada  Tribal colleges in North Dakota include Fort Berthold Community College  New Town  Cankdeska Cikana Community College  Fort Totten  Turtle Mountain Community College  Belcourt  Sitting Bull College  Fort Yates  and United Tribes Technical College  Bismarck  We must help provide a quality education to help Native Americans of all ages reach their full potential  Senator Conrad said   Tribal colleges are more than educational institutions  They also serve as doors of opportunity to America s Indian communities  Senator Conrad  a senior member of the Senate Indian Affairs Committee  helped form a bipartisan Task Force on Tribal Colleges and Universities  The task force serves to strengthen tribal colleges and universities through legislation
