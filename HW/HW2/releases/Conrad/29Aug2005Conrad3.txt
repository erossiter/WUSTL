Conrad Unveils Plan to Lower Gas Prices Senator Urges Long Term Fixes to Avert Greater Fuel Dependency                    August 29  2005  Grand Forks    Citing rising fuel costs and the nation s dependency on foreign supplies of petroleum  Senator Kent Conrad today unveiled a detailed plan that calls for immediate steps to reduce fuel costs while taking long term measures to avert a potential energy crisis  Living and working in North Dakota means long drives  Our homes  our businesses and our cities are all long distances apart  and those long distances mean big fuel bills  Senator Conrad said  The cost of gasoline and diesel is becoming a severe burden on families  on businesses  on farmers    on all walks of life  We need a plan to keep fuel costs affordable  At his press conference today  Senator Conrad released the details of his plan  Letter to the President urging him to pressure OPEC to increase output and lower oil prices  Authorization of releases from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve  Substantial increase in the use of renewable fuels  like ethanol and bio diesel  The plan includes tax credits for people who purchase flexible fuel vehicles that can run on E85 ethanol blends  Tax credits for all cars and trucks that get high gas mileage  just like there are credits available for hybrid vehicles and advanced diesel vehicles  Major government effort to spur development and commercialization of coal liquefaction technologies by having the government invest  500 million to test different coal to liquid technologies  Major government effort to increase research and development spending on advanced lightweight materials for vehicles  Doubling research and development funds to  7 5 billion to promote the transition to a hydrogen economy  It was right here in Grand Forks that I first outlined my vision to make North Dakota a leader in energy production for our nation  The Energy Bill that we recently passed takes steps toward that  but it is time for bolder measures  before fuel prices grow to become a crisis  Senator Conrad said
