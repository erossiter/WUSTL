Delegation Salutes Vets on WWII Honor Flight ND Lawmakers Offer Thanks to Heroes of America s Greatest Generation                   November 2  2007 Washington  Senators Kent Conrad and Byron Dorgan and Congressman Earl Pomeroy will pay special tribute today to North Dakota veterans and their families visiting the nations capital as part of the WWII Honor Flight   The statewide initiative was created to help North Dakota and Minnesota World War II veterans travel to Washington  DC to visit the National World War II Memorial  for the first time  This trip is provided at no cost to the veterans  This latest Honor Flight includes 250 WWII veterans  147 are from North Dakota It is third and final Honor Flight planned by the organizers  To date  the Honor Flight has helped more than 600 World War II veterans travel from Fargo  North Dakota to Washington  DC to visit the National World War II Memorial   Because of the generosity of so many North Dakotans and Minnesotans  hundreds of veterans have traveled to Washington at no expense to receive a well deserved heros welcome  the delegation said in a joint statement This trip represents a grateful nations way of saying thank you to a group of heroes that is deservingly known as The Greatest Generation   Veterans from across North Dakota and Minnesota will be guests of honor at a Capitol Hill reception and dinner today hosted by the North Dakota and Minnesota Congressional delegations  The patriotic salute features personal recollections and remembrances from veterans and a keynote address by ABC News correspondent Sam Donaldson   A visit to Arlington National Cemetery and a tour of the National WWII Memorial and are also planned as part of the two day trip organized by the Fargo based media company WDAY and local radio host  Tracy Briggs   Open to the public in 2004  the World War II Memorial honors the 16 million who served in the armed forces of the United States  the more than 400 000 who died  and all who supported the war effort at home  Spanning the length of a football field  the Memorial features waterfalls  fountains  and two 43 foot arches at each end symbolizing the Atlantic and Pacific theaters of the war   We have been honored to play a small a part in organizing the WWII Honor Flight  the delegation said This trip to our nations capital serves as an opportunity to personally thank the generation of Americans who fought to preserve our freedom It is also giving many of North Dakotas WWII veterans a chance to visit and reflect at the memorial that honors their service and sacrifice
