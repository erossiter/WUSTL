Conrad  Dorgan Bill Doubles Bounty on bin Laden Nearly Six Years After September 11 Attacks  Terrorist Mastermind Still at Large                   July 12  2007  Washington  Senators Kent Conrad and Byron Dorgan introduced legislation Thursday doubling the reward for information leading to the capture of Osama bin Laden  the mastermind of the 9 11 attacks  and requiring classified reports to be made to Congress detailing efforts to apprehend the al Qaeda leadership  The Conrad Dorgan amendment comes on the heels of a new warning from the nations homeland security chief that al Qaeda may soon attack the United States    It has been nearly six years  more than 2 130 days  since the attacks of 9 11  Yet Osama bin Laden remains at large and capable of attacking our country again   It is unfathomable this man remains alive and well and continues to pose such a serious threat to our nation   Whether we capture bin Laden or kill him  it is long past time that bin Laden be brought to justice  Senator Conrad said   Our bill sends a clear message to those seeking to inflict harm on American citizens  We will spare no expense to hold you to account    Capturing Osama bin Laden must be a top priority  said Senator Dorgan  Almost six years ago  Osama bin Laden directed the terrorist attack against America  yet today he remains free and continues to threaten our country  We must strengthen the effort to apprehend Osama bin Laden   The whole world must know  there will be no escape from justice for Osama bin Laden and those who follow him    The Conrad Dorgan amendment doubles the reward for the capture or information leading to the capture of Osama bin Laden to  50 million  The legislation also requires the White House to issue a classified report to Congress every 90 days on the activities of the federal government related to bringing bin Laden and other top associates of al Qaeda to justice    Senators Conrad and Dorgan introduced their amendment to the Defense Authorization bill in an effort to refocus the countrys attention and some of the governments resources toward finally bringing bin Laden to justice     On Tuesday  Homeland Security Secretary Michael Chertoff warned of an increased risk of a terrorist attack this summer in the United States
