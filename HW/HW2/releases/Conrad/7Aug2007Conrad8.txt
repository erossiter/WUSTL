Conrad  Dorgan Cut Ribbon at Dickinson Vets Clinic New Clinic Improves Access to Health Care for Veterans in SW North Dakota                   August 7  2007  Dickinson  Senators Kent Conrad and Byron Dorgan were joined today by community leaders and thankful veterans in opening the new veterans health care clinic in Dickinson  The new clinic will dramatically reduce the travel time many veterans face to receive the health care they need  This is a terrific day for North Dakotas veterans  especially those in the southwest corner of our state who previously had to travel hundreds of miles for care  Senator Conrad said  Our government has a responsibility to care for the men and women who have worn our nations uniform  The opening of the Dickinson VA clinic is one important way we can honor their service and sacrifice  Our veterans were willing to sacrifice their lives in defense of this nation  There is no way to fully repay them for their service  Senator Dorgan said  The least we can do is make sure they have access to medical care  Weve fought for years for this new facility  and Im proud to help open this new clinic that will give our veterans in Dickinson and the surrounding region the health care they deserve  The establishment of the Dickinson clinic is the result of a five year effort by Senators Conrad and Dorgan and Congressman Earl Pomeroy to make health care more readily available to North Dakotas veterans  From 2002 to 2004  both Senators participated in the VA CARES Commission process  The Commission performed a comprehensive evaluation of all VA health care services to determine how best to meet the growing and diverse health care needs of veterans across the country  Due in part to the Senators efforts  the CARES Commission placed a greater emphasis on meeting the needs of veterans in rural areas  The VA approved five new clinics in North Dakota to supplement existing clinics in Bismarck  Grafton and Minot  as well as the Medical Center in Fargo  Community based clinics in Williston and Jamestown also recently opened  Senator Conrad  Senator Dorgan and Congressman Pomeroy have written the VA Undersecretary of Health calling on the VA to keep its commitment to opening clinics in Devils Lake and Grand Forks Air Force Base this year
