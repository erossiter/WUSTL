ND Leaders Rally Support for Conrad Farm Disaster Bill Senator s Legislation Provides Badly Needed Relief to Farmers   Ranchers                    April 25  2006  Washington    North Dakota Agriculture Commissioner Roger Johnson  Governor John Hoeven and state agriculture leaders are in Washington  DC today calling on Members of Congress and the Bush Administration to support Senator Kent Conrad s ag disaster bill  Our family farmers are in serious trouble  They ve been battered by bad weather and beaten by skyrocketing fuel prices  They need our help and they need it now  said Senator Conrad  a senior member of the Senate Agriculture Committee  Last month  Senator Conrad introduced the Emergency Agriculture Disaster Assistance Act of 2006  The legislation  which has 27 bipartisan cosponsors  provides emergency funding to farmers and ranchers who have suffered weather related crop production shortfalls  quality losses and damage to livestock feed supplies  It will also provide payments for the loss of livestock and help farmers overcome losses as a result of energy prices that spiked following last year s hurricanes  Senator Conrad said his legislation would provide about  175 million to North Dakota farmers and ranchers  The bill also includes  6 million for flooded lands in the Devils Lake region  The Senate Appropriations Committee recently adopted Senator Conrad s ag disaster legislation in a unanimous vote as part of a larger spending bill for Hurricane Katrina relief and Iraqi war costs  The supplemental spending bill  including the disaster aid legislation  now goes before the full Senate for a vote next week  Commissioner Johnson and 18 other state agriculture leaders met with Senator Conrad today to develop a strategy to garner additional support for the legislation leading up to the vote  Commissioner Johnson presented Senator Conrad with a letter from the National Association of State Departments of Agriculture  which will be delivered to Senators  House members and Agriculture Secretary Mike Johanns urging them to support the Conrad agriculture disaster package  The Administration has openly stated that it is against disaster aid to America s farmers and ranchers  Last week  Secretary Johanns told reporters he could not support the ag disaster legislation  I m worried the leadership in the House of Representatives and the White House will once again derail assistance to farmers  Senator Conrad said  America s farmers and ranchers have been hit hard  The Administration must not turn its back on them again
