Conrad Helps Break Ground on Biodiesel Plant Dakota Skies Will Help Lessen US Dependence on Foreign Energy  Senator says                    August 18  2006  Minot    Senator Kent Conrad helped lead a ceremony today to break ground on  the future site of Dakota Skies  a  75 million facility that will produce 80 000  gallons of biodiesel a day from canola oil  I am a firm believer that we need to look to the Midwest rather than the Mid  East for our energy  We need to look to American farm fields instead of overseas  oil fields  Senator Conrad said  Dakota Skies is making this vision a reality  and is another example of how North Dakota has the potential to serve as the  nation s powerhouse  Senator Conrad was instrumental in bringing together the partners that helped  finance the project  Dakota Skies Biodiesel  LLC  expects to be the anchor  tenant of a new agro industrial park in southeast Minot  Construction is  scheduled to begin next month and could create more than 300 jobs  When up and  running  both the biodiesel plant and adjacent crushing facility will employ  more than 50 people  Biodiesel is a renewable  clean burning fuel based on vegetable oil  Dakota  Skies will use canola based oil  North Dakota grows more than 900 000 acres of  canola annually  Dakota Skies will use about 355 000 acres each year    more  than a third of the state s total canola crop  From his position on the Senate Finance Committee  Senator Conrad wrote a  series of provisions into last year s Energy Bill to promote development in  alternative fuels  including biodiesel  Senator Conrad supports a national energy policy that secures reliable  sources of affordable energy by tapping into America s domestic supplies of  coal  wind  ethanol and biodiesel  He recently introduced a sweeping energy bill  he calls Breaking Our Long term Dependence  or BOLD  Energy Act  Through a mix  of tax credits  grants and new renewable fuel standards  the 125 page bill would  reward conservation  fund research into new energy technology  upgrade our  nation s electricity grid and increase production of renewable energy and  alternative fuels  This new facility will create clean fuel to pump into our cars as well as  pump new money into North Dakota s economy  Senator Conrad said  Dakota Skies  proves we can use our state s vast resources to reduce our dependence on foreign  oil and grow our state s economy with new  high paying jobs
