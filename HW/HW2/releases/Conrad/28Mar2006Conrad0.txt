Family Farmers  Ranchers Call for Disaster Aid Farm Groups Endorse Senator Conrad s Bipartisan Bill                    March 28  2006  Washington    A coalition of farm and ranch organizations rallied on Capitol Hill today in support of Senator Kent Conrad s bipartisan legislation to provide assistance to farmers and ranchers recovering from natural disaster  including drought  disease and Hurricane Katrina  The legislation has 24 co sponsors  including Senators Max Baucus of Montana and Byron Dorgan of North Dakota  both of whom appeared with Senator Conrad and the farm groups at today s press conference on Capitol Hill  The lead Republican co sponsor of the bill is Senator Mel Martinez of Florida  North Dakota s farmers and ranchers have suffered from a wave of losses    rain  frost  floods and even anthrax  Every single county in North Dakota was designated a primary disaster county last year  Senator Conrad said  Disastrous weather kept farmers from planting more than a million acres last year in North Dakota  This bill is a priority for our family farmers in rural America because our producers are in jeopardy  Twenty two national farm organizations  including the National Farmers Union and the American Farm Bureau Federation  have written Congress to endorse disaster legislation  Weather related disaster losses  declining commodity prices and skyrocketing energy costs are putting farmers in an extreme financial bind  NFU President Tom Buis said  Our nation has the best farmers and ranchers in the world  but one thing that we can t do is control the weather  This bipartisan legislation will help mitigate the economic impact Mother Nature had on our members last year  The bill would provide emergency funding to farmers and ranchers who have suffered weather related crop production shortfalls  quality losses and damage to livestock feed supplies  It will also provide payments for the loss of livestock  The bill will also help farmers overcome losses as a result of energy prices that spiked following last year s hurricanes  Energy related farm expenses rose by more than  5 billion last year compared to 2004  unlike other industries  farmers and ranchers are largely unable to pass on these costs  meaning those higher energy costs directly reduce farm income  This bill gives our farmers and ranchers a chance to stay in business by overcoming some of the losses due to events outside of their control  Senator Conrad said  The following is a list of cosponsors of Senator Kent Conrad s legislation to help farmers and ranchers
