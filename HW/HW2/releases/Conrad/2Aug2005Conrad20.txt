Conrad Works to Make Web Safer for Kids  Senator Introduces Legislation to Protect Children from Internet Pornography                    August 2  2005  Fargo    Senator Kent Conrad was joined by community leaders  law enforcement officials and concerned parents today to announce legislation he recently introduced that would clamp down on Web pornographers and fund efforts to battle pedophiles using the Internet to lure victims  We need to do everything we can to protect our children  Senator Conrad said  There are already laws in place to prevent young kids from purchasing alcohol  tobacco  or tickets to R rated movies  Now we need to better shield our children from Internet pornography  This bill will do exactly that and help fight against pedophiles who lure their victims through the Internet  Becky Berg of the North Dakota PTA and Keith Ternes  Fargo s Assistant Police Chief  joined Senator Conrad at today s announcement  Last week  Senator Conrad introduced the Internet Safety and Child Protection Act  which would require adult oriented Web sites to conduct the on line equivalent of an ID check  using age verification software that already exists and is in use on other Web sites that sell products  such as tobacco  that are inappropriate for minors  Although technology exists to prevent minors from accessing pornographic Web sites  only 3 percent of these sites use the technology  Senator Conrad s bill would also establish an Internet Safety and Child Protection Trust Fund to coordinate federal law enforcement resources and others to combat Internet related crimes against children  The Trust Fund would be financed from a 25 percent federal excise tax on fees paid by the viewers of Internet pornography  The widespread use of the Internet has provided many positive opportunities  especially for North Dakotans  It has helped us communicate across long distances  helped businesses grow and aided students in their studies  Senator Conrad said  But the Internet can be a parent s worst nightmare  exposing children to inappropriate material and helping sexual predators find their victims  This bill will fight these dangers and help give concerned parents some peace of mind  A national report  prepared by Third Way  a think tank and advocacy group  found that there are more than 420 million pornography Web sites on the Internet  and children between the ages of 12 and 17 are among the largest consumers of these Web sites  It also found that Internet pornographers drive children to their Web sites with slight misspellings of words popularly searched by children  such as Disneyland  TeleTubbies and Pokemon
