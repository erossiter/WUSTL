Statement of Senator Kent Conrad on Federal Marriage Amendment                    June 7  2006  Washington    Senator Kent Conrad issued the following statement today in  response to questions about the proposed marriage amendment to the Constitution  I m opposed to gay marriage  Marriage should be reserved for a union between  a man and a woman  That s why I voted for the Defense of Marriage Act  which  gives states the right to bar gay marriage  Marriage should be determined at a  state level  as it always has been  In North Dakota  there is no gay marriage   None  Our state law does not permit it  and I voted for the federal Defense of  Marriage Act that allows North Dakota the right to make this decision  We shouldn t amend the United States Constitution unless it is absolutely  necessary  The Defense of Marriage Act is now the law of the land  and it has  not been overturned  so there is no need for a Constitutional amendment
