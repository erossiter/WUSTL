Delegation Asks Bush to Discuss CAFTA with Family Farmers During Visit to North Dakota                     February 1  2005  Washington  Senators Kent Conrad and Byron Dorgan and Congressman Earl Pomeroy Tuesday asked President Bush to meet with Red River Valley family farmers  particularly those who grow sugar beets  during his trip to Fargo Thursday  The delegation said that at the meeting  the President should listen to concerns about the how the pending Central America Free Trade Agreement  CAFTA  will likely impact the futures of family farmers in the region  In a letter to President Bush  the North Dakota delegation said CAFTA is the first step to dismantling the nation s sugar program  which operates successfully and at no cost to the U S  taxpayer  CAFTA would also open the door to future trade agreements that threaten to flood our domestic market with additional cheap foreign sugar  they said  Under CAFTA  the Central American nations would have increased access to America s sugar market at a time when our sugar market is already oversupplied  the delegation wrote  The sugar beet industry provides thousands of jobs and billions of dollars in economic activity for North Dakota  they wrote  Sugar beets are one of the most important crops our family farmers produce  The citizens of North Dakota rarely have the opportunity to hear directly from their President  the delegation continued  We hope that while you are visiting Fargo you will take the opportunity to meet with a small group of family farmers to hear their concerns about the impact CAFTA will have on their futures
