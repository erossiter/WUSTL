Conrad Bill Helps Fight Meth Epidemic Legislation Would Help Displaced Children  Add Muscle to Police Effort                    October 4  2005  Fargo    Senator Conrad met with students  local police and school officials to discuss his Combat Meth Act  which is near final approval in Congress  In addition  Senator Conrad announced the creation of a new bipartisan Senate Meth Caucus  which he will Co Chair  The Combat Meth Act is a significant step in combating one of the nation s most serious epidemics    methamphetamine addiction    by making sure our communities have the resources they need  Senator Conrad said  Time after time  I hear from families who have suffered from meth addiction  I am hopeful that our legislation will help coordinate the federal and local response to prevent this devastating addiction and bring to justice those who profit from it  Joining Senator Conrad today were Fargo Police Chief Chris Magnus and Grant Benjamin  an officer from Discovery Middle School and President of the North Dakota Fraternal Order of Police  The police officials said Senator Conrad s legislation was well focused on providing millions of additional dollars for law enforcement  treatment and prevention efforts and environmental clean up The Senate has already passed the Combat Meth Act  and lawmakers are urging passage in the House of Representatives  Senator Conrad said his elevation to co chair of the Senate Meth Caucus means he can exert greater influence in directing the legislation  Fellow members include Senator Conrad Burns  Republican of Montana  and Senator James Talent  Republican of Missouri  Meth is not welcome in North Dakota  I refuse to surrender to the continued destruction of the family  Senator Conrad said  The Combat Meth Act gives prosecutors additional muscle to fight meth trafficking  We know that 95 percent of the meth used in North Dakota is brought into the state    only 5 percent is made here  We must also coordinate local  state and federal agencies to help children who become victims of meth abuse when their addicted parents enter either jail or treatment  Our bill gives help to those families  The specific provisions of this bill include  Provide an additional  15 million under the Community Oriented Policing Services  COPS  program to train  This funding will provide state and local law enforcement  to investigate and lock up methamphetamine offenders Expand the methamphetamine hot spots program  This program will make funds available for personnel and equipment for enforcement  prosecution and environmental clean up  Provide  5 million to hire additional federal prosecutors and train local prosecutors in state and federal meth laws  Cross designate local prosecutors as Special Assistant U S  Attorneys  This provision allows local prosecutors to bring legal action against cooks and traffickers in federal courts under tougher guidelines  Restrict the sale of necessary ingredient to make meth  The following nationwide initiative will amend the Controlled Substances Act to limit and record the sale of medicines containing pseudoephedrine by placing them behind the pharmacy counter  Set a limit of 7 5 grams month  This initiative limits the amount of products used to make meth a person can by and also requires his or her signature and identification to purchase them  Provide  5 million for states and businesses that legally sell ingredients used to cook meth with resources to monitor purchases of methamphetamine precursors  Provide  5 million in grant funding for Drug Endangered Children rapid response teams to promote collaboration among federal  state  and local agencies to assist and educate children that have been affected by the production of meth  Authorize  13 million to enhance treatment options  This funding will create the Methamphetamine Research  Training and Technical Assistance Center  which will research effective treatments for meth abuse and disseminate information  Create alternate procedures for stores without pharmacies and stores in rural areas  The DEA and States will now be able to develop additional procedures to continue to allow cold medicine that meet security criteria to be sold at retail stores without pharmacies and in rural areas  Establish a uniform federal standard that strengthens all existing state laws by allowing states the authority to determine appropriate penalties
