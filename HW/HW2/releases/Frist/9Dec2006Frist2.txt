SEN. FRIST PRAISES FINAL PASSAGE OF U.S.-INDIA NUCLEAR DEAL
U.S. Senate Majority Leader Sen. Bill Frist, M.D., (R-Tenn) made the following statement after final passage of the U.S.-India Civil Nuclear Cooperation bill:  
 "I am very pleased that the Congress has finally passed the U.S-India Civil Nuclear Cooperation bill and that it will be presented to the President for his signature in the near future. 
 "As I have said before, the enactment of this legislation will begin a new era in relations between two of the world's great democracies, the United States and India. I am confident that one day historians will look back on today's action by the Congress as an important turning point in our nation's foreign policy."
