SENS. FRIST, DASCHLE ANNOUNCE ENHANCED VEHICLE SECURITY MEASURES FOR U.S. CAPITOL
  U.S. Senate Majority Leader Bill Frist (R-TN) and Senate Democratic Leader Tom Daschle (D-SD) announced today that effective immediately, the U.S. Capitol Police will establish temporary perimeter security checkpoints at all streets leading into the U.S. Capitol complex and will close First Street N.E. between the Russell and Dirksen Senate office buildings to vehicular traffic. 
   "The safety and security of the American people, the Senate staff and our democratic institutions is our highest priority. These enhanced security measures will help protect all those who work in, visit, or drive by the Capitol. We are working diligently with the Capitol Police and the District of Columbia to minimize the traffic disruptions that may arise."
