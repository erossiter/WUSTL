SEN. FRIST HAILS APPOINTMENT OF SPECIAL ENVOY ON ANTI-SEMITISM
  U.S. Senate Majority Leader Bill Frist, M.D., (R-Tenn.) today made the following statement about President Bush's appointment of Gregg Rickman as the Special State Department Envoy on Anti-Semitism:  
   "I'm delighted that President Bush has appointed Gregg Rickman as the Special State Department Envoy on Anti-Semitism. Through his work in Congress investigating Swiss Banks' involvement in the Holocaust, Mr. Rickman has shown that he has all of the talents and skills needed to carry out the vital tasks before him. 
   "This is yet another step in President Bush and Secretary of State Rice's significant, ongoing efforts to combat Anti-Semitism around the globe."
