and their Families                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Blasts Bush Administration for shortchanging military families                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON   Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  today joined colleagues Frank Lautenberg  D NJ  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  in introducing an amendment to the Department of Defense  DOD  Authorization Bill that would ensure military families do not have to face the burdens of rising pharmaceutical co pays for TRICARE  the armed forces health care plan  next year  The amendment passed by a unanimous voice vote            Our military families sacrifice for us daily     said Lieberman     It is unbelievable to me that now these same families have to not only be concerned about loved ones abroad  but they also have to address rising co payments for much needed prescription drugs  The Administration should be funding this program properly instead of expecting military families to take on a greater burden when their loved ones are already serving their country             The amendment would prohibit increasing retail pharmacy copays for TRICARE beneficiaries through fiscal year 2007  The President   s budget submission proposed raising copays from  3  generic  and  9  brand name  to  5 and  15  respectively  Senators Bingaman  D NM  and Harkin  D IO  are also cosponsors of the amendment          According to the Military Officers Association of America  of all drugs dispensed through TRICARE  about 6  are through the mail order system  about 43  through the retail system and about 51  through military pharmacies  Therefore  the copay increase would place a significant burden on already stretched military families          30
