                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Senator seeks federal investigation of mental health screening for troops   deploying to Iraq                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WASHINGTON    Thursday  May 17  2006 Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  called for a thorough investigation of the military   s pre deployment mental health screening practices for troops heading into battle  In separate letters sent to the Inspector General at the Department of Defense  DOD  and the General Accountability Office  GAO  the Senator cited particular concern that only a small percentage of pre deployed service members who report a mental health problem are actually referred to an appropriate mental health professional            We put our faith in the men and women of our Armed Services to protect us everyday  they should expect and deserve to have the same faith in us     said Lieberman     Yet  the excellent and important investigation done by the Hartford Courant reveals that the deployment system   s apparently faulty implementation of mental health screening may have betrayed the trust of our soldiers and their families  If true  this is unacceptable and inexcusable  I have requested a thorough investigation into this matter and I am currently exploring legislative options to ensure our soldiers receive the treatment and help they need           The letters were prompted by the Hartford Courant   s three part series on alleged military mental health system deficiencies  The articles detailed numerous examples of soldiers who had long histories of serious mental illness  such as bipolar disorder and who resided in institutional settings  that were then deployed into combat situations         Please click  and  to view copies of the letters          30
