                                                                                                                                         Lieberman Introduces Colon Cancer Screen for Life Act                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        WASHINGTON   Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  on Thursday reintroduced the Colon Cancer Screen for Life Act  a bill he first introduced in 2002 that aims to improve utilization of lifesaving colorectal cancer screening among Medicare beneficiaries and to improve Medicare coverage of procedures        Senator Rick Santorum  R PA  joined Lieberman as the lead co sponsor and other original cosponsors of the legislation include Senators Susan Collins  R ME   Jeff Bingaman  D NM   Olympia Snowe  R ME   Ben Nelson  D NE   Lindsey Graham  R SC   Jim Jeffords  I VT   and Mary Landrieu  D LA            Heightened awareness and greater access to treatment are working  but we can do more     Lieberman said     Modern technology has blessed us with extremely accurate screening tools  in particular the colonoscopy   which results in higher colon cancer identification rates and better long term survival rates due to early detection  Colon cancer is a formidable foe  but this bill will make a difference in the fight against it           Colon cancer  the third most commonly diagnosed cancer in both men and women  is the second most common cause of cancer related death in the United States   If passed  this bipartisan bill will restore reimbursement for colorectal cancer screening and diagnostic tests to the level established in 1997 when the Medicare screening benefit was first enacted   It would also exempt colorectal cancer screening procedures from the customary Medicare deductible requirement  and provide Medicare coverage for a preoperative visit or consultation prior to a screening colonoscopy              30
