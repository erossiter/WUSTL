                                                                                                                                       Witnesses Will Describe Measures for Containing Costs and Encouraging Emissions Reductions Abroad                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      WASHINGTON  D C    Senators Joseph Lieberman  ID CT  and John Warner  R VA   the chairman and ranking minority member  respectively  of the Senate Subcommittee on Private Sector and Consumer Solutions to Global Warming and Wildlife Protection  announced today that they will hold a hearing in their subcommittee next Tuesday  July 24  on options for containing costs and encouraging overseas emissions reductions in legislation to curb global warming   The two senior members of the Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works announced last month that they will unveil a new economy wide  cap and trade climate bill by early August   Tuesday s hearing will be the third they have held on climate legislation in their subcommittee this year     Our bill will curb global warming without imposing economic hardship on Americans  and it will ensure that growing economies abroad follow our lead and do their part   said Lieberman    The experts we have invited to testify will describe measures that can achieve these aims within the economy wide  cap and trade bill that Senator Warner and I are writing     The hearing will take place at 2 30 p m  on Tuesday  July 24  in Room 406 of the Dirksen Senate Office Building    The witnesses will
