                                             Dodd  Lieberman Announce NEARLY  5 Million for   Child Development Initiatives In Connecticut                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 WASHINGTON     Two Connecticut Head Start initiatives will receive  4 987 719 in federal funds to boost education and child development for low income children  Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman announced today   The U S  Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  has awarded two grants to education and childcare centers in New Haven and New Britain which administer Head Start initiatives locally             Head Start provides Connecticut children with the necessary skills to begin the life long journey of education     Dodd said     By providing them with the right start we ensure that our youth grow up in a healthy environment and with the possibility of unlimited opportunity  We need to continue to invest in America   s future by fully funding Head Start                           The seeds of learning must be nourished at the earliest stages of life     Lieberman said     The child development initiatives funded with these resources will help ensure that Connecticut s neediest children start school ready to learn and can achieve the same high academic levels as other children                       The following non profit organizations will receive grants        New Haven Public Schools                                                        3 587 719      New Britain Human Resources Agency children in low income families including health  nutrition  parent involvement  and school readiness and literacy initiatives                                                            Head Start is
