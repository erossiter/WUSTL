Erika Masonhall  202 224 4041                                                                                                Dodd  Lieberman Announce Major Boost to Funding for Submarine Construction  Connecticut Defense Jobs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Washington  D C   Senators Chris Dodd  D CT  and Joe Lieberman  I CT  announced today that an additional  588 million for submarine construction has been included in the 2008 Defense Appropriations conference report released by the Appropriations Committee today  The funding will help to increase Virginia Class submarine production to two submarines per year by 2010  two years earlier than originally planned by the Navy  These new funds  which will be used to begin the procurement of critical materials at the Electric Boat shipyards  will impact thousands of jobs throughout Connecticut  especially in the southeast region of the state    With this announced new funding  it is a new day for our national security  said Dodd  The increased funding will allow the United States Congress to significantly strengthen our deterrence to hostile foreign navies  and safeguard some of our most critical intelligence and surveillance tools  The  588 million made available in this bill not only enhances America s national defense  but it also invests in the most skilled shipbuilders in the world  the workers of Electric Boat  I am proud to have worked so closely with Senator Lieberman and Representative Courtney to bring this funding to Connecticut   This additional funding is vital to our nations defense program  said Lieberman  Senator Dodd  Congressman Courtney  and I have fought hard to not only ensure that the U S  government provides the financial support necessary for a cutting edge submarine force  but also to secure resources for Connecticuts high skilled workers who play a key role in our national security   The House of Representatives and the Senate are expected to take up this measure in the next week  If approved by Congress  the bill will go to the President for his signature   30
