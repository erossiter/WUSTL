                                                                                  September 4  2005 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Statement of Senator Joe Lieberman on the passing of Chief Justice William Rehnquist                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          In both the length and depth of his service on the Supreme Court  Chief Justice William Rehnquist has earned an eminent place in the history of American law  His legacy of selfless dedication to the law  the courts and his nation will be a source of inspiration for generations to come  Our thoughts and prayers are with his family        In 1985  as attorney general for the state of Connecticut  Lieberman argued and lost 8 1 a First Amendment case before the Supreme Court  with only then Associate Justice Rehnquist voting for his position       In that case   The Estate of Thornton vs  Caldor   Donald E  Thornton a devout Presbyterian had  sued his employer for the right to be excused from working on Sunday as was allowed under Connecticut law at the time       The majority opinion  authored by then Chief Justice Warren Burger  said Connecticut s law was unconstitutional  Rehnquist was the sole dissenter but issued no written opinion         I will always have a warm spot in my heart for Justice Rehnquist because in the one case I had the honor to argue before the Supreme Court  in which we were trying to protect the right of a Christian man not to be forced to work on Sunday  Justice Rehnquist was the only member of the Court to agree with our position   Lieberman said       30
