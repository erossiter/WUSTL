                                                                                  October 28  2005 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Honors Rosa Parks                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Senator joins colleagues in authorizing the remains of Rosa Parks to lie in the rotunda of the Capitol       WASHINGTON  Senator Joseph Lieberman  D CT  joined twenty eight Senate Colleagues in co sponsoring   titled  A concurrent resolution authorizing the remains of Rosa Parks to lie in honor in the rotunda of the Capitol  The resolution passed by Unanimous Consent yesterday  October 27  2005  The House of Representatives is expected to pass a similar resolution today  adding to Ms  Parks    ground breaking achievements  With the consent of Ms  Parks    family she will be the first woman to lie in repose at the United States Capitol            This is the first nation founded not just on a set of borders  but on a set of ideals  that we should all be free to pursue our dreams and realize our God given potential     Lieberman said     Rosa Parks embodied those ideals in the struggle for social justice that she waged throughout her life   But it is her brave act of keeping her seat on a bus that made her a catalyst and an icon in the great American movement towards social justice          30
