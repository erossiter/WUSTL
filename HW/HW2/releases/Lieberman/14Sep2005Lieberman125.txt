                                                                                  September 14  2005 Contact                                                                                               Contact    202 224 9965                                                                                                Lieberman Statement on Senate Vote to Uphold EPA Mercury Rule                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON      Senator Joe Lieberman issued the following statement today on the Senate   s 51 47 vote to defeat a resolution offered by Senators Patrick Leahy  D VT  and Susan Collins  R ME   and co sponsored by Lieberman  which rejected EPA s rule for reducing mercury emissions from power plants           I am disappointed that the Senate today failed to hold the EPA accountable for failing to protect Americans from mercury pollution from power plants   It is clear the EPA has not lived up to its responsibility to follow the law and impose tough standards that would cut mercury emissions substantially below today s levels   When an estimated 600 000 plus American children born each year have unsafe levels of mercury in their blood  and we can   t fish in any fresh waters in my home state of Connecticut without being concerned about mercury pollution in the fish  it is beyond belief that the Senate would choose to uphold regulations that require next to no affirmative action on the part of industry   It is my hope that the courts will step in and follow the Clean Air Act and send the rule back to the EPA to get it right          30
