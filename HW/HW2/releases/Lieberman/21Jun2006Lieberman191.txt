                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Senator Praises New Computer Hacking and Intellectual Property  CHIP  Unit Opening in New Haven                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        WASHINGTON    Senator Joe Lieberman today applauded the Department of Justice   s increase in the number of Computer Hacking and Intellectual Property  CHIP  Units in operation  including the creation of a new CHIP office in New Haven  This news came as Attorney General Alberto Gonzales announced today that the Department of Justice has fully implemented all 31 recommendations put forth by the Intellectual Property Task Force in its 2004 report  The number of CHIP Units was nearly doubled  from 13 to 25  in less than two years            Protecting intellectual property rights is one of the most difficult challenges of the 21st century     said Lieberman     I am very pleased that the Department of Justice has recognized the need to meet these challenges  and in particular  has chosen to make Connecticut one of the areas of focus for this important endeavor            Other recommendations from the report which have been implemented include the deployment of a federal prosecutor to serve as an Intellectual Property Law Enforcement Coordinator to Southeast Asia and secured funding for an IPLEC in Eastern Europe to handle regional efforts to enforce and protect intellectual property rights  In addition  the Department of Justice has been working on dismantling international criminal organizations that commit intellectual property offenses  increasing the number of extradition and mutual legal assistance treaties that include intellectual property offenses  and creating innovative intellectual property educational programs for America s youth        To download a copy of the Progress Report of the Department of Justice s
