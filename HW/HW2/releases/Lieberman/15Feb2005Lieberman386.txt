                                                                                  February 15  2005 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Dodd  Lieberman Join Bi Partisan Call to Reverse Bush Decision and Fully Fund Amtrak                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Senators join letter to Senate budget leaders requesting adequate Amtrak funding       WASHINGTON     In a letter to Senate budget leaders  Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman expressed serious concerns over the President   s proposal to eliminate funding for Amtrak  In the letter  a bipartisan group of 38 senators asked the top Republican and Democrat on the Senate Budget Committee to reject the President   s budget that would essentially liquidate Amtrak and stop passenger rail service across the country   The senators asked Senators Kent Conrad  D ND  and Judd Gregg  R NH  to provide the necessary funding in order for Amtrak to continue to serve the more than 25 million passengers that ride on Amtrak each year           At a time when Amtrak is setting ridership records and congestion at our airports and on the highways continues to increase  we believe it would be a grave mistake to cut the essential federal funds that keep Amtrak operating     the letter said     Without such funds or other intervening action  Amtrak would quickly enter bankruptcy and shutdown of all Amtrak services  leaving millions of riders and thousands of communities without access to the essential and convenient transportation that Amtrak provides           The letter pointed out that reasonable funding levels have been proposed for this fiscal year  Amtrak s 5 year Strategic Plan  which was approved by Amtrak s Board of Directors on June 10  2004  specifies that approximately  1 8 billion will be required for fiscal year 2006 to provide safe and efficient operation of the railroad  In addition  the most recent reauthorization proposal from the Administration would require a funding level of at least  1 5 billion for fiscal year 2006  according to the Department of Transportation Inspector General           If Amtrak had the same opportunity to receive Federal infrastructure investments as highway and aviation interests  with a federal match comparable to funds available to those modes of transportation  many more communities would avail themselves of passenger rail service     the lawmakers wrote        Dodd met today with Amtrak President and CEO David Gunn to discuss the issue   Lieberman met with Gunn on the issue on February 4  2004                                                                                           30
