                                   Lieberman  Bush Budget Will Harm Hispanic Families                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Proposed cuts reflect wrong priorities  limits opportunities                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Washington   Today Senator Joe Lieberman joined colleagues Jeff Bingaman  D NM   Bob Menendez  D NJ   and representatives of major Hispanic advocacy groups in hosting a discussion about the impact of President Bush   s budget on Hispanic families and to call for better priorities    The President   s new budget slashes funding for health care  education and nutrition programs vital to the Hispanic community in order to benefit multi millionaires and special interests          Across the board  the President   s budget has wrongly cut healthcare funding for our underserved populations     said Lieberman  co chair of the Senate Democratic Hispanic Task Force     The Administration   s healthcare cuts leave Latino families struggling with rising healthcare costs at time when the uninsured rate among Latinos is higher than any other racial and ethnic group in America   A reduction to the State Children   s Health Insurance Program  SCHIP  and Medicaid of approximately  13 5 billion over five years means that Latinos will have to overcome severe obstacles to ensure that their children and elders receive proper healthcare          This budget also fails to address the documented health disparities that exist in our healthcare system leaving Latino families to face poorer quality of care and poorer access   The President needs to back a    Faircare    system like I have proposed    it establishes standards of quality medical care for diseases  holds health professionals responsible to these standards and gives grants to hospitals and clinics to gather care information electronically so that there is transparency in our healthcare system  In other words  if there is unequal care being delivered in a health care system  the public should know about it   This is the kind of plan that was so notably absent in the President   s budget proposal   The Latino community took notice and so did the rest of America            Despite the substantial challenges still facing Latino students  the President   s budget would reduce federal education spending by  2 1 billion  and would completely eliminate 42 federal education programs that help Latino students succeed         Representatives of the National Council of la Raza  the Hispanic Federation  the Hispanic Association of Colleges and Universities  the National Hispanic Medical Association  the Cuban American National Council  ASPIRA  the Labor Council for Latin American Advancement  and NALEO also participated in the discussion        On behalf of the Senate Democratic Hispanic Task Force  Senators Menendez and Bingaman pledged that Senate Democrats will fight for a federal budget that expands opportunity  protects our national security  and helps
