                                                                                                                                             Dodd  Lieberman Announce Nearly  875 000 For CT Firefighters                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 WASHINGTON   Six Connecticut fire departments will receive a total of  874 166 in federal grants to improve operations and firefighter safety  Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman announced today  The grants are made available through the FIRE Act of 2000  which is administered by the Department of Homeland Security                                             Connecticut firefighters often are the first on the scene of a catastrophe  They shouldn t be last when it comes to getting critically important resources     said Dodd     Thankfully this assistance     while only a start     can begin to address those tremendous shortcomings and provide our firefighters with the help they need and deserve             First responders are there to protect us at times of crisis  and the least we can do is provide them with the resources they need to serve our communities     Lieberman said     These grants will help train and equip Connecticut firefighters to save lives          The following localities will receive grant awards        Columbia Volunteer Fire Department Inc                                               225 000      Glastonbury Fire Department                 270 000      Center Groton Fire District                                          124 857      Kensington Fire Department                                         88 740      Washington Volunteer Fire Department  Inc                                            78 845      New London Fire Department                                        86 724      The awards were made possible by the FIRE Act of 2000  a law authored by Dodd and co sponsored by Lieberman that gives local fire departments the ability to purchase new equipment  hire new personnel  and initiate education and training programs  Since the FIRE Act was enacted  the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA   now a part of DHS  has distributed 247 grants for a total of more than  22 8 million in Connecticut to help fire departments strengthen their response capabilities
