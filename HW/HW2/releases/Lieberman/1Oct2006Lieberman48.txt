Disaster Victims                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Legislation Will Provide Translation  Language Services for Victims                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    WASHINGTON     Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee Ranking Member Joe Lieberman  D Conn   praised passage of provisions he championed that will protect non English speakers from discrimination during national disasters and will provide translated disaster preparedness and response information  which were included in the FY 2007 Department of Homeland Security Appropriations bill approved on Friday   The legislation is expected to be signed into law by the President this weekend            Hurricane Katrina underscored the importance of good communications before a disaster  to help people evacuate and protect themselves  and afterward  so that devastated communities understand the resources available to them to help their recovery     said Lieberman     We can   t afford to have any community members left in the dark during disaster situations  and these provisions will ensure that all Americans  including those with limited English skills  receive information in their native languages before and after disaster strikes           The legislation  which is supported by the Congressional Asian Pacific Caucus  the Asian American Justice Center  the National Immigration Law Center  and National Council of La Raza   adds non English speakers to the list of people who are legally guaranteed equal access to relief materials and assistance during and after a national disaster or emergency  It directs the Administrator of FEMA to work in coordination with state and local governments to address the needs of non English speakers in all of FEMA   s disaster plans        In the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina  many non English speaking communities faced trouble accessing information and disaster relief services due to language and cultural differences  Senator Lieberman insisted that measures to address this problem be included in legislation he and Chairman Susan Collins  R  Me  drafted in response to hurricane Katrina  to ensure that the cultural and linguistic needs of all communities are met in future disaster preparation and response       30
