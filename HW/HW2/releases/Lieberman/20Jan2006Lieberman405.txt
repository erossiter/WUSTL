                                                                                                                                             Lieberman Fights for CT Seniors to Receive Prescription Drugs and Customer Service They Need                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Senator joins colleagues in Introducing Legislation to Fix Crisis for America   s Seniors                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON        Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  joined Democratic colleagues on addressing the needs of the hundreds of thousands of low income seniors who have been victimized by the flawed Medicare prescription drug program  Lieberman is co sponsoring two pieces of legislation introduced by Senator John D   Jay  Rockefeller IV  D WV  and Senator Frank Lautenberg  D NJ  that will ensure all seniors and disabled persons receive the drugs they need and are entitled to             In 2003  I opposed the establishment of the Medicare Part D program because I felt that the prescription drug benefit was not generous enough toward seniors  too generous toward drug makers  and confusing  Through direct correspondence with HHS Secretary Leavitt I have pushed HHS hard to do a better job implementing this overly complicated legislation with a focus on vulnerable seniors    poor seniors and those in long term care     said Lieberman     I am now actively working with Senate colleagues to pass legislation to fix the implementation problems we anticipated        The Rockefeller bill  the REPAIR  Requiring Emergency Pharmaceutical Access for Individual Relief  Act of 2006 would provide guaranteed access to prescription drugs for all seniors transitioning to Medicare Part D plans  with special provisions for dual eligible beneficiaries   those that previously received drugs through the Medicaid program  Many seniors are enrolled in drug plans that do not cover the drugs they need   Many dual eligibles were supposed to have been automatically transferred to the new Part D program but in some cases they were not  leaving them without their medicines or with exorbitant bills mistakenly charged to them        The Medicare State Reimbursement Act  introduced by Senator Lautenberg  requires the federal government to reimburse the states for the cost of prescription drugs for low income seniors and people with disabilities while HHS fixes its part D implementation problems and directs HHS to recover overpayments made by states to private prescription drug plans and return that money to the Medicare Trust Fund   Many states are incurring significant costs due to implementation and administrative failures  For instance  Connecticut has announced plans to ensure low income and dual eligible residents receive their medicines even if pharmacies cannot confirm coverage through the Part D benefit  forcing the state to bear additional costs  California is spending  70 million dollars in just two weeks to cover the cost of drugs that were supposed to have been paid for by part D         For several years  Senate Democrats had warned about an impending crisis that would begin with the Medicare prescription drug program   s inception on January 1  particularly for low income seniors and the disabled   However  Republicans almost unanimously thwarted Democratic legislative efforts to fix this crisis  relying on the administration   s claim that the transition would be seamless          30
