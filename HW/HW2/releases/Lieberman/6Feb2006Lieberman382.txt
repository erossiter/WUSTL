                                             Dodd  Lieberman Announce a  2 5 Million  Homeland Security Grant for Connecticut                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Funding will improve emergency preparedness in the state                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               WASHINGTON     The state of Connecticut will receive a  2 587 485 grant from the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS  to help state and local officials develop  maintain  and improve their ability to prepare and respond to possible terrorist attacks and natural disasters  Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman announced Monday           On a day when the President released a budget proposal that clearly shows he is out of touch with the security needs of the country  this funding provides a bit of good news     said Dodd      While much more must be done  this assistance will help ensure that our state is better prepared to handle possible terror threats or disasters                First responders are the eyes and ears of our communities  and make tremendous sacrifices to ensure our safety     said Senator Joe Lieberman     The very least we can do is provide them with the tools they need to protect us   This is a significant grant that will help the state s first responders mitigate  prepare for  respond to  and recover from natural disasters or terrorist attacks         The Emergency Management Performance Grant  EMPG  allows states to allocate funds according to risk and to address the most urgent state and local needs in disaster preparedness  response  and recovery   The EMPG provides support services that state and local governments need to improve emergency management preparedness which include hazard identification and risk assessment  hazard management  resource management  training  and public education             30
