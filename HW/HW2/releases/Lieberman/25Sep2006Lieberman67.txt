  Catherine McKenna Ribeiro  202 224 0975                                                                                                Dodd  Lieberman Announce  13 7 Million to Help Protect Connecticut Children    Others From Lead Poisoning                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     WASHINGTON     Senators Chris Dodd  D CT  and Joe Lieberman  D CT  announced today that they have helped secure  13 714 771 in federal funding to help protect Connecticut   s children and families from lead poisoning   The grants were awarded by the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development   s Office of Healthy Homes and Lead Hazard Control  and will be disbursed to the State of Connecticut  the City of Hartford and St  Francis Hospital and Medical Center            With the appropriate resources and awareness  lead poisoning can be prevented     said Dodd      No child should be exposed to lead hazards  and this funding helps protect Connecticut   s most innocent citizens by creating lead free homes              Even today  although lead is no longer used in residential paints or piping  the poisonous legacy of old paint and pipes continues to threaten the healthy development of millions of children   We must do everything we can to protect our children from the danger of lead poisoning     said Lieberman     These grants will play a crucial role in helping identify and clean up the living environments for Connecticut children who with their families are at unnecessary risk from lead poisoning           The State of Connecticut will receive  7 million to complete lead hazard control in more than 650 housing units in 14 cities and towns throughout the state  The State of Connecticut Lead Action for Medicaid Primary Prevention  LAMPP  project provides early intervention and prevention services to Medicaid enrolled and other low income children under six years old         Additionally  the City of Hartford   s Department of Health and Human Services will be awarded  6 416 713 to help reduce and control lead hazards in more than 600 housing units within the city  and will be able to improve its community outreach and education techniques to educate its residents         The Saint Francis Hospital and Medical Center in Hartford will also receive funding   HUD will provide  298 058 for the center   s outreach programs  which target residences that have been identified as housing children with elevated blood lead levels         Long time supporters of measures that protect children from lead poisoning  Dodd and Lieberman were both cosponsors of legislation that designated October 23  29  2005  as    National Childhood Lead Poisoning Prevention Week      The measure was unanimously adopted in the Senate            30
