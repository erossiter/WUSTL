                                                                                  December 6  2005 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Calls on Bush to Reengage in International Global Warming Negotiations                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  joined a bipartisan group of 24 Senate colleagues in calling on the Bush Administration to reengage in international efforts to combat global warming   The Senators sent the letter last night  at the beginning of a critical week of meetings in Montreal  Canada among 189 countries to discuss actions that can be taken under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change   The Senators reminded the Administration that their deliberate decision not to participate in the implementation of the Kyoto Protocol undercuts the obligations of the United States as set forth in the UNFCCC treaty           I strongly urge President Bush to reengage in international global warming negations and become part of the solution to this problem     Lieberman said      The Bush Administration s lack of leadership in tackling the impending calamity of climate change has put the United States behind the rest of the developed world   They must come back to the international table and they must start solving the problem here at home   The best way to fill that leadership vacuum is market based carbon cap system set forth in the McCain Lieberman Climate Stewardship and Innovation Act
