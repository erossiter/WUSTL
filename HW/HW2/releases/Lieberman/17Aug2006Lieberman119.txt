                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Awarded for Action Against Genocide                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Taking a stand to prevent escalating humanitarian crisis in Darfur                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     WASHINGTON     Today the Genocide Intervention Network  a non profit organization working to stop genocide around the world  awarded Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  an A  rating for his consistent support and leadership in pressing for decisive U S  action against the genocide in Darfur  Sudan  The group released their first ever scorecards grading members of Congress on their support of legislative efforts to address the crisis in the region           To ignore this tragedy is to turn our backs on the fundamental humanitarian values of our society and to forget the lessons of our history     said Lieberman     There is no cause so important as to save our fellow human beings from this suffering  The people of Darfur deserve this defense            The Senator co sponsored bipartisan legislation that first identified the brutal actions of the Janjaweed militia in Darfur as genocide  and he was pleased to see the Bush Administration follow the lead of the Congress in that designation shortly thereafter   He also co sponsored the Darfur Accountability Act  which would block the assets of  and deny visas to  those who committed the genocide in Darfur  enhance U S  and international assistance to the current African Union peacekeeping force in Sudan  and deny the Government of Sudan access to oil revenues should it fail to live up to past agreements         Senator Lieberman has also urged President Bush and Secretary of State Rice to accelerate their efforts to stop the genocide in Darfur   In July 2006  he initiated Senate Resolution 531 to urge the President to appoint a Special Presidential Envoy for Sudan and take other steps to increase U S  engagement   That resolution has now gained support from more than 25 co sponsors from both sides of the aisle in the Senate        30
