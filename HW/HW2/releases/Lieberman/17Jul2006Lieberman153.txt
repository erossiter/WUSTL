                                                                                                                                         Funding Awarded to Programs Targeting Connecticut   s Neediest Citizens                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  WASHINGTON    Senators Chris Dodd  D CT  and Joe Lieberman  D CT  announced today the allocation of  26 058 615 in funding from the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  for new housing initiatives in Bristol  Hartford  Waterbury  New Haven  Meriden  New Britain and West Haven  The money is being awarded through the Community Development Block Grant  CDBG   the Emergency Shelter Grant  ESG   HOME and Housing Opportunities for Persons with AIDS  HOPWA   to help some of Connecticut   s neediest residents            These are critically important investments     said Dodd      They can help provide shelter  economic opportunities  and health care to those who need it most   In short  they provide help and hope to people in need in Connecticut              This funding will help to improve the quality of life in our communities and the availability of affordable housing for Connecticut   s residents     Lieberman said     I m especially pleased that these funds are targeted to some of our most vulnerable residents  including the working poor  the homeless  and persons living with HIV AIDS           Community Development Block Grants enable state and local government to develop viable urban communities by providing decent housing and a suitable living environment  and by expanding economic opportunities  principally for low and moderate income residents  The Emergency Shelter Grants will provide funding to state  local  and private entities to improve the quality and number of emergency homeless shelters         The HOME allocations will help expand the supply of decent  affordable housing to low  and very low income families by providing grants to state and local governments to fund housing initiatives that meet local needs and priorities  HOPWA will help provide housing assistance and related supportive services to government and non profit organizations for projects that benefit low income people diagnosed with HIV AIDS  as well as their families          30
