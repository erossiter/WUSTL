                                                                                                                                         Lieberman Urges Colleagues to Approve Increased Transportation Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   During first meeting of conferees  Senator presses for swift agreement to higher funding levels passed by the Senate       WASHINGTON     During today   s first meeting of Senate and House conferees negotiating a new transportation reauthorization bill  Senator Joe Lieberman urged his colleagues to swiftly agree  to the  295 billion  six year bill passed by the Senate last month   The Senate bill has a higher funding level   295 billion versus  284 billion  than the House bill  The House bill also contains a    reopener    clause that would force a renegotiation of the funding formula after two years  a major problem for northeast states like Connecticut           Congress has been working on this legislation for two years now     Lieberman said following the conference      I urged my colleagues to finish the bill as soon as possible so that states can pay for current projects and fund new ones   There are some key differences between the Senate and House versions of this bill and I will keep fighting for our state throughout the conference negotiations  Together with the rest our state delegation  I am committed to defend the Senate version of the bill and secure every dollar we can for Connecticut           The Senate bill contains an amendment Lieberman offered to provide a 90  federal match for bridge projects on the Interstate Highway System that will give the Connecticut Department of Transportation more flexibility in how it plans construction projects in and around the state  The bill also includes an amendment by Senators Chuck Schumer  D NY  and Hillary Clinton  D NY  designed to promote transit usage by raising pre tax benefit dollars for transit users to the levels enjoyed by those who drive and park in employer designated parking garages         30
