                                                                                                                                          Connecticut to Receive More Than  1 5 Million for Emergency Energy Assistance                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Dodd  Lieberman call for more help for low income households coping with winter cold       WASHINGTON     Emphasizing the need to protect children and seniors from the dangerous effects of extreme winter cold  Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman today welcomed news that Connecticut would be receiving more than  1 5 million to help low income households with their energy needs   The funds are part of nearly  50 million released yesterday by the Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  through the Low Income Home Energy Assistance  LIHEAP  program           It s critically important that Connecticut families in need aren t left out in the cold this winter   said Dodd    This assistance   while a step in the right direction   should only be considered that  a first step   I would urge the Administration to release even more funding to ensure that this vitally important protection for those in greatest need is adequately addressed            LIHEAP funding is a vital tool to help low income families keep warm during these cold winter months     said Lieberman     The freezing temperatures in the Northeast underscore the importance of acting now to get low income families the help they need before this price crunch becomes a price crisis            The Connecticut Department of Social Services will distribute the resources to low income households to help them cover increased home energy bills that are a result of the low temperatures experienced over the past six weeks   The funds are taken from the fiscal year 2005 LIHEAP contingency fund  which allows HHS and states to respond to emergency situations such as extreme weather conditions  supply disruptions  or price spikes          Dodd and Lieberman cosigned a letter earlier this year to President Bush calling on him to release the remaining  200 million in emergency Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  funding provided in the fiscal year 2005 Consolidated Appropriations Act   Yesterday  Senators Dodd and Lieberman cosigned a bipartisan letter to Senate Budget Committee Chairman Judd Gregg  R NH  and Ranking Member Kent Conrad  D ND  requesting the Senate provide  3 billion in the FY 2006 budget for LIHEAP          30
