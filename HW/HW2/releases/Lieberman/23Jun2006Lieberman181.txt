                                                                                                                                          Lieberman Welcomes New Congressional Action to Fight Global Warming                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Praises legislation introduced by Jeffords  Boxer  Waxman                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  today praised the efforts of several colleagues in Congress to join the fight to combat global warming   Senators Jim Jeffords  I VT  and Barbara Boxer  D CA  have announced that they soon will introduce a bill to reduce aggressively the US economy   s emissions of global warming pollution and on Tuesday  Rep  Henry Waxman  D CA  introduced a House companion of the Jeffords Boxer bill             I strongly support Senators Jeffords    and Boxer   s plan to introduce new legislation to curb global warming     Lieberman said      While I will continue to push for enactment of the bill that I have introduced with John McCain  I believe the Jeffords Boxer bill is a highly productive contribution to the Senate   s work on this issue  and I will work closely with them in the weeks and months ahead to advance toward our shared goal of enacting meaningful climate legislation   After all  how much more obvious does this problem need to get before the US Congress does something about it   How much longer must we wait before the US Senate enacts the solution that it has already said it wants to enact   It is time to put two and two together and pass a strong bill to curb global warming           In a report issued yesterday on a study requested by Representative Sherwood Boehlert  the National Academy of Sciences has found that the last few decades of the 20th century have been warmer than any comparable period in the last 400 years  Yesterday was also the one year anniversary of the successful addition to the Energy Policy Act of 2005 of a resolution expressing the sense of the US Senate that that Congress should enact a comprehensive and effective national program of mandatory  market based limits and incentives on emissions of greenhouse gases that slow  stop  and reverse the growth of such emissions        30
