                                                                                                                                              Lieberman Introduces Bill to Tax Crude Oil Windfall Profits                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Emergency Legislation would institute a one time 50  tax for oil companies on profits earned from oil sold above  40 per barrel                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  today introduced a bill that would institute a one time 50  tax on windfall profits from oil produced by integrated oil companies in the 2005 tax year   The Temporary Windfall Profits Tax on Crude Oil Act would tax profits exceeding a base price of  40 per barrel   The revenue from the windfall tax would be used to offset the increased burden of home heating and cooling costs for taxpayers with adjusted gross incomes of less than  40 000   The one time payment to each taxpayer would be  450           Skyrocketing energy costs are causing American families to have to choose between heating their homes and putting food on the table     Lieberman said      This legislation is a temporary solution to help lessen the impact of these increased oil prices   The long term solution is for America to break its dependence on foreign oil by making greater use of alternative and renewable energy sources           Winter home heating costs are expected to dramatically exceed those of last year   Most of the increase is the direct result of increased energy prices  a burden that falls most heavily on those with the lowest incomes   Households with incomes below  15 000     about one fifth of all households     will spend approximately one tenth of their income just on gasoline   Natural gas for January delivery is at a near record price of  15 per million British thermal units  BTU   s           Households that heat with natural gas are expected to pay an average of  282  or 38   more than last winter   Although crude oil prices have retreated slightly from the post Katrina high of  70 per barrel  prices are still higher than last year  and the Energy Information Administration predicts that they will stay near  50 for years to come   Households that heat with oil are expected to pay  255  or 21   more than last winter         Meanwhile  the five largest integrated oil companies have announced record breaking profits of  32 9 Billion for the third quarter of 2005   Exxon alone announced profits of  9 9 Billion for the quarter  Shell exceeding  9 Billion  and BP exceeded  6 5 Billion         Last month  as part of a long term strategy to break America s costly dependence on the global oil market  Lieberman introduced the Vehicle and Fuel Choices for American Security Act  which would mandate 10 million barrels in oil savings in 25 years and would also require the mass production of cars that can burn any combination of gasoline and alternative fuels or  alternatively  cars powered by efficient hybrid engines          30
