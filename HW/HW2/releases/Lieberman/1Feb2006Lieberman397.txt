                                                                                  February 1  2006 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Reacts to the State of the Union Address                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  today made the following statement regarding President Bush   s State of the Union Address            I am glad that in tonight   s State of the Union Address President Bush focused on some of the issues that are really in the hearts and minds of Connecticut residents  whether they   re going to have a good job  or a job at all   the high cost of healthcare  and increasing energy prices   Now whether he will back up tonight   s rhetoric with meaningful action on these problems remains to be seen           In just the past few weeks  hundreds of Connecticut workers have lost their jobs and today millions of other Americans are struggling to find jobs that provide a living wage and decent benefits for them and their families  and far too many Americans are seeing their jobs eliminated or shipped off to countries like India and China   I would have liked to hear the President drop his demands for a permanent tax cut for the wealthiest Americans     a tax cut which would likely come at the expense of programs that help the neediest in our society  Our tax policy should instead be one of fairness to working families  and should not result in increasing our deficit           America   s dependence on foreign oil has increased since President Bush first promised to focus on energy independence in his 2003 State of the Union Address   Reducing the high costs of energy and reducing our dependence on oil producing nations in the Middle East that don   t have our best interest at heart are crucial to our long term security and economic growth   The President spoke again tonight about his commitment to the goal of energy independence and security   If he is serious about it he will support the legislation I have already introduced with a bipartisan group of 9 Senate colleagues that will reduce our consumption of oil by 10 million barrels a day             We remain a nation at war   at war in Iraq and also at war with Islamist terrorism around the world and it was important for the President to discuss it with the nation tonight   Now we must look to the details of his forthcoming budget to see how well funded his plans are for achieving progress and victory in the years ahead           30
