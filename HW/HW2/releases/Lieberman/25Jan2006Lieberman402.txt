                                                                                                                                             Dodd  Lieberman Announce  700 000 for Hartford Youth                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Funds will train young adults to build new homes for the needy                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON     The Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  has awarded Co Opportunity  Inc   of Hartford a grant of  700 000  Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman announced today   The grants are part of HUD   s Youthbuild initiative  which provides funding to train young people to build or renovate homes and apartments for the needy           Funding for Youthbuild serves a dual need in Hartford by providing employment opportunities for young people while also helping meet the city   s housing needs     said Dodd      This initiative will help give many of Connecticut   s youth a leg up in finding a good job  ultimately providing them and Hartford with a brighter future                The Youthbuild program offers a wonderful opportunity for kids to learn important life skills     Lieberman said     This funding is important because it not only helps prepare America   s youth for future success  it also provides housing for those who need it the most            Co Opportunity in Hartford will use these funds to build upon its nine year success in teaching young people a new trade enabling them to build new homes or renovate them for people in need   Each year  Co Opportunity provides opportunities to low income young adults to receive a combination of job training  education  counseling  job placement  and leadership development   Through both classroom and on site work experiences  participants develop valuable leadership skills and career training as they prepare for their GED exams and also put their newly acquired skills to good use rehabilitating and building housing for low income and homeless people in their communities          Senators Dodd and Lieberman actively fought to ensure that Co Opportunity in Hartford was successful in being awarded this funding   More than  485 million in grants have been awarded since the program s inception in 1993 enabling more than 47 000 young people to construct or rehabilitate nearly 13 000 affordable housing units in their communities                30
