                                                                                  August 1  2007 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Welcomes Approval of Army Corps of Engineer Studies to Benefit CT Waterways                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Senator Lauds Committee Passage of Key Wildlife Legislation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            WASHINGTON  Senator Joe Lieberman  ID CT   Chairman of the Environment and Public Works  EPW  Committee s Subcommittee on the Private Sector and Consumer Solutions to Global Warming and Wildlife Protection  welcomed the passage of two Army Corps of Engineers study resolutions to benefit Connecticut        I applaud the approval of these studies  which will make certain that the water resources of Connecticut are maintained to preserve safe navigation and ecosystem health   said Lieberman     One study will investigate sediment control  navigation  environmental restoration and preservation of New Haven Harbor  this study will assure that the depth draft of the federal navigation channel is appropriate for the continued safe navigation of the harbor   The second study will evaluate environmental restoration opportunities in the watersheds and coastal regions of the State of Connecticut with a particular emphasis on the Thames River Basin     Lieberman also lauded the passage of important wildlife legislation passed by the committee       The committee approved two bills  H R  50 and H R  465  passed by the House on July 23  2007 to reauthorize the African Elephant Conservation Act  the Rhinoceros and Tiger Conservation Act of 1994  and the Asian Elephant Conservation Act  which re establish conservation funds for these species through the Multinational Species Conservation Fund  The passed bills are similar to the reauthorization bill introduced by Senators Lieberman  John Warner  R VA   and Barbara Boxer  D CA        The passage of this legislation will continue the protection of these vital species by providing grants for conservation projects that preserve habitat  educate the public  and improve law enforcement to prevent poaching and illegal trade   Lieberman said     Senator Lieberman was also pleased by the passage of the Captive Primate Safety Act  S  1498   which he co sponsored along with Senators Boxer  Vitter  R LA   Lautenberg  D NJ   and Menendez  D NJ    The bill amends the Lacey Act by adding monkeys  apes  and other nonhuman primates to the list of animals that cannot be transported across state lines for the pet trade      30
