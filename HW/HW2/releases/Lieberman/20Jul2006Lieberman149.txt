                                                                                  July 20  2006 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Welcomes New Bill to Fight Global Warming                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Praises Efforts of Senators Jeffords and Boxer                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  welcomed new legislation introduced today by Senators Jim Jeffords  I VT  and Barbara Boxer  D CA    The bill would cut US global warming pollution aggressively  to 20  of 1990 levels by 2050            While I will continue to push for enactment of the bill that I have introduced with John McCain  I believe the Jeffords Boxer bill is a highly productive contribution to the Senate   s work on this issue     Lieberman said      If John and I do not get our bill through the U S  Senate this year  I am confident that we will succeed in the next Congress   Senator Boxer will be a powerful ally in the battle ahead  and I predict that ideas found in this bill will help us achieve victory            In 2003  Lieberman and McCain introduced the first Senate bill to reduce aggregate US global warming emissions from all major industrial sectors   They forced the Senate to vote on their bill in 2003 and again in 2005   They have pledged to continue bringing the bill to a vote until it passes
