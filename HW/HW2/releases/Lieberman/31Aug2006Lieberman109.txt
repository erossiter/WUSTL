Catherine McKenna Ribeiro  202 224 0975                                                                                                Lieberman Praises Security Council Authorization of UN Darfur Force to Prevent Escalation of Humanitarian Crisis in Darfur                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    WASHINGTON    Senator Joe Lieberman praised the adoption by the United Nations Security Council this morning of a resolution authorizing deployment of a UN military and police force to Darfur Sudan under Chapter 7 of the UN Charter   He strongly endorsed repeated calls by the U S  government and other Security Council members for the President of Sudan to accept deployment of such a force             The adoption of this resolution by the Security Council is the first step  and a very important step     said Lieberman     Now the international community needs to begin the hard work of assembling and deploying this force as soon as possible to protect the very vulnerable civilians of Darfur before the humanitarian crisis deepens even further            The Senator co sponsored bipartisan legislation that first identified the brutal actions of the Janjaweed militia in Darfur as genocide  and he was pleased to see the Bush Administration follow the lead of the Congress in that designation shortly thereafter   He also co sponsored the Darfur Accountability Act  which would block the assets of  and deny visas to  those who committed the genocide in Darfur  enhance U S  and international assistance to the current African Union peacekeeping force in Sudan  which should now be succeeded by a larger UN Darfur force   and deny the Government of Sudan access to oil revenues should it fail to live up to past agreements         Senator Lieberman has urged President Bush and Secretary of State Rice to accelerate their efforts to stop the genocide in Darfur   In July 2006  he initiated Senate Resolution 531 to urge the President to appoint a Special Presidential Envoy for Sudan and take other steps to increase U S  engagement  That resolution has now gained support from more than 25 co sponsors from both sides of the aisle in the Senate          30
