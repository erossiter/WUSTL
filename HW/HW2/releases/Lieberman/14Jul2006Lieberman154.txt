                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Senator seeks Senate Resolution to relieve Sudan                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  today sponsored a resolution urging President Bush to appoint a Presidential Special Envoy for Sudan   As the situation in Darfur continues to deteriorate  the Bush Administration is failing to provide the leadership necessary to address the problem   This lack of leadership on a major humanitarian crisis prompted Lieberman to call for this Special Presidential Envoy to ensure that U S  efforts to stop the genocide in Darfur do not take a back seat to other global crises             Stopping the genocide in Darfur is a crucial test for the United States and other like minded nations     said Lieberman      If we fail to do more to prevent the death and suffering of millions of vulnerable and isolated people  we are turning our backs on the fundamental humanitarian values of our societies  and forgetting the lessons of our history             Earlier this year  Lieberman commended the participants in the Save Darfur Coalition   s Rally to Stop Genocide  which took place in Washington  D C   He has declared support for the Darfur Peace and Accountability Act and fully funding Darfur peacekeeping   He also continues to urge President Bush and Secretary Rice to accelerate their efforts to stop the Genocide in Darfur and has called for NATO to elevate its role in bolstering the existing African Union peacekeeping force         30
