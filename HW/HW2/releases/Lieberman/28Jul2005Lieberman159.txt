                                                                                  July 28  2005 Contact                                                                                                 202 224 0975                                                                                                Lieberman Disappointed in Weak Bush Administration Climate Pact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              WASHINGTON   Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  made the following statement today in reaction to news that the United States plans to join China and India in an Asian Pacific climate agreement intended to replace the Kyoto pact with a much weaker  voluntary method to control greenhouse emissions           The Five Nation  agreement  on climate change technology  announced today by the Bush Administration  continues the unfortunate record of inaction on the one true measure of serious climate policy    mandating and achieving actual reductions in the pollution that causes climate change   Without a comprehensive program targeting real emissions reductions  meaningful investments in clean technologies simply will not get off the ground   If the Bush administration were serious  it would embrace the approach of the Climate Stewardship and Innovation Act that Senator John McCain  R AZ  and I have put forward  combining mandatory national limits with a market based strategy for reducing emissions and a systematic mechanism for promoting technological innovation and deployment           30
