Catherine Ribeiro                                                                                                Dodd  Lieberman Announce More Than  700 000     in Grant Awards for Connecticut Firefighters                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 WASHINGTON   Connecticut firefighters will receive  786 735 in federal grants from the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS   Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman announced today   Fire departments in Bethel  Germantown  East Haddam  Colchester  Hebron  Suffield  Washington  and the Mohegan Tribal Department will get funds for new equipment and safety and training programs           The brave men and women who serve in our fire departments should have every possible resource at their disposal to keep themselves and the people they protect safe     said Dodd      I will continue to work to make sure our firefighters have the tools necessary to carry out their important duties safely and effectively              Connecticut firefighters put their lives on the line every day for the rest of us     said Lieberman     The least we can do is make certain they have the training and equipment they need to do their job right  This grant is a good beginning toward ensuring that first responders are as prepared as they can possibly be           The following localities will receive grant awards        Bethel Volunteer Fire department             69 445      Germantown Volunteer Fire Department  Danbury              261 250      Town of East  AFG  Program  administered by the U S  Fire Administration   Since the FIRE Act was enacted  the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA   now part of DHS  has distributed 329 grants for a total of
