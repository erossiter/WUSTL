                                                                                  March 15  2005 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Protests EPA Mercury Rule                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WASHINGTON   Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT   a senior member of the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee  joined several Senate colleagues in protesting the weak mercury rule announced today by the Environmental Protection Agency              The EPA has a responsibility to protect Americans from toxic pollutants like mercury     Lieberman said      It had an opportunity today to follow the law and impose tough standards that would lead directly to lower concentrations of mercury in the environment   When an estimated 600 000 plus American children born each year have unsafe levels of mercury in their blood  and we can   t fish in any fresh waters in my home state of Connecticut without being concerned about mercury pollution in the fish  it is beyond belief that EPA would choose to issue regulations that require no affirmative action on the part of industry             The EPA rule will set a limit on the emissions of mercury  a potent neurotoxin  from power plants in the United States   The EPA s rule  however  sets the initial cap at 38 tons  a level that does not need to be reached until 2010  and one that could be reached by emitters without installing any mercury pollution control equipment   A second phase cap of 15 tons is set for 2018  representing a decrease of only 70  from current emissions levels   Recent reviews of the rulemaking process by the EPA Inspector General and the Government Accountability Office concluded that EPA did not conduct appropriate cost benefit analysis of its proposed rule  and appeared to choose its reduction target based on the levels the President proposed in his Clear Skies Initiative  not based on the technology review required by the Clean Air Act           EPA tells us that we need to move slowly because specific technology to control mercury emissions isn   t ready     Lieberman said      This flies in the face of reality     states like Connecticut and Massachusetts have implemented tough state wide standards calling for deep cuts in mercury emissions     and businesses are bidding on contracts to provide the systems to meet these standards   If we can reduce emissions by 85 90  in New England right now  there is no reason why should we have to wait until 2018 to achieve much weaker reductions nationwide            30
