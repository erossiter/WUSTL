                                                                                                                                                 At Senator   s Urging  FEMA Director Paulison extends disaster relief to Hartford County                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  lauded the Federal Emergency Management Agency   s  FEMA  decision to amend their December 16  2005 Disaster Declaration to include Hartford County in the wake of mid October 2005 storms  In a phone call to FEMA Director R  David Paulison on January 26  2006  Lieberman urged the Agency to review and reissue their initial county designations to help include local governments and residents dealing with the damage caused by severe flooding           The mid October 2005 severe storms and flooding  exhausted the State of Connecticut and local municipalities  storm budgets   Lieberman said   This federal funding will help ensure that Litchfield  New London  Tolland  Windham and  now  Hartford Counties have the resources necessary for continued cleanup operations and to ensure public safety         The federal government declared a state of emergency in Connecticut to help communities recover from the severe weather conditions that struck the state in mid October 2005  Now eligible towns and cities in five Connecticut counties may apply for federal funding through the State of Connecticut   s Hazard Mitigation Grant Program to assist with damage from severe storms and flooding that
