                                                                                  October 24  2005 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Dodd  Lieberman Hail Senate Passage of Transportation  Housing Appropriations Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Millions included for Connecticut rail transit and community development projects       WASHINGTON     Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman today lauded the Senate   s approval of more than  61 million in new federal funds for Connecticut to help improve rail and transit and promote affordable housing and community development   The Senate approved the funds late last night as part of the annual appropriations bill that covers the Departments of Transportation and Housing and Urban Development  THUD  for fiscal year 2006   The legislation must now be reconciled with the House version of the bill before becoming law         Senators Dodd and Lieberman joined 32 Senate colleagues  in offering a bipartisan amendment to the THUD appropriations bill to provide  3 1 billion in emergency federal home heating assistance to low income and elderly residents  The Senators continued their long fought effort to ensure low income and elderly residents are able to afford home heating costs during the cold winter months by cosponsoring the amendment that was voted down        Included in the spending package is  3 77 billion for Community Development Block Grants to states  The CDBG program  which is administered by the Department of Housing and Urban Development  was created in 1974  CDBG provides resources for community development projects that benefit low  and moderate income residents including support for local health and senior services  housing rehabilitation  and improvements to parks and public facilities  President Bush announced earlier this year that he had plans to eliminate funding for the Community Development Block Grant  CDBG  program in his FY 06 budget and fold it into a new block grant  called    Strengthening America   s Communities     within the Commerce Department   Dodd and Lieberman  along with other congressional colleagues  successfully fought to keep the CDBG program alive            I   m proud to help secure more than  60 million for community redevelopment efforts and transit improvements across Connecticut   Our communities are the best in the country and this funding will help them continue to thrive and live up to their full potential     said Dodd            This bill is good news for Connecticut   Lieberman said   The funding approved today will make significant strides in improving Connecticut s commuters on rail and transit  and our communities a strong and vital part of Connecticut   s character  This bill also helps support redevelopment and modernization initiatives by restoring community development funds and funding other key state projects           Below are the Connecticut projects included in the legislation  Non transit highway and transportation projects will not be decided on until the final stage of conference committee
