                                                                                                                                         Lieberman Opposes Federal Marriage Amendment                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Calls on Republican Leadership to tackle urgent problems facing the nation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  today made the following statement on the Federal Marriage Amendment   A motion to invoke cloture on the motion to proceed to the amendment failed in the Senate today  effectively ending Senate consideration of the amendment             I have opposed the Federal Marriage Amendment when it has come up in the past and I oppose it again today because I believe it should be up to each state to define marriage within its own borders   The amendment could even jeopardize the viability of Connecticut   s civil union law and equally jeopardize the ability of other states to extend benefits and obligations to same sex couples in committed relationships   What   s more  the 1996 Defense of Marriage Act already makes clear that no state will have to recognize a same sex marriage performed in another state   There is simply no justification for this unprecedented federal intrusion into matters that have always been left to the states to decide           When the Republican leadership brought this unnecessary and unnecessarily divisive amendment to the Senate two years ago  it failed overwhelmingly  and it did so again today   In the two years since the last failed effort to pass the amendment  the challenges facing the people of Connecticut and across the nation have continued to mount   surging gas prices  skyrocketing health care costs that are bankrupting families and businesses alike  an uncertain economic and employment climate  and increasing college tuition   I have been proud to work with many of my colleagues on both sides of the aisle to develop comprehensive  long term solutions to each of these problems  With only 60 legislative days left until the Senate   s targeted adjournment  solving these problems should be the Senate   s priority           By bringing such a divisive issue that had no chance of passage to the Senate floor at a time when the American people are facing so many real challenges Senator Frist and the Republican leadership are engaging in the kind of partisan  election year politics that has caused so many Americans  who want their elected representatives to tackle the problems that directly affect their lives  to lose faith in the direction of our government            I call on Senator Frist to set a Senate agenda that shows real leadership in tackling the urgent problems facing our nation
