                                                                                  May 23  2005 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Hails Bi Partisan Senate Agreement to Stop the Nuclear Option                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      WASHINGTON   Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  today made the following statement on the agreement reached by a bi partisan group of 14 Senators to stop the nuclear option and preserve the supermajority rule            We came together and did the unexpected   In a senate that has become increasingly partisan and polarized  the bi partisan center held   Each of us accepted parts of this agreement which were not perfect for our desires  but we did it for a larger purpose  to save the right of unlimited debate and to take the Senate back from the precipice   If the nuclear option had been passed  it would have led to a cycle of increasing divisiveness in the Senate and decreasing productivity in terms of the people   s business   I thank all those who worked so hard to make this happen   I   m proud to have been a part of this  and I hope maybe this empowered bi partisan center will decide it   s been good to work together  and will keep on working together to get things done for the American people             30
