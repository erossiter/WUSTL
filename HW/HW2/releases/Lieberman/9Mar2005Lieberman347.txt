                                                                                                                                          Dodd  Lieberman Announce More Than  900 000 For Connecticut Firefighters                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     WASHINGTON   Six Connecticut fire departments will receive a total of  932 528 in federal grants to improve operations and firefighter safety  Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman announced today  The grants are made available through the FIRE Act of 2000  which is administered by the Department of Homeland Security                                             We can and should do much more to protect families from the devastating effects of fire  and firefighters deserve to have the resources to provide that security     said Dodd     After working hand in hand with firefighters for years to make this vision a reality  I   m pleased that these funds will be the first step in making sure these brave men and women have the training they need to keep our communities safe             Firefighters and other first responders make tremendous sacrifices to protect and serve our communities  and the least we can do is make sure they have the training  resources and equipment they need     Lieberman said     We must continue to do all we can to ensure that cities and towns across Connecticut receive the federal funding necessary to prepare them to respond to fires and other emergency situations           The following localities will
