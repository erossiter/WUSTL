                                                                                  February 2  2007 Contact                                                                                               Marshall Wittmann  202 224 4041                                                                                                Lieberman Welcomes UN Panel   s Global Warming Report                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           WASHINGTON    Senator Joseph Lieberman  ID CT  welcomed today   s Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change  IPCC  report  which found a greater than ninety percent probability that greenhouse gases released by human activities  such as burning oil in our cars and coal in our power plants  are causing most of the global warming observed over the last fifty years          This puts the final nail in denial   s coffin     said Lieberman      We are causing global warming   We can and must act now to solve the problem  or else we will bequeath a diminished world to our children and grandchildren   The bipartisan Climate Stewardship and Innovation Act that John McCain and I have reintroduced would reduce the nation   s greenhouse gas emissions substantially enough and quickly enough to forestall catastrophic global warming          Established as a joint project of the World Meteorological Organization and the United Nations Environment Programme  UNEP  in 1988  the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change  IPCC  brought together 2 500 scientists from more than 130 nations to spend six years preparing the report released today        30
