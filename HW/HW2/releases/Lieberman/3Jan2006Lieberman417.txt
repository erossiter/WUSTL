                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Decries Medicare Part D Implementation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Points to difficult transition for CT seniors                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WASHINGTON     Senator Lieberman made the following statement today in response to the Medicare Prescription Drug Benefit which took effect January 1  2006            In 2003  I opposed the establishment of the Medicare Part D program because I felt that the prescription drug benefit was not generous enough toward seniors  too generous toward drug makers  and confusing  I have pushed HHS hard to do a better job implementing this overly complicated legislation and explaining it to seniors because they deserve an easier transition process  As Medicare Part D goes into effect this week for millions of seniors in Connecticut and across the nation I will try to make this transition easier for seniors   During future Senate debate on ways to improve the delivery of health care and prescription drug services  be assured that I will also continue to support a stronger  streamlined Medicare program that treats current and future beneficiaries fairly               30
