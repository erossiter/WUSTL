                                                                                                                                           Lieberman Blasts Republican Budget for Shortchanging America   s Cities  Seniors                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Senate rejects Democratic Amendments to fund CDBG and Medicare                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON   Senator Joe Lieberman today blasted Senate Republicans for shortchanging millions of Americans by voting down two key Democratic amendments to the FY 2007 Federal budget proposal  These amendments  sponsored by Lieberman and his Democratic colleagues were aimed at rebuilding America   s cities and helping seniors pay for Medicare prescription drugs         Senate Republicans are ignoring cities in Connecticut and across the nation and turning their backs on America   s seniors     said Lieberman      By rejecting these critical amendments  they have shown that their priorities are not in sync with mainstream America   These cuts will make seniors more vulnerable and weaken our communities  undercutting our efforts to combat drugs  crime and poverty             The Democratic budget amendments rejected by Republicans last night include        Community Development Block Grant   Senator Lieberman cosponsored Senator Murray   s amendment to restore funding for the Community Development Block Grant Program to the fiscal 2004 level by closing tax loopholes previously slated for elimination in Senate passed legislation   Several Connecticut programs could be hurt by cuts to the CDBG program  including CASASTART  a Bridgeport drug prevention center and Hartford Children   s Theatre   Earlier this week  Lieberman heard the concerns of several Connecticut mayors at the annual Connecticut Conference of Municipalities breakfast in Washington        Medicare Prescription Drugs   This amendment  offered by Senator Bill Nelson  and co sponsored by Senator Lieberman  would have established a deficit neutral reserve fund to protect Medicare beneficiaries who enroll in the prescription drug benefit during 2006 by extending the open enrollment period through the end of 2006 without imposing a late enrollment penalty and would have allowed seniors to make a one time change in plan enrollment at any point in 2006 to correct any mistakes they may have made           30
