                                                                                  September 13  2006 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Lauds Authorization of Studies That Will Help Protect and Restore Neighborhoods  Parks  and a Harbor In Connecticut                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT   a senior member of the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee  welcomed the committee   s passage today of resolutions requesting three Army Corps of Engineers studies to benefit Connecticut           I welcome the committee   s approval of these important studies  which will help protect homeowners  repair ecosystems  and restore parks and a harbor in Connecticut     said Lieberman      Specifically  the studies will take a vital first step toward increasing protections for homeowners in New Haven  improving conditions in Mystic Harbor  and restoring a park and ponds in Milford  Meriden  and Stamford           One study will investigate the potential causes  extent  and scope of sinking homes in the Beverly Hills area of New Haven   A second study will evaluate water resource priorities in Mystic Harbor and make recommendations for environmental restoration  improved navigation  shore erosion protection  and flood damage reduction   The third study will set the stage for restoring three important Connecticut resources  Eisenhower Park in Milford  Hanover Pond in Meriden  and Holly Pond in Stamford         30
