                                                                                                                                        Lieberman Hails Bill to Extend Voting Rights Act                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  today hailed the introduction in both the House and the Senate to legislation to reauthorize the Voting Rights Act for an additional 25 years   The Senate Judiciary committee plans to consider the bill in the coming weeks  and Lieberman pledged his enthusiastic support to the effort           The right to vote is a civic entitlement of every American citizen  It is democracy s most essential right  It is the ultimate guarantor of our freedom and the enabler of our best hopes for the future     Lieberman said     The Voting Rights Act is a crucial part of who we are as Americans and I intend to help lead the charge to extend it as the Senate considers its reauthorization this year           The legislation introduced today would extend key provisions of the Voting Rights Act that are set to expire in 2007   The first is the    Preclearance    provision that requires jurisdictions in sixteen states to demonstrate to the Department of Justice that any changes to voting laws or practices do not deny or abridge the right to vote on account of race  color  or membership in a language minority group   The second provision to be extended requires jurisdictions with large non English speaking populations to make election material including registration forms  ballots and voting instructions available in languages spoken in that jurisdiction          30
