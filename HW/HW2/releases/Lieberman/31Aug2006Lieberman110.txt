Catherine McKenna Ribeiro  202 224 0975                                                                                                Lieberman Lauds Historic California Effort to Combat Global Warming                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Praises bipartisan legislation to cap greenhouse gas emissions                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  today praised the California State Senate   s passage late Wednesday of a broad reaching  bipartisan bill to combat global warming             I hail California s historic bipartisan agreement to implement a plan to reduce its greenhouse gas emissions to 1990 levels by 2020   Similar to Connecticut s participation in state emission reduction programs and the Regional Greenhouse Gas Initiative  RGGI   California is rising to the challenge to combat global warming while the federal government continues to refuse to take action   California s plan to reduce greenhouse gases employs a cap and trade system similar to the one included in the bill I have co sponsored with Senator John McCain  the Climate Stewardship and Innovation Act   California s decisive action to combat one of the most serious global environmental threats we face adds another powerful force to national and international efforts to curb global warming           The bill will establish a first in the nation  statewide market based cap and trade system to reduce California   s global warming emissions beginning in 2012 and eventually reaching 1990 levels by 2020   The legislation also directs the California Air Resources Board  CARB  to track and monitor global warming emissions from every major pollution source in the state  develop emission targets  and establish penalties for non compliance   Two recent economic analyses have shown the potential for the bill to spur both economic and job growth in California   The bill  which passed the California State Senate by a vote of 23 14 on Wednesday evening  is expected to be approved by the California State Assembly as early as today and signed into law soon after            30
