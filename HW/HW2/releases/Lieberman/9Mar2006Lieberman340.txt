                                             Senators Dodd  Lieberman Reject Bush Budget Shortfalls                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Senators urge Budget Committee to oppose CDBG Program funding cut in    07 budget                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        WASHINGTON     In a bid to help Connecticut communities stave off the Bush Administration   s proposed 2007 budget cuts to the Community Development Block Grant Program  CDBG   Connecticut Senators Chris Dodd  D CT  and Joe Lieberman  D CT  today joined a bipartisan group of their colleagues in urging the Senate Budget Committee to reject the proposed 25  funding cut to this successful program   In a letter  the senators instead urged support for  4 3 billion in funding           This is the second year in a row that President Bush has sought to undercut the CDBG program  which is a crucial tool used by state and local governments to improve housing  create jobs  and help protect children and the elderly           Connecticut relies on this funding to continue important work that helps our communities grow and thrive     said Dodd      It   s shortsighted to make cuts to efforts to improve housing  crime prevention and economic development  and I will fight them every step of the way               As you know all too well  this cut strikes at the heart of our communities     Lieberman said     By shortchanging and possibly bankrupting vital programs such as CASASTART  the President   s proposed cuts would weaken our communities  undercutting our efforts to combat drugs  crime  poverty and a host of other afflictions that stunt the growth of Connecticut   s cities   The CDBG program has been at the center of these efforts in Connecticut for many years  and should be protected  not cut
