                                                                                                                                           Lieberman Statement on FTC M Rated Video Game Survey                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  made the following statement in response to the Federal Trade Commission   s  FTC   s  release today of a    secret shopper    survey  designed to reveal the extent to which children age 16 or younger were able to purchase M rated video games intended for players age 17 or older             Research has shown that exposure to violent media  and in particular violent and sexually explicit videos  is harmful to our children     Lieberman said      The good news in this latest FTC survey is that there has been significant progress since the last FTC secret shopper survey     the percentage of minors who were able to purchase M rated video games has dropped from 69  to 42     In addition  major retail chains have been more successful in improving compliance with the industry   s rating system intended to ensure that M rated games are purchased by buyers over age 17   The bad news is that 42  of minors were still able to purchase M rated video games     a percentage that is still way too high   Senator Clinton and I have introduced the Family Entertainment Protection Act to help reduce this number further by imposing fines only on those retailers who continue to sell M rated games to minors             30
