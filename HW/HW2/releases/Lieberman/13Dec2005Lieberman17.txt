                                                                                  December 13  2005 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Urges Bush Administration to Take Action on Global Warming                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  made the following statement today in response to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change in Montreal           I applaud the progress made by the participants of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change on key aspects of the critical battle against global warming   Specifically  the conference launched the process for establishing commitments to reduce greenhouse gases beyond 2012  opened the door to the inclusion of greenhouse gas emissions from tropical rainforests in the overall strategy for addressing climate change  prompted moves toward increasing voluntary actions by developing countries and agreed to a series of informal talks aimed at creating broader  more global participation in reducing greenhouse gas emissions   In light of those achievements  the Bush Administration s stubbornness in resisting binding emissions limits in both the international and domestic context is unacceptable   The progress made in Montreal only confirms the urgency of our efforts to enact the McCain Lieberman climate change bill               30
