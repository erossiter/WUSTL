                                                                                                                                         Dodd  Lieberman Announce more than  4 5 Million   to Assist Victims of Violent Crimes in Connecticut                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Resources can be used to support communities affected by terrorism       WASHINGTON   Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman announced today that Connecticut has received  4 581 000 from the U S  Department of Justice to provide victims of violent crimes and terrorism with critical services such as counseling  emergency shelter  transportation  and crisis intervention                       Violent crime and terrorism     like storms     leave terrible destruction in their wake  particularly when victims are innocent children     said Dodd      This assistance can help communities and victims   young and old     cope with the trauma associated with violent crime and terrorism                         These funds will help to end the cycle of violence  empowering crime victims and their families in Connecticut     Lieberman said     Thanks to these grants  crime victims will have access to the treatment and support they need to regroup and go on with their lives                       The funds provided through the Victims of Crime Act  VOCA  grants are used in part to support the Terrorism and Mass Violence Crimes Initiative which provides support services to victims and communities affected by terrorism   These support services include crisis response  criminal justice support  and crime victim compensation   The funds are also directed to other initiatives for victims of violent crime that aim to train federal criminal justice system employees on victims    issues  develop materials that help victims understand their rights and the assistance available to them  and create and expand services available to them
