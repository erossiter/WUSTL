                                                                                                                                                   Washington    The Connecticut congressional delegation yesterday wrote to President George W  Bush in support of Governor M  Jodi Rell   s request that a major disaster declaration be made for the State of Connecticut as a result of a storm on June 2  2006  that flooded portions of the state  and particularly the City of Waterbury  The letter was initiated by Senator Chris Dodd      According to National Weather Service data  a stationary cold front caused significant rainfall on June 2     the delegation wrote     The storm was particularly acute in Waterbury  which recorded 7 4 inches of rain over approximately three hours  The city   s hilly topography allowed for flash floods to occur and caused widespread damage to transportation infrastructure and low lying buildings  The floods were further enhanced by debris which collected in the municipal stormwater and sewer systems      Governor Rell requested Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  funding for the cleanup costs associated with the storm  which Governor Rell has
