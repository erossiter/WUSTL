                                                                                                                                          Linda White Epps Post Office Naming Bill Passes Senate                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Bill honors White Epps for her efforts to raise awareness of breast cancer       WASHINGTON   A bill to honor the late Linda White Epps by renaming the Whitneyville Branch Post Office in Hamden  Conn  after her is on its way to the President   s desk   The Senate approved the bill by unanimous consent Wednesday   The Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs  of which Senator Joe Lieberman  D Conn   serves as Ranking Member  approved the measure June 22  Introduced by Rep  Rosa DeLauro  D Conn   and co sponsored by the entire Connecticut House delegation  the bill passed the House of Representatives on May 16       Until her death in October 2003  New Haven resident White Epps worked tirelessly to raise awareness of breast cancer  particularly among African American women  and created Sisters    Journey  a non profit organization that provides education  support and comfort to women with breast cancer and their friends and families  She also helped raise money for the American Cancer Society through the    Relay for Life    and the annual sale of calendars featuring breast cancer survivors   Well known among the Hamden community  White Epps   s work and words gave comfort to many women and their families
