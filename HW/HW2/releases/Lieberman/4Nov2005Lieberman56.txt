                                                                                                                                             Dodd  Lieberman Announce More Than  130 000   for Oakdale Volunteer Firefighter Company                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       WASHINGTON     The Oakdale Volunteer Fire Company in Oakdale  CT  will receive a  130 000 federal grant from the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS   Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman announced today   The fire company will use the funds to recruit and retain personnel necessary to fight fires and respond to other emergencies           Firefighters are indispensable foot soldiers here at home   This grant can help ensure that they have reinforcements on our nation   s front lines     said Dodd      It   s time to provide our homeland defenders with the resources and staffing they need to do a very difficult job            We are constantly reminded of how valuable firefighters are to our communities   Lieberman said  But in some cities and states today  firefighters are actually being laid off because of budget cutbacks  That s like reducing your troop force in a time of conventional warfare  It s crazy and it must stop and only more money from Washington can make it stop         The Oakdale Fire Company will use the funds to help increase the number of competent firefighters who are trained  certified  and capable of responding to fire related emergencies in its area          A U S  Fire Administration study indicated two thirds of all fire departments in the U S  operate with inadequate staffing   In addition  the study found that only 10 percent of fire departments have the personnel to handle a building collapse with 50 occupants or more in it        The award was made possible by the Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response  SAFER  Act originally authored by Senator Dodd and cosponsored by Senator Lieberman   The SAFER Act was enacted with bipartisan support as part of the FY 2004 Department of Defense Authorization Act          Dodd also authored  and Senator Lieberman cosponsored  the Firefighters Investment and Response Enhancement  FIRE  Act  enacted in 2000  which for the first time provided federal resources to local fire departments for training and equipment   Since the FIRE Act was enacted  the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  has distributed 314 grants for a total of more than  31 million in Connecticut to help fire departments strengthen their response capabilities           30
