                                                                                                                                        Dodd  Lieberman Announce More Than  63 000 in Grants For Connecticut Firefighters                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            WASHINGTON   Two Connecticut fire departments will receive a total of  63 112 in federal grants from the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS   Senators Chris Dodd and Joe Lieberman announced Wednesday   Firefighters in New Britain and Waterbury will receive funds for fire operations and firefighter safety        Firefighters are indispensable foot soldiers here at home  Thankfully these grants can help ensure that we have reinforcements on our nation s front lines   said Dodd   It s time to provide our homeland defenders with the resources and staffing they need to do a very difficult job         We depend on firefighters and other first responders who risk their lives every day to protect our safety     Lieberman said     The least we can do is make sure they have the equipment and training they need to serve our communities in such an important way  These grants will help the New Britain and Waterbury fire departments upgrade their equipment to keep those communities safe            The following localities will receive grant awards       New Britain Fire Department                             21 000       Waterbury Fire Department                             42 112          The awards were made possible by the FIRE Act of 2000  a law authored by Dodd and co sponsored by Lieberman that gives local fire departments the ability to purchase new equipment  hire new personnel  and initiate education and training programs  Since the FIRE Act was enacted  the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA   now part of DHS  has distributed 273 grants for a total of more than  25 5 million in Connecticut to help fire departments strengthen their response capabilities        30
