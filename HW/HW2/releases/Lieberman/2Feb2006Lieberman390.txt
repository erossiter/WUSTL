                                                                                  February 2  2006 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Calls on Bush Administration to Stick to President   s Pledge to Break America   s    Oil Addiction                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON    Senator Joe Lieberman  D CT  today made the following statement in response to news reports that officials from the White House and Department of Energy claim the President    didn t mean it literally    when he pledged to break America   s addiction to foreign oil during his State of the Union address           I was encouraged the other night when I heard President Bush s call for America to break our dependence on foreign oil  Unfortunately  it appears the Administration has now seen its shadow  prolonging our nation s long winter of oil addiction   Last November  I introduced legislation with a large bipartisan coalition that understands the urgency of the issue and remains committed to passing comprehensive energy security legislation that will reduce America   s consumption of oil by 10 million barrels a day in 25 years    and we are determined to hold the Administration to the President   s words in his State of the Union address           Lieberman and Senator Sam Brownback  R KS  led a bi partisan group of 10 Senators who introduced the Vehicle and Fuel Choices for American Security Act  which would mandate 10 million barrels in oil savings in 25 years and would also require the mass production of cars that can burn any combination of gasoline and alternative fuels or  alternatively  cars powered by efficient hybrid engines            30
