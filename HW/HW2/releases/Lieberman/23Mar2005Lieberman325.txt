                                                                                  March 23  2005 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Hails Important Role of First Responders                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    East Great Plain Fire Department Gets  102 000 Federal Grant       NORWICH  Conn      Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee Ranking Member Joe Lieberman  D Conn   congratulated the East Great Plain Fire Department Wednesday on receiving a recent  101 858 federal grant for training and equipment purchases and said that first responders across the nation were the linchpin to safe communities                       First responders risk their lives to keep the rest of us safe and they should be provided with the equipment and training they need to perform their jobs safely and effectively     Lieberman said      Our communities depend upon them  This grant is a start toward getting the East Great Plain Fire Department as prepared as it can be for when disaster strikes          Lieberman said the Administration   s proposed Fiscal Year 2006 budget dramatically cuts funding for first responders at a time when funding should be increasing    Overall  the Administration has proposed cutting state Homeland Security first responder grants by 32 percent this year  the same cut as for Fiscal 2005           Incredibly  the Administration has proposed cutting money to first responders for the past two years  despite the advice of experts that we increase our investment     Lieberman said       In an effort to turn back the Administration   s proposed cuts  Lieberman and Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee Chairman Susan Collins last week won an amendment offered during Congressional debate over the Fiscal Year 2006 budget that would restore  565 million in Department of Homeland Security first responder programs that the Administration has proposed to cut     including the programs that aid local fire departments   The amendment also would restore  140 million in Department of Justice first responder grants   the COPS and Edward Byrne programs law enforcement programs   and  150 million specifically for port security        Earlier in March  in a letter to the Chairman and Ranking Member of the Senate Budget Committee  Lieberman proposed a more generous  4 2 billion increase in the FY 2006 budget first responder training  hiring  and equipment  including interoperable communications equipment to enable first responders of different jurisdictions to speak to one another   He proposed an  8 4 billion boost for homeland security spending overall         30
