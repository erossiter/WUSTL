  Catherine McKenna Ribeiro  202 224 0975                                                                                                Dodd  Lieberman Announce More Than  6 Million in Heating Assistance for Colder Months Ahead                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Washington  Senators Chris Dodd  D CT  and Joe Lieberman  D CT  today announced that Connecticut is in line to receive  6 073 328 in assistance targeted for the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP   which will help low income families in Connecticut afford to heat their homes in the colder months ahead  The United States Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  released the funding as part of the  79 9 million contingency fund provided by last year   s HHS appropriations bill            As the temperature drops  the need for home heating increases     said Dodd   The release of this emergency funding will be critically important in helping ensure that people don t have to choose between heating their homes or putting food on the table this fall and winter  It   s a step in the right direction to make these resources available now  and I   ll continue to work in the future to ensure adequate funding to meet crucial energy needs           I am glad I could help get these vital funds released to Connecticut     Lieberman said      I will not let the federal government leave low income families in Connecticut out in the cold this winter           LIHEAP funding helps low income individuals with their home energy bills  including energy emergencies such as cold temperatures in the winter  spikes in energy costs and extreme heat in the summer  HHS previously released  600 million in assistance to states this past winter to address record high heating fuel costs  The additional funds released today result in a total of approximately  3 1 billion made available under LIHEAP this year     including more than  71 million in Connecticut          30
