                                                                                  January 31  2007 Contact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Lieberman Lauds Increased Transportation Funding                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Connecticut improvement projects benefit from Senator   s efforts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        WASHINGTON     Senator Joe Lieberman  I D CT  today lauded the increase in transportation funding levels approved by the Senate Committee on Appropriations    Subcommittee on Transportation   This increase will help keep Connecticut transportation projects on schedule          I am pleased that my colleagues on the Transportation Appropriations Subcommittee listened to our pleas to stick with the funding guidelines laid out in the transportation bill passed in 2005     Lieberman said      This increase in funds will ease congestion by allowing Connecticut and states throughout the country to continue the critical work of improving and expanding our roadways and increasing the efficiency and capacity of mass transit            The continuing resolution  which will set funding levels for the Department of Transportation  DOT  until permanent FY 2007 funds are approved  was originally slated to hold DOT funding at FY 2006 levels   Lieberman  however  pushed his colleagues on the subcommittee to keep the funding levels in the continuing resolution consistent with the Transportation bill that was passed in July 2005       Lieberman  who was instrumental in crafting the 2005 Transportation Bill  wrote a letter to Subcommittee Chairwoman Patty Murray  D WA  and Ranking Member Kit Bond  R MO  outlining the devastating impact it would have on Connecticut if DOT funding were to remain at FY 2006 levels   Connecticut would have lost an estimated  53 million dollars in construction funding and another 7 8 million in public transportation funding  which would have resulted in a projected loss of more than 2 500 jobs       The continuing resolution is being taken up in the House  where it is expected to pass before coming to the full Senate for approval next week         30
