SEN. DOMENICI PLEASED WITH JUSTICE DEPARTMENT GRANT FOR SILVER CITY SCHOOLS TO COMBAT JUVENILE DELINQUENCY
  Sen. Pete Domenici announced that the U.S. Department of Justice (DOJ) has awarded a grant worth $296,168 to the Silver Consolidated School District to support an education program for at-risk or disruptive students. 
   The DOJ grant will fund the Secure School Program, which sets up a structured alternative education setting for students who are at risk, disruptive, or upset the learning environment of the general student population.
   "This program focuses on students who have already been expelled or are in grave danger of being expelled and need special attention and a different teaching approach. It provides every student with a fair shot at an education," Domenici said.
   The grant was awarded through the Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention. Domenici serves on the Commerce, Justice and Science Appropriations Subcommittee which passed a bill FY2007 CJS Appropriations Bill containing funds for DOJ grant programs, including juvenile justice initiatives.
