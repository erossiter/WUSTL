SAN FELIPE, POJOAQUE PUEBLOS AWARDED HUD GRANTS
  Sen. Pete Domenici announced that the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) has awarded two grants totaling over $1 million to two New Mexico pueblos to assist in community development construction projects. 
   The San Felipe Pueblo will receive $523,971 through the Indian Housing Block Grant (IHBG) Program and will be used to fund the construction of five new affordable housing units and assist in the renovation of up to 20 privately-owned homes for persons of low and moderate incomes.
   The Pueblo of Pojoaque will receive $605,000 through the Indian Community Development Block Grant (ICDBG) Program and will be used to construction 20 new homes for persons of low and moderate incomes.
   "I have always maintained that these HUD grants are useful to provide affordable housing for more New Mexicans. I am pleased to see that our local communities can benefit from these types of federal grants," Domenici said.
   Domenici is a member of the Senate appropriations subcommittee that sets funding levels for HUD and its programs.
