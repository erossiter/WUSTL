DEPARTMENT OF HOMELAND SECURITY PROVIDES FUNDS FOR DEMING, PINOS ALTOS FIREFIGHTERS
  Sen. Pete Domenici announced today that firefighting programs in Deming and Pinos Altos will receive a total of $120,627 in grant funding from the Department of Homeland Security (DHS).  
   The Deming Fire Department will receive $75,454 while the Pinos Altos Fire Rescue in Grant County will receive $45,173. Each will use the money to enhance its program's operations and safety. 
   "Making sure that local fire departments have the resources necessary to equip their forces is crucial to maintaining the safety of New Mexico communities," said Domenici. "DHS provides funds to ensure that these areas are able to maintain the safety of their respective areas. It was encouraging to see that residents of Deming and Pinos Altos will benefit from this DHS grant money." 
   The funds will be administered as part of the Assistance to Firefighters (AFG) grant program within DHS which provides funding intended to help local fire departments protect their communities and firefighting forces. For more information, visit http://www.firegrantsupport.com. 
   Domenici is a long-time member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, which is responsible for determining all funding for the Department of Homeland Security.
