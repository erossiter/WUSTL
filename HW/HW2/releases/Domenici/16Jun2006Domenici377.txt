LEA SMALL BUSINESS GETS USDA LOAN
  Sen. Pete Domenici announced that the U.S. Department of Agriculture's (USDA) has guaranteed a $1.2 million loan to TRM, LLC in Hobbs which transports water to oil and gas producers and their servicing companies. 
   The funds will be used by TRM to purchase new machinery and equipment, consolidate debt, and pay related fees and costs. The funds will allow TRM to expand its operations by creating six new positions and retaining 13 current positions.
   "Small businesses in rural New Mexico utilize these funds to create jobs and stir economic growth. I have confidence in the work that TRM does to improve the rural economy in Lea County," Domenici said.
   The loan was approved through the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Rural Development program. The Rural Development program plays an important role in providing better community facilities to rural communities. The term of the TRM loan is 35 years at the U.S. Treasury rate.
   Domenici is a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, which designates funding for government initiatives like the USDA Rural Development program.
