HUD RELEASES EID FUNDING FOR SANDOVAL, OTERO COUNTIES
  Sen. Pete Domenici announced today that the Department of Housing and Urban Development has awarded a total of $445,500 to Sandoval and Otero counties under its Economic Development Initiative program.  
   Sandoval County will receive $247,500 to be directed toward construction and equipment for its Community Health Alliance. 
   Otero County will receive $198,000 for construction of its Veteran's Museum. 
   "Money is made available to local communities so that they can enhance the services and amenities available in their respective areas," said Domenici. "These monies will be put to good use in helping improve the health options in Sandoval County and in the construction of the Veteran's Museum in Otero County." 
   The Economic Development Initiative is administered by HUD as part of its Section 108 Loan Program. EDI grants provide funds to secure the projects funded by the Section 108 Loan Program. 
   Domenici is a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, including the Subcommittee on Transportation, Housing and Urban Development, and Related Agencies (TTHUD), which helps determine funding levels for all HUD programs.
