ESPA?OLA-BASED AKAL SECURITY RECEIVES NEW CONTRACT TO PROTECT FEDERAL FACILITIES
  Sen. Pete Domenici announced today that Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE)/Federal Protective Services (FPS) has awarded a $15.1 million contract to Akal Security based in Espa?ola.  
   The contract will initially award $1.7 million to Akal Security to provide armed guard services for five federal buildings in the state of Hawaii. The contract is expected to result in a cumulative value of $15.1 million. 
   "Akal Security has a proven track record of providing security services for the federal government," said Domenici. "This contract award is confirmation of that. I look forward to hearing more about Akal Security's success in the near future." 
   Founded in 1981, Akal Security Inc. is one of the top five security companies in the United States and provides security guard services, including thousands of security officers for federal courthouses, detention facilities, and military installations. It employs 15,000 people worldwide. 
   Domenici is a long-time member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, which determines funding for federal programs. He is also a member of the Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee.
