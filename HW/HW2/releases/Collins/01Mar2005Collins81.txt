                                      March 1  2005  Washington  DC   Senators Olympia Snowe and Susan Collins today announced the formation of a new Franklin County Community Task Force to assist workers who are expected to be laid off from the International Paper mill in Jay   The announcement was made today by International Paper officials  who said the company will be shutting down one of its paper machines at the Androscoggin Mill   The company indicated that close to 90 workers would lose their jobs because of the shut down          It is always troubling when workers lose their jobs through no fault of their own   This is a tough blow to the families affected and to a community that has been hard hit in recent years     said Senators Snowe and Collins in a joint statement      We will do everything possible to assist the workers during this difficult time   We expect to work together with state and community agencies to create a local task force that would help to provide job training and support services          The Franklin County Community Task Force will be modeled after other successful efforts the Senators have spearheaded in communities across Maine in order to help displaced workers access available programs for job retraining  education  job search and relocation  and re employment services  as well as other community based services   The Senators will also support any effort by the company to obtain federal emergency funds to provide workers with Trade Adjustment Assistance       International Paper employs around 1200 employees at the Androscoggin
