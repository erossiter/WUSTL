                                     Legislation would authorize Army Corps to remove dams to complete the Penobscot River Restoration Project January 29  2007 Washington  DC         U S  Senators Olympia J  Snowe  R ME  and Susan Collins  R ME  today introduced the Penobscot River Restoration Act  This legislation would authorize the Army Corps of Engineers to carry out phase two of the Penobscot River Restoration Project      The Penobscot River Restoration Project is a five year   50 million  project to restore the natural flow of Maines largest watershed  This Project is a partnership of the State of Maine  the U S  Department of Interior  the National Park Service  the U S  Fish and Wildlife Service  the Bureau of Indian Affairs  the Penobscot Indian Nation  the Atlantic Salmon Federation  PPL Corporation  the Natural Resources Council of Maine  American Rivers  Maine Audobon  and Trout Unlimited  Through collaboration with dam owners  the project will maintain virtually all of the hydroelectric generation on the river through a series of energy enhancements higher up in the Penobscot watershed     The Penobscot River Restoration Project consists of two phases  Phase one consists of raising  25 million in order to purchase two dams on the Penobscot River  Previously  Senators Snowe and Collins secured  4 5 million in federal funding for this project  and nearly  8 million has been raised privately  Last week  the Administration announced an additional  10 million request is included in the Presidents budget for this phase of the project      The second phase will require an estimated additional  25 million in order to remove the two dams and bypass a third  Doing so will open up 100  of the historic habitat for species such as the endangeredshortnose sturgeon and greatly improve access to over 1000 miles for endangered Atlantic salmon The Penobscot River Restoration Act directs the Army Corps of Engineers to remove the two dams and bypass the third  with the federal government picking up 65  of the cost   Growing federal support of the Penobscot River Restoration Project and the continued work and dedication of private groups in the state  have brought the completion of this historic project within sight  Snowe said  Through this legislation and the completion of this restoration effort  the Penobscot River will continue to be a fixture of Maines economy and culture    The Penobscot River Restoration Project is one of the largest  most creative river restoration projects in our nations history  said Senator Collins  Not only will it have far ranging benefits for endangered species  migratory birds  and a diversity of riverine and estuarine wetlands  but it will also help revive the social  cultural and economic traditions of New Englands second largest river  I am pleased to introduce legislation today which will authorize the funding necessary to complete this  50 million project
