                                     PNS Community  remains steadfast in our unified commitment to protect the Shipyard  January 25  2005   Washington  D C     Members of the Maine and New Hampshire Congressional Delegations joined members of the Portsmouth Naval Shipyard  PNS  Community Support Group today to reassert their determination to protect PNS from a Department of Defense Base Realignment and Closure Commission  BRAC  round  scheduled for later this year   The unified declaration came at a Capitol Hill meeting of Delegation members and their staffs with the Community Support Group members who are also talking with key Pentagon officials this week at meetings arranged by the Joint Delegation        The Portsmouth Naval Shipyard has earned its right to continue its strategically vital mission through the skill and hard work of its employees  the efficiency and creativity of its managers and PNS  s unmatched record for overhauling submarines under budget and ahead of schedule   Maine Senators Olympia Snowe and Susan Collins  New Hampshire Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  Maine Representative Tom Allen and New Hampshire Representative Jeb Bradley said in a joint statement    Although performance alone ought to be sufficient to protect PNS from the upcoming BRAC round  we know from experience with past BRACs that success will also require the Shipyard Community to continue its unified  aggressive campaign on the Yard  s behalf   The Shipyard community  which includes those of us in the Congressional delegation  along with state and local elected officials  Shipyard employees  the Seacoast Shipyard Association  Seacoast area Chambers of Commerce and area citizens  remains steadfast in our commitment to protecting the Shipyard
