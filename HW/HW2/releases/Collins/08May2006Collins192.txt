                                      May 8  2006 WASHINGTON  DC  Senator Susan Collins has released the following statement on this mornings expected announcement that the President will nominate General Michael Hayden to be the new CIA Director  Senator Collins introduced Gen  Hayden before his nomination hearing of the Senate Armed Services Committee  of which she is a member   I worked closely with General Hayden during the negotiations on the Collins Lieberman Intelligence Reform Act in 2004  I always found him to be straightforward and willing to share his candid professional judgments   even when they differed from those of Secretary Rumsfeld    Nevertheless  to send a signal of independence from the Pentagon  General Hayden may want to consider retiring from the Air Force  That would put to rest questions about whether an active duty military officer should lead the CIA at this time    While I will reserve final judgment on the nomination until after the hearings  I believe that General Hayden would bring impressive credentials and a wealth of experience to the CIA director s position
