                                      August 9  2005  WASHINGTON  D C    Senators Olympia Snowe  R ME  and Susan Collins  R ME  today announced that the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  has awarded grants totaling  1 602 277 to Lewiston and the Indian Township of Passamaquoddy  These grants will be used to foster community development and improve the quality of life for these two areas           It should continue to be a national priority for all Americans to have access to safe  affordable housing     remarked Senators Snowe and Collins in a joint statement     There grants will go a long way to create a better standard of living in the communities to which they were awarded          The funds are allocated as follows         1 177 277 for The City of Lewiston to improve housing  create a suitable living environment  and expand the economic opportunities for the citizens of Lewiston             425 000 for the Indian Township Passamaquoddy to assist with the replacement of a wastewater treatment facility that is discharging sewage on the reservation   The Tribe hopes to replace the absorption field and increase lagoon capacity       The Indian Community Development Block Grant  ICDBG  provides funds for Indian and Alaskan Native communities to develop better housing and create a healthy living environment       The Community Development Block Grant  CDBG  is one of the oldest programs in HUD and provides annual grants to various programs that help develop urban communities and decent housing       The  HUD  funds private housing developments to assist in housing opportunities for low income residents   HUD provides rental assistance to approximately 1 5 million low income households  It also funds community revitalization projects to meet local needs and expand opportunities for Americans                                                                                   Browse by
