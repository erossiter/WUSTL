                                      January 21  2005  WASHINGTON  D C    Senators Olympia Snowe  R ME  and Susan Collins  R ME  today announced that two Fire Departments in Maine have been awarded  154 313 from the Department of Homeland Security   The funding is provided through the    Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program     or FIRE Act           Homeland Security funding has allowed Maine fire stations to enhance their resources     Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement     The new grants will help the Hermon and Cambridge Fire Departments better serve their communities          The following departments will receive funding            85 706 for the Hermon Fire Department        68 607 for the Cambridge Fire Department         The funds that have been allocated to the Hermon Fire Department will be used for a Fire Prevention program  which includes public awareness  fire code enforcement activities  inspector certifications and fire prevention equipment activities  The Cambridge Fire Department will use their funds for an Operations and Firefighting Safety Program which consists of training  equipment  wellness and fitness as well as modifications to the fire station   s facilities        FIRE Act Grants were created with strong endorsement from Senators Snowe and Collins  These grants are awarded to fire departments across the United States to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations  firefighter health and safety programs  Emergency Medical Service programs  and Fire Prevention and Safety programs and to purchase new fire
