                                     Senator Collins Authored Provision Requires DOD to Open Small Arms Procurements to American Manufacturers May 25  2007 Washington  DC         The Senate Armed Services Committee  of which Senator Collins is a Member  has approved the fiscal year 2008 Defense Authorization bill that included a provision authored by Senator Collins to ensure that American arms manufacturers are not shut out of competitions to supply small arms for Iraqi and Afghani police  security  and military forces  In the past  some competitions for weapons to supply Iraqi forces  administered through the Department of Defense  DOD   have been limited to foreign manufacturers     Senator Collins explained to her colleagues on the committee  This current small arms buying practice by DOD is not only unfair  but it costs taxpayers dollars and Americans jobs  and hurts an important industry  It is estimated that American made small arms would cost DOD  and in turn taxpayers  an average of  150 less per gun than the foreign made pistols that are currently being purchased    Senator Collins provision would open the door for American manufactures  like Smith  Wesson  to compete for these DOD contracts  which could benefit the Houlton facility  Currently the facility employs 115 individuals  and this provision could enable the company to grow     I am pleased that my colleagues on the Senate Armed Services Committee recognized the need to allow American arms manufacturers to compete for these contracts  added Senator Collins
