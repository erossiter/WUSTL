                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               May 17  2005  WASHINGTON  D C    Senators Olympia Snowe  R ME  and Susan Collins  R ME  today announced that rural development funds totaling  317 500  have been awarded to Maine from the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA    The funds are in the form of a Rural Development loan of  300 000 to the Berwick Library Association and a grant of  17 500 to the Elderly Social Action Council in Fort Kent            The funding assistance provided by the USDA is critical to small communities in Maine since they often lack the resources to pursue community development programs on their own     Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement     These funds will ensure that Fort Kent and Berwick have the ability to provide expanded facilities for their communities         Funding for the Elderly Social Action Council in Fort Kent will be used for landscaping and paving a driveway in the senior citizens    center  The unpaved parking lot was a health hazard for the senior citizens as the uneven ground made it easy for them to lose their balance   The loan awarded to the Berwick Library Association will be used to renovate the interior for the completion of the new library  This facility will include a community meeting area  children   s area for story time and activities  and the needed space for the expansion of the library         The USDA Rural Development mission provides guaranteed loans  approved loans  and approved grants   Rural Development is committed to helping improve the economy and quality of life in all of rural America   The loans and grants are used for community programs  which seek to construct or improve community facilities for health care  public safety  and public services
