                                     Leahy Collins resolution could come up on the Senate floor this week September 8  2005  Washington  D C    Senator Susan Collins  R ME   who has been pressing the Environmental Protection Agency  EPA  to minimize the harmful effects of mercury in the environment joined several other Senators today  including Sen  Patrick Leahy  D VT  in calling for support of a provision that could overturn the EPA   s flawed rule on mercury emissions        The Leahy Collins resolution of disapproval ensures a Senate vote on the mercury rule   The resolution may come up on the Senate floor as early as this week  The resolution was filed in June under the terms of the Congressional Review Act  CRA   which gives Congress the ability to repeal rules proposed by federal agencies             There is overwhelming and undisputable scientific evidence that the EPA   s mercury rule is flawed and should be overturned     said Senator Collins      Mercury is a toxin that is extremely harmful to children and pregnant women  in particular   Maine   s waterways are threatened by emissions from power plants located in others states  yet the EPA   s recent rule does little to address this serious problem               Despite repeated requests  EPA has completely failed to correct this flawed rule   Therefore  I am pursuing legislative remedies to overturn it     she added        At issue are the EPA   s proposed regulations regarding mercury and the amount of toxin that can be legally emitted by power plants   The EPA   s rule  which was issued earlier this year  removed mercury emissions from power plants from the list of toxic pollutants that must be regulated under the most stringent provisions of the Clean Air Act  therefore  enabling the EPA to devise a more lenient cap and trade mercury program          Senator Collins has been working to reverse or overturn the EPA   s rule   The Joint Resolution of Disapproval and discharge petition are being led by Senator Leahy   Senator Collins was the first Republican to sign on to both the measure and the discharge petition
