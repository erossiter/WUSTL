                                      September 20  2007 Washington  DC     U S  Senator Susan Collins today spoke on the Senate floor urging the President not to veto the extension of the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  SCHIP     Last month  the Senate passed legislation to extend SCHIP and increase funding for the program by  35 billion over the next five years  The bill provides funding sufficient enough to cover all 6 6 million children currently enrolled and to expand to cover an additional 3 3 million low income children  The bill also includes provisions backed by Senator Collins that would provide greater dental coverage for low income children  require plans offering mental health benefits to provide coverage that is equivalent to other health services  and give states the option of covering low income pregnant women     The following is an excerpt from Senator Collins floor statement    Earlier today  the President announced his intention to veto the extension of the State Childrens Health Insurance Program bill  I believe such a veto would be a terrible mistake     One of the very first bills that I cosponsored when I first came to the Senate was legislation to create the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  or SCHIP  as it has become know  It provides health insurance for children where families do not have sufficient income to purchase health insurance and are not getting health insurance in the workplace  Yet  these families make a bit too much money to qualify for coverage under the states Medicaid program  These low income children and working families are falling through the cracks    Since 1977  the SCHIP program has contributed to a one third decline in the rate of uninsured low income children  Today  an estimated 6 6 million children  including more than 14 500 children living in Maine  receive health care coverage through this program  Still there is more that we could do  While Maine ranks among the top four states in reducing the number of uninsured children  we still have more than 20 000 children in our state who lack coverage    I urge the President to reconsider his veto threat to this vital program  If the President does proceed to veto the bill  I will vote to override his veto  We simply cannot allow this program to expire  and the extension and expansion that we are proposing will enable us to more fully cover these children                                                                               Browse by
