                                      September 19  2005  WASHINGTON  D C    Senators Olympia J  Snowe  R ME  and Susan Collins  R ME  today announced that the U S  Department of Veterans Affairs has awarded a total of  760 065 in funding for Community Housing of Maine  Inc  and Volunteers of America Northern New England   The funds will be used to reconstruct two buildings that will provide beds to homeless veterans            Those who have served and protected our nation deserve the utmost respect and care     said Senators Snowe and Collins in a joint statement     It is imperative that we provide housing for veterans in need of assistance   The extra beds in Portland and Biddeford will aid those veterans who are most in need of help and allow them a renewed sense of hope                      260 065 for Community Housing of Maine  Inc for the Acquisition and renovation of a building in Biddeford to create transitional housing that will provide 8 beds for homeless veterans                   400 000 to Volunteers of America Northern New England for the new construction of a building in Portland to create transitional housing that will provide 10 beds for homeless veterans            The Department of Veteran   s Affairs  VA  aims to provide excellence in patient care  veterans   benefits and customer satisfaction  They strive for high quality  prompt and seamless service to veterans  The Department of Veterans Affairs continues to offer their dedication and commitment to help veterans get the services they have earned
