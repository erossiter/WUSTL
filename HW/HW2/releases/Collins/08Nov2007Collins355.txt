                                     Senators Snowe  Collins Requested TAA Assistance November 8  2007 Washington  DC         U S  Senators Olympia J  Snowe and Susan Collins released the following statement today after the U S  Department of Labor approved the Trade Adjustment Assistance  TAA  petition filed on behalf of the 140 employees ofSensata Technologies in Standish who lost their jobs after the company announced that it is moving its operations to the Dominican Republic  Senators Snowe and Collins wrote to Labor Secretary Elaine Chao last monthurgingrapid approval oftheTAApetition    This vital assistance from the federal government comes not a moment too soon for the hardworking  productive  and skilled employees ofSensata Technologies who lost their jobs through no fault of their own  noted Senators Snowe and Collins in a joint statement  In receiving Trade Adjustment Assistance  these workers and their families will be one step closer to rebuilding their livelihoods  although much more remains to be done to help them fully recover     The purpose of the TAA program is to aid workers who lose their jobs or whose hours of work and wages decrease as a result of increased imports  Now that the TAA petition is approved  displaced Sensata Technologies workers will be eligible for employment training in another job or career  income support  job search allowance  and relocation services for individuals who obtain jobs outside of their normal commuting area                                                                               Browse by
