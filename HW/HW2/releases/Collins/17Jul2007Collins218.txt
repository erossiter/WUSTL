                                     Town   s water infrastructure heavily damaged during recent storms July 17  2007 Washington  DC         In a letter to Department of Homeland Security  DHS  Secretary Michael Chertoff  U S  Senators Olympia J  Snowe and Susan Collins today requested support for local efforts to repair Bethel Water Districts reservoir and watershed after each was severely damaged during last weeks storm    Following is the full text of the letter   Dear Secretary Chertoff    We are writing to express our concern over the recent flooding in Bethel  Maine  that has caused severe damage to the district s water supply  Last week  Bethel experienced more than five inches of rain in two hours  destroying its ability to treat its water supply  Currently  local officials are bringing in 8000 gallons of water by truck to ensure that Bethel residents have water  Even this supply of water may be insufficient to meet Bethels needs if a serious fire emergency occurred     We understand that Governor John Baldacci may submit a request to the President for federal assistance on behalf of the residents of Bethel  The Federal Emergency Management Agency has already sent preliminary assessment teams to survey the damage  and we appreciate this rapid response by the Department  If the Governor submits a request for federal assistance to address this dire situation  we encourage your support for its rapid approval    Thank you for your time and consideration                                                                             Browse by
