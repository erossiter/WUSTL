SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING LOCAL FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security Fire Administration's Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program has awarded $524,832 in federal grants to seven Virginia fire departments.  
   "Improving the safety and security of our nation and its citizens is one of my top priorities. By enhancing the training and support of our emergency first responders, we are making great strides in this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that Virginia's firefighters will be better equipped and prepared. Our communities will be safer because of this assistance." 
   All seven of the fire departments received funding for the Operations and Firefighter Safety Program: 
   * Abingdon Fire Department Abingdon, VA (Washington Co.) $143,955 
   * Lyn-Dan Heights Volunteer Fire Dept, Inc Lynchburg, VA $49,680 
   * Rockbridge Baths Volunteer Fire Department Rockbridge Baths, VA (Rockbridge Co.) $66,075 
   * Axton Community Volunteer Fire Department Axton, VA (Henry Co.) $91,782 
   * Brosville Community Volunteer Fire Department Danville, VA $81,000 
   * Boonsboro Volunteer Fire and Rescue Co. Inc. Lynchburg, VA $27,000 
   * Rocky Mount Volunteer Fire Department Rocky Mount, VA (Franklin Co.) $65,340 
   The Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the U.S. Fire Administration and assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. These funds are used by the nation's firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations and for firefighter health and safety programs, new fire apparatus, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
