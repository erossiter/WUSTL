SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING TO BENEFIT LOCAL FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security Fire Administration''s Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program has awarded $560,513 in federal grants to local fire departments.  
   "Improving the safety and security of Virginians and our communities is one of my top priorities. By recruiting firefighters and enhancing the fire prevention training and support of our emergency crews, we are making great strides in this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that Richmond and Danville firefighters will be better equipped and prepared. Our communities will be safer because of this assistance." 
   Two fire departments received funding through the Fire Prevention program: 
   Richmond Fire and Emergency Services $512,513 Richmond, VA ( Richmond City ) 
   City of Danville Fire Department $48,000 Danville, VA ( Danville City ) 
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the U.S. Fire Administration and assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the . These funds are used by the nation''s firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations and for firefighter health and safety programs, new fire equipment, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
