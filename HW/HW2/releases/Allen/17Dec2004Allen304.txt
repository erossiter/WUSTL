SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES $1 MILLION TO HELP 18 LOCAL HOUSING AUTHORITIES
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development has awarded a total of $1,085,726 to 18 housing authorities and local governments in Virginia.  
 This funding is being awarded to pay the salaries of the Family Self-Sufficiency (FSS) program coordinators and FSS homeownership coordinators for the Housing Choice Voucher FSS program. 
   "I am pleased that these housing authorities are receiving grants which will allow them to pay the men and women who serve as program and homeownership coordinators," said Senator Allen. "Once again HUD has played a key role in improving the lives of Virginia's families." 
   The following organizations received funding: 
   * Chesapeake Redevelopment & Housing Authority $47,557 
   * City of Virginia Beach $46,498 
   * County of Loudoun $63,000 
   * Danville Redevelopment and Housing Authority $34,000 
   * Fairfax County Redevelopment and Housing Authority $63,000 
   * Hampton Redevelopment and Housing Authority $44,139 
   * Harrisonburg Redevelopment and Housing Authority $22,628 
   * James City County $45,650 
   * Newport News Redevelopment and Housing Authority $127,887 
   * Norfolk Redevelopment and Housing Authority $98,173 
   * Petersburg Redevelopment and Housing Authority $43,430 
   * Prince William County Office of Housing $52,293 
   * Richmond Redevelopment and Housing Authority $125,620 
   * Roanoke Redevelopment and Housing Authority $48,480 
   * Virginia Housing Development Authority $186,561 
   * Waynesboro Redevelopment and Housing Authority $36,810 
   The FSS program is intended to promote the development of local strategies to coordinate the use of assistance under the Housing Choice Voucher programs with public and private resources to enable participating families to achieve economic independence and self-sufficiency. An FSS program coordinator assures that the program participants are linked to the supportive services they need to achieve self-sufficiency. An FSS homeownership coordinator helps FSS families move to homeownership.
