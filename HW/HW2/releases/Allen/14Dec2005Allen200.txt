SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES RURAL DEVELOPMENT DISTANCE LEARNING, TELEMEDICINE GRANT
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Agriculture''s Rural Development office has awarded a Distance Learning and Telemedicine Grant of $500,000 to the Southwest Virginia Education and Training Network. 
   "The folks of Southwest Virginia will greatly benefit from this assistance. These important funds will allow the folks in Southwest Virginia to update and enhance distance learning system in their communities," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that the USDA is once again able to provide support to improve the lives of Virginians."
   Rural Development funds will be used to update and enhance the technical capability of their distance learning system by installing interactive video classrooms in 48 schools in their project area. The project area includes the counties of Lee, Scott, Wise, Dickenson, Buchanan, Russell, Washington, Smyth, Grayson, Tazewell, Bland, Wythe and Carroll. Services offered will include academic classes, college and university classes for undergraduate and graduate credit, teacher training, educational video conferences, virtual field trips, cultural events, and electronic town meetings for local government agencies. More than 74,000 students, teachers, and residents in 13 counties will benefit from the project.
