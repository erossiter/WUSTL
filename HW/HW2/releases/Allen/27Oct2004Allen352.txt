SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING TO LOCAL FIRE DEPARTMENTS IN CENTRAL VIRGINIA
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security Fire Administration's Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program has awarded $497,716 in federal grants to four Central Virginia fire departments. 
   "Improving the safety and security of Virginians and our communities is one of my top priorities. By enhancing the training and support of our emergency crews, we are making great strides in this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that Virginia's firefighters will be better equipped and prepared. Our communities will be safer because of this assistance."
   All four fire departments received funding from the Fire Operations and Firefighter Safety Program:
   * Colonial Beach Volunteer Fire Department: $75,285, Colonial Beach, VA (Westmoreland Co.) 
   * Chesterfield Department of Fire and EMS: $246,229, Chesterfield, VA (Chesterfield Co.) 
   * Richmond County Volunteer Fire Department: $88,380, Warsaw, VA (Richmond Co.) 
   West Point Volunteer Fire Dept. & Rescue Squad: $87,822, West Point, VA (King William Co.)
   The Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the U.S. Fire Administration and assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. These funds are used by the nation's firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations and for firefighter health and safety programs, new fire apparatus, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
