SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING TO BENEFIT LOCAL FIRE DEPARTMENTS $328,054 AWARDED TO FIVE VIRGINIA COMMUNITIES
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security Fire Administration''s Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program has awarded $328,054 in federal grants to local fire departments. 
   "Improving the safety and security of Virginians and our communities is one of my top priorities. By recruiting firefighters and enhancing the training and support of our emergency crews, we are making great strides in this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that local firefighters will be better equipped and prepared. Our communities will be safer because of this assistance."
   Five Fire Departments received funding from the Operations and Safety Program:
   * Brooke Fire Safety Association Inc. $42,750 Stafford, VA (Stafford County)
   * Horsepasture Volunteer Rescue Squad $15,504 Ridgeway, VA (Henry County)
   * Crozet Volunteer Fire Department $58,900 Crozet, VA (Albemarle County)
   * Woodstock Fire Department $108,300 Woodstock, VA (Shenandoah County)
   * CCDF Volunteer Fire Department & Rescue Squad $102,600 Stuart, VA (Henry County)
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the U.S. Fire Administration and assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. These funds are used by the nation''s firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations and for firefighter health and safety programs, new fire equipment, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
