SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING TO LOCAL FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the Department of Homeland Security Fire Administration's Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program has awarded $200,306 in federal grants to five Virginia fire departments.  
   "Improving the safety and security of Virginians and our communities is one of my top priorities. By enhancing the training and support of our emergency crews, we are making great strides in this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that Virginia's firefighters will be better equipped and prepared. Our communities will be safer because of this assistance." 
   All five fire departments received funding for the Fire Operations and Firefighter Safety Program: 
   * Renan Volunteer Fire Department Gretna, VA (Pittsylvania Co) $32,940 
   * Craigsville Fire Department Craigsville, VA (Augusta Co) $49,613 
   * Wagstaff Circle Volunteer Fire Dept Richmond, VA $11,027 
   * Elliston Fire Department Elliston, VA (Montgomery Co) $31,500 
   * Nokesville Volunteer Fire & Rescue Dept Nokesville, VA (Prince William Co) $75,236 
   The Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the U.S. Fire Administration and assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. These funds are used by the nation's firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations and for firefighter health and safety programs, new fire equipment, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
