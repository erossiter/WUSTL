SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING TO HIRE NEW POLICE OFFICERS
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Justice Office of Community Oriented Policing Services (COPS) has awarded a total of $1,228,836 to five Virginia police departments to fund the hiring of 16 additional law enforcement officers.  
   "Improving the safety and security of our nation and its citizens is one of my top priorities," said Senator Allen. "By giving local law enforcement agencies the resources to hire new officers, we are making great strides in this important mission. I am pleased that these federal funds will help our hard-working police officers be better equipped to protect people in communities throughout Virginia." 
   The following five law enforcement offices received money to hire new officers: 
   * Petersburg Police Bureau $103,836 1 full-time positions 
   * Poquoson Police Department $150,000 2 full-time positions 
   * Radford University $75,000 1 full-time position 
   * Roanoke County Police Dept $450,000 6 full-time positions 
   * Spotsylvania County Sheriff's Dept $450,000 6 full-time positions 
   These grants were awarded by the COPS Universal Hiring Program Funding and will help law enforcement agencies pay for the salary and benefits to hire additional sworn officers to perform community policing and homeland security activities. Grants will be made for up to 75 percent of the total salary and benefits of each officer over three years, up to a maximum of $75,000 per officer.
