SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR KENTUCK VOLUNTEER FIRE DEPARTMENT
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program has awarded an Operations and Firefighter Safety grant in the amount of $160,200 to Kentuck Volunteer Fire Department, Inc in Ringgold, VA.  
   "Improving the safety of Virginians is one of my top priorities. Supporting local volunteers through grants like these will help them make great strides in accomplishing this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that Ringgold firefighters will be better equipped for protecting and serving their community." 
   Kentuck Volunteer Fire Department, located in Pittsylvania County, received this grant from the Operations and Firefighter Safety grant for training, equipment, personal protective equipment, wellness and fitness, and modifications to fire stations and facilities. 
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. These funds are used by the nation's firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations, firefighter health and safety programs, new fire apparatus, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
