SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING TO VIRGINIA FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security Fire Administration's Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program has awarded $943,489 in federal grants to ten Virginia fire departments.  
   "Improving the safety and security of our nation and its citizens is one of my top priorities. By enhancing the training and support of our emergency first responders, we are making great strides in this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that Virginia's firefighters will be better equipped and prepared. Our communities will be safer because of this assistance." 
   Two fire departments received funding for a Firefighting Vehicle: 
   * Bland Volunteer Fire Department Bland, VA (Bland Co.) $162,728 
   * Hose Company #4 Harrisonburg, VA $157,500 
   Eight fire departments received funding for the Operations and Firefighter Safety Program: 
   * York County Dept. of Fire & Life Safety Yorktown, VA (York Co.) $135,485 
   * Huguenot Volunteer Fire Department Powhatan, VA (Powhatan Co.) $51,867 
   * LaCrosse Volunteer Fire Department LaCrosse, VA (Mecklenburg Co.) $68,527 
   * Singers Glen Volunteer Fire Department Singers Glen, VA (Rockingham Co.) $95,328 
   * Orange Volunteer Fire Company, Inc Orange, VA (Orange Co.) $57,087 
   * Rural Retreat Volunteer Fire Department Rural Retreat, VA (Wythe Co.) $60,435 
   * Rugby Volunteer Rescue Squad & Fire Dept Mouth of Wilson, VA (Grayson Co.) $64,350 
   * Saltville Volunteer Fire Department Saltville, VA (Smyth Co.) $90,182 
   The Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the U.S. Fire Administration and assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. These funds are used by the nation's firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations, firefighter health and safety programs, new fire apparatus, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
