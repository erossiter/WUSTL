SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR HEAD START PROJECTS TO HELP CHILDREN IN BRISTOL, BUCHANAN, DICKENSON, RUSSELL, WASHINGTON
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services has awarded two grants totaling $2,426,320 to continue the Head Start program for children in the city of Bristol and the counties of Buchanan, Dickenson, Russell and Washington.  
   "The fine folks who are working in the Head Start program are serving their communities and having a significant, positive impact on families in their area" Senator Allen said. "A quality education is the foundation for our children's future success. Head Start affords children the opportunity to build a solid set of skills so they can better compete and succeed in life." 
   The first grant of $578,607 was awarded to the Buchanan County Board of Supervisors for the continuation of the Head Start program for children in their county. 
   The second grant of $1,847,713 was awarded to People Incorporated of Southwest Virginia to fund the multiple Head Start centers in the city of Bristol and the counties of Buchanan, Dickenson, Russell and Washington. 
   These grants will allow the continuation of comprehensive child-focused development programs that are designed to serve the children of low-income families. In all of its programs, Head Start focuses on incorporating parents, community and students as partners in education.
