SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES AWARD OF HEAD START FUNDING
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services has awarded several grants to local Virginia communities to continue the Head Start program across the Commonwealth.  
   "The fine folks who are working in the Head Start program are serving their communities and having a significant, positive impact on families in their area," Senator Allen said. "A quality education is the foundation for our children''s future success. Head Start affords young students the opportunity to build a solid set of skills so they can better compete and succeed in life." 
   The amount of $2,143,544 was awarded to People Inc. of Southwest Virginia to fund the multiple Head Start centers in the city of Bristol and the counties of Buchanan, Dickenson, Russell and Washington. 
   The amount of $1,099,240 was awarded to Augusta County Public Schools to fund the Shenandoah Valley Head Start programs provided through the local school system. 
   The amount of $1,717,556 was awarded to Lynchburg Community Action Group to fund its Preschool program to prepare economically disadvantaged 3 and 4-year old children to enter public school through its 13 centers serving Amherst, Bedford and Lynchburg . 
   These grants will allow the continuation of comprehensive child-focused development programs that are designed to serve the children of low-income families. In all of its programs, Head Start focuses on incorporating parents, community and students as partners in education.
