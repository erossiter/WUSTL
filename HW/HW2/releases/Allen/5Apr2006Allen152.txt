SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FIRE PREVENTION, SAFETY GRANT AWARDED TO INTERNATIONAL ASSOCIATION OF FIRE CHIEFS
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security Fire Administration''s Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program has awarded $999,996 to the International Association of Fire Chiefs (IAFC) in Fairfax.  
   "Improving the safety and security of Virginians and our communities is one of my top priorities. By recruiting firefighters and enhancing the fire prevention training we are making great strides in this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am very pleased that IAFC has received these funds vital to continuing their services across the nation and the Commonwealth. Our communities will be safer because of this assistance." 
   Established in 1873, the IAFC is a powerful network of more than 12,000 chief fire and emergency officers. Their members are among the world''s leading experts in fire fighting, emergency medical services, terrorism response, hazardous materials spills, natural disasters, search & rescue, and public safety legislation. Their mission is to provide leadership to career and volunteer chiefs, chief fire officers and managers of emergency service organizations throughout the international community through vision, information, education, services and representation to enhance their professionalism and capabilities.
