SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FIRE PREVENTION, SAFETY GRANT AWARDED TO ROANOKE COUNTY FIRE, AND RESCUE
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security Fire Administration''s Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program has awarded $60,000 in federal grants to the Roanoke County Fire and Rescue.  
   "Improving the safety and security of Virginians and our communities is one of my top priorities. By recruiting firefighters and enhancing the fire prevention training we are making great strides in this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that Roanoke firefighters will be better equipped and prepared. Our communities will be safer because of this assistance." 
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the U.S. Fire Administration and assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the . These funds are used by the nation''s firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations and for firefighter health and safety programs, new fire equipment, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
