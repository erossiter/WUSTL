SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR PROJECTS IN NORTHERN VIRGINIA
  Today, Sens. John Warner, R-Va., and George Allen, R-Va., announced that the United States Senate voted Saturday night to approve funding for the following projects in Northern Virginia.  
   Following is a list of the projects: 
   * $25 million for the construction of the Dulles Corridor Rapid Transit Project, a 23.1 mile heavy rail extension of the Washington Area Transit Authority's Metrorail system from West Falls Church to Route 772 in Loudoun County. 
   * $500,000 for Stafford County Airport Improvement. 
   * $600,000 for the protection of Dragon Run. These funds will be used to provide a link between the James River National Wildlife Refuge and the Rappahannock River Valley National Wildlife Refuge. The Dragon Run, which is situated mostly on the Middle Peninsula, is a 90,000 acre watershed that has a unique ecosystem with pristine forests and untouched swamplands. 
   * $2.3 million to Manassas National Battlefield Park for the necessary rehabilitation of Brawner Farm. 
   * $660,000 for the Virginia Community Policing Institute to continue providing community policing, training and technical assistance to Virginia's law enforcement agencies and communities. 
   * $400,000 for the Fairfax County Water Authority to undertake reliability improvement projects for their treatment and distribution systems. 
   * $1.2 million for the nationwide Communities in Schools program to coordinate community resources to help young students stay in school. 
   * $1.05 million for the Claude Moore Health Education Center at INOVA Health Systems in Fairfax. The funds will provide support for the education and training of doctors, nurses and allied health care professionals. 
   * $1.25 million to complete the removal of the Embrey Dam on the Rappahannock River in Stafford County and Fredericksburg. 
   * $2 million for the Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS) research at George Mason University. 
   * $2 million for the City of Alexandria to install fiber-optic cable for traffic signal coordination along the Route 1 Corridor. 
   * $1 million for the Richmond Highway Public Transportation Initiative in Northern Virginia. 
   * $800,000 for the Potomac Rappahannock Transit Commission (PRTC) service expansion project. 
   * $300,000 for the Virginia Economic Development Bridge to promote the economic vitality and external competitiveness of the Commonwealth through business, industry and educational partnerships between Southwestern and Northern Virginia and other areas of the Commonwealth. 
   * $150,000 for the Virginia Community College System in Northern Virginia to address several healthcare workforce shortages in these colleges' service areas. 
   * $660,000 for the Virginia Community Policing Institute to continue providing community policing, training and technical assistance to Virginia's law enforcement agencies and communities. 
   Senator Warner said, "This funding will provide important resources to key regional projects, from transportation improvements such as continuing the project of extending rail service to Dulles, to important environmental restoration activities around the Chesapeake Bay." 
   Senator Allen said, "These significant federal funds will help to support and continue efforts to improve the lives of the people of Northern Virginia and make this vibrant region a better place to live, work, visit, learn and raise a family. 
   "I am especially pleased that funding has been approved for several of these important transportation projects, including $25-million for the Dulles Corridor project. These important transportation improvements will play an important role in helping to bolster the transportation infrastructure to move traffic more efficiently and safely in Northern Virginia. 
   Now passed by the Senate as part of the FY2005 Omnibus Appropriations Bill, the legislation will await action by the House of Representatives on an enrolling correction which is expected on Wednesday before being sent to the President's desk to be signed into law.
