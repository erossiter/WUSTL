SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING TO LOCAL FIRE DEPARTMENTS IN SOUTHSIDE, PIEDMONT VIRGINIA
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security Fire Administration's Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program has awarded $1,490,342 in federal grants to ten Southside and Piedmont Virginia fire departments. 
   "Improving the safety and security of Virginians and our communities is one of my top priorities. By enhancing the training and support of our emergency crews, we are making great strides in this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that Virginia's firefighters will be better equipped and prepared. Our communities will be safer because of this assistance."
   Seven fire departments received funding from the Fire Operations and Firefighter Safety Program:
   * Monelison Volunteer Fire Department: $105,228, Madison Heights, VA (Amherst Co.) 
   * Riverbend Volunteer Fire Department: $69,070, Danville, VA 
   * Lynchburg Fire and EMS Department: $128,285, Lynchburg, VA 
   * Dolphin Volunteer Fire Department: $36,000, Dolphin, VA (Brunswick Co.) 
   * Farmville Volunteer Fire Dept & Rescue Squad: $94,414, Farmville, VA (Prince Edward Co.)
   * Collinsville Volunteer Fire Department, Inc: $130,551, Collinsville, VA (Henry Co.)
   * Albemarle County Department of Fire Rescue: $301,339, Charlottesville, VA 
   Three fire departments received funding from the Firefighting Vehicle Program:
   * Wakefield Volunteer Fire Department: $225,000, Wakefield, VA (Sussex Co.) 
   * Moneta Volunteer Fire Department, Inc: $175,455, Moneta, VA ( Bedford Co.) 
   * Appomattox Volunteer Fire Department: $225,000, Appomattox, VA (Appomattox Co.) 
   The Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the U.S. Fire Administration and assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. These funds are used by the nation's firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations and for firefighter health and safety programs, new fire apparatus, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
