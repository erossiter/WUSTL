SEN. ALLEN ANNOUNCES FUNDING TO LOCAL FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. George Allen (R-VA) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security Fire Administration's Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program has awarded $184,294 in federal grants to three Virginia fire departments.  
   "Improving the safety and security of our nation and its citizens is one of my top priorities. By enhancing the training and support of our emergency first responders, we are making great strides in this important mission," said Senator Allen. "I am pleased that Virginia's firefighters will be better equipped and prepared. Our communities will be safer because of this assistance." 
   All three fire departments received funding for the Fire Operations and Firefighter Safety Program: 
   * Bowling Green Volunteer Fire Department Bowling Green (Caroline Co) $66,240 
   * Little Creek Volunteer Fire Department Dublin (Pulaski Co) $21,618 
   * Wytheville Fire Department Wytheville (Wythe Co) $96,436 
   The Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the U.S. Fire Administration and assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. These funds are used by the nation's firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations, firefighter health and safety programs, new fire apparatus, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
