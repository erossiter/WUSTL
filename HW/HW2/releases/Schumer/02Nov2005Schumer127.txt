IMMEDIATE RELEASE  November 2  2005 Schumer Announces  400 000 For Gates Fire District In Rochester                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Federal Funds Awarded as Part of Department of Homeland Security Assistance to Firefighters Grant SAFER Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  U  S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the Gates Fire District in Rochester will receive a total of  400 000 from the U S  Department of Homeland Security Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response  SAFER  program  New York State communities received a total of  922 780 in federal funds in the second round of SAFER grants  which awards funds directly to volunteer  combination  and career fire departments to help the departments increase the number of firefighters       This funding will be a shot in the arm for our local fire departments     Schumer said    We can never fully prepare for fires and disasters  However  this federal money will help make sure that fire departments in Rochester have the staff they need keep our communities safe  This is even more necessary when local budget cuts and high energy costs are leaving first responders without enough funding        Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response grants aim to increase the number of firefighters who are trained  certified  and capable of responding to emergencies likely to occur within the fire department  s geographic response area  Volunteer  combination  and non profit career fire departments are eligible to receive grants  which are used to pay a portion of the salaries of newly created firefighter positions over the course of five years  Ultimately  the SAFER aims for grantees to enhance their ability to attain 24 hour staffing and thus ensure their communities have adequate protection from fire and fire related hazards  Nearly 3 000 communities across the United States received a total of  2 9 billion from SAFER grants in this round     The SAFER grant program is part of the larger  Assistance to Firefighters Grant  AFG  program administered by the U S  Department of Homeland Security  in cooperation with the U S  Fire Administration  AFG aims to assist local fire departments to purchase firefighting equipment  fund firefighter health and safety programs  enhance emergency medical services programs  and conduct fire education and prevention programs throughout the United States
