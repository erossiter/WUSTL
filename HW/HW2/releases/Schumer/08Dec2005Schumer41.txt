IMMEDIATE RELEASE  December 8  2005 Schumer Announces  56 000 for Capitol Region Arts and Cultural Programs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          National Endowment For The Arts Grant Will Support Programs In Albany  Saratoga And Washington Counties   Schumer  Federal Funds Will Increase Arts   Exposure And Boost Tourism U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  24 000 in federal funds for the Albany Symphony Orchestra   7 500 in federal funding for the Skidmore Jazz Institute   10 000 for the Lake George Opera of Saratoga Springs and  15 000 for the Mettawee River Theatre Company  Inc  of Washington County  The groups will receive grants through the National Endowment for the Art  s  NEA  Access to Artistic Excellence program  This program aims to foster and preserve excellence in the arts and increase access to the arts         This is great news for the Capitol Region   Schumer said    An investment in the arts is an investment in the Capitol Region  s future as a tourist destination and as a vibrant place to live  This money will go a long way to ensure that New Yorkers can enjoy both the process of creating art and the finished product and presents an opportunity to allow artistic growth and appreciation          The Albany Symphony Orchestra will use the federal funds to support the American Music Festival  This month long celebration of American music will include a salute to film composer Bernard Herrman and the commissioning and presentation of twenty eight new works by American composers  The Albany Symphony Orchestra  founded in 1931  is the oldest and only professional symphony orchestra based in the Capital Region and serves over 150 000 people within a 75 mile radius        Skidmore College will use its funds to support the Skidmore Jazz Institute  a two week summer jazz program hosted by the college  s Bernhard Theater  The program will include master  s classes  daily classes on improvisational techniques  private instruction  and a jazz festival  The Institute  founded in 1987  is open to high school age students and older  The program is geared toward participants whose ability levels range from intermediate to professional and culminates with the Freihofer  s Jazz Festival at the Saratoga Performing Arts Center       The Lake George Opera will use the federal funds to support the production of   Our Town     a new opera based on Thorton Wilder  s novel  The Lake George Opera is entering its forty fifth season in 2006  The opera performs at the 500 seat Spa Little Theater in the Saratoga Spa State Park      The Mettawee River Theatre Company will use the federal money to support the creation  production and tour of a new play  Artistic Director Ralph Lee will design and direct the piece based on English Chap books from the 17th and 18th centuries  English Chap books are simple books developed using woodblock printing on one single sheet of paper  The Mettawee River Theatre Company has been creating original plays incorporating masks  giant figures and puppetry for thirty years       Established by Congress in 1965 as an independent agency of the federal government  the NEA is dedicated to supporting excellence in the arts and bringing the arts to all Americans  Over 120 000 grants have brought art to all 50 states  including rural areas  inner cities and military bases and have generated an average of seven dollars for each dollar awarded  Grants provided during FY 2006 will help support projects that involve the creation and presentation of artistically excellent work
