IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 21  2005 Schumer Announces Key Senate Panel Backs  200 000 For the Robert H  Jackson Center in Jamestown                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Senator Aggressively Lobbied Senate Appropriations Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill  Federal Dollars will Help with Capital Restoration of Auditorium                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved  200 000 for the Robert H  Jackson Center in Jamestown under the FY06 Transportation  Treasury  HUD Appropriations Act  The federal funds will go to partial capital restoration of the auditorium  the last major capital project required to refurbish the structure honoring New Yorker Robert H  Jackson who served the nation as Solicitor General  Attorney General  U S  Supreme Court Justice and Chief American Prosecutor at Nuremberg  The bill must now be approved by the full Senate before being sent to a House and Senate Conference Committee  then eventually being signed into law  Schumer had aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill       Justice Jackson was one of the truly great Americans Chautauqua County gave to us  and the Robert H  Jackson Center keeps his ideas and his accomplishments alive for us all to experience     said Schumer    It is an important part of our history  and an important economic development tool  bringing hundreds of people to Jamestown every year        The Jackson Center will use the federal funds to partially renovate our auditorium with plumbing  fire protection  and heating electrical sound systems as the last major part of capital improvements  More than  1 2 million has been contributed by foundations  New York State  the City of Jamestown and private individuals to invest in this project  Local benefactors have invested in a new insulated roof  masonry restoration  window replacement and restoration  sidewalks  signage  handicapped ramps  railings  auditorium door  elevator  visitor center room  new electrical service  web site  Jackson Nuremberg papers on microfilm from the Library of Congress and many dollars to assist with programs and program endowment      Today  the Appropriations Committee included  200 000 for this project in the Transportation  Treasury  HUD spending bill  The bill was passed out of the Appropriations Committee today and now heads to the floor for a vote before the Senate  A vote is expected later this summer  and after final passage a conference committee will reconcile the differences between the House and Senate versions of the Transportation  Treasury  HUD Appropriations Bill before sending it to the President to become law
