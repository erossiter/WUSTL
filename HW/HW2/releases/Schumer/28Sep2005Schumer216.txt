IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 28  2005 Schumer Announces  150 000 For Cornell University                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Federal Funds will be used to Promote Job Creation and Investment U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  150 000 for Cornell University from the U S  Department of Commerce  s Economic Development Administration  EDA   Cornell University will use this federal funding to promote job creation and private investment in Tompkins County       This is great news for Cornell and Tompkins County     Schumer said    This money will be a shot in the arm for the community and help Cornell continue to be an engine of economic growth in the region  I will continue to push for more funding for programs that help the growth of New York State        Cornell University will use this money to support the efforts of the EDA  s University Center Program to fuel economic growth that will benefit families and businesses  The University Center Program is a partnership between the federal government and academic institutions  This partnership enables universities to make their vast resources available to the economic development community in distressed areas  The money will be used by Cornell to help fill any information gaps that may prevent University leaders from making decisions that have a positive effect on local economic development issues     EDA was established under the Public Works and Economic Development Act of 1965 to generate jobs  help retain existing jobs  and stimulate industrial and commercial growth in economically distressed communities  EDA is guided by the basic principle that distressed communities must be empowered to develop and implement their own economic development and revitalization strategies  Program assistance is available to areas experiencing high unemployment  low income  or other severe economic distress  EDA helps distressed communities address problems associated with long term economic distress  as well as sudden and severe economic dislocations including recovering from the economic impacts of natural disasters  the closure of military installations and other Federal facilities  changing trade patterns  and the depletion of natural resources
