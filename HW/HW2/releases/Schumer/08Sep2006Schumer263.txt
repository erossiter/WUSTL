IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 8  2006 Schumer Announces Over  80 000 Coming To National Association Of State Fire Marshals In Albany                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Funds Will Support Advocacy Programs That Raise Awareness About Fire Safety In The Home                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the National Association of State Fire Marshals  NASFM   in Albany  has received an  80 000 federal grant from the United States Fire Administration  USFA  under the Residential Fire Safety Institute  RFSI  grant program    Funds will be used for the development and implementation of advocacy programs throughout the state that emphasize built in protection of residencies to mitigate the impact of fires       This funding will go a long way to enhance the safety and preparedness of all New Yorkers     said Schumer    Fires have the potential to destroy lives  homes  and many families   most treasured possessions  This funding will raise awareness of potential fire hazards and allow people to be better prepared should a fire occur  helping to mitigate the fire  s impact while enhancing safety         Federal funds will be used by the NASFM to develop public education and advocacy programs that emphasize the use of smoke alarms  automatic sprinklers  and other safety measures designed to detect fires and minimize their damage   These programs will be a continuing resource for current information regarding fire safety so that residents will be informed of the latest resources to protect their homes     The mission of the USFA is to reduce life and economic losses due to fire and related emergencies  through leadership  advocacy  coordination  and support   The USFA serves the nation independently  in coordination with other Federal agencies and in partnership with fire protection and emergency service communities  providing public education  training  technology and data initiatives   The Residential Fire Safety Institute  RFSI  is a public interest group created in 1982 to promote fire safe homes through built in fire protection and fire safety education
