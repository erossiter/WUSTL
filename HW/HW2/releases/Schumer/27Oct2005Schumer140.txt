IMMEDIATE RELEASE  October 27  2005 Schumer Throws Support Behind Initiative To Reimburse Communities For Prosecuting Border Crimes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  The Bipartisan   Northern Border Prosecution Initiative Reimbursement Act    Will Aid Local Prosecutor  s Offices For Resources They Spend On Prosecuting Border Crimes   Schumer  Keeping Our Borders Secure Is Critical  And The Federal Government Must Help Localities With The Financial Burden   Senator Joins Coalition Of 14 Northern Border States In An Initiative Similar To A Program On Southwest Border Today  U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer signed onto the bipartisan   Northern Border Prosecution Initiative Reimbursement Act    which will aid local prosecutor  s offices for resources they spend prosecuting border crimes  This program would be eligible for localities in fourteen states to reimburse local prosecutors handling cases originating from arrests made by the Border Patrol and other cases involving federal immigration charges       We must keep our borders secure  but it is unfair to rely on our local communities to bare this financial burden     Schumer said    Municipalities are already strained from budget cuts  and homeland security costs are increasingly being put on states and localities  We must provide these communities with the recourses they need to protect and patrol our northern border        Currently  if a U S  Attorney or other federal law enforcement agency is investigating a potential federal criminal violation  and the federal government then decides to no longer pursue federal criminal charges against the defendant  they often turn over the case to local law enforcement agencies  If the   Northern Border Prosecution Initiative Reimbursement Act    is signed into law  local agencies would be reimbursed for the costs of prosecution and court costs  as well as pre  and post trial detention     The   Northern Border Prosecution Initiative Reimbursement Act    is modeled after the Southwest Border Prosecution Initiative  SWBPI  where over  112 million is provided to assist southern border states in offsetting the costs of prosecuting cases  The   Northern Border Prosecution Initiative Reimbursement Act    would authorize  28 million in its first year for reimbursement of local prosecutors handling cases originating from arrests made by the Border Patrol and other cases involving federal immigration charges     Fourteen states would be eligible for reimbursement of prosecution related costs  including Alaska  Idaho  Maine  Michigan  Minnesota  Montana  New Hampshire  New York  North Dakota  Ohio  Pennsylvania  Vermont  Washington and Wisconsin  In the past  the bill was referred to the Judiciary Committee  of which Schumer is a member
