                                   Federal Funding will be Used for Curriculum Development  Schumer  Clinton Urged Committee to Include Funding In Spending Bill Bill will now be sent back to Senate for Final Approval  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that a House Senate Conference Committee has agreed on a final version of the Labor  Health and Human Services and Education FY08 Appropriations Bill  which includes  300 000 for SUNY New Paltz  The bill now goes to the full House and Senate for final approval before heading to the President for his signature  Schumer and Clinton urged the committee to include funding for the project in this years spending bill     This is great news for the Hudson Valley and another critical step in the appropriations process  said Schumer  This project will help SUNY New Paltz create a stronger and more specialized academic program for our students  providing them with the absolute best in higher education  I will work hard to continue to secure funding for these critical programs     This is welcome news for SUNY New Paltz and the Hudson Valley  said Senator Clinton  This funding will go a long way to help SUNY New Paltz continue to provide its world class curriculum to students     SUNY New Paltz will use  300 000 in federal funds for curriculum development in economic development and governance     The bill now goes to the full House and Senate for final approval before heading to the President for his signature
