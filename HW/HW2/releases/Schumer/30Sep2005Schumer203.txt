IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 30  2005 Schumer Announces  86 000 For Lake Champlain Lake George Regional Planning Board                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Federal Funds will be used to Encourage Economic Development Planning U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  86 000 for the Lake Champlain Lake George Regional Planning Board  the Board  from the U S  Department of Commerce  s Economic Development Administration  EDA   The Board will use this federal money to fund economic development planning in Clinton  Essex  Hamilton  Warren and Washington Counties       This money will be a shot in the arm for the North Country     Schumer said    Real economic development demands planning and this funding helps to ensure that any growth is sustainable  I will continue to push for funding for programs that help New York State communities becoming booming centers of business activity        The Board will use this funding to help with the costs of economic planning  This planning involves analyzing the local economy  defining concrete development goals  determining project opportunities and devising and implementing an economic development program that includes systematic efforts to reduce unemployment and increase families   incomes  The Board received this money through the EDA  s Partnership Planning Program which helps support local organizations with their long term planning efforts and their outreach to the economic development community on EDA  s programs and policies     EDA was established to generate and help retain existing jobs  as well as stimulate industrial and commercial growth in economically distressed communities  EDA works to empower distressed communities by encouraging development and implementation of their own economic development and revitalization strategies  Program assistance is available to areas experiencing high unemployment  low income  or other severe economic distress  EDA helps communities address problems associated with long term economic distress  as well as sudden and severe economic dislocations including recovering from the economic impacts of natural disasters  the closure of military installations and other Federal facilities  changing trade patterns and the depletion of natural resources
