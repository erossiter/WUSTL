                                    Federal Funds Will Be Used To Address Domestic Violence Issues By Encouraging Arrests and Protecting Victims                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  399 972 has been awarded to Rensselaer County by the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  Office on Violence Against Women under the Grants to Encourage Arrest Policies Program   The federal funds will be used to continue to address domestic violence in Rensselaer County by encouraging arrest policies and assisting victims in securing orders of protection         This is great news for Rensselaer County     said Schumer     Domestic violence is an awful problem in our communities  Battered women should never have to think twice about whether or not to report their abusers  and creating comprehensive legal services to secure victims   safety must be top priority  These dollars will protect hundreds of women by ensuring law enforcement agencies have the tools they need  and I will fight hard to increase funding to such programs        Rensselaer County will continue to be pro active in addressing the issues of domestic violence in the community by encouraging arrest policies and assisting victims in securing orders of protection  The project will use funds to improve prosecution of domestic violence cases through a specialized domestic violence prosecutor within the District Attorney  s Office  provide comprehensive domestic violence services to victims through the Unity House  domestic violence court and policy advocates  integrate the tracking of all domestic violence cases  in addition to increased communication between project partners  facilitated by the Domestic Violence Information Coordinator  monitor offenders and increase communications with victims through the Rensselaer County Probation Department  train project partners and other significant agencies on domestic violence issues  and provide training to local magistrates regarding coordinated community response  available services and resources  and the effectiveness of batterers programs  Rensselaer County currently has two courts dedicated to handling domestic violence cases  the countywide Integrated Court comprised of three parts and the Troy City Domestic Violence Court   The Domestic Violence Court centralizes all criminal domestic violence cases in the City of Troy into one court setting   These two courts will be the focus of this project       The Grants to Encourage Arrest Policies and Enforcement Protection Order Program  Arrest  implements certain provisions of the VAWA  which was enacted in 1994 as Title IV of the Violent Crime Control and Law Enforcement Act of 1994 and reauthorized in the VAWA of 2000   The program enhances victim safety and offender accountability in case of domestic violence and dating violence by encouraging jurisdiction to implement mandatory and pro arrest policies as an effective domestic violence intervention that is part of a coordinated community response  An integral component of Arrest Program initiatives is the creation and enhancement of collaborative partnerships between criminal justice agencies  victim service providers  and community organizations which respond to domestic violence     Schumer led the effort last year to reauthorize the 1994 Violence Against Women Act  VAWA   Schumer fought to strengthen the VAWA by expanding grant programs  supporting rural areas  and protecting victims  The original VAWA bill was authored by Schumer in the House of Representatives  This legislation will renew several successful programs and provides funding for training  education and outreach so that state and federal agencies can do a better job of preventing violence against women and assisting victims of domestic violence
