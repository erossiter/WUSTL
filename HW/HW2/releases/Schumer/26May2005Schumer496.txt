IMMEDIATE RELEASE  May 26  2005 Schumer  Clinton Announce  4 Million For Greater Binghamton Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Federal Money will be used to Rehabilitate Runway US Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the US Department of Transportation has awarded  4 000 000 in Federal Aviation Administration  FAA  funds to the Greater Binghamton Airport  The money will be used for runway rehabilitation         This is great news for Greater Binghamton   said Schumer   The Greater Binghamton Airport welcomes travelers to New York each year  and these improvements are important for local businesses and strengthening the local economy  I will continue to fight for federal funds to help the airport remain a thriving center of activity and a hub for economic growth in the region          Binghamton Airport is one of the key gateways to the Southern Tier and as an important tool in the growth of our upstate economy  we cannot do enough to support it     said Senator Clinton    I am pleased that we can continue to make these kinds of investments in the region  s future          This grant will provide federal funding to rehabilitate runway16 34  Greater Binghamton Airport has seen strong passenger growth over the past year  boarding 10 3 percent more passengers in March 2005 then in March 2004  Currently  the Greater Binghamton Airport offers service with 4 of the nation s 7 largest airlines providing service to most domestic and international destinations  The airlines currently serving the Greater Binghamton Airport include Delta Connection  Northwest Airlink  United Express  and US Airways Express  Of the 34 total flights offered at BGM  over 50  are operated on jet aircraft
