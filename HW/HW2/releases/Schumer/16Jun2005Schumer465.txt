IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 16  2005 Schumer Announces Key Senate Panel Backs  51 Million For Laboratory For Laser Energetics At University Of Rochester                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Senator Aggressively Lobbied Senate Appropriations Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill      Federal Funding will be used for Research and Operations at OMEGA Facility US Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved  51 million under the FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations Act for the Laboratory for Laser Energetics at the  University of Rochester  The bill must now be approved by the full Senate before being sent to a House and Senate Conference Committee and eventually being signed into law  Schumer had aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill        The University of Rochester s laser lab is one of the most advanced and important facility of its kind in the world   said Schumer   In addition to being home to the OMEGA laser   a critical element of our nuclear defense system   it is also one of the nation s top sites for laser development and education programs  This funding allows the lab to continue research that is crucial to our national security while Rochester remains a center for cultivating the next generation of laser scientists        Funds will be used for research and operations at the current OMEGA facility  located at the University of Rochester  In addition  construction of the additional beam lines for the OMEGA facility which was initiated by Congress last year would continue  Research at the present OMEGA facility supports the science  based stockpile stewardship program for the National Nuclear Security Administration  Funds are requested from the Department of Energy  Atomic Energy Defense Activities account       Today  the Appropriations Committee included  51 million in the Energy and Water spending bill  The bill was passed by the Appropriations Committee today and now heads to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  A vote is expected this summer  and after final passage a conference committee will reconcile the differences between the House and Senate versions of the Energy and Water Appropriations Bill before sending it to the President to become law
