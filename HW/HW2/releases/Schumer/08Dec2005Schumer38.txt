                                   National Endowment For The Arts Grants Will Support Artist Residency Programs At Two Galleries And Buffalo Philharmonic                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  20 000 in federal funding for Big Orbit Gallery  Inc    20 000 for Hallwalls  Inc  and  20 000 for the Buffalo Philharmonic Orchestra  The groups will receive the federal money through the National Endowment for the Art  s  NEA  Access to Artistic Excellence program  This program aims to foster and preserve excellence in the arts and increase access to the arts         This is great news for Buffalo and New York  s art lovers   Schumer said    An investment in the arts is an investment in Buffalo  s future as a tourist destination and as a vibrant place to live  I will continue to fight hard for programs that support artistic growth and appreciation          Both Big Orbit Gallery and Hallwalls will use the federal money to support artist in residency programs that will result in the display of new works  Big Orbit Gallery will support artists from the Northeast and Mid Atlantic States selected from an open call by a committee of artists and curators form the western New York region  Hallwalls will provide artists in residence stipends  allotments for materials  fabrication and workspace  access to equipment and technical support  and opportunities for public presentation       Big Orbit Gallery s Visual Arts Program provides an environment of experimentation for established artists while giving emerging artists opportunities to participate in exhibitions and solo shows  Hallwalls aims to provide a center for contemporary art and to provide an artistic presence which is global in its outlook  challenging in its ideas  pluralistic in its concerns and diverse in its expression        Federal money will allow The Buffalo Philharmonic to perform   Shining Brow     a two act opera that chronicles the life of Frank Lloyd Wright  The Buffalo Philharmonic was founded in 1935       Established by Congress in 1965 as an independent agency of the federal government  the NEA is dedicated to supporting excellence in the arts and bringing the arts to all Americans  Over 120 000 grants have brought art to all 50 states  including rural areas  inner cities and military bases and have generated an average of seven dollars for each dollar awarded  Grants provided during FY 2006 will help support projects that involve the creation and presentation of artistically excellent work
