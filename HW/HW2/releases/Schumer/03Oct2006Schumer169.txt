IMMEDIATE RELEASE  October 3  2006 Schumer Announces  450 000 Coming Erie County To Combat Human Trafficking                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Sheriff  s Office Will Use Federal Funds To Develop A Human Trafficking Task Force                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  450 000 has been awarded to the Erie County by the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  Bureau of Justice Assistance  BJA    The federal funds will be used to develop a human trafficking task force by training law enforcement in trafficking victim identification and through coordination with trafficking victim  s service providers and the U S  Attorney  s Offices         This is great news for Erie County     said Schumer     Human trafficking is a despicable crime that must be eradicated wherever it exists   This new task force will help to get dangerous criminals off the streets and provide these victims with the services they need to recover        The Erie County Sheriff  s office will use federal funds to develop a human trafficking task force to provide support for state and local law enforcement to work more collaboratively with victim services organizations  federal law enforcement  U S  Attorney  s Offices  and the Civil Rights Division of the U S  Department of Justice in the identification and rescue of human trafficking victims who are in the United States   The primary goal of this program is to increase trafficking victim saves by 15  each year through the training of law enforcement in trafficking victim identification and the development of proactive law enforcement tactics   The Sheriff  s office will collaborate with trafficking victims service providers for the rescue of trafficking victims and provision of needed services   Finally  the Sheriff  s office will also coordinate with the U S  Attorney  s Offices to successfully prosecute human traffickers     The Bureau of Justice Assistance  BJA  supports law enforcement  courts  corrections  treatment  victim services  technology  and prevention initiatives that strengthen the nation  s criminal justice system  BJA provides leadership  services  and funding to America  s communities by emphasizing local control  building relationships in the field  developing collaborations and partnerships  promoting capacity building through planning  streamlining the administration of grants  increasing training and technical assistance  creating accountability of projects  encouraging innovation  and ultimately communicating the value of justice efforts to decision makers at every level
