IMMEDIATE RELEASE  March 22  2006 Schumer Urges Feds To Commit To Disaster Preparedness Program In Peekskill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       DHS May Be Considering Budget Cut To Program That Keeps Equipment In Peekskill  NY In Case Of Disaster Or Emergency   Senator  This Essential Program Cuts Reaction Time In Case Of Disaster U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today called on the Department of Homeland Security to support the Pre Positioned Equipment Program  PEP   resist any attempt to downside or relocate resources  and commit full funding to the program  Peekskill  New York is home to one of nine PEP sites  and would react to a disaster in the Hudson Valley  New York City  much of New York State and the entire northeast  The goal of the PEP is to provide first responders across the country with the equipment they need to handle disasters in the critical hours and days before Federal assistance  including the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA   can arrive  Firefighters and other first responders strongly support this program as it provides them with the equipment and support staff they so sorely need in the unfortunate event of a disaster         This is an important tool for ensuring our nation is prepared in case of disaster  be it terrorist or natural     Schumer said    The PEP located at Camp Smith in Peekskill  New York  is an essential program for not only the Hudson Valley and New York City  but America at large  This program is a relatively small price to pay for security and safety          Schumer explained that there are two primary reasons that this possible shift to downsize or mothball the program would be detrimental to our national preparedness  The first is that  in potentially moving from eleven sites to two  the reaction times for various geographical locations will be vastly increased  There is currently a PEP site located in Peekskill  NY at Camp Smith  If the equipment were moved to a warehouse in a remote location  the reaction time for a disaster in the Hudson Valley and New York City could significantly increase  The second problem is that if the equipment is warehoused  it would have to be checked and approved before a pod could be sent out to a disaster area  thus increasing the wait time  Currently  the equipment is checked by a maintenance crew every six months and is ready to go at a moment  s notice  Without dedicated staff  it may take valuable time to locate people to test and drive the equipment to the disaster area       Established after Sept  11  2001  the program has already demonstrated its effectiveness  delivering seven pods of equipment to first responders after Hurricane Katrina hit the Gulf Coast in August 2005  PEP also responded in January 2005 to Graniteville  SC  after a train derailment resulted in a chlorine gas leak that killed nine people and overwhelmed local emergency resources        Currently  the PEP is operational at nine out of eleven sites throughout the country  This equipment includes personal protective devices and clothing  detection equipment for radiological  chemical and biological agents  medical equipment  communication equipment  and other vital supplies  All of these are packaged in  pods   which remain loaded at all times onto large flatbed trucks at each PEP site  The loaded trucks can be put on the road immediately upon notification of a disaster  When operating at full capacity  the program is capable of sending support to any major population area in America within twelve hours  The annual cost of the program is  14 million        In a personal letter to Michael Chertoff Secretary of the Department of Homeland Security  Schumer wrote    Reports indicate that DHS may shut down the nine operational sites and simply warehouse the equipment in just two locations  If true  I am concerned that this move would significantly undermine the effectiveness of the program  There is an enormous difference between rolling out trucks with pre tested equipment on board and pulling equipment out of storage which is not ready to function
