IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 20  2005 Schumer Invites Chertoff To Upstate New York To Hear Directly From Business Owners On Proposed Passport Rule Change                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Schumer Held Border Crossing Forums In Buffalo and Rochester Earlier This Week    President Has Said He Is Open To New Ideas  Schumer Brings Ideas Back to President During Public Comment Period and Advocates for New York  s Business Community  Consumers  and Tourism Industry US Senator Charles E  Schumer today invited Homeland Security Secretary Michael Chertoff to visit the Northern Border of New York to view firsthand the impact that a rule requiring U S  citizens to present passports at the US Canadian border would have on the communities that host border crossings  On Monday  Schumer hosted forums in Buffalo and Rochester  and pledged to bring ideas back to the Administration on ways to achieve both security and efficient commerce on the border       We can have both security and efficient commerce at the border     Schumer said    The President is giving it a second look  and now Secretary Chertoff should have the opportunity to hear first hand from New Yorkers who would be directly impacted by this change  Any new rule must address cross border commerce and tourism interests throughout New York        In his letter to Chertoff  Schumer emphasized that national security is always his top priority  but that the federal government must protect the homeland without crippling cross border commerce or the travel and tourism industry  A final plan must address the concerns of businesses leaders  consumers  and the law enforcement community in New York  where the economy is dependent upon Canadian trade and commerce  According to the Canadian Consulate  Canada is New York  s largest trading partner and Canada trade is related to 348 000 jobs in New York State     On April 5  2005  the Departments of State and Homeland Security announced a proposed rule that would require all U S  and Canadian citizens to have a passport or other accepted secure document to enter or re enter the U S  by January 1  2008  The proposal refers to passports as the   preferred document of choice     Following a sixty day public comment period  a formal rule will be issued
