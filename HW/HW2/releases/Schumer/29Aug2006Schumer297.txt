IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 29  2006 Schumer Announces Over  727 000 Coming To Monroe County To Fight Domestic Violence                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               County  Domestic Violence Consortium will Use Funds to Support Prosecution  Strengthen Response to Violence                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  727 698 has been awarded to Monroe County by the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  Office on Violence Against Women   The federal funds will be used to coordinate the county  s efforts to respond to and prevent domestic violence  including support funding for a prosecutor and staff attorneys in the Monroe County District Attorney  s Office       This is great news for Rochester     said Schumer     Domestic violence is an awful problem in our communities  Battered women should never have to think twice about whether or not to report their abusers  and creating comprehensive legal services to secure victims   safety must be top priority  These dollars will protect hundreds of women by ensuring law enforcement has the tools they need  and I will fight hard to increase funding to such programs        Monroe County will work in partnership with the Rochester Monroe County Domestic Violence Consortium  DVC  to implement the Monroe County Accountability Program  which will identify where gaps exist in the fight against domestic violence   The project will strengthen the ability of the Monroe County District Attorney and Probation Office to prosecute and supervise offenders through dedicated units  increase legal representation for victims of intimate partner violence in immigration matters and civil orders of protection  improve the coordinated community response system by conducting a safety audit of the county  s criminal justice system  support an assistant district attorney position dedicated to the prosecution of misdemeanor level offenses  support two staff attorney  s to provide representation and advise to victims seeking to obtain and enforce civil orders of protection  and hire an Intensive Supervision Probation Officer to monitor domestic violence offenders      The new funding for Monroe County comes through the Grants to Encourage Arrest Policies and Enforcement Protection Order Program  Arrest  implements provisions of the VAWA  which was enacted in 1994 as Title IV of the Violent Crime Control and Law Enforcement Act of 1994 and reauthorized in the VAWA of 2000   The program enhances victim safety and offender accountability in case of domestic violence and dating violence by encouraging jurisdiction to implement mandatory and pro arrest policies as an effective domestic violence intervention that is part of a coordinated community response  An integral component of Arrest Program initiatives is the creation and enhancement of collaborative partnerships between criminal justice agencies  victim service providers  and community organizations which respond to domestic violence     Schumer led the effort last year to reauthorize the 1994 Violence Against Women Act  VAWA   Schumer fought to strengthen the VAWA by expanding grant programs  supporting rural areas  and protecting victims  The original VAWA bill was authored by Schumer in the House of Representatives  This legislation will renew several successful programs and provides funding for training  education and outreach so that state and federal agencies can do a better job of preventing violence against women and assisting victims of domestic violence
