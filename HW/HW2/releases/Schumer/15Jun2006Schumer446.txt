IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 15  2006 Schumer  Clinton Welcome Key Senate Committee Approval Of Bill To Rename Tuckahoe Post Office After 9 11 Hero Ronald Bucca                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Schumer Spearheaded Efforts to Support Bill  Dedicated Public Servant and First FDNY Fire Marshal Ever Killed in the Line of Duty will be Commemorated by Tuckahoe Post Office  Senators Welcome Committee  s Vote to Approve Renaming to Honor Hero Washington D C    U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton announced today that legislation to honor hometown hero and former New York Fire Department Marshal Ronald Bucca was passed by the Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Government Affairs  Schumer and Clinton welcomed approval of the bill after sending a letter to the committee lobbying for the United States Postal Office in Tuckahoe to be designated as the  Ronald Bucca Post Office   On September 11  2001  the former Fire Marshal sacrificed his life in the line of duty while rescuing fellow New Yorkers from the World Trade Center         Ronald Bucca epitomized bravery  patriotism and public service but his life was tragically cut short     said Schumer    It is only fitting that the post office in Bucca  s hometown will now bear the name of this great American hero who will be honored in the Tuckahoe community forever          Ronald Bucca was a true hero who served his community and gave his life trying to rescue others  Today we pay tribute to his bravery  his selflessness and his sacrifice  I am very pleased that our colleagues on the Senate Homeland Security and Government Affairs Committee have approved this legislation so that we can create this memorial to his memory and legacy     Senator Clinton said       Mr  Bucca  who dedicated his life to serving the people of New York and the nation  was the first New York City Fire Marshal to ever die while in the line of duty  On September 11  2001  Bucca had just reached the fire on the 78th floor of the South Tower of the World Trade Centers when the Tower collapsed  Bucca served in many capacities during his career in public service  Following 29 years of proud service in the United States military  Mr  Bucca protected the people of New York for 23 years as a firefighter  the last nine of which he was Fire Marshal        Schumer and Clinton sent a letter Tuesday urging the Senate Homeland Security and Government Affairs Committee to designate the facility of United State Postal Service located at Seven Columbus Avenue in Tuckahoe  New York  as the   Ronald Bucca Post Office     The legislation was introduced in the House by Congresswoman Nita Lowey and is expected to pass on the Senate floor
