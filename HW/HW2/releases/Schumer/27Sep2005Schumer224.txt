IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 27  2005 Schumer Announces Over  600 000 For Cornell University  s Center For Radiophysics And Space Research                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              NASA Funds will be used to Furthering Human Understanding of the Universe U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  618 644 for the Center for Radiophysics and Space Research  CRSR  at Cornell University  Planetary Polarization Nephelometer from the National Aeronautics and Space Administration  NASA   CRSR will use this federal funding to improve upon their existing devices       This is great news for Cornell University and the CRSR program     Schumer said    Cornell has taken a leading role in furthering our understanding of the universe  and the development of this technology in the Southern Tier ensures that they stay at the forefront of space exploration research     The Planetary Polarization Nephelometer will use a technique to determine the makeup of a planetary atmosphere by breaking up the wavelengths of compounds and elements in the air  While there have been polarization nephelometers in the past  there has never been any that have been produced for use outside a laboratory environment  This represents a new leap in space exploration technology and this grant award will develop Cornell University  s already impressive Space Sciences department and attract further interest and investment in their program  This federal funding is part of the NASA  s 2005 Planetary Instrument Definition and Development Program  PIDDP   NASA reviewed proposals from across the country that will advance research in space exploration  particularly for spacecraft based instruments and technology that has the potential to further scientific research on future missions in space  PIDDP projects have contributed greatly to the success of missions of exploration to the moon and planets across the solar system
