IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 6  2007 Schumer Announces  525 000 Coming To Our Lady Of Lourdes Memorial Hospital In Binghamton                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Federal Dollars Will Be Used To Offer Research Based  Family Focused and Strength Based Services for At Risk Youth and Families U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  525 000 has been awarded to Our Lady of Lourdes Memorial Hospital in Binghamton by the U S  Department of Justices  DOJ  National Institute of Justice  NIJ  under FY07 High Risk Youth Offender Reentry and Family Strengthening Initiatives  The federal funds will be used to offer research based  family focused and strength based services for at risk youth and families in a variety of venues   This is great news for Our Lady of Lourdes Memorial Hospital and the Binghamton area  said Schumer  I applaud their efforts to help at risk youth and their families and will continue to work hard on behalf of these programs  These federal funds will bring only good things to Binghamton residents and their families   Our Lady of Lourdes Memorial Hospitals Youth Services will use these funds to offer research based  family focused and strength based services for at risk youth and families in a variety of venues  This project will allow Lourdes Youth Services to strengthen the development of children from infancy through late adolescence by offering family centered services that address the needs of the family as a whole   The High Risk Youth Offender Reentry and Family Strengthening Initiatives is designed to support innovations and enhancements of juvenile justice related practice at the local  state and tribal government levels as authorized by the Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention Act of 2002  Under this initiative  OJJDP will fund both high risk youth offender reentry strategies and family strengthening strategies and programs that focus on improving family economic success  family support systems  and building thriving and nurturing communities in which healthy families can purse long term goals
