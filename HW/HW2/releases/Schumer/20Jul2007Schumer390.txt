IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 20  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Over  275 000 For Le Roy Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Federal Money Will Be Used To Acquire Land for Le Roy Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the U S  Department of Transportation  DOT  has awarded  275 646 in Federal Aviation Administration  FAA  funds to the Le Roy Airport in Genesee County   This is great news for the Rochester Finger Lakes Region  said Schumer  The Le Roy Airport is an important general aviation facility in this region  Not only does it provide local aviators  and tourists with a base of operation  but it allows businesspeople visiting New York to access the region easily  I will continue to fight for federal funds to help these airports remain a key component of economic growth in the region   Le Roy Airport has served Genesee County and Western New York for over 40 years  said Senator Clinton  This grant provides Le Roy Airport with the necessary funding to comply with airport master plan findings and current runway regulations  It is essential to help safeguard New  Yorks general aviation airports and enable them to keep serving local regions throughout New York   The Le Roy Airport will use the  275 646 of federal funds to acquire land in fee simple title for the extension of Runway 28 end  The airport master plan update concluded that the existing runway length is inadequate
