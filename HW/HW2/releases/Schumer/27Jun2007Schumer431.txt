IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 27  2007 Schumer Announces Suffolk County Fire  Rescue   Emergency Services Receives Over  1 Million In Federal  Safer  Grant                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Schumer  Emergency Response Program will Receive Funding As Part of U S  Department of Homeland Security s SAFER Grant Program   Schumer says the Federal Grant will Help Suffolk hire new firefighters  and recruit and retain volunteer firefighters   Schumer has Pushed for Additional Funding for Assistance to Fire Departments and First Responders                                    U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that Suffolk County Fire  Rescue and Emergency Services has received a  1 168 283 00 federal grant under the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Programs Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response  SAFER  program administered by the U S  Department of Homeland Security  The county will use the grant to hire new firefighters  and recruit and retain volunteer firefighters  They will receive the award on Friday of this week  This funding will bolster the ranks of Suffolk County firefighters and emergency first responders  said Schumer  We can never truly repay our firefighters and first responders for their heroic efforts and dedication to duty  However  we can better increase their safety  and our own  by ensuring that there are enough firefighters and first responders to quickly and properly respond to emergencies  This federal money will help make sure that the brave men and women of Suffolk who protect our communities have the resources they need to keep us safe  Schumer was among the first senators who recognized the need for the federal government to establish a funding program for local governments and fire departments to help defray the rising costs of fire prevention  Since its inception in 2001  the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program has delivered over  130 million to New Yorks fire departments  The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program assists local fire departments in programs and activities to improve the effectiveness of firefighting operations through specialized emergency training for response to situations like terrorist attacks  enhancement of emergency medical services programs  development of health and safety initiatives  establishment of fire education and prevention programs  creation of wellness and fitness programs  and equipment  hiring  and facility upgrades  Local fire departments have been strained from budget cuts  rising costs of equipment and stress from being on heightened alert because of the war on terrorism  said Schumer  These funds are truly a life saving investment will that provide local fire departments with essential resources and support  so they can be better prepared for emergencies  The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the Department of Homeland Security in cooperation with the Departments United States Fire Administration  The grants are awarded on a competitive basis to the applicants that most closely address the program s priorities and demonstrate financial need  For more information on the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  please visit the website at http   www firegrantsupport com
