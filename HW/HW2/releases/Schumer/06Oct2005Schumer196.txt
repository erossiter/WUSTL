Schumer says Federal Funds will Allow the Sea Cliff Fire Department to Pay for Operations and Safety Improvements During Difficult Fiscal Times   Schumer has Pushed for Additional Funding for Assistance to Fire Departments and First Respond U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the Sea Cliff Fire Department in Nassau County has received a federal grant of  163 210 from the 2005 Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program administered by the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS   The Sea Cliff fire department will use the grant to improve operations safety throughout the station          This funding will go a long way in improving the ability of our firefighters to rapidly  safely  effectively respond to emergencies in Nassau County     said Schumer    We owe it to our fire fighters and first responders to ensure that they have the best training  equipment and resources available and at their disposal  This is even more necessary when local budget cuts are leaving them without enough funding  This federal money is crucial to make sure that first responders in Sea Cliff are able to keep us safe          The  163 210 grant for Sea Cliff is part of the eighth round of DHS FY05 grants providing support to thousands of fire departments throughout the country        Schumer was among the first senators to call for federal assistance to first responders and recognized early on the need for Congress to establish a funding program for local governments and fire departments in order to offset the rising costs of equipment and fire prevention  Since its inception in 2001  the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program has delivered close to  115 million to New York  s fire departments       Schumer saw that the Administration  s cut to the program in their federal budget for FY05 could leave New York emergency response programs underfunded by upwards of  16 million  In response  Schumer urged the Congress and White House to bolster the Administration  s proposal by sending the full  900 million originally promised by Congress        The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  AFGP  assists local fire departments in programs and activities to improve the effectiveness of firefighting operations through specialized emergency training for response to situations like terrorist attacks  enhancement of emergency medical services programs  development of health and safety initiatives  establishment of fire education and prevention programs  creation of wellness and fitness programs  and equipment and facility upgrades         This grant ensures that the firefighters in Nassau County continue to have the basic resources they require to do their jobs  improve safety and save lives    said Schumer    Budget cuts  rising costs of equipment and stress from being on heightened alert because of the war on terrorism have strained local fire departments  These funds are literally a life saving investment will that provide local fire departments with essential resources and support  so they can be better prepared for emergencies          The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program awards funding on a competitive basis to the applicants that most closely address the program s priorities and demonstrate financial need  For more information on the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  please visit the website at http
