IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 12  2006 Schumer Announces  125 000 Coming To The Town Of Southport For Public Sewer System Improvements                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Federal Funds Will Be Used To Install New Sewer Lines and a Pump Station                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced a grant of  125 000 has been awarded to the Town of Southport in Chemung County by the Appalachian Regional Commission  ARC    The federal funds will go toward the installation of approximately two miles of sewer lines and a pump station       This is great news for the Town of Southport     Schumer said     Poor sewer systems create serious health concerns and endanger the welfare of the community   This funding will allow for major improvements in the sewer system  which will in turn improve health safety and the quality of life and allow for increased economic development in the area          This project will allow for the installation of approximately two miles of sewer lines and a pump station to serve a section of the Town of Seaport that includes several commercial businesses and vacant industrial commercial lots   The new lines will replace a large number of malfunctioning septic systems that have led to serious public health concerns in the commercial area of Southport   The health problems in the area have affected residents and limited the economic development of prime commercial and industrial properties located in the center of the Town   The public sewer system will eliminate on lot septic systems that currently are on lots too small to properly accommodate septic systems   These improvements will enhance economic development opportunities in the commercial district as well as improve the marketability of several vacant lots     Each year ARC provides funding for several hundred projects that create thousands of new jobs  improve local water and sewer systems  increase school readiness  expand access to health care  assist local communities with strategic planning  and provide technical  managerial  and marketing assistance to emerging new businesses  ARC is a federal state partnership that works with the people of Appalachia to create opportunities for self sustaining economic development and improved quality of life  The ARC awards grants from funds appropriated to the Commission annually by Congress
