IMMEDIATE RELEASE  February 2  2007 Schumer Announces Over  742 000 For Upstate Medical University In Syracuse                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Federal Funds Will Be Used For Genetic Research Projects                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that two grants totaling  742 485 have been awarded to the Upstate Medical University in Syracuse by the U S  Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  through the National Institute of General Medical Sciences under the Pharmacology  Physiology  and Biological Chemistry Research Program   The university will use  445 768 in federal funds for genetic studies of yeast and  296 717 for the study of the enzyme ATPase          This is great news for Syracuse   Schumer said   Without this necessary funding  medical professionals and scientists would not be able to seek cures to diseases and further understand the genetic makeup of humans  I adamantly believe in these research programs  and I will continue to advocate for the health and wellness of New York s communities        The Upstate Medical University will use the federal funds for two separate research projects   The  445 786 grant will go toward research to create a complete genetic description of the yeast actin cytoskeleton  By studying the genetics of yeast  researchers will use simple organisms to model human gene relationships   The  296 717 grant will be used for research that looks at the molecular structure of Proton pumping ATPase   By analyzing the structure  researchers will better understand how the molecule works   This understanding could hold some significance for human disease such osteoporosis  cancer  and renal tubular acidosis         The National Institute of General Medical Sciences  NIGMS  primarily supports basic biomedical research that lays the foundation for advances in disease diagnosis  treatment  and prevention  The Institute s training programs help provide the most critical element of good research  well prepared scientists  The Institute s programs encompass the areas of  cell biology  biophysics  genetics  developmental biology  pharmacology  physiology  biological chemistry  bioinformatics  computational biology  and minority biomedical research and training  NIGMS is one of the National Institutes of Health  NIH   the principal biomedical research agency of the Federal Government  NIH is a component of the U S  Department of Health and Human Services
