IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 2  2006 Schumer  Clinton Announce Over  5 Million In Grants For JFK Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Two DOT Grants to Help Pay for Runway Safety Area Improvements and Access Road Improvements Today  U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton announced that the U S  Department of Transportation has awarded two grants totaling  5 265 000 to the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey  The federal funds will be used to upgrade runway safety areas and widen the outbound expressway at John F  Kennedy International Airport        With regional air traffic reaching an all time high  it  s crucial that New Yorkers have safe and efficient air service     said Senator Schumer    This money will help JFK remain a world class airport by making essential improvements to its infrastructure          As one of the busiest airports in the world  JFK needs to maintain its infrastructure  This funding will contribute to runway safety and access road improvements which are critical to ongoing operations and to the safety and security of planes and passengers     Senator Clinton said     One grant for  4 365 000 will provide federal funding for access road improvements  This project will include widening a portion of the outbound expressway to increase efficiency  The other grant for  900 000 will go toward upgrading JFK  s runway safety  specifically toward improvements to the Runway 13R 31L safety area  The runway area improvement project will help ensure safe daily aircraft operation at JFK  These improvements are required to comply with Federal Aviation Regulations Part 139     In April 2005  Schumer and Clinton announced three DOT grants totaling  10 million dollars for the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey  The federal grants were used for projects at John F  Kennedy International Airport  including two of the grants  totaling  8 million  which went towards runway area safety improvements
