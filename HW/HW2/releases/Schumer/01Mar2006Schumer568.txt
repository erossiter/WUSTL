IMMEDIATE RELEASE  March 1  2006 Schumer  s Town Hall Meeting On   Quiet Zones   For The Hamburg Community Tonight                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Hamburg Town Leaders  Representatives From FRA  CSX  Norfolk Southern And Residents Meet Today To Discuss Establishing A   Quiet Zone     WNY Is The Crossroads Of Freight Rail Traffic In The Northeast And Hamburg Has Over 100 Trains Passing Through Residential Areas Every Day   Schumer  This Meeting Is An Important Step In the Right Direction To Get Safe And Quiet Rail Crossings In The Hamburg Communi The town hall meeting with the Federal Railroad Administration  FRA   the CSX Corporation and Norfolk Southern and the Hamburg community on establishing a   Quiet Zone   in the region organized by U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer will happen tonight  In January  after hearing from local leaders and concerned residents  Schumer asked that representatives from the FRA  CSX and Norfolk Southern to come to Hamburg to and hear community concerns regarding noise from constant freight train horn blasts  Community leaders asked for help in creating   Quiet Zone   designations for the crossings in their town  which would allow trains to pass by without sounding their horns       Bringing everyone together today is an important step in the right direction to get safe and quiet rail crossings in the Hamburg community     Schumer said    The meeting today has allowed Hamburg town leaders and residents be heard  I applaud the FRA  CSX and Norfolk Southern for stepping up to the plate and engaging the community in a meaningful way  Now we need to work together to move this forward  and get safe and quiet rail crossings in Hamburg  I will fight tooth and nail to get Hamburg the federal resources they need  and will work hard to keep this project on track         At today  s meeting  residents and Hamburg town leaders will  ask questions about the process of establishing a   Quiet Zone    voice their concerns  and receive feedback from the companies who operate railroads in the region  The FRA will provide clarification on what the current regulations are governing safety at railroad crossings in quiet zones  as well as the definition of the minimum and maximum decibel levels for horn blasts in residential communities      Western New York is the crossroads of freight rail traffic in the Northeast  and the Town of Hamburg has eight surface crossings and over 12 miles worth of freight track that carry over 100 trains a day through populated residential areas  Approximately  20 000 town residents live within audible range of these tracks  In order to reduce the constant annoyance of train horns blasts  for several years Town leaders have tried to reduce freight train noise at their crossings  The Town has done some preliminary estimates of what types of improvements need to be made  and is eager to implement the improvements as soon as possible      A   Quiet Zone    designation allows communities who meet the safety needs at particular highway rail grade crossings to have train horns silenced  Federal law requires on coming trains to sound their horns if they are approaching a grade crossing without advanced safety precautions  The establishment of a new quiet zone requires at minimum that each grade crossing be equipped with flashing lights and gates  and additional safety measures may be required to compensate for the absence of the horn as a warning device  New quiet zones can be in effect 24 hours a day or just during the overnight period between 10 p m  and 7 a m
