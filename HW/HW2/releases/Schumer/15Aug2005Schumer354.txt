IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 15  2005 Schumer Announces Over  479 000 For Western Ny Crime Control And Prevention Programs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Grant awarded as part of Justice Assistance Grant Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  479 927 has been awarded to the City of Buffalo and the surrounding areas by the U S  Department of Justice  s Office of Justice Programs  OJP  under the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  The federal funds will be used for crime control and prevention programs         When it comes to protecting our citizens  we need to be tough on crime and smart on prevention   Schumer said    These much needed funds will help to bring the quality people and first rate technology that allow Western NY families to live safely and comfortably          The City of Buffalo will use  204 772 in JAG funding to provide the following   89 222 to the Police Department for portable radios  bullet proof vests and upgraded computer technology   55 550 to the City Department of Community Services for police overtime and program costs for two juvenile delinquency prevention programs and for the City Drug Court Program for contracted case management services  and  60 000 to the City Office of Strategic Planning Office of Citizen Services for a   Save Our Streets    Program Coordinator position       The County of Erie will use  56 000 to partially fund an Assistant District Attorney position focused on gangs and juvenile violent crimes  and to partially fund a Regional Community Policing Coordinator position       The Town of Amherst will use  89 651 to provide additional patrol office overtime hours and graffiti reduction supplies       The Town of Cheektowaga will use  86 283 in JAG funds for additional overtime hours and equipment to support the following programs  the Patrol Highway Inderdiction  the K 9 Patrol  Bicycle Patrol  Motorcycle Patrol  First Platoon Safety Patrol  Deer Vehicle Accident Reduction Program  Flea Market Detail  and the School Liaison Emergency Response Plan         The Town of West Seneca will use  43 221 for additional overtime hours for community policing patrols  police equipment and Police Department computer hardware and software upgrades       The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime based on their own local needs and conditions  Grant funds can be used for state and local initiatives  technical assistance  training  personnel  equipment  supplies  contractual support  and information systems for criminal justice for any one or more of the following purpose areas  1  law enforcement programs  2  prosecution and court programs  3  prevention and education programs  4  corrections and community corrections programs  5  drug treatment programs  and 6  planning  evaluation  and technology improvement programs
