IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 16  2006 Schumer Urges Feds To Reverse Decision Denying Buffalo Veteran Compensation He Is Due                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            WNY Vet Has Appealed VA Decision To Deny Compensation Request   Schumer  Forcing Veteran To Bear The Burden Of Administrative Mistakes Is Unfair  Case Must Be Reconsidered                                                                                                                                                                                                                              U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today urged the U S  Department of Veterans Affairs  VA  to reverse their decision denying a claim by a Buffalo veteran seeking retro active compensation for a service related illness  In his second personal letter to the Buffalo VA Regional Office Director  Schumer supported Vietnam Veteran Jeff Sundquist  s effort to appeal the VA  s decision  asserting that it is unfair for Mr  Sundquist to pay for health care costs because of administrative errors made when the claim was originally filed   In addition to his own letter  Senator Schumer also facilitated getting a letter from Erie County to support the veteran in his appeal efforts        The VA  s decision in this case was unwise  unfounded  and unfair     Schumer said    This veteran risked his life for this country and now it is time for his country to stand up for him  He should not have to pay for administrative errors far beyond his control  This wrong can be righted very quickly         The 62 year old veteran from Depew was diagnosed with Type 2 diabetes  which is linked to Agent Orange  the chemical defoliant used during the Vietnam War  Upon discovering his claim was lost  he refilled with Chapter 77 of Vietnam Veterans of America in October of 2005   Veteran should receive an estimated  4 100 in retroactive compensation to cover the time period when the claim was lost  The Department of Veterans Affairs   disability compensation is a monetary benefit paid to veterans who are disabled by injury or disease incurred or aggravated during active military service  Mr  Sundquist filed his original claim with the Erie County office of Veterans Services when he was first diagnosed in February of 2004  After hearing on July 25  2006 that his claim was denied  Mr  Sundquist has decided to formally appeal this decision        In his letter to Donna Terrell  the Director of the Buffalo VA Regional Office  Schumer wrote    I understand that the Veteran  s Administration should not have to correct the errors made by other agencies  however  Mr  Sundquist is not requesting a correction of the administrative error   He is merely seeking the compensation due to him as a disabled veteran who made every good faith effort to file his claim in a timely fashion   As I had stated previously  these circumstances that delayed the filing of Mr  Sundquist  s claim were beyond his control   To have him bear this burden would seem to contradict the VA  s mission to serve veterans in need of assistance from the country they served   I hope you reconsider this case in a way that is consistent with my positive past interactions with your agency   I look forward to a positive outcome on Mr  Sundquists appeal
