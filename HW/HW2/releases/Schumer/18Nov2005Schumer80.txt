IMMEDIATE RELEASE  November 18  2005 Schumer  Clinton Announce Congressional Approval Of  19 9 Million In Military Construction Funds To Benefit Rochester                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Schumer  Clinton  Military Construction Projects are Vitally Important for Rochester  the New York State Economy and National Security U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton announced that the Joint House  Senate Appropriations Conference Committee has released the conference report on the FY 2006 Military Construction Appropriations Bill  which includes over  19 9 million for the construction of an Army National Guard Army Aviation Support Facility       The men and women of New York are second to none in supporting our military  and the brave individuals who serve in our National Guard deserve equipment and facilities that are second to none  This expansion project will accommodate more helicopters for missions and training  It  s great news for Rochester and for our country  s military     Schumer said        The men and women of the National Guard are making sacrifices for our freedom every day    both here at home and overseas  This funding recognizes the valuable work they do every day and demonstrates a real commitment to ensuring they have the best equipment and training available  I am glad our colleagues in Congress agreed with us on the importance of this funding     Senator Clinton said     The funds will be used to expand the existing Army Aviation Support Facility  AASF  to accommodate the assignment of 6 CH 47 and 6 UH 1 helicopters  The addition to the AASF will serve the peacetime missions of the assigned units and permit all personnel to improve the units   readiness posture     The FY 2006 Military Construction Appropriations Bill was approved by the Senate in September  and now having been released by the conference committee  must be approved by both chambers of Congress and sent to the President for his signature  Schumer and Clinton urged members of the Conference Committee to keep the funding for these projects in this year  s spending bill
