                                     Schumer  Clinton  Defense Project is Vitally Important for Clarkson University and National Security                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the Department of Defense Appropriations bill conference report filed late yesterday included  1 million for Clarkson University  s Basic Research in Chemical Mechanical Planarization  CMP  for Defense Applications   The House and Senate must approve the bill before being sent to the President and signed into law         This is great news for Clarkson University   said Schumer   Additional funds will not only boost the area economy  but also put Clarkson University and New York State at the forefront of defense technology  As the men and women of our military bravely serve our country all over the world  the funding for these Clarkson University defense projects will vastly improve national security technology and will give our armed forces an even greater edge          This is wonderful news for Clarkson University  This is cutting edge defense technology being made locally  supporting our men and women in uniform and our national security  This is an important investment that will in turn help the economy as well  I look forward to its final approval by the Senate     Senator Clinton said      Clarkson University  Basic Research in Chemical Mechanical Planarization  CMP  for Defense Applications   1 Million  Clarkson University will develop innovations in an essential manufacturing process  Chemical Mechanical Planarization  CMP   that are required for fabricating next generation semiconductor chips for new military weapons  communications and other electronic systems   CMP is a process using chemical slurries and nanoabrasives to planarize layers of semiconductor chips flat and smooth to nanoscale   The process is a critically important step when adding interconnection layers for electronic circuits in the semiconductors without creating reliability problems that are caused by damaging  unplanned contacts and other defects between layers  which can result in weapons targeting errors and malfunctions in communications and other systems
