IMMEDIATE RELEASE  November 1  2006 Schumer Announces Over  2 2 Million In Federal Dollars For Senior Housing Project In Rochester                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Federal Funds Awarded To The Urban League Of Rochester Economic Development Corporation For Development Of An Apartment Building For Low Income Seniors                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  2 247 700 has been awarded to the Urban League of Rochester Economic Development Corporation  ULREDC   in Monroe County  by the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  under the Section 202 Supportive Housing for the Elderly Grant program   The funds will be used to develop an apartment building for low income seniors in the Town of Henrietta   In May  2006 Schumer sent a personal letter to the Secretary of HUD on behalf of ULREDC supporting its application for this funding             This is great news for Monroe County     said Schumer    For far too many seniors  the cost of living is so high that it forces them to move elsewhere  or move into substandard housing   This funding will increase the number of affordable apartments available for low income seniors so that they enjoy the quality of life that the area has to offer and remain an active and vibrant part of the community            Federal funds will be used by ULREDC to develop a 20 unit apartment building for seniors with incomes at or below 50  of the area median income   The apartments will cover 540 square feet and will be designed to enable the home delivery of services for seniors such as assistance with bathing  meal preparation and medicinal administration   Individual units will be equipped with grab bars  emergency call systems and non slip floor surfaces to provide additional safety measures for residents   The building will also hold a community room  property management office and on site laundry room       The Section 202 program allows elderly persons  including the frail elderly  to live as independently as possible in the community by increasing the supply of rental housing with the availability of supportive services   Under this program  capital advances are made to private non profit organizations or non profit consumer cooperatives to build  acquire  and or rehabilitate rental housing with supportive services for elderly persons  including the frail elderly  who are 62 years of age or older   The capital advance is interest free and does not have to be repaid as long as the housing remains available for very low income elderly persons for at least 40 years   Project rental assistance is used to cover the difference between the HUD approved operating cost per unit and the tenant  s rent
