IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 22  2006 Schumer Announces Over  202 000 In Federal Funds Going To Schenectady City Schools                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Grant Will Be Used To Bring Mandarin Chinese Language Classes To Students                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that a  202 728 grant was awarded to Schenectady City Schools in Schenectady County by the U S  Department of Education  DOE  under the Foreign Language Assistance Program Local Education Agency  FLAP LEA    Federal dollars will be used to provide foreign language instruction in Mandarin Chinese        This is great news for Schenectady City Schools   Schumer said   Foreign language classes will help kids understand other cultures and broaden their horizons   Learning a second language will sharpen their communications skills and give them a competitive edge in their future endeavors        The Schenectady City School District will use the funds to start a Chinese language program in their elementary schools with the hopes of extending the program into their middle and high school in the upcoming years  The District will hire NYS certified Chinese language teacher for these classes   The program will also utilize various computer resources including videoconferencing and electronic partnerships with classrooms in China   Finally  the District plans to develop a cultural partnership with the Chinese Community Center of the Capitol District         The Foreign Language Assistance Program Local Education Agency provides federal funding for foreign language instruction in one or more less commonly taught languages including  Arabic  Chinese  Korean  Japanese  Russian  and some Indic  Iranian and Turkic languages   The innovative nature of the program  its use of technology  and other similar factors are also considered as part of the competitive application process
