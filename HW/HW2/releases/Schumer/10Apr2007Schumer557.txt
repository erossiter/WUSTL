IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 10  2007 Schumer Announces Nearly  400 000 In Federal Funds Going To Yonkers Public Schools                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Federal Funds Will Be Used To Enhance School Counseling Programs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that Yonkers Public Schools in Westchester County will receive a  399 530 grant from the United States Department of Education s Elementary and Secondary School Counseling Program  Federal dollars will be used to establish or expand counseling programs in target elementary schools         Our children deserve the resources they need to excel in school   Schumer said   It is essential that our students have access to counseling and developmental programs that will provide them with support and guidance  This funding will help schools in Yonkers to provide their students with the tools and opportunities to gain more from their education and build a foundation for a lifetime of success        Yonkers Public Schools will use the federal dollars to expand their counseling programs through the hiring of school counselors  social workers  psychologists and psychiatrists  With the new personnel  the schools will expand the range  availability  quality and quantity of available counseling services  The programs will use a developmental  prevention approach and will be designed and applied with the participation of school parents        The Elementary and Secondary School Counseling Program provides support to local educational agencies to establish or expand elementary and secondary school counseling programs  To achieve program goals and address the Government and Performance and Results Act measures  the schools will address the ratio of students to mental health professionals  and will reduce the number of disciplinary referrals and suspensions among participating students  Local schools have identified other specific goals that include improved student attendance and academic performance  social skills development  parental involvement  counselor and teacher professional development  and collaboration with community based organizations that provide mental health and other services to students
