IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 11  2007 Schumer Announces Federal Loan Awarded To Firefighters In Chautauqua County  With Bids For New Sheridan Fire Hall Higher Than Expected  Federal Dollars Will Cover Additional Construction Costs  U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced today that a  434 700 loan has been awarded to the Sheridan Fire District in Chautauqua County by the United States Department of Agriculture s  USDA  Rural Development program as a Community Facility Loan   The Sheridan Fire District will use the loan to cover the construction costs of a new fire hall      This is great news for Chautauqua County   Schumer said   We can never truly repay our first responders for their heroic efforts and dedication to duty  but we can ensure they have the equipment and resources they need to do their jobs well   The new fire hall will be larger and more modern  allowing first responders to better serve the various rural communities throughout the county      The Sheridan Fire District will use federal funds to cover the construction costs of a new fire hall to replace the current one which is more than 60 years old   Upon receiving bids that were higher than expected  the Sheridan Fire District applied for this loan to cover the unanticipated differences in cost   The new 10 000 square foot fire station is much larger than the current building and will be able to accommodate all the equipment they own   The new building will be constructed according to modern codes and requirements including making it handicap accessible and correcting and improving septic violations   The new fire hall is expected to serve nearly 3 000 people in several communities throughout Chautauqua County    Community Programs  a division of the Housing and Community Facilities Programs  is part of the United States Department of Agriculture s Rural Development mission area  Community Programs administers programs designed to develop essential community facilities for public use in rural areas  These facilities include schools  libraries  childcare  hospitals  medical clinics  assisted living facilities  fire and rescue stations  police stations  community centers  public buildings and transportation  Through its Community Programs  the Department of Agriculture is striving to ensure that such facilities are readily available to all rural communities  Community Programs utilizes three flexible financial tools to achieve this goal  the Community Facilities Guaranteed Loan Program  the Community Facilities Direct Loan Program  and the Community Facilities Grant Program
