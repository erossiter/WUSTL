IMMEDIATE RELEASE  November 19  2006 Schumer Urges President  FEMA To Expedite Federal Assistance For Broome And Delaware Counties                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today urged President George W  Bush and R  David Paulison  Director of the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  to expedite federal assistance for Broome and Delaware Counties after last Thursday s rains   Late yesterday  the Governor declared a state of emergency for the area  and is seeking federal aid   Schumer released the following letter  President George W  Bush  President of the United States  The White House  1600 Pennsylvania Avenue  NW  Washington  DC 20500   R  David Paulison  Director for Federal Emergency Management  500 C Street  SW  Washington  DC 20472   November 19  2006   Dear President Bush  Director Paulison       As you already know  severe storms hit areas of New York Thursday night  causing significant flooding and dangerous mudslides    It is my understanding that the situation in Broome and Delaware counties is dire enough that Governor Pataki has declared them to be in a state of emergency   He is now seeking technical assistance from the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  to assess the extent of the damage caused by the heavy rains   I urge you to ensure that the preliminary disaster assessments  PDAs  are completed as quickly as possible so that the residents  businesses and governments of these communities can move forward with their cleanup and restoration efforts without fear of having to bear the financial burden alone       These two counties are among those that suffered most greatly from the devastating floods this summer   I have heard from many residents who had just finally finished fixing their homes from the June destruction  only to see their work washed away and the damage redone on Thursday night   One hundred and sixty three families are still living in trailers  as their homes were left uninhabitable after the summer storms   It is essential that these families are safe and secure in their temporary trailers  and that progress on their permanent homes is not delayed by the most recent rains   The county governments are still steeped in heavy costs and restoration projects from six months ago  they can not shoulder the new expenses and labor that Thursday  s storms have necessitated alone         I urge you expeditiously complete the PDAs and to do everything required to assist those hit by the severe storms  paying particular attention to those who have now had to endure the financial and emotional costs of flood devastation twice in the past six months       If you have any questions or concerns  please do not hesitate to contact Stacey Warady  in my office  at  202  224 7276   Thank you for your consideration of this important matter       Sincerely        Charles E  Schumer  U S  Senator
