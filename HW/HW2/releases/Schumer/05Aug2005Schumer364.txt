IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 5  2005 Schumer Announces Over  500 000 Coming To Cornell University For Research Projects                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Federal funds will support two research projects in the Departments of Plant Biology and Entomology                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that Cornell University will receive  597 000 from the United States Department of Agriculture  USDA  as part of its National Research Initiative Competitive Grants Program  A project entitled   Mechanisms of Plant Response to Non Extreme Temperatures     directed by the Department of Plant Biology  was awarded  357 000  The Department of Entomology  s project   Evolution and Applied Consequences of VSSC Alleles Conferring Pyrethroid Resistance in Musca Domestica    was awarded  240 000        Improving science for our farmers is key to their success     said Schumer    This funding will help these two projects shed new  vital light on the field of agriculture and keep Cornell University at the forefront of agricultural research        Cornell University  s Department of Plant Biology is dedicated to educating students in and conducting research on the structure  classification  ecology  function  development and molecular biology of plants  The Department of Entomology at Ithaca has responsibility to increase our knowledge of insects and to apply this knowledge to benefit society  Both departments are part of the College of Agriculture and Life Sciences at Cornell University in Ithaca  NY  which aims to be the preeminent college for research  teaching and extension of agriculture and the life sciences  developing leaders to address the global challenges of the 21st century      The purpose of the National Research Initiative Competitive Grants Program  NRI  is to support research  extension  and education grants that address key problems of national  regional  and multi state importance in sustaining all components of agriculture  farming  ranching  forestry including urban and agro forestry  aquaculture  rural communities  human nutrition  processing  etc    Providing this support requires that NRI advance fundamental sciences in support of agriculture and coordinate opportunities to build on these discoveries  Building on these discoveries will necessitate new efforts in education and extension that deliver science based knowledge to people  allowing them to make informed practical decisions
