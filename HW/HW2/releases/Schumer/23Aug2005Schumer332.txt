IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 23  2005 Schumer  Rochester Will Get Over  384 000 to  Weed  Out Crime and  Seed  in Prevention and Intervention                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          DOJ  Weed and Seed  program provides funds for local law enforcement to  weed out  criminals and communities to  seed in  prevention  intervention  and treatment services    City of Rochester will use funds to pay for community policing initiatives                                                                                                                                                         U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the Department of Justice s Operation Weed and Seed has awarded  384 901 to be used by the City of Rochester to prevent violent crime and fight gang activity        These funds are going toward a program which works to shut down criminals and eliminate gang s   said Schumer   The great thing about this program is that it is more than just a short term response  it also provides long term solutions by identifying criminals  keeping them away  and bringing treatment and rehabilitation in to the area        The Weed and Seed program works to prevent  reduce and control violent crime  drug abuse and gang activity in specifically targeted high crime areas across the country  Its name comes from a two part approach to these areas  One involves law enforcement agencies and prosecutors getting together to  weed out  individuals who participate in violent crime or drug abuse in an attempt to keep them out of the targeted area  The second part   seeding   includes bringing social services to the area in order to push for prevention  intervention and treatment  as well as neighborhood restoration        The City of Rochester will use  159 903 of the federal dollars for the Juvenile Intervention Team  JIT  and Anti Gang Special Emphasis Program  JIT  which includes the Youth Intervention Specialist and School Resource Officers and others  will be working with youth to reduce their involvement in gangs  During this project period  the Northwest and Southwest Rochester Weed and Seed sites will expand its Anti Gang Special Emphasis Program  The goal of this program is to address youth violence by identifying the perpetrators or violence and developing the strategies to address it        In addition  Rochester will use  224 998 at their Southwest site  Law enforcement will coordinate and cooperate with a variety of agencies to combine resources to investigate and reduce violent crime in the target area  Community policing goals will reduce crime through environmental design in the enhancement of lighting in common areas  theft prevention  neighborhood watch programs  and other crime reducing methods  Prevention  intervention  and treatment strategies will establish partnerships with social service providers  residents  and business owners in collaborative programs to provide assistance to residents of all ages with a special emphasis on serving the youth  Neighborhood restoration efforts will actively involve residents in the development of safe neighborhoods with quality housing and excellent schools  The site allocated  153 813 of these funds to support weeding activities  including community policing        Since 1994  the Department of Justice has allocated at least  9 million annually nationwide for Weed and Seed related task forces administered through the Drug Enforcement Agency  the Federal Bureau of Investigation and other DOJ agencies
