IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 14  2005 Schumer  Over  295 000 Coming to Hudson Valley Fire Organizations                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Schumer announces that 12 fire departments and organizations across New York State will receive over  660 000 as part of U S  Department of Homeland Securities   FIRE GRANT program    Schumer says federal grant will help Hudson Valley pay for fire operations and improve fire safety during tough fiscal times    In March  Schumer fought to restore  400 million to the FIRE grants program which would b US Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced over  295 000 in federal funds for four fire departments and organizations in the Hudson Valley to pay for fire operations and fire safety  The funds are being awarded through the Assistance to Firefighters program administered by the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS         This is a great day for the Hudson Valley   said Schumer   In the post 9 11 world  it is vitally important for our first responders be well trained and properly equipped to deal with any situation  This can be difficult to accomplish because of budget cuts  but these federal grants will help ensure that firefighters and other first responders have the money they need to do their jobs        Four grants for a total of  295 673 were awarded to fire departments  organizations  and ambulance corps in the Hudson Valley  In Dutchess County  the Rhinebeck Volunteer Fire Department   80 873  received funds for operations and fire safety  Funds were also awarded in Westchester County to the Valhalla Volunteer Ambulance Corps   45 600   the Village of Pelham Fire Department   86 265   and Thornwood   82 935  for operations and fire safety  The grants were part of the fifth round of DHS FY 2005 grants across the country  with more rounds to come       A Schumer analysis of the Administration s federal budget for FY05 found that cuts to the program could shortchange New York fire departments by  16 million  In March  Schumer urged the Congress and the White House to bolster the Administration s proposal by sending the full  900 million promised to fire departments by Congress       At Schumer s urging  fire departments and emergency services providers across New York have taken advantage of the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  which sends funds directly for specialized emergency response training to terrorist attacks and similar situations  the creation of wellness and fitness programs for firefighters  equipment and facility upgrades  new fire trucks  and fire prevention programs        Our local fire departments are in desperate need of an infusion of cash from the federal government   Schumer said   Budget gaps are straining local fire departments   capabilities  In addition  our first responders are on heightened alert because of the war  This opportunity for fire departments to get federal financial support is just what the doctor ordered        The grant program  called the USFA Grants to Firefighters Program  awards grants directly to fire departments to enhance their ability to protect the health and safety of the public  as well as that of firefighting personnel and other first responders who face fire and fire related hazards        The USFA awards the grants on a competitive basis to the applicants that most closely address the program s priorities and demonstrate financial need  Funding is available for specialized emergency response training to terrorist attacks and similar situations  the creation of wellness and fitness programs for firefighters  equipment and facility upgrades  and fire prevention programs
