    Latest News             TOPICS                                                                                                           Latest              News                           FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  November 8  2007 Schumer Announces Counties Across Western New York Eligible For Federal Disaster Aid                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Counties in Western New York Designated As Primary and Contiguous Disaster Areas As A Result Of June Drought Farm Operators in Allegany  Cattaraugus  Chautauqua  Erie  Livingston  Steuben and Wyoming Counties Can Apply For Federal Assistance from U S  Department Of Agriculture U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  has designated Allegany and Cattaraugus Counties as primary natural disaster areas and Chautauqua  Erie  Livingston  Steuben and Wyoming Counties as contiguous disaster areas  due to losses caused by the June drought     This is great news for Western New York farmers who have had a particularly difficult year due to severe weather conditions  said Schumer   Farmers are the backbone of our economy and when they are hurting  the whole state feels the impact   Assistance from USDA is essential for farmers to cover losses  and I am pleased to see the government stepping up to provide this aid    This designation makes farm operators in both primary and contiguous counties eligible to be considered for low interest emergency loans from USDAs Farm Service Agency  FSA    FSA will consider each application on its own merit by taking into account the extent of losses  security available  and repayment ability
