IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 8  2006 Schumer Announces Over  150 000 For Two Organizations In Westchester To Enhance School Safety                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Federal Funds Will Be Distributed By Law Enforcement Agencies To Local Schools For New School Safety Equipment U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that two grants have been awarded to law enforcement agencies in Westchester by the U S  Department of Justice Office of Community Oriented Policing Services  COPS    The Village of Sleepy Hollow Police Department will receive a grant of  100 900 and the City of White Plains will receive a  50 027 grant  These agencies will distribute funding to various schools in the region for new security equipment           This is great news for Westchester     said Schumer     It is imperative that school safety be one of the highest priorities within the community   These funds will make our schools a safer environment  allowing schools to pursue their primary goals of teaching and learning          Federal funds distributed by the Village of Sleepy Hollow Police Department and the City of White Plains will be used by local schools to improve school safety   These funds will cover up to half of the total cost to install metal detectors  locks  lighting  and other equipment that can help deter crime   Funding will also go toward the cost of security assessments and training of officers  This year  the Secure Our Schools program has distributed nearly  15 million dollars to help schools improve safety and security       The mission of the COPS Office is to advance community policing as an effective strategy in communities   efforts to improve public safety   COPS provides grants to tribal  state  and local law enforcement agencies to hire and train community policing professionals  acquire and deploy cutting edge crime fighting technologies  and develop and test innovative policing strategies  COPS grants support innovative community policing initiatives  fund the purchase of crime fighting technology  provide technical assistance resources  and fund the hiring and training of local law enforcement officers
