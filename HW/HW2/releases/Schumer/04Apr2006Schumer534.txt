IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 4  2006 Schumer Announces Syracuse Will Receive  225 000 To  Weed Out    Crime And  Seed In    Prevention And Intervention                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   DOJ  Weed And Seed  Program Provides Funds For Local Law Enforcement To  Weed Out  Criminals And Communities To  Seed In  Prevention  Intervention  And Treatment Services U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the Department of Justice s Operation Weed and Seed has awarded  225 000 to be used by the Home HeadQuarters in Syracuse to help communities with serious crime problems bring together people and resources to prevent and control crime  and improve the quality of life in the neighborhoods  Home HeadQuarters is a not for profit organization dedicated to revitalizing and stabilizing neighborhoods in Syracuse and Onondaga County        This money funds a program that shuts down criminals and gang members  while also building up neighborhoods   said Schumer   The great thing about this program is that it is more than just a short term response  it also provides long term solutions by identifying criminals and locking them up  while also bringing treatment  prevention and community rehabilitation in to the area        The Weed and Seed program employs a two part approach to prevent  reduce and control violent crime  drug abuse and gang activity in specifically targeted high crime areas across the country  The first approach brings law enforcement agencies and prosecutors getting together to  weed out  individuals who participate in violent crime or drug abuse in an attempt to keep them out of the targeted area  The second part   seeding   includes bringing social services to the area in order to push for prevention  intervention and treatment  as well as neighborhood restoration        The Syracuse Northside Weed and Seed site received Official Recognition from the DOJ in February 1996 and again in February 2001  This is the site  s fifth  and final  award to implement its strategy  During this project period  the law enforcement goal is focused on the coordination of Federal  state and local agencies to develop strategies to impact criminal activity in the Weed and Seed site through special operations teams and bi weekly interventions addressing drug and violent crime  Community policing is implemented through the Syracuse Police Department  s Team Oriented Policing Section with the goal of working closely with residents  schools  businesses  faith based organizations  and human service agencies to conduct problem solving activities  Prevention  intervention  and treatment efforts will focus on youth prevention and larger issues such as the impact of substance and alcohol use on individuals  families  and neighborhoods  which will be addressed through a community court as a way to assist individuals in changing their behavior  Neighborhood building efforts include increasing homeownership and the development of a safer neighborhood and improved quality of life in which to retain and attract businesses in the designated area  The site has thus far allocated  128 400 of the total  225 000 award to support weeding activities including community policing       The city s weeding goals include improving relations between the police and the community by encouraging residents to participate in crime prevention  problem solving  and neighborhood restoration  The city s seeding goals include mentoring and training youth and families to reduce drug abuse and domestic violence       Since 1994  the Department of Justice has allocated millions of dollars annually nationwide for Weed and Seed related task forces administered through the Drug Enforcement Agency  the Federal Bureau of Investigation and other DOJ agencies
