IMMEDIATE RELEASE  October 9  2007 Schumer  Groundbreaking For New Buffalo Courthouse A Historic Moment For Downtown s Revitalization                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Schumer Delivered over  83 Million in Funding for Construction of New Court House and Cut Through Bureaucratic Red Tape that was Bogging Down the Project New Federal Courthouse In Buffalo will be Centerpiece Of Downtown Development and Guarantee The Federal Judiciary Of WNY Has The Resources To Serve Its Citizens  After spending years fighting for federal funding for the construction of Buffalos new Federal Courthouse  today  U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer attended the groundbreaking for the historic project that will anchor downtowns revitalization  Schumer worked to secure over  83 million in federal funding to begin construction of the new building and cut through bureaucratic red tape that was bogging down the project   The new courthouse will alleviate the space shortages at the current facility by including new office space for judges and magistrates  the US attorney and the US Marshal s Service  Centrally located in the downtown area  the Courthouse will anchor the ongoing downtown revitalization effort   One year after last Octobers devastating snow storm  todays groundbreaking on this new Federal Courthouse shows Buffalos remarkable resilience and how this city came together to start building a magnificent building that will anchor downtowns ongoing revitalization  said Senator Schumer    Over the last five years  Senator Schumer steadfastly worked to acquire over  83 million in federal funding for the construction of the new courthouse    The fate of the new courthouse was in jeopardy when the Presidents 2007 Budget slashed all funding for its construction  Without funding in the 2007 budget  the construction would have been put off for another year  leaving the property undeveloped even though the federal government had already acquired the four buildings located on the property and scheduled them for demolition   In depriving funding for the new Federal Courthouse  the Presidents 2007 budget endangered what is seen as the centerpiece of downtown development for Buffalo    Schumer responded by relentlessly working to have the federal funding restored in the Senate Budget Committee  In his personal letter to Senator Judd Greg  Chairman of the Budget Committee at the time  and Senator Kent Conrad  Ranking Member of the Budget Committee at the time  Schumer requested that the Committee include the remaining  93 7 million needed to complete the Buffalo Courthouse in the budget resolution    Schumer stated at the time  the omission of this project from the administrations budget is galling  Now is the time to follow through on the federal commitment to Buffalo  This new courthouse is a lynchpin of Downtown Buffalos revitalization  For 2 years now GSA has listed this project as its top priority    it is high time the administration put its money where its mouth is  and allocate the monies to get this project built  There are both safety and security concerns here  we need to ensure that the federal judiciary of Western New  York has the necessary resources to serve the citizens of the region   In addition  Schumer successfully called on the Office of Management and Budget  OMB  Director Mitchell Daniels to release approved funds for the courthouse  Although the General Services Administration  GSA  had allocated  9 8 million for site selection and design of the new courthouse building  the OMB had failed to give final approval for the GSA to spend the money   Construction of the building will begin at Niagara   Square and Delaware Avenue   Congress initially authorized the Buffalo Courthouse in 2002  The Administrations Fiscal Year 2002 and Fiscal Year 2003 budgets both included funding for demolition and site design of the project  GSA has identified the Buffalo Courthouse as its highest priority for courthouse construction for two years in a row  In addition  GSA has already taken the necessary first steps to make this courthouse a reality by acquiring land and approving demolition of existing structures at the site
