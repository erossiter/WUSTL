IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 22  2006 Schumer Announces Over  200 000 Coming To The City Of Binghamton For Crime Prevention And Policing Initiatives                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   DOJ  Weed And Seed  Program Provides Funds For Local Law Enforcement To  Weed Out  Criminals And Communities To  Seed In  Prevention  Intervention  And Treatment Services   City Of Binghamton Will Use Funds To Pay For Community Policing Initiatives                                                                                                                                                U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  200 000 has been awarded to the City of Binghamton in Broome County to reduce drug use and crime through community policing initiatives   The funds are being awarded through the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  Weed and Seed Program        This is great news for the City of Binghamton     said Schumer     These funds are going toward a program that works to shut down criminals and reduce crime and drug activity   Implementing community policing programs is not just a short term response  it provides long term solutions by focusing on prevention and community partnerships          The City of Binghamton will use federal dollars to continue to strengthen collaborative law enforcement efforts to reduce drug use and crime within the target area   There will be a special emphasis on firearm enforcement   Community policing goals will create a stronger presence in the community  build partnerships  and empower residents to be active participants in reducing crime   Prevention  intervention  and treatment goals include the implementation of educational and training programs that address unemployment problems along with the implementation of programs that will reduce crime   Neighborhood restoration goals will increase the number of owner occupied homes and decrease housing code violations   The Center City Weed and Seed site received Official Recognition in January 2002   Weed and Seed street boundaries include Northern Suffolk railroad tracks to the north  Prospect Avenue  Commercial Alley  Exchange and Isbell Streets to the west  Brandywine Highway and Liberty Street to the east  and the southern boundary extends along North Shore Drive        The Weed and Seed program works to prevent  reduce and control violent crime  drug abuse and gang activity in specifically targeted high crime areas across the country   Its name comes from a two part approach to these areas   One involves law enforcement agencies and prosecutors getting together to  weed out  individuals who participate in violent crime or drug abuse in an attempt to keep them out of the targeted area   The second part   seeding   includes bringing social services to the area in order to push for prevention  intervention and treatment  as well as neighborhood restoration  Since 1994  the Department of Justice has allocated at least  9 million annually nationwide for Weed and Seed related task forces administered through the Drug Enforcement Agency  the Federal Bureau of Investigation and other DOJ agencies
