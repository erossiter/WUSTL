IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 20  2005 Schumer Announces  80 000 for Jeffersonville Public Radio Station                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Jeffersonville  s WJFF FM Public Radio Station to Receive Federal Funds From the Corporation for Public Broadcasting                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  80 000 for WJFF FM radio of Jeffersonville as part of the Corporation for Public Broadcasting  CPB  initiative to assist local public radio stations in making the transition to digital technology  The federal funds will be used to purchase the equipment needed to begin broadcasting using digital transmission  significantly enhancing the quality and scope of program services       This is great news for the Catskills region     says Senator Schumer    WJFF FM plays a critical role in the community  and in order to maintain this service  they need to keep up with current technology  This investment ensures that everyone in the region will have access to their local public radio station     The rural station is the only public radio station to penetrate the deep valleys of the Catskills  Upper Delaware  and Mid Hudson regions  It is also one of the few alternatively powered radio stations in the country  using energy from water and wind to power its studios and broadcasts  WJFF FM radio carries popular national programming and local broadcasts  as well as producing internet feeds of live and archived broadcasts      Jeffersonville  s WJFF FM is one of the six New York public radio stations which are receiving funding in the fourth round of CPB grants  This round includes  8 8 million dollars in federal funds for 119 radio stations throughout the country  including 78 stations serving rural and minority audiences  The federal funding comes as part of a  190 million grant provided to CPB by Congress over the last five years  In total  CPB has distributed grants to over 405 public radio stations and 285 public television stations  all of which will assist public broadcasting in making the transition from analog to digital transmission
