IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 20  2006 Schumer Announces Key Senate Panel Backs  250 000 For The Clemens Center For The Performing Arts In Elmira                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Senator Aggressively Lobbied Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill  The Federal Funding Will Enhance Education For Children Through The Use Of Theater Productions U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee is expected to approve later today  250 000 under the FY07 Labor  Health  and Human Services and Education Appropriations Bill for the Clemens Center for the Performing Arts in Elmira  Schumer aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill       This is great news for all of Elmira    said Schumer    By combining classic literature with engaging performances  the Clemens Center will remind kids that reading and learning is fun  This new series will allow children to experience professional theatre as well as attract tourists from all over the state  boosting the Chemung economy        The Clemens Center for the Performing Arts provides a diverse range of performing arts programming to the citizens of New York and enhances the regional community through the creativity and excitement of the performances  This federal funding will allow the Clemens Center to produce a five show series of professional touring performances of productions dramatizing classical literature for children  The programs will reach 10 000 students with curriculum based performances and support materials to enhance education goals and promote literature     Later today  the Appropriations Bill for Transportation  Treasure  the Judiciary  Housing  and Urban Development is expected to pass the Appropriations Committee and will head to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  The bill will then proceed to the Conference Committee and finally will be sent to the President before becoming law
