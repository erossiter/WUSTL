IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 21  2006 Schumer Announces Key Senate Panel Approves  500 000 For Educational Programs At Lincoln Center And Carnegie Hall                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 250 000 will go towards the National Music Education Program at Carnegie Hall  250 000 will go towards Youth Orientated Jazz Programs at Lincoln Center Senator Aggressively Lobbied Senate Appropriations Committee to Include Funding In Spending Bill U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced that a key Senate Appropriations Committee approved  500 000 for educational programs at Lincoln Center and Carnegie Hall  The funding  included in the FY07 Labor  Health and Human Services and Education Appropriations Bill  will go toward the National Music Education Program at Carnegie Hall and Jazz at Lincoln Center     There are few gifts that we can bestow upon our children that are more rewarding than a love of music     said Senator Schumer    With funding for Jazz at Lincoln Center  we will help future generations of New Yorkers participate in one of America  s most unique and exciting musical creations      250 000 will fund Jazz at Lincoln Center  JALC  and its music education programs and publications dedicated to encouraging youth participation in Jazz  JALC offers Jazz performances and music instructors to more than 4 000 children in elementary  middle  and high schools throughout New York City  Programs include family concerts  Jazz curriculum for elementary and middle school students and a program dedicated to giving high school and college Jazz students the opportunity to attend open rehearsals at the Lincoln Center  Next season  JALC expects to bring Jazz to over 300 000 students  teachers  and musicians    250 000 will fund Carnegie Hall  s efforts to improve children  s education through national music education programs that target school and community orchestras  The music education programs focus on teaching music curriculums and empowering school and community orchestras by providing them with the fundamental music tools to customize their own music education programs  Over the past three years  approximately 100 000 elementary school students and teachers in 12 states have participated in Carnegie Hall  s music education programs    By supporting music education for thousands of students  we can help insure that Carnegie Hall  s fabled concert hall will someday be filled with brilliant performances from today  s school children     said Senator Schumer  Yesterday  the Labor  Health and Human Services and Education Appropriations Bill was passed by the Appropriations Committee and now heads to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  The bill will then proceed to the House Senate Conference Committee and finally will be sent to the President before becoming law
