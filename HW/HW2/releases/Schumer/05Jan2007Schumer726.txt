IMMEDIATE RELEASE  January 5  2007 Schumer Announces TSA To Add Screeners To Stewart Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Today  U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced that the Transportation Security Administration has approved nearly 10 additional full time equivalent screeners for Stewart International Airport   Yesterday  Schumer called on TSA to increase Stewart  s allocation from 24 full time equivalent screeners to 42 after reports of growing security lines and delays because of increased passengers demand from new JetBlue and AirTran routes  Schumer called on the TSA to recalibrate its allocation for Stewart  arguing it did not provide enough screeners to manage new passenger flow   Today  the TSA said it would increase Stewart  s screener allocation from 24 7 FTE  s to 35 3 FTE  s   Tomorrow  Schumer will send a personal letter to TSA Administrator Kip Hawley asking him to deliver all 42 necessary FTE screeners necessary to adequately staff the facility         It  s high time the TSA realized that when you have more planes and more passengers  you need more screeners     Schumer said    TSA must finish the job and provide the manpower and equipment to ensure that the thousands of new passengers coming to Stewart to take advantage of the new JetBlue and AirTran service pass through quickly and efficiently   Long lines and back ups will only hamper our long fought to see Stewart thrive         U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer
