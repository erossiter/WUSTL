IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 19  2005 Schumer To MTA  Extend Bus Service To Connect Staten Island And New Jersey                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Proposal to connect existing MTA bus lines to New Jersey Transit  s Hudson Bergen Light Rail System will provide a more efficient route for thousands of New Yorkers and reduce commute times U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today called on the MTA to move forward on a proposal that would link existing MTA bus lines to New Jersey Transit  s Hudson Bergen Light Rail line  s station at 34th street in Bayonne  NJ       Creating direct link from the North Shore of Staten Island to the Bayonne transit hub is not only sensible  it is essential     Schumer said    This service will go a long way toward alleviating congestion on Staten Island  which will be a much needed boost to the entire economy        Schumer noted that the additional stop at the end of some existing MTA bus routes would go a long way toward alleviating the ongoing congestion on Staten Island  s roads and expressways  and provide an efficient route for thousands of New Yorkers to commute to work  It is estimated that this proposal would only cost  1 million annually  while reducing commute times dramatically      The additional service  originally proposed in the late 1990  s  would create a direct link from the North Shore of Staten Island to the Bayonne transit hub which provides a more direct route to Lower Manhattan and Jersey City  where many Staten Islanders work  According to the Staten Island Chamber of Commerce  the service would reduce commuting times to Lower Manhattan to under an hour  something currently impossible from the North Shore     Schumer  s  in a letter to the MTA Chairman Peter Kalikow  wrote    It is up to all of us to work together to ensure that the people of Staten Island have access to convenient and efficient transportation  In addition  I encourage you to work with Staten Island political and business leaders  New Jersey Transit and the State of New Jersey  to find an equitably cost sharing plan        Staten Island was the fastest growing county in the State of New York from 1990 to 2000  with significant population and economic gains expected over the next 30 years  At the same time  however  Staten Islanders have the longest average commute in the nation  averaging 43 8 minutes each way  Staten Island residents also endure the most frequent   extreme commutes    of 90 minutes or longer in the nation  Congestion and traffic hampers economic growth and creates an environment that businesses try to avoid
