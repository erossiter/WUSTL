IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 25  2005 Schumer Announces  400 000 for Massena International Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Massena Airport will use the money to encourage commercial air service  Federal Funds Awarded as Part of the Small Community Air Service Development Program  SCASDP  Grants                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  400 000 in federal funds was awarded to the Massena Airport by the U S  Department of Transportation  The federal dollars come from the Small Community Air Service Development Program  SCASDP  Grants  The funds will be used to help the airport study air service in the community        This is great news for Massena  since its goal of this program is to improve air service and lower prices for residents in smaller communities   Schumer said   One of the first things businesses look at when they consider moving to a new location is whether there are good air service options in the area  This study will help the North County recruit airlines and become a thriving center of activity and a hub for economic growth in the region        Massena Airport will use these federal funds to study the feasibility of establishing self sustaining air service in the community  DOT received more than 80 applications for the 37 spots to receive SCASDP grants  which is funded by an FY2005 Congressional appropriation of  20 million  Only those communities with insufficient air service or unreasonably high airfares are eligible to participate in the program  The law limits the eligibility to communities with small hubs and smaller airports  According to the legislation  DOT may use funds to provide assistance to implement measures considered appropriate to improve air service both in terms of the cost of such service to consumers and the availability of such service
