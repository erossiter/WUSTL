IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 29  2006 Schumer Announces Over  127 000 For Hastings Center  Inc  To Adresss Children  s Mental Health Issues                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Funds Will Be Used to Conduct Workshops on Safety Concerns Surrounding Children Treated with Psychotropic Medications                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  127 825 has been awarded to Hastings Center Inc of Garrison in Putnam County by the U S  Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  under the National Institute of Mental Health  NIMH  Office as a Mental Health Research grant   Federal funds will be used to put on a series of workshops to address the safety and efficacy concerns surrounding children treated with psychotropic medications         This is great news for Putnam County     Schumer said    It is so important to make sure that current treatments for children with mental health problems are safe and effective   This research will benefit the local community and could have an impact on a national scale        The Hastings Center  Inc  will use federal funds to put on a series of five workshops to address an assortment of controversies about the safety and efficacy using psychotropic medications to treat children with emotional and behavioral disturbances   This series will bring together a diverse group of practitioners and scholars debate and analyze issues such as the science supporting the diagnoses of behavioral and emotional disturbances and the values at stake in providing medical treatment   The goals of the series are to promote productive discussion  ultimately leading to a public symposium and a series of jargon free clear and balanced publications       According to the NIMH  in the field of mental health  new studies are needed to understand the best treatments for children with emotional and behavioral disturbances  Children are in a state of rapid change and growth during their developmental years  therefore diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders must be viewed with these changes in mind  Not long ago  it was thought that many brain disorders such as anxiety disorders  depression  and bipolar disorder began only after childhood  It is now know that these illnesses can begin in early childhood  An estimated 1 in 10 children and adolescents in the United States suffers from mental illness  such as anxiety disorders  depression or bipolar disorder  severe enough to cause some level of impairment  While there have been studies done on the safety and efficacy of prescribing psychotropic medications to young children  these studies to date are incomplete  and much more needs to be learned about young children who are treated with medications for all kinds of illnesses     The NIMH mission is to reduce the burden of mental and behavioral disorders through research on mind  brain  and behavior  Their goal is to generate research that will transform prevention of and recovery from mental disorders
