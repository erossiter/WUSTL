IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 18  2007 Schumer Announces IRS To Extend Federal Tax Filing Deadline Additional Week                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Today  U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced that the IRS has granted an additional week long extension to the federal tax filing deadline to taxpayers affected by the recent storm    IRS Commissioner Mark Everson granted the additional week federal extension after Schumer requested it at a Senate Finance Committee hearing this morning  Earlier this week  the IRS granted only a two day extension for storm ravaged regions in New York and across the Northeast  On going power outages and public transportation problems have made it difficult in some cases  if not impossible  for some taxpayers and tax professionals to meet the April 17 filing deadline  Affected taxpayers can mark their paper tax returns with the words  April 16 Storm   Taxpayers who e file their returns can use their software s  disaster  feature  if available        Senator Schumer released the following statement         This will relieve an enormous burden for taxpayers who are trying to recover from the devastating storms  One or two days are not nearly enough time for thousands of farmers  families  and local businesses to get control of this very difficult situation  much less pay their taxes  I want to thank Commissioner Everson for his responsiveness and attention to this issue          U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer
