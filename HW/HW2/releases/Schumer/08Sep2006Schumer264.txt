IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 8  2006 Schumer Announces Over  194 000 For Law Enforcement Agencies In Western New York To Enhance School Safety                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Federal Funds Will Be Distributed By Law Enforcement Agencies To Local Schools For New School Safety Equipment                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that four grants have been awarded to law enforcement agencies in Western New York by the U S  Department of Justice Office of Community Oriented Policing Services  COPS    The total grant amount was  194 717 including  29 114  for the Cattaraugus County Sheriff  s Department  19 987 for the Town of Cheektowaga   25 000 for the Niagara County Sheriff  s Department and  120 616 for the Salamanca Police Department   These organizations will distribute funding to various schools in the region for new security equipment           This is great news for Western New York     said Schumer     It is imperative that school safety be one of the highest priorities within the community   These funds will make our schools a safer environment  allowing schools to pursue their primary goals of teaching and learning          Federal funds distributed by the Cattaraugus County Sheriff  s Department  the Town of Cheektowaga  the Niagara County Sheriff  s Department and the Salamanca Police Department  will be used by schools to improve school safety   These funds will cover up to half of the total cost to install metal detectors  locks  lighting  and other equipment that can help deter crime   Funding will also go toward the cost of security assessments and training of officers  This year  the Secure Our Schools program has distributed nearly  15 million dollars to help schools improve safety and security       The mission of the COPS Office is to advance community policing as an effective strategy in communities   efforts to improve public safety   COPS provides grants to tribal  state  and local law enforcement agencies to hire and train community policing professionals  acquire and deploy cutting edge crime fighting technologies  and develop and test innovative policing strategies  COPS grants support innovative community policing initiatives  fund the purchase of crime fighting technology  provide technical assistance resources  and fund the hiring and training of local law enforcement officers
