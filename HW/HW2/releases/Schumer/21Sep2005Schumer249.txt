IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 21  2005 Schumer Urges NRC to Conduct Thorough Investigation into Indian Point and Improve Communications with Community                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Senator  Latest Safety Lapses Warrant Comprehensive Federal Investigation    Information Must Be Relayed Faster and Better to Community                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today called on the National Regulatory Commission  NRC  to launch an immediate and thorough investigation into Indian Point  including the spent fuel storage system and the warning sirens system  Schumer  in a letter to NRC Chairman Nils J  Diaz  expressed grave concern over the leak of radioactive fluid and asked for the steps being taken to ensure better communication with the community        When it comes to nuclear security  we expect the highest safety and security standards to be met  Safety must be the number one priority  The NRC needs to step up to the plate and launch a vigorous investigation so that we can accurately assess what is going on at the plant once and for all         Schumer pointed to several recent security lapses that warrant an immediate investigation  including reports that some warning sirens in Westchester  Rockland  Orange and Putnam did not work properly when tested  and that the backup system may not be operational  Schumer also cited a small leak of radioactive water found during a construction project      In his letter  Schumer said    Operating a nuclear power plant in the heart of a densely populated urban area requires an exceedingly high level of public communication and accountability  Although your initial evaluation reveals that the leak posed no hazard to plant employees or Westchester residents  the failure to make the leak public is a throwback to the bad old days of secrecy and lack of accountability which I worked with Entergy and the NRC and others to change
