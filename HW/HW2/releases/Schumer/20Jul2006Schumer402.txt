IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 20  2006 Schumer Announces Key Senate Panel Approves  250 000 For Benedictine Hospital In Kingston                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Senator Aggressively Lobbied Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill  Schumer  New Technology Will Increase Productivity and Satisfaction U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee is expected to approve later today  250 000 under the FY07 Labor  Health and Human Services and Education Appropriations Bill for Benedictine Hospital in Kingston  The bill must now be approved by the House Senate Conference Committee before being signed into law by the President  Schumer aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill       This is great news for Benedictine Hospital and the entire Hudson Valley     said Schumer    These federal funds will purchase new technology that can make the experience of physicians and patients easier and more efficient  As this region continues to grow  Benedictine Hospital must be able to implement the resources they need to continue their impressive job of serving the community        Benedictine Hospital is a progressive  222 bed care facility that serves the growing Northern Hudson Valley  The federal funding will be used by the hospital to purchase hardware and software systems  These technologies will include further automation of patient medical information and revenue enhancement through increased productivity and patient and physician satisfaction     Later today  the Appropriations Bill for Transportation  Treasure  the Judiciary  Housing  and Urban Development is expected to pass the Appropriations Committee and will head to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  The bill will then proceed to the Conference Committee and finally will be sent to the President before becoming law
