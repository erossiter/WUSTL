IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 22  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Approves  500 000 For NY Botanical Garden                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Senators Worked Closely with Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill   Funds will go toward the Garden s Virtual Herbarium  a Digital Database of Thousands of Plants Used for Research   Bill will now be sent to the Senate Floor                                                                                                                                                      Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton announced that the full Senate Appropriations Committee approved  500 000 for the New York Botanical Garden  which will go toward the gardens virtual herbarium for science and research under the FY08 Labor  Health and Human Services and Education Appropriations Bill    With this funding  well ensure that the New York Botanical Garden remains at the forefront of botanical research  said Senator Schumer  These gorgeous gardens draw thousands of visitors every year and offer a much needed oasis for the entire community   This funding is great news for the New York Botanic Gardens and will provide an essential tool for scientists and researchers  said Senator Clinton  These gardens not only offer beauty and splendor for New Yorkers and people from all over the world  they are a living laboratory  foster research and study into everything from conservation to molecular biology     500 000 will go towards software tools for the Virtual Herbarium  a data set of electronically accessible information pertaining to thousands of plant specimens  The software tools not only catalog the 7 million plant specimens  it also makes them electronically accessible for research and offers high quality digital images  Additional software will give scientists and researchers greater access to the herbarium collection free of charge    Yesterday  the Appropriations Bill for Labor  Health and Human Services and Education passed the Appropriations Committees and will now head to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  The bill will then proceed to the Conference Committee and finally will be sent to the President before becoming law
