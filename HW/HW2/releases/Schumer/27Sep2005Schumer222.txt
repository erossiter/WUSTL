IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 27  2005 Schumer Announces Over  218 000 To Auburn Elementary Schools                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Federal Funds will be used to Establish and Support Counseling Programs in  Local Elementary Schools U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the Auburn Enlarged City School District will receive  218 554 in federal funds from the United States Department of Education  s Elementary and Secondary School Counseling Demonstration Program  The grant will be used to support counseling programs in target elementary schools        It is crucial that children in their most formidable years have access to counseling and developmental programs     Schumer said    This funding will help Auburn elementary schools provide their students with the best possible education by improving their experience and environment and leading them to a lifetime of success        Auburn schools will establish or expand counseling programs through hiring school social workers  school counselors  and school or adolescent psychiatrists  With the new personnel  the schools will expand the availability  quality and quantity of available counseling services  The programs will use a developmental  prevention approach and will be designed and applied with the participation of parents of the students      To achieve program goals and address the Government Performance and Results Act measures  the schools will attend to the ratio of student to mental health professional  and will reduce the number of disciplinary referrals and suspensions among participating students  Local schools have identified other specific goals that include improved student attendance and academic performance  social skills development  parental involvement  counselor and teacher professional development  and collaboration with community based organizations that provide mental health and other services to students     The Elementary and Secondary School Counseling Program is an initiative from the United States Department of Education  The program provides support to local educational agencies to establish or expand elementary and secondary school counseling programs
