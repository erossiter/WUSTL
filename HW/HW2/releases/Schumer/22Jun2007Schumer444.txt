IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 22  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Approves  900 000 For LI Cancer Research                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Senators Worked Closely with Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill   Federal Funding Will Be Used at Cold Spring Harbor Lab on LI to Conduct Women s Cancer Research   Bill will now be sent to the Senate Floor                                                                                                                                                                      Senators Charles Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the full Senate Appropriations Committee approved  900 000 in federal funding for the Cold Spring Harbor Laboratorys Womens Cancer Genomic Center  The funding will be used to continue research to find effective diagnostic and therapeutic tools for treating womens cancer under the FY08 Labor  Health and Human Services  Education  and Related Agencies Appropriations Bill   The medical community has made impressive progress in the fight against womens cancer  but theres still much more to be done to treat these illnesses that devastate so many families  Schumer said  The Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory has already made important strides in bettering our understanding of how and when breast cancer starts  and how we can treat it  This funding will be integral to their continued success  and with time could save an untold number of lives   If an ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure  then investments like this in breast cancer research  treatment  and care are worth their weight in gold to women  said Senator Clinton  Every one of us has been affected by breast cancer  whether it is through our own personal battle  or our experiences offering love and support to our friends  our mothers  our sisters  Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory is helping the scientific community to gain a better understanding of the causes of this disease and ways to treat it  I am proud to support federal investments like these so that we can offer more hope to women whose lives have been turned upside down by a breast cancer diagnosis and we can help reduce the number of women with that diagnosis in the first place   The  900 000 in federal funds will be used to develop a DNA biopsy for breast cancer that will allow physicians to make more accurate prognoses  and determine the most appropriate treatment recommendations for their patients  The CSHL Womens Cancer Genomics Center is already employing a powerful DNA chip technology developed at CSHL known as ROMA  which scans the genome for copy number changes in DNA that lead to cancer  This funding will allow the scientists at the Center to continue their genetic analyses on tumor samples from a varied group of women and to analyze samples of early breast lesions to increase our ability to diagnose and treat breast cancer   Today  the FY08 Appropriations Bill for Labor  Health and Human Services  Education  and Related Agencies passed the Appropriations Committees and will now head to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  The bill will then proceed to the Conference Committee and finally will be sent to the President before becoming law
