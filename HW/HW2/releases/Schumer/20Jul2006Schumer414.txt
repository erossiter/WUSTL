                                North West Shore Light Rail System would Unclog Borough  s Notoriously Clogged Traffic Arteries   Funds will go toward Alternative Analysis of the North West Shore Rail   Senators Aggressively Lobbied Senate Appropriations Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill Washington  DC    Today  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton announced that a key Senate panel approved  3 million for the Staten Island North West Shore Rail Plan Study  The funding  included in the Fiscal Year 2007 Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary  and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations Bill  will go towards developing a North Shore rail link to ease Staten Island  s notorious traffic  The bill will now proceed to the Senate floor for consideration        Because it is the fastest growing county in the state  Staten Islanders have the longest commutes and suffer from some of the worst traffic congestion in New York City     said Senator Schumer    Staten Islanders deserve better options for their commute  and this study is a step in that direction          Staten Islanders know how difficult traffic conditions can be at the best of times and with more and more people discovering how wonderful it is to live on the Island  we need to address the congestion problems as soon as possible  This funding will be a tremendous boost to the North Shore rail link project and to our combined efforts to ease congestion on the Island     Senator Clinton said     A North Shore rail link will offer crucial transportation infrastructure in the form of a 5 1 mile commuter rail link between the Arlington section of Mariners Harbor and the St  George ferry terminal  The new light rail route would make 6 stops and utilize an existing Right of Way once utilized by the North Shore freight rail line that runs parallel to Richmond Terrace  Richmond County  the fastest growing county in the state between 1990 2000  is plagued by an overburdened transportation system that hinders local economic growth and subjects borough residents to onerous commutes  Staten Islanders have the longest average commute in the nation  averaging 43 8 minutes
