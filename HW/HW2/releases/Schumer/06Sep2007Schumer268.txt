IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 6  2007 Schumer Announces Over  525 000 To Cornell University For Development Of Body Armor For Law Enforcement                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Federal Funds Will Be Used To Aid in Body Armor Design and Development                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  527 546 has been awarded to Cornell University in Ithaca  New York by the U S  Department of Justices  DOJ  National Institute of Justice  NIJ  under the Body Armor Solicitation Program  The federal funds will be used to develop a computer based tool to aid in designing body armor with the lowest possible weight and highest comfort for a required level of ballistic protection   This is great news for Cornell University and Ithaca  said Schumer  When it comes to crime prevention and control  we need our law enforcement agencies to be equipped with the most cutting edge technology  This funding will ensure first rate forensic science services that officials can use to solve crimes  making the city a safer place for everyone   Cornell University will use these federal funds to develop a computer based tool to aid in designing body armor with the lowest possible weight and highest comfort for a required level of ballistic protection  Their extensive research on body armor advanced designs will lead to the potential development of tools and technologies for use by local law enforcement agencies in order to better protect law enforcement officers   The Body Armor Solicitation Program funds proposals to develop  test  evaluate and demonstrate technologies in the area of soft body armor  as used in law enforcement and corrections  Specific areas of interest include  advanced ballistic resistant materials  nondestructive evaluation of soft body armor  life cycle of exposure protocols and equipment for ballistic resistant body armor and cooling technologies for wearers of body armor
