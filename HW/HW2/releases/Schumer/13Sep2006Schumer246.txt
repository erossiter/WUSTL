IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 13  2006 Schumer Announces Over  370 000 Coming To Odyssey Research Associates In Ithaca                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Federal Dollars Will Be Used To Develop New Crime Research Tools                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  370 121 has been awarded to Odyssey Research Associates  Inc in Ithaca  by the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  National Institute of Justice  NIJ  under the Electronic Crime Research and Development Solicitation  ECRDS  program   The federal funds will be used to develop a new tool for crime research         This is great news for Ithaca     said Schumer     New crime solving technology will give Ithaca law enforcement the tools they need to reduce crime and get dangerous criminals off the streets  making all of Tompkins County a safer place to live         Odyssey Research Associates  Inc  will use federal funds to develop an extensible tool  File Marshall  to automatically gather  in a forensically sound way  all of the files related to the Peer to Peer  P2P  usage on a target computer   File Marshall will show the investigator the files that have been downloaded from a P2P network  as well as related information such as the P2P servers used       The Electronic Crime Partnership Initiative  ECPI   who sponsors the ECRDS program  is comprised of Electronic Crime Subject Matter Experts representing Law Enforcement  Academia and the Private Sector from across the Country  working in cooperation on projects and programs to improve the capacity of State and Local Law Enforcement to effectively address Electronic Crime
