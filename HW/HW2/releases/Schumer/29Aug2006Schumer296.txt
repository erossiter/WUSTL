IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 29  2006 Schumer Announces Over  766 000 City Of Buffalo From The Department Of Justice                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   City Will Use The Federal Funds To Encourage Arrest Policies Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  766 150 has been awarded to the City of Buffalo by the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  Office on Violence Against Women under Grants to Encourage Arrest Policies Program   The federal funds will be used to implement certain provisions of the Violence Against Women Act  VAWA  Buffalo and Erie County       This is great news for Buffalo     said Schumer     Domestic violence is an awful problem in our communities  Battered women should never have to think twice about whether or not to report their abusers  and creating comprehensive legal services to secure victims   safety must be top priority  These dollars will protect hundreds of women by ensuring law enforcement have the tools they need  and I will fight hard to increase funding to such programs        The Grants to Encourage Arrest Policies and Enforcement Protection Order Program  Arrest  implements provisions of the VAWA  which was enacted in 1994 as Title IV of the Violent Crime Control and Law Enforcement Act of 1994 and reauthorized in the VAWA of 2000   The program enhances victim safety and offender accountability in case of domestic violence and dating violence by encouraging jurisdiction to implement mandatory and pro arrest policies as an effective domestic violence intervention that is part of a coordinated community response  An integral component of Arrest Program initiatives is the creation and enhancement of collaborative partnerships between criminal justice agencies  victim service providers  and community organizations which respond to domestic violence     BE SAFE  Buffalo and Erie County Stopping Abuse in the Family Environment  is a multi disciplinary cooperative effort of the Buffalo Police Department  Erie County District Attorney  s Office  the Eric County Sheriff  s Office  battered women  s shelter  victim service agencies and local judiciary to develop a coordinated community response to domestic violence  Through this continuation award  the project will provide specialized training to police officers and prosecutors on recognizing   investigating and prosecuting domestic violence case where the victim is from a traditionally under served population  an elderly victim or a person with disabilities  develop a tracking system at the Domestic Violence Court Central Intake Office to ensure communication between Criminal  Family and Supreme  Matrimonial  courts  and support a bilingual advocate to provide outreach  home and hospital visits  case management  counseling and court advocacy services for all Hispanic victims  continues and expand training of all local law enforcement agencies in accessing and utilizing Eric County and New York State Order of Protection Registries and support a full time Assistant District Attorney  criminal investigator and senior caseworker dedicated exclusively to the domestic violence unit      Schumer led the effort last year to reauthorize the 1994 Violence Against Women Act  VAWA   Schumer fought to strengthen the VAWA by expanding grant programs  supporting rural areas  and protecting victims  The original VAWA bill was authored by Schumer in the House of Representatives  This legislation will renew several successful programs and provides funding for training  education and outreach so that state and federal agencies can do a better job of preventing violence against women and assisting victims of domestic violence
