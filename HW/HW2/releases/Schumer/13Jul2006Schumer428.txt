IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 13  2006 Schumer Secures  1 Million For American Museum Of Natural History                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Senator Aggressively Lobbied Senate Appropriations Committee to Include  1 Million in Spending Bill   Funding will go toward Marine Research and Educating the Public on Oceans  Coasts  and Atmosphere in the Global Ecosystem Today  U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved  1 million funding for the Museum of Natural History  The money  included in the Fiscal Year 2007 Commerce  Justice and Science Appropriations bill  will fund marine research and raise awareness of the paramount role oceans and coasts play in the global ecosystem        One of greatest benefits of living in New York City is access to exceptional educational institutions like the Museum of Natural History     said Senator Schumer    This funding will help the Museum continue its passion of marine research and educating the public on the crucial role oceans and coasts play in our daily lives          The American Museum of Natural History  AMNH   established in 1869  continues to be a leader in the exploration and the advancement of natural sciences  In possession of 32 million specimens and artifacts  the AMNH boasts one of the greatest museum collections in the world  The AMNH is supported by more than 200 scientific personnel who work in curating  laboratory work  training  and collections management  AMNH also offers one of most diversified doctoral training programs which is connected to five universities  Yale  Cornell  Columbia  New York University and the City University of New York  The museum continues its exploration efforts by commissioning over 100 expeditions and field projects annually       Schumer had aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill  The  1 million in funding will be used to advance a partnership with NOAA  initiated in FY04  for a marine research and education initiative that will promote knowledge and public understand of the role oceans  coasts  and atmosphere in the global ecosystem        Today  the Appropriations Committee passed the Commerce  Justice and Science spending bill  The bill now heads to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  and then a House Senate conference committee will reconcile the differences between the versions before sending it to the President to become law
