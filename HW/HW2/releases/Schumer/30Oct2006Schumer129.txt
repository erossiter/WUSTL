IMMEDIATE RELEASE  October 30  2006 Schumer Announces NY  ers Can Apply For LIHEAP Funds Starting November 1st                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  Provides Federal Funds To Help Households Meet Home Energy Needs   Schumer Pledges To Fight For Additional Funding For Program This Winter   Schumer  Nobody Should Have To Win The Lottery In Order To Heat Their Home                                                                                                                     U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the application period for funding under the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  HEAP  will open on November 1st   Under this program  low income New Yorkers will have the opportunity to receive federal financial assistance to assist in payments for winter home heating costs   Schumer vowed to fight for additional funding for the program this winter      Nobody should have to win the lottery in order to heat their home     Schumer said     Everyone should apply early for this program  and I will fight hard to ensure that there are enough resources available for all families to heat their homes   With oil prices constantly fluctuating  the Home Energy Assistance Program is as important now more than ever   This assistance ensures that families and seniors will not have to worry about staying warm throughout the long winter season       Applicants may obtain and submit applications to their county Department of Social Services HEAP Office for eligibility determination  Contact information for county Department of Social Services offices may be obtained from the list of HEAP contacts in each county or the Blue Pages of the telephone directory   For additional information on this program or further questions  applicants are encouraged to contact the HEAP Hotline at 1 800 342 3009     Schumer has been a leader of a bipartisan push to expand LIHEAP funding in Congress   Last winter Schumer led a bipartisan campaign for an additional  2 92 billion for the Low Income Heating Assistance Program  LIHEAP  as part of an emergency spending bill   Schumer joined a coalition of Democratic and Republican Senators urging the Senate Leadership to take action on the emergency designation funding   Schumer has previously co sponsored a bipartisan Senate measure to provide  2 92 billion for LIHEAP offset by placing a tax on the windfall profits of integrated oil companies
