IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 13  2005 Schumer Announces  200 000 For Simon Wiesenthal Center In NYC                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Funds will be used for Simon Wiesenthal New York Tolerance Center Senator Charles E  Schumer announced today that the Department of Housing and Urban Development has approved  213 732 for the New York Tolerance Center at the Simon Wiesenthal Center  The funding will be used to help with facility renovation and maintenance         The New York Tolerance Center provides great tools and resources   Schumer said   Especially in New York  where our communities are so diverse  this center really helps to promote cooperation  empathy and understanding  and I am pleased to announce funding to help the Center          The New York Tolerance Center  which is modeled after the successful Tools for Tolerance     Program at the Museum of Tolerance in Los Angeles  offers one and two day seminars to uniformed and civilian personnel in all law enforcement fields  In addition to law enforcement officers  participants range from educators to public service professionals and corporate professionals  Utilizing interactive workshops  exhibits  and videos  the New York Tolerance Center helps participants explore issues of prejudice  diversity  tolerance  and cooperation in the workplace and in the community  The facilities includes state of the art exhibits  a multimedia theater  and classroom space       The Simon Wiesenthal Center is an international Jewish human rights organization dedicated to preserving the memory of the Holocaust by promoting tolerance and understanding through community involvement  educational programming  and activism  The Center addresses important contemporary issues including racism  antisemitism  terrorism  and genocide  It has a membership of over 400 000 families  and is headquartered in Los Angeles  with offices in New York  Toronto  Miami  Jerusalem  Paris  and Buenos Aires
