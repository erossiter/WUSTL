IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 13  2007 Schumer Announces Over  320 000 Coming To City Of Rochester To Expand Pathways To Peace Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  City of Rochester will use Funds to Expand Pathways to Peace Program  Focuses on Reducing Violence Among Inner City Youth by Finding Alternatives to Violent Behavior                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  324 143 has been awarded to the City of Rochester by the U S  Department of Justices  DOJ  Bureau of Justice Assistance  BJA  under the FY 07 Targeting Violent Crime Initiative  The federal funds will be used to expand Pathways to Peace  a city program dedicated to reducing violence among inner city youth by finding alternatives to violent behavior through outreach efforts  home visits and the utilization of multiple community resources   This is great news for Rochester  said Schumer  We must confront head on the serious problem of violence among our inner city youth  Programs like Pathways to Peace do an outstanding job of utilizing resources in the community to reach out to these young people and guide them away from a life of violence and crime  I will continue to fight for these much needed funds to keep our children off the streets and our families and communities safe   The City of Rochester will use these federal funds to expand Pathways to Peace  a city program dedicated to reducing violence among inner city youth by finding alternatives to violent behavior through outreach efforts  home visits and the utilization of multiple community resources  This combined effort serves to stem the growing number of gangs and gang members on city streets  Through the award  the City will hire Youth Intervention Specialists assigned to community outreach and data collection that will be shared with partnering agencies in the identification of gang activity   BJA s Targeting Violent Crime Initiative assists jurisdictions in responding to violent crime by expanding the use of intelligence led policing among state  local  and tribal agencies as they collect and analyze information to identify threats  develop tactics and partnerships to respond to violent crime  and then measure the effectiveness of their responses  A major objective of this initiative is to establish data driven  multi jurisdictional responses to violent crime   The Bureau of Justice Assistance  BJA  supports law enforcement  courts  corrections  treatment  victim services  technology  and prevention initiatives that strengthen the nations criminal justice system  BJA provides leadership  services  and funding to Americas communities by emphasizing local control  building relationships in the field  developing collaborations and partnerships  promoting capacity building through planning  streamlining the administration of grants  increasing training and technical assistance  creating accountability of projects  encouraging innovation  and ultimately communicating the value of justice efforts to decision makers at every level
