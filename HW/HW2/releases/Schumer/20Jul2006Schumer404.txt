IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 20  2006 Schumer Announces Key Senate Panel Approves  500 000 For Cvph Medical Center In Plattsburgh                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Senator Aggressively Lobbied Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill  The Federal Funding Will Enable The Construction And Expansion of Laboratories And A New Surgical Services Department U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee is expected to approve later today  500 000 under the FY07 Labor  Health and Human Services and Education Appropriations Bill for the CPVH Medical Center in Plattsburgh  The bill must now be approved by the House Senate Conference Committee before being signed into law by the President  Schumer aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill       This is great news for Plattsburgh     said Schumer    CVPH is a sophisticated  advanced medical center and a valuable resource in North Country  In order to continue providing top notch care  CVPH must stay on the cutting edge of medical science  These federal funds will help CVPH continue to be a center of both high technology and warm care        CVPH is a medical center that provides a full range of health care services to Clinton  Franklin  Essex and St  Lawrence counties  CVPH is one of only two rural open heart and angioplasty centers in New York State  This federal funding will allow CVPH to expand its angioplasty and cardiac catheterization laboratories and open heart surgery suite in addition to the construction of a new ten OR surgical services department     Later today  the Appropriations Bill for Transportation  Treasure  the Judiciary  Housing  and Urban Development is expected to pass the Appropriations Committee and will head to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  The bill will then proceed to the Conference Committee and finally will be sent to the President before becoming law
