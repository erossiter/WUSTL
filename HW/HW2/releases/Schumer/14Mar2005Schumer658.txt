                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Schumer Secured  10 million to Build Syracuse University Global Affairs Institute US Senator Charles E  Schumer attended the dedication of the Daniel Patrick Moynihan Institute of Global Affairs at Syracuse University today  Schumer secured  10 million for the new state of the art global affairs center  the final  5 million installment of which was approved last year as part of the Omnibus Appropriations Bill  Schumer said that the institute will attract the world s best professors to the school and could be a stimulus to help attract people to the region         This is such a great day for Syracuse  and for the entire country     Schumer said    I was proud to work with my colleagues to get the  10 million from the federal government to give Senator Moynihan the legacy he deserves and support the causes that were so close to his heart  The Maxwell School is the ideal site to carry on Senator Moynihan s work because of its commitment to scholarship and its pursuit of excellence       Schumer secured a  10 million endowment for the new state of the art global affairs institute from the federal government  In 2003  Schumer obtained  5 million in budget process and last year secured another  5 million in the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations Bill       The Maxwell School s Global Affairs Institute was established in 1993 to extend  integrate  and focus the Maxwell School s commitment to exploring current international and global concerns  The Institute organizes conferences  credit bearing seminars  and workshops  and provides research fellowships and internship opportunities to graduate students  Daniel Patrick Moynihan  a one time junior faculty member at the Maxwell School from 1959 to 1961  was elected to the United States Senate in 1976 where he served four terms  He retired from the Senate in 2000 renowned as a foreign affairs expert  The Almanac of American Politics once described him as  the nation s best thinker among politicians since Lincoln and its best politician among thinkers since Jefferson
