IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 17  2005 Schumer Gets Restoration Of  2 5 Million For Montauk To Fire Island Reformulation Study In Budget                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Senator Aggressively Lobbied Senate Appropriations Committee to Include Funding In Spending Bill      Schumer  Fire Island to Montauk Point Reformulation Vitally Important to the Community and Economic Health on South Shore of Long Island US Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved  2 5 million under the FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations Act for the Fire Island to Montauk Point reformulation Study  President Bush  s fiscal year 2006 budget proposal zeroed out all funding for the FIMP study  However  after working with his colleagues on both sides of the aisle  Schumer was able to get  2 5 million included for the FIMP study in the FY  06 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill  The bill must now be approved by the full Senate before being sent to a House and Senate Conference Committee and eventually being signed into law  Schumer had aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill         The Fire Island to Montauk Point Reformulation Study is critically important to the south shore of Long Island and it needed a final push to ensure that it will be completed  this money means that we can finish what we started     said Schumer      The goal of the reformulation study is to identify and evaluate alternative methods of providing the authorized beach erosion control and hurricane protection for the south shore of Long Island between Fire Island Inlet and Montauk Point  Scheduled for completion in 2006  the project provides for hurricane protection and beach erosion control along five reaches of the south shore of Long Island  a distance of approximately 83 miles and comprising about 70 percent of the total ocean frontage of Long Island       The authorized project includes widening of the beaches along the developed areas between Kismet and Mecox Bay  raising of dunes from Fire Island Inlet to Hither Hills State Park  and at Montauk and opposite Lake Montauk Harbor by artificial placement of suitable sand  grass planting of the dunes and interior drainage structures at Mecox Bay  Sagaponack Lake and Georgica Pond  The project also authorizes federal participation in periodic nourishment       Today  the Appropriations Committee included  2 5 million in the Energy and Water spending bill  The bill was passed by the Appropriations Committee today and now heads to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  A vote is expected this summer  and after final passage a conference committee will reconcile the differences between the House and Senate versions of the Energy and Water Appropriations Bill before sending it to the President to become law
