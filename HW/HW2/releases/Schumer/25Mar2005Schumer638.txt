IMMEDIATE RELEASE  March 25  2005 Schumer  Over  68 000 Coming To Central New York Area Health Education Center In Cortland                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Funds will be used for a New Webs Based In Service Training for Health Care Workers and Health Care Professional Students US Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  68 605 in federal funds was awarded to the Central New York Area Health Education Center in Cortland to assist in establishing a centralized  dynamically driven website for annual in service training as a service to health care facilities and health professional schools  The funds are being awarded through the Appalachian Regional Commission  ARC         This is great news for health care professionals and students in Central New York   Schumer said   In service training is vital to health care services  and the entire region will benefit from these improvements        The Central New York Area Health Education Center will host a website for annual in service training in collaboration with partnering hospitals  health departments  long term care facilities  and health professions schools  The project will have four main components  curriculum development and standardization  website development allowing for customization between facilities  training and technical assistance  and tracking and evaluation  This program will aid hospitals and other agencies in seeing that their staffs meet all New York State requirements for annual training and recertification in a cost effective and efficient manner  The ARC funds specifically will support a project coordinator  a server  and one computer dedicated to in service training at each of six pilot sites  After piloting  the program will be made available to all providers in the Health Education Center s 14 county service area and possibly throughout the state       The ARC is a federal state partnership that works with the people of Appalachia to create opportunities for self sustaining economic development and improved quality of life  The ARC awards grants from funds appropriated to the Commission annually by Congress
