IMMEDIATE RELEASE  October 11  2006 Schumer Announces Over  814 000 In Federal Funds Going To Suny College Of Environmental Science And Forestry                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Federal Funds Will Be Used To Research The Use Of Willow Biomass As An Alternative Energy Source                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  813 450 has been awarded to SUNY College of Environmental Science and Forestry  ESF   located in Syracuse  by the U S  Department of Energy  DOE  and the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  under the Biomass Research and Development Initiative program   Funds will be used to research the potential uses of Willow biomass as an alternative energy source   In July 2006  Schumer wrote a personal letter to the DOE on behalf of SUNY ESF to support their application for this grant        This is great news for SUNY College of Environmental Science and Forestry   Schumer said   Alternative energy sources are essential in reducing our dependence on foreign oil  With rising costs of gasoline continuing to affect the average consumer  the need for alternative energy resources is more important now than ever before          SUNY ESF will use federal funds to research potential uses of willow biomass as an alternative energy source   Researchers will try to determine if using high energy radiation employing commercially proven electron beam technology can enhance the quantity of sugars extracted from mixed northern hardwoods and the shrub willow energy crop to be used for biomass conversion and fermentation to transportation fuels  chemicals and biodegradable polymers   Results will then be assessed to determine whether woody biomass might be a practical alternative energy source   SUNY ESF has been a leading university in environmental research for over 95 years  Since 1983  more than 83 patents have been issued to ESF faculty and students as a result of their innovative and important discoveries         Under the Biomass Research and Development Initiative  a joint USDA DOE effort established in 2000 and reauthorized in the comprehensive Energy Policy Act of 2005  award projects must demonstrate collaboration among experts in the field of biomass   The Initiative aims to enhance creative approaches in developing next generation advanced technologies  and promote research partnerships among colleges  universities  national laboratories  federal and state research agencies and the private sector   These projects are designed to address one of the greatest challenges facing the world  increasing the availability of clean and affordable energy to reduce global dependence on fossil fuels         In July 2006  Schumer wrote to the DOE on behalf of SUNY ESF to secure this research grant   In a personal letter to Samuel W  Bodman  Secretary for the U S  Department of Energy  Schumer wrote   This  research  will aid in the economic vitality of the farm and forest communities  enhance renewable energy options and contribute directly to energy independence and national security   I applaud SUNY ESF for its foresight
