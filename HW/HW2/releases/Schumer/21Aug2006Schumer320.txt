IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 21  2006 Schumer Announces Federal Dollars For The Sheridan Fire District                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Funds Will Be Used For Fire Hall Construction And Improvements                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced a  30 000 grant and a  1 165 300 loan to the Sheridan Fire District in Chautauqua County for fire hall construction and improvements   The funds are being awarded through the United States Department of Agriculture s  USDA  Rural Development program as a Community Facility Loan and Grant        This is great news for the Sheridan Fire District   Schumer said   Fire safety is essential to a community and we need to ensure that firefighters have the facilities and the equipment to do their job well   This new fire hall will be a huge improvement for the Town of Sheridan and the surrounding community            The Sheridan Fire District will use federal dollars to construct a new fire hall to accommodate the equipment they have  to address the handicapped accessibility issues and to correct and improve septic violations   The current facility is over 60 years old and is not large enough to accommodate the number of emergency vehicles needed   They are also operating with inadequate water and have sanitation problems   The new facility will serve over 2 800 people         The USDA Rural Development Community Facility Loan and Grant program administers programs designed to develop essential community facilities for public use in rural areas  These facilities include schools  libraries  childcare  hospitals  medical clinics  assisted living facilities  fire and rescue stations  police stations  community centers  public buildings and transportation  Through its Community Programs  the Department of Agriculture is striving to ensure that such facilities are readily available to all rural communities
