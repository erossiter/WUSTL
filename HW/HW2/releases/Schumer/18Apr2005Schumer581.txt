IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 18  2005 Schumer Joins Buffalo Business Leaders To Discuss Alternatives To Passport Rule That Take Into Account Security And WNY Business Interests                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       President Has Said He Is Open To New Ideas  Schumer To Bring Ideas Back to President During Public Comment Period and Advocate for WNY  s Businesses  Peace Bridge    New Citizenship and Immigration Services Proposal Would Require Passports at US Canada Border US Senator Charles E  Schumer today joined Buffalo business leaders to discuss the local impact of a new proposal to require passports at the US Canada border  Speaking with Peace Bridge officials  Schumer detailed the importance of securing our border  and heard from community leaders concerned with the economic impact of requiring passports to travel to or from Canada and the US  Schumer specifically addressed how progress with the Peace Bridge could be reversed if new proposal is approved as it currently stands       This plan was poorly thought out and showed no understanding of how the border works in Western New York  where people cross it all the time for work  shopping  and leisure     Schumer said    The President is giving it a second look  which is a good first step  I have already raised this issue with top officials at the Department of Homeland Security and will bring back all of these ideas discussed today so that we can have both security and efficient commerce        Schumer today emphasized that national security is always his top priority  but that the federal government must protect the homeland without crippling cross border commerce or the travel and tourism industry  A final plan must address the concerns of businesses leaders and the law enforcement community in Western New York  where the economy is dependent upon Canadian trade and commerce  According to the Canadian Consulate  Canada is New York  s largest trading partner and  360 million of trade goes through Buffalo area everyday     On April 5  2005  the Departments of State and Homeland Security announced a proposed rule that would require all U S  and Canadian citizens to have a passport or other accepted secure document to enter or re enter the U S  by January 1  2008  The proposal refers to passports as the   preferred document of choice     Following a sixty day public comment period  a formal rule will be issued     Schumer also presided over the dismantling the toll facilities on the US side  which are being moved to Canada as the first physical step toward the implementation of Shared Border Management  Schumer said that everything possible should be done to ensure the border crossing receives the maximum benefit of the shared border agreement  both making commerce more efficient and the border more secure
