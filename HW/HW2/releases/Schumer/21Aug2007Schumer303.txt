IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 21  2007 Schumer Announces Over  30 000 For Rockland County Crime Control And Prevention Programs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Village Of Spring Valley and Clarkstown Will Use Federal Funds for Police Overtime Compensation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  30 363 has been awarded to the Village of Spring Valley to be shared with Clarkstown by the U S  Department of Justices Office of Justice Programs  OJP  under the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  The federal funds will be used by the Village of Spring Valley and Clarkstown to fund police overtime programs   When it comes to protecting our citizens  we need to be tough on crime and smart on prevention  Schumer said  These much needed funds will help support a dependable police force that allows families in Rockland County to live safely and comfortably   The Village of Spring Valley will use  16 234 of the federal funding for overtime to deploy additional officers within the community at targeted areas to handle violent crime issues and trends  The Town of Clarkstown will use the remaining  14 129 for overtime to continue the Clarkstown Police Departments Operation Safe Shopper Program  This program deploys additional manpower to the Palisades Center Mall during peak operating hours of the holiday season   The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime based on their own local needs and conditions  Grant funds can be used for state and local initiatives  technical assistance  training  personnel  equipment  supplies  contractual support  and information systems for criminal justice for any one or more of the following purpose areas  1  law enforcement programs  2  prosecution and court programs  3  prevention and education programs  4  corrections and community corrections programs  5  drug treatment programs  and 6  planning  evaluation  and technology improvement programs
