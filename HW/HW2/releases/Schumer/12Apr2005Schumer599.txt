IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 12  2005 Schumer Pushes FEMA To Speed Up Damage Assessments For Fourteen New York Counties And Send Federal Aid As Soon As Possible                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Schumer Calls Top FEMA Official for Individual and Public Assistance for Impacted Counties  Broome  Chenango  Cortland  Delaware  Greene  Montgomery  Orange  Otsego  Rensselaer  Schenectady  Schoharie  Sullivan  Tioga  Ulster Counties Suffered Severe Damage  Schumer Pushed State to Declare Disaster  Now Urges Feds To Grant State  s Request Quickly US Senator Charles E  Schumer today called Michael D  Brown  Under Secretary of Homeland Security for Emergency Preparedness and Response at the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  and urged him to quickly complete preliminary damage assessments  PDAs  so that Governor George Pataki may request federal disaster assistance for fourteen New York Counties and expedite federal assistance for individuals and municipalities suffering losses  Last week  Governor Pataki declared Broome  Chenango  Cortland  Delaware  Greene  Montgomery  Orange  Otsego  Rensselaer  Schenectady  Schoharie  Sullivan  Tioga  and Ulster counties in states of emergency and is now seeking federal aid from FEMA  Brown was receptive to Schumer  s request and hoped to have the damage assessment reports completed shortly       The bottom line is that those who have lost their homes and suffered severe damages cannot afford to wait much longer     Schumer said    I called Secretary Brown because we must do everything we can to get federal dollars to these areas as soon as possible  This is the only way residents and counties impacted by the storms will be able to get back on their feet         In a personal phone call today  Schumer urged Secretary Brown to expeditiously grant Governor Pataki  s request for federal disaster assistance and speed up their PDAs to estimate the extent of the disaster and its impact on individuals and public facilities  Last week  Schumer urged the State to declare the region a disaster area  a prerequisite for obtaining FEMA funds  Now that the State has declared the area a disaster  FEMA will determine if the area is eligible for funds and how much relief it will get     Thunderstorms that ripped though New York April 2nd 3rd dumped many inches of rainfall in just two days  Broome  Chenango  Cortland  Delaware  Greene  Montgomery  Orange  Otsego  Rensselaer  Schenectady  Schoharie  Sullivan  Tioga  and Ulster counties sustained the most severe damage as a result of wind and floods  These counties now face repairs and restoration of hundreds of homes as well as numerous roads  culverts  bridges  ditches  and streams     Reported rainfall amounts from across the state averaged 1 3 inches with much higher totals of 2 4 inches in western New York and 3 6 inches in the Catskill region  As rain swollen rivers crested and overflowed  roads were closed and hundreds of residents were forced to evacuate their homes  Hundreds of homes have been condemned     The state is currently seeking aid from the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  for disaster assistance for the following categories of aid  1  Individual Assistance for citizens who suffered losses  including the Individual and Households program  disaster unemployment insurance  and Small Business Administration loans  and 2  Public Assistance to reimburse communities for debris removal  emergency response efforts and restoration of public infrastructure to counties in New York State
