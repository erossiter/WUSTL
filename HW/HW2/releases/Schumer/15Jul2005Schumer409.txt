IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 15  2005 Schumer  Over  137 000 in Federal Dollars Coming For Gateway Harbor Riverfront Park                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Funds will be Used to Expand Facilities at the Waterfront in North Tonawanda                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     US Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that a  137 375 grant was awarded to the City of North Tonawanda for the Gateway Harbor Riverfront Park  The funds are being awarded through the National Park Service s Land and Water Conservation Fund         This is great news for the Tonawanda Riverfront   Schumer said   It is important to preserve this park while expanding and enhancing the facilities available for families throughout Western New York  I will continue to fight for funds for projects like this        In response to the oversubscribed existing docking facilities as Gateway Harbor Park  the City of North Tonawanda is expanding facilities at the waterfront park  Completed in 1999  Gateway Harbor Park  located at the western gateway to the state  s 524 mile canal system  features a waterfront esplanade  transient docking facilities  a pavilion  benches  light and parking facilities  Existing docking facilities are inadequate to handle the increasing volume of boat traffic experienced on the canal and the Niagara River  The City will install a 600 l f  floating dock attachment system along the Sweeney Street canal wall between Main and Oliver Streets  a handicapped accessible gangway at Gateway Harbor Park  fourteen 6 foot wide floating finger docks perpendicular to the canal wall  and boater service pedestals with electric and portable water service       The National Park Service  s Land and Water Conservation Fund is a partnership program that provides matching funds to states and local communities for acquisition  development  and conservation of parks and open space
