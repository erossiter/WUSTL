                                      Schumer Backs Amendment Designating  5 million to Help Federal Health Officials Prevent Further Spread of Staph Drug Resistant Staph  MRSA  Now Blamed for Over 90 000 Infections  18 500 Deaths Every Year More Cases Found in NYC Metro Area Today Today U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced he is backingan amendment that would devote  5 million in emergency funding to help the Agency for Health Research Quality  AHRQ  prevent the spread of the deadly staph infection now blamed for over 90 000 infections and 18 500 deaths annually  At least two high school students in Connecticut have been diagnosed with the superbug MRSA  They are the latest in a series of people nationwide to contract the staph infection that is resistant to antibiotics   Without immediate action to combat this superbug  more and more kids could get sick  Schumer said  This dangerous and incurable superbug has jumped out of hospitals and in to our communities  and we must do everything we can to stop further exposure  This bacteria has evolved to survive the drugs weve invented  so we must evolve quickly to fight back  by fighting the spread through vigilance  sanitation and aggressive investment in research and innovation   MRSA  a deadly strain of drug resistant staph  was believed to primarily be a concern in health care settings and hospitals until reports this week that the bacteria had spread across the nations schools  infecting dozens and killing one student in Virginia  The bacteria  which generally reside in the nose or on the skin of about a third of the population  can be deadly if allowed to penetrate the skin or enter the blood stream  Schools are particularly prone to outbreaks in gyms or locker rooms  where students share towels  razors or sports equipment    The news coincided with a Center for Disease Control report which found that the bacteria was responsible for approximately 18 650 deaths in the United States in 2005  a much larger number than previously expected    The Schumer backed amendment would designate an additional  5 million in the Fiscal Year 2008 Labor  Health  and Human Services appropriations bill  now being considered on the Senate floor
