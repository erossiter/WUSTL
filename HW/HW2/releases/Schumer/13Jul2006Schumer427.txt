IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 13  2006 Schumer Secures  600 000 To Restore Long Island Shellfish Population                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Senator Aggressively Lobbied Senate Appropriations Committee To Include  600 000 Funding In Spending Bill   Long Island Nature Conservancy Will Use Funding To Boost Shellfish in Waterways  Improve Water Quality and Seagrass Meadows  and Enrich Local Habitat for Hard Clams  Bay Scallops  and Other Fish and Crustaceans Today  U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved  600 000 in funding for the Long Island Nature Conservancy  The money  included in the Fiscal Year 2007 Commerce  Justice and Science Appropriations bill  will fund the improvement of Long Island  s bays by restoring seagrass meadows and improving the local water quality  These improvements  in turn  will enrich the local habitat for fish  shellfish  and crustaceans       Long Island has been blessed with a trove of natural treasures     said Senator Schumer    This funding will help The Nature Conservancy of Long Island preserve the pristine beauty of local bays and bolster the area  s finfish and shellfish populations        The  600 000 will support the Long Island Nature Conservancy  s efforts to restore the seagrass meadows which are vital habitats and food sources for fish  shellfish  and crustaceans  Since it  s annihilation in the 1980s by the Brown Tide  Long Island  s seagrass population has failed to fully recover and is blamed for the major declines in the area  s finfish and shellfish populations  The restoration of local seagrass meadows not only improves aquatic life  but also improves Long Island  s water quality  economy and overall quality of life     The Nature Conservancy of Long Island  NCLI  is devoted to protecting the island  s land  water  and farmland  The NCLI  s current initiative    Long Island  s Last Stand     is a 10 year project focused on rescuing uninhabited land and farmland and preserving local bays  harbors and public parks  The plan includes the preservation of 10 000 agricultural acres and 25 000 acres of uninhabited land  Supporters of   Long Island  s Last Stand    include farmers  residents  business leaders and conservation organizations     Schumer had aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill  The  600 000 will be used to improve the health of Long Island bays by restoring the water quality and sea grass  The restoration will also help the keystone species of hard clams and bay scallops  Funds will specifically include key components for each of these elements including collection and synthesis of baseline documentation  applied research and monitoring and the building of an estuarine stewardship ethic within the general public     Today  the Appropriations Committee passed the Commerce  Justice and Science spending bill  The bill now heads to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  and then a House Senate conference committee will reconcile the differences between the versions before sending it to the President to become law
