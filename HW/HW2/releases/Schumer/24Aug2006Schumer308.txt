IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 24  2006 Schumer Announces Over  200 000 Coming To Utica Neighborhood Housing Service Inc  For Crime Prevention And Policing Initiatives                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  DOJ  Weed And Seed  Program Provides Funds For Local Law Enforcement To  Weed Out  Criminals And Communities To  Seed In  Prevention  Intervention  And Treatment Services   Utica Neighborhood Housing Service Inc  Will Use Funds To Pay For Community Policing Initiatives                                                                                                                           U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  200 000 has been awarded to Utica Neighborhood Housing Service Inc  in Oneida County to reduce violent crime and drug activity through community policing initiatives   The funds are being awarded through the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  Weed and Seed Program        This is great news for the City of Utica     said Schumer     These funds are going toward a program that works to shut down violent criminals and reduce drug activity   Implementing community policing programs is not just a short term response  it provides long term solutions by focusing on prevention and community partnerships          Utica Neighborhood Housing Service Inc  will use federal dollars to continue to strengthen collaborative law enforcement efforts to reduce violent crime and drug activity within the target area   Community policing goals will improve the communications and positive interactions between the law enforcement agency and the residents of the target area through community activities and attendance at block association meetings   Prevention  intervention  and treatment goals include increased community services in education  leadership training  and services for at risk youth   Neighborhood Restoration goals include removing blight and providing homeownership opportunities   The Utica Weed and Seed site received Official Recognition in January 2002   The target area is bordered by Oriskany Street  Park Avenue  Broad Street  Kossuth Avenue  Rutger Street  St  Vincent Street  Eagle Street  Mohawk Street  Emily Street  Clementine Street  Pleasant Street  Steuben Street  James Street  Oneida Street  Sunset Avenue  Jewett Place  North South Arterial  and Burrstone Road       The Weed and Seed program works to prevent  reduce and control violent crime  drug abuse and gang activity in specifically targeted high crime areas across the country   Its name comes from a two part approach to these areas   One involves law enforcement agencies and prosecutors getting together to  weed out  individuals who participate in violent crime or drug abuse in an attempt to keep them out of the targeted area   The second part   seeding   includes bringing social services to the area in order to push for prevention  intervention and treatment  as well as neighborhood restoration  Since 1994  the Department of Justice has allocated at least  9 million annually nationwide for Weed and Seed related task forces administered through the Drug Enforcement Agency  the Federal Bureau of Investigation and other DOJ agencies
