IMMEDIATE RELEASE  December 7  2006 Clinton  Schumer Welcome Congressional Approval Of Bill To Name Post Office In Honor Of Tito Puente                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Vote paves the way for renaming of United States Postal Office at 167 East 124th Street in New York  NY as the  Tito Puente Post Office Building                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Washington D C    U S  Senators Hillary Rodham Clinton and Charles E  Schumer announced that legislation to honor Latin jazz music composer and musician Ernest Anthony   Tito    Puente was passed late yesterday by the United States Senate  H R 1472  which was sponsored in the House of Representatives by Congressman Charles Rangel  was approved by the House in September and was passed by the Senate unanimously late last night  It will now head to the President for his signature       Senators Clinton and Schumer welcomed the Senate  s unanimous approval of the bill after sending a letter to the Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Government Affairs earlier this year in support of the legislation  The bill allows for the United States Postal Office at 167 East 124th Street in New York  New York to be designated as the  Tito Puente Post Office Building          This was the right thing for the Senate to do and the fact that it was approved unanimously and around the 65th anniversary of the attack on Pearl Harbor is even more of a tribute to this modern musical innovator  hero and icon to so many  This post office naming will be a fitting honor for a man who not only contributed generously to American music but also contributed to the defense of our country as a decorated veteran of WWII     Senator Clinton said         For lovers of music worldwide  the Mambo King was true royalty     said Senator Schumer    Throughout his life  he carried the stirring and infectious rhythms of Spanish Harlem across not only New York but across the entire globe  This Post Office will ensure that Tito Puente continues to inspire musicians and New Yorkers alike for generations to come          Tito Puente authored over 400 compositions  and was the recipient of many awards  including six Grammys  the Smithsonian Medal and several honorary doctorates  He died in 2000 at the age of 73  In a career than spanned more than 50 years  Puente became a musical icon  popularizing Latin music in this country and around the world  later incorporating the jazz idiom   His creations have now become part of the great American musical tradition that includes Jazz  Country and Western  Blues and Blue Grass
