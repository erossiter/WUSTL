IMMEDIATE RELEASE  December 8  2005 Schumer Announces Over  67 000 for Arts Programs in Syracuse                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     National Endowment for the Arts Grants Will Support Creation of Original Music and Visual Arts   Schumer  Federal Funds Will Allow Artists   and Composers   Expression of Creativity U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  67 500 in federal funds for three Syracuse programs  Syracuse Children  s Chorus  Society for New Music  and Light Work Visual Studies will receive grants from the National Endowment for the Art  s  NEA  Access to Artistic Excellence program  This program aims to foster and preserve excellence in the arts and increase access to the arts         This is great news for Syracuse and art lovers throughout New York   Schumer said  AArtistic expression is a great freedom that we have in America  This money will go a long way to ensure that New Yorkers can enjoy both the process of creating art and the finished product          The Syracuse Children s Chorus  founded in 1981  teaches artistry and excellence through the study and performance of choral music  It will receive  7 500 to support the commission and performance of a new work by Zhou Long  The work will be performed by a children  s chorus and traditional Chinese instruments and will incorporate Chinese folk and mountain music and Chinese poetry       The Society for New Music has acted as a catalyst for the continued growth of the central New York musical community since 1971  The group will receive  10 000 to support concert performances and the commission and presentation of a work by composer Robert Morris       Light Work  founded in 1973  supports artists working in photography and digital imaging  The group will receive  50 000 to support an artist residency program and the publication of Contact Sheet  Light Work  s annual publication  Artists   work will also be made available on Light Work  s online database  a collection of over 2 100 pieces       Established by Congress in 1965 as an independent agency of the federal government  the NEA is dedicated to supporting excellence in the arts and bringing the arts to all Americans  Over 120 000 grants have brought art to all 50 states  including rural areas  inner cities and military bases and have generated an average of seven dollars for each dollar awarded  Grants provided during FY 2006 will help support projects that involve the creation and presentation of artistically excellent work
