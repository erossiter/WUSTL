IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 11  2007 Schumer Announces Over  120 000 To Syracuse University For Waste Management Training In Lake Ontario Communities                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Syracuse University will use Funds to Provide Technical Assistance and Training to Rural and Low Income Lake Ontario Communities in the Areas of Waste and Wastewater Management                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that a grant of  123 694 has been awarded to Syracuse University through the United States Department of Agriculture s  USDA  Technical Assistance Training Grant  Federal funds will be used to provide technical assistance and training to rural and low  income Lake Ontario communities in the areas of waste and wastewater management    This is great news for Syracuse University and the Lake Ontario communities  Schumer said  Waste management is critical to the upkeep of our communities and the health of local residents  These funds and the subsequent training will improve the quality of life for everyone in the area   Syracuse University will use these funds to provide technical assistance and training to rural and low income Lake Ontario communities in the areas of waste and wastewater management  This training will focus on efforts to establish working collaborations among government officials and nonprofit and private sector programs that provide technical assistance    USDAs Technical Assistance Training Grant Program works to identify and evaluate solutions to water and waste disposal problems in rural areas  The program also funds projects to improve the operation and maintenance of existing water and waste disposal facilities in rural areas
