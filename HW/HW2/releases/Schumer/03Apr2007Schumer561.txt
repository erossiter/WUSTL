IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 3  2007 Schumer Announces  80 000 In Federal Funds Going To Hamilton College                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Grant Will Be Used To Enhance Study Abroad Programs in China                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that an  80 000 grant was awarded to Hamilton College for their associate School in China by the U S  Department of Education  DOE  under the Fulbright Hays Group Projects Abroad Program  Federal dollars will be used for intensive language training and internship opportunities for students studying abroad in China         This is great news for Hamilton College   Schumer said   Access to internships abroad provides students with unique opportunities to broaden their cultural experience and develop new skills  Mastering an additional language will sharpen their communications skills and give them a competitive edge in their future endeavors          Federal funds will be used by the Hamilton College to enhance study abroad opportunities for their students in China   Twelve nationally selected students  who have already completed a semester or more of study abroad in China  will participate in a language intensive internship in China during the summer of 2007  Upon their return  these students will discuss their experiences in elementary and secondary school classrooms and introduce students to Chinese society and culture        The Fulbright Hays Group Project Abroad Program provides grants to support overseas projects in training  research  and curriculum development in modern foreign languages and area studies for teachers  students  and faculty engaged in a common endeavor  Projects may include short term seminars  curriculum development  group research or study  or advanced intensive language programs  Projects must focus on the humanities  social sciences and languages  and must focus on one or more of the following areas  Africa  East Asia  South Asia  Southeast Asia and the Pacific  the Western Hemisphere  Central and South America  Mexico  and the Caribbean   East Central Europe and Eurasia  and the Near East
