IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 23  2005 Schumer Announces Over  5 1 Million For Greater Binghamton Area Organizations In Housing Funds                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Grants awarded as part of the Community Development Block Grant  CDBG   Emergency Shelter Grant  ESG   and HOME Programs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  5 122 223 was awarded to organizations in Broome County by the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD   The federal funding comes from several programs  including  4 267 648 from the Community Development Block Grant  CDBG    103 326 from Emergency Shelter Grant  ESG  and  751 249 from HOME       Every New Yorker deserves the opportunity to have a home     Schumer said    With housing costs on the rise  these programs are essential to help hardworking families in Greater Binghamton establish homes within the community  I will continue to work hard to increase funding for housing opportunities in the area        The City of Binghamton will receive  2 670 244 in CDBG   103 326 in ESG and  751 249 in HOME funds  The Town of Union will receive  1 597 404 in HOME funds      The CDBG program provides annual grants to states and local governments to develop viable urban communities by providing housing and a suitable living environment  and by expanding economic opportunities  principally for low and moderate  income persons  This year  Senator Schumer supported the amendment to the budget which restored funding to the CDBG program  The amendment  introduced by Senator Norm Coleman  R MN   expresses the sense of the Senate that CDBG should be fully funded and remain with the Department of Housing and Urban Affairs     The ESG program provides annual grants to State  local and private entities to improve the quality and number of emergency homeless shelters      The HOME program helps to expand the supply of decent  affordable housing to low and very low income families by providing grants to state and local governments to fund housing programs that meet local needs and priorities      Housing and community revitalization are at the heart of HUD  s mission  and these grants support these goals  They hope to create more affordable housing and empower large and small communities across the country so that every citizen can live the American Dream
