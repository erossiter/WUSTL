IMMEDIATE RELEASE  December 8  2005 Schumer Announces  16 000 for Sculpture Space  Inc                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               National Endowment For The Arts Grant Will Support Sculptor Residency Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  16 000 for Sculpture Space  Inc  of Oneida County  Sculpture Space received the federal money through the National Endowment for the Art  s  NEA  Access to Artistic Excellence program  This program aims to foster and preserve excellence in the arts and increase access to the arts         This is great news for Utica   Schumer said    An investment in the arts is an investment in Utica  s future as a tourist destination and as a vibrant place to live  I will continue to fight hard for programs that support artistic growth and appreciation          Sculpture Space will use the federal funds to support a residency program for sculptors from outside New York State  The artists will have the opportunity to create new work during two month residencies at a former steam engine and boilerworks plant  Artists in residence are provided with space  equipment  technical assistance and specialized resources available in the Mohawk Valley to make sculpture on a scale which they otherwise might not be able to afford and in an environment conducive to experimentation       The program currently selects 20 artists per year for two month residencies  and has helped advance the careers of over 400 national and international artists since 1976  Four times a year the public is invited to a work in progress reception at the end of a residency period       Established by Congress in 1965 as an independent agency of the federal government  the NEA is dedicated to supporting excellence in the arts and bringing the arts to all Americans  Over 120 000 grants have brought art to all 50 states  including rural areas  inner cities and military bases and have generated an average of seven dollars for each dollar awarded  Grants provided during FY 2006 will help support projects that involve the creation and presentation of artistically excellent work
