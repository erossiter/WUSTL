                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Town of West Seneca Will Use Federal Funds to Enhance Emergency Response Team                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that a grant of  60 000 has been awarded to the town of West Seneca as AmeriProCorps funding from the Corporation for National and Community Service   Federal funds will be used to support AmeriProCorps volunteers as they recruit  train  and coordinate community volunteers in disaster preparedness        This is great news for West Seneca   said Schumer    All of our towns and cities need to be fully prepared to deal with emergencies  The AmeriProCorps program will give the people of West Seneca the resources they need to protect their community against disasters  We are grateful to AmeriCorps for their help in this vital initiative         The Town of West Seneca AmeriProCorps program will use this new federal funding to train community volunteers to serve as Second Responders in the event of disasters or emergencies  An AmeriProCorps Community Education Response Team will offer family preparedness training to the town       AmeriCorps members serve in hundreds of nonprofit organizations  public agencies  and community and faith based organizations  in rural and urban communities throughout the nation  Members mentor youth  build affordable housing  help communities respond to disaster and support community volunteer activities  Funding from AmeriCorps  State Competitive grants make it possible for existing programs to expand and enhance their existing services in partnership with AmeriCorps
