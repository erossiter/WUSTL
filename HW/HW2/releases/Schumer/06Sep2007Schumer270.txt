IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 6  2007 Schumer Announces Nearly  100 000 For Onondaga County Health Department For Forensic Science Improvments                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Federal Funds Will Be Used To Help Meet Information Management Needs U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  94 960 has been awarded to the Onondaga county Health Department in Syracuse  New York by the U S  Department of Justices  DOJ  National Institute of Justice  NIJ  under the Paul Coverdell Forensic Sciences Improvement Program  The federal funds will be used to update their database system  improve and centralize their information and help meet their information management needs  This is great news for Onondaga County  said Schumer  When it comes to protecting our citizens  all aspects of the justice system must be running efficiently and effectively  This funding will ensure first rate forensic science services that officials can use to solve crimes  making the city a safer place for everyone   The Onondaga County Health Department will use these federal funds to update their database system  improve and centralize information and help meet all of their information management needs   The Paul Coverdell Forensic Sciences Improvement Grant Program provides funding to State and local governments to improve the quality and timeliness of forensic science and medical examiner services and or to eliminate backlogs in the analysis of forensic evidence  including controlled substances  firearms examination  forensic pathology  latent prints  questioned documents  toxicology  and trace evidence  NIJ is the research  development  and evaluation agency of the U S  Department of Justice and is dedicated to researching crime control and justice issues  NIJ provides objective  independent  evidence based knowledge and tools to meet the challenges of crime and justice  particularly at the State and local levels
