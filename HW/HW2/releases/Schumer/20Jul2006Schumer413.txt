IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 20  2006 Schumer Announces Key Senate Panel Backs  200 000 For Syracuse  s Landmark Theatre                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Senator Aggressively Lobbied Senate Appropriations Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill   Restoration Projects Will Help Revitalize The Economy And Draw In Tourism U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee is expected to approve  200 000 for Syracuse Area Landmark Theatre Renovation under the FY07 Appropriations Bill for Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary  Housing and Urban Development  Schumer aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill       This will be a big boost for Syracuse     Schumer said    Restoration and revitalization of this downtown centerpiece is key to both increasing local tourism and providing a shot in the arm for the economy  Preservation of places like Landmark theatre  which embody a piece of local history  will ensure that future generations will also be able to enjoy our history and culture         The Syracuse Area Landmark Theatre Renovation project will use the funds for the demolition of the existing stagehouse and the reconstruction of a new larger stagehouse  The Landmark Theatre was designed by Thomas W  Lamb and constructed in 1928  Named Loew  s State Theatre  it hosted many famous stage acts and first run motion pictures  By the 1970s the theatre had fallen into disrepair and a group of concerned citizens formed the Syracuse Landmark Theatre organization which had the local landmark place on the National Register of Historic Places  The stagehouse expansion is an important part of the ongoing renovation project aimed at restoring the theatre to its original splendor  The now theatre features a full schedule of performances by the Syracuse Symphony Orchestra  touring Broadway shows  and a wide variety of renowned musicians      Later today  the Appropriations Bill for Transportation  Treasure  the Judiciary  Housing  and Urban Development is expected to pass the Appropriations Committee and will head to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  The bill will then proceed to the Conference Committee and finally will be sent to the President before becoming law
