IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 30  2007 Schumer Announces Federal Dollars For Coming To Three Central New York Communities                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Funds Will Be Used For Water and Waste Disposal System Improvements in the Town of DeWitt  the Town of Scriba and the Village of Chittenango                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that federal funds have been awarded to three communities in Central  New York  The Town of DeWitt in Onondaga County will receive a  687 000 loan for the construction of a new sewer district  The Town of Scriba in Oswego County has been granted a loan of  140 000 and a grant of  288 000 for improvements to the water distribution system  Finally  the Village of Chittenango in Madison County has been awarded a  4 490 000 loan for upgrades to the local Sewage Collection Treatment Facility  The funds are being awarded through the United States Department of Agriculture s  USDA  Rural Development program as Water and Waste Disposal Grants and Loans   This is wonderful news for the Town of DeWitt  the Town of Scriba and the Village of Chittenango  said Schumer  Proper water and waste disposal is essential to maintaining our strong communities in this area  Without continued upkeep and maintenance  Central New York cannot grow and sustain its population  I look forward to seeing the benefits these federal loans and grants bring to the economy of Central  New York and the lives of its residents    The Town of DeWitt has been awarded a  687 000 loan for the construction of the Jamesville Sewer District  Currently  surveys indicate that many of the private subsurface sewage disposal systems being utilized by the residents have a variety of problems that could pose a health threat and effect the water quality of Rush and Butternut Creeks  The proposed project for the construction of public sanitary sewers and appurtenances would serve approximately 100 homes and a number of businesses in the area   The Town of Scriba has been awarded a  140 000 loan and a  288 000 grant to construct a water distribution system known as the Churchill Road Water Project  Residents in the area have wells that are inadequate  that run dry in the summer  and are of poor water quality forcing them to purchase bottled water    The Village of  Chittenango has been awarded a loan of  4 490 000 for to upgrade the Sewage Collection Treatment Facility  Key components are 20 to 25 years old and need upgrading to allow increase in capacity  This will comply with State Pollutant Discharge Elimination System discharge requirements and allow further expansion in residential construction  The project will nearly 5 000 people in the area   The USDA Rural Development Water and Waste Disposal Grant program provides funds to reduce water and waste disposal costs to a reasonable level for rural users  The Grant program also sends funds to help develop water and waste disposal systems in rural areas and towns  Grants can be used to construct  repair  modify  expand  and improve water supply and distribution systems  acquire needed land and water sources  and pay for legal and engineering fees when necessary to build facilities
