IMMEDIATE RELEASE  April 1  2005 Schumer  Clinton Announce  10 Million For JFK Airport Improvements                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Three DOT grants to help pay for runway safety area improvements and additional lighting US Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton announced today that the US Department of Transportation has awarded three grants totaling  10 million dollars to the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey  The federal funds will be used for projects at John F  Kennedy International Airport        In this post 9 11 world  airport security needs to remain a number one priority  This funding will allow John F  Kennedy International Airport to carry out its plans for a series of improvements   Senator Schumer said   These projects will help ensure that every step is being taken to provide the best possible airport safety measures         John F  Kennedy International Airport is the gateway to New York and it is important that we continue to make these kinds of investments in this critical transportation hub   Senator Clinton said   As people are taking to the skies now more than ever  we must make the necessary upgrades to keep our airports safe  secure and efficient        Two of the grants  totaling  8 million will be used for runway area safety improvements  The runway area improvement project will help ensure safe daily aircraft operation at JFK  A third grant for  2 million will help to install runway lighting
