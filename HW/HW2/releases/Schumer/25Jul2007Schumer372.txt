IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 25  2007 Schumer Announces  200 000 Coming To The City Of Niagara Falls For Crime Prevention And Policing Initiatives                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     DOJ  Weed And Seed  Program Provides Funds For Local Law Enforcement To  Weed Out  Criminals And Communities To  Seed In  Prevention  Intervention  And Treatment Services    Niagara Falls Housing Authority Will Use Funds For Community Policing Initiatives                                                                                                                                               U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  200 000 has been awarded to the City of Niagara Falls in Niagara County to prevent violent crime and reduce drug trafficking through community policing initiatives  The funds are being awarded through the U S  Department of Justices  DOJ  Weed and Seed Program   This is great news for Niagara Falls  said Schumer  These funds are going toward a program that works to shut down criminals and reduce drug activity  Implementing community policing programs is not just a short term responseit provides long term solutions by focusing on prevention and community partnerships   The Niagara Falls Housing Authority will use the  200 000 of federal dollars for law enforcement efforts that will focus on reducing juvenile and youth adult violent crime and gang activity  drugs  robberies  and prostitution  Community policing goals will create a higher visibility through police presence and stronger interaction with local businesses  block clubs  and community partnerships  Prevention  intervention  and treatment goals will continue to provide constructive programs and services for families specifically targeting programs to address problems of alcohol and drug abuse  Neighborhood restoration goals include an increase in affordable house and an increase in homeownership and businesses  The site has allocated half of the total award to support weeding activities  which include community policing  The Niagara Falls Weed and Seed site received Official recognition in May 2003  and this award represents the sites fourth to implement its strategy    The Weed and Seed program works to prevent  reduce and control violent crime  drug abuse and gang activity in specifically targeted high crime areas across the country  Its name comes from a two part approach to these areas  One involves law enforcement agencies and prosecutors getting together to weed out individuals who participate in violent crime or drug abuse in an attempt to keep them out of the targeted area  The second part  seeding  includes bringing social services to the area in order to push for prevention  intervention and treatment  as well as neighborhood restoration  Since 1994  the Department of Justice has allocated at least  9 million annually nationwide for Weed and Seed related task forces administered through the Drug Enforcement Agency  the Federal Bureau of Investigation and other DOJ agencies
