                                    Ticks Have Become More Prevalent In North County    Lyme Disease Investigations Up Nearly 60 Percent This Year In Upstate New York                                                                                     Schumer Pushes For Immediate Passage Of New Bipartisan Legislation Funding Prevention  Education  Research                                                                        With Lyme disease active in Jefferson County for the first time  U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today pushed for immediate passage of new bipartisan legislation that would fund prevention  education and research for Lyme disease   In Upstate New York  Lyme disease investigations are up nearly 60 percent this year  In July  2005  there had only been 629 investigations of Lyme disease in all of upstate  but as of July 1  2006  there had already been 994  Other tick borne illnesses  including ehrlichiosis and babesios  are also spreading          Lyme disease is a problem we  ve seen for decades  but we haven  t done nearly enough at the federal level to tackle it     Schumer said    Funding research for a disease that impacts millions of Americans should not be a Herculean task  The time is now to substantially increase federal funding for research  educating families  treatment  and promoting prevention  With milder winters and the rain from this summer  the North Country is more vulnerable than ever to ticks and tick borne diseases  We need to do more to combat this sooner rather than later          To combat the rise in Lyme disease cases in the North County  Schumer announced that he is kick starting a new push to pass the Lyme and Tick borne Disease Prevention  Education  and Research Act   S  1479   Specifically  the bill would             Authorize  100 million over five years to increase and coordinate federal prevention  treatment and research of Lyme disease and other tick borne diseases  including developing sensitive and accurate diagnostics for tick borne diseases and increasing public education for such diseases         Provide  250 000 over two years to establish a Department of Health and Human Services advisory committee to facilitate communication between federal agencies  such as the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention and the National Institutes of Health  NIH    and members of the community  including patient organizations  doctors and scientists   as they attempt to raise awareness  treat  and cure tick borne diseases   Additionally  the committee would compile state and local data to improve information about the Lyme and other tick borne diseases        Schumer also said that there is a new type of tick that called the Lone Star tick  Amblyomma Americanum  which is one of the most aggressive ticks and actually will pursue a potential human host from 30 to 40 feet away   Though it has not appeared in the North County  according to the Lyme Disease Association  the Lone Star tick  typically found in the South  is migrating in to the Northeast  The Lone Star tick can infect a person with a Lyme like disease with symptoms similar to and as serious as regular Lyme disease   A bite from a Lone Star tick can cause a skin rash as well as neurological and arthritic ailments       Schumer announced he is urging federal health officials to investigate the rise in the Lone Star tick in New York possible health effects on the region  In a letter to CDC  s director Julie Gerberding  Schumer wrote    I urge you to investigate the migration of the Lone Star tick to the inland areas of the Northeast and to analyze the health implications of the Lone Star tick  s increased presence in New York State            Lyme disease  though highly curable if it is detected in its early stages  is difficult to diagnose as its symptoms are similar to the common flu  The   bull  s eye    rash that accompanies infection of the disease at the site of the tick bite often goes undetected especially on darker skin tones  and there is no reliable blood test for the disease  In addition  there is no vaccine  and medical authorities disagree over how to treat the illness  particularly when it persists after short term antibiotic treatment
