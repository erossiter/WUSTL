IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 20  2005 Schumer Announces  500 000 in Research Money for University at Buffalo                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Center for International Rehabilitation Research Information and Exchange at the University at Buffalo will receive Funding for Disability and Rehabilitation Research                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced  500 000 for the SUNY Research Foundation at the University at Buffalo from the U S  Department of Education  Disability and Rehabilitation Research Projects Program  The federal funds will be used to expand the University at Buffalo  s Center for International Rehabilitation Research Information and Exchange  CIRRIE   which links rehabilitation researchers  practitioners  and consumers in the United States with their counterparts in the international community       This is great news for Western New York     says Senator Schumer    The University at Buffalo is on the cutting edge of rehabilitation research  and this money will ensure this critical work continues  Not only will this solidify the research status of the University  but it will also help all those who live with disabilities overcome the challenges they face on the road to rehabilitation        The federal funds will be used to expand the existing Database of International Rehabilitation Research by enhancing its contents and increasing the distribution and translation of the research projects  making it available to more researchers around the world  In addition  the funding will support specialized programs and activities between the United States and the international research community  including short term individual exchanges and institutional collaboration  CIRRIE will also create new curriculum materials  including a new textbook  for university rehabilitation programs throughout the United States to improve the cultural competence and sensitivity of disability service providers     CIRRIE was created at the University at Buffalo with the objective of sharing information and expertise in disability and rehabilitation research  CIRRIE is primarily funded by the National Institute on Disability and Rehabilitation Research  NIDRR   a division of the Department of Education
