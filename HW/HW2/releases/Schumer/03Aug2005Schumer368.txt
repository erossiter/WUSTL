IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 3  2005 Schumer To Feds  Do Not Cut TSA Security Staff At Albany International Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Albany Could Lose 14 Screeners Due to Across  the Board Staffing Reduction  Schumer  As Passenger Numbers are on the Rise we Shouldn  t be Cutting Critical Screeners The Albany International Airport might be losing 14 Transportation Security Administration  TSA  screeners due to a nationwide across the board staffing reduction  U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer revealed today  The reduction will effect the entire state  but Albany is one of the hardest hit  with a nearly 12 percent reduction in TSA screening personnel  Today Schumer called on the TSA to reverse the staffing cuts immediately       These numbers simply don  t add up     Schumer said    Business at Albany Airport is through the roof now that Southwest has established itself in the market and is growing  and with more people discovering the service of Independence Air continues to grow  Cutting TSA staff at Albany runs completely against the grain  Albany should be at the top of the list when deciding assigning critical safety personnel  Instead the Feds have shortchanged New Yorkers again when it comes to homeland security        The Albany International Airport has had an all time record in passenger traffic growth last year  From 2003 to 2004  boarding  s rose 8 4 percent  To accommodate the added passenger growth  the airport has undertaken an aggressive multi million dollar expansion project that would add two new TSA screening lanes and expand the busy waiting and concession areas      Schumer has been a leader on improving upstate air security since 9 11  He has written to the TSA questioning screener hiring practices and has fought to increase upstate screener force in light of record growth  Schumer is a lead cosponsor of the Transportation Security Act of 2005  which would directly address screener failures and staffing shortfalls  The legislation would specifically require TSA to review the adequacy of existing screener training and require DHS develop a pilot program to establish screener workforce internships at airports in order to increase screener retention  The comprehensive piece of security legislation covers air  rail  mass transit  and port security
