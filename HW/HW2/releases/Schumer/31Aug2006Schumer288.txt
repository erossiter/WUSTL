IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 31  2006 Schumer Urges Governor To Appeal FEMA Decision Denying Extension Of Flood Aid Through End Of July                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Currently  Only Those Requesting Aid From June 26   July10 Floods Qualify For Flood Aid   FEMA Has Denied Request To Extend Eligibility Period And Without Extension  Victims Of Late July Floods Will Be Left Ineligible For Aid                                                                                                                                                                     U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today urged Governor George Pataki to appeal the Federal Emergency Management Agency  s  FEMA  decision to deny an extension of the incident period for this summer  s flooding   If FEMA does not extend the incident period through July 31  2006 many flood victims will be ineligible for aid       It is so important that this decision is appealed so that those who are still trying to recover their losses from this summer  s flooding can receive FEMA aid     said Schumer     The current incident period is arbitrary and does not reflect the actual timing of the floods   We must do everything possible to help these victims get back on their feet        The current incident period qualifies those who suffered flood damage during the June 26 July 10 storms for FEMA aid   An extension would lengthen this period through July 31  2006  allowing more people who were affected by the late July floods to apply for aid   The storms from June 26 July 31  2006 devastated 21 counties throughout upstate New York   The residents of upstate New York suffered from an unusually long period of summer storms this year  and the resulting floods left behind destroyed homes  ruined infrastructure and drowned crops   The counties affected include  Broome  Chenango  Cortland  Delaware  Fulton  Greene Hamilton  Herkimer  Madison  Montgomery  Oneida  Orange  Otsego  Rensselaer  Schenectady  Schoharie  Sullivan  Tioga  Tompkins  Ulster  and Westchester Counties     In a personal letter to Governor Pataki  Schumer wrote    I urge you to appeal FEMA  s decision before the deadline on September 28  2006   Extending the declaration period will allow aid to continue to flow to those upstate communities still grappling with the damage they have suffered
