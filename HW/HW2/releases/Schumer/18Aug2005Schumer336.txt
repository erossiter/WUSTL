IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 18  2005 Schumer Announces Over  266 000 for Central NY Crime Control and Prevention Programs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Grant awarded as part of Justice Assistance Grant Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  266 169 has been awarded to the City of Utica  City of Syracuse  Herkimer County  and surrounding areas by the U S  Department of Justice  s Office of Justice Programs  OJP  under the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  The federal funds will be used for crime control and prevention programs         When it comes to protecting our citizens  we need to be tough on crime and smart on prevention   Schumer said    These much needed funds will help to bring the quality people and first rate technology that allow Central NY families to live safely and comfortably          The City of Syracuse will use  114 050 in JAG funding to purchase 11 laptop computers with docking stations for patrol cars  and 44 digital ready  portable radios  The computers will enhance the Police Department  s ability to analyze crime data  and the digital ready radios will support the implementation of a communications network for law enforcement and emergency response agencies throughout the County  In addition  the County of Onondaga will use  91 039 to purchase security and surveillance equipment  to pay for drug buys and confidential informants  to partially fund an additional law enforcement officer for Project START  and to purchase a truck capable of towing the County Mobile Command Center       The City of Utica will use  50 057 for the following purposes  to purchase an unmarked vehicle for the Utica Police Department for joint County probation officer and City police officer community supervision to support the Oneida County Electronic Monitoring Device Program  to provide 435 hours of County probation officer overtime  to purchase five handheld GPS units for the County Sheriff  s Office  and to purchase electronic fingerprinting equipment to link the Sheriff  s Office in Camden with the Police Department  s dual county network as well as the New York State Division of Criminal Justice Services       The County of Herkimer will use  11 023 to partially fund the purchase of iris recognition biometric technology to capture  store and retrieve identification information from the national data repositories maintained by the Nation  s Missing Children Organization and the National Center for Missing Adults       The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime based on their own local needs and conditions  Grant funds can be used for state and local initiatives  technical assistance  training  personnel  equipment  supplies  contractual support  and information systems for criminal justice for any one or more of the following purpose areas  1  law enforcement programs  2  prosecution and court programs  3  prevention and education programs  4  corrections and community corrections programs  5  drug treatment programs  and 6  planning  evaluation  and technology improvement programs
