IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 26  2006 Schumer  Clinton Announce The Release Of Over  685 000 For The Capital District Transportation Authroity                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Federal Money Will Be Used For Improvements To The Bus Transit System                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the U S  Department of Transportation  DOT  has released  685 300 in Federal Transit Administration  FTA  funds to the Capital District Transportation Authority in Albany   The money will be used to implement Bus Rapid Transit  a program to develop connecting bus stations along a route which connects the City of Albany to the City of Schenectady         This release of this money is great news for the Capital Region    said Senator Schumer   Many people rely on buses as their primary mode of transportation to get to and from work  This funding will help to improve the speed  quality and efficiency of bus services  allowing travelers to reach more destinations in a shorter period of time          This is a welcome investment for the Capital region    said Senator Clinton     Connecting these two cities through public transportation will help provide more economic opportunity for residents and businesses alike           Federal funding will be used to implement a Bus Rapid Transit system on the 17 mile corridor connecting the City of Albany to the City of Schenectady   Funds will also be used to advance the 20 paired Stations through the design  environmental clearance  and regulatory approval stage  as well as develop the operations plan   Competitively procured consultant services will be used to support the design and regulatory approval process  Additional funding will provide expanded traffic signal priority for transit  expanded shared use park and ride lot capacity  and road improvements at critical intersections       Bus Rapid Transit  BRT  systems provide a higher quality of service than traditional bus lines at a lower cost than rail systems   Through innovations in running ways  fare collection  signal priority and vehicle design  transit service is improved while maintaining the basic infrastructure of traditional bus systems   BRT projects can also include Intelligent Transportation Systems  ITS   branding and marketing strategies and land use benefits   The FTA supports BRT through demonstration  evaluation and informative programs
