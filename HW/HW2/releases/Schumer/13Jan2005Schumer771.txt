IMMEDIATE RELEASE  January 13  2005 Schumer Urges Top Fed Official To Fully Fund Visiting Airmen  s Quarters At NARS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  New DOD Comptroller Tina Jonas Recently Replaced Dov Zakheim  Who Had Agreed To Fully Fund Niagara Air Reserve Station  Schumer Places Personal Phone Call to Push for Funding for NARS  Jonas Receptive to Request US Senator Charles E  Schumer in a phone call late Wednesday urged the current Department of Defense Comptroller  Tina Jonas  support the inclusion of full funding for the construction of a Visiting Quarters at the Niagara Air Reserve Station  NARS  in the Department of Defense budget  In making this request  Schumer asked that Comptroller Jonas honor a commitment made by her predecessor  Schumer  who aggressively lobbied the Department of Defense to support funding for NARS  said funding for the Visiting Quarters must be included the Defense Budget this year  Jonas was receptive to his request and said she would do everything she could to fund the program       When we secured the funding commitment from the previous Defense Department Comptroller  it meant we would be getting money to Niagara quickly and moving this project closer to the finish line     Schumer said    I called Comptroller Jonas to ensure that she would keep her predecessor  s commitment to funding the new Visitor  s Quarters  Since the station is the second largest employer in the county this project is important to the local economy and area jobs  Most importantly  we need to do everything we can to help the men and women of the 914th Airlift Wing and the 107th Air Reserve         In March  Schumer joined Senator Clinton  Representative Slaughter  and Representative Reynolds in meeting with Defense Department Comptroller Dov S  Zakheim and urged him to include funding for the visiting quarters in the 2006 budget  Construction for a Visiting Quarters for airmen at NARS is needed to replace deteriorating billeting at NARS which currently does not meet Americans with Disabilities Act standards  New York Safety Codes  or US Air Force Quality of Life Standards  Delays in construction  or a failure to replace the current Visiting Airman  s Quarters at NARS would increase the government  s investment in maintenance of the facility and costs to the base associated with energy and high maintenance costs of the current facilities     Zakheim told Schumer and other lawmakers that he would push to include the funding for the Visiting Quarters in his Fiscal Year 2006 budget request  Zakheim stepped down in April 2004 and was replaced by Jonas
