IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 22  2007 Schumer Announces Over  62 000 For City Of Schenectady Crime Control And Prevention                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              City of Schenectady and Schenectady County Will Use Federal Funds For Improvements in Crime Control and Prevention Programs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced today that  62 755 has been awarded to the City of Schenectady to be shared with the County of Schenectady by the U S  Department of Justices Office of Justice Programs  OJP  under the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  The City of Schenectady and Schenectady County will use the federal funds to improve a variety of crime control and prevention programs   When it comes to protecting our citizens  we need to be tough on crime and smart on prevention  Senator Schumer said  These much needed funds will help bring quality people and first rate technology to Schenectady  so families can live safely and comfortably   Schenectady and Schenectady County will use the grant to jointly fund several programs  These include the Law  Order  and Justice Center of Schenectady to continue to oversee the operation of a Community Accountability Board to adjudicate non violent quality of life offenses from the city court  The funds will also be used to purchase two laptop computers for the Schenectady Patrol Officer Intelligence Initiative  which is aimed at improving opportunities for patrol officers to receive and share intelligence information with Schenectadys Office of Field Intelligence  The City and County will also purchase supplies to continue the Schenectady Police Departments Gun Tell Tip Line Program that consists of public information and education through the use of billboards  ads in local papers  and media releases  Finally  the funding will be used to cover overtime hours for the Schenectady Police Departments Warrant Sweep Initiative to reduce the backlog of outstanding warrants   The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime based on their own local needs and conditions  Grant funds can be used for state and local initiatives  technical assistance  training  personnel  equipment  supplies  contractual support  and information systems for criminal justice for any one or more of the following purpose areas  1  law enforcement programs  2  prosecution and court programs  3  prevention and education programs  4  corrections and community corrections programs  5  drug treatment programs  and 6  planning  evaluation  and technology improvement programs
