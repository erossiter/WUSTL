IMMEDIATE RELEASE  June 20  2005 Schumer  More Than  99 000 Awarded To St  John Fisher College For New School Of Pharmacy                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Federal dollars will help build facility and bring state of the art technology to laboratories  curriculum US Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  99 200 in federal funds is coming to St  John Fisher College in Pittsford for the construction and development of a new School of Pharmacy  The funds are coming as part of the Department of Education  s Directed Grants Program       This is great news for the Finger Lakes Region     Schumer said    It is so important that local communities have access to smart  well educated pharmacists  and this new school at St  John Fisher is going to produce some of the best and brightest pharmacists to meet the needs of the region  St  John Fisher has a long tradition of high quality education and I know that this funding for their new School of Pharmacy will mean great things for their students        St  John Fisher  s new School of Pharmacy will house pharmacy practice  patient assessment  pharmaceutics and sterile products laboratories  The money will help to integrate the evolving field of biomedical informatics into the laboratory curriculum  In addition  the state of the art technology provided for in the Grant will also allow students access to experiential educational software     Last year  as part of the FY05 Omnibus Appropriations bill  Senator Schumer secured  1 05 million for technology  curriculum development and training  The funding was included in the Labor  Health and Human Services Appropriations portion of the bill  St  John Fisher College is working with local and state government and with other universities in the region to meet the current and projected shortfalls of pharmacists for communities and hospitals in Central and Western New York  The proposed School of Pharmacy will grow to graduate classes for 50 pharmacists per year by 2009     The Directed Grants Program is part of the Fund for the Improvement of Postsecondary Education in the United States Department of Education  The Fund seeks to enhance postsecondary education and increase student access to important programs for Institutions of Higher Learning and Nonprofit Organizations
