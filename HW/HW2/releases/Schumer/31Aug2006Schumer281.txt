IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 31  2006 Schumer Announces Over  155 000 Coming To Erie County For DNA Analysis Technology                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Organization Will Use Funds to Improve DNA Laboratory Infrastructure and Analysis Capacity                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  155 424 has been awarded to the Erie County Forensic Laboratory by the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  National Institute of Justice  NIJ  under the DNA Capacity Enhancement Program Solicitation program   The federal funds will be used to improve laboratory infrastructure and analysis capacity so that DNA samples can be processed efficiently and cost effectively       This is great news for Erie County     said Schumer     This funding will allow law enforcement to make DNA analysis techniques more accurate and efficient   Better technology for crime investigation will increase safety for the entire community          The Erie County Forensic Laboratory will use funds to buy a genetic analyzer  which performs the final step in the DNA analytical process   The lab will also purchase other new instruments and supplies as well as equipment to upgrade current instruments     The National Institute of Justice DNA Capacity Enhancement Program furthers the mission of the Department of Justice by offering an opportunity for states and units of local government with existing crime laboratories that conduct DNA analysis to improve laboratory infrastructure and analysis capacity so that DNA samples can be processed efficiently and cost effectively   These improvements are critical to preventing future DNA backlogs and to helping the criminal justice system use the full potential of DNA technology
