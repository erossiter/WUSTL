IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 13  2006 Schumer Announces  150 000 Coming To The City Of Olean For Sewer System Improvements                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Federal Funds Will Be Used To Replace Sewer Infrastructure in East Olean                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that a grant of  150 000 has been awarded to the City of Olean in Cattaraugus County by the Appalachian Regional Commission  ARC    The federal funds will go toward the installation of new sewer piping that will improve the system infrastructure         This is great news for the City of Olean     Schumer said     Poor sewer systems create serious health concerns and endanger the welfare of the community   This funding will allow for major improvements in the sewer system  which will in turn improve health safety and the quality of life and allow for increased economic development in the area          This project will allow the city to replace a deficient sanitary sewer system in the East Olean area  The project entails the installation of approximately 1 850 linear feet of new sewer piping that will serve 600 households and 40 businesses   Residents currently experience unsanitary conditions on a regular basis due to deficient wastewater infrastructure   The sewer problems result in backup sewage that flows into residences  causing property damage and imposing a health risk   The backup problems also extend to adjacent greenspaces  parks and recreational facilities  making them periodically unusable   The new system will protect public health and safety and will provide the infrastructure necessary to assist area industries in sustaining and expanding their business activities  retaining and creating jobs  and reducing operating costs  thereby providing an incentive for industries to remain in the area       Each year ARC provides funding for several hundred projects that create thousands of new jobs  improve local water and sewer systems  increase school readiness  expand access to health care  assist local communities with strategic planning  and provide technical  managerial  and marketing assistance to emerging new businesses  ARC is a federal state partnership that works with the people of Appalachia to create opportunities for self sustaining economic development and improved quality of life  The ARC awards grants from funds appropriated to the Commission annually by Congress
