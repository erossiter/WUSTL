IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 8  2006 Schumer Announces Over  203 000 Coming To The New York State Police                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Federal Dollars Will Be Used To Improve DNA Analysis Capacity To Help Solve Criminal Cases                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  203 919 has been awarded to the New York State Police  headquartered in Albany  by the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  National Institute of Justice  NIJ  under the Forensic Casework DNA Backlog Reduction program   The federal funds will be used to analyze backlogged forensic DNA casework samples from criminal cases       This is great news for New York     said Schumer     DNA analysis is the new frontier in law enforcement  and numerous crimes are now solved utilizing the database    The problem is that there is a backlog of unprocessed evidence   By culling that backlog authorities will yield new information that will solve crimes  put criminals behind bars  and provide justice to victims     added Schumer      The New York State Police will use funds to increase analytical capabilities of the lab and to better handle the numerous requests for homicide related DNA analysis   In addition  this funding will help to eliminate the backlog and decrease the turn around time associated with DNA testing     Senator Schumer has long been involved in securing funding to improve DNA analysis capabilities for New York State Police   In September 2004  Senator Schumer secured nearly  1 5 million dollars to establish the Northeast Forensic Training Institute  NERFI    NERFI is a joint effort between the University at Albany and the New York State Police  which established curriculums and graduate degrees in forensic science      The Forensic Casework DNA Backlog Reduction Program furthers the mission of the Department of Justice by offering an opportunity for states and units of local government with existing crime laboratories that conduct DNA analysis to analyze backlogged forensic DNA casework samples from forcible rape and murder non negligent manslaughter  whether in government owned accredited laboratories or through accredited fee for service vendors   These analyses are critical to preventing future DNA backlogs and to helping the criminal justice system use the full potential of DNA technology
