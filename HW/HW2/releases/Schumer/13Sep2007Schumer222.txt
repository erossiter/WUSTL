IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 13  2007 Schumer Announces Over  135 000 Coming To Wetstone Technologies In Cortland                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Federal Dollars Will Be Used To Develop Forensic Examination Technology                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  137 500 has been awarded to Wetstone Technologies  Inc  in Cortland  by the U S  Department of Justices  DOJ  National Institute of Justice  NIJ  under the Electronic Crime Research and Development Solicitation  ECRDS  program  The federal funds will be used to develop new forensic examination technology  This is great news for Cortland  said Schumer  New crime solving technology will give local law enforcement the tools they need to reduce crime and get dangerous criminals off the streets  making all of Cortland County a safer place to live    Wetstone Technology  Inc  will use the federal funding to develop a USB Live Acquisition and Triage Tool to provide the ability for forensic examinations to be performed on a live system prior to seizure  US LATT is intended to capture common stateful information from systems and in addition  target data immediately relevant to cases involving subject matter such as youth exploitation  parole violations  and possible terrorism  The tool can be used as both a pre seizure function to collection the live system information before the stem is turned off and as a triage tool to determine if further consideration is merited for a given system    The Electronic Crime Partnership Initiative  ECPI   who sponsors the ECRDS program  is comprised of Electronic Crime Subject Matter Experts representing Law Enforcement  Academia and the Private Sector from across the Country  working in cooperation on projects and programs to improve the capacity of State and Local Law Enforcement to effectively address Electronic Crime
