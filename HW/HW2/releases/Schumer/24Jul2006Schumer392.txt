IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 24  2006 Schumer Announces Key Senate Panel Approves  250 000 For Rockland Community College  s Science Nursing And Allied Health Labs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Senator Aggressively Lobbied Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill  Schumer  The Science Nursing and Allied Health Labs Are Vitally Important To The Economy of Rockland U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer announced that late last week  the Senate Appropriations Committee is approved  250 000 under the FY07 Labor  Health  and Human Services and Education Appropriations Bill for the Rockland Community College Science Nursing and Allied Health Labs in Suffern  Schumer aggressively lobbied members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this year  s spending bill       Rockland Community College plays a critical role in training the next generation of scientists  nurses and health care professionals     said Schumer    These bright and motivated students deserve to learn and be trained in state of the art facilities  and these federal dollars will greatly improve these important labs        The federal funds for the Science Nursing and Allied Health Labs will be used to renovate  upgrade and replace Rockland Community College  s twelve science  nursing and allied health labs   This will allow the labs to meet Rockland County  s current and future labor force and economic development needs in these fields by preparing students to be skilled employees   Students  employers and the whole Rockland County community will benefit from this funding since one fifth of the workers in the area are employed in chemical  allied products  nursing and health related social services     Late last week  the Appropriations Bill for Transportation  Treasure  the Judiciary  Housing  and Urban Development is expected to pass the Appropriations Committee and will head to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  The bill will then proceed to the Conference Committee and finally will be sent to the President before becoming law
