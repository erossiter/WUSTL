IMMEDIATE RELEASE  August 21  2006 Schumer Announces Federal Dollars Coming To Organizations In Central New York                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Funds Will Be Used By The Town of LaFayette And The Village Of Vernon For Water System Improvements                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that federal dollars have been awarded to two organizations in Central New York by the United States Department of Agriculture s  USDA  Rural Development Water and Waste Disposal Grant and Loan Program  The Town of LaFayette in Onondaga County has been awarded a  1 392 000 loan for the development of a town water district   The Village of Vernon in Oneida County has been awarded a  7 500 grant to produce a report to develop a plan for the proposed water system improvement project       This is great news for both Central New York and the Mohawk Valley   Schumer said   The water systems are critical to the livelihood of the communities and the health of local residents   Clean drinking water is a basic need and this funding will help these communities meet that need  improving the quality of life for everyone in the area        The Town of LaFayette will use the federal dollars to develop a town water district   Currently  gravity fed waterlines  which are over 100 years old  carry spring water to residents for drinking water   Since the operating pressures in LaFayette are substandard  many customers had to install individual water pumps   In addition  the low water pressure means that no fire service is provided in LaFayette   The new water district will include 19 500 linear feet of water main  28 hydrants and a pressure reducing valve       The Village of Vernon will use federal funds to develop a plan for the proposed water system improvement project  The current water pumping station was constructed in the 1930  s and does not have the sufficient capacity to meet the Village  s current water demand       The USDA Rural Development Water and Waste Disposal Grant and Loan program provides funds to reduce water and waste disposal costs to a reasonable level for rural users  The Grant program also sends funds to help develop water and waste disposal systems in rural areas and towns   Grants can be used to construct  repair  modify  expand  and improve water supply and distribution systems  acquire needed land and water sources  and pay for legal and engineering fees when necessary to build facilities
