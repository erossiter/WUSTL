IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 6  2005 Schumer  Over  1 Million in Federal Dollars Coming to North Country For Water Infrastructure Assistance                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Funds will be used by the Towns of Indian Lake  Alexandria  and Westport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  1 150 000 was awarded to three North Country towns for emergency water assistance  Indian Lake was awarded  500 000  Alexandria was allotted  500 000  and Westport will receive  150 000  The funds are being awarded through the United States Department of Agriculture s  USDA  Rural Development program as an Emergency Community Water Assistance Grant         This is great news for the North Country     Schumer said    The water system is critical to the livelihood of the communities and the health of local residents  This funding will greatly improve the water system and improve quality of life in the area          The rural development funds will be used by Indian Lake to cover well improvements and installation of a distribution main that is needed to connect the wells into the existing distribution system  These improvements will assist in resolving an emergency water district crisis in Water District  2       Alexandria will use the funds to extend municipal water to the Hamlet of Redwood  to be known as the Redwood Water District  Residents in this area currently rely on private wells that yield an inadequate water supply and pose health concerns  This project will serve 214 users  Leveraging funds include  2 820 500 from the Drinking Water State Revolving Fund  DWSRF    2 000 000 from a DWSRF grant  and  1 800 000 from a New York State Spill Fund Grant       The funds will be used by Westport to install 3800 linear feet of new transmission pipeline  Heavy rain in April 2005 caused three major transmission water lines to break by saturating the soil and pulling the pipes apart at the joints  The transmission lines carry water from the source into the village  The new lines will be installed along Stevenson Rd   allowing the town to abandon the existing pipeline in the unstable clay embankment  This project will serve 442 users  Leveraging funds include an applicant contribution of  80 000 and town funds of  800       USDA Rural Development Emergency Community Water Assistance Grants assist rural communities that have had a significant decline in quantity or quality of drinking water  The grants can be used to extend  repair  or perform significant maintenance on existing water systems  construct new water lines  wells or other sources of water  reservoirs  and treatment plants  replace equipment  and pay costs associated with connection or tap fees
