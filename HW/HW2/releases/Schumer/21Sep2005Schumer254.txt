IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 21  2005 Schumer Announces Over  720 000 For Organizations In Amherst For Fingerprint Identification Research                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Federal Funds Awarded as Part of Friction Ridge Analysis Research Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  723 079 has been awarded to two research organizations in the Town of Amherst by the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  National Institute of Justice  NIJ   The federal funds will be used to aid researchers to improve the efficiency and accuracy of fingerprint analysis          Criminals must be caught and brought to justice   Schumer said    With better technology for fingerprint analysis  the police will have the information they need to make Amherst a safer community for families to live in  The money for research will also help police to find missing children  whose fingerprints are likely to change as they grow          The Research foundation of SUNY Amherst will receive  596 478 in federal funds to improve the assessment of exiting statistical models of friction ridge individuality and explore new ways in which to improve upon the existing models        The Ultra Scan Corporation will receive  126 601 in federal funding to pay for a project studying the changes that occur on fingerprints during maturity  The result will be a new model for fingerprint identification that will be able to predict changes in finger prints over a long period of time        The National Institute of Justice is the research  development  and evaluation arm of the U S  Department of Justice  Friction ridge analysis is the study of the prints left by fingers  palms  or feet  The uniqueness of these friction ridge patterns has long been accepted by the scientific community and by the courts  During the past thirty years  law enforcement has attempted to raise professional standards of fingerprint identification by encouraging further advances in the science of friction ridge analysis  in order to ensure the certainty of the results
