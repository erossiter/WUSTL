IMMEDIATE RELEASE  September 5  2006 Schumer Announces Over  77 000 Coming To Erie County Forensic Laboratory For DNA Analysis                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Funds Will Be Used To Improve DNA Analysis Capacity To Help Solve Crimes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         U S  Senator Charles E  Schumer today announced that  77 978 has been awarded to the Erie County Forensic Laboratory by the U S  Department of Justice  s  DOJ  National Institute of Justice  NIJ  under the Forensic Casework DNA Backlog Reduction program   The federal funds will be used to analyze backlogged forensic DNA casework samples from criminal cases       This is great news for Erie County     said Schumer     There are many backlogged cases which have the potential to be solved through advancements in DNA technology   This funding will allow law enforcement agencies to identify and apprehend individuals who commit vicious crimes and might otherwise continue to remain on the streets endangering the lives of others        The Erie County Forensic Laboratory will use funds to increase analytical capabilities of the lab and better handle the increasing number of requests for DNA analysis on evidence associated with homicides   The Erie County Forensic Laboratory provides forensic DNA analysis for law enforcement agencies from four neighboring counties   With the success of the Combined DNA Index System  CODIS   casework requests have been steadily increasing  especially in the area of forcible sexual assault   It is anticipated this funding will help to eliminate the backlog and decrease the turn around time associated with DNA testing  resulting in the completion of 110 additional cases        The Forensic Casework DNA Backlog Reduction Program furthers the mission of the Department of Justice by offering an opportunity for states and units of local government with existing crime laboratories that conduct DNA analysis to analyze backlogged forensic DNA casework samples from forcible rape and murder non negligent manslaughter  whether in government owned accredited laboratories or through accredited fee for service vendors   These analyses are critical to preventing future DNA backlogs and to helping the criminal justice system use the full potential of DNA technology
