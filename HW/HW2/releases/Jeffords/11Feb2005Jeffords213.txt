SEN. JEFFORDS ANNOUNCES FOUR MORE FIRE GRANTS
  Sen. Jim Jeffords, I-Vt., announced today that the Department of Homeland Security has awarded four more Vermont fire departments a total of $482,322 in grants to support the departments' roles as first responders in their communities.  
 The following departments will be receiving grants:
   * South Burlington Fire Department $153,030 
  * Williamstown Fire Department $180,549 
  * Burlington Fire Department $116,744 
  * Peacham Fire Department $31,999
   Jeffords said, "Firefighters are crucial to maintaining safe communities throughout Vermont. We must continue to work to provide these brave men and women with the resources they deserve." Jeffords served as a volunteer firefighter with the Shrewsbury Volunteer Fire Department. 
   These funds are used by the nation's firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations; bolster firefighter health and safety programs; improve wellness and fitness; defend against weapons of mass destruction; and provide new fire apparatus, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
   The Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program (AFGP) assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States by awarding one-year grants. To date, Vermont fire departments have received 46 grants for a total of $3,207,649. For more information, please visit www.firegrantsupport.com.
