SEN. JEFFORDS ANNOUNCES $670,393 GRANT FOR BRATTLEBORO FIRE DEPARTMENT
  Sen. Jim Jeffords, I-Vt., announced today that the Department of Homeland Security has awarded the Brattleboro Fire Department a grant of $670,393 to support the department's role as first responders in the community.  
   Jeffords said, "The Brattleboro Fire Department is essential to the safety of southern Vermont and I am pleased to announce they have been awarded such a significant grant. We must continue to ensure that all of Vermont's first responders are provided the resources they need." Jeffords served as a volunteer firefighter with the Shrewsbury Volunteer Fire Department. 
   These funds are used by the nation's firefighters to increase the effectiveness of firefighting operations; bolster firefighter health and safety programs; improve wellness and fitness; defend against weapons of mass destruction; and provide new fire apparatus, emergency medical service programs, and fire prevention and safety programs.
   The Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program (AFGP) assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States by awarding one-year grants. To date, Vermont fire departments have received 47 grants for a total of $3,878,042. For more information, please visit www.firegrantsupport.com.
   For more information on the grant, contact Brattleboro Fire Chief David J. Emery at (802) 254-4831.
