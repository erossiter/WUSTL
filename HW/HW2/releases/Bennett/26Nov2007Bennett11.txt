  Senator Bennett's Staff to Hold Office Hours in Monticello and Moab in Early December     Staff will be available to assist with federal agency issues  WASHINGTON, D.C.  Representatives from Senator Bennetts office will bein Moab and Monticello on December 5th and 6th, respectively, to help individuals with federal agency questions or problems.  Over the pasttwoyears, Bennett staff members have held remote office hours throughout central and southeastern Utah, giving Utahans the opportunity to meet face-to-face with caseworkers who can help them with federal agency issues. Bennett caseworkers can provide assistance with federal agencies, including: Internal Revenue Service, Bureau of Land Management, Social Security Administration, U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services,Veterans Affairs, Forest Service, and Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services.  Below is information on the remote offices:Moab:Moab Mayor's Office,217 East Center, on Wednesday, December 5th from2:30 to 4:30 p.m.Monticello: San Juan County Building,117 South Main, conference room,onThursday, December 6thfrom9:30 to11:30a.m. If individuals are unable to attend theseoffice hours, but would still like to request Senator Bennetts help, they are encouraged to call the senators Provo office, which handles constituent services for central and southeastern Utah, at (801) 851-2525. # # #
