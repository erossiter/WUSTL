SEN. HATCH: UTAH RECEIVES MILLIONS FOR COMMUNITY, TRANSIT PROJECTS
  Tonight Congress passed the Fiscal Year 2006 Transportation, Treasury and Housing and Urban Development (HUD) Appropriations conference report, H.R. 3038, which includes several Utah community development and transportation projects pushed by Sen. Orrin G. Hatch (R-Utah).  
   The bill contains $1 million in economic development funding for community projects Hatch requested for Provo, Eagle Mountain, and Hyrum: 
   * $600,000 for the Provo to build the Provo Community Arts Center. "Utah County has sought for years to have a high-quality arts center to host community and national theater and performing arts," Hatch said. "This funding will help Provo make this dream a reality." 
   * $200,000 for Eagle Mountain for community development and park facilities. "Eagle Mountain is one of the fastest growing communities in Utah," Hatch said. "These funds will help develop a park for recreational activities that will greatly benefit the many families and individuals in Eagle Mountain." 
   * $200,000 for Hyrum to build the Hyrum Library and Museum Complex. "The Hyrum Library and Museum Complex will provide the community with a vast collection of books, periodicals, videos, and other media," Hatch said. "This will new library and museum will greatly help in revitalizing downtown Hyrum." 
   The bill also contains several transit projects requested by Hatch and also Sen. Bob Bennett (R-Utah), who serves on the Senate Appropriations Committee, and other members of the Utah delegation. 
   "One of the state's highest priorities is improving transportation," Hatch said. "Along with the millions secured through the transportation bill earlier this year, this funding will make a significant impact on increasing our transit capacity." 
   Among the projects funded in today's bill are: 
   * $9 million for commuter rail 
   * $3.6 million for the Bluff Street Interchange in St. George 
   * $2 million for the SR-92/I-15 Interchange to SR-146 in Utah County 
   * $1.75 million for 200 West from Syracuse Road to Midland Drive 
   * $1.7 million for statewide bus and bus facilities 
   * $1.5 million for regional bus and bus facilities: Intermodal Terminals, including Gateway TRAX station 
   * $1.5 million for Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS) in St. George 
   * $1.5 million for I-15 North and Commuter Rail coordinated construction in Davis County 
   * $1.5 million for the I-15 Layton interchange 
   * $1.25 million for a Westminster College Intermodal Transportation Facilities Expansion for Shuttle Buses 
   * $575,000 for the Bluff Street Corridor in St. George 
   * $500,000 for a Transit Center at 9400 South in Sandy 
   * $500,000 for a Mid-Jordan Light Rail Transit Line 
   * $500,000 for U.S. 6 in Carbon County 
   * $500,000 for widening the Northern Corridor in St. George 
   * $500,000 for Geneva Road from 1600 N. in Orem to University Avenue in Provo 
   * $375,000 for a West Valley City Intermodal Terminal 
   * $250,000 for Sun-Tran Operations in St. George 
   * $200,000 for Utah Intermodal Transit Hubs 
   * $75,000 for the Commuter Rail Hub Planning Project in Brigham City
