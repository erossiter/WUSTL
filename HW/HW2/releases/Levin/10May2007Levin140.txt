                     Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMay 10  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Honors Detroit area    Crime Fighting Moms                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Local mothers recognized for dedication to their community      WASHINGTON     In honor of Mother   s Day  Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   is honoring three local    Crime Fighting Moms    whose outstanding work in crime prevention efforts has made their communities safer            Citizens who dedicate their time and effort to make the streets safer for their families and the community are an integral part of the success of community policing     said Levin     I am often told by Michigan law enforcement how important community volunteers are in their effort to reduce crime  These Crime Fighting Moms have demonstrated their commitment to improving the quality of life in their communities and they should inspire us all to be more involved            According to preliminary FBI data  violent crime in 2006 was down more than 30 percent since 1994   Officials attribute this promising trend in part to the increased activity of neighborhood law enforcement and safety organizations  which provide the extra    eyes and ears    needed to alert police to potential trouble        In a March 27th letter  Levin asked sheriffs and prosecutors across Michigan to nominate mothers who have been influential in local efforts to reduce crime  Levin sent each of these community activists a certificate of recognition from the U S  Senate         Since 1997  Levin has recognized Mother s Day by presenting  Crime Fighting Mom  awards to Michigan women    A total of 14 women are being honored this year  three of whom are from the Detroit area   Information on this year   s recipients from the Detroit area follows      Lenawee County  Honoree  Deputy Heather Sikkenga  Nominated by  Sheriff Lawrence Richardson  Jr   Deputy Heather Sikkenga is a dedicated member of the Lenawee County Sheriff   s Office who serves the people of Lenawee County with dignity and respect   She shows tremendous leadership qualities and holds herself and others to the highest standards   She is a total team player who works tirelessly and efficiently for the safety of the community      Monroe County  Honoree  Gail Keane  Nominated by  Prosecuting Attorney William Paul Nichols  Gail Keane assisted in forming a coalition of local residents and business owners into the    Temperance Action Committee      The goal of this group is the revitalization of the downtown area of Temperance  MI   Mrs  Keane organized the cleanup of blighted areas that were frequented by juveniles  and she assisted in raising money to purchase one of the formerly blighted areas that will be the future home of a veterans park   She also organized an adopt a road program and was the driving force behind a Neighborhood Watch group that collaborates with local law enforcement to provide extra    eyes and ears    in an effort to reduce local crime      Wayne County  Honoree  Shirley Burch  Nominated by  Sheriff Warren C  Evans  Shirley Burch is the Founder Coordinator of Community United for Progress  C U P    a local community improvement organization   She has been instrumental in keeping the residential and commercial area surrounding the Belmont Shopping Center and the Shina Family Detroit Independent Supermarkets safe for residents and visitors   Ms  Burch is also one of the key coordinators of the Dad Butler Park Renovation Project in her community   Ms  Burch has been a leading advocate for a proposed Satellite Police Mini Station at the Belmont Shopping Center that would house the Detroit Police Department as well as the Wayne County Sheriff   s Office
