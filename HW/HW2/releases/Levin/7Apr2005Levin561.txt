    04 07 2005   Army Corps Releases Restoration Plan for Lake St  Clair                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEApril 7  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Army Corps Releases Restoration Plan for Lake St  Clair                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     WASHINGTON    Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   Sen  Debbie Stabenow  D Mich   and Rep  Sander Levin  D Royal Oak  announced today that the Army Corps of Engineers released a report with recommendations for restoring Lake St  Clair   In recent years  Lake St  Clair has battled pollution from industry  sewage systems  storm water runoff and agricultural discharges  as well as aquatic invasive species and high bacterial counts  and the lawmakers are hopeful that the Army Corps    recommendations    which follow a six year assessment    will help to develop a workable restoration plan   They stressed that federal  state  and local governments will need to work together to prioritize and implement the Corps    recommendations          Lake St  Clair is a vital gateway between the Great Lakes  but for years it has struggled with pollution  erosion  and other environmental problems     Sen  Levin said      We   re hopeful that this restoration plan will help get Lake St  Clair moving in the right direction  and we look forward to working with local partners to help make that happen             Many Michiganians rely on Lake St  Clair for drinking water  fishing  recreational boating and commercial shipping  and nearly six million people live in its watershed     Stabenow said     This area of the Great Lakes needs our immediate attention  and we need to be moving forward with cleanup plans             In the past  Lake St  Clair has been described as  the forgotten lake     said Rep  Levin      No longer   The recommendations contained in the Management Report will help shape Lake St  Clair s future  but only if they are implemented   We simply can t let this be another study that sits and gathers dust   Everyone must take responsibility for turning these recommendations into actions          Earlier this year  the lawmakers requested additional authorization for the Corps to implement the recommendations of this study   This request would allow the Corps to partner with other federal  state  and local partners on implementation and spend up to  10 million in funding each year
