                 Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASESeptember 22  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce over  115 000 in Funding for Community Based Solutions to Crime in Sault Ste  Marie                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       WASHINGTON    U S  Senators Carl Levin  D MI  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  announced today that the U S  Department of Justice  DOJ  is awarding  115 272 to the Sault Ste  Marie Tribe of Chippewa Indians to fund a community based problem solving initiative          This is great news for Sault Ste  Marie   s Chippewas     said Levin      It is critical that we stay up to date when it comes to fighting crime in Michigan   s communities   These federal dollars will allow local authorities to develop innovative new strategies for preventing crime  punishing criminals and assisting victims             The Problem Solving Court movement has shown great promise in focusing the efforts of both prosecution and defense to look for solutions that may be unique to each defendant and each community     Stabenow said     It is hoped that these judicial innovations will reduce crime and ensure that our courts are viewed by all as effective and fair           This marks the first time that the Bureau of Justice Assistance  a component of DOJ   s Office of Justice Programs  has funded a Problem Solving Criminal Justice Initiative   Research has shown that if implemented properly the problem solving approach can decrease recidivism  reduce crime  improve coordination among justice agencies  enhance services to victims  and increase trust in the justice system   Specifically  this grant will fund a Peacemaker Court  in partnership with the Peacemaker Steering Committee  as a culturally appropriate criminal justice alternative
