                                                                                                                                                              Local mothers recognized for dedication to their community      WASHINGTON     In honor of Mother   s Day  Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   is honoring three    Crime Fighting Moms    in northern Michigan and the Upper Peninsula whose outstanding work in crime prevention efforts has made their communities safer           Citizens who dedicate their time and effort to make the streets safer for their families and the community are an integral part of the success of community policing     said Levin     I am often told by Michigan law enforcement how important community volunteers are in their effort to reduce crime  These Crime Fighting Moms have demonstrated their commitment to improving the quality of life in their communities and they should inspire us all to be more involved            According to preliminary FBI data  violent crime in 2006 was down more than 30 percent since 1994   Officials attribute this promising trend in part to the increased activity of neighborhood law enforcement and safety organizations  which provide the extra    eyes and ears    needed to alert police to potential trouble        In a March 27th letter  Levin asked sheriffs and prosecutors across Michigan to nominate mothers who have been influential in local efforts to reduce crime  Levin sent each of these community activists a certificate of recognition from the U S  Senate         Since 1997  Levin has recognized Mother s Day by presenting  Crime Fighting Mom  awards to Michigan women    A total of 14 women are being honored this year  three of whom are from northern Michigan and the Upper Peninsula   Information on this year   s recipients from northern Michigan and the U P  follows      Cheboygan County  Honoree  Debra Turnbull  Nominated by  Prosecuting Attorney Catherine M  Castagne  As the Executive Director of the Cheboygan Youth Center  Debra Turnbull   s mission has been to keep children safe from drugs and alcohol   She has conducted numerous classes for both adults and children to educate them on substance abuse  and her positive demeanor makes her message effective   Her many hours of dedication to the community have made a difference      Chippewa County  Honoree  Cheryl Jones  Nominated by  Sheriff Jeffrey L  Moran  Cheryl Jones is a dedicated employee of the Chippewa County Prosecutor   s Office as a Victims Crime Advocate and as a trainer for other advocates and forensic interviewers   Ms  Jones truly cares for the well being of people and is very involved with the local TRIAD  Police  Sheriff and Seniors crime prevention and education program  group  organizing monthly meetings and helping to coordinate events      Manistee County  Honoree  Deanne Gancarz  Nominated by  Prosecuting Attorney Ford K  Stone  Deanne Gancarz suffered the loss of a family member in a drunk driving auto accident   Since that time  she has worked tirelessly in the prevention of alcohol related incidents
