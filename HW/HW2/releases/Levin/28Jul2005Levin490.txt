    07 28 2005   Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce More than  1 1 Billion in Annual Funding for Michigan in Multi Year Transportation Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJuly 28  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce More than  1 1 Billion in Annual Funding for Michigan in Multi Year Transportation Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   WASHINGTON    The U S  Senate is expected to approve and send to the president a  286 4 billion multi year transportation funding bill that will provide more than  1 123 billion annually to fund transportation projects in Michigan  and create 61 500 new jobs across the state  U S  Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Carl Levin  D MI  said today        The legislation authorizes surface transportation projects such as highways and highway safety projects through fiscal year 2009   In final negotiations on the legislation this week  Stabenow and Levin won an additional  100 million for Michigan highway work termed Projects of National and Regional Significance          This transportation bill will create good paying jobs in Michigan  provide funds that are desperately needed for Michigan roads  and provide essential assistance to Michigan   s public transportation     Stabenow said     Over the last four years Michigan has lost jobs  and the 61 500 good paying jobs that this bill creates will help thousands of Michigan families pay their mortgages  save for retirement  and pay for the children   s college tuition costs              Funding for transportation infrastructure such as roads  bridges and border crossings is an important investment that increases the mobility of people and goods  enhances economic competitiveness  reduces traffic congestion  and improves air quality     said Levin     In addition  the funds in this bill will have a large and immediate impact on job creation and economic growth throughout Michigan          Michigan would receive more than  594 8 million in transit formula funding  an increase of  167 million  or 39 percent  over TEA 21      In addition to the  594 8 million  as a member of the Senate Banking  Housing and Urban Affairs Committee  I was able to include an additional  164 4 million for bus and rail projects in Michigan     Stabenow said       The bill also allows funds to be used by the State of Michigan for the operation and maintenance of intelligent transportation systems to reduce congestion  The continued use of these systems is critical for Michigan to achieve its air quality goals  as reduced congestion results in lower automobile emissions   Allowing the continued use of these funds to operate ITS systems thus will help the state meet EPA s new ozone standards        The senators noted that        In addition to the  1 123 billion per year  Stabenow and Levin also included a total of  100 million in funding for Michigan   s highway Projects of Regional and National Significance        The  1 123 billion in average annual funding represents an increase of  239 million over TEA 21         Stabenow and Levin said individual projects across Michigan would be announced as details of the final conference report were made available to members of Congress  Both the House and Senate are expected to complete action on the bill this week and send it to the president for his signature
