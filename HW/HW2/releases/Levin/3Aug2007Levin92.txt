    08 03 2007   Levin Hails Senate Approval of    America COMPETES Act                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEAugust 3  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Hails Senate Approval of    America COMPETES Act                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Bill includes important provisions to aid manufacturing and the Great Lakes      WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   hailed Senate approval of the America COMPETES Act conference report  which includes important provisions that support manufacturing in Michigan and benefit the Great Lakes   The bill     officially titled the America Creating Opportunities to Meaningfully Promote Excellence in Technology  Education  and Science Act     is intended to expand opportunities in the areas of technology  science  and education    The Senate approved the bill late last night          American companies can compete with any company in the world if we have a level playing field  but the problem is that our manufacturing companies often are not competing against foreign companies  but foreign governments     Levin said      We need to find ways to support our manufacturers  and two of the programs that have proven successful at doing so    the Advanced Technology Program and the Manufacturing Extension Partnership    have lost funding in the past several years   This bill will give a boost to both programs and will promote many other initiatives to advance America   s competitiveness          The America COMPETES bill includes the following provisions that are important to Michigan    It expands and increases funding for the Advanced Technology Program  ATP      which the bill renames the Technology Innovation Program  TIP      which invests federal R resources in public private partnerships   The program enhances U S  competitiveness by accelerating development  commercialization  and application of promising technologies created by small and medium sized manufacturers   TIP will receive  100 million in 2008 and funding will increase up to  140 5 million in 2010   The funding includes  40 million each year for new grants      The bill increases funding for the Manufacturing Extension Partnership  MEP  to  110 million for FY2008  and continually increases the funding up to  131 8 million in FY2010   The bill puts MEP on a path to doubling over 10 years  The MEP provides small and medium sized manufacturers with the services and resources they need to succeed and compete globally      It includes a Levin provision to include the Great Lakes in R and science education programs of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration      The bill contains a number of provisions that help increase math and science education in the U S  and help to build a vital bridge between universities and private companies  which will help Michigan and our nation to remain on the forefront of innovation    It promotes basic physical science research by calling for a 100  increase over the next ten years  gives tools to new teachers to improve math and science curricula  establishes an Advanced Research Projects Agency for Energy to engage in high risk  high reward energy research  and includes numerous provisions to broaden participation in science and engineering fields at all levels   The bill authorizes a total of  33 6 billion dollars over fiscal years 2008 2010 for science  technology  engineering and math education programs across the federal government
