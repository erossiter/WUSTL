    01 31 2005   Senators Levin  Stabenow Introduce Bipartisan Legislation to Improve Monitoring of Great Lakes Water                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJanuary 31  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin  Stabenow Introduce Bipartisan Legislation to Improve Monitoring of Great Lakes Water                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        WASHINGTON     Michigan Democratic Senators Carl Levin and Debbie Stabenow joined their Republican colleagues from Ohio  Senators Mike DeWine and George Voinovich  today in introducing bipartisan legislation to improve the monitoring of Great Lakes water quality by establishing science based assessments to measure the health of the Great Lakes         The legislation directs the Environmental Protection Agency   s  EPA  Great Lakes National Program Office to work with other federal agencies and Canada to develop indicators of water quality and related environmental factors in the Great Lakes   The legislation also directs EPA to establish a comprehensive network to monitor those indicators regularly throughout the Great Lakes basin  to collect initial benchmark data within four years  and to report to Congress on the changes in water quality   The legislation authorizes  28 million over four years to establish the indicators and monitoring system          The Great Lakes are a treasured natural resource     said Levin      Unfortunately  the lakes have suffered from decades of toxic discharges  urban and agricultural runoff and other environmental challenges   We   ve made some progress in improving water quality  but we still have a long way to go   This legislation moves us in the right direction by giving EPA the mandate to work with other federal agencies and with Canada to measure water quality and other environmental factors on a regular basis   With a better picture of the health of the Great Lakes  we can better meet the most pressing demands             The Great Lakes are a source of safe drinking water for thirty three million people and 20 percent of the world   s fresh water supply     said Stabenow     Millions visit the beaches and swim in the waters each year   While we   ve made good progress protecting our Great Lakes  preventing drilling for oil and gas  and improving water quality  it   s critical that we take the next step   This legislation will ensure that we are collecting critical data needed to develop a real plan to ensure the long term health of the Great Lakes  so that they will continue to be an invaluable national resource          In April 2003  the General Accounting Office  now known as the Government Accountability Office  GAO   issued a report  Great Lakes   An Overall Strategy and Indicators for Measuring Progress Are Needed to Better Achieve Restoration Goals   The report looked at almost 200 federal and state Great Lakes restoration programs and found that a lack of coordination  poorly defined goals and insufficient data on the health of the lakes make it difficult to evaluate the success of the programs   The report also said there are no data collected regularly throughout the Great Lakes to assess water quality and other environmental conditions and the progress of restoration efforts   The GAO report recommended that EPA   s Great Lakes National Program Office lead an effort to develop indicators and a monitoring network       In September 2004  GAO issued a follow up report  Great Lakes   Organizational Leadership and Restoration Goals Need to Be Better Defined for Monitoring Restoration Progress  which reaffirmed its earlier recommendation that EPA develop methods to ensure the Great Lakes monitoring system is    complete  accurate  and consistent          The two reports on the Great Lakes  GAO 03 515 and GAO 04 1024  are available at GAO   s website
