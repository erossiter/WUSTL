    03 28 2005   Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce More than  30 Million in Funding for Detroit Metro Airport Projects                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 28  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce More than  30 Million in Funding for Detroit Metro Airport Projects                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       WASHINGTON    The Federal Aviation Administration has awarded a grant of  1 337 500 for new work and  29 438 854 for completed work at Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport  U S  Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Carl Levin  D MI  said today          This funding supports ongoing improvements at one of the nation   s most important air traffic hubs     Stabenow said     Improvements at Detroit Metro increase air travel safety  boost business and industry  enhance tourism  and ultimately benefit everyone in the state             Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport is one of the busiest airports in the country and these grants will provide for needed projects     said Levin     Travelers flying into and out of the airport will benefit from the safety and infrastructure improvements and those living near the airport will appreciate the noise and environmental mitigation projects underway          The new construction includes installation of in pavement stop bars on an east west runway to improve safety for taxing aircraft in low visibility        Completed work at the airport will continue to receive reimbursement funding from the FAA  The latest round of funding earmarks  17 638 854 for runway  apron and taxiway construction and  11 800 000 for noise mitigation for several hundred residences in the vicinity of the airport
