    07 13 2006   Levin Amendment Would Halt Canadian Trash If Homeland Security   Department Cannot Prove It Is Adequately Screened                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJuly 13  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Amendment Would Halt Canadian Trash If Homeland Security   Department Cannot Prove It Is Adequately Screened                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WASHINGTON     The Senate today approved an amendment by Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   that would require the Department of Homeland Security to stop trash trucks from entering the United States if the department cannot prove that the methods and technologies used to screen the trucks for chemical  nuclear  biological  and radiological weapons are as effective as the screening given to other commercial vehicles crossing the border   Sen  Debbie Stabenow  D Mich   and Sen  George Voinovich  R Ohio  cosponsored Levin   s amendment          We have seen significant evidence that Canadian trash trucks pose a security risk and that the trucks are not screened adequately     Levin said     If we can   t be sure the cargo is safe  the trash should not be let in             There is no issue more important than keeping our communities safe     said Stabenow      At the very least  we need to be sure Canadian trash trucks are thoroughly screened and inspected for threats   This is critical for our security  and I congratulate Senator Levin for his hard work on this issue          The Levin amendment would give the Bureau of Customs and Border Protection 90 days to assess and report to Congress whether or not the screening of municipal solid waste is comparable to its screening of other items of commerce entering the U S   The report would also identify actions the Bureau would take to achieve the same level of effectiveness in the screening of municipal solid waste  if in fact inadequacies are found   The Bureau would then be given 180 days to implement these changes   If the changes are not fully implemented  municipal solid waste trucks would not be allowed to enter the U S       The amendment is included in the Department of Homeland Security appropriations bill for the 2007 fiscal year   After final Senate passage  the bill will be considered by a conference committee that resolves the differences between the bills passed by the House and Senate
