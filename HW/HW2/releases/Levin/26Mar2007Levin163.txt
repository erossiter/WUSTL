    03 26 2007   Levin Introduces Bill to Provide Loans   to Expanding Small Businesses                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 26  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Introduces Bill to Provide Loans   to Expanding Small Businesses                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      WASHINGTON   Citing the important role played by small businesses in creating jobs and fueling the economy  Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   introduced a bill today that would establish a Small Business Intermediary Lending Pilot Program to provide midsize loans to help meet the needs of small businesses          This pilot lending program is aimed at businesses that need loans larger than those available under the SBA Microloan Program but for a variety of reasons   including lack of sufficient or conventional collateral   are unable to secure the credit they need at the terms they need through conventional lenders   Levin said   By helping start up and expanding small businesses  we will help to create jobs  including jobs for low income individuals   We will help to stimulate the economy by facilitating new lending and investing in businesses        The pilot lending program is designed to work through local non profit lending intermediaries   This proposal authorizes the SBA to make one percent  20 year loans on a competitive basis to up to 20 non profit lending intermediaries around the country   These loans would be used to capitalize a revolving loan fund through which the intermediary would make loans of between  35 000 and  200 000 to small businesses        Many states  including Michigan  are fortunate to have a healthy network of community based  non profit intermediary lenders that are experienced and successful in meeting the needs of these businesses   This pilot program will give them additional tools to help them create badly needed jobs among small businesses       Levin introduced similar legislation in the 108th Congress  which was approved by the Senate but never enacted into law  and in the 109th Congress  which was approved by the Small Business Committee but not considered by the full Senate
