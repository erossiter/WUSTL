                   Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASENovember 14  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Statement on the President   s Meeting with the Big Three Automakers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   issued the following statement on the meeting the President held today with General Motors chairman and CEO Rick Wagoner  Ford Motor Company president and CEO Alan Mulally  and Chrysler Group president and CEO Tom LaSorda          In meeting with the U S  automotive industry today the President finally heard a list of actions the federal government can take to help level the playing field for U S  manufacturing   Some of those actions I hope the President will take include insisting on ending harmful currency manipulation  specifically by Japan  China and Korea  opening export markets for U S  goods by increasing the focus on non tariff barriers  identifying federal initiatives to combat rising health care costs  and providing incentives for the development of advanced automotive technologies and an infrastructure for alternative fuels   We need to turn around the drain of manufacturing jobs in the United States    We   ve already lost 2 9 million manufacturing jobs lost since 2001 with a large chunk of those coming from the automotive sector   The federal government needs to be an aggressive and active partner with our manufacturers just as other governments are for their manufacturers         All 17 members of the Michigan Congressional Delegation wrote a letter to President George W  Bush following his meeting with the Big Three automakers calling for  vigorous federal action  to address the problems facing the domestic auto industry   You can read the letter
