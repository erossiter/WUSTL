                 Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEDecember 8  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin  Stabenow Hail Passage of Bill to Study Monroe County River Raisin Sites for Inclusion in National Park System                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Remember the Raisin    spread across America as battle cry to avenge the massacre of wounded American soldiers      WASHINGTON     Senators Carl Levin and Debbie Stabenow  both D Mich   today hailed the passage of the River Raisin National Battlefield Study Act to examine the suitability and feasibility of including sites relating to the River Raisin Massacre in Monroe County  Michigan  as part of the U S  National Park System   In January of 1813  U S  forces clashed fiercely with their British and Native American enemies in the Battles of the River Raisin during the War of 1812   Dozens of wounded American soldiers were massacred after the battle  which prompted a national battle cry to    Remember the Raisin    and avenge their deaths          The Battle of the River Raisin and the massacre that followed hold a distinct place in our nation   s history     said Levin      With the 200th anniversary of the War of 1812 approaching  now is an important time to recognize and celebrate this piece of our heritage   While these events are well known in Michigan  the story remains largely untold at the national level and I am hopeful that this study will help to change that             Including the River Raisin battlefield in our National Park system is an important step in honoring Michigan   s past and paying tribute to those who fought to protect our state and our nation     said Stabenow      This legislation will help preserve Michigan   s rich history and I thank Senator Levin for his leadership on this issue          Between January 18 and 22  1813  about 400 American soldiers and militiamen were killed as they fought the Battles of the River Raisin to re take Frenchtown  now Monroe  a critical step in the campaign to re take the crucial port of Detroit   American forces had to leave many of the wounded behind as they retreated   Early on the morning of January 23  Native Americans allied with the British killed these wounded American soldiers   Of the approximately 1000 Americans who fought in these fierce clashes  only 33 escaped death or captivity   As news of the deaths of helpless Americans spread across the country  an intense fervor grew around the battle cry to    Remember the Raisin     helping to rally American forces in the War of 1812       The River Raisin National Battlefield Study Act was sponsored by Congressman John D  Dingell  D Dearborn  in the House and by Senators Levin  Stabenow and Jim Bunning  R Ky   in the Senate   The study will recommend whether these sites should be included within the National Park System
