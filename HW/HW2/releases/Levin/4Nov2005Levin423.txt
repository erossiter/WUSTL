    11 04 2005   Bipartisan Group of Great Lakes Congress Members  Urge President to Expand Resources for Great Lakes Restoration                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASENovember 4  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Bipartisan Group of Great Lakes Congress Members  Urge President to Expand Resources for Great Lakes Restoration                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            WASHINGTON    Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   helped lead a bipartisan group of Congress members from the Great Lakes region in writing the President regarding the need for greater resources for Great Lakes restoration   A recent report by the Great Lakes Interagency Task Force indicated that recommendations for a collaborative restoration strategy need to stay within current budget projections  contradicting earlier administration indications that they would consider new budget initiatives   In the letter  the Congress members voice their concern over this revised position           When Region Collaboration was initiated last year  nothing indicated that the Regional Collaboration Strategy needed to stay within current budget projections  and we were led to believe that the administration would consider some new budget initiatives     the members wrote      A serious consideration of the challenges faced in the Great Lakes region requires a commitment of federal resources           The members write about the need for all levels of government to work together to achieve meaningful Great Lakes restoration      The problems impacting the Great Lakes will only get worse over time  and the implementation of the solutions will only become more expensive     they wrote           We cannot wait to start restoring the Great Lakes to a healthy condition for future generations   The Great Lakes are vital to our cities     states    and the nation   s environment  economy and way of life     they concluded      We appreciate your past support for improving the Great Lakes and strongly urge that you maintain your commitment to the Great Lakes by not limiting restoration recommendations to the current budget projections
