    04 25 2007   Levin Secures Provisions in    America COMPETES Act     to Aid Manufacturing and the Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEApril 25  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Secures Provisions in    America COMPETES Act     to Aid Manufacturing and the Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON     As the Senate completes consideration of the America COMPETES Act today  Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   secured the inclusion of provisions that will support manufacturing in Michigan and benefit the Great Lakes   The bill is intended to expand opportunities in the areas of technology  science  and education           American companies can compete with any company in the world if we have a level playing field  but the problem is that our manufacturing companies often are not competing against foreign companies  but foreign governments     Levin said      Two of the programs that have helped to give a boost to our manufacturing companies are the Advanced Technology Program and the Manufacturing Extension Partnership  but they have both lost funding in the past several years   This bill would help to fund both of these important programs           The America COMPETES bill includes the following provisions that are important to Michigan           The bill significantly increases the authorization for the National Institute for Standards and Technology  NIST   which administers the Advanced Technology Program  ATP    Levin secured a commitment from Energy and Natural Resources Committee Chairman Jeff Bingaman  D N M   to support a funding increase for the ATP in the conference committee between the Senate and the House of Representatives   The ATP invests federal R resources in public private partnerships  enhancing U S  competitiveness by accelerating development  commercialization  and application of promising technologies  and by improving manufacturing techniques of small and medium sized manufacturers             Levin authored an amendment  which was approved by the Senate by unanimous consent  to include the Great Lakes in R and science education programs of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration           Levin cosponsored an amendment to increase funding for the Manufacturing Extension Partnership  MEP  to  115 million for FY2008  and continually increasing the funding up to  142 million in FY2011   The MEP provides small and medium sized manufacturers with the services and resources they need to succeed and compete globally
