    01 20 2006   Levin Statement on the Iraqi Election Results                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJanuary 20  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Statement on the Iraqi Election Results                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich  released the following statement today on the results of the Iraqi election          Now that the Iraqi election results are in and Iraq is about to form a new government and constitutional commission  the Bush Administration must make our position crystal clear to the Iraqis   That message should be  our willingness to commit further lives and resources to your future depends on your willingness to amend your constitution so there is a future as an Iraqi nation          Once a new government is formed  the Iraqis will have four months to amend their current constitution   That constitution is divisive and an obstacle to political unity   A recent development has been discouraging in that regard    the head of the main Shiite party said last week that he is not interested in making the necessary changes            The Bush Administration has failed thus far to pressure the Iraqi parties to make the necessary constitutional changes  instead calling the current constitution a    bold constitution that guarantees the rule of law and freedom of assembly  and property rights  and freedom of speech and the press  and women s rights  and the right to vote      The President s effusive praise is the wrong message because it reduces the likelihood that the necessary compromises will be made          Only the Iraqis can make the necessary changes   They are more likely to do so if the Bush Administration changes course and sends the message that the new Iraqi government must amend the constitution to share power and resources more fairly
