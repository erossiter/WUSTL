    09 25 2006   Detroit to Receive Nearly  2 Million in Homeland Security Grants                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASESeptember 25  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Detroit to Receive Nearly  2 Million in Homeland Security Grants                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Port of Detroit will receive more than  1 million in security funding      WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Carl Levin  D MI  today announced that  1 900 644 in federal grants from the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS  will be allocated for port and transit security in Detroit          As one of the nation   s busiest border cities  Detroit faces many unique security challenges     Stabenow said     This funding will help secure Detroit   s waterfront and transportation systems  ensuring that trade  travel and economic activity between the U S  and Canada are protected              It is critical that we do everything we can to protect commerce in and around Detroit     said Levin      Southeast Michigan is an important commercial hub in the state and across the country  and these federal dollars will help to keep people and goods flowing freely and securely             The Detroit River is the State of Michigan   s    international maritime highway     seeing over 5 000 trips through Detroit annually by hundreds of domestic and international freighters     said Curtis Hertel  Executive Director of the Detroit Wayne County Port Authority      I want to thank Senator Stabenow  Senator Levin and Congresswoman Carolyn Cheeks Kilpatrick for helping to secure federal funds to improve security at the Port of Detroit         Port Security Grant Program  The Detroit Wayne County Port Authority will receive  1 024 815 to enhance waterfront security at the Port of Detroit   Senators Stabenow and Levin led members of the Michigan Congressional delegation last year in a letter to DHS Secretary Michael Chertoff stressing the importance of the Port of Detroit and urging federal support  The Detroit Wayne County Port Authority has identified a number of security needs including fencing  surveillance systems  communication systems  lighting and access control       Transit Security Grant Program   The City of Detroit will receive  507 797 for rail system security funding   The Detroit Department of Transportation and Suburban Mobility Authority for Regional Transportation  SMART  will receive  368 032 for intra city bus system security
