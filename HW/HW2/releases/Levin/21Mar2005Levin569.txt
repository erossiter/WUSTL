    03 21 2005   Levin Releases Previously Withheld Portions of FBI Document Critical of DOD Interrogation Practices                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 21  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Releases Previously Withheld Portions of FBI Document Critical of DOD Interrogation Practices                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   today released previously withheld portions of an FBI document critical of interrogation practices used by the Department of Defense  DOD  at Guantanamo Bay in 2002  disclosing information in that document previously withheld by the Department of Justice  DOJ    In a letter to DOJ on February 10  2005  Levin and Sen  Joe Lieberman  D Conn   had requested reconsideration of the decision to withhold portions of that FBI document and  in response  DOJ released a new version of the FBI document with the additional information         The newly disclosed information reveals that DOJ attorneys expressed concerns about DOD interrogation practices to the DOD General Counsel   s office   The new version also discloses that the law enforcement agencies at Guantanamo Bay considered the results obtained from certain DOD interrogations to be    suspect at best       Also disclosed are the names of DOJ Criminal Division senior personnel who met with FBI officials and discussed concerns about DOD interrogation practices          The facts related to interrogation practices used against some detainees are slowly being forced to the surface and we will keep pushing for more     said Levin     Today we were able to obtain some information that had previously been blacked out in an FBI document critical of DOD interrogation practices    As I suspected  the previously withheld information had nothing to do with protecting intelligence sources or methods  and everything to do with protecting DOD from embarrassment          DOJ has indicated that it is awaiting word from DOD about releasing still more withheld information in the FBI document   Levin has also asked DOJ why the names of some FBI personnel are still being withheld       A copy of the document is attached  with the previously withheld information highlighted
