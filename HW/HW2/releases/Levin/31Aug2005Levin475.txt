    08 31 2005   Levin Asks VA Secretary for Answers on Great Lakes National Veterans    Cemetery in Holly                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEAugust 31  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Asks VA Secretary for Answers on Great Lakes National Veterans    Cemetery in Holly                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     WASHINGTON     In a letter to Secretary of Veterans Affairs James Nicholson  Senator Carl Levin  D Mich   asked for answers on the continuing construction delays at the Great Lakes National Cemetery in Holly  Michigan  and the proposed resolution to the problem         Levin attended the groundbreaking ceremony for Great Lakes National Cemetery last October   At that time  burials were slated to begin in May  and phase one of the construction was to have been completed by this summer   However  construction delays have prevented any burials from taking place   Families  veterans    organizations and funeral homes have expressed their concerns to Levin about this situation        In his letter  Levin asked Secretary Nicholson for answers on the terms of the contract  the timeline  remedial actions  congressional oversight and the absolute deadline to open the first phase of the cemetery   Levin wrote     The sacred remains of their loved ones are being held in various temporary locations until this facility is able to accommodate them  and it is simply unacceptable to delay the closure and finality that these families deserve          Read  to Secretary Nicholson
