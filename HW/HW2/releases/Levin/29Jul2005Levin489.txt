                 Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJuly 29  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Stabenow  Levin  Announce Funding for Keweenaw  Sleeping Bear Dunes  Other Michigan Projects in Interior Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Final version of bill adds  400 000 for Fort Gratiot lighthouse      WASHINGTON     The fiscal year 2005 Interior Appropriations bill  approved by the Senate today  includes millions in funding for Michigan water and sewer infrastructure projects  as well as funding for Keweenaw National Historic Park  Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore  and the Automobile National Heritage Area  U S  Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Carl Levin  D MI  said today   Passed by both the House and Senate  the bill now goes to the president for his signature          I was pleased to be able to add an addition  400 000 to this bill for the Fort Gratiot lighthouse  and I am proud of the other funding that Sen  Levin and I have been able to obtain to protect Michigan   s environment and enhance its beautiful national parks    Stabenow said     In particular  these investments in Keweenaw National Historical Park  Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore and the Automobile National Heritage Area will benefit all who visit these parks and aid our efforts to promote Michigan tourism             This bill provides funding for some of our state s most cherished landmarks  including Keweenaw National Historic Park  Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore and the Automobile National Heritage Area     said Levin      The  5 3 million we were able to secure for Sleeping Bear Dunes is the result of many years of effort   The park will use the money to purchase 104 acres of land along the Crystal River and preserve this unique  pristine property for generations to come          Specific appropriations of interest to Michigan include the
