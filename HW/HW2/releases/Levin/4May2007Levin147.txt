                 Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMay 4  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Statement on Draft CAFE Proposal                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   made the following statement regarding the draft CAFE proposal circulated by the Senate Commerce Committee today          Our goal is to find a solution that will reduce oil consumption and reduce greenhouse gas emissions but does not discriminate against American manufacturers or cost American jobs   The new draft is an improvement in one sense in that it clarifies that it would not require each company to meet an unrealistic combined car truck standard  and it would also appear to give NHTSA greater discretion in setting standards between now and 2020   However  the new draft is complicated and has a lot of problems  including some issues that were not present in the first draft   For instance  it includes a requirement to increase CAFE standards by four percent per year after 2020   I continue to believe that more progress can be made in reducing oil consumption and greenhouse gas emissions if we focus our resources on leap ahead technologies instead of forcing companies to make incremental improvements to meet an arbitrary standard set by the Congress
