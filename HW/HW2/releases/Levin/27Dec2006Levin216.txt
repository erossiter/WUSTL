                                                                                                                                                              WASHINGTON    U S  Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Carl Levin  D MI  announced today that Skandia West Branch Volunteer Fire Department will receive  23 242 to pay for fire operations and fire safety programs  including training  equipment  personal protective equipment  wellness and fitness  and modifications to facilities   The funds are being awarded through the fiscal year 2006 Assistance to Firefighters Grant program          Protecting our communities begins by ensuring that Michigan   s first responders have the best equipment and training available     Stabenow said      I am pleased that these funds will help the brave men and women that protect Skandia and West Branch do their jobs as safely and effectively as possible             This grant will help the Skandia West Branch Volunteer Fire Department fulfill its mission of protecting the public     said Levin      Our firefighters and emergency medical personnel put themselves on the line every day and deserve our support          The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  administered by the U S  Department of Homeland Security s Office of Grants and Training  in cooperation with the U S  Fire Administration  helps rural  urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States to prepare for emergencies and acts of terrorism  This is the 11th round of supplemental awards for fiscal year 2006  with two fire departments in Michigan receiving a total of  187 474 in federal funds
