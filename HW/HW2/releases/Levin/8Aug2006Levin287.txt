    08 08 2006   Levin  Stabenow Introduce Bill to Study Monroe County River Raisin Sites for Inclusion in National Park System                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEAugust 8  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin  Stabenow Introduce Bill to Study Monroe County River Raisin Sites for Inclusion in National Park System                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Remember the Raisin    spread across America as battle cry to avenge the massacre of wounded American soldiers      WASHINGTON     Senators Carl Levin and Debbie Stabenow  both D Mich   today announced the introduction of legislation to study the suitability and feasibility of including sites relating to the River Raisin Massacre in Monroe County  Michigan  as part of the U S  National Park system   In January of 1813  American forces clashed fiercely with their British and Native American enemies in the Battles of the River Raisin during the War of 1812   Dozens of wounded American soldiers were massacred after the battle  which prompted a national battle cry to    Remember the Raisin    and avenge their deaths          The Battle of the River Raisin and the massacre that followed hold a distinct place in our nation   s history     said Levin      With the 200th anniversary of the War of 1812 quickly approaching  it is critical that we act now to properly recognize and celebrate this important part of our heritage   While these events are well known in Michigan  the story remains largely untold at the national level and including these important sites in our National Park system will go a long way towards changing that             Allowing the River Raisin battlefield to become part of our National Park System is an important step in honoring Michigan   s past     said Stabenow      By ensuring that future generations have access to this national historic treasure  we preserve the memories of the individuals who gave their lives to protect our state and our nation          Between January 18 and 22  1813  about 400 American soldiers and militiamen were killed as they fought the Battles of the River Raisin to re take Frenchtown  now Monroe  a critical step in the campaign to re take the crucial port of Detroit   American forces had to leave many of the wounded behind as they retreated   Early on the morning of January 23  Native Americans allied with the British killed these wounded American soldiers   Of the approximately 1000 Americans who fought in these fierce clashes  only 33 escaped death or captivity   As news of the deaths of helpless Americans spread across the country  an intense fervor grew around the battle cry to    Remember the Raisin     helping to rally American forces in the War of 1812       The River Raisin National Battlefield Study Act is sponsored by Congressman John D  Dingell  D Dearborn  in the House and co sponsored by Senator Jim Bunning  R Ky   in the Senate   The study will recommend whether these sites should be included within the National Park system
