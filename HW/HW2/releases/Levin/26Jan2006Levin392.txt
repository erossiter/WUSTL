    01 26 2006   Levin Disputes President s Comments on U S  Auto Manufacturers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJanuary 26  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Disputes President s Comments on U S  Auto Manufacturers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              WASHINGTON  DC    Reacting to the Wall Street Journal   s report today that President Bush said that U S  auto manufacturers    should develop    a product that   s relevant       Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   issued the following statement         During the Bush Administration  we have seen a loss of 2 8 million manufacturing jobs  whereas we actually gained manufacturing jobs during the prior eight years            Saying the American auto industry does not make    relevant products    is like saying the consumer electronics industry doesn   t make relevant products   That industry and so many other manufacturers have been driven overseas even though their products are in great demand in the U S   The President is simply blaming the auto industry without acknowledging that there are serious imbalances in the playing field of trade            U S  automakers are making    relevant products     but they are also struggling with immense costs not faced by their foreign counterparts  including ballooning pension and health care costs   They are competing with currency manipulation by other countries  including China and Japan  which gives their vehicles and other products an unfair price advantage in our market    Our manufacturers also face trading partners that have markets that are closed to U S  products  and a number of other unfair trade practices           Auto manufacturers  like all manufacturers  have a responsibility to make products that respond to the market   But our government has a responsibility to fight for a level playing field for our manufacturers and to understand the challenges faced by our companies   We need a government that will fight as hard for our manufacturing industries and jobs as other governments are fighting for theirs   The Bush Administration has refused so far to engage in that task   If it fails to take initiatives on manufacturing to allow manufacturers to compete fairly  millions more manufacturing jobs will be lost on President Bush   s watch
