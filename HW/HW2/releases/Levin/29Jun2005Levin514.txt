    06 29 2005   Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce  1 744 636 in Funds to Help Protect Michigan   s Critical Infrastructure                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJune 29  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce  1 744 636 in Funds to Help Protect Michigan   s Critical Infrastructure                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    WASHINGTON    The state of Michigan has received a grant of more than  1 7 million from the U S  Department of Homeland Security to help the state protect critical infrastructure like power plants and  bridges  U S  Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Carl Levin  D MI  said today       Termed a Buffer Zone Protection Program grant  the  1 744 636 allocation is intended to help local law enforcement agencies and first responders deal with potential terrorist attacks by expanding the protective zone around critical infrastructure and key resources           Michigan   s critical infrastructure     such as chemical plants  dams  financial institutions  and of course  our major bridges     is protected by our first responders  and they greatly need this assistance     Stabenow said     Local law enforcement  who know their own communities better than anyone else  have been protecting their critical pieces of infrastructure  but we need to give them the resources to do the job            Protecting our state   s vital and most critical sites is no easy task  but our local law enforcement agencies have been doing an outstanding job     said Levin     We must continue to support their efforts to ensure they have the resources necessary to do their jobs  As a border state  Michigan is faced with unique challenges and this funding will help them address those challenges           Among other uses  the funds can be used to obtain key equipment  including interoperable communications gear  that can link law enforcement agencies  The funds can also be used to obtain biological  chemical and radiological detection equipment  install physical barriers around potential targets  or    harden    potential targets     making them less vulnerable to attack       Stabenow noted that when she conducted her statewide tour  meeting with hundreds of local law enforcement officials  the problem of interoperable communications gear was one of their biggest concerns       The Department of Homeland Security defines    critical infrastructure and key resources    as sites that    are potential terrorist targets deemed most crucial in terms of national level public health and safety  governance  economic and national security  and public confidence consequences          The Michigan State Police is the state agency designated to distribute the grant funds in Michigan
