    06 29 2006   Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce  4 Million for Biotechnology Research and Development                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJune 29  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce  4 Million for Biotechnology Research and Development                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Research Consortium includes University of Michigan  Michigan State University and Michigan Technological University      WASHINGTON     U S  Senators Carl Levin  D MI  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  today announced the approval of a  4 million biotechnology research grant to take greater advantage of previously under utilized agricultural resources  including the development of alternative ways to produce ethanol   The funding was approved by the Senate Appropriations Committee as part of the FY 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations bill  and will be awarded to the Consortium for Plant Biotechnology Research  CPBR           The development of biotechnology and alternative fuels such as ethanol is a critical step towards ending our dependence on fossil fuels     said Levin      Dramatically better ways of producing ethanol are within reach with a reasonable commitment of federal dollars   CPBR studies have a solid record of encouraging private research and producing valuable results  and this funding will help them continue their important research             Further development of the biofuels industry will create good jobs and help farmers create new markets for Michigan crops     said Stabenow      By the end of this year  Michigan will have five ethanol plants running  refining 279 million gallons of corn based ethanol and creating hundreds of new jobs   We need to continue down this path  paving the way for us to buy our fuel from middle America instead of the Middle East          CPBR is a non profit corporation whose membership includes 40 leading U S  research universities and 36 agribusiness companies and trade associations including the University of Michigan  Michigan State University and Michigan Technological University   The federal funding will be distributed through a competitive application process   In the past  CPBR has funded research into converting forest and agricultural residues and other energy crops into ethanol and creating biodegradable plant derived plastics at a cost competitive with petroleum derived plastics       The bill will now move to the full Senate for consideration
