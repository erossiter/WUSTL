    11 04 2005   Levin Amendment for Demonstration Projects on Interoperability of Communications Included in Senate Passed Budget Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASENovember 4  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Amendment for Demonstration Projects on Interoperability of Communications Included in Senate Passed Budget Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      WASHINGTON     The Senate approved on Thursday an amendment authored by Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   that would establish at least two International Border Community Interoperable Communications Demonstration Projects   The projects would assess the international communications needs of border agents and emergency first responders          The ability to communicate quickly and easily is an integral part of keeping our borders safe and secure     said Levin      First responders in border states like Michigan face unique challenges and must be able to communicate with a number of federal  state and local agencies in addition to police  firefighters and emergency medical personnel in the event of a large scale disaster or terrorist attack   What is often overlooked is that first responders near border crossings must also be able to maintain seamless communication with their Canadian or Mexican counterparts across the border   These demonstration projects will help ensure that those needs are met          At least one demonstration project would be established at both the northern and southern borders   The goals of the demonstration projects include        Evaluating communication equipment needs   Fostering the standardization of interoperable communications equipment   Identifying solutions that will facilitate communications across national borders   Ensuring that emergency response providers can communicate with one another during an emergency   Providing training and equipment for first responders at the northern and southern borders      The amendment was included in the Senate   s budget reconciliation bill   The projects would be established by the Secretary of Homeland Security and would report their findings to the Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs
