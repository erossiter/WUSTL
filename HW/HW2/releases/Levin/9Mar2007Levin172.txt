    03 09 2007   Levin Co Sponsors Bill to Establish Commission Commemorating the War of 1812                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 9  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Co Sponsors Bill to Establish Commission Commemorating the War of 1812                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON     Senator Carl Levin  D MI  this week joined a bipartisan group of colleagues in introducing The Star Spangled Banner and War of 1812 Bicentennial Commission Act to ensure a suitable national observance of the 200th anniversary of the War of 1812  which includes the Battle of the River Raisin   In Monroe County  Michigan  in January of 1813  U S  forces clashed fiercely with their British and Native American enemies in the Battles of the River Raisin during the War of 1812   Dozens of wounded American soldiers were massacred after the battle in what came to be known as the River Raisin Massacre  prompting a national battle cry to    Remember the Raisin    and avenge their deaths          Establishing a Bicentennial Commission would help to ensure that the 200th anniversary of the War of 1812 is appropriately recognized and celebrated in Michigan and across the country     said Levin  a co sponsor of the bill     The Battle of the River Raisin and the massacre that followed are prominent in Michigan   s history  but this important part of our heritage is largely unknown at the national level   I am pleased that this commemoration of the bicentennial will help to promote the River Raisin battlefield in Monroe  the site of one of the bloodiest and most important battles of the War of 1812          The bill  authored by Senator Ben Cardin  D MD   would establish a 21 member commission to commemorate the 200th anniversary of the War of 1812 by encouraging War of 1812 observances  supporting international involvement  facilitating marketing efforts for a commemorative coin  stamp and related activities  promoting the protection of important War of 1812 resources and assisting in the appropriate development of heritage tourism       Between January 18 and 22  1813  about 400 American soldiers and militiamen were killed as they fought the Battles of the River Raisin to re take Frenchtown  now Monroe  a critical step in the campaign to re take the crucial port of Detroit   American forces had to leave many of the wounded behind as they retreated   Early on the morning of January 23  Native Americans allied with the British killed these wounded American soldiers   Of the approximately 1000 Americans who fought in these fierce clashes  only 33 escaped death or captivity   As news of the deaths of helpless Americans spread across the country  an intense fervor grew around the battle cry to    Remember the Raisin     helping to rally American forces in the War of 1812
