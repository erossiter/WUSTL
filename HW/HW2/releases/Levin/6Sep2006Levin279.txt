    09 06 2006   Statement of Senator Carl Levin   on the new Field Manual on Interrogations                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASESeptember 6  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Statement of Senator Carl Levin   on the new Field Manual on Interrogations                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   the ranking Democrat on the Senate Armed Services Committee  today made the following statement regarding the Department of Defense   s new Field Manual on Interrogations     Pending a comprehensive review of the Department of Defense   s Field Manual on Interrogations  I am reassured by the briefings I have received  particularly that it has been reviewed and approved by the senior military lawyers of the Armed Forces        If the Administration had listened to its military lawyers several years ago  much of the damage done to our credibility caused by detainee mistreatment in Afghanistan  Guantanamo and at Abu Ghraib might have been avoided        The briefers have assured us that the Field Manual complies with the Detainee Treatment Act of 2005 and its prohibition against subjecting detainees to cruel  inhuman or degrading treatment or punishment  with the standards of Common Article 3 of the Geneva Conventions  and with the Supreme Court decision on Hamdan          The Field Manual is important because it will set the standard for the interrogation and treatment of our troops  should they be taken prisoner
