    01 31 2006   Levin Reaction to State of the Union Address                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJanuary 31  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Reaction to State of the Union Address                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Washington     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich  issued the following statement regarding the President   s State of the Union address     The President tried to strike an optimistic tone tonight  but the details he offered were not encouraging       The President is right that we need to break our nation   s addiction to oil  but the proposals he offered are not adequate to the task   We need a comprehensive  Apollo type program with resources to match the vision                     At the same time  we must use this initiative to achieve another goal that the President didn   t mention at all tonight     revitalizing our domestic manufacturing industry   The government must be far more active in leveling the playing field for domestic manufacturers by reducing catastrophic health care costs to businesses and making our trading partners play by the rules  including ending currency manipulation   We need to make far reaching changes in the tax code if we are going to keep jobs in America and encourage manufacturers to develop and use advanced technologies       Also  tonight  while the President acknowledged rising health care costs  he ignored the elephant in the room  the Republican Part D Medicare prescription drug benefit is a disaster   Since the program began  we have seen real confusion and anger from seniors who are facing a bewildering array of options  who have often actually lost the retiree drug coverage they had  or who have to pay more for prescription drugs than they did previously       We also need to stop insurance companies from arbitrarily dropping covered drugs from their plans   The President   s failure to address the massive problems with the new Medicare prescription drug law will be deeply disappointing to millions of seniors     I was also disappointed the President did not make a more urgent call for lobbying and ethics reform   We need to do more than change the tone in Washington     we need to change the way business is done   Unless the President provides real leadership on ethics reform  including within his own Administration  abuses of the past few years will continue  and the American people will continue to pay the price in higher health care costs  in higher heating bills  and higher gasoline prices
