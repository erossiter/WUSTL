                 Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEFebruary 10  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce  453 774 in Firefighter Grants for Michigan                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               WASHINGTON     Senators Carl Levin  D Mich   and Debbie Stabenow  D Mich   announced today that five fire departments and one department of public safety in Michigan have received  453 774 in grants from the U S  Department of Homeland Security  Departments in Caro  Grand Rapids  Metamora  Saginaw and Suttons Bay will receive funding for fire operations and firefighter safety programs   Plainfield Township Fire Department in Hale will receive funding through the firefighting vehicles acquisition program          It is very important to equip our first responders with the tools necessary to do their jobs effectively and safety     said Levin     Providing funds for new vehicles  training  and equipment will help keep our communities and our firefighters safer             Every community in Michigan increasingly counts on its local fire department to protect citizens and property from a variety of hazardous situations     Stabenow said     These grants to six different agencies will help local firefighters protect the communities they serve  while protecting themselves with better equipment  training and conditioning          These grants have been provided through the 2004 Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  a joint effort of the Department of Homeland Security  the Federal Emergency Management Agency and U S  Fire Administration   The fire operations and firefighter safety programs provide firefighters with equipment  training and modifications to facilities  The firefighting vehicles acquisition program allows departments to purchase vehicles such as ambulances  rescue vehicles  fireboats and brush
