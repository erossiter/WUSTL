Trash Deal                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Ontario announces landfill expansion to meet terms of Stabenow Levin agreement      WASHINGTON     U S  Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Carl Levin  D MI  today announced that the Ontario Ministry of the Environment has approved plans to expand the Township of Warwick landfill in order to meet the terms of the Stabenow Levin agreement  which will phase out all municipally managed waste coming to Michigan by 2010  The Stabenow Levin agreement requires a 40 percent reduction of municipally managed waste by the end of 2008  and this landfill expansion is a critical component in meeting that goal          Everyone in Michigan should be encouraged by this concrete step taken by the Canadian government to implement our agreement and stop the importation of trash     said Stabenow      We will continue to work with Ontario to make sure they meet their obligation to stop all municipally managed waste coming into Michigan by 2010   This is a major step toward the ultimate goal of stopping all trash that comes across the Canadian border into our great state             I m pleased to hear that Ontario is moving forward in working to stop its waste shipments to Michigan     said Levin      As Ontario is more than four times larger than Michigan  they should be able to find somewhere to dump their own trash  and I m pleased to see this is happening          The expansion of the Warwick landfill in Ontario will stop 121 000 metric tons of municipally managed waste from coming to Carlton Farms landfill located in Wayne County by 2008   Under the terms of the Stabenow Levin agreement  there will be a 20 percent reduction of Canadian trash by the end of this year  a 40 percent reduction by the end of 2008  and a complete end to these shipments by 2010
