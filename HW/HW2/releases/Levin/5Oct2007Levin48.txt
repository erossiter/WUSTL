    10 05 2007   Levin  Community Development Dollars Coming to Port Huron                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEOctober 5  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin  Community Development Dollars Coming to Port Huron                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   today announced that a community development investment company headed by Citizens First Savings Bank in Port Huron has been awarded  15 million in tax credits to stimulate economic and community development and create jobs through the Treasury Department   s New Market Tax Credit  NMTC  program   The federal tax credits will be used by the development company to attract investors from the private sector to finance projects aimed at revitalizing downtown Port Huron          By backing a centralized engine to bring resources  ideas and residents into the downtown area  this funding will help stimulate development in Port Huron     said Levin      These federal tax credits will provide the glue needed to bring savvy local investors together with community development experts for the benefit of the entire region          Senator Levin has actively supported an increase in funding for the Community Development Financial Institutions  CDFI  Fund  which administers the NMTC program   Under this program  Community Development Entities  CDEs  are allocated funding in the form of tax credits to work as local  privately managed investment vehicles   Investors in the CDEs are provided these tax credits  and the CDEs are required to use the investments to finance development activities in the community       The Port Huron CDE has pledged to use this allocation to revitalize downtown Port Huron and alleviate high rates of vacancy and unemployment   They will target real estate and business investments and other job creation engines with a focus on health care  industry  mixed use projects and shared community facilities   They will also offer financing at rates up to 50 per cent below market to ensure scheduled projects are able to proceed          This important federal program could provide a significant boost for Michigan  and I will work with the Treasury to raise awareness and encourage more successful CDEs across the state     added Levin      The potential benefits are immense for communities throughout Michigan in need of a spark to attract critical funds for restoration and redevelopment          Information on CDFI programs  including help
