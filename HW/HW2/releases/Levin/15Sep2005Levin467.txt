    09 15 2005   Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce a  2 3 Million Grant for Lansing to Help Emergency Personal Communicate During a Crisis                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASESeptember 15  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce a  2 3 Million Grant for Lansing to Help Emergency Personal Communicate During a Crisis                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Interoperable communications grant will aid in first responder coordination      WASHINGTON     Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Carl Levin  D MI  announced today that the Department of Justice has awarded  2 304 405 to the City of Lansing to develop an integrated communications system that will allow law enforcement and other first responders to talk to one another at the scene of a community disaster          Effective emergency response requires that all first responders be able to share vital information by radio in real time  but first responders across the state of Michigan have told me in numerous meetings that they didn   t have the right communications equipment     Stabenow said     We learned during the 9 11 attacks  and we learned again in the first hours of Hurricane Katrina response that the inability to share information costs time and costs lives  I will continue to work until all our first responders agencies have communications capability          Stabenow has spearheaded efforts in Congress to provide funding for interoperable communications for first responders nationwide            When a disaster strikes  it is imperative that emergency personnel     medical workers  fire fighters  police officers     have the ability to communicate with each other     Levin said     If the communication system is slow or unreliable  it can cost lives  This new funding will enhance our emergency communication systems and will help our first responders do their jobs more effectively          This Interoperable Communications Technology program grant is being awarded under the auspices of Community Oriented Policing Services  better know as COPS  The grant can be used for the purchase of voice and data communications equipment  other improvements to communications systems  and project management  The local community is required to provide a 25 percent match for the federal grant
