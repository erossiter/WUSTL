    02 06 2006   Levin Reaction to President Bush   s FY 2007 Budget Proposal                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEFebruary 6  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Reaction to President Bush   s FY 2007 Budget Proposal                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  WASHINGTON    Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   issued the following statement regarding the budget proposal released by the White House this morning     The budget the President has sent us is a totally unrealistic and reckless budget urging Congress to make permanent large tax reductions that mostly benefit the wealthiest Americans while adding more record deficits to the national debt  which will be shifted onto our children and grandchildren        The President   s budget hides the true size of the deficit ditch it digs   For example  the known cost of the war in Iraq is not included  At the same time  the President   s budget does not reflect our nation   s priorities in other ways  During the Bush Administration  we have seen a loss of 2 8 million manufacturing jobs  and yet the budget fails to propose the kind of bold initiatives we need to retain a manufacturing base in Michigan and across the nation   Instead  it would take us in the wrong direction through massive cuts in the successful Manufacturing Extension Partnership Program and by eliminating the Advanced Technology Partnership Program completely        Additionally  the budget reduces Medicare funding by  36 billion over the next five years even as Medicare struggles to implement the disastrous prescription drug plan   It would eliminate the Community Services Block Grant and greatly reduce the Social Services Block Grant  important programs which help to reduce poverty
