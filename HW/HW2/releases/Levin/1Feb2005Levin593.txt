    02 01 2005   Statement of Senator Carl Levin on U S  Exit Strategy from Iraq                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEFebruary 1  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Statement of Senator Carl Levin on U S  Exit Strategy from Iraq                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   made the following statement today regarding the U S  exit strategy from Iraq    First of all  we should see who the new government is        Second  we should discuss with the new government what our exit strategy will be   We have to find a way  as quickly as possible  to end our status as an occupying power  The insurgents have used that status against us in their propaganda         Third  the new government hopefully will invite the international community  including us  to stay on  And with that invitation  the    occupying power    status would be eliminated because we would then be there at the invitation of a democratically elected Iraqi government   Such an invitation could also bring the forces of other Muslim nations into the international coalition       Fourth  in the meantime we should move ahead on an accelerated basis with the training and equipping of Iraqi security forces  and we should stop exaggerating the number of forces that have already been fully trained   Statements like that of President Bush on September 23  2004  that    nearly 100 000 fully trained and equipped Iraqi soldiers  police officers  and other security personnel are working today    are simply not accurate       I do not think it is wise at this time to say what specific number of troops we would be withdrawing at a particular moment  I think that is putting the cart a little bit ahead of the horse  as important as it is that we finally obtain some kind of exit strategy
