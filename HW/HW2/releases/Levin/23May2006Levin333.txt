    05 23 2006   Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce over  687 000 for Michigan Law Enforcement                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMay 23  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce over  687 000 for Michigan Law Enforcement                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON    U S  Senators Carl Levin  D MI  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  announced today that the U S  Department of Justice is awarding  687 988 for law enforcement programs to five Michigan cities and counties as part of the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistant Grant Program   Grant recipients include the Counties of Muskegon and Oakland and the Cities of Wyoming  Lansing and Port Huron          These Byrne grants are a critical way for Michigan   s law enforcement agencies to remain up to date and effective in the fight against crime     said Levin      This localized funding will help provide the resources and the technology they need to keep our communities safe             Law enforcement agencies need every available tool to solve and prevent crime and to protect our homes  businesses and families     Stabenow said     These grants give our police the flexibility they need to meet new challenges and ensure the safety of Michigan communities          The Byrne grant program supports states  Indian tribes and local governments in their efforts to prevent and control crime   The program takes local needs and conditions into account and provides funding for a variety of purposes  including targeted initiatives  technical assistance  personnel  training  equipment and supplies   Please see the table below for the details regarding each individual grant        Grant Recipient  Amount  Purpose    City of Wyoming   24 647  to fund special programs such as a neighborhood watch  senior youth initiatives  and other community events  and to purchase basic computer equipment    Muskegon County   50 538  to continue Project Cornerstone II  aimed at enhancing the quality of life for inner city residents through aggressive crime prevention and prosecution efforts    City of Lansing   104 952  to improve the efficiency of law enforcement through the purchase of dictation equipment and mobile video recorders for patrol cars    City of Port Huron   14 237  to purchase a new integrated computer system and updated software for the police department    Oakland County   493 614  to implement a transitional component for the Sheriff s Office Regimented Inmate Discipline Program  a boot camp to reduce recidivism and help with reintegration into family and community life
