    03 29 2007   Senator Carl Levin s Remarks at the Tuskegee Airmen Congressional Gold Medal Ceremony                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 29  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senator Carl Levin s Remarks at the Tuskegee Airmen Congressional Gold Medal Ceremony                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   delivered the following remarks at the ceremony today awarding the Congressional Gold Medal to the Tuskegee Airmen   Levin authored the Senate bill that designated the airmen as recipients of the medal     Mr  President   Tuskegee Airmen  Distinguished Leaders and Members of Congress  especially my partner in getting this legislation passed  Congressman Charlie Rangel   Honored guests       This majestic place is where we come together to pay tribute to those who have served our country in extraordinary ways   So it is with heartfelt joy and gratitude that we all welcome the Tuskegee Airmen and their families today as we bestow upon them Congress   s highest honor  the Congressional Gold Medal        It is the ultimate act of patriotism to love your country even when that love is not reciprocated   So what a special love of country exists when one must suspend a sense of anger and resist feelings of despair to fight for the right to fight     and perhaps die     in defense of a nation  many of whose citizens and institutions are denigrating your very humanity       What is it that those young men saw in our nation which drove them to defend it in the face of the pain inflicted upon them by laws promoting segregation and practices reflecting racism   They must have sensed that  despite the bigotry  this was a nation worth fighting for   They must have seen a spark of hope that this was a country worth dying for and sensed that they  in their courage and patriotism  could help that spark become a flame of justice        The first class of 13 cadets began at the Tuskegee Institute in July of 1941   By the time the last class completed its training  nearly 1 000 black pilots had graduated  alongside thousands of aircraft and engine mechanics  armament specialists  control tower operators and other positions essential for conducting flying operations       The statistics of their heroism are overwhelming     more than 15 000 combat sorties  260 enemy aircraft destroyed  150 Distinguished Flying Crosses and Legions of Merit  and more than 700 Air medals and clusters   They were so proficient that white bomber pilots did not just come to accept escorts by the Tuskegee Airmen  they requested the Tuskegee Airmen       But the spiritual legacy of the Tuskegee Airmen transcends any statistics   They not only fought for us on the battlefield   They fought for our very soul as a nation       Tuskegee Airmen     thank you for not giving up on the promise of America   Our nation has been strengthened by your faith in it   Future generations will be inspired by that faith  by your moral and physical courage  and by the extraordinary gift you have given to this land   Your place in our history is not established by this medal  it was assured when  as young men  you put on the uniform of this nation and defended it  demonstrating your faith in what it could become
