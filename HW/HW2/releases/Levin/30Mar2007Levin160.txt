    03 30 2007   Levin Urges Increased Funding for Clean Water and Drinking Water State Revolving Fund                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 30  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Urges Increased Funding for Clean Water and Drinking Water State Revolving Fund                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       WASHINGTON     In a bipartisan letter to the Interior Appropriations Subcommittee  Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   and 41 colleagues urged the subcommittee to provide  1 35 billion for the Clean Water State Revolving Fund and  853 million for the Drinking Water State Revolving Fund  SRF    The Clean Water and Drinking Water SRFs help communities ensure safe drinking water through upgrades to wastewater infrastructure in order to meet the requirements of the Clean Water Act          We take clean drinking water for granted in this country  but filling this most basic need requires an ongoing commitment to the infrastructure that makes clean water possible     said Levin      This bipartisan effort to secure additional funds to help clean up and monitor drinking water and upgrade sewer facilities reflects how crucial this issue is to communities across the country   These improvements are especially important to Michigan because sewer upgrades can also improve the water quality of our precious Great Lakes          The letter to the appropriations subcommittee reads in part     Despite important progress over the last three decades  EPA reports that 40 percent of our nation   s assessed waters are still too impaired for fishing or swimming  Discharges from aging and failing sewerage systems  urban storm water  and other sources continue to pose serious threats to our nation   s lakes  rivers  and estuaries  endangering not only public health but fishing and recreation industries   Population growth and development are placing additional stress on the nation   s water infrastructure and its ability to sustain hard won water quality gains          The letter concludes      W e believe that the federal government must sustain a strong partnership with states and local governments to avert the massive projected backlog and fulfill its share of the burden of maintaining and improving the nation   s water infrastructure and water quality protections          Forty two senators signed the letter to the Interior Appropriations Subcommittee including 33 Democrats and Independents and nine Republicans
