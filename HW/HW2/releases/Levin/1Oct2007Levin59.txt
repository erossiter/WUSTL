    10 01 2007   Levin  Reauthorization of Small Business Innovation Research Program Will Create Jobs and Promote Innovation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEOctober 1  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin  Reauthorization of Small Business Innovation Research Program Will Create Jobs and Promote Innovation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON     With the Senate expected to approve the Department of Defense authorization bill later today  Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   hailed inclusion of legislation to reauthorize the highly successful Small Business Innovation Research  SBIR  program for two years   The SBIR program  which was due to expire on September 30  2008  gives small high technology companies access to federal research and development dollars and  in turn  gives the federal government access to some of the world   s best innovation          The SBIR program creates jobs  increases our capacity for technological innovation and boosts our international competitiveness     said Levin  who is chairman of the Senate Armed Services Committee and also a senior member of the Small Business Committee      The program has demonstrated that federal agencies can work with small businesses to develop and commercialize technologies in a cost effective and innovative way          The SBIR program was established in 1982 as part of the Small Business Innovation Development Act   To participate in the program  a small business must employ no more than 500 people  must be American owned and independently operated  must be for profit  and the principal researcher must be employed by the business  More information on the program  including state by state numbers  can be found        The SBIR program mandates each federal agency that has a research and development budget in excess of  100 million and contracts with outside vendors to designate 2 5 percent of its external research and development budget for awards to small businesses  In the 2006 fiscal year  funding for SBIR was  1 6 billion  approximately half of which went to Department of Defense contracts   Eleven federal agencies participate in the program       A recent assessment of the SBIR program by the National Research Council of the National Academy of Sciences found that SBIR awards have a substantial impact on company creation  research initiation  company growth  and partnering   More than 20  of responding companies said they were founded as a result of a prospective SBIR award  and 70  of companies claimed SBIR as the cause of their decision to pursue a research project         Following Senate passage  a conference committee will work to resolve the differences between the House and Senate versions of the defense authorization bill
