    06 06 2007   Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce Over  24 000 for   Fire Prevention in Reading                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJune 6  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce Over  24 000 for   Fire Prevention in Reading                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             WASHINGTON    U S  Senators Carl Levin  D MI  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  today announced that Reading Emergency Unit in Reading  Michigan  will receive  24 120 for fire prevention and safety programs   The funds are awarded under the fiscal year 2006 Department of Homeland Security Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  AFGP            These federal dollars will help bring important fire prevention programs to Reading     said Levin     Outreach and awareness efforts play a critical role in protecting Michigan   s communities and stopping disasters before they start             Protecting our communities from fires and other hazards must start with public education and public awareness     said Stabenow     I   m pleased this funding will provide Reading with the resources needed to prevent fires and other emergencies          The primary goal of the AFGP Fire Prevention and Safety Program  administered by the Federal Emergency Management Agency in cooperation with the U S  Fire Administration  is to mitigate or prevent the danger to the public and first responders posed by fire and other hazards   Projects supported by the program include fire prevention and public safety education campaigns  media outreach  and arson
