    07 28 2005   Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce Transportation Funding for Port Huron Area                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEJuly 28  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce Transportation Funding for Port Huron Area                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON    The U S  Senate is expected to approve and send to the president a  286 4 billion multi year transportation funding bill that will provide more than  1 123 billion annually to fund transportation projects in Michigan  and create 61 500 new jobs across the state  U S  Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Carl Levin  D MI  said today        The legislation is an extension of the Transportation Equity Act for the 21st Century  or TEA 21  which was enacted in 1998  It authorizes surface transportation projects such as highways and highway safety projects through fiscal year 2009   In final negotiations on the legislation this week  Stabenow and Levin won an additional  100 million for Michigan highway work termed Projects of National and Regional Significance          This transportation bill will create good paying jobs in Michigan  provide funds that are desperately needed for Michigan roads  and provide essential assistance to Michigan   s public transportation     Stabenow said     Of special importance to the Port Huron area  the bill will fund major work at the Blue Water Bridge  make improvements to the 7th Street Bridge  and allow continued improvements of Blue Water Area Transportation Commission equipment and facilities              This transportation funding is an important investment that increases the mobility of people and goods  enhances economic competitiveness  reduces traffic congestion  and improves air quality     said Levin     I am especially pleased funding was provided for the rehabilitation of the 7th Street Bridge  the oldest bridge in Port Huron and a main artery for citizens and commerce in the city  The years and weather have deteriorated this historic bridge  and this funding will help the city ensure the safety of its residents and the flow of traffic and commerce          Michigan would receive more than  594 8 million in transit formula funding  an increase of  167 million  or 39 percent  over TEA 21      In addition to the  594 8 million  as a member of the Senate Banking  Housing and Urban Affairs Committee  I was able to include an additional  164 4 million for bus and rail projects in Michigan     Stabenow said       The bill also allows funds to be used by the State of Michigan for the operation and maintenance of intelligent transportation systems to reduce congestion  The continued use of these systems is critical for Michigan to achieve its air quality goals  as reduced congestion results in lower automobile emissions   Allowing the continued use of these funds to operate ITS systems thus will help the state meet EPA s new ozone standards        The bill will provide funding for the following Port Huron projects        Port Huron      5 5 million     Funding for Blue Water Area Transportation Commission for bus replacement and facility construction     Port Huron      43 million     Blue Water Bridge Plaza expansion  including construction of a new plaza about 1 5 miles west of the current facility in an underdeveloped area     Port Huron      5 million     Rehabilitation of the 7th Street Bridge lift over the Black River     Port Huron      400 000     Integrated highway realignment and grade separations to eliminate road blockages from rail traffic
