    10 07 2005   Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce  350 000 for Supervised Visitation Program for Victims of Domestic Violence  Families in Saginaw                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEOctober 7  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce  350 000 for Supervised Visitation Program for Victims of Domestic Violence  Families in Saginaw                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WASHINGTON    U S  Senators Carl Levin  D MI  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  announced today that the U S  Department of Justice Office on Violence Against Women is awarding  350 000 to Saginaw County to fund a supervised visitation and exchange program for families with a history of domestic violence  child abuse  sexual assault or stalking          Domestic violence tears families apart every day and it is imperative that we do everything we can to help them recover     said Levin      This funding will give families in Saginaw the opportunity to put their lives back together in a safe and positive environment   This will be a valuable program for children and families in the Bay Thumb area             Kids who have suffered from or witnessed violence at home should not have to worry about more violence during family visits     Stabenow said     With SafePlace  we have one more resource to help break the cycle of violence in Michigan homes and communities            Saginaw County will collaborate with the 10th Circuit Court of Michigan  Saginaw County Court and the Underground Railroad  Inc  to establish SafePlace  a supervised visitation and exchange program aimed at providing greater options for families with a history of violence and or abuse   Specifically  the 10th Circuit Court and Saginaw County Court will submit referrals to the program and provide staff training on the role of the legal system in situations of domestic violence   The program will also collaborate with the Police Chief   s Association  the City of Saginaw Police Chief and the director of Saginaw County 911 to develop an emergency response protocol  provide input on proposed safety policies and procedures and draft an orientation booklet that clearly outlines the program and its guidelines
