    12 08 2006   Senate OK   s Levin Amendment to Expand Access to Medication to Treat Heroin Addiction                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEDecember 8  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senate OK   s Levin Amendment to Expand Access to Medication to Treat Heroin Addiction                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        WASHINGTON     The Senate today approved an amendment by Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   that would allow qualified physicians to more than triple the number of patients they are permitted to treat with buprenorphine  a medication that has proven successful in combating heroin addiction             The Drug Addiction Treatment Act  DATA  of 2000 made a dramatic change in the way America fights heroin addiction and provided treatment for tens of thousands of patients who likely never would have sought treatment in highly restricted  centralized methadone programs     Levin said      Due to the great success of buprenorphine treatment  however  many patients have not been able to begin treatment because of the restrictions on the number of patients that each physician can treat   This bill will more than triple the number of patients that a physician can treat with buprenorphine  significantly increasing access to the medication            DATA  which was sponsored by Levin and Sen  Orrin Hatch  R Utah  made it legal for physicians to prescribe and dispense buprenorphine in their private offices  and the FDA approved its use in 2002   This law created an entirely new treatment modality for millions of Americans who suffer from dependence on opioids  which include heroin  morphine  and some prescription painkillers  Prior to passage of this law  medical treatment for opioid addiction and dependence was restricted to centralized clinics         At a Senate symposium hosted by Levin and Hatch in August 2006  experts and patients testified about the success of buprenorphine in treating heroin addiction but described how the patient restrictions have limited access to the medication   The bill approved by the Senate today will significantly improve access to treatment  a major concern raised in the Hatch Levin symposium       You can read Senator Levin s floor statement
