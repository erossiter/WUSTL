    05 19 2005   Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce More than  1 5 Million to Flint Mass Transportation Authority to Promote Job Access                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMay 19  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Stabenow  Levin Announce More than  1 5 Million to Flint Mass Transportation Authority to Promote Job Access                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       WASHINGTON     U S  Senators Debbie Stabenow and Carl Levin  D MI  announced today that the Flint Mass Transit Authority has been awarded a grant of  1 526 738 from the U S  Department of Transportation under the terms of Job Access and Reverse Commute Program          This grant plays a role in allowing individuals to get and keep better jobs  because it allows them to reliably get to work or to training for work  whether they are commuting into a city or out to a suburb     Stabenow said     This is an important grant for both workers and employers served by the Flint Mass Transit Authority             Public transportation provides an affordable  reliable and environmentally friendly way to commute to work     said Levin      But most importantly  this program allows for greater access to areas where there are more jobs  which benefits both employees and employers          This grant will help provide funds for work related transportation to Genesee County residents accessing jobs within the county and in the counties of Oakland  Livingston  Lapeer  Saginaw and Washtenaw        The purpose of the Job Access and Reverse Commute Program is to develop transportation services designed to transport welfare recipients and low income individuals to and from jobs and to develop transportation services that give residents of urban centers and rural and suburban areas access to suburban employment opportunities  Emphasis in awarding these grants is placed on projects that use mass transportation services        Job Access and Reverse Commute grants may finance capital projects and operating costs of equipment  facilities  and associated capital maintenance items related to providing access to jobs  The grants can also be used to promote use of transit by workers with nontraditional work schedules  promote use by appropriate agencies of transit vouchers for welfare recipients and eligible low income individuals  and promote use of employer provided transportation  including a transit pass benefit program
