    08 01 2007   Levin and Stabenow Urge President to Show Strong Leadership on Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEAugust 1  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin and Stabenow Urge President to Show Strong Leadership on Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  WASHINGTON     In a   PDF  from the Great Lakes Task Force  Senators Carl Levin and Debbie Stabenow  both D Mich   joined 34 members of the House and Senate in urging President Bush to make good on an executive order he signed three years ago to address nationally significant environmental and natural resources involving the Great Lakes       The officials write      W e are disappointed that since the Great Lakes Regional Collaboration Strategy Report was released  agency efforts and enthusiasm for implementing the Executive Order have waned         We also are concerned that coordination between the Federal agencies with state  tribal  local and other partners remains inconsistent at best and non existent at worst          Executive Order 13340 established two tracks for improving coordination of Great Lakes programs   First  the executive order established an interagency task force of all federal agencies with jurisdiction over the Great Lakes   Second  the executive order outlined a collaborative process where federal  state  local  tribal and other groups would come together to address Great Lakes issues       The second part of the executive order led to the creation of the Great Lakes Regional Collaboration  comprised of the federal government led by the Environmental Protection Agency  the Great Lakes congressional delegation  governors  mayors  tribes and other stakeholders   In 2005  the Great Lakes Regional Collaboration issued a report  which the principals agreed to use as a blueprint for restoration of the Great Lakes   However  two years after the release of the report and three years after the president   s executive order  progress remains elusive       The letter continues     The Great Lakes needs strong leadership to ensure that restoration priorities are being implemented   We urge you to provide adequate resources to implement restoration priorities          Levin is co chair of the Senate Great Lakes Task Force          PDF
