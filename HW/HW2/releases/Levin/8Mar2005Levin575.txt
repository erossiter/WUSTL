    03 08 2005   Levin Presses Homeland Security Nominees on Inspection of Canadian Waste                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 8  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Presses Homeland Security Nominees on Inspection of Canadian Waste                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    WASHINGTON     As the Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee has recently considered the nominations for a new Secretary and Deputy Secretary of the Department of Homeland Security  Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   has pressed the nominees to commit to implementing new technologies to inspect municipal waste trucks entering the country  or to stop the shipments entirely  For several years  Levin has raised concerns about the potential of hazardous materials entering the United States on municipal solid waste vehicles     hundreds of which enter Michigan each day     due to the difficulty of inspecting such trucks and their cargo          In a hearing yesterday  Levin asked Michael Jackson  the nominee for Deputy Secretary  about the trucks that come into Michigan from Canada each day and the homeland security threat that they pose   Levin said that the trucks should not be allowed in for environmental reasons alone  but a second reason to stop the trucks from coming in is    if we can   t inspect those trucks to make sure that their cargo does not contain the type of weapons that I have talked about     chemical  biological  or nuclear          Jackson promised to    dig into this issue and to come back and report what the credible options seem to look like and to counsel with the committee about steps that might be taken here           Similarly  Levin questioned the new Secretary  Michael Chertoff  about this topic at the time of his nomination hearing last month  and he responded that he would    work to ensure that we develop and utilize the latest technology to inspect trucks transporting municipal waste into the United States    and said that we would work to    guarantee layered security measures are in place           These commitments are a significant step in a long fought battle waged by Michigan lawmakers  who for years have voiced concern about the security threat  health concerns  and environmental issues posed by the waste shipments   In October 2003  Levin wrote a letter along with Sen  Debbie Stabenow  D Mich   and Rep  John Dingell  D Dearborn  asking the Inspector General of the Department of Homeland Security to investigate whether the methodologies and technologies used by the bureau to screen municipal solid waste are as effectives as those used to screen other items of commerce entering the country by commercial motor vehicle transport   To date  the report has not been completed
