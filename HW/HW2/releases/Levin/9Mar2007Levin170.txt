    03 09 2007   Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce Over  600 000 to Support Department of Defense Research at Michigan Tech                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 9  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce Over  600 000 to Support Department of Defense Research at Michigan Tech                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Carl Levin  D MI  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  announced today that the Department of Defense has awarded two grants to Michigan Technological University totaling  618 086   The funding will come from the Air Force Office of Scientific Research  AFOSR  as part of the Defense University Research Instrumentation Program  DURIP   which supports the purchase of state of the art equipment to help universities perform cutting edge defense research          Michigan has a proud tradition of supporting our nation   s military services  and with this funding Michigan Tech will continue to play an important role in critical defense research programs     said Levin     These investments in advanced military technology and robotics help ensure that our armed forces remain the best equipped and most advanced in the world             Michigan   s Universities have a strong tradition of being on the cutting edge of innovation and research     said Stabenow      I am pleased that Michigan Tech is receiving the funding they need to continue their state of the art research and further solidify our state   s position as a leader in defense technologies          The DURIP program assists university researchers with defense related research by providing funding for critical equipment   Expensive equipment needs are often difficult for researchers to fill under existing research contracts and grants   These awards are the result of a merit competition conducted by the Army Research Office  the Office of Naval Research and AFOSR   The research offices received a total of 780 proposals  199 awards were made to 112 academic institutions       Details on the awards are below   Each award is subject to the successful completion of negotiations between the research offices and MTU  the size of each award reflects the amount proposed      Michigan Technological University    467 017   Air Force Office of Scientific Research  Human Robot Interactions   This funding will provide equipment for a laboratory of Human Robot Interaction  HRI  at Michigan Tech   Students and researchers from six departments at MTU will work collaboratively to develop critical technologies related to human robot teams  which combine human cognitive abilities that are difficult to automate with the physical and mission related advantages of robots      Michigan Technological University    151 069   Air Force Office of Scientific Research  Microplasma Device Characterization   This funding will support a Microplasma Device Characterization Facility  which will be used to develop and test micro scale ion thrusters for Department of Defense spacecraft   The ion thrusters can be used to maneuver a new class of small satellites  known as nanosatellites  to maintain and enhance the United States  space based defense system
