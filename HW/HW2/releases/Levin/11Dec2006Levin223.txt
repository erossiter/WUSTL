    12 11 2006   Levin  Stabenow Hail Passage of Provision to Provide Equity to U S  flag Ships in Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEDecember 11  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin  Stabenow Hail Passage of Provision to Provide Equity to U S  flag Ships in Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   and Sen  Debbie Stabenow  D Mich   praised the inclusion of a provision to provide equity to U S  flag ships operating in the Great Lakes in the Tax Relief and Health Care Act of 2006  which passed the Senate very early Saturday morning   The amendment exempts ships operating in the Great Lakes from the limitation of 30 days per year of domestic trade  allowing U S  flag ships to qualify for the alternative tonnage tax rather than pay the normal corporate income tax from operations in foreign trade            I   m pleased that the Senate acted to provide equity for our shippers     said Levin      This provision levels the field  so to speak  for our U S  flag ships on the Great Lakes             This measure is of critical importance to our local shipping industry and our state   s economy     said Stabenow      The passage of this bill gives American shippers a level playing field against their Canadian competitors on the Great Lakes          Current law requires shippers to pay either the normal corporate income tax on income from operations in foreign trade or an alternative tonnage tax   The alternative tonnage tax was enacted in 2004 to increase the competitiveness of the U S  maritime industry   However  U S  flag ships that operate in the Great Lakes were precluded from taking advantage of this option because the law prohibits any vessel that operates in domestic service for more than 30 days from using the tonnage tax   In the Great Lakes  U S  flag ships move regularly between the United States and Canada  easily exceeding 30 days in domestic trade per year       The tonnage tax is designed to help U S  flag vessels compete in international routes like those on the Great Lakes and is applied only to revenues generated from international operations        The President is expected to sign the Tax Relief and Health Care Act
