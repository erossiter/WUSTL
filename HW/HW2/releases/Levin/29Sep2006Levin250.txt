    09 29 2006   Stabenow  Levin Announce Over  250 000 Coming to Marquette General Hospital                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASESeptember 29  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Stabenow  Levin Announce Over  250 000 Coming to Marquette General Hospital                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Funds will support development of telecommunication networks within a tri state region      WASHINGTON     U S  Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Carl Levin  D MI  today announced that the U S  Department of Health and Human Services has awarded Marquette General Hospital  259 955 through the Telehealth Resource Center Grant Program   The funds will establish the Midwest Alliance for Telehealth and Technology Resources  MATTeR   and will be used to build and expand telecommunication networks between health care researchers  providers and patients to improve continuity of care          These funds will allow Marquette General Hospital to develop and implement telehealth networks around the state     said Stabenow      Adopting the latest technology to modernize our health system will ensure every Michigan community has access to quality  affordable health care              These federal dollars will help Marquette General Hospital to more effectively serve the Upper Peninsula of Michigan  and that   s good news     said Levin      Improving telecommunications will allow U P  residents easier access to the medical care they need             This funding puts Michigan in the national spotlight as a leader in telemedicine     said Sally Davis  Program Director for Telehealth  at Marquette General Hospital      The project will provide technical assistance to new and evolving telehealth networks  pursue research opportunities and serve as a clearing house for telehealth and technology information          Telehealth networks utilize technology such as video conferencing  streaming media and wireless communications to allow specialists to share information and provide patient care over long distances   Marquette General Hospital has become a leader in telehealth through its commitment to meet the needs of rural and underserved residents       Michigan State University  the Kansas University Medical Center  and Purdue University are the other members of MATTeR   Each alliance partner has a defined role  with Marquette General Hospital taking the lead in grant administration  overall Alliance coordination and technical assistance in program development
