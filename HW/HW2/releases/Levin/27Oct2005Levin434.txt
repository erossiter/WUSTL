    10 27 2005   Senate Adopts Levin Amendment to Correct  Catch 22                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEOctober 27  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senate Adopts Levin Amendment to Correct  Catch 22                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WASHINGTON     The Senate yesterday adopted an amendment authored by Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   to correct a situation that has put some individuals seeking U S  citizenship in a  catch 22   The amendment would shorten the residency requirement for  aliens of extraordinary ability  from five to three years between the receipt of their green card and the date of their eligibility for naturalization   The amendment would apply to  aliens of extraordinary ability  who began their naturalization process prior to July 2002 and who will be representing the United States at an international event        This amendment corrects an absurdity in the law   said Levin    Some rule changes in 2002 unfairly affected some people and needed to be fixed  which this amendment does        Before July 2002  an  alien of extraordinary ability  needed to be approved for an immigrant worker visa before they could apply for their green card   Upon receipt of a green card  the immigrant faces a mandatory five year residency period before he or she can apply for naturalization   In July 2002  U S  Citizenship and Immigration Services changed the rules so that an individual can now file their immigrant worker application and green card application at the same time  significantly shortening the overall processing time   As a result  individuals who began the naturalization process after July 2002 could receive their citizenship in less than five and a half years   However  individuals who started the process earlier have often had to wait up to eight years       The amendment would benefit an Olympic eligible ice dancer  Tanith Belbin  born in Canada and currently living in Michigan  who would not have U S  citizenship in time for the 2006 Olympics without the current legislative proposal   Her immigrant worker visa was approved in 2000  but she did not receive her green card until July 2002   Had she received the benefit of the current processing provisions  she would be eligible to represent the U S  in the 2006 Olympics         The Levin amendment  S A  2268  to the Departments of Labor and Health and Human Services  HHS  appropriations bill  H R  3010  was adopted by voice vote   The Senate is expected to pass the appropriations bill today  The House of Representatives passed its version of the bill without such a provision earlier this year   Members of the House and Senate will meet in a conference committee to work out the differences between the two pieces of legislation   The Labor HHS conference report then goes back to the House and Senate for a final vote and to the President for his signature into law
