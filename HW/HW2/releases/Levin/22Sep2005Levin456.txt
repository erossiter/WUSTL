    09 22 2005   Senators Levin and Stabenow Announce  23 Million   for Michigan National Guard Military Construction Projects                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASESeptember 22  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin and Stabenow Announce  23 Million   for Michigan National Guard Military Construction Projects                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Senate approves funding for new facilities in Lansing  Alpena and Grayling      WASHINGTON     Senators Carl Levin  D Mich   and Debbie Stabenow  D Mich   announced today that the funding bill for military construction projects in the 2006 fiscal year  which the Senate approved by a vote of 98 0  includes  23 million for Michigan National Guard facilities in Lansing  Alpena and Grayling          The military construction bill funds much of the infrastructure for our military facilities in this country and across the world     said Levin     Michigan   s National Guard and Reserve play critical roles in both our homeland security and national security  The new construction and safety improvements included in this legislation will contribute significantly to the training and readiness of the men and women in the Guard and Reserve in Michigan             The Michigan National Guard plays a vital role in protecting our state and our nation  At this moment Michigan National Guard units have met the call of duty and are on the front line in Iraq and are aiding residents of the Gulf states     Stabenow said     We must always work to ensure that our Michigan National Guard units have the best equipment and the best facilities           The bill appropriates over  23 million in military construction projects for Michigan         11 8 million for the construction and renovation of the Lansing U S  Property and Fiscal Office  The renovation of the USPFO will enable the Michigan National Guard to consolidate its Joint Forces Headquarters  USPFO  and Homeland Security staff in to one facility     the Baker Olin Office Buildings  The renovated facilities will mean the MING and the U S  Marine Corps Reserve can combine their forces and resources  increase the logistical effectiveness  and abandon outdated buildings       9 5 million for the construction of a Squadron Operations Facility  at the Alpena Combat Readiness Training Center  CRTC   The Alpena CRTC routinely supports operational flight training conducted by the Air National Guard  Air Force  Army  Army National Guard  Navy  and Marine Corps  The Alpena CRTC requires two separate  functional squadron operations facilities for use by visiting units       1 9 million for the construction of a Multi Purpose Machine Gun Range  at Camp Grayling   The new machine gun range will improve the training equipment used by Army National Guard soldiers in the Midwest   Camp Grayling is already home to one of the most advanced tank ranges in the country
