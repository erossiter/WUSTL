    03 23 2006   EPA Restores  7 5 Million in Funding for Rouge River Clean Up  Says Levin                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 23  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      EPA Restores  7 5 Million in Funding for Rouge River Clean Up  Says Levin                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   today hailed a decision by the Environmental Protection Agency to  restore  7 5 million in funding for clean up efforts along the Rouge River    Levin and other members of the Michigan Congressional delegation had urged the EPA to restore the funding after it was frozen in 2005 as part of an agency wide review of EPA supported projects           The Rouge River Project has been pivotal in reducing pollution  improving water quality and restoring the natural habitat in  around  and downstream of the Rouge River     Levin said      Unfortunately  funding for the project was wrongly cut in 2005  and since that time I   ve urged the agency to restore the money so that clean up efforts can continue   This is great news for the environment  and all the communities along the river          In 2005  the EPA rescinded funding for projects for which the original project period had expired   Although work on the Rouge River Project was ongoing  the agency rescinded  12 7 million in funding   Later that year  the EPA restored  5 1 million  but Levin teamed with other members of the Michigan delegation     Sen  Debbie Stabenow  D MI   Rep  John Dingell  D Dearborn   Rep  Thaddeus McCotter  R Livonia   and Rep  Carolyn Kilpatrick  D Dearborn      to urge the agency to restore the remaining funding for this critical project
