    10 17 2007   Levin  COPS Funding Boost Will Put More Officers on the Street                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEOctober 17  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin  COPS Funding Boost Will Put More Officers on the Street                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              WASHINGTON     Senator Carl Levin  D Mich   today applauded the passage of an amendment to add  110 million to the Department of Justice Community Oriented Policing Services  COPS  program   The amendment  cosponsored by Levin and a bipartisan group of 29 Senators and passed late yesterday as part of the 2008 funding bill for the Department of Justice and other government agencies  directs the additional funding towards the Universal Hiring Program  UHP  to help local law enforcement agencies hire new officers          In Michigan and across the country  the COPS program provides much needed federal dollars to help local law enforcement agencies put more cops on the beat     said Levin     We are asking a lot of our police departments and this program will help them provide the critical services and the neighborhood presence that many communities have come to depend on          The COPS program seeks to promote community policing in jurisdictions of all sizes across the country   Community policing represents a shift from more traditional law enforcement in that it focuses on prevention of crime and the fear of crime on a very local basis       The UHP provides funding directly to local  state and tribal jurisdictions  regardless of size  to assist in paying the salaries and benefits of newly hired officers engaged in community policing   To date  more than  5 billion has been invested in hiring more than 73 000 law enforcement professionals through UHP and earlier COPS administered hiring programs
