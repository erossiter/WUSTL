    04 07 2006   Levin  DeWine Lead Effort to Increase Funding for   Advanced Technology Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEApril 7  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin  DeWine Lead Effort to Increase Funding for   Advanced Technology Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   helped lead a bipartisan group of 24 Senators in urging the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science to provide  140 million in funding for the Advanced Technology Program  ATP    By supporting R on new technologies  ATP helps to increase the ability of U S  businesses to engage in technological innovation and  in turn  create jobs          Advances in technology account for more than 50 percent of U S  economic growth  and technological development and innovation are vital to our nation remaining competitive in world markets     the Senators wrote to Subcommittee Chairman Richard Shelby  R Ala   and Ranking Member Barbara Mikulski  D Md      ATP bridges the gap  often called the    valley of death     between promising ideas in the research lab and access to market capital to develop them          The ATP forges partnerships between the government and the private sector to engage in research and development projects that will provide technological advances for the nation  The program enables innovators     including companies  universities  and non profits     to take on R projects that could lead to major technological breakthroughs for the nation without bearing all of the risks of such research on their own   The Department of Commerce estimates that ATP has produced eight times more in technologies developed than the amount of money that has been put into the program       In FY 2006  the Senate funded ATP at  140 million but  because the House zeroed out the program  ATP ended up funded at  80 million in the conference committee between House and Senate negotiators   President Bush   s proposed budget eliminated the program   Last month  the Senate approved a Levin amendment to the Budget Resolution to provide  140 million to the ATP for fiscal year 2007
