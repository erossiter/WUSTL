    02 02 2005   Levin Urges Quick Approval of Bill to Provide Relief to Small Businesses Hurt by Rising Fuel Costs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEFebruary 2  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Urges Quick Approval of Bill to Provide Relief to Small Businesses Hurt by Rising Fuel Costs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Levin provision allows small businesses to use money to convert to renewable or alternative energy sources      WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   a senior member of the Senate Small Business Committee  today urged the committee to approve legislation that would provide relief to small businesses and farmers hurt by the rising costs of heating fuel by offering low interest disaster assistance loans from the Small Business Administration  SBA  and the Department of Agriculture             High natural gas prices combined with colder than normal temperatures have led to skyrocketing heating bills in Michigan and across the country     Levin said      These costly heating bills can be particularly devastating to small businesses and farmers  who tend to have limited cash flows and limited access to conventional bank loans   Sometimes these unexpected costs can even threaten the viability of the entire business           The Small Business and Farm Energy Emergency Relief Act of 2005  which was introduced today by Sen  John Kerry  D Mass   includes a provision authored by Levin that would allow small businesses to use the loans to convert to alternative energy sources  reducing their dependence on non renewable fuels           Alternative energy sources offer small businesses reliable  long term energy options whose prices are less vulnerable to market fluctuations     Levin said      By enabling small businesses to use these loans to convert to alternative energy sources  we help them to become less dependent on non renewable fuel and avoid vulnerability to future price spikes          To be eligible for a disaster assistance loan from SBA  the applicant must be a small business  must have used all reasonably available funds  and must have been unable to obtain credit elsewhere   Applications for the loans must be submitted within six months from the day the disaster is declared   The legislation extends similar loan assistance to small agricultural producers through the Department of Agriculture   s emergency loan program
