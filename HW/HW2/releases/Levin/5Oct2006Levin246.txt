    10 05 2006   Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce Over  1 5 Million for Upper Peninsula Fire Safety                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEOctober 5  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce Over  1 5 Million for Upper Peninsula Fire Safety                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON    U S  Senators Carl Levin  D MI  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  announced today that seven Upper Peninsula fire departments have been awarded  1 507 578 through the fiscal year 2006 Department of Homeland Security Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  AFGP    The funding will be used to purchase vehicles and fund operations and safety programs          Upper Peninsula residents depend on local firefighters and first responders to be ready in an emergency     said Levin     These federal dollars will help ensure that fire departments across the U P  have the resources to respond effectively when they are needed most   For communities that have been trying for years to upgrade their outdated firefighting vehicles  I am happy to report that help is on the way             Michigan   s firefighters work tirelessly to protect our communities and our families     Stabenow said      These grants will help ensure fire departments across the Upper Peninsula have the equipment and training they need          The AFGP awards grants directly to fire departments and EMS organizations to improve the effectiveness of firefighting operations and firefighter health and safety programs  and to establish or expand fire prevention programs throughout the United States   Grants are awarded competitively through an application process       Fire Departments in Wakefield  Bruce Crossing  Ontonagon and Calumet will receive funding for new firefighting vehicles   Bay Ambulance  Inc   in Baraga and Breitung Township Fire Department in Quinnesec will both receive funding for operations and firefighter safety programs   Covington Township will receive funding for a regional communications project       Individual grants awards can be found in the table below        Organization    Grant Amount    Calumet Village Volunteer Fire Department  180 500    Breitung Township Fire Department  31 578    Ontonagon Volunteer Fire Department  142 405    Bruce Crossing Volunteer Fire Department  147 250    Covington Township  792 000    Bay Ambulance  Inc   33 345    Wakefield Volunteer Fire Department  180 500
