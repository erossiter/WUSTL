    03 30 2006   Subcommittee Releases DHS Report   on the Screening of Canadian Trash Containers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 30  2006 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Subcommittee Releases DHS Report   on the Screening of Canadian Trash Containers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Following today   s hearing before the U S  Senate Permanent Subcommittee on Investigations  entitled    Neutralizing the Nuclear and Radiological Threat  Securing the Global Supply Chain     the Subcommittee released a report prepared by the Department of Homeland Security   s Office of Inspector General  DHS OIG  on the ineffectiveness of technology to screen Canadian trash containers being transported by truck into the United States  The DHS OIG   s disturbing report found that U S  Customs    does not have an effective method to screen and inspect the 350 truckloads of municipal solid waste that enter the U S  daily through the Detroit and Port Huron ports of entry     A bipartisan Subcommittee staff report also released today recommends that DHS immediately ban the import of trash containers due to this gaping security hole  Senator Norm Coleman is the Chairman of the Subcommittee  and Senator Carl Levin is the senior Democrat  Michigan Members of Congress  Senator Levin  Senator Debbie Stabenow  and Congressman John Dingell  had requested the DHS report in 2003
