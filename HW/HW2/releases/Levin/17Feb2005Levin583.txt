    02 17 2005   Levin Bill Would Help Businesses Hurt by Low Water Levels in the Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEFebruary 17  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Bill Would Help Businesses Hurt by Low Water Levels in the Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON     In recent years  low water levels in the Great Lakes have devastated many businesses  including marinas  charter fishing operations and other small businesses that depend on the lakes for their livelihood   To address this concern  Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   today introduced legislation that would make these Great Lakes area businesses eligible for emergency financial assistance from the Small Business Administration  SBA   which is currently available to small businesses hurt by other disasters           In the same way that floods  hail storms  tornadoes  and other natural phenomena can devastate small businesses  the harm caused by unusually low water levels on the Great Lakes can be irreparable to businesses who depend on the waterways     Levin said      The Small Business Act provides disaster assistance to businesses who have been victim to a number of natural disasters  so I   m hopeful we will be able to broaden that assistance to include businesses who have been hurt by below average water levels on the Great Lakes           The Small Business Act provides low interest loans to small businesses that have suffered economic injury as a result of a disaster  and Levin   s bill would amend the definition of    disaster    in the act to include below average water levels in the Great Lakes   Under Levin   s bill  for example  SBA low interest disaster loans could help marina owners finance the expensive and unexpected costs of dredging needed to return draft in channels to pre low water levels   The loan would allow the marina operator to spread the cost of dredging over the expected life of the benefit             Providing relief to these small businesses in their efforts to keep water access open also provides effective relief to the larger recreational boating industry     Levin continued      As long as boaters have access to the lakes  they will continue to patronize related small businesses such as bait and tackle shops           Levin is a member of the Committee on Small Business and Entrepreneurship
