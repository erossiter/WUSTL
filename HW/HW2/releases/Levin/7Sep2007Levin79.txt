    09 07 2007   Senate OKs Levin Amendment on Iraqi Refugees                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASESeptember 7  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senate OKs Levin Amendment on Iraqi Refugees                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON     The spending bill for the Department of State and foreign operations  which the Senate approved last night  included a provision authored by Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   that calls on the President to quickly respond to the deepening humanitarian and refugee crisis in Iraq   The United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees estimates that 2 2 million refugees have fled Iraq  and another two million people have been displaced within Iraq   Between 40 000 and 50 000 Iraqis are displaced every month           The crisis in Iraq does not end with the raw and visible security crisis     Levin said      There is a massive refugee crisis that is weakening Iraq   s society and infrastructure as the professional class flees  and it is burdening Iraq   s neighbors who must absorb the enormous influx of desperate people   The United States has a moral obligation to help these refugees  and we should act swiftly to do so           Levin   s amendment calls for the President   s swift action on the refugee crisis and  in particular  stresses that the U S  should provide assistance to people who have assisted the United States and to religious minorities  as it has become increasingly clear that these groups cannot safely return to Iraq   The amendment points out that the Department of State has yet to use all of the available allocation that could be used for Iraqi refugees  and calls for an increase in the allocation of refugee slots for Iraqi refugees to settle in the U S    The annual United States worldwide ceiling for refugees has been 70 000 since 2002        The amendment was approved by voice vote    The House passed the State Department and foreign operations spending bill in June  and it will now proceed to a House Senate conference committee
