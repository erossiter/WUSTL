    09 07 2007   Levin Hails Senate Passage of Provisions to Combat Sea Lamprey  Study Outflows in the Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASESeptember 7  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin Hails Senate Passage of Provisions to Combat Sea Lamprey  Study Outflows in the Great Lakes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           WASHINGTON     Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   today praised the passage of two provisions that would provide millions to benefit the Great Lakes   The provisions   17 2 million for the Great Lakes Fishery Commission to control sea lamprey and  7 5 million for the International Joint Commission  part of which will be used on a study of Lake Superior outflows  were included in the State Department and foreign operations spending bill for fiscal year 2008  which passed the Senate late Thursday          Sea lamprey are a historic problem on the Great Lakes and this funding will help prevent them from becoming even more costly and destructive     said Levin      The Great Lakes are important to Michigan in many ways and it is critical that we protect and restore the lakes so we can all use them safely and responsibly          The Great Lakes provisions are as follows     17 2 million for the Great Lakes Fishery Commission  an increase over the Administration   s  12 14 million budget request   This funding would help control sea lamprey in the Great Lakes  a parasitic invasive species that has threatened indigenous fish populations    7 5 million for an International Joint Commission study  an increase over the Administration   s  6 77 million budget request   The Commission would study whether the regulation of Lake Superior outflows could be improved to better address the needs of users on Lakes Superior  Huron  Michigan and Erie  potentially impacting shoreline property owners  recreational and tourism needs and habitat protection     The House passed the State Department and foreign operations spending bill in June  and it will now proceed to a House Senate conference committee
