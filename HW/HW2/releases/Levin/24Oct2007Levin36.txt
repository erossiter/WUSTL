    10 24 2007   Statement of Senator Carl Levin on Release of Commodity Futures Trading Commission Report on Oversight of Energy Trading                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEOctober 24  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Statement of Senator Carl Levin on Release of Commodity Futures Trading Commission Report on Oversight of Energy Trading                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              WASHINGTON     Senator Carl Levin  D MI  released the following statement regarding the release of a CFTC report regarding energy trading oversight          The CFTC report is a very positive development  The CFTC acknowledges the vulnerability of U S  energy markets to price manipulation and excessive speculation  calls for tougher market oversight  and recommends legislative changes to put a cop on the beat in all U S  energy markets   All four of the CFTC   s key recommendations    large trader reporting  position limits or accountability levels  self regulatory oversight  and emergency authority to require traders to reduce positions    are already in the Close the Enron Loophole Act I   ve introduced   The CFTC   s support will advance the prospects for getting a bill passed this Congress to close the Enron loophole and stop trading abuses that distort U S  energy prices          Senator Levin has introduced  and Senator Byron Dorgan  D ND  has cosponsored  S  2058  the Close the Enron Loophole Act  to strengthen government oversight of energy trading by large traders to prevent price manipulation and excessive speculation
