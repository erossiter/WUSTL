    03 09 2007   Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce Over  350 000 to Support Department of Defense Research at MSU                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEMarch 9  2007 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Senators Levin  Stabenow Announce Over  350 000 to Support Department of Defense Research at MSU                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Carl Levin  D MI  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  announced today that the Department of Defense plans to award two grants to Michigan State University totaling  363 545   The funding will come from the Army Research Office  ARO  and the Office of Naval Research  ONR  as part of the Defense University Research Instrumentation Program  DURIP   which supports the purchase of state of the art equipment to help universities perform cutting edge defense research          Michigan has a proud tradition of supporting our nation   s military services  and with this funding Michigan State University will continue to play an important role in critical defense research programs     said Levin     These investments in efficient military technologies help ensure that our armed forces remain the best equipped and most advanced in the world             Michigan   s Universities have a strong tradition of being on the cutting edge of innovation and research     said Stabenow      I am pleased that Michigan State is receiving the funding they need to continue their state of the art research and further solidify our state   s position as a leader in defense technologies          The DURIP program assists university researchers with defense related research by providing funding for critical equipment   Expensive equipment needs are often difficult for researchers to fill under existing research contracts and grants   These awards are the result of a merit competition conducted by the ARO  ONR and the Air Force Office of Scientific Research   The research offices received a total of 780 proposals  199 awards were made to 112 academic institutions       Details on the awards are below   Each award is subject to the successful completion of negotiations between the research offices and MSU  the size of each award reflects the amount proposed      Michigan State University    263 545   Office of Naval Research  Powder Processing Instrumentation for Thermoelectrics Research   This funding will allow MSU to obtain equipment which will enable efficient powder processing of new thermoelectric materials which can directly convert waste heat from transportation and other commercial activity to useful electricity      Michigan State University    100 000     Army Research Office  Polymer Processing Equipment for Optimization in Materials Research   This funding will support the development and testing of new bio based and bio degradable nanocomposite materials for flexible food packaging   The purpose of this research is to minimize dependence on petroleum products for packaging materials  as well as to minimize the environmental impact of waste materials
