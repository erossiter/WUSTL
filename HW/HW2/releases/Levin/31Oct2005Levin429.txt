    10 31 2005   Levin  Stabenow Say New Passport Initiative Could Have  Devastating Impact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Senator Carl Levin  News Release              FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASEOctober 31  2005 Contact  Press OfficePhone  202 228 3685      Levin  Stabenow Say New Passport Initiative Could Have  Devastating Impact                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  WASHINGTON     U S  Senators Carl Levin  D MI  and Debbie Stabenow  D MI  wrote a  today to Homeland Security Secretary Michael Chertoff voicing concern about the proposed initiative that would require anyone entering the United States via a U S  Canadian border to have a passport or other acceptable alternative document         In 2004  1 7 million Michiganians visited Canada and 1 1 million Canadians visited Michigan  generating over  640 million for their economies   the Senators wrote  noting the  potentially devastating impact  that the initiative could have on travel between the two countries    People and businesses in Michigan are concerned that the initiative would create a border that is neither safe nor convenient for most people to cross         In their letter  Sens  Levin and Stabenow note provisions in the Intelligence Reform and Terrorism Prevention Act of 2004 that require the Department of Homeland Security to implement a plan to verify the citizenship of everyone entering the United States  but the Senators maintain that the requirement can be satisfied  in a way that does not inhibit the safe and secure travel between the United States and Canada
