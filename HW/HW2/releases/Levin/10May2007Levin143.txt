                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Local mothers recognized for dedication to their community      WASHINGTON     In honor of Mother   s Day  Sen  Carl Levin  D Mich   is honoring four local    Crime Fighting Moms    whose outstanding work in crime prevention efforts has made their communities safer            Citizens who dedicate their time and effort to make the streets safer for their families and the community are an integral part of the success of community policing     said Levin     I am often told by Michigan law enforcement how important community volunteers are in their effort to reduce crime  These Crime Fighting Moms have demonstrated their commitment to improving the quality of life in their communities and they should inspire us all to be more involved            According to preliminary FBI data  violent crime in 2006 was down more than 30 percent since 1994   Officials attribute this promising trend in part to the increased activity of neighborhood law enforcement and safety organizations  which provide the extra    eyes and ears    needed to alert police to potential trouble        In a March 27th letter  Levin asked sheriffs and prosecutors across Michigan to nominate mothers who have been influential in local efforts to reduce crime  Levin sent each of these community activists a certificate of recognition from the U S  Senate         Since 1997  Levin has recognized Mother s Day by presenting  Crime Fighting Mom  awards to Michigan women    A total of 14 women are being honored this year  four of whom are from the Lansing area   Information on this year   s recipients from the Lansing area follows      Eaton County  Honoree  Nancy Campbell  Nominated by  Sheriff Michael Raines  When some homes in Nancy Campbell s neighborhood were broken into  Ms  Campbell contacted the Sheriff   s Department and quickly organized a neighborhood watch   Watch members began to call in any suspicious activity in the area  which was very helpful in stopping the crime spree in the neighborhood   Ms  Campbell s efforts gave the neighborhood a sense of ownership and control and calmed many fears  as the neighbors now had a good working relationship with the Sheriff s office   Ms  Campbell also helped organize a town hall meeting involving members of her community  the Sheriffs office and the Lansing Police Department   With everyone working together  two suspects were identified and taken into custody for the crimes      Gratiot County  Honorees  Marla Macha and Kay Graham  Nominated by  Sheriff Robert L  Beracy  Marla Macha and Kay Graham work daily with county law enforcement officials to provide needed local statistics   This helps to ensure that proper contact is made with county residents who serve as the    eyes and ears    for law enforcement and make the community safer      Ingham County  Honoree  Kathy Cole  Nominated by  Sheriff Gene Wriggelsworth  Kathy Cole   s husband Paul was a Deputy at the Ingham County Sheriff s Office who was killed in the line of duty in 1996   She and her family suffered greatly as a result of their loss  but Ms  Cole decided to take action and joined a group called MICOPS  Michigan Concerns of Police Survivors  who assist families in coping when a death occurs   Ms  Cole has done this on many occasions and went on to become the Treasurer of MICOPS   Her leadership has been invaluable to families throughout Michigan and the nation in their greatest time of need
