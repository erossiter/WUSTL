SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS WITH NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS ABOUT HIS BILL TO IMPROVE SCHOOLS, BOOST GRADUATION RATES IN COUNTRY
  During his weekly radio press conference with New Mexico radio reporters Sen. Jeff Bingaman today talked about a bill he has introduced aimed at improving America's public high schools and high school graduation rates. Bingaman also answered questions on a variety of other topics.  
   00:00 - Bingaman talks about legislation he introduced aimed at dramatically improving America's public high schools and high school graduation rates and about the America COMPETES Act currently on the Senate floor. 
   03:04 - Bingaman says he has joined other members of the New Mexico Congressional delegation in asking the army to look in to questions regarding the search of New Mexico National Guards' troops for tattoos. 
   05:17 - Bingaman talks about the Iraq Supplemental Spending bill. 
   06:14 - Bingaman talks about the American COMPETES Act that is currently being debated on the Senate floor. 
   07:19 - Bingaman talks about legislation he introduced yesterday that would work to improve America's public high schools and boost high school graduation rates. 
   10:39 - Bingaman talks about the Iraq Supplemental Spending bill. 
   12:32 - Bingaman comments on calls for Attorney General Alberto Gonzalez to resign. 
   16:00 - Bingaman comments on the U.S. House of Representative committee hearings into security issues at Los Alamos National Labs. 
   17:52 - Bingaman talks about his letter calling on Postmaster John Potter to reevaluation the decision to move the Las Cruces processing operations to El Paso and called on the USPS to take the steps needed to improve delivery to the area.
