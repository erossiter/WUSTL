SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS WITH NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  U.S. Senator Jeff Bingaman today talked to New Mexico radio reporters and answered questions on a variety of other topics. Bingaman's remarks can be retrieved by going to his website or clicking on the link to the right: 
   00:00 - Bingaman gives an overview of what the Senate is trying to complete before the end of the year. 
   03:51 - Bingaman comments on the Federal Communications Commissions efforts to adopt new media ownership rules. 
   05:36 - Bingaman talks about the energy bill currently working its way through Congress. 
   07:35 - Bingaman comments on reports that Los Alamos National Laboratories will be cutting jobs. 
   10:43 - Bingaman says FLETC does a great job of training the country's Border Patrol agent and he is confident that the federal government will continue to invest in the Artesia training center. 
   12:47 - Bingaman talks about a federally approved plan that would expand the Foreign Trade Zone (FTZ) in Do?a Ana County. 
   14:57 - Bingaman comments on the wolf reintroduction program in New Mexico. 
   17:40 - Bingaman comments on a proposal by the U.S. Department of Interior to increase the federal share of royalties from mineral resources extracted from federal land. 
   18:47 - Bingaman express his thoughts regarding the recent passing of Robert O. Anderson. 
   19:58 - Bingaman says Congress is likely to bundle most of the remaining spending bill together into an omnibus bill in order to get it passed before the end of the year. 
   21:44 - Bingaman comments on a new U.S. intelligence review that concludes Iran stopped developing a nuclear weapons program in 2003.
