SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS TO NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today answered questions on a variety of topics discussed during his weekly radio press conference with New Mexico radio reporters. 
  Bingaman's remarks can be retrieved by clicking on the following link:
   http://bingaman.senate.gov/Bingaman_Press_Release/bi ngaman_press_release_0.html
   00:00 - Bingaman talks about a letter he sent to the Department of Homeland Security calling on them to help cover the cost of emergency measures being taken in New Mexico to help secure the border.
   01:18 - Bingaman talks about yesterdays Energy Committee hearing on high gas prices.
   03:30 - Bingaman talks about Senator Domenici's statement that he is interested in looking at improving vehicle fuel efficiency.
   05:17 - Bingaman talks about the world wide demand for gasoline and how it is affecting the United States.
   07:57 - Bingaman talks about the reality of "price gauging" at the gas pump. 
   09:38 - Bingaman talks about the concern over having too much of the country's refinery capacity concentrated in a vulnerable area like the gulf coast. 
   10:10 - Bingaman comments on the government's lack of preparation and slow response to Hurricane Katrina. 
   12:10 - Bingaman comments on the confirmation process of John Roberts to the Supreme Court.
   13:37 - Bingaman talks about having additional hearings in the Energy Committee to further examine the price of gas, as well as the price of natural gas.
