SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS TO NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  U.S. Senator Jeff Bingaman today talked to New Mexico radio reporters legislation aimed at addressing the country's current housing crisis and help Americans avoid foreclosure. Bingaman's remarks can be retrieved by going to his website or clicking on the link to the right: 
   00:00 - Bingaman talks about legislation currently on the Senate floor aimed at addressing the country's current housing crisis and help Americans avoid foreclosure.
   01:15 - Bingaman comments on a report compiled by the National Priorities Project on where tax dollars go.
   04:02 - Bingaman talks about his how the Foreclosure Prevention Act is going to help New Mexicans.
   06:25 - Bingaman talks about some of the wildfires in Southeastern New Mexico.
   07:38 - Bingaman says he continues to press the Bush administration to suspend the filling the Strategic Petroleum Reserve with the price of oil as high as it is.
   09:02 - Bingaman says he has not yet had a chance to review General David Petraeus testimony before the Senates Armed Services Committee.
   10:50 - Bingaman talks about a Colombian Free Trade Agreement that is expected to come to the senate in the near future.
   13:32 - Bingaman talks about federal funding for the Federal Law Enforcement Training Center (FLECT) in Artesia and for WIPP in Carlsbad.
