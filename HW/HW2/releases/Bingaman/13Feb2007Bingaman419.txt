SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS GLOBAL WARMING/BUSH BUDGET CUTS WITH NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman answered questions on a variety of topics discussed during his weekly radio press conference with New Mexico radio reporters. 
   00:00 - Bingaman gives a brief overview of what is happening on the Senate floor. 
   01:36 - Bingaman talks about President Bush's fiscal year 2008 budget proposal and what that means for New Mexico's federal laboratories. 
   02:28 - Bingaman talks how the president's fiscal year 2008 budget cuts to Medicaid would affect New Mexicans. 
   04:09 - Bingaman talks about this morning's Energy Committee hearing on a British report on the economic impacts of global warming. 
   07:16 - Bingaman talks about the continuing resolution bill currently being debated on the Senate floor. 
   10:17 - Bingaman talks about a Senate resolution that expresses opposition to the president's plan for a troop surge in Iraq. 
   12:11 - Bingaman talks about how the Congress will likely respond to the president's budget proposal during this year's appropriations process. 
   13:06 - Bingaman comments on the proposed ban on cockfighting in New Mexico. 
   14:35 - Bingaman says that, unlike other years, this year's White House budget proposal does include funding for Iraq. 
   19:19 - Bingaman talks about his plans to introduce legislation to settle the Navajo Nation's water rights claims in the San Juan River Basin.
