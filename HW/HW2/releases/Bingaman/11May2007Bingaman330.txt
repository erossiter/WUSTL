SEN. BINGAMAN: SOUTHWESTERN NEW MEXICO AIRPORTS TO BENEFIT FROM $2.17 MILLION IN DOT FUNDS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today announced that five airports in southwestern New Mexico will receive funding from the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) to make improvements to their facilities and runways.  
   "An investment in our state's airports is an investment in our state's economy. I am pleased that the federal government is making this important investment in New Mexico's airports," Bingaman said. 
   The following airports received funding: 
   * Lordsburg Municipal Airport $735,000: Funding will be used to construct a parallel taxiway to the airport only single paved runway. The project will enhance safety and capacity at the airport. 
   * Truth or Consequences Municipal Airport $642,573: Funding will be used to construct a new apron designed to accommodate corporate aircraft that frequently fly to this airport. 
   * Hatch Municipal Airport $300,000: Funding will be used for the second phase of a new taxiway that will provide access to the aircraft hanger area. 
   * Do?a Ana County Municipal Airport in Santa Teresa $289,000: Funding will be used to purchase a sweeper to assist in control of debris and sand collecting during periods of high winds at the airport. Funding will also be used for fencing around the airport to help keep animals and trespassers out of airport property. 
   * Grant County Airport in Silver City $210,000: Funding will be used to build a perimeter fence to assist in keeping wildlife outside of the area.
