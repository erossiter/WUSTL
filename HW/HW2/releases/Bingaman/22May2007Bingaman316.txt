SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS IMMIGRATION WITH NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  During his weekly radio press conference with New Mexico radio reporters Sen. Jeff Bingaman talked about the immigration bill currently on the Senate floor. Bingaman also answered questions on a variety of other topics. 
   00:00 - Bingaman talk about the immigration bill that is currently being debated on the Senate floor and about his amendment to reduce the size of the guest worker program. 
   01:40 - Bingaman said he does not support attempts to designate English as the national language. 
   03:02- Bingaman said he is pleased to learn that the City of Roswell has purchased a new rescue and fire fighting vehicle for the Roswell Airport. 
   04:15 - Bingaman talks about how the immigration bill will affect the U.S. labor force and about an amendment he plans to introduce today that would cap the number of guest workers at 200,000. 
   05:42 - Bingaman said he has concerns that some of the hurdles that are being demanded from those seeking legalization might actually keep immigrant from coming forward. 
   06:44 - Bingaman talks about the guest worker programs being proposed in the current bill. 
   08:35 - Bingaman said that the U.S. Immigration Service would need to be revamped to in order to implement a new immigration law. 
   10:25 - Bingaman talks about how the immigration bill would affect New Mexico's agricultural sector. 
   11:52 - Bingaman talks about Roswell's efforts to establish regional jet service between Dallas-Ft. Worth (DFW) and Roswell. 
   17:05 - Bingaman said that the Federal Law Enforcement Training Center (FLETC) in Artesia will play an important role in training additional border patrol agents. 
   17:48 - Bingaman said he hopes Americans will take the time this Memorial Day to remember and thank those who have served our country.
