SEN. BINGAMAN: ALBUQUERQUE GROUP GAINS $600,000 TO IMPROVE JUVENILE JUSTICE PROGRAM
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman announced today that the Native American Alliance Foundation (NAAF) has been awarded a $600,000 grant from the federal government to support efforts to improve juvenile justice in Indian Country.  
 NAAF is based in Albuquerque and has field offices in Tulsa, OK; Bethel, AK; and Riverside, CA.
   "The Native American Alliance Foundation has made many important contributions toward reducing juvenile delinquency. I am pleased that the funds awarded today will allow them to continue their work," Bingaman said. 
   The funds are being provided through the U.S. Department of Justice's Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention (OJJDP). The Foundation will provide services to help support the development, planning and implementation of solutions aimed at reducing juvenile delinquency, and violence in Indian Country.
