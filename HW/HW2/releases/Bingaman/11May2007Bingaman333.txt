SEN. BINGAMAN: FEDERAL FUNDS WILL HELP IMPROVE FOUR CORNERS, GALLUP, CROWNPOINT, & GRANTS AIRPORTS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today announced that Four Corners Regional Airport and other Northwestern New Mexico communities will share about millions in federal funds to make airport improvements.  
   Four Corners Regional Airport has been awarded $3.7 million to rehabilitate runway 05/23, rehabilitate airfield lighting and signs on runways, and purchase a replacement aircraft rescue and firefighting vehicle. The Gallup Municipal Airport will receive $1.9 million to assist with the reconstruction of 1300 feet of the parallel taxiway. 
   Shiprock Airstrip will receive $292,500 to rehabilitate the taxiway by improving drainage and reconstruction. Crownpoint airport will receive $150,000 to make drainage improvements. And the Grants-Milan Municipal Airport has been awarded $47,500 to develop an updated master plan that will identify needed improvements. 
   "Federal funding is essential to helping ensure these airports are safe and can be properly maintained," Bingaman said. 
   The funding comes from the U.S. Department of Transportation.
