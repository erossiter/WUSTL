SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS TO NEW MAXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today answered questions on a variety of topics discussed during his weekly radio press conference with New Mexico radio reporters.  
   00:00 - Bingaman talks about the immigration legislation currently on the Senate floor.
   01:26 - Bingaman talks an amendment he plans to introduce to the immigration bill that would limit the cap for the bill's guest worker program to 200,000. 
   03:24 - Bingaman talks about the Aamodt water settlement.
   05:33 - Bingaman talks about the president's proposal to send 6,000 National Guardsmen and women to assist Border Patrol agents in supportive roles. 
   09:08 - Bingaman said he was disappointed that months after signing a law authorizing the hiring of 2,000 border patrol agents a year, President Bush's fiscal year 2006 budget request contained funding for only 210 agents.
   13:14 - Bingaman talks about the different amendments that will be offered to the immigration bill.
   15:46 - Bingaman talks about the nomination of Air Force Gen. Michael Hayden to be the new director of the CIA.
   16:42 - Bingaman says he is very concerned about the NSA's wiretapping program and said more questions remained to be answered. 
   17:45 - Bingaman talks about different ways the United States can help secure our borders.
   21:58 - Bingaman talks about the deadline to sign up for Medicare Part D. The deadline expired yesterday. Those who enroll at a later date will face a financial penalty.
