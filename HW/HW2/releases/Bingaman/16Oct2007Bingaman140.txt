SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS WITH NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS ABOUT A PROVISION HE WROTE TO HELP FIGHT METH IN INDIAN COUNTRY
  U.S. Senator Jeff Bingaman today talked to New Mexico radio reporters and answered questions on a variety of other topics. Bingaman's remarks can be retrieved by going to his website or clicking on the link on the right: 
   00:00 - Bingaman talks about the Commerce, Justice and Science spending bill currently on the Senate floor and about a provision he worked to get included in the bill that gives Native Communities access to grant programs aimed at combating the production and distribution of meth. 
   01:57 - Bingaman says he has been working with the state of New Mexico to try to get federal funds committed for the Rail Runner train project. 
   03:53 - Bingaman comments on efforts to revisit the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act. 
   05:24 - Bingaman talks about energy legislation he helped write that is working its way through Congress. 
   10:19 - Bingaman says he looks forward to learning more about the planned Grant County uranium mill. 
   10:56 - Bingaman talks about the children's health insurance bill that the president vetoed. 
   16:05 - Bingaman talks about the solvency of Social Security.
