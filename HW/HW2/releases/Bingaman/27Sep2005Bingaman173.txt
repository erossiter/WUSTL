SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS TO NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today answered questions on a variety of topics discussed during his weekly radio press conference with New Mexico radio reporters. 
   00:00 - Bingaman talks about legislation he introduced to allow the federal government to prosecute those who overcharge for gasoline and diesel during a major disaster. (more at 06:50)
   02:06 - Bingaman talks about the president's statement's urging the American public to conserve gasoline.
   03:36 - Bingaman talks about Corporate Average Fuel Efficiency standards (CAFE). 
   04:37 - Bingaman talks about Otero Mesa.
   05:33 - Bingaman talks about his position on building nuclear power plants in the United States.
   06:49 - Bingaman talks about legislation he introduced that would allow the federal government to prosecute those who overcharge for gasoline and diesel during a major disaster. 
   09:21 - Bingaman talks about his decision to vote to confirm John Roberts to the U.S. Supreme Court.
   10:58 - Bingaman talks about his letter to the administration calling for the completion of vehicle barricades along the New Mexico-Mexico border.
   18:27 - Bingaman comments on response efforts to hurricane Rita. 
   22:28 - Bingaman says he supports having an independent group look into the federal, state and local response to hurricane Katrina.
   21:07 - Bingaman talks about last week's Energy Committee hearing on global warming.
   25:49 - Bingaman talks about his bill before the Energy Committee tomorrow, which would authorize the U.S. Department of the Interior, through the Bureau of Reclamation, to establish a program to plan, design and construct rural water supply projects.
