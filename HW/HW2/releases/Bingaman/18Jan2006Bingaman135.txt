SEN. BINGAMAN: NEW MEXICO FARMERS TO BENEFIT FROM USDA FUNDS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today announced that a total of $420,000 has been awarded to assist disadvantaged farmers and ranchers in New Mexico.  
 Funding was awarded through the U.S. Department of Agriculture's Cooperative State Research, Education and Extension Services (CREES).
   The following organizations were awarded:
   * New Mexico State University's Sustainable 
  Agriculture Science Center in Alcalde $270,000 
   * Crownpoint Institute of Technology $150,000 
   As part of the CREES's Outreach and Assistance for Socially Disadvantaged Farmers and Ranchers Program, the grants will help Crownpoint and New Mexico State University conduct outreach and technical assistance to encourage and assist socially disadvantages farmers and ranchers to own and operate farms and ranches. The grants will also help deliver a wide range of support activities including: farmer management, financial management and marketing to New Mexico farmers and ranchers.
   "The funding awarded today will help strengthen New Mexico's family-owned farms and ranches," Bingaman said. "I'm glad the federal government is making an investment in our state's agriculture industry."
