SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS TO NM RADIO REPORTERS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today answered questions on a variety of topics discussed during his weekly radio press conference with New Mexico radio reporters.  
 Bingaman's remarks can be retrieved by clicking on the following link:
   http://bingaman.senate.gov/Bingaman_Press_Release/bi ngaman_press_release_3.html
   * 00:00 - Bingaman talks his amendment in the Senate that would bolster New Mexico's health care safety net by $66 million.
   * 04:35 - Bingaman talks about his efforts to increase border patrol agents along the New Mexico-Mexico border.
   * 06:09 - Bingaman talks funding he secured in a spending bill to restore and maintain riparian areas along the Rio Grande and Pecos River where the state is in its third year of working to treat and kill the noxious salt cedar.  
   * 07:13 - Bingaman talks about the status of a budget bill that passed the Senate last week that would makes deep cuts in health care while setting aside $1 billion for an unnecessary milk subsidy that benefits farmers in only a few states
   * 09:28 - Bingaman talks about Wednesday's Energy Committee and Commerce Committee joint hearing that will look at energy pricing and profits.
   * 11:36 - Bingaman talks about efforts in Congress to provide funding to help a school improve their performance.
   * 15:13 - Bingaman says he is excited to learn more about the Navajo-Gallup water settlement during his trip to the region this weekend.
   * 17:07 - Bingaman talks about elections taking place today around the country.
