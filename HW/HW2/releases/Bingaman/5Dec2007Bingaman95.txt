SEN. BINGAMAN: NEW MEXICO COMMUNITIES TO SHARE $576,477 IN FEDERAL FUNDS FOR SCENIC BYWAYS
  U.S. Senator Jeff Bingaman today said he is pleased that communities across the state will share $576,477 in scenic byway grants from the Federal Highway Administration.  
   "This funding is a major investment in New Mexico communities and in our state's tourism efforts," Bingaman said. 
   * Corridor Management Plan implementation Trail of the Mountain Spirits National Scenic Byway $168,280 
   * Gallup bike/pedestrian trail Route 66 National Scenic Byway $50,000 
   * Marketing byways on tribal lands New Mexico Tourism Department $50,000 
   * Corridor Management Plan La Frontera del Llano Scenic Byway $38,000 
   * Corridor Management Plan implementation Billy the Kid National Scenic Byway $33,344 
   * Interpretive Exhibit Billy the Kid National Scenic Byway $44,000 
   * Marketing and wayshowing Geronimo Trail National Scenic Byway $20,800 
   * Route 66 neon gateway, Grants Route 66 National Scenic Byway $52,500 
   * Santa Fe Trail kiosk, Raton Santa Fe Trail National Scenic Byway $17,953 
   * Gateway on Fourth and Vineyard, Los Ranchos de Albuquerque, Route 66 and El Camino Real National Scenic Byways $40,000 
   * Wayfinding, Santa Rosa, Mesalands Scenic Byway and Route 66 National Scenic Byway $61,600
