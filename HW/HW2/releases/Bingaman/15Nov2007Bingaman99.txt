SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS WITH NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  U.S. Senator Jeff Bingaman today talked to New Mexico radio reporters and answered questions on a variety of other topics. Bingaman's remarks can be retrieved by going to his website or clicking on the link to the right: 
   00:00 - Bingaman gives an overview of what the Senate is trying to complete before Thanksgiving Recess. 
   02:25 - Bingaman discusses the role of Los Alamos National Laboratory in the production of plutonium pits. 
   05:30 - Bingaman discusses the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee's hearing this week about the Global Nuclear Energy Partnership. 
   07:53 - Bingaman further discusses LANL's role in plutonium production. 
   09:56 - Bingaman discusses his pending trip to Mexico City and his support for a proposal aimed at reducing the flow of drugs from Mexico. 
   12:49 - Bingaman further discusses LANL's role in plutonium production. 
   15:124 - Bingaman's discusses the conditions under which he would support $50 billion supplemental spending bill for Iraq. 
   16:36 - Bingaman says he is dismayed by President Bush's veto of the Labor, Health and Human Services Spending Bill and the impact of that action on New Mexico. 
   18:10 - Bingaman discusses the status of bills that fund the national laboratories and other agencies in the federal government.
