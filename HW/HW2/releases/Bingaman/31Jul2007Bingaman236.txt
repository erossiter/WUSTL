SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS WITH NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS ABOUT LEGISLATION TO STRENGTHEN HEALTH CARE COVERAGE
  U.S. Senator Jeff Bingaman today talked to New Mexico radio reporters today about legislation to reauthorize the State Children's Health Insurance Program (SCHIP). Bingaman also answered questions on a variety of other topics.  
   00:00 - Bingaman talks about the State Children's Health Insurance Program (SCHIP) reauthorization bill currently being debated on the Senate floor.
   03:27 - Bingaman comments on the spending funding outlooks for Los Alamos National Labs.
   06:10 - Bingaman talks about what the Senate is working to accomplish before the August state work period.
   07:35 - Bingaman says insuring children have access to health care coverage is the first step to working to make coverage available to adults.
   09:05 - Bingaman says the Senate Armed Services Committee should hold hearing into whether the U.S. should sell weapons to Saudi Arabia.
   09:35 - Bingaman talks about the ongoing war in Iraq.
   11:55 - Bingaman talks about the Bush Administration's statement that it will veto the SCHIP health care bill.
   13:35 - Bingaman talks the Higher Education Bill that passed the Senate last week.
   15:57 - Bingaman says he will be in New Mexico for the August sate working period and will be traveling around the state to meet with constituents.
   16:40 - Bingaman talks about the fiscal year 2008 Department of Homeland Security spending bill and said he is pleased it contains $3 billion for border security.
