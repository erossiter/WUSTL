SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS TO NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today answered questions on a variety of topics discussed during his weekly radio press conference with New Mexico radio reporters. Bingaman's remarks can be retrieved by going to his website or clicking on the following link:  
   00:00 - Bingaman talks about legislation currently on the Senate floor that would open up an area on the Gulf of Mexico to oil and gas drilling. 
   02:32 - Bingaman comments on reports that Cuban President Fidel Castro is sick. 
   04:15 - Bingaman talks about the Estate Tax cut bill currently being debated on the Senate floor. 
   05:37 - Bingaman talks about his concerns regarding the legislation to open up an area on the Gulf of Mexico to oil and gas drilling. 
   08:45 - Bingaman talks about the need to crack down on people illegally evading taxes. 
   09:51 - Bingaman comments on a House of Representatives Committee hearing in Las Cruces to look at illegal immigrants voting illegally. 
   11:43 - Bingaman talks about a letter he wrote with Senator Domenici and Congressman Pearce asking the Army to delay a study aimed at privatizing some jobs at White Sands Missile Range. 
   12:50 - Bingaman talked about immigration and the current status of the immigration bill pending in Congress. 
   14:33 - Bingaman comments on the Bush Administrations handling of the current situation in the Middle East. 
   17:35 - Bingaman comments on Prime Minister Nuri al-Maliki's remarks to Congress.
