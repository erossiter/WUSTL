SEN. BINGAMAN: FUNDING TO BENEFIT NORTHERN NEW MEXICO SCHOOLS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman reported today that Northern New Mexico Network for Rural Education has been awarded a $1.1 million grant from the U.S. Department of Education. 
   The Northern New Mexico Network is comprised of 28 rural school districts, which work to create positive learning environments in rural school districts in the state. Funding will be used to support the Network's Teacher Quality Enhancement Grant Program, which seeks to recruit and prepare teachers to serve the needs of Northern New Mexico schools.
   "The funding awarded today is an important investment in our state's schools and will help increase the number of qualified teachers in New Mexico," Bingaman said.
   Member schools are: The Chama Valley Independent Schools; Cimmarron Public Schools ; Clayton Public Schools; Cuba; Independent Schools; Des Moines Municipal Schools; Dulce Independent Schools; Espanola Public Schools; Grants-Cibola County Schools; Jemez Mountain Public Schools; Jemez Valley Public Schools; Las Vegas City Public Schools; Las Vegas West Public Schools; Logan Municipal Schools; Los Alamos Public Schools; Mesa Vista Consolidated Schools; Mora Independent Schools; Pecos Independent Schools; Penasco Independent Schools; Pojoaque Valley Public Schools; Questa Independent Schools; Raton Public Schools; Roy Municipal Schools; Santa Rosa Consolidated Schools; Santa Rosa Consolidated Schools; Taos Municipal Schools; Tucumcari Public Schools; Vaughn Municipal Schools; Wagon Mound Public Schools.
