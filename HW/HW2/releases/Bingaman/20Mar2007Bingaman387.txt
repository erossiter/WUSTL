SEN. BINGAMAN: FUNDS WILL HELP BRING HIGH-TECH JOBS TO NEW MEXICO'S BORDER REGION
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today announced that the U.S. Economic Development Administration has awarded a $600,000 grant to a help bring high-tech businesses to the U.S.-Mexico border.  
   Funding was awarded to the Bi-National Sustainability Laboratory (BNSL), a non-profit organization located in Santa Teresa that works to promote research collaboration between the U.S. and Mexico to create spin-off companies through their technological research and development. BNSL works with start-ups, small and medium sized businesses, and large well-established companies. 
   Bingaman said funding will help support expansion of BNSL's programs. 
   "The funding awarded today will help BNSL continue its efforts to promote economic development opportunities, bringing good jobs to southern New Mexico," Bingaman said.
