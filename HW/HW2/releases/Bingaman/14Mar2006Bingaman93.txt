SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS TO NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today answered questions on a variety of topics discussed during his weekly radio press conference with New Mexico radio reporters.  
   Bingaman's remarks can be retrieved by going to his website or clicking on the following link:
   http://demradio.senate.gov/actualities/bingaman/bingama n060314.mp3.
   00:00 - Bingaman talks about his Energy Amendment to the 2007 Budget Resolution.
   02:03 - Bingaman talks about his meeting this week with President Bush to discuss education and competitiveness.
   03:42 - Bingaman says his office is looking into what federal assistance is available for those affected by the grass fires in southeastern New Mexico.
   04:32 - Bingaman talks about Senator Feingold's effort to censure President Bush.
   09:35 - Bingaman the various immigration bills floating around the Senate. 
   11:57 - Bingaman talks about the Pentagon's plans to base two F-22A Raptor squadrons at Holloman Air Force Base in Alamogordo, a mission that would replace the F-117 stealth fighters currently at the base.
   14:39 - Bingaman comments on new reports that state the U.S. was aware that Iraq had no weapons on mass destruction. 
   16:05 - Bingaman comments on Secretary Gale Norton's resignation as head of the Department of the Interior.
   19:00 - Bingaman talks about what that Budget Resolution will do to help increase U.S. Competitiveness in the area of science and tech.
   22:07 - Bingaman talks about his legislation to help address the problems associated with the implementation of the prescription drug program. 
   24:04 - Bingaman talks about funding he helped secure to help maintain school bus routes on the Navajo Nation.
