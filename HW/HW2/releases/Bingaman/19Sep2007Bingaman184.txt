SEN. BINGAMAN: FUNDING TO HELP UNIVERSITY OF NEW MEXICO PREPARE STUDENTS FOR DOCTORAL STUDIES
  U.S. Senator Jeff Bingaman announced today that the University of New Mexico has been awarded $231,448 to help disadvantaged students pursue doctoral degrees. 
   Funding was awarded through the U.S. Department of Education can be used on projects that prepare students for doctoral studies through involvement in research and other academic activities. Projects include: academic counseling, financial aid assistance, mentoring, research opportunities, seminars, summer internships and tutoring. 
   "This funding will help provide support and guidance to students seeking to earn advanced agrees at UNM," Bingaman said.
