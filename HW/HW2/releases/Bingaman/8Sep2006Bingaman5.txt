SEN. BINGAMAN: FUNDING TO BENEFIT NATIVE AMERICAN LIBRARY, MUSEUM PROJECTS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today announced that four Native American Communities have been awarded grants for library and museum projects. Funding was awarded through the Institute of Museums and Library Services.  
   "This funding is an important investment in libraries and museums that serve Native American communities in our state," Bingaman said. 
   Pueblo of Jemez - $150,000: Funding will help the Jemez Pueblo Community Library provide year-round literacy programs, after school tutoring and family literacy nights, which are designed to teach adults how to read to their children. 
   Pueblo of Sandia - $150,000: Funding Hill help the Santa Clara Pueblo Community Library address a variety of community needs, including provide resources for substance abuse prevention and mentorship programs for high school students. 
   Mescalero Apache Tribe - $49,494: Funding will be used to help the Mescalero Apache Tribe commission the noted Hopi film director Victor Masayesva to develop a series of five to eight short documentaries on the Apache experience. 
   Ohkay Owingeh Pueblo - $45,880: Funding will help the pueblo complete the master plan for the Ohkay Owingeh First Capital Heritage Center and Museum.
