SEN. BINGAMAN: NEW MEXICO RECEIVES GRANTS TO FIGHT DRUG ABUSE
  U.S. Senator Jeff Bingaman today welcomed news that the federal government has awarded over $900,000 in federal grants to fight drug and alcohol abuse in New Mexico communities.  
   Funding was provided by the Office of National Drug Control Policy's (ONCDP) Drug Free Communities Support Program, which helps community coalitions mobilize their neighborhoods to prevent youth alcohol, tobacco, illicit drug and inhalant use. 
   Carlsbad Community Anti-Drug/Gang Coalition $100,000 
  Hands Across Cultures Corporation in Espa?ola $100,000 
  North Central Community Based Services, Inc. in Chama $75,000 
  Lea County $100,000 
  New Mexico Voices for Children in Albuquerque $100,000 
  Torrance County $90,710 
  San Juan County Partnership $100,000 
  Presbyterian Medical Services in Albuquerque $100,000 
  The Wellness Coalition in Silver City $100,000 
   "Our state is experiencing a rise in the use of meth, a very dangerous and often deadly drug. New Mexico communities need help fighting meth and other drugs, and this federal investment can help make a difference," Bingaman said.
