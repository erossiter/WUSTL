SEN. BINGAMAN: FEDERAL FUNDS TO BENEFIT SOLAR ENERGY RESEARCH AT NEW MEXICO STATE UNIVERSITY
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman announced today that the New Mexico State University has been awarded an $875,000 grant from the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) for solar energy research and development at the university. 
   Funds will benefit NMSU's Southwest Region Experiment Station (SWRES), which provides critical support to the DOE National Center for Photovoltaics. SWRES, establish by DOE in 1980, assistance with the implementation of solar and wind energy programs, as well as assistance in evaluating the feasibility of solar energy projects, creating business plans, and standards development. The funding will be used to continue to support the DOE. 
   "I am pleased the federal government is making an investment in NMSU's efforts to further solar energy research," Bingaman said.
   Bingaman is the top Democrat on the Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee and a strong supporter of committing federal funds to renewable energy research.
