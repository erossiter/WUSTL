SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS ENERGY, OTHER TOPICS WITH NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  U.S. Senator Jeff Bingaman today talked to New Mexico radio reporters and answered questions on a variety of other topics. Bingaman's remarks can be retrieved by going to his website or clicking on the link to the right: 
   00:00 - Bingaman comments on today's Energy Committee hearing on the affects market speculation is having on the cost of oil. 
   01:50 - Bingaman says a provision repealing a tax incentive for oil producers would not affect consumers. 
   03:31 - Bingaman talks about a bill he is cosponsoring that would help determine how to improve the flow of commercial and passenger traffic between the United States and Mexico. 
   05:30 - Bingaman talks about the Corporate Average Fuel Economy (CAFE) standards and the renewable portfolio standard (RPS) that are in the energy bill currently pending in Congress. 
   08:17 - Bingaman says he looks forward to hearing New Mexico State Engineer John D'Antonio's testimony before the Energy Committee today on the SECURE Water Act, which Bingaman introduced. 
   10:56 - Bingaman says he is hopeful to that legislation to Navajo Nation's water claims to San Juan River Basin water will be able to move forward. 
   12:25 - Bingaman talks about how the energy bill pending before Congress would benefit New Mexico if passed and signed into law. 
   13:47 - Bingaman comments on new reports that the CIA destroyed videotaping of the agency's interrogations of detainees.
