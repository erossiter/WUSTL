SEN. BINGAMAN: NEW MEXICO SCHOOL DISTRICTS AWARDED FEDERAL FUNDS
  Sen. Jeff Bingaman today announced that two New Mexico school districts have been awarded federal funding to improve U.S. history curriculum. 
   The Albuquerque Public School District will receive $998,929 for a project to improve students' understanding of American history through teacher preparation efforts. The project, entitled "Reading, Writing and Researching American History in the Rio Grande Valley", will serve 152 teachers in grades 3-12 and has the potential to benefit approximately 116,746 students.
   Santa Fe Public Schools will receive $498,549 for a project similar project that benefits students by providing education and raining to teachers. The school district's project helps to organize history institutes, workshops and working field trips to facilitate teacher improvement U.S. history instruction.
   "Investing in teacher preparedness is an investment in our children's education," Bingaman said. "This funding will help many New Mexico students improve their knowledge of United States history."
   Funding was awarded through the U.S. Department of Education's Teacher American History Grant Program.
