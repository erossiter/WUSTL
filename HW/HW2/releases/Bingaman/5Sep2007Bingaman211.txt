SEN. BINGAMAN TALKS WITH NEW MEXICO RADIO REPORTERS
  U.S. Senator Jeff Bingaman today talked to New Mexico radio reporters and answered questions on a variety of other topics. Bingaman's remarks can be retrieved by going to his website or clicking on the link to the right:  
   00:00 - Bingaman talks about the Senate's plans for the remaining of the year. 
   01:41 - Bingaman comments on President Bush's continuing support for the troop surge in Iraq. 
   02:51 - Bingaman says he did not support legislation to renew the government's electronic surveillance program, which Congress approved in last month. 
   03:46 - Bingaman comments on the president's threats to veto measures that he feels exceed spending limits. 
   05:08 - Bingaman talks about his appropriation priorities for New Mexico. 
   06:30 - Bingaman talks about the energy and water appropriations bill that funds New Mexico's federal laboratories. 
   07:27 - Bingaman talks about the Bush Administration's threat to veto the SCHIP health care bill, which would boost health care coverage for many of New Mexico's children. 
   08:46 - Bingaman talks about the ethics reform bill that Congress is sending to the president to be signed into law. 
   10:52 - Bingaman comments on Mexican commercial truck being allowed onto U.S. highways for the fist time as part of the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA). 
   12:37 - Bingaman says there will most likely be continued efforts in Congress to find a solution to the Iraq conflict.
