U S  Releases  1 B  For      Home Heating Assistance        Delegations Efforts Help Bring       18 5 M  In New Funds To Vermont WASHINGTON       Friday  Mar  24   The Department of Health and Human Services has      released  1 billion in emergency funds to help needy Americans      across the country heat their homes as cold temperatures continue      into the spring season  Vermont will receive more than  6 million      from the funding  which was approved in legislation advocated by      Sens  Patrick Leahy and Jim Jeffords  and Rep  Bernie Sanders in      Congress earlier this month  Any unused funds from the new release      also can be carried over to the next season to help Vermonters and      those in need in other states   The Low Income      Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  is a federally funding      program designed to help low income homeowners meeting home heating      needs  Last week Congress passed legislation reallocating mandatory      funds for the program  making  1 billion available in 2006 instead      of 2007  In light of record heating bills this winter  Leahy       Jeffords and Sanders have supported congressional efforts to      increase funding for LIHEAP  including failed attempts to approve      four separate delegation backed initiatives for additional funding      in recent months  all of which were denied votes in the Senate       The calendar      may say its spring  but for hundreds of Vermonters  the temperatures      people to keep the heat turned on  said Leahy  Despite reluctance      from some in the Congress and the Administration  making this      additional funding available now is the right thing to do  This      will also give us a head start on the funds we will need for next      season  While still      falling short of what is truly needed  I am delighted that the Bush      Administration has released the LIHEAP funding recently approved by      Congress  said Sanders  In the richest country on earth  we have      a moral responsibility to ensure that no one goes cold this      winter  The new funding      brings the total allocations for the program to  3 billion for FY      2006  Vermont has received more than  18 5 million in LIHEAP      funding in 2006
