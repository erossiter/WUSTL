SENS. STABENOW, LEVIN ANNOUNCE $5 MILLION GRANT TO AID DEVELOPMENT OF SOUTHWEST MICHIGAN REGIONAL AIRPORT IN BENTON HARBOR
  The U.S. Department of Transportation has awarded a $5 million to the State of Michigan for continued development of Southwest Michigan Regional Airport in Benton Harbor, Sens.  
 Debbie Stabenow (D-MI) and Carl Levin (D-MI) announced today. 
   This grant will support land acquisition and construction of a runway safety area as part of the extension of Runway 9/27 at the airport.
   "Southwest Michigan Regional Airport is an important facility for corporate and general aviation aircraft flying into southwestern Michigan," Stabenow said. "These infrastructure improvements at Michigan airports are essential to boost tourism, help create jobs and ultimately benefit everyone in the state." 
   "This grant will help Southwest Michigan Regional Airport continue to serve as a commercial and tourist hub for the state," said Levin. "This airport has been estimated to contribute $14 million to the local economy and these funds will allow the airport to make needed improvements so that it can continue benefiting Michigan."
