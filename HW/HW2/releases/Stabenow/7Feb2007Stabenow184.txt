SENS. LEVIN, STABENOW ANNOUNCE OVER $1.3 MILLION FOR FIRE SAFETY
  Sens. Carl Levin (D-MI) and Debbie Stabenow (D-MI) announced on Wednesday, Feb. 7, that sixteen Michigan fire departments have been awarded $1,318,654 through the fiscal year 2006 Department of Homeland Security Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program. The funding will be used to support operations and firefighter safety.  
   "This is good news for communities and families across Michigan," said Levin. "Better training and equipment will help our brave firefighters and first responders to do their jobs safely and effectively."
   "Michigan's first responders work tirelessly keeping our families and homes safe," said Stabenow. "These grants will allow communities across the state to provide these brave men and women with the best training and equipment available."
   The AFGP awards grants directly to fire departments and EMS organizations to improve the effectiveness of operations and health and safety programs, and to establish or expand fire prevention programs throughout the United States.
