SENS. STABENOW, LEVIN: OVER $72,000 COMING TO COOPER CHARTER TOWNSHIP FOR FIRE SAFETY
  U.S. Senators Debbie Stabenow (D-MI) and Carl Levin (D-MI) today announced that Cooper Charter Township Fire Department has been awarded $72,905 through the fiscal year 2007 Department of Homeland Security Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program (AFGP). The funding will be used to support operations and firefighter safety.  
   "Michigan's first responders deserve nothing less than the very best training and equipment," said Stabenow. "I'm pleased this funding will assist Cooper Charter Township firefighters in continuing to keep their community safe."
   "This grant will help the Cooper Charter Township Fire Department fulfill its mission of protecting the public," said Levin. "Our firefighters and emergency medical personnel put themselves on the line every day and deserve our support."
   The AFGP awards grants directly to fire departments and EMS organizations to improve the effectiveness of operations and health and safety programs, to purchase protective equipment and response vehicles, to establish or expand fire prevention programs throughout the United States and for public safety education. Grants are awarded competitively through an application process. More information can be found at http://www.firegrantsupport.com/.
