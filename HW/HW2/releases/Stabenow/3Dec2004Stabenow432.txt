SENS. STABENOW, LEVIN ANNOUNCE $64 MILLION IN HOMELAND SECURITY GRANT FUNDING FOR MICHIGAN
  The state of Michigan is slated receive more than $64 million in six grant programs from the Department of Homeland Security for fiscal year 2005, Sens. Debbie Stabenow (D-MI) and Carl Levin (D-MI) said today. 
  "Every dollar of homeland security funding for a border state like Michigan is funding that is crucial for the safety of the people of Michigan and the security of the nation," Stabenow said. "Our communities have critical needs and this funding will help them prevent or respond to any potential terrorist acts." 
   "We have made real progress in our efforts to improve homeland security and protect our citizens," Levin said. "These grants will build on that foundation by directing resources into programs that will assist high-risk urban areas such as Detroit and Wayne County protect against a terrorist attack. This funding will also provide valuable assistance to our law enforcement agencies, emergency medical systems, emergency management operations and citizen preparedness campaigns."
   A total of $64,074,873 will be allocated to the state of Michigan and Michigan communities under the terms of six key programs coordinated by the Homeland Security Department:
   * State Homeland Security Grant Program - $29,739,980 This program provides assistance directly to states to prevent, respond to, and recover from acts of terrorism.
   * Urban Areas Security Initiative - $17,584,608 for Detroit and Wayne County This program provides financial assistance to address the unique planning, equipment, training and exercise needs of high-risk urban areas and to assist them in building an enhanced and sustainable capacity to prevent, respond to, and recover terrorist acts.
   * Law Enforcement Terrorism Prevention Program - $10,814,538 This program provides law enforcement communities with funds to support such anti-terrorist efforts as information sharing, target hardening, security planning and interoperable communications. 
   * Emergency Management Performance Grant Program - $4,875,422 These funds support the improvement of mitigation, preparedness, response and recovery capabilities for all hazards.
   * Metropolitan Medical Response System Program - $682,776 These grants are used to enhance integrated, systematic mass casualty incident preparedness to respond to mass casualty events during the first hours of response. 
   * Citizen Corps Program - $377,549 This is a grass-roots initiative to actively involve all citizens in hometown security through personal preparedness, training and volunteer service.
