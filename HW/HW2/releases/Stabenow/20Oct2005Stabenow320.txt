SENS. LEVIN, STABENOW ANNOUNCE OVER $630,000 FOR TRAINING IN ADVANCED MANUFACTURING AT SOUTHWEST MICHIGAN COLLEGE
  Sens. Carl Levin, D-Mich., and Debbie Stabenow, D-Mich., announced today that the U.S. Department of Labor has awarded $634,677 to Southwest Michigan College under the Community-Based Job Training grant program.  
 The funds will be used to expand the college's Precision Production program to support the advanced manufacturing industry. 
   "This is great news for Western Michigan," said Levin. "As the manufacturing sector comes to depend more and more on computers and technology, these federal dollars will help put the students of Southwest Michigan College in a great position to succeed."
   "Retaining good-paying manufacturing jobs is one of my top priorities," Stabenow said. "This grant is an important part of the effort to ensure that Michigan workers continue to be the best in the world and are prepared to meet the manufacturing challenges of tomorrow."
   Specifically, the grant will be used to recruit new students and install equipment for the Precision Production advanced manufacturing program. The program is aimed at preparing students for the rapidly growing and changing field of computer-based production machining. This money will help train approximately 148 students, including partial scholarships for those who qualify.
