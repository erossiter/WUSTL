SENS. STABENOW, LEVIN ANNOUNCE $1,319,947 IN FIREFIGHTER GRANTS FOR MICHIGAN
  Sens. Debbie Stabenow (D-MI) and Carl Levin (D-MI) announced today that five fire departments in Michigan have received a total of $1,319,947 in grants under the terms of the fiscal year 2005 Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program.  
   "Every fire department in Michigan faces critical challenges in protecting local residents and their property," Stabenow said. "Through these grants, we help ensure that Michigan's firefighters have the equipment, training, and personnel they need to respond to all threats and hazards." 
   "Michigan's first responders continue to play a vital role in ensuring our safety and security," said Levin. "These federal dollars are an important step in providing them with the resources they need to adapt to and manage a wide variety of potential dangers."
   The Operations and Firefighter Safety Program provides funding for training, equipment, personal protective equipment, wellness and fitness, and modifications to facilities. The Recruitment and Retention of Volunteer Firefighters grants are intended to increase the number of competent firefighters who are trained, certified, and capable of responding to emergencies likely to occur within the fire department's geographic area.
