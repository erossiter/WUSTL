SENS. LEVIN, STABENOW ANNOUNCE OVER $41 MILLION IN DOT GRANTS FOR SEVERAL MICHIGAN AIRPORTS
  Sens. Carl Levin, D-Mich., and Debbie Stabenow, D-Mich., announced today that the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) has awarded $41,395,659 in grants benefiting several Michigan airports.  
 The DOT grants will be used for safety improvements and development projects.
  "As millions of people fly into and out of Michigan's airports every year, we must continually update and improve facilities," said Sen. Levin. "The funding will help ensure the continued safety of our air travelers and will support additional improvements to our airports." 
   "Like all our other transportation infrastructure, our airport facilities need constant improvement and added security," Stabenow said. "This funding will improve travel for Michigan residents and visitors, and it will aid state business and commerce - building jobs and making the state an excellent place to live and work."
