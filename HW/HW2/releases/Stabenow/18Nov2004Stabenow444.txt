SENS. STABENOW, LEVIN ANNOUNCE $776,500 IN FIREFIGHTER GRANTS FOR 15 MICHIGAN COMMUNITIES
  Sens. Debbie Stabenow (D-MI) and Carl Levin (D-MI) announced today that the U.S. Department of Homeland Security has awarded $776,500 in grants to 15 Michigan fire departments under the terms of the fiscal year 2004 Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program.  
   "This grant program provides funding directly to local fire departments to improve their ability to respond to fires and fire-related emergencies in their communities," Stabenow said. "These federal grants will help local firefighters protect the communities they serve, while protecting themselves with better equipment, training and conditioning."
   "Investing in our firefighters by providing them with the best equipment and training available is an investment in our communities," said Levin. "These grants will help ensure that these fire departments are prepared for the emergencies they face each day."
   The grants were awarded for the Fire Operations and Firefighter Safety program, which provides firefighters with funding for equipment, training and modifications to facilities.
