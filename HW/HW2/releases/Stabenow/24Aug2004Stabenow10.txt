SENS. LEVIN, STABENOW ANNOUNCE GRANT FOR OVER $190,000 FOR CARRIEVILLE STATE FOREST IN LAKE COUNTY
  Sens. Carl Levin, D-Mich., and Debbie Stabenow, D-Mich., announced today that the National Parks Service has awarded the Carrieville State Forest a grant for $194,022 for campground renovation.  
 The grant will cover half the cost of the renovation, and will be used for various projects including roadwork, amenities and meeting the standards of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). 
   "The renovations to this campground will make it a more accessible place for all people to enjoy Michigan's outdoors," said Levin. "The roadwork funded by this grant will improve the campground and make it a more enjoyable place for recreation."
   "This campground restoration effort in the Carrieville State Forest will not only update the campground with new equipment and picnic tables but also will make this wonderful recreation spot a more accessible place for all Americans," Stabenow said. 
   The funds from this grant will be used for the following projects:
   * Road renovation at the campground's entrance and four main loops.
   * Putting in camping pads, fire rings and picnic tables.
   * Bringing the campground up to ADA standards.
   Funding for the grant comes through the National Park Service's Land and Water Conservation Fund. The goals of these grants are to promote the ideals of preservation and aid already existing public parks. For more information regarding LWCF grants, please visit: www.nps.gov.
