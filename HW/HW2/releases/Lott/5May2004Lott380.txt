SENATORS ANNOUNCE HOMELAND SECURITY GRANT
 The U.S. Department of Homeland Security has awarded a $29,547,000 grant to the Mississippi Department of Public Safety for homeland security preparedness, Sens.  
 Thad Cochran and Trent Lott announced today.
   The $29,547,000 million grant issued by the Office of Domestic Preparedness will be used to purchase emergency responder equipment, for costs associated with planning, conducting, and evaluating chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear and explosive terrorism exercises, and for implementing the State Homeland Security Strategy.
   Senator Cochran said, "I am very pleased that our state has received this substantial assistance for homeland security preparedness." 
   "Mississippi has several key military installations, defense related facilities and ports which we must work diligently to protect from any threat," Senator Lott said. "This initiative will help enhance our national security and provide the state and local governments with resources that could help save lives and property."
