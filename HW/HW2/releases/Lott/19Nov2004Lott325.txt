SENS. LOTT, COCHRAN ANNOUNCE FUNDING FOR LOCAL FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  The Department of Homeland Security has approved $882,079 for Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program awards in 15 Mississippi communities, Sens.  
 Thad Cochran and Trent Lott announced Friday. 
   Each local fire department may use the money to increase firefighting effectiveness, fund firefighter health and safety programs, buy new fire equipment, and promote fire prevention programs. 
   Senator Cochran said, "I am pleased the Department of Homeland Security has recognized the good work of Mississippi's fire departments and has provided this important funding to improve the safety and security of these communities."
   "Mississippi has benefitted greatly from the Assistance to Firefighters program this year and in previous years," Senator Lott said. "This initiative helps both volunteer and career fire fighters by providing much needed equipment for personal protection as well as for increasing any department's firefighting capability and efficiency."
