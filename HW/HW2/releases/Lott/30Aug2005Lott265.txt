SEN. LOTT ANNOUNCES KATRINA DISASTER ASSISTANCE
  President Bush has declared a major disaster for the state of Mississippi, designating 15 counties for assistance to individuals and households for Hurricane Katrina-related damage and expenses, Sen.  
 Trent Lott of Mississippi announced today. 
   Lott said that under this declaration, Amite, Forrest, George, Greene, Hancock, Harrison, Jackson, Lamar, Marion, Pearl River, Perry, Pike, Stone, Walthall, and Wilkinson residents and households will be eligible to apply for temporary housing, home repair grants, construction loans and other expenses including medical, funeral, moving or storage. 
   In addition, Lott said the president's declaration makes 52 Mississippi counties eligible for public assistance to repair or replace disaster-damaged state and local government facilities. Those counties, in addition to the 15 above individual counties, include Adams, Amite, Attala, Chickasaw, Choctaw, Claiborne, Clarke, Clay, Copiah, Covington, Franklin, Hinds, Itawamba, Jasper, Jefferson, Jefferson Davis, Jones, Kemper, Lauderdale, Lawrence, Leake, Lee, Lincoln, Lowndes, Madison, Monroe, Neshoba, Newton, Noxubee, Oktibbeha, Rankin, Scott, Simpson, Smith, Warren, Wayne, Webster and Winston.
   This disaster declaration triggers the release of federal funds to help Mississippians recover from the impacts of Hurricane Katrina.
   "I stand ready to assist the people of Mississippi in recovering from this disaster," Senator Lott said. "My offices and staff in Jackson, Oxford and Washington will be open on Tuesday to help Mississippians and work with the Federal Emergency Management Agency in their recovery."
