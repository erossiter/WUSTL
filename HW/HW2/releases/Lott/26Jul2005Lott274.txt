SEN. LOTT'S AMTRAK PRESS CONFERENCE SET FOR JULY 27
  Sens. Trent Lott of Mississippi, chairman of the Surface Transportation and Merchant Marine Subcommittee; Ted Stevens of Alaska, chairman of the Commerce, Transportation and Science Committee; and Frank Lautenberg of New Jersey will hold a press conference at 11 a.m. Eastern Time on Wednesday, July 27, in room S-219 of the Capitol to announce the introduction of their Passenger Rail Investment and Improvement Act (PRIIA). 
   The legislation which also is originally cosponsored by Senator Daniel K. Inouye of Hawaii, Ranking Democrat on both the Committee and Subcommittee, reauthorizes Amtrak for six years and calls for Amtrak to institute reform and accountability, cost cutting and the creation of funding options for states.
   WHO: Senators Lott, Stevens, Lautenberg
   WHAT: Amtrak Reform Legislation Press Conference
   WHEN: 11 a.m., Wednesday, July 27, 2005
   WHERE: Room S-219 of the Capitol
   NOTE: S-219 will be available for camera setup at 10 a.m. Wednesday, July 27
   Contact: Susan Irby, 202/224-4239.
