SEN. LOTT TO CHAIR JOINT COMMITTEE ON PRINTING
  During an organizational meeting today of the Joint Committee on Printing (JCP), Sen.  
 Trent Lott of Mississippi was elected the panel's chairman for the 109th Congress. 
   Representative Bob Ney of Ohio is the new vice chairman, and Rep. Juanita Millender-McDonald of California is the ranking Democrat.
   JCP alternates its chairmanship between the two houses of Congress and is composed of the members of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee and the House Administration Committee. Senator Lott is the chairman of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee.
   JCP has broad authority over the Government Printing Office, Congressional printing, and printing and publications distribution generally throughout the government under Title 44 of the U.S. Code. By law, JCP is the publisher of record of several key congressional documents, from the Congressional Record to other publications authorized by the Congress, including the Congressional Directory, the Congressional Pictorial Directory, Our Flag, and How Our Laws Are Made.
   The remaining JCP members are: Senators Thad Cochran of Mississippi, Saxby Chambliss of Georgia, Daniel Inouye of Hawaii and Mark Dayton of Minnesota, and Representatives John Doolittle of California, Tom Reynolds of New York, and Robert Brady of Pennsylvania.
