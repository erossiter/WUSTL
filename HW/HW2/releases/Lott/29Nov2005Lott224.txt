SEN. LOTT: $12 MILLION AWARDED FOR KATRINA RECOVERY
  Federal grants totaling about $12 million are approved for various rebuilding projects in Hancock, Harrison, Simpson and Jackson counties, Sens. Trent Lott and Thad Cochran said today. The awards include funds to rebuild structures, utilities and to clear debris.  
   "Mississippi continues to recover from Katrina at a slow but sure pace," Senator Lott said. "An unprecedented storm like this will require many initiatives of this sort, but I know the communities which this latest round of grants impacts are very appreciative of this help. Grant funding like this will remain an important component of Mississippi's recovery effort, and Senator Cochran and I will work hard to make sure this type of federal support continues."
   Senator Thad Cochran said: "I am pleased the Federal Emergency Management Agency continues to release much needed funds that will help in the restoration of Mississippi communities. These funds are essential in helping hurricane damaged communities with debris removal and reconstruction." 
   Today's grants are broken down as follows:
   * $1.2 million for Bay St. Louis - to rebuild the Jimmy Rutherford Fishing Pier;
   * $1.2 million for Simpson County - for debris removal;
   * $2 million for the West Jackson County Utility District - to install control panels and rebuild pump stations and parts of the sewage system;
   * $3.5 million to rebuild two buildings at Pass Christian Middle School, including cafeteria, band hall, choir room, science lab and computer facilities;
   * $2.6 million to the Mississippi Gulf Coast Regional Watershed County Utility District - for restoration of public utilities, specifically at the Escatawpa Wastewater Treatment plant located in Moss Point;
   * $1.5 million to Mississippi Gulf Coast Regional Wastewater - to help restore treatment facilities of the plant located in Gautier;
   The grants will be administered through the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)
