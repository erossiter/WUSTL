$25 MILLION FOR GULFPORT, GREENVILLE, RAYMOND AIRPORTS
  The U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) has awarded grants totaling $24.6 million to airports in Gulfport, Greenville and Raymond for major improvements to improve safety, extend runways and replace buildings destroyed during Hurricane Katrina, Sen. Trent Lott of Mississippi announced today. 
   Gulfport/Biloxi International Airport will receive more than $19 million in part to replace an air cargo facility destroyed during Katrina, Lott said, as well as for a new taxiway, apron and access road to that facility. Lott said the project also will fund general aviation terminal repairs, jet bridge rehabilitation and repairs of terminal parking, drainage and roadway lighting.
   DOT approved $3.2 million for the John Bell Williams Airport in Raymond, according to Lott, for a runway extension, taxiway lighting and the installation of an automated weather observation system.
   The Mid-Delta Regional Airport in Greenville is the recipient of a $2.3 million grant, Lott said, to improve runway safety.
   "Efficient, safe and accessible airports are Mississippi 's tickets to economic recovery and jobs creation," Lott, a senior member of the Senate's Commerce, Science and Transportation Committee, said. "This is a wel com e investment in our infrastructure for the future."
