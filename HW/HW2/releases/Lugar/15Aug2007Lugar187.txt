USDA AWARDS RURAL DEVELOPMENT GRANTS TO HOOSIER COMMUNITIES
  U.S. Sen. Dick Lugar's office was notified that four Indiana communities will receive rural development grant funding from the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA). 
   The Town of Bloomingdale has been awarded a $28,720 Community Facility grant. Funds will be used to purchase a used ladder fire truck, which will be the only one in the area, along with other truck equipment. Bloomingdale has also been approved to receive a $49,000 Community Facility Loan from USDA for this purpose.
   The Town of Dana has been awarded a $11,000 Water and Waste Disposal Predevelopment Planning grant. These funds will support a preliminary engineering report and an environmental assessment for a new sewer system. Currently, the Town of Dana has a water utility, but no sanitary sewer.
   The Town of Dunkirk has been awarded a $15,000 Water and Waste Disposal Predevelopment Planning grant. These funds will support a preliminary engineering report for the design of a water treatment system to enhance water quality in Dunkirk.
   The Frankfort Community Public Library has been awarded a $67,560 Community Facility grant. Funds will be used to make improvements to the existing library and purchase a new Bookmobile.
