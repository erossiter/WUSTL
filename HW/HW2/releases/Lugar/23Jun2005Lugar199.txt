INDIANA TO RECEIVE MORE THAN $1.2 MILLION IN FEDERAL FUNDS FOR AIRPORTS
  Sen. Dick Lugar was notified of grants awarded by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) to Hoosier airports.  
 These funds will be used for a variety of projects that will enhance the safety and capacity at air facilities throughout Indiana. 
   "This is good news for Indiana airports," Lugar said. "I commend our officials for their work. Airport improvements are important in our combined effort to strengthen Hoosier communities." 
   Award recipients include: 
   Recipient: Federal Share: Project 
   * Kentland Board of Aviation Commissioners: $259,892: Expand Terminal Apron 
   * LaPorte Municipal Airport Authority: $336,300: Construct a partial parallel taxiway 
   * Wabash Board of Aviation Commissioners: $187,263: Construct a new aircraft fuel facility to replace the existing fuel facility 
   * Peru Board of Aviation Commissioners: $467,001: Construct a partial parallel taxiway from the terminal apron
