SENATE RATIFIES TREATIES, CONFIRMS NOMINEES
  The U.S. Senate today ratified two treaties and confirmed a series of nominations. Senate Foreign Relations Committee Chairman Dick Lugar had been pushing for prompt consideration by the Senate of these priorities of President Bush.  
   TREATIES: 
   * Inter-American Convention Against Terrorism (Treaty Doc. 107-18) 
   * United Nations Convention Against Transnational Organized Crime, with two supplementary protocols: (1) The Protocol to Prevent, Suppress and Punish Trafficking in Persons, Especially Women and Children, and (2) The Protocol Against Smuggling of Migrants by Land, Sea and Air (Treaty Doc. 108-16) 
   NOMINATIONS: 
   * Robert A. Mosbacher, Jr. to be President of the Overseas Private Investment Corporation 
   * Jan E. Boyer to be U.S. Alternate Executive Director of the Inter-American Development Bank 
   * Francis Rooney to be Ambassador to the Holy See 
   * Alfred Hoffman, Jr. to be Ambassador to the Republic of Portugal 
   * Thomas A. Shannon, Jr. to be Assistant Secretary of State for Western Hemisphere Affairs 
   * Charles A. Ford to be Ambassador to the Republic of Honduras 
   * Mark Langdale to be Ambassador to the Republic of Costa Rica 
   * Brenda LaGrange Johnson to be Ambassador to Jamaica 
   * The Honorable Alexander R. Vershbow to be Ambassador to the Republic of Korea 
   * Patricia Louise Herbold to be Ambassador to the Republic of Singapore 
   * William Paul McCormick to be Ambassador to New Zealand and serve concurrently as Ambassador to Samoa 
   * The Honorable John J. Danilovich to be Chief Executive Officer of the Millennium Challenge Corporation 
   * John F. Hillen, III to be Assistant Secretary of State for Political-Military Affairs 
   * Barry F. Lowenkron to be Assistant Secretary of State for Democracy, Human Rights, and Labor 
   * The Honorable Kent R. Hill to be Assistant Administrator of USAID for Global Health 
   * The Honorable Jacqueline Ellen Schafer to be Assistant Administrator of USAID for Economic Growth, Agriculture, and Trade 
   * The Honorable Josette Sheeran Shiner to be U.S. Alternate Governor of the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development for a term of five years; U.S. Alternate Governor of the Inter-American Development Bank for a term of five years; U.S. Alternate Governor of the African Development Bank for a term of five years; U.S. Alternate Governor of the African Development Fund; U.S. Alternate Governor of the Asian Development Bank; and U.S. Alternate Governor of the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development 
   * The Honorable Jendayi Elizabeth Frazer to be a Member of the Board of Directors of the African Development Foundation for the remainder of the term expiring September 27, 2009
