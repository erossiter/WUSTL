MORE THAN $3 MILLION IN HUD GRANTS AWARDED TO CITY OF HAMMOND
  Sen. Dick Lugar was notified that the City of Hammond will receive three grants totaling $3,090,959 from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development. 
  "As a former mayor and also as the former Chairman of the Housing Subcommittee, I recognize the value of federal housing programs and the needs of many Americans for housing assistance. The funds announced today will help ensure Hoosiers have the opportunity to live in safe, decent, affordable housing in economically stable communities," said Lugar. 
  The types of grants awarded include a Community Development Block Grant (CDBG), the HOME Award and an Emergency Shelter Grant (ESG).
  The CDBG program provides annual grants to states and local units of government to develop viable urban communities by providing decent housing and a suitable living environment and by expanding economic opportunities, primarily for low-and-moderate-income persons. The City of Hammond will receive $2,367,226 in CDBG funds.
  The HOME program helps to expand the supply of decent, affordable housing to low-income families by providing grants to state and local governments to fund housing programs that meet local needs and priorities. The City of Hammond will receive $621,745 in HOME funds.
  The ESG program provides annual grants to states, local and private entities to improve the quality and number of emergency homeless shelters. The City of Hammond will receive $101,988 in ESG funds.
