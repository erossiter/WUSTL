COMMITTEE APPROVES NOMINEES, TREATIES
  The U.S. Senate Foreign Relations Committee approved the following agenda items at today's business meeting:  
   TREATIES APPROVED UNANIMOUSLY: 
   * Agreement between the United States of America and the Government of Canada on Pacific Hake/Whiting (Treaty Doc. 108-24) 
   * Convention on the Conservation and Management of Highly Migratory Fish Stocks in the Western and Central Pacific Ocean (Treaty Doc. 109-1) 
   NOMINATIONS APPROVED BY UNANIMOUS VOICE VOTE: 
   * The Honorable David B. Dunn to be Ambassador to the Togolese Republic 
   * Carmen Maria Martinez to be Ambassador to the Republic of Zambia 
   * Michael R. Arietti to be Ambassador to the Republic of Rwanda 
   * David M. Hale to be Ambassador to the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan 
   * Benson K. Whitney to be Ambassador to Norway 
   * Susan Rasinski McCaw to be Ambassador to the Republic of Austria 
   * Nicholas F. Taubman to be Ambassador to Romania 
   * The Honorable Anne W. Patterson to be Assistant Secretary of State for International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs 
   OTHER NOMINATIONS: 
   * The nomination of C. Boyden Gray to be Representative to the European Union, with the rank and status of Ambassador, was approved by a vote of 10-7. 
   * The nomination of the Honorable Jennifer L. Dorn to be U.S. Alternate Executive Director of the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development was approved by voice vote. 
   FSO PROMOTIONS LIST APPROVED BY UNANIMOUS VOICE VOTE: 
   * Deanna Hanek Abdeen, et al dated July 14, 2005 
   THE FOLLOWING NOMINATIONS WERE HELD OVER TO A FUTURE BUSINESS MEETING: 
   * Donald A. Gambatesa to be Inspector General of the U.S. Agency for International Development 
   * Roland Arnall to be Ambassador to the Kingdom of the Netherlands 
   * Jeffrey Thomas Bergner to be Assistant Secretary of State for Legislative Affairs
