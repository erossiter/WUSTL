INDIANA TO RECEIVE FEDERAL FUNDS FOR AIRPORTS
  Sen. Dick Lugar's office was notified of grants awarded by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) to Hoosier airports.  
 These funds will be used for a variety of projects that will enhance the safety and capacity at air facilities throughout Indiana. 
   "This is good news for Indiana airports," Lugar said. "I commend our officials for their work. Airport improvements are important in our combined effort to strengthen Hoosier communities." 
   Award recipients include: 
   Recipient - City of Huntington
  Federal Share - $573,240
  Project - Construct Parking Lot; Construct Building
   Recipient - Evansville-Vanderburgh Airport Authority
  Federal Share - $1,909,684
  Project - Acquire Aircraft Rescue & Fire Fighting Vehicle; Replace Rescue 
   Recipient - Greenwood Board of Aviation Commissioners
  Federal Share - $1,000,000
  Project - Extend Runway 
   Recipient - Kokomo Board of Aviation Commissioners
  Federal Share - $215,000
  Project - Rehabilitate Runway Lighting; Rehabilitate Apron
   Recipient - Indianapolis Airport Authority
  Federal Share - $2,000,000
  Project - Noise Mitigation Measures for Residences within 65 - 69 DNL.
