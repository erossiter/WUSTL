Bayh Announces $23,800 for Economic Development in Spencer County Washington-- U.S. Senator Evan Bayh today announced that the U.S. Department of Commerces Economic Development Administration has awarded $23,802 to Lincolnland Economic Development Corporation.   The grant will be used in partnership with the Indiana 15 Regional Planning Commission to study the potential industrial development in the Lincoln Industrial Corridor and use the findings to help local communities attract desired economic growth.  Its important that we continue to position our communities to remain competitive in this ever-evolving global marketplace, Senator Bayh said. The Lincoln Industrial Corridor in Spencer County has much to offer businesses, and these funds will be an important resource in helping bring together a unified vision for the cooridor that will spur economic growth, strengthen our communities and create good-paying jobs for Hoosiers.  The Economic Development Administration (EDA) serves as a venture capital resource to meet the economic development needs of distressed communities throughout the United States. EDAs mission is to lead the federal economic development agenda by promoting innovation and competitiveness, preparing American regions for growth and success in the worldwide economy.
