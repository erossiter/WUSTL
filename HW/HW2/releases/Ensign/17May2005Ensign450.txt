 Washington  D C     Working to create jobs and spur economic growth in Henderson  Nevada  s Congressional delegation Senators Harry Reid and John Ensign  Congresswoman Shelley Berkley  and Congressmen Jim Gibbons and Jon Porter introduced legislation today to develop an employment and business center       The Southern Nevada Limited Transition Area Act would convey 547 acres of land from the Bureau of Land Management  BLM  to the City of Henderson         As Henderson continues to grow  it is important that its economy is able to handle the changing conditions     said Reid    This project will help the city  s leaders realize their vision of planned development and more diversified employment            The use of Henderson land to create an employment center is an ideal pairing     said Ensign    With Henderson  s rapid population growth  the community will benefit immensely from such a facility            This bill will enable the City of Henderson  a fast growing and economically thriving community  to allow for responsible development  continued economic diversity  and planned growth     stated Congressman Gibbons         The lion s share of the money generated by these sales will go toward preserving open space and it s a win win for the Valley s families  our businesses and the community as a whole     said Rep  Shelley Berkley  D NV     Turning this federal land into private property for business development in fast growing Henderson will help southern Nevada s economic diversification efforts and will create added revenues to fund vital public services            I am proud to be an original cosponsor of the Southern Nevada Limited Transition Area Act  As with all of Southern Nevada  Henderson has experienced tremendous growth in recent years  and this bill will empower our local leaders to direct our growth as effectively as possible  I am hopeful that this bill will be quickly considered by Congress so that the residents of Henderson will soon experience its benefits     said Congressman Porter       The BLM has designated land  located adjacent to the Henderson Executive Airport  for disposal because of urban surroundings and isolation from other public land make it difficult for the agency to manage  Aircraft noise and overfllights from the nearby airport make the land unsuitable for residential use       The city would subdivide and sell lots at fair market value  As was established in the Southern Nevada Public Lands Management Act  eighty five percent of the proceeds from sales would return to the BLM  s Special Account for acquiring environmentally sensitive land  five percent would go to the State of Nevada  s general education fund  and the City of Henderson could use the remaining ten percent to cover expenses associated with subdividing the property and providing infrastructure
