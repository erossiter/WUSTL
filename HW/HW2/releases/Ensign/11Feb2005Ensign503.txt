 Washington  D C     Senators John Ensign and Harry Reid and Congressman Jim Gibbons today announced a federal grant for almost  114 000 to the Pahrump Valley Fire Rescue Service for its operations and firefighter safety programs  which includes training  personal protective equipment  wellness and fitness  and facility modifications        Pahrump  s tremendous growth makes it essential that we provide our first responders with the necessary equipment and training     Ensign said     The fire rescue team funding will allow for a faster response to emergencies  which will better protect the community           Firefighters put their own lives on the line to protect our lives and property     said Reid     They deserve the best equipment and training available  and these resources will help provide those           As the community of Pahrump continues to grow  more and more strain is placed on the town s public services  including the Pahrump Valley Fire Rescue Service     Gibbons said     This fire grant will provide much needed training  equipment  and facility improvements to Pahrump s Fire Department and in the end  better protect the residents of Pahrump         Pahrump  s grant is a part of the 34th round of the Fiscal Year 2004 Assistance to the Firefighters Grant Program managed by the Department of Homeland Security   The 8 000 fire department awards throughout the country will total nearly  750 million in direct assistance      The Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the Department of Homeland Security  s Office of Domestic Preparedness   The program assists rural  urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States   These funds are used by the nation  s firefighters for firefighting operations  firefighter health and safety programs  new firefighting equipment  emergency medical services programs and fire prevention and safety programs
