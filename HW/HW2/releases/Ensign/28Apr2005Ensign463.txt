 Washington  D C     Senators John Ensign and Harry Reid today announced a federal fire prevention grant to the Southern Nevada Fire Prevention Association   The nearly  55 000 grant will be used for the association  s Youth Firesetters Intervention Program and to expand the National Fire Prevention Association  NFPA  Risk Watch Program into the Clark County School District        We can never do enough to get the word to young students in Nevada that devastating fires can be prevented if we work together     Ensign said     These funds will increase public awareness and help protect Nevadans from fire related disasters         The Youth Firesetters Intervention Program  a free 11 week course for parents and teens  promotes strengthening the family  education on substance abuse and violence prevention  The NFPA Risk Watch Program uses volunteer teachers to provide Risk Watch safety lessons for students in the Clark County School District         Educating our young people about fire safety is the most effective form of prevention     said Reid     Although our firefighters do an excellent job fighting fires  it is important that we do our part to prevent fires from starting in the first place          Southern Nevada  s grant is a part of the Fiscal Year 2004 Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program managed by the Department of Homeland Security   The 8 000 fire department awards throughout the country will total nearly  750 million in direct assistance      The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the Department of Homeland Security  s Office of Domestic Preparedness   The program assists rural  urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States   These funds are used by the nation  s firefighters for firefighting operations  firefighter health and safety programs  new firefighting equipment  emergency medical services programs and fire prevention and safety programs
