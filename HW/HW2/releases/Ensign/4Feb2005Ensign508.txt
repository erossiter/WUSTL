 Washington  D C     Senators John Ensign and Harry Reid and Congressman Jim Gibbons today announced a grant to assist the Ryndon Volunteer Fire Department in Elko   The  225 000 grant will go toward acquiring firefighting vehicles        Having the necessary equipment for our firefighters is an important part of keeping our communities safe     Ensign said     These funds to purchase firefighting vehicles will be an essential step in protecting rural Nevadans            This grant will make Elko a safer place to live by putting more fire trucks on the streets   Reid said    That means faster response in case of a fire  when every second counts          Our rural communities often cannot afford the quality equipment and vehicles necessary to keep their residents safe     Gibbons said     This federal grant will go a long way to supporting the volunteer Ryndon Fire Department in Elko and help these brave firefighters protect the people of Elko         The Elko grant is part of the 33rd round of the 2004 Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program managed by the Department of Homeland Security   The 8 000 fire department awards throughout the country will total nearly  750 million in direct assistance      The Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program is administered by the Department of Homeland Security  s Office of Domestic Preparedness   The program assists rural  urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States   These funds are used by the nation  s firefighters for firefighting operations  firefighter health and safety programs  new firefighting equipment  emergency medical services programs and fire prevention and safety programs
