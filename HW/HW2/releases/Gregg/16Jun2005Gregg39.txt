                 GREGG                ANNOUNCES  2 MILLION FOR COCHECHO RIVER                DREDGING MOVING THROUGH CONGRESS                                                           WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today              announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved              a measure that includes  2 million for the continued dredging of              the Cochecho River in Dover  As a member of the Senate Appropriations              Committee  Senator Gregg was able to include this funding in the              Fiscal Year 2006 Energy and Water Appropriations bill  after securing               2 million for the dredging project over the last two years  The              dredging consists of removing sand and silt from the bottom of              the Cochecho River and disposing of the material in an upland disposal              site operated by the City of Dover  The measure now heads to the              Senate floor for its consideration    Senator Gregg stated   After the successful completion of this dredging project the Cochecho River will be a critical piece in the growth and expansion of downtownDover  A navigable Cochecho can serve as a link between Dover and the Great Bayand Portsmouth Harbor and would attract new commerce and visitors to the downtownarea  I am pleased the Appropriations Committee supported this project and recognizedthe positive effects it will have on the Dover community   Dover Mayor Scott Myers stated   This is very exciting news and I would liketo thank Senator Gregg for his efforts in obtaining additional funding for theCochecho River Dredge Project  This funding is the final piece of the puzzlerequired to complete this very important project  The dredging of the river isa crucial component of the City s ongoing efforts to redevelop the downtown waterfrontarea  The City very much appreciates the hard work of Senator Gregg
