Senator Gregg Announces Senate Support Of Federal Funds For Granite Street Bridge Project Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate has approved a bill containing  1 5 million for the initiative to widen the Granite Street Bridge and upgrade Granite Street into downtown Manchester  These funds will be used for the third stage of construction and will specifically expand Granite Street to seven lanes from Main Street to Elm Street  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  Senator Gregg was able to include this funding in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation  Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill  The bill now heads to a joint House Senate conference committee before returning to each chamber for final passage  To date  Senator Gregg has secured a total of  15 million for the Granite Street project  Funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation for Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures      Senator Gregg stated  I recently visited Mayor Guinta and community leaders in Manchester to discuss the completion of this critical infrastructure project  As Manchester continues to grow  it is essential that the Granite Street Bridge expansion be completed in a timely manner to ensure both the safety and convenience of travelers in the Queen City  The completion of the project will provide a great boon for the City economy and growing downtown area  Frank Guinta  Mayor of Manchester  stated  Senator Gregg has continued to track this important project and has shown to be a champion of economic development for Manchester  As the economic driver of this state  these funds will help improve an important gateway to the city   both in terms of safety and aesthetically
