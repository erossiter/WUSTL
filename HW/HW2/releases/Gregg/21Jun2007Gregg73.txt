Senator Gregg Announces Senate Appropriations Committee Support For Elliot Health Initiative  Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved  250 000 in federal funding for the Elliot Health System in the Fiscal Year 2008 Senate Labor Health and Human Services  Education Appropriations bill  The funding will allow the Elliot healthcare system to improve its electronic medical records system for Elliot Hospital at an off site facility in Londonderry  The initiative will provide software  training and information technology support and create a back up information system of all the records in case of emergency situations  The appropriations bill is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  The funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures   Senator Gregg  a member of the Senate Appropriations Labor HHS Education Subcommittee  stated  The Elliot Health System has a outstanding reputation for delivering high quality service in the Manchester area  These funds will help the hospital with its efforts to back up their electronic medical records in the event of an emergency situation  better serving their patients  Doug Dean  CEO and President of Elliot Health System  stated  The appropriations funding will be used for the initiative emergency preparedness to assure continuity of care in the face of local or regional disasters  The funding will help to develop a Back up and Support Data Center in the event that EHSs main data center is partially or completely destroyed or not accessible due to a pandemic situation  Elliot Health System is grateful to Senator Gregg for his support and assistance in including the Elliot in the HHS appropriation bill
