Senator Gregg Announces Senate Appropriations Committee Support For Manchester Water Project   Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the City of Manchester stands to receive  500 000 for its Combined Sewer Overflow initiative in a bill moving through the Senate  The Combined Sewer Overflow project separates sewer lines from storm drains  improving public health for the safety of Manchester residents  Senator Gregg included funding for this project in the Senate Fiscal Year 2008 Interior EPA Appropriations bill  which is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  The funding for these projects is included within the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Senator Gregg  a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on the Interior EPA  stated  As the overall population of New Hampshire continues to grow  cities such as Manchester are experiencing an increased strain on public water systems  It is essential to ensure that all residents in the Granite State have access to safe and clean drinking water  in addition to proper and safe disposal of wastewater  Manchester is doing an outstanding job addressing water infrastructure issues and this award will allow them to continue that work   Frank Guinta  Mayor of Manchester  stated  I want to personally thank Senator Gregg for his efforts in getting the City of Manchester these needed funds  As this process moves along  Sen  Gregg recognizes that there are many hurdles still to come  but it is comforting to know that he will be keeping an eye on this legislation every step of the way
