Senator Gregg Announces Little Bay Bridges Reconstruction Effort Receives Senate Appropriations Committee Support Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved a measure containing  2 5 million for the expansion and upgrade of the Little Bay Bridges Spaulding Turnpike in Newington and Dover  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  Senator Gregg was able to include these funds in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation  and Housing and Urban Development funding bill  which is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  Funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation for Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Since 2003  Senator Gregg has secured  28 million for the Little Bay Bridges project  Senator Gregg stated  The Little Bay Bridges project has consistently been identified as a top priority for New Hampshires Seacoast region  As New Hampshires population continues to grow exponentially  and  with more tourists visiting the Granite State each year  it is crucial that the resources are put in place to get this project moving forward  These additional federal funds will help expand the Bridges  providing a boost to the regional and state economies and easing the travels of drivers in the future   Scott Myers  Mayor of Dover  stated  The city of Dover is extremely appreciative of Senator Gregg and his efforts in appropriating funds for the Little Bay Bridge and Spaulding Turnpike project  For Dover  and Strafford County  this roadway expansion is the single most important transportation project in the region  It will enhance transportation efficiency  reduce safety problems and have minimal environmental impacts to the area  It will also have a positive effect on the economic growth of Dover and the surrounding area  I wish to personally thank Senator Gregg for his continued support of the citizens of Dover  Dick Ingram  President of the Greater Portsmouth Chamber of Commerce  stated  In a challenging budget environment  it is encouraging to learn that Senator Gregg has been able to secure funding for this important project  one that is critical to the regional economy
