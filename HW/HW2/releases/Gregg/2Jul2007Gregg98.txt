Senator Gregg Announces Senate Appropriations Committee Support For Law Enforcement Technology Program At The University Of New Hampshire   Contact  Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  announced that the Consolidated Advanced Technologies Laboratory  CATlab  at the University of New Hampshire stands to receive  1 million in a federal funding measure approved by the Senate Appropriations Committee late last week  The CATlab program works to address problems with electronic devices inside police cruisers and to improve the communication ability between law enforcement officers and their headquarters  The innovations being pioneered through the project significantly enhance the information available to officers in the field  including voice recognition commands and linking law enforcement radios through computers  The program is a collaborative effort between UNH and the New Hampshire Department of Safety  Senator Gregg  a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice and Science  included funding for this project in the Fiscal Year 2008 funding bill  which is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  Funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Since 1999  Senator Gregg has secured  32 5 million for the CATlab program  Senator Gregg stated  Every day countless police officers in our state put their lives on the line to protect their communities and the people who live there  New Hampshire has a national reputation for the high quality of life its citizens enjoy  and this is due in no small part to the many men and women who watch over our neighborhoods  These federal funds will continue to support a program that provides law enforcement with the necessary and most up to date tools and resources they need to carry out their responsibilities safely and effectively      New Hampshires law enforcement and public safety communities are immensely grateful for Senator Greggs ongoing support of our program bringing cutting edge technology to police cruisers and incident command vehicles  says Dr  Andy Kun  principal investigator for the Department of Justice RD program with UNHs Consolidated Advanced Technology Laboratory  Our Project54 work on integrating devices into a system controlled by voice is pioneering  this is the most sophisticated in car voice interface in the world and is now successfully deployed to the New Hampshire Department of Safety and to over 150 communities around New Hampshire  helping to improve public safety operations
