Senator Gregg Meets With Manchester Mayor For Update On Federal Funding For Granite Street Bridge Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today met with Manchester Mayor Frank Guinta  Manchester city officials  business leaders and community members to provide an update on continued federal funding for the Granite Street Bridge project and to tour the construction progress  The Senate Appropriations Committee recently approved  1 5 million in a fiscal year 2008 funding measure for the initiative to widen the Granite Street Bridge and upgrade Granite Street into downtown Manchester  These funds will be used for the third stage of construction and will specifically expand Granite Street to seven lanes from Main Street to Elm Street  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  Senator Gregg was able to include this funding in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill  which is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  In the past  Senator Gregg has secured a total of  15 million for the Granite Street project   Senator Gregg stated  The Granite Street Bridge  which provides central access to the Queen City  has unfortunately been a serious bottleneck problem for drivers  As the City continues to grow and expand  it is essential that the bridge be of appropriate size to quickly and safely get residents and visitors to their destinations  whether it be the Verizon Wireless Arena  the Fisher Cats stadium  downtown or many of the surrounding residential areas  The completion of this project will provide an additional boost to the ever growing economy of Manchester and allow for continued enhancement of the downtown area  Mayor Frank Guinta stated  I am excited that Sen  Gregg  city officials and Manchester business leaders are here today  The Granite Street widening project is an important piece of Manchester s redevelopment and will be the catalyst to other major economic initiatives during the coming years
