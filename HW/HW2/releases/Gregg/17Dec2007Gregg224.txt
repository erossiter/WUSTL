Senator Gregg Announces Funding For Little Bay Bridges And Cocheco Dredging Included In Fiscal Year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations Bill Contact  Andrea Wuebker Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Fiscal Year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations bill contains important federal funding for two Seacoast projects  The measure includes  1 75 million for the expansion and upgrade of the Little Bay Bridges Spaulding Turnpike in Newington and Dover and  2 85 million for the continued dredging of the Cocheco River in Dover  which consists of removing sand and silt from the bottom of the River and disposing of the material in an upland disposal site operated by the City of Dover  To date  Senator Gregg has secured  28 million for Little Bay Bridges and  3 8 million for Cocheco  Funding for these projects is included within the Appropriations Committee allocation for Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures   The House is expected to vote as early as tonight night on the omnibus package that contains 11 out of the 12 annual spending measures  the Senate will likely debate the measure later this week  If passed by both chambers  the legislation will then be sent to the Presidents desk for his signature  Senator Gregg stated  The Little Bay Bridges project has consistently been identified as a top priority for New Hampshires Seacoast region  Currently drivers are subject to severe bottleneck traffic during daily rush hours  and it is crucial that the resources are put in place to get this project moving forward and ensure the safety of travelers  These additional federal funds will help expand the Bridges  providing a boost to the regional and state economies and easing the travels of drivers in the future  Gregg added  The City of Dover  its residents  and neighboring communities on the Seacoast will benefit greatly from these critical funds which will bring the dredging of the Cocheco River closer to completion  The project has been a priority for the local community for several years  and I am pleased my colleagues in Congress have recognized the tremendous impact that future development along the River will have when the dredging is finally finished  This project is a foundation for a revitalization of this area in Dover  and will be an economic stimulus for the City and the entire region  Gregg continued  Though the Democratic Leadership has pushed this years appropriations process into the 11th hour  it is important to note that Republicans efforts to control spending were successful and forced the elimination of  18 billion in new spending  I am pleased my colleagues were able to reach across the aisle and come up with an omnibus package which is more in line with the budget request and still includes funding for important New Hampshire initiatives  Scott Myers  Mayor of Dover  stated  On behalf of the city of Dover  I would like to thank Senator Gregg and his staff for their efforts in securing this funding for the Little Bay Bridges project  This transportation project along the Spaulding Turnpike is of significant importance to Dover  and the region  in areas of public safety  reducing traffic congestion  improving air quality and enhancing highway capacity in order to be able to promote economic development opportunities  I am appreciative of Senator Gregg s continued support of this project  Myers continued  I am extremely pleased that Senator Gregg has been able to secure additional funding to complete the dredging of the Cocheco River  The dredge project is one of the final pieces of a long term community vision in Dover toward the redevelopment of a twenty acre parcel  in our downtown  along the banks of the Cocheco  My thanks to Senator Gregg for his ongoing support of this project over the past several years  We are looking forward to a completed dredge of this federally designated channel  Ted Diers  Program Manager of the New Hampshire Coastal Program  stated  This funding is essential to finish the long anticipated Cocheco River project  The project is a good example of what is possible through close coordination of local  state and federal agencies  In the end  the success of this navigation project will be a testament of the hard work of the city of Dover  the Army Corps of Engineers and our congressional delegation
