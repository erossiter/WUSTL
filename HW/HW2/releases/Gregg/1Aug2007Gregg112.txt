Senator Gregg Supports Preventing Youth Smoking  Supports FDA Regulation Of Tobacco Industry Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH   a member of the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee  today joined 11 Democrats and one Republican in a 13 8 vote supporting legislation calling for regulation of the tobacco industry by the U S  Food and Drug Administration  The Family Smoking Prevention and Tobacco Control bill  S 625  passed out of the Senate HELP Committee and is now ready for consideration by the full Senate   Committee passage today of the Family Smoking Prevention and Tobacco Control Act is an important step forward in the fight against youth smoking The harmful and addictive effects of tobacco use have long been recognized by the public health community  and it is time to take serious action  stated Sen  Gregg   The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention  CDC  estimates that tobacco use causes nearly 440 000 deaths each year  and remains the number one cause of preventable disease and death in the United States  The CDC also estimates that 31 000 children currently under the age of 18 will ultimately die prematurely from the effects of smoking in the state of New Hampshire alone   The new authority in this legislation over tobacco products is essential to help protect the public  and kids nationwide  from the dangerous effects of tobacco  stated Sen  Gregg  The Campaign for Tobacco Free Kids applauds Senator Gregg for voting today to protect America s kids from tobacco addiction and save lives  Senator Gregg voted in the Senate HELP Committee to approve legislation to grant the FDA authority over tobacco products  including the authority to crack down on tobacco marketing and sales to kids  We appreciate Senator Greggs longstanding interest in this issue and look forward to working with him to enact this life saving legislation into law this year  stated William V  Corr  Executive Director of the Campaign for Tobacco Free Kids  The American Cancer Society  the American Heart Association  the American Lung Association  the American Medical Association  the Campaign for Tobacco Free Kids and dozens of other major public health organizations have endorsed the Family Smoking Prevention and Tobacco Control Act
