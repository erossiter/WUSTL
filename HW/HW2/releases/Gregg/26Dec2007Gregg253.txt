Senators Gregg and Sununu Announces Funding For St  Joseph Hospital Signed Into Law Contact  Andrea Wuebker Laena Fallon Gregg   Barbara Riley Liz Chamberlain Sununu   WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  R NH  announced that the Fiscal Year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations bill which contains  563 972 in federal funding for St  Joseph Hospital in Nashua was signed into law today  These funds will be used to continue development and expansion of their electronic medical record system to improve the quality of care provided to Nashua residents  The funding for this project is included within the Appropriations Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Senator Gregg  a member of the Senate Appropriations Labor HHS Education Subcommittee  who has previously secured  2 5 million for St  Joseph Hospital  stated  The Greater Nashua area continues to be home to an expanding population  and ensuring quality health care is a necessity  St  Joseph Hospital has a distinguished reputation for providing excellent service and resources  making a tremendous contribution to the high quality of life in Nashua  These funds will help ensure that the hospital has the means to keep accurate and secure medical records  eliminating an element of both error and delay  Gregg continued  Though the Democratic Leadership pushed this years appropriations process into the 11th hour  it is important to note that Republicans efforts to control spending successfully eliminated nearly  18 billion in new spending  I am pleased my colleagues were able to reach across the aisle and come up with an omnibus package which is more in line with the budget request  provides critical funding for troops in the field  and still includes funding for important New Hampshire initiatives  Senator Sununu stated  Bringing technological advancements to our hospitals and clinics helps modernize care and controls the cost of its delivery  Nashuas St  Joseph Hospital delivers a comprehensive range of services to thousands of patients in southern New Hampshire in its acute care hospital and trauma center  The funding included in this legislation is slated to be used to automate clinical charts  medical records  and administrative functions  generating efficiencies that are passed on to patients in the form of quality  streamlined treatment  Peter Davis  President CEO of St  Joseph Hospital stated  St  Joseph Hospital believes that strengthening our electronic medical record system is one of the keys to improving the health care system in Nashua and neighboring communities  The funds Senator Gregg and Senator Sununu have secured will be critical in allowing St  Joseph to adopt the latest Information Technology  We appreciate their recognition of how IT technology can greatly improve patient care and thank them for their commitment to helping the hospital carry out our mission
