Senator Gregg Announces Federal Funding For Lancaster Drinking Water Project Signed Into Law Contact  Andrea Wuebker Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Fiscal Year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations bill which contains  221 490 for the Town of Lancasters drinking water project was signed into law on Wednesday  This funding will help the Town of Lancaster address issues with its water system  The funding for this project was included within the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending bills  Senator Gregg stated  It is essential to ensure that all residents in the Granite State have access to safe and clean drinking water  in addition to proper and safe disposal of wastewater  Lancaster is working to improve the drinking water system  and this award will allow them to continue that work   Gregg continued  Though the Democratic Leadership pushed this years appropriations process into the 11th hour  it is important to note that Republicans efforts to control spending successfully eliminated nearly  18 billion in new spending  I am pleased my colleagues were able to reach across the aisle and come up with an omnibus package which is more in line with the budget request  provides critical funding for troops in the field  and still includes funding for important New Hampshire initiatives  David Stickney  Chairman of the Lancaster Board of Selectman  stated  The efforts of Senator Gregg in securing a STAG Appropriation for the Lancaster Water Project are greatly appreciated  With the recent development of the Wausau Paper Mill closing and the loss of another 300   jobs in the North Country  appropriations of funds for public works projects is vital to a regional recovery effort  The funds will be used for Lancasters water system  including increasing the water pressure in various parts of the Town to meet DES requirements  This improvement will protect the Town in the event of a fire emergency and will improve overall water quality and prevent the risk of the water system being contaminated due to inadequate pressure and risk of back siphoning  In addition  funds will be used to address an outdated 2 5 million gallon earthen covered reservoir  which needs to be replaced with a pre stressed concrete water tank  Presently  the reservoir has an overflow feature to keep water circulating so as to prevent stale water  By closing the water system and eliminating this overflow  chlorinated water will no longer be wasted  saving water users money in the long run
