Gregg Amendment To Farm Bill Improves Women s Access To OB GYNs In Rural Areas Contact  Andrea Wuebker Laena Fallon  Washington  DCTo address the severe shortage of OB GYN doctors for women and expectant mothers in rural areas due to the excessive liability burden placed on providers  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today offered the Healthy Mothers and Healthy Babies Rural Access To Care amendment to this years farm bill  The amendment is cosponsored by Senators Lamar Alexander  R TN   George Voinovich  R OH   John Cornyn  R TX   Wayne Allard  R CO   and Elizabeth Dole  R NC   and is supported by the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists  ACOG    See attached for ACOG letter of support   Greggs amendment is designed to improve womens access to obstetrical and gynecological care in rural areas by providing full compensation to patients for medical costs  lost wages  and other tangible costs  while at the same time preventing frivolous lawsuits for OB GYNs  A member of the Health  Education  Labor  and Pensions Committee  Sen  Gregg stated  In many rural areas of this country  women are losing access to local doctors and hospitals  often when they need them the most when they are having children  It is not fair to the mother or the child  but it is happening too often because of frivolous lawsuits and the costs they put on the health care system   Doctors are now put in a position where they can no longer practice medicine  Here in New Hampshire  physicians have faced double digit premium increases  and as a result  there is only one OB GYN practicing in the North Country  Nationally  70 percent of OB GYNs have made changes to their practice because of the lack of available or affordable medical liability insurance and nearly eight percent of doctors have stopped practicing obstetrics altogether  We are at a point where women who are on their way to the hospital to deliver a baby will have to stop at the courthouse to find their doctor  Gregg continued  This amendment will ease the struggle that so many women go through when seeking access to good doctors for themselves and for their babies  Its a choice between helping these women and families or protecting trial lawyers  This amendment falls on the side of families and womens health  Senator Gregg understands the real issue at stake  making sure not one pregnant woman in America ever has to wonder who will deliver her baby  said Kenneth L  Noller  MD   President of ACOG  Pregnant woman in rural communities often have to travel for miles to see an ob gyn when even one physician closes his doors because liability insurance has become unaffordable  Senator Gregg s amendment  targeting this critical crisis area  is good policy and should be passed unanimously in the Senate  Todays amendment incorporates the successful models used by the State of Texas in their 2003 reforms  Before Medical Liability Reform in Texas  2 out of every 3 Texas counties had no obstetrician  1 out of every 3 doctors practicing could be expected to be sued in any given year  and insurance premiums increased between 22 and 128 percent over the preceding four years  Since the 2003 reforms  more than 4 000 doctors have applied for licenses to practice in Texas  and the state has gained 186 new obstetricians
