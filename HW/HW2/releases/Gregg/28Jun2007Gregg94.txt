Senator Gregg Announces Senate Appropriations Committee Supports Continued Funding For Great Bay Partnership Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved  5 million in the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration budget for the Great Bay Resource Protection Partnership  These funds will allow the Great Bay Resource Protection Partnership to continue conservation efforts of the Bay and surrounding areas  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Commerce  Justice and Science Subcommittee  Senator Gregg included funding for Great Bay in the Fiscal Year 2008 Appropriations bill  which is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  The funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending bills  Senator Gregg  who has secured  50 million in federal funds for the Partnership since 1997  stated  The Great Bay is one of the most extraordinary environmental resources in our state and ensuring its protection is critical  The work being done by the Partnership continues to make sure that generations of New Hampshire residents and visitors will be able to enjoy the Bay in all its natural splendor  I applaud the Partnership for its continued success in protecting the Bay and look forward to its future accomplishments which will be aided by this funding   There are three essential ingredients to the success of the Great Bay Resource Protection Partnership  said Daryl Burtnett  state director of The Nature Conservancy in New Hampshire  One is the remarkable ecological qualities of Great Bay itself  it is a special and delicate place that needs protection  Second is the robust working relationship of the partners  the government agencies and organizations that have worked hard and carefully to protect 4 800 acres in the past 13 years  And third is the leadership and vision of Senator Gregg  He understands the importance and urgency of conservation at Great Bay  These funds will support the continued conservation efforts around Great Bay  Current priority areas include Cronmet and Lubberland Creeks  lower Lamprey River  Tuttle Swamp  and the Great Bay and Little Bay shorelines  The GBRPP is led by the Nature Conservancy and includes the NH Fish and Game Department  Ducks Unlimited  NH Audubon  and the Great Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve  It seeks to permanently protect critical habitats at Great Bay  Since 1999  federal funds have helped the Partnership protect 4 456 acres  Local communities and other organizations have protected an additional 3 020 acres
