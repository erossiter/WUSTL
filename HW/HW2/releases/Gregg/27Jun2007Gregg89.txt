Senator Gregg Announces Senate Appropriations Committee Support For Civic Education At Saint Anselm College  Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved  250 000 for a civic education program at Saint Anselm College in Manchester late last week  These funds will be used to create a new civic education initiative to benefit students and community members in the surrounding areas  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services and Education  Senator Gregg has included these funds in the Fiscal Year 2008 funding bill  which is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  The funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures   Senator Gregg stated  Teaching young people about civic engagement and providing community forums on civic participation are important exercises in democracy  Saint Anselm College and the New Hampshire Institute of Politics are leaders in the Granite State in providing civic education programming to both students and members of the community  These funds will help continue a long tradition of vibrant political participation and understanding in New Hampshire  We are most thankful to Sen  Gregg for supporting our commitment to develop thoughtful and engaged citizens through a liberal arts education  said Father Jonathan DeFelice  O S B   President of Saint Anselm College  This generous funding will allow our faculty  under the leadership of Dean of the College Father Augustine Kelly  to design new curriculum and teaching strategies to enhance the academic experience of our students  to develop their sense of intellectual curiosity  and to encourage their sense of civic engagement
