Gregg Iraq Resolution Passes Senate With Strong Support WASHINGTON Last night the Senate overwhelmingly supported a resolution supporting the troops authored by U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH   The amendment  which passed by a vote of 82 16  states the strong support of Congress for the troops and that funding for the troops will not be cut off  Full text of the amendment is below  Senator Gregg stated  There is no more important role for Congress at this time than supporting the men and women in the field fighting the war on terrorism  We must make sure that our troops have all the support they need to carry out their missions  and we must make sure they know they have the strong support of Congress as they fight to prevent our nation from being attacked by terrorists again  Last nights overwhelming vote in favor of this amendment  for which I have been trying to get a vote since last month  demonstrates the resolve that we have as a nation and our unwavering admiration and high regard for our servicemen and women around the world     110th CONGRESS  1st Session S  CON  RES  20 CONCURRENT RESOLUTION   Whereas under Article II  Section 2  of the Constitution of the United States  the President is the  commander in chief of the Army and Navy of the United States   and in such capacity the President has the command of the Armed Forces  including the authority to deploy troops and direct military campaigns during wartime    Whereas under Article I  Section 8  of the Constitution of the United States  Congress has the power of the purse specifically as it relates to the Armed Forces  and in such capacity Congress has the responsibility to fully and adequately provide funding for United States military forces  especially when they are at war and are defending the Nation  and   Whereas when United States military forces are in harm s way and are protecting our country  Congress and the Nation should give them all the support they need in order to maintain their safety and accomplish their assigned missions  including the equipment  logistics  and funding necessary to ensure their safety and effectiveness  and such support is the responsibility of both the Executive Branch and the Legislative Branch of Government  Now  therefore  be it   Resolved by the Senate  the House of Representatives concurring   That it is the sense of Congress that Congress should not take any action that will endanger United States military forces in the field  including the elimination or reduction of funds for troops in the field  as such action with respect to funding would undermine their safety or harm their effectiveness in pursuing their assigned missions    110th CONGRESS  1st Session S  CON  RES  20  CONCURRENT RESOLUTION   Expressing the sense of Congress that no funds should be cut off or reduced for American troops in the field which would result in undermining their safety or their ability to complete their assigned missions
