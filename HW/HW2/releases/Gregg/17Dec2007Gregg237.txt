Senators Gregg and Sununu Announce Omnibus Appropriations Bill Contains  2 6 Billion For LIHEAP Contact  Andrea Wuebker Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  R NH  today announced that the Fiscal Year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations bill contains  2 6 billion in funding for the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  which provides federal energy assistance to low income families and seniors in need  Of the  2 6 billion   596 million is emergency contingency funding to help states that are facing severe cold temperatures this winter provide heating assistance for the most vulnerable in their communities  In response to the growing demand for this critical funding  the  2 6 billion contained in this legislation is  450 million higher than the amount approved by the Senate in September in the Fiscal Year 2008 Labor Health and Human Services Education Appropriations bill  Senator Gregg stated  With home heating costs at a record high  LIHEAP is an important program which provides federal heating assistance to those who need it the most  It is critical that we act now so that low income families and seniors can afford to heat their homes this winter  I am pleased that my colleagues in Congress have recognized the importance of this federal program to those of us in cold weather states and have increased the amount of LIHEAP funding available to ensure that those most vulnerable stay warm this winter  Senator Sununu  who has supported LIHEAP since he came to Congress in 1997  said  Higher energy prices and freezing temperatures have gripped New Hampshire and other states across the nation  serving as a reminder that those in need deserve access to adequate funding to help them to stay warm this winter  While the funding level in this bill is less than what many of us requested  I remain committed to helping individuals and families handle heating bills this season
