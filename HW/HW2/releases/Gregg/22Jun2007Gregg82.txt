Senator Gregg Announces Senate Appropriations Committee Support For Littleton Opera House Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee has  at his request  included  500 000 for the conservation and restoration of the Littleton Opera House in the Fiscal Year 2008 Interior EPA Senate Appropriations bill approved this week by the full committee  The funding  included within the Save Americas Treasures program  will help to protect the Littleton Opera House  restoring the local icon  while reinvigorating the award winning Main Street and bringing back cultural and local activities to the town  With todays approval  the bill is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  The funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Senator Gregg  a member of the Senate Appropriations Interior EPA Subcommittee  stated The Littleton Opera House has been a central fixture of the Town since it was established in 1895 and the Town of Littleton continues to come together to support the conservation and restoration of this historic place  It has served in many capacities  from a fire department to a town hall  and it is important to help preserve such an important piece of our states history  Tony Mincu  Littleton Town Manager stated  On behalf of the Board of Selectmen and the Town of Littleton  New Hampshire  we would like to thank Senator Gregg for his efforts and initial success in appropriating a  500 000 Grant towards the town jewel known as the Opera House  These potential monies will enable the project to go beyond a simple stabilization of the building and hopefully accomplish a modest rehabilitation and occupation of the now vacant building  The Town of Littleton is very grateful for Senator Greggs support and efforts to help Littleton with our efforts to restore this important historical building  The Littleton Opera House was originally completed in 1895  The initial phase of funding will go towards making the building structurally sound and to address safety issues  Presently  the building is off limits to any occupation  Until closure  the building was used for many community activities and artistic performances
