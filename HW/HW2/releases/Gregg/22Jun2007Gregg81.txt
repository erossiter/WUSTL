Senator Gregg Announces Senate Appropriations Committee Support Lancaster Drinking Water Project Contact erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved a measure containing  225 000 for the Town of Lancasters drinking water project  This funding will help the Town of Lancaster address issues with its water system  Senator Gregg included funding for this project in the Fiscal Year 2008 Senate Interior EPA Appropriations bill  which is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  The funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending bills  Senator Gregg  a member of the Senate Interior EPA Appropriations Subcommittee  stated  It is essential to ensure that all residents in the Granite State have access to safe and clean drinking water  in addition to proper and safe disposal of wastewater  Lancaster is working to improve the drinking water system  and this award will allow them to continue that work   David Stickney  Chairman of the Lancaster Board of Selectman  stated  We are thankful for the efforts of Senator Gregg in securing this STAG grant in the Senate Appropriations Bill  The grant would be a tremendous benefit to the community given the conditions of our local economy and the lack of snow and other situations that have caused a downturn in the North Country economy  This grant would certainly be a good shot in the arm for Lancasters economy and the water system as a whole  The funds will be used for Lancasters water system  including increasing the water pressure in various parts of the Town to meet DES requirements  This improvement will protect the Town in the event of a fire emergency and will improve overall water quality and prevent the risk of the water system being contaminated due to inadequate pressure and risk of back siphoning  In addition  funds will be used to address an outdated 2 5 million gallon earthen covered reservoir  which needs to be replaced with a pre stressed concrete water tank  Presently  the reservoir has an overflow feature to keep water circulating so as to prevent stale water  By closing the water system and eliminating this overflow  chlorinated water will no longer be wasted  saving water users money in the long run
