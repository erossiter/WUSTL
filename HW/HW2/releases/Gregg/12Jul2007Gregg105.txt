Senator Gregg Announces Granite Street Bridge Project Gains Support Of Senate Appropriations Committee Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved a bill containing  1 5 million for the initiative to widen the Granite Street Bridge and upgrade Granite Street into downtown Manchester  These funds will be used for the third stage of construction and will specifically expand Granite Street to seven lanes from Main Street to Elm Street  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  Senator Gregg was able to include this funding in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill  which is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  In the past  Senator Gregg has secured a total of  15 million for the Granite Street project      Senator Gregg stated  The Granite Street Bridge provides central access to the Queen City  which unfortunately has been a serious bottleneck problem for drivers  As the City continues to grow and expand  it is essential that the bridge be of appropriate size to quickly and safely get residents and visitors to their destinations  whether it be the Verizon Wireless Arena  the Fisher Cats stadium  downtown or many of the surrounding residential areas  The completion of this project will provide an additional boost to the ever growing economy of Manchester and allow for continued growth and expansion of the downtown area  Frank Guinta  Mayor of Manchester  stated  We are thrilled to hear that Senator Gregg was able to secure the funds for this much needed project  The Granite Street widening project is one of the cornerstone public works construction efforts going on in Manchester today  and these funds can help assure its timely completion
