Senator Gregg Announces Funding For Granite Street Bridge Project Signed Into Law Contact  Andrea Wuebker Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  announced that the Fiscal Year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations bill which contains  1 67 million in funding for the initiative to widen the Granite Street Bridge and upgrade Granite Street into downtown Manchester was signed into law today  These funds will be used for the third stage of construction and will specifically expand Granite Street to seven lanes from Main Street to Elm Street  To date  Senator Gregg has secured a total of  15 million for the Granite Street project  Funding for this project is included within the Appropriations Committee allocation for Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Senator Gregg stated  The Granite Street Bridge provides central access to the Queen City  and unfortunately has been a serious bottleneck problem for drivers  As the City continues to grow and expand  it is essential that the bridge be able to safely get residents and visitors to their destinations  whether it be the Verizon Wireless Arena  the Fisher Cats stadium  downtown or to many of the surrounding residential areas  The completion of the project will provide a great boon for the City economy and growing downtown area  Gregg continued  Though the Democratic Leadership pushed this years appropriations process into the 11th hour  it is important to note that Republicans efforts to control spending successfully eliminated nearly  18 billion in new spending  I am pleased my colleagues were able to reach across the aisle and come up with an omnibus package which is more in line with the budget request  provides critical funding for troops in the field  and still includes funding for important New Hampshire initiatives  Frank Guinta  Mayor of Manchester  stated  Senator Gregg has continued to track this important project and has shown to be a champion of economic development for Manchester  As the economic driver of this state  these funds will help improve an important gateway to the city   both in terms of safety and aesthetically
