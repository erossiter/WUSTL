Senator Gregg Announces Senate Support For Reconstruction Of The Robert Frost Farm Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate has approved a measure containing  300 000 in federal funding to restore  repair  and preserve the Robert Frost Farm in Derry  The State of New Hampshire Department of Resources and Economic Development  which has owned and managed the Frost Farm since 1965  will oversee the future reconstruction efforts  Robert Frost  a world renowned poet  wrote many of his notable literary works while living at the Farm  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  Senator Gregg was able to include this funding in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation  Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill  The bill now heads to a joint House Senate conference committee before returning to each chamber for final passage  Funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation for Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Senator Gregg stated  The Robert Frost Farm is an important historic  cultural and educational landmark in the Granite State  Each year  many children take school trips to the Farm where they learn about the accomplished poet who has played a significant role in shaping New Hampshires rich literary tradition  These funds will not only preserve the Farm  but will also ensure that future generations will have the opportunity to enjoy this resource  Laura Burnham  Chairman of the Robert Frost Farm Board of Trustees stated  I am delighted to learn that Senator Judd Gregg has procured the necessary funding to address critical preservation issues at the Robert Frost Homestead  His tireless efforts on behalf of the poets former Derry home will ensure that future generations of Frost enthusiasts from across the globe will be able to visit this significant literary landmark  On behalf of the Frost Farm Trustees  I wish to extend my sincere gratitude to the Senator for this noteworthy achievement
