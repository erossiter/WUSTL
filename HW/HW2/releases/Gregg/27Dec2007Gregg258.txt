Senator Gregg Announces Funding For Littleton Opera House Signed Into Law Contact Andrea Wuebker Laena Fallon   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Fiscal Year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations bill which includes  492 200 for the conservation and restoration of the Littleton Opera House was signed into law yesterday  The funding  included within the Save Americas Treasures program  will help to protect the Littleton Opera House  restoring the local icon  while reinvigorating the award winning Main Street and bringing back cultural and local activities to the town  With todays approval  the bill is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  The funding for this project was included within the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Senator Gregg  a member of the Senate Appropriations Interior EPA Subcommittee  stated  The Littleton Opera House has been a central fixture of the Town since it was established in 1895 and the Town of Littleton continues to come together to support the conservation and restoration of this historic place  It has served in many capacities  from a fire department to a town hall  and it is important to help preserve such an important piece of our states history  Gregg continued  Though the Democratic Leadership pushed this years appropriations process into the 11th hour  it is important to note that Republicans efforts to control spending successfully eliminated nearly  18 billion in new spending  I am pleased my colleagues were able to reach across the aisle and come up with an omnibus package which is more in line with the budget request  provides critical funding for troops in the field  and still includes funding for important New Hampshire initiatives  Charles Connell  Littleton Town Manager stated  This is great news for the Town of Littleton  The Opera House is an important and defining feature within the Town of Littleton  That the Senator was able to provide this much needed support is deeply appreciated within the Town  We look forward to inviting Senator Gregg to Opening Day  The Littleton Opera House was originally completed in 1895  The initial phase of funding will go towards making the building structurally sound and to address safety issues  Presently  the building is off limits to any occupation  Until closure  the building was used for many community activities and artistic performances
