Senator Gregg Announces Funding For Manchester Water Project Signed Into Law Contact  Andrea Wuebker  Laena Fallon   WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Fiscal Year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations bill which contains  492 200 for the Manchester Combined Sewer Overflow initiative was signed into law yesterday  The Combined Sewer Overflow project separates sewer lines from storm drains  improving public health for the safety of Manchester residents  The funding for this project was included within the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Senator Gregg stated  As the overall population of New Hampshire continues to grow  cities such as Manchester are experiencing an increased strain on public water systems  It is essential to ensure that all residents in the Granite State have access to safe and clean drinking water  in addition to proper and safe disposal of wastewater  Manchester is doing an outstanding job addressing water infrastructure issues and this award will allow them to continue that work  Gregg continued  Though the Democratic Leadership pushed this years appropriations process into the 11th hour  it is important to note that Republicans efforts to control spending successfully eliminated nearly  18 billion in new spending  I am pleased my colleagues were able to reach across the aisle and come up with an omnibus package which is more in line with the budget request  provides critical funding for troops in the field  and still includes funding for important New Hampshire initiatives  Frank Guinta  Mayor of Manchester  stated  The sewer separation project is a vital part of Manchester s commitment to improving its infrastructure  These funds will go to a very important use and will benefit city residents for many years to come  I want to thank Sen  Gregg for his efforts on this project as well as many others that will have a positive benefit on Manchester s quality of life
