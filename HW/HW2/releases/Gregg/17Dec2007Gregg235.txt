Senators Gregg and Sununu Announce  343 000 For Nashua Veterans Housing Initiative Contained In Fiscal Year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations Legislation Contact  Laena Fallon Gregg   Barbara Riley Sununu    WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  R NH  today  12 17  announced that the fiscal year 2008 Omnibus Appropriations Bill contains  343 000 in funding for a Nashua Veterans Housing initiative  The resources are targeted for Harbor Homes Inc  to help defray the costs Harbor Homes has incurred from the construction of Buckingham Place  the organizations 20 unit transitional housing development for honorably discharged homeless veterans and their families   The United States House of Representatives is expected to take up the legislation on Monday  with Senate action scheduled later this week  Senator Gregg stated  Veterans and their families who have given so much in service to our country deserve our help in their time of need  Buckingham Place does an extraordinary job providing these critical services to many individuals and families who need help getting back on their feet  and I am pleased to support the initiative  Gregg continued  Though the Democratic Leadership has pushed this years appropriations process into the 11th hour  it is important to note that Republicans efforts to control spending were successful and forced the elimination of  18 billion in new spending  I am pleased my colleagues were able to reach across the aisle and come up with an omnibus which is more in line with the budget request and still includes funding for important New Hampshire initiatives   Senator Sununu stated  This bill provides a critical portion of the funding to help Harbor Homes and Buckingham Place continue to offer housing and valuable services to veterans in need  Our veterans have made tremendous sacrifices to protect our nation and the ideals for which it stands  Through Harbor Homes  veterans will have access to the resources they need to quickly and comfortably transition back into their communities  Harbor Homes specializes in helping homeless individuals  with and without mental illnesses  return to mainstream society and has built a transitional housing development  Buckingham Place  for homeless veterans in Nashua  This 20 unit apartment building is one of the first facilities in the country to provide services to both homeless male and female veterans and their families  In addition to housing  clients have access to meals  job training and vocational services  employment assistance  and medical and mental health care
