Senator Gregg CoSponsors Clean Air Bill   Contact  Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today renewed his support for clean air legislation by again co sponsoring the bipartisan Clean Air Planning Act  introduced today  The legislation addresses both air quality and climate change through an aggressive schedule for power plants to reduce emissions and alleviate some of the worst air related health and environmental problems      Senator Gregg stated  This bill makes a significant and immediate impact in combating two of the most serious environmental issues facing our nation and world today  air quality and climate change  Pollution and climate change are threatening our forests  our fisheries and the natural resources we cherish  We must act now to address the impact that our lifestyles are having on our environment and our planet  and this bill takes important steps to do that through the implementation of a market based system to reduce several of the most harmful pollutants  I am pleased that so many of my colleagues recognize the urgency of this matter and are joining with me today  While I have sponsored similar legislation in the past  I am optimistic that this bill will move forward and be enacted  in large part due to the tremendous amount of attention these issues are receiving  Senator Gregg is joined in co sponsoring the CAPA legislation by Senators Tom Carper  D DE   Joe Lieberman  ID CT   Susan Collins  R ME   Dianne Feinstein  D CA   Chris Dodd  D CT   Blanche Lincoln  D AR  and John Sununu  R NH   The bill is similar to legislation Senator Gregg has supported in previous Congresses   Specifically  the CAPA legislation would  Cut sulfur dioxide emissions by 82 percent by 2015 through a market based cap and trade program  This acid rain causing pollution would be cut from 11 million tons to a cap of 2 million tons in 2015 Cut emissions of nitrogen oxides by 68 percent by 2015 through a market based cap and trade program  Ozone pollution would be cut from 5 million tons to a cap of 1 6 million tons in 2015  Cut mercury emissions at each power plant by 90 percent in 2015  This achievable goal would greatly reduce the risks that this neurotoxin poses to children and pregnant women  Caps carbon dioxide emissions at 2007 levels in 2012 and achieves at least a 57 percent reduction from 2007 levels by 2050
