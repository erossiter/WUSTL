Senator Gregg Announces Senate Approval Of Federal Funds For Little Bay Bridges Reconstruction Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate has approved a measure containing  2 5 million for the expansion and upgrade of the Little Bay Bridges Spaulding Turnpike in Newington and Dover  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  Senator Gregg was able to include these funds in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation  Housing and Urban Development funding bill  The bill now heads to a joint House Senate conference committee before returning to each chamber for final passage  Funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation for Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Since 2003  Senator Gregg has secured  28 million for the Little Bay Bridges project  Senator Gregg stated  The Little Bay Bridges project has consistently been identified as a top priority for New Hampshires Seacoast region  As New Hampshires population continues to grow exponentially  and with more tourists visiting the Granite State each year  it is crucial that the resources are put in place to get this project moving forward  These additional federal funds will help expand the Bridges  providing a boost to the regional and state economies and easing the travels of drivers in the future   Scott Myers  Mayor of Dover  stated  I appreciate the continued efforts of Senator Gregg and his staff in securing this funding for the Little Bay Bridge project  This remains the single most important transportation issue facing Dover and Strafford County  Improvements and expansion to this stretch of highway will allow for continued economic development opportunities  enhanced safety and a reduction in serious traffic delays  I look forward to continuing to work with Senator Gregg as this project moves forward
