Senator Gregg Announces Senate Appropriations Committee Support For Souhegan Valley Boys And Girls Club Contact Erin Rath Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON   U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved a bill containing  200 000 for the Souhegan Valley Boys and Girls Club in Milford late last week  The Club suffered over  250 000 in damages from the federally declared flooding disaster in April 2007  These funds will allow the Club to repair and rebuild from the significant damages sustained during the flood  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  Senator Gregg included funding for this project in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill  which is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  Funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation for Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures   Senator Gregg  who previously secured  400 000 in federal funds for the Club  stated  Boys and Girls Clubs make a tremendous difference in the lives of thousands of kids nationwide  and the clubs in New Hampshire provide exceptional service to the communities  The Souhegan Valley Boys and Girls Club does an extraordinary job providing programming and services for young people in the region  These funds will help ensure that the Club can make necessary repairs and continue to provide for its young members as it rebuilds  Dave Parker  Chief Professional Officer of the Souhegan Valley Boys and Girls club  stated  The BGC of Souhegan Valley can not thank Senator Gregg enough for his support during not only the building of our club but most recently the rebuilding of this club after the horrendous flood of April 16th  We are deeply appreciative but the following is a quote from a letter written by one of our members parent that best shows how important the club is and how important a role the Senator plays  From the letter  When I first heard of the damage that the flood did to the club  I was numb  What would I do without the Boys  Girls Club  How would I be able to go to work  Where would my child go  In Milford  there are not a lot of options for school aged children  And of course money is a concern  That night my daughter cried herself to sleep  Her club was flooded  My phone rang off the hook that night and into the next day  Everywhere in the community people wanted to know what they could do to help put the Boys  Girls Club back together  The amazing thing is instead of just talking about it  myself and others in the community got involved  Even people who did not have children  were showing up at the club and working side by side with those that did  That in itself shows that this club is important to more then just the kids  or parents but the community  What would I do if there was no Boys  Girls Club  I dont honestly know  My two daughters and I have become better people from our involvement with the club  My children are growing up with the values  hopes and belief in themselves that every staff member gives them  They are better friends  better members of our family  better students  and eventually they will be an asset to whatever community that they live in from their Boys  Girls Club experience  The letter ends here but this sentiment extends through out the Club from child to child  parent to parent and staff to staff  To Senator Gregg  thank you for supporting us and believing that the Boys  Girls Club of Souhegan Valley is important to its community  Boys and Girls Clubs provide young people  ages 6 18  with a safe  positive environment during non school hours and in the summer  Two years ago  the club moved into the new facility which was damaged by the flooding  The new center includes a performing arts center  a fitness room  computer and technology center  teen center  fine arts room and a canteen lounge
