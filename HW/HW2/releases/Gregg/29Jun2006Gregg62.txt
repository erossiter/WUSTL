               SENATE COMMITTEE APPROVES MEASURE                                 TO IMPROVE               BORDER SECURITY                WASHINGTON U S               Senator Judd Gregg  R NH   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations              Subcommittee on Homeland Security today announced that the full              Senate Appropriations Committee has approved the Fiscal Year 2007              funding measure for the Department of Homeland Security  This legislation              builds upon major investments in border security started last year               The bill provides a total of  32 793 billion in funding  which              is  715 million more than the President requested and  1 52 billion              more than funding in Fiscal Year 2006  excluding emergency supplemental              appropriations  The bill is now ready for consideration by the              full Senate at a date to be determined by the Majority Leader   This funding measure provides a total of  14 3 billion for the              defense of our nations borders and enforcement of immigration              and customs laws  This includes  2 1 billion for border security              and control  providing for an additional 1 000 Border Patrol Agents              and  1 2 billion for custody and case management  providing for              an additional 1 000 new detention beds  Among other initiatives               it includes   3 8 billion for maritime border security  of which               1 9 billion is for port security   1 7 billion for border security              inspections and trade facilities   1 3 billion for immigration              and customs investigations   131 million for border security technology              and  645 million for air and marine operations of Customs and Border              Protection  A full breakout of border related provisions in the              bill is attached    Senator Gregg stated  This bill builds on the goals set              out last year  ensuring funding is concentrated on the greatest              threats facing our nation  border security  preventing the use              of weapons of mass destruction  and intelligence gathering capacity               The bill increases the number of border agents and detention beds               as well as providing the capital infrastructure improvements and              resources necessary to support the increase in boots on the ground               I am pleased that the legislation has been moved through committee              expeditiously  and I hope to have
