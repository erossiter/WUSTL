Senators Gregg and Sununu Announce North Country Broadband Initiative Wins Senate Appropriations Committee Support Contact Erin Rath Gregg   Barbara Riley Sununu   WASHINGTON  U S  Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  R NH  today announced the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved a funding measure containing  1 million in federal funds for an initiative to bring affordable and reliable broadband service to the North Country  This program will initiate an open access  fixed wireless broadband infrastructure throughout Coos  Carroll and Grafton Counties  Broadband has been identified as a critical infrastructure need for economic growth  but it remains inconsistent throughout the North Country  These funds will be utilized primarily for the design  acquisition and construction of the infrastructure  Funding for this project was included in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation  and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill  which is now ready for consideration by the full Senate later this year  These funds are included within the Committee allocation set out for Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures  Senator Gregg  a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee stated  The Internet has quickly become one of the most used tools in our society  it aids people in almost every facet of everyday life from entertainment and personal communication to research and business transactions  To date  much of the North Country has been unable to access the Internet at high speeds  It is critical that we provide this service to ensure the economic opportunity and vitality of this region  I applaud the Littleton Industrial Development Corporation  NCIC and those individuals and groups who are working hard to provide these much needed services for the North Country  Senator John Sununu  who has worked extensively to advance initiatives and legislation to expand broadband access across the state  stated  North Country access to affordable broadband is essential to the regions economic future and a top priority for me  The Northern Community Investment Corporation has demonstrated its commitment and ability to navigate significant geographic and infrastructure challenges in broadband projects  These resources are critical to helping the group build on this success by expanding wireless broadband deployment across Coos  Grafton and Carroll counties  Empowering northern New Hampshire residents with access to these networks is a matter of great importance  and I will continue to advocate strongly for these funds as the appropriations process moves forward  Greg Eastman  President of Littleton Industrial Development Corporation  stated  The efforts of Senators Gregg and Sununu of adding an appropriation of  1 000 000 for the North Country Broadband LINC Project is fantastic news  Affordable broadband has been recognized as one of the top two or three needs of the North Country  especially for continued economic development  We know that job creation and increased economic activity will follow the implementation of an affordable broadband system  and NCIC and LIDC are working hard to make it happen  The Senators efforts are clearly the best news we have to date and we truly appreciate their actions  Jon Freeman  President of Northern Community Investment Corporation  stated  We are truly appreciative of Senators Gregg and Sununus long term commitment to the care of the North Country  Through this funding  a critical infrastructure is to be created that will greatly improve business activity and service the region with improved health care  safety and educational opportunities
