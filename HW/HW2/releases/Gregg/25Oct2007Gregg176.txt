Senator Gregg Announces Senate Support For Technology Initiative At Franklin Pierce University Contact  Andrea Wuebker Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  today announced that the Senate has approved  400 000 in federal funding for Franklin Pierce University in a Fiscal Year 2008 funding measure  The funds will be used for technology based educational programs and services for students and the surrounding community  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and the Labor  Health and Human Services  Education Subcommittee  Senator Gregg included these funds in the Labor HHS Education Appropriations bill  which now heads to a joint House Senate conference committee before returning to each chamber for final passage  The funding for these projects is included in the Committee allocation set out for the Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures   Senator Gregg stated  These funds will help Franklin Pierce address the problem of access to technology and training  which is particularly challenging to communities in rural New Hampshire  This project will allow the University to network with all of its satellite campuses throughout the state and provide technology and assistance to communities in the surrounding areas  Dr  George J  Hagerty  President of Franklin Pierce University  stated  We are immensely grateful to Senator Gregg for his continued commitment to higher education in New Hampshire and his willingness to help Franklin Pierce achieve its mission of educational service in the Monadnock region  Funds from this appropriation will be used by the University to strengthen our technology infrastructure and  in so doing  extend our ability to serve the Monadnock region through new services and access to training  Educational programs will be made available both online and through traditional means to assist school districts  senior citizens  and patrons of our Library and programming providing through the Fitzwater Center  Special appropriations  such as the one made possible through Sen  Greggs leadership  are instrumental in assisting both institutions of higher education and rural communities in the rapidly advancing world of education and communication technology  The program will provide technology based educational programs and services to the communities and students served by Franklin Pierce University  Through the Franklin Pierce Library and the Marlin Fitzwater Center for Communication  the University has become a cultural and educational resource  not only for the Rindge campus and community  but also for the communities served by satellite campuses  including  Concord  Keene  Lebanon  Manchester  Nashua and Portsmouth  Beyond wireless based training  tangible project initiatives include  1  the use of current and evolving technology to increase public access through the broadcast of town meetings and other educational and public policy gatherings directly into peoples homes  2  expansion of training on new technology for high school students and senior citizens  and 3  cooperative ventures with schools and businesses in the Monadnock region and other rural locations served by the Universitys satellite campuses
