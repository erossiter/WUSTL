Senator Gregg Announces Funds For Granite Street Bridge Project On Way To Final Vote Contact  Andrea Wuebker Laena Fallon  WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  tonight announced that a joint House Senate committee of conference has approved a measure containing  1 7 million for the initiative to widen the Granite Street Bridge and upgrade Granite Street into downtown Manchester  These funds will be used for the third stage of construction and will specifically expand Granite Street to seven lanes from Main Street to Elm Street  As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  Senator Gregg was able to include this funding in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation  Housing and Urban Development Appropriations conference report  It is expected that the House will vote on the conference report later this week  allowing the Senate to consider it thereafter  To date  Senator Gregg has secured a total of  15 million for the Granite Street project  Funding for this project is included within the Committee allocation for Fiscal Year 2008 spending measures      Senator Gregg stated  The Granite Street Bridge provides central access to the Queen City  and unfortunately has been a serious bottleneck problem for drivers  As the City continues to grow and expand  it is essential that the bridge be able to safely get residents and visitors to their destinations  whether it be the Verizon Wireless Arena  the Fisher Cats stadium  downtown or to many of the surrounding residential areas  The completion of the project will provide a great boon for the City economy and growing downtown area
