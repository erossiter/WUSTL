          U S  SEN ATE PASSES MILITARY CONSTRUCTION FUNDING BILL INCLUDES  52 MILLION FOR DENVER VA HOSPITAL AT FITZSIMONS          November 14th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced Tuesday that the U S  Senate passed the FY 2007 military construction funding bill  which included  52 million for the replacement Denver Veterans Medical Center  Allard helped secure this funding in the bill through his work on the Military Construction   Veterans Affairs  VA  Appropriations Subcommittee  on which he serves      Senate passage of the military construction bill with this funding for a new VA hospital at Fitzsimons is an important step in making this project a reality     said Allard    The Senate voted in September to authorize the military construction funding  and today we voted to put  52 million in federal funding toward the project      The U S  House of Representatives passed the bill earlier this year that contained no funding for Fitzsimons  but due to Allard  s position on the Appropriations Committee  the Senate added  52 million for a new VA hospital at Fitzsimons      I have worked closely with the Veterans Administration on the development and funding for a new veterans hospital at Fitzsimons  and I am pleased that our hard work has resulted in funding for building this new facility     said Allard    As a member of the Conference Committee  I will be doing everything possible to ensure we keep funding for this project in the bill      Allard has worked closely with the Fitzsimons Redevelopment Authority  Aurora Mayor Ed Tauer and Veterans Affairs Secretary  Jim Nicholson  in developing a plan to build the new replacement VA hospital at Fitzsimons       This new medical center is critical to the thousands of Colorado veterans in need of accessible  quality medical care     Allard added    The funding will allow us to obtain the building site  provide additional design work and begin to prepare the site for construction      Mayor Ed Tauer echoed Allard  s sentiments    We re thrilled to work with Senator Allard to bring Colorado veterans this new state of the art medical facility  It s great for the vets  it s great for Aurora and it s great for the entire region      In addition  Allard worked with U S  Sen  Kay Bailey Hutchison  R Texas  in successfully attaching an amendment to the Senate Military Construction Appropriations bill that authorizes the VA to proceed forward with planning and site acquisition  This amendment was essential to continuing the progress on the hospital and efficiently using tax payer funds   The military construction funding bill passed the U S  House of Representatives earlier this year and will now move to a House Senate Conference Committee where a final version will be produced  Allard  as a member of the Appropriations Committee  will serve on the Conference Committee                              November 2006 Press Releases
