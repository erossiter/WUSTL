          ALLARD ANNOUNCES NEARLY  1 75 MILLION IN PILT FUNDING FOR COLORADO Funding Up Over Half a Million Dollars from Last Year         June 19th  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator   R    CO  announced Monday that Colorado will receive nearly  17 5 million in PILT funding to help rural communities provide necessary services  such as fire protection and public school  for fiscal year 2006      I  m pleased to announce that this year Colorado will receive nearly 17 5 million dollars in PILT funding     said Senator Allard    This is an increase in payments of more than a half million dollars from last year          This is a very tight budget environment  but I was able to secure an increase  in PILT funding that is so critical to our local governments     said Senator Allard  who has been an advocate of PILT funding for many years and has been championing this cause as a member of the Interior Appropriations Subcommittee    The PILT program provides federal payments to local governments to help offset the property tax revenue they are unable to collect because there are nontaxable federal lands within their boundaries  These payments help local governments provide services such as firefighting and police protection  construction of public schools and roads  and search and rescue operations      The PILT program is vital to the health of local governments in Colorado  particularly in rural counties     Senator Allard said    Coloradans can be sure I will continue to support this program in the future      PILT payments are made annually for tax exempt federal lands administered by the BLM  the National Park Service  the U S  Fish and Wildlife Service  all agencies of the Interior Department  part of the U S  Department of Agriculture  and for federal water projects and some military installations                              June 2006 Press Releases
