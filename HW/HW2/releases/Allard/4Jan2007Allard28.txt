          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD INTRODUCES BILL TO CURB METH TRAFFICKING          January 4th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   today  on the first day of session in the 110th Congress  introduced the Methamphetamine Trafficking Enforcement Act of 2007 to help curb the trafficking of methamphetamine  meth  both within and across the nation  s borders     Just this week  a Colorado Methamphetamine Task Force report cited Denver as a major distribution center for meth in the United States     said Allard    We must step up enforcement to reduce the amount of meth being trafficked into our country by establishing stricter penalties for meth offenders and improve coordination with foreign law enforcement officials      Allard  s bill would address the distribution issue by dramatically lowering the quantity and dollar amount thresholds for federal criminal prosecution of leaders of meth distribution rings  The bill also presses upon the U S  Trade Representative  the U S  Attorney General and the secretaries of the U S  Department of Homeland Security and U S  Department of State work with their foreign counterparts to include new ways to curb the illicit use and shipment of pseudoephedrine  ephedrine and similar chemicals in multilateral and bilateral negotiations   According to the U S  Department of Justice  the use  production and distribution of meth on Indian lands has increased in the past decade  With limited numbers of tribal law enforcement officials  meth can easily flow into and be trafficked out of many Indian reservations  This bill urges the Attorney General to research and report to Congress the challenges faced by all Indian reservations and make recommendations to help them  address meth trafficking and abuse     This bill is a first step to fighting meth trafficking  All of Colorado faces challenges associated with this growing epidemic  I  ve worked with many local drug task forces  law enforcement officials and District Attorneys around the state on this issue     said Allard    We must recognize the immediacy of the issue of meth trafficking  And it  s important that we protect the United States and its borders to ensure both national security and the safety of our communities                                 January 2007 Press Releases
