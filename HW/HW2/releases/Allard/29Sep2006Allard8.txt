          SENATOR ALLARD COMMENTS ON ROCKY FLATS SIGNING CEREMONY Says Action is Next Step in   Weapons to Wildlife    Transformation         September 29th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   applauded the signing of the Rocky Flats Record of Decision on Friday  which is a key step in accelerating the closure process and the eventual delisting of Rocky Flats from the U S  Environmental Protection Agency  s Superfund National Priorities Listing  NPL      Today  s signing of the Rocky Flats Record of Decision officially moves Rocky Flats one step closer to becoming the Rocky Flats National Wildlife refuge and the transformation from   weapons to wildlife     said Allard    Initial estimates suggested that the site clean up would take over 70 years and cost upwards of  35 billion dollars  however  through public private partnerships and by using a performance based contract  the physical clean up has been completed  in the record time of 6 years  saving tax payers an estimated  20 billion dollars      In 2001  Congress enacted Allard  s legislation that accelerated the site  s clean up and closure  and upon certified clean up  creates the Rocky Flats National Wildlife Refuge  His legislation requires the U S  Department of Energy  DOE  to transfer most of the Rocky Flats Environmental Technology Site to the U S  Department of Interior for the purpose of creating a wildlife refuge to preserve Colorado  s unique Front Range habitat   The document signed today by the EPA  the DOE and the state of Colorado outlines the selected clean up remedy at Rocky Flats and will serve as the basis for a post closure regulatory control document  Following a public comment period  information within these documents will be used as support for NPL delisting criteria     In 1997  I made its accelerated clean up and closure of one of my top legislative priorities  The successful clean up and closure of Rocky Flats has been a collaborative team effort     said Allard    The DOE and Kaiser Hill worked to accelerate the project with aggressive clean up methods  The workers at Rocky Flats performed with determination and pride  Local governments and citizen activists strongly supported the clean up  The state of Colorado and the EPA provided exceptional oversight  Together we have made the impossible  possible          The people of Colorado can be proud of the unprecedented accelerated closure and clean up processes that were done at the site  Rocky Flats is a model of what can be done when people unite behind a common cause     Allard said    I will continue to champion the Rocky Flats project and I am looking forward to the next big Rocky Flats celebration    the stand up of the Rocky Flats National Wildlife Refuge and the beginning of a new era at Rocky Flats                                 September 2006 Press Releases
