                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Asks FEMA to Stand Ready to Help Colorado Deal with Flooding          May 24th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  on Tuesday asked the head of the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  to prepare to help Colorado communities who are experiencing flooding as a result of heavy spring runoff   In a letter to Under Secretary of Homeland Security for Emergency Preparedness Michael Brown  Senator Allard cited melting snow packs in certain Colorado mountain areas that are more than 340 percent higher than last year  resulting in near record levels of runoff     Recent history has shown how devastating spring flooding can be in Colorado     Senator Allard said    The thawing of these very deep snow packs is causing flooding in some Colorado communities already         I am asking Undersecretary Brown to ensure that FEMA is ready to provide any possible assistance that our Colorado communities might need as quickly as possible     Senator Allard said    FEMA has the organization  manpower and the resources that can be swiftly brought to bear in these kinds of situations  and make all the difference         Specifically  I have asked FEMA to be prepared to move should an official disaster be declared     Senator Allard said    I look forward to hearing from the Under Secretary                                 May 2005 Press Releases
