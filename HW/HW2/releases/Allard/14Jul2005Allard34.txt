          Senator Allard Secures Higher than Anticipated Funding for Fort Collins CDC Lab from Key Senate Panel          July 14th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  said Thursday that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved a 2006 HHS appropriations bill that includes additional funding for the new Centers for Disease Control  CDC  laboratory in Fort Collins     The good news is that there is  25 million in the bill for the Fort Collins lab  This proves the importance of this project to the overall work of the CDC     said Senator Allard  a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  who worked to secure the additional funds for the Fort Collins CDC Laboratory     I appreciate that Senator  Arlen  Specter  s Labor  Health and Social Services  Education and Related Agencies Appropriations Subcommittee put an emphasis on this facility  and its mission     Senator Allard said   At the beginning of the appropriations process  Senator Allard had requested  22 4 million in 2006 funding for the new laboratory  New information provided to the Senator by the CDC  reflecting what its final costs are likely to be  revealed a shortfall that could result in part of the new facility remaining unfinished     The extra funding we obtained from the Appropriations Committee for the new lab may go to complete a bit more of the 4th floor of the facility  or to purchase more of the equipment needed to get it up and running     Senator Allard said     The state of the art research performed at the CDC lab is critical to our nation  s ongoing public health efforts        Senator Allard has met several times with CDC Director Dr  Julie Gerberding to discuss the construction and staffing of the new laboratory   For the 2005 fiscal year  the final CDC appropriations provided  22 million in funding for the lab  part of more than nearly  50 million in federal appropriations for the lab since fiscal year 2003
