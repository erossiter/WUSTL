          SENATOR ALLARD SECURES MORE THAN  52 MILLION FOR NEW DENVER VETERANS HOSPITAL AT FITZSIMONS          July 18th  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator   R Colorado  announced Tuesday that he was able to secure  52 million for the replacement Denver Veterans Medical Center in the 2007 military construction funding bill approved today by the Military Construction   Veterans Affairs Appropriations Subcommittee  of which he is a member      Today is a red letter day for the new VA hospital at Fitzsimons     said Senator Allard     We have taken the first real step in turning the new VA hospital from a dream into reality with today  s action by the Appropriations Committee        I have worked closely with the Veterans Administration on the development and funding for a new veterans hospital at Fitzsimons  and I am pleased that our hard work has paid off   The Military Construction and Veterans Affairs Appropriations Subcommittee has agreed to my funding request of  52 million towards building this new facility        This new medical center is of paramount importance to the thousands of Colorado veterans in need of accessible  quality medical care  The funding will allow us to obtain the building site  provide additional design work and begin to prepare the site for construction      Mayor Ed Tauer echoed Allard  s sentiments    We  re thrilled to work with Senator Allard to bring Colorado veterans this new state of the art medical facility  It  s great for the vets  it  s great for Aurora and it  s great for the entire region      Allard has worked closely with the Fitzsimons Redevelopment Authority  Aurora Mayor  Ed Tauer and Veterans Affairs Secretary  Jim Nicholson  in developing a plan to build the new replacement VA hospital at Fitzsimons   Today  s Appropriations Committee action is the culmination of those efforts                              July 2006 Press Releases
