          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD REQUESTS INTERIOR SECRETARY S INVOLVEMENT IN GUNNISON WATER SOLUTION          September 26th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   sent a letter Tuesday to the U S  Department of the Interior Secretary Dirk Kempthorne requesting his involvement in restarting negotiations regarding water flows through the Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park     While a legislative solution may be warranted at some point  I still believe an out of court agreement negotiated between the experts at the Department of the Interior and the state of Colorado is the proper solution     Allard wrote in the letter    An outright rejection of the agreement  or court quantification of federal water rights  could lead to the largest water grab in my state  s    and possibly the nation  s    history      A federal district court judge in Colorado recently set aside a 2003 agreement made between then Secretary Gale Norton  on the behalf of the U S  Park Service  and the state of Colorado regarding water flows through the Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park  The loss of the ability to utilize in stream flows negotiated in the 2003 agreement will have dramatic negative impacts on traditional water users in the area   The area was established as a national monument in 1933  and in 1999  Allard cosponsored legislation that converted the monument into a national park  During this time  the state of Colorado attempted to settle water rights issues without court action  but in late 2001  the Clinton Administration filed for last minute quantification of that right  commonly known as the   Strickland filing     This filing was opposed by more than 350 interested parties  The 2003 agreement was the settlement of the case      Without the common sense approach negotiated in the 2003 settlement  a great deal of uncertainty resurfaces for farmers  ranchers  recreational interests  hydro power interests  local governments and many others     Allard wrote   In the letter  Allard urged Kempthorne   in the strongest terms to push forward negotiations to broker another common sense based agreement that will protect water users throughout the
