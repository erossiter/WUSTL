          Senator Allard Secures  12 Million for Widening  Improving Highway 50          July 27th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R CO   Chairman of the Senate Housing and Transportation Subcommittee  has secured  12 million to widen Highway 50 from Las Animas to Lamar into a four lane roadway in the final version of the transportation authorization bill that emerged from a conference committee on Wednesday  The transportation bill is expected to be approved by the House and Senate by Friday and signed into law by President Bush early next week     Senator Allard was a member of the House Senate Conference Committee that ironed out differences between the U S  House and Senate versions of the transportation legislation and secured a total of  110 million for specific transportation projects above the statewide funding formula for Colorado    Upgrading Highway 50 to a four lane highway from Pueblo to the state line has been a transportation priority of the people of the Lower Arkansas Valley for more than a generation     said Senator Allard    Today  we can say that their voices have been heard       Improving Highway 50 will be an enormous benefit for those who reside in southeastern Colorado     Senator Allard said    Six years ago  a Colorado task force recommended that U S  50 should be upgraded to four lanes across the state as a way of taking traffic off Interstate 70 through the Denver metro area  We are now beginning to make that recommendation a reality       My conference committee appointment put me in the position to ensure that these projects were included the final version of the bill     Senator Allard said    I felt it was important that communities whose transportation needs have previously been ignored or not made a priority  be made a priority                                  July 2005 Press Releases
