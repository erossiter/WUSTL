          SENATOR ALLARD ANNOUNCES JOBS TO BE RESTORED AT NATIONAL RENEWABLE ENERGY LABORATORY          February 19th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  announced Sunday that Secretary of Energy Samuel Bodman has informed him that the jobs that had been cut at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory  NREL  in Golden are being restored immediately     This is the best news we  ve had in a long time     said Senator Allard  co chair of the Senate Renewable Energy and Energy Efficiency Caucus    I am pleased that my efforts to persuade the Department of Energy to find a way to restore those jobs have paid off        When we received the phone call today  I was very excited  To be able to restore the positions that had been cut at NREL is a great thing to hear about on a holiday weekend     Senator Allard added     I am pleased that the researchers and other staff members who were laid off can get back to work in time for President Bush  s visit to the lab     Senator Allard said    The research work they are doing is vital to our country  s continued well being and prosperity      The NREL job cuts were the result of an increasing number of congressional earmarks that have been placed in the renewable energy research accounts at the Department of Energy over the last several years  The department already had shifted some funds around in order to reduce potential job losses  but earlier this month it was announced that the laboratory would have to lay off 32 members of the staff  The job restorations were accomplished DOE by shifting unused funding from other accounts                              February 2006 Press Releases
