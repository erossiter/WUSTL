          Senator Allard Chites Outstanding Performance of Workers on Day Last Nuclear Shipment Leaves Rocky Flats          April 19th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  had high praise for the U S  Department of Energy  s Rocky Flats cleanup project on Tuesday  the day when the final shipment of transuranic nuclear waste left the site outside of Denver     This is an outstanding achievement  We are under budget and ahead of schedule at Rocky Flats     Senator Allard said    Eight years ago  no one would have believed it was possible        I want to commend the Rocky Flats workers for their outstanding performance  Their story at Rocky Flats is one of heroism and dedication     the Senator said    The same workers who risked their lives for their country during the Cold War have worked tirelessly to clean up and close one of the most contaminated sites in America  demonstrating what can be accomplished when people work together to meet a common goal      Rocky Flats is owned by the U S  Department of Energy  DOE  and manufactured components for nuclear weapons for the nation  s defense until 1992  The current mission of the plant is closure   environmental cleanup  waste management and decommissioning    by December 2006  The industrial complex of more than 100 buildings is located in the center of about nine square miles of undeveloped land 16 miles northwest of downtown Denver     It is a magnificent accomplishment that this project is going to be complete ahead of schedule     Senator Allard said    When the clean up is complete  Coloradans and people from around the country will be able to take pride in what has been done here  the cleanup and closure of the first former nuclear weapons facility in the United States      Last month the Government Accountability Office  GAO  notified Senator Allard that it has started conducting an assessment of the cleanup project at the Rocky Flats Environmental Technology site  a former nuclear production facility  Senator Allard requested that the GAO perform such an assessment in November  2004  The GAO has previously conducted assessments of the Rocky Flats closure project in 1999 and 2001 at Senator Allard  s request  Since his election to the U S  Senate in 1996  Senator Allard has worked to successfully roll back the target date for the cleanup to be completed from 2070 to 2006                               April 2005 Press Releases
