                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Introduces Rural Colorado Water Infrastructure Bill Legislation would authorize  50 million for water  wastewater projects for Colorado         June 9th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado   member of the Senate Interior Appropriations Subcommittee  on Thursday announced that he has secured  650 000 in State and Tribal Assistance Grant  STAG  funding for the City of Alamosa  s water system in the FY 2006 Interior Appropriations bill     The City of Alamosa is facing a huge financial crisis as it tries to comply with the stringent new water quality standards that have recently been imposed by the EPA     Senator Allard said     I  ve worked hard for more than a year to secure this funding so that we can reduce the burden on the city as they upgrade their water system      The EPA recently lowered its acceptable contaminant levels for arsenic from 50 micrograms per liter to 10 micrograms per liter  and has set a deadline for compliance of January 2006   This change has forced the City of Alamosa to make expensive upgrades to its drinking water system to comply with the new rule     I will continue to my efforts in the Senate to make sure that rural communities like Alamosa receive the federal support that they need as they try to comply with federal water quality regulations  and I will be working hard to make sure that the Interior Appropriations bill will be approved by the full Senate as soon as possible     added Senator Allard                              June 2005 Press Releases
