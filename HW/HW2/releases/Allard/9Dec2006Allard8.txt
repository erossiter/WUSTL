                                                                                                                                                                                                                   ALLARD ANNOUNCES SENATE PASSAGE OF TEMPORARY FIX FOR PHYSICIAN REIMBURSEMENTS          December 9th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced that the U S  Senate has avoided a decrease in physician reimbursements by passing a provision in the tax extender package on Friday    Without passage of this provision  physicians would take a 5 1 percent reduction in payments for treating Medicare patients beginning January 1  According to the American Medical Association  a reduction of this size could potentially limit access to health care for seniors as physicians are forced to stop providing care to Medicare patients     Many Colorado physicians have approached me and asked for a solution to the physician reimbursement problem     said Allard    This provision provides a temporary fix through the end of 2007  however  I am committed to finding a long term solution next Congress       The physician reimbursement provision in the tax extender package will avoid a cut to physician payments for 2007  and allow Congress and the Administration additional time to determine an appropriate permanent solution   The bill also passed the U S  House of Representatives Friday and is now headed to the president for his signature                              December 2006 Press Releases
