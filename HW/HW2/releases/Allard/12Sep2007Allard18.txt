          ALLARD SECURES  142 MILLION FOR DENVER TRANSPORTATION PROJECTS  Transportation Bill Includes  70 Million for the New West Corridor Light Rail Line         September 12th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo    a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Transportation and Housing and Urban Development  announced today that he has secured  142 million for Denver projects as part of the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill  The Senate approved the measure 88 7     These funds will complete the federal funding for the T REX project and allow us to begin construction on the new West Corridor light rail line     said Allard    The additional funding will go toward improving access to the Fitzsimmons campus in Aurora and completion of the Wadsworth and Highway 128 interchange in Broomfield          Once again  Senator Allard has capably and effectively represented the needs of this district on a project that has and continues to serve as a model for transportation projects throughout the United States     said Cal Marsella  General Manager  Regional Transportation District    The federal funding in this bill includes      70 million for the new West Corridor light rail project line      70 million to complete federal funding for the T REX project      1 million for the intersection at I 225 and Colfax Avenue and 17th to improve access to the Fitzsimmons redevelopment project  The funding will help preliminary engineering and right of way acquisition to assist the interchange construction      1 million for the Wadsworth   state Highway 128 interchange in Broomfield  Colorado to relieve peak hour congestion along 120th Avenue  state Highway 128 and the interchange at US 36    Allard said the funding was approved today by the Senate and will be reviewed by a conference committee with the U S  House of Representatives before heading to the President for his signature
