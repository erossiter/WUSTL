                                                                                                                                                                                                                   U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD COSPONSORS BILL TO TELEVISE FEDERAL COURT PROCEEDINGS Bipartisan Sunshine in the Courtroom Act of 2007 Would Allow Media Coverage of Federal Court Proceedings         February 20th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   recently co sponsored S 352  the Sunshine in the Courtroom Act of 2007  to bring more openness to the court system by permitting judges at all court levels to allow the television or radio broadcasts of court proceedings     This bipartisan bill would grant the public greater transparency in the judicial process     said Allard    Allowing media coverage of federal court proceedings will give people a better idea of how the courtroom works       The bill Allard co sponsored would give judges at all court levels the ability to choose whether the news media could cover a trial  including Supreme Court justices  The bill allows the presiding justice to permit the photographing  recording  broadcasting or televising of a trial unless it would interfere with due process   This bill is similar to legislation that Allard also co sponsored in the last Congress in April 2005  but now includes due process protections and prohibits the televising of jurors   The Sunshine in the Courtroom Act of 2007 was referred to the U S  Senate Committee on the Judiciary  which held a hearing on the bill last week                               February 2007 Press Releases
