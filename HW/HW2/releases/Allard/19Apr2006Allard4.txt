                                                                                                                                                                                                                   SENATOR ALLARD ANNOUNCES USDA TO ALLOW GRAZING ON CRP LAND IN BACA COUNTY Decision will help ranchers survive drought         April 19th  2006   SPRINGFIELD  COLORADO    U S  Senator   R Colorado  announced Wednesday that the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  has decided to allow emergency grazing in Baca County  Colorado on land that is part of the Conservation Reserve Program  CRP       I am very pleased the USDA has authorized emergency grazing on CRP lands in Baca County  That part of Colorado is still dealing with a multi year drought  which has had far reaching  negative effects on nearly the entire state     Senator Allard said     Today  s announcement will give ranchers in Baca County the option to graze their animals on CRP land to get them through this continuing drought     Senator Allard added    CRP participants voluntarily enroll highly erodible and other fragile cropland in CRP through contracts of from 10 to 15 years duration  Participants receive annual rental payments and payment of up to 50 percent of the cost of establishing conservation covers    Generally  CRP acreage may not be hayed or grazed unless it is authorized by the USDA to improve the quality of habitat  or to provide emergency relief to livestock producers in the event of natural disasters such as drought    The decision announced today does not permit haying on CRP lands  only grazing  The emergency grazing is authorized through July 1  2006                              April 2006 Press Releases
