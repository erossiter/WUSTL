          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD ASKS FTA TO PROVIDE MORE FUNDING TO T REX AND WEST CORRIDOR PROJECTS Senate Approves Fiscal Year 2007 Funding for Federal Transit Administration         February 15th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   sent a letter today to Federal Transit Administration  FTA  Administrator James Simpson requesting that the agency provide  106 million in funding for the Southeast Corridor Light Rail Transit project  commonly known as T REX  and  35 million in funding for the West Corridor Light Rail Transit project     It is clear that  after fully funding all of the projects outlined in the President  s FY 2007 budget  the FTA will have enough money and flexibility in FY 2007 to decide between providing   catch up   funding for projects like T REX or providing additional funding to projects that are much earlier in the development cycle     Allard wrote to Simpson    The FTA  s commitment to fund T REX on time should match the project sponsor  s commitment in staying on time and on budget      Allard  a member of the Appropriations Committee  requested that the FTA provide  80 million plus an additional  26 million in funding for T REX from its discretionary spending account  for a total of  106 million in funding because of a funding shortfall in meeting the terms of the full funding grant agreement during the first few years of the project  Allard requested the funding for T REX because the project has been very successful and has opened on time and under budget   In addition  Allard requested that the FTA provide  35 million for the West Corridor project  The president  s budget request and the Fiscal Year 2007 U S  Senate and U S  House of Representatives Appropriations bills indicated this was the appropriate level of funding for the project this year   The Senate approved the Continuing Resolution bill on Wednesday  which provided  463 5 billion in domestic and foreign aid spending for Fiscal Year 2007  ending on September 30  The bill already passed the House of Representatives and now goes to the president to be signed into law                              February 2007 Press Releases
