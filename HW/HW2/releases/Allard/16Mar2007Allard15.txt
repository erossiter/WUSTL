          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD PUSHES COLORADO WATER PROJECTS IN WATER RESOURCES DEVELOPMENT ACT          March 16th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   sent a letter this week to Senate Environment and Public Works  EPW  Committee Chairman Barbara Boxer of California and Ranking Member James Inhofe of Oklahoma requesting that two water projects in Colorado be included in the Water Resources Development Act  WRDA  of 2007      Two important projects were included for the state of Colorado in last year  s Senate passed bill and I would like to request that those projects remain in the bill that will be considered by the Senate this year     Allard wrote in the letter   The first project is an authorization of  5 million for a selenium study in the state of Colorado  which would allow state resource officials to work with the U S  Army Corps of Engineers to conduct regional and watershed wide studies to develop plans on how to deal with the selenium concentrations in the state   Allard also requested an authorization for a second project that is an environmental restoration project in the Denver County Reach of the South Platte River through Denver  The city and county of Denver have already done extensive work to restore this area and Army Corps involvement would be very helpful and complimentary to work already completed or in progress  The federal share included in last year  s bill was  12 2 million and authorized the use of in kind contributions to meet the non federal share of the cost     As a former member of the EPW Committee I am very familiar with the WRDA reauthorization process and I am cautiously optimistic that we can move this important bill forward soon     said Allard    Allard will continue to work with Chairman Boxer and Ranking Member Inhofe to ensure these two water projects for Colorado are included in the committee  s bill  He will also work with his colleagues in the U S  House of Representatives to protect Colorado projects in the final product passed by Congress                               March 2007 Press Releases
