                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Asks Secretary of the Army for Full Investigation into Recruitment Tactics Scandal Says practices are wrong  contrary to the Army s core values of duty  honor and country         May 13th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  has requested that Secretary of the Army Francis Harvey initiate an investigation into reports of questionable recruiting tactics being used by Army recruiters in Colorado     I am gravely concerned about how these alleged activities could have been allowed to take place  the possible illegality of these activities  and the degree to which those responsible will be held accountable for their actions     the Senator  s letter said    Senator Allard said that alleged recruiting practices such as encouraging individuals to falsify documents and coaching them on how to defeat the U S  Army  s drug testing program are    Simply unacceptable  I know recruiters have a difficult job  but there are standards and rules under which they  re supposed to operate  According to these reports  the rules weren  t just bent  they were broken and tossed away        These kinds of unprofessional practices are not what we want our military recruiters engaging in    they are wrong  and contrary to the Army  s core values of duty  honor and country     Senator Allard said      I expect the Secretary of the Army to not only investigate these allegations in Colorado  but to explore just how pervasive such practices might be in other locations     he said                              May 2005 Press Releases
