                                                                                                                                                                                                                   U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD ANNOUNCES  3 3 MILLION GRANT FOR MONTROSE SENIORS HOUSING          November 1st  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced today that the Volunteers of America will receive more than  3 3 million from the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  for a new low income senior housing complex in Montrose     This affordable housing for Colorado seniors will help those that have very limited financial resources     said Allard  Chairman of the Banking Subcommittee on Housing and Transportation    I appreciate the Volunteers of America for its effort to provide seniors in Montrose with an affordable place to call home      The  3 358 400 grant will be used to construct a 31 unit  three story building for 30 low income elderly residents and a resident manager  The site is located across the street from the Senior Center and is within two miles of a hospital  grocery store  shopping venues  churches and the library   In addition  the town sponsors a senior shuttle that serves the elderly in town  This service will allow the residents to take part in a wide variety of activities   This federal funding was acquired through HUD  s Section 202 Program  which provides grants to non profits to acquire  build or rehabilitate rental properties for senior citizens                              November 2006 Press Releases
