                                                                                                                                                                                                                   ALLARD CHAIRS FINAL LEGISLATIVE BRANCH COMMITTEE HEARING Hopes Next Chairman will Continue the Practice of Regular Hearings          November 15th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   chaired his 15th and final hearing as Chairman of the Legislative Branch Appropriations Subcommittee today  continuing to monitor and provide oversight to the most significant expansion of the U S  Capitol Building since the 1850  s   The Capitol Visitors Center project is now 88 percent complete and some progress has been made in the critical area of the fire protection system  one of the most significant challenges of the project at this point      Allard has advocated for continued oversight would help the project stay on schedule saying    It is my hope that the next chairman continues the practice of regular hearings until the project  s completion  requiring a full reporting of progress  an assessment of milestones  and accountability for project results by the Architect and his contractors                While the Architect of the Capitol recently submitted an action plan for getting the project on track  which Allard requested in a September hearing  it is not clear whether it is sufficient to complete the project by the end of 2007 and without breaching  600 million in total costs   Allard concluded the hearing by reiterating that his hope that the next Chairman of the Subcommittee will continue the practice of regular hearings and that oversight of this project is a critical function of the Subcommittee at this juncture                              November 2006 Press Releases
