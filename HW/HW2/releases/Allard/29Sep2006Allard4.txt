          GREELEY ICE OFFICE STUDY INCLUDED IN HOMELAND SECURITY APPROPRIATIONS BILL          September 29th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced that the final Homeland Security Appropriations Bill passed on Friday includes his amendment requiring the U S  Department of Homeland Security to determine the cost and evaluate the need for an U S  Immigrations and Customs Enforcement  ICE  office in Greeley  Colo     I  m pleased my amendment requiring a cost evaluation for a new Greeley ICE office was included in the final Homeland Security Appropriations Bill     said Allard  a member of the Homeland Security Appropriations Subcommittee    I look forward to working with ICE and to get necessary resources in place to ensure that our immigration laws are enforced in Colorado      In July  Allard successfully attached an amendment to the Senate Homeland Security Appropriations Bill to require the Assistant Secretary for ICE to submit a report to Congress on the costs and need for establishing a sub office in Greeley  Allard  s amendment was approved unanimously by the U S  Senate and remained in the final version produced by a House Senate Conference Committee   Allard introduced the amendment after consultation with Weld County District Attorney Kenneth R  Buck and local law enforcement officials who have petitioned the Denver ICE office to put a sub office in Greeley     We are pleased that the bill passed     said Buck    Illegal immigration is an important issue  and the fact that a study will now be done regarding an ICE office in Greeley is a step in the right direction  Senator Allard has worked tirelessly for the people of Weld County  and we appreciate that        I plan on meeting with ICE officials in October to determine a time frame for the cost report  to ensure we can get the funding necessary from Congress to open the sub office as soon as possible     said Allard   The Department of Homeland Security Appropriations bill funds all operations of the Department of Homeland Security  including Immigration and Customs Enforcement  ICE   Customs and Border Protection and Citizenship and Immigration Services  The U S  House of Representatives has already passed the conference report and the bill now moves to the president for his signature                              September 2006 Press
