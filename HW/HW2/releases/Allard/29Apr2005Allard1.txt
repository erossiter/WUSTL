                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senate Passes Bill to Strengthen Federal Anti Cockfighting Legislation Amplifies animal fighting regulations  prohibits transport of cockfighting knives across state lines         April 29th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     The U S  Senate on Thursday approved legislation co sponsored by U S  Senator   R Colorado  that provides additional clarification to animal fighting regulations by making it illegal to transport the knives and gaffs used in cockfighting in interstate commerce     It also clarifies that crossing the borders of a tribal reservation constitutes  crossing a state line  with regard to enforcement of the Animal Welfare Act  Finally  it increases penalties for violations of this law       As a veterinarian  I view cockfighting as an inherently cruel  barbaric and inhumane practice     Senator Allard said     This legislation gives law enforcement officials even greater leverage to enforce current state and federal laws that prohibit this activity      The legislation amplifies an earlier bill that Senator Allard successfully added to the 2002 Farm Bill that removed a loophole in the Animal Welfare Act exempting birds from a prohibition on interstate movement of animals for the purpose of fighting     This important provision has gone a long way toward assisting state and local officials enforce the laws                               April 2005 Press Releases
