          SENATOR ALLARD PUSHING TO PRESERVE JOBS  AT NATIONAL RENEWABLE ENERGY LABORATORY           December 20th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado   member of the Senate Energy and Water Appropriations Subcommittee  said Tuesday he has been working diligently for the past several weeks to minimize any possible job cuts at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory  NREL  in Golden     I have had numerous talks with high level Energy Department officials  as recently as Friday evening  to keep any job losses as low as possible     said Senator Allard  who is co chair of the Senate Renewable Energy and Energy Efficiency Caucus    I have been getting the message out about the importance of NREL research programs to our country  our economy and our energy independence        I continue to work with the Energy Department to address this challenge     Senator Allard said      This situation is the result of a very disappointing trend in Congress  using the earmark process to take funding away from the critical research programs NREL conducts to pay for pet projects that are only distantly related    or not related at all    to renewable energy research     Senator Allard said    Exhibit A would be  Senate Minority Leader  Senator  Harry  Reid  s  D Nevada  earmarks that took  33 55 million from NREL  s budget  or about a fifth of its funding  and dedicated it instead to projects in his state        Instead of being sucked dry with earmarks  NREL  s funding for the research it conducts that will lead to energy independence needs to be accelerated     Senator Allard said     NREL  s research must and will go on  I secured  10 515 million in 2006 for a new science and technology facility at NREL  where the laboratory will expand its research into hydrogen fuel cells  advance solar technologies and other future energy sources     Senator Allard said                               December 2005 Press Releases
