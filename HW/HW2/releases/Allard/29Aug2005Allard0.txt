          Senator Allard Announces Homeland Security Funding for Colorado Fire Departments More than  1 million in grants awarded         August 29th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R CO   a member of the Homeland Security Appropriations Subcommittee  announced on Monday that nine Colorado fire departments were selected to receive grants through the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  AFGP  administered by the Department of Homeland Security  DHS        I am pleased that the Department of Homeland Security has recognized the needs of these fire departments  and the important role they play in our homeland security    Senator Allard said     These grants will have a positive impact on the safety of Coloradoans by providing funding in key areas like firefighter training  vehicle purchase and public awareness programs       The DHS provided the following grants to fire departments in the Denver metro area    177 468 to the Denver Fire Department to improve operations and safety  237 500 to the Skyline Fire Department to purchase a new firefighting vehicle  8 160 to Rampart Search and Rescue in Northglenn to improve operations and safety  235 640 to South Metro Fire Rescue to improve operations and safety  68 305 to the Delta County Fire Protection District  2 to purchase a new firefighting vehicle  54 758 to the Volunteer Fire Department of Big Elk to improve operations and safety  84 041 to the Glen Haven Area Volunteer Fire Department to improve operations and safety  159 125 to the Poudre Canyon Fire Protection District to purchase a new firefighting vehicle  132 383 to the Franktown Fire Protection District to improve operations and safety    Our emergency responders work hard every day to protect public safety     Senator Allard said     Since they form the front line of protection for America  s citizens  it  s key that emergency responders receive federal support where they need it       The AFGP provides funding in three categories  Operations and Firefighter Safety  which provides funding for training  equipment  personal protective equipment and modifications to fire stations and facilities  Fire Prevention  which provides funding for public education and awareness efforts  fire code enforcement  purchase and installation of smoke alarms and wildland fire mitigation  and Vehicle Acquisition  which funds the purchase of rescue vehicles  ambulances  pumpers  tankers and other firefighting vehicles                               August 2005 Press Releases
