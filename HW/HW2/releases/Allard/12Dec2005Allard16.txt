          SENATOR ALLARD HAILS REOPENING OF JAPANESE MARKET TO U S  PRODUCED BEEF          December 12th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  said that Japan  s decision to re open its market to American beef is great news for the American economy after Secretary of Agriculture Mike Johanns made the announcement Monday     This is great news for our export economy and Americas for beef producers   I am appreciative of the leadership displayed by the Japanese Government in making this decision     Senator Allard said      I have been working with my colleagues here in the Senate on this issue for nearly two years now  it is good to see that our work with Japan is paying off        The Japanese government has formally acknowledged what I have been saying for nearly two years now  and what American consumers have known all along   That beef produced in the United States is safe and of the highest quality     Senator Allard said    For U S  beef producers and exporters  and for Japanese consumers  this means a very happy New Year      The United States has been prohibited from exporting beef to Japan since December of 2003  when the Japanese forbid the importation of U S  beef following the discovery of a single case of Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy  BSE   or Mad Cow Disease  here in the United States  Before the border closure  Japan was largest importer of American Beef with imports totaling  1 7 billion in 2003     I am also grateful for the work of Secretary of Agriculture Mike Johanns  Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice  U S  Trade Representative Ambassador Rob Portman and the administration for their diligence in seeking a resolution of this issue     Senator Allard said   During the ban  Senator Allard has been one of the most outspoken supporters of beef produced in the United States  and repeatedly pressed the Japanese to reconsider their merit less border closure   Earlier this year he organized a bipartisan coalition of 14 Senators who met with Japanese Ambassador Ryozo Kato to discuss the border closure   In addition  Senator Allard encouraged President Bush and Secretary of State Rice to make the reopening of the Japanese boarder one of their top priorities  and  in a bold move  was an original sponsor of legislation that would place tariffs on Japanese products in response to their erroneous boarder closure   Under the agreement announced Monday  the United States will be able to again export beef from cattle 20 months of age and younger to Japan                              December 2005 Press Releases
