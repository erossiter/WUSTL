                                                                                                                                                                                                                   ALLARD INTRODUCES LEGISLATION TO ADDRESS HOMELESSNESS Colorado Lawmaker Sponsors Bipartisan Community Partnership to End Homelessness Act          May 25th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sens    R Colo   and Jack Reed  D RI  today introduced the bipartisan   The legislation will consolidate and streamline the existing homeless assistance grant programs at the U S  Dept  of Housing and Urban Development into a single flexible  outcome based program     We need to bring more accountability to homeless assistance  increasing funding for successful programs and initiatives and replacing those that are ineffective     said Allard    The Community Partnership to End Homelessness Act is an important step toward ending and preventing homelessness in communities across Colorado and America and will help promote innovate  outcome based approaches to addressing this important issue         Denver  s Road Home project to end homelessness is an excellent example of a creative  collaborate local decision making approach that will result in benefits for the homeless from a single  flexible program     said Allard    Consolidation of programs reduces the administrative burden on communities caused by different program requirements and allows funding to be used for an array of eligible activities  maximizing flexibility and creativity  It is my hope that this legislation will help us all make strides toward ensuring that everyone can live a life off the streets and in the safety of adequate housing                                 May 2007 Press Releases
