          SENATOR ALLARD SECURED OVER  111 MILLION FROM KEY MILITRAY CONSTRUCTION APPROPRIATIONS PANEL FOR FORT CARSON  COLORADO SPRINGS          July 18th  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator   R Colorado  has secured  50 1 million for improvements at Fort Carson  and an additional  61 8 million for other Colorado Springs installations in a 2007 military construction funding bill approved Tuesday by the Military Construction Appropriations Subcommittee  of which he is a member     I will continue to fight for the military community in the Colorado Springs area  and I will ensure their facilities receive the adequate funding to achieve success     said Senator Allard  a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee     These numbers only reflect the high priority that our military installations in Southern Colorado have been given by the Appropriations Committee   I am confident that these projects will remain funded through the rest of the legislative session  and I  ll work hard to make sure that happens     said Senator Allard  a member of the Senator Appropriations Committee   New funding for Fort Carson improvements approved by the subcommittee includes         24 million for phase 2 of the airfield arrival departure complex        26 1 million for the Special Operations Complex   Other Colorado Springs area military construction funding provided by the bill includes         41 8 million for further construction at the Pueblo Chemical Weapons Depot        21 million for the Space Test and Evaluation Facility at Schriever Air Force Base
