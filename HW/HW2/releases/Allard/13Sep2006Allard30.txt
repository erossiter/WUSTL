                                                                                                                                                                                                                   U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD ANNOUNCES COMMITTEE PASSAGE OF GOOD SAMARITANS BILL          September 13th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen   announced today that the U S  Senate Committee on Environment and Public Works  EPW  passed the Cleanup of Inactive and Abandoned Mines Act  known as the Good Samaritan bill  The legislation was drafted and introduced by Allard and Colorado U S  Sen  Ken Salazar     This is the biggest step for the Good Samaritan bill in nearly a decade  Our legislation will allow these contaminated  abandoned mines to be cleaned up free of taxpayers   expense     said Allard    This legislation will help to clean up thousands of mine that otherwise would continue to pollute our water and blemish our western landscape      The passage of Senators Allard and Salazar  s legislation is the culmination of more than two years of hard work by the two lawmakers on addressing the issue of cleaning up abandoned mines throughout the Rocky Mountain West     Anyone who has driven westward up the I 70 corridor in Colorado from Denver  or on many other mountain roads throughout the Rocky Mountain West  has seen the impact of abandoned mines on the landscape     Allard said    Our legislation will allow these contaminated  abandoned mines to be cleaned up  This legislation has been years in the making and has involved the environmental community  industry and government      The Good Samaritan bill will now move to the full U S  Senate for a vote                              September 2006 Press Releases
