                                                                                                                                                                                                                   U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD SPONSORS BILL TO ALLOW ELK HUNTING IN ROCKY MOUNTAIN NATIONAL PARK          March 19th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   today introduced a bill in the U S  Senate that would allow hunters to help reduce the elk population in Rocky Mountain National Park  The current population of elk is around 3 000  is approximately three times higher than the recommended elk population for the Park     The Colorado Wildlife Commission recently recommended allowing the public hunting of elk to help control the population     said Allard    This bill would save the park the up to  18 million that would be needed to reduce the elk population  while generating revenue for the Colorado Division of Wildlife through hunting licenses      Allard  s bill would require the U S  Department of Interior Secretary to consult with the Colorado Division of Wildlife regarding guidelines for hunting  require a resident big game hunting license and limit elk hunting in compliance with the Elk Management Plan   While hunting is usually prohibited in national parks  there are exceptions  For example  white tailed deer  black bear and ruffed grouse can be hunted in the Apostle Islands National Lakeshore in Wisconsin  which is operated by the National Park Service   U S  Rep  Mark Udall has introduced similar legislation in the U S  House of Representatives in February                              March 2007 Press Releases
