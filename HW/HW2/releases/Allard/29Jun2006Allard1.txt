                                                                                                                                                                                                                   ALLARD ENSURES ASSISTANCE FOR SMALL COMMUNITIES STRUGGLING TO MEET WATER QUALITY STANDARDS Dollars will Fund Local Assistance and Research         June 29th  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator   R CO   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced he was successful in helping get a provision included in the Interior Appropriations bill that will provide  5 million for the Environmental Protection Agency to award competitive grants to small water treatment facilities that are working to address concerns such as arsenic levels     Too many small towns are suffering under the burden of huge fines for not meeting certain water quality standards     said Senator Allard     I believe it is a far better use of a town  s funds to spend them updating their treatment facilities  rather than spending them paying fines to the federal government        Fines are not solutions and do not solve water quality problems  investments in new water treatment technology will   These grants will help put towns on the path toward those new technologies that improve their water quality     Allard said   The provision will also provide  5 million for the EPA to conduct research to develop alternative treatment technologies that are cost effective for small systems                              June 2006 Press Releases
