                                                                                                                                                                                                                   ALLARD REJECTS SENATE AMENDMENTS TO WITHDRAW TROOPS FROM IRAQ          June 22nd  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator   R CO  voted no on two Senate amendments to the Defense Authorization Bill Thursday that would have called for withdrawal of U S  troops in Iraq   Both amendments were defeated by the Senate   The Kerry Feingold amendment was defeated 86 to 13 and the Levin Reed Salazar amendment was also defeated 60 to 39       I remain opposed to politically driven timelines or deadlines for the withdrawal of U S  troops from Iraq     said Senator Allard     Deadlines should be set by U S  Military officials in consultation with Iraqi leaders    not politicians in Washington D C         We also need to think about the vacuum a premature withdrawal would create  and how that would impact Iraq and their neighbors     Allard said     This in not a decision we should make unilaterally           What we need to be doing is commending our troops and supporting them in their mission to defeat the terrorists     added Allard     I  ve recently visited with troops from the 3rd Armored Cavalry division at Fort Carson who had just returned from their second tour of duty in Iraq  They told me about the great progress we have made in Iraq and how important it is that they be allowed to finish the job     Allard said     Our nation will never be safe if we give up the fight and surrender the battlefield to the enemy     concluded Allard                              June 2006 Press Releases
