          Senator Allard Gets  26 Million Added to Supplemental Spending bill for New Fort Carson Barracks          April 6th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  announced Wednesday that the Senate Appropriations Committee has added  26 million to the FY 2005 Supplemental Appropriations bill it has approved for the construction of new barracks at Fort Carson     These new barracks are being built to house Army Reserve and other units when they are sent to Fort Carson     said Senator Allard  a member of the Appropriations Committee    It is important to provide these soldiers  who are going to be putting their lives on the line for the country  with up to date  technologically advanced facilities they can use for training and preparation for deployment      This funding is being provided in addition to the  161 million worth of funding for military construction projects in Colorado the Congress approved as part of the FY 2005 military construction appropriations bill  which included for Fort Carson  12 4 million for arrival departure air control group   14 1 million for a barracks complex in the hospital area   33 million for a digital multi purpose training range   3 65 million for a multi purpose machine gun range   3 2 million automated qualification training range     We have an obligation to make sure that our men and women in uniform have the resources they need to train and conduct their missions effectively     Senator Allard said    I am pleased to have been able to ensure that the funding for this project was included in the bill                                  April 2005 Press Releases
