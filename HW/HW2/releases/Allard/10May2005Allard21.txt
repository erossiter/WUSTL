          Senator Allard  Senator Sessions Introduce Legislation to Put Permanent End to Death Tax          May 10th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C  B U S  Senator   R Colorado  and Senator Jeff Sessions  R Alabama  Tuesday unveiled legislation they are co sponsoring that would permanently and immediately do away with the death tax     Under current law  the estate tax will be fully repealed in 2010  But it will be back in full force in 2011 if Congress does not act     Senator Allard said    Our bill will allow thousands of families to be spared the economic hardship of the death tax    an unfair levy that taxes a person  s estate when they pass on  regardless of the fact that they have already paid taxes on the property and income at least once        The death tax discourages savings and investment  sending a message that it  s better to consume today than invest and make more money in the future  It undermines job creation and stifles economic growth  The death tax is a dead weight on our economy     Senator Allard said   The senators   legislation will make the death tax repeal permanent  retroactive to January 1  2005   The Economic Growth and Tax Relief Reconciliation Act  EGTRRA  of 2001 increased the amount that taxpayers can exempt from estate and gift taxes and slowly reduced the rate over the period 2002 through 2009     This relief was a start  but it did not go far enough     Senator Allard said    Today farmers and small businesses continue to suffer economic hardship under the phase out schedule created under EGTRRA        I look forward to working with my colleagues in the Senate to see that our entrepreneurs are rewarded    not penalized   for their initiative and determination     Senator Allard said                              May 2005 Press
