          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD VOTES FOR SENATE MILITARY TRIBUNAL BILL          September 28th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   voted in support of the Military Commissions Act of 2006  which would create military commissions to try war criminals and preserve the terrorist interrogation program      I voted in support of the compromise between the Bush Administration and Senators McCain  Warner and Graham     said Allard    This compromise still allows us to protect our sources and information gathered on terrorists while maintaining the proper balance of ensuring that those we capture are treated in a fair manner      This bill provides for the creation of military commissions and the procedures governing them to try enemy combatants for violations of the law of war  Specifically  the bill provides that the military commissions include commissioned officers  with at least five members per commission  and a minimum of 12 members in a death penalty case  The bill also ensures that no statement obtained by torture will be admissible in a military commission  These commissions will provide the necessary forum to effectively prosecute and bring terrorist war criminals to justice   This legislation also allows for the continuation of the Central Intelligence Agency  s terrorist interrogation program  In addition  the bill protects U S  personnel from prosecution for vague interpretations of the Geneva Conventions by amending the War Crimes Act with a list of   grave breaches    of Common Article 3 of the Geneva Conventions
