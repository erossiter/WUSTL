          ALLARD SECURES  1 6 MILLION FOR I 76 FROM FORT MORGAN TO NEBRASKA STATE LINE Allard secures funding for badly needed project          November 18th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  announced Friday he has secured  1 6 million for improvements to I 76 from Fort Morgan to the Nebraska state line in the 2006 Transportation Appropriations Bill approved by a House Senate conference committee Friday  Senator Allard is a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee that put together the bill  Colorado is to receive  23 85 million in highway funding for specific projects     This is great news for every Northeastern Coloradoan  Once these construction and improvement projects are complete  I 76 will be safer and smoother     Senator Allard said    I requested specific funding for this project because it is a high priority item for the Colorado Department of Transportation  Work will now be able to proceed on schedule      Also provided with funding for FY 2006 were    Highway 56 and Quebec interchange     2 8 million  Thornton Grade Separation     500 000  Colorado Boulevard in Denver     2 million  Ports to Plains Corridor     3 million  Garfield County Rio Grand Bike Trail     1 million  U S  287 corridor     2 7 million   Wadsworth State Highway 128     1 5 million  Frisco West Main Street safety study     1 million  Pikes Peak     1 2 million  State Highway 145  Dolores to Stoner     4 8 million  I 25 through Pikes Peak region     1 25 million  West Vail wildlife overpass     500 000   Funding for these specific projects is being provided in addition to Colorado  s share of FY 2006 highway funding under the Safe  Accountable and Efficient Transportation Equity Act  which passed earlier this year  That share is expected to total more than  490 million in 2006                              November 2005 Press Releases
