          SENATOR ALLARD CO SPONSORS LEGISLATION TO OVERHAUL MEDICAL LIABILITY SYSTEM          May 4th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  is an original co sponsor of two pieces of legislation designed to improve Americans   access to health care by overhauling the medical liability system     Across the nation  doctors are closing their practices leaving significant groups of patients without quality health care     Senator Allard said    This trend is the direct result of our out of control liability system and the astronomical cost of medical liability insurance  This has to be fixed      The first bill  Senate Bill 22  the Medical Care Access Protection Act of 2006  would change the medical liability system by allowing awards of up to  750 000 for non economic damages   A patient would be able to recover up to  250 000 from a healthcare provider and up to two healthcare institutions each for a total of  750 000   Patients will still be allowed unlimited economic damages  The bill also guarantees timely resolution of claims by mandating that healthcare lawsuits are filed within three years of the date of injury    It also establishes standards for expert witnesses in medical liability trials and limits attorneys   fees on a sliding scale  Its primary sponsor is Senator John Ensign  R Nevada    The second bill  Senate Bill 23  the Healthy Mothers and Healthy Babies Access to Care Act  provides specifically targeted medical liability reform to improve women  s access to health care services and provides improved medical care by reducing the excessive burden the existing liability system places on the delivery of obstetrical and gynecological services  Its primary sponsors are Senators Rick Santorum  R Pennsylvania  and Judd Gregg  R New Hampshire      We cannot continue with the status quo  Patients   options are dwindling as doctors have been forced to abandon their practices  In some rural areas  residents have no access to medical care at all and have to drive for an hour or more even in the case of an emergency     Senator Allard said    We have to do better than that                                 May 2006 Press
