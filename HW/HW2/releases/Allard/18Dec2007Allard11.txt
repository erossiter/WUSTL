          ALLARD LEGISLATION PASSES SENATE IN MEDICARE PACKAGE Bill to Extend Medicare Cost Contracts         December 18th  2007   Washington  D C    Legislation introduced by U S  Senator   R Colo   to extend the availability of Medicare cost contracts was approved by the U S  Senate today as part of the Medicare  Medicaid and SCHIP Extension Act of 2007  The bill provides alternatives to help rural patients in Colorado in addition to funding increases in reimbursements for physicians under Medicare for six months  Allard has been an outspoken advocate for the protection of rural health care providers who are vital to the healthcare system in Colorado   Also included in the Medicare  Medicaid  and SCHIP package is a year long extension of the current State Children  s Health Insurance Program  SCHIP  program     I am pleased to see that even if Congress could not come to a compromise on a new SCHIP program they were willing to extend the current program to ensure that Colorado  s kids continue to receive care     said Allard   Allard also announced today that his bill to help Medicare beneficiaries in many rural areas and small cities throughout the country was included in the Medicare package that passed the Senate  The bill provides citizens with an affordable  high quality option to the traditional Medicare fee for service plan by extending the option of cost contracts  The bill was cosponsored by U S  Sen  Ken Salazar  D Colo      Many seniors enjoy Medicare health plans  called cost contracts     said Allard    Under this legislation seniors will be able to continue utilizing these valuable health plans  Medicare cost contracts provide Medicare beneficiaries in many rural areas and small cities with an affordable  high quality option to the traditional Medicare fee for service plan and allows them access to physicians in their own communities         I would like to thank Senator  and our congressional delegates for the deferment of the proposed physician fee schedule reduction in payment     said Rick O Connell  CEO of Penrose St  Francis Health Services    Coming from an underserved physician population you have made a difference in our medicare patients  ability to be served by physicians in this community     The Medicare extension of reasonable cost contract plans would extend the sunset date of Medicare cost contracts by two years  from December 31  2007  to December 31  2009  Medicare cost contracts are managed care plans that are reimbursed on a cost basis for providing health services  Cost contracts are one option for Medicare beneficiaries under current law  and these premiums cover Medicare deductibles and additional benefits not covered by basic Medicare  For the costs of a normal Medicare fee for service copayment  seniors with cost contracts can use any Medicare provider  regardless of the provider  s participation in the health plan  s network  This is critical in rural areas where physicians are scarce     Cost contracts have been a bipartisan issue  with bipartisan support in the past     concluded Allard  a member of the U S  Senate Committee on Health  Education  Labor and Pensions    I am pleased to see that this provision which will protect rural Coloradoans access to care has been included in a larger Medicare package                               December 2007 Press Releases
