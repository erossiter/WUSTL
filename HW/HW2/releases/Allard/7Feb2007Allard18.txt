          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD ANNOUNCES NEW VET CENTER IN GRAND JUNCTION New Facility to Open in 2007         February 7th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced that the U S  Department of Veterans Affairs  VA  will open 23 new vet centers around the country  including one in Grand Junction  Colo   to provide readjustment counseling and outreach services to combat veterans     This vet center will help to provide greater care for western Colorado  s increasing veterans population  Grand Junction and Mesa County already have one of the highest percentages of Vets in the state     said Allard    These facilities are an important resource for veterans  especially those returning from Iraq and Afghanistan  and their families      All vet centers are community based facilities that provide counseling on mental health and employment and services related to family issues  education  bereavement and outreach to combat veterans  Vet centers are staffed by small teams of counselors  outreach specialists and other specialists  many of whom are combat veterans themselves   The VA will now work with local officials to determine where and when the vet center will open  The Grand Junction Vet Center is one of 23 new facilities scheduled to open in the next few years nationwide  but one of four scheduled to open in 2007      The vet center program was established by Congress in 1979  Veterans who served in combat are eligible for care at a VA vet center at no cost  The center also serves veterans who were sexually assaulted or harassed while on active duty and the families of service members who die on active duty  The VA maintains 209 veterans centers in all 50 states  the District of Columbia  Guam  Puerto Rico and the U S  Virgin islands
