          ALLARD CALLS FOR BIPARTISAN SUPPORT OF TROOPS IN IRAQ Colorado Lawmaker Says Opponents of U S  Effort to Win the War in Iraq are   Covering Their Ears and Not Listening to the Facts            April 26th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   took to the floor of the U S  Senate today to express his frustration with the political games being played surrounding the emergency troop funding bill that passed the Senate today    Allard has called the measure the   Iraq Surrender Document   and opposed final passage of the Conference Report  It will now head to the President  s desk  President Bush has consistently threatened to veto any funding bill that would set arbitrary deadlines for withdrawal   A portion of Allard  s comments are below     Any realistic discussion of today  s political climate must revolve around the fact that the current Majority is refusing to work in any meaningful way with the Administration and the Minority party   The circus that surrounded Gen  Petraeus   briefing yesterday is surprising  What I found remarkable was that the original instinct of the Speaker of the House and our Senate Majority Leader was to avoid meeting the General here on Capitol Hill  Can you imagine that  The most important issue of our day is Iraq  and the man we unanimously approved to lead our efforts isn  t worth their time to hear from   The only explanation for this is that the disdain felt by the Majority in working with the Minority and the Administration was also extended to working with our military   Of course  once it was clear that there was public outcry in not meeting General Petraeus  they relented  But what was also evident is that there is an effort to avoid actually believing anything the General had to say about the situation on the ground     General Petraeus is not giving us information that has been filtered through some political process  He is giving a factual and sobering account of what is happening block by block in Iraq     Yet the other side of the aisle  with a few exceptions  wants to cover their ears and not listen to the facts  They would rather pretend that they know what is going on in Iraq  rather that hear the truth from General Petraeus  The situation in Iraq is a dynamic and ever changing one  and after yesterday  s briefing is it more clear to me than ever that we must resist arbitrary deadlines to our fight in Iraq  But my Democrat colleagues would rather play politics with our men and women in the field and score a few points for the far left wing of their party  They would rather play politics on the Floor than work to pass meaningful legislation   I ask the Majority Leader and the other side of the aisle to put politics aside and do the right thing    work in a truly bipartisan manner to do what the American people expect us to do    This obstruction and unwillingness to work in a true bi partisan effort to provide funding to our troops who are even now in harm  s way is outrageous and disappointing                                  April 2007 Press Releases
