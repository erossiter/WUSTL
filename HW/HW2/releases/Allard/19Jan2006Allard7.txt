                                                                                                                                                                                                                   SENATOR ALLARD APPLAUDS SINGAPORE DECISION TO RESUME IMPORTS OF U S  BEEF          January 19th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  on Thursday hailed a decision by the government of Singapore to resume the import of U S  produced beef after a 23 month ban     This is another positive step in our push to regain the ground we  ve lost in sales of U S  beef to Asia     Senator Allard said    Today we regained access to another million dollar market  This can only mean good news for U S  producers      In December 2005  the Japanese government announced it was ending its nearly two year ban on U S  beef     We saw Japan as the lynch pin to restoring U S  access to these key Asian markets  Singapore  s announcement today seems to indicate we were right     Senator Allard said   Senator Allard has been one of the most outspoken defenders of beef produced in the United States  and has repeatedly pressed the Japanese to reconsider their two year old decision to close their ports to American beef imports  In February 2005  Senator Allard headed up a bipartisan coalition of 14 Senators who met with Japanese Ambassador Ryozo Kato to discuss the import ban  In addition  he also encouraged President Bush and Secretary of State Rice to make the reopening of Japan to American beef imports one of their top priorities in talks with the Japanese government  He also was an original cosponsor of legislation that would place tariffs on Japanese products in response to the beef import ban  which became moot when Japan resumed importing U S  beef                              January 2006 Press Releases
