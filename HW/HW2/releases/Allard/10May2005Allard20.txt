          Senator Allard s Pueblo Depot Provisions Gain Congressional Passage Language in supplemental bill requires more than  372 million to be spent on cleanup  forbids studies on shipping weapons from current locations         May 10th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C  B The U S  Senate on Tuesday gave final approval  by a vote of 100 to zero  to the FY 2005 Supplemental Appropriations Act that includes provisions added by U S  Senators   R Colorado  and Mitch McConnell  R Kentucky   requiring the Department of Defense  DoD  to spend  372 38 million previously appropriated but not spent for cleaning up chemical weapons stockpiles at the Pueblo Depot in Colorado and the Blue Grass Depot in Kentucky  The House passed the bill Thursday on a vote of 368 to 58  and President Bush is expected to sign it as soon as it reaches his desk this week   The final version of the bill requires the DoD to spend or contract for  100 million worth of work at the sites in no more than 120 days     We have  in effect  engraved this in stone     said Senator Allard  who was a member of the House Senate conference committee that finalized the bill and ensured that the language stayed in the legislation    This supplemental appropriations bill forbids  by law  any consideration of moving the chemical weapons out of Pueblo for disposal        Additionally  it directs the Department of Defense to begin contracting for disposal of the chemical weapons on site     the Senator said    Now that this bill is law  there is no way the department can misconstrue or second guess Congress   intent      The supplemental appropriations legislation also forbids the expenditure of the funding provided for Pueblo and Blue Grass on any other program  and it specifically forbids any expenditure whatsoever  either directly or indirectly  on any study related to the transportation of chemical weapons across state lines     It is my hope that the Pueblo Depot cleanup project will now move forward toward meeting the 2012 Chemical Weapons Convention treaty deadline     Senator Allard said                              May 2005 Press Releases
