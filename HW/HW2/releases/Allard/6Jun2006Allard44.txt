                                                                                                                                                                                                                   ALLARD STATEMENT ON MARRIAGE PROTECTION AMENDMENT DEBATE          June 6th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  made the following statement on Tuesday regarding the Marriage Protection Amendment that is currently being debated on the floor of the U S  Senate      The definition of marriage crosses all bounds of race  religion  culture  political party  ideology and ethnicity     said Senator Allard     It is the root from which families and communities are grown        Marriage is the one bond on which all other bonds are built  and it is under attack     Allard said     Nine states currently face lawsuits challenging their traditional marriage laws  a federal judge in Nebraska struck down a state constitutional amendment approved by 70 percent of voters      Allard said      The Constitution will be amended whether we pass the Marriage Protection Amendment or not  the only question is whether it will be amended through the amendment process or by unelected  activist judges     Allard said                              June 2006 Press Releases
