          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD ASKS ARMY CORPS OF ENGINEERS TO MAKE FOUNTAIN CREEK A PRIORITY Senate Approves Fiscal Year 2007 Funding for Army Corps of Engineers         February 15th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   sent a letter today to the Assistant Secretary for Civil Works at the U S  Army  John Paul Woodley  requesting that the agency continue providing federal funding to help complete the Fountain Creek Watershed study and the continuation of the Chatfield Reservoir Reallocation Environmental Impact Statement  EIS       In the absence of specific Congressional direction in the Fiscal Year 2007 Continuing Resolution  I am hopeful that you will recognize the critical importance of these projects     Allard wrote to Woodley   Allard  a member of the Appropriations Committee  requested that the U S  Army Corps of Engineers provide funding to help complete the Fountain Creek Watershed study  The primary goal of the study is to analyze the watershed from a regional perspective  benefiting all participating governments by identifying solutions to the problems of flooding  extensive erosion and sedimentation and degradation of the watershed  s environmental assets    The remaining amount needed to complete the Fountain Creek Watershed study is  449 000  which is the amount that was included in the U S  Senate version of the Fiscal Year 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations Committee   The Colorado Congressional delegation has been working with the Corps of Engineers  the Colorado Water Conservation Board and the Chatfield Reservoir Reallocation Coalition on efforts to complete the Chatfield EIS so that a Record of Decision can be issued by the Corps of Engineers  The study will help address the great need for the water supply in the South Platte River watershed  and will also examine the potential for reassigning storage in the reservoirs to joint uses  including flood control and conservations and fishery habitat protection and enhancement   The Senate approved the Continuing Resolution bill on Wednesday  which provided  463 5 billion in domestic and foreign aid spending for Fiscal Year 2007  ending on September 30  The bill already passed the House of Representatives and now goes to the president to be signed into law                              February 2007 Press Releases
