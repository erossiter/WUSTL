          ALLARD S RIO GRANDE NATURAL AREA LEGISLATION GAINS FINAL PASSAGE Bill to be Signed into Law by President         September 28th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    s  R Colo   legislation to establish the Rio Grande National Area in southern Colorado cleared its last legislative hurdle Wednesday evening when the U S  House of Representatives passed his bill unanimously     The passage of this legislation is a huge victory for the citizens of the San Luis Valley     said Allard    The Rio Grande Natural Area Act designates a pristine area around the Rio Grande River stretching from Alamosa into New Mexico  With Colorado  s ongoing drought  passage of this bill is more important than ever because it will safeguard our state  s water rights and protect one of the most scenic natural areas in our state      The Natural Area would consist of pristine land encompassing a quarter of a mile on each side of the Rio Grande River extending from the southern boundary of the Alamosa National Wildlife refuge to the New Mexico state line  In addition  the act would prevent a federal water right from being imposed on the protected area     I have been working on this project for several years  My legislation is the result of a collaborative effort among property owners  community members and elected representatives that has incorporated key agricultural and environmental concerns  These disparate groups have joined together to protect the San Luis Valley and Colorado  s unique heritage     Allard said    I am pleased that the U S  House of Representatives agreed with me that this is a very worthy effort      The bill passed the U S  Senate in July 2005  and will now head to the president for his signature  where he is expected to sign it into law next month
