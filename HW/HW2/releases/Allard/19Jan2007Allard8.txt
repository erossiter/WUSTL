          OPENING STATEMENT OF U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD JOINT SENATE APPROPRIATIONS AND HEALTH  EDUCATION  LABOR AND PENSIONS COMMINTTEE HEARING ON STEM CELL RESEARCH          January 19th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C      I would like to thank the Chairmen for holding today  s joint hearing  I think that it is obvious that stem cell research has broad interest  Stem cell research poses important but controversial questions and I am pleased that we will be able to discuss the different aspects of the debate today     I am supportive of stem cell research  Because I have a scientific background  I can understand the promise of stem cell research  I believe it is possible to derive the benefits of stem cell research without utilizing embryonic stem cells     Personally  I have ethical concerns with embryonic stem cell research  I do not support the destruction of embryos for stem cell research  This is especially true when there have been so many advances in the area of adult stem cell research  as well as alternative sources of stem cells such as amniotic fluid and umbilical cord  I will not support federal funding that would drive the destruction of embryos for new stem cell lines  As legislators we must take into account all sides of a debate  including moral and ethical concerns      As a veterinarian with a scientific background  I am compelled to examine theories for future research advancements  Recent reports regarding the possibilities of deriving pluripotent stem cells from amniotic fluid may bridge the gap between embryonic stem cell research and adult stem cell research     I would like to thank the witnesses for being here today  I look forward to partaking in the discussion today and hearing their testimony                                 January 2007
