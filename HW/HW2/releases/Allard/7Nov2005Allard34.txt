                                                                                                                                                                                                                   SENATOR ALLARD SECURES FUNDING FOR NATIONAL RENEWABLE ENERGY LABORATORY IN GOLDEN  10 515 million for new science and technology facility  1 185 billion for overall renewable energy effort         November 7th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado   member of the Senate Energy and Water Appropriations Subcommittee  announced that he has secured  10 515 million for the new science and technology facility at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory  NREL  in Golden Colorado in the final version of the FY 2006 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill  which was approved by a House Senate conference committee Monday     This funding will allow NREL to continue work on a badly needed new science and technology facility     Senator Allard said    Recent events  such as Hurricane Katrina  have made the National Renewable Energy Laboratory  s importance abundantly clear        The search for energy independence needs to be accelerated  and NREL is leading the way in this vital national mission     the Senator said    This new facility will allow NREL to expand its research into hydrogen fuel cells  advanced solar technologies and other energy sources for the future      The FY 2006 Energy and Water Appropriations bill contains  1 185 billion overall for renewable energy  including funding for hydrogen fuel cell research  wind and solar energy and many other renewable technologies  A final vote on the conference version of the legislation is expected this week                              November 2005 Press Releases
