                                                                                                                                                                                                                   SENATOR ALLARD BEGINS 10TH YEAR Colorado Lawmaker upholds commitment to meet regularly with constituents         January 6th  2006   Washington  D C     Consistent with his belief that elected officials should be citizen legislators  U S  Senator  has held more than 670 town meetings across Colorado  This week Senator Allard began his 10th consecutive year of conducting town meetings with gatherings in Denver  Larimer County  Jackson County and Boulder   This commitment dates back to when Senator Allard was initially elected to Congress in 1991  During his time in the Senate  starting in 1997  Allard has held town meetings in each of Colorado s sixty four counties  while maintaining a 99 percent voting record       These meetings have served as an extremely effective conduit allowing me to stay in  touch with issues important to Coloradoans  and to bring those concerns back to Washington to be addressed     Senator Allard said    I want to thank everyone who has taken the time to participate in my town meetings over the years  I look forward to continuing with these informal meetings to better serve the citizens of our great state       Allard is a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee where he is Chairman of the Legislative Branch Subcommittee  the Senate Banking  Housing and Urban Affairs Committee where he is Chairman of the Housing and Transportation Subcommittee  and the Senate Budget Committee  He serves as Deputy Majority Whip and is Chairman of the Senate Renewable Energy and Energy Efficiency Caucus  and the Space Caucus                              January 2006 Press Releases
