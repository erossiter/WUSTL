          SENATOR ALLARD  20 COLLEAGUES INTRODUCE BILL TO IMPOSE TARIFFS ON JAPANESE IMPORTS IN RETALIATION FOR JAPAN  S CONTINUED BAN ON U S  BEEF             October 26th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado   Senator Pat Roberts  R Kansas  and 19 of their Senate colleagues introduced legislation Wednesday that would impose additional tariffs on Japanese imports to the U S  by the end of the year if the Japanese continue to block the import of American beef     Retaliating in a trade dispute by raising tariffs is always a last resort  but I think we have reached that point     Senator Allard said    We have been talking to the Japanese about ending their embargo on American beef for more than a year now  with no results to report  We have no choice but to up the ante        Until December of 2003  Japan was the largest importer of U S  beef  Each passing day of the Japanese embargo strikes another blow against the livelihoods of America  s ranchers and the rural communities where they live     Senator Allard said    Colorado has lost more than  340 million in exports  nearly one third of a billion dollars  since this embargo began      In December of 2003  a single  foreign born  cow tested positive for bovine spongiform encephalopathy  BSE  in the State of Washington  As a result of this finding  Japan immediately closed its markets to U S  beef  Since then  the U S  Department of Agriculture has implemented new  scientifically sound BSE controls that exceed the internationally recognized standards of the World Organization for Animal Health  and has substantially expanded its surveillance program     These new actions complement other BSE controls dating back to 1989   Economic loss to the U S  beef industry is estimated to be  3 14 billion a year because of Japan  s refusal to reopen its markets    In October of 2004  the United States and Japan established a process that was supposed to lead to resumption of trade between the two  The United States has lived up to its part of the bargain  Japan has not     Senator Allard added    Despite all these efforts  Japan remains closed to U S  beef       In January of this year  Senator Allard organized a bipartisan coalition of 14 senators in a meeting with Ambassador Ryozo Kato of Japan to discuss the embargo  He also met with the Taiwan representative to discuss the reopening of its market  On April 16  Taiwan resumed the trade of boneless beef products from cattle under 30 months of age with the United States  Last March  at Senator Allard  s urging  Secretary Rice pressed the Japanese government to lift its embargo on beef imports from the United States during her visit to Japan   The legislation the Senators are sponsoring directs the U S  Trade Representative  USTR  to submit to Congress a certification  by Dec  15  that Japan is no longer prohibiting the importation of beef from the United States  If the certification is not made  the Secretary of Treasury will  by December 31  2005  impose additional tariffs on Japanese articles in an amount equal to  2 7 billion dollars annually  These tariffs would last until the USTR certifies to Congress that Japan is no longer prohibiting the importation of beef from the United States                              October 2005 Press Releases
