                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Secures  2 Million for Wadsworth U S  36 Interchange          July 28th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R CO   Chairman of the Senate Housing and Transportation Subcommittee  has secured  2 million for the Wadsworth U S  36 Interchange in the final version of the transportation authorization bill that emerged from a conference committee on Wednesday   The transportation bill is expected to be approved by the House and Senate by Friday and signed into law by President Bush early next week     Senator Allard was a member of the House Senate Conference Committee that ironed out differences between the U S  House and Senate versions of the transportation legislation and secured a total of  110 million for specific transportation projects      More than three years of hard work and negotiations on the transportation bill have paid off  with  2 million specifically directed to Wadsworth U S  36 Interchange     said Senator Allard     This is great news for every Coloradoan who drives and commutes in the northwest metro area         Colorado  s transportation needs continue to grow     added Senator Allard     As a member of the conference committee  I was pleased to be able to secure  110 million for a long list of critically needed highway projects throughout Colorado                                 July 2005 Press Releases
