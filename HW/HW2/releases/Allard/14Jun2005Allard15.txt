                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Appropriations Subcommittee Approves Allard Request of  400 000 for Tamarisk Eradication in Colorado          June 14th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  announced Tuesday that the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Energy and Water  of which he is a member  has approved  400 000 requested by Senator Allard for tamarisk eradication efforts in Colorado     It is well known throughout the West that the tamarisk poses a major threat to our water supplies     Senator Allard said     The tamarisk has invaded nearly every major river system in Colorado  and this funding will further efforts in the state to eradicate this destructive plant      The tamarisk  also called the salt cedar  has invaded the margins of streams  lakes and wetlands throughout the western United States   An individual plant can consume up to 200 gallons of water per day  which has exacerbated drought conditions in the West  and is highly disruptive to native habitats and species     I have also cosponsored S  177  the Salt Cedar and Russian Olive Control Demonstration Act  which would provide millions to states and public private partnerships to eradicate the tamarisk     added Senator Allard     I will continue working in the U S  Senate to make sure that we do everything we can to exterminate the tamarisk in Colorado and other affected areas across the West      The FY 2006 Energy and Water Appropriations bill will now go to the full Senate for consideration                              June 2005 Press Releases
