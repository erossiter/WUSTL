                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Secures  1 Million for Glenwood Bridge          July 28th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R CO   Chairman of the Senate Housing and Transportation Subcommittee  has secured  1 million for the Glenwood Bridge in Glenwood Springs  Colorado in the final version of the transportation authorization bill that emerged from a conference committee on Wednesday   The transportation bill is expected to be approved by the House and Senate by Friday and signed into law by President Bush early next week     Senator Allard was a member of the House Senate Conference Committee that ironed out differences between the U S  House and Senate versions of the transportation legislation and secured a total of  110 million for specific transportation projects above the statewide funding formula for Colorado     More than three years of hard work and negotiations on the transportation bill have paid off  with  1 million specifically directed to the Glenwood Bridge     said Senator Allard     Glenwood Springs is experiencing major congestion  and the transportation bill will help to alleviate that congestion by funding the Glenwood Bridge project        Colorado  s transportation needs continue to grow     added Senator Allard     As a member of the conference committee  I was pleased to be able to secure  110 million for a long list of critically needed highway projects throughout Colorado                                 July 2005 Press Releases
