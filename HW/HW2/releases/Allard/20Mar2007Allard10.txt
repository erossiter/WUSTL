          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD PUSHES FOR FUNDING TO FINISH FOUNTAIN CREEK WATERSHED STUDY          March 20th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    This week the U S  Army Corps of Engineers informed U S  Sen    R Colo   that they will use  300 000 from its Fiscal Year 2007 funds toward the continuation of the Fountain Creek Watershed study     I am pleased that the Army Corps recognized the importance of this study and plans to direct these funds to it     said Allard  a member of the U S  Senate Committee on Appropriations    While this is not the full amount that I was able to secure in the Senate version of the Energy and Water Appropriations Bill  the fact that the Army Corps chose to use  300 000 for Fountain Creek clearly demonstrates that they are supportive of completing the study      Allard was able to secure  449 000 in the Fiscal Year 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill last Congress for the continuation of the Fountain Creek Watershed study  Because the Continuing Resolution passed this year lacked congressional direction  Allard sent a letter to the Corps of Engineers on February 15 asking that the Corps use as much of the Fiscal Year 2007 funding as possible for this study     As you develop funding priorities for the 2007 fiscal year  I would like to urge you to strongly consider continuing support for the completion of the Fountain Creek Watershed study     Allard wrote in a letter to John Paul Woodley  the assistant secretary for civil works at the Corps of Engineers   An additional  149 000 in federal funding will be needed to complete the study  which Allard will request in this year  s Energy and Water Appropriations Bill on March 30                              March 2007 Press Releases
