          U S SEN  WAYNE ALLARD CRITICIZES PREDATORY LENDING PRACTICES DIRECTED AT ARMED FORCES          September 19th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   participated in last week  s U S  Senate Committee on Banking  Housing and Urban Affairs hearing on   A Review of the Department of Defense  s Report on Predatory Lending Practices Directed at Members of the Armed Forces and Their Dependents     Allard is a member of the Banking Committee      We owe our military personnel a great debt    they have volunteered to risk their lives to protect freedom and democracy  Therefore  it is only reasonable to expect that we would protect them against predatory lending     Allard said at the hearing    Predatory lending is an abhorrent practice  but especially when it takes advantage of our men and women in uniform      Earlier this year  Allard supported the Military Personnel Financial Services Protection Act  which passed the Senate in July  This bill would help prevent predatory practices aimed at those in uniform  During Committee hearings on that bill  it was clear that financial literacy is the key to preventing predatory practices  and the Department of Defense  DOD  has a need to fill regarding financial education     The DOD report includes information on its strategy to educate members of the Armed Forces and their families regarding predatory lending  and programs to reduce or eliminate predatory lending       Unfortunately  too many people today lack basic financial literacy and skills  and military personnel can face the same challenges     Allard said at the hearing    It is important that the Department of Defense help provide financial education that will enable its personnel to make appropriate financial decisions
