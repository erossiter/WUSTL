          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD INTRODUCES BILL TO HELP RURAL CITIZENS ACCESS TO HEALTH CARE SERVICES Allard Bill to Extend Medicare Cost Contracts         January 5th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   on Thursday  the first day of session in the 110th Congress  introduced a bill to help Medicare beneficiaries in many rural areas and small cities throughout the country with an affordable  high quality option to the traditional Medicare fee for service plan  The bill was cosponsored by U S  Sen  Ken Salazar  D Colo      Many American seniors enjoy Medicare health plans  called cost contracts     said Allard    Under legislation I am introducing today  seniors will be able to continue utilizing these valued health plans      The Medicare Cost Contract Extension and Refinement Act of 2007 would extend the sunset date of Medicare cost contracts by five years from December 31  2007  to December 31  2012  Medicare cost contracts provide Medicare beneficiaries in many rural areas and small cities with an affordable  high quality option to the traditional Medicare fee for service plan and allows them access to physicians in their own communities   Medicare cost contracts are managed care plans that are reimbursed on a cost basis for providing health services  Cost contracts are one option for Medicare beneficiaries under current law  and these premiums cover Medicare deductibles and additional benefits not covered by basic Medicare  For the costs of a normal Medicare fee for service copayment  seniors with cost contracts can use any Medicare provider  regardless of the provider  s participation in the health plan  s network  This is critical in rural areas where physicians are scarce     Cost contracts have been a bipartisan issue  with bipartisan support in the past     said Allard  who is a member of the U S  Senate Committee on Health  Education  Labor and Pensions    U S  Sen  Ron Wyden of Oregon worked to get an extension for cost contracts in the 109th Congress and I look forward to working with him again during the 110th                                 January 2007 Press Releases
