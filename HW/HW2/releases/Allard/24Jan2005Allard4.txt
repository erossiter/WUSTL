                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Introduces Bill to Create Rio Grande Natural Area in Southern Coloradl          January 24th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Senator   R Colorado  introduced legislation on Monday that would establish the Rio Grande Natural Area  The Natural Area would consist of pristine land encompassing a quarter of a mile on each side of the Rio Grande River extending from the southern boundary of the Alamosa National Wildlife refuge to the New Mexico state line  In addition  the act would prevent a federal water right from being imposed on the protected area     I think most Coloradans would agree that our state is the most beautiful in the nation  and the water flowing through our borders is its life blood  The passage of the Rio Grande Natural Area Act would designate a pristine area around the Rio Grande River stretching from Alamosa to New Mexico  and would protect our state  s water rights     Senator Allard stated      This legislation brings to fruition a plan that Coloradans have been working on for years     the Senator said    It represents a collaborative effort among property owners  community members and elected representatives that takes into account key agricultural and environmental concerns  These disparate groups have joined together to protect the San Luis Valley and Colorado  s unique heritage      Senator Allard hit the ground running by introducing this legislation on January 24th    the first day Senators were able to submit legislation in the new session of Congress                                January 2005 Press Releases
