          ALLARD FUNDING FOR RESEARCH PROJECTS IN COLORADO IN AGRICULTURE APPROPRIATIONS BILL HEADED FOR PRESIDENT  S DESK          November 3rd  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced that he has obtained  2 2 million for agriculture research and information in Colorado in the final conference version of the FY 2006 Agriculture Appropriations bill approved by the Senate on Thursday  The House approved the legislation on October 28  It now goes to the President  s desk for his signature     Colorado continues to be a center of valuable agricultural research  as well as being one of the pre eminent agricultural production states in the nation     Senator Allard said     This funding represents a significant investment in our agricultural future  not only in Colorado  but in the nation at large     Senator Allard said    I am proud of the quality agricultural research that Coloradans continue to produce        I am also pleased that the final bill includes my amendment directing the U S  Department of Agriculture  s  USDA  Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service to establish baseline national standards for handling chronic wasting disease  CWD      Senator Allard said       CWD continues to be a serious concern in Colorado and throughout our nation  In order to prevent the spread of this disease  and eventually conquer it  it is vital that we have a uniform federal plan  This legislation will help create such a plan        This bill also provides  23 million for several renewable energy programs to be administered by the USDA     Senator Allard said    As chairman of the Senate Renewable Energy and Energy Efficiency Caucus  I am glad to have secured this funding       The FY 2006 Agriculture Appropriations Act approved includes the following funding requested by Senator Allard      306 000 for the Cooperative State Research  Education and Extension Services for the Russian Wheat Aphid Resistance  Stress Tolerance and Quality Enhancement Project  This project is a cooperative program between the States of Colorado  Nebraska and Kansas  The direct value of the Colorado wheat crop is estimated at  260 million annually  but the Russian Wheat Aphid causes direct losses of more than  11 million in Colorado      817 000 for infectious disease research to the Center for Economically Important Infectious Animal Diseases at Colorado State University  This center is the only institution conducting multidisciplinary research focused entirely on animal diseases that either cause  or have the potential to cause  serious economic losses for animal agriculture such as West Nile virus  foot and mouth disease and BSE       880 000 for the National Beef Cattle Evaluation Consortium  comprised of Colorado State University in Fort Collins  Cornell University and the University of Georgia  The project is located at CSU      50 000 to the State of Colorado for cooperative state research  education and extension services for Chronic Wasting Disease research       174 000 for the Livestock Marketing Information Center in Denver
