Contact  Steve WymerEmail             ALLARD ANNOUNCES APPROVAL OF  3 MILLION FOR NEW RAIL CAR SAFETY TESTING          July 13th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo    a member of the Senate Transportation Appropriations Subcommittee  announced today that the Appropriations Committee has approved  3 million for the Federal Railroad Administration in the FY 2008 Transportation  Housing and Urban Development Appropriations Bill to develop new rail car safety standards  including extensive tank car testing to be done at the Transportation Technology Center Incorporated  TTCI  in Pueblo      While current rail tank cars have a strong record of performance in accident situations we must always strive to achieve the next level of safety and security     said Allard    The Next Generation Rail Tank Car Project is a logical progression in rail transportation safety for hazardous materials and will help us discover even better ways to keep important chemicals secure during transportation  There is no place better to do important rail safety work than at Pueblo  s TTCI      Rail is a primary mode of distribution for shipments of chemicals  which makes rail security an important part of the nation  s homeland security plans  TTCI focuses on chemical  biological  radiological  nuclear or explosive incidents and other activities associated with potential terrorist events     Pueblo  s TTCI is the premier location in the country to conduct these types of tests and training     said Allard    Each year an average of 1 700 first responders travel to Pueblo to participate in TTCI  s unique training programs  In its 20 year history  more than 20 000 public   and private sector emergency responders have traveled to Pueblo from all over the world for this training and the economic impact of the work done at the TTCI reverberates throughout southern Colorado       Allard introduced legislation in the 109th and 110th Congresses seeking inclusion of TTCI in the Department of Homeland Security  s National Domestic Preparedness Consortium   Allard worked with U S  Sens  Patty Murray  D Wash   and Debbie Stabenow  D Mich   to secure the funding for the tests
