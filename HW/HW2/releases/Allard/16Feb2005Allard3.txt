                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senate Energy Committee Approves Allard Bill to Create Rio Grande Natural Area in Southern Colorado          February 16th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C    The U S  Senate Energy Committee on Wednesday approved legislation by U S  Senator   R Colorado  that would establish the Rio Grande Natural Area  The Natural Area would consist of pristine land encompassing a quarter of a mile on each side of the Rio Grande River extending from the southern boundary of the Alamosa National Wildlife refuge to the New Mexico state line  In addition  the act would prevent a federal water right from being imposed on the protected area     The Rio Grande Natural Area Act would designate a pristine area around the Rio Grande River stretching from Alamosa to New Mexico  protecting one of the most beautiful natural areas in our state  and our state  s water rights as well     Senator Allard said     Coloradans have been working toward this legislation for years     the Senator said    It represents a collaborative effort among property owners  community members and elected representatives that takes into account key agricultural and environmental concerns  These disparate groups have joined together to protect the San Luis Valley and Colorado  s unique heritage      Senator Allard hit the ground running by introducing this legislation on January 24th    the first day Senators were able to submit legislation in the new session of Congress  U S  Senator Ken Salazar is a cosponsor of the legislation  The bill will next go to the full Senate for approval                               February 2005 Press Releases
