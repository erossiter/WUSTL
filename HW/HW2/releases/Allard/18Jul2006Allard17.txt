          ALLARD RESTORES PUEBLO DEPOT FUNDING CUT BY HOUSE Senate Appropriations Bill Includes  41 8 Million for Construction at Pueblo Depot          July 18th  2006   Washington  D C     U S  Senator   R CO   a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Military Construction  announced today that he has restored  40 million in the Senate version of the Fiscal Year 2008 Military Construction Appropriations bill for the continuation of construction activities in the Assembled Chemical Weapons Alternatives Program   A total of  140 9 million was provided   Of that amount   41 8 million was provided for the Pueblo Chemical Agent Destruction Pilot Plant and  99 1 million for the Blue Grass Chemical Agent Destruction Pilot Plant         I am pleased that my colleagues on the Senate Military Construction Appropriations Subcommittee recognized the importance of this program to the people of Colorado and to the nation     said Senator Allard     Full funding of this program was absolutely necessary if we were to continue to move forward in destroying the hundreds of thousands of aging chemical weapons stored at Pueblo      The House version of the Fiscal Year 2007 Military Construction Appropriations bill reduced funding for the Assembled Chemical Weapons Alternatives program by  40 million       In my view  cutting the funding to this program was irresponsible   It was a pennywise  pound foolish decision     said Senator Allard     Such cuts would set the program back at least a year and add as much as  190 million to the program life cycle costs      The Senate and House are expected to meet to put together a final version of the Fiscal Year 2007 Military Construction Appropriations bill in early September   Senator Allard is expected to be named a conferee for this meeting        It is imperative that we keep this program moving    the safety and well being of the people in Pueblo depend on it     said Senator Allard     You can be assured that I will do everything in my power to protect this funding level as it proceeds through the appropriations process                                    July 2006 Press Releases
