Contact  Steve WymerEmail             ALLARD SECURES FUNDING FOR COLORADO PROJECTS IN LABOR  HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES APPROPRIATIONS BILL          June 19th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo    a member of the Senate  s Appropriations Committee  announced today that  3 million in funding for Colorado will be included in the 2008 Labor  Health and Human Services Appropriations bill  Allard specifically requested  1 2 million of the funding to support higher education programs at several Colorado colleges and universities   Allard said the funding was approved today by the subcommittee  The full committee is expected to approve the funding bill on Thursday     The projects that I included in this bill will help address Colorado  s needs in higher education and other important research and development needs     said Allard    Higher education is the future of our state and I am pleased that my seat on the Appropriations Committee puts me in a position to help secure this important funding for Colorado  I look forward to continuing to work with my colleagues on the Senate and House Appropriations Committees to ensure that these projects remain funded      The following projects for Colorado were included in the bill   Sen  Allard requests        300 000 for the Linda Crnic Institute for Down Syndrome located at the University of Colorado at Denver Health and Sciences Center         200 000 for the Children  s Hospital at the Fitzsimmons Medical Campus in Aurora         200 000 for National Center for Low Incidence Disabilities  located at the University of Northern Colorado in Greeley for the purpose of furthering postsecondary education         200 000 for the Colorado School of Mines  in Golden for the purpose of Mine Safety and Rescue through Sensing Networks and Robotics Technology  Mine SENTRY          150 000 for Fort Lewis College  in Durango to expand postsecondary business education         150 000 for Metro State College  in Denver for the purpose of educational flight simulation technology for the World Indoor Airport Flight Simulation Lab    Other Funded Colorado Projects        100 000 for Mobile Mammography Program in Denver at the Exempla St  Joseph  s Hospital         200 000 for Mercy Regional Medical Center Primary Care   Safety Net    Clinic located in Durango for La Plata County         200 000 for Denver
