                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senate Energy Committee Approves Allard s Tamarisk Control Bill          February 14th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     The U S  Senate Energy Committee has approved legislation cosponsored by U S  Senator   R Colorado  on Wednesday  along with Senator Pete Domenici  R New Mexico  for the control and eradication of the salt cedar plant  also known as the tamarisk   The tamarisk has invaded the margins of streams  lakes and wetlands throughout the Western United States  An individual plant can consume up to 200 gallons of water per day  which has exacerbated drought conditions in the West  and is highly disruptive to native habitats and species     The tamarisk is causing severe problems throughout the West     Senator Allard said     The Energy Committee  s approval of the Salt Cedar Control Demonstration Act marks a significant step in the ongoing effort by Congress to provide critical resources for the removal of this invasive species        Eradication of this unquenchable shrub will save water  lower salinity levels and create a more congenial habitat for the Southwest Willow Flycatcher and a number of other riparian species     Senator Allard said    This is an important element in mitigating the water shortages and environmental damage associated with the tamarisk      The bill will provide millions of dollars to states and public private partnerships to control and eradicate the tamarisk                               February 2005 Press Releases
