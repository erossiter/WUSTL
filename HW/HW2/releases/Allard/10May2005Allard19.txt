          Senator Allard s Funding for New Fort Carson Barracks Approved by Congress          May 10th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     The U S  Senate on Tuesday gave final approval to the FY 2005 Supplemental Appropriations Act that includes a provision added by Senator Allard that provides  26 million for the construction of new barracks at Fort Carson  The Senate approved the measure by a vote of 100 to zero  The House passed the bill last Thursday by a vote of 368 to 58  and President Bush is expected to sign it when it reaches his desk this week     This funding will be used to provide new barracks to house Army Reserve and other units when they are sent to Fort Carson to train before being deployed overseas     said Senator Allard  who  as a member of the House Senate conference committee that finalized the legislation last month  ensured that the Fort Carson funding remained part of the final bill     The most important thing we can provide these soldiers  who are going to be putting their lives on the line for their country  is state of the art facilities they can use for training and preparation for deployment     Senator Allard said    This funding is being provided in addition to the  161 million worth of funding for military construction projects in Colorado the Congress approved as part of the FY 2005 military construction appropriations bill  which included for Fort Carson  12 4 million for arrival departure air control group   14 1 million for a barracks complex in the hospital area   33 million for a digital multi purpose training range   3 65 million for a multi purpose machine gun range   3 2 million automated qualification training range     Congress is making sure that our men and women in uniform get the training and resources they need to carry out their missions as efficiently as possible     Senator Allard said    I am pleased to have been able to ensure that the funding for this project stayed in the final version of this bill                                 May 2005 Press Releases
