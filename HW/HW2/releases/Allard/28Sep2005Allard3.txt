          Senator Allard Endorses Agreement to Allow Betty Dick to Keep House in Rocky Mountain National Park          September 28th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  on Wednesday said he supports a proposed agreement  accepted by the Senate Subcommittee on National Parks  that will allow Rocky Mountain National Park resident Betty Dick to remain on an 8 acre parcel on the property  calling the compromise worked out late Tuesday a   win win    situation     I want to commend Senate Energy and Natural Resources Committee Chairman Pete Domenici and National Parks Subcommittee Chairman Craig Thomas for their commitment and work on getting this issue resolved     Senator Allard said     This is a true win win proposal for everyone involved     Senator Allard said    Mrs  Dick will be allowed to remain in the house  and park visitors will able to enjoy the renewed access to the Colorado River that the agreement provides to the public and the Park Service        Rocky Mountain National Park is one of the great natural treasures of Colorado  and any legislation affecting it is of great interest to me  My office and I have been working to find a non legislative solution to this problem for nearly six years  But in light of the fact that the National Park Service currently does not possess the authority to resolve this issue administratively  I support S  584 as amended  and I will actively work with Senator Salazar to ensure its final passage     Senator Allard said     In addition  in the near future I plan to introduce legislation that will provide the Park Service with the administrative tools necessary to resolve 186 potentially similar disputes in 29 Park Service properties throughout the country without having to resort to private legislation     Senator Allard said                              September 2005 Press Releases
