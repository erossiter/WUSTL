                                                                                                                                                                                                                   ALLARD PANEL TO EXAMINE FUTURE OF FHA          June 20th  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator   R CO   Chairman of the Subcommittee on Housing and Transportation  made the following statement Tuesday in a hearing on Federal Housing Administration  FHA       The housing finance markets have changed a great deal in just the last decade  and FHA  s market share has declined     said Senator Allard     This leads to what I believe is one of the central questions for today  s hearing  what is the role for FHA today and into the future     The answer to this question undoubtedly shapes the kind of reforms that Congress should be considering         It is crucial that we make careful  responsible changes to ensure that FHA is available to homebuyers for years to come   Today  s hearing will give the subcommittee an opportunity to better understand the changes being proposed  as well as the implications for FHA  homebuyers  and the taxpayers     Senator Allard said     I want to be absolutely clear that I do not oppose FHA reform   I simply want to be certain that the reform is done right   While I believe that FHA is in need of reform  we must first ensure that FHA is on solid financial footing and has the capacity to implement and manage any changes     Senator Allard said                              June 2006 Press Releases
