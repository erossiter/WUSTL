                                                                                                                                                                                                                   SENATOR ALLARD VOTES YES ON NEW SUPPLEMENTAL APPROPRIATIONS BILL          June 15th  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator Allard  R CO  voted in support of the Supplemental Appropriations Bill Thursday after the House Senate Appropriations Conference Committee  of which Senator Allard is a member  agreed to a total of  94 5 billion in funding for the Supplemental Appropriations Bill   The Senate approved the bill by a vote of 98 to 1     A month ago  I voted against the Supplemental Bill in the U S  Senate because I felt the spending in it  which totaled almost  107 billion  was unnecessary and extravagant  I am pleased the other members of the Appropriations Conference Committee agreed with me  The actions we took to reduce the cost of this legislation will save the taxpayers  14 7 billion  I call that a good month  s work for the taxpayer      The Supplemental Appropriations Bill contains funding for the war effort  hurricane recovery in the Gulf Coast  pandemic flu preparation  border security  and levee repair and flood control projects  It also includes avian flu preparedness funding                              June 2006 Press Releases
