                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Secures Brush Water System Funding in Final Interior Appropriations Bill          July 29th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  announced that he has secured  100 000 in State and Tribal Assistance Grant  STAG  funding for the City of Brush  s wastewater treatment system in the final version of the 2006 Interior Appropriations bill  which was approved by the Senate on Friday   The House of Representatives approved the legislation Thursday night  the bill will now go to the White House for the President  s signature     Brush  like many communities across Colorado  is trying to deal with the challenges of increasing federal regulation and population growth     said Senator Allard     I was pleased to include this funding to help the city meet the financial demands of building a new wastewater treatment facility        As a member of the conference committee that worked to iron out the differences between the House and Senate versions of the Interior Appropriations bill  I was in a position to ensure that this funding for Brush stayed in the final version of the legislation     added Senator Allard    The City of Brush  s current wastewater treatment facility was constructed in 1965   With new discharge standards requiring that Brush remove nitrogen and phosphorus levels from their wastewater  the city has determined that a new facility will be more cost effective than making further upgrades to the old system                              July 2005 Press Releases
