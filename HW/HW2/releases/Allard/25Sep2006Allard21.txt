          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD ANNOUNCES  1 15 MILLION GRANT FOR REGIONAL TRANSPORTATION DISTRICT          September 25th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced today that the Regional Transportation District  RTD  in Denver will receive a  1 15 million federal grant for transit security      This Department of Homeland Security grant will help RTD continue its ongoing efforts to keep its transit system secure     said Allard  a member of the Homeland Security Appropriations Subcommittee    This funding to RTD is the highest amount they have ever received for light rail security  With our new Southeast Light Rail Line coming on line November 17  this funding is crucial to maintain a safe and secure light rail system      Allard successfully included an amendment in the Senate passed version of the Port Security bill to increase funding for transit security  The amendment would allocate funding based on transit authority needs with input from the Department of Homeland Security  the Department of Transportation and the intelligence community   For fiscal year  FY  2006  the Transit Security Grant Program includes funds for rail transit systems  including commuter  light and heavy rail  in addition to funding for intra city bus and ferry systems  Transit systems were allocated funding through a risk based formula  which included the ability to reduce risk  cost effectiveness and ability to complete the proposed project with funds awarded   Last year RTD received  600 000 for light rail security   The Transit Security Grant Program is managed by the Department of Homeland Security  s Infrastructure Protection Program  IPP   The purpose of IPP is to strengthen the nation  s ability to prevent  protect against  respond to and recover from terrorist attacks  major disasters and other emergencies that could impact the country  s critical infrastructure                              September 2006 Press Releases
