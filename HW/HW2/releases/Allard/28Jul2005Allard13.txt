          Senator Allard Secures  6 Million for Highway 160 Improvements in Southwest Colorado          July 28th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R CO   Chairman of the Senate Housing and Transportation Subcommittee  has secured  6 million for highway improvements in southwest Colorado in the final version of the transportation authorization bill that emerged from a conference committee on Wednesday  The transportation bill is expected to be approved by the House and Senate by Friday and signed into law by President Bush early next week     The bill provides  4 million for improvements to U S  160 over Wolf Creek Pass and  2 million for U S  160 between Highway 3 and the Florida River   Senator Allard was a member of the House Senate Conference Committee that ironed out differences between the U S  House and Senate versions of the transportation legislation and secured a total of  110 million for specific transportation projects     More than three years of hard work and negotiations on the transportation bill have paid off  with  6 million specifically directed to Durango and southwest Colorado in the bill     said Senator Allard     Highway 160 is a key link in southwest Colorado  and the transportation bill will help to make this route faster and safer        Colorado  s transportation needs continue to grow     added Senator Allard     As a member of the conference committee  I was pleased to be able to secure  110 million for a long list of critically needed highway projects throughout Colorado                                 July 2005 Press Releases
