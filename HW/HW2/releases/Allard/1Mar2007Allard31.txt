          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD ATTACHES DENVER TV FOR SOUTHWEST COLORADO AMENDEMENT TO 9 11 COMMISSION BILL          March 1st  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   introduced an amendment to the Improving America  s Security Act of 2007  also referred to as the 9 11 Commission bill  that would allow residents of La Plata and Montezuma counties in southwest Colorado to receive television stations from Denver     Colorado residents deserve Colorado television  but more than that  they should be able to receive Colorado news statewide in case of a national emergency     said Allard    My amendment would allow the citizens of La Plata and Montezuma counties to receive television stations from Denver  not Albuquerque  New Mexico      Currently  satellite and cable television providers are unable to transmit Denver television signals to La Plata and Montezuma counties because the counties are included in the Nielsen Designated Market Area  DMA  for Albuquerque  N M   instead of the DMA for Denver   Allard  s amendment would allow approximately 9 400 television households in Montezuma County and 17 300 television households in La Plata County to receive Denver broadcast television signals through satellite or cable  The amendment would create an exception to the copyright law that generally prohibits the transmission of Denver signals to La Plata and Montezuma counties   All amendments to the 9 11 Commission bill are currently being considered for inclusion in the bill  and the U S  Senate is expected to vote on the bill next week
