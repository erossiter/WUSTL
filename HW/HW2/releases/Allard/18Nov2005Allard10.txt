          SENATOR ALLARD SECURES  112 MILLION FOR MILITARY CONSTRUCTION PROJECTS  AT FORT CARSON  COLORADO SPRINGS          November 18th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  has secured  70 6 million for improvements at Fort Carson  and an additional  41 62 million for other Colorado Springs military installations in a 2006 military construction funding bill approved by a joint House Senate conference committee Friday  The bill is now in its final form and cannot be amended  Final passage of the bill is expected before the Thanksgiving holiday     This funding will ensure that Fort Carson stays among the premier military training centers in the nation     said Senator Allard  a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee     Congress has signaled it is committed to seeing that Fort Carson has the infrastructure and facilities it needs to continue providing first rate training to our men and women in uniform  and make sure they have the resources they need to carry out their missions     said Senator Allard    This funding is especially critical at this time  when thousands of soldiers are being transferred to Fort Carson as part of the BRAC process      New funding for Fort Carson improvements approved by the conference panel includes      14 6 million to continue work on a troop deployment facility  at Peterson Air Force Base     25 5 million for the barracks complex    28 million for the Combined Arms Collective Training Facility    2 5 million for two urban combat training centers  one for regular troops and one for Special Forces   Other Colorado Springs area military construction funding provided by the bill includes      12 8 million for Peterson Air Force Base West Gate Force Protection Access    9 million for Peterson Air Force Base to privatize family housing    1 82 million for Peterson Air Force Base Life Skills Support Center    13 million for the U S  Air Force Academy to upgrade its academic facilities    5 million for the U S  Air Force Academy to privatize family housing                             November 2005 Press Releases
