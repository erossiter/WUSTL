          ALLARD CALLS FOR GAO INVESTIGATION ON FEDERAL AIR MARSHAL SERVICE Asks Allegations Raised in KMGH TV Report be Investigated         July 27th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   has requested a Government Accountability Office  GAO  investigation into the Federal Air Marshal Service  The request was made in a letter sent to the GAO Comptroller General David Walker on Tuesday     I write today to request a formal investigation by your office to follow up on recommendations made by your office in November 2005 regarding the policies in place to maintain the anonymity of Federal Air Marshals and the agency  s efforts to improve communication between marshals and management     Allard said in the letter     It has been brought to my attention that there have been allegations regarding management policies in place at the Federal Air Marshal Service  FAMS      Allard wrote   Allard initially requested a FAMS investigation on June 6  after being contacted by KMGH TV Channel 7 with concerns raised by Denver FAMS employees  The initial phase of the FAMS investigation yielded 16 recommendations  and the second phase began last week and will be completed by the Transportation Security Administration in September     I requested a formal investigation by FAMS officials as to the validity of these concerns  and what  if any  changes need to be implemented at the Federal Air Marshal Service in Denver  Colorado     Allard continued    Upon further investigation  I have found that similar concerns exist at other FAMS offices across the country      A GAO investigation would include examining FAMS offices around the country  including the Denver field office     I look forward to working with GAO to ensure that the Federal Air Marshal Service is fulfilling its vital mission     Allard wrote                                July 2006 Press Releases
