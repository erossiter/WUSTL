          Senator Allard Secures  13 Million for Jefferson County Highway Improvements           July 28th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R CO   Chairman of the Senate Housing and Transportation Subcommittee  has secured  13 million for improvements to Jefferson County highways in the final version of the transportation authorization bill that emerged from a conference committee on Wednesday  The transportation bill is expected to be approved by the House and Senate by Friday and signed into law by President Bush early next week     The bill provides  6 million for the I 70 Highway 58 Interchange   2 million for Wadsworth and Bowles intersection   4 million for Wadsworth Bypass and  1 million for the U S  285 and Deer Creek Interchange   Senator Allard was a member of the House Senate Conference Committee that ironed out differences between the U S  House and Senate versions of the transportation legislation and secured a total of  110 million for specific transportation projects      More than three years of hard work and negotiations on the transportation bill have paid off for Jefferson County  with  13 million specifically directed to the county in the bill     said Senator Allard     The Denver metro area continues to grow  and the transportation bill will help ensure that the infrastructure grows along with it        Colorado  s transportation needs continue to expand     added Senator Allard     As a member of the conference committee  I was pleased to be able to secure  110 million for a long list of critically needed highway projects throughout Colorado                                 July 2005 Press Releases
