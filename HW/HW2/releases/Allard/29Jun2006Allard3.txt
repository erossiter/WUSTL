          ALLARD SECURES  600 000 IN FUNDING FOR ARKANSAS VALLEY CONDUIT          June 29th  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator   R CO   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced Thursday that he has secured  600 000 in Interior Appropriations funding for the Arkansas Valley Conduit    This is a major step forward for the Conduit   said Senator Allard   For the first time we actually have money flowing to the project      The Arkansas Valley Conduit represents a vital component of improving the water quality and quality of life in the Arkansas Valley     said Senator Allard     The Conduit will provide the Arkansas Valley with the opportunity to offer high quality water at reasonable cost       Due to Senator Allard s hard work and the support of other members of the Colorado delegation we are building momentum for this project   said President of the Southwestern Colorado Water Conservation District  Bill Long     The availability of the water supply will help revitalize the local economies and encourage growth     Allard said     Funds  which will come in the form of a State and Tribal Assistance Grant  will be used for ongoing studies  evaluation  and Arkansas Valley project coordination leading to the design and construction of the Conduit project   When completed  the Conduit will supply 16 towns and 25 water agencies in Bent  Crowley  Kiowa  Prowers  Pueblo and Otero counties with clean  fresh water  serving a geographic area slightly larger than the state of New Hampshire  The project was initially authorized by Congress in 1962 with the enactment of the Fryingpan Arkansas Project                              June 2006 Press Releases
