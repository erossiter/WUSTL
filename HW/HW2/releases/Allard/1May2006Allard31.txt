          SENATOR ALLARD CO SPONSORS BILL MAKING HEALTH CARE INSURANCE MORE AFFORDABLE FOR SMALL BUSINESS          May 1st  2006   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  is co sponsoring a landmark bill introduced by Senator Mike Enzi  R Wyoming  that would make it easier and more affordable for small businesses to provide health insurance for their employees     The Health Insurance Marketplace Modernization and Affordability Act     S 1955  will allow small businesses and trade associations to band their members together to obtain affordable group health insurance     Senator Allard Monday    Runaway costs have driven far too many employers and working families from being able to afford comprehensive health insurance        This is a significant step toward making health insurance options more affordable for small business and working families     Senator Allard said    These are the people and businesses that are the foundation of the American economy  Nothing should stand in the way of them getting the kind of coverage that those who work for large companies and the government take for granted        The Health Insurance Marketplace Modernization and Affordability Act    is designed to                Enhance the market leverage of small groups as well as individual policy holders               Give associations a meaningful role on a level playing field with other group health plans                 Streamline the current hodgepodge of varying state regulation  but preserve the primary role of the states in health insurance oversight and consumer protection                Make lower cost health plan options available  and achieve meaningful reform without a big price tag    A report prepared by the Milwaukee firm of Mercer Oliver Wyman  Inc  for the National Small Business Association found that the legislation would reduce the cost of health insurance for small employers by 12 percent    or  1 000 per employee  The bill would also cover more than one million currently uninsured Americans in working families    or one out of every 12 people who live in a family headed by someone who works for a small company     This will put small business owners and working families in the driver  s seat     Senator Allard said    It gives them the power to negotiate together for the health benefits they want and need  at prices they can afford                                 May 2006 Press Releases
