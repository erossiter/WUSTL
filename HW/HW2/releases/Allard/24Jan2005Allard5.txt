          Senator Allard Introduces Legislation to Improve Access to Rocky Mountain National Park Twin Owls bill protects MacGregor Ranch  protects property rights  improves public access         January 24th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Senator   R Colorado  introduced legislation designed to improve public access to Rocky Mountain National Park while protecting private property rights  The Rocky Mountain National Park Boundary Adjustment Act of 2005 will create a better access road and parking area for park visitors  and will protect the MacGregor Ranch  an historic 132 year old homestead adjacent to the park     I introduced this bill because it allows us to be good stewards of our public lands  while also protecting private property rights  It demonstrates that the concerns of private landowners and the need to safeguard our national parks are not mutually exclusive     Senator Allard said   Public use of Rocky Mountain National Park has grown considerably over the last few years  One of the increasingly popular spots is the Twin Owls Trail Head  which is now only accessible by traversing a one lane dirt access road across the MacGregor Ranch  This heavy traffic affects the pristine  historic nature of the homestead     This is a common sense piece of legislation that benefits the people of Colorado by increasing our ability to share these spectacular public lands and protect one of Colorado  s most historic and beautiful homesteads     Senator Allard said   The National Park Service has determined that a boundary adjustment would be in the best interests of the park  as well as the historic MacGregor Ranch                                January 2005 Press Releases
