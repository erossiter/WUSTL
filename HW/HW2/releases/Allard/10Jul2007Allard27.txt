Contact  Steve WymerEmail             ALLARD SECURES  3 MILLION FOR STATE HIGHWAY 150 Funding will improve access to Great Sand Dunes National Park         July 10th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo    a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Transportation and Housing and Urban Development  announced today that he has secured  3 million for state Highway 150  from state Highway 160 to the Great Sand Dunes National Park  as part of the 2008 Transportation and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill  Allard  who sponsored legislation to make the Great Sand Dunes a national park  said he requested the funding to improve the access for visitors to the park      State Highway 150 is the main access road to the Great Sand Dunes National Park  which was designated as a national park in September 2004     said Allard    This highway also provides access to San Isabel National Forest Lands and is part of the Los Caminos Antiguos Scenic and Historic Byway a tour through the San Luis Valley and some of the oldest communities in the state        We thank Senator Allard for his leadership on securing this important funding     said Art Hutchison  Great Sand Dunes National Park and Preserve Superintendent    This funding allows us to create a safe and inviting road to Great Sand Dunes National Park  which will increase our visitation by encouraging people to come to Great Sand Dunes         State Highway 150 currently has substandard shoulder widths and has not undergone any major construction work since 1983  The pavement is due for rehabilitation to preserve the road base and postpone the need for a much more expensive reconstruction project   Traffic on State Highway 150 has increased because of the status of the national park    This funding request will accommodate a portion of a 16 mile resurfacing project  from M P  8 0 to 16 0  eight miles from the intersection of U S  160 and State Highway 150    north to the entrance of the park   The resurfacing project will also include upgrading approaches to the park and signing and striping activities                              July 2007 Press Releases
