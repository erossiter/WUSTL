                                                                                                                                                                                                                   U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD ANNOUNCES  2 9 MILLION GRANT FOR HOUSING FOR PERSON WITH DISABILITIES IN GREELEY          November 1st  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced today that the ASI  Inc  will receive more than  2 9 million from the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  for a new low income housing complex for the disabled in Greeley     This affordable housing for Colorado persons with disabilities will help those that have very limited financial resources     said Allard  Chairman of the Banking Subcommittee on Housing and Transportation    I appreciate the ASI for its effort to provide those with physical disabilities in Greeley with an affordable place to call home      The  2 945 500 grant will be used to construct a 23 unit  two story building for 22 low income persons with severe physical disabilities and a resident caregiver  The residents will have access to various activities  including attending school or work  participating in sheltered workshops and volunteering at local social service agencies to help the residents maximize their independence   This federal funding was acquired through HUD  s Section 811 Program  which allows persons with disabilities to live as independently as possible in the community by increasing the supply of rental housing with the availability of supportive services                              November 2006 Press Releases
