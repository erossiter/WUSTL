          ALLARD ANNOUNCES THAT BEEF TRADE WITH JAPAN RESUMES          July 27th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Senator  today announced that Japan will resume the importation of U S  beef     This is good news for America  s Ranchers     said Allard    I have been working with the Japanese on this issue for nearly three years now  and I  m very pleased to see that work pay off for rural America        Japanese officials completed inspections of U S  processing plants last week and reported positive findings back to Japanese officials who have decided to resume the import of U S  beef from 33 approved facilities      We opened our facilities to Japanese inspectors because we wanted to show them that the safety of beef produced in the U S  is above reproach     Allard said    I am pleased that U S  beef will once again be on dinner tables in Japan        Japan originally banned the import of beef from the United States in December of 2003 following the discovery in the United States of a single case of Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy  BSE   or Mad Cow Disease  Before the border closure  Japan was the largest importer of American beef  with imports totaling  1 4 billion in 2003   Under the agreement announced today approved packing plants will be able to ship beef from animals that are under 20 months of age     This announcement just confirms what I have been saying all along  that U S  beef is safe and delicious     said Allard    While this is a positive step  we still have a way to go before we are able to build back the trading levels that existed in 2003      Allard has been one of the most outspoken supporters of beef produced in the United States and has continually pressed the Japanese to reconsider their border closure  including in several meetings with Japanese Ambassador Ryozo Kato  In addition  Allard has encouraged President Bush and Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice to make the reopening of the Japanese border one of their top priorities and has sponsored legislation to place tariffs on Japanese products if the border closure was not lifted                              July 2006 Press Releases
