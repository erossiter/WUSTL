          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD ANNOUNCES  931 500 GRANT FOR LAFAYETTE FIRE DEPARTMENT          September 25th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced today that the Lafayette Fire Department in Boulder County will receive a  931 500 federal grant to help increase its number of frontline firefighters      This Department of Homeland Security grant will help the Lafayette Fire Department add full time firefighters  allowing the department to respond to fires and emergency situations in the community more effectively     said Allard  a member of the Homeland Security Appropriations Subcommittee    The Lafayette Fire Department is one of 28 recipients of the U S  Department of Homeland Security Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response  SAFER  grant awards for 2006  The department  s four full time firefighters currently rely on a large volunteer force to respond to local emergencies  This federal funding will be used to hire three additional full time firefighters   A lifetime resident of Lafayette  Fire Chief Gerry Morrell is eager to welcome three additional firefighters to the force and expects them to be on board by January 1  2007     Coupled with our outstanding volunteer firefighters  the additional of three   24 hour firefighters   to the force addresses a critical need and will result in Lafayette having a great contingent to address fire and emergency services     said Morrell   The SAFER grants support the hiring of firefighters and the recruitment and retention of volunteer firefighters to help local fire departments increase their staffing and deployment capabilities to ultimately attain 24 hour staffing                              September 2006 Press Releases
