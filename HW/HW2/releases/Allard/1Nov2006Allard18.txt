                                                                                                                                                                                                                   U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD ANNOUNCES  5 8 MILLION GRANT FOR LONGMONT SENIORS HOUSING          November 1st  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced today that the Longmont Housing Development Corporation will receive more than  5 8 million from the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  for a new low income senior housing complex     This affordable housing for Colorado seniors will help those that have very limited financial resources     said Allard  Chairman of the Banking Subcommittee on Housing and Transportation    I appreciate the Longmont Housing Development Corporation for its effort to provide seniors in Longmont with an affordable place to call home      The  5 884 200 grant will be used to construct a 50 unit  three story building for 49 low income elderly residents and a resident manager  The design of the building will be environmentally friendly  incorporating wind and solar energy to enhance the building  s performance    Bus service is available within one block of the site  and shopping  restaurants  a movie theater and the Senior Center are located within a three mile radius  A service coordinator will be available to assist the residents in accessing a variety of services    This federal funding was acquired through HUD  s Section 202 Program  which provides grants to non profits to acquire  build or rehabilitate rental properties for senior citizens                              November 2006 Press Releases
