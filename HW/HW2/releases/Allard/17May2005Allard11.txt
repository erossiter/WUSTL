          Subcommittee Chairman Senator  Opening Statement on Capitol Visitors Center Hearing          May 17th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  made the following statement Tuesday at a hearing of the Senate Legislative Branch Appropriations Subcommittee   We meet today to take testimony from the Architect of the Capitol Alan Hantman and the Comptroller General David Walker on the progress of the Capitol Visitor Center   As Chairman of the Legislative Branch Subcommittee  I intend to monitor progress of this critical project closely to ensure the Architect is doing all in his power to finish this project in a timely and cost effective fashion   While the AOC met a major milestone of ensuring the East front plaza was in sufficient condition to accommodate the requirements of the 2005 inaugural  we are a long way from completion of this project  It is at least 20 months behind the original schedule and many dollars over the 2002 budget which included new security requirements and the expansion space   The budget and schedule for this 580 000 square foot underground facility have been concerns for at least 3 years  Today  s hearing will focus on getting further clarification on the budget and schedule  There are many other CVC related issues  particularly those associated with the operation of the Visitor Center  but we will save those for another day   We have asked the Comptroller General to testify today as the Government Accountability Office has been monitoring the project closely from the start  We will first hear the Architect  s opening statement  followed by Mr  Walker  s  and then we will have 5 minute rounds of questions                              May 2005 Press Releases
