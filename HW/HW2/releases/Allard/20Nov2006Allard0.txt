          ALLARD INTRODUCES BILL TO ALLOW LA PLATA AND MONTEZUMA COUNTIES TO RECEIVE DENVER BROADCAST TELEVISION SIGNALS          November 20th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   introduced a bill on Thursday to allow both satellite and cable television providers to broadcast Denver television signals to subscribers in both La Plata and Montezuma counties  Currently  satellite and cable television providers are unable to broadcast Denver television signals to these subscribers because La Plata and Montezuma counties are in the Nielsen Designated Marked Area  DMA  for Albuquerque  not the Denver DMA     This bill is a complement to the legislation I introduced with Senator Patrick Leahy last Thursday  Under that bill  some residents of La Plata and Montezuma counties may lose their distant signals from Denver because some of them do not qualify under the law  s existing exemptions     said Allard    This second bill fills in the gaps and would cover subscribers in these counties that are not covered by the other legislation  Colorado residents deserve Colorado television  and should be able to receive Colorado news statewide and cheer on the Broncos like the rest of the state      This second bill from Allard would allow approximately 9 400 television households in Montezuma County and 17 300 television households in La Plata County to receive Denver broadcast television signals through satellite or cable  While these counties are located in the Albuquerque DMA  Allard  s bill creates an exception to the copyright law that generally prohibits the transmission of Denver signals to La Plata and Montezuma counties   Allard introduced another bill on November 16 with U S  Sen  Patrick Leahy  D Vermont  in a bipartisan effort to prevent rural households in Colorado and around the country from permanently losing access to distant network television signals  Under an injunction issued last month by a federal court in Florida  all EchoStar DISH Network subscribers receiving distant signals    even those that are legally eligible    will be cut off from receiving these signals by December 1 unless Congress enacts legislation
