          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD SPONSORS BILL TO HELP EXTEND ENERGY EFFICIENCY INCENTIVES          March 8th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   today sponsored the Energy Efficiency Incentives Act of 2007  which is bipartisan legislation introduced by U S  Sens  Olympia Snowe of Maine and Dianne Feinstein of California  The bill would extend the temporary tax incentives for energy efficient buildings established in the Energy Policy Act of 2005 for a sufficient length of time that the business community can make rational investments in complying with the requirements for the incentives     As the founder and co chair of the Senate Renewable Energy and Energy Efficiency Caucus  I am proud to support this bipartisan legislation     said Allard    It is important to offer incentives to taxpayers who consciously make the decision to be energy efficient      This bipartisan bill provides four years of assured incentives for most situations  and some additional time for projects with particularly long lead times  such as commercial buildings  The Energy Efficiency Incentives Act of 2007 also extends the applicability of the incentives to include the entire commercial and residential building sectors   The primary modifications to the Energy Policy Act incentives in the bill are to phase out incentives based on the cost incurred in saving or producing energy with incentives based on the actual performance  The goal of the bill is to provide a transition from the Energy Policy Act retrofit incentives  which are based partially on cost and partially on performance  to a new system that can provide larger dollar amounts of incentive based on performance                               March 2007 Press Releases
