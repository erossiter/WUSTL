                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Votes Yes on Bankruptcy Reform Bill          March 10th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado   a member of the Senate Committee on Banking and Urban Affairs  made the following statement after a final Senate vote on the bankruptcy reform bill  which was enacted by a vote of 74 to 25     I voted for this legislation because it provides an even handed balance between debtor protection and creditor remedies  Abuse and fraud in our bankruptcy system costs millions of dollars  slowing economic growth and overwhelming the bankruptcy courts      The costs of bankruptcy impact American consumers in many ways  higher prices and interest rates because others have defaulted  higher down payment requirements for auto and home loans  shorter grace periods for paying bills and higher late fees      The individuals hurt most by this   bankruptcy tax   are those who need credit the most  lower income families that need access to credit for unexpected expenses  but who face higher overall borrowing costs as a result  in part  of others   bankruptcies                                 March 2005 Press Releases
