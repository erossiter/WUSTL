                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Secures  800 000 for New Water Treatment Plant in Walden in Interior Appropriations Bill          June 9th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado   member of the Senate Interior Appropriations Subcommittee  on Thursday announced that he has secured  800 000 in State and Tribal Assistance Grant  STAG  funding for the Town of Walden  s water treatment plant     The Town of Walden is working to bring its water treatment process in compliance with the new Environmental Protection Agency and State of Colorado water quality control regulations     Senator Allard said    I  ve worked for more than a year to secure this funding to help reduce the financial burden on the town as it upgrades its infrastructure for the future      Walden  s current treatment plant and filtration module was built in 1980   The plant  s current processes are insufficient to filter and treat newly regulated agents such as cryptosporidian  Replacement parts and equipment are becoming scarce because of the age of the system  and the plant is also in need of providing safety redundancy     I will continue my efforts in the Senate to make sure that rural communities such as Walden receive the federal support that they need as they try to upgrade their aging facilities  and I will be working hard to make sure that the Interior Appropriations bill will be approved by the full Senate as soon as possible     added Senator Allard                              June 2005 Press Releases
