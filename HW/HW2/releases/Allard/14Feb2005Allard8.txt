                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Introduces Legislation Demanding Return of USS Pueblo from North Korea          February 14th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  introduced a resolution in the U S  Senate Monday demanding that North Korea return the USS Pueblo  an intelligence ship that was attacked and seized by North Korean naval vessels and MiG jets in 1968   The attack resulted in the death of one American and the wounding of several more  82 surviving crew members were captured  held prisoner and tortured for 11 months  The American vessel remains in North Korean hands     North Korea  s inhumane treatment of our sailors  and the refusal of Pyongyang to return this vessel should not be forgotten     Senator Allard said    It has been more than 30 years since this disgraceful episode occurred  and I believe that the United States government should demand the return of the USS Pueblo to the United States Navy without further delay        I will continue this fight that my former colleague  Senator Ben Nighthorse Campbell began  and I look forward to pressing for passage of this resolution solution during the 109th Congress     Senator Allard said    I look forward to working with the veterans of the USS Pueblo and their respective veterans   organizations so that we can take some positive steps towards getting the USS Pueblo back      The vessel  s captain  Cmdr  Lloyd   Pete    Bucher  was an eloquent advocate for the return of his ship until his death last year                              February 2005 Press Releases
