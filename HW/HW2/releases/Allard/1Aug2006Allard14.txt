          ALLARD DISAPPOINTED PILT AMENDMENT WON T BE CONSIDERED          August 1st  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   expressed disappointment Tuesday over the fact that his Payment in Lieu of Taxes  PILT  amendment to the Gulf of Mexico Energy bill will not be considered due to a procedural maneuver in the U S  Senate that prevented any amendments from being considered     I was hopeful that we would have the chance to address an amendment I offered on funding for the Payment in Lieu of Taxes  PILT  Program  This particular program is extremely important to many western states  including Colorado  that have a high percentage of federally owned land     said Allard     Many people are unaware of the fact that 35 percent of Colorado is owned by the Federal Government  Federal ownership of these lands can be beneficial  but there is an unseen cost to local communities  to local governments  The Federal Government does not pay property taxes  and this translates into reduced revenue for local governments while at the same time there are costs they are burdened with in trying to meet the needs of the Federal agencies that are in that county or local community     Allard added      For Colorado  it means  129 million each year in lost property tax revenue  This is funding that could be used for education  law enforcement efforts or road building  Unfortunately  PILT  or payment in lieu of taxes  is chronically underfunded  and the amendment I introduced would have helped to overcome this annual shortfall     said Allard   Allard said that he would continue his efforts to address the annual shortfall in the PILT Program                              August 2006 Press Releases
