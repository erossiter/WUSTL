          SENATOR ALLARD   REP  MUSGRAVE SEEK TO EXTEND AUTHORIZATION    FOR CACHE LA POUDRE HERITAGE AREA          December 14th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C        U S  Senator   R Colorado  and Representative Marilyn Musgrave  CO 04  introduced legislation    Wednesday that would extend Congressional authorization for the Cache la Poudre Heritage Area in northern Colorado  The bill would also give local citizens greater management authority over the area          This area was the site of a rich  unique story that  like much in Colorado  revolves around water     Senator Allard said    The Cache la Poudre Heritage Area has much to offer us as we seek to remember and interpret our history  It enjoys strong  broad based local support        The Cache la Poudre River played an important role in the development of water law in the West  including the legal concept of prior appropriation     Senator Allard said    Understanding the significance and history of prior appropriation is vital to understanding our history  as well as our present day concerns  because much of the water law in the Western United States is based on it        The preservation of this corridor is a great benefit for our area   said Representative Musgrave        It is an example of how Coloradans have engineered the management of this precious resource           The Heritage Area was originally authorized by Congress under legislation authored by former Colorado Senator Hank Brown  The objective of the Heritage Area is to interpret the area s cultural  historic and natural resources within a western water development theme        Under the original legislation  the Secretary of Interior was to appoint a commission to work with the National Parks Service and manage the Area  but because of a technicality  the Secretary was unable to appoint the commission      In response  local citizens stepped up and formed the Poudre Heritage Alliance to support the Heritage Area until an official commission could be named      Senator Allard s and Rep  Musgrave s legislation would rectify this  and empower local residents to continue the work they have been doing on behalf the heritage area   The Cache la Poudre River Corridor is one of 23 National Heritage Areas and was among the first established west of the Mississippi      It runs for 45 miles along the Cache la Poudre River beginning in Larimer County at the eastern edge of the Roosevelt National Forest and ends just east of Greeley approximately 1 4th of a mile west of where it joins with the South Platte River   The Cache la Poudre Heritage Area works as a collaborative project between the Cache la Poudre Heritage Alliance and the National Park Service      This program is a good example of how the Federal Government and local community groups can work together to achieve extraordinary things  It simply makes sense to extend the program     Senator Allard said
