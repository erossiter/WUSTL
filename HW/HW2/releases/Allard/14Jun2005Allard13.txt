          Senator Allard Secures Funding for Fountain Creek Project Study  250 000 for regional flood control study         June 14th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado   member of the Senate Energy and Water Appropriations Subcommittee  on Tuesday announced that he has secured  250 000 for the Fountain Creek watershed flood control project in the FY 2006 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill     Fountain Creek has for several years been among my top energy and water funding priorities  so I am pleased we once again have secured money for this important project     Senator Allard said     I have been seeking federal support for the Fountain Creek watershed project since major flooding occurred in 1999  causing approximately  100 million in damages     Senator Allard said    What is needed is a detailed analysis of flood control  erosion and sedimentation in the Fountain Creek watershed  and I believe it warrants environmental restoration on a regional scale  addressing the 150 miles of Fountain Creek and its tributaries        My office has been in constant contact with the Army Corps of Engineers  urging them to move this study along     the Senator added    The Corps re programmed an additional  200 000 toward completion of the study this year  so  250 000 in next year  s appropriation will allow us to go forward to determine exactly what needs to be done on Fountain Creek        To adequately address this problem  the study needs to focus on not only Colorado Springs  but the area of southern Colorado that has been adversely impacted  as well     the Senator said   Senator Allard already has secured more than  1 061 million in funding for the Fountain Creek project since 2002  The full Senate is expected to vote on this funding by the end of this month                              June 2005 Press Releases
