                                                                                                                                                                                                                   ALLARD AMENDMENT TO STUDY NEED AND COSTS OF POTENTIAL IMMIGRATION OFFICE IN GREELEY INCLUDED IN HOMELAND SECURITY BILL          August 11th  2006   GREELEY  CO   U S  Sen    R Colo   succeeded in getting an amendment included in the Homeland Security Appropriations bill  which would require the Assistant Secretary for Immigration and Customs Enforcement  ICE  of the Department of Homeland Security  DHS  to submit a report on the costs and need for establishing a sub office in Greeley  Allard  s amendment was approved unanimously by the Senate       I  m pleased that my amendment to move forward with a Greeley ICE office was included into the Homeland Security Appropriations bill     Allard said     I look forward to working with ICE to put the necessary resources in place to ensure that our immigration laws are enforced in Colorado      Allard introduced the amendment at the request of Weld County District Attorney Ken Buck and local law enforcement officials who have been petitioning the Denver ICE office to put a sub office in Greeley     It is great that Senator Allard is working hard for Weld County     said Weld County District Attorney Kenneth R  Buck     He has done all he can to help us with the illegal immigration problem and now it is up to the administration      The Department of Homeland Security Appropriations bill funds all operations of the Department of Homeland Security  including Immigration and Customs Enforcement  ICE   Customs and Border Protection and Citizenship and Immigration Services  The Senate passed the bill on July 13  2006  by a vote of 100 0                                August 2006 Press Releases
