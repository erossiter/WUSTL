          ALLARD REQUESTS SENATE   HOUSE IMMIGRATION FIELD HEARINGS BE HELD IN COLORADO Says having both hearings will ensure Coloradans   voices will be heard         June 27th  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator   R CO  has requested  in separate letters sent to Senator Specter  Chairman of the Senate Judiciary Committee  and Congressman Sensenbrenner  Chairman of the House Judiciary Committee   that both the U S  Senate and the House of Representatives hold immigration field hearings in Colorado      We need to take this important debate out of Washington  D C  and place it directly in the hands of the American people  where it belongs     Allard said    Colorado is an ideal location for a field hearing due to its close proximity to the Southern border  and the number of agricultural  tourism  and business interests situated within the state        We commend you for your decision to conduct a series of immigration field hearings and respectfully request that you hold a hearing in the State of Colorado     Senator Allard said in his letter to House Judiciary Chairman James Sensenbrenner    We look forward to working with you to coordinate a successful hearing that will allow the voice of Coloradans to be heard in the  immigration  debate       Allard was joined in his letter to Sensenbrenner by Colorado Fourth District U S  Representative Marilyn Musgrave and Seventh District U S  Representative Bob Beauprez      Should you decide to hold a hearing in Colorado     said Senator Allard in his letter to Senate Judiciary Committee Chairman Arlen Specter    I look forward to working with you to ensure that the voice of Colorado is heard and ultimately reflected in any legislation we pass in the Congress       Allard said he is optimistic that when the two committees select sites for the upcoming field hearings  Colorado will be selected to host hearings                              June 2006 Press Releases
