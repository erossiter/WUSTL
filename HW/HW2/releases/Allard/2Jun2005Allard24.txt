          Senator Allard Goes to Bat  for Fountain Creek Funding Allard effort to provide funding began in 2000         June 2nd  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  said Thursday that he has requested funding for the Fountain Creek Watershed Study in the FY 2006 Senate Energy and Water Appropriations bill     This appropriations request was made in March   It is near the top of my list of energy and water funding priorities  and has been for several years     Senator Allard said    As a member of the Senate Energy and Water Appropriations Subcommittee  I think we have a good chance of getting the funding again this year        I have a longstanding involvement with this project  and have sought federal support for it since the major flooding that occurred in 1999  causing approximately  100 million in damages     Senator Allard said    What is needed is a detailed analysis of flood control  erosion and sedimentation in the Fountain Creek watershed  and I believe it warrants environmental restoration on a regional scale  addressing the 150 miles of Fountain Creek and its tributaries        My office has been in constant contact with the Army Corps of Engineers  urging them to move this study along     the Senator said    The Corps has re programmed an additional  200 000 toward completion of the study this year        To adequately address this problem  the study needs to focus on not only Colorado Springs  but the area of southern Colorado that has been adversely impacted  as well     the Senator said   Senator Allard already has obtained more than  1 061 million in funding for the project to date                              June 2005 Press Releases
