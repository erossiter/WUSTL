          U S  SENATE PASSES MILITARY CONSTRUCTION FUNDING BILL   INCLUDES  41 8 MILLION FOR CONSTRUCTION AT PUEBLO DEPOT          November 14th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced Tuesday that the U S  Senate passed the FY 2007 military construction funding bill  which included  41 8 million for the continuation of construction activities in the Assembled Chemical Weapons Alternatives Program at Pueblo Depot that Allard helped secure as a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee   The U S  House of Representatives version of the Fiscal Year 2007 Military Construction Appropriations bill reduced funding for the Assembled Chemical Weapons Alternatives program by  40 million  Because of Allard  s work on the Senate Appropriations Committee  the Senate restored  41 8 million for Pueblo      Full funding of this program was absolutely necessary if we were to continue to move forward in destroying the hundreds of thousands of aging chemical weapons stored at Pueblo     said Allard    In my view  cutting the funding to this program was irresponsible   It was a pennywise  pound foolish decision  Such cuts would set the program back at least a year and add as much as  190 million to the program life cycle costs      A total of  140 9 million was provided for the program  Of that amount   41 8 million was provided for the Pueblo Chemical Agent Destruction Pilot Plant and  99 1 million for the Blue Grass Chemical Agent Destruction Pilot Plant         It is imperative that we keep this program moving    the safety and well being of the people in Pueblo depend on it     said Allard    You can be assured that I will do everything in my power to protect this funding level as it proceeds through the appropriations process         The military construction funding bill passed the U S  House of Representatives earlier this year and will now move to a House Senate Conference Committee where a final version will be produced  Allard  as a member of the Appropriations Committee  will serve on the Conference Committee                              November 2006 Press Releases
