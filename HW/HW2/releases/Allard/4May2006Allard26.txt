          SENATOR ALLARD TAKES STAND FOR FISCAL RESPONSIBILITY  Votes against Emergency Supplemental that was nearly  17 billion higher than President  s original request         May 4th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  on Thursday voted no on the 2006 Emergency Supplemental spending bill  noting that the final version of the legislation turned out to be  15 billion more than what President Bush originally asked Congress to provide for emergency spending needs     To me this was a vote to impose fiscal sanity on the appropriations process     said Senator Allard  a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee     I voted against this legislation because the final cost of the bill turned out to be  109 billion    17 billion over the President  s original request of  92 billion     Senator Allard said       This is not fiscally responsible        I believe emergency supplemental spending should be for actual emergencies not pork barrel spending  and some of the provisions in the final bill turned out to be pork barrel at its most egregious     Senator Allard said    As a member of the Budget Committee  I am acutely aware of the need to stand back and decide what is    and what isn  t    an emergency spending need        My vote should have come as no surprise  Last week I signed a letter to President Bush  along with 34 of my colleagues  asking him to veto the Supplemental if it totaled more than  92 billion     Senator Allard added    I believe there are enough votes in the Senate to uphold a Presidential veto if the bill  as it is written  isn  t significantly changed                                  May 2006 Press Releases
