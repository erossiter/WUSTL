                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Says BRAC Vote to Close Aurora DFAS Facility  Disappointing           August 25th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  said today  s vote by the Base Realignment and Closure Commission  BRAC  to recommend that the Defense Finance and Accounting Service  DFAS  facility in Aurora be closed is    disappointing and short sighted        I am disappointed by today  s vote  and believe the BRAC  s decision is extremely short sighted and not in the best interest of the taxpayers     Senator Allard said      I had hoped that when the BRAC commissioners heard all the facts  they would have realized that the Denver DFAS facility is a valuable asset that should not be closed      added Senator Allard    We took that message to the BRAC hearing in Monterey  California on August 9 and unfortunately that message fell on deaf ears      On August 9 Senator Allard headed a delegation of Colorado elected officials  including Colorado Governor Bill Owens  U S  Senator Ken Salazar  Denver City Council President Rosemary Rodriguez and Denver Metro Chamber President Joe Blake to the BRAC hearing in Monterey where the DFAS facility was reviewed by the entire BRAC commission    DFAS Denver is housed in the 600 000 square foot Buckley Air Force Base Annex on the former Lowry Air Force Base  with more than 1 500 employees  The BRAC Commission recommendations will now go to President Bush for his approval and then Congress will have 45 days to either reject or approve the recommendations in total                              August 2005 Press Releases
