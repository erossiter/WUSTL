          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD TO TESTIFY TO SENATE COMMITTEE ON ARKANSAS VALLEY CONDUIT ACT          September 20th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   will testify on Thursday before a U S  Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources Subcommittee on Water and Power hearing on the Arkansas Valley Conduit Act   S  1106   which Allard introduced in May 2005      This hearing is another important step in calling attention to the Arkansas Valley Conduit and will help us move toward Senate passage of the bill     said Allard    With Colorado  s ongoing drought  it is more urgent than ever that this bill moves forward      Allard  s legislation creates a cost share formula that requires the federal government to pay for 80 percent of the conduit  s construction costs  The remaining 20 percent will be paid for by local entities  Allard and U S  Rep  Marilyn Musgrave worked closely with citizens and elected officials from communities in the Lower Arkansas Valley in drafting the legislation    Bill Long  president of the Southeastern Colorado Water Conservancy District  is also expected to testify at the hearing in support of Allard  s legislation   When completed  the Arkansas Valley Conduit will supply 16 towns and 25 water agencies in Bent  Crowley  Kiowa  Prowers  Pueblo and Otero counties with clean  fresh water  serving a geographic area slightly larger than the state of New Hampshire  The project was initially authorized by Congress in 1962 with the enactment of the Fryingpan Arkansas Project                              September
