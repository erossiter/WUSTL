          Senator Allard Secures Alamosa Water System Funding in Final Interior Appropriations Bill          July 29th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  announced that he has secured  650 000 in State and Tribal Assistance Grant  STAG  funding for the City of Alamosa  s water treatment system in the final version of the 2006 Interior Appropriations bill  which was approved by the Senate on Friday   The House of Representatives approved the legislation Thursday night  the bill will now go to the White House for the President  s signature     After more than a year  s hard work  this funding is finally a done deal     said Senator Allard     The City of Alamosa  like so many communities across the state  has to shoulder a huge financial burden as it tries to comply with stringent new water quality standards that have recently been imposed by the EPA   This federal funding will reduce some of that burden        As a member of the conference committee that worked to iron out the differences between the House and Senate versions of the Interior Appropriations bill  I was in a position to ensure that this funding for Alamosa stayed in the final version of the legislation     added Senator Allard    The EPA recently lowered its acceptable contaminant levels for arsenic from 50 micrograms per liter to 10 micrograms per liter  and has set a deadline for compliance of January 2006   This change has forced the City of Alamosa to make expensive upgrades to its drinking water system to comply with the new rule                              July 2005 Press
