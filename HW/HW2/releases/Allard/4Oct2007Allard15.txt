Contact  Steve WymerEmail             ALLARD TESTIFIES ON HOMELESSNESS LEGISLATION BEFORE HOUSE SUBCOMMITTEE ON HOUSING AND COMMUNITY OPPORTUNITY Subcommittee Chairwoman Maxine Waters Invites Allard to Discuss Legislation to Prevent and End Homelessness         October 4th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   testified today  at the invitation of Chairwoman Maxine Waters  D Calif    before the U S  House of Representatives Subcommittee on Housing and Community Opportunity regarding legislation he has introduced addressing homelessness  Allard  s bill  the Community Partnership to End Homelessness Act  S 1518  was approved by the Senate Committee on Banking  Housing and Urban Affairs last week and has been endorsed by Denver Mayor John Hickenlooper   A portion of Allard  s remarks to the subcommittee are below      Homeless people are our country  s most at risk population  and reauthorization of McKinney Vento is critical in addressing their needs     Currently  the federal government devotes significant resources to the homeless  Yet despite the enormous federal resources directed towards homelessness  the problem persists  We need to bring more accountability to homeless assistance  increasing funding for successful programs and initiatives and replacing those that are ineffective     I believe that we all share the goal of wanting to prevent and end homelessness in America   There are many different people with many different and laudable ideas of how to accomplish this goal  The Community Partnership to End Homelessness Act strikes a balance between these many viewpoints  This balance is demonstrated by the unanimous support the bill received in the Senate Banking Committee  Republicans and Democrats  urban areas like New York and rural areas like Wyoming  everyone came together to say that they believed the Community Partnership to End Homelessness Act would help us prevent and end homelessness      The bipartisan legislation introduced by Allard and Sen  Jack Reed  D R I   will consolidate and streamline the existing homeless assistance grant programs at the U S  Dept  of Housing and Urban Development into a single flexible  outcome based program                              October 2007 Press Releases
