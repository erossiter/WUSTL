          Senator  Secures  153 7 Million in Mass Transit Funding for Colorado Key Senate panel signs off on approrpriations legislation         July 21st  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     Senator   R Colorado   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  has secured more than  153 7 million for mass transit projects in Colorado next year   The funding was included in a 2006 transportation appropriations bill approved by the Senate Appropriations Committee Thursday     This funding will allow us to finish up the Southeast light rail component of T REX in Denver  maintain older portions of the system and begin work on the West corridor     said Senator Allard  a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee    It also will allow us to maintain  expand and improve the Colorado Transit Coalition  s projects that provide bus services in rural areas  along with transportation options for the elderly and disabled         Enlarging light rail will provide better options for Denver residents seeking alternative ways to commute  as well as critical public transportation for rural Coloradoans     the Senator said    In a growing state like Colorado  there is enormous pressure on our transportation systems and infrastructure   The funding in this legislation will help our state meet those challenges and continue to prosper      Colorado funding approved by the panel includes      58 9 million in formula funding  which can be spent at the discretion of the state     3 6 million to improve and maintain existing light rail lines     80 million to finish the Southeast Light Rail line     5 million to begin work on the new West Corridor Light Rail line     6 million for bus and bus facilities   Senator Allard also is chairman of the Senate Banking Subcommittee on Housing
