          SENATOR ALLARD SECURES  51 MILLION FOR PUEBLO  BLUE GRASS CHEMICAL WEAPONS DEMILITARIZATION IN FINAL VERSION OF DEFENSE APPROPRIATIONS BILL Funding level  20 million above President  s budget request         December 21st  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     The U S  Senate Wednesday gave final approval to a 2006 defense appropriations bill that includes  51 million specifically earmarked by U S  Senator   R Colorado  for the two Assembled Chemical Weapons Alternatives programs under development  one at the Pueblo Depot in Colorado and the other at the Blue Grass Depot in Kentucky  The funding level is  20 million more than the President  s budget had requested  The U S  House passed the bill last week     This funding reflects Congress   commitment to the well being of the people of Pueblo  as well as meeting our nation  s obligations under the Chemical Weapons Treaty     said Senator Allard  a member of the Appropriations Committee    We have ensured that this project will continue to move forward toward completion        This bill also gives the program manager greater flexibility to use the funding provided to be used either for research and development  or for actual construction of the chemical weapons depots     Senator Allard said     Bureaucratic impediments cannot be allowed to interfere with this project      Earlier this year  Senators Allard and Senator Mitch McConnell  R Kentucky  added a provision to the FY 2005 Supplemental Appropriations Act that ensured that the  372 3 million in funding budgeted in earlier years would not be transferred from Pueblo or Blue Grass to other sites  In addition  it directed the Department of Defense to send at least  100 million to the ACWA sites within four months of the bill  s enactment  and prohibited the Department from conducting a study on the transportation of chemical weapons across state lines  That direction was fulfilled in September of this year                              December 2005 Press
