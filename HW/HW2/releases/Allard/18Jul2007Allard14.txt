member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that he has secured  20 773 million in funding for Colorado as a part of the Fiscal Year 2008 Agriculture Appropriations bill     Funding for these important projects will help Colorado maintain its position as a leader    both nationally and internationally    in agriculture     said Allard    Agriculture remains one of the strongest and most important parts of our economy and is a key component of Colorado  s heritage      The following projects for Colorado were included in the bill at Senator Allard  s request         18 27 million for research and work to combat Chronic Wasting Disease         880 000 for the National Beef Cattle Evaluation Consortium         306 000 for research into Russian Wheat Aphid Resistance  Stress Tolerance  and Quality Enhancement of Wheat         817 000 for the Program for Economically Important Infectious Animal Diseases         500 000 for the federal cost share for water management system improvements in the South Platte River basin by installing totalizing flow meters and automated data loggers with communications devices on existing irrigation wells to enable sophisticated usage data and analysis        The Department is directed to give special consideration to an application for a Rural Telemedicine Infrastructure Upgrades Grant to the Colorado and Western Region        The Department is also directed to give special consideration to an application for a Rural Business Opportunity Grant for a Sustainable Environmental and Economic Development Park in the San Luis Valley    Allard also supported         1 5 million for research at CSU on the effects of UV radiation on crops  ecosystems and livestock   Allard said the funding was approved Tuesday by the subcommittee  The full committee is expected to approve the funding bill on Thursday                              July 2007 Press Releases
