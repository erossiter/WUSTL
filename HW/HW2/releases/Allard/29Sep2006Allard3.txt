          U S  SEN  WAYNE ALLARD APPLAUDS FINAL PASSAGE OF HOMELAND SECURITY APPROPRIATIONS BILL          September 29th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   applauded the U S  Senate passage of the FY 2007 Homeland Security Appropriations Bill conference report  The bill includes  34 8 billion in discretionary spending for the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS       Our most important priority is keeping our homeland safe     said Allard  a member of the Homeland Security Appropriations Subcommittee    The Homeland Security Appropriations Bill that we passed today makes a significant investment to address vulnerabilities facing our nation  including funding to help secure our nation  s porous border      Allard successfully attached an amendment to the Senate version of the bill to determine the cost and evaluate the need for U S  Immigrations and Customs Enforcement  ICE  offices in Colorado Springs and Greeley  This amendment was included in the final conference report and requires the Assistant Secretary for ICE to submit a report on the costs and need for establishing these sub offices   The conference report provides  8 billion for U S  Customs and Border Protection to secure the nation  s borders  including  2 24 billion for border staffing between the ports of entry and 1 500 new Border Patrol agents   1 19 billion for fencing  infrastructure and technology   777 98 million for air and marine operations  including unmanned aerial vehicles and helicopters  and  232 98 million for facilities construction along the border   ICE will receive a total of  3 96 billion for investigating and enforcing immigration and customs laws  including  1 44 billion for investigations and intelligence   1 98 billion for detention and removal   15 million for automation modernization  and  56 million for infrastructure improvements at detention facilities   The Transportation Security Administration will receive  6 37 billion  excluding  2 42 billion of aviation security offsetting collections  to be used for securing aviation transportation  including  2 67 billion for passenger and baggage screener workforce and air cargo security  and  13 2 million for the safety and security of the nation  s railways   The final version of the bill also includes a provision making it unlawful to construct a tunnel under the international land border of the United States  Allard co sponsored the original Border Tunnel Prevention Act  S  2652  in May 2006   Also in this conference report is the Federal Emergency Management Agency Reauthorization  which elevates the standing of the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  within DHS  promotes the FEMA Administrator to the level of Deputy Secretary and restores the nexus between emergency preparedness and response   A drug importation provision was also included in the conference report to authorize the importation of FDA approved prescription drugs from Canada  This provision allows individuals to carry  on their person  a 90 day supply of prescription medicines into the United States from Canada for personal use   The U S  House of Representatives has already passed the conference report and the bill now moves to the president for his signature                               September 2006 Press Releases
