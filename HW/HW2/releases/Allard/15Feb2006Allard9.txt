                                                                                                                                                                                                                   ALLARD SUPPORTS BLM IN GETTING CITIZEN S INPUT ON ENERGY POLICY ACT          February 15th  2006   Washington  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado  expressed his support for the Bureau of Land Management  BLM  and its decision to hold a series of listening sessions to get citizen feedback on split estate issues in their ongoing effort to implement the Energy Policy Ace of 2005      I  ve always felt it  s important to get input from people impacted by the actions of our government     said Senator Allard     Which is why I  m pleased the BLM has responded in this open manner      The BLM is interested in hearing recommendations for ways to update its policies and procedures for managing Federal oil and natural gas resources that underlie privately owned land     So far the BLM has shown a great willingness and sense of urgency in implementing the Energy Policy Act  and it  s good to see they  re involving the public in a meaningful way to develop ideas for how to handle this cumbersome issue in Colorado and around the West     Senator Allard said   A listening session is to take place in Grand Junction on Wednesday  March 22   The BLM will announce the exact time and location at least 15 days prior to the meeting  For more information visit the Split Estate website at  www blm gov blm                              February 2006 Press Releases
