          SENATE APPROVES ALLARD AMENDMENT TO DEFENSE BILL TRANSFERRING TITLE TO AIR FORCE PROPERTY AT LA JUNTA AIRPORT TO CITY OF LA JUNTA           November 10th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     An amendment introduced by U S  Senator   R Colorado  that will transfer surplus Air Force property at the La Junta City Airport to the city was included in the 2006 Defense Authorization bill by the U S  Senate Wednesday  A vote on the full bill is expected Thursday   The property was formerly referred to as the La Junta Strategic Training Range Complex Air Force installation site  and was used for electronic scoring  It now sits vacant and has been declared surplus     The Senate agreed with me that this transfer was the right thing to do  The Air Force no longer has any use for this property  and La Junta has plans to put it to constructive use     Senator Allard said   The property is located at the La Junta Airport and Industrial Park adjacent to the Otero Junior College Police Academy training area  The City of La Junta plans to use part of it as a training facility for the police academy  and for economic development    Although the property is to be transferred to the city at no cost  La Junta would be responsible for all survey  environmental assessment and conveyance costs   The U S  House of Representatives approved its version of the 2006 Defense Authorization bill in May  the two versions will now go to a joint House Senate conference for their differences to be ironed out before being returned to the Senate and House for a final vote  Senator Allard said he is confident the La Junta airport provision will be in the final version                              November 2005 Press Releases
