          ALLARD ANNOUNCES PRE DISASTER MITIGATION FINALIST IN COLORADO FEMA Selects Colorado Springs Wildfire Project as a Finalist in National Mitigation Competition         May 4th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced today that the City of Colorado Springs Wildfire Mitigation Project is a finalist to receive over  1 million from the Federal Emergency Management Agency  s  FEMA  Pre Disaster Mitigation  PDM  Program       I am pleased with today  s news that FEMA has selected Colorado Springs as a finalist for over a million dollars in fire prevention funding     said Allard     The forests in Colorado are choked with a dangerous buildup of bark beetle killed trees and other hazardous fuels   The prolonged drought leaves forest lands throughout Colorado and the front range vulnerable to wildfires   Prevention is important because the best way to fight forest fires is to keep them from starting        The mitigation activities in Colorado show that the State and local communities are working together with FEMA and other partners to make mitigation happen     said R  David Paulison  Administrator of FEMA      The PDM Program  first authorized in the Disaster Mitigation Act of 2000 and funded by President Bush in his Fiscal Year 2003 budget  provides funding for pre disaster mitigation planning and the implementation of cost effective mitigation activities before a disaster occurs   FEMA received 470 applications for the 2007 national competition   All funding is contingent upon completion of required pre award activities   The City of Colorado Springs Wildfire Mitigation Project will be eligible to receive  1 000 626 in federal funding once meeting additional criteria                                May 2007 Press Releases
