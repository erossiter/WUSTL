          RYAN LAND EXCHANGE LESGISLATION SIGNED INTO LAW          December 4th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Sen    R Colo   announced that on Friday President Bush signed legislation sponsored by Allard to complete the Ryan land exchange in Pitkin County  Colo      The folks in Pitkin County are getting an early Christmas present  thanks to President Bush  His signature officially makes the Pitkin County Land Exchange law  This bill was a good piece of common sense legislation     said Allard    This law will ease federal land management strains and benefit the local folks who have been working toward this for almost a decade  The exchange has enjoyed bipartisan support from numerous members of the Colorado Congressional Delegation  Without the groundwork laid by the bill  s original sponsors  former Senator Campbell and former Congressman McInnis  we would not be celebrating this victory       Allard  s bill will make the protection of this land possible by facilitating a multi party land exchange among the Bureau of Land Management  the U S  Forest Service and Pitkin County  As a result of federal regulations  a three party land exchange cannot be accomplished without Congressional action     This is a victory a long time in the making  it has been a long road    especially for the folks in Pitkin County  Seven years ago Pitkin County stepped up to the plate  spending almost  3 million to purchase and protect this threatened land     said Allard    Allard introduced this legislation on January 24  2005  the first day of the 109th Congress and it has been one of his top priorities this session  The Senate Energy Committee passed the bill in April after Allard and his fellow Colorado Senator Ken Salazar pushed hard for the committee to act on the legislation  The U S  Senate passed the legislation in late September 2006  and the U S  House of Representatives passed the bill mid November 2006   Part of the land protected is the training camp of the famed 10th Mountain Division  where World War II soldiers trained for winter and mountain warfare  The special skills they trained for included skiing  rock climbing and cold weather survival
