          Allard Legislation to Upgrade Access to Rocky Mountain National Park Wins Approval in House of Representatives Twin Owls bill previously passed by Senate protects MacGregor Ranch  guards property rights  improves public acces to RMNP         October 18th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   s legislation to adjust the boundaries of Rocky Mountain National Park to improve public access was unanimously approved by the U S  House of Representatives on Tuesday afternoon  It had been approved by the U S  Senate in July and now goes to President Bush for his signature     The Rocky Mountain National Park Boundary Adjustment Act of 2005 will create a better access road and parking area for park visitors  and will protect the historic MacGregor Ranch  a homestead adjacent to the park     Senator Allard said     This legislation allows us to be good stewards of our public lands  while also protecting private property rights  It shows that we can safeguard our national parks  while taking into account the concerns of private landowners     added Senator Allard     I want to thank Rep  Musgrave for shepherding this bill through the House so quickly        I had hoped to have this bill on President Bush  s desk for his signature before the end of the year  but due to the hard work of Representative Musgrave it looks like that it is going to happen ahead of schedule     Senator Allard added   U S  Rep  Marilyn Musgrave introduced Senator Allard  s bill in the House of Representatives following its approval by the U S  Senate this summer  and helped shepherd it through the House     This is a common sense bill that respects private property rights  protects a historic Estes Park homestead  and improves access to the national park     said Rep  Musgrave following passage of the bill       The National Park Service has determined that a boundary adjustment would be in the best interests of the park  as well as the MacGregor Ranch  Park managers want this  and the people at MacGregor Ranch want it  because it is mutually beneficial to both parties      Public use of Rocky Mountain National Park has grown considerably over the last few years  One of the increasingly popular spots is the Twin Owls Trail Head  which is now only accessible by traversing a one lane dirt access road across the MacGregor Ranch                              October
