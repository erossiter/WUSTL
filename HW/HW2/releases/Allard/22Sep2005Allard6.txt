                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Senator Allard Secures Additional Emergency Funding for Veterans in Military Construction Bill          September 22nd  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator    R Colorado  announced that the Senate has approved more than  70 7 billion in funding for veterans  including  1 9 billion in emergency healthcare funding  in the 2006 Military Construction and Veterans Affairs funding bill  which passed unanimously on Thursday     We owe our veterans a tremendous debt for their service to our country  and Congress has an obligation to meet their healthcare needs     said Senator Allard  a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Military Construction and Veterans Affairs     The Department of Veterans Affairs needs more support as our veterans return from combat abroad  and the Senate has stepped up to the plate to make sure that both the short and long term needs of our veterans are met      The  70 7 billion approved by the Senate includes an additional  1 9 billion in emergency funding for veterans   medical care on top of the  1 5 billion emergency appropriation provided earlier this year   Early this summer  the Department of Veterans Affairs told Congress that its healthcare costs had grown faster than expected and that the agency was confronted with a major shortfall in its budget this year  and was probably facing a similar crisis next year   Congress responded with an emergency appropriation of  1 5 billion                              September 2005 Press Releases
