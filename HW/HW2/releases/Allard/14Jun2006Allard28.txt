          ALLARD STRIKES DEAL WITH SENATE ARMED SERVICES COMMITTEE ON PINON CANYON MANEUVER SITE EXPANSION Introduces Amendment to DOD Authorization Bill         June 14th  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator Allard  R Colorado  has struck a deal with the Senate Armed Services Committee and offered an amendment on Wednesday to the Fiscal Year 2007 Defense Authorization bill in order to get answers form the Pentagon on whether or not to move forward with the Pinon Canyon Maneuver site   Senator Allard expects the amendment to be approved with in the next week by the full Senate     The expansion of Pinon Canyon was a key issue raised by several community leaders during my recent town meetings in Southeastern Colorado in April     said Senator Allard     There has been an absence of information pertaining to the Army  s proposal    Fort Carson has done a great job of reaching out and providing what information it could to the local communities   However  the Pentagon has not been as forthcoming        I believe the Congress and  more importantly  the local communities in Southeastern Colorado need more information before we can decide whether this proposed expansion is necessary and appropriate     Senator Allard said      I have offered an amendment to the Fiscal Year 2007 Defense Authorization that should get the Army  s attention     said Senator Allard     My amendment prohibits the Army from going forward with any proposal that would expand Pinon Canyon until the Army provides the information that I and many community leaders have requested      Allard  s Amendment to the 2007 DOD Authorization bill requests the following information                  Fort Carson  s current and future training requirements              Whether the Army can complete the expansion with willing sellers              Whether alternatives were considered that do not require the expansion of Pinon Canyon              The economic impact on local communities              The environmental impact of the expansion              The estimated costs of the expansion              Whether the options for compensating local communities for the loss of property tax revenues were considered              Whether options for the expansion can be carried out with transactions such as land exchanges  leases  or the purchase of easements with willing sellers      The Pentagon needs to provide this information as soon as possible so we can decide whether the project is in the best interests of the Army and the people of Southeastern Colorado     Senator Allard said
