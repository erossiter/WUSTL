          ALLARD SECURES  2 8 MILLION IN AGRICULTURE RESEARCH APPROPRIATIONS FOR COLORADO          June 22nd  2006   Washington  D C    U S  Senator   R CO   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced that he has secured more than  2 8 million in the FY 2007 Agriculture Appropriations Bill for Colorado agricultural research projects  Colorado State University secured the lion  s share of the agriculture research funding  receiving more than  2 2 million     These funds represent a significant investment in our agricultural future not only in Colorado  but in the nation at large     said Senator Allard     I am proud of the first rate agricultural research that Colorado institutions continue to produce       I am proud that Senator Allard continues to recognize Colorado State University s excellence in agricultural research   said Colorado State President Larry Penley   His support through this funding allows our researchers to focus on the important issues facing Colorado agricultural interests and those facing the nation    The FY 2007 Agriculture Appropriations Act includes the following funding requested by Senator Allard         871 000 for the National Beef Cattle Genetic Evaluation Consortium comprised of Colorado State University in Fort Collins                        Cornell University  and the University of Georgia   The project is located at CSU          809 000 for infectious disease research to the Center for Economically Important Infectious Animal Diseases at Colorado State                       University   The center is the only institution conducing multidisciplinary research focused entirely on animal diseases that either                       cause  or have the potential to cause serious economic losses for animal agriculture such as West Nile Virus  foot and mouth                       disease and BSE          303 000 for Russian Wheat Aphid research at CSU   The direct value of the Colorado Wheat Crop is estimated at  260 million                        annually   The Russian Wheat Aphid causes direct losses of more than  11 million in Colorado each year          250 000 for Onion Thrip Research at Colorado State University          250 000 for Waste Water Irrigation Research in Akron  Colorado   As Colorado struggles through an extended drought period this                       research continues to become more important          172 000 for the Livestock Marketing Information Center located in Denver          150 000 to the State of Colorado for Chronic Wasting Disease research
