          ALLARD RECEIVES UPDATE ON ICE OFFICE IN COLORADO SPRINGS          October 26th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    In a letter sent today to Senator   R Colo   Assistant Secretary of the U S  Immigration and Customs Enforcement  ICE   Julie L  Myers  assured the Senator that ICE   supports the establishment of an office in Colorado Springs       In the letter  Assistant Secretary Myers highlights the fact that three ICE agents have been sent to Colorado Springs to respond to increasing law enforcement needs and will operate out of the Colorado Springs Police Department until office space is located for an ICE sub office      I commend the Assistant Secretary for responding to the needs of Coloradans     said Senator Allard    Establishing an ICE sub office in Colorado Springs will provide additional resources and a vital law enforcement presence as well as ensure the integrity of the rule of law       Allard has worked to secure these resources in consultation with El Paso County Commissioner Jim Bensberg and local law enforcement officials    We remain committed to establishing an ICE office in El Paso County   said Commissioner Jim Bensberg   Our citizens overwhelmingly support this effort and I am pleased that ICE recognizes our need to address jail overcrowding in Colorado s most populous county     Last year Allard successfully added an amendment to the final 2007 Homeland Security Appropriations Bill requiring a cost evaluation for the allocation of additional resources to Colorado Springs  In a response to a requirement in the FY 2007 Homeland Security Appropriations Act  ICE surveyed their operational needs  determining that there is a sufficient need to locate operations in Colorado Springs                              October 2007 Press
