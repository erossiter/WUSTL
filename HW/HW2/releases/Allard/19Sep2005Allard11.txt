          Department of Defense Reports Funding Being Spent  As Ordered  on Chemical Weapons Cleanup Allard language in supplemental bill forces DoD to move forward  as well as report         September 19th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C  B Defense Under Secretary Kenneth Kreig reported to Congress Monday that funding is flowing as required to chemical weapons cleanup projects in Pueblo  Colorado and Blue Grass  Kentucky   The report was mandated by a provision included in the March supplemental spending bill by Senator   R Colorado  and Senator Mitch McConnell  R Kentucky     The report states that  as of July 31  2005   16 million of the funds previously withheld by DoD had been sent to Pueblo  and that an additional  17 million for the Pueblo cleanup project was obligated during the month of August  Finally  the report projects that by the end of this month the department will be in compliance with the statutorily mandated goal of obligating  100 million for the entire Assembled Chemical Weapons Alternatives  ACWA  program  which includes Pueblo Depot      At last the Department of Defense is doing what the bill mandated  and that is spend money to move the Pueblo Depot cleanup forward    as Congress has intended all along     Senator Allard said    Senator Allard and Senator McConnell added a provision to the FY 2005 Supplemental Appropriations Act that ensured that the  372 3 million in funding budgeted in earlier years would not be transferred from Pueblo or Blue Grass to other sites  In addition  it directed the Department of Defense to send at least  100 million to the ACWA sites within four months of the bill  s enactment  and prohibited the Department from conducting a study on the transportation of chemical weapons across state lines      The report accompanying the final bill also directed DoD to update Congress regularly on how the funding is being spent     The provisions we included in the bill were critical in turning this project around  and I  m pleased that DoD is in compliance with the requirements     Senator Allard said      The health and safety of the people in Pueblo must be our paramount consideration     Senator Allard said    I will do everything in my power to make sure DoD moves forward with this project                                 September 2005 Press Releases
