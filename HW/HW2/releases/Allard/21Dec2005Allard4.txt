          SENATOR ALLARD SECURES  4 6 MILLION IN FUNDING FOR NETWORK INFORMATION AND SPACE SECURITY CENTER AT UNIVERSITY OF COLORADO AT COLORADO SPRINGS          December 21st  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator   R Colorado   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  said he again has secured  4 6 million in funding for the UCCS Network Information and Space Security Center  NISSC  in the final 2006 Defense Appropriations bill approved by the U S  Senate Wednesday and the U S  House last week     This appropriation will allow for the NISSC to sharpen its cutting edge education and training programs which are vital to homeland defense  I am happy we were able to continue to provide this funding for a third year     said Senator Allard     It will allow us to continue to engage university faculty  academics and industry partners in this high priority research     Senator Allard said      The federal appropriation of  4 6M for the Colorado Springs campus   Network Information and Space Security Center is great news for the campus and CU   The funding represents a continued investment by the federal government in what CU is doing in cyber security and space education and research   We are extraordinarily grateful for Senator Allard s work and that of his staff to help CU further critical research and education initiatives that will place Colorado as a leader in the space industry     said CU President Hank Brown     I am deeply appreciative of Senator Allard s support for the Network Information and Space Security Center based at UCCS and the university s goal of working on important homeland security issues     said UCCS Chancellor Pam Shockley Zalabak   The Network Information and Space Security Center plans to use theses resources to provide education and training to a broad range of individuals such as first responders  small business operators  the space cadre and K 12 educators                              December 2005 Press Releases
