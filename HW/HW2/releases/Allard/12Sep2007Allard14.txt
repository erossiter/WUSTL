Contact  Steve WymerEmail             SENATE COMMITTEE HOLDS HEARING ON ALLARD  S BACA NATIONAL WILDLIFE REFUGE PURPOSE BILL          September 12th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    The U S  Senate Energy and Natural Resources National Parks Subcommittee held a hearing this week on U S  Sen    s  R    Colo   Baca National Wildlife Refuge Purpose bill  S 127   This legislation would give the U S  Fish and Wildlife Service additional management tools to oversee the refuge       This legislation will clarify the purpose of the refuge and provide the Fish and Wildlife Service with the tools to run the Baca Wildlife Refuge in a way that achieves the most beneficial use of this wonderful natural resource     said Allard    It will ensure that the refuge is managed in a manner that emphasizes the importance of the preservation of native wildlife habitat so Coloradans can enjoy the natural opportunities it offers      The Baca National Wildlife Refuge consists of 92 500 acres of wetlands  sage brush and riparian lands adjacent to the Great Sand Dunes National Park in southern Colorado  The legislation that created the national park  sponsored and pushed through Congress by Allard and former Third District U S  Rep  Scott McInnis in 2000  authorized the federal acquisition of the Baca Refuge lands and determined that the area offers unique education  wildlife  recreation and other diverse resources     My bill is the culmination of more than a year  s worth of discussions with local and federal officials  The Nature Conservancy and the Fish and Wildlife Service     added Allard    I am encouraged by the committee  s hearing on my legislation and I am optimistic that we will be able to move this bill through the legislative process by the end of the year      Allard also secured  3 million for state Highway 150 to improve visitor access to the Great Sand Dunes National Park in the 2008 Transportation and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill  which was approved by the U S  Senate on Wednesday                              September 2007 Press Releases
