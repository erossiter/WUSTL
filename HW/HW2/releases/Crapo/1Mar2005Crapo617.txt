 Washington  DC     As the Senate Budget Committee prepares to begin consideration of the Fiscal Year 2006 federal budget resolution  Idaho Senator Mike Crapo will discuss those proposals with Idahoans during the March airing of his    Capitol Watch    program    Crapo is a member of the Senate Budget Committee  which will vote on a proposed budget later next week  as well as the Senate Finance Committee  which will review tax reform and simplification proposals and plans to reform Social Security           Capitol Watch    airs statewide on Idaho cable television stations the second Tuesday of each month   Idahoans are invited to call in and ask questions of Senator Crapo during the program  which runs from 7 to 7 30 p m  Mountain 6 to 6 30 p m  Pacific on Tuesday  March 8th      Crapo will also discuss his efforts to improve and streamline the Endangered Species Act   His bill to create wilderness and preserve ranching in Owyhee County through the    Owyhee Initiative    will also be a topic for the March 8th program           Capitol Watch    will air in the following cities over the following cable channels                 Boise Nampa Caldwell     11                Coeur d   Alene                     19                Idaho City                             37                Idaho Falls Blackfoot        19                Lewiston                               13             Moscow                                11                Pocatello                              22                Twin Falls                             60      The program is sponsored by the Idaho Cable Telecommunications Association  ICTA  and local cable systems throughout Idaho   Local cable systems provide access and the channel numbers may vary from month to month   Due to equipment upgrades or scheduling conflicts  the program is not always available on every cable system in the state
