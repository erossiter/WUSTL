                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Sen  Jeff Sessions Announces Congressional Passage Of Deep Gulf Exploration Oil and Gas Revenue Sharing Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      President Bush Expected To Sign Legislation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Saturday  December 9  2006         WASHINGTON    Historic legislation that would allow Alabama and three other states to share part of the federal revenues generated by new oil and gas production in the Gulf of Mexico cleared Congress early today  said U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL                  The Gulf of Mexico Energy Security Act  passed by the Senate in August  was included in a massive tax and trade package that is now on its way to the White House  where President Bush is expected to sign it  The Senate passed the bill in August                     There  s nothing we can do this year that will have more impact in helping us to contain the price of gasoline and natural gas than getting this bill signed into law     said Sessions  who helped negotiate the measure with fellow Gulf state senators and the Senate leadership    When we buy our energy from abroad  we transfer our wealth to those foreign countries  many of which are unstable and hostile to the United States  Enactment of this measure will keep that wealth at home  creating American jobs and revenues for the American treasury                    The bill will open energy production in about 8 million acres of the Gulf of Mexico that lie to the south and east of the Alabama coast  The drilling would occur in Gulf waters at least 125 miles off the Alabama shoreline                 For the first time  Alabama and three other energy producing states    Louisiana  Texas and Mississippi    will share 37 5 percent of the federal revenues generated by the lease sale and production royalties of oil and natural gas outside state waters                 Although numbers are not yet available  Sessions said the bill will result in hundreds of millions of dollars in revenue for Alabama in future years  Distribution among the four states will be based on proximity to production  with historical production levels taken into account                 The legislation is expected to produce more than 1 3 billion barrels of oil and at least six trillion cubic feet of natural gas                 In addition to the share allocated to the four energy producing states  the bill  allows 50 percent of the total revenues to remain in the U S  treasury  with the remaining 12 5 percent dedicated to the Land and Water Conservation Fund                   We  ve worked with Gov  Riley and the Alabama Oil and Gas Board to ensure that Alabama gets its fair share     Sessions said    We  think it  s only fair that Alabama and those Gulf states already producing oil and gas are rewarded for their willingness to bear a disproportionate share of our country  s energy needs                                 2004
