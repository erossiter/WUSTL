                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Senate Ag Committee Adopts Sessions  Water Reservoir Bill as Part of Comprehensive Farm Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Thursday  October 25  2007          WASHINGTON  The Senate Committee on Agriculture today adopted a measure championed by U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL  to the comprehensive farm bill that would assist agricultural producers in the construction of water reservoirs and irrigation systems on their farms    The committees action is a significant step toward passage of Sessions reservoir bill by the full Senate     The measure  called the Regional Ground and Surface Water Enhancement Program Act of 2007  would authorize the United States Department of Agriculture  USDA  to provide  100 million in grants each year to agricultural producers to assist in the construction of collection ponds and implementation of new irrigation systems that would improve water quantity and quality    I am pleased that members of the Senate Agriculture Committee recognized the value in assisting our farmers in construction of water reservoirs  The recent drought in Alabama has shown the importance of having irrigation systems in place that can enable farmers to mitigate crop damage during times of drought  Some of the crop losses could have been avoided if more farmers had the infrastructure necessary to capture rain during the rainy season and bank it for later use     Under the legislation  federal grants would be awarded nationally on a competitive basis  with a priority given to those projects that would result in a savings of ground or surface water on agricultural lands  In addition to individual producers  multiple entities  including state and local governments  could partner together to apply for grant funding under the bill    The grant program would be part of the federal Environmental Quality Incentives Program  a division of USDA     The construction of water reservoirs would also facilitate the growth of energy crops such as corn and soybeans  which are critical to the development of Americas renewable energy industry    Rep  Terry Everett  R AL  introduced a similar measure in the U S  House of Representatives  That measure was adopted as part of a comprehensive farm policy bill which passed the House earlier this year                              2004 United States Senator Jeff Sessions  Alabama                     All rights reserved
