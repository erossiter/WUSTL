                                                                               President Bush To Appoint Birmingham Businessman Lee Styslinger To the President s Export Council                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Friday  February 17  2006      WASHINGTON     President Bush has announced his intention to appoint Birmingham businessman Lee J  Styslinger III to the President  s Export Council  the principal national advisory committee on international trade  U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL  said today                Sessions  who recommended Styslinger  s appointment to Bush  called Styslinger a   patriotic American and distinguished Alabama businessman uniquely qualified    to serve on the Council               Styslinger is president and chief executive officer of Altec Industries  an exceptional Alabama company which designs  manufactures and markets equipment for the electric and telecommunications industries                 Lee will bring a much needed U S  based private sector manufacturing perspective to the Council     Sessions said    Altec sells and services its equipment in more than 120 countries providing Lee with extensive  first hand experience dealing with both the positive and negative aspects of export trade policy and its bureaucracy                  The President  s Export Council advises the president of government policies and programs that affect U S  trade performance  promotes export expansion and provides a forum for discussing and resolving trade related problems among the business  industrial  agricultural  labor and government sectors                 It will be good to have someone in this important position who knows Alabama and our business climate and growth potential     Sessions said               The president appoints 28 private sector members to the Council  The Council also is composed of five members of the United States Senate and five members of the U S  House of Representatives  along with a number of cabinet secretaries and government trade and banking officials                            2004 United States Senator Jeff Sessions
