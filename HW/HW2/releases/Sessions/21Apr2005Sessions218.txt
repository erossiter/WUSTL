                                                                               Senate Passes  81 Billion War Spending Bill That Includes  Sessions   Enhanced Military Death Benefits Proposal                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Thursday  April 21  2005      WASHINGTON    The Senate today passed an  81 billion supplemental war spending bill that includes Sen  Jeff Sessions   proposal to pay up to  500 000 for military personnel killed since combat operations began in Afghanistan in 2001       The spending package to fund military operations in Iraq and Afghanistan would authorize an increase in the death gratuity to  100 000 from  12 420 for all service members who die while on active duty  and the Servicemen  s Group Life Insurance maximum benefit would rise to  400 000 from  250 000       The enhanced benefits would be retroactive to October 7  2001  the day that combat operations began in Afghanistan       There is no amount of compensation that can replace the loss of a loved one   Sessions said   Not for a soldier  not for a police officer  not for a teacher or a fireman  However  our military service members volunteer to leave their families and engage in a very difficult and dangerous campaign to defeat terrorists and secure peace and prosperity not only for America but for countless millions around the world  The enhanced death gratuity and life insurance benefit included in this bill reflect the risks and dangers faced by our service men and women as they serve us around the world     According to the emergency war spending bill  military personnel who have died since October 7  2001  would receive a one time lump sum payment of  238 000  replicating the increased SGLI coverage of  150 000 and additional death gratuity of  88 000        The supplemental also would require the military to provide written notification to a spouse of a service member who elects less than the maximum SGLI coverage of  400 000  Sessions   original proposal had included the expanded death gratuity and life insurance benefits for military personnel killed in combat and training  but it was expanded during floor debate to include all military personnel       Senate and House negotiators are expected to act quickly to iron out differences in each chambers   bill before sending a final version to President Bush for his signature  The Pentagon says its needs the money by the first week of May                            2004 United States Senator Jeff Sessions  Alabama                     All
