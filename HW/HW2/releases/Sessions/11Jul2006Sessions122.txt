                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Sessions Comments On Improved Budget Deficit Figures                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Tuesday  July 11  2006        WASHINGTON    U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL   a member of the Senate Budget Committee  made the following statement today in response to news that the federal budget deficit will be 30 percent lower than the Administration  s February forecast  The White House Office of Management and Budget  OMB  estimates that this year  s deficit will be  296 billion  or 2 3 percent of GDP                       Today s new OMB deficit projection is a very encouraging sign that our pro growth tax policies are working  Clearly  these numbers indicate that real progress is being made on bringing our country s deficit under control  Federal spending is still too high  however  and more work certainly needs to be done  These numbers are encouraging because they show that if we contain the growth of spending  balancing the budget is certainly achievable  Business as usual spending increases cannot continue                      We should build on the progress we have made by enacting the budget process reform package that cleared the Senate Budget Committee last month  This package  which includes the president s line item veto plan  biennial budgeting  and a number of additional reforms  will give Congress the tools it needs to finally lick our current deficit problems                               2004 United States Senator Jeff Sessions  Alabama                     All rights reserved
