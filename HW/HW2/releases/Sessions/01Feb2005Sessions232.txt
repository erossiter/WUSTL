                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Sen  Sessions Welcomes President Bush  s Support Of Proposal To Enhance Military Death Benefits                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Tuesday  February 1  2005      WASHINGTON    Welcoming the Bush administration  s endorsement of his enhanced military death benefits bill  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL  today suggested that the White House should include the proposal in an emergency supplemental spending bill that will clear Congress in the near future       The Senate Armed Services Committee held a hearing this morning on Sessions   proposal to raise  the death gratuity to  100 000 from  12 420 for military personnel killed in combat and combat related training  and increase the Servicemen s Group Life Insurance maximum benefit to  400 000 from  250 000  The increased death gratuity and life insurance would be retroactive to October 7  2001  to cover those killed while deployed in Operation Iraqi Freedom and Operation Enduring Freedom in Afghanistan        I think the opportunity that we have today is to step forward as a nation  to be generous to those who have given their lives for their country  and to take care of their families in an effective way   Sessions  a committee member  said at the hearing   All soldiers who serve their country and put their lives at risk need to know that if something happens to them  their families will be well taken care of  That  s the bond we have with our military men and women and their families        David Chu  undersecretary of defense for personnel and readiness  announced the administration  s support for Sessions   bill  and he said the administration would consider adding the proposal to the forthcoming emergency supplemental spending bill to fund the war on terrorism in Iraq and Afghanistan  That bill will be sent to Capitol Hill next week and is expected to pass Congress and be signed into law within weeks       Chu said the proposal will be included in President Bush  s Fiscal Year 2006 defense budget proposal  but he agreed with Sessions that making it a part of the supplemental bill would speed payments to families of service members killed in the combat theater or in training missions for combat       Under the proposal  the military would provide  150 000 of insurance for service members serving in a combat zone and electing coverage under SGLI  A second provision would require the service member to discuss opting out with a spouse as is done with the Survivor Benefit Program       Sessions   proposal would index the death gratuity and life insurance benefit to the rate of annual pay raises  The Defense Department estimated the cost of Sessions   bill at about  460 million the first year  though that figure would be expected to drop in future years by more than half once retroactive benefits are paid        Sessions last year got a provision included in the Fiscal Year 2005 defense authorization bill directing the Defense Department                            2004 United States Senator Jeff Sessions  Alabama
