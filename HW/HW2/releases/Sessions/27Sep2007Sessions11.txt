                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Sen  Sessions Announces  1 Million Grant for Children of Prisoners Mentor Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Thursday  September 27  2007        WASHINGTON  U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL  announced today that the Alabama Department of Child Abuse and Neglect Prevention will receive a  1 million federal grant to support a program that provides mentors for children with incarcerated parents   The funding will allow the Alabama Department of Child Abuse and Neglect Prevention to continue running the Mentoring Children of Promise program  which is a collaboration of service agencies and volunteers throughout the state working to support children of prisoners  The agencies work together to identify children of incarcerated parents and match them with qualified and approved mentors    The grant was awarded by the U S  Department of Health and Human Services Family and Youth Services Bureau  The Mentoring Children of Promise program serves children in 22 Alabama counties   I am pleased to announce this grant for the Mentoring Children of Promise program  said Sessions  There are  unfortunately  too many children in our state who are lacking positive role models because their parents are incarcerated  This worthy program provides mentors for those vulnerable children who can greatly benefit from having a caring adult figure in their lives                             2004 United States Senator Jeff Sessions  Alabama                     All rights reserved
