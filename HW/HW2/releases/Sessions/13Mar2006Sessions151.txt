                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Sessions Says Kia  s Move To Georgia Will Also Boost Alabama  s Economy                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Monday  March 13  2006      WASHINGTON    U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL  today issued the following statement regarding  Kia Motors  s announcement that it will open an auto assembly plant in Georgia         The next best thing to having yet another major automobile manufacturer announce plans to open a plant in Alabama is to learn that an auto producer will locate in a neighboring state  I welcome Kia Motors  s announcement that it will open a  1 2 billion plant in West Point  Georgia  not only creating 5 500 good jobs for the Peach State but also generating jobs for Alabama and nearby Alabama automotive suppliers  Kia  s parent company  the Hyundai Motor Company  is already producing automobiles in Montgomery  Kia  s move to West Point  just across the Alabama state line  will provide a huge boost to the economies of East Alabama and West Georgia  and further cements the notion that the Southeast has become the new American auto manufacturing hub                                2004 United States Senator Jeff Sessions  Alabama                     All rights reserved
