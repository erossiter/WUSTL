                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Sen  Jeff Sessions Lauds Passage Of Broadcast Decency Enforcement Legislation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Friday  May 19  2006      WASHINGTON    U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL  announced today that the Senate passed   The Broadcast Decency Enforcement Act     legislation that would increase the penalty against television and radio broadcasters who transmit obscene  indecent or profane material                  The legislation would increase Federal Communications Commission fines levied against broadcasters to  325 000 from  32 500 per violation  Sessions was an early co sponsor of the bill  which was authored by Sen  Sam Brownback  R KS   The measure passed by voice vote Thursday evening  The House has passed similar legislation and the bills will go to conference                  Sessions said                   I share the concerns of parents across Alabama who are worried about the increasing amount of inappropriate material pushed on their children when they watch TV  I strongly believe that we must safeguard our children from obscene  indecent and vulgar material  and I am pleased that the Senate passed legislation last night to do that  Raising the fine to  325 000 per violation will give the FCC the teeth it needs to enforce the law and make broadcasters think twice before they air questionable material                               2004 United States Senator Jeff Sessions  Alabama
