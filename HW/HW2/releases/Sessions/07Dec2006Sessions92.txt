                                                                               U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions Announces A  400 000 Federal Grant For The Sixteenth Street Baptist Church                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Thursday  December 7  2006                    WASHINGTON    U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL  today announced that the Sixteenth Street Baptist Church in Birmingham will receive a  400 000 grant from the National Park Service to replace the roof and repair structural damage caused by moisture                  The Sixteenth Street Baptist Church is a prominent symbol of our nation  s civil rights history  It served as a lecture hall and social center for African Americans during the Segregation Era  and functioned as a headquarters for civil rights meetings and rallies  On September 15  1963  members of the Ku Klux Klan bombed the church  killing four young girls                    The Sixteenth Street Baptist Church became known throughout the world on that terrible day in 1963     said Sessions    It is important that we never forget our past  both the good and the bad  The church serves as a cultural and historical landmark  and this grant will allow renovations that will help preserve it for generations to come                      Sessions  s office worked with the Sixteenth Street Baptist Church to secure the funding from the National Park Service  In 2004  Sessions sponsored legislation to commemorate the lives of the four victims of the deadly bombing
