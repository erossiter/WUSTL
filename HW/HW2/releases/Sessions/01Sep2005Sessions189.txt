                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Sessions Announces  4 Million Federal Grant To Help With Katrina Recovery In Alabama                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Thursday  September 1  2005      WASHINGTON    U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL  today announced a  4 million Department of Labor grant to fund about 350 temporary jobs to dislocated workers to help in the clean up and recovery associated with the damage caused by Hurricane Katrina        The Labor Department National Emergency Grant  given to the Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs  will be used for demolition  renovation and reconstruction of public structures  facilities and lands  according to the Labor Department        Hurricane Katrina left many of our residents in need of immediate assistance  and I am glad to announce that Secretary Chao has moved quickly to help out our state     Sessions said       The Labor Department said the grant funds also will be used to perform work on the homes of economically disadvantaged individuals who are eligible for the federally funded weatherization program  with priority given to services for the elderly and individuals with disabilities         Alabamians affected by Hurricane Katrina need rapid assistance in clean up and recovery efforts     said Chao    This grant will also provide temporary jobs as workers are hired to provide food  clothing  shelter and humanitarian assistance for victims
