                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Senate Adopts Sessions  Amendment to Ensure Fair Tanker Competition                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Tuesday  September 18  2007        WASHINGTON  The U S  Senate last night adopted an amendment  offered by U S  Sen  Jeff Sessions  R AL   to direct the Air Force to hold a full and open competition for its next air refueling tanker     The provision was adopted as part of the Department of Defense Authorization bill that is now pending before the Senate    The measure  agreed to by unanimous consent in a package of amendments  expresses the sense of Congress that the Air Force should hold a full and open competition to choose the best possible joint aerial refueling capability at the most reasonable price    The measure also states that the Air Force should be discouraged from taking any actions that would limit the ability of any potential manufacturer of the KC X tanker from competing for the contract    The KC X tanker competition is a critically important acquisition program for the Air Force  I am pleased that my colleagues in the Senate unanimously agreed that the goal of this program should be to select the best aircraft through a robust and open competition that is focused on the capabilities of the airplane  Sessions said  This amendment sends the message that merit  not politics  should be the basis for the selection  I will continue to closely watch this process as we move forward     Final passage of the Defense Authorization bill is expected later this week                            2004 United States Senator Jeff Sessions  Alabama                     All rights
