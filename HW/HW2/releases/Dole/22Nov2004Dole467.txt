SEN. DOLE ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR NEW BERN, MOREHEAD CITY AREA PROJECTS
  Sen. Elizabeth Dole announced the passage of the Omnibus Appropriations bill for Fiscal Year 2005. The $388 billion federal spending bill contains millions of dollars designated for important projects in North Carolina.  
   The Fiscal Year 2005 appropriations bill allocates funds to North Carolina for building and expanding vital infrastructure, establishing and growing educational programs and research, strengthening our law enforcement agencies, and improving health care facilities. Listed below are the appropriations for the New Bern and Morehead City area and other important projects in North Carolina. 
   New Bern and Morehead City Area Projects 
   Atlantic Intracoastal Waterway $2 million 
   Morehead City Harbor $4 million 
   Morehead City Harbor Sec 933 $3 million 
  Carteret County is participating in the second phase of the Section 933 Project that enables the distribution of beach quality sand from the upland disposal site of Brandt Island to be distributed over a greater area of Bogue Banks. 
   Bogue Beach $75,000 
   Graveyard of the Atlantic Museum $100,000 
  The only U.S. museum dedicated to the history of shipwrecks. Due in part to its location in Cape Hatteras, North Carolina where an estimated 3.0 million visitors tour the Outer Banks each year, the GOAM is uniquely positioned to provide the public with full understanding of the cultural and historical significance of our Nation's maritime past. 
   Tryon Palace $150,000 
  Funds will be used to remove contaminated soil and install a groundwater monitoring system. 
   Other NC Highlights 
   * Triangle Transit Authority regional rail project - $20 million 
   * Charlotte South Corridor Light Rail project - $30 million 
   * Wilmington Harbor Deepening - $22 million 
   * National Textile Center - $10 million 
   The Center is involved in developing new innovative materials, training personnel and seeking ways to strengthen the nation's textile research and education. It is composed of the textile departments of Auburn, Clemson, Cornell, Georgia Tech, UMASS-Dartmouth, NC State, Philadelphia University, and UC-Davis.

   * Blue Ridge Parkway Visitors Center - $3 million 
   * Smokey Mountain National Park infrastructure repairs - $2.7 million
