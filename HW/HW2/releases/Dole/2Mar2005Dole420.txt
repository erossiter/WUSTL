SEN. DOLE ANNOUNCES $2.47 MILLION LOAN FOR PENDER VOLUNTEER EMS AND RESCUE
  Sen. Elizabeth Dole today announced that USDA Rural Development will loan $2,465,000 to Pender Volunteer EMS and Rescue.  
 The funding will help expand rescue and emergency services in the county. 
   "I applaud Pender Volunteer EMS and Rescue for securing this significant funding," said Senator Dole. "Our first responders are there in times of need and are literally on the front lines defending our homeland. It is so important that they have adequate facilities and equipment so that they can safely do their jobs and protect the communities they serve." 
   According to USDA, the funding will be used for the construction of a single story, three bay building for Rocky Point EMS station and a single story one bay building for Maple Hill EMS station. The funding will also provide renovations for Burgaw, Hampstead, Surf City, and Atkinson substations. This project will serve 41,082 residents in this rural area.
