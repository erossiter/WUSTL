SEN. DOLE ANNOUNCES SENATE PASSAGE OF NATIONAL DEFENSE APPROPRIATIONS BILL
  Sen. Elizabeth Dole announced the Senate passage of the Defense Appropriations bill (H.R. 4613).  
 The bill provides $416.2 billion to the Department of Defense for Fiscal Year 2005, including $13.85 million for projects at North Carolina textile companies. 
   "The North Carolina textile industry has a prominent position in the growing field of high-tech military clothing, and our state's universities are gaining a strong reputation in defense research and development," said Senator Dole. "I am so impressed with the quality and innovation behind the growing defense industry in our state, which is able to work hand-in-hand with neighboring military customers." 
   North Carolina textile funding highlights include: 
   * $6.3 million for Ultra-Light Camouflage Net System for Army and Marine forces (Saab Barracuda, Lillington) 
   * $1.25 million for Next Generation Chemical/Biological Agent Protective Garment (Dupont, Fayetteville) 
   * $1.4 million for Odor Signature Reduction Products for Special Forces (Milliken & Company, Columbus and Bostic) 
   * $400,000 for Odor Signature Reduction Base Layer Garment evaluation (Milliken & Company, Columbus and Bostic) 
   * $500,000 for Anti-Microbial/Medical Base Layer Garment Technology (Milliken & Company, Columbus and Bostic) 
   * $1.5 million for Electro-textiles (Milliken & Company, Columbus and Bostic) 
   * $1 million for Smart Apparel for Warriors (Sara Lee, Winston-Salem) 
   * $1.5 million for Advanced Antimicrobial Technology (Microban, Huntersville).
