SEN. DOLE APPLAUDS CONFIRMATION OF THOMAS SCHROEDER TO SERVE AS U.S. DISTRICT COURT JUDGE FOR MIDDLE DISTRICT OF N.C.
  U.S. Sen. Elizabeth Dole applauded the Senate's confirmation of Thomas Schroeder to serve as a U.S. District Judge in the Middle District of North Carolina.  
   "Tom Schroeder is a highly accomplished attorney with an impressive record of legal achievement and community service," said Dole. "I am confident that as a member of the federal judiciary, he will serve the people of North Carolina with honor and distinction." 
   Schroeder is from Winston-Salem and is a leading partner at Womble, Carlyle, Sandridge, and Rice, PLLC. He has briefed and argued many cases in the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Fourth Circuit and the North Carolina Court of Appeals and has handled matters in all federal trial courts in North Carolina and in the U.S. Courts of Appeals for the 11th and D.C. Circuits. Additionally, Tom has been at the forefront of some of the largest litigation cases in North Carolina and the country. He received the top rating by The Best Lawyers in America for 2006 and 2007 and was selected as a "North Carolina Super Lawyer" by Law and Politics magazine. 
   Schroeder is a graduate of the University of Kansas, the University of Cincinnati's Conservatory of Music and the University of Notre Dame Law School. 
   The Senate unanimously approved Schroeder's nomination late Friday evening.
