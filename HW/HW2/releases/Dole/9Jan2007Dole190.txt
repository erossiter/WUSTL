SEN. DOLE APPLAUDS NOMINATIONS OF FOUR NORTH CAROLINIANS TO U.S. DISTRICT JUDGESHIPS
  Sen. Elizabeth Dole today applauded President Bush's re-nomination of four North Carolinians to be U.S. District Judges: Thomas A. Farr for the Eastern District, William Lindsay Osteen, Jr. and Thomas D. Schroeder for the Middle District, and Martin Karl Reidinger for the Western District. Dole recommended Farr, Osteen, Schroeder and Reidinger to the president.  
   "I was proud to recommend these outstanding individuals to serve as U.S. district judges in North Carolina and am delighted that President Bush has re-nominated them," said Dole. "I congratulate them on this well deserved honor, and I will work with my colleagues to swiftly move these nominations through the Senate confirmation process." 
   Background on Thomas A. Farr 
   Farr currently is a shareholder in the law firm of Ogletree, Deakins, Nash, Smoak & Stewart, P.C., in Raleigh. Farr received his law degree from Emory University and obtained a Master of Laws degree from Georgetown University. 
   Background on William Lindsay Osteen, Jr. 
   Osteen has been a member of Adams & Osteen, a Greensboro law firm, since 1987. He is a graduate of the college and law school at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. 
   Background on Thomas D. Schroeder 
   Schroeder is from Winston-Salem and a litigation partner at Womble, Carlyle, Sandridge, and Rice, PLLC. He is a graduate of the University of Notre Dame Law School. 
   Background on Martin Karl Reidinger 
   Reidinger currently has a successful litigation practice in Asheville. He earned his undergraduate and law degrees from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.
