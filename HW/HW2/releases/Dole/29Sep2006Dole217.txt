SEN. DOLE APPLAUDS NOMINATIONS OF NORTH CAROLINIANS TO U.S. DISTRICT JUDGESHIPS
  Sen. Elizabeth Dole today applauded President Bush's nominations of three North Carolinians to be U.S. District Judges: William Lindsay Osteen, Jr. for the Middle District, Martin Karl Reidinger for the Western District, and Thomas D. Schroeder for the Middle District. Dole recommended Osteen, Reidinger, and Schroeder to the president.  
   "I was proud to recommend these outstanding individuals to serve as U.S. district judges in North Carolina," said Dole. "I congratulate them on this well deserved honor, and I will work with my colleagues to swiftly move these nominations through the Senate confirmation process." 
   Background on William Lindsay Osteen, Jr. 
   Osteen has been a member of Adams & Osteen, a Greensboro law firm, since 1987. He is a graduate of the college and law school at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. 
   Background on Martin Karl Reidinger 
   Reidinger currently has a successful litigation practice in Asheville. He earned his undergraduate and law degrees from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. 
   Background on Thomas D. Schroeder
   Schroeder is from Winston-Salem and a litigation partner at Womble, Carlyle, Sandridge, and Rice, PLLC. He is a graduate of the University of Notre Dame Law School.
