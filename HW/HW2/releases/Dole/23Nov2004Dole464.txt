SEN. DOLE ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR WILIMINGTON AREA PROJECTS
  Sen. Elizabeth Dole announced the passage of the Omnibus Appropriations bill for Fiscal Year 2005. The $388 billion federal spending bill contains millions of dollars designated for important projects in the Wilmington area and throughout North Carolina, including $22 million for deepening the Wilmington Harbor.  
   "I am pleased we were able to secure funding for deepening the Wilmington Harbor," said Senator Dole. "The Port of Wilmington provides vital shipping services and is important to the state economy. We must ensure it operates efficiently and remains competitive." 
   The Fiscal Year 2005 appropriations bill allocates funds to North Carolina for building and expanding vital infrastructure, establishing and growing educational programs and research, strengthening our law enforcement agencies, and improving health care facilities. Listed below are the appropriations for the Wilmington area and other important projects in North Carolina. 
   Wilmington Area Projects 
   Wilmington Harbor Deepening $22 million 
   Wilmington Harbor $8,157,000 
   Atlantic Intracoastal Waterway $2 million 
   Morehead City Harbor $4 million 
   Surf City and North Topsail Beach $214,000 
  Shoreline erosion, coupled with recent hurricanes, has severely damaged the dune system and structures in Surf City and Topsail Beach. Funds will be used to continue the feasibility phase protection that involves real estate coordination, economic, and environmental impacts. 
   West Onslow Beach and New River Inlet $175,000 
  The authorized project for West Onslow Beach and New River Inlet, North Carolina provides for flood damage protection along Topsail Island. 
   Brunswick County Beaches Shore Protection $200,000 
   Bogue Beach $75,000 
   Lockwoods Folly River $500,000 
  Funding will be used to continue operations and maintenance. 
   New Topsail Inlet $200,000 
  Federal funding will be used to continue operation and maintenance. 
   Cape Fear River Above Wilmington $123,000 
  The project extends from the confluence of the Northeast Cape Fear River at Wilmington to Fayetteville. The 115 mile project has varying depths of 8 to 25 feet. 
   UNC Wilmington Mariculture $320,000 
  UNC-Wilmington has developed an impressive mariculture facility and research program in North Carolina. The facility hopes to expand its mariculture program to investigate how to increase the productivity of shellfish and crustaceans, how to take advantage of different offshore culture techniques, and how to better address social-economic issues faced by fishermen and farmers.
