SEN. DOLE ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR CATAWBA COUNTY AREA PROJECTS
 Sen. Elizabeth Dole announced the passage of the Omnibus Appropriations bill for Fiscal Year 2005. The $388 billion federal spending bill contains million of dollars designated for important projects in North Carolina, including $1 million for upgrading the U.S. 321 corridor of Burke, Caldwell and Catawba Counties.  
   "I am pleased we were able to secure funding for improving U.S. 321 at the corridor of Burke, Caldwell and Catawba Counties," said Dole. "It is important that commuters and business travelers can safely and efficiently travel this route. With travel over this bridge projected to increase, these upgrades will allow the area to accommodate greater traffic volume." 
   The Fiscal Year 2005 appropriations bill allocates funds to North Carolina for building and expanding vital infrastructure, establishing and growing educational programs and research, strengthening our law enforcement agencies, and improving health care facilities. Listed below are the appropriations for Catawba Valley and other large projects for North Carolina. 
   Catawba Valley Projects 
   U.S. 321 $1 million 
   Funds will be used to upgrade the U.S. 321 corridor between Burke, Caldwell and Catawba Counties, mitigating severe congestion near the bridge over the Catawba River. 
   The Historic Harper House $100,000 
  Will provide funding for an addition to the Catawba County Museum. 
   Caldwell County Community College Teacher Initiative $112,000 
  Will fund a program for baccalaureate degrees for teachers in a partnership between Caldwell Community College & Technical Institute and Appalachian State University. 
   Hickory Metro Higher Education Center $112,000 
   HMHEC will provide a specialized training program for highly skilled telecommunications, furniture, and textile professionals to develop entrepreneurial skills and ideas to grow businesses.
