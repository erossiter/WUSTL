SEN. DOLE SUPPORTS PASSAGE OF NATIONAL INTELLIGENCE REFORM ACT
  Sen. Elizabeth Dole today voted in favor of the National Intelligence Reform Act of 2004, which passed the Senate 89-2.  
 The House voted yesterday for passage, 336-75. 
   "Securing our homeland has been a top priority at all levels of government since the events of September 11, 2001," said Senator Dole. "This bill incorporates many of the recommendations of the 9-11Commission and paves the way for making the changes necessary as we confront different challenges in keeping Americans safe and secure." 
   Bill Highlights 
   Creates a strong Director of National Intelligence, with full budget authority, while preserving the chain of command within departments and agencies. 
   Creates a National Counterterrorism Center with the authority to plan and coordinate intelligence and counterterrorism operations. 
   Strengthens current laws to make certain we can arrest those aiding terrorists. 
   Creates a Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board to ensure civil liberties are protected in the conduct of the War on Terror. 
   Requires states to follow uniform national standards for what documents they accept in issuing driver's licenses and determining who is eligible to receive a license. 
   Border Security - 2,000 agents would be added to protect U.S. borders and unmanned aerial vehicles would be used to monitor the border with Canada. The agreement also adds 8,000 beds the detention space for the Department of Homeland Security to house illegal aliens and terrorist suspects.
