     United States Senator   Gordon Smith                                                                                                                                                                                                    Smith Pushes for Emergency LIHEAP Funds          October 19th  2005   Washington  DC      Today  Senator Gordon Smith  R OR  and a bipartisan group of senators offered an amendment to the Treasury  Transportation  and HUD appropriations bill  H R  3058  to include an additional  3 1 billion in emergency Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  funds   The Senate is expected to pass H R  3058 this week          Oil and gas prices are high and we can expect them to go higher as the weather turns cold     Smith  said     Hurricane Katrina was a national disaster and we have all felt its effects   Rather than wait until the heating bills pile up  we need to have assistance available now to the Oregonians who need it         Even before Hurricane Katrina  the Energy Information Administration  EIA  was predicting a significant increase in heating oil costs   Natural gas costs also rose dramatically and are now more than double last year  s prices   It is widely expected that families across the nation will face home heating cost hikes of between 20 and 80 percent          I  m concerned that the human tragedy of Hurricane Katrina will only grow if families and seniors can  t afford to heat their homes     Smith said     States like Oregon may face crisis conditions if the needy can  t keep warm this winter   It  s essential that we send them significant financial aid                                                                                                  current Press Release
