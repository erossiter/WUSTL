                         Contact  Kimberly CollinsPhone  202 224 5364           Smith Strikes Deal to Protect Oregon  s Mental Health Parity Laws               September 19th  2007   Washington  DC   Senator Gordon H  Smith  R OR  worked with his colleagues Senators Edward Kennedy  D MA  and Pete Domenici  R NM  to broker an agreement and pass landmark mental health legislation to ensure greater health insurance parity for persons with mental illness  The proposal reached by the Senators will create a national standard to protect persons with a mental illness from insurance discrimination  It also will ensure that Oregons more stringent mental health parity law  which was implemented in January  is not weakened    I know all too well the indiscriminate nature of a mental illness and the frightening statistics of its regular occurrence in our society  said Senator Smith  Mental illness is just as deadly and serious as a physical illness  Mental health parity is good for our nation  I thank my colleagues Senators Kennedy and Domenici for working with me to protect Oregons exemplary work in area of mental health parity and to put the rest of the nation on a similar path towards taking care of those who suffer from a mental illness  We know that effective treatment exists for most people  Help is out there  and this bill will help make it available   I am elated that the Senate has at long last passed a broad mental health parity bill  said Senator Domenici  Senator Smith was a key part of the broad bipartisan effort we needed to take the next step toward ending the insurance discrimination imposed on millions of people with serious  but treatable mental health problems  Domenici said  We need to lift the restrictive standard being applied to mental health coverage across the nation and allow these diseases of the brain to be treated under the broader standard applied to medical and surgical coverage   The proposal passed yesterday evening is modeled after the Mental Health Parity Act of 2007 introduced by the bi partisan group of senators earlier this year  The new policy would build on the landmark 1996 Mental Health Parity Act  a law authored by Senator Domenici and the late Minnesota Senator Paul Wellstone that began the process of ending health insurance discrimination against people with mental illness
