                         Contact  Kimberly CollinsPhone  202 224 5364           Smith Votes to Lower the Cost of Prescription Drugs Through Importation               May 7th  2007   Washington  DC   Senator Gordon H  Smith  R OR  voted to increase access to lower cost prescription drugs today by allowing for the importation of U S  Food and Drug Administration  FDA  approved medications from other countries  The policy  which was offered as an amendment to S  1082  the FDA Authorization bill  passed on the Senate floor by a voice vote   While importation currently is illegal in the United States  over two million Americans utilize this means to obtain more affordable prescription drugs  said Senator Smith  I have heard from many Oregonians who tell me that their choice is simple  purchase drugs from Canada or another foreign source or go without their life saving medications  Rather than limiting consumer access to lower cost medications  we should be implementing measures to ensure that drugs being sold to Americans from across our borders are safe and effective   The amendment was adapted from the Pharmaceutical Market Access and Drug Safety Act of 2007  The provision waives the limitation on the importation of prescription drugs by allowing for the purchase of FDA approved drugs from outside of the United States  The provision includes several measures to ensure the quality and safety of drugs sold to consumers  such as strict labeling requirements stating the origin of the medication  Final passage of the FDA Authorization bill is expected to take place early this week   Senator Smith has long supported the legalization of drug importation  In both the 108th and the 109th Congress  Senator Smith joined his colleague Senator Judd Gregg  R NH  in introducing the Safe IMPORT Act  which would allow individuals to import FDA approved prescription drugs from Canada for personal use
