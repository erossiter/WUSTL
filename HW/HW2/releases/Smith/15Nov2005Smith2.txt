     United States Senator   Gordon Smith                                                                                                                                                                                                    Smith Votes to Allow Guantanamo Detainees to Appeal Military Tribunal Verdicts  Senator Also Supports Judicial Review of   Enemy Combatant    Status          November 15th  2005   WASHINGTON  DC    Today  Senator Gordon Smith voted with a bipartisan majority of his Senate colleagues to provide access to federal courts for terrorism suspects convicted by U S  military tribunals   The amendment made to the Fiscal Year 2006 Defense Authorization bill  S  1042  passed by a vote of 84 14      We have a duty as Americans to apply high standards when it comes to taking away someone  s freedom     Smith said     By allowing federal appeals of convictions  we will be living by the principles that have made our nation  s legal system the envy of the world               Smith also voted to pass an amendment offered by Senator Jeff Bingaman  D NM  that would have allowed detainees in the war on terror to challenge their status as   enemy combatants    in federal court   The Bingaman amendment failed by a vote of 44 54     Even when we are at war  I believe it is important for the government to have checks against its own actions     Smith said     America is a great nation because of our respect for the rule of law and the role these checks have in protecting our freedom   I believe allowing terrorism suspects to challenge their detention will safeguard our principles while not unnecessarily compromising the vigor of our war effort                                                                                     current Press Release
