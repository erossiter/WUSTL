                         Contact  Gordon Smith Press OfficePhone  202 224 3753           Relief for Salmon Fishermen Hooked To War Funding              Senator Smith listens to concerns about the 2006 restricted season    May 25th  2007   Washington  DC   Senators Gordon H  Smith  R OR  and Ron Wyden  D OR  today praised the passage of over  60 million for Oregon and California salmon fishermen attached to the war funding legislation headed to the White House for approval  Adjustments to the bill were made to avoid a presidential veto      The gears of government grind slowly  but relief is finally on its way to Oregons coast and rural counties  Senator Smith said  Our salmon fishermen and rural counties are in a tough spot  and this money will go a long way towards easing the hardship    This is long overdue relief for Oregon s fishing families  Wyden said  This funding will go a long way towards preventing a disastrous fishing season from becoming an even longer term tragedy   Senators Smith and Wyden first requested and secured support in 2006  All funding projects  known as earmarks were stripped from final spending bills in December 2006  delaying approval of the funding by nearly six months  Senators Smith and Wyden along with Senators from California worked diligently to attach the  60 4 million in funding to the must pass Iraq funding bill    Senators Smith and Wyden have both visited with Oregon salmon fishermen to discuss long term solutions  They will continue to work with impacted parties as long as there is a threat to their livelihoods
