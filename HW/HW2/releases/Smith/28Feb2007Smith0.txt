                         Contact  Phone             Keep America Up To Speed by Investing in Rural Broadband Networks Smith Calls for  500 million annual investment to connect un served areas         February 28th  2007   WASHINGTON  DC    Today  U S  Senator Gordon H  Smith  R OR  advocated for the swift passage of his Universal Service reform bill at a hearing before the Senate Commerce Committee  In an effort to encourage economic growth in rural communities  the legislation will invest  500 million annually into the construction of broadband internet infrastructure in rural America and other un served areas    Creating broadband networks in rural America helps grow local economies  Smith said  My legislation will ensure that our nations communications infrastructure will continue to develop  allowing previously unconnected areas to explore new economic opportunities and new businesses to enter the community    Smiths legislation allocates  500 million annually from the Universal Service Fund for construction of broadband infrastructure  The  500 million annual allotment will be made available to facilities based providers in un served areas on a merit based and competitive basis    Although cable and telecommunications companies are making great strides in bringing broadband services to urban and suburban areas  many rural areas of the country remain un served  Smith said  Direct investment in broadband infrastructure is the key to keep American on the forefront of communications technology and availability  The small cost to providers is far outweighed by the economic growth potential for rural communities    Senator Smith introduced the Universal Service for the 21st Century Act with Senators Byron Dorgan  D ND  and Mark Pryor  D AR   The bill will stabilize and broaden the base of contributions to the fund and bring the benefits of high speed Internet networks to un served areas of the country
