     United States Senator   Gordon Smith                                                                                                                                                                                                    Congress Passes Comprehensive Energy Bill Legislation Includes Smith Provisions Protecting Northwest  Expanding Alternative Energy         July 29th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C    Today  Senator Gordon Smith  R OR  voted to pass the Energy Policy Act of 2005  H R  6    The legislation  which passed by a vote of 74 26  is designed to increase energy production and conservation  diversify fuel supplies and employ new technologies to make energy production more efficient and environmentally friendly   The legislation will now go to President Bush  who has indicated his intention to sign it into law      Every year  our energy technologies are getting cleaner and more efficient     Smith said     But we have to recognize that the private sector is driving most of these advances   Our energy policy needs to encourage the innovation that translates these gains into viable products       The bill includes a number of provisions championed by Smith to advance alternative and renewable energy production as well as energy efficiency   These include      Extension of Section 45 tax credit for electricity generation from renewable sources   Currently  only facilities placed in service by the end of 2005 are eligible to receive the credit  which runs for 10 years for most eligible resources   The Act extends the credit to facilities placed in service before 2008   In addition  certain hydropower will qualify for the credit  and biomass generation will receive the credit for 10 years  up from the 5 year eligibility in current law        Energy efficient appliance tax credits based on Smith  s legislation  S  1022    The bill provides a manufacturer  s tax credit in 2006 07 for the production of energy and water efficient dishwashers  clothes washers  and refrigerators   The more efficient the appliance  the higher the credit      Clean Renewable Energy Bonds for consumer owned utilities   The bill allows local governments and consumer owned utilities to issue tax credit bonds for the construction of qualifying renewable energy facilities     Tax credits for alternative motor vehicles  hybrids  fuel cell  and alternative fuel vehicles  and for alternative fuel refueling properties     Investment tax credits for the purchase of solar and fuel cell equipment      The goal of having both clean and affordable energy is becoming easier to achieve     Smith said     By using advancing technology  I believe we can meet our growing energy needs while safeguarding the environment       The bill also terminates FERC  s proposed rulemaking on standard market design  SMD   and prohibits its reissuance    It makes participation in regional transmission organizations voluntary  and authorizes federal power marketing administrations to participate in regional transmission organizations   It bans manipulation of electricity markets  increases transparency electricity for transactions and increases penalties under the Federal Power Act   The bill also includes mandatory transmission reliability provisions                                                                            current Press
