                         Contact  Phone             Senator Smith Backs Ethics Reform Bans Gifts  Eliminates Pensions for Felon Members of Congress               January 19th  2007   WASHINGTON  DC   On Thursday  the U S  Senate approved strict new ethics rules for Senators and their staff  U S  Senator Gordon H  Smith  R OR  supported the reforms stating they will shed more light on the activities of Congress   New rules will close several loopholes and shed more light on the legislative process  Smith said  When the new rules become law  the public will have prompt access to legislation  gifts will be banned  and there will be tougher rules for lobbyists   The Legislative Transparency and Accountability Act  S  1  passed the Senate with Senator Smiths support by a vote of 96 to 2  The bill makes the following reforms   Bans gifts from registered lobbyists   Requires Members to disclose holds on legislation and on nominees   Suspends pensions of retired Members of Congress who are convicted of a felony   Increases penalties for lobbyists who do not properly disclose their activity   Increases reimbursement for use of corporate jets to be equitable to charter rates and requires disclosure of noncommercial air travel   Requires earmarks in spending bills to identify ownership and explain purpose    Creates new online databases so the public can access information about lobbyists    Eliminates floor privileges for former Members of Congress and Senate officers who are registered lobbyists  In addition  the bill increases post employment restrictions for Members of Congress and for Senate employees regarding lobbying Senate offices and committee staff after leaving the Senate    The House of Representatives and the President need to act before the bill becomes law                                                                                                                                               current Press Release
