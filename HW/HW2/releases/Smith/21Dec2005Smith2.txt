                         Contact  Phone             Smith Kohl Introduce Legislation to Extend Manufacturing Partnership Program          December 21st  2005   WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senators Gordon H  Smith  R OR  and Herb Kohl  D WI  introduced legislation  S  2134  on December 16  2005 to re authorize the Manufacturing Extension Partnership  MEP  program that will establish a new grant program and invest in critical training and research programs       To remain competitive manufacturers need to constantly adapt to the global marketplace     said Smith     Knowledge is power and that is why the bill establishes an advance fellowship program to make sure the intellectual capability is in place so American businesses can remain an economic leader      MEP is a nationwide network with centers in all 50 states that provide assistance to help small and medium sized manufacturers succeed   This bill authorizes funding through fiscal year 2008 for the Manufacturing Extension Partnership  MEP  and the National Institute of Standards and Technology  NIST    The legislation establishes a post doctoral and senior research fellowship program in manufacturing sciences at NIST  funded at  1 5 million in FY 2006 and increasing to  2 million in FY 2008        Our manufacturing center must continue to be adaptive and innovative if we are going to compete in a global economy     said Kohl    The Manufacturing Extension Partnership program helps American business develop the skills necessary to help create the high wage jobs that promote economic growth      The legislation also reauthorizes the Advanced Technology Program  ATP  which supports research and development of high risk  cutting edge technologies   ATP partners with private sector entities to invest in early stage  innovative technologies that enable U S  companies to develop next generation products and services                                                                                  current
