                         Contact  Kimberly CollinsPhone  202 224 5364           Smith Pushes for Agreement on Children  s Health Measure              Sen  Smith advocated for the passage of SCHIP at a recent press conference    November 1st  2007   Washington  D C     Senator Gordon H  Smith  R OR  urged his colleagues today to work together quickly to reach a comprise on legislation to reauthorize the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  SCHIP   His statement comes following a vote on the second legislative attempt in the Senate  H R  3963  to reauthorize the program after the initial measure was vetoed in early October  H R  3963 also is expected to be vetoed by the White House  Though the bill passed with a veto proof majority in the Senate  it was several votes shy of this threshold in the House of Representatives  who voted on the measure last week   I regret that another childrens health measure will fall victim to a veto  said Senator Smith  We must rise above the partisanship in Washington and deliver a bill that will make a difference in the health care of millions of kids  I hope quality childrens health care will be part of the legacy this Congress builds rather than more walls from partisan battle   SCHIP is a joint federal state program that provides health care coverage to low income children  The legislation made adjustments to provisions opposed by some members  but retained funding levels from the original legislation which allows to program to cover up to 10 million uninsured children  over 100 000 kids total in Oregon
