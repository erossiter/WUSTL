from the Mansfield Room in the U S  Capitol shortly after introducing the Matthew Shepard Hate Crimes Prevention Act of 2007   Senator Ted Kennedy looks on       April 12th  2007   WASHINGTON  DC   Today  for the fifth consecutive Congress  Senator Gordon H  Smith re introduced legislation to protect all Americans against violence motivated by hate  Senator Smith was joined by Senator Kennedy to introduce the Hate Crimes Prevention Act of 2007  which Senators Smith and Kennedy are renaming in honor of Matthew Shepard    A principal responsibility of government is to protect and defend its citizens and to come to the aid of the mistreated  As a nation founded on the ideals of tolerance and justice  we simply cannot accept violence that is motivated by bias and hate  Smith said  Current law is limited  Our proposal would change that  and change it permanently  As a tribute to Matthew and in recognition of the tireless effort of his mother Judy  this legislation will be known as the Matthew Shepard Bill   Supported by a broad coalition of over 210 law enforcement  religious  and civil rights groups  this bill will strengthen our nations ability to provide justice for victims of hate motivated crimes  It corrects problems in the current law by expanding the definition of a hate crime to include sexual orientation  gender and disability    Judy Shepard  mother of Matthew Shepard joined Senators Smith and Kennedy today at the U S  Capitol to help introduce the bill
