                         Contact  Phone             Keep America Up To Speed By Investing in Rural Broadband Networks Smith Calls for  500 million annual investment to connect unserved areas         March 2nd  2006   WASHINGTON  DC    Stating it will spur economic development in rural areas and make America more competitive globally  U S  Senator Gordon H  Smith  R OR  advocated for swift passage of his Universal Service reform bill at a hearing of the Senate Commerce Committee today  The legislation will invest  500 million annually into the construction of broadband internet infrastructure in rural America and other unserved areas    Reaffirming and stabilizing universal service and using the fund to spur the deployment of broadband networks in rural America helps grow the economy  Smith said  My legislation will ensure that our nations communications infrastructure will continue to grow  and be the robust and connected network that Americans expect and deserve   Smiths legislation allocates  500 million annually to fund construction of broadband infrastructure via a new Broadband for Unserved Areas Account  The new fund will be capped at  500 million each year and made available to one facilities based provider in under served areas on a merit based and competitive basis    Smith said  Investment is needed now  The U S  is falling behind other nations in broadband availability  Direct investment in broadband infrastructure is the solution to this disturbing trend    Smith pointed to a recent International Telecommunications Union study that shows the United States fell from 13th to 16th in global broadband penetration    Smith has introduced the Universal Service for the 21st Century Act with Senators Byron Dorgan  D ND  and Mark Pryor  D AR   The bill will stabilize and broaden the base of contributions to the fund and bring the benefits of high speed Internet networks to unserved areas of the country  Smith is going to work with his colleagues on the Commerce Committee to include Universal Service reform in the upcoming telecommunications reform package
