                         Contact  Gordon Smith Press OfficePhone  202 224 3753           Senator Smith Proposes Pairing Up Veterans to Help Them Cope with War Zone Stress New Legislation Creates Mentoring Programs for Returning Veterans             U S  Senator Gordon H  Smith visiting with U S  Troops during a trip to Iraq    May 3rd  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Gordon H  Smith  R OR  introduced legislation last week creating a peer to peer support network to assist veterans returning from the War on Terror adjust to life outside of the war zone  Recent studies have shown increasing instances among troops of divorce  alcoholism  and issues with anger and aggression    It is our duty to aid our returning veterans who have risked their bodies  minds and lives for this country  Smith said  This program will ease the struggles faced by our returning vets as they attempt to rejoin their communities  Having the ear and the shoulder of a comrade who shared in the same experiences can not be matched   Deployed soldiers face extreme stress which can have devastating long term consequences  Army researchers have found that instances of alcohol misuse have gone from 13 percent to 21 percent one year after returning from Iraq and Afghanistan  reports of soldiers with anger and aggression issues doubled from 11 percent to 22 percent after deployment and those planning to divorce their spouse increased from nine percent to 15 percent  In addition  unemployment for soldiers returning to civilian life is three times the national average  Our combat veterans are returning home and suffering from war related conditions that may put them at risk for homelessness  said Cheryl Beversdorf  President of the National Coalition for Homeless Veterans  They may need a variety of services  from mental health programs and peer support to housing  employment training and job placement assistance  Senator Smiths Heroes Helping Heroes program will serve as an excellent starting point to help these returning heroes address their many needs   The Heroes Helping Heroes Program  cosponsored by Senator Tom Harkin  D IA   authorizes  13 5 million over three years to create and expand peer support programs for veterans and assess their feasibility and efficacy  The program is strongly supported by a broad array of veterans organizations including the Disabled American Veterans  the Iraq and Afghanistan Veterans of American and the National Coalition for Homeless Veterans
