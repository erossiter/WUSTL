                                    Smith  Wyden Secure Approval for  115 Million in Oregon Projects in Final Highway Bill          July 27th  2005   WASHINGTON  DC   U S  Senators Gordon Smith  R Ore   Ron Wyden  D Ore   today announced the authorization of  115 million for Oregon transportation projects in the conference report for the Transportation Equity Act of 2005   That legislation reauthorizes the Federal aid surface transportation program  which includes Federally aided highways  highway safety programs  and transit programs   The Senate  s version of the   highway bill    was approved in May and has now been reconciled with the House version of the legislation   The bill including the Oregon projects is expected to be given final Congressional approval this week and sent to the President for signature into law     Dollar for dollar  transportation funding is one of the best economic investments the Federal government can make in Oregon communities   These highway and transit projects will create good  living wage jobs and at the same time build the infrastructure our economy will need to stay strong     said Wyden       Efficient transportation infrastructure is crucial to Oregon  s economy     Smith said    From I 5 to Main Street we are improving our roadways and financing community development         Following is a list of Oregon funding authorized by the
