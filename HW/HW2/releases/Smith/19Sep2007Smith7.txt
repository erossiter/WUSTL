Committee on the Aging    September 19th  2007   Washington  DC   Senator Gordon H  Smith  R OR  questioned federal and private stakeholders in an Aging committee hearing today on their efforts to ensure that television service to senior households will not be interrupted following the upcoming digital television  DTV  transition  The DTV transition requires that by February 17  2009  all full power television stations broadcast entirely in a digital format  Senator Smith serves as the Ranking Member of the Senate Special Committee on Aging and the Chairman of the Republican High Tech Task Force   There is no shortage of crooks willing to capitalize on the confusion and fears that surround the DTV transition  said Senator Smith  With seniors as their main prey  I guarantee that these bad actors are already scheming to dupe seniors into the purchase of unneeded televisions or converter boxes  We must ensure that the federal government is taking the necessary steps to guarantee a smooth transition without fraud or interrupted TV service for elderly households   The hearing  Preparing for the Digital Television Transition  Will Seniors Be Left in the Dark  sought to bring attention to the unique needs and vulnerabilities of older individuals  their families  caregivers and aging support networks as the nation transitions from free over the air analog TV to digital TV  At the hearing  Senator Smith stressed the importance of providing outreach and education to elderly households to ensure the digital TV transition runs smoothly for them without disruption of their television service or incident of fraud    To ensure a successful transition  Senator Smith called on the Federal Communications Commission  FCC  and the National Telecommunications and Information Administration  NTIA   and other stakeholders in the transition  to work closely with the Aging Network  particularly the National Association of Area Agencies on Aging  N4A  which can offer access to hundreds of thousands of volunteers  Senator Smith asked the FCC to ensure that Area Agencies on Aging have the necessary resources  including appropriate funding  to carry out the mission to inform and assist seniors with the transition                                                                                                                                                         current
