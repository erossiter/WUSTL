     United States Senator   Gordon Smith                                                                                                                                                                                                    Key Senate Committee Passes Smith Wyden Wallowa Dam Bill          February 9th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C    Today  Senators Gordon Smith and Ron Wyden announced that the Wallowa Lake Dam Rehabilitation and Water Management Act of 2005 passed out of the Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources   The Senate has passed identical legislation  also sponsored by Smith and Wyden  in 2002 and 2004      This collection of projects is a top tier priority in the region     said Smith     Effective water management in the Wallowa Valley translates into a better economy  environment and general standard of living        The Wallowa water management projects make the local economy stronger in two ways  by maintaining Wallowa Lake as a magnet for tourism and getting more water for local agriculture  So many local  state and federal leaders have worked together on this issue  and that  s what it takes to get things done for the benefit of local communities in Oregon as a whole     said Wyden    The Act would authorize the Secretary of the Interior  acting through the Commissioner of Reclamation  to participate in  and provide funding for  the rehabilitation of the Wallowa Lake Dam and the Wallowa Valley Water Management Plan   Local entities have developed these two projects with the participation of numerous state and federal agencies and the support of local tribal leaders                                                                                                            current Press Release
