     United States Senator   Gordon Smith                                                                                                                                                                                         Contact  Gordon Smith Press OfficePhone  202 224 3753           Iran  Shut down Nuclear Ambition Senator Smith leads Bipartisan Effort Targeting Iran  s Finance and Trade         March 23rd  2007   Washington  DC   U S  Senator Gordon H  Smith  R OR  is leading a group of bipartisan Senators who want to impose tougher economic sanctions on Iran for its continued pursuit of a nuclear program    We should use every diplomatic and economic tool at our disposal to stop Iran from developing nuclear weapons  Smith said  The message is clear  the international community will not tolerate flagrant violations    Iran has pursued a nuclear weapons program while consistently misleading the United Nations and the international community about the objective and scope of its nuclear activities  Iran has repeatedly refused to comply with UN regulations calling for the suspension of its programs  The Iran Counter Proliferation Act of 2007  S  970  strengthens economic sanctions against Iran  The legislation     Prohibits all imports and exports to Iran   Urges U S  banks and financial institutions not to handle any transactions  direct or indirect  with Irans state owned banks   Urges the United Nations Security Counsel to take measures to prevent new investment in Irans energy sector   Forbids any action that would extend preferential trade treatment to Iran or that would lead to Iranian accession to the World Trade Organization   Designates  10 million to enhance direct relationships with the people of Iran by identifying young Iranians to visit the United States    Freezes the assets of senior Iranian officials and their families   Prevents nuclear co operation with Russia until Russia has suspended its nuclear assistance to Iran   Reduces U S  contributions to the World Bank by the percentage of total money the World Bank loans to entities in Iran  Senator Smith is joined by bipartisan coalition of Senators that includes  Senators Joe
