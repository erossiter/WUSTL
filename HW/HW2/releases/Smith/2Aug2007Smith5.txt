                         Contact  Kimberly CollinsPhone  202 224 5364           Let  s Deliver a Victory to Our Kids  Smith Says          August 2nd  2007   Washington  DC   Legislation championed by Senator Gordon H  Smith  R Oregon  to provide health care coverage for millions of low income children was passed today in the United States Senate  The bill reauthorizes the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  SCHIP   which will provide health care coverage to over 9 million low income children    The reauthorization of the State Childrens Health Insurance Program fulfills the governments responsibility to take care of our nations most vulnerable children  said Senator Smith  Our kids deserve a government that is committed to their health and well being  But  in order to get these kids covered  we need to finish the job and get this bill into law before the program expires  I hope that on this one  we can ultimately put politics aside and deliver a victory for our kids    SCHIP is a joint federal state program that provides health care coverage to low income children and is set to expire at the end of September 2007  The SCHIP reauthorization package utilizes a proposal introduced by Senator Smith to implement a 61 cent increase in the cigarette tax and a commensurate increase on all other tobacco products to pay for the cost of the reauthorization    In May  Senators Smith and John Kerry  D MA  introduced The Childrens Mental Health Parity Act to prohibit discriminatory limits on mental health care in SCHIP plans  Language from the Senators bill was included in the SCHIP measure passed today   The package also adopts a proposal by Senator Smith that allows states to develop health care pooling arrangements with employers  This policy permits employers  with less than 250 employees  access to affordable coverage for their employees and to receive an SCHIP subsidy for eligible persons   Other bill highlights include    Allowing states to cover pregnant women up to 200 percent of poverty   34 340 for a family of four  through a state plan amendment    Allowing states to apply for a waiver to cover children and pregnant women between 200 and 300 percent of poverty    Freezing coverage of parents and creates a path for states to move childless adults into Medicaid  and   Corrects problems from the 2005 Deficit Reduction Act that prevents citizens from accessing the program because the citizen documentation requirements are too stringent    Senator Smith was an original co sponsor of the effort that created the SCHIP program
