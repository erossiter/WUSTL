                                    Smith Introduces Legislation Leading To Vietnam Joining the WTO Approval Will Reduce Export Tariffs on Oregon Agriculture Products               June 14th  2006   WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senator Gordon Smith  R OR  and U S  Senator Max Baucus  D MT  have introduced legislation that will grant Vietnam  an important Oregon trading partner  Permanent Normal Trade Relations  PNTR  status and lead to its entry into the World Trade Organization  WTO   Once a member of the WTO  Vietnam will drastically reduce tariffs on all U S  exports  including Oregon agriculture products   As a businessman  Ive seen how trade can raise standards of living both in America and around the world  Smith said  International commerce creates new growth opportunities for our manufacturers and agricultural producers  and WTO membership for Vietnam will help ensure that everyones playing by the same rules  It will mean the virtual elimination of tariffs on important Oregon crops and open a market of 83 million new customers   A grant of PNTR is a promise not to treat imports from one country more harshly than those from another country  A section of U S  law known as the Jackson Vanik amendment prohibits a country from being granted PNTR status if they are or were communist  The United States typically terminates a countrys Jackson Vanik status and grants that country PNTR when it joins the World Trade Organization  WTO   Senator Smiths legislation would lead to Vietnam joining the WTO    On May 31  Vietnam and the United States completed their bilateral market access agreement  one of the steps in Vietnam joining the WTO  Vietnam hopes to become a WTO member before it hosts the Asia Pacific Economic Cooperation  APEC  summit in Hanoi in November  which President Bush and other APEC leaders are planning to attend   U S  Senators John McCain  R AZ   John Kerry  D MA   Chuck Hagel  R NE   Richard Lugar  R ID   Lisa Murkowski  R AK   and Tom Carper  D DE   joined Senators Smith and Baucus as original co sponsors  In the House of Representatives  Congressmen Jim Ramstad  R MN   Mike Thompson  D CA   and twenty other House members introduced a companion bill that grants PNTR to Vietnam  making permanent what was formerly called most favored nation trade status  The Senate version of Vietnam PNTR is expected to be referred to the Senate Committee on Finance  of which Senator Smith is a member    In December of 2005 Senator Smith led a congressional delegation to Vietnam to discuss economic and trade issues with the Vietnam President Tran Duc Luong                                                                                                                                                                    current
