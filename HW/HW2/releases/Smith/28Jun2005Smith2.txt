                                    Smith   Junk Fax    Prevention Act Passes Congress  Heads to the President for Signature Bill Brings Consumer Groups and Business Together         June 28th  2005   WASHINGTON  DC   Today  Senator Gordon Smith announced that Congress has passed his Junk Fax Prevention Act of 2005  S 714    The Act allows companies to correspond via facsimile where an established business relationship exists and provides consumers with additional protections  including the opportunity to opt out of faxes   The bill will now go to President Bush to be signed into law                 From the outset the goal was to find a compromise between business and consumers     said Smith     Nobody wants to be bothered with unwanted faxes  but it makes no sense to regulate them into obscurity        Today  the House of Representatives passed the bill by a voice vote  which followed the Senate  s passage by unanimous consent on Thursday June 23rd               In July 2003  the Federal Communications Commission  FCC  reconsidered its Telephone Consumer Protection Act  TCPA  rules and issued a rule eliminating the ability of businesses to contact their customers even where there exists an established business relationship   The effect of the FCC  s rule would be to prevent a business from sending a fax solicitation to any person  whether it is a supplier or customer  without first obtaining prior written consent  imposing significant costs on businesses      Smith  s legislation ensures that the 1992 FCC order permitting businesses and associations to send unsolicited facsimile advertisements where there exists an established business relationship will remain law   Among the consumer protections included in the bill is a 24 hour hotline service that can be contacted by users to opt out of fax transmissions                                                                                   current Press Release
