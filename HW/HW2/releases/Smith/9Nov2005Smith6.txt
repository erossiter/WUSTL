     United States Senator   Gordon Smith                                                                                                                                                                                                    Smith Continues Push for Gas Price Gouging Legislation Bill Protects Against Price Spikes Following Disasters         November 9th  2005   Today  Senator Gordon Smith  R OR  continued to urge passage of S  1743  the Post Disaster Consumer Protection Act  legislation aimed at prohibiting price gouging of oil and gas products in the immediate aftermath of a declared disaster       Strong  effective federal price gouging laws are an idea whose time has come     said Smith     We must aggressively go after profiteers who prey upon a vulnerable public in the wake of a disaster and protect our consumers from such despicable practices      Under Smith  s legislation  the president must declare a major disaster under the Stafford Act  For 30 days following the disaster declaration  it will be unlawful to engage in price gouging of oil or gas products   The bill defines price gouging as a gross disparity in the price for the product charged after the disaster declaration as compared to prices charged by the same supplier during the 30 days immediately preceding the disaster   Price gouging will not include price increases attributable to increased wholesale or operational costs  international market trends  loss of production capability or loss of pipeline transmission capability     Until we become less reliant on foreign oil  American consumers will be susceptible to price gouging  especially after a tragedy like Hurricane Katrina     said Smith     The government must be given the tools and the ability to root out such egregious profiteering and to prosecute the criminals who perpetuate it      The bill authorizes the Federal Trade Commission to determine what represents a gross disparity in pricing   The FTC will to punish violations under this act using its existing authorities under the Federal Trade Commission Act   Those authorities include seeking civil penalties of  11 000 per violation  assessing fines or repayment of illegal gains  freezing assets  and seeking preliminary injunctions  cease and desist orders or temporary restraining orders
