                         Contact  Phone             Smith Secures Increased Meth Funding in Budget                Photo Courtesy of the Drug Enforcement Administration      March 14th  2006   WASHINGTON  DC    Today  U S  Senator Gordon Smith  R OR  and a bipartisan group of senators led the way to increase funding budgeted this year to combat the methamphetamine epidemic to  99 million  The amount corresponds to that provided by the recently passed Combat Meth Act   Meth is a deadly and fiercely addictive drug  Smith said  We must equip law enforcement with the resources they need to shut down labs and ensure that states have adequate resources to fight addiction  I will work over the coming months to dedicate as much funding as possible to fight meth   Smith joined with Senators Jim Talent   R MO   Dianne Feinstein   D CA   Blanche Lincoln   D AR   Joe Biden   D DE   Maria Cantwell   D WA   Tom Harkin  D IA  and Herb Kohl  D WI  to offer the amendment that provides  99 million over five years for the COPS Meth Hot Spots program as authorized in the Combat Meth Act  The funding will help train state and local law enforcement to investigate and prosecute meth offenders  It also expands the funding available for enforcement and environmental clean up  The U S  Senate agreed to the amendment by voice vote    Earlier this month President Bush signed the Combat Meth Act into law as part of the PATRIOT Act reauthorization  Senator Smith was an original co sponsor of the Combat Meth Act which makes critical funding available to states faced with a growing methamphetamine problem  The U S  Senate is expected to complete consideration on the FY 2007 Budget Resolution later this week
