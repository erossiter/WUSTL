                                    Smith  Wyden Lead Senate Delegation to Meet with Asian Leaders          December 1st  2005   WASHINGTON  DC    From December 5 to December 13  Senators Gordon Smith and Ron Wyden will lead a group with five other colleagues to meet with top leaders in Asia   The primary purpose of the trip will be to promote trade and open export markets for Oregon businesses     Oregon  s businesses are highly dependent on international trade and I believe the future growth of our economy will be inextricably tied to growth of our export markets     Smith said     Tens of thousands of jobs  whether growing wheat or building computer chips  will benefit from increased access to overseas consumers        Oregon is a major U S  gateway to the rapidly growing economies of Asia  so I look forward to talking with officials from China and other nations about improving trade relations     said Wyden     I also look forward to discussing matters of mutual security interest to our respective countries      The delegation will travel to four key Asian countries some of which are among the fastest growing economies in the world    China  Thailand  Cambodia and Vietnam   In China  the senators will meet with President Hu Jintao  Vice Premier Wu Yi  and U S  Ambassador Clark Randt  Jr   In Thailand  the delegation will discuss economic opportunities with Prime Minister Thaksin Chinnawat  Foreign Minister Kantathi Suphamongkhon  and Commerce Minister Somkhit Chatusiphithak     While in Vietnam  the senators are scheduled to discuss trade with President Tran Duc Luong  Deputy Prime Minister He Vu Khoan  and U S  Ambassador Michael Marine   Finally  in Cambodia  the delegation will meet with top government leaders including Deputy Prime Minister Sok An and Ambassador at large Roland Eng                                                                                                          current Press Release
