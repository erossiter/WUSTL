COMMERCE COMMITTEE TO HOLD HEARING FOR ALASKAN NOMINATED TO DOT
  The Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation will hold a hearing tomorrow (Tuesday, July 31) to consider the nomination of retired Vice Admiral Thomas J. Barrett, United States Coast Guard (USCG), to serve as Deputy Secretary for the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT). The hearing will be held at 10 a.m. in Room 253 of the Russell Senate Office Building.  
  Vice Admiral Barrett has served as DOT's Acting Deputy Secretary since March 2007. In this capacity, he is the principal advisor to Secretary of Transportation Mary Peters and responsible for the day-to-day management of the Department's $61.1 billion budget and more than 60,000 employees. Barrett also continues to serve as the first-ever Administrator of the Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration, where he directs national safety programs for the transport of hazardous materials through pipelines.
   Often stationed in Alaska during his more than 35 years of distinguished service in the Coast Guard, VADM Barrett commanded the Seventeenth Coast Guard District; served as Commander, Naval Forces Alaska; and directed USCG operations in the North Pacific, a region which includes the Gulf of Alaska, the Eastern Bering Sea, and the Arctic Ocean. Vice Admiral Barrett also commanded the Coast Guard base in Kodiak, coordinated the Coast Guard Leadership Council, and is a recipient of the Coast Guard's Distinguished Service Medal. 
  VADM Barrett and his wife, Sheila, live in Arlington, Virginia. They have four children, including two sons who are Operation Iraqi Freedom combat veterans. He graduated from LeMoyne College in New York and earned his law degree from George Washington University.
