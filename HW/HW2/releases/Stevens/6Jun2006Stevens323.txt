ALASKA DEPARTMENT OF FISH AND GAME OFFICIAL TO TESTIFY AT SENATE HEARING ON OFFSHORE AQUACULTURE
  Mr. David Bedford has accepted an invitation from Senate Commerce Committee Chairman Sen.Ted Stevens (R-Alaska) to testify at a hearing on offshore aquaculture. The hearing will be held by the National Ocean Policy Study Subcommittee on Thursday, June 8 at 10:00 a.m. in Room 562 of the Dirksen Senate Office Building.  
   During Thursday's hearing, government officials and interested parties will testify about the potential for offshore aquaculture facilities to operate within the United States Exclusive Economic Zone. Bedford will discuss the need for federal regulations to address existing state regulations for fisheries management. 
   Bedford is the deputy commissioner the Alaska Department of Fish and Game. He also serves as one of the United States' commissioners on the Pacific Salmon Commission. Bedford earned his BA from the University of Washington and his JD from the Northwestern School of Law at Lewis and Clark College. 
   A live webcast of this hearing will be available at www.commerce.senate.gov.
