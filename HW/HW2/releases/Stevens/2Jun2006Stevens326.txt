GRANTS AWARDED TO ALASKA PROGRAMS
  The Alaska Congressional Delegation today announced that federal agencies have awarded a series of funding grants to Alaska groups and communities. The grants total more than $6.4 million.  
   The Department of Rural Development has awarded a $49,304 Rural Business Opportunity Grant to the Lake and Peninsula Business Development Center in King Salmon to generate economic development opportunities associated with regional mining projects. The Department of Rural Development has also awarded Rural Business Enterprise Grants to two groups in Alaska. The Sitka Tribe of Alaska will receive $93,865 to fund business technical assistance and the Lower Kuskokwim Economic Development Council will receive $23,100 to continue its Salmon Quality Enhancement Project. 
   The Department of Transportation has awarded two grants to the State of Alaska Department of Transportation and Public Facilities. The State of Alaska will receive $1,700,000 to continue airport system planning efforts and $1,800,000 to update the Master Plan for Anchorage International Airport. 
   Through its National Institute on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism grant, the Department of Health and Human Services has awarded $175,156 to fund alcohol research programs at the University of Alaska Fairbanks. 
   The Department of Housing and Urban Development has awarded a $2,564,943 Capital Fund grant to the Alaska Housing Finance Corporation. This award may be used to fund various activities, including modernization, development, financing, and management improvements.
