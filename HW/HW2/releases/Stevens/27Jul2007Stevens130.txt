GRANTS AWARDED TO ALASKA PROGRAMS
  The Alaska Congressional Delegation today announced that federal agencies have awarded a series of funding grants to Alaska groups and communities. The grants total more than $19 million. 
  Department of Commerce - National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)
   * $150,000 for the Taiya Inlet Watershed Council. The Council will work with the NOAA Restoration Center to restore habitat and fish passage in Pullen Creek, Skagway. 
   * $257,371 to assess the status of ice seal populations in the Bering and Chukchi seas, and to support priority projects identified by the Ice Seal Committee. This project will collect traditional knowledge, harvest information, and habitat and movement data about ice seals. 
  Department of Education
   * $268,096 to the Kashunamiut School District and $243,872 to the Klawock City School District. These Literacy and School Library grants will provide up-to-date library resources and expand library hours in Klawock and Kashunamiut. 
  Department of Homeland Security - Federal Emergency Management Agency
   * $1,143,450 to repair Willow Fishhook Road north of Wasilla. This grant will restore the road to its pre-damaged condition by repairing slope failures, road, and culvert washouts and replacing lost embankments. The Willow Fishhook Road was damaged by flooding, landslides, and mudslides caused by severe storms in August 2006. 
  Department of Transportation
   * $1,560,000 to the Alaska Marine Highway System for an administrative office building in Ward Cove, Ketchikan. 
   * $4,000,000 to the Alaska Railroad. The grant will provide funds to build and operate an 80-mile rail line from Eielson Air Force Base near Fairbanks to the Delta Junction area. The rail line will ultimately provide improved transportation options for the U.S. military, mass transit, and freight. 
   * $12,000,000 to the Alaska Railroad at Fort Wainwright. This grant will improve general safety at Fort Wainwright by eliminating at-grade rail crossings; reducing rail-related traffic congestion, noise, and interference with base operations; enhancing security and force protection; and providing better rail service to a proposed loading facility. 
  Environmental Protection Agency
   * $17,150 to enhance environmental education opportunities in the Sitka School District. This grant will refurbish and expand the environmental trail and preserve in Sitka and update teachers' curricula.
