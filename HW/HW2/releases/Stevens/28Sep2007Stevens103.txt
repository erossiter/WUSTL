DELEGATION ANNOUNCES GRANTS TO ALASKA PROGRAMS
  The Alaska Congressional Delegation today announced that federal agencies have awarded a series of funding grants to Alaska groups and communities. The grants total over $50 million. 
   Department of Health and Human Services
  * $500,000 to the Cook Inlet Tribal Council to address the needs of families affected by substance abuse, especially methamphetamines. 
   * $160,000 to Big Brothers Big Sisters of Alaska to mentor children of prisoners. 
   * $219,018 to the University of Alaska Fairbanks to fund a project entitled: "Health Promotion in a Yup'ik Community: Improving Health Through Collaboration." This grant was given by the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute.
   * $742,331 to the Healthcare Facilities Partnership Program. 
   Department of Justice
   * $193,044 to the Alaska Association of Chiefs of Police. This grant will continue funding for the Alaska Law Enforcement Information Sharing System and increase the number of police stations that participate in the program. 
   * $299,956 to the Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention. This grant will enable the Anchorage Police Department to develop a multi-agency and multi-disciplinary response to violations of the Internet Crimes Against Children act.
   * $937,000 to the Arrest Policies and Enforcement of Protection Orders Program. This grant will add one full-time attorney and one full-time witness paralegal who will focus on sexual assault in Bethel. In addition, this grant will provide funds for one part-time witness paralegal in the rural Barrow office, two part-time attorney positions in the Anchorage special assaults unit, and one full-time appellate attorney.
  National Science Foundation
   * $349,075 to KCAW-FM Raven Radio in Sitka. 
  United States Transportation Command
   * $47,370,492 to Lynden Air Cargo of Anchorage.
   This is a minimum amount awarded by the United States Military Transportation Command.
