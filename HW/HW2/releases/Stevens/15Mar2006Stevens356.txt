ALASKA VOLCANO SCIENTIST TO TESTIFY AT SENATE HEARING ON VOLCANIC HAZARDS
  Dr. John Eichelberger has accepted an invitation from Senate Commerce Committee Chairman Ted Stevens (R-Alaska) to testify at a hearing that will be held by the Senate Commerce Subcommittee on Disaster Prevention and Prediction on Thursday, March 16, 2006 at 10:30 a.m. Dr. Eichelberger, a professor of volcanology at the University of Alaska Fairbanks and a coordinating scientist at the Alaska Volcano Observatory, will discuss volcanic hazards and their impact on aviation.  
   Thursday's hearing will also examine the current state of volcanic observation and monitoring, technological and regional gaps in volcanic hazards monitoring, and aviation safety issues associated with volcanic activity. Captain Terry McVenes, executive air safety chairman for the Airline Pilots Association, and Mr. James E. Quick, program coordinator for the Volcano Hazards Program, U.S. Geological Survey will also testify at the hearing. 
   While the Commerce Committee expects to offer a live webcast of this hearing, it is not known at this time if the webcast will be available via audio or video. Both the live and archived webcast will be available at www.commerce.senate.gov.
