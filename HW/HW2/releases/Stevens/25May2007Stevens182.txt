GRANTS AWARDED TO ALASKA PROGRAMS
  The Alaska Congressional Delegation today announced that federal agencies have awarded a series of funding grants to Alaska groups and communities. The grants total more than $16 million.  
  U.S. Department of Commerce - National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration 
  * $313,803 to the Alaska Department of Fish and Game for Southeast Alaska Anadromous Salmon Research. This grant will be used to complete four sub-projects, including Coho Salmon Research, Troll Fishery Management Methods Research, the Pink and Chum Salmon Stock Evaluation Program, and the Salmon Catch Sampling Project.. 
  U.S. Department of Health and Human Services
  * $1,349,557 to the Association of Village Presidents in Bethel, $1,420,131 to the Southcentral Foundation in Anchorage, and $1,443,244 to the Tlingit & Haida Tribes Central Council in Juneau for Head Start programs.
  * $1,332,235 in Health Center Cluster Demonstration Grants to the Bristol Bay Area Health Corporation. 
  * $411,333 to the City of Skagway, $150,320 to the Seldovia Village Tribe, and $541,667 to the Tanana Chiefs Conference for community health centers. 
  U.S. Department of Transportation - Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)
  * $5,500,000 to the Ketchikan International Airport to construct a snow removal equipment building and a sand and chemical storage facility.
  * $3,000,000 to the State of Alaska's Department of Transportation & Public Facilities to construct a taxiway, apron, and runway at the Lake Louise Airport. 
  * $730,000 to the Ouzinkie, New Stuyahok, and Hooper Bay Airports for new snow removal equipment.
