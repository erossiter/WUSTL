ALASKA CONGRESSIONAL DELEGATION ANNOUNCES GRANTS TO ALASKA
  The Alaska Congressional Delegation today announced that federal agencies have awarded a series of funding grants to Alaska groups and communities. The grants total more than $40.7 million.  
   The Department of Homeland Security's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program has awarded a $112,684 Fire Prevention and Safety Grant to the North Star Volunteer Fire Department. The funds will be used to enhance the safety of the public and firefighters. 
   The Department of Transportation has awarded $4,080,000 to the City of Kodiak for the improvement of the Trident Basin seaplane base for Kodiak. The Department of Transportation has also awarded the following grants to the State of Alaska Department of Transportation and Public Facilities: 
   * $1,750,000 for the construction of a sand and chemical storage building at the McGrath Airport in McGrath, Alaska. 
   * $3,900,000 to finish paving projects at the Unalakleet Airport and to construct a general aviation apron. 
   * $3,600,000 to complete construction of a storage building that will house aircraft rescue, firefighting, and snow removal equipment at the Nome Airport in Nome. 
   * $3,000,000 to continue the Anchorage International Airport residential sound insulation program. 
   * $9,466,000 for runway rehabilitation at Anchorage International Airport. Rehabilitation efforts will prepare the airport to receive new large cargo planes in 2007. These efforts will also help reduce ground and air delays. 
   * $14,800,000 to complete construction of a replacement airport and a snow removal equipment storage building for the community of Tuntutuliak.
