GRANTS AWARDED TO ALASKA PROGRAMS
  The Alaska Congressional Delegation today announced that federal agencies have awarded a series of funding grants to Alaska groups and communities. The grants total more than $42 million.  
  National Science Foundation
   * $695,364 to the University of Alaska Fairbanks for a project entitled "Collaborative Research: Moved by the State: Perspectives on Relocation and Resettlement in the Circumpolar North," which is under the direction of Peter Schweitzer. 
  U.S. Department of Education 
   * $268,559 to Hermon Hutchens Elementary School/Valdez City School District through the Elementary and Secondary School Counseling Demonstration Grant Program. 
  U.S. Department of Transportation - Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)
   * $17,498,046 to the Anchorage International Airport for the rehabilitation of heavy aircraft parking aprons. 
   $4,500,000 to the Anchorage International Airport to continue the airport's Residential Sound Insulation Program. This grant will be used to purchase sound insulation, which helps reduce interior noise levels for homes located near the airport. 
   * $10,427,060 to complete improvements to the runway safety area at the Ketchikan International Airport. The existing runway safety area does not meet FAA standards. 
   A Phase II grant of $3,541,997 to complete improvements to the seaplane base and runway safety area at the Kenai Municipal Airport. The existing runway safety area does not meet FAA standards and needs to be expanded to the greatest possible extent. The existing water lane length and width is insufficient for the airport's current aircraft mix. 
  U.S. Department of Transportation - Federal Transit Administration 
   * $594,709 to the Central Area Rural Transit System in Soldotna for the Kenai Peninsula Job Access & Reverse Commute Program. Funds will be used to purchase hardware to improve communication, ride reservations, and ride coordination. 
   $4,700,000 to the Alaska Railroad Corporation for the purchase of a Diesel Multiple Unit (DMU) rail vehicle. This DMU will provide rail service to backcountry recreation areas in Chugach National Forest.
