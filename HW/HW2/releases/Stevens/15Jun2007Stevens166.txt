GRANTS AWARDED TO ALASKA PROGRAMS
  The Alaska Congressional Delegation today announced that federal agencies have awarded a series of funding grants to Alaska projects and universities. The grants total almost $11 million.  
  National Science Foundation
   * $9 million to the University of Alaska Fairbanks. This grant will support a project entitled "Resilience and Vulnerability in a Rapidly Changing North: The Integration of Physical, Biological and Social Processes," under the direction of University of Alaska Fairbanks professor Peter Schweitzer. 
  U.S. Department of Commerce - National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration
   * $971,786 to the Alaska Groundfish Monitoring Project to continue cooperative management of the Fishery Management Plans associated with crab, scallop, and rockfish in federal waters. This grant will support 16 projects with a mix of research, administrative support, and fishery management activities. 
  U.S. Department of Education
   * $742,154 to renovate the University of Alaska, Southeast Sitka Campus. This grant was awarded through the Tribally Controlled Colleges and Universities, Alaska Native Serving Institution Program.
