STATE OF ALASKA DEPUTY COMMISSIONER OF AVIATION TO TESTIFY AT U.S. SENATE HEARING ON RURAL AIR SERVICE
  Mr. John Torgerson has accepted an invitation from Sen. Ted Stevens (R-Alaska), Chairman of the Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation, to testify at an oversight hearing to examine the Federal Rural Air Service program. This Aviation Subcommittee hearing will be held on Thursday, September 14, 2006 at 10:00 a.m. in Room 253 of the Russell Senate Office Building.  
   Mr. Torgerson is Deputy Commissioner of Aviation for the Alaska Department of Transportation and Public Facilities. He will testify about the purpose, importance, and challenges of the Essential Air Service and the Small Community Air Service Development programs, which are administered by the U.S. Department of Transportation. Mr. Torgerson has also been asked to discuss possible reforms needed to improve these programs, which are essential to efforts to provide air service in rural Alaska. 
   Other hearing witnesses include Mr. Michael Reynolds, Deputy Assistant Secretary for Aviation for the U.S. Department of Transportation; Mr. Gerald Dillingham, Director of Civil Aviation Issues for the U.S. Government Accountability Office; Ms. Faye Malarkey, Vice President of Legislative Affairs for the Regional Airline Association; and Mr. Doug Kaercher, Hill County Commissioner for the National Association of Counties. 
   A live webcast of this hearing will be available at commerce.senate.gov.
