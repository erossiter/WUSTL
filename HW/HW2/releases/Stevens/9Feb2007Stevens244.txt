GRANTS AWARDED TO ALASKA PROGRAMS
  The Alaska Congressional Delegation on Friday, Feb. 9, announced that federal agencies have awarded a series of funding grants to Alaska groups and communities. The grants total nearly $3 million.  
   National Science Foundation 
   * $270,173 to the University of Alaska Anchorage for a project entitled "IPY: Collaborative Research: Study of arctic ecosystem changes in the IPY using the International Tundra Experiment." This project is under the direction of Jeffrey Welker. 
   U.S. Department of Health and Human Services 
   * $2,474,432 to the Rural Alaska Community Action Program for Head Start projects. 
   * $129,412 to the Alaska Business Development Center's Assets for Independence Demonstration Program. 
   U.S. Department of Homeland Security 
   * The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program (AFG) has awarded $43,946 to Kachemak Emergency Services. The primary goal of the AFG Program is to meet the firefighting and emergency response needs of fire departments and nonaffiliated emergency medical services organizations.
