SEN. SNOWE ISSUES STATEMENT ON CONFIRMATION OF AMBASSADOR NEGROPONTE AS FIRST DIRECTOR OF NATIONAL INTELLIGENCE
  Sen. Olympia J. Snowe (R-Maine), a member of the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence, today congratulated John Negroponte on his confirmation by the Senate as the first Director of National Intelligence (DNI) by a vote of 98 to 2.  
 Snowe has been integral to the discussions, examinations, debates and policymaking work regarding our nation's intelligence agencies over the last two years. Snowe issued the following statement: 
   "John Negroponte has an extraordinary record of service to his nation, vast experience in foreign policy and a devotion to serving America's interests to the best of his ability. I am confident he will successfully bring his considerable ability to bear on the crucial task of providing leadership and coordination to our nation's intelligence community as well as accurate and reliable intelligence in a post-9/11 world. 
   "The creation of a Director of National Intelligence is essential to strengthening our overall intelligence to better protect ourselves from a dangerous and ever-changing enemy. I cosponsored legislation to create a DNI, and I am pleased that this sensible reform is finally coming to fruition."
