SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE OVER $2.2 MILLION IN FUNDING FROM ROBERT WOOD JOHNSON FOUNDATION
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation has awarded grants totaling $2,281,229 to four groups in Maine. Since the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation became a national philanthropy foundation in 1972, it has awarded $32,057,820 to 127 programs throughout Maine.  
   "The Robert Wood Johnson Foundation has a long tradition of supporting health initiatives in our state," said Snowe and Collins in a joint statement. "We are pleased to see this practice continue with their award of over $2.2 million to entities throughout Maine. We are sure that the grants they have provided will contribute greatly to the continued health and well-being of residents throughout the state." 
   The funding will be distributed as follows: 
   * $35,000 to the Christian Life Center for Faith in Action 
   * $100,000 to the Maine Center for Public Health for InformationLinks: Connecting Public Health with Health Information Exchange 
   * $328,000 to the Penobscot Community Health Center for providing integrated mental health and substance abuse services for those at highest risk of homelessness 
   * $1,818,229 to the University of Southern Maine, Edmund S. Muskie School of Public Service for technical assistance and direction for community partnership for older adults 
   The Robert Wood Johnson Foundation is the nation's largest philanthropy organization dedicated exclusively to improving the health and health care of all Americans and particularly the care of the most vulnerable. Their projects include investing in improving the quality of health care, haling the epidemic of childhood obesity, building the health care workforce, ensuring that all Americans are covered by health insurance, addressing the nursing shortage, improving the public health infrastructure, ending racial and ethnic disparities in care, and reducing the negative effects of tobacco, alcohol and other drugs.
