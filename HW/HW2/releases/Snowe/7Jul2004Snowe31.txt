SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $3.7 MILLION IN HHS GRANTS
  Sens. Olympia Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced $3,762,541 in federal funding to various health projects throughout Maine, including $3,602,541 to the MDI Biological Laboratory in Mount Desert for comparative functional genome studies.  
 The grants are awarded by the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services (HHS).
   "This federal funding is critical to educating Mainers on the health services available to them. Additionally, these awards will help to ensure cutting-edge research institutions, such as Mount Desert Lab, remain at the forefront of the medical community," Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement.
   The federal funding was allocated as follows:
   * $3,602,541 to the MDI Biological Laboratory, for comparative functional genomic INBRE studies in Maine.
   * $160,000 to the Maine Department of Human Services, for the Maine Medicare Education Partnership.
