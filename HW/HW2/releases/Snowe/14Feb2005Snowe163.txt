SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE $198,820 IN HUD FUNDING TO CENTRAL MAINE COMMUNITY COLLEGE
  Sens. Olympia Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) has awarded Central Maine Community College $198,820 in funding.  
   "Funding used to promote education in the state of Maine is always well-received and much-appreciated," Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "This grant money provided by HUD will contribute greatly to the development of technical skills and opportunities for Mainers." 
   The grant awarded to Central Maine Community College will be used to assist in development of a technical college center at Western Maine University and Technical Center, South Paris, Maine. 
   The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) funds private housing developments to assist in housing opportunities for low income residents. HUD provides rental assistance to approximately 1.5 million low-income households. It also funds community revitalization projects to meet local needs and expand opportunities for Americans.
