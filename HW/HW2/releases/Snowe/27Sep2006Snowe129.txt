SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $208,000 IN HOUSING AND URBAN DEVELOPMENT FUNDING
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) has awarded $208,632 in Housing Counseling Program awards to various agencies in Maine.  
   "The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development has long supported projects in Maine that provide access to affordable housing," said Senators Snowe and Collins. "These funds will provide a variety of housing counseling services to homebuyers, homeowners, low- to moderate-income renters, and the homeless." 
   Funding is allocated as follows: 
   * 26,507 grant to Coastal Economic Development Corporation in Bath. 
  * 25,857 grant to Coastal Enterprises, Incorporated in Wiscasset. 
  * 100,000 grant to the Maine State Housing Authority in Augusta. 
  * 27,158 grant to Peoples Regional Opportunity Program in Portland. 
  * 29,110 grant to Pine Tree Legal Services, Incorporated in Portland. 
   The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) funds private housing developments to assist in housing opportunities for low income residents. HUD provides rental assistance to approximately 1.5 million low-income households. It also funds community revitalization projects to meet local needs and expand opportunities for Americans.
