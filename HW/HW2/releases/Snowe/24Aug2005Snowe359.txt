SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $1 MILLION IN HHS FUNDS FOR MAINE
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) has awarded four grants totaling $1,083,882 to various entities in Maine for research and health services.  
   "Health and Human Services funds support essential health and research programs throughout Maine," Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "Such funding allows health service providers to better respond to the needs of Maine residents. It also permits research institutions all over the state to perform groundbreaking and innovative research." 
   The funding will be allocated as follows: 
   * $344,400 to Jackson Laboratory for research on kidney disease 
   * $221,295 to the University of Maine Orono for clinical research related to neurological disorders 
   * $196,413 to Katahdin Valley Health Center for health care and other facilities 
   * $100,000 to Maine Medical Center for a poison control stabilization and enhancement program 
   * $98,206 to the Community Counseling Center in Portland for a rural health outreach special initiative 
   * $49,105 to Houlton Regional Hospital for health care and other facilities 
   * $40,000 to the Regional Medical Center at Lubec for a health center cluster 
   * $29,463 to Fish River Rural Health in Eagle Lake for health care and other facilities 
   * $5,000 to Jackson Laboratory for human genome research 
   The Department of Health and Human Services is the United States government's principal agency for protecting the health of all Americans and providing essential human services, especially for those who are least able to help themselves. HHS works closely with state and local governments and administers more grant dollars than all other federal agencies combined.
