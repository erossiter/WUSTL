SEN. SNOWE ANNOUNCES $257 MILLION FOR DETAIL DESIGN OF NAVY DESTROYER AT BATH IRON WORKS
  Sen. Olympia J. Snowe (R-Maine) on Wednesday, January 31, announced that the Department of the Navy has awarded Bath Iron Works in Bath, Maine a contract valued at more than $257 million for the detail design of the next generation Guided Missile Destroyer 1000 Zumwalt Class Destroyer.  
   "The DDG Destroyer will be the backbone of the United States Navy in the 21st century," Snowe said. "After many years of moving toward the construction of the DDG destroyers, this award for detail design of the next generation destroyer marks another milestone for the men and women of Bath Iron Works and puts them another step closer to doing what they do best - building the greatest military ships in the world." 
   The total value of the cost-plus-award-fee contract is $257,473,700. The DDG 1000 Zumwalt Class Destroyer, formerly known as the DD(X) Destroyer Program, will provide affordable and credible independent forward presence and deterrence as well as advanced land attack capability for the United States Navy. The total value of the detail design effort is $337,367,205. $79,893,505 was awarded for advanced zone detail design as part of the basic contract.
