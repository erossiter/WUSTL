SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE OVER $1 MILLION IN RURAL DEVELOPMENT FUNDS FOR MAINE
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Rural Development program has awarded $1,132,100 in grants and loans to entities throughout the state of Maine.  
   "The USDA Rural Development program provides vital opportunities for rural Mainers to enhance community services and boost economic growth," Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "This funding will make possible a variety of rural community projects and essential equipment acquisitions." 
   The funding is distributed as follows: 
   * $149,000 loan and $36,000 grant to the Fryeburg Rescue Association to purchase an ambulance and crash vehicle to replace dated vehicles that are not up to code 
   * $250,000 grant to the Port Clyde Water District to replace a majority of the water distribution system 
   * $50,000 grant to the Pleasant Point Passamaquoddy Reservation to purchase equipment for a Head Start Program for the safety and security of participating children 
   * $12,100 grant to the Indian Township Passamaquoddy Reservation to purchase daycare equipment and install a sprinkler system 
   * $75,000 grant to Rangeley Lakes Cross Country Ski Club to create a 46.5 mile trail system for year-round use 
   * $180,000 loan to Penobscot Valley Hospital to support a new surgical services suite 
   * $380,000 loan to Harrington Family Health Center for an addition to the health center 
   The USDA Rural Development program provides financial loans and grants to help improve the economy and quality of life in all of rural America. The funds are used for community programs, which seek to construct or improve community facilities for health care, public safety, and public services.
