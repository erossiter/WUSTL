SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $3.6 MILLION TO HOUSING PROJECTS THROUGHOUT MAINE
  Sens. Olympia Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced $3,650,468 in federal funding to various housing programs throughout Maine from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development.  
 These federal grants will support innovative housing and economic development activities in rural Maine.
   "Investment in urban development through expanding access to economic opportunities is vital to ensuring quality housing to all Mainers," Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement.
   The federal grants were awarded to the following recipients in Maine:
   * $2,567,000 to the city of Portland through the Community Development Block Grant program, which helps to provide annual grants to states and local units of government to develop viable urban communities.
   * $751,084 to the city of Portland through the HOME program, which seeks to expand the supply of decent, affordable housing to low and very low-income families.
   * $150,000 to the Growth Council of Oxford Hills, to aid in the improvement of economic and housing growth.
   * $95,034 to the city of Portland through the Emergency Shelter Grant Program, which provides annual grants to state, local, and private entities to improve the quality and number of emergency homeless shelters.
   * $87,350 to the Penobscot Indian Nation of Maine.
