SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $75,000 IN RURAL DEVELOPMENT FUNDS FOR MAINE
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Rural Development program has awarded $75,006 in grants to farmers, ranchers, and rural small businesses throughout the state of Maine.  
   "The funding provided by the USDA will help farmers and small business owners make their operations more energy-efficient," Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "We are pleased to see funding that is geared toward helping rural businesses and the environment." 
   The funding is distributed as follows: 
   * $15,510 grant to L. Herbert York of Farmington. 
  * $17,996 grant to R. Christian Andrus of Hope. 
  * $41,500 grant to Maple Hill Farm, Inc. of Hallowell. 
   As a part of the Renewable Energy Systems and Energy Efficient Grant Program, the funds will be used to assist farmers, ranchers, and rural small businesses in developing renewable energy systems and making energy-efficient improvements to their operations. 
   The USDA Rural Development program provides financial loans and grants to help improve the economy and quality of life in all of rural America. The funds are used for community programs, which seek to construct or improve community facilities for health care, public safety, and public services.
