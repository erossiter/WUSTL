SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $300,000 IN RURAL DEVELOPMENT FUNDS FOR MAINE COMMUNITY GROUPS
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Rural Development program has awarded $329,752 in grants to community groups throughout the state of Maine.  
   "These USDA Rural Development funds will provide vital opportunities for rural Mainers to enhance community services and boost economic growth," Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "The acquisition of affordable housing made possible by this funding will improve the quality of life for Mainers in Oxford , Penobscot, and Waldo counties." 
   The funding is distributed as follows: 
   * $259,752 grant to Community Concepts, Inc in South Paris . The funds will be used to provide loans to a private non-profit organization to help single families acquire housing. 
   * $30,000 grant to the Penquis Community Action Program, Inc in Bangor . The funds will be used to support housing rehabilitation and repair activities in Penobscot County . 
   * $30,000 grant to Western Maine Community Action in East Wilton . The funds will be used to support housing rehabilitation and repair activities in Penobscot County . 
   * $10,000 grant to Waldo Community Action Partners in Belfast . The funds will be used to support housing rehabilitation and repair activities in Waldo County . 
   The USDA Rural Development program provides financial loans and grants to help improve the economy and quality of life in all of rural America . The funds are used for community programs, which seek to construct or improve community facilities for health care, public safety, and public services.
