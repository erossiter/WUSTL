SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE $1.6 MILLION IN TRANSPORTATION GRANTS TO MAINE
  Sens. Olympia Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT) has awarded Maine $1,630,000 in federal funding for transportation projects throughout the state.  
   "Airport infrastructure is a critical part of our transportation system," Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "Funding from the department of transportation will allow recipient airports to make much-needed improvements to their facilities, thereby increasing the safety of their services." 
   The following towns will receive funding: 
   $780,000 to Bangor to construct an access road to improve public access to Bangor International Airport $420,000 to Bar Harbor to acquire land that will be a part of a runway protection zone at the Hancock County-Bar Harbor Airport $260,000 to Bangor to reconstruct a customs clearing apron and taxilane at Bangor International Airport $120,000 to Dexter to conduct an environmental study for potential improvements to a runway approach at Dexter Regional Airport $50,000 to the Town of Jackman to acquire property easements for Newton Field that will allow them to clear and maintain runway approaches 
   The Department of Transportation (DOT) aims to improve safety, increase mobility in support of the nation's economy, protect human and natural environment, achieve organizational excellence, and support the national security strategy. DOT serves the United States by ensuring a fast, safe, efficient, accessible and convenient transportation system that meets our vital national interests and enhances the quality of life of the American people.
