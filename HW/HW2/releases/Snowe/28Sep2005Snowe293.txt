SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $1 MILLION FOR MAINE ENERGY, ENVIRONMENT PROJECTS
  Sens. Olympia Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Energy has awarded $1,332,000 to the University of Southern Maine and Maine Public Utilities Commission to pursue projects related to clean energy and environmental health.  
   "As Hurricane Katrina has plainly shown us, energy efficiency and conservation is absolutely vital to the health of our economy," said Senators Snowe and Collins in a joint statement. "The recent funding awarded throughout Maine by the Department of Energy will encourage research and innovation relating to the more effective use of our energy resources." 
   The University of Southern Maine has received $1,157,000 to assist the expansion of the Center for Integrated and Applied Environmental Toxicology. 
   A $75,000 grant was awarded to the Maine Public Utilities Commission. They, in conjunction with Frontier Energy and Turners Island LLC will design and construct a biodiesel wholesale distribution facility with technical assistance and support provided by Maine Clean Communities Clean Cities Program, E2 Tech Council, and the Maine Center for Enterprise Development. The facility will be located at an existing short line railroad in South Portland. 
   A grant of $100,000 was awarded to the Maine Public Utilities Commission. They will work with the University of Maine to initiate the Rebuild America High Performance Campus Project to oversee energy conservation decisions through education and development of "design templates" demonstrating energy efficiency technologies to be considered for seven university campuses.
