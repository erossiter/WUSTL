SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCES $600,000 IN FUNDING TO MAINE DEPARTMENT OF CORRECTIONS
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Justice has awarded $600,000 to the Maine Department of Corrections. As a part of the Formula Grants program, the funds will be utilized to support State and local delinquency prevention and intervention efforts and juvenile justice system improvements. 
   "The Department of Justice funds awarded to the Maine DOC will help keep our kids and communities safe," said Senators Snowe and Collins in a join statement. "An investment in Maine's youth is an investment in their future and the future of our state."
   The Formula Grants Program is authorized under the Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention (JJDP) Act of 2002. The grant program areas include planning and administration, state advisory group allocation, compliance monitoring, juvenile justice issues for American Indian Tribes, and prevention of substance abuse and serious and violent crimes by juveniles.
