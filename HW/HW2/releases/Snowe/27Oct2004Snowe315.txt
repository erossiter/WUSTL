SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $2.5 MILLION IN WELFARE GRANTS
  Sens. Olympia Snowe (R-Maine) and Susan Collins (R-Maine) today announced an additional award of $2,563,677 in federal funding for Maine's Food Stamps program.  
 This funding was awarded by the Department of Health and Human Services' Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) program.
   "This funding was awarded based on the Maine's success in transitioning low-income families into the workforce with assistance from the Food Stamps program," Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "This federal funding award is critical to further enhancing the state's efforts to assist low income families." 
   Temporary Assistance for Needy Families' (TANF) Food Stamps program provides monthly benefits that help low income households buy the food they need for good health. Every year High Performance Bonuses, such as the one Maine is receiving, are awarded to Food Stamp programs with proven success rates.
