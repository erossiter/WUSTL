SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE DIGITAL CONVERSION GRANT FOR SALT POND COMMUNITY BROADCASTING
  U.S. Senators Olympia J. Snowe and Susan Collins (R-Maine) announced today that the Corporation for Public Broadcasting (CPB) has awarded a grant in the amount of $110,000 to WERU-FM, a division of Salt Pond Community Broadcasting, Inc. in Blue Hill. These funds will be used to assist the local public radio station convert from analog to digital broadcasting. 
   "From reporting local news to broadcasting public safety alerts, local public radio stations provide an invaluable service to Maine communities," Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "The switchover from analog to digital technology will improve the sound quality of local radio broadcasts and broaden the range of services each station offers. The funding provided by the CPB will support WERU-FM's digital conversion and, in turn, enhance and expand the local airwaves of Eastern Maine."
   Congress appropriated $29.2 million in Fiscal Year 2008 to the Corporation for Public Broadcasting to help local public broadcasters acquire the digital equipment necessary to transition to all-digital broadcasting.
