SEN. SNOWE COMMENTS ON DEATH OF MAINE SOLDIER AFTER HIS RETURN TO U.S.
  Sen. Olympia J. Snowe (R-Maine) issued the following statement today on the death of Michael D. Jones, who served in the 133rd Engineer Battalion of the Maine Army National Guard:  
   "Our thoughts and prayers are with the family and friends of Michael Jones. We will forever remember his honorable and courageous service. 
   "For all our men and women who continue to serve bravely in harm's way, we offer them our unwavering support and heartfelt appreciation. 
   "I am in close contact with senior officials at the Department of Defense regarding Michael Jones' death, and I stand ready to assist his family in any way I can during this difficult time." 
   SFC Jones served in A Company 133rd Engineer Battalion that arrived last week in Fort Drum, N.Y., after serving a year in Iraq. Shortly after the flight overseas, Jones became seriously ill. He was rushed to State University of New York (SUNY) Hospital where he underwent emergency surgery. SFC Jones' condition grew progressively worse until he died this morning.
