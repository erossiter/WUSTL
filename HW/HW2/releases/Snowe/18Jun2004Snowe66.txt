SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $4 MILLION FOR COASTAL ZONE MANAGEMENT PROJECTS
  Sens. Olympia Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced a total of $4,551,164 in federal funding for coastal zone management projects in Maine.  
 The federal grants were awarded by the National Oceanic & Atmospheric Administration under the U.S. Department of Commerce.
   "This federal funding is critical for implementing the approved coastal zone management programs that are vital to the protection and long-term prosperity of Maine's pristine coastline," said Snowe and Collins in a joint statement.
   Federal grants awarded for Coastal Zone Management projects include:
   * $2,968,000 to the Maine State Planning Office, to implement a federally approved coastal management program including conservation of coastal habitats, management of storm water runoff, shoreline development, improve water quality, and perform maintenance dredging.
   * $1,583.164 to the Town of Yarmouth, to acquire at least two sites along the Royal River estuary to conserve open space and provide recreational opportunities for the public.
