SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $4.2 MILLION IN RURAL DEVELOPMENT FUNDS FOR MAINE
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Rural Development program has awarded various entities in Maine a total of $4,211,000 in funding to assist development projects throughout the state.  
   "The USDA Rural Development program provides essential opportunities for Maine communities," Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "This funding will help enhance community facilities, create jobs, and boost economic growth for rural areas throughout the state." 
   The funding is distributed as follows: 
   * $1,613,000 loan to MCL Realty, LLC and Anania & Associates. The funds will be used to assist the construction of a light-manufacturing facility and to refinance existing debt. 
  * $2,000, 000 loan to trustees of Washington Academy in East Machias. The funds will be used to construct a dormitory that will house 44 students as well as apartments for adult supervisors. 
  * $120,000 loan to the Brixham Montessori Friends School in York. The funds will be used to expand the school in terms of classrooms, additional programs, and administrative space. 
  * $7,300 grant to the Harmony Volunteer Fire Department in Harmony.. The funds will be used to replace the roof on the fire station. 
  * $32,000 grant to the Patriachs Club in Harmony. The funds will be used for the construction of ADA accessible bathroom facilities and storage area at the fair grounds. A new surface wastewater treatment system will also be installed and new maintenance equipment will be purchased. 
  * $7,500 grant to the Upper Kennebec Valley Ambulance Service in Bingham. The funds will be used for the construction of a new addition to the ambulance station. The space will house quarters for the on-call Emergency Medical Technician staff. 
  * $25,000 grant to the Heritage Vivant - Living Heritage Society in Van Buren. The funds will be used to renovate two historic structures critical to the operation of the Acadian Living Heritage Village. 
  * $100,000 grant to the Penobscot Indian Nation on Indian Island. The funds will be used to replace and extend water lines on the Island. 
  * $6,200 grant to the Swans Island Educational Society on Swans Island. The funds will be used to replace a failing septic system and to make repairs to the driveway. 
  * $200,000 grant to the Pleasant Point Passamaquoddy Reservation in Perry. The funds will be used to construct a community center for the purpose of providing cultural education and other community social events. 
  * $50,000 loan to Family Focus in Bath. The funds will be used to renovate an existing building for the purpose of expanding a classroom at the childcare facility. 
  * $50,000 grant to Center Theater, Inc. in Dover Foxcroft. The funds will be used to equip the Center Theater building with theatrical lighting and sound systems. 
   The USDA Rural Development program provides financial loans and grants to help improve the economy and quality of life in all of rural America. The funds are used for community programs, which seek to construct or improve community facilities for health care, public safety, and public services.
