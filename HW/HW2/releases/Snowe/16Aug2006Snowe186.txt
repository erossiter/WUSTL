SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $130,000 IN U.S DEPARTMENT OF HOMELAND SECURITY FUNDING FOR MAINE FIREFIGHTER PROGRAMS
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) announced today that the Assistance to Firefighters Grant program has awarded $15,824 in funding to the Topsfield Volunteer Fire Department and $72,629 in funding to the Sebago Fire Department. The Fire Prevention and Safety Grant program will award $41,828 in funding to the Maine State Fire Marshal's office in Augusta. These funds will be utilized to enhance safety operations and to purchase equipment. 
   "Firefighters risk their safety each and every day to save the lives of others," said Snowe and Collins in a joint statement. "These funds will only improve the overall effectiveness of Fire Departments throughout Maine, and protect those men and women whose bravery protects us all."
   The Department of Homeland Security's Office of Grants and Training (G&T) administers the program, in cooperation with the U.S. Fire Administration. Assistance to Firefighters Grants and the Fire Prevention Safety Grants are awarded to fire departments to enhance their ability to protect the public and fire service personnel from fire and related hazards.
