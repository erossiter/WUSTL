SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MORE THAN $7.5 MILLION IN HEAD START FUNDING TO MAINE
  Sens. Olympia Snowe and Susan Collins today announced nearly $7,543,906 in federal funding to continue Head Start programs around the state.  
 The funding, which comes through the Department of Health and Human Services, will help fund child care and handicapped services. 
   "Head Start provides our neediest children with the services they need to get a jump start in their education," said Senators Snowe and Collins in a joint statement. "We applaud the continued funding for these programs that offer a variety of services to improve the readiness of young children, contributing to their long-term success in life." 
   Head Start is a comprehensive child development program that serves children from birth to age five, pregnant women, and their families. Since its inception in 1965, the program has served more than 18 million children throughout the country. 
   The Head Start funding was allocated as follows: 
   * $1,893,906 for Community Concepts Inc in South Paris. 
  * $1,760,000 for the Penquis Community Action Program in Bangor. 
  * $1,460,000 for the Peoples Regional Opportunity Program in Portland. 
  * $1,230,000 for the Southern Kennebec Child Development Corp. in Augusta. 
  * $1,200,000 for the Aroostook County Action Program in Presque Isle.
