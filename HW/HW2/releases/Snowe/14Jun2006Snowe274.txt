SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE $114,000 IN ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT FUNDS FOR MAINE
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Commerce (USDOC) Economic Development Administration (EDA) has awarded a total of $114,000 in investments to the Androscoggin Valley Council of Governments and the Northern Maine Development Commission.  
   "The U.S. Department of Commerce Economic Development Administration investments provide vital financial support for rural Mainers to boost economic growth," Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "We applaud the recipient groups for their initiative and commitment to improving the lives of the Mainers they serve." 
   The funding is distributed as follows to support long-term regional economic development planning efforts: 
   * $60,000 investment to the Androscoggin Valley Council of Governments, based in Auburn, Maine and;
   * $54,000 investment to the Northern Maine Development Commission, based in Caribou, Maine.
   U.S. Department of Commerce Economic Development Administration investments are focused on locally-developed, regionally-based economic development initiatives that achieve the highest return on the taxpayers' investment and that directly contribute to economic growth.
