SEN. SNOWE NAMED ONE OF WORLD'S 100 MOST POWERFUL WOMEN
  Forbes Magazine has named Sen. Olympia J. Snowe (R-Maine) as one of the "World's 100 Most Powerful Women".  
 Ranked 54th in this year's rankings, Snowe was listed at 58 last year. 
   Snowe joins her Senate colleagues Hillary Rodham Clinton (D-NY), Elizabeth Dole (R-NC), Dianne Feinstein (D-CA) and Kay Bailey Hutchison (R-TX) on the listing by the business magazine. Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice was selected as the "World's Most Powerful Woman". 
   "I am honored that Forbes Magazine would include me in such an accomplished and distinguished group of women. For 26 years, I have proudly represented the people of Maine in the U.S. Congress. If my inclusion in this list has any impact at all, I sincerely hope it will inspire the next generation of women in Maine and across the country to follow their dreams and realize that there are no limits to what they can achieve," Snowe said. 
   The full list of Forbes' "100 Most Powerful Women" can be found at www.forbes.com.
