SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE $5.4 MILLION IN HEAD START FUNDING FOR MAINE
  Sens. Olympia Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) has awarded Maine $5,435,663 in Head Start funding.  
 The money will go toward funding full year/part day instructors for numerous centers around the State.
   "Head Start programs and their services are vital to many families in the State of Maine," Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "The recent funds granted by HHS will be integral to providing greater opportunities for children and their families." 
   Specifically, the funding will go to: 
   * Child and Families Opportunities, Inc., Ellsworth, ME - $843,244 
   * Coastal Economic Development, Inc., Bath, ME - $834,815 
   * Kennebec Valley Community Action Program, Waterville, ME - $1,326,700 
   * Androscoggin Head Start and Child Care, Lewiston, ME - $1,294,985 
   * York County Community Action Corporation, Sanford, ME - $1,135,919
   Head Start is a comprehensive child development program that serves children from birth to age five, pregnant women, and their families. Since its inception in 1965, the program has served more than 18 million children throughout the country. It was established as part of the War on Poverty, which embodied a basic belief in education as the solution to poverty.
