SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE $600,000 FOR HOMELAND SECURITY EFFORTS
  Sens. Olympia Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced $600,000 in federal funding for research by the Orono Sensor Research Development Corporation (SRD) to develop a Lightweight Autonomous Chemical Identification System (LACIS) The federal grant is awarded by the Department of Homeland Security.  
   "In these uncertain times, we must utilize the innovation and ingenuity of our small businesses in developing advanced technology that will help protect our nation. This funding will help us increase our capabilities to detect and prevent biological and chemical agents that could be used in attacks," Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "We applaud the Department of Homeland Security for recognizing the quality work of the Orono Sensor Research Development Corporation and their contribution to protecting our country." 
   The Lightweight Autonomous Chemical Identification System (LACIS) is a detection system for Chemical Weapons Agents (CWAs). The LACIS detector's greater sensitivity and allow quicker response time for emergency responders to areas having dangerous chemical concentration levels.
