SEN. SNOWE MEETS WITH ARCHBISHOP DEMETRIOS OF GREEK ORTHODOX CHURCH
  Sen. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) today met with His Eminence Archbishop Demetrios, Primate of the Greek Orthodox Church in America. Sen.  
 Snowe and the Archbishop discussed recent events pertinent to both Greek-Americans and the international Greek community. 
   "It was an honor to meet with the esteemed Archbishop Demetrios today," said Snowe. "As a Greek-American and member of the Greek Orthodox Church, I am especially delighted to have this opportunity to discuss issues of mutual concern with His Eminence. His work and accomplishments are a testament to his strength of character and to his efforts on behalf of the Greek community." 
   In the time following his enthronement, His Eminence Archbishop Demetrios has labored together with the Hierarchs, clergy, and laity of the Greek Orthodox Archdiocese in an intense effort to strengthen conditions of unity and peace and to advance the administrative and ecclesiastical stability of the work of the Church in America. 
   His Eminence Archbishop Demetrios was elected Archbishop of America on August 19, 1999, by the Holy and Sacred Synod of the Ecumenical Patriarchate convened by His All Holiness Ecumenical Patriarch Bartholomew. He was enthroned as the spiritual leader of 1.5 million Greek Orthodox Christians in America at the Archdiocesan Cathedral of the Holy Trinity in New York City on Saturday, September 18, 1999. Archbishop Demetrios, Primate of the Greek Orthodox Church in America and Exarch of the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, is the sixth Archbishop of America since the Greek Orthodox Archdiocese was established in 1922.
