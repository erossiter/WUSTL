SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE $66,670 IN FUNDING FROM NATIONAL PARK SERVICE
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) announced today that the National Park Service has allocated $66,670 from the Land and Water Conservation Fund (LWCF) to Maine for park renovations in the towns of Rumford and Pittsfield. 
   "Maine's parks are one of its most precious resources, facilitating outdoor recreation and enhancing quality of life," said Senators Snowe and Collins in a joint statement. "These funds from the Land and Water Conservation Fund will be instrumental in improving and expanding the park facilities in the towns of Rumford and Pittsfield."
   The funding will be distributed as follows: 
   (1) $50,000 for Riverside Park in Rumford. The funding will be used to develop trails along the scenic greenway and to connect to other parks and open spaces. 
   (2) $16,670 for Stein Park in Pittsfield. The funding will be used to develop a complete recreational area with access to the pond including pathway, lighting, landscaping, and amenities to upgrade public access and improve safety.
   The Land and Water Conservation Fund is a partnership program that provides matching funds to states and local communities for acquisition, development, and conservation of parks and open space. The program is administered by NPS in partnership with state governments.
