SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE TRADE ADJUSTMENT ASSISTANCE FOR BURLINGTON HOMES OF MAINE WORKERS
  U.S. Senators Olympia J. Snowe and Susan Collins (R-Maine) announced today that the U.S. Department of Labor has certified Trade Adjustment Assistance for approximately 159 employees of Burlington Homes of Maine, Inc., in Oxford, who became partially or totally separated from employment on or after March 11, 2007. Burlington Homes of Maine, which produces manufactured and modular homes, experienced a significant decline in sales, production, and employment last year as a result of increased competition from global trade. These workers can access their trade adjustment benefits by going to their local one-stop career center. The easiest way to locate the closest one-stop is by going to www.servicelocator.org and entering their zip code. 
   "The loss of employment is a devastating reality for many Mainers as our weakened economy continues to depress the production and profits of small business across the country," Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "These trade adjustment services and benefits will assist the workers of Burlington Homes obtain new jobs and regain their livelihoods."
