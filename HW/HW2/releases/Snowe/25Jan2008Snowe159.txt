SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE HEALTH AND RESEARCH GRANTS
  U.S. Senators Olympia J. Snowe and Susan Collins (R-ME) announced today that the United States Department of Health and Human Services has awarded $1,188,666 in health and research grants to multiple Maine-based health and educational facilities. 
   "This funding will enable our best and brightest scientists and health care professionals to continue the pursuit for cures to some of our most degenerative and debilitating illnesses," Senator Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "The work that Maine's doctors, scientists and researches are doing is narrowing the gap between life and death."
   The Health and Human Service grants were distributed as follows: 
   ? Maine Medical Center in Portland received $319,813 from the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive Kidney Diseases for kidney research
   ? Jackson Laboratory in Bar Harbor received $379,895 from the National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke for research related to neurological disease
   ? The Maine State Department/Health and Human Services in Augusta received $218,958 from the national Center for Chronic Disease Prevention and Health Promotion for preventative health services
   ? The University of Southern Maine received $270,000 from the National Center for Research Resources for Micro-and-Nano Explorations of Health and Disease
