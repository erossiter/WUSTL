SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE $549,900 IN RURAL DEVELOPMENT FUNDS FOR MAINE
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Rural Development program has awarded $549,900 in grants and loans to entities throughout the state of Maine.  
   "The USDA funding will help boost economic growth in rural communities throughout the state," Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "The improvements made in Van Buren, Eagle Lake, and Richmond will establish a better quality of life while helping to create and save jobs." 
   The funding is distributed as follows: 
   * $99,900 grant to the town of Van Buren. The funds will be used to update buildings as a part of the Van Buren Main Street Downtown Revitalization Program. This project is expected to create/save 18 jobs. 
  * $360,000 loan to Eagle Lake Development Corporation in Eagle Lake. The funds will be used to update 40 units of existing elderly housing by replacing roofing, siding, and cabinets. 
  * $90,000 to loan Richmond Utilities District in Richmond. The funds will be used to replace 1050 feet of water lines. 
   The USDA Rural Development program provides financial loans and grants to help improve the economy and quality of life in all of rural America. The funds are used for community programs, which seek to construct or improve community facilities for health care, public safety, and public services.
