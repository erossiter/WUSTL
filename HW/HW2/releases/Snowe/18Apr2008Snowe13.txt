SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE $966,500 FROM NATIONAL ENDOWMENT FOR ARTS
  U.S. Senators Olympia J. Snowe and Susan Collins (R-Maine) announced today that the National Endowment for the Arts (NEA) has awarded 12 grants for groups throughout Maine, totaling $966,500 for artistic endeavors. 
   "The funds from the National Endowment for the Arts will undoubtedly enhance Maine's cultural and artistic landscape and benefit the lives of its citizens," Senators Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "Each project provides an exciting opportunity for people of all ages to create or appreciate art while promoting the cultural enrichment of our great state." 
   The funding is distributed as follows:
   * $769,500 to the Maine Arts Commission to support the activities of the NEA Partnership Agreement 
   * $10,000 to the Bowdoin Summer Music Festival, Inc. in Brunswick to support the EuroFest classical music concert series 
   * $10,000 to the Figures of Speech Theater in Freeport for its rural Maine Initiative. This project features a tour of repertory works and teaching residences in schools with accompanying outreach activities 
   * $23,000 to the Watershed Center for the Ceramic Arts in Newcastle to support the MudMobile, a van that exhibits a mobile ceramic gallery and resource center. This mobile gallery will visit as many as 24 elementary and middle schools throughout Maine as well as four rural schools, promoting and supporting ceramic arts 
   * $20,000 to the Portland Stage Company in Portland to support the production of "Peer Gynt," a play by Henrik Ibsen 
   * $34,000 to the Portland Museum of Art in Portland to support the Summer Institute for Teachers, a week-long summer workshop studying the art of Winslow Homer $15,000 to the Maine Humanities Council in Portland to support its New Books, New Readers program. The program promotes reading for adults with low literacy skills by providing books and organizing discussions for participants 
   * $10,000 to Greater Portland Landmarks, Inc. in Portland to support a convention for historic preservationists, design professionals, and community leaders to educate and promote the rehabilitation of historic structures as well as creating new buildings for the cultural, education, and residential enrichment 
   * $15,000 to the Center for Maine Contemporary Art in Portland to support Fear No Art, a statewide educational outreach program to make the work of contemporary Maine artists available to people across the state. 
   * $20,000 to Cultural Resources, Inc. in Rockport to support Story Bank, a folk and traditional arts project that will assist community leaders document local stories and present them to a wider audience. 
   * $10,000 to Maine Fiberarts in Topsham to support the production of Maine Fiberarts Tour Map: Studio and Farms. This publication will include a state map, a calendar of events, and educational content to promote fiberarts in Maine 
   * $30,000 to the Maine Indian Basketmakers Alliance in Old Town to support the Traditional Arts Apprenticeship Program and Maine Indian Basketmakers Gathering/Festival, which will support basketmakers and apprentices in the ancient traditions of ash and sweetgrass basketry among the Maliseet, Passamaquoddy, and Penobscot tribes of Maine 
   National Endowment for the Arts matching grants help support projects that involve the creation and presentation of artistically excellent work- both new and established. Projects include commissions, residencies, rehearsals, workshops, performances, exhibitions, publications, festivals and training programs. Grants from the National Endowment for the Arts generate, on average, seven dollars for each dollar awarded.
