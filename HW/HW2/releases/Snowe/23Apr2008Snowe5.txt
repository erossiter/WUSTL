SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE HOUSING GRANT FOR AROOSTOOK BAND OF MICMACS
  U.S. Senators Olympia J. Snowe and Susan Collins (R-Maine) today announced that the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development Indian Housing Block Grant (IHBG) Program has awarded a $1,101,844 grant to the Aroostook Band of Micmacs in Aroostook County. These funds will be used to expand affordable housing and support community development for the Presque Isle Native American community. 
   "With more than 1,000 members in Northern Maine, the Aroostook Band of Micmacs deserves access to quality affordable housing," Senators Snowe and Collins said. "These funds not only provide decent housing for this historical community, but also develop economic and cultural opportunities to ensure the successful legacy of the Micmac Nation."
   As a part of the Native American Housing Assistance and Self-Determination Act of 1996, the Indian Housing Block Grant (IHBG) Program provides an assortment of housing activities on Indian Reservations and Indian areas. These activities include housing development and assistance, housing management for low-income housing, crime prevention and safety, and model activities intended to solve affordable housing problems.
