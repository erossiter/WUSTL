SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE $1.5 MILLION IN RURAL DEVELOPMENT FUNDING
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Agriculture Rural Development Project has awarded several grants and loans totaling $1,520,000 to various entities throughout Maine.  
   "The funding recently awarded by the U.S. Department of Agriculture will allow the Penobscot Indian Nation, Washington Hancock Community Agency, and Marine Animal Lifeline to undertake projects that will greatly enhance the rural communities that they serve," said Senators Snowe and Collins in a joint statement. 
   The following have been awarded funding: 
   $1,000,000 loan to the Penobscot Indian Nation. The funds will be used to construct 8 units of family housing.
   $10,000 grant to Washington Hancock Community Agency. The funds will be used to provide supervision and technical assistance to 12 families who will build their homes. 
   $500,000 to Marine Animal Lifeline. The funds will be used to construct a new administrative, educational and rescue programming facility in Scarborough. 
   The USDA Rural Development mission provides guaranteed loans, approved loans, and approved grants. Rural Development is committed to helping improve the economy and quality of life in all of rural America. The loans and grants are used for community programs, which seek to construct or improve community facilities for health care, public safety, and public services.
