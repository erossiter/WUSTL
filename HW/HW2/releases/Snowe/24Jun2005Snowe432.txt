SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE ALMOST $1 MILLION IN U.S. DEPARTMENT OF LABOR GRANTS
  Sens. Olympia Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the Maine Department of Labor has received two grants totaling $949,777 from the U.S. Department of Labor.  
 The grants will support veterans employment and training programs. 
   "We must always support those who have served our country honorably and bravely and so it is critical that we make the health and well-being of veterans one of our country's greatest priorities," Snowe and Collins said in a joint statement. "These generous grant awards will allow the Maine Department of Labor to give the veterans of Maine some of the support they need to prepare them for employment and re-entrance into the workforce." 
   $199,777 has been awarded to the Maine Department of Labor, Bureau of Employment Services in Augusta for the Homeless Veterans' Reintegration Program (HVRP). HVRP aims to provide services to assist in reintegrating homeless veterans into meaningful employment within the labor force and to stimulate the development of effective service delivery systems that will address the complex problems facing homeless veterans. 
   $750,000 has been awarded to the Maine Department of Labor for the Veterans' Workforce Investment Programs (VWIP). VWIP aims to support employment and training programs, through grants or contracts, program to meet the needs for workforce investment activities of veterans with service-connected disabilities, veterans who have significant barriers to employment, veterans who served on active duty in the armed forces during a war or in a campaign or expedition for which a campaign badge has been authorized, and recently separated veterans.
