SEN. SNOWE ANNOUNCES NAVY SHIPS TO VISIT MAINE THIS SUMMER
  Today Sen. Olympia J. Snowe (R-Maine) announced that two U.S. Navy ships are scheduled to visit Maine this summer for festivals and celebrations. Sen.  
 Snowe formally requested each visit by letter to the Navy. 
   "Due to its location and abundant coastline, Maine has had a long and proud history of seafaring and shipbuilding," said Snowe. "Because of these traditions, having Naval ships visit Maine and participate in our local festivals is extremely meaningful. We look forward to welcoming them in grand style." 
   The festivals and visiting ships are as follows: 
   Portland 4th of July Celebration (Jul 1-5, 2005) 
   USS Iwo Jima (LHA 7) 
   Boothbay Windjammer Festival (June 20-24, 2005) 
   USS Vicksburg (CG 69) 
   Also, the USS Leyte Gulf (CG 55) that was originally scheduled to participate in the Belfast Bay Festival July 20-24, 2005 will longer able to visit due to new operational commitments. The USS Cape St. George (CG 71) will come to the festival in its place.
