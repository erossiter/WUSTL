SENS. SNOWE, COLLINS ANNOUNCE MAINE COMPANY AWARDED $100,000 GRANT FOR FIBROMYALGIA RESEARCH
  Sens. Olympia J. Snowe (R-ME) and Susan Collins (R-ME) today announced that the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services (DHHS) National Institute of Arthritis And Musculoskeletal and Skin Diseases has awarded a Kittery Point firm a $100,000 grant for Fibromyalgia research. 
   "This funding from the Department of Health & Human Services will help Collinge and Associates in their pursuit to develop innovative treatments for the symptoms of Fibromyalgia," said Snowe and Collins in a joint statement. "We are pleased to see continued federal support for Maine's ongoing effort to facilitate advanced medical research throughout our state."
   The funding is allocated as follows:
   * $100,000 to Collinge and Associates for their project entitled "Web-Based Program for Symptom Management in Fibromyalgia".
   The Department of Health & Human Services (DHHS) is the United States government's principal agency for protecting the health of all Americans and providing essential human services, especially for those who are least able to help themselves. The Department includes more than 300 programs, covering a wide spectrum of activities, and administers more grant dollars than all other federal agencies combined.
