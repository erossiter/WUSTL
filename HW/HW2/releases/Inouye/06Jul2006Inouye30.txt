HAWAII TO RECEIVE HOMELAND SECURITY FUNDS  SENATORS INOUYE AND AKAKA ANNOUNCE          HAWAII TO RECEIVE HOMELAND SECURITY FUNDS  SENATORS INOUYE AND AKAKA ANNOUNCE   Thursday  July 6  2006           For Immediate Release                                      WASHINGTON  U S  Senators Daniel K  Inouye and Daniel K  Akaka announced today that Hawaii will receive a grant of  189 000 from the Department of Homeland Security to protect key chemical infrastructure in the state    For security reasons  the infrastructure sites receiving federal funds cannot be disclosed  said Senator Inouye  a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Homeland Security  But Senator Akaka and I consider the Departments Chemical Sector Buffer Zone Protection Program  through which Hawaii is receiving this grant  to be an important part of our nations homeland security posture    Senator Akaka  a senior member of the Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs  added  This grant will help to secure Hawaiis high priority chemical sector infrastructure  and will strengthen the security and safety of the people of Hawaii    The State of Hawaii is responsible for developing the chemical sector protection measures to which the federal funds will be applied       30
