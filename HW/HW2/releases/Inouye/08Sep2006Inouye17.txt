SENATE APPROVES  470 4 MILLION FOR DEFENSE RELATED PROJECTS IN HAWAII DURING FISCAL YEAR 2007  INOUYE ANNOUNCES          SENATE APPROVES  470 4 MILLION FOR DEFENSE RELATED PROJECTS IN HAWAII DURING FISCAL YEAR 2007  INOUYE ANNOUNCES   Friday  September 8  2006           For Immediate Release           WASHINGTON  U S  Senator Daniel K  Inouye announced today that the Senate has approved a bill that provides approximately  470 4 million for defense related initiatives in Hawaii during Fiscal Year 2007  The funding is part of the  469 billion Department of Defense Appropriations Bill for Fiscal Year 2007 that the Senate passed Thursday night  September 7  2006   The vote was 98 0    The measure now moves to conference between the House and the Senate for completion  and Senator Inouye  the ranking Member of the Senate Defense Appropriations Subcommittee  will serve as one of the Senates lead conferees    Coupled with the Fiscal Year 2007 Military Construction Appropriations Bill  which is pending before the Senate and provides nearly  208 1 million for projects in Hawaii  the state could receive as much as  678 5 million in military related spending  excluding day to day operations and payroll  during Fiscal Year 2007    The defense appropriations measure underscores once more the strategic importance of Hawaii in our nations defense  Senator Inouye said  The Hawaii related initiatives will ensure that our United States will be ready for the challenges that may confront us  particularly in the Asia Pacific region  I am also pleased that the initiatives include numerous cutting edge high technology projects that will further ensure the protection of our uniformed men and women  and will also provide for enhanced medical and other support for our military personnel and their dependents    The Hawaii highlights are   Compatible Use Buffer Program  25 million    Specifically  the initiative supports cooperative agreements with states and local communities  and other interested stakeholders to acquire key conservation easements around military bases and ranges  These additional funds will help the proposal that Hawaii has already submitted for areas including Pohakuloa Training Area on the Big Island and on Oahu   Maui Space Surveillance System  MSSS   25 million  MSSS is a state of the art electro optical facility that combines operational satellite tracking facilities with research and development activities  It houses the U S  Department of Defenses largest telescope  the 3 67 meter Advanced Electro Optical System  and several other telescopes   U S  Army  Pacific Command  USARPAC  C4I  24 95 million   Funding shared by units throughout the Pacific   These funds would be used to support USARPACs command  control  communications  computer  and intelligence system  known as C4I  This system is crucial to USARPACs ability to manage information   Pacific Missile Range Facility  PMRF   Upgrades and Equipment  16 2 million PMRF  the worlds largest instrumented  multi environment range  is capable of supporting surface  subsurface  air  and space operations  It has an instrumented underwater range of 1 000 square miles  and controlled airspace of more than 42 000 square miles  making PMRF one of the worlds best facilities for supporting operations   Other Hawaii related projects in the defense appropriations
