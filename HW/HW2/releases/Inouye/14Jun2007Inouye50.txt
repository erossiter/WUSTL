INOUYE ANNOUNCES SENATE PANEL APPROVES  583 6 MILLION FOR MILITARY CONSTRUCTION PROJECTS IN HAWAII IN FY2008          INOUYE ANNOUNCES SENATE PANEL APPROVES  583 6 MILLION FOR MILITARY CONSTRUCTION PROJECTS IN HAWAII IN FY2008     Thursday  June 14  2007           For Immediate Release        WASHINGTON  The Senate Appropriations Committee today approved more than  583 6 million for 12 military construction projects in Hawaii  U S  Senator Daniel K  Inouye announced    The projects are part of the Fiscal Year 2008 Military Construction Bill that now advances to the full Senate for consideration    These initiatives will strengthen our national defense and improve the quality of life for our men and women in uniform  said Senator Inouye  a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  Furthermore  the funding for these projects underscores the fact that Hawaiis strategic location is key to our readiness for answering the challenges in the Asia Pacific region    A total of nearly  209 million is allocated for barracks and living quarters construction and improvements  Nearly  100 million will be appropriated to the Navy for the construction of a facility at Pearl Harbor that will make it difficult for U S  submarines to be detected by enemy sensor systems    Separate legislation for defense related projects in Hawaii is still under consideration by the Senate Defense Appropriations Subcommittee  which is chaired by Senator Inouye    The following are the Hawaii initiatives in the military construction funding measure    ARMY
