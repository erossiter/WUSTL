    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         March 30  2006     Press Release        Statement by Senator Pryor on ICANN  s Dismissal of Establishing an Adult Domain                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Senator Mark Pryor made the following statement today following a decision by the Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers  ICANN  to delay approval of a separate Internet domain for adult content  Press reports indicate that the U S  Department of Commerce is responsible for ICANN  s decision  ICANN  an international Nongovernmental Organization  is charged with selecting domain names  such as  com   org   net  edu  and  gov          Earlier this month  Senators Pryor and Max Baucus  D MT  introduced the Cyber Safety for Kids Act  to require the Secretary of Commerce to negotiate with ICANN to develop a special domain name for websites containing adult content          It is hard to believe that the Commerce Department has stalled international efforts to move pornographic material to an adult domain   This approach seems to be the most effective tool we can give parents to shield their children  s access to inappropriate content   I intend to question officials at the Commerce Department about their actions  and I remain committed to passing legislation that protects kids on the Internet   America should be leading the world in cleaning up the Internet  not dragging our feet and allowing the status quo to continue
