    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         September 21  2007     Press Release        Trip Advisory   September 23 24                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Pryor to Attend World War II Screening  Little Rock Central High School Visitor Center Dedication                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  WASHINGTON  D C   Senator Mark Pryor today said he is looking forward to several significant events on Sunday and Monday that pay tribute to Arkansas role in American history   On Sunday  September 23  Pryor will join 200 veterans for events surrounding AETNs premier of the Ken Burns documentary  The War  The screenings will occur at AETNs studio located at 350 S  Donaghey in Conway  Arkansas    3 30 4 pm  Arrival of Veterans and Guests  4 pm   Beyond their Words Live Show  A Look at AETNs WWII Oral History Project  which includes 300 hours of Arkansas war series on their web site and a visit to AETNs exhibit of artifacts and more than 4 000 photographs from World War II    5 pm  Premier of AETNs Their Journey  A Veterans Tribute Tour  which features 47 Arkansas veterans journey to the World War II memorial in Washington  D C  Senator Pryor spoke during the groups Tribute Tour    Although Pryor will not be in attendance  AETNs program will continue with the Countdown to The War Live Show at 6 30 pm and the Premier of The War at 7 pm     On Monday  September 24th at 10 am  Pryor will speak at the dedication of the Little Rock Central High Schools new Visitor Center  The Senator  a former student at Central High  secured  6 million to build the Center in time to commemorate the 50th Anniversary of the desegregation crisis
