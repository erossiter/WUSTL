    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         June 15  2006     Press Release        Pryor  s Solution to Protect Homeland Security and Farmers   Interests Passes Senate Committee                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Senator Says Fertilizer Component Must Be Kept Out of Criminal Hands   WASHINGTON D C     The Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee today passed an amendment sponsored by Senator Mark Pryor that boosts homeland security efforts by securing the trade and handling of ammonium nitrate while also enabling farmers to continue using the substance as fertilizer   Pryor  a member of the Committee  offered the provision as part of a larger Chemical Security Bill       Pryor said ammonium nitrate  a popular fertilizer used among Arkansas farmers  could also be used to create highly explosive bombs  which has prompted calls for better regulation and oversight of the chemical  He cited the recent arrests of 17 individuals in Canada who allegedly plotted to bomb Ontario landmarks using ammonium nitrate as a primary component   The substance was also used in the 1995 Oklahoma City bombing that killed 168 people       There is a valid concern that ammonium nitrate can create havoc in the wrong hands  and we need to step up efforts to secure this substance from those with criminal intent  Government can do this without taking away a product that provides our farmers with a number of economic  agronomic and environmental benefits     Pryor said    I  m pleased the Committee approved my provision  which I believe strikes the right balance  It requires the Department of Homeland Security to work with farmers  not against them         Pryor said his amendment  similar to legislation he sponsored with Senator Thad Cochran  R Miss  last year  would require producers and distributors of ammonium nitrate to register with the Department of Homeland Security  DHS  and maintain records of sale or distribution   It would also require purchasers of the substance to register with the agency  The provision also encourages DHS to enter cooperative agreements with state departments of agriculture to carry out registration and enforcement requirements  and requires adequate funding for these activities to be provided to the state
