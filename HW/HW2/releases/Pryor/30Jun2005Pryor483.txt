    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         June 30  2005     Press Release        Pryor Bill Gives Consumers Control Over Credit Records  Senator Introduces Legislation Allowing Individuals to Opt for   Security Freeze    on their Credit Reports to Prevent Identity Theft                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WASHINGTON  DC  Senator Mark Pryor today introduced the strongest legislation to date targeted at preventing identity theft  Pryor  s legislation gives Americans the right to control access to their credit files and prevent unsolicited companies and individuals from retrieving their personal financial information   Pryor said the Consumer Report Security Freeze Act would prevent identity thieves from opening up new credit accounts to accumulate debt in their victims   names   Under the legislation  individuals would be able to opt for a   security freeze    to shield their credit files and scores from being accessed by a third party  The freeze would remain in place until the consumer asked for their files to be   thawed    or unlocked   Consumers could authorize the release of all or sections of their credit information  to specific parties  or for a specific period of time  all of which the consumer could manage by telephone  mail and email           Identity theft is devastating to individuals  both financially and emotionally  and it  s occurring at exponential rates  This legislation gives consumers the tools they need to protect themselves by allowing them control over their own personal financial information     Pryor said     This bill stops the fraud where it often begins  And that  s good for consumers  businesses and commerce              Under Pryor  s legislation  an individual would be required to inform one credit agency of their desire for a   security freeze     and that agency would inform the remaining credit bureaus   Additionally  the legislation directs credit agencies to execute a security freeze within two business days after the request and inform consumers of all third parties that seek information from the individual  s credit files      My legislation ensures that no one will be able to open up a credit account using someone else  s information and it eliminates the need for consumers to keep a fearful eye over their credit files     Pryor said    With over 10 million people at risk from security breaches this year alone  it  s only right to place privacy rights first             All consumers deserve the right to freeze credit files so they can prevent thieves from using stolen identities to open new accounts   said Susanna Montezemolo  Policy Analyst with Consumers Union    At a time when identity theft has become increasingly common  Senator Pryor s bill offers consumers a powerful tool to keep crooks from damaging their financial futures      Pryor said that California already allows consumers to put a security freeze on their credit reporting file at any time  Legislators in Colorado  Connecticut  Louisiana  Maine and Nevada have passed similar security freeze laws that have not gone into effect yet        The Federal Trade Commission reports 27 3 million Americans have been victims of identity theft in the last five years  costing businesses and financial institutions  48 billion  In Arkansas  1 397 identity theft cases were filed in 2004  an 8 percent increase from the previous year  Consumers Union  a nonprofit publisher of Consumer Reports  estimates Americans have spent  5 billion and 600 hours to clear their credit reports after an identity theft incident
