    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         September 27  2007     Press Release        Delegation Announces Fire Grants                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     WASHINGTON  U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor  D AR  and Representative John Boozman  R AR  today announced  724 633 in grants for Third District rural fire departments and first responders   The grants come from the Department of Homeland Securitys Assistance to Firefighters Grants  AFG  program  which is to award grants directly to fire departments and nonaffiliated emergency medical service  EMS  organizations  These awards aim to enhance first responders ability to protect the health and safety of the public  as well as that of first responder personnel  with respect to fire related hazards   The largest grant was awarded to Greenwood Rural Fire Rescue  which received  541 854 for the purchase of communications devices compatible to the Arkansas Wireless Information Network  a statewide interoperable wireless communication system for emergency responders and Arkansas public service entities   The EMP rural Fire Association of Greenwood will also receive  140 790 to go toward the purchase of a tanker truck  The Prairie Grove Fire Department will receive  18 525 toward the purchase of a thermal imaging camera  Finally  the Johnson County Rural Fire District  1 will receive  23 464 to go toward fire safety education   As first responders  our local firefighters play an integral role in keeping our communities safe  Lincoln said  I applaud ourrural Arkansas fire departments for seeking outthese federal dollars for additional training and better equipment so they cancontinue to protectthe citizens of their communities   Our firefighters put their lives on the line to keep us safe  and it s important that we provide them with equipment and training they need to protect themselves and our communities  said Pryor  These grants will go a long way to help ensure that Arkansans lives and property are out of harm s way   The men and women of our rural and volunteer fire departments are on the front line of protecting the lives and property of many in the Third District  Boozman said  It is imperative that we keep them as well equipped as possible  and this money will go far toward that goal   The AFG program is an important component of the Administrations larger  coordinated effort to strengthen the Nations overall level of preparedness and ability to respond to fire and fire related hazards  Since 2004  with the Fiscal Year 2007 awards  AFG has provided a total of almost  2 2 billion to first responder organizations to purchase response equipment  personal protective equipment  and vehicles
