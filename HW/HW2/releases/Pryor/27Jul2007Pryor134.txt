    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         July 27  2007     Press Release        Lincoln  Pryor  Boozman Announce Grants for Barling  Charleston  and Hickory Creek Fire Departments                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  WASHINGTON  D C   Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor along with U S  Representative John Boozman  AR 03  announced today that the Barling Fire Department  the Charleston Volunteer Fire Department and the Hickory Creek Volunteer Fire Department have been awarded grants through the Department of Homeland Security s  DHS  Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program  These awards will help the departments enhance current operations and improve firefighter safety    The Barling Fire Department has been awarded  40 009 and plans to use the funding for 17 sets of turn out gear and an air compressor  The Charleston Volunteer Fire Department will receive  26 819 for new breathing apparatus  The Hickory Creek Volunteer Fire Department has been awarded  22 294 and will use the grant to purchase a portable air cascade system to refill breathing equipment at the fire scene    The purpose of the grant program is to enhance the response capabilities of fire departments and other first responders  The program also helps these organizations protect the health and safety of the public and emergency response personnel in fires and other hazards  The DHS Office of State and Local Government Coordination and Preparedness administers the grant program in cooperation with the U S  Fire Administration   As first responders  our local firefighters play a critical role in keeping our communities safe  Lincoln said  I applaud our Arkansas fire departments for seeking out available resources so that they can continue to provide the best possible service to the citizens of their communities   Homes and families can be destroyed as a result of uncontained fires  said Pryor  This grant provides northwest Arkansas fire departments with the equipment they need to stay safe and effectively respond to emergencies   The men and women of our fire departments represent the first line of defense in securing our lives and property  I am pleased that this funding will be used to improve their capabilities and protect their lives  Boozman said    In 2007  the Assistance to Firefighters Grant  AFG  will award approximately  490 million directly to fire departments and EMS organizations
