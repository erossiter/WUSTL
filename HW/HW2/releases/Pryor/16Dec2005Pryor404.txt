    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         December 16  2005     Press Release        U S  Congress Extends Pryor  s Tax Measure to Correct Combat Pay Inequity for Servicemen and Women    Measure Included in Gulf Coast Tax Relief Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 WASHINGTON D C     Senator Mark Pryor today applauded the Senate and House for extending his legislation to correct a discrepancy in the tax code that penalizes lower income servicemen and women serving in combat situations   The measure was included in the Gulf Opportunity Zone Act of 2005         In 2003  Pryor and Senator Max Baucus  D MT  along with Senate Finance Committee Chairman Chuck Grassley  R IA  requested a General Accounting Office  GAO  study into military compensation and its tax treatment   The findings revealed that some lower income earning military personnel receiving tax exclusions for serving in combat zones were being forced to forfeit other tax advantages  including the Earned Income Tax Credit  EITC  and the Additional Child Tax Credit       To fix this glitch  Pryor introduced the Tax Relief for Americans in Combat Act  The legislation  which passed in October 2004  allows soldiers to treat combat pay    which is not taxed  as income for the purposes of the Child Tax Credit and the Earned Income Tax Credit  EITC     The EITC element of the bill was set to expire this year  but today  s action by the Senate and House extends the credit for another year         Pryor said in the future he would push for a permanent extension of the legislation and thanked Senators John Kerry  D Mass  and Barack Obama  D Ill  for their lead role in securing an extension of the provision           Our soldiers serving in combat risk life and limb every hour of every day  they do not deserve to be penalized when it comes to tax relief     Pryor said  Extending this legislation sends a clear message to our brave service members that while they are away fighting for us we will be here in the Senate fighting for them and their families          Pryor said that the tax glitch affects as many as 10 000 lower income soldiers serving in combat zones who would be penalized by as much as  4 500 and  3 200 for enlisted members and officers  respectively         This is make or break money for our soldiers and their families     Allowing this tax benefit to expire would have meant shortchanging men and women serving heroically in combat who are not making much money  have families to provide for and have little or no savings or spousal income           The combat pay tax provision was included in a larger bill to provide tax incentives to residents of the Gulf Coast region to revitalize their storm ravaged economy       Pryor said Gulf Opportunity Zone Act of 2005 is the second installment in tax relief for the victims of the recent hurricanes that Congress has passed this year   The first package was approved earlier in the fall as emergency relief         Hundreds of thousands of our fellow Americans are still in need as a result of Hurricanes Katrina and Rita which is why we have to remain focused on solving the many challenges that the people of the Gulf Coast face  This legislation is a positive step forward in helping the Gulf Coast residents rebuild their communities and businesses     Pryor said
