    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         December 20  2007     Press Release        Pryor  Lincoln  Ross Applaud Passage of Bill to Name Pine Bluff Federal Building and Courthouse after Judge George Howard  Jr                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Legislation Heads to President s Desk                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              WASHINGTON D C   U S  Senators Mark Pryor  Blanche Lincoln  and Congressman Mike Ross  AR 04  today announced that legislation to designate the Pine Bluff federal building and courthouse after the late U S  District Court Judge George Howard  Jr  passed the Senate  thereby clearing the way for the President to sign the bill into law  The legislation passed the House on June 25  2007   Members of the Arkansas Congressional delegation said the designation is a small token of appreciation for the work of Judge Howard who served Arkansans as a remarkable lawyer and civil rights leader  The federal building and courthouse to be named after Howard is located at 100 East 8th Avenue    Judge Howard touched many lives in Pine Bluff and throughout Arkansas  Indeed  his unwavering dedication to his community and profession  and historic contributions to the State of Arkansas should never be forgotten  For these reasons  I have been proud to help shepherd this legislation  which honors the life and legacy of Judge Howard  through Congress  said Pryor    Judge Howard was a true pioneer becoming the first African American in Arkansas to hold a number of state judicial positions and to be appointed as a federal judge  said Lincoln  His many contributions to civil rights and the legal community have made a lasting impact on our state and our nation  I am proudthat the Senate passed this legislation that will honor Judge Howards legacy   I can think of no better way to recognize Judge George Howards legacy and his steadfast commitment to justice and equality than by officially renaming this federal building and courthouse in the city he loved and called home  Ross said  This is a great day for the city of Pine Bluff and for all of Arkansas  and I am honored to have worked with my colleagues in the Arkansas Congressional Delegation on this designation   As Arkansas  first black federal judge  Howard was named by President Carter to a lifetime appointment as U S  District Court Judge for Arkansas s Eastern and Western districts in 1980  Prior to this office  Howard worked as an attorney in private practice and served as President of the State Council of Branches of the National Association of Colored People  NAACP   Howard graduated from law school at the University of Arkansas at Fayetteville in 1954  He also served in the U S  Navy during World War II
