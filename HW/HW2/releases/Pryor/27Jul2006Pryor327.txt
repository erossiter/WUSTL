    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         July 27  2006     Press Release        Pryor  Lincoln  Boozman Announce John Paul Hammerschmidt Post Office One Step Closer to Reality                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      WASHINGTON D C     Senators Mark Pryor and Blanche Lincoln  and Congressman John Boozman  AR 03  today announced that the John Paul Hammerschmidt Post Office in Harrison is one step closer to becoming a reality       The Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs today passed legislation originally introduced by Congressman John Boozman to name a Harrison postal facility after former Congressman John Paul Hammerschmidt   Pryor  a member of the committee  helped overcome objections raised by other members that a new rule prevents post offices being named after living individuals   The legislation will now be considered by the full Senate  it passed the House of Representatives on May 2   The postal facility building is located at 215 West Industrial Park Road        John Paul Hammerschmidt has spent decades improving the lives of others  and I am proud to help designate this Post Office in his honor     said Pryor     I look forward to working with Senator Lincoln  and with my colleagues on both sides of the aisle  to gain the full Senate  s approval on this legislation         John Paul Hammerschmidt defines what it means to be a public servant   Lincoln said   We in Arkansas are grateful for his service to our state  and I am pleased that we can honor him in this way          I am grateful for the support of Senators Lincoln and Pryor as we work to honor Congressman Hammerschmidt   He has given so much to his country and to Arkansas   We want to show our appreciation for his service and to teach future generations about the important role he has played in our state  s history     Boozman said        For 26 years  Congressman Hammerschmidt represented the Third District of Arkansas in the U S  House of Representatives   Elected in 1966  he became the first member of his party to represent Arkansas in Congress since Reconstruction  By the time he retired  he was the Ranking Member on the House Transportation and Infrastructure Committee and is known throughout the country for his work securing roads and infrastructure improvements       John Paul Hammerschmidt also served during World War II as a combat pilot with the U S  Army Air Corps Third Combat Cargo Group in the China Burma India Theater  He continued to serve as a reservist in the Air Force from 1945 1960 and in the District of Columbia Army Reserve from 1977 1981
