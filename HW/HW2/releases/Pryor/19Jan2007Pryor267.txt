    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         January 19  2007     Press Release        Senate Passes Meaningful Ethics Reform                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Includes Amendment to Stop Pension Payments to Convicted Congressmen                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               WASHINGTON  D C     Senator Mark Pryor today announced he was pleased that the Senate was able to break through a difficult stalemate and pass meaningful lobbying and ethics legislation   The bipartisan package was assigned the number S 1 to signify the high priority it carries in the 110th Congress and the new Democratic majority         The Legislative Transparency and Accountability Act will ban gifts and travel paid for by lobbyists and organizations that employ lobbyists  extend the ban on lobbying by former Members of Congress to two years  ban the spouse of a Member from lobbying other Members  require lobbyists to provide more information on their activities and provide transparency to the earmark process   It will also require Senators to pay the charter rate for private jet travel instead of the cost of a first class ticket   In addition  it will establish a website to allow the public access to records of reported Congressional official travel          This reform is long overdue     Pryor said    I am proud to be a part of cleaning up Washington by adding new layers of transparency between Congress  lobbyists and the American people   It is my hope that this legislation will restore the faith of the American people in Congress          An additional amendment introduced by Senator John Kerry and cosponsored by Senators Mark Pryor  Ben Nelson and Ken Salazar will deny Federal retirement benefits to former Members of Congress convicted of bribery and conspiracy to commit fraud   This amendment passed the Senate January 12th by an 87 0 Roll Call vote         This is a very important provision that makes good common sense     said Pryor     It has never made sense for the American people to continue to pay with their tax dollars the pensions of convicted former Members of Congress            The House adopted its own broad ethics package this month and now will meet with the Senate in Conference
