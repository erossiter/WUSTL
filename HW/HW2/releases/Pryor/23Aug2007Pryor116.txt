    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         August 23  2007     Press Release        Delegation Announces Justice Grants                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Money to increase community safety  protect children from predators                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                WASHINGTON  U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor  D AR   and Representative John Boozman  R AR  today announced over  225 000 in justice grants for Third District cities and counties   The grants are funded by the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG   which allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime based on their own local needs and conditions   The City of Russellville will receive  23 892 which will be used by the city and Pope County for a Citizens Police Academy created by the Russellville Police Department   Sebastian County will receive  118 339 to upgrade the Sheriffs Office Mobile Data System  The upgrade will include the purchase of the digital system and all the equipment necessary to complete installation   Fayetteville  Springdale and the Washington County Sheriffs Office will split  83 183 in grant money  The Sheriffs Office will get  17 163 to purchase tactical equipment  while the Fayetteville Police Department will get  39 739 for the purchase of mobile radar units  tasers  personnel carriers  and a technology license to expand its Internet Crimes Against Children Program  The Springdale Police Department will get  26 281 to install cameras in patrol cars  purchase digital equipment  weapons and tasers  and upgrade its Kidprint Program to enhance its ability to locate missing children   Our local police and sheriff officials must have the proper resources  equipment  and technology necessary to ensure safety in our communities  I am proud of the work of these law enforcement agencies in Arkansas in seeking this assistance so that they can continue to provide for the security of the citizens they serve  Lincoln said    The Byrne grant program continues to be an invaluable resource for law enforcement  Pryor said  Here  we see first hand how communities in northwest Arkansas will be safer as a direct result of these federal dollars  Thats why I have fought the Bush Administration from slashing this program  and I will continue to do so in the future   The men and women of the 3rd District rely on law enforcement of service and protection  and this money will help each department increase their efforts on behalf of Arkansans  Boozman said  This money will make communities safer  provide police with tools to arrest criminals  and protect missing and exploited children  These are important duties  and I am proud to see these departments receive this needed funding   The Grant funds can be used for state and local initiatives  technical assistance  training  personnel  equipment  supplies  contractual support  and information systems for criminal justice for any one or more of the following purpose areas  law enforcement programs  prosecution and court programs  prevention and education programs  corrections and community corrections programs  drug treatment programs  and planning  evaluation  and technology improvement programs
