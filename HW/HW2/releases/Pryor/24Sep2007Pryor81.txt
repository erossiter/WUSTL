    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         September 24  2007     Press Release        Delegation Announces HHS Grants                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Money to help shelter runaways  provide life skills   WASHINGTON  U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln  D  and Mark Pryor  D   and U S  Representative John Boozman  R  today announced  370 000 in grants designed to assist the youth of the Third District  The grants  from the Department of Health and Human Services  were awarded to Fayetteville based Youth Bridge  Inc  The first grant  worth  170 000  will go toward establishing a Basic Center Program for runaways and homeless youth  This emergency shelter will assist youths until a guardian can be found   The second grant  for  200 000  is to establish a new transitional living program for females at Youth Bridge  The program  aimed at 17 and 18 year olds  will focus on developing live skills to prepare them for the real world   It is important that our nation makes a commitment to identify at risk youth and provide a safe place to nurture and support them  Lincoln said  I applaud Youth Bridge  Inc  for the work they are doing to assist teens and families across Northwest Arkansas  These dollars are a critical investment in our most precious resource  our children  and will provide them with the guidance and training to succeed in todays world   The services provided Youth Bridge have been so important in helping teens choose the right path  Vulnerable youths need safe  supportive places in order to turn their lives around and acquire constructive skills  This federal funding will benefit the participants as well as the entire Northwest Arkansas community   The work performed at Youth Bridge is important  and vital  for the boys and girls they support  Boozman said  This money will benefit at risk youth with a place of safety and guidance  I am proud of the leadership Youth Bridge is providing for the benefit of the Third District
