    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         July 12  2006     Press Release        Pryor Seeks Adequate Funding for Pine Bluff Arsenal Training Program for First Responders                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Introduces Amendment to Homeland Security Spending Bill   WASHINGTON D C     Senator Mark Pryor today said he is seeking additional funds for a program based in Pine Bluff  AR that has been critical in providing over 70 000 emergency responders nationally with on site equipment training and technical assistance for chemical  biological  radiological  nuclear and explosive detection        I believe equipment and training should go hand in hand     Pryor said     That  s why I  m working to properly fund Pine Bluff  s premiere and highly popular on site training program  I am proud this successful program is based out of Arkansas  but even more impressed with the positive impact it has on first responders from around the nation and the communities they protect        Pryor said he is concerned about the shrinking budget for Pine Bluff  s Domestic Preparedness Equipment Technical Assistance Program  DPETAP   and said his amendment to the Fiscal Year 2007 Homeland Security Appropriations Bill  if passed  would make this program a higher priority within the Department of Homeland Security  s  DHS  training budget  Specifically  his provision will provide an additional  13 5 million to the Department of Homeland Security  s training budget  with the requirement that a total of  25 million be reserved for technical assistance programs  such as Pine Bluff  s popular program      The Senator added that DPETAP  since 1999  has trained emergency responders in 45 states how to choose  operate  and maintain their chemical  biological radiological  nuclear and explosive detection and response equipment  Based on demand  DPETAP grew from one mobile assistance team and 5 employees in 1999 to 20 teams and 116 employees in 2005  However  due to budget restrictions for fiscal year 2006  49 personnel and 14 teams were eliminated       Last year  s budget cuts undermined the potential of this program  eliminating critical personnel and canceling training programs around the country     Pryor said    Instead  when we have such a successful homeland security program  we should be boosting its capabilities  I  m working to do just that
