    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         December 21  2006     Press Release        Arkansas Congressional Delegation Announces  1 3 Million for Emergency Food   Shelter Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Washington    U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln and Mark Pryor and U S  Representatives Marion Berry  AR 01   Vic Snyder  AR 02   John Boozman  AR 03   and Mike Ross  AR 04  announced today that  1 355 196 has been awarded to the State of Arkansas through the Emergency Food and Shelter  EFS  Program  EFS is administered by the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA       The Arkansas lawmakers said the federal funding will support social service agencies in 54 counties across Arkansas to help prevent homelessness and to help feed and shelter those in need  In each of these counties  grants will be awarded to organizations such as shelters  soup kitchens  and food banks by the EFS Program  s local representatives  The funds will be used to supplement food  shelter  rent  mortgage  and utility assistance programs for individuals and families with non disaster related emergencies      In addition to funding awarded directly to the 54 Arkansas counties   92 785 has been set aside for state grants to aid those in need      The EFS Program  entering its twenty fourth year  is administered by a National Board of voluntary agencies chaired by FEMA  The National Board qualifies local jurisdictions for annual EFS awards based on criteria involving current population  unemployment  and poverty levels  Member agencies of the National Board include the American Red Cross  Catholic Charities  USA  National Council of the Churches of Christ in the U S A   The Salvation Army  United Jewish Communities  and United Way of America      The EFS Program was authorized under the McKinney Vento Homeless Assistance Act of 1987 and has distributed more than  2 952 billion to communities nationwide since its inception
