    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         September 13  2005     Press Release        Pryor Calls for Urgent Investigation into Gas Price Gouging   Study to Explain Oil Company Profits  Economic Impact of Gas Prices                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   WASHINGTON D C     Senator Mark Pryor today introduced legislation directing the Federal Trade Commission to begin an immediate investigation into nationwide gasoline prices in the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina   He offered the language as an amendment to the pending appropriations bill for the Commerce  Justice and State Departments for Fiscal Year 2006         It is hard for the average consumer to fill up their car and pay record highs for gas  only to open their newspaper and read about oil companies making record profits     Pryor said      We must find out  not speculate  not accuse  not assume    but find out whether or not gas price gouging is occurring through the supply chain or distribution markets  And if price gouging is occurring  then we must punish those who would take advantage of a national tragedy           Pryor said his measure provides the necessary resources for the Federal Trade Commission to conduct a thorough and immediate investigation to determine if gas price gouging is occurring throughout the supply chain and distribution markets   Additionally  Pryor said the investigation will include            A comparison and reasons for changes in profit levels over the past year for companies involved in the production  distribution  or sale of petroleum products             A summary of tax expenditures for companies involved in the production  distribution  or sale of petroleum products            The effects of increased gasoline prices and price gouging on economic activity in the United States            The overall cost of increased gas prices and price gouging to the economy  including the impact on consumers   purchasing power in both state and federal disaster areas  and other areas affected by disaster  and           Recommendations to Congress on legislative action to protect consumers from price gouging        Pryor said the FTC will provide an initial report to Congress within 30 days  updates every 30 days afterwards  and a final report no later than 90 days after
