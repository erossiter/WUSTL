    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         May 2  2006     Press Release        Pryor Calls for FEMA to be Independent Again                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         WASHINGTON D C     Senator Mark Pryor today said he believes FEMA can be a more effective disaster response agency if it is restored to an independent agency as opposed to a sub agency within the Department of Homeland Security     Pryor  a member of the Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee  said he disagrees with the committee Chairman  s recommendation that FEMA should be renamed and reshuffled within the Department of Homeland Security   Instead  Pryor said FEMA needs to return to an independent  cabinet level status where the primary focus is on preparedness  response and recovery   This authority  he added  would ensure the director has a direct line of communication with the President during times of crisis and during the normal course of business and make certain budgetary and personnel priorities are continually met       Pryor  s decision follows a seven month investigation by the Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee on FEMA  s missteps before and after Hurricane Katrina  and the release of the Committee  s final report and recommendations         We know FEMA can prepare and respond to catastrophes because we saw it function the way it should under the leadership of James Lee Witt  FEMA was once like the Calvary coming in to save the day  Today  it  s broken     Pryor said    It  s time to fix FEMA by giving it an independent voice and the right level of authority and funding  That means taking steps beyond a new name and desk arrangement          Pryor said while he disagrees with the most significant recommendation in the report  he does strongly support the recommendations that FEMA have qualified leadership  adequate communication equipment and better coordination among local  regional and federal operations
