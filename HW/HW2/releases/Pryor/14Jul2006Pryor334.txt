    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         July 14  2006     Press Release        Senate Approves Pryor Provision to Increase Funding for Emergency Responder  s Training Program in Pine Bluff                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        WASHINGTON D C     The Senate passed Senator Mark Pryor  s amendment to allocate additional funds for a program based in Pine Bluff  AR that provides our nation  s emergency responders with on site equipment training  His modified amendment    to provide an additional  6 5 million to the technical assistance fund that supports the Pine Bluff program    was accepted as part of the Senate  s Fiscal Year 2007 Homeland Security Appropriations Bill on Thursday evening     Pryor said the Domestic Preparedness Equipment Technical Assistance Program  DPETAP   staffed by employees at the Pine Bluff Arsenal  has trained over 75 000 first responders how to select  operate  and maintain their chemical  biological  radiological  nuclear and explosive detection and response equipment  He added DPETAP is one of the largest Department of Homeland Security  DHS  Technical Assistance programs  funded entirely by the federal government so local jurisdictions do not have to incur the cost of training  Pryor  s original amendment sought to increase DHS   technical assistance fund from  11 5 million to  25 million  but was modified to increase the fund by  6 5 million to a total of  18 million to accommodate budget concerns         DPETAP Mobile teams have been critical in training our nation  s first responders  and demand for their expertise continues to climb     Pryor said     I  m pleased my efforts to raise awareness about this program and increase the investment in it paid off   This additional funding is a win win for Pine Bluff  first responders across the nation and the communities they protect         The Senator added that DPETAP  since 1999  has trained emergency responders in 43 states  Based on demand  DPETAP grew from one mobile assistance team and 5 employees in 1999 to 20 teams and 116 employees in 2005  However  due to budget restrictions for fiscal year 2006  49 personnel and 14 teams were eliminated  causing DPETAP to cancel 200 training trips
