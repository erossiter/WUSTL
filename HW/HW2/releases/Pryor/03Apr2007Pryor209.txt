    Senator Mark Pryor Press Releases                                                         April 3  2007     Press Release        Delegation Announces  2 9M in Grants                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Grants to benefit Arkansas Head Start Programs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Blanche Lincoln  D AR  and Mark Pryor  D AR   and U S  Representative John Boozman  R AR  today announced over  2 9 million in discretionary grants to two Third District Head Start programs       Rogers based Northwest Arkansas Head Start will receive  1 574 659 from the Department of Health and Human Services under the Full Year Center Based grant project  Also  Ozark Opportunities  Inc   based in Harrison  will receive  1 326 476 from the Full Year Center Base   Training Technical Assistance grant program        The stimulation and care children receive in Head Start programs is essential to preparing them for future success in the classroom   said Lincoln   These federal dollars will benefit programs that are devoted to educating Arkansas  youth in order to ensure a bright future for our children  our state  and our country         Head Start programs are proven to help students stay on track throughout school   Pryor said   I m pleased these grants will allow children in Northwest Arkansas acquire critical developmental skills needed to enter kindergarten and learn         Head Start plays a pivotal role in the development of our young children in Arkansas   Boozman said   Being a former school board member  I understand the importance and impact that funding has on the classroom and the services offered our children  These million dollar grants will greatly benefit the efforts of these two programs in the early education of children in the 3rd District
