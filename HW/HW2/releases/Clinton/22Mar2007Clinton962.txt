March 22  2007 Senator Clinton Introduces Legislation to Expand Teach for America                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Washington  DC   Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton introduced legislation to increase the number of highly accomplished recent college graduates teaching in underserved communities across America  The Teach for America Act will help Teach For America grow to over 7 500 corps members in 32 communities  teaching over 600 000 low income students every day           As the teaching population ages  more and more schools are facing significant shortages of qualified teachers  Our nation s inner cities will be even harder hit as existing teachers move to suburban schools or leave the teaching profession altogether  Teach For America s corps members and alumni become lifelong leaders in the effort to ensure that all children in our nation have an equal chance to succeed in life   said Senator Clinton          The Teach For America Act will award funding to Teach For America to expand its program of recruiting  selecting  training  and supporting new teachers  Additionally  grants will be used to serve a substantial percentage of students who are eligible for free or reduced priced meals            Teach For America is the national corps of outstanding recent college graduates of all academic majors who commit to teach for two years in public schools   Since its inception in 1990  more than 12 000 individuals have joined Teach For America  directly impacting the lives of over 2 million students in under resourced schools across the country          Senator Clinton has been a tireless advocate of public service   Senator Clinton has led the fight to increase funding for Teach For America  helping to secure an increase in Teach For America appropriations last Congress   She has introduced legislation to establish a U S  Public Service Academy to create a pipeline of future leaders  She has also fought to preserve funding for AmeriCorps  founded during the Clinton Administration  and was a leader in the effort to rename the AmeriCorps education award the  Segal AmeriCorps Education Award   in honor of AmeriCorps founder  Eli Segal  who served as the first director of the Corporation for National Service          The legislation is cosponsored by Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid  D NV  and Senators Lamar Alexander  R TN   Barbara Mikulski  D MD   Robert Menendez  D NJ   Christopher J  Dodd  D CT   and Richard Durbin  D IL
