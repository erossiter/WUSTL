July 20  2006 Senator Clinton Secures Key Senate Panel Approval of  500 000 for the Hospital for Special Surgery                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Funding included in the Fiscal Year 2007 Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton welcomed approval by the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee of the Fiscal Year 2007 Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations Bill  which includes  500 000 that the Senator secured  for the Hospital for Special Surgery  s clinical facilities          The bill will proceed to the Senate floor for consideration following Full Appropriations Committee approval this afternoon   Following consideration and passage on the Senate floor  the bill will head into a Conference Committee where the House and Senate will resolve the differences between their two bills  Following conference  the bill will again be sent to the House and Senate for final approval  after which it will be sent to the President for his signature            With more and more people seeking care at New York City  s hospitals  we must do everything we can to support them  This funding will be a real boost to the Hospital for Special Surgery  s expansion efforts and will help them continue to provide the highest quality care and treatment to those who are in need     Senator Clinton said          The Hospital for Special Surgery  HSS   located in New York City  was founded in 1863 as the first orthopedic hospital in the United States   Since its establishment  HSS has been the hospital of choice for countless individuals of all ages  from infants to older adults  suffering from musculoskeletal conditions   The funds approved today will be used to modernize and expand its clinical facilities in order to provide the highest level of care to the growing number of patients seeking its expertise   Since the Hospital  s patient care facilities were expanded 10 years ago  both surgical volume and outpatient visits have increased by more than 60    In 2004  the Hospital performed over 16 000 inpatient and ambulatory surgeries and had 228 000 outpatient visits  The Hospital  s new facilities will be completed by 2009 and encompass 201 000 square feet of new construction and 75 000 square feet of renovated existing space   On site patient services will be significantly expanded and redesigned for greater efficiency and comfort
