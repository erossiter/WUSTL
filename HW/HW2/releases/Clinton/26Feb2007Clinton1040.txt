February 26  2007 Senator Clinton Announces Legislation to Require Investigation of Disability Claims for Wounded Soldiers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Washington  DC   Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that she will introduce legislation to address reports of widespread problems wounded soldiers are facing with disability claims   Senator Clinton s bill will require the Department of Defense to commission an outside  independent review of each individual disability claim filed as a result of service in Afghanistan and Iraq to evaluate whether the claims were evaluated properly and servicemembers received the level of disability compensation that they deserve  Her bill would also require the Government Accountability Office  GAO  to conduct a simultaneous review of the disability claims process             It is unconscionable that our brave men and women in uniform should have to suffer the indignity of having their disability claims improperly questioned or denied   We owe it to them to make sure they get the care and support they need   said Senator Clinton    Because of the reported problems in the consideration of disability claims by servicemembers  an independent  outside review of these claims is necessary to ensure that our soldiers are getting the assistance they deserve             A member of the Senate Armed Services Committee  Senator Clinton has made the plight of wounded soldiers one of her top priorities   Senator Clinton introduced legislation signed into law as part of the FY 2007 Department of Defense  DOD  Authorization bill to require the Department of the Army to conduct an audit of continuing problems for wounded soldiers withdrawn from combat on the basis of medical issues and experiencing payroll problems   Alarmingly  the audit found that 24 percent of wounded soldiers from May 1  2005 to April 30  2006 had underpayment issues totaling almost  400 000  meaning they were not paid what they were owed on time  1216 wounded soldiers were audited over the yearlong period and 295 had underpayment problems  Of the total underpayments experienced  more than 20 soldiers were underpaid more than  3 000  a significant sum
