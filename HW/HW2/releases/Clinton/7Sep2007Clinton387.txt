September 7  2007 Senator Clinton Applauds Unanimous Senate Approval of Resolution to Increase Awareness of Ovarian Cancer                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Washington  DC  Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today applauded unanimous Senate approval of a resolution that increases the awareness of ovarian cancer  a leading cause of cancer deaths among women in the United States   The resolution  co sponsored by Senator Clinton  recognizes and supports the goals and ideals of National Ovarian Cancer Awareness Month      The resolution unanimously passed today draws much needed attention to a disease that is the fifth leading cause of cancer deaths for women in the United States  said Senator Clinton   It is critically important to educate the public about the factors that are known to reduce the risk for ovarian cancer and that play an important role in preventing the disease  National Ovarian Cancer Month will help increase awareness and impress upon the public that early recognition of ovarian cancer symptoms is currently the best way to save womens lives    The resolution passed today outlines the difficulty in detecting ovarian cancer and the necessity of increasing public awareness of the disease   If ovarian cancer is diagnosed and treated at an early stage before the cancer spreads outside of the ovary  the treatment is less costly  and the survival rate is as high as 90 percent   However  due to a lack of a reliable early screening test  75 percent of cases of ovarian cancer are detected at an advanced stage  when a 5 year survival rate is only 50 percent      Senator Clinton has long been a champion of increasing awareness of cancer and promoting womens health   Senator Clinton is an original co sponsor of the Breast Cancer and Environmental Research Act that would create NIH funded research centers to explore the links between environmental pollutants and breast cancer  A resolution she introduced along with Senator Chuck Hagel  R NE  earlier this year  urging the President to declare lung cancer a national public health priority  increasing funding for lung cancer research  and appointing an advisory committee to coordinate efforts to cut lung cancer mortality rates in half by 2015  also unanimously passed the Senate last month   Senator Clinton also joined with Senator Gordon Smith  R OR  to introduce a resolution in support of pancreatic cancer month   Senator Clinton was joined by Senators Debbie Stabenow  D MI  and Elizabeth Dole  R NC  in co sponsoring the resolution passed today
