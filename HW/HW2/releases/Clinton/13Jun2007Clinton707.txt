June 13  2007 Clinton Measures to Help Wounded Servicemembers Included in Armed Services Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Washington  DC  Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that legislation the Senate Armed Services Committee will consider this week includes key provisions of bills she has introduced to address problems facing our wounded servicemembers  The Dignified Treatment of Wounded Warriors Act was introduced today by Senator Carl Levin  Chairman of the Senate Armed Services Committee  Senator Clinton is an original cosponsor of the bill  We have a responsibility as a nation to make sure that the men and women who wear our uniform receive the care and support they need  These are urgently needed steps to ensure we uphold that solemn commitment I amproud that these measureshave been included in the Senate Armed Services Committee sbill to address the problems our soldiers have faced at Walter Reed and elsewhere ingetting the healthcare and benefits they need  said Senator Clinton   We applaud Senator Clinton for her leadership on these issues and her efforts to ensure that our nation s wounded warriors are treated in a manner befitting their sacrifices  said Jeremy Chwat Wounded Warrior Project Executive VP for Policy and Public Affairs  We are gratified to see many ofthe provisions that we have worked ontogether on included in this new legislation  The Dignified Treatment of Wounded Warriors Act includes two key proposals from Senator Clintons Heroes at Home Act of 2007  The bill also includes critical provisions from Senator Clintons Bridging the Gap for Wounded Warriors Act  In addition  the bill includes a proposal from the TBI Access to Options Act  introduced by Senator Clinton along with Senator Evan Bayh  D IN   The bill also expands on a measure Senator Clinton introduced as part of her Heroes at Home Act of 2006  The bill includes the following provisions from Senator Clintons Restoring Disability Benefits for Injured and Wounded Warriors Act  As New York s first Senator to serve on the Senate Armed Services Committee  Senator Clinton has made it one of her top priorities to ensure that our brave men and women in uniform have the healthcare and support they need  She has introduced legislation to improve the detection  assessment and treatment of traumatic brain injury and expand support systems for members and former members of the Armed Services with traumatic brain injury and their families  Senator Clinton has also introduced legislation to help ensure wounded soldiers receive the disability benefits they need and deserve and to further protect military family financial benefits  She has pressed for an independent investigation of the conditions at Walter Reed and called for a new GI Bill of Rights to once again honor the service and invest in the future of our men and women in uniform  She authored legislation signed into law last year that required an audit of widespread pay issues wounded soldiers are facing and was recently released by the Army showing continuing problems  Senator Clinton has fought to ensure servicemembers have the body armor they need  She has also secured in law access to TRICARE military health benefits for all drilling members of the National Guard and
