November 29  2007 Senator Clinton Welcomes FEMA Extension of Temporary Housing Program For Victims of June 2006 Floods                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Extension Will Allow Six Additional Months to Help Flood Victims Put Their Lives Back in Order                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Washington  DC  Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today welcomed the news that the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  will be granting a request by New York State for an extension of the temporary housing program for victims of the severe flooding in June 2006  The extension will allow seventeen families from Upstate New York to remain in FEMA travel trailers located on their property for an additional six months past the programs scheduled expiration date in early January 2008     I have worked hard with FEMA  SEMO  and local community officials since day one to make sure that the victims of last Junes floods had the full resources needed to put their lives back in order  said Senator Clinton  While much has been accomplished  there are still families who bore the brunt of the damage  and are still struggling with the burdens of restoring what was lost  FEMAs extension is welcome news  and a needed step in their continuing efforts to assist these hardworking families in the recovery process    Following the severe storms and flooding that hit Upstate New York in June 2006  Senator Clinton immediately called on the FEMA Administrator Paulison and President Bush to provide aid and assistance to the flooded areas throughout the State  This natural disaster also prompted Senator Clinton to call on her Senate colleagues  FEMA and other officials to consider whether to amend existing law so that small businesses impacted by such disasters might be eligible in the future for direct assistance    Twelve counties were given disaster declaration status following the June 2006 storms and ensuing floods  including  Broome  Chenango  Delaware  Herkimer  Montgomery  Oneida  Orange  Otsego  Schoharie  Sullivan  Tioga and Ulster
