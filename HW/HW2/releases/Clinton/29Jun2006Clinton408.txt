June 29  2006 Senator Clinton Welcomes Key Senate Panel Approval of  400 000 to Develop Heart Failure Treatment Device for Community at Rochester General Hospital                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Lifesaving Technology being developed with doctors at Rochester General Hospital      Funds will help Rochester General  MYOTECH and Greater Rochester Enterprise to establish manufacturing center for devices in Monroe County Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today welcomed approval by the Senate Appropriations Committee of the Fiscal Year 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill  which includes  400 000 to help Rochester General  MYOTECH and Greater Rochester Enterprise establish a manufacturing center for portable heart failure treatment devices that can be used in rural and regional hospitals            These funds will play an important role in enhancing the cardiac treatment services that will be available in the Rochester region and beyond  I look forward to continuing to work with Rochester General  MYOTECH and the Greater Rochester Enterprise and watching this initiative expand in the coming years     Senator Clinton said          The Energy and Water Appropriations bill  which was approved by the Committee today  now proceeds to the Senate floor   Following consideration and passage on the Senate floor  the bill will head into a Conference Committee where the House and Senate will resolve the differences between the two bills          With the aid of this funding  Rochester General Hospital and MYOTECH will develop a program to bring a new generation of minimally invasive cardiac assist devices to patients both in urban and rural communities  Existing cardiac assist devices are limited to patients of a few specialized health providers   The new generation technology can easily and safely be used in virtually every hospital including small rural health care providers   The new low cost cardiac assist device  called the MYO VAD  can be installed by a general surgeon in three minutes or less  Physicians in community and rural hospitals can also use the MYO VAD to restore and stabilize the cardiac output of patients for transport to urban cardiac centers for any sophisticated surgical therapy that may be required but which is unavailable in the community or rural hospital          MYOTECH and Greater Rochester Enterprise  GRE  plan to establish manufacturing for the MYO VAD devices in Monroe County  NY   Rochester General will develop additional clinical research and medical support facilities in Monroe County as a result   They will train physicians and hospitals in small and rural communities in New York to install these devices  and provide clinical   back up    and support to those physicians and hospitals who use them
