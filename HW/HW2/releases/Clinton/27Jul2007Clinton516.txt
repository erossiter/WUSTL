July 27  2007 Schumer  Clinton  Funds to Preserve Montauk Lighthouse  Long Island Sound Oyster Beds and Peconic River Dams Included in Final Water Resources Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Funding for Key Projects Included in Water Resources Development Act Approved by House Senate Conferees Today                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Washington  DC  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that a House Senate Conference Committee has agreed on a final version of the Water Resources Development Act  WRDA  which includes  14 million under the for a shoreline protection project at Montauk Point to protect the historic Montauk Point Lighthouse and  25 million for oyster bed restoration in Long Island Sound     The Water Resources Development Act  which was approved by the House Senate Conferees today  authorizes Corps of Engineers civil works projects  including navigation  ecosystem restoration and shoreline protection projects  Under the authorizations  the Corps partners with local sponsors to conduct the project  A project authorization in WRDA is a necessary first step towards securing funding in an appropriations bill at a later date  The bill now goes to the full House and Senate for final approval before heading to the President for his signature     For more than 200 years the Montauk Lighthouse has been a beacon that welcomes the thousands of visitors that flock annually to the beaches of Montauk and the thousands of residents that call The End home  I m thrilled the committee authorized funding to help the Corps properly maintain this cherished landmark  said Senator Schumer  I m also pleased the bill includes funding to help restore Long Island Sound s remarkable oyster beds  This funding will be a huge boon to the economic vitality of the coast and the fishers who rely on it for their livelihood    The Montauk Lighthouse is a historic landmark and beautiful symbol of Long Island s rich history  as well as a testament to the stunning aesthetic beauty of the Island s natural environment  This essential funding will help the Corps to keep it maintained and preserved for generations to come  Senator Clinton said  I am also pleased that this bill includes vital funds for the restoration of the Sound s valuable oyster beds     The Senators announced that the bill includes a  14 million authorization  with a  7 million federal share  for a shoreline protection project to the Montauk Point Lighthouse  The Lighthouse  which is on the National Register of Historic Places and under consideration as a National Landmark  was commissioned for construction by General George Washington  construction was completed in the year 1797  Without the Corps of Engineers  participation in a shore protection project  the lighthouse is likely to be destroyed  Once WRDA is approved by Congress and signed by the President  the project could go forward     The bill also authorizes  25 million for the Corps of Engineers to help construct and restore oyster beds in Long Island Sound  The federal share of this project would be  18 million    The bill further included  12 million for the Army Corps to work with local authorities on dam safety measures for 15 dams across the nation  including Peconic River Dams in the Town of Riverhead
