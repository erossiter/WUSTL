April 4  2007 Schumer  Clinton  Senate Committee Authorizes Funds to Preserve Montauk Lighthouse and Long Island Sound Oyster Beds                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Funding for Key Projects Included in Water Resources Development Act Approved by Senate Committee                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Washington  DC    Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton announced that the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee authorized  14 million under the Water Resources Development Act  WRDA  for a shoreline protection project at Montauk Point to protect the historic Montauk Point Lighthouse and  25 million for oyster bed restoration in Long Island Sound           The Water Resources Development Act  which was approved by the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee late last week  authorizes Corps of Engineers civil works projects  including navigation  ecosystem restoration and shoreline protection projects  Under the authorizations  the Corps partners with local sponsors to conduct the project  The bill now goes to the full Senate for approval            For more than 200 years the Montauk Lighthouse has radiantly welcomed millions of visitors traveling from thousands of miles away to the shores of Long Island  said Senator Schumer   Today  I m thrilled the EPW Committee authorized funding to help the Corps properly maintain this cherished landmark  In addition  I m also relieved the bill includes funding to help restore Long Island Sound s remarkable oyster beds            The Montauk Lighthouse is a historic landmark and beautiful symbol of Long Island s rich history  as well as a testament to the stunning aesthetic beauty of the Island s natural environment  This essential funding will help the Corps to keep it maintained and preserved for generations to come   Senator Clinton said   I am also pleased that this bill includes vital funds for the restoration of the Sound s valuable oyster beds            The Senators announced that the bill passed by the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee includes a  14 million authorization  with a  7 million federal share  for a shoreline protection project to the Montauk Point Lighthouse  The Lighthouse  which is on the National Register of Historic Places and under consideration as a National Landmark  was commissioned for construction by General George Washington  construction was completed in the year 1797  Without the Corps of Engineers  participation in a shore protection project  the lighthouse is likely to be destroyed  Once WRDA is approved by Congress and signed by the President  the project could go forward  The bill also authorizes  25 million for the Corps of Engineers to help construct and restore oyster beds in Long Island Sound   The federal share of this project would be  18 million
