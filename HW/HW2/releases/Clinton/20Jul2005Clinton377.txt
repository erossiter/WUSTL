July 20  2005 Clinton Bill to Protect Long Island Sound Approved by Key Senate Panel                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton announced that the Long Island Sound Stewardship Act of 2005  LISSA  was approved unanimously by the Senate Environment and Public Works committee today   Senator Clinton cosponsored the bill with Senators Joe Lieberman  CT   Charles Schumer  NY   and Christopher Dodd  CT              More than a decade ago  the Environmental Protection Agency identified the Long Island Sound  LIS  region as vulnerable to degradation and the Long Island Sound Study was established to develop a plan for protection of the Sound  Study results released in 2002 concluded that coordinated action to save the Sound was necessary             Building on several of the study  s recommendations and on feedback from subsequent public hearings throughout Connecticut  New York  and Washington  DC  the members first introduced the Long Island Sound Stewardship Act  LISSA  in 2004   LISSA would encourage protection of the Sound by providing financial incentives for owners of land parcels within the LIS area to preserve the environmental quality of and public access to this endangered habitat   The legislation passed the Senate last fall but did not pass the House  and so it was reintroduced in the 109th Congress                 Long Island Sound is a vital resource for New York  and passing this legislation will help provide funding to protect and restore it     said Senator Clinton     I am particularly pleased that this year  s legislation will include the Peconic Bay as well as the Long Island Sound                  The bipartisan legislation establishes a broad based   Long Island Sound Stewardship Committee    comprised of all LIS stakeholders  including federal  state  public interest and landowners representatives   The Committee would be charged with evaluating the parcels of land within the LIS region and designating crucial parcels as   Stewardship Sites    eligible for special preservation funds               Under the legislation  the Committee would give owners of Stewardship Sites financial incentives to sell their land to preservation groups or to preserve the environmental and public access features of that land themselves by volunteering to sell development rights to the Committee itself   The legislation designates  40 million for the Committee  s budget  to be taken out of the funds authorized for LIS preservation under the EPA  s Nation Estuary Program               The legislation provides specific criteria for the Committee to use in designating Stewardship Sites that evaluate the contribution of the land to open space on and public access to the LIS  as well as the ecological value of the land   At the urging of Senator Clinton  this year s bill includes the Peconic Bay as an area which is eligible for projects funding under the bill in addition to the Long Island Sound             The bill will now move to the full Senate for consideration
