April 13  2005 Schumer  Clinton  Key Senate Panel Authorizes Additional  20 Million in Funds For Onondaga Lake                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Funding included in Water Resources Development Act approved by the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Washington  DC    Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton announced today that the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee authorized a  20 million increase in funds under the Water Resources Development Act  WRDA  to help restore Onondaga Lake  The bill  which was passed by the Committee today  must now be approved by the full Senate before being sent to a House and Senate Conference Committee         The Water Resources Development Act authorizes Corps of Engineers civil works projects  including navigation  ecosystem restoration and shoreline protection projects   Under the authorizations  the Corps partners with local sponsors to conduct the projects           Cleaning up Onondaga Lake is good for Syracuse and for all of Central New York     Senator Schumer said    This is an important environmental project that can boost the economy and ensure New Yorkers will be able to enjoy Onondaga Lake for generations to come             I have been pleased to work with Senator Schumer and Congressman Walsh to help move the Onondaga cleanup forward     Senator Clinton said     Onondaga Lake is a critical resource  with tremendous promise as an economic tool for the communities that surround it and I am pleased that this funding will help the Corps continue its important restoration work there for future generations           The Senators announced that the bill passed by Committee today modifies an existing authorization for an Onondaga Lake ecosystem restoration project   The modification increases the authorization from  10 million to  30 million  and enables non profit entities to serve as the non federal sponsor with the consent of the local government  This modification was included last year in the House WRDA bill by Congressman Walsh
