June 29  2006 Senator Clinton Welcomes Key Senate Panel Approval of  750 000 for the Center for Coronary Disease Prevention and Reversal at the Heart Center of Niagara                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today welcomed approval by the Senate Appropriations Committee of the Fiscal Year 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill  which includes  750 000 for the Center for Coronary Disease Prevention and Reversal at The Heart Center of Niagara            Serving one of the largest populations of cardiac disease in the nation  these funds will go a long way toward helping the Heart Center of Niagara to continue in their efforts to expand diagnostic  emergency  and prevention services     Senator Clinton said    I look forward to continuing to work closely with the Heart Center of Niagara in the years to come             The Energy and Water Appropriations bill  which was approved by the Committee today  now proceeds to the Senate floor   Following consideration and passage on the Senate floor  the bill will head into a Conference Committee where the House and Senate will resolve the differences between the two bills          With the aid of this funding  the Heart Center of Niagara will expand its non invasive  medical management approach to the prevention and reversal of coronary artery disease to include PET CT angiography  a cutting edge non invasive technique for early diagnosis  The Center is acquiring a 64 slice CT scanner to complement its use of PET technology to diagnose and monitor heart disease  technology so new that the first 64 slice CT scanners are just becoming available  to combine this high speed  high resolution CT technology with PET scanning capability           The combination of new technology with groundbreaking research  in a market that has some of the worst death rates from cardiac disease in the United States  has attracted interest from several partners in both the university and business communities  The Center is partnering with GE Healthcare and the leaders of Western New York  s bioinformatics and life sciences community to develop and research innovative care paths that maximize patient safety while minimizing costs  This request will support one time technology upgrades and structural improvements necessary for the success of this initiative
