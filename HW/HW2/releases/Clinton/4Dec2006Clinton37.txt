December 4  2006 Senator Clinton Calls on President to Approve Disaster Declaration Following Last Month  s Floods                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Disaster Declaration Would Release Much Needed Funds in the Wake of the Most Recent Floods    Declaration Would Cover Broome  Chenango  Delaware  Herkimer  Hamilton  Montgomery  Otsego  and Tioga Counties                                                                                                                                                                                                 Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today called on the President to approve a major disaster declaration following the severe flooding and devastation that occurred on November 16  2006 in the New York counties of Broome  Chenango  Delaware  Herkimer  Hamilton  Montgomery  Otsego  and Tioga   In a letter to the President today  Senator Clinton pointed out that Governor Pataki has already submitted a request for a major disaster declaration and many individuals and businesses are in need of additional assistance given that many of these same areas suffered severe flooding earlier this year              Since the recent floods  my office has been in contact with both local officials and FEMA to assess the community  s needs and secure the appropriate assistance for individuals  businesses  and others in need  I have asked FEMA and the President to expeditiously approve the Governor  s disaster declaration request  Binghamton and other areas have been hit very hard this year and they need all the help they can get to recover and rebuild     Senator Clinton said after sending the letter today          Senator Clinton has worked tirelessly since the floods earlier this year to secure the necessary assistance and support for the impacted communities  Following the earlier floods  Senator Clinton wrote a similar letter to President Bush  asking that the White House expedite FEMA s assessment of the storm damage and issue a Presidential Disaster Declaration  Read more     A copy of Senator Clinton  s letter from today is attached        December 4  2006      The Honorable George W  Bush  The White House  Washington  D C  20500      Dear Mr  President       I write to urge your expedited approval of a major disaster declaration due to severe flooding and devastation that occurred on November 16  2006 in the New York counties of Broome  Chenango  Delaware  Herkimer  Hamilton  Montgomery  Otsego  and Tioga            The Governor has submitted a request for a major disaster declaration and many individuals and businesses are in need of additional assistance given that many of these same areas suffered severe flooding earlier this year   The initial damage assessments indicate significant debris removal needs  road closures  and other assistance due to the repeated flooding in these areas          The Governor has determined that this event is of such severity and magnitude that effective response is beyond the capabilities of the State and local governments and warrants federal assistance   I ask that FEMA provide individual and public assistance to the impacted counties and stand ready to help people and businesses rebuild their lives          I look forward to hearing from you and thank you for your consideration          Sincerely yours       Hillary Rodham Clinton
