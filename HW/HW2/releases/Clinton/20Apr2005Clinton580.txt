April 20  2005 Schumer  Clinton Announce Over  600 000 for Central New York Airports                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Airports in Cortland  Fulton and Hamilton Will Receive Federal Money for Improvements                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Washington DC   US Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the US Department of Transportation has awarded  626 875 in Federal Aviation Administration  FAA  grants to airports throughout Central New York  The money will be used for a series of improvements  ranging from equipment purchases to runway rehabilitation          This is great news for Central New York     said Schumer   These airports welcome travelers to New York each year  and these improvements are important for strengthening the local economy  I will continue to fight for federal funds to help the airport remain a thriving center of activity and a hub for economic growth in the region             These airports are an integral part of the local economy and it is important that we do everything we can to support them     Senator Clinton said    Any opportunity we have to improve access to these airports is critical  because more flights and better service simply means more jobs and more opportunities for the region           Three Central New York airports will receive federal money under today s award   Cortland County Chase Field in Cortland County will receive  209 000 to install weather reporting equipment   An automated weather observing system will provide pilots with localized weather information   This information will improve efficiency and lead to safer use of the facilities   Oswego County Airport will receive  267 407 for T Hanger development   Construction of the T Hanger will provide needed indoor space for based aircraft   Hamilton Municipal Airport will receive  150 468 to rehabilitate Runway 17 35  including reconstruction of the existing runway surface and the extension of Runway 35 end   The extension is necessary to compensate for the threshold displacement on Runway 17 and to provide the useable runway length required to meet the needs of the aircraft that use the airport        New York State received 15 grants this round  totaling  7 793 367 in federal funds  The grants were approved under the Federal Aviation Administration Airport Improvement Program
