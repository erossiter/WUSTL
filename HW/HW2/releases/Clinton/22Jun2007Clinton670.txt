June 22  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Approves  300 000 for Flood Protection Measures in Sidney                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Senators Worked Closely with Committee to Include Funding in Spending the Bill   Federal Funds Will be Used to Ensure Village of Sidney s Jobs and Economy are Protected from Future Flood Damage                                                                                                                                                                                                        Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced full Senate Appropriations Committee has approved the FY08 Interior  Environment  and Related Fields FY08 Appropriations Bill which includes  300 000 for Flood Protection Measures for the Village of Sidney in Delaware County  The bill will now proceed to the Senate Floor as the next step in the appropriations process   Schumer and Clinton worked closely with members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this years spending bill    These funds are crucial to ensuring that the damage from last years floods is not repeated  said Schumer   The fate of thousands of Delaware County jobs and homes is inextricably linked to the ability of communities like Sidney to anticipate and efficiently respond to natural disasters   These funds are vital to insuring the homes and livelihood of Sidney residents    This is another part of our ongoing commitment to providing the critical funds needed by the Village of Sidney following the devastating floods last year  Almost one year later  the memories of the floods are still very real as is the amount of work and funding needed to repair critical infrastructure  I am very pleased that we can continue to help the community and local officials as they continue their rebuilding efforts and I will do everything I can to ensure that this funding is passed by Congress so it can get to the community as soon as possible  Senator Clinton said     The federal funds will be used to design and implement flood protection measures  consisting of pump stations and flap gates in conjunction with existing flap gated culverts   Following the 2006 floods  the Village of Sidney suffered unrivaled damages to their infrastructure and water and wastewater treatment plants  The Amphenol Military and Aerospace Industrial Operations plant in Sidney  which employs over 1 500 people  suffered  21 million in losses to their facility  machinery  and equipment following the floods in June 2006   The flooding filled the 700 000 square ft  plant with 4 ft  of water and led to a 3 week shutdown  which directly resulted in  15 million losses in the 2nd quarter and an additional  6 million in losses in the 3rd quarter of 2006 to Amphenol Corp   A comprehensive study to implement flood protection measures will help the Village of Sydney mitigate against future flood damage to prevent repeat devastation    Schumer and Clinton toured the area following last Junes devastating flood  In November  Schumer also visited flood hammered Amphenol Military and Aerospace Industrial Operations  one of the largest employers in Delaware County  which is located in Sidney     Now that the bill has been approved by the full Senate Appropriations Committee  it will be sent to the Senate floor  Following approval by the Senate  the bill will move towards Conference with the House and then to the President for signature
