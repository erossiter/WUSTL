November 1  2007 Statement of Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton on Senate Vote on Children s Health Insurance                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Washington  DC  The Senate has once again joined together  across party lines  to do what is right for our children   There is bipartisan consensus that we have a responsibility as a nation to make sure children have access to healthcare   However  once again  President Bush stands alone   It is beyond the pale that President Bush has yet again vowed to block healthcare for nearly four million uninsured children  including nearly 17 000 children in New York   There is no greater investment than the health of our children and I will continue to fight for the healthcare they need and deserve    This bill also includes a measure Senator Dodd and I put forward to provide an urgently needed extension of family and medical leave for the families of wounded servicemembers  who are facing extraordinary circumstances as they struggle to care for their loved ones   This extension will allow these families to take up to six months of unpaid leave under the Family and Medical Leave Act  enacting a key recommendation of the Dole Shalala Commission on the Care for Wounded Warriors   We owe it to these families who have sacrificed so much for our country to enact this bill into law     Read more statements by Senator Clinton concerning children s health insurance
