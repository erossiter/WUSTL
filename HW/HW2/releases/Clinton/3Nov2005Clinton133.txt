November 3  2005 Senator Clinton to Travel to Israel                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Attending Saban Forum  First Trip to Israel Since Joining Senate Armed Services Committee Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton plans to travel to Israel to attend the Saban Forum 2005  an annual United States Israel dialogue organized by the Saban Center for Middle East Policy at the Brookings Institution and the Jaffee Center for Strategic Studies of Tel Aviv University    The Saban Forum  held from November 11th through the 13th  will bring a delegation of American officials and opinion leaders together with their Israeli counterparts  in Jerusalem  to discuss   Dealing with 21st Century Challenges                Senator Clinton will also meet with Israeli Prime Minister Ariel Sharon  and other Israeli officials  to discuss developments since Israel  s disengagement from Gaza  the security situation in the region  and to reiterate her strong condemnation of remarks made recently by Iranian President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad       Her Senate schedule allowing  Senator Clinton also plans to               Attend the official memorial for Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin in Jerusalem  and the Opening of the Rabin Center in Tel Aviv    both marking the 10 year anniversary of the assassination of Prime Minister Rabin               Meet with Magen David Adom  MDA  officials and Israeli health officials to underscore her devotion to the vital life saving work that MDA has performed for 75 years   Senator Clinton will reiterate her commitment to ensuring that the MDA gains entry into the International Red Cross  a goal she has been pursuing since she came into office   In August 2001  Senator Clinton and Senator Fitzgerald announced their leading a group of senators in urging Secretary of State Colin Powell and Red Cross officials to push for full and immediate Magen David Adom membership in the International Red Cross  They sent letters to Powell  Jakob Kellenberger  President of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent  and Dr  Astrid Hieberg  President of the International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Society  urging swift recognition and full membership for MDA   Though MDA has been unofficially recognized as the emblem of the medical and first aid services society in Israel  the organization does not have voting rights in the International Red Cross movements or equal status with other emergency relief societies under the organization  s rules  Senator Clinton  s MDA efforts continued with the 2002 Authorization Bill for the U S  State Department  which included a   Sense of Congress    that the International Red Cross should immediately recognize MDA  The Sense of Congress also declared that the Red Shield of David should be accorded the same protections under international law as the Red Cross and the Red Crescent and that the United States should continue to press for full membership for the MDA in the International Red Cross   In January 2003  Senator Clinton along with Senator Elizabeth Dole and Senator Peter Fitzgerald  offered an amendment to withhold the United States   contributions to the headquarters of the International Committee of the Red Cross until MDA is no longer denied participation in the activities of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent  IRC  Movement   The amendment passed the Senate on January 23  2003            Meet with the Bi National Disaster Preparedness Consortium that links the expertise of UJA Federation  s New York agencies with that of the Israel Trauma Coalition   She will meet with human service professionals  community leaders and individuals whose lives have been impacted by terrorism and whose personal and professional stories can demonstrate the power of the bi national ties forged    Israel and New York share experience with terrorism and trauma   And out of that shared experience  they have learned from each other important lessons about dealing with trauma and work together to share their expertise with communities all over the world   Senator Clinton hopes to highlight the unique contributions of both New Yorkers and Israelis in the delivery systems the Bi National Disaster Preparedness Consortium have put in place  with a particular focus of its impact on parents  their young children and first responders   The goal of the visit is to demonstrate the power of the community resiliency model as a vehicle for disaster preparedness  early intervention and treatment               Tour a section of the Security Fence near Jerusalem   Senator Clinton was a leader in supporting Israel  s right to build the fence          Senator Clinton last visited Israel in February of 2002       Click here to read Senator Clinton s trip report
