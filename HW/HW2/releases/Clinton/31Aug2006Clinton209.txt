August 31  2006 Schumer  Clinton Urge USPS to Create a Stamp Commemorating NY Painter Jasper Cropsey                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Senators Urge USPS   Citizens Stamp Advisory Committee To Issue Commemorative Stamp      Life long New Yorker Was An Integral Part Of the Internationally Renown Mid 19th Century Landscape Painters Known As the   Hudson River School                                                                                                                                                                          Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today urged the U S  Postal Service to encourage the Citizens Stamp Advisory Committee  CSAC  to issue a commemorative stamp honoring New York Painter  Jasper F  Cropsey   Mr  Cropsey is famous for his colorful landscapes and his association with the internationally renowned landscape painters known as the Hudson River School             New York  s own Jasper Cropsey captured the true spirit of America in his moving paintings of New York and other national landscapes     said Schumer    He created profoundly beautiful images that vaulted our still young nation to the forefront of international culture  As one of this country  s premier 19th century landscape painters  he deserves to be honored with a commemorative stamp               Jasper Cropsey was one of the foremost American painters  an artist with deep ties to New York  A native Staten Islander and a lifelong New Yorker  Cropsey  s relationship with our state and his incredible talent  enabled him to accurately capture its beauty  As we prepare for the 400th anniversary of the voyages of Hudson and Champlain and the 200th anniversary of the voyage of Fulton  it is only fitting that we take this opportunity to promote this great American Master and New York icon     Senator Clinton said          Mr  Cropsey  a life long New Yorker  was an integral part of the mid 19th century American group of landscape painters called the Hudson River School   Cropsey was best known for his bold and brilliant use of color to depict American landscapes   His paintings are owned by most of America  s major museums  and are found in the collections of the Department of State  the White House  and the National Gallery of Art  On August 27  1998  the U S  Post Office issued a collection of stamps titled   Four Centuries of American Art    featuring three Hudson River painters  however  paintings by Mr  Cropsey were not included   The request to CSAC for this stamp has already generated Congressional support in the form of Senate Concurrent Resolution 54 and House Concurrent Resolution 138          In a letter to John E  Potter  Postmaster General for the U S  Postal Service  and CSAC  Sens  Schumer and Clinton wrote    Mr  Cropsey  s intimate relationship with New York State and with the Hudson River Valley allowed him to depict the region that served first as a door to the exploration of the continent  and later a principal artery of our national trade and economic growth  His paintings captured the American land in its purest sense  and earned him lasting place in American art and culture   For this  we believe he deserves the honor of commemoration by a United States postage stamp   We would recommend the portrait of jasper F  Cropsey  by Edward Mooney  as well as three famous paintings by Mr  Cropsey for a commemorative stamp   Autumn  On the Hudson River  Niagara Falls  or Wyoming Valley  Pennsylvania
