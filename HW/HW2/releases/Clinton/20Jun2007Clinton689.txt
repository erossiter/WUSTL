June 20  2007 Senator Clinton Applauds Passage of Head Start Reauthorization Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Increases Funding  Doubles Early Head Start  Enhances Teacher Quality  Suspends the NRS  and Retains Decision Making Role of Parents                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Washington  DC  Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today hailed the Senate passage of the Head Start for School Readiness Act  S 556   a bill to reauthorize the Head Start Act     I am so pleased that this bi partisan Head Start reauthorization bill has been passed by the Senate  More than 50 000 families in New York have benefited from the comprehensive services Head Start provides for more than 40 years  The increased funding in this bill will strengthen teachers skills and training  while ensuring that parents voices are heard in the day to day operations of Head Start  It also increases eligibility guidelines so more families can enroll in Head Start and suspends the National Reporting System  NRS  until a better tool can be developed to fairly test children enrolled in the program  Im also pleased this bill expands Early Head Start to reach more infants and toddlers so they are ready to learn when they begin kindergarten  The Senate passage of this bill is a victory for our neediest children and the Head Start community that serves them     The bill passed by the Senate increases Head Start authorization to  7 350 billion for FY 2008   7 650 billion for FY2009  and  7 995 billion for FY2010  an increase of 6  in the first year and 4  in the next two years  The bill enhances teacher quality  retains the role of parents per the policy council  suspends the National Reporting System  NRS   and increases the income eligibility guidelines from 100  to 130  of the poverty level  The bill also expands Early Head Start   a program created in the Clinton Administration to reach children from birth to age three  to reach more children across New York and the country
