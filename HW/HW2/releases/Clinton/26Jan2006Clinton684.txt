January 26  2006 Senator Clinton Calls On Bush Administration To Address Bungled Medicare Prescription Drug Program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Highlights Sensible Remedies in Comprehensive Legislation Introduced by Clinton   Senate Colleagues                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Cohoes  NY   Amid continuing reports of disarray in the implementation of the new Medicare prescription drug benefit program  Senator Clinton visited a pharmacy in Cohoes  NY today and called for immediate action to aid local pharmacists and seniors who are bearing the brunt of the problems plaguing the new program   The Senator Clinton was joined by pharmacists  seniors and advocates to outline some of the specific challenges facing the hundreds of thousand New York seniors who are dependent on the Medicare prescription drug program           The new prescription drug benefit has created unnecessary obstacles for far too many New Yorkers who can least afford it    our poorest  most vulnerable seniors and disabled     Senator Clinton said    To date  the Bush Administration has failed to adequately address these problems   It is time for the Administration to take immediate action   we owe it to our seniors and disabled Americans to get this right           The Senator outlined how comprehensive legislation that she recently introduced with her Senate colleagues would address the problems plaguing implementation of the new program   The legislation includes measures that Senator Clinton called for to ensure that states are reimbursed by the federal government for taking emergency action to keep access to medications for our most vulnerable  to increase the resources for outreach and education for beneficiaries and to ensure that telephone hotlines are able to handle high volumes of inquiries  and to protect seniors who also have coverage from a retiree health plan         Another Clinton measure of the legislation would help those dually eligible for Medicare and Medicaid  transition into the new Medicare prescription drug benefit by requiring outreach and education to pharmacies on dually eligible beneficiaries and waiving the transaction fees that pharmacies incur when assisting these beneficiaries         Senator Clinton has been a vocal advocate for the need to fix the problems plaguing the new Medicare prescription drug program and ensure that Medicare recipients receive the assistance they need and deserve  Senator Clinton is also a co sponsor of legislation that would extend the amount of time seniors have to enroll in the program  In addition  Senator Clinton has released a resource guide to prescription drug coverage under Medicare  available in English and Spanish  to help New Yorkers navigate this transition  The guide has been distributed to more than 75 000 New Yorkers and is posted on Senator Clinton  s website at www clinton senate gov
