May 3  2006 Senator Clinton Reiterates Call For Extension Of Deadline For Medicare Part D Sign Ups                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Confusion  program difficulties may leave almost 1 million New Yorkers in the lurch                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                The fact that so many eligible New Yorkers  particularly those Upstate  have not enrolled in the new Medicare prescription drug benefit means we still have work to do to fix the problems plaguing the program   Too many seniors and disabled rightly have been confused and overwhelmed in trying to navigate the enrollment process  Seniors and disabled in many areas of Upstate New York may face a particular challenge with more limited access to federal  state  local or community based resources for getting assistance with the sign up process     Senator Clinton said     Instead of penalizing our seniors and disabled who are clearly struggling with this complex program  we should fix the problems and give potential beneficiaries the time and assistance they need to make an informed choice about enrolling in the new prescription drug benefit            I have introduced legislation along with Senate colleagues on both sides of the aisle to extend the enrollment deadline and we are pressing the Senate Republican leadership to bring this legislation to the floor   In the meantime  we need to do everything we can to make sure potential beneficiaries are aware of the deadline and have all the information they need at their disposal to evaluate their options     the Senator said       Senator Clinton recently joined a bipartisan group of 48 senators calling on Senator Frist to bring legislation to the Senate floor immediately to allow an extension of the new Medicare prescription drug benefit enrollment deadline without penalty to potential beneficiaries   With the enrollment deadline rapidly approaching  13 million eligible seniors and disabled Americans    almost 1 million of those in New York   have yet to sign up and face stiff penalties for enrolling after the May 15 deadline         Senator Clinton has been a vocal advocate for the need to fix the problems plaguing the new Medicare prescription drug program and ensure that Medicare recipients receive the assistance they need and deserve   Senator Clinton has co sponsored comprehensive legislation that would make these needed fixes  including helping those dually eligible for Medicare and Medicaid transition into the new Medicare prescription drug benefit by requiring outreach and education to pharmacies on dually eligible beneficiaries and waiving the transaction fees that pharmacies incur when assisting these beneficiaries   She has also co sponsored legislation that would prevent Medicare prescription drug plans from ending coverage of drugs that were available when beneficiaries enrolled in the plan and extend the amount of time seniors have to enroll in the program  In addition  Senator Clinton has released a resource guide to prescription drug coverage under Medicare  available in English and Spanish  to help New Yorkers navigate this transition  The guide has been distributed to more than 75 000 New Yorkers and is posted on Senator Clinton  s website at www clinton senate gov
