June 29  2006 Statement of Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton on the Recommendations to the CDC On HPV Vaccine                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     WASHINGTON  DC   Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton released the following statement on the recommendations of the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices  ACIP  for the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention  CDC  regarding the vaccine for several strains of the human papilloma virus  HPV   the leading cause of cervical cancer            Today  s action by the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices  ACIP  is an important step for women  s health  While deaths from cervical cancer have declined significantly since the advent of diagnostic tools like the Pap smear  the HPV vaccine holds the promise of eradicating cancer causing strains of the virus among women  I urge the Department of Health and Human Services to implement these recommendations and help protect millions of American girls and women from developing cervical cancer             Earlier today  the ACIP unanimously recommended that all girls aged 11 12 receive the three dose HPV vaccine  and that the vaccine be included as part of the Vaccines for Children program  These recommendations must be approved by the CDC and the Department of Health and Human Services   Over 10 000 new cases of cervical cancer occur in the United States each year  and almost 4 000 women die from this disease  In New York  there are approximately 1 000 new cases of cervical cancer each year           Last year  press reports indicated that some groups asserted that the vaccine may encourage sexual activity among youth    a claim that is not based on any scientific evidence  In light of these reports  Senator Clinton sent a letter to Department of Health and Human Services Secretary Michael Leavitt urging him to make sure that science and the public health  not ideology  were the basis of ACIP  s decision
