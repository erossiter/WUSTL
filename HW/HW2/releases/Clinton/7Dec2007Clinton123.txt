December 7  2007 House Senate Conference Committee Approves Clinton Amendment Calling for Comprehensive Assessment of Military Readiness                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Amendment Included as Part of the FY 2008 National Defense Authorization Act                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Washington  DC   Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that a joint House Senate Conference Committee has included an amendment in the Fiscal Year 2008 National Defense Authorization Act which she introduced that requires the Government Accountability Office  GAO  to conduct a comprehensive assessment of the readiness of the Ground Forces within the Army and Marine Corps   This years modification requires the GAO to assess the impact on the ground forces of the troop surge initiated by President Bush in January  2007     The bill  having been approved by the Conference Committee  will now return to both chambers of Congress for final passage  whereupon it will be sent to the President for his signature    Our men and women in uniform have done everything asked of them  but the strain on our ground forces directly threatens their ability to meet other pressing challenges as well as tomorrows threat requirements  Including an assessment of the impact that the surge has had on our ground forces to GAOs ongoing efforts will be critical to understanding the true cost of this war  said Senator Clinton  who as part of the Senate Armed Services Committee serves on the Subcommittee on Readiness and Management Support     Senator Clintons measure will provide greater transparency on the true readiness of the military to better identify where Congress can help focus its efforts  Her amendment builds on last years GAO requirements      Assess the ability of ground forces to provide trained and ready forces to meet the requirements of increased force levels of Operations Iraqi freedom and Enduring Freedom above current force levels in effect on January 1  2007      Assess trained and ready forces to meet ongoing operations simultaneously with such increased force levels      Asses the strategic depth of the Army and Marine Corps and their ability to provide trained and ready forces to meet requirements of the high priority contingency war plans of regional combatant commands      Identify and evaluate strategic and operational risks associated with current and projected forces of current and projected readiness      Identify and evaluate the time required to make forces available and prepare them for deployment  and     Identify and evaluate likely strategic tradeoffs necessary to meet the requirements of high priority contingency war plans     GAOs ongoing assessment from the FY07 National Defense Authorization Act includes     Assess trained and ready forces for ongoing operations as well as future requirements      Assess the availability and readiness of equipment for training and ongoing operations      Review current personnel tempo with a particular focus deployment and retention rates for both unusually high and low demand skills      Evaluate service efforts to mitigate high personnel operational tempos  and     Describe current reserve component mobilization policy with an analysis of projected future availability     The measure requires the GAO to complete all requirements of its report to the Senate and House Armed Services Committees no later than June 1  2008     Senator Clintons amendment is part her ongoing efforts improve evaluation of the current state of our ground forces and better highlight where Congress should focus its efforts to ensure the continued preparedness of our Army and Marine Corps
