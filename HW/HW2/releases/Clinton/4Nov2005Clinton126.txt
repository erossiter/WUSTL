November 4  2005 Schumer Clinton Law to Make Deadbeat Diplomats Pay Parking Tickets   Property Taxes Enters Final Stages                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Schumer Clinton proposal would cut foreign aid for nations that have unpaid New York City parking tickets and back property taxes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Washington  DC   US Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that an amendment that would cut foreign aid to nations that owe New York City unpaid parking tickets and back property taxes has been included in the final report of the House Senate Conference on the Fiscal Year 2006 Foreign Operations Appropriations Bill  The bill now awaits final approval by Congress before heading to the President for his signature           Despite significant progress over the last year to collect parking tickets from deadbeat diplomats and to crack down on diplomatic building owners who owe property taxes  New York City is still owed millions from foreign consulates and embassies  This amendment would withhold foreign aid money from countries that owe these types of outstanding debts           The Schumer Clinton amendment will reduce a country  s foreign aid package by the amount that country owes in unpaid New York City parking tickets and back property taxes  plus an additional ten percent penalty for a total of 110 percent  The foreign aid will be withheld until the Secretary of State has assured Congress that the debts have been fully paid  The amendment would also apply to unpaid debts owed to Washington  D C            Few things are as crushing as getting one of those little orange envelopes from a traffic cop  If you or I ignore a parking ticket  we have to pay double the fine  If a diplomat ignores a parking ticket  we send him foreign aid  That makes no sense  And forget about not paying your property tax bill    they  ll take your house  Well  regardless of who you are or where you are from  you re not above the law in New York   Senator Schumer said            No one likes getting a parking ticket   But when New Yorkers get a ticket  they have to pay and when they don t pay property tax  they risk losing the roof over their heads  This practice has gone on for far too long now   It is simply outrageous for foreign diplomats and consular officials to flout our laws and shirk their responsibilities by parking illegally and ignoring their parking tickets   New Yorkers have to face severe financial penalties if they do this and so should diplomats   Senator Clinton said
