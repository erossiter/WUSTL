August 16  2007 Senator Clinton Welcomes Appalachian Regional Commission Award of  17 500 to Hancock Partners  Inc                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Senate Recently Approved Clinton Backed Legislation to Reauthorize the Appalachian Regional Commission                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Washington  DC  Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton welcomed the recent announcement by the Appalachian Regional Commission  ARC  of a  17 500 grant to the Hancock Partners  Inc  for the Hancock Economic and Community Development Plan  The Senate recently approved passage of the Appalachian Regional Commission  ARC  Act which Senator Clinton co sponsored   The bill reauthorizes the work of the ARC until 2011  and provides an appropriate federal matching rate for ARC funded projects in eligible counties    Coordinated development is critical to any region and this grant for Hancock Partners will help them develop a local economic and community development plan with a focus on Hancocks future  Senator Clinton said  These grants are a great example of how valuable the ARC is to economic growth of the region  I am very pleased that my colleagues voted to reauthorize the ARC  so that it can continue to support organizations like Hancock Partners and others like it  Reauthorization of the ARC will allow the agency to continue its important work to support communities  encourage small business and foster economic growth     According to the ARC  the grant will assist Hancock Partners efforts to develop an economic and community based development plan  along with stronger organizational capacity for the Village of Hancock and surrounding area within the Town of Hancock  Development of the plan will include a strong public participation process and development of projects and initiatives to implement plans necessary to enhance the economic vitality of the greater Hancock area  The plan will cover gateway and corridor improvements  housing and neighborhood revitalization  greenways parks and recreation youth centers  economic and community development  downtown revitalization and zoning  In addition to ARC funds  local sources will provide  17 500 bringing the total project funding to  35 000    The ARC provides eligible communities with tools to help bolster businesses and spur local economic development  The Appalachian Regional Commission  ARC  Act continues all existing ARC general programs  including the special program in telecommunications and technology  which Senator Clinton has fought to fully fund  The bill also provides funding of over  95 million annually for the next five years and permits ARC to fund projects up to 70 percent of the cost of the project     Senator Clinton has long fought to support the ARC  joining in past years with Senator Voinovich  as well as Senator John D  Rockefeller  D WV  to write President Bush urging him to retain vital funding for this Commission  Senator Clinton also worked to pass amendments to the Appalachian Regional Development Act     Created by Congress in 1965  the ARC is a partnership between governors of 13 states  a federal representative and a grassroots network of local elected officials  and community and business leaders  It includes several counties in New York s Southern Tier  including Allegany  Broome  Cattaraugus  Chautauqua  Chemung  Chenango  Cortland  Delaware  Otsego  Schoharie  Schuyler  Steuben  Tioga  and Tompkins
