November 8  2005 Senator Clinton Calls For Action to Address Looming Home Heating Crisis  Urges Oil Companies to be Part of Energy Solution                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       New Department of Energy Report Projects Record High Home Heating Costs This Winter                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Washington  DC    Following release of new U S  Department of Energy projections showing staggering price increases for home heating oil and natural gas this winter  Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today called on the Republican leadership in Congress to act now to fund a desperately needed increase in low income home heating assistance   With oil company leaders set to testify before Senate Committees tomorrow  Senator Clinton also noted the need for oil companies to step up to the plate and become part of our energy solution  reiterating her call for a Strategic Energy Fund to help consumers cope with spiraling energy costs  promote adoption of existing clean energy and conservation technologies and stimulate research             The bottom line is that millions of Americans will face the most daunting energy costs they have ever faced this winter     Senator Clinton said     Unless we act now  we will have a crisis on our hands   However the Republican leadership has repeatedly rejected needed increases to low income home heating assistance   I will continue to fight to prevent the looming home heating crisis and for a long term solution to our energy challenges              Senator Clinton today called for a  5 1 billion increase in funding for the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP   which would boost funding for New York by  500 million   LIHEAP  which offers federal funds to help low income households meet their home heating needs  will be critical to help thousands of low income New Yorkers make ends meet this fall and winter as the struggle to cope with the soaring cost and diminishing availability of home heating fuel   Last year  an estimated 800 000 New Yorkers received LIHEAP assistance  with benefits ranging from  150 to  400 per household   However  the program has been losing ground to inflation and a significant increase in funding is needed to even keep pace with last year            Adding to the financial strain  a new report released today by the U S  Energy Information Administration  EIA  at the U S  Department of Energy estimates that heating costs will rise 27 percent for New York households that use natural gas and 25 percent for home heating oil over last winter   Statewide  this would mean additional heating costs of  1 7 billion this year over last year   According to the report  colder weather would increase these estimates significantly            Senator Clinton is working on all fronts to help New Yorkers who are affected by the skyrocketing energy prices and to address our long term energy challenges   Senator Clinton has called for a new national energy strategy to help consumers and stimulate energy alternatives and challenged the oil industry to reinvest their profits in America  s energy future or contribute to a new Strategic Energy Fund   Senator Clinton also continues to press the Bush Administration to investigate gas price gouging
