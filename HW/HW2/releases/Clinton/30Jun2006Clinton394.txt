June 30  2006 Senator Clinton Welcomes Key Senate Panel Approval of  250 000 for Buffalo River Cleanup                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton welcomed approval by the Senate Appropriations Committee of the Fiscal Year 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill  which includes  250 000 for Buffalo River sediment removal and remediation            The Energy and Water Appropriations Bill  which was approved by the Committee yesterday  now proceeds to the Senate floor   Following consideration and passage on the Senate floor  the bill will head into a Conference Committee where the House and Senate will resolve the differences between the two bills   Congressman Brian Higgins secured  200 000 in funding for the project in the House version of the bill              This funding will enable the Army Corps of Engineers and Buffalo Niagara Riverkeeper to continue working to clean up the Buffalo River     said Senator Clinton     Restoring this river is a key environmental priority and it is also a necessary step for us to realize the real potential of this resource to the city             The funding targets the removal and remediation of areas with contaminated sediments that have been identified as   Areas of Concern      These contaminated sediment areas have resulted from past pollution of the River by industrial sources   The contamination has a number of harmful effects  including destruction of fish and wildlife habitat  restrictions on fish and wildlife consumption  and restriction on dredging activities   The Army Corps of Engineers has partnered with Buffalo Niagara Riverkeeper to carry out the project
