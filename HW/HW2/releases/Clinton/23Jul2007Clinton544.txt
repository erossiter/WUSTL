July 23  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Approval of  600 000 to Protect the Great Lakes from Soil Erosion                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Funds Will Be Awarded for Great Lakes Basin Soil and Erosion Control to Address Sedimentation Problems   Senators Worked Closely with Committee to Include Funding in Spending Bill   Bill Will Now Be Sent to the Senate Floor                                                                                                                                                                 Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the full Senate Appropriations Committee has approved the Agriculture  Rural Development  Food and Drug Administration  and Related Agencies FY08 Appropriations Bill  which includes  600 000 for Great Lakes Basin Soil and Erosion Control   The bill will now proceed to the Senate Floor as the next step in the appropriations process   Schumer and Clinton worked closely with members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this years spending bill    It is essential that we work to preserve the Great Lakes  both to preserve efficient shipping and to keep them clean  Schumer said  This funding will prevent erosion and help keep these unique wonders clear of contaminated sediment that can get into drinking water   I will continue to fight for funding to ensure that the Great Lakes are protected  so that future generations are able to enjoy their beauty for years to come    This funding is certainly good news for the Great Lakes  We are all too aware of the pressure that the Great Lakes are under  While we continue to fight to improve the overall health of the lakes  these funds for the Soil and Erosion Control project will certainly help  Senator Clinton said  The threats from sedimentation  pollution and invasive species simply cannot not be ignored  While we will continue to push to implement the recommendations of the Great Lakes Regional Collaboration Strategy  this funding will at least go part of the way to addressing the very serious needs of these incredible national assets    The Great Lakes Basin Soil and Erosion Control project is designed to prevent soil  nitrogen  and phosphorus from entering the Great Lakes and regional waters by reducing soil erosion and controlling sedimentation   Sediment from agriculture is a major contaminant in drinking water   Funding for this project will be split between New York  Minnesota  and Michigan        Now that the bill has been approved by the full Senate Appropriations Committee  it will be sent to the Senate floor  Following approval by the Senate  the bill will move towards Conference with the House and then to the President for signature
