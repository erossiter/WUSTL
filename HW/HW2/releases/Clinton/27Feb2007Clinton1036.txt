February 27  2007 Senators Hagel and Clinton Reintroduce Lung Cancer Resolution                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Washington  DC   Senators Chuck Hagel  R NE  and Hillary Rodham Clinton  D NY  today reintroduced a resolution that calls on President Bush to declare lung cancer a national public health priority  The Senators  resolution calls on the Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  to establish a coordinated strategy that encompasses research  screening and drug approval initiatives to combat lung cancer and meet the goal of halving deaths as a result of this disease by 2015           Lung cancer is the most lethal form of cancer for men and women in the United States   The American Cancer Society estimates that over 900 Nebraskans will die of lung cancer in 2007   We have made great advancements in prostate and breast cancer survival rates  we must commit ourselves to making the same progress in lung cancer survival rates   Hagel said            Lung cancer touches millions of families across America and more than 12 000 new cases are diagnosed in New York State every year  We must do our part to increase awareness of this disease and support ongoing early detection and treatment research   said Senator Clinton          The lung cancer resolution also calls on the Department of Defense  DoD  and Department of Veterans Affairs  VA  to develop lung cancer screening and disease management programs for military personnel and veterans  To coordinate the establishment and regulation of these proposed programs  the resolution establishes a National Lung Cancer Public Health Policy Board along with a Scientific and Medical Advisory Board            Senators Clinton and Hagel first introduced the lung cancer resolution during the 109th Congress  The resolution passed the Senate but was never acted upon by the House of Representatives  Senators Clinton and Hagel will work to get the resolution enacted into law during the 110th Congress
