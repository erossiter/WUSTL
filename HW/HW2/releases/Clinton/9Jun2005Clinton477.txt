June 9  2005 Clinton  Key Senate Panel Approves Funding for New York Projects                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Erie Canalway National Heritage Corridor Commission  Hudson River Valley National Heritage Area  Mohawk Valley NY Water Authority all slated for funding under Senate Interior Appropriations Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                               Washington  DC   Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today applauded the Senate Interior Appropriations Subcommittee for providing funds for several important New York projects that Senator Clinton supported        The Interior Appropriations bill  which was approved by the Committee today  now proceeds to the Senate floor  The House of Representatives passed their bill on May 19  2005  Following consideration and passage on the Senate floor  the bill will head into a Conference Committee where the House and Senate will resolve the differences between the two bills         These are important projects for New York and I am pleased that my colleagues have also seen their true value   Senator Clinton said   These are investments not just in our environment and our historical heritage  but in our state s future  I will continue to work to ensure these projects are funded by the full Senate         Senator Clinton announced that the Senate Interior Appropriations Committee had approved the following         700 000  Erie Canalway National Heritage Corridor  The funds would continue to support the overall operations  planning and projects for the Erie Canalway National Heritage Corridor Commission serving Upstate  NY  This funding would enable the Commission to complete the management plan and begin to implement early action items to preserve and revitalize local canal communities          400 000  Hudson River Valley National Heritage Area  The mission of the Hudson River Valley National Heritage Area is to recognize  preserve  protect and interpret the nationally significant cultural and natural resources of the Hudson River Valley for the benefit of the nation  In FY 2006  the program will continue to highlight Heritage Sites of the Hudson River Valley through the website www hudsonrivervalley com  and brochures interpreting these historic places  Other projects to be supported through funding include  gateway and wayfinding signage  regional marketing of the Heritage Area  education grants for local teachers and a partnership with Hudson River Valley Institute at Marist College  Special events scheduled for 2006 include  Revolutionary War Celebrations  Sixth Annual Great Hudson River Paddle  7th Annual Hudson River Valley Ramble  and The Great Hudson Valley Pedal         250 000  Mohawk Valley NY Water Authority  The project goal is to develop and enhance methodologies for rapid bacterial detection used to identify and isolate a microbial contamination of a drinking water distribution system  In the event of a suspected contamination of a distribution system  these methods can potentially analyze a sample for bacterial contamination in real time  providing utilities and government officials with reliable data to assist on site decisions that maximize protection of public health
