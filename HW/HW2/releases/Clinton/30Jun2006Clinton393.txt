June 30  2006 Senator Clinton Welcomes Key Senate Panel Approval of  750 000 for the Erie Canalway National Heritage Area                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today welcomed approval by the Senate Appropriations Committee of the Fiscal Year 2007 Interior Appropriations Bill  which includes  750 000 for the Erie Canalway National Heritage Corridor Commission          The Interior Appropriations bill  which was approved by the Committee today  now proceeds to the Senate floor   Following consideration and passage on the Senate floor  the bill will head into a Conference Committee where the House and Senate will resolve the differences between the two bills            From one end of the state to the other  communities are building on this incredible asset to develop their waterfronts  their park and their economic opportunities  The National Heritage Corridor Commission will not only give communities the tools to make the most of their investments  it will give the state the capability to market this wonderful tourism opportunity     Senator Clinton said    I have long been a supporter of the movement to embrace the Erie Canal as part of our economic development and I am very pleased that we can continue to support our heritage tourism efforts in this way             In 2000  Congress established a federal Commission to oversee the Corridor and directed the National Park Service to assist in the development and implementation of a Preservation and Management Plan for the Heritage Corridor   These funds will be used to complete the management plan and implement priority actions to preserve the best of the Erie Canal s past  enhance educational and recreational opportunities in the Corridor and foster economic revitalization in Canal communities
