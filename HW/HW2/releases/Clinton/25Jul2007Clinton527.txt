July 25  2007 Senator Clinton Welcomes Key Senate Committee Approval of the Alzheimer s Breakthrough Act of 2007                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee Approves Clinton Backed Legislation that Advances Alzheimer s Research  Support  and Education                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Washington  DC  Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today welcomed approval by the Senate Health  Education Labor and Pensions  HELP  Committee  of which the Senator is a member  of the Alzheimers Breakthrough Act of 2007 which she cosponsored earlier this year with Senator Barbara Mikulski  This important legislation will fund breakthroughs in Alzheimers research  educate the public on healthy lifestyles  and provide support for the millions of Americans who care for patients suffering from Alzheimers and other dementias      Every day  Alzheimers disease robs millions of Americans of the thoughts  memories  and dreams that define them as individuals  Countless children  spouses  loved ones and caretakers also suffer as they watch the disease unfold in a family member  friend  or patient  said Senator Clinton  As co chair of the Congressional Task Force on Alzheimers Disease  I have worked tirelessly to address the health and mental health needs of older Americans  and I am so pleased that my colleagues have recognized how vital this legislation is to our researchers and to those who struggle daily with this devastating disease    By authorizing a doubling in the funding for Alzheimers research from  640 million to  1 3 billion  the Alzheimers Breakthrough Act of 2007 will help ensure that researchers have the resources they need to better understand the pieces of this devastating puzzle   In particular  this legislation would prioritize research into early onset Alzheimers  to help the estimated half a million Americans age 55 to 64 that have cognitive impairment at disabling levels as a result of early onset Alzheimers disease or other dementias      The bill would also establish a National Alzheimers Summit to promote greater scientific discourse and future advances in research  and would create an education campaign to improve the publics awareness of health promotion strategies that may reduce the risk of developing Alzheimers  Finally  this legislation would support the efforts of family caregivers by authorizing  25 million for respite  home health care  and day care services    Across the nation  approximately 5 1 million Americans suffer from the ravages of Alzheimers  including an estimated 330 000 individuals with Alzheimers in New York alone  As the Baby Boomer generation ages  there will be a dramatic increase in the number of Alzheimers cases in the country  By the year 2050  up to 16 million older Americans are expected to suffer from the disease      The Alzheimers Breakthrough Act was approved today by the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions  HELP  Committee  The bill now goes to the Senate floor
