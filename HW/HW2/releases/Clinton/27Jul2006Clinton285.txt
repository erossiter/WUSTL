July 27  2006 Senator Clinton Applauds Enactment of Adam Walsh Child Protection and Safety Act                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Senator Clinton Co Sponsored Senate Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton applauded the signing into law today of the Adam Walsh Child Protection and Safety Act  which creates important new protections for children and communities   Senator Clinton co sponsored the Senate version of the bill  the Sex Offender Registration and Notification Act  which was approved unanimously by the Senate on May 8  2006              Today we have taken an important step forward in our efforts to protect our children   By strengthening our sex offender laws and creating a nationwide registry of sex offenders  we have improved our ability to track sex offenders and prevent repeat offenses   The bill also creates a national child abuse registry to further our ability to protect our most vulnerable children   I was proud to co sponsor the Senate version of this important bill and I am heartened that these important protections are being enacted for our families and communities            There are more than 20 000 registered sex offenders in New York and over 550 000 in the United States   However  too many convicted sex offenders receive and serve limited prison sentences and too many are able to live among the general public unchecked and unknown to their communities   We need to do everything we can to prevent the next child from becoming a victim and the legislation enacted today will greatly aid our efforts to meet this critical goal          While this bill includes important protections for New York s children and families  the final version of the bill signed into law today includes measures that impact the system in place in New York and several other states to conduct criminal record checks   I will work with my colleagues to address this issue and enable New York to continue its current rigorous system
