July 26  2005 Clinton Announces Key Senate Panel Backs  250 000 for El Museo Del Barrio                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Senator Urged Appropriations Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved  250 000 for El Museo del Barrio in the FY06 Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary  and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations Bill              One of the most important things we can give to our children and to future generations is an understanding of culture and history     Senator Clinton said    Hispanic culture is so much a part of our shared heritage and El Museo del Barrio is truly a center for the celebration of Latin American and Hispanic culture in the United States  I am so pleased that we can help provide these funds for El Museo  s further enhancements  so that we can help this important institution grow     Senator Clinton said           The funding will help El Museo del Barrio with the goal of maximizing its presence as the Latino cultural destination on Museum Mile  Specifically  the funding will assist with the completion of capital improvements required to enhance the ground floor of the Heckscher Building  its home since 1977 and other image enhancements  The project includes the modernization of the Fifth Avenue and 104th Street lobbies  the reconstruction and addition of a new caf      expanded museum shop and rooms  new elevators  and a reconstructed courtyard  which faces Fifth Avenue and the Central Park Conservatory Garden and serves as the main entry into the museum             The FY06 Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary  and Housing and Urban Development Appropriations Bill was approved by the Senate Appropriations Committee last week and now heads to the Senate floor for a full vote  After final passage through the Senate  a conference committee will reconcile the differences between the House and Senate versions of the Bill before sending it to the President
