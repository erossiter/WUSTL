September 25  2007 Schumer  Clinton  Authorization of  14 Million to Protect Montauk Lighthouse Sent to President for Final Approval                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Final Conference Report on Federal Funding Authorizations for Water Resources Development Act Approved by Senate Last Night    Now Sent to President for Signing                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the Senate has approved the final conference report on Water Resources Development Act  WRDA  which authorizes  14 million for a shoreline project to protect the Montauk Lighthouse  The bill passed House Senate conference in July and now will be sent to the President for signing  The Montauk Lighthouse is one of the most scenic  treasured destinations for many Long Island families and tourists  but it has suffered from the torrential downpours and erosion  said Senator Schumer  Im thrilled to announce that the Corps is a giant step closer to having the necessary funding to properly maintain this cherished beacon  For more than two centuries the historic Montauk Lighthouse has been an icon and beautiful symbol of Long Island s rich history  as well as a testament to the stunning aesthetic beauty of the Island s natural environment  said Senator Clinton  Senate approval of this essential funding will help the Corps to keep it maintained and preserved for generations to come  The WRDA bill authorizes  14 million for projects to reduce hurricane and storm damage at Montauk Point     The Water Resources Development Act authorizes Corps of Engineers civil works projects  including navigation  ecosystem restoration and shoreline protection projects  Under the authorizations  the Corps partners with local sponsors to conduct the project  A project authorization in WRDA is a necessary first step towards securing funding in an appropriations bill at a later date
