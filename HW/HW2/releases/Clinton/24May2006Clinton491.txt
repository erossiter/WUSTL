May 24  2006 Statement of Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton on  Inaugural Meeting of the National Surface Transportation Policy and Revenue Study Commission                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton issued the following statement on the launch today of a new commission created under an amendment she authored as a member of the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee             After a four month delay  I am grateful that the National Surface Transportation Policy and Revenue Study Commission convened its inaugural meeting today to discuss the future of our nation  s transportation policy and revenue needs   I authored the amendment that created this Commission within the surface transportation reauthorization bill that passed Congress last August   The charge to this Commission is to conduct a thorough  independent review of our nation  s transportation needs that is free of partisan and political influence to ensure that an expert report is presented to Congress on sound  policy based recommendations   The Commission must address several key issues related to our nation  s transportation policy and finances at a time when we are experiencing record high gas prices and increasing traffic on our road  bridge  rail  mass transit  and other transportation systems   I commend the Commissioners for gathering today and for their commitment to present a report to Congress next year   New York  s future transportation needs will depend upon the integrity and thoroughness of this Commission  s work and I look forward to reviewing their recommendations              Public Law 109 59  the Safe  Accountable  Flexible  Efficient Transportation Equity Act  A Legacy for Users  SAFETEALU   was signed into law by President Bush on August 10  2005  and contains the provision creating the National Surface Transportation Policy and Revenue Study Commission that Senator Clinton authored as a member of the Environment and Public Works Committee   This Commission will determine the future transportation needs of New York and the nation   Senator Clinton worked with former NYSDOT Commissioner  and current Federal Railroad Administrator  Joe Boardman to implement this idea   The objective of this Commission is to establish a comprehensive evaluation of the future of transportation and its financing   The Commission will provide a roadmap about how to better allocate transportation funding resources based on future needs
