October 7  2005 Senator Clinton  Congressman Boehlert Join Local Community Leaders To Celebrate Securing  2 Million In Federal Funding For York Street Improvement                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Auburn  NY   Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton and Congressman Sherwood Boehlert today joined with Mayor Tim Lattimore and local community and business leaders to celebrate the new  2 million in federal dollars that will now be available to upgrade York Street under the   Safe  Accountable  Flexible  and Efficient Transportation Equity Act  A Legacy for Users     SAFETEA LU         Senator Clinton and Congressman Boehlert  who served as members of the Conference Committee on the bill  also helped secure an additional  10 billion in federal highway funding for the State of New York that will be available over the six years of the bill and over  6 billion for the state  s transit needs          This funding is a testament to the commitment and perseverance of all those in the local community who have worked so hard to upgrade York Street and to make the necessary improvements to the City  s transportation infrastructure  With this allocation for York Street and the  10 billion in additional funding that will be available to New York over the six years of the bill  we are making a tremendous investment in Auburn and the entire Finger Lakes region     Senator Clinton said    I am proud that our efforts have resulted in an increase in New York s share of highway and transportation funds and I look forward to seeing the positive impact this funding will have on the local Finger Lakes economy           The proposed improvements to York Street will include the addition of a turn lane  an improved water system  storm drainage and curbing which will make travel more efficient and reduce congestion  while also opening up a large section of commercial and residential land along the route for development        Working with their colleagues in Congress  Senator Clinton and Congressman Boehlert were also able to secure a substantial increase of approximately  3 billion in additional funding for New York State highways and transit   a total of over  16 5 billion in total federal spending for New York   Specifically  the legislation included  10 07 billion in highway funds and  6 5 billion for New York State  s transit systems  a 19  increase in annual average highway spending and a 30  increase in public transit funding compared to the previous six year transportation authorization bill
