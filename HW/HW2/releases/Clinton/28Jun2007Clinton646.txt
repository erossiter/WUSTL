June 28  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Approves  78 8 Million for West Valley Demonstration Project                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Senators Worked Closely with Committee to Include Funding in Spending Bill   West Valley Demonstration Project Will Receive  24 5 Million Above the President s Request    Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the full Senate Appropriations Committee has approved the Energy and Water Development FY08 Appropriations Bill which includes  78 895 million for the West Valley Demonstration Project  The bill will now proceed to the Senate Floor as the next step in the appropriations process   Schumer and Clinton worked closely with members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this years spending bill    This is great news for Western New York  said Schumer   The funding for the West Valley Project will provide the resources necessary to accelerate the clean up at this toxic site   This project has created many jobs for the region and represents an effort to improve the quality of life for all residents in the area   I am pleased that we have restored the administrations cuts and will continue to fight for funding until the DOE completes its clean up    I am pleased that we were able to restore the shortsighted cuts to West Valley that were included in the Presidents budget  The Presidents budget cuts would not only have jeopardized local jobs but would have hindered the clean up work efforts at the site    I will continue to press the Administration and DOE to fulfill their responsibilities to fully remediate this site for the people of western New York  Senator Clinton said    West Valley Demonstration Project will use this grant to achieve several purposes   The West Valley Demonstration Project is a unique operation within the Department of Energy  It came into being through the West Valley Demonstration Project Act of 1980   The Act requires that the Department is responsible for solidifying the high level waste  disposing of waste created by the solidification  and decommissioning the facilities used in the process  The land and facilities are not owned by the Department  Rather  the project premises are the property of the New York State Energy Research and Development Authority  NYSERDA  and represents only 200 acres of the larger Western New York Service Center  which is approximately 3 300 acres  also owned by NYSERDA  After DOE s responsibilities under the Act are complete  the Act requires that the premises be returned to New York State  Until that time  the Act requires New York State to pay 10 percent of the Project costs  and the federal government pays the remaining 90 percent    Now that the bills have been approved by the full Senate Appropriations Committee  they will be sent to the Senate floor  Following approval by the Senate  the bills will move towards Conference with the House and then to the President for signature
