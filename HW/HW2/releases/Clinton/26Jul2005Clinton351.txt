July 26  2005 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Backs Over  56 8 Million for Fort Drum                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Backs Over  56 8 Million for Fort Drum Senators Urged Senate Appropriations Committee To Include Funding In Spending Bill For New Airfield Vehicle Support Facility                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee approved over  56 8 million for military construction projects at Fort Drum            Ft  Drum s 10th Mountain Division has been at the front line in our nation s war on terror around the world  and the men and women stationed at Ft  Drum make a vital contribution to the community surrounding the base   said Senator Schumer     We must do everything we can to support Fort Drum and ensure that our troops have the most up to date facilities available when they are called to duty   This new project will improve their ability to operate in almost any weather condition  cement Ft  Drum at the forefront of defense for New York and the nation             Now more than ever  I am pleased that we can make meaningful investments in Fort Drum     Senator Clinton said    With so many of its troops serving in critical missions in both Iraq and Afghanistan  Fort Drum continues to play a critical role in supporting our national defense   I can  t say enough about how important Fort Drum  s work is to our troops overseas  our national security and our local economy  This funding is great news once again for the installation and for the community           The federal funding is awarded to Fort Drum for their Airfield Vehicle Support Facility   As the Aerial Port of embarkation for the 10th Mountain Division  Ft Drum must maintain a perpetual operational capability   This necessitates that all snow removal and airfield ground equipment be available year round   There are currently no dedicated parking facilities for snow removal and airfield ground equipment   The equipment is thus stored in the deployment muster area  where it must be relocated during muster   Additionally  and significantly  the extreme below freezing periods produce an increase in maintenance and repair costs for this valuable equipment        Today  the Appropriations Committee included  9 7 million for this project in the Military Construction spending bill  as well as  38 5 million for a Barracks Complex   1 85 million for an Ammunition Supply Point Pallet Processing Facility and  6 8 million for a Physical Fitness Facility  The bill was passed by the Appropriations Committee today and now heads to the floor for a full vote before the Senate  After final passage a conference committee will reconcile the differences between the House and Senate versions of the Military Construction Appropriations Bill before sending it to the President to become law        The FY 2006 Military Construction Appropriations Bill was approved by the Appropriations Committee late last week and now heads to the Senate floor  The bill must be approved by the full Senate before being sent to a House and Senate Conference Committee  then eventually being signed into law   Schumer and Clinton urged members of the Appropriations Committee to add funding for a new Airfield Vehicle Support Facility in this year  s spending bill
