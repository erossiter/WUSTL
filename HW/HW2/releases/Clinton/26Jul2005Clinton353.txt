July 26  2005 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Backs  19 9 Million in Military Construction Funds to Benefit Rochester                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Schumer  Clinton  Military Construction Projects are Vitally Important for Rochester  the New York State Economy and National Security                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved the FY 2006 Military Construction Appropriations Bill  which includes  19 9 million for the construction of an Army National Guard Army Aviation Support Facility          This is great news for the Army Aviation Support Facility in Rochester     Senator Schumer said     We must do everything we can to support our troops and ensure that they have the most up to date facilities and equipment available   This expansion project will accommodate more helicopters for missions and training             Now more than ever  I am pleased that we can make this meaningful investment in Rochester  s Army Aviation Support Facility     Senator Clinton said    This facility  as with all of New York  s military personnel and installations  is playing a critical role in supporting both our national defense and our homeland security   I can  t say enough about how important their work is to our troops  our national security and our local economy  This funding is great news for the facility and for the community           The funds will be used to expand the existing Army Aviation Support Facility  AASF  to accommodate the assignment of 6 CH 47 and 6 UH 1 helicopters   The addition to the AASF will serve the peacetime missions of the assigned units and permit all personnel to improve the units readiness posture        The FY 2006 Military Construction Appropriations Bill was approved by the Appropriations Committee late last week and now heads to the Senate floor  The bill must be approved by the full Senate before being sent to a House and Senate Conference Committee  then eventually being signed into law   Schumer and Clinton urged members of the Appropriations Committee to keep funding for the project in this year  s spending bill
