July 21  2005 Graham Clinton TRICARE Expansion Passes Senate                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Amendment Provides Access to Healthcare Benefits for Guard and Reserve Members                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Washington  DC    Senators Lindsey Graham  SC  and Hillary Rodham Clinton  NY  today hailed unanimous Senate passage of their measure to expand access to healthcare benefits for members of the National Guard and Reserve            The bipartisan initiative  which has gained widespread support in the Senate  cuts across party and ideological lines   The Chairman of the Armed Services Committee  John Warner  also expressed strong support for the legislation on the floor of the Senate               The expansion  which passed as an amendment to the Department of Defense Authorization bill  extends TRICARE to all drilling members of the Guard and Reserve members regardless of whether they are on active duty or not   Reservists would be required to pay modest monthly premiums               This is an important step towards easing the burden on members of the National Guard and Reserve and their families     Senator Clinton said    I am proud that the Senate today joined me in honoring the service our National Guard and Reserve members proudly give our nation by giving them and their families the healthcare benefits they need and deserve                Senators Graham and Clinton have worked diligently to expand access to TRICARE benefits for Guard and Reserve members and their families   In October 2004  Senators Graham and Clinton co sponsored legislation that was part of the approved Department of Defense Authorization bill  which provided an extension of one additional year of TRICARE eligibility to Guard and Reserve members for every 90 days the Reservist or Guardsman serves               This expanded eligibility program went into effect in April 2005   The amendment passed today further expands eligibility by lifting the 90 day cap and allowing any drilling reservists to buy into TRICARE at any time                Guardsmen and Reservists are the only part time federal employees who are currently ineligible for federal government full time health care benefits     said Graham  a Colonel in the U S  Air Force Reserves     We  re going to continue pushing for a better benefit package for our citizen soldiers   The National Guard and Reserves are playing a vital role in Iraqi and Afghanistan and are an integral part of the war on terrorism   Our proposal will also help in our lagging recruiting and retention efforts                The Graham Clinton TRICARE efforts have gained support from many groups including the National Guard Association of the United States  the Reserve Officers Association  and the National Governors Association             The legislation now goes to a House Senate conference for consideration
