August 28  2007 Senator Clinton Welcomes FEMA Grant of  1 170 062 to Help Repair Close Hollow Road                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Washington  DC  Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today welcomed the announcement by the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  to award the State of New York Public Assistance  PA  funds totaling  1 170 062 84 to aid in covering the cost of repairs to Close Hollow Road which was severely damaged by flooding in June 2006      This is good news for local residents who depend on Close Hollow Road being open and drivable  This funding from FEMA is essential in helping the local community repair and rebuild without bearing the heavy financial burden alone    During the incident period  flooding on Close Hollow Road caused heavy damage to 0 8 miles of the road and its supporting embankments  The road was completely destroyed     FEMA provides the funding for these projects directly to New York State  who will then ensure that eligible sub grantees receive these awards  FEMA funding represents 75 percent reimbursement of the cost of approved public assistance projects  New York State pays the other 25 percent and manages the PA program
