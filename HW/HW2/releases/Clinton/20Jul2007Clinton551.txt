July 20  2007 Senate Passes Measures to Increase Student Aid  Help Student Borrowers and Non Traditional Students                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Washington  DC   Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced Senate passage of the Higher Education Access Reconciliation Act  which will address the need for increased college access for low income and non traditional students   The measure includes two provisions offered by Senator Clinton to cap monthly loan payments at 15 percent of the borrowers discretionary income and allow working students to keep more of their income without losing crucial student aid      This is welcome news for millions of students in New York State and across the country  said Senator Clinton   We have the best higher education system in the world  but costs and the size of loans and loan payments have spiraled ever upward in the past 20 years   We must do all that we can to ease this growing burden and keep the doors of college open to all who want to further their education   The measures passed today will help remove the economic handcuffs our students are placed in by addressing the problems in the student loan industry and increasing grant aid to students in need     The Higher Education Access Reconciliation Act will produce  17 3 billion in additional benefits for students  including critical help for low income New York students   The legislation passed today will increase Pell grants to  5100 immediately and grow to  5400 by 2011  providing  200 million in increased grant aid to New York students next year and an additional  1 7 billion over the next five years   The legislation also raises the family income level at which a student is automatically eligible for the maximum Pell grant from  20 000 to  30 000   In addition  this bill creates a new loan forgiveness plan for public service employees  simplifies the financial aid application process and extends loan deferment for active duty service members    Senator Clinton has long been a champion of higher education and supporting college students   Two of the provisions from her Student Borrower Bill of Rights were enacted into law during the 109th Congress through the Emergency Supplemental Appropriations Act for Defense 2006   Two of the provisions from her previously introduced Nontraditional Student Success Act were also enacted into law through the Deficit Reduction Act of 2005
