February 14  2007 Senator Clinton Announces Key Senate Committee Approval of Legislation Critical to Improving Mental Health Treatment                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Legislation Co sponsored by Senator Clinton Would Benefit Over Seven Million New Yorkers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Washington  DC   Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton announced today that the Health  Education  Labor and Pensions  HELP  Committee has approved the Mental Health Parity Act of 2007  S  558   The bill  of which Senator Clinton is a co sponsor  improves mental health treatment and builds on the mental health parity provided to federal employees almost a decade ago            We made important progress during the 1990 s in treating mental illness on par with other biologically based disorders but we still have work to do to make sure that those with mental illness receive the care they need   The Mental Health Parity Act is another critical step towards improving mental health treatment for over seven million New Yorkers and countless numbers of Americans  who by virtue of their condition  have struggled to be treated fairly   I will work with my colleagues to ensure full Senate passage   said Senator Clinton          Based on the Surgeon General s groundbreaking 1999 report  which concluded that mental illnesses are largely biologically based disorders like many other medical disorders  the Clinton Administration established full parity for mental health and substance abuse benefits under the Federal Employee Health Benefits Plan  FEHBP   The Mental Health Parity Act of 2007 requires mental health parity that is equal to that provided through the executive order for health insurance plans that include mental health benefits  The bill will ensure that over seven million New Yorkers will no longer have mental health coverage that is subject to arbitrary treatment limits and financial restrictions  including approximately 3 1 million New Yorkers who currently have state regulated insurance plans through large employers and an estimated 4 2 million New Yorkers who are covered by Employee Retirement Income Security Act  ERISA  plans          According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention  CDC   37 percent of New York adults report poor mental health  which is higher than the national average   The most recent data available shows that over 170 000 New Yorkers are served by programs offered by the New York State Office of Mental Health with diagnoses ranging from schizophrenia to anxiety disorder to substance related disorders           Senator Clinton has been a champion of mental health parity since the 1990s when full parity was established through Executive Order by the Clinton Administration  Last year  Senator Clinton joined with Senator Susan M  Collins to include significant portions of the Positive Aging Act into the reauthorization of the Older Americans Act  which allows for competitive grants for the development of systems for the delivery of mental health screening and treatment services for older individuals who lack access to these services  Also last year   3 million was appropriated for community based programs that provide mental health and readjustment assistance to members of the National Guard and Reserve and their families on their return from deployment through legislation that Senator Clinton introduced
