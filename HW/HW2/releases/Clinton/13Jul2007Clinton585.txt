July 13  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Approves  2 Million for Expansion of Nassau County Hub                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Senators Worked Closely with Committee to Include Funding in Spending Bill   Funding is Vital to Developing Region s Economic and Cultural Center  Easing Traffic Congestion  and Offering Improved Transportation Options   Bill Will Now Be Sent to the Senate Floor                                                                                                                          Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the full Senate Appropriations Committee has approved the Transportation  Housing and Urban Development  and Related Agencies  TTHUD  FY08 Appropriations Bill  which includes  2 million for the expansion of the Nassau County HUB   The bill will now proceed to the Senate Floor as the next step in the appropriations process   Schumer and Clinton worked closely with members of the Appropriations Committee to include funding for the project in this years spending bill  This funding is a win win for all Long Islanders  said Senator Schumer  Nassau Countys Hub has tremendous potential to spur regional economic growth  become a first class venue for recreational and cultural opportunities  and to provide transportation alternatives  This funding is a critical step towards realizing the important goal of modernizing Nassaus centre while maintaining the quality of life that makes Long Island a great place to live  This is wonderful news for Nassau County  said Senator Clinton  The Nassau Hub project is a key element in the County s plans to create a more sustainable and effective transportation system and to promote economic development  critical goals for so many Long Island residents  This development will not only help to rejuvenate the area  it will be a win for the environment and represents a significant investment in our combined efforts to sustain and enhance Long Islands future and quality of life  The  2 million in federal funds will go to the Nassau Hub Nassau Centre to integrate state of the art public transportation systems with commercial  recreational  and residential development in order to stimulate regional economic expansion  alleviate local transit congestion and improve air quality and health related issues  The Hub  located in central Nassau County  is home to the highest density of businesses and commercial establishments in the County and includes the Nassau Coliseum  Flanked by the LIRRs Main line and Hempstead Turnpike  the Hub is currently undergoing plans for a future expansion that includes a light rail system  new roads and park facilities  and pedestrian and bicycle ways  Under the plan  the Hubs commercial  recreational and industrial areas will also be interconnected  Now that the bill has been approved by the full Senate Appropriations Committee  it will be sent to the Senate floor  Following approval by the Senate  the bill will move towards Conference with the House and then to the President for signature
