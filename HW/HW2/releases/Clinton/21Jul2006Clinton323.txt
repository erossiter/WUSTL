July 21  2006 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Approval of  23 7 Million in Military Construction Funding for the Hudson Valley                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Funds included in Fiscal Year 2007 Military Construction Appropriations Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Washington  DC   Senators Charles Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton welcomed approval today by the Senate Appropriations Committee of the Fiscal Year 2007 Military Construction Appropriations Bill  which includes  23 7 million that will benefit the Hudson Valley  including  1 8 million in Military Construction funding from the President  s Budget for Stewart  s USMCR Aviation Support Equipment Shop and  21 9 million in Military Construction funding from the President  s Budget for Camp Smith Readiness Center          The bill will now proceed to the Senate floor for consideration   Following consideration and passage on the Senate floor  the bill will head into a Conference Committee where the House and Senate will resolve the differences between the two bills  Following conference  the bill will again be sent to the House and Senate for final approval  after which it will be sent to the President for his signature           This is great news for the Hudson Valley   said Schumer   Additional funds will not only boost the area economy  but also put the Hudson Valley and New York State at the forefront of defense technology  As the men and women of our military bravely serve our country all over the world  the funding for these Hudson Valley military construction projects will vastly improve national security technology and will give our armed forces an even greater edge             As our men and women in uniform continue to fight bravely overseas  we must ensure that they have the necessary support and services here at home  This funding will help make sure that we are doing everything we can to ensure their readiness for combat and we are also providing the necessary support structures for them when they are here at home     Senator Clinton said          Funding in the FY 2007 Military Construction Appropriations Bill includes          21 9 million in Military Construction funding from the President  s Budget for Camp Smith Readiness Center    The Military Construction Appropriations bill includes  21 9 million to fund a consolidated readiness center to support units currently stationed at the Camp Smith Training Site  the Peekskill Armory  and Valhalla Armory   The readiness center will provide the necessary administrative  training  and storage areas for multiple units consisting of over 600 personnel   It will replace facilities constructed between 1918 and 1986   Planned facilities will be state of the art and improve readiness and morale of the units benefiting from the new construction        1 834 million in Military Construction funding from the President  s Budget for Stewart    USMCR Aviation Support Equipment Shop    The Military Construction Appropriations bill includes  1 834 million to fund construction of a facility needed to support requirements of the 4th Marine Aircraft Wing  s Rotary Wing Combined Contingency Support Package and the C 130 Peculiar Contingency Support Package   Current facilities are substandard and require much of the critical equipment to be stored outdoors   This facility will enhance the Marine Aviation Logistics Squadron  s ability to perform its mission and improve the conditions and readiness of their equipment
