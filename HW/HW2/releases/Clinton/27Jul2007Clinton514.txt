July 27  2007 Schumer  Clinton   10 Million for Clean Water Projects in Suffolk County Included in Final Water Resources Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Funding for Key Projects Included in Water Resources Development Act Approved by House Senate Conferees Today                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Washington  DC  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that a House Senate Conference Committee has agreed on a final version of the Water Resources Development Act  WRDA  which includes  10 million for projects to upgrade sewer and other wastewater facilities in Suffolk County    The Water Resources Development Act  which was approved by the House Senate Conferees today  authorizes Corps of Engineers civil works projects  including navigation  ecosystem restoration and shoreline protection projects  Under the authorizations  the Corps partners with local sponsors to conduct the project  A project authorization in WRDA is a necessary first step towards securing funding in an appropriations bill at a later date  The bill now goes to the full House and Senate for final approval before heading to the President for his signature     The WRDA bill authorizes  5 million for projects in the Village of Patchogue in Suffolk County  and an additional  5 million for projects in the Town of Babylon in Suffolk County    This is great news for Suffolk County residents and the local economy  said Senator Schumer  The water system is critical to the livelihood of the community and the health of local residents  Clean drinking water and an efficient sewage system are basic needs and this funding will help the community meet those needs  improving the quality of life for everyone in the area    I am pleased that this bill will help to pay for much needed upgrades to wastewater systems  The federal government has a responsibility to partner with local governments to make sure that New Yorkers have clean water  said Senator Clinton
