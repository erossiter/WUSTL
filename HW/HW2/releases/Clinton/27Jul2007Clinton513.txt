July 27  2007 Schumer  Clinton   10 Million for North Hempstead Aad Glen Cove Waterfront Revitalization Projects Included in Final Water Resources Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Funding for Key Projects Included in Water Resources Development Act Approved by House Senate Conferees Today                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Washington  DC  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that a House Senate Conference Committee has agreed on a final version of the Water Resources Development Act  WRDA  which includes  10 million for the Army Corps of Engineers to participate in the ecosystem restoration  navigation  flood damage reduction  and recreation components of the North Hempstead and Glen Cove Waterfront Revitalization Project  North Hempstead and Glen Cove  New York    The Water Resources Development Act  which was approved by the House Senate Conferees today  authorizes Corps of Engineers civil works projects  including navigation  ecosystem restoration and shoreline protection projects  Under the authorizations  the Corps partners with local sponsors to conduct the project  A project authorization in WRDA is a necessary first step towards securing funding in an appropriations bill at a later date  The bill now goes to the full House and Senate for final approval before heading to the President for his signature     This funding is necessary for the continued health of the North Hempstead and Glen Cove waterfronts  and for the growth of the area economies  Schumer said  With recent storm damage taking its toll on beachfronts and navigation channels  this money will allow the federal government to better respond to  prevent and reverse damage to North Hempstead and Glen Coves shorelines     I am pleased that we were able to authorize  10 million in funding for North Hempstead and Glen Cove  said Senator Clinton   This funding will enable the federal government to partner with North Hempstead and Glen Cove to restore and revitalize the waterfront
