August 3  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Nearly  95 000 for the Schenectady County Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Federal Money Will Be Used for Improvements to the Airport Drainage System                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the U S  Department of Transportation  DOT  has awarded  94 900 in Federal Aviation Administration  FAA  funds to the County of Schenectady for the Schenectady County Airport   Federal funds will be used to improve airport drainage systems    This is great news for Schenectady  said Schumer  The Schenectady County Airport is an important general aviation facility in this region    It is critical that we continue to provide the funds necessary to keep the airport safe and efficient   With so many local aviators  tourists and businesspeople using the airport as a base of operation  it remains a key component of economic growth in the region     This is welcome news for Schenectady County  said Senator Clinton   Schenectady County Airport serves as a vital hub for commercial air service in the Capital Region and is home to the 109th Airlift Wing of the Air National Guard   Ensuring continued operation and maintenance of this airport is a wise use of these funds  and Ill continue to fight for federal support for regional airports throughout New York    The Schenectady County Airport will use the federal funds for drainage improvements   The existing system is in poor condition
