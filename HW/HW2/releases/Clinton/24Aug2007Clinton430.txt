August 24  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Over  1 7 Million Dollars for Griffiss Airfield                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Federal Money Will Be Used to Improve Snow Removal Equipment Building and Rehabilitate Apron                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the U S  Department of Transportation  DOT  has awarded  1 710 000 in Federal Aviation Administration  FAA  funds to the Griffiss Airfield in the County of Oneida   Federal funds will be used to improve the airports snow removal equipment building and rehabilitate the apron    This is great news for Oneida County  said Schumer  The Griffiss Airfield is an important general aviation facility in this region    It is critical that we continue to provide the funds necessary to keep the airport safe and efficient   With so many local aviators  tourists and businesspeople using the airport as a base of operation  it remains a key component of economic growth in the region     I am pleased that the DOT is continuing to provide funding for improvements at Griffiss  said Senator Clinton  Griffiss continues to offer new development opportunities for tourism  aviation  and businesses alike  This funding is an investment in the future  not only of Griffiss Airfield but also of the Mohawk Valley  and I will continue to fight for federal resources so that Griffiss Airfield remains a vital partner in the growth of the region    Federal funds will be used to improve the snow removal equipment building and rehabilitate the apron  These projects will better position the airport to serve the surrounding business community more effectively  efficiently and safely
