September 25  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Over  900 000 Coming to New York City to Improve Transportation Options for Elderly and Disabled                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Funds Will Be Used to Buy Specialized Vans and Buses to Increase Transportation Options for the Elderly and Disabled Throughout the Five Boroughs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the U S  Department of Transportation  DOT  has released  950 028 in Federal Transit Administration  FTA  funds from FY2005 and FY2006 to the New York City Department of Transportation to buy specialized vans and buses for the elderly and disabled  While New York boasts one of the best public transportation infrastructures in the world  many elderly and disabled New Yorkers with limited mobility have difficulties accessing the system  said Schumer  This funding will increase options for these New Yorkers  allowing easier access to doctors appointments  grocery shopping  social centers  and other important activities that would otherwise be outside their reach  This funding is great news for New York s seniors and those with restricted mobility who aren t able to fully utilize our world class mass transit system  In addition to providing a valuable service for those who desperately need it  these specialized vehicles will give people greater access to services that were previously out of their grasp  said Senator Clinton  In total  four separate grants totaling  950 028 will be used to purchase 15 vans and 3 buses to provide free  specialized transportation services for disadvantaged people and elderly persons who are unable to transport themselves or purchase transportation  public or private  to obtain access to important  life sustaining activities throughout the five boroughs
