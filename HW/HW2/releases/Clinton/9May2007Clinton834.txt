May 9  2007 Senator Clinton Welcomes Announcement of More than  8 6 Million in U S  House Bill for Areas Hit by Recent Flooding                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Will Push to Secure Funds in the Senate                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Washington  DC   Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today praised the efforts of Congresswoman Nita Lowey who announced that  8 665 000 is included in the House Emergency Supplemental Appropriations bill to fund flood mitigation projects in parts of New York affected by the recent Nor easter   The funding will be used by the Army Corps of Engineers to address and curb future flooding in the Hudson Valley          Senator Clinton said she would work with the Senate Appropriations Committee to secure desperately needed funds for the flooding in New York            These floods have been heartbreaking for so many  As we continue to help individuals  families and businesses we must also look to the future and what we can do to prevent this from happening again  Flood mitigation is one of the most critical components in minimizing damage and long term problems in the face of a disaster like this  We want to do what we can to help local our communities prepare for the future  I will be working with Congresswoman Lowey and my colleagues in the Senate to get this funding approved so that we do not have to face this kind of devastation again   Senator Clinton said
