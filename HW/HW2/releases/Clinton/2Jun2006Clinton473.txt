June 2  2006 Schumer  Clinton Announce Over  4 Million in Grants for Macarthur Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Washington  DC    Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced a total of   4 263 931 in grants for the Long Island MacArthur Airport in Islip  NY from the U S  Department of Transportation  DOT             New Yorkers are well versed in the need for secure and efficient travel  This grant gives MacArthur Airport the necessary resources to upgrade security measures and prepare for the winter snow blizzards that often slow down our air travel  Continuing to improve the MacArthur Airport is key for both business and economic development and this grant will be well used     said Schumer            This is a great investment in MacArthur Airport and Long Island  Maintaining a local airport that is effective and efficient is important for both the safety of passengers and the ability of our airports to sustain continued air traffic  which is good for business and economic development  Keeping passengers safe and moving smoothly means more flights arriving and leaving on time and that  s good news for business travelers  commuters and tourists alike  This is especially important in poor weather conditions  and part of this funding will help the airport transport travelers during every season of the year     Senator Clinton said          Of the funding announced today   484 500 will allow the MacArthur Airport to replace its snow removal equipment   501 931 will provide for security enhancements and  3 277 500 will be utilized to construct an additional partial parallel taxiway which will improve aircraft security by minimizing runway incursions and improve fuel savings by reducing taxiway time and the length of routes
