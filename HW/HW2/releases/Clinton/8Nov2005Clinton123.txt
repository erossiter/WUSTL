November 8  2005 Clinton  Key Congressional Committee Approves Over  1 Million for Fire Island to Montauk Point Reformulation Study                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Announcement follows Clinton efforts to have the funds restored for the second year in a row                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the Joint House Senate Energy and Water Appropriations Conference Committee has approved  1 075 million under the FY06 Energy and Water Appropriations bill for the Fire Island to Montauk Point Reformulation Study          The bill was passed by the Joint House Senate Conference Committee late yesterday and now heads to the Senate and House for a vote before being sent to the President for his signature            It is outrageous that we have to continue to fight for funds for a study that is almost completed after decades of work and is so vital to the future of Long Island     Senator Clinton said    I was extremely disappointed that the funds for the Fire Island to Montauk Point Reformulation Study were eliminated from the President  s Budget earlier this year  so I am thrilled that our hard work has paid off and we have been able to restore these funds in the Congress               This critical study must be completed  The Fire Island to Montauk Point Reformulation plan will provide the roadmap for what we must do in the future to ensure Long Island s beaches and communities remain strong and healthy and I am pleased that we have won the support of both the House and the Senate to get the funding this project deserves     Senator Clinton said           Senator Clinton has been working for years now to ensure that this essential study is funded so that decades worth of work does not go to waste and the restoration project can proceed           The study was zeroed out in the Administration s FY 2005 budget  even though it is almost completed after decades of federal  state and local participation  At the time  Senator Clinton fought to have the funds restored   But again this year  the funding was not included in the President s Budget for Fiscal Year 2006 so Senator Clinton worked with the Army Corps of Engineers and her Senate colleagues during the Appropriations process to ensure that the funding is continued
