September 25  2007 Schumer  Clinton  Authorization of  10 Million for Clean Water Projects on Long Island Sent to President for Final Approval                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Final Conference Report on Federal Funding Authorizations for Water Resources Development Act Approved by Senate Last Night    Now Sent to President for Signing                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the Senate has approved the final conference report on Water Resources Development Act  WRDA  which authorizes  10 million for projects to upgrade sewer and other wastewater facilities in the Village of Patchogue and the Town of Babylon in Suffolk County  The bill passed House Senate conference in July and now will be sent to the President for signing    This is great news for Long Island  said Senator Schumer   A first rate water and sewer system is critical to the livelihood of the community and the health of local residents   Clean drinking water and an efficient sewage system are basic needs and this funding will help the community meet those needs  reduce pressure on local ratepayers and improve the quality of life for everyone in the area    Senate passage of this bill is great news  and it is the essential next step in ensuring that federal funds will be provided to help pay for the upgrades to Long Islands wastewater systems  said Senator Clinton  It is important that the federal government can partner in this way with local officials  not only with the expertise but with the financial assistance to make sure that everyone has clean water    The WRDA bill authorizes  5 million for The Village of Patchogue in Suffolk County and  5 million for the Town of Babylon in Suffolk County       The Water Resources Development Act authorizes Corps of Engineers civil works projects  including navigation  ecosystem restoration and shoreline protection projects  Under the authorizations  the Corps partners with local sponsors to conduct the project  A project authorization in WRDA is a necessary first step towards securing funding in an appropriations bill at a later date
