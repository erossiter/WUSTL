June 28  2007 Schumer  Clinton Announce Key Senate Panel Approves  3 Million for Jones Inlet Dredging                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Senators Worked Closely with Committee to Include Funding in Spending Bill   Funding Will Go Toward Dredging the Inlet  Allowing for Easier Navigation and Better Absorption of Storm Surge   Bill Will Now be Sent to the Senate Floor                                                                                                                                                         Washington  DC   U S  Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Rodham Clinton today announced that the full Senate Appropriations Committee approved  3 million for dredging and maintenance at Jones Inlet  The funds will go toward dredging the inlet under the FY08 Energy and Water Development Appropriations Bill  The bill will now proceed to the Senate Floor for approval    This is great  long awaited news for Long Island and the residents of the Town of Hempstead  Schumer said today  Jones Inlet has been overlooked for too long  leaving boaters  fishermen  and homes in harms way  With these funds  the residents  fishermen and recreational boaters who use Jones Inlet will have peace of mind to enjoy the waterway  and first responders will have an easier time getting to emergencies  I will continue to fight for these necessary funds until the job is done    There has been a very real need to address the problems at Jones Inlet for over a year now and this funding will finally allow the Army Corps to start work  We have to address the hazardous conditions that exist for commercial and recreational boaters and for the surrounding communities  The time to address these concerns is now  said Senator Clinton  We have repeatedly called upon the Army Corps of Engineers to address this important and immediate issue and now they will finally have the funds to begin work  I will keep a close eye on these funds to ensure that they remain in the final version of the bill so that the Army Corps can address the Inlets safety risks and protect the Town of Hempsteads beaches as soon as possible    Though the Army Corps recommends dredging Jones Inlet every four to five years  the inlet hasnt been dredged for twelve years   Over that time  the inlet has filled with sand  making both the channel perilously shallow and water conditions unpredictable for mariners  A primary concern is also for the safety of thousands of recreational boaters who are either forced to use other water channels or navigate outside Jones Inlets designated ones  since the water depth has shifted making the official lanes unsafe  According to a deputy commissioner at the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation  the inlet currently poses a grave threat to navigation and safety  The inlet also protects the Town of Hempsteads precious beaches by absorbing the brunt of storms  but has been unable to adequately do so with its diminished depth  The  3 million in federal funding will go toward dredging the inlet to allow commercial fishermen  recreational boaters  and emergency responders to navigate the inlet with more ease  while giving home owners an additional barrier against storm related surge  Now that the bill has been approved by the full Senate Appropriations Committee  it will be sent to the Senate floor  Following approval by the Senate  the bill will move towards Conference with the House and then to the President for signature
