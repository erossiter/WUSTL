October 3  2005 Senator Clinton Holds Forum on U S  Border Passport Requirement                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Messena  NY   Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today joined with the St  Lawrence Chamber of Commerce  local community and business leaders and elected officials at a forum to discuss the impact on northern border communities of the U S  Department of Homeland Security  s proposed Western Hemisphere Travel Initiative   Senator Clinton has repeatedly expressed her serious concerns about the local economic impact of the initiative  which would require a passport to cross the border between the United States and Canada          National security  and in particular  security at our borders  must continue to be paramount  I have made security at New York  s northern border a top priority since joining the Senate  However  it is crucial that any new requirement be sensitive to the potentially serious impact on tourism and the regional economy  There is tremendous concern among New Yorkers that this passport requirement would devastate the tourism and retail industry on both sides of the Canadian American border     Senator Clinton emphasized         On September 1  2005  the Department of Homeland Security  DHS  announced they would be formally submitting the Western Hemisphere Travel Initiative for public comment  Although the initiative has been altered to come into effect by the end of 2006 and the Department has indicated that other documents issued under various programs designed to speed border crossings will be considered  according to the DHS release announcing the comment period    the passport will be the document of choice for travel within the Western Hemisphere or re entry            Senator Clinton has underscored her deep reservations about the Western Hemisphere Travel Initiative since it was first previewed in April of this year  The Senator has written to both Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice and Homeland Security Secretary Chertoff and met with Secretary Chertoff on this critical issue
