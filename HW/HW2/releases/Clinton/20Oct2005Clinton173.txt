October 20  2005 Senator Clinton Decries Vote by Senate Republicans Against Needed Increase in Home Heating Assistance                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Vows to Continue Fighting for Funding New York  s Low Income Families Need to Meet Record Energy Costs This Winter                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today criticized Senate Republicans for voting against a desperately needed increase in home heating assistance for low income families who will face record home heating costs this winter as a result of Hurricanes Katrina and Rita and vowed to keep fighting to secure the increase in future legislation   Senator Clinton cosponsored an amendment to the Transportation Appropriations bill reintroduced today to provide an additional  3 1 billion in emergency funding for the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP    If approved  the amendment would have provided about an additional  375 million in funding to New York  desperately needed to help meet the huge increase in heating costs that has been forecast by the U S  Department of Energy              I am disappointed that Senate Republicans chose to vote against heating aid for New Yorkers     said Senator Clinton     New York will face the most daunting energy costs they have ever faced this winter  and without help  seniors and low income families will face impossible choices between paying their heating bills or paying for housing  food and medicine   I will keep fighting until we get the increased funding that we need               Last year  an estimated 800 000 New Yorkers received LIHEAP assistance  with benefits ranging from  150 to  400 per household   However  the program has been losing ground to inflation and a significant increase in funding is needed to even keep pace with last year            Making matters worse  the U S  Department of Energy projects that heating costs will be sharply higher for families in New York and across the country this year because of the impact of Hurricanes Katrina and Rita on our energy infrastructure   Statewide  New Yorkers are expected to face additional heating costs of more than  2 billion   For the 52 percent of New York households that use natural gas to heat their homes  the Department of Energy projects that heating costs will rise from  1 029 last winter to  1 353 this winter  rising to as much as  1 518 if the winter turns out to be very cold   For the 33 percent of New York households that use home heating oil  heating costs are projected to rise from  1 237 last winter to  1 609 this winter   A very cold winter could mean an increase to as much as  1 929
