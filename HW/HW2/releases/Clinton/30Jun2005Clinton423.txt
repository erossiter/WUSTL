June 30  2005 Senator Clinton Introduces Kevin s Law to Protect Nation s Food Supply                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Legislation would strengthen meat and poultry safety rules                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton joined today with a bipartisan group of Senators to introduce legislation to protect the nation s food supply and strengthen food safety rules to protect against microbial pathogens in meat and poultry        The Meat and Poultry Pathogen Reduction and Enforcement Act of 2005  also known as  Kevin s Law   spells out the U S  Department of Agriculture s  USDA  authority to enforce its sanitary requirements and requires the USDA to identify potentially harmful pathogens found in meat and poultry and set performance standards to reduce them  Senator Clinton is a sponsor of the legislation   along with Senators Tom Harkin  D IA  and Arlen Specter  R PA  The bill is named after Kevin Kowalcyk of Mount Horeb  Wisconsin who died in 2001 at the age of 2      from E  coli infection  Representatives Anna Eshoo  D CA  and Phil English  R PA  introduced a companion bill in the House of Representatives         It is a tragedy that someone could become seriously ill or die from a food borne pathogen  especially when there is so much we can do to prevent it  Yet every year  millions of people become sick and thousands die   Senator Clinton said   This legislation is an important and necessary step towards increasing the safety of our food supply and helping prevent needless tragedies         In 1996  the USDA adopted new rules to help the Department enforce basic safety and sanitation standards in the production of meat and poultry  These rules  called Pathogen Reduction and Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points  HACCP   are essential to ensuring that microbial pathogens are kept out of meat and poultry sold to consumers in restaurants and on grocery store shelves  Unfortunately  the meat and poultry industry has used lawsuits since then to stop the government from enforcing these important food safety standards  The courts struck down the USDA s ability to enforce pathogen standards for Salmonella and to require meat grinders to stop using low quality  potentially contaminated beef trimmings  These lawsuits threaten the USDA s ability to shut down a plant that repeatedly violates basic sanitation regulations        Kevin s Law gives the USDA the authority to enforce existing protections against pathogens such as Salmonella and E  coli  It requires the USDA to set standards for food borne pathogens based upon the best available science and technology to reduce contamination  The law also makes clear that the USDA has the authority to enforce basic sanitation requirements in meat and poultry plants         Food contamination is a serious problem in the United States  The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention estimate that food borne diseases cause approximately 76 million illnesses  325 000 hospitalizations  and 5 000 deaths each year
