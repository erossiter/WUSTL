March 3  2006 Schumer  Clinton Announce Over  142 000 For Syracuse Hancock International Airport                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Federal Dollars Will Be Used For a Obstructions Removal Study                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Washington  DC    Senators Charles E  Schumer and Hillary Clinton today announced  142 500 for the Syracuse Hancock International Airport from the Airport Improvement Program  AIP  at the U S  Department of Transportation  Federal Aviation Administration  FAA           This is great news for the Syracuse Airport     said Senator Schumer    This airport welcome travelers to New York each year  and these improvements are important for local businesses and strengthening the local economy   I will continue to fight for federal funds to help this airports remain a thriving center of activity and a hub for economic growth in the region             Syracuse International is integral to the local economy and it is important that we do everything we can to support it   Senator Clinton said    Maintaining our critical airport infrastructure is important for both the safety of passengers and the ability of our airports to sustain continued air traffic  The more flights we have  the better the economic opportunity  so this funding is a very welcome investment    not only in the airport itself but in the region as a whole           At Syracuse Hancock International Airport  this grant will provide federal funding to study removal of obstructions   This study will determine what obstructions exist and assist in developing a plan for remediation
