June 29  2006 Senator Clinton Welcomes Key Senate Panel Approval of  2 5 Million for Fire Island to Montauk Point Study                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today welcomed approval by the Senate Appropriations Committee of the Fiscal Year 2007 Energy and Water Appropriations Bill  which includes  2 5 million for the Fire Island to Montauk Point Reformulation Study            During the last two fiscal years  the Administration failed to include the study in its budget  Senator Clinton fought to restore funding during the appropriations process  and pressed the Administration to change course and include the study in its budget  which it did earlier this year            This funding will bring us one step closer to completing this important study  which will provide a roadmap for restoring and protecting Long Island  s beaches     Senator Clinton said    I will continue to work with my colleagues in Congress and the Army Corps of Engineers to ensure that this study is completed              In 2005  Senator Clinton strongly criticized the Bush Administration for abandoning the Army Corps of Engineers  study of the shoreline from Fire Island to Montauk Point  In private meetings and in a hearing of the Senate Environment and Public Works committee  Senator Clinton urged Corps of Engineers officials to urge Corps officials to put the study back in the budget           Two years ago  when the President  s FY 2005 budget also zeroed out the study  Senator Clinton worked with Congressman Bishop and County Executive Levy to get the Administration to restore funding for the study  After raising the issue in a Senate hearing in March of 2004  Senator Clinton received a commitment from the Corps of Engineers in June to restore funding for fiscal year 2005  and the study was funded at  1 4 million  Last year  the Administration again zeroed out the study  and Senator Clinton worked to restore funding in Congress           The reformulation study is a partnership between the Corps of Engineers and other federal and state agencies to develop a comprehensive  long term plan to protect coastal areas that are prone to flooding  erosion and other storm damage  The plan would replace ad hoc measures that have been used to protect individual properties with a comprehensive management approach that considers the entire coastal system  For example  completion of the plan would enable expedited response to emergencies  such as the winter erosion in the Tiana Beach area of Dune Road in Hampton Bays that occurred last year           The plan covers the 83 miles of ocean and bay shorelines from Fire Island Inlet to Montauk Point  It includes some of Long Island s most densely populated communities  such as Lindenhurst  Bay Shore  Patchogue  and the Mastics  as well as the entire South Fork  It also contains some of the most remarkable environmental communities  providing a unique natural resource for the entire metropolitan region and for the nation  A study completed by the Suffolk County Budget Review Office in May  2003  showed that in Suffolk County alone  south shore tourism and beach use pours more than  250 million annually into the county economy  After years of federal  state and local participation  the study is close to completion
