April 6  2005 Clinton Welcomes Decision to Send Disaster Teams to Assess Flooded Areas                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         FEMA announces teams to begin assessment process                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton welcomed an announcement by the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  that teams would be assessing the damage in over a dozen New York counties impacted by severe storms and flooding   Earlier today Senator Clinton wrote to President Bush urging him to send in teams to assess the damage and expedite the process of declaring a Major Disaster Designation  Damage assessments are a necessary step in the process towards securing a Major Disaster Declaration from the President      According to the announcement today  joint federal  state and local preliminary damage assessments  PDAs  have now begun in order to assess the potential for Individual Assistance  IA  and Public Assistance  PA  in Broome  Chenango  Cortland  Delaware  Orange  Rensselaer  Schenectady  Schoharie  Sullivan  Tioga  and Ulster counties  FEMA also announced that PDA s for Public Assistance would also be conducted in Green  Montgomery  and Otsego Counties        These communities need help and they need it fast  I am glad that FEMA will be conducting the necessary damage assessments so that a Major Disaster Declaration can be made     Senator Clinton said in releasing the letter        A Presidential Major Disaster Declaration would put into motion long term federal recovery programs  some of which are matched by state programs  designed to help disaster victims  businesses and public entities  A Major Disaster Declaration is requested by the Governor  based on the damage assessment and an agreement to commit state funds and resources to the long term recovery  FEMA then evaluates the request and recommends action to the White House based on the disaster  the local community and the state s ability to recover  If declared  funding comes from the President s Disaster Relief Fund  which is managed by FEMA  and disaster aid programs of other participating federal agencies       A copy of the Senator  s letter is attached        April 6  2005       The Honorable George W  Bush  President of the United States  Washington  D C   20500    Dear Mr  President        I write to ask for your full  expeditious consideration of the Governor of New York  s request for a Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  team to conduct a damage assessment and if warranted  for you to make a major disaster declaration for 14 New York counties impacted by storms and massive flooding   Specifically  I urge you to speed FEMA assistance for the counties of Broome  Chenango  Cortland  Delaware  Greene  Montgomery  Orange  Otsego  Rensselaer  Schenectady  Schoharie  Sullivan  Tioga and Ulster  in New York        Excessive rains over the weekend of April 2 3  2005  caused damage and flooding to several parts of New York State   Assistance is needed in the form of debris removal  infrastructure repair  small business loans  and other assistance available from FEMA        Many areas received record or near record levels of flooding resulting in closed roads and caused widespread damage   As hundreds of residents were forced from their homes by the rising flood waters  State emergency personnel were called upon to direct evacuation   I ask that you instruct FEMA to conduct the necessary damage assessments and provide a recommendation to you for a disaster declaration as soon as possible          I thank you for your consideration of this request        Sincerely yours        Hillary Rodham Clinton
