October 18  2005 Senator Clinton Recommends Frank McArdle to National Transportation Commission                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Clinton Made Recommendation to Minority Leader Harry Reid to Appoint McArdle to Commission she Authored as Part of Surface Transportation Reauthorization Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Washington  D C     Minority Leader Harry Reid  at the request of Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton  has appointed Frank McArdle of New York to serve on the National Surface Transportation Policy and Revenue Study Commission  McArdle  who is the Managing Director of the General Contractors Association of New York  has represented the heavy construction industry in New York for the last 22 years   Minority Leader Harry Reid was authorized to appoint two Commissioners to the National Surface Transportation Policy and Revenue Study Commission that Senator Clinton created as part of the Safe Accountable Flexible and Efficient Transportation Equity Act    A Legacy for Users  SAFETEA LU  which funds highway and transit needs across the nation                I am so glad Frank was appointed to the National Surface Transportation Policy and Revenue Study Commission     Senator Clinton said     His knowledge of transportation construction and labor relations will give New York a strong voice on this important Commission which will determine the future needs of our national transportation system   Frank  s experience will serve as a great resource to the commission as it works to make recommendations to Congress and the President about how best to improve transportation in New York and across the nation              I am honored that Senator Clinton has selected me for this very important role on the Commission and I am committed to working towards developing strategies that will provide our country with a strong  reliable method for financing the nation s transportation system  We need a system of bridges  highways and mass transit that is safe and dependable to move people and goods   Our economy depends on this   Frank McArdle said           As Managing Director of the General Contractors Association of New York  Frank has been actively involved in the legislative  regulatory  and labor relations activities of the Association and its 106 active contractor members   In these last 22 years  the Association has actively worked on the issues of transportation financing  competitiveness in bidding  contractor qualification  business integrity  noise and environmental pollution  steel and material pricing  project advocacy and diversity development          The National Surface Transportation Policy and Revenue Study Commission  authored by Senator Clinton  was created as part of Safe Accountable Flexible and Efficient Transportation Equity Act    A Legacy for Users  SAFETEA LU    This Commission will determine the future transportation needs of New York and the nation and will combine the National Transportation System Study and the Commission on Future Revenue Sources to Support the Highway Trust Fund into one single commission  Senator Clinton worked with former NYSDOT Commissioner and current Federal Railroad Administrator Joe Boardman on the concept for the Commission   The objective of combining these two bodies is to provide a comprehensive evaluation of the future of transportation and related financing to produce a roadmap for effective allocation of transportation funding resources   A better allocation will mean funding based on needs  which can only help New York  which contributes to a more sustainable transportation network   The Commission will submit its report to Congress on these and related topics by July 1  2007
