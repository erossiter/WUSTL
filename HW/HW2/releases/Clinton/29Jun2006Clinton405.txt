June 29  2006 Senator Clinton Welcomes Key Senate Panel Approval of  1 Million for Long Island Sound Cleanup                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Washington  DC    Senator Hillary Rodham Clinton today welcomed approval by the Senate Appropriations Committee of the Fiscal Year 2007 Interior Appropriations Bill  which contained  1 million for clean up and restoration of Long Island Sound  This funding comes in addition to  198 000 recently approved by the Senate Agricultural Appropriations Conference Committee under the Fiscal Year 2007 Agriculture Appropriations Bill to implement the Long Island Sound Restoration Act          The Interior Appropriations Bill  which was approved by the Committee today  now proceeds to the Senate floor  Following consideration and passage on the Senate floor  the bill will head into a Conference Committee where the House and Senate will resolve the differences between the two bills            There is no question that we must do everything we can to preserve this essential recreational and commercial resource     said Senator Clinton    The Long Island Sound continues to be one of our region  s most incredible assets  one that is enjoyed by millions every year  I am very pleased that my Senate colleagues approved this funding so we can continue the protection and restoration efforts on the Sound              The Long Island Sound Restoration Act authorized federal assistance for cleaning up the Sound   The funds approved today will be used to enhance projects designed to restore the water quality of the Sound
