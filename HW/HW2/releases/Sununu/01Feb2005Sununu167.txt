    SENATORS GREGG AND SUNUNU ANNOUNCE  1 4 MILLION   IN HEATING ASSISTANCE HEADING TO NEW HAMPSHIRE             SENATORS GREGG AND SUNUNU ANNOUNCE  1 4 MILLION   IN HEATING ASSISTANCE HEADING TO NEW HAMPSHIRE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Funding released less than a week after Senators call on Administration to release assistance   Contact  Barbara Riley      Tuesday  February 1  2005       WASHINGTON    U S  Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  R NH  today announced that the U S  Department of Health and Human Services is releasing  1 41 million in emergency funds from the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program for the State of New Hampshire   The funding is part of  100 million in nationwide emergency funding released by HHS   Late last week Senators Gregg and Sununu joined many of their Senate colleagues in calling on President Bush to release the emergency LIHEAP funding  citing the bitter cold weather experienced in the northeast this winter       Senator Gregg stated     The release of these funds is great news for New Hampshire families hit hard by the high cost of heating oil this winter   February and March can be particularly brutal in the northeast and the release of these funds now will help many low income families prepare for the remainder of winter in the Northeast           Senator Sununu stated     Given the recent cold temperatures in New Hampshire and in many parts of the Northeast  the release of these emergency LIHEAP funds could not be more timely  These critical resources will help ensure that many low income families and seniors have access to funds when they need them the most                         231309
