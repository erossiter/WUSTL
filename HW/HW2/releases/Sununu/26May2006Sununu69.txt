IN 1898                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Contact  Barbara Riley      Friday  May 26  2006       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  5 26  applauded a ruling by the United States Treasury Department that ends a significant portion of the nearly 108 year old Federal telephone excise tax and will allow affected consumers to apply for refunds covering the past three years of long distance service on their 2006 tax forms         President Ronald Reagan often said        the nearest thing to eternal life we will ever see on this earth is a government program     Certainly  nearly 108 years after the end of the Spanish American War  we have tested the upper limits of these words     said Sununu  a member of the Senate Committee on Commerce  Science  and Transportation     The ruling to end collection of the long distance portion of this tax is a victory for telephone consumers and other users of communications networks  The futile court cases have proven the tax to be outdated with current technology  and a barrier to the deployment of advanced communications services            Key Facts Regarding Tax Refunds      No immediate action is required by taxpayers      Refunds will be a part of 2006 tax returns filed in 2007       Refund claims will cover all excise tax paid on long distance service over the last three years  time allowed given statute of limitations   Interest will be paid on refunds      The IRS is working on a simplified method for individuals to use to claim a refund on their 2006 tax returns        Refunds will not include tax paid on local telephone service  which was not involved in the litigation      Originally established in 1898 as a    luxury    tax on wealthy Americans who owned telephones  the federal excise tax on telephone calls is not compatible with today   s modern information age society          30       256219
