    SUNUNU SECURES  350 000 FOR ACADEMIC CENTER IN MOUNT WASHINGTON VALLEY TECHNOLOGY VILLAGE                    SUNUNU SECURES  350 000 FOR ACADEMIC CENTER IN MOUNT WASHINGTON VALLEY TECHNOLOGY VILLAGE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Senate grants final approval to funding for education consortium in North Conway                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   Contact  Barbara Riley                                                                                                                                               Friday  November 18  2005       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  11 18  secured  350 000 for the NH Community Technical College to establish an academic center in North Conway   s Mount Washington Valley Technology Village  The resources are contained in the Fiscal Year 2006 Transportation  Treasury  Judiciary  and HUD Conference Report  which passed the Senate on Friday        Through this funding for the Technical College  the Mount Washington Valley Technology Village will move another step closer to offering a wide range of educational resources to local residents  who often must travel long distances to attain the academic training they need     said Sununu  who as a Member of the United States House of Representatives secured  190 000 for the Technology Village incubator project     Through classes  targeted training and seminars  area residents will have the opportunity to enhance their careers and continue to play an integral role in strengthening a diverse local economy  I commend the learning institutions and those officials who have dedicated themselves to a project that benefits so many      The New Hampshire Technical College Berlin Laconia in partnership with Plymouth State College and Granite College  formerly UNH College for Lifelong Learning  expects to utilize the technology village as a satellite education center to provide career and technology programming  Associates Degrees  diplomas and certificates along with Bachelor Degree programs  Upon completion  the building is designed to have state of the art computer labs  classrooms  academic office space  and conference space with teleconferencing capabilities for seminars  courses  and workshops   Further information on the project can be obtained by contacting NHCTC System Director of Communications Shannon Reid at 603 271 2795        30    250371
