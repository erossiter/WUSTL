Monday  May 23  2005       WASHINGTON  DC     Members of the Maine and New Hampshire Congressional Delegations will meet with Base Realignment and Closure Commission Chairman  BRAC  Anthony Principi at a Capitol Hill meeting on Friday  May 27 to underscore their case that the Department of Defense deviated from BRAC criteria and erred in recommending the Portsmouth Naval Shipyard for closure  Maine Governor John Baldacci and New Hampshire Governor John Lynch have been invited to the meeting      The bottom line is clear  Portsmouth Naval Shipyard is a critical component of this nation   s national security infrastructure  and when released  the data will prove the facility deserves to stay open  As we all know  the Department of Defense erred in its decision to recommend the yard for closure  but that doesn   t mean that the Base Realignment and Closure Commission has to make the same mistake     said New Hampshire Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  Maine Senators Olympia Snowe and Susan Collins  New Hampshire Representatives Jeb Bradley and Charles Bass  and Maine Representatives Tom Allen and Mike Michaud     That is why we are meeting with Commissioner Principi on Friday where we will outline for him   in specific fashion   how the Department of Defense deviated from its own criteria in recommending Portsmouth for closure  With this information in hand  he will be able to see for himself and make other BRAC Commissioners aware that Portsmouth consistently completes its work under budget and ahead of schedule  in addition to playing a major role in America   s military infrastructure        Members of the joint delegation have yet to receive a response to their May 17 letter to Secretary of Defense Donald Rumsfeld urging the expeditious release of essential
