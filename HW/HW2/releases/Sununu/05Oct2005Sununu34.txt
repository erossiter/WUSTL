                                                                                          Wednesday  October 5  2005       WASHINGTON  DC      United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  announced today  10 5  that the Department of Homeland Security Appropriations Conference Report will include an amendment he introduced with Senator Russell Feingold in July to require the Department of Homeland Security  DHS  to report on the use and development of data mining  Senate action on the Conference Report is expected by the end of the week   The amendment  also co sponsored by Senators Patrick Leahy  D VT  and Jon S  Corzine  D NJ   requires DHS to disclose to Congress information about data mining programs it is using in connection with anti terrorism and law enforcement efforts  Disclosure must detail why DHS is using the data mining technology  if the programs work as intended  and what steps are being taken to protect privacy and due process rights       While data mining technology can be a useful tool for many purposes  including detecting illegal activities on the part of terrorists and criminals  the highest levels of care must be taken to ensure that federal agencies are properly using this technology     said Senator Sununu     With appropriate oversight     the purpose of this amendment   we can better understand the effectiveness of data mining and its impact on the privacy rights of all Americans       Data mining technology is capable of reviewing and analyzing millions of both public and private records on each and every American  The use of this technology by government law enforcement or intelligence agencies to search for patterns of criminal or terrorist activity raises privacy concerns as well as questions about the technology   s
