    SUNUNU FIGHTS TO ENACT PERMANENT BAN ON  UNFAIR  DISCRIMINATORY INTERNET TAXES                               SUNUNU FIGHTS TO ENACT PERMANENT BAN ON  UNFAIR  DISCRIMINATORY INTERNET TAXES                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Joins McCain in pushing for passage of the  Permanent Internet Tax Freedom Act    Contact  Barbara Riley      Thursday  September 20  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  9 20  urged Senate colleagues to join him in his fight to permanently ban Internet access taxes by passing the Permanent Internet Tax Freedom Act  Sununu and Senator John McCain  R AZ  introduced the bill with Senator Ron Wyden  D OR  on the first day of the 110th Congress  Sununu  a long time champion of banning Internet taxes  spoke out a Capitol Hill news conference on Thursday  September 20   A moratorium on Internet access taxes is set to expire on November 1  and the legislation would make this ban permanent  The Act would forever prohibit three types of taxes  including taxes on Internet access  double taxation  for example  by two or more states  of a product or service bought over the Internet  and discriminatory taxes that treat Internet purchases differently from other types of sales  Taxing access to the Internet is short sighted policy  said Sununu  It will discourage innovation  slow broadband deployment  and raise prices for consumers  The Internet is an essential national and global communication network  it shouldnt be subject to taxes by every state  county  and city in America     Senator McCain said  Keeping Internet access affordable to all Americans is a worthy policy goal  If Americans want to know what their Internet access bill will look like if this moratorium expires  all they need to do is look at a phone bill  Taxes and government fees add as much as 20 percent to Americans telephone and cell phone bills and we cannot allow that happen to the Internet    likely the most popular invention since the light bulb   Sununu has worked consistently in his time in the Senate to permanently ban taxes on Internet use  In 2005  he became an original co sponsor of the bipartisan Internet Tax Non Discrimination Act  During the 108th Congress  Sununu was an original co sponsor of legislation to permanently ban the Internet from access taxes  Portions of that bill were ultimately incorporated in the Internet Tax Non Discrimination Act of 2003  which President Bush signed into law in December 2004    30    283796
