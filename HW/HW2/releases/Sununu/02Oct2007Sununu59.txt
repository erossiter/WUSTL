    SUNUNU REAFFIRMS COMMITMENT FOR STRONGER BORDERS                                                             SUNUNU REAFFIRMS COMMITMENT FOR STRONGER BORDERS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Backs amendment to strengthen nation s borders against illegal immigration                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Contact  Barbara Riley       Tuesday  October 2  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  10 2  announced his strong support for the Border Security First Act of 2007  an amendment to the Fiscal Year 2008 Department of Defense Appropriations Bill  currently under debate on the Senate floor  Sununu is an original co sponsor of the measure  Border security remains a top priority for residents in New Hampshire and across the nation  While more work needs to be done  this amendment takes the necessary steps to help secure our nations borders and reduce a steady tide of illegal immigration  said Sununu  who backed a similar initiative this summer  The bills  3 billion in emergency funding will cover personnel  equipment  and border and interior provisions to reduce illegal immigration  restrict benefits to lawbreakers  and further protect the homeland from terrorists and criminals  I strongly support such a worthy investment in the future security of our nation  The Act requires and provides funding for key provisions  including   Fully funding the installation of 700 linear miles of fencing between the United States and Mexico  The hiring and training of full time border patrol agents   60 million for employment eligibility verification improvements  and Increasing the removal and detention of aliens overstaying their visas and those illegally entering the United States              30    284722
