                                                                                                                      Contact  Barbara Riley      Thursday  December 13  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH   a member of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee  today  12 13  underscored the need for reauthorization of the Presidents Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief  PEPFAR  and welcomed Global Health Council President Nils Daulaire and other witnesses to a Senate Foreign Relations Committee hearing regarding the global fight against AIDS  tuberculosis  and malaria  Dr  Daulaire shared his vast knowledge at the World AIDS Day conference at Dartmouth College that we both attended on December 2  and I was pleased to welcome him and the other witnesses to this important Foreign Relations Committee hearing  said Sununu  who addressed attendees of Dartmouths World AIDS Day conference at the colleges Filene Auditorium  The United States has stepped up to the financial plate in the global fight against AIDS  Tuberculosis  and Malaria  however  the commitment must continue unabated  We are the strongest and wealthiest country in the history of the world  and have a moral obligation to address this deadly epidemic  Moreover  dealing with this crisis abroad is in the United States security interest as these diseases can cripple nations leading to economic and political instability  Sununu is a strong supporter of PEPFAR which is currently up for reauthorization by Congress  The United States monetary commitment to the Global pandemic has risen from 200 million in 1999 to more than  6 billion in 2007  Reauthorization of PEPFAR would provide the necessary funding to fight against HIV AIDS  Tuberculosis  and Malaria over the next five years
