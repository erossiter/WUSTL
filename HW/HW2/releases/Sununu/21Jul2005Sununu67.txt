    COMMERCE COMMITTEE APPROVES BILL TO ENHANCE CORAL REEF                                                       COMMERCE COMMITTEE APPROVES BILL TO ENHANCE CORAL REEF                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Contact  Barbara Riley                                                                                                                                               Thursday  July 21  2005       WASHINGTON  DC   The U S  Senate Committee on Commerce  Science  and Transportation today voted unanimously to approve the  Coral Reef Conservation Amendments Act of 2005   S  1390   introduced by Senator Daniel Inouye  D Hawaii  and co sponsored by Senators John Sununu  R N H   and Daniel Akaka  D Hawaii       Committee approval of the    Coral Reef Conservation Amendments Act of 2005    underscores a broader commitment to the preservation of some of the most fragile ecosystems on earth     said Sununu  chair of the Commerce Committee   s National Ocean Policy Study     Once this bill is enacted  the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration will have the tools it needs to help stem damage to coral reefs from ocean vessels and prevent collisions from occurring in the future  I look forward to continuing to work with my Senate colleagues to ensure that this important goal is achieved        The legislation  which reauthorizes and amends the Coral Reef Conservation Act of 2000  CRCA   enhances the resources and damage prevention measures needed to ensure the long term health of the nation   s coral reefs  The bill includes the following key provisions       Funding   The bill increases annual program authorizations from  16 million to  30 million in FY2006  up to  35 million by FY2009 2012  It also requires the use of at least 30  of funds for the Coral Reef Conservation Grants Program  which the bill expands and refocuses       Emergency Response Actions  The bill authorizes the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration  NOAA  to undertake emergency response actions to prevent or mitigate imminent coral reef destruction from vessel or other physical damage  It also allows public private funding for emergency response and prevention actions through the Coral Reef Conservation Fund  for which up to 10  of authorized funds would be made available       Community Based Solutions  The legislation creates a one time  8 million Community Based Planning Grants program to implement locally designed coral management and protection plans       Abandoned and Grounded Vessels  The bill authorizes NOAA to maintain a vessel grounding inventory and develop a list of recommendations for preventative measures         Coordination  The legislation directs coordination among federal  state and territorial governments to implement the U S  Coral Reef Action Strategy  It also streamlines and focuses reporting requirements   The Committee unanimously accepted an amendment by Senators Inouye and Sununu that made technical corrections to the bill  The Committee also accepted a second degree amendment by Senator Bill Nelson  D Fla    The amendment stipulated that existing law  which provides a minimum 40  share of Coral Reef Conservation Grant funds to both the Atlantic and Pacific regions  be retained  The underlying bill had reduced the minimum percentage for the Atlantic to 30   but would have still allowed the Atlantic to compete for unallocated funds   The bill now proceeds to the full Senate for its consideration
