    SUNUNU COMMENDS HOUSE COMMITTEE ACTION ON CHILD CAR SAFETY LEGISLATION                                       SUNUNU COMMENDS HOUSE COMMITTEE ACTION ON CHILD CAR SAFETY LEGISLATION                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Cameron Gulbransen Kids Transportation Safety Act passes House Energy and Commerce Committee   Contact  Barbara Riley      Wednesday  December 19  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  commended the House Energy and Commerce Committee following its passage today  12 18  of the bipartisan Cameron Gulbransen Kids Transportation Safety Act  H R  1216   which protects children in and around new vehicles  The bill moves next to the floor of the House of Representatives for consideration    The House bill is similar to Senate legislation  S  694  Sununu introduced with Senator Hillary Clinton  D NY  earlier this year and identical to the version of the Clinton Sununu bill approved by the Senate Committee on Commerce  Science  and Transportation in May    The House Energy Committees mark up is great news  since we are reminded of the need for this legislation nearly every day  People of all ages  especially children  are being killed or injured in non traffic  non crash incidents  said Sununu  The bills responsible  affordable steps   such as utilizing backover avoidance technologies  power window auto reverse mechanisms  and brake transmission shift interlocks   will help save lives    Sununu has worked closely on this child car safety legislation with Farmington  New Hampshire resident Packy Campbell  whose son Ian died tragically in an April 2004 accident  Upon learning of the House Committees action on the bill  Campbell said  The Energy and Commerce Committee action is a wonderful step toward ending preventable tragedies  The language being identical to a previously passed Senate legislation is testament to the leadership on this issue  Stakeholders working together for the common goal of protecting children is a fitting memorial to the children that have been lost This legislation now needs to become law    The Cameron Gulbransen Kids and Cars Safety Act directs the U S  Secretary of Transportation to enact changes in the following areas  Power window anti strangulation mechanisms  Requires a rulemaking process for the Secretary to determine if requiring an auto reverse safety standard is reasonable  practicable  and appropriate  If determination is made that standards are not needed  notification to Congress is required  Backover warning systems  Requires a rulemaking process within 12 months of enactment of the law so that drivers would be able to detect areas behind their vehicle  Within 36 months of enactment of rules  Secretary would have to issue final standards that could be met with additional mirrors  sensor devices  cameras  or relevant technology  Brakeshift interlocks  Requires all cars made for sale in the United States as of September 1  2010  be equipped with brakeshift interlocks to prevent children from shifting vehicles out of park  Death injury database   Requires the Secretary to establish and maintain a database of injuries and deaths in non traffic  non crash events involving motor vehicles and make it public  Child Safety Information System  Requires the Secretary to make information available about hazards to children in non traffic  non crash situations  utilizing the database  in order to help avoid backover incidents  hyperthermia  accidental activation of power windows and other risks  Deadlines  If the Secretary determines deadlines in making changes cannot be met  new deadlines would be established and the relevant House and Senate Committees would be notified    30    289452
