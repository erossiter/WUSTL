                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Contact  Barbara Riley       Friday  August 3  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  8 2  voted to fully reauthorize the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  SCHIP  by supporting the final Senate version of the bill  written by Senate Finance Committee Chairman Max Baucus  D MT  and Ranking Member Chuck Grassley  R IA   The SCHIP bill passed the Senate by a vote of 68  31 and now moves to a committee of conference with the House passed measure  I strongly support full reauthorization of SCHIP   a valuable program that must remain focused on reducing the number of children from lower income families who are uninsured  said Sununu  The Senate bill meets this goal and rejects drastic and unacceptable cuts to Medicare in the House passed proposal   measure that jeopardizes millions in funding to New Hampshires hospitals and nursing homes and all but eliminates the Medicare Advantage program that provides health care to 3 500 New Hampshire seniors   BACKGROUND  Congress enacted SCHIP in 1997 to provide health care for children from lower income families  At that time  New Hampshire already had a program in place to cover uninsured children  The New Hampshire Healthy Kids Program  which is still in existence  has consistently provided high quality health care to the states children in a cost effective manner  The Congressional Budget Office estimates that cuts to the Medicare Advantage Program in the House version of SCHIP legislation will total  157 billion over the next ten years and cuts to hospitals will total  15 3 billion over the next ten years  Medicare Advantage covers 7 million seniors
