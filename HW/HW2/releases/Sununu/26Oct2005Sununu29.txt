                                                                               Wednesday  October 26  2005       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  10 26  backed Senator Judd Gregg   s  amendment to increase Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  funding by  1 276 billion  The amendment to the Labor HHS Appropriations Bill was designed to be offset by a 0 9   across the board cut in discretionary programs in this appropriations bill and would bring total LIHEAP program funding to approximately  3 5 billion for Fiscal Year 2006     With energy prices at historic levels  it s essential that Congress provide additional fuel assistance funds before winter arrives     said Sununu  a long time supporter of LIHEAP funding     I have supported other LIHEAP measures that get us to the goal of providing the maximum assistance possible to those in need  however  those efforts fell short  Senator Gregg   s amendment would have offered a path to pay for LIHEAP increases  At nearly  1 3 billion  his amendment was a 58  increase over the regular funding LIHEAP is scheduled to receive under this bill  and it would not have increased the budget deficit       Senator Gregg   s amendment failed by a vote of 46 53  Earlier in the day  the Senate considered a separate LIHEAP amendment to the Labor HHS bill  offered by Senators Susan Collins  R ME  and Jack Reed  D RI   calling for a  2 9 billion increase to the program under the designation of  emergency spending  That amendment  which required 60 votes to pass  failed by a vote of 54 43
