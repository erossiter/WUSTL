                                                                                      Tuesday  September 20  2005       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  will cosponsor bipartisan legislation this week to establish a Special Inspector General for Relief and Reconstruction to provide oversight of federal funds appropriated in response to devastation caused by Hurricane Katrina  Sununu plans to back legislation that will be introduced by Senators Susan Collins  R ME  and Joseph Lieberman  D CT  to ensure that taxpayer dollars targeted for Katrina recovery     totaling over  60 billion to date     are spent responsibly and without waste  fraud  and abuse         Americans expect a strong federal response to the devastation caused by Hurricane Katrina     and demand that their taxpayer dollars are spent responsibly in the process  We have spent as much as  2 billion per day on the recovery  creating an urgent need for visible  ongoing oversight     said Sununu     Establishing an inspector general to oversee federal Hurricane Katrina expenditures will help ensure that funds are spent effectively and reach their intended recipients         The legislation proposed by Collins and Lieberman     who serve as Chairman and Ranking Member  respectively  of the Senate Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs Committee     would establish inspector general oversight of federal Hurricane Katrina recovery efforts by expanding the jurisdiction of the Special Inspector General for Iraq Reconstruction  SIGIR   Establishing oversight of Katrina recovery efforts through an existing and proven entity such as SIGIR helps ensure that review of federal expenditures for the disaster begins as soon as possible  To ensure that SIGIR has the necessary resources for its expanded objective  the legislation would provide  80 million over two years to bolster SIGIR   s existing resources  enabling it to rapidly establish an on the ground presence in the Gulf States      The Hurricane Katrina inspector general   s responsibilities would include             Establishment of a 24 hour fraud  waste  and abuse hotline that would be widely publicized in the Gulf Coast region            Efforts to quickly hire  coordinate  and deploy auditors and investigators to the Gulf Region            Development of a strategic oversight plan that includes audits of no bid contracts            Coordination with oversight in the agencies and states involved in reconstruction  and           Regular reports to Congress on the status of Hurricane Katrina activities                   Senators Richard Durbin  D IL  and Mark Pryor  D AR  are also expected to cosponsor the legislation        30    246112
