                                                                                                                     Contact  Barbara Riley Jeff Grappone      Friday  January 19  2007       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  supported the    Legislative Transparency and Accountability Act of 2007        legislation to reform lobbying rules and reduce wasteful spending     in a vote on the Senate floor on January 18  He issued the following statement regarding passage of the measure  S  1   which was approved by a vote of 96 to 2         This lobbying and earmark reform bill is a real step in the right direction  It bans gifts and travel that are paid for by lobbyists  and it forces much greater disclosure of connections  ties and contributions         Perhaps most importantly  it includes real reforms for earmarks  pork barrel spending  and special interest provisions that too often get stuck in bills at the very last minute  That is why I voted against the energy bill and the transportation bill during the last session of Congress     they were full of special interest  big spending provisions that broke the budget and increased the deficit         The bill we passed last night will make it harder to stick provisions in at the last minute in conference  It forces greater disclosure  shines some light on the process  and improves transparency     which is key to getting fiscal responsibility back into play in Washington     and will help Congress avoid the kind of big spending bills that we   ve seen on energy and transportation that were really unnecessary and not in the taxpayers    interest          Passage of this bill is a step in the right direction and I hope we can have a final package to send to the President in the coming
