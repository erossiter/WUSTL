                                                                                                                 Reauthorization of federal statute governing commercial fisheries scheduled for mark up by Senate Commerce Committee on Thursday  December 15                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Contact  Barbara Riley                                                                                                                                               Wednesday  December 14  2005       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  will bring the concerns of New Hampshire   s fishing community to the debate over the reauthorization of the Magnuson Stevens Bill when the Commerce Committee meets on Capitol Hill to mark up the legislation on Thursday  December 15    Sununu  a member of the Commerce Subcommittee on Oceans  Fisheries  and the Coast Guard  has worked with Senate colleagues to modify specific provisions of the legislation to retain the current method for regulating fishing in New England  This modification is included in an amendment package that will be brought up for consideration at Thursday   s markup   Provisions in the Magnuson Stevens Reauthorization  as introduced  sought to change this process          Over the last few years  New Hampshire   s fishing community has been willing to take the tough steps necessary to improve and strengthen the fishery and deserves our support for this continuing hard work     said Sununu  who has consulted with David Goethel  a biologist and commercial fisherman in New Hampshire and a Member of the New England Fishery Management Council     As Congress moves to reauthorize the Magnuson Stevens Bill   the law which governs our nation   s commercial fisheries   we must take care not to change mid stream into a new process  Such a step could be harmful to the fisherman and continuing conservation  and I look forward to making these points at Thursday   s mark up
