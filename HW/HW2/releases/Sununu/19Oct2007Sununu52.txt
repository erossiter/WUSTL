    GREGG AND SUNUNU SUPPORT AMENDMENT TO FIGHT DRUG RESISTANT BACTERIA                                          GREGG AND SUNUNU SUPPORT AMENDMENT TO FIGHT DRUG RESISTANT BACTERIA                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Contact  Barbara Riley      Friday  October 19  2007      WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  R NH  today lauded the passage of an amendment to the Fiscal Year 2008 Labor  Health and Human Services  Education Appropriations bill for  5 million to combat methicillin resistant Staphylococcus aureus  MRSA  and related infections  MRSA is a drug resistant staph infection responsible for more than 94 000 life threatening infections and nearly 19 000 deaths in the United States in 2005  according to recent data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention  MRSA infections most frequently originate in hospitals and other healthcare settings  but recently there have been numerous cases reported in other locations  especially at schools and among student athletes  This amendment  supported by Gregg and Sununu  was overwhelmingly approved by the Senate   Senator Gregg  a member of the Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations Subcommittee  stated  MRSA is a serious and growing threat to children and families throughout the country  Unfortunately  as a state  we have experienced the tragic and devastating consequences of this infection firsthand  I am pleased to have supported this amendment which will advance activities to reduce MRSA infections nationwide   Senator Sununu stated  Hospitals  schools  healthcare facilities  and other locations continue to battle the spread of this potentially  life threatening infection  Already  our state has already lost one young child  I am hopeful these additional resources will play a role in helping to reduce health risks for New Hampshire and other states across the country
