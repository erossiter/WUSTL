                                                                                                                         Contact  Barbara Riley      Wednesday  September 12  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  9 12  announced that the United States Senate has approved  400 000 for the repair of the retaining wall on the Mohawk River in Colebrook  The resources are contained in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation  Housing and Urban Development legislation  which passed the Senate by a vote of 88 7  The bill now heads to a joint House Senate conference committee before returning to each chamber for final passage  After sustaining heavy damage in the floods of 2006 and 2007  the Mohawk River retaining wall is in need of immediate repair  said Sununu  Addressing this priority now will help Colebrook avert millions of dollars in damages should the wall fail during the next heavy rain  Repairing the severe cracks in the wall and the nearby water mains will help protect Academy Street  Colebrook Academy  and downtown Colebrook  and I will strongly advocate for final approval for these funds as the appropriations process moves forward  Crystal Ball  owner of Howards Restaurant  a historical building located on the wall  said  When John came to town  he asked what he could do for Colebrook  I spoke up to ask if he could help with the wall  and he immediately responded  Much appreciation goes to John and his staff for all the hard work theyve done for us  Sununu requested the Mohawk River retaining wall funding from the Appropriations Committee earlier this year  Last month  Senator Sununu was successful in obtaining funding through the Economic Development Administration to enable the town to begin a technical study of the damage
