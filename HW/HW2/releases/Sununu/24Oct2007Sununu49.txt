                                                                                                                         Contact  Barbara Riley      Wednesday  October 24  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  10 24  announced that the United States Senate has approved  550 000 for the Community College System of New Hampshire  CCSNH    300 000 for expansion and modernization of engineering programs at NHCTC Nashua and the New Hampshire Technical Institute  NHTI  in Concord  and  250 000 for program standardization system wide  The funding is contained in the Fiscal Year 2008 Labor  Health and Human Services and Education  and Related Agencies Appropriations Act  which now moves to a committee of conference with House passed legislation  Curricula and training opportunities at the Community College System of New Hampshire allow thousands of students across the state to reach their career goals and participate in New Hampshires high technology economy  said Sununu  who first secured funding for CCSNH as a Member of the United States House of Representatives  These resources will be applied to programs in engineering  which has become a career choice for more and more students  With an updated and expanded accredited engineering technology program at the colleges Nashua campus and at the New Hampshire Technical Institute in Concord  a growing need for specialized training will be met  Additionally  the appropriations bill includes  250 000 to standardize technology and learning programs across the seven colleges of the community college system in Berlin  Laconia  Claremont  Concord  Manchester  Nashua  and Stratham  In addition to the seven main campuses  CCSNH has six affiliated learning centers in Conway  Littleton  Woodsville  Keene  Franklin  and the Pease International Tradeport in Portsmouth  making community college learning opportunities accessible to students in all parts of the state  This funding will help us ensure our technical programs remain cutting edge  and our students are well prepared for the jobs of today and tomorrow  said Community College System Chancellor Richard Gustafson  Ninety five percent of our students are New Hampshire residents  In securing federal dollars for New Hampshires community colleges  Senator Sununu is working to strengthen a key resource that keeps the state
