                                                                                 Friday  July 22  2005       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today announced his co sponsorship of the    Breast Cancer Research Stamp Reauthorization Act of 2005     S  37      legislation that allows the U S  Postal Service  USPS  to continue selling the fund raising stamp through 2007  Without a change in current law  sales of the stamp will end at the end of this year         The Breast Cancer Research Stamp has raised awareness of an important health issue and over  46 million for research efforts     said Sununu     Given the stamp   s success and the ongoing need for breast cancer research  Congress must act to ensure that this important fund raising tool continues to support study of the disease         Since it was first issued in 1998  the USPS has sold over 637 million breast cancer research fundraising stamps  raising about  46 4 million and contributing to public awareness of the disease  Under S  37  the    semipostal    stamp would continue to be sold with a surcharge of up to 25  above the value of a first class stamp  with surplus revenue being devoted to breast cancer research  Semipostal stamps are issued to support causes that the USPS identifies as being in the national public interest      The    Breast Cancer Research Stamp Reauthorization Act of 2005        introduced by Senator Dianne Feinstein  D CA      has been approved by the Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs and now awaits action
