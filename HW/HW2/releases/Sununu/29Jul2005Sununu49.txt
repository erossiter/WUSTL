    SENATORS GREGG  SUNUNU ANNOUNCE  4 MILLION TO STUDY I 93 WATER QUALITY IN HIGHWAY BILL REAUTHORIZATI         SENATORS GREGG  SUNUNU ANNOUNCE  4 MILLION TO STUDY I 93 WATER QUALITY IN HIGHWAY BILL REAUTHORIZATION CONFERENCE REPORT                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Funding will address road salt issues relating to expansion of I 93                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Contact  Barbara Riley                                                                                                                                               Friday  July 29  2005       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  R NH  today  7 29  announced that New Hampshire is in line to receive  4 million in the Highway Bill Reauthorization Conference Report  The funding will be applied to study water quality and develop effective strategies for salt control along I 93 as it relates to expansion of the Interstate    Senator Gregg stated     As New Hampshire   s population continues to grow  and more and more people travel through our state every day  we need to ensure that our transportation infrastructure is adequate to meet the growing needs  These funds will be used to continue the work being done to examine the implications surrounding the widening and improvements to I 93  The funds will help the State meet its responsibilities to build an efficient and safe roadway while balancing the need to protect our environmental resources      Senator Sununu stated     The widening of Interstate 93 remains a key transportation initiative that will relieve traffic congestion along one of the most heavily traveled roadways in the state  As this highway improvement initiative moves forward  the funding provided by this transportation bill presents a welcome opportunity to study regional water quality  and to develop a method to reduce and manage salt levels in the area       Specifically  the  4 million will be utilized by the New Hampshire Department of Transportation in cooperation with the New Hampshire Department of Environmental Services  and other community  state  and federal agencies to research water quality as it relates to the expansion of I 93  Additionally  the funding will be applied to the development of a cost effective strategy to reduce sodium chloride levels and to identify safe alternatives to the roadway application of sodium chloride   Additional information on this project can be obtained from New Hampshire Highway Commissioner Carol Murray at 603 271 3734 and Federal Highway Division Administrator Kathy Laffey at 603 228 0417                  243685
