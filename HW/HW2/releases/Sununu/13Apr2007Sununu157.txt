    SUNUNU FIGHTS ON FOR SPECIAL EDUCATION FUNDING                                                               SUNUNU FIGHTS ON FOR SPECIAL EDUCATION FUNDING                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Urges Senate Appropriations Subcommittee to fully fund  the federal government s share of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Contact  Barbara Riley Jeff Grappone      Friday  April 13  2007       WASHINGTON  DC   Continuing his fight for special education funding  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  is urging the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health  and Human Services  and Education to fully fund the federal government s share of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act by providing  8 74 billion beyond the Administration s  10 49 billion request for Fiscal Year 2008       Since I was first elected to Congress in 1996  I have fought for the federal government to fully fund its portion of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act because there are few greater priorities for our schools in New Hampshire and across the nation   said Sununu    When full funding of IDEA is achieved  communities will be in a position to utilize their local resources on new initiatives such as structural improvements to schools  technology upgrades  or to hire new teachers  rather than paying for the federal government s share of special education costs       In the ten years since Sununu first served in the United States House of Representatives  the federal share of IDEA funding has increased  7 67 billion or 247  from  3 11 billion in Fiscal Year 1997 to  10 78 billion in Fiscal Year 2007      In a letter to the Chairman and Ranking Member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education  Sununu and Senate colleagues wrote       As you work to prepare the Fiscal Year  FY  2008 Labor  Health and Human Services  and Education Appropriations bill  we urge you to provide the highest possible funding for Part B of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act  IDEA        More than thirty years ago  Congress passed IDEA to help states provide all children with disabilities a free  appropriate public education in the least restrictive environment  At that time  Congress set a goal of bearing 40 percent of the excess cost of serving these students  Today  that level of commitment remains at 17 1 percent       When Congress re authorized IDEA in 2004  we committed to funding this program at  19 23 billion for FY08  However  the Administration requested  10 49 billion for IDEA in its FY08 Budget request       All children benefit when states and school districts receive the funding they need for IDEA  We appreciate the leadership you have demonstrated and look forward to your continued support of this program         30      272390
