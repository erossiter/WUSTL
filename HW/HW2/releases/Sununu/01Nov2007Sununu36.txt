    PRESIDENT BUSH SIGNS SUNUNU SEVEN YEAR INTERNET ACCESS TAX BAN INTO LAW                                      PRESIDENT BUSH SIGNS SUNUNU SEVEN YEAR INTERNET ACCESS TAX BAN INTO LAW                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Senator attends White House Oval Office signing ceremony on Wednesday afternoon   Contact  Barbara Riley      Thursday  November 1  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  10 31  thanked President George Bush for making his seven year ban on Internet access taxes law  Sununu attended the White House Oval Office ceremony where the President signed the bill into law one day before the current November 1 moratorium expires  It took a Halloween expiration to scare Congress into action  but today  consumers can breathe easier  Internet users are free from the tax goblins for seven more years thanks to this bill that bans access taxes and protects e mails and instant messaging from taxation as well  The fight to make this ban permanent will continue  despite the great strides for Internet tax freedom that have been made this week  Taxing the Internet is wrong for consumers and wrong for the economy  said Sununu  On Thursday  October 25  the United States Senate reached agreement on Sununus measure to ban Internet access taxes for the next seven years   three years longer than the original four year  the United States House of Representatives passed bill  The legislation was passed unanimously by voice vote and sent back to the House for consideration  The House passed the stronger Senate version on Tuesday  October 30 and sent the measure to the Presidents desk for his signature  The current moratorium on Internet access taxes  last extended in 2004  will expire on November 1  Sununu  a member of the Senate Committee on Commerce  Science  and Transportation  has worked consistently to permanently ban taxes on Internet use  introducing the Permanent Internet Tax Freedom Act with Senators John McCain  R AZ  and Ron Wyden on the first dayof the 110th Congress in January  Previously  Sununu was an original co sponsor of the bipartisan Internet Tax Non Discrimination Act in 2005  During the 108th Congress  Sununu was original co sponsor of legislation to permanently ban the Internet from access taxes  Portions of that bill were ultimately incorporated into the Internet Tax Non Discrimination Act of 2003  which President Bush signed into law in December of 2004    30    286610
