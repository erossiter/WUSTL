                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          In letter to President Bush  Senators highlight need to help New Hampshire seniors and low income families pay for home energy costs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Contact  Barbara Riley                                                                                                                                               Friday  January 27  2006       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  R NH  today  1 27  called for an immediate release of emergency Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  funds to help seniors and low income families pay their energy bills this winter  In a letter to President Bush  the Senators emphasize that a substantial increase in heating costs in New Hampshire underscores the need to ensure that LIHEAP assistance is available to those who are most vulnerable to price increases      The text of the letters follows      January 27  2006      The President  The White House  Washington  DC   20500  Dear Mr  President       We are writing to request that you release emergency Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  funding without delay to meet the energy needs of individuals and families in our state of New Hampshire and across the nation      As winter   s grip takes hold  heating costs for all Americans are going up    Seniors and low income families are particularly vulnerable to these price increases    Responding to this issue  Congress appropriated  2 billion in regular fiscal year 2006 program funding and  183 million in 2006 emergency LIHEAP funding to help meet the energy needs of these citizens    An additional  20 75 million in fiscal year 2005 emergency LIHEAP funding also remains available to be spent      On January 5  2006  your Administration released  100 million in fiscal year 2006 emergency funds    Of this  approximately  913 000 was distributed to New Hampshire    While these funds will help mitigate the problem  they fall short of meeting the increased needs of Granite State residents and our state   s LIHEAP program      According to the Energy Information Administration   s Short Term Energy Outlook released earlier this month  homes that use heating oil are likely to pay 23 percent more on average this winter than last    Such an increase can make a tremendous difference for those who live on a fixed income    New Hampshire is a primary user of home heating oil  and as more resources are spent on fuel  less remains to cover necessities like food  housing and health care         With the allocation of the regular LIHEAP funds and the release of  100 million in emergency funds  more than  100 million in emergency LIHEAP funds remains to assist seniors and low income families in paying their energy bills this winter      The immediate release of emergency LIHEAP assistance to address the substantial increase in heating costs in states such as New Hampshire is a critical step in meeting the energy needs of millions of citizens throughout America      Thank you for your consideration of our request       Sincerely   Judd Gregg  United States Senator  John Sununu  United States Senator      30    250836
