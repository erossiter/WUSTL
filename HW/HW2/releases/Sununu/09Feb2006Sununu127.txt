                                                                                                                    Pork Barrel Reduction Act    aims to insert transparency in the appropriations process                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Contact  Barbara Riley                                                                                                                                               Thursday  February 9  2006       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  2 9  announced his support for the    Pork Barrel Reduction Act        legislation aimed at bringing transparency to the appropriations process   Sununu joined other members of the Fiscal Watch Team at a Capitol Hill news conference on Thursday morning to announce the introduction of the bill  Senators John McCain  R AZ   Russell Feingold  D WI    Jon Kyl  R AZ   Evan Bayh  D IN   John Ensign  R NV   Lindsey Graham  R SC    Tom Coburn  R OK   and Jim DeMint  R SC   all members of the Fiscal Watch Team  are co sponsors of the legislation      This will shine a bright light on spending bills and dramatically reduce the number of earmarks     said Sununu  who has been working with the Fiscal Watch Team on this issue since the fall of 2005     Lawmakers and constituents alike deserve to know exactly what is in the spending bills that are before Congress  By allowing Members to raise a red flag on the floor against bills that contain unauthorized appropriations  Congress will bring more accountability and fiscal discipline to the budget process         Provisions of the    Pork Barrel Reduction Act    would      Require all earmarks   including the cost  recipient  Congressional requestor  and  justification   to accompany committee and conference reports    Require that all earmarks appear in bill text   Establish a new procedure which would allow a 60 vote point of order to be raised against specific provisions that contain unauthorized appropriations  including earmarks  as well as unauthorized policy changes in appropriations bills and conference reports   Require that recipients of federal funds disclose the names and costs associated with  lobbying  and  Amend Senate Rules to require that all appropriations conference reports be publicly  available 48 hours prior
