                                                                                                                 U S  Customs and Border Protection to site  new Border Patrol sub station in North Country                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Contact  Barbara Riley      Monday  December 10  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  recently urged U S  Customs and Border Protection  CBP  Commissioner Ralph Basham to consider locating the agencys new Northern New England Border Patrol sub station in Colebrook  In a letter to the Commissioner  Sununu expressed his strong support for a 10 acre site in Colebrook to provide the agency with a New Hampshire presence similar to those in Vermont and New York  the other border states in CBPs Swanton Sector   The Colebrook site offers a strategic advantage for Customs and Border Protection because it is within easy access of the New Hampshire and Vermont roadways that connect to the Canadian highway system  said Sununu  In addition  access to the Internet via a high speed broadband fiber optic and secure wireless system is available in Colebrook  This region of New Hampshire has suffered a devastating economic setback with the planned closing of the Wausau Paper Mill in Groveton  and a new federal sub station in nearby Colebrook would be welcome news to the North Country    CBP is the unified border agency within the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS   It has more than 41 000 employees to manage  control  and protect
