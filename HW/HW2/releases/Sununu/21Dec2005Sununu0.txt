                                                                                                                 Terrorism prevention law extended to June 30  2006   civil liberties debate to continue                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            Contact  Barbara Riley                                                                                                                                               Wednesday  December 21  2005       WASHINGTON  DC     To provide Congress with time to consider adding additional civil liberty safeguards into the PATRIOT Act advocated by United States Senator John Sununu  R NH   the Senate today passed legislation to reauthorize the USA PATRIOT Act for six months  Passage of the bipartisan bill  S  2167   which Sununu introduced with Senator Patrick Leahy  D VT  today  will ensure that the PATRIOT Act remains in effect while lawmakers continue their work to improve the balance between the needs of law enforcement and protections for civil liberties in legislation to reauthorize the PATRIOT Act         Reauthorizing the PATRIOT Act to help law enforcement investigate and prevent terrorist attacks     without compromising the American principles of due process and judicial review     has been a top priority since I came to the Senate  In the absence of a final agreement on a conference report  this extension ensures that the tools provided to law enforcement in terrorist investigations in the PATRIOT Act remain in effect while Congress works out the few differences that remain     said Sununu     During the coming months  I look forward to continued debate with colleagues on both sides of the aisle as Congress continues to refine this key terrorism prevention tool         The legislation moves now to the House of Representatives for its approval        30    250098
