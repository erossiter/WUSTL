                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Emergency funds will help individuals  families prepare for cold  winter months                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Contact  Barbara Riley      Tuesday  September 25  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  9 25  announced that he has learned that the Office and Management and Budget has agreed to release  131 million from the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  contingency fund  The state of New Hampshire will receive  3 2 million of that amount  As we all know  Northeast weather can be severe and these funds will help ensure that individuals and families have access to all needed utilities before the winter season begins  said Sununu  who has supported LIHEAP since he was first elected to Congress in 1996  I thank the Administration for responding to last weeks request  urging that these emergency funds be released so the all households are prepared to handle the colder weather  The  131 million in funding was due to expire from the LIHEAP contingency fund on September 30  On September 11  Sununu joined Senate colleagues in writing a letter to President Bush  which stated  Earlier this summer  the National Energy Directors Association  NEADA  conducted a national survey of utility arrearages and shutoffs  The survey found that millions of low income households this year are facing a severe hardship paying arrearages from last winters heating bills  coping with impending and actual shutoff of service  and meeting rising air conditioning costs  The survey estimated that at least 1 2 million households have been disconnected from electric and natural gas service during the three month period following the end of state shutoff moratoriums  LIHEAP is a federal block grant program that provides states with annual funding to operate home energy assistance programs for low income households  In addition to helping to pay energy bills for low income families and the elderly  LIHEAP helps to fund energy crisis intervention programs  low cost residential weatherization and other energy related home repairs   30    284188
