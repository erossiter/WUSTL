                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Legislation would provide tax credit to homeowners who use biomass                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Contact  Barbara Riley Jeff Grappone      Tuesday  June 26  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  introduced legislation today  6 26  that would enable homeowners who install biomass energy systems  such as wood pellet stoves and boilers  to claim a  2 000 tax credit  The Renewable Energy Tax Parity Act of 2007 expands the current tax credit already in place to include high efficiency biomass fuel systems  joining solar and fuel cells   Heating accounts for one third of energy use in the United States  Since high efficiency biomass has at least 75 percent fewer carbon emissions than petroleum  it has the potential to significantly reduce the amount of carbon emitted in the atmosphere  said Sununu  As Americans look for affordable and clean alternatives to heat their homes  it makes sense to provide the same incentives for renewable fuels such as wood pellets as for solar and fuel cells    The Energy Bill of 2005 created a  2 000 renewable energy tax credit for homeowners who install solar power  and  500 for fuel cells  hydrogen batteries   Senator Sununus legislation establishes a similar  2 000 credit for homeowners who install biomass fuel based systems   Sununu continued  For states like New Hampshire  biomass is a growing source of renewable  affordable  and clean energy  In regions where solar or fuel cells are not cost effective  this legislation levels the playing field by encouraging the use of wood products as a home heating source   Wood pellets are used nationwide to heat more than 1 million homes  businesses and schools  A typical homeowner would use 3 tons of pellets per year rather than 500 gallons of heating oil that generate more than 5 5 tons of carbon emissions  An average homeowner that installs a wood pellet stove stands to save 25 percent or more on their heating costs    Senators Judd Gregg  R NH  and John Thune  R SD  are original co sponsors of this legislation     30     277861
