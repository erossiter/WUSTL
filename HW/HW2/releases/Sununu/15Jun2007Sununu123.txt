                                                                                                                        Contact  Barbara Riley Jeff Grappone      Friday  June 15  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  6 15  announced that the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration  NOAA  has named his wife Catherine Kitty Sununu as sponsor of one of its newest vessels  the Ferdinand R  Hassler  She participated in a keel laying at VT Halter Marine Inc  shipyard in Moss Point  Mississippi on Friday  June 15  It is a great honor for Kitty to be the sponsor of this vessel  which will be homeported in New Castle  New Hampshire  said Sununu  The Ferdinand R  Hassler is designed to help researchers accurately map the ocean floor and will advance NOAAs goal of learning more about marine ecosystems  In her remarks  at the keel laying  Mrs  Sununu stated  This is a first for NOAA and for me  the ship is the first of its class ever designed for NOAA  and this is the first time Ive ever been sponsor of a ship  Although construction has just begun  I am delighted to know that when it is built and sailing along the nations coast  the Ferdinand R  Hassler will bear both my signature and my spirit as it charts Americas waterways  Mrs  Sununu is attending the keel laying as the sponsor of the Ferdinand R  Hassler and will sign the keels plaque  According to maritime tradition  sponsors imbue vessels with their spirit during their years of service  When construction of the Ferdinand R  Hassler is completed and the ship is commissioned  it will be homeported at NOAAs Coastal Marine Laboratory near the Coast Guard station in New Castle  New Hampshire  The vessel will use ocean mapping sonar developed at the Joint Hydrographic Center at the University of New Hampshire  The Ferdinand R  Hassler is the first coastal mapping vessel of its kind to be constructed for NOAA  Fridays keel laying also included the NOAA vessel Bell M  Shimanda  a fisheries survey ship  the last of four such vessels constructed by VT Halter Marine  Both ships were named by student teams through the regional NOAA ship naming contest   30    Remarks as
