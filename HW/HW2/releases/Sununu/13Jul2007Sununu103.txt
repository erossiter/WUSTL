                                                                                                                     Contact  Barbara Riley Jeff Grappone      Friday  July 13  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  7 13  announced that the Senate Appropriations Committee has approved  400 000 for the repair of the retaining wall on the Mohawk River in Colebrook  The resources are contained in the Fiscal Year 2008 Transportation  Housing and Urban Development legislation  which now moves to the full Senate for consideration  Flooding in 2006 and 2007 caused significant damage in the town of Colebrook  particularly to the Mohawk River retaining wall  which protects Academy Street  said Sununu  With these resources  Colebrook officials can help avert millions of dollars in damages from any potential wall failure by repairing horizontal and vertical cracks in the wall along with two adjacent water mains  I will strongly advocate for this investment in the local infrastructure as the appropriations process moves forward  Colebrook selectman Benoit Lamontagne stated  Last year  when Senator Sununu visited Colebrook he met with local officials who expressed great concern about the flood damage that occurred in 2006  He asked how he could help and immediately took our concerns to heart  Senator Sununu has always looked out for us and even though Washington is a long way from Colebrook  it certainly isnt with Senator Sununu  Sununu requested the Mohawk River retaining wall funding from the Appropriations Committee earlier this year  Last month  Senator Sununu was successful in obtaining funding through the Economic Development Administration to enable the town to begin a technical study of the damage   30    278987
