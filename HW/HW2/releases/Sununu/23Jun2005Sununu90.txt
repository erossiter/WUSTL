                                                                                Thursday  June 23  2005       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senator John Sununu  R NH   6 23  today offered an amendment to the National Aeronautic and Space Administration Authorization Act of 2005 to direct funding to basic NASA space science research  the type of which has involved University of New Hampshire  UNH  and Dartmouth College  The amendment failed on a 9 13 vote in Senate Commerce  Space  and Transportation Committee mark up on Thursday morning          Recent shifts in NASA   s priorities reflect a disturbing trend away from basic space science research to other NASA activities such as Moon Mars exploration and the potential repair of the Hubble Telescope  While these initiatives are important  they are supported at the expense of funding NASA   s core space science     said Sununu  a member of the Senate Commerce  Space  and Transportation Committee     My amendment aimed to restructure NASA   s budget and preserve funding for the agency   s science mission  While the measure failed  I remain committed to funding basic science research and will consider offering this a floor amendment if resolution cannot be achieved before a final Senate vote         Basic research initiatives  including upcoming unmanned launches in which UNH and Dartmouth have played a key role through the years  have been delayed or potentially eliminated because funds have been diverted to other NASA projects     Sununu continued     Our country cannot afford to lose what these basic scientific initiatives can teach us       On May 5  2005  Sununu toured UNH   s Institute for the Study of Earth  Oceans  and Space where he had the opportunity to learn about the University   s work on various space missions through the past 40 years         239435
