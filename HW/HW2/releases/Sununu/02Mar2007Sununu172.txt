                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Senate passes measure requiring State Department to report on current process for cross border frequency and channel coordination with Canada   Contact  Barbara Riley Jeff Grappone      Friday  March 2  2007      WASHINGTON  DC   The United States Senate today  3 2  passed an amendment offered by Senator John Sununu  R NH  to expand reporting requirements for interoperable emergency communications by requiring the State Department to report on the status of radio licensing between the United States and Canada  Sununu s work is part of a continuing effort to help North Country law enforcement agencies and fire rescue departments gain access to critical radio channels and frequencies  On February 13  Sununu secured Commerce Committee approval on a separate amendment requiring the Federal Communications Commission to examine the application process for public safety radio licenses near international borders  Both amendments are now included in the Reid Substitute Amendment to Homeland Security Legislation  S  4   A vote on final passage of Homeland Security legislation is expected at the end of the week of March 5  Law enforcement and other emergency personnel in northern New Hampshire have been prevented from accessing radio channels and frequencies as an unintended consequence of a licensing approval process that has resulted from diplomatic agreements between the United States and Canada  The two countries coordinate on radio licensing in border areas  Even as we focus on national security  we must ensure that red tape does not arbitrarily stop law enforcement from securing the tools they need  In northern New Hampshire  law enforcement agencies have been kept from using available radio channels and frequencies   representing a situation in which government bureaucracy may be putting personnel and American citizens in harm s way  said Sununu  who has worked with North Country law enforcement to address this issue  Radio channel and frequency spectrum in the northern parts of the state overlap with portions of Canada  Public safety radio systems are governed by bilateral agreements between the United States and Canada  In accordance with terms negotiated by the State Department  each nation submits radio spectrum requests to its neighbor for review  providing an opportunity to determine whether the frequencies will cause harmful interference  The Senate passed amendment would specifically require the State Department to report to Congress on   the current process for considering applications by Canada for frequencies and channels by United States communities near the Canadian border  the status of current negotiations to reform and revise this process and the estimated date for conclusion for those negotiations  whether current process allows for automatic denials or dismissal of initial applications by the Government of Canada  and whether such denials and dismissals are occurring  and the status of communications between the Department of State and the Federal Communications Commission on the
