    SUNUNU ANNOUNCES PARTICIPANTS INVITED TO NOVEMBER 19 ROUNDTABLE ON GROVETON S WAUSAU PAPER MILL CLOS         SUNUNU ANNOUNCES PARTICIPANTS INVITED TO NOVEMBER 19 ROUNDTABLE ON GROVETON S WAUSAU PAPER MILL CLOSURE                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           U S  Department of Labor  Commerce Officials to join federal  state  local and union representatives for Groveton meeting   Contact  Barbara Riley      Tuesday  November 13  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  today  11 13  announced the list of officials and organizations invited to his November 19 roundtable meeting in Groveton to discuss the upcoming closure of the Wausau Paper Mill and the regions economic strategy for the future with top officials from the U S  Departments of Labor and Commerce  Senator Sununu has invited New Hampshire Governor John Lynch  U S  Senator Judd Gregg  and Second District Congressman Paul Hodes to accompany him to the 4 00 p m  meeting at Groveton High School  Other invited officials include Executive Councilor Ray Burton  State Senator John Gallus  and members of the Coos County legislative delegation  In addition  representatives from the New Hampshire Department of Resources and Economic Development  New Hampshire Department of Labor  Workforce Opportunity Council  United Steelworkers Local 61  Groveton Regional Economic Action Team  GREAT   and other regional economic development organizations will participate in the roundtable  which will be open to the public  The residents of Groveton need and deserve any available assistance  and the federal government can play an important role in helping the North Country to recover from this and other recent mill closures said Sununu  This meeting will be a valuable and unique opportunity for the people of Groveton as well as state and local officials to speak directly with the administrators of these programs  Senator Sununu will moderate the meeting on the short and long term impact of the plant closure on Grovetons workforce and economy  which will also include Benjamin Erulkar  Deputy Assistant Secretary of Commerce for Economic Development  EDA   and Emily Stover DeRocco  Assistant Secretary of Labor for Employment and Training  ETA   The workers in Groveton have applied for ETAs Trade Adjustment Assistance  which offers retraining and other help for those who are laid off  The community may also apply for EDAs Economic Adjustment Assistance Program  which funds planning and economic development projects
