    SENATORS GREGG  SUNUNU URGE PRESIDENT TO RELEASE  200 MILLION IN EMERGENCY LOW INCOME ENERGY ASSISTA         SENATORS GREGG  SUNUNU URGE PRESIDENT TO RELEASE  200 MILLION IN EMERGENCY LOW INCOME ENERGY ASSISTANCE PROGRAM FUNDS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Contact  Barbara Riley      Thursday  January 27  2005       WASHINGTON  DC     United States Senators Judd Gregg and John Sununu  R NH  today  1 27  urged President George W  Bush to release  200 million in remaining emergency Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  funding       Congress provided  300 million in emergency monies in the Fiscal Year 2005 Consolidated Appropriations Act to help consumers meet the costs of rising energy prices and reduce energy debt from last winter  In December  the President released  100 million of those funds at the urging of Gregg  Sununu  and several dozen Senators      Today   s letter  signed by Gregg  Sununu  and 48 Senate colleagues urges the President to    immediately    release the remaining  200 million  and states         Many low income consumers entered this winter without heat or electricity due to unaffordable energy prices last year  and thousands of these families remain without heat   Emergency funding is needed immediately to help low income families and seniors restore disconnected utility service         Senator Gregg stated     Over the past few weeks  New Hampshire and the entire northeast has been pounded by multiple storms and temperatures well below freezing  During these winter months  the high cost of heating oil makes it extremely difficult for many in our state to heat their homes  The emergency funding in LIHEAP is set aside for exactly times like these and I hope the money can be released in a timely manner     Senator Gregg continues to be a strong proponent of adequate LIHEAP funding through his position as the former Chairman of the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee  and as a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services      In urging the President to release the emergency funds  Senator Sununu said     Recent  weather in New Hampshire and the Northeast has sent temperatures plummeting  and more of the same can be expected before the winter is out  This severe weather serves to underscore the need to reassure families and individuals that heating funds will be available to them         Gregg and Sununu are long time supporters of the LIHEAP program  which has provided must needed energy assistance to low income families and seniors in New Hampshire and other states during winter   s coldest months        30    231046
