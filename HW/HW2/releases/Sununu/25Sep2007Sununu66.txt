    SUNUNU URGES WHITE HOUSE TO RELEASE ADDITIONAL LOW INCOME HOME HEATING FUNDS                                 SUNUNU URGES WHITE HOUSE TO RELEASE ADDITIONAL LOW INCOME HOME HEATING FUNDS                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       131 Million in LIHEAP Contingency Funds Set to Expire September 30                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Contact  Barbar Riley      Tuesday  September 25  2007      WASHINGTON  DC  United States Senator John Sununu  R NH  has joined a bi partisan group of 52 Senators in urging President Bush to release  151 5 million from the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  contingency fund   131 million is due to expire from the Program on September 30  The funds would be used to help low income families and seniors pay arrearages and shutoff notices from last winters heating bills  and could also be used to assist households this winter     In their letter to the President  the Senators wrote  Earlier this summer  the National Energy Directors Association  NEADA  conducted a national survey of utility arrearages and shutoffs  The survey found that millions of low income households this year are facing a severe hardship paying arrearages from last winters heating bills  coping with impending and actual shutoff of service  and meeting rising air conditioning costs  The survey estimated that at least 1 2 million households have been disconnected from electric and natural gas service during the three month period following the end of state shutoff moratoriums    The weather in the Northeast can be severe  and these funds will help ensure that individuals and families in New Hampshire have access to all needed utilities before the winter season begins  said Sununu  who has supported LIHEAP since he was first elected to Congress in 1996  I urge President Bush to release these emergency funds immediately  Our autumn days will soon turn colder  and LIHEAP must be available so that every household is prepared     30     284174
