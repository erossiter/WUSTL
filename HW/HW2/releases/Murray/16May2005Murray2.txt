                                                         Murray  Senator Murray Applauds Supreme Court Ruling   Allowing Interstate Sales of Washington Wine  05 16 2005                                                                                                                    News Release          Senator Murray Applauds Supreme Court Ruling   Allowing Interstate Sales of Washington Wine    For Immediate Release  Monday  May 16  2005     WASHINGTON  D C      Senator Patty Murray  D Wash   today applauded a 5 4 Supreme Court decision that allows consumers to buy wine directly from out of state producers   This ruling will pave the way for Washington wineries to increase sales by making their wines available to out of state consumers         This ruling is great new for our state s wine industry   Murray said    Through hard work  research  and creativity Washington state has built a world class wine industry   Now  consumers all over the nation will be able to enjoy our outstanding Washington wines and our wineries will benefit from increased orders           Over the past decade  wine has become a  2 9 billion industry in Washington state   Washington is now the second largest wine producer in the nation  with over 300 wineries    nearly double the number five years ago   Wine production has doubled over the past decade  with wine grapes now the state s 4th largest fruit crop   The industry supports more than 13 000 related jobs        Murray has been an instrumental player in supporting that rapid industry growth   As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  over the past several years  she has worked to         Boost USDA s Market Access Program funding    which allows industry groups to get reimbursed for overseas marketing and promotional activities    from  90 million to  200 million annually        Secure  8 4 million over the past two years to widen U S  Highway 12 from Burbank to Walla Walla  making this key tourist route safer and less congested      Secure  850 000 to develop the motherblock    a disease and virus  free rootstock for distribution to new and existing growers    and support other viticulture research throughout the Northwest       and Secure  250 000 in FY 2004 to support the Walter Clore Wine and Culinary Center being established in Prosser
