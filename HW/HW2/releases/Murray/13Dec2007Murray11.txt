                   U S  Senator Patty Murray   Senator Murray s Statement on Bush s Second Veto of Children s Health Care Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Murray  Senator Murray s Statement on Bush s Second Veto of Children s Health Care Bill  12 13 2007                                                                                                                    News Release          Senator Murray s Statement on Bush s Second Veto of Children s Health Care Bill      For Immediate Release  Thursday  December 13  2007     Washington  D C    Today  U S  Senator Patty Murray  D WA  issued the following statement after President Bush vetoed the Children s Health Insurance Program  CHIP  for a second time this year  The bill he vetoed is a compromise version which addressed many of his original objections  The CHIP bill would have provided coverage for 4 million more American children  It passed the Senate early last month by a vote of 64 30   President Bush left a lump of coal in the stockings of America s kids this holiday season  By vetoing the Children s Health Insurance Program  CHIP  for a second time  he has said no to America s children and no to a bi partisan majority of Congress  This President has made it clear that he would rather pander to his dwindling base than address the needs of American families  It s time for him to explain to millions of parents across this country why their kids wont have the care they need to have a healthy start to life  It s time for him to explain to these families why next year they will remain one bike accident away from bankruptcy
