SENS. CHAMBLISS, ISAKSON ANNOUNCE FORT GORDON GEORGIA RESIDENTIAL COMMUNITIES INITIATIVE AWARD
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Johnny Isakson, R-Ga., today announced that the Army is awarding a contract to GMH/Benham/Centex Military Communities, LLC to prepare a plan to improve the quality of life for military families at Fort Gordon in Augusta, Georgia, through new housing, community centers and playgrounds.  
   The highlights include, in the first three years, building 716 new four-bedroom homes in the first three years, building new community centers, walking trails, parks, and playgrounds, and in the first six years, the renovation of 716 existing homes. The project is valued at approximately $750 million over fifty years. Final implementation of the plan requires Department of Defense and Congressional approval.
   "I am pleased to announce this important project to improve the quality of life of our military families at Fort Gordon," said Chambliss. "This initiative shows the important role that Fort Gordon plays in the national security of the United States as well as the amount of growth that is expected to take place at this base for years to come."
   "Fort Gordon is a vital component of our military, and this large investment signals its importance to our future national defense," said Isakson. "I am very pleased that these funds will provide a better quality of life for the brave men and women at Fort Gordon who have volunteered to serve our nation."
