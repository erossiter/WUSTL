SENS. CHAMBLISS, MILLER ANNOUNCE ENERGY, WATER FUNDS FOR GEORGIA
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Zell Miller, D-Ga., today announced that several Georgia energy and water projects have been included in the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill. 
   "Maintaining dams, lakes, waterways and our harbors makes a down payment on Georgia's future," Chambliss said. "I am pleased to have secured these important funds, which makes a great investment in our water resources for future generations."
   "From shore protection to waterway navigation, this funding recognizes the importance of the Army Corps of Engineer projects to our nation and especially to Georgia, " Miller said. "I am especially pleased that this bill funds the bio-refinery project at the University of Georgia because this research is going to prove invaluable to our nation's efforts to find alternative energy sources." 
   The bill includes funds for the following projects:
   * $5.2 million for the Apalachicola Chattahoochee Flint River for dredging annual operations and maintenance of the George W. Andrews Locke 
   * $102,000 for Tybee Island Shore protection 
   * $250,000 for the City of Albany for the study of locke and dam flood control 
   * $1.5 million for the bio-refinery and hydrogen fuel cell research at the University of Georgia 
   * $9.6 for the Brunswick Harbor 
   * $7.3 million for the Buford Powerhouse rehabilitation 
   * $733,000 for rehabilitation at the Hartwell Lake Powerhouse 
   * $4.6 million for the Richard B. Russell dam and lake 
   * $250,000 for restoration of the Savannah Harbor Ecosystem 
   * $500,000 to planning for the Savannah River expansion.
