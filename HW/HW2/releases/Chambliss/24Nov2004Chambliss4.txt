SENS. CHAMBLISS, MILLER ANNOUNCE LABOR, HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES FUNDS FOR ATLANTA AREA
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Zell Miller, D-Ga., today announced that several Georgia projects will receive funds from the fiscal year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill, which the Senate recently approved.  
   "These funds are extremely important for metro Atlanta, which is the fastest growing region in our state," Miller said. "This funding will help contribute to the area's growth and long-term success, and I am particularly glad to see support for the numerous outstanding projects at the region's universities."
   "This bill provides important funds for Georgia's colleges and universities," said Chambliss. "I am pleased to announce these critical funds that will provide an important boost for many projects across the metro-Atlanta region." 
   The following Atlanta area funds have been approved under the labor, health and human services portion of the bill: 
   * $668,000 for Emory University's Southeastern Center for Emerging Biological Threats in Atlanta. 
   * $100,000 for the Jewish Federation of Greater Atlanta for aging services programs.
  * $600,000 for Morehouse College's education technology project in Atlanta. 
   * $75,000 for Georgia Environmental Training and Education for a water fluoridation program.
  * $100,000 for the U.S. Disabled Athletes Fund's Blaze Sports Clubs in Atlanta.
  * $75,000 for The University of Georgia's Croatian Partnership for Higher Education in Athens. 
   * $500,000 for Earth University in Atlanta.
  * $450,000 for Spelman College's Health and Wellness Initiative. 
   * $600,000 for Morehouse College's education technology and telecommunications project. 
   * $160,000 to conduct public and social research on issues affecting African American and minority males at the Morehouse Research Institute. 
   * $200,000 for Children's Healthcare of Atlanta Scottish Rite to expand the emergency department. 
   * $150,000 for DeKalb Crisis Center Decatur for mobile mental health outreach assessment and intervention services,
  * $40,000 for the Atlanta Interfaith AIDS network for the Common Ground day program for adults living with AIDS. 
   * $50,000 for Emanuel County Hospital Authority for facilities for rural health clinic in Twin City. 
   * $640,000 for health department facilities and equipment at the J.P. Carr Human Services Complex in Rockdale County. 
   * $14,000 for the City Schools of Decatur to implement the international baccalaureate program. 
   * $628,000 Philadelphia College of Osteopathic Medicine Philadelphia, Pa., for facilities and equipment for the Lawerenceville, Georgia campus. 
   The bill now heads to President George W. Bush for his signature. For more information, contact Chambliss' press office at 202-224-3423 or Miller's press office at 202-224-7777.
