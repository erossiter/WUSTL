SENS. CHAMBLISS, MILLER ANNOUNCE PASSAGE OF LABOR, HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES APPROPRIATIONS
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Zell Miller today announced that Congress has finalized it version of the Labor Health and Human Services conference report which includes important funds for Georgia. 
   'These funds will enhance the programs and quality of education and health care needs across middle and south Georgia," said Chambliss. "I am proud to have helped secure these important funds."
   "Middle Georgia is one of the fastest growing regions in our state, and this funding will help contribute to its growth and long-term success," said Miller. "These funds will deliver an important boost to healthcare and education needs across the area."
   Georgia specific projects include:
   * $100,000 Georgia College and State University, Milledgeville, Senator Paul Coverdell Institute/Archives
   * $100,000 Phoebe Putney Memorial Hospital, Albany, Georgia School Nurse Program 
   * $250,000 Museum of Aviation in Warner Robins
   * $100,000 Wesleyan College, Macon, Georgia Willet Memorial Library 
   * $100,000 City of Macon, Georgia, After School Programs
   * $600,000 Tubman African American Museum, Macon
   * $400,000 Valdosta State University, Valdosta
   * $250,000 Critical Personnel Development Program (CPDP) at Mercer University 
   * $75,000 Georgia Environmental Training and Education Water Fluoridation Program
   * $650,000 Darton College Technology Project, Albany 
   * $150,000 Georgia Southern University, Statesboro, Georgia, Rural Nursing Service 
   * $75,000 The University of Georgia, Athens, Georgia, Croatian Partnership for Higher.
