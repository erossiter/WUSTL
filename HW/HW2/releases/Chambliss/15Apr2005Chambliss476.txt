WEEKLY REPORT FROM WASHINGTON BY SEN. CHAMBLISS, APRIL 15
  CHAMBLISS-ISAKSON OFFER MEASURE TO BLOCK PENTAGON FROM CUTTING C-130J 
   This week, Senator Saxby Chambliss and Senator Johnny Isakson offered an amendment to the Iraq funding bill that would prevent the Pentagon from spending any money to eliminate the C-130J program. To read more, click here. http://www.chambliss.senate.gov/News/singleNews.cfm?NewsID=276
   CHAMBLISS CALLS FOR TAX REFORM
   This week, Senator Chambliss called for an end to our current federal tax code and proposed replacing the current system with one that is fair, simple, honest and consistent. To read Chambliss' op-ed that was published in the April 15, 2005 edition of the Savannah Morning News, click here. http://www.chambliss.senate.gov/News/singleNews.cfm?NewsID=277
   NATIONAL REVIEW WRITES ABOUT CHAMBLISS' INTEL REFORM PROPOSAL
   This week, the National Review online reported on Sen. Chambliss' intelligence reform proposal, to read the full story click here: http://www.nationalreview.com/comment/pfeiffer200504120910.asp
   CHAMBLISS-ISAKSON URGE TSA NOT REDUCE NUMBER OF SCREENERS AT HARTSFIELD
   This week, Senators Chambliss and Isakson urged the Transportation Security Administration not to reduce the number of passenger screeners at Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport. The senators said the move would not make sense because Hartsfield-Jackson is one of the busiest airports in the South and the nation, and such a decision, would have a crippling effect on air travel across the nation. To read more, click here. http://www.chambliss.senate.gov/News/singleNews.cfm?NewsID=273
