SENS. CHAMBLISS, ISAKSON ANNOUNCE $19 MILLION FOR MEDICAL RESEARCH, PREVENTION PROGRAMS IN GEORGIA
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Johnny Isakson, R-Ga., today announced that the state of Georgia has been awarded $19,178,474 in funding for disease and disability research and prevention programs. 
   "Georgians will benefit from the valuable medical resources and research produced by this grant," Chambliss said. "These groups work hard to ensure that all Georgians are healthy and safe and I support their efforts to treat and prevent diseases and disorders."
   "I am pleased to announce these important grants for Georgia," said Isakson. "The work these groups do in prevention and medical research is valuable to all of Georgia's citizens."
   The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services' CDC Procurement and Grants Office will distribute the funding to the following departments within the Georgia Department of Human Resources.
   The Georgia Department of Human Services will receive: 
   * $18,815,652 for Immunization and Vaccines for Children 
   Georgia Department of Human Resources will receive:
   * $120,000 for Public Health Injury Surveillance and Prevention Program 
   * $172,000 for Early Hearing Detection and Intervention Tracking, Surveillance and Integration 
   Georgia Department of Health and Human Resources will receive:
   * $70,822 for Chronic Disease Prevention and Health Promotion Programs
