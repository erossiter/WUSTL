WEEKLY REPORT FROM WASHINGTON BY SEN. CHAMBLISS
  * Chambliss Meets with Georgia Agri-business Leaders, Producers at Sunbelt Expo 
   U.S. Senator Saxby Chambliss, a member of the Senate Agriculture Committee, attended the Sunbelt Agricultural Exposition in Moultrie. Chambliss, Georgia Governor Sonny Perdue, and America's chief agriculture negotiator, the United States Trade Representative Ambassador Allen Johnson, toured the expo, delivered remarks at the 27th Willie B. Withers luncheon honoring the Sunbelt Expo Southeastern State Farmers of the Year, and participated in a roundtable discussion with Georgia agri-businessmen and producers. 
   * Chambliss Speaks to Nearly 1,000 Thomas County Students 
   This week, U.S. Senator Chambliss spoke to nearly 1,000 Thomas County students via teleconference. Chambliss spoke to the school children about the importance of participating in the election process and voting. The teleconference was part of the Kids Voting 2004 campaign, which educates young people about government, the importance of voting, and the election process.
   * Chambliss Joins USDA in Launching Georgia Bobwhite Quail Initiative
   This week, U.S. Senator Saxby Chambliss joined the United States Department of Agriculture's Farm Service Agency (FSA) in launching an initiative that would restore 8,600 Georgia acres of habitat for bobwhite quail. The goal of the program is to create 250,000 acres of habitat for the northern bobwhite quail nationwide. The program targets specific geographic areas in states with the greatest potential to restore bobwhite quail habitats. The FSA estimates the initiative will increase bobwhite quail numbers by 750,000 annually. To be eligible to participate in the program, cropland must be suitably located and adaptable to the establishment of bobwhite quail. Additional requirements are available on FSA's web site at: http://www.fsa.usda.gov/pas/publications/facts/pubfacts.htm
   * Defense Bills Will Help Our Troops
   On Tuesday, October 19, 2004, the Columbus Ledger-Enquirer published a guest editorial by U.S. Senator Saxby Chambliss regarding two important defense bills the Congress has approved. Chambliss highlighted how the bills will help our military men and women and their families.
