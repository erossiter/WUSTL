SENS. CHAMBLISS, ISAKSON ANNOUNCE FUNDS FOR PREVENT CHILD ABUSE GEORGIA
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Johnny Isakson, R-Ga., announced today that Prevent Child Abuse Georgia (PCA Georgia) has been awarded $208,334 for its Collaborative Efforts to Prevent Child Abuse.  
   U.S. Department of Health and Human Services' CDC Procurement and Grants Office will distribute the funding. Prevent Child Abuse Georgia is Georgia's only private, statewide, community-based nonprofit organization with the sole mission of preventing child abuse and neglect. Headquartered in Atlanta, PCA Georgia also has regional staff that provides training and technical assistance for program development to more than 50 PCA Georgia-chartered councils throughout Georgia.
    "As a father and a grandfather, I am firmly committed to ensuring the safety and security of all children," said Chambliss. "Prevent Child Abuse Georgia helps to ensure child safety by providing information, support, and resources to Georgia families so they can be successful in raising children."
   "I am pleased to announce this funding for Prevent Child Abuse Georgia to continue its vital efforts in this area," said Isakson.
