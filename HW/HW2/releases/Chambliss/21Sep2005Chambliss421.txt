SENS. CHAMBLISS, ISAKSON ANNOUNCE $1.5 MILLION FOR GEORGIA TECH
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Johnny Isakson, R-Ga., today announced that Georgia Tech Applied Research Corporation will receive a $1,524,010 grant for its program to improve technology used by law enforcement and first responders to share information. 
   The grant will come from the U.S. Department of Justice's Bureau of Justice Assistance. The Georgia Tech Applied Research Corporation, in conjunction with national practitioner advisory groups, national technology experts, and the private sector, will conduct further analysis, design, prototyping, and testing required for the Global Justice XML Data Model (GJXDM) and the National Information Exchange Model (NIEM). The program will provide the necessary engineering, training, and technical assistance to improve the systems. 
   "This important program will help law enforcement officers and information technology professionals share information among justice and public safety agencies at all levels of government," said Chambliss. "I am honored to assist Georgia Tech in their efforts to make our communities safe places to live."
   "It is essential that all first responders have the necessary equipment and tools for communication to be able to perform their jobs effectively," said Isakson. "I am pleased to announce this grant for Georgia Tech to study better technology for our first responders."
