SENS. CHAMBLISS, ISAKSON ANNOUNCE FUNDS FOR COLUMBUS REGIONAL HEALTHCARE SYSTEM
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Johnny Isakson, R-Ga., announced today that the Columbus Regional Healthcare System has been awarded $248,000 for its Cancer Control and Prevention program.  
   U.S. Department of Health and Human Services' CDC Procurement and Grants Office will distribute the funding. The Columbus Regional Healthcare System is a not-for-profit, health services holding company that offers a range of health and medical services for children and adults. The Medical Center was established in 1836, and has recently added the John B. Amos Cancer Center. 
   "As a cancer survivor, I know first hand how important this research is to Georgians and all Americans," said Chambliss. "These funds will help the Columbus Regional Healthcare System continue their efforts to discover new treatments for this devastating disease."
   "Cancer affects nearly every American in some way, and I am pleased to announce funding for Columbus Regional Healthcare System that will aid in the control and prevention of this serious disease," said Isakson.
