SENS. CHAMBLISS, ISAKSON ANNOUNCE $11.5 MILLION GRANT FOR HEAD START
  Sens. Johnny Isakson (R-Ga.) and Saxby Chambliss (R-Ga.) today announced that Ninth District Opportunity, Inc. in Gainesville, Ga., will receive an $11,517,612 grant for Head Start programs.  
   The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services' Region IV Office for Children and Families will distribute the funding. 
   "I am pleased to announce these funds for Head Start at Ninth District Opportunity, Inc. in Gainesville," said Isakson. "Our children need every opportunity for a good, strong education and Head Start gets many children started on the right path to that goal." 
   "Georgia has a strong Head-Start program that impacts thousands of children and their families each year," said Chambliss. "I know this funding will provide the resources needed by Ninth District Opportunity Inc. in Gainesville to continue providing this valuable service to communities." 
   Head Start and Early Head Start are comprehensive child development programs that serve children from birth to age 5, pregnant women, and their families. 
   The Head Start program has a long tradition of delivering comprehensive and high quality services designed to foster healthy development in low-income children. Head Start grantee and delegate agencies provide a range of individualized services in the areas of education and early childhood development, medical care, dental care, mental health services, nutrition, and parental involvement.
