SENS. CHAMBLISS, ISAKSON ANNOUNCE AGRICULTURE FUNDS FOR PEANUT PRODUCTION
  Sen. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., Chairman of the Senate Committee on Agriculture, Nutrition, and Forestry, and Sen.  
 Johnny Isakson, R-Ga., today announced that the Senate version of the fiscal year 2006 funding bill for the U.S. Department of Agriculture includes $283,130 in funding for peanut production and research in Dawson, Georgia. 
   "Peanuts are an integral part of Georgia's agriculture industry," said Chambliss. "As Chairman of the Senate Agriculture Committee, I will continue working to secure funding for these important programs." 
   "The peanut industry in Georgia produces 45 percent of our nation's supply and is a vital part of our state's economy," Isakson said. "I am pleased the Senate saw fit to support these worthy programs."
   $133,130 will go to develop sustainable management strategies for irrigated and non-irrigated peanut systems in all peanut producing regions of the US.
   $150,000 will go to continued research of the factors affecting the production, harvesting, storage and quality of peanuts.
