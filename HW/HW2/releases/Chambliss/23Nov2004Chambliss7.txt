SENS. CHAMBLISS, MILLER ANNOUNCE IMPORTANT AGRICULTURE FUNDS FOR GEORGIA
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Zell Miller D-Ga., both members of the Senate Committee on Agriculture, announced today that the Senate and the House of Representatives have come to an agreement on Fiscal Year 2005 Agriculture Appropriations for Georgia.  
 The appropriations omnibus bill now awaits final approval by President Bush. 
   "Georgia will benefit from these important investments in agricultural programs, education and research," said Chambliss. "I am pleased to have helped secure this funding for our agriculture community which will allow our farmers to continue to produce the highest quality food in the world.""This agricultural funding is critical to helping Georgia's top industry and I am pleased Congress saw fit to support these worthy projects," Miller said.
   Georgia-specific funds in the bill include:
   * $100,000 for Pecan Scab research at the Southeastern Fruit and Tree Nut Research station in Byron
  * $898,000 Agriculture Water Policy Research 
   * $372,000 for Information Technology Research at Georgia Southern University 
   * $3.6 million for Georgia Soil and Water Conservation Commission Cooperative Agreement for water conservation 
   * $350,000 for Community Nutrient Management Facilities 
   * $474,000 for Water Use Efficiency and Water Quality Enhancement Research 
   * $158,000 for Integrated Fruit and Vegetable Research in Tifton 
   * $625,000 Game Bird Restoration
  * $474,000 for Cotton Fiber Quality Research in Tifton 
   * $450,000 for Water Use Reduction and Producer Enhancement Research. 
   * $200,000 for Peach and Pecan Entomology Research - Southeastern Fruit and Tree Nut Research Lab in Byron 
   * $50,000 for Poultry Enteritis-Mortality Syndrome in Athens
   Also included are funds for Water Use Management and Technology as well as Water Resources Management research in Tifton, Georgia.
