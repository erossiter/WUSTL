SEN. CHAMBLISS ANNOUNCES FUNDS FOR GEORGIA MILITARY CONSTRUCTION NEEDS
  Sen. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., today praised the Senate's passage of an important annual funding bill for America's military construction projects nationwide that includes key funds for Georgia's military construction needs. The appropriations bill provides funds for construction and housing at U.S. military installations across the nation.  
   "This important bill ensures our men and women in the armed forces, especially those based in Georgia, can do their jobs to the best of their abilities," said Chambliss. "As a member of the Senate Armed Services, I will remain steadfast in my efforts to support measures that ensure we have a strong national defense and the best equipped and best trained fighting force in the world."
   Georgia military construction projects included in the bill are:
   * $49.56 million at Fort Benning for a Barracks Complex at Kelly Hill; 
   * $32.9 million at Fort Stewart for the second phase of a Barracks Complex;
   * $24.69 million at Fort Stewart for a Command and Control Facility;
   * $21.1 million at Hunter Army Air Field for a Aircraft Maintenance Hangar for Special Operations Forces;
   * $18.36 million at Fort Benning for a Physical Fitness Training Center; 
   * $17.6 million at Hunter Army Air Field for a Special Operations Forces Battalion Operations Complex;
   * $16.5 million at Savannah Army National Guard for Army Aviation Support Facility;
   * $16 million at Kings Bay Naval Submarine Base for Enclave Fencing and Parking;
   * $15 million at Robins Air Force Base for an Aircraft Ramp;
   * $10.2 million at Fort Stewart for a Tactical Equipment Complex;
   * $10 million at Dobbins Air Reserve Base to upgrade Maintenance Bays
   * $9.6 million at Moody Air Force Base for a Consolidated Base Support Center;
   * $9.5 million at Fort Stewart for a Chapel;
   * $7.1 million at Fort Benning for a Consolidated Health Clinic;
   * $6.9 million at Robins Air Force Base for a Fire/Crash Rescue Station;
   * $5.8 million at Fort Gillem for a Recruiting Brigade Operations Center;
   * $4.9 million at Fort McPherson for a Child Development Center; and
   * $3.85 million at Fort Benning for a Hazardous Cargo Loading Apron.
