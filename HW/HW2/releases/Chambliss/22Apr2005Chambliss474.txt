WEEKLY REPORT FROM WASHINGTON BY SEN. CHAMBLISS, APRIL 22
  SENATE APPROVES CHAMBLISS' C-130J MEASURE 
   This week, the United States Senate approved an amendment authored by U.S. Senators Saxby Chambliss and Johnny Isakson that would prohibit the Pentagon from spending fiscal year 2005 supplemental funds included in the Iraq funding bill to terminate the C-130J program. The measure was passed by voice vote and attached to the Senate version of the Iraq speding bill that President Bush is expected to sign into law.  
   SENATE APPROVES FUNDING BILL FOR U.S. TROOPS IN THE WAR ON TERROR 
   This week, the United States Senate unanimously approved an important funding bill that supports the efforts of U.S. troops in Iraq and Afghanistan. The measure also includes emergency relief funds for the Asian tsunami victims and funds to construct a U.S. embassy in Baghdad. 
   CHAMBLISS MEETS WITH GEORGIA STUDENTS ON CAPITOL HILL
   On Capitol Hill each week, Senator Chambliss meets with many visiting Georgians. This week, Chambliss met with Georgia students from Cairo High School, Buford High School, the Christian Academy in Griffin, the Trinity Chapel Academy in Powder Springs and Mercer University.
   CHAMBLISS OFFERS IMMIGRATION REFORM MEASURE
   This week, Senator Chambliss offered an amendment to the Iraq supplemental spending bill that would provide a new workable guest worker program to meet the needs of agricultural labor. Specifically, Chambliss' measure did not include amnesty provisions that were included in another proposal. To read more, see: http://chambliss.senate.gov/Issues/singleissue.cfm?IssueID=30
   CHAMBLISS-ISAKSON MEET WITH TSA OFFICIALS 
   This week, Georgia Senators Chambliss and Isakson met with officials from the Transportation Security Administration. The Senators reinforced their concerns that reducing the number of passenger screeners at Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport in Atlanta would have a devastating effect on air travel at Hartsfield, throughout the South and the nation. Chambliss said that Hartsfield-Jackson is one of the busiest airports in the nation and that such a decision "simply does not make any sense." 
   CHAMBLISS-ISAKSON ANNOUNCE FUNDS FOR DEKALB PEACHTREE AIRPORT 
   This week, Georgia Senators Chambliss and Isakson announced that the DeKalb-Peachtree Airport in DeKalb County will receive a total of $713,281 from the U.S. Department of Transportation's Federal Aviation Administration. The grant is one of several issued by FAA to Georgia airports to assist with needed construction and safety improvements. Chambliss said the funds will help with much needed modernization and safety improvement projects currently underway at the airport.
