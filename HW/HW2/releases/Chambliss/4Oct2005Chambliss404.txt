SENS. CHAMBLISS, ISAKSON ANNOUNCE FUNDS FOR BYRON HERBERT REECE FARM
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss (R-Ga.) and Johnny Isakson (R-Ga.) today announced that Byron Herbert Reece Farm and Interpretive Center, Inc. in Union, Georgia will receive $150,000 in grant funds to restore a historic farm site.  
   The Reece Farm, Established in the early 1900s, was the home of Georgia poet and novelist Byron Herbert Reece. Original facilities on the property, including the barn, are deteriorating. The farm's home place will be converted into a welcome center and will provide tourism information such as lodging, attractions, heritage and cultural sites. The Reece Farm project is an important component in developing Union County's tourism industry. 
   The historic farm site will create a tourism attraction that will bring 5,000 new visitors and $250,000 new tourism dollars into the area each year. The project will also support the creation of 30 new jobs. Activities on the site will include festivals, concerts, plays, craft shows, Indian Pow Wows, family reunions and recreational activities. The 210-mile State Bicycle Route 90/ Mountain Crossing will also make use of the facilities each year. 
   "I am pleased to see the Byron Herbert Reece Farm receive the money necessary to improve its facilities to better preserve the area's heritage," said Chambliss. "It is important that we keep our historic landmarks properly maintained to ensure the region remains strong as it continues thrive and draw interest."
   "I am pleased to announce this grant for the Byron Herbert Reece Farm and Union County," said Isakson. "This grant will increase visitors' knowledge of the area's history and will help spare this historic farm from further deterioration."
   In a joint effort, the Appalachian Regional Commission will provide $50,000, the county will provide $25,000 and local sources have committed $75,000 for a total cost of $150,000.
