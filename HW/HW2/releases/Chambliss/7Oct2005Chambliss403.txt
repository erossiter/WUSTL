WEEKLY REPORT FROM WASHINGTON BY SEN. CHAMBLISS
  CHAMBLISS EDUCATION AMENDMENT WINS SENATE APPROVAL
   Senator Saxby Chambliss, a member of the Senate Armed Services Committee, successfully secured Senate approval of a measure he authored that will establish pilot projects that promote early childhood literacy on military installations. Chambliss' amendment was accepted as a part of the Senate version of the annual defense funding bill for fiscal year 2006. To read more about Saxby's amendment, click here: http://www.chambliss.senate.gov/News/singleNews.cfm?NewsID=426 
   DEFENSE FUNDING BILL BENEFITS GEORGIA
   Senator Chambliss praised Senate passage of the fiscal year 2006 Defense Appropriations Bill saying that the bill strengthens our national security, addresses important quality of life issues for our troops and includes funds for weapons systems made in Georgia. To read more about the bill, click here: http://www.chambliss.senate.gov/News/singleNews.cfm?NewsID=427
   CHAMBLISS SAYS: STAY THE COURSE IN IRAQ
   Senator Chambliss, a member of the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence, commended President Bush for his commitment to stay the course with the war on terrorism and praised our United States military forces for their efforts to defeat terrorism and protect America.    CHAMBLISS SECURES $600,000 FOR CAMP WHEELER CLEAN UP IN TWIGGS COUNTY
   Senator Chambliss successfully won Senate approval for a measure he authored making $600,000 available for clean up at Camp Wheeler in Twiggs County. Chambliss attached his amendment to the Senate version of the annual defense funding bill for fiscal year 2006. http://www.chambliss.senate.gov/News/singleNews.cfm?NewsID=422
   CHAMBLISS INTERVIEWS COMMERCE SECRETARY GUTIERREZ FOR THE CAPITOL REPORT
   Senator Chambliss interviewed U.S. Commerce Secretary Carlos Gutierrez for an upcoming edition of Chambliss' public affairs tv program called The Capitol Report. During the show, Chambliss interviews key Georgians and opinion leaders regarding the latest news and current affairs. Gutierrez discussed efforts underway at the Department of Commerce to help with the economic recovery of Hurricanes Katrina and Rita, issues important to jobs in Georgia, the department's minority business outreach efforts and the Secretary told his own personal story of working hard and achieving the American dream. The Capitol Report airs on numerous TV stations across Georgia. Check your local listing for show times in your community.
