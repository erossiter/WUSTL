SENS. CHAMBLISS, ISAKSON ANNOUNCE HUD FUNDING FOR COVENANT HOUSE IN ATLANTA
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Johnny Isakson, R-Ga., today announced that the Covenant House in Atlanta, Ga., will receive $144,336 to purchase and construct a new crisis shelter for homeless youth.  
   The funds will be distributed through the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development's FY 2005 Special Project Grants program.
   "I am proud to announce these funds for the Covenant House. These important programs fund a wide range of community initiatives, such as housing and rehabilitation, support services, public improvements and economic development projects in Atlanta," said Chambliss. "I will continue to work with my colleagues in support of the programs that mean so much to so many communities." 
   "I am pleased to announce this funding for Covenant House, which will aid many of our young people in Atlanta who need it most," said Isakson. 
   For more information please visit the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development's website at http://www.hud.gov/.
