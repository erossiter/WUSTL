WEEKLY REPORT FROM WASHINGTON BY SEN. CHAMBLISS
  CHAMBLISS PRAISES SENATE CONFIRMATION OF JOHN ROBERTS AS CHIEF JUSTICE FOR THE UNITED STATES
   John Roberts was confirmed by the United States Senate and sworn in to be the next Chief Justice of the United States. Senator Chambliss, who supported John Roberts, said he was honored to join the majority of his colleagues in the United States Senate in a strong vote to approve Judge John Roberts to be our next Supreme Court Chief Justice.    CHAMBLISS ASKS RUMSFELD TO RETAIN COMMISSARY, PX AT FT. GILLEM
   Georgia Senators Chambliss and Isakson and Governor Perdue urged Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld to retain the commissary and post-exchange complex at Ft. Gillem in light of the 2005 Base Realignment and Closure round, which called for the closure of Ft. McPherson, Ft. Gillem, and Naval Air Station Atlanta. The lawmakers made their request on behalf of over 36,000 retired military personnel, their over 54,000 family members, over 15,000 National Guardsmen and Reservists in the metro-Atlanta area, as well as the active duty personnel who are not being relocated, who rely heavily on the facilities.    CHAMBLISS ANNOUNCES SEVERAL GEORGIA SCHOOLS RECEIVE AWARDS
   Senator Chambliss announced that several Georgia schools received awards from the U.S. Department of Education. Specifically, the Rigdon Road Elementary School in Muscogee County, Coosa Middle School in Floyd County, Patterson Elementary School in Pierce County, and Sequoyah High School in Cherokee County were all named No Child Left Behind Blue Ribbon Schools by the U.S. Department of Education. The No Child Left Behind Blue Ribbon School award is given to public and private K-12 schools that are either academically superior in their states or that demonstrate dramatic gains in student achievement. Coosa Middle School is one of 295 schools recognized as Blue Ribbon Schools in the Nation.
