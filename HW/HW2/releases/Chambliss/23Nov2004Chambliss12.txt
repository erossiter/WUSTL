SENS. CHAMBLISS, MILLER ANNOUNCE TRANSPORTATION FUNDS FOR SOUTH, COASTAL GEORGIA
  Sens. Saxby Chambliss, R-Ga., and Zell Miller, D-Ga., today announced that several transportation projects in Southwest and Coastal Georgia will receive funding in the Fiscal Year 2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill.  
   "This bill includes important funds for South Georgia's transportation needs," said Chambliss. "I am pleased to have helped secure funds for the City of Moultrie's Intermodal facility, which will boost economic development for the entire community." 
   "These appropriations for our airports in South Georgia will help make the entire region more attractive to new businesses and residents," Miller said. "It is a major win for economic development in the region."
   Specifically, the bill includes the following funds for South Georgia:
   * $500,000 for the Moultrie Intermodal Facility. 
   * $700,000 for Terminal Air Traffic Control facilities in Savannah. 
   * $1 million for the runway extension at the Albany Airport. 
   * $1 million for the runway extension of the Southwest Georgia Regional Airport in Albany. 
   * $500,000 for the Savannah water taxi. 
   * $2 million for a job access and reverse commute grant for Chatham County. 
   * $200,000 for the Dooly-Crisp Transportation System. 
   * $1 million for the Flint River Bridge. 
   * $3 million for the Horse Stamp Road/I-95 Interchange in Camden County.
