WEEKLY REPORT FROM WASHINGTON BY SEN. CHAMBLISS
  CHAMBLISS APPLAUDS BROWNS MILL ELEMENTARY SCHOOL
   This week, Senator Saxby Chambliss announced that Browns Mill Elementary School in Lithonia, Georgia, has been named a No Child Left Behind Blue Ribbon School by the U.S. Department of Education. The No Child Left Behind Blue Ribbon School award is given to public and private K-12 schools that are either academically superior in their states or that demonstrate dramatic gains in student achievement. Browns Mill Elementary is one of 295 schools recognized as Blue Ribbon Schools in the United States. Chambliss praised the school, teachers, students and parents for their commendable efforts.  
   FUNDS WOULD HELP GEORGIA PROVIDE ASSISTANCE TO HURRICANE KATRINA VICTIMS
   This week, the Department of Labor provided a $3 million grant for assistance for Hurricane Katrina evacuees in Georgia. Currently, more that 35,000 families from the Gulf Coast are in Georgia and this includes more than 9,000 students who have enrolled in Georgia's public schools. The grant will help fund temporary disaster relief employees including supporting shelter operations and assisting with humanitarian efforts. It will also provide payments for those who are not receiving Disaster Unemployment Assistance or unemployment insurance.

   CHAMBLISS SECURES AGRICULTURE FUNDS FOR GEORGIA 
   As the Chairman of the Senate Agriculture Committee, Senator Saxby Chambliss works hard for Georgia farmers. This week, the Senate approved its version of the fiscal year 2006 agriculture appropriations funding bill, which provide key funds for agriculture projects in Georgia and throughout the United States. Visit www.chambliss.senate.gov for more information.
