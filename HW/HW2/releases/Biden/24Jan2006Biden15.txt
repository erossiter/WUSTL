
            	Biden to Israel and West Bank to Monitor Palestinian Election
             January 24, 2006 
 Washington, DC  U.S. Senator Joseph R. Biden, Jr., the top Democrat on the Foreign Relations Committee, will be in Israel and the West Bank tomorrow, Wednesday, January 25 to observe the Parliamentary elections scheduled to take place throughout the Palestinian territories.   In addition to visiting several polling sites in Ramallah, East Jerusalem and Bethlehem, Biden will meet with Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas and Israels acting Prime Minister, Ehud Olmert. Biden also will be briefed by Palestinian election commissioners and international observers.   Before leaving late Wednesday night, he will meet with American, Israeli and Palestinian reporters covering the election.
