 
		    
            Friday, September 29, 2006 
           

  WASHINGTON - U.S. Senator Sam Brownback today applauded final passage of the fiscal year 2007 Department of Defense Appropriations bill by a 100-0 vote.     
"America is undoubtedly safer because of the bravery of our men and women in uniform," said Brownback.  "I'm glad that my colleagues recognize the importance of a strong military and of ensuring the safety of our troops.  I'm thankful we could set aside partisan politics to pass the Defense Appropriations bill."     
The Defense Appropriations bill provides $436 billion for the United States military.  The funds will be distributed between personnel, maintenance, procurement, and research and development accounts.  The bill also will allow for the proper equipping of 1,332,300 active military personnel.       
The bill now heads to President Bush's desk to be signed into law.
