 
		    
            Friday, September 29, 2006 
           

  WASHINGTON - U.S. Senator Sam Brownback today applauded Senate passage of H.R.5441, the fiscal year 2006 Homeland Security Appropriations bill.  The bill includes $34.8 billion in discretionary spending for the Department of Homeland Security and now heads to the White House for the president to sign into law.     
"I'm pleased that Congress will support homeland security operations before leaving for the fall recess," said Brownback.  "We've completed this bill within spending guidelines and on time so that essential homeland security programs are fully funded."     
Items of interest include:   Immigration and Customs Enforcement - $3,958,281,000 for investigating and enforcing immigration and customs laws, including $1,984,345,000 for detention and removals and $15,000,000 for automation modernization. U.S. Customs and Border Protection - $8,036,356,000 for securing the nation's borders, including $2,239,586,000 for border staffing and $1,187,565,000 for fencing, infrastructure and technology along the border.  This funding will support the hiring of 1,500 new Border Patrol agents. Transportation Security Administration - $6,374,392,000 for securing air transportation. Tunnel Statute - The bill makes it unlawful to tunnel under the border of the United States. Drug Importation - The bill authorizes limited importation of FDA-approved prescription drugs from Canada by individuals for personal use.   Senator Brownback is a member of the Appropriations Committee.
