 
		    
            Friday, September 29, 2006 
           

  WASHINGTON - U.S. Senator Sam Brownback today announced the U.S. Department of Labor has awarded a $158,849 grant to Kansas State University.       
"I am pleased to see that Kansas State University has been awarded this much needed grant money," said Brownback.  "With this grant, K-State will be able to provide valuable training for horticulture workers."      
According to the Department of Labor, the grant will be used for safety training for hard-to-reach employers in landscape and horticulture services in Kansas and Iowa.  The training materials will address the most serious hazards facing such employees.       
Senator Brownback is a member of the Appropriations Committee and the Judiciary Committee.
