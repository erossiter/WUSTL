SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $200,000 FOR VALUE-ADDED AGRICULTURAL DEVELOPMENT CENTER
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson's (D-SD) announced today that the Value-Added Agriculture Development Center in Hughes County will receive a federal grant of $200,000 to assist cooperatives to improve economic conditions in rural South Dakota. Johnson wrote to the United States Department of Agriculture in May to show his support for the Center's initiative. 
  "This funding for the Value-Added Agriculture Development Center will lead to additional growth opportunities in our state and ensure that our agricultural producers get the assistance they need," said Johnson. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment." 
  The grant will be provided by the United States Department of Agriculture's Rural Development. Funds will help strengthen the rural economy and assist farmers, ranchers, and rural business owners establish and market cooperatives.
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
