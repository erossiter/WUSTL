SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $50,000 FOR RURAL BUSINESS PLANNING
  Sen. Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that the South Dakota Rural Development Council, Inc. will receive a $50,000 grant to conduct economic development planning with ten communities in nine Eastern South Dakota Counties.  
   The community sites are located in Clark, Aurora, Jones, Campbell, Hyde, Gregory, Grant, Minor, and Brule counties. The South Dakota Rural Development Council will work with leaders in each community to create plans that would promote sustainable economic development. 
   "Every community has unique opportunities and unique challenges when it comes to promoting growth," said Johnson. "I will continue to work with organizations like the South Dakota Rural Development Council and local leaders to bring innovative economic development strategies to South Dakota communities." 
   This Rural Business Opportunity Grant comes from the Rural Development Agency within the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA).
