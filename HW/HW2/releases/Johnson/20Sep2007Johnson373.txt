SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES MORE THAN ONE MILLION IN FUNDING FOR STATE DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that the South Dakota Department of Health in Pierre will receive a $1,630,322 federal grant from the Department of Health and Human Services' to continue the Bioterrorism-Hospital Preparedness Program across South Dakota.  
  "We must be prepared for the threats we face. This funding for the South Dakota Department of Health will help ensure that our local hospitals and healthcare facilities have the resources they need to prepare for and respond to the threat of bioterrorism," Johnson said. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of an investment that will help all South Dakotans." 
  The Hospital Preparedness Program helps area hospitals and health care providers plan for and respond to the threat of bioterrorism and other public health emergencies.
