COMMERCE-JUSTICE-SCIENCE APPROPRIATIONS FUNDING PASSES COMMITTEE
  Sen. Tim Johnson (D-SD), a member of the powerful Senate Appropriations Committee, today announced the full Senate Appropriations Committee approved his requests for $4.15 million in South Dakota projects in the Fiscal Year 2008 Commerce-Justice-Science Appropriations bill. The bill now moves to the full Senate for consideration.  
  "This bill includes funding for projects and programs that directly affect South Dakota's law enforcement and our nation's efforts to be competitive in scientific research in the global economy," Johnson said. "I worked with Chairman Byrd to make sure projects and programs that will help South Dakota were funded in this bill, and appreciate his help throughout the appropriations process."
   S.D. Projects Johnson Secured in the Senate Report:
   * $400,000 to the South Dakota Children's Home Society in Sioux Falls 
   * $100,000 for A Child Is Missing (ACIM) satellite mapping technology and telephone computer system 
   * $500,000 for Crow Creek Sioux Tribe Criminal Justice System's law enforcement, court, and detention equipment and operations. 
   * $1 million for law enforcement, court, and detention equipment and operations of the Oglala Sioux Tribe Criminal Justice System. 
   * $500,000 for law enforcement, court, and detention equipment and operations of the Cheyenne River Sioux Tribe Criminal Justice System in South Dakota.
   * $200,000 for technology upgrades to the 911 system on the Pine Ridge Indian Reservation.
   * $200,000 for technology upgrades to the 911 system on the Cheyenne River Indian Reservation.
   * $500,000 for law enforcement, court, and detention equipment and operations of the Standing Rock Sioux Tribe Criminal Justice System in South Dakota and North Dakota.
   * $500,000 for law enforcement, court, and detention equipment and operations of the Rosebud Sioux Tribe Criminal Justice System in South Dakota.
   * $250,000 for expansion of residential facilities and programs at St. Joseph's Indian School in Chamberlain, South Dakota. 
   Funding for programs that affect South Dakota include:
   * $250 million for the Economic Development Administration
   * $550 million for the Community Oriented Policing Services (COPS) Program which includes $35 million for Tribal Law Enforcement Grants
   * $80 million to state and local law enforcement programs to combat methamphetamine production and distribution
   * $28 million for Indian Tribal Assistance funding within the State and Local Law Enforcement portion of the budget. 
   * $117 million for the National Science Foundation's Experimental Program to Stimulate Competitive Research.
