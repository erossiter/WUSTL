SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $200,000 FOR WHETSTONE VALLEY ELECTRIC COOPERATIVE
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that the Whetstone Valley Electric Cooperative, Inc. in Milbank will receive a $200,000 federal grant from the United States Department of Agriculture's Rural Development to renovate an office building. The project will create five new jobs.  
  "This funding for the Whetstone Valley Electric Cooperative will help improve the local community and create new jobs in the area," Johnson said. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment."
  The renovated building will house the Northeast South Dakota Economic Development Corporation and the Northeast South Dakota Community Action Program. The organizations work with multiple counties in South Dakota, including Beadle, Brown, Miner, Roberts, Spink and Walworth. 
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Planhttp://johnson.senate.gov. is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks."
