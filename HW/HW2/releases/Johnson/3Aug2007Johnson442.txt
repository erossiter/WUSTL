SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $5 MILLION IN FUNDING FOR BLACK HILLS AIRPORT
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that Rapid City will receive $1.5 million in federal funding from the Department of Transportation for the renovation of a transit storage facility. 
  "I am glad that the renovations will be made to the building in Rapid City," Johnson said. "It is important that the community has a safe and modern transit storage facility," Johnson said. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment."
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
