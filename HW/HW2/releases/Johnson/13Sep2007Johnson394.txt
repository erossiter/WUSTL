SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $545,264 TO WOMEN'S CIRCLE
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that Women's Circle, Inc. in Sisseton will receive a federal grant of $545,264to decrease violent crime and help to prosecute violent offenders. The funding comes through the Department of Justice's Office on Violence Against Women. 
  "This funding will provide significant resources for American Indians who are victims of violent crimes and help Women's Circle, Inc. increase safety," Johnson said. "We must do everything we can to provide communities in South Dakota and across the nation with the resources necessary to prevent violent crime and help victims."
  The grant will be used to train personnel and enhance victim safety and prosecute cases of violent crime including domestic violence, dating violence, sexual assault, and stalking. Women's Circle, Inc. is a nonprofit organization on the Lake Traverse Reservation that provides shelter and advocacy services to Native Americans.
