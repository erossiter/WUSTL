SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $379,322 IN ICDBG FUNDING TO LOWER BRULE SIOUX TRIBE
  Sen. Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that the Lower Brule Sioux Tribe will receive a $379,322 Indian Community Development Block Grant (ICDBG) to renovate the lagoon system.  
   "This grant will allow the Lower Brule Sioux Tribe to make the necessary renovations to the lagoon system," Johnson said. "The ICDBG Program is an important source of federal assistance for tribes. As a member of the Senate Indian Affairs Committee and the Senate Appropriations Committee, I will continue to work to ensure full funding for programs for Indian Country." 
   The ICDBG Program provides direct grants for use in developing viable communities, including decent housing, a suitable living environment, and economic opportunities. The program is administered by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD).
