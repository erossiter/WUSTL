SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $831,000 IN FUNDING FOR BIG SIOUX COMMUNITY WATER SYSTEM
  Sen. Tim Johnson's (D-SD) office announced today that the Big Sioux Community Water System, Inc. in Moody County will receive a low-interest federal loan of $831,000 to construct a 500,000 gallon clearwell storage reservoir. The funding is provided by the United States Department of Agriculture's (USDA) Rural Development program.  
   "This funding for Big Sioux Community Water will lead to additional growth opportunities in our state and ensures surrounding communities have reliable access to water," said Johnson. "My Hometown Prosperity Plan calls for increased investment in the public good, and this funding helps realize that goal." 
   Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
