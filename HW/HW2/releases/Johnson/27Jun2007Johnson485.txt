SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $90,000 IN FUNDING FOR CORNERSTONES CAREER CENTER
  Sen. Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that the Cornerstones Career Learning Center, Inc. in Huron will receive a $60,000 discretionary federal grant and $30,000 in continued funding from the Department of Labor to provide higher education and skills based training. 
  "I am glad to see the Cornerstones Career Learning Center has received funding to help train and prepare South Dakotans for skilled careers in the area," Johnson said. "My Hometown Prosperity Plan calls for increased investments in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment."
  The funding is specially meant to help individuals facing significant challenges in gaining employment. The Cornerstones Career Learning Center will provide career counseling and mentoring. The center links to other local career centers and offers services designed to prepare individuals to enter the workforce. 
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
