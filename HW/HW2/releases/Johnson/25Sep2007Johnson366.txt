SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR NATIONAL YOUTH LEADERSHIP NETWORK
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that the National Youth Leadership Network in Pierre will receive a federal grant of $150,000 through the Department of Health and Human Services' Administration on Developmental Disabilities. The funds will go to their National Youth Information Center, which provides support and resources to youth with disabilities.  
  "The National Youth Leadership Network provides critical support to disabled youth, and this funding will ensure that South Dakotans continue to receive the resources and support they need," Johnson said. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment."
  The National Youth Information Center is a national clearinghouse that provides services to young adults ages 16 to 28 with disabilities. Services include tele-conferencing and resource consulting. 
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Planhttp://johnson.senate.gov. is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks."
