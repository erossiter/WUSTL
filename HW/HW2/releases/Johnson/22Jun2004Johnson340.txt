SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $2.07 MILLION IN FUNDING FOR GETTYSBURG AIRPORT
  Sen. Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that the Gettysburg Airport has been awarded $2,065,000 from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) for runway reconstruction and other maintenance needs.  
   "This funding will improve transportation conditions at Gettysburg Airport," Johnson said. "As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, I support ongoing efforts to improve safety conditions at our airports."
   The FAA award comes from the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT). The award will go towards reconstructing Runway 13/31, taxiway, and apron, as well as the installation of Medium Intensity Runway Lighting and Precision Approach Path Indicators. For more information on the DOT, please visit their website at www.dot.gov.
