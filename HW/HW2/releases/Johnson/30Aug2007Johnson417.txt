SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR LOWER BRULE SIOUX TRIBE
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson's (D-SD) office announced today that the Lower Brule Sioux Tribe in Lower Brule will receive a federal grant of $99,800 to be used to provide technical assistance and training to small businesses. The grant will be provided by the United States Department of Agriculture's Rural Development.  
  "This funding for the Lower Brule Sioux Tribe will lead to additional growth opportunities in our state and ensures that small businesses get the assistance they need," said Johnson. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment to help entrepreneurs."
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
