SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR PINE RIDGE CHAMBER OF COMMERCE
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that the Pine Ridge Area Chamber of Commerce in Kyle will receive a $195,490 federal grant from the Department of Health and Human Services' Administration for Children and Families. The funding will expand and implement social and economic development strategies for Native Americans. 
  "I'm pleased that the Pine Ridge Area Chamber of Commerce will receive this funding to help foster tourism and grow the area's economy," Johnson said. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of an investment that will help all South Dakotans." 
  The Pine Ridge Area Chamber of Commerce is a nonprofit organization established to create, sustain, and enhance Indian owned businesses and improve the quality of life of the Pine Ridge Indian Reservation. 
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Planhttp://johnson.senate.gov. is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks."
