SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $690,000 IN FUNDING FOR SOUTH DAKOTA PUBLIC BROADCASTING
  Sen. Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that South Dakota Public Broadcasting will receive a $690,000 grant for local and national programming and community projects. 
   "I am pleased that South Dakota Public Broadcasting will receive this funding," Johnson said. "This grant will help connect parents, teachers and caregivers to educational programs and resources offered by public broadcasting. Public Broadcasting provides educational and cultural programming for children and adults through out South Dakota."
   These funds will be used by stations to help pay for production or the acquisition of local and national programming as well as strengthening local services such as outreach initiatives and educational workshops, and for operational expenses.
   The funds are provided by the Corporation for Public Broadcasting (CPB). The CPB is a private, non-profit organization established by Congress in 1967. It is the single largest source of funding for public television.
   South Dakota Public Television, Vermillion: $458,796
   KILI-FM, Porcupine: $72,160
   KLND-FM, McLaughlin: $63,095
   KUSD-FM, Vermillion: $97,004
