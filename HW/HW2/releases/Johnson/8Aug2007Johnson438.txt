SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $350,000 IN FUNDING FOR OGLALA LAKOTA COLLEGE CONSTRUCTION
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that Oglala Lakota College will receive a $350,000 federal grant to upgrade campus facilities. The funding is provided by the United States Department of Agriculture's Rural Development. 
  "This funding will ensure that Oglala Lakota College makes necessary improvements to their campus to offer students the best possible learning environment," said Johnson. "One of the pillars of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment."
  The funds will be used to repair buildings, pave parking lots, and construction.
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
