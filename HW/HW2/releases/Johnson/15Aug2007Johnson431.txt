SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES CHEYENNE RIVER SIOUX TRIBE TO RECEIVE $149,998 TO HELP VIOLENT CRIME VICTIMS
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that the Cheyenne River Sioux Tribe will receive a grant of $149,998 from the Department of Justice to provide services to victims of violent crimes. 
   "This funding will provide significant resources for American Indians who are victims of violent crimes," Johnson said. "It is a great help for tribes to receive the necessary services available for victims in need."
   This grant comes from the U.S. Department of Justice from the Office of Justice Programs. Funding will allow American Indian communities to provide direct services for victims of violent crimes such as child abuse, sexual assault, elder abuse, drunk driving and gang violence. Services provided include counseling, referrals, emergency services, court accompaniment and assistance obtaining victim compensation.
