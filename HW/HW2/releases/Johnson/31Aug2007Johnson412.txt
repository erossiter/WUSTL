SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES MATH, SCIENCE EDUCATION FUNDING FOR UNIVERSITY OF SOUTH DAKOTA
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that the University of South Dakota will receive a $263,415 federal grant from the U.S. Department of Education's Upward Bound Math and Science program to prepare high school students for careers in the fields of math and science.  
  "I'm pleased to see the University of South Dakota receive this funding, and know that students throughout the state will excel because of it," said Johnson. "It is crucial that we continue encouraging students from all corners of our state to succeed in higher education, and Upward Bound has a long track record of providing the preparation that students need." 
  The Upward Bound Math and Science program is designed to strengthen the math and science skills of students, with the goal of encouraging them to pursue postsecondary degrees in those fields. Services include summer programs, year-round counseling and other training and research opportunities.
