SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR VIEWFIELD RURAL WATER ASSOCIATION, FOR NEW WATER SYSTEM
  Sen. Tim Johnson's (D-SD) office announced today that Viewfield Rural Water Association, Inc. in Meade County will receive a low-interest federal loan of $1.3 million for the construction of a new rural water system. The loan will be provided by the United States Department of Agriculture's (USDA) Rural Development.  
  "This funding for the Viewfield Rural Water Association will lead to additional growth opportunities in our state and ensures surrounding communities have reliable access to water," said Johnson. "My Hometown Prosperity Plan calls for increased investment in the public good, and this funding helps realize that goal by creating a new system to provide safe, clean water."
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
