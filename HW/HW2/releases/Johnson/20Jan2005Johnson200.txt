SEN. JOHNSON COMMENTS ON BUSH'S INAUGURATION
  Sen. Tim Johnson (D-SD) today released the following statement regarding the Inauguration of President George W. Bush for his second term.  
 The ceremony took take place at 12 noon Eastern on the West front of the U.S. Capitol, in Washington, DC.
   "I congratulate the President on his Inauguration. The President's second term is a golden opportunity to look beyond old boundaries, reach across party lines, and work with past opponents to forge policies that will strengthen our country. I stand ready to work with the President on a bipartisan basis to address the challenges we face." 
   Johnson attended the swearing-in ceremonies. Last week, Johnson made tickets to the swearing-in ceremony available to South Dakotans. All of the requests were filled.
