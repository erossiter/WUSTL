SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES OVER ONE MILLION DOLLARS FOR TWO TRIBAL COLLEGES
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that Sinte Gleska University and Oglala Lakota College will receive federal grants totaling $1,203,484 from the U.S. Department of Education's Native American Career and Technical Education Program. The funds will be used to educate students in technical studies through the Native American Career and Technical Education Program. 
  "It is crucial that all students in South Dakota have access to the best education and resources possible to further their careers," said Johnson. "Oglala Lakota College and Sinte Gleska University reach out to students to expand their opportunity for education. This grant will help students gain the skills they need to contribute to the economic development of their communities."
  Sinte Gleska University, located on the Rosebud Sioux Reservation, will receive a grant of $601,742. The funds will be used to annually train 120 students enrolled in their Institute of Technologies, in technical studies such as data processing, nursing, and accounting. Upon completion of the program, students will be placed in jobs identified by tribal economic development planners.
  Oglala Lakota College, located on the Pine Ridge Indian Reservation will also receive a grant of $601,742. The funds will be used to improve and expand on current career and technical education programs in an effort to meet the continuing needs of the tribe and its members. Upon graduation, students will be placed in employment positions at native businesses.
