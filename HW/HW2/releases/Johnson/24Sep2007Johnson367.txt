SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES FUNDING TO REDUCE YOUTH VIOLENCE IN SCHOOLS
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that the Todd County School District will receive a federal grant of $163,975 from the U.S. Department of Education. The funding will be used to reduce incidences of violence in schools through conflict resolution training, partnering with local law enforcement, and intervention training. 
  "I applaud the Todd County School District for tackling the problem of school violence head on. This funding will help ensure that students in the Todd County School Districtwill be able to thrive in a safe and healthy educational environment," said Johnson. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment that will combat violence in our schools."
  The funding will be used to provide a network of services, supports and activities that promote safety and create a safe school environment through training and partnerships with local law enforcement and ensuring ensure that only authorized students and personnel are in schools. Todd County School District will provide services to more than 2200 students in 12 schools.
  Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
