SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $520,600 IN FUNDING FOR SPRINGFIELD AIRPORT
  Sen. Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that the Springfield Airport has been awarded $520,600 from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) for runway construction and other renovations improvements.  
   "This funding will go towards the maintenance and repairs of the Springfield airport," Johnson said. "As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, I support ongoing efforts to improve safety conditions at our airports."
   The FAA award comes from the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT). The award will go towards reconstructing Runway 15/33, taxiways, aprons, construction of a new taxiway, as well as the acquisition and removal of an existing hangar. For more information on the DOT, please visit their website at www.dot.gov.
