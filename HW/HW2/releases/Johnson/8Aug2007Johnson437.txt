SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR CREATION OF LOWER BRULE SIOUX TRIBE DRUG COURT
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that the Lower Brule Sioux Tribe of South Dakota will receive a federal grant of $150,000 to develop and implement a Healing to Wellness Drug Court. The funding will come through the United States Department of Justice's (DOJ) Tribal Courts Assistance Program. 
  "Indian Country faces a substantial substance abuse problem, and this funding will help the Lower Brule Sioux Tribe constructively combat it," said Johnson.
  The funding will be used for staff training and recruitment, along with the purchase of office space. 
  The U.S. DOJ's Tribal Courts Assistance Program supports the development, implementation, and enhancement of tribal justice systems. The program improves case management, supports diversion and alternative sentencing programs, and enhances prosecution and indigent defense, among other initiatives.
