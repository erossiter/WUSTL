SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $1.14 MILLION IN FUNDING FOR RAPID CITY REGIONAL AIRPORT
  Sen. Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that the Rapid City Regional Airport has been awarded $1,143,142 from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) for rehabilitating the taxiway and relocating the existing parallel taxiway in order to meet FAA design standards.  
   "This funding will improve transportation conditions at Rapid City Regional Airport," Johnson said. "As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, I support ongoing efforts to improve safety conditions at our airports."
   The FAA award comes from the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT). The grant will go towards the first phase of rehabilitating Taxiway "A" to meet FAA design standards at the Rapid City Regional Airport. For more information on the DOT, please visit their website at www.dot.gov.
   This grant is being increased from $2,799,393 to $3,942,535. This increase is based on the rehabilitation of Taxiway A.
