SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR SOUTH DAKOTA VALUE ADDED FINANCE AUTHORITY
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that the South Dakota Value Added Finance Authority in Hughes County will receive a low interest federal loan of $750,000. The funding has been awarded by the United States Department of Agriculture's Rural Development through the Intermediary Relending Program to continue economic development and the revolving loan fund.  
  "This funding for the South Dakota Value Added Finance will provide funding for economic development opportunities and help local communities," Johnson said. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of an investment that will help people of South Dakota."
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
