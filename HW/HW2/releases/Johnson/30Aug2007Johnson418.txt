SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR NORTHEAST SOUTH DAKOTA ECONOMIC CORPORATION
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson's (D-SD) office announced today that the Northeast South Dakota Economic Corporation in Roberts County will receive a federal grant of $99,000 to provide funding for business development. The grant will be provided by the United States Department of Agriculture's Rural Development.  
  "This funding for the Northeast South Dakota Economic Corporation will lead to additional growth opportunities in our state and ensures that small business owners get the assistance they need," said Johnson. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment."
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
