BDM RURAL WATER SYSTEM TO RECEIVE FUNDS FOR WATER LINE BETWEEN HECLA AND KIDDER
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today BDM Rural Water System, Inc., in Marshall County will receive a $1,210,000 federal grant and a $575,000 low-interest federal loan to run a new water line. The funding is provided by the United States Department of Agriculture's Rural Development, and will benefit the city of Hecla and Kidder by establishing access to a safer supply of water. 
   "Funding like this is critical to ensuring that rural communities the resources to provide access to a safe water supply," said Johnson. "One of the pillars of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment."
   Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
