SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $74,000 IN FUNDING FOR NARROWS EARTHLODGE INTERPRETIVE CENTER
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that the Lower Brule Sioux Tribe will receive a $74,000 federal grant to promote Native American history through the creation of the Narrows Earthlodge Interpretive Center along the Native American Scenic Byway. The funding is provided through the United States Department of Transportation's Federal Highway Administration.  
  "This funding for the Lower Brule Tribe will not only provide travelers with a comfortable place to stop during long travels, it will also promote the contributions Indian Country has made throughout history," said Johnson. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment"
  Byway visitors will have the chance to immerse themselves in tribal culture and learn more about early Arikara life and the history of Native American settlement in the Missouri River Valley through a series of interpretive panels. It will focus on the parallel between the modern road and the historic river valley it skirts.
  The center will provide visitors with the chance to learn more about tribal culture and the history of Native American settlement in the Missouri River Valley. 
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
