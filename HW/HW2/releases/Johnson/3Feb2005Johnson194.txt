SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $16,000 IN FUNDING FOR OUTDOOR RECREATIONAL EQUIPMENT IN LEBANON
  Sen. Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that the town of Lebanon will receive $16,000 in funding to build an outdoor recreational area in the city of Lebanon.  
 After the construction, Lebanon will have a new sports playing field.
   "Building this outdoor recreational area will give a place for the Lebanon community to gather and enjoy the outdoors," said Johnson. "Projects like this promote a sense togetherness. "Projects like this promote a sense of togetherness. As a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee, I am a strong supporter of the Land and Water Conservation Fund, it is an important program for the development of small communities. I will continue to work with my Senate colleagues to ensure that these funds are available in the future. I am pleased to announce this funding."
   The grant is made available by the National Park Service under the Land & Water Conservation Fund (LWCF) State Grant program.
