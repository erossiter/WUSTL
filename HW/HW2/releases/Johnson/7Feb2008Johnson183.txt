SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $192,680 FOR NEIGHBORHOOD HOUSING SERVICES
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that the Neighborhood Housing Services of the Black Hills will receive a $192,680 grant from NeighborWorks America. Funding will be used to expand affordable housing opportunities and community development activities.  
  "Neighborhood housing services is an essential partner for families in the Black Hills as they to try to find and maintain affordable housing," said Johnson. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of an investment."
  The NeighborWorks Program makes homeownership more accessible for lower-income families around the state and provides counseling for homeowners who are at risk of losing their homes. 
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Planhttp://johnson.senate.gov. is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
