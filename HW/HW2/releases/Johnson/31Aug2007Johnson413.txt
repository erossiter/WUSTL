SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR BADLANDS SOUTH CENTRAL ENTERPRISE FACILITATION
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) announced today that the Badlands South Central Enterprise Facilitation in Tripp County will receive a $22,118 federal grant to provide technical assistance to entrepreneurs. The grant will be provided by the United States Department of Agriculture's Rural Development.  
  "This funding for the Badlands South Central Enterprise Facilitation will lead to additional growth in our state and ensure that entrepreneurs can make the most of business opportunities," said Johnson. "A key factor of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a great example of that kind of investment."
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
