SEN. JOHNSON ANNOUNCES $286,656 IN FUNDING FOR YANKTON SIOUX INTERPRETIVE CENTER
  U.S. Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) today announced that the Yankton Sioux Tribal Tourism Association will receive a $286,656 federal grant to promote Native American history through the rehabilitation of a former tribal hall. The funding is provided through the United States Department of Transportation's Federal Highway Administration.  
  "This funding for the Yankton Sioux Tribal Tourism Association will not only provide travelers with a comfortable place to stop during long travels, it will also promote the contributions Indian Country has made throughout history," said Johnson. "A key part of my Hometown Prosperity Plan is investing in the public good, and this funding is a wonderful example of that kind of investment"
  The renovated hall will include a permanent collection of tribal artifacts, a lecture hall, an art studio, and multi-media exhibits. 
  Senator Johnson's Hometown Prosperity Plan is a four-pillar agenda for advancing South Dakota's economy from the federal level. The four pillars, or strategies, include "Promoting Partnerships," "Emphasizing Entrepreneurship," "Investing in the Public Good," and "Protecting Pocketbooks." The full plan includes specific actions related to each strategy and can be downloaded from http://johnson.senate.gov.
