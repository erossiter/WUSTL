Sen  Landrieu Secures Key Funding for La  Military Facilities and Veterans          FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  06 13 2007    Sen  Landrieu Secures Key Funding for La  Military Facilities and Veterans  Veterans Affairs Military Construction Spending Bill includes six projects requested by Landrieu     WASHINGTON    United States Senator Mary L  Landrieu  D La   a member of the Veterans Affairs Military Construction Appropriations Subcommittee  announced today that she secured more than  275 million in the Fiscal Year 2008 Veterans Affairs and Military Construction Appropriations bill   Sen  Landrieu secured  9 8 million to construct a new headquarters for the 4th Brigade 10th Mountain Division at Fort Polk  Louisiana   Since the unit was activated in January 2005  it has operated out of a thirty year old facility   But as the Brigade continues its transformation to a Brigade Combat Team Unit of Action as part of the Army Modular Force  a new state of the art facility is essential to supporting the Brigade s enhanced mission capabilities   Our military servicemen and women continue to take on new mission capabilities  but they are stuck in an arcane facility   Sen  Landrieu said    We must ensure that they have the resources they need to protect Americans as we continue to fight global terrorism   I am proud to have secured this essential funding  and I will continue to fight to provide our soldiers with top of the line facilities and support   Sen  Landrieu also secured  6 1 million to construct a new childcare facility at Fort Polk   Army Transformation at the base will put undue strain on the existing facility by increasing the number of soldiers stationed there by approximately 1 438 and family members by 1 600   Childcare is a major quality of life issue for all Americans  and particularly our military families   Sen  Landrieu said    The availability of high quality  affordable childcare can have a significant economic impact on Army families by allowing Army spouses to contribute to household incomes   Support for these families is essential to the recruitment and retention of high quality personnel   Sen  Landrieu secured three Veterans  Affairs projects  including  250 million in grants to fund new construction for state veterans home  five of which are in Louisiana   President Bush requested  85 million  but Sen  Landrieu secured an additional  165 million   Our military veterans put their lives on the line to protect our lives and our values   Sen  Landrieu said    The least we can do is ensure that they have adequate housing as they grow older   With Korean War and World War II veterans now in their seventies and eighties  and with a wave of Vietnam veterans aging as well  we must be able to provide long term care for our men and women who served in uniform   Sen  Landrieu also secured language in the bill to standardize diabetes monitoring and supply equipment to provide adequate care for veterans living with diabetes   In addition  she secured  10 million for the Veterans Blind Rehabilitative Service
