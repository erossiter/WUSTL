Sen  Landrieu Secures Agreement to Fully Fund Coastal Projects         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  04 06 2005    Sen  Landrieu Secures Agreement to Fully Fund Coastal Projects Bill includes substantial increases over 2004 bill    WASHINGTON    Sen  Mary L  Landrieu  D La   today announced with Sen  Christopher Bond  R Mo   and 13 other co sponsors the introduction of the Water Resources Development Act  WRDA  of 2005  which after more than a year of close cooperation and consultation with Sen  Bond  state officials and independent groups  fully authorizes Louisiana s coastal restoration efforts by the Army Corps of Engineers   Sen  Bond is Chairman of the Senate Environment   Public Works Infrastructure Subcommittee  A recent Corps report identified  1 1 billion in projects for which the Corps recommends authorization  while last year s version of the bill had only authorized  325 million in coastal spending   I am very proud that after more than a year of hard work  we were able to secure this important victory for Louisiana s coast   Sen  Landrieu said    Our coastal areas have a significant and immediate need  and I appreciate Chairman Bond s commitment to addressing it   The January 2005 Report of the Chief of Engineers recommended authorizing  1 1 billion in critical restoration projects  Mississippi River Gulf Outlet Environmental Restoration   105 3 million   Small Division at Hope Canal   68 6 million   Barataria Basin Barrier Shoreline Restoration   242 6 million   Small Bayou Lafourche Reintroduction   133 5 million   Medium Diversion with Dedicated Dredging at Myrtle Grove   278 3 million   Science and Technology program   100 million   Demonstration Program   95 million   Beneficial Use of Dredged Material Program   100 million   The WRDA bill also authorizes more than  1 billion in additional projects that will greatly benefit the state  including  788 million for the Morganza to the Gulf Hurricane Protection Project in Terrebonne Parish and  194 million for the Port of Iberia to deepen and widen their access channel to the Gulf of Mexico so that larger oil and gas platforms may be fabricated at the port   This is a wonderful bill for our state  and an important first step   Sen  Landrieu said    But there is still a lot of work to do to save our coast and protect America s Wetland   I look forward to continuing to work with my colleagues in the Senate and other members of our delegation as we move toward final passage
