Landrieu Cosponsors Warner Compromise Resolution on Iraq         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  02 01 2007    Landrieu Cosponsors Warner Compromise Resolution on Iraq Resolution represents common ground on Senate opposition to 21 500 troop surge     WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Mary L  Landrieu  D La   today cosponsored a compromise resolution with Senators John Warner  R Va   and Bill Nelson  D Neb   and others that expresses disagreement with President Bush s troop surge strategy in Iraq   The Senate must be united behind an alternative plan to success in Iraq   Sen  Landrieu said   I have worked with my colleagues in both parties to find common ground on our approach  This resolution disagrees with the President s surge of American troops and sets clear objectives for our commitment in Iraq  It also ensures our troops continue to get the resources they need   Sen  Landrieu  Co chair of the consensus building Common Ground Coalition with Sen  Olympia Snowe  R Maine  had cosponsored a similar Warner resolution January 24  The new resolution represents a united stand of Democrats and Republicans against the surge policy    The new resolution includes language stating that Congress  should not take any action that will endanger United States military forces in the field  including the elimination or reduction of funds for troops   It also requires that benchmarks for the Iraqi government be put in writing and agreed to by the Iraqi Prime Minister   I will continue to collaborate with my colleagues on both sides of the aisle to pass a bipartisan resolution on U S  strategy in Iraq   Sen  Landrieu said   The Senate is expected to vote on the Warner resolution next week
