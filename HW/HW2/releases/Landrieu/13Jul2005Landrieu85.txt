Sen  Landrieu Announces Funding for Shreveport         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  July 13  2005    Sen  Landrieu Announces Funding for Shreveport LSU Shreveport to receive more than  450 000     WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Mary L  Landrieu  D La   announced more than  450 000 in funding from the U S  Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  today for the Louisiana State University Health and Sciences Center  LSU HSC  in Shreveport   This funding will provide equipment for the LSU Health Sciences Center in Shreveport to broaden the research opportunities the university can offer its students   said Sen  Landrieu    With our students using the newest and best possible resources in their studies  there will be no end to their future potential in the area of biomedical research   The Biomedical Research Support Shared Instrumentation Grant Program provides facilities with state of the art equipment to be used in both basic and clinical research   Some examples of instruments provided or updated include nuclear magnetic resonance systems  electron microscopes  mass spectrometers  protein sequencer amino acid analyzers  and cell sorters    30
