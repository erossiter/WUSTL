Norman Francis Receives Presidential Medal of Freedom         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  12 15 2006    Norman Francis Receives Presidential Medal of Freedom LRA Chairman  Xavier President honored at White House ceremony      WASHINGTON    Norman Francis  the Louisiana Recovery Authority  LRA  chairman and president of New Orleans  Xavier University  today was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom  the highest of civilian honors  U S  Senator Mary L  Landrieu  D La   had recommended him for the honor   As Chairman of the LRA  Norman has taken on the immense responsibility of managing recovery in South Louisiana  and I am proud that President Bush has chosen him for such an appropriate honor   Sen  Landrieu said   Louisiana is lucky to have such an able administrator as an overseer of our state s enormous recovery effort from the worst hurricanes in American history  Katrina and Rita  Together  Norman and I will continue to work toward making sure homes  schools and churches are rebuilt  businesses can reopen and our communities can thrive in South Louisiana    Francis is the longest serving university president  beginning his tenure as his alma mater s president in 1968   Former Transportation Secretary Norman Mineta  who served in the Bush Administration from 2001 to 2006 and also served in the Clinton cabinet  was also honored with a Presidential Medal of Freedom  Longtime friends  Sen  Landrieu and Secretary Mineta worked together particularly closely in the aftermath of hurricanes Katrina and Rita  Sen  Landrieu s father  Moon Landrieu  served as Mayor of New Orleans when Secretary Mineta served as Mayor of San Jose  creating a bond between the families that continues today     Secretary Mineta s wisdom and compassion made him one of Louisiana s strongest partners in the Administration following hurricanes Katrina and Rita and the devastating levee breaks that followed  and we are all grateful for his leadership   The Secretary is a strong  intelligent and honorable leader whose career has been one of notable firsts  having broken many barriers for Asian Americans in government    first as the Mayor of San Jose  then as the Chairman of the House Transportation Committee and as a Cabinet secretary under two presidents  But much more than that  he is a good friend   Sen  Landrieu will on Tuesday visit New Orleans transportation projects with Secretary Mineta s successor  Mary Peters
