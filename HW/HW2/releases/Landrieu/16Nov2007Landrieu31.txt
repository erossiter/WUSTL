Partisan Tactics Block Farm Bill Critical to La  Farmers         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  11 16 2007    Partisan Tactics Block Farm Bill Critical to La  Farmers Landrieu reaches out to new La  Agriculture Commissioner Elect     WASHINGTON    The Senate this morning failed to stop a filibuster on the 2007 Farm Bill that would have reauthorized agricultural programs through 2012 for Louisiana s more than 20 000 farmers  The procedural vote of 55 to 42 fell five votes shy of the 60 required to overcome the partisan delay  Most Republicans  including Sen  David Vitter  R La   opposed the bill in a partisan effort to force votes on unrelated amendments    No piece of legislation has more impact on our farmers   said United States Senator Mary L  Landrieu  D La   The bill the Agriculture Committee passed is critical for Louisiana because it maintains vital market assistance and other fundamental programs our producers rely on to stay in business   Louisiana s agricultural industry  which contributes more than  13 billion to our economy each year  is recovering from the devastating 2005 hurricanes and their economic ripple effect throughout the state   The vote today dims the prospect for the Farm Bill s reauthorization  I will work with my colleagues to revive its chances for passage this year because our farmers deserve the Senate s attention   The Farm Bill protects domestic sugar producers from the North American Free Trade Agreement  NAFTA   to be fully implemented January 1  that leaves uncertainty in the U S  sugar market  Sugar is one of Louisiana s most important commodities  representing a nearly  280 million a year industry  The bill gives the Secretary of Agriculture flexibility in setting the quota for imports  and provides additional security by increasing the government backed loan rate for the sugar program from 18 cents to 19 cents per pound   Despite dramatic surges in energy and equipment costs  the last time the loan rate was increased was in 1985   Language Sen  Landrieu included in the bill will give Louisiana sweet potato producers the ability to apply for crop disaster assistance  Natural disasters  such as hurricanes  droughts or floods can wipe out an entire year s crop  as Hurricane Katrina did in Louisiana  This language fixes a flawed accounting method that left sweet potato producers ineligible for disaster assistance    Also included in the Farm Bill is a provision Sen  Landrieu authored to help the Louisiana oyster industry recover in the aftermath of a disaster  The industry suffered a  266 million loss as a result of Hurricanes Katrina and Rita  and the provision will create a crop insurance pilot program to protect it from future storms   At Sen  Landrieu s request  bill also establishes separate loan rates for long grain and medium short grain rice   The current loan rates unfairly penalize producers of long grain rice  much of which is farmed in the South   Sen  Landrieu joined with other Senators in pushing for the bill s expansion of the USDA Snack Program to include all 50 states  The current pilot program helps schools provide healthy snacks to students during after school activities  and the expansion will help promote healthy eating among Louisiana youngsters   The House passed a version of the Farm Bill in July   This month  Sen  Landrieu reached out to Louisiana Agriculture Commissioner Elect Mike Strain  promising to work with him for the future of Louisiana farmers and sharing with him the importance of the Farm Bill   I am a proud supporter of Louisiana agriculture and I look forward to working with you in the future to establish policies that support Louisiana agriculture and forestry   Sen  Landrieu wrote  A full copy of the letter to
