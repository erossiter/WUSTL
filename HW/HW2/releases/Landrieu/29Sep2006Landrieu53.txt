Landrieu Announces Passage of Emergency Preparedness Bill         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  09 29 2006    Landrieu Announces Passage of Emergency Preparedness Bill Legislation reorganizes FEMA to be better prepared for emergencies     WASHINGTON    United States Senator Mary L  Landrieu  D La   today announced the passage of the Homeland Security Appropriations bill  which includes several changes to the Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  and the Stafford Act to better prepare Louisiana in the event of a future hurricane   The legislation changes the organization of FEMA to be more effective in managing emergencies   It is imperative that we take what we ve learned from the disastrous response to Hurricanes Katrina and Rita and the flooding that followed and use it to ensure that we are better prepared for future emergencies   Sen  Landrieu said    This legislation makes some necessary changes to the agency  but we still have a long way to go to fully prepare for the future   The legislation reorganizes FEMA to be a distinct entity within the Department of Homeland Security and makes the Administrator of FEMA the principal advisor to the President for emergency management  including elevating the Administrator to Cabinet level at the President s discretion during disasters   In addition to the progress made in this bill  Sen  Landrieu will continue to fight to restore FEMA to cabinet level status where it can be more effective in handling emergencies   The bill also creates stricter qualification requirements to become Administrator of FEMA  requiring at least five years of executive leadership and management experience  including a demonstrated ability in and knowledge of emergency management and homeland security  Also included in the legislation are provisions that will aid hurricane victims with housing issues   It creates a National Disaster Housing Strategy  removes reimbursement caps on home repair and replacement  allows alternative housing approaches like  Katrina cottages  and allows funding to be used for rental property rehabilitation  The bill cleans up fraud  waste and abuse by implementing stricter oversight requirements  strengthening local contracting preference language and placing a cap on the percentage of funds that can be awarded to contractors not directly involved in repairs   The legislation also strengthens communications during and after hurricanes by creating a communications office and strategy within the Department of Homeland Security and a database to assist families in locating their loved ones
