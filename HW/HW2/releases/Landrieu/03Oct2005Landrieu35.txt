Sen  Landrieu Statement on the Nomination of Harriet Miers to the U S  Supreme Court         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  10 03 2005    Sen  Landrieu Statement on the Nomination of Harriet Miers to the U S  Supreme Court     WASHINGTON    United States Senator Mary L  Landrieu  D La   issued the following statement today regarding the nomination of Harriet Miers to the United States Supreme Court    Sen  Landrieu said   With the President s nomination today of White House Counsel Harriet Miers  the Senate must now fulfill its responsibility to determine whether she is fit to serve as an Associate Justice of the United States Supreme Court   In nominating a woman  the President has taken an important step toward filling Justice Sandra Day O Connor s shoes    but those shoes are big    From the day she became the first woman to sit on the Court  Justice O Connor has been an inspiration for millions of Americans  particularly our women and girls   But her legacy reaches beyond her gender   Throughout 24 years of service  she has proven herself to be one of our nation s leading legal scholars  consistently putting the rule of law ahead of politics     Ms  Miers is indeed an accomplished woman  yet there is still a great deal for the Senate to learn about her    Justice O Connor should be succeeded by a woman who  like her  will inspire our nation and embody the fundamental American values of freedom  equality and fairness   I look forward to working with my Senate colleagues to determine if Ms  Miers is that woman   Sen  Landrieu is a member of the  Gang of 14   a group of Senators who brokered a compromise on judicial nominees earlier this year  allowing the Senate to move forward to other matters important to Louisiana voters and the nation
