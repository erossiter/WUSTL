                                                                                                                   Alexander  Frist Applaud  1 1 Million in Head Start Funding for Anderson County Anderson County Board of Education among grant recipients        November 1st  2005   WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Bill Frist  R Tenn   and Lamar Alexander  R Tenn   today applauded a  1 1 million grant to the Anderson County Board of Education in Clinton through the U S  Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  Head Start program      I  m pleased that the Department of Health and Human Services continues to support Anderson County  s children and families     Frist said     We owe the gift of education to all of our children  and Head Start helps ensure that Tennessee  s underserved students are given every opportunity to succeed   Senator Alexander and I appreciate the commitment of HHS to the growth and development of all America  s children         The Head Start program helps ensure that all of our children are given the opportunity to succeed     said Alexander    I appreciate this important funding from the Department of Health and Human Services for Anderson County  s most precious resource  its children  I look forward to continuing working with my colleagues in on strengthening this valuable program       The grant is  1 165 974 for the Anderson County Board of Education to support Head Start projects     The Head Start program delivers comprehensive and high quality services to foster healthy development in low income children   Head Start and Early Head Start are comprehensive child development programs which serve children from birth to age five  pregnant women  and their families   They are child focused programs and have the overall goal of increasing the school readiness of young children in low income families
