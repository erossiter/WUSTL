                                                                                                                   Alexander  Frist Comment on Pentagon Consideration of Reductions in National Guard Combat Units Letter Hand Delivered to Rumsfeld Today          January 19th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C     In a letter to Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld  U S  Senators Bill Frist  R Tenn   and Lamar Alexander  R Tenn   today expressed concerns that Pentagon plans to transform the National Guard could bring reductions in the number of Brigade Combat Teams  BCTs  such as Tennessee  s 278th Armored Cavalry Regiment     On several occasions throughout the last two weeks I have shared my deep opposition to a reduction in Guard numbers with Secretary Rumsfeld  the National Guard Bureau  and Tennessee  s adjutant general     Frist said     The men and women of the National Guard have made significant contributions to our nation  s military   As the 278th Armored Cavalry Regiment demonstrated so honorably during its recent tour in Iraq  a strong National Guard is a vital component of winning the War on Terror          It  s important that as the Defense Department conducts its four year strategic review  it remember the importance of our National Guard  s combat forces  including the 278th Armored Cavalry Regiment that just returned from a successful mission in Iraq     Alexander said    That  s why I raised the issue this morning with Secretary Rumsfeld at the Pentagon      In the letter  delivered this morning by Alexander during a meeting at the Pentagon with Sec  Rumsfeld and Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff General Peter Pace  the senators stated     While we understand the difficulty you face in this important process  we are concerned about further reductions in the United States ground combat forces    especially during time of war  We are particularly troubled that reductions in the number of Brigade Combat Teams  BCTs  currently operating in the Army National Guard are being considered      We believe that the National Guard provides our nation effective  flexible and highly capable reserves  while also promoting efficiency and cost effectiveness in this experienced and qualified force      The National Guard has proven to be a vital part of our efforts to fight the war on terror  is a deterrent to potential adversaries abroad  and stands ready to assist in homeland security and domestic crises  As such  it is imperative that the Guard maintain its combat strength and continue to be fully utilized to safeguard our nation  s security
