                                                                                                                   Alexander  Frist Applaud  3 6 Million in Head Start Funding for Kingsport         March 7th  2006   WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Bill Frist  R TN  and Lamar Alexander  R TN  today applauded the announcement of a Head Start grant of more than  3 6 million through the U S  Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  for the Upper East Tennessee Human Development Agency in Kingsport         I  m pleased that the Department of Health and Human Services continues to support Kingsport  s children and families     Frist said        We owe the gift of education to all of our children  and Head Start helps ensure that Tennessee  s underserved students are given every opportunity to succeed      Senator Alexander and I appreciate the commitment of HHS to the growth and development of all America  s children             Head Start programs have been beneficial to both communities and participants across Tennessee     Alexander said    I  m very happy to see this investment in Kingsport  s children by the Department of Health and Human Services  and the Head Start program          The grant is  3 602 094 for the Upper East Tennessee Human Development Agency and will support Head Start programs         The Head Start program delivers comprehensive and high quality services to foster healthy development in low income children   Head Start and Early Head Start are comprehensive child development programs which serve children from birth to age five  pregnant women  and their families   They are child focused programs and with a goal of increasing the school readiness of young children in low income families
