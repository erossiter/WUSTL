                                                                                                                   Alexander Comends President Bush for Signing Tax Relief Bill Into Law         May 17th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator Lamar Alexander  R TN  today commended President Bush for signing into law the Tax Increase Prevention and Reconciliation Act of 2005  The bill will prevent tax increases for more than 425 000 Tennesseans and will provide a tax cut for thousands of Tennessee songwriters   The Tax Increase Prevention bill extends certain provisions of the tax code that have expired or will soon expire  including expensing for equipment purchases by small businesses  and lower tax rates on dividends and capital gains  It also provides relief from the Alternative Minimum Tax  AMT      This legislation prevents a tax increase for more than 425 000 Tennesseans  one third of whom make less than  50 000 a year     said Alexander    It does this by extending Alternative Minimum Tax relief and maintaining a low tax rate on capital gains and dividends  These low rates have helped to keep the economy growing      The bill also includes a provision which will alter the manner in which a songwriters  work product is taxed      Tennessee songwriters have been taxed at a rate of 35 percent on the sale of their work     Alexander said    This legislation provides fairness to our songwriters by dropping the rate they pay on their catalog sales from 35 to 15 percent  the same capital gains tax rate that other businesses pay      Alexander voted for the legislation when it passed the Senate on May 11 by a vote of 54 44
