                                                                                                                   Alexander Visits Sharp Manufacturing to Highlight Solar Provisions in Energy Bill         August 24th  2005   MEMPHIS  TN    U S  Sen  Lamar Alexander  R TN  today visited Sharp Manufacturing in Memphis to highlight solar provisions in the Energy Policy Act of 2005 which President Bush signed into law on August 8  2005      I believe the tax credits I introduced in the energy bill  which apply to Sharp  s plant  combined with the innovative work being done at Sharp  will help make Memphis the new solar energy jobs capital of the United States     said Alexander while visiting Sharp Manufacturing    For the first time since 1985  homeowners who install solar energy systems will receive a tax credit worth 30 percent of the system cost of up to  2000  Businesses can also receive this tax credit      Simply put     said Alexander    the energy bill will save Tennesseans money and create good jobs in the state  The solar provisions in this energy legislation support individual Americans who make a real contribution towards cleaner air and U S  energy independence      The Solar Energy Industries Association estimates that the tax credits in the energy bill will result in over 20 000 new jobs and over  15 billion in economic investment throughout the U S  by 2015 including engineering  manufacturing and installation jobs in all 50 states  The global solar industry continues to double in size every three years  yet the U S  now manufactures just 10 percent of the  7 billion annual global demand
