                                                                                                                   Alexander  Frist Announce Support for Tennessee Energy Projects Conference Report Includes Significant Funding for ORNL  Y 12        November 8th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senators Bill Frist  R TN  and Lamar Alexander  R TN  today announced that the Fiscal Year  FY  2006 Energy and Water Appropriations Conference Report includes significant funding for a number of programs at the Oak Ridge National Laboratory and the Y 12 National Security Complex as well as other critical projects across Tennessee      This funding demonstrates the federal government  s commitment to strengthening America  s competitiveness and our national security     Frist said     By ensuring that our nation remains a global leader in technological innovation and scientific advancement  we benefit both the safety of our citizens and the economy   Senator Alexander and I have recognized the importance of federal investment in scientific research  and together we will continue our support for such initiatives       This legislation funds vital research across Tennessee     Alexander said   The future of important scientific research activities at the Oak Ridge National Laboratory and the Y 12 weapons facilities will be strengthened by this funding  Oak Ridge is on track to develop the most powerful non classified computer on the planet  Senator Frist and I will continue working to support Tennessee s leadership in world class research     The FY2006 Energy and Water Appropriations Conference Report includes funding for the following Tennessee projects    OAK RIDGE NATIONAL LABORATORY  ORNL          30 million increase above the president  s budget request for the Center for Computational Sciences   Funds will be used to acquire additional hardware to support the Leadership Class Computational Facility          156 7 million for the Spallation Neutron Source  SNS   including  41 7 million for construction   106 9 million for operations and  8 1 million for instruments   Upon completion  SNS will be the most powerful research facility of its kind  providing researchers an opportunity to study basic and applied research  as well as develop technology in the fields of condensed matter physics  materials sciences  magnetic materials  polymers  chemistry  biology  earth sciences and engineering          290 5 million to support domestic fusion research   Programs at ORNL will be among the primary beneficiaries of this funding    Y 12 NATIONAL SECURITY COMPLEX  Y 12          60 million increase above the budget request to support enhanced safeguards and security at Y 12          40 million increase above the budget request to modernize operations and facilities at Y 12          9 74 million for Compressed Air Upgrades   7 7 million for the Beryllium Capability project  and  729 000 for the steam plant life extension project at Y 12   ADDITIONAL PROJECTS         500 000 for the St  Jude Children  s Research Hospital in Memphis to support a nuclear chemistry laboratory dedicated to producing tracers for specific anti cancer agents  St  Jude  s  recognized throughout the world as the leading children  s research hospital  provides cutting edge medical research and treatment for children regardless of their ability to pay          66 million for the Appalachian Regional Commission  ARC   which is composed of counties throughout 13 states including Tennessee  ARC  s mission is to create opportunities for self sustaining economic development and improved quality of life for the people of Appalachia           4 46 million for the University Research Program in Robotics and  3 5 million for the Consortium for Plant Biotechnology Research  Both programs support ongoing research activities at the University of Tennessee   The FY2006 Energy and Water Appropriations Conference Report must now be considered by the full House and Senate and then signed by President Bush before it becomes law
