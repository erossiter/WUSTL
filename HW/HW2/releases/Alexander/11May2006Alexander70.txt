                                                                                                                   Alexander Applauds Senate Passage of Legislation Preventing Tax Increases Bill Also Cuts Taxes for Tennessee Songwriters         May 11th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senator Lamar Alexander  R TN  today praised Senate passage of the Tax Increase Prevention and Reconciliation Act of 2005 Conference Report   The bill will prevent tax increases for more than 425 000 Tennesseans and will provide a tax cut for thousands of Tennessee songwriters     Tennessee songwriters have been taxed at a rate of 35 percent on the sale of their work     Alexander said    This legislation provides fairness to our songwriters by dropping the rate they pay on their catalog sales from 35 to 15 percent  the same capital gains tax rate that other businesses pay      The Tax Increase Prevention bill also extends certain provisions of the tax code that have expired or will soon expire  including expensing for equipment purchases by small businesses  relief from the Alternative Minimum Tax  AMT   and lower tax rates on dividends and capital gains     This legislation prevents a tax increase for more than 425 000 Tennesseans  one third of whom make less than  50 000 a year     said Alexander    It does this by extending Alternative Minimum Tax relief and maintaining a low tax rate on capital gains and dividends  These low rates have helped to keep the economy growing      The Conference Report  which now goes to the President for his signature  passed the Senate today by a vote of 54 to 44
