                                                                                                                   Alexander  Frist Announce  75 Million for Radiation Detection Systems in Oak Ridge         August 26th  2005   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senators Bill Frist  R TN  and Lamar Alexander  R TN  today announced that the Department of Defense has awarded a  75 million grant to Nuclear Safeguards and Security Systems  Inc  in Oak Ridge   This funding will be used to develop radiation detection systems to be operated in Oak Ridge      The War on Terror has brought unparalleled threats against the security of our homeland     said Frist     I  m pleased that this unprecedented work will be conducted in Oak Ridge  and proud of the leading role that Tennessee  s businesses play in securing our homeland   The best way to defend against a nuclear attack is to prevent one from ever occurring   This investment helps ensure we have the resources to swiftly track down dangerous materials before they can be used by terrorists         This funding will help to develop a modern detection system which will keep Tennesseans and all Americans safe from a nuclear attack     said Alexander     Senator Frist and I are pleased that Nuclear Safeguards and Security Systems are on the cutting edge of emerging technologies that continue to keep the United States one step ahead of the enemy       The contract with Nuclear Safeguards and Security Systems is for the development of near term  scalable solid state radiation detection systems   These systems will be used to search for and verify the presence or absence of nuclear Weapons of Mass Destruction  improvised nuclear devices  special nuclear materials  radiological dispersion exposure devices and chemical or biological weapons on land  at sea and in the air
