                                                                                                                   Alexander  Corker  Cohen Praise Bill to Identify Vital Energy and Water Projects for Tennessee   9th Congressional District    Water Resources Bill Also Sets Limits on   Wasteful Washington Spending           September 24th  2007   Washington  D C    U S  Senators Lamar Alexander  R TN   Bob Corker  R TN  and U S  Representative Steve Cohen  D TN 09  today announced that the Senate has approved legislation that would authorize repairs and replacements for aging Tennessee dams and locks  revitalize riverfront developments  bolster flood control efforts and aid more than a dozen state water quality projects     This bill is exactly the legislation we need because its sets the appropriate funding limits     Alexander said    The way to keep down wasteful Washington spending is to pass an authorization bill like this one that sets limits on what Congress should spend  Within those limits are vitally important energy and water projects for the people of Tennessee such as wastewater infrastructure projects  keeping Wolf Creek Dam safe and providing clean drinking water in small towns like Blaine  Tennessee        The water related infrastructure projects in this bill help drive commerce across our state  provide essential services to Tennesseans  promote public safety and protect the environment     said Corker     While I wish this legislation established broader efficiency guidelines within the Army Corps process  authorization of these projects is long overdue        Representing a district located on the Mississippi  I realize how important this bill is for the 9th District     said Cohen     I m pleased that this bill provides improvements for a wide range of projects   The bill is fiscally responsible  using resources wisely in the areas of greatest need      The Water Resources Development Act of 2007 authorizes funding for the U S  Army Corps of Engineers and helps provide the Corps with the confidence to make long term contract agreements and allocate their budget efficiently   This legislation must now be sent to the president to be signed into law     Projects in the bill for the Ninth Congressional District include     Requires a study on how to reduce flood damage and provide flood protection along Wolf River and Nonconnah Creek in Memphis  including the repair and replacement of the pumping stations at Cypress Creek  Nonconnah Creek  Ensley  Marble Bayou and Bayou Gayoso    Requires the Corps of Engineers to provide dredging at Pidgeon Industrial Harbor in Memphis    Authorizes the U S  Army Corps of Engineers to provide a study for Howard Road Outfall  Mitch Farm Ditch and Lateral D  and Wolf River Tributaries in Shelby County for emergency streambank protection     5 000 000 for the Upper Mississippi Embayment to coordinate the protection of groundwater supply and groundwater quality     2 5 million for the reconstruction of Nonconnah Weir in Memphis  and    4 million to design and improve Shelby County  s inadequate stormwater runoff system    Alexander sits on the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee  which approved the legislation in April
