                                                                                                                   Alexander  Frist Cosponsor Resolution Honoring Howard Baker          February 17th  2005   WASHINGTON   U S  Sens  Bill Frist  R TN  and Lamar Alexander  R TN  today introduced a Senate resolution honoring retiring U S  Ambassador to Japan and former Tennessee Senator and Senate Majority Leader Howard Baker  The resolution recognizes Ambassador Baker s 30 years of dedicated public service to the president  the United States Congress  and the citizens of Tennessee     Senator Baker has distinguished himself as one of America s most trusted and valued public servants   said Frist   I first met Howard when I was considering my run for the United States Senate  and since then  I ve had the privilege of Senator Baker s wise counsel and friendship  Always sensible  always decent  Senator Baker was a giant in this institution and deeply admired by his colleagues on both sides of the aisle      Howard Baker is the reason I m in public service today  He s the reason there is a two party system in Tennessee   said Alexander   We used to say the best thing about Howard Baker was that when people saw him on TV  he always made Tennesseans look good  We can now say that about the country  When people see Howard Baker around the world  he makes us Americans look even better  He represents the best of us  We welcome him home     Howard H  Baker  Jr  was sworn in as the 26th  13th post WWII  U S  Ambassador to Japan on June 26  2001  Ambassador Baker served in the United States Senate from 1967 until January of 1985  and as President Reagan s Chief of Staff from February 1987 until July of 1988  He announced his retirement as Ambassador of Japan effective today
