                                                                                                                   Senator Alexander Honored at GRAMMYs on the Hill Event           September 8th  2005   Washington  D C     U S  Sen  Lamar Alexander  R TN  was honored by the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences at its Fifth Annual Grammy on the Hill awards September 7 in Washington  Alexander was honored for his lifelong commitment to education and for recognizing the value of the creative community as governor  secretary of education and senator     Lamar Alexander is a true friend to all musical artists     said singer Crystal Gayle  who presented Alexander with his Grammy on the Hill award and performed at the event     From the Carter family in Bristol  to Music City in Nashville  to the blues and gospel of Beale Street  Tennessee is the home of some great American music     said Alexander  who is chairman of the Senate Songwriters   Caucus    Music is an important part of America  s culture and history  and I look forward to continuing to support the music industry          Also honored at last night  s event were Rep  Steny Hoyer of Maryland for his work on behalf of creators   rights and singer Gloria Estefan for her 25 year musical career    Established in 1957  the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences is an organization of musicians  producers  engineers and recording professionals
