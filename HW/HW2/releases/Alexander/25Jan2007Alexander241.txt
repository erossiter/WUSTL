                                                                                                                   Alexander Cosponsors Amendment Providing Flexible Work Schedule Options for Private Sector Employees          January 25th  2007   WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Senator Lamar Alexander  R TN  today cosponsored an amendment offered by Senator Judd Gregg  R NH   the Employee Option Time Act  that would allow private sector employers the ability to offer the same flexible work schedule options to their employees that many public sector employees have     Many Americans are looking for greater flexibility in their work schedules so they can spend more time with their families     Alexander said    This gives employees a way set up more flexible work schedules if they choose to do so       Under the Employee Option Time Act  private sector employees could move up to 10 hours of work from one week to the next  within a two week  80 hour work period  without accruing overtime pay  For example  if an employer offers Employee Option Time  the employee could opt for a schedule where he or she works 48 hours one week to offset only working four 8 hour days during the following week  The Gregg amendment has strong worker protections in place and stresses that participation in a flexible work schedule program would be completely voluntary  If an employee works more hours than agreed to in the Employee Option Time schedule  regardless of when those hours occur  then he or she is entitled to overtime pay  Participation is optional and employees cannot be fired for refusing to participate   Federal employees have had flextime since 1978  but this option is not available to private sector employees as the Fair Labor Standards Act prohibits formalized employee option time
