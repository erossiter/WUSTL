                                                                                                                   Alexander  Frist Applaud Over  1 Million in Head Start Funding for Montgomery County         January 26th  2006   WASHINGTON   U S  Senators Bill Frist  R TN  and Lamar Alexander  R TN  today applauded a Head Start grant of over  1 million through the U S  Department of Health and Human Services  HHS  for the Clarksville Montgomery County Community Action Agency  Inc     I  m pleased that the Department of Health and Human Services continues to support Montgomery County  s children and families     Frist said     We owe the gift of education to all of our children  and Head Start helps ensure that Tennessee  s underserved students are given every opportunity to succeed   Senator Alexander and I appreciate the commitment of HHS to the growth and development of all America  s children         Head Start programs have been beneficial to both communities and participants across Tennessee     Alexander said    I  m very happy to see this investment in Montgomery County  s children by the Department of Health and Human Services  and the Head Start program      The grant is  1 082 443 for the Clarksville Montgomery County Community Action Agency to support Head Start programs     The Head Start program delivers comprehensive and high quality services to foster healthy development in low income children   Head Start and Early Head Start are comprehensive child development programs which serve children from birth to age 5  pregnant women  and their families   They are child focused programs and have the overall goal of increasing the school readiness of young children in low income families
