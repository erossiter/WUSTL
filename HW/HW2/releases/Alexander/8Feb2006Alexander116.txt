                                                                                                                   Alexander  Frist Hail Passage of Resolution Seeking Consultation Between Pentagon and Congress on Guard Restructuring         February 8th  2006   WASHINGTON  D C     U S  Senators Bill Frist  R Tenn   and Lamar Alexander  R Tenn   today hailed the passage of a bipartisan U S  Senate resolution supporting the National Guard and urging that the Department of Defense consult with Congress and governors when considering future changes to the National Guard personnel   Both senators were cosponsors of the measure     The National Guard is an effective and cost efficient military force that has proven itself repeatedly in the War on Terror and at home during countless crises     Frist said     I applaud the Pentagon  s decision not to pursue a reduction in Guard personnel    a decision that is consistent with the reasoning Senator Alexander and I put forward in our recent letter to Secretary Rumsfeld   I look forward to working with my colleagues to ensure the Administration is consulting with Congress to maintain a Guard that is well funded and well equipped to carry out its mission both domestically and abroad   I am confident the Secretary of Defense and his senior officers at the Defense Department share these same goals and views        Thousands of the brave men and women of the Tennessee National Guard have returned from successful missions in Iraq and Afghanistan over the past few months     Alexander said    They also keep us safe at home  by responding to disasters like Hurricane Katrina  A well equipped National Guard is vital to our nation  s security  The Pentagon should consult with Congress and the nation  s governors before restructuring or cutting back our forces      During a January 19 meeting at the Pentagon  Alexander delivered a joint letter with Frist to Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld expressing concerns that Pentagon plans to transform the National Guard could bring reductions in the number of Brigade Combat Teams  such as Tennessee  s 278th Armored Cavalry Regiment   Senate Resolution 355  which passed the Senate unanimously last night  expresses the Senate  s support for the vital missions of the Army and Air National Guards  including support of ongoing missions in Iraq and Afghanistan  and calls for the Department of Defense to        Fully fund the equipment needs of the National Guard         Consult with Congress and the nation  s governors as soon as possible on any proposed changes to the National Guard force structure        Consider the National Guard  s role in homeland defense when considering the National Guard  s force structure  and       Create budget projections that detail cost savings from any National Guard force structure changes  as well as projected costs in the event large personnel increases are necessary to respond to a homeland defense emergency
