SENS. LAUTENBERG, CORZINE ANNOUNCE $202 MILLION FOR N.J. ENVIRONMENT, WATER PROJECTS
  Sens. Frank Lautenberg and Jon Corzine today announced that $202.223 million in projects for the state of New Jersey are included in the final version of the FY 2006 Energy and Water Appropriations bill, which was approved by a joint Senate-House conference committee on Monday.  
   These projects will provide much-needed resources to protect New Jersey's beaches and wetlands and control flooding in the state. 
   "These projects will enhance the quality of life for all New Jerseyans," said Corzine and Lautenberg. "We are proud that we have been able to help secure these projects by working together." 
   New Jersey projects in the FY 2006 Energy and Water bill include: 
   $101 million for the dredging of the New York and New Jersey Harbor. 
   $1.25 million for Hackensack University Medical Center (HUMC) for its Adult Cancer Center (NJ) Funding will go to a new Cancer Center at HUMC. The Adult Cancer Center will be constructed as a "green" building. 
   $250,000 for the College of New Jersey Genomic Analysis Facility (NJ) Funding will go to new Genomic Analysis Facility at the College of New Jersey. This facility will further cancer research at the school. 
   $2.25 million for Joseph G. Minish Waterfront Park in Newark 
   Funding will go to the continued construction of the Waterfront park in Newark. 
   $1 million for the Jersey City Medical Center (NJ) 
   $200,000 for Rancocas Creek, NJ

   $500,000 for Centenary College laboratory (NJ) 
   $300,000 for Meridian Health Riverview Medical Center oncology program (NJ)
