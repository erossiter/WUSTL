SEN. MCCONNELL SECURES OVER $98 MILLION IN FUNDING FOR KENTUCKY IN DEFENSE RELATED APPROPRIATIONS BILLS
  U.S. Senate Majority Whip Mitch McConnell announced today that two Senate Appropriations subcommittees have approved his requests for over $98 million in funding for several Kentucky defense-related projects. The funding, included in the FY'07 Defense Appropriations bill and FY'07 Military Construction and Veterans Affairs (VA) Appropriations bill, is expected to be approved by the Senate Appropriations Committee this week and then will go to the full Senate for consideration. 
   "Kentucky's military installations, defense facilities and universities are helping to bolster national defense," said Senator McConnell. "This funding will be used to conduct important defense-related research and production activities and I am pleased to have secured the money."  
   Senator McConnell, a senior member of the Appropriations Committee, secured the following funding for Kentucky: 
   BAE SYSTEMS in Louisville, formerly United Defense ($46.8 MILLION) - FY'07 Defense 
   * $16.8 million for Mk 45 Mod 5" Gun Mount Overhauls 
   * $12 million for Mk 110 57 mm Naval Gun 
   * $8 million for Mk 295/Mk 296 Ammunition for Mk 110 57 mm Naval Gun 
   * $5 million for the Mk 38 Minor Caliber Gun System 
   * $3 million for Mk 32 Surface Vessel Torpedo Tube Remanufactures 
   * $2 million for the All-Electric Torpedo Launcher 
   RAYTHEON in Louisville ($6.5 MILLION) - FY'07 Defense 
   * $5 million for Phalanx Next Generation 
   * $1.5 million for Rolling Airframe Missile (RAM) Weapon System - Launcher Switching Multiplexing Unit 
   UNIVERSITY OF LOUISVILLE ($8.5 MILLION) - FY'07 Defense 
   * $3 million for the Biometric Signatures Research project 
   * $2 million for the Center for National Security Research - Signature Exploitation project 
   * $2 million for the Bio/Nano-MEMS Center for Defense Applications 
   * $1.5 million for the High Technology Mass Spectrometry Laboratory for the Identification of Chemical Signatures 
   UNIVERSITY OF KENTUCKY (OVER $3 MILLION) - FY'07 Defense 
   * $1 million for the UK partnership with Ft. Knox to develop a Rapidly Deployable Visualization Center 
   * $1 million for the Military Fuels Research Program 
   * $4 million for the Advanced Carbon Nanotechnology Program Consortium (UK is a member and will receive a share of this amount) 
   WESTERN KENTUCKY UNIVERSITY ($2 MILLION) - FY'07 Defense 
   * $2 million for a National Network Security Test Bed 
   FT. KNOX ($3.3 MILLION) - FY'07 Defense 
   * $3.3 million for an Area Surveillance Radar at Godman Airfield 
   OTHER PROJECTS ($16.9 MILLION) - FY'07 Defense 
   * $6 million for the McConnell Technology Training Center (MTTC)/Innovative Productivity Inc. (IPI) in Louisville 
   * $3.5 million for the Kentucky National Guard marijuana eradication efforts in the Daniel Boone National Forest and eastern Kentucky 
   * $3 million for Rapid Wall Breaching Kits (Ensign Bickford in Muhlenberg County) 
   * $3 million for Soldier Portable Solid Fuel Hydrogen Generator Cartridge development (Ensign Bickford in Muhlenberg County) 
   * $1.4 million for development of Weapons Shot Counters (AccuCounter in Kenton County) 
   FORT CAMPBELL ($11.8 MILLION) - FY'07 Military Construction & VA 
   * $11.8 million for construction of a Warrior Rehabilitation and Fitness Center 
   Senator McConnell also successfully secured $360 million in funding for the Assembled Chemical Weapons Alternatives (ACWA) program in the FY'07 Defense and FY'07 Military Construction and VA bills. McConnell fought to keep the President's budget request of $350 million for ACWA in the bills and also secured an additional $10 million in the Military Construction and VA Appropriations bill.    The FY'07 Defense Appropriations bill and FY'07 Military Construction and VA bill must now be approved by the Senate Appropriations Committee.
