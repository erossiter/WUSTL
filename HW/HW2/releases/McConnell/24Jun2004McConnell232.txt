SEN. MCCONNELL SECURES MORE THAN $126 MILLION IN FUNDING FOR KENTUCKY DEFENSE PROJECTS
  Sen. Mitch McConnell announced today that the Senate has approved his request of more than $126 million for Kentucky defense, research, and training projects.  
 The funding is included in the Senate Defense Appropriations bill, which was approved unanimously. The bill now goes to a House/Senate conference for consideration. 
   McConnell, a senior member of the Appropriations Committee, secured the following earmarks in the Senate-passed funding bill: 
   * $79 million for naval gun manufacturing at United Defense and Raytheon in Louisville. 
   * $8 million for naval research at the McConnell Technology Training Center in Louisville. 
   * $8.075 million for defense and research projects at the University of Louisville. 
   * $5 million for the 160th Special Operations Aviation Regiment at Fort Campbell that will be used to protect Chinook helicopters from missile attack. 
   * $3.95 million for Fort Knox for troop training and support. 
   * $2.5 million for defense and research projects at the University of Kentucky. 
   * $3.6 million for the Kentucky National Guard to conduct marijuana eradication in the Daniel Boone National Forest. 
   * $3 million for production of a Weapons Shot Counter for the Special Operations Command by Accu-Counter in Kenton County. 
   * $7 million for development of the Army's Rapid Fuel Transfer System by KECO Industries in Florence. 
   * $6 million for production of an Obstacle Breeching System for the Marine Corps by the Ensign-Bickford Company in Muhlenberg County. 
   "I am pleased that the Senate approved my funding request for these important Kentucky projects," said McConnell. "These facilities are assisting the military by providing much-needed support and research for our troops in the field."
