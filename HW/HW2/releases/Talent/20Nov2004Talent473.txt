SEN. TALENT SECURES $2 MILLION FOR ANTI-BLACKOUT TECHNOLOGY TO BE DEVELOPED AT UNIVERSITY OF MISSOURI-ROLLA
  Sen. Jim Talent (R-Mo.) secured $2 million for the University of Missouri-Rolla's Power Grid Modernization Project in the fiscal 2005 spending package, which was approved today by the Senate.
   "The University of Missouri-Rolla is already a leader in science and engineering and with this funding will allow them to prevent blackouts like the one experienced on August 14, 2003," said Sen. Talent, a member of the Senate Energy Committee. "University of Missouri-Rolla is already a leader in science and engineering and this funding will keep them on the cutting edge of new technologies as they work to modernize our electricity grid."
