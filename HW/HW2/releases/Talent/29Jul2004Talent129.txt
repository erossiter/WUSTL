SEN. TALENT ANNOUNCES FIRE GRANT FOR BERKELEY
  Sen. Jim Talent (R-Mo.) today announced that the Department of Homeland Security has awarded an $11,169 grant for the Berkeley Fire Department in the St.  
 Louis area. The funds will be used for fire prevention and safety.
   "These funds will assist the dedicated firefighters in Berkeley with enhancing fire prevention programs," Sen. Talent said. "The federal government should continue to provide funding opportunities like this for the safety of Missourians. I am very pleased to announce that our firefighters will receive much needed resources to prevent fire related injures."
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant program awards one-year grants directly to local fire departments, enhancing their abilities to respond to fire and fire-related hazards in their communities. For fiscal year 2004, $750 million in firefighter grants are to be distributed by the Department of Homeland Security.
