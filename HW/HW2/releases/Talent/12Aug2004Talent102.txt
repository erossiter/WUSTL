SEN. TALENT, REP. GRAVES ANNOUNCE $13,509 FIRE GRANT FOR BETHANY
  Sen. Jim Talent and Rep. Sam Graves announced that the Department of Homeland Security has awarded a $13,509 grant for the Bethany Fire Department.  
 The funds will be used to for training, wellness and fitness, firefighting equipment, personal protective equipment and modifications to fire stations and facilities.
   "I am pleased to announce that the firefighters that protect the homes and business in Bethany will be receiving these funds," Sen. Talent said. "Enhancing safety programs will provide our first responders with the necessary safety training and equipment to their jobs in the event of a fire. I appreciate the Administration for providing this funding for Harrison County's first responders and for other fire departments across Missouri."
   "I appreciate the service of the firefighters in Bethany," Graves said. "We must invest in their safety and effectiveness and this grant will help supply them with the training and equipment necessary to carry out their duty. Firefighters are trained to handle a wide range of problems and disasters and this grant provides the needed investment to help them meet the challenge."
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant program awards one-year grants directly to local fire departments, enhancing their abilities to respond to fire and fire-related hazards in their communities. For fiscal year 2004, $750 million in firefighter grants are to be distributed by the Department of Homeland Security.
