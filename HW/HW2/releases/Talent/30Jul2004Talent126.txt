SEN. TALENT ANNOUNCES $6 MILLION GRANT FOR MISSOURI TO WEATHERIZE HOMES FOR LOW-INCOME FAMILIES
  Sen. Jim Talent (R-Mo.) today announced that the U.S. Department of Energy has awarded a $6 million grant to the State of Missouri to make home improvements for low-income families.  
 The funds will be used to make homes more energy efficient.
   "I am very pleased to announce this grant for Missouri," said Sen. Talent, a member of the Energy Committee. "These resources will be used to help lower energy costs for low-income homeowners while conserving energy resources for everyone. Through the Weatherization Assistance Program, Missouri families can make necessary improvements to make their homes more energy efficient."
   The Congressional appropriation for the Weatherization Assistance Program is $227.166 million in FY 2004 which is expected to cover approximately 94,450 homes, which Missouri share is an estimated $6 million. The funding this year is up slightly from the $223.5 million in FY 2003. The President has requested $291.2 million for FY 2005.
   Additional information about the grant program is available online at http://www.eere.energy.gov/weatherization.
