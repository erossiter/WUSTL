SENS. TALENT, BOND ANNOUNCE $49,500 FIRE GRANT FOR BENNETT SPRING
  Sens. Jim Talent and Kit Bond today announced that the Department of Homeland Security has awarded a $49,500 grant to the Bennett Spring Fire Protection District.  
 The funds will be used to purchase replacement gear, bunker gear and portable radios.
   "I am very pleased to announce that the Bennett Spring Fire Protection District will be receiving this funding," Sen. Talent said. "This grant will allow the department to purchase new safety equipment to enhance service for the area in the event of a fire. I appreciate the Administration for providing resources for training and supplies for firefighters serving Laclede County."
   "For firefighters all over the state, the new money for firefighting vehicles and safety will make a real difference in their ability to protect local citizens," said Sen. Kit Bond. "Our first responders are our first line of defense against any disaster, from fires to traffic accidents to oil spills to terrorism. It is important that we equip them with the resources they need to do their jobs."
   "I am so pleases we received this funding," said Frank Bellew, Secretary/Treasurer of the Board of Directors of the Bennett Spring Fire Protection District. "This grant will help us stay safe and help people. I want to thank everyone involved in helping us get this grant."
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant program awards one-year grants directly to local fire departments, enhancing their abilities to respond to fire and fire-related hazards in their communities. For fiscal year 2004, $750 million in firefighter grants are to be distributed by the Department of Homeland Security.
