SEN. TALENT ANNOUNCES USDA RURAL DEVELOPMENT GRANT FOR CLARK COUNTY
  Sen. Jim Talent (R-Mo.) today announced that the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA), Rural Development, has approved awarded a $99,900 Rural Business Enterprise Grant to the City of Wayland.  
 These funds will be used to construct a new water and sewer infrastructure system.
   Sen. Talent, a member of the Senate Agricultural Committee, said, "I am very pleased to announce that the City of Wayland will be receiving this funding. Having access to efficient water systems is essential to recruit new businesses to Clark County. I applaud USDA Rural Development for its commitment to meeting local needs in rural Missouri."
   The new water system will include construction of a new water and sewer infrastructure system to serve new business in the rural community of Wayland. The new business will create approximately 16 new jobs. The purpose of the Rural Business Enterprise Grant is to assist local entities finance and facilitate the development of small, emerging private business enterprises, provide technical assistance, and establish revolving loan programs.
   Additional information about the Rural Business Enterprise program is available at: http://www.rurdev.usda.gov.
