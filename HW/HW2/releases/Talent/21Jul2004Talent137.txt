SEN. TALENT ANNOUNCES FIRE GRANT FOR GOLDEN CITY
  Sen. Jim Talent (R-Mo.) today announced that the Department of Homeland Security has awarded a $5,544 grant for the Golden City Fire Department.  
 The funds will be used for operations and firefighter safety.
   "I am very pleased to announce that the Golden City fire department will be receiving these necessary funds for training and equipment," Sen. Talent said. "Ensuring the safety of our communities on the local level by working with fire departments across the state of Missouri is a priority for the federal government. I am very pleased that with these funds, the citizens of Barton County, and surrounding areas, will be better protected in the event of a fire."
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant program awards one-year grants directly to local fire departments, enhancing their abilities to respond to fire and fire-related hazards in their communities. For fiscal year 2004, $750 million in firefighter grants are to be distributed by the Department of Homeland Security.
