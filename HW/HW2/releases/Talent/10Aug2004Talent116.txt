SEN. TALENT ANNOUNCES USDA RURAL DEVELOPMENT LOAN FOR STODDARD COUNTY
  Sen. Jim Talent (R-Mo.) today announced that the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA), Rural Development, has approved a $42,500 Community Facility Direct Loan to the Stoddard County Development Foundation.  
 The funds will be used to complete the purchase of eight warning sirens.
   Sen. Talent, a member of the Senate Agricultural Committee said, "I am very pleased to announce that the Stoddard County Development Foundation will be receiving this loan. These new warning sirens will help protect the citizens of Stoddard County by alerting them in the event of threatening weather. I appreciate USDA Rural Development for their continued commitment to safety in Southeast Missouri."
   The new countywide warning system will have sirens in Advance, Bernie, Bell City, Bloomfield, Dudley, Essex, Puxico and Dexter. In FY 2003, they had previously received two grants totaling $123, 600. Community Programs, a division of the Rural Housing Service, is part of the United States Department of Agriculture's Rural Development mission area. Community Programs administers programs designed to develop essential community facilities for public use in rural areas, such as schools and healthcare facilities.
   Additional information about the Community Programs is available at: http://www.rurdev.usda.gov/rhs/cf/brief_cp_direct.htm.
