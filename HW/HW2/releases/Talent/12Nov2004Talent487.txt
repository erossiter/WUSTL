SEN. TALENT ANNOUNCES $33,570 FIRE GRANT FOR MT. VERNON
  Sen. Jim Talent today announced that the Department of Homeland Security has awarded a $33,570 grant to the Mt.  
 Vernon Rural Fire Association Inc. The funds will be used to purchase personal protection equipment.
   "I am very pleased to announce that the Mt. Vernon Rural Fire Association Inc. will be receiving this funding," Sen. Talent said. "This grant will allow the department to purchase new safety equipment to enhance service for the area in the event of a fire. I appreciate the Administration for providing resources for training and supplies for firefighters serving Lawrence County."
   "We're quite pleased to be receiving this funding," said Brad DeLay, Assistant Fire Chief. "The Department of Homeland Security grants are a great way for our rural fire departments to supplement income where otherwise we might not be able to." 
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant program awards one-year grants directly to local fire departments, enhancing their abilities to respond to fire and fire-related hazards in their communities. For fiscal year 2004, $750 million in firefighter grants are to be distributed by the Department of Homeland Security.
