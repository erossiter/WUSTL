SEN. TALENT ANNOUNCES $49,380 FIRE GRANT FOR CLAYTON
  Sen. Jim Talent (R-Mo.) today announced that the Department of Homeland Security has awarded a $49,380 grant for the Clayton Fire Department.  
 The funds will be used for training, wellness and fitness, firefighting equipment, personal protective equipment, and modifications to fire stations and facilities.
   "I am very pleased to announce that the Clayton Fire Department will be receiving this funding," Sen. Talent said. "This grant will allow the department to purchase new safety equipment to enhance service for the area in the event of a fire. I appreciate the Administration for providing resources for training and supplies for firefighters serving St. Louis County."
   he Assistance to Firefighters Grant program awards one-year grants directly to local fire departments, enhancing their abilities to respond to fire and fire-related hazards in their communities. For fiscal year 2004, $750 million in firefighter grants are to be distributed by the Department of Homeland Security.
