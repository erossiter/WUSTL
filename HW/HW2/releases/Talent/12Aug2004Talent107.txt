SEN. TALENT ANNOUNCES $5,118 FIRE GRANT FOR FAIR PLAY
  Sen. Jim Talent (R-Mo.) today announced that the Department of Homeland Security has awarded a $5,118 grant for the Fair Play Fire Department.  
 The funds will be used for training, wellness and fitness, firefighting equipment, personal protective equipment, and modifications to fire stations and facilities.
   "I am very pleased to announce that the Fair Play Fire Department will be receiving this funding," Sen. Talent said. "This grant will allow the department to purchase new safety equipment to enhance service for Fair Play and surrounding areas in the event of a fire. I appreciate the Administration for providing resources for training and supplies for the firefighters in Polk County so they can do their jobs as safely and effectively as possible."
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant program awards one-year grants directly to local fire departments, enhancing their abilities to respond to fire and fire-related hazards in their communities. For fiscal year 2004, $750 million in firefighter grants are to be distributed by the Department of Homeland Security.
