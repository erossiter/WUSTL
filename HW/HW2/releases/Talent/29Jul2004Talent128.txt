SEN. TALENT ANNOUNCES FIRE GRANT FOR KANSAS CITY
  Sen. Jim Talent (R-Mo.) today announced that the Department of Homeland Security has awarded a $64,127 grant for the Kansas City Fire Department.  
 The funds will be used for fire prevention and safety programs.
   "I am pleased to announce that the Kansas City fire department will be receiving this important funding," Sen. Talent said. "Creating and developing new programs that continue to ensure the safety of Kansas City residents is vital to the area. The federal government should continue to have a proactive role in providing the necessary resources to local fire departments so they can do their job and prevent fire-related injuries."
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant program awards one-year grants directly to local fire departments, enhancing their abilities to respond to fire and fire-related hazards in their communities. For fiscal year 2004, $750 million in firefighter grants are to be distributed by the Department of Homeland Security.
