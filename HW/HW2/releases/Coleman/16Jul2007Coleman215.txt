SEN. COLEMAN ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR AIRPORTS IN SAUK CENTRE, LONG PRAIRIE, PINE RIVER, BRAINERD AND WALKER
  Senator Norm Coleman today announced that local airports in Sauk Centre, Long Prairie, Pine River, Brainerd and Walker have been selected to receive federal grants from the U.S. Department of Transportation's Federal Aviation Administration (FAA).  
   "Local airports are a source of economic opportunity for rural areas and are an important part of our state's transportation infrastructure," said Coleman. "During my visits across Minnesota, community leaders collectively stress the need to improve and expand local airports. Each of these airports is an important part of the local economy for their communities and I applaud the FAA for awarding these grants." 
   Specific funding projects are as follows: 
   $104,500 for Sauk Centre Municipal for the construction of general aviation hangar 
   $72,500 for Todd Field in Long Prairie to rehabilitate a runway 
   $148,200 for Pine River Regional to install a perimeter fence, acquire snow removal equipment, rehabilitate a runway and update the airport master plan study 
   $230, 818 for Brainerd Lakes Regional to acquire snow removal equipment to enhance operation and safety during winter months 
   $327,075 for Walker Municipal to design the extension of a runway, rehabilitate the apron, rehabilitate the taxiway and remove obstructions 
   The FAA's Airport Improvement Program provides grants to public and private airports to promote the development and modernization of local airports. Funds for these projects generally go toward airport safety, capacity, security, and environmental concerns. 
   For more information, please visit http://www.faa.gov/airports_airtraffic/airports/aip.
