DOD APPROPRIATIONS BILL PROVIDES OVER $447 BILLION FOR MILITARY DEPARTMENTS, AGENCIES
  In a move that will bolster the safety efforts aimed at protecting our citizens both at home and abroad, Sen. Norm Coleman today praised the Senate's passage of the FY07 Department of Defense Appropriations Conference Report. The bill provides $447.4 billion in funding for our nation's military departments and agencies, National Guard, and the War on Terror. Coleman secured $39.4 million in funding for defense projects in Minnesota. Additionally, this bill will fund a 2.2 percent across-the-board pay raise for military personnel. 
   "Now more than ever, bolstering our troops' ability to defend and protect our citizens is of the utmost importance," Coleman said. "I am pleased that we were finally able to pass this bill so that we can provide our troops with the necessary resources they need to fight the War on Terror and provide themselves with adequate protection."  
   Provisions of the appropriations bill include: 
   * $70 billion in emergency supplemental funding to support ongoing War on Terror operations in Iraq and Afghanistan. This supplemental includes $2.9 billion for equipping the Army National Guard and the Army Reserve, $1.1 billion for body armor and personal protection equipment, $1.5 billion for the Afghanistan Security Forces Fund and $1.7 billion for the Iraq Security Forces Fund. 
   * $80.96 billion for Procurement of military equipment, which will include significant spending on military aircraft, shipping building, and the development of weapons and combat vehicles; 
   * $75.72 billion for Research, Development initiatives which includes projects in Minnesota. 
   "Through cutting-edge research, the development of new defense technologies, and the construction of new military structures, we can better ensure that our military is equipped with the tools necessary to perform their duty. I am pleased that we were able to secure over $39 million for Minnesota companies who are leading that effort," Coleman added. 
   Among the Minnesota-based defense projects secured by Sen. Coleman are: 
   * $4.5 million to Minnesota Thermal to purchase Golden Hour Containers for the transportation of blood by Special Operations medics in all of their missions 
  * $4 million for PEMSTAR to develop the Land Warrior, an integrated fighting system for individual infantry soldiers 
   * $4 million for Lockheed Martin to fund a Common Submarine Radio Room, which provides an automated, high capacity interoperable communications systems across submarine classes 
   * $3.25 million to MTS for a Rotorcraft Survivability project that will include a test system to simulate realistic combat conditions for helicopters 
   * $2.9 million for Third Wave Systems to support cost-saving titanium machining technology 
   * $2.4 million to Gradient Technology to fund a demilitarization program 
   * $2.4 million for SpeechGear to develop an English-Iraqi instant language translation system 
   * $2.2 million to Polaris to provide light tactical vehicles to the Army that are useful in rugged, off-road terrain 
   * $2 million to the University of Minnesota to create a National Hypersonic Research Center 
   * $1.8 million for Humanetics to develop a vaccine for radiation 
   * $1.35 million for Victory Systems to develop a functional Unmanned Aerial Vehicle system 
   * $1.3 million to Medafor to produce invasive hemostatic agents 
   * $1.3 million for a Joint U.S./Norwegian Telemedicine Program 
   * $1 million to Phygen for a plasma sterilizer to allow for cold sterilization of medical instruments on the battlefield 
   * $1 million to Twin Star Medical to develop ultrafiltration catheter technology for the prevention of compartment syndrome in battlefield injuries 
   * $1 million to Sensera to develop an early and rapid analyzer for diagnosing heart attacks 
   * $1 million to MATTRACKS to develop a track conversion system for light-wheeled vehicles 
   * $1 million for Primordial to develop off-road navigation software that will enhance the decision-making capability and safety of our soldiers navigating through unfamiliar and dangerous terrain 
   * $1 million for Honeywell to produce navigation and control devices for precision guided weapons
