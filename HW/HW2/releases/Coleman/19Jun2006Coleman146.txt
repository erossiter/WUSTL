SEN. COLEMAN ANNOUNCES $1,091,927 FOR SOUTHERN MINNESOTA AIRPORTS
  Sen. Norm Coleman today announced that Houston County Airport, Pipestone Municipal Airport, Waseca Municipal Airport, and New Ulm Municipal Airport will receive grants totaling $1,091,927 from the United States Department of Transportation's Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) as part of the FAA's Airport Improvement Program.  
   "It is with great pleasure that I announce these airports in southern Minnesota have been selected to receive these grants," said Coleman. "Local airports are an important part of our state's transportation infrastructure and a source of economic opportunity for rural areas, and these grants represent a commitment to these communities by ensuring these airports will be able to operate safely and efficiently. I am delighted Houston County, Pipestone, Waseca, and New Ulm have received this funding, and extend my thanks to the FAA for recognizing the importance of this funding in rural Minnesota." 
   The FAA's Airport Improvement Program provides grants to public and private airports to promote the development and modernization of local airports. Funds for these projects generally go toward airport safety, capacity, security and environmental concerns. 
   Specific funding projects are as follows: 
   * $343,000 for Houston County Airport to acquire land for development, conduct a storm water planning study, and construct a fuel farm and terminal building. 
   * $301,577 for Pipestone Municipal Airport to construct a terminal building to provide all-weather protection for the flying public. 
   * $265,200 for Waseca Municipal Airport to construct a terminal building to provide all-weather protection for the flying public. 
   * $182,150 for New Ulm Municipal Airport to conduct an environmental assessment for the proposed construction of a crosswind runway.
