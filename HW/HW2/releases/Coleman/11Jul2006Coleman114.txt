SEN. COLEMAN ANNOUNCES $331,700 FOR AIRPORTS IN ST. CLOUD, WILLMAR, LONG PRAIRIE
  Sen. Norm Coleman today announced local airports in St. Cloud, Willmar, and Long Prairie have been awarded a combined $331,700 in federal grant funding from the United States Department of Transportation's Federal Aviation Administration (FAA). The grants will be aimed at upgrading the airports through improvements such as installing perimeter fencing, land development and storage expansion.  
   "Local airports continue to be an extremely valuable resource across the state and I am pleased these central Minnesota airports were selected for this funding," said Coleman. "From greater access for residents and visitors, to the potential for future economic development, these airports are important to their communities and I applaud the FAA for recognizing their value by awarding these grants." 
   Specific funding for the individual projects is as follows: 
   * $100,000 for the City of St. Cloud to acquire land for development at St. Cloud Regional Airport. The grant will also provide environmental assessments for future enhancements and airport expansion. 
   * $150,000 for the City of Willmar to expand the airport apron to enhance capacity for aircraft operations at Willmar Municipal. 
   * $81,700 for the City of Long Prairie to install a perimeter fence to enhance security and safety at Todd Field.
