SEN. COLEMAN ENCOURAGES MINNESOTANS CONCERNED OVER FRIENDS, FAMILY IN LEBANON TO CONTACT STATE DEPARTMENT
  With the conflict and tensions in Lebanon continuing to escalate, Sen. Norm Coleman announced that Americans are being urged not to travel to the troubled country and that Minnesotans concerned for family or friends living in Lebanon should contact the U.S. State Department by phone or over the Internet. Updated information on travel and security in Lebanon may be obtained from the State Department by calling 1-888-407-4747. In addition, American citizens may notify U.S. authorities of individuals currently in Lebanon by going online and registering them at https://travelregistration.state.gov/ibrs.  
   "The situation unfolding in Lebanon is a serious one and I am deeply concerned for the safety of American citizens in the region," Coleman said. "If you are concerned about a loved one or friend in Lebanon, I encourage you to contact the State Department and register their presence with the U.S. Embassy in Beirut." 
   The U.S. Embassy in Beirut has been granted authorized departure status. Family members and non-emergency American employees have permission to depart Lebanon. In addition, American citizens are being urged to strongly consider departing in light of the unstable situation in Lebanon.
