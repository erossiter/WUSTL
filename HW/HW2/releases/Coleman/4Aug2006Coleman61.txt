SEN. COLEMAN ANNOUNCES $21,350 FOR CITY OF MADISON
  Sen. Norm Coleman today announced the City of Madison has been awarded a $21,350 grant from the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) Rural Development program. The funding will be used to help the city purchase a new ambulance.  
   "I am pleased residents in and around Madison will receive the benefit of a new ambulance," Coleman said. "In towns across Greater Minnesota, first responders go to great lengths to protect their neighbors, and they deserve the best resources available. I am pleased USDA Rural Development understood this need in Madison, and I applaud them for awarding this grant." 
   The USDA Rural Development's mission is to help rural individuals, communities and businesses obtain the financial and technical assistance they need in order to address their diverse and unique needs. 
   Since, 2001, the Rural Development program has invested over $2.5 billion throughout Minnesota for a variety of rural development projects.
