SEN. COLEMAN ANNOUNCES $210,433 FOR SOUTHERN MINNESOTA FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. Norm Coleman today announced that four fire departments in southern Minnesota have received $210,433 in funding as part of the Assistance to Firefighters Grants (AFG) Program, which is designed to provide resources for the firefighting and emergency response needs of fire departments and nonaffiliated emergency medical services organizations. The grants will be used to improve the departments' operations, resources, and safety measures.  
   "I am pleased the Assistance to Firefighters Grants Program has chosen to help these important fire departments in Minnesota improve their emergency capabilities," said Coleman. "Improving resources for local fire departments is critical to the safety of citizens, and this funding will empower our brave firefighters to protect the residents in and around these areas." 
   Specific fire department grants awarded are as follows: 
   * Magnolia Volunteer Fire Department received $104,500 for Vehicle Acquisition; 
   * Elysian Fire Department received $19,950 for Operations and Safety; 
   * Hanley Falls Fire Department received $81,166 for Operations and Safety; 
   * Ridgeway Fire Department received $4,817 for Operations and Safety. 
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, administered within the Department of Homeland Security, awards grants directly to local fire departments to support firefighters, first responders, and the services they deliver. For more information on the program, please visit www.firegrantsupport.com/afg/.
