SEN. COLEMAN OBTAINS FUNDING FOR MINNESOTA PROJECTS IN FISCAL 2007 LABOR, HHS AND EDUCATION APPROPRIATIONS BILL
  Underscoring the importance of programs and agencies like Head Start and the National Institute of Health to Minnesotans and all Americans, Sen. Norm Coleman today applauded the Senate Appropriations Committee for approving the fiscal year 2007 Labor, Health and Human Services and Education Appropriations bill. Coleman was successful in allocating funding for Minnesota projects, which focus on medical and veteran community service centers. The bill now awaits consideration by the full Senate.  
   "One of our responsibilities to the folks in Minnesota and around the country is to support and equip programs that offer quality education, good health care, and effective job training," said Coleman. "The resources provided by this legislation will help to ensure that citizens have access to the basic tools necessary to improve and sustain their quality of life." 
   Key Minnesota projects in the legislation include: 
   * $150,000 for a Veteran's Re-Entry Education Program to secure civilian reemployment of all separating veterans in Minnesota, with special attention given to younger veterans and those who are medically discharged or disabled; 
   * $300,000 for All Children Excel programs to reduce problem behaviors, increase school participation and success, and to prevent participants from entering the juvenile justice system 
   * $100,000 for Jewish Children and Family Services of Minneapolis to fund a Medical Alley/MNBIO Medical Device Bioscience Industry Learning Center; and 
   * $150,000 for the Duchenne's Muscular Dystrophy Center at the University of Minnesota to help fund instrumentation and construct laboratory facilities within a new building adjacent to the Center for Magnetic Resonance Research on the Twin Cities campus.
