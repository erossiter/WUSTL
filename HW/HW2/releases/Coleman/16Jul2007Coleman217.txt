SEN. COLEMAN ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR AIRPORTS IN HIBBING, CLOQUET, COOK AND ORR
  Senator Norm Coleman today announced that local airports in Hibbing, Cloquet, Cook and Orr have been selected to receive federal grants from the U.S. Department of Transportation's Federal Aviation Administration (FAA).  
   "In northern Minnesota, where distance between many communities and regional or statewide hubs is large, local airports play an extremely valuable role," said Coleman. "Each of these airports is important economical assets to their respective communities, and these grants will help ensure their safety and operability. I am pleased each of these airports was selected for this funding, and I applaud the FAA for recognizing the importance of each of them." 
   Specific funding projects are as follows: 
   $305,900 for Chisholm-Hibbing Airport for taxiway rehabilitation, design of a taxiway extension and land acquisition to prevent the encroachment of obstructions to the airplane's approach. 
   $38,000 for Cloquet Carlton County to acquire snow removal equipment in order to enhance safety and operability during winter months. 
   $126,249 for Cook Municipal to acquire snow removal equipment and conduct a study to analyze a future runway extension 
   $441,750 for Orr Regional to acquire snow removal equipment, construct a general aviation hangar and install a visual guidance system for arriving aircraft. 
   The FAA's Airport Improvement Program provides grants to public and private airports to promote the development and modernization of local airports. Funds for these projects generally go toward airport safety, capacity, security, and environmental concerns. 
   For more information, please visit http://www.faa.gov/airports_airtraffic/airports/aip.
