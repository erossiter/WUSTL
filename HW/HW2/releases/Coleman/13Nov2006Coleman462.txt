SEN. COLEMAN ANNOUNCES $ $1.6 MILLION IN FUNDING FOR SEVEN NORTHERN MINNESOTA FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. Norm Coleman today announced that seven fire departments in northern Minnesota have received grants totaling $1,604,785 as part of the Assistance to Firefighters Grants (AFG) Program, which is designed to provide resources for the firefighting and emergency response needs of fire departments and nonaffiliated emergency medical services organizations. The grants will be used to improve the departments' operations, resources, and safety measures.  
   "I am pleased that the Assistance to Firefighters Grants Program has chosen to help these important fire departments in Minnesota improve their emergency capabilities," said Coleman. "Improving resources for local fire departments is critical to the safety of citizens, and this funding will further empower our brave firefighters to protect the residents in and around these communities." 
   Specific fire department grants awarded are as follows: 
   * St. Martin Fire & Rescue received $78,351 for Operations and Safety; 
   * Brooten Fire Department received $152,808 for Vehicle Acquisition; 
   * Backus Volunteer Fire Department received $19,681 for Operations and Safety; 
   * Cohasset Fire Department received $427,680 for Operations and Safety; 
   * Crooked Lake Township Volunteer Fire Department received $21,717 for Operations and Safety; 
   * Elbow - Tulaby Lakes Volunteer Fire Department Inc received $871,944 for Operations and Safety; 
   * County Emergency Medical Services, based in Fertile, received $32,604 for Operations and Safety. 
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, administered within the Department of Homeland Security, awards grants directly to local fire departments to support firefighters, first responders, and the services they deliver. For more information on the program, please visit www.firegrantsupport.com/afg/.
