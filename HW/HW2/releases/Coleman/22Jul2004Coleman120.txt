SENATE PASSES DOD APPROPRIATIONS CONFERENCE REPORT; INCLUDES $123 MILLION IN MILITARY, DEFENSE PROJECTS SECURED BY SEN. COLEMAN FOR MINNESOTA
  Sen. Norm Coleman today joined a majority of his colleagues in passing the Fiscal Year 2005 Defense Appropriations Bill, which includes more than $123 million for Minnesota projects secured by Coleman. 
   "This funding not only benefits our communities but it allows us to move forward with important projects meant to keep our country safe," Coleman said. "Minnesota is the home for some pretty advanced and important technology and this funding will help that technology see the light of day."
   Several defense projects in the Twin Cities and in greater Minnesota will benefit from this funding, including:
   * $8 million for Honeywell's military aircraft and missile navigation systems.
   * $19.3 million for Cray's super computers.
   * $8 million for Lockheed Martin to update communications equipment in submarines.
   * $10.2 million for Goodrich's surveillance system that provides additional security for military ships sitting in port.
   * $9 million for Polaris' work on light-weight, tactical all-terrain vehicles used by U.S. Special Operations.
   * $8.6 million to help 3M's development of medical records technology for military hospitals and $6 million for the company's equipment to detect biological contamination.
   * $2 million for Aeromet to develop military aircraft components.
   * $2 million for SAGE to continue research on electrochromic windows technology.
   * $2 million to help the University of Minnesota establish the National Hypersonic Research Center and
   * $3.5 million for the university's work on muscle and motor nerve research.
   * $1 million for MATTRACKS' track conversion systems.
