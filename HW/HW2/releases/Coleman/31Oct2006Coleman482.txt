SEN. COLEMAN ANNOUNCES $2,231,000 GRANT TO EXPAND SENIOR HOUSING IN MORA
  Sen. Norm Coleman today announced that Horizon Health Inc in Mora, Minnesota has been awarded a grant of $2,231,000 from the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) as part of a grant program to support housing development for low income elderly residents. The grant will be used to construct 22 one-bedroom units and one two-bedroom unit for a resident manager. The grant also includes a $242,100 three-year rental subsidy to make the new units affordable for low income elderly residents.  
   "This funding will provide low income seniors with access to available and affordable housing," said Coleman. "I applaud HUD and Horizon Health for their combined vision and efforts to ensure that our state's seniors are given access to the basic necessities that they deserve." 
   HUD also awarded grants to two other Minnesota companies as part of its program to support housing development for low income elderly residents. The Ebenezer Society in Wyoming, MN received $3,415,500, while Accessible Space in Albert Lea received $2,334,500.
