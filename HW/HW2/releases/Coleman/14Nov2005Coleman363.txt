SEN. COLEMAN TO SPEAK ON RURAL RENAISSANCE AT 118TH ANNUAL MEETING OF NATIONAL ASSOCIATION OF STATE UNIVERSITIES AND LAND GRANT COLLEGES
  Sen. Norm Coleman, member of the Committee on Agriculture, Nutrition, and Forestry, will address the 118th Annual Meeting of NASULGC tomorrow morning on the importance of his Rural Renaissance Act as it relates to the public universities' mission to align with the goals of the act.  
 The Rural Renaissance Act is legislation to establish a private-public partnership to provide rural development bonds and grants to critical rural infrastructure problems. NASULGC is the nation's oldest higher education association and represents 215 of the major public universities. The focus of tomorrow's meeting is "The Role of Engagement at Public Universities: Lessons Learned." 
   WHEN: Tuesday, November 15, 2005 8:30 AM EST, (Q & A to follow) 
   WHERE: Hilton Washington and Towers, 1900 Connecticut Ave NW, Washington D.C. 
   SESSION PARTICIPANTS: 
   Presidents, Chancellors, and senior administrators of NASULGC's member institutions.
   Contact: Andrea Wuebker, 651/645-0323.
