SEN. COLEMAN SECURES FUNDING FOR KEY MINNESOTA PROJECTS IN TRANSPORTATION, TREASURY, JUDICIARY, AND HUD APPROPRIATIONS BILL
  Sen. Norm Coleman today announced the inclusion of critical state funding in the fiscal year 2007 Transportation, Treasury, Judiciary, and Housing and Urban Development (HUD) Appropriations bill which was approved today by the Senate Appropriations Committee. Coleman secured nearly $14 million in funding for key Minnesota initiatives to improve transportation infrastructure and construct and expand economic development initiative projects for housing Minnesota's homeless and providing help to Minnesota children facing emotional crises. The bill now awaits consideration by the full Senate.  
   "This funding is essential to improving Minnesota's transportation network by keeping drivers safe, congestion low, and business and industry competitive," said Coleman. "The bill also provides the resources needed to build and maintain essential housing and community development projects across the state. By paying attention to the most basic aspects of our state's infrastructure such as transportation and housing, we will be able to continue improving Minnesota's high quality of life and economic competitiveness." 
   Funding for Minnesota transportation projects secured by Coleman include: 
   * $2,000,000 for Trunk Highway 14 between Waseca and Owatonna 
   * $1,000,000 for the Trunk Highway 610 Corridor from Trunk Highway 169 in Brooklyn Park to I-94 in Maple Grove 
   * $1,000,000 for the Northstar Commuter Rail project 
   * $3,700,000 for Cedar Avenue Bus Rapid Transit in Dakota County 
   * $1,350,000 for Central Corridor bus funding in Ramsey County 
   * $750,000 for interstate maintenance on Interstate 94 between Barnesville and Highway 336 
   * $1,000,000 for the Glencoe Railroad Mitigation Project 
   * $2,000,000 for Minnesota Valley Regional Rail Authority Rehabilitation Improvements 
   * Funding for Housing and Urban Development projects secured by Coleman include: 
   * $200,000 for the Red Lake Criminal Justice Complex 
   * $200,000 for the Minnesota Housing Finance Agency in St. Paul, MN 
   * $200,000 Mesabi Academy Kids Peace in Buhl, MN 
   * $200,000 for Sheriff's Youth Programs in Rochester, MN 
   * $200,000 for the Audubon Center of the Northwoods in Sandstone, MN
