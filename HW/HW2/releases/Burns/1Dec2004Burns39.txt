SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES FUNDING FOR MONTANA FIRE DISTRICTS
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R-Mont.) today announced that five of Montana's fire stations were named recipients in this year's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Awards program.  
 In this round of funding, $273,734 have been awarded to the following fire districts: 
   * Hilger Volunteer Rural Fire Department, $6,491 
  * City of Missoula Fire Department, $81,236 
  * Pendroy Volunteer Fire and Rescue, $72,450 
  * Ronan Volunteer Fire Department, $54,360 
  * Livingston Fire and Rescue, $59,197 
   "Our fire fighters face challenges that involve their own safety and the safety of the citizens they serve, and these funds will help them meet that challenge," said Burns. "These vital resources directly impact our communities by helping firefighters respond safely and effectively to all emergencies." 
   The U.S. Fire Administration, a division of FEMA, administers the Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program. The program assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. A list of all current award recipients announced to date is posted on the USFA website at www.usfa.fema.gov/grants.
