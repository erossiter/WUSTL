SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES FUNDS TO SUPPORT MONTANA FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R-Mont.) today announced $314,424 in funding from the Department of Homeland Security's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program to go towards operations and firefighter safety.  
   "Montana's firefighters perform life-threatening services for us day in and day out. They deserve the very best in training and equipment, so I'm happy to see this money making its way out to our state," said Burns. "It will improve their ability to do their job safely and effectively, and I'll continue working to make sure our communities have all they need to be safer from the dangers of fire in the future." 
   The following fire departments received funding in this round of grants: 
   * Columbus Rural Fire District #3 - $40,320 
  * Gateway Hose Co. in Gardiner - $18,547 
  * Manhattan Rural Fire Dept. - $60,903 
  * Florence Volunteer Fire Dept - $10,800 
  * Lewistown Fire Dept. - $80,354 
  * Whitefish Fire Dept. - $103,500 
   The U.S. Fire Administration, a division of FEMA, administers the Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program. The program assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. A list of all current award recipients announced to date is posted on the USFA website at: http://www.usfa.fema.gov/grants.
