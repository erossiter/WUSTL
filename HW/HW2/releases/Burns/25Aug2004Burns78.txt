SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES FUNDS FOR MONTANA FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R-Mont.) today announced five local Montana fire departments will soon be awarded a total of $344,777 from the Department of Homeland Security's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program.  
 These funds are directly aimed at improving the proficiency and safety of each fire department. 
   "I am pleased to see that the diligence and valor of Montana's firefighters will not go unnoticed," said Burns. "As long as these brave men and women continue to ensure the safety of Montanans, I will continue working to make sure they have the funds they need to do their jobs successfully." 
   The following fire departments will receive funding in this round of grants; 
   * Seeley Lake Rural Fire Department will receive $6,260 for operations and firefighter safety. 
  * Glasgow Volunteer Fire Department will receive $52,920 for operations and firefighter safety. 
  * Miles City Fire Department will receive $125,487 for operations and fire safety. 
  * Amsterdam Rural Fire Department in Manhattan will receive $52,900 for operations and firefighter safety. 
  * Dawson County Rural Fire Department in Glendive will receive $125,000 for a firefighting vehicle. 
   The U.S. Fire Administration, a division of FEMA, administers the Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program. The program assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. A list of all the current award recipients announced to date is posted on the USFA website at: http://www.usfa.fema.gov/grants.
