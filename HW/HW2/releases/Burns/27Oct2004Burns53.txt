SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES MONTANA FIRE DEPARTMENT GRANTS
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R-Mont.) announced today that 11 fire departments and districts in Montana will receive a total of $856,231 in funding through the Department of Homeland Security's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program.  
   "Fighting fire is not an easy job, and folks who face these blazes day in and day out need the proper equipment and training to succeed in their job," said Burns. "They need to be prepared to fight fires in a safe and effective manner, and these grants help make sure they can. I am glad to see 11 grants come to our state in this round, and I look forward to working to make sure all our firefighters across the state have what they need to succeed." 
   The following fire departments and districts will receive funding for operations and fire safety: 
   * Ferndale Volunteer Fire Department in Bigfork, $33,930 
   * Lakeside Volunteer Fire Department in East Helena, $58,349 
   * Bridger Canyon Rural Fire District in Bozeman, $31,199 
   * Missoula Rural Fire District, $178,443 
   * Fairfield Volunteer Fire Department, $85,779 
   * Pinesdale Volunteer Fire Department, $16,361 
   * Ekalaka Volunteer Fire Department, $27,900 
   * Augusta Volunteer Fire Department, $30,600 
   The following fire departments will receive funding for new firefighting vehicles: 
   * Wibaux Volunteer Fire Department, $157,500 
   * Broadus Volunteer Fire Department, $175,500 
   * Lavina Volunteer Fire Department, $60,750 
   The U.S. Fire Administration, a division of FEMA, administers the Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program. The program assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. A list of all current award recipients announced to date is posted on the USFA website at: http://www.usfa.fema.gov/grants.
