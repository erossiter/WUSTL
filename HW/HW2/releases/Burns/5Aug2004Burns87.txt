SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES FUNDS FOR MONTANA FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R-Mont.) today announced another round of funding in this year's Assistance to Firefighters Grant Awards program.  
 In this round of grants, Montana will receive a total of $778,991. 
   The following fire stations and districts will receive funding in this round of grants: 
   * The Lewistown Rural Fire Department will receive $225,000 for new firefighting vehicles. 
   * The Clarkston Fire Service Area #6 in Three Forks will receive $84,843 for firefighting operations and firefighter safety. 
   * The West Valley Volunteer Fire & Rescue in Kalispell will receive $179,100 for new firefighting vehicles. 
   * The Charlo/Moiese Rural Fire District will receive $90,000 for new firefighting vehicles. 
   * The Montecahto Rural Fire District in Polson will receive $143,982 for new firefighting vehicles. 
   * The Culbertson Volunteer Fire Department will receive $11,970 for firefighting operations and firefighter safety. 
   * The Chester Volunteer Fire Department will receive $44,096 for firefighting operations and firefighter safety. 
   "In Montana, we know the devastation that fire can cause, particularly through the dry summer months," said Burns. "These grants are critical to our firefighting communities and I will continue working to put our first responders behind the best equipment they need to save Montana's people, property and lands." 
   The U.S. Fire Administration, a division of FEMA, administers the Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program. The program assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. A list of all current award recipients announced to date is posted on the USFA website at: http://www.usfa.fema.gov/grants.
