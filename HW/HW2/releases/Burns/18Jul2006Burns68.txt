SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES BUTTE DEFENSE PROJECTS ADVANCE TOWARD FUNDING
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R - Mont.) announced today that at least five major defense and military construction appropriation projects for Butte have been included in an Appropriations bill that is expected to be approved by their respective subcommittees today and the full Senate Appropriation Committee Thursday. 
   The following projects are advancing toward federal funding in the fiscal year 2007 Defense and Military Construction Appropriations bills:
   * Titanium Mining and Powder Refining - Senator Burns expects to deliver major funding for titanium mining and powder refining research, with the goal of leading to refining titanium at the Columbia Falls Aluminum Plant.
   * Research for Combat Wound Treatment - Senator Burns expects to deliver major funding to research three different technologies to help treat combat wounds in the field. This money will fund adult stem cell research and new approaches to heal wounds more quickly and re-grow tissue and bone. This research is critical to help rehabilitate our veterans returning from Iraq and Afghanistan with serious injuries.
   * Thermal Sprays for Polymeric-Based Mitigation Materials - Senator Burns expects to deliver major funding to develop these spray-on chemicals which will help military vehicles absorb explosive blasts.
   * Mariah Hypersonic Wind Tunnel - Senator Burns expects to deliver major funding to develop technologies to accelerate airflow to Mach 5-8 for high speed missile and aircraft research.
   * Montana Tech: Low-Cost Titanium Design - Senator Burns expects to deliver major funding that will help the Defense Department design weapon system components from Low-Cost Titanium. Montana Tech will be able to continue development of methods to extract titanium components out of the soil and fabricate a lower-cost from of titanium.
   * Advanced Energy Technology for Munitions: Dominator Program - Senator Burns expects to deliver major funding to develop fuel cell technology to power military unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) with tremendous possibility for future energy savings propulsion.
   "These Butte area projects are critical to maintaining our state's key role in our national defense," said Senator Burns. "I have worked with my colleagues and local folks to make sure these projects are a high priority."
   Senator Burns served in the United States Marine Corps from 1955-1957 and is a senior member of the Senate Defense and Military Construction Appropriations Subcommittees.
