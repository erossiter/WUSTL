SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES FIRE GRANT FOR BLACK EAGLE
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R-Mont.) announced today that Black Eagle Volunteer Fire Department will receive a $58,500 grant through the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Awards program to go towards a new firefighting vehicle.  
   "Fighting fires in not an easy job, and the men and women who are out there battling these blazes deserve the best training and equipment possible to make sure they can get the job done safely," said Burns. "This funding goes directly towards reaching that goal, and I'm glad to see this important funding go to Black Eagle this week." 
   The U.S. Fire Administration, a division of FEMA, administers the Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program. The program assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States.
