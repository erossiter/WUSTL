SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES $4,242,026 IN HEALTH CARE GRANTS FOR MONTANA
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R-Mont.) today announced four grants awarded the state of Montana by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS). The grants were: 
  1) $3,028,816 for the Yellowstone City & County Health Department - Billings
  This financial assistance will be discretionary funding to improve the health services at the City-County health department.
   2) $765,970 for the Montana Primary Care Association - Helena
  This will be a three year project focusing on the state and regional primary care associations.
   3) $332,295 for Montana State University - Bozeman
  The National Center for Research Resources has awarded $332,295 for Biomedical Research Support Shared Instrumentation studies at MSU. The project will begin April 15, 2006 and last until April 14, 2007.
   4) $114,945 for the State Department of HHS, State Primary Care Offices - HelenaThis project began in 1990 and has been planned to go through March 31, 2009. The funds will be used for the technical assistance of the office to promote primary care.
  Senator Burns said, "These grants will go along way towards improve the level of health care service across the state of Montana in Yellowstone County, Bozeman, and Helena. These funds will ensure our research centers remain on the cutting edge. We will all benefit from these grants."
  Senator Burns is a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and a three term U.S. Senator.
