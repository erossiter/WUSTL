SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES FIREFIGHTING FUNDS FOR WEST HELENA VALLEY VFD
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R-Mont.) today announced another round of funding in this years Assistance to Firefighters Grant Awards program.  
 In this round of grants, the West Helena Valley Volunteer Fire Department will receive $180,000 for the purchase of a new firefighting vehicle. 
   "A lot of alarms have gone off in the past few summers in Montana's fire stations, and those fires put a lot of the men and women working in them at risk," said Burns. "The folks we have working at the West Helena Valley VFD are some of our best, and with all they do for us every day, I'm pleased to know that this money will go toward making them safer." 
   The U.S. Fire Administration, a division of FEMA, administers the Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program. The program assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. A list of all current award recipients announced to date is posted on the USFA website at: http://www.usfa.fema.gov/grants.
