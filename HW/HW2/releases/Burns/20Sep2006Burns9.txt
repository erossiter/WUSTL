SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES 3.5 PERCENT UNEMPLOYMENT RATE IN MONTANA
  Today Sen. Conrad Burns (R - Mont.) announced Montana's unemployment rate has dropped to 3.5% in August 2006, compared to 4.0% at this time last year, according to state by state labor statistics released this morning by the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS). 
  Montana's unemployment rate was 3.8% in July 2006 and the U.S. Department of Labor announced on September 1st that the national unemployment rate for August 2006 was 4.7%.
  "Montana has created 14,000 new net jobs this year and we are near the lowest state unemployment in the nation," said Senator Burns. "Montanans know that we have an outstanding climate for business because of the federal investment in our communities through infrastructure and local projects. I'll continue to advocate for low taxes and federal investment in Montana."
  Senator Burns is a senior member of the Senate Commerce and Small Business Committees.
