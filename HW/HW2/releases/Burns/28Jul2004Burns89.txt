SEN. BURNS ANNOUNCES FUNDS TO SUPPORT MONTANA FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R-Mont.) today announced that three of Montana's fire departments will soon be receiving a total of $163,685 in funding to support their operations and improve the programs that are in place to secure the safety of their firefighters.  
   The following fire stations will receive funding in this round of grants: 
   * The Belfry Rural Fire District #9 - $14,400 
  * The Dixon Rural Volunteer Fire Department - $78,405 
  * The Greenough Potomac Volunteer Fire Department - $70,880 
   "We rely upon the invaluable work of our firefighters to protect the safety of Montana's people and property," said Burns. "They repeatedly put their lives on the line in their service to us and deserve all the resources we have available to continue doing their jobs well. I'm pleased to know that these stations will be receiving these funds and I will continue working to make sure they are outfitted with all the equipment they need." 
   The U.S. Fire Administration, a division of FEMA, administers the Assistance for Firefighters Grant Program. The program assists rural, urban and suburban fire departments throughout the United States. A list of all current award recipients announced to date is posted on the USFA website at: http://www.usfa.fema.gov/grants.
