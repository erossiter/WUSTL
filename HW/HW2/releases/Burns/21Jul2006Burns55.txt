SEN. BURNS: MONTANA UNEMPLOYMENT DOWN TO 3.5 PERCENT
  Sen. Conrad Burns (R - Mont.) today announced that the U.S. Department of Labor has released updated state unemployment statistics which confirm Montana's unemployment through June 2006 fell to 3.5%, from 3.8%. 
   National unemployment remains historically low at 4.6%, following a period of economic expansion evidenced by 5.6% gross domestic product growth nationally for the first quarter of 2006.
   "Montana is in fine economic shape, with more folks working than ever before," said Senator Burns. "We will continue pushing for federal investment in our communities and advancing tax relief wherever possible to aid hard-working Montanans meet their own personal financial needs."
   Senator Burns is a senior member of the Senate Commerce and Small Business committees and former small business owner.
