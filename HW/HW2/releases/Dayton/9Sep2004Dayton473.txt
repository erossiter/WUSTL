SEN. DAYTON: ELEVEN MINNESOTA FIRE DEPARTMENTS TO RECEIVE OVER $1.3 MILLION IN FEDERAL FUNDING
  Sen. Mark Dayton announced today that 11 Minnesota Fire Departments have been awarded over $1.3 million in federal grants from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.  
 The funding, issued through the Assistance to Firefighters Program, will be used to purchase new fire trucks and equipment and to support training programs.
   "It's vital that we continue to provide our firefighters with the tools and resources necessary to keep our communities safe," said Dayton. "This funding ensures that these brave men and woman have the equipment and training they need to respond to any emergency."
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, administered by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Office of Domestic Preparedness, in cooperation with the U.S. Fire Administration, provides direct assistance to local fire departments for general firefighter operations, training and safety programs, fire prevention, and emergency-vehicle acquisition. This is the 15th round of awards for FY 2005.
   Communities receiving grants include: 
   * Mankato Fire Department - $375,000
  * New Germany Fire Department - $93,366
  * St. Paul Fire Department - $375,742
  * Alpha Fire Department - $151,000
  * Oslo Fire & Rescue Department - $19,800
  * Rockford Volunteer Fire Department - $27,855
  * Hill City Fire Department - $47,120
  * New Prague Fire Department - $82,125
  * Cotton Volunteer Fire Department - $16,200
  * Cherry Volunteer Fire and Rescue - $33,557
  * Winsted Fire Department - $78,300
   For more information on these grants please visit: www.firegrantsupport.com or www.usfa.fema.gov/fire-service/grants/afgp/grants.
