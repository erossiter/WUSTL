SEN. DAYTON ANNOUNCES OVER $3.5 MILLION FOR IMPROVEMENTS TO SEVEN MINNESOTA AIRPORTS
  Sen. Mark Dayton today announced that seven airports in Minnesota will receive a total of $3,553,229 from the U.S. Department of Transportation, through the Federal Aviation Administration. 
   "As Minnesota's population continues to grow, meeting our state's expanding transportation needs becomes more important to the safety of travelers and to our economy," said Dayton. "I am delighted that these FAA grants will help to update our local airports for the 21st Century." 
   Funding will be delivered to the following Minnesota communities: 
   *$1,823,158 for Worthington Municipal Airport, to rehabilitate and make safety improvements to taxiway "C". 
   *$1,293,900 for Elbow Lake Municipal Airport, to construct a paved runway to replace the current turf runway. 
   *$153,000 for Fairmont Municipal Airport, to acquire new snow removal equipment and conduct an airport layout plan update. 
   *$90,300 for Le Sueur Municipal Airport, to upgrade the airport fuel facility. 
   *$86,925 for Montevideo-Chippewa County Airport, to conduct a study on the environmental impact of a new taxiway, to install perimeter fencing in the general terminal area, and to rehabilitate the access road, which is more than 20 years old. 
   *$67,946 for Glenwood Municipal Airport, to acquire new snow removal equipment. 
   *$38,000 for Quentin Aanenson Field in Luverne, to conduct a study on the environmental impact of a new runway.
