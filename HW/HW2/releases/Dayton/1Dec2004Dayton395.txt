SEN. DAYTON: SQUAW LAKE, MIDDLE RIVER FIRE DEPARTMENTS AWARDED OVER $100,000 IN FEDERAL GRANTS
  Sen. Mark Dayton announced today that Squaw Lake and Middle River fire departments have been awarded over $100,000 in federal grants from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.  
 The funding, provided through the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, will be used to purchase new equipment, support firefighter training and fitness programs, and make improvements to current fire station facilities. 
   "Minnesota's firefighters work tirelessly everyday to protect our homes and neighborhoods," said Dayton. "I am pleased that this funding will provide these brave men and women with the resources they need to do their jobs and keep our communities safe."
   Administered by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Office of Domestic Preparedness, the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program teams with the U.S. Fire Administration, to provide direct assistance to local fire departments for general firefighter and first responder operations. The program also supports the acquisition of emergency vehicles. 
   This twenty-fourth round of awards for fiscal year 2004 include: 
   * Squaw Lake Fire Department - $31,865 
   * Middle River Fire Department - $80,359
   For more information on these grants please visit: http://www.firegrantsupport.com or http://www.usfa.fema.gov/fire-service/grants/afgp/grants.
