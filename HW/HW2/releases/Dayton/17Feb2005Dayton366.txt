SEN. DAYTON: FEDERAL GRANT OF MORE THAN $724,000 AWARDED TO MINNEAPOLIS FIRE DEPARTMENT
  Sen. Mark Dayton announced today that the Minneapolis Fire Department has been awarded a federal grant of more than $724,000 from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.  
 The funding, provided through the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, will be used to purchase new equipment and firefighting vehicles, improve fire station facilities, and support firefighter training and wellness programs. 
   "Minnesota's first responders risk their lives every day to keep our communities safe," said Dayton. "This funding is very helpful in making sure first responders in Minneapolis have the necessary equipment and resources to respond effectively to any emergency." 
   Administered through the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Office of Domestic Preparedness, the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, provides direct financial assistance to local fire departments and emergency responders for improving of firefighter and first-responder operations. 
   For more information on these grants please visit: www.firegrantsupport.com or www.usfa.fema.gov/fire-service/grants/afgp/grants. 
   Other Minnesota communities receiving funding include: 
   * Karlstad Fire Department - $67,221 
   * Fayal Fire Department - $136,800 
   * Wadena Fire Department - $31,752 
   * Richfield Fire Department - $145,787 
   * Long Prairie Fire Department - $405,000 
   * Cologne Fire and Rescue Department - $225,000.
