SEN. DAYTON : OVER $10 MILLION COMING TO RURAL MINNESOTA FOR WATER, WASTE DISPOSAL PROJECTS
  Sen. Mark Dayton today announced that seven rural communities will receive a total of $10,290,500 to develop safe and affordable sewage treatment and waste disposal systems. The funding is provided by the U.S. Department of Agriculture's (USDA) Rural Development Water and Waste Disposal Loans and Grants Program.  
   "Few things are more vital to a community than a reliable supply of clean, safe water drinking water," said Dayton. "I am delighted that these USDA funds will help to update the infrastructure of these communities for the 21st Century." 
   * Watkins will receive a $1,172,500 grant and a $4,771,000 loan 
   * Faribault County - Huntley area will receive a $732,000 grant and a $360,000 loan 
   * Bigelow will receive a $667,000 grant and a $680,000 loan 
   * Kent will receive a $354,000 grant and $195,000 loan 
   * Hatfield will receive a $295,000 grant and a $40,000 loan 
   * Lake Bronson will receive a $256,00 grant and $708,000 loan 
   * Boyd will receive a $6,000 grant and $54,000 loan.
