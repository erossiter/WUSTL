SEN. DAYTON ANNOUNCES OVER $400,000 FOR SEVEN MINNESOTA FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. Mark Dayton announced today that fire departments in seven Minnesota communities have been awarded over $400,000 in federal grants from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.  
 The funding will be used to purchase new fire trucks and equipment and support training and safety programs for local firefighters. 
   "Everyday Minnesota firefighters put their lives on the line protecting our local communities," said Dayton. "I am pleased this funding will help make sure that our fire fighters are prepared when they are called to action, by providing them with the equipment and training they need."
   The Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, administered by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Office of Domestic Preparedness, in cooperation with the U.S. Fire Administration, provides direct assistance to local fire departments for general firefighter operations, training and safety programs, fire prevention, and emergency-vehicle acquisition. This is the 16th round of awards for FY 2005. 
   Communities receiving grants include:
   * Kandiyohi- $99,000 
   * St. Paul Fire Department - $375,742 
   * Okabena Fire Department - $76,365 
   * Amboy Fire Department - $50,670 
   * Battle Lake Fire Association - $29,664 
   * Brainerd Fire Department - $31,500 
   * Cokato Volunteer Fire Department - $29,178 
   * Hermantown Volunteer Fire Department - $89,109 
   For more information on these grants please visit: www.firegrantsupport.com or www.usfa.fema.gov/fire-service/grants/afgp/grants.
