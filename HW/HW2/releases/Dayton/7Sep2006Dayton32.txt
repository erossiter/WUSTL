SEN. DAYTON: OVER $4 MILLION FOR IMPROVEMENTS TO ST. PAUL - DOWNTOWN HOLMAN FIELD AIRPORT
  Sen. Mark Dayton today announced that St. Paul - Downtown Holman Field Airport will receive two grants totaling $4,022,565 from the U.S. Department of Transportation, through the Federal Aviation Administration.  
   "Maintaining and improving Minnesota airports is important to ensuring passenger safety," said Dayton. "Well-maintained airports contribute to high quality customer service, increased business opportunities, and a strong tourism industry." 
   The first grant, in the amount of $2,716,698, will be used to improve the runway safety area and the second grant, in the amount of $1,305,867, will improve airport drainage to avoid flooding of the airport during heavy precipitation.
