SEN. DAYTON ANNOUNCES BROOKLYN PARK NATIVE'S APPOINTMENT TO WEST POINT MILITARY ACADEMY
  Today, Sen. Mark Dayton announced that Brooklyn Park native and Bethel College graduate Nolan Anliker has been offered an appointment to the United States Military Academy at West Point, New York.  
 Dayton nominated Anliker for the appointment. 
   Nolan Anliker attended Park Center High School, in Brooklyn Park, where he was a member of the National Honor Society and the varsity hockey team. After graduation, Anliker attended Bethel College in St. Paul and the University of North Dakota (UND), where he was a member of the UND Military Aviation Organization. 
   "I am very proud that Nolan has been appointed to the U.S. Military Academy at West Point," said Dayton. "Nolan is an outstanding student and a very well-rounded young man. I have every confidence that he will meet the Academy's rigorous academic, military, and athletic challenges and will go on to serve honorably as a military officer." 
   Each year, Dayton considers it an honor to nominate Minnesotans to serve their country and broaden their education by attending a military service academy. Students are encouraged to contact Dayton's Office in the spring of their junior year to begin the military academy nomination process.
