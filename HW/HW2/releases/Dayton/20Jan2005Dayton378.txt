SEN. DAYTON: OVER $525,000 IN FEDERAL GRANTS FOR WABASHA, COON RAPIDS, AITKIN, HENDERSON, ANDOVER, BYRON FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. Mark Dayton announced today that six Minnesota fire departments have been awarded over $525,000 in federal grants from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.  
 The funding, provided through the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, will be used to purchase new equipment and firefighting vehicles, improve fire station facilities, and support firefighter training and wellness programs. 
   "Providing Minnesota's firefighters and first responders with the resources they need to keep our communities safe is a top priority," said Dayton. "These grants will provide our firefighters and first responders with the equipment and training they need to protect themselves and local residents from any emergency." 
   Administered through the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Office of Domestic Preparedness, the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program along with the U.S. Fire Administration provides direct assistance to local fire departments and emergency responders for the improvement of general firefighter and first-responder operations. 
   For more information on these grants please visit: www.firegrantsupport.com or www.usfa.fema.gov/fire-service/grants/afgp/grants. 
   Minnesota communities receiving funding include: 
   * City of Wabasha - $116,284 
   * Coon Rapids Fire Department - $193,200
   * Aitkin Fire Department - $53,766 
   * Henderson Fire and Rescue Department - $34,200 
   * Andover Fire Department - $108,180 
   * City of Byron Volunteer Fire Department - $19,665.
