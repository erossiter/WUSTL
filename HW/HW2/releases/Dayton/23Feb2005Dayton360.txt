SEN. DAYTON: FEDERAL GRANT OF $52,000 AWARDED TO MENTOR VOLUNTEER FIRE AND RESCUE DEPARTMENT
  Sen. Mark Dayton announced today that the Mentor Volunteer Fire and Rescue Department has been awarded a federal grant of $52,000 from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.  
 The funding, provided through the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, will be used to purchase new equipment and firefighting vehicles, improve fire station facilities, and support firefighter training and wellness programs. 
   "Minnesota's first responders risk their lives every day to keep our communities safe," said Dayton. "This funding is very helpful in making sure first responders in Mentor have the necessary equipment and resources to respond effectively to any emergency." 
   Administered through the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Office of Domestic Preparedness, the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, provides direct financial assistance to local fire departments and emergency responders for improving of firefighter and first-responder operations. 
   For more information on these grants please visit: www.firegrantsupport.com or www.usfa.fema.gov/fire-service/grants/afgp/grants. 
   Other Minnesota communities receiving funding include: 
   * Little Canada Fire Department - $41,147;
   * Rockville Fire Department - $26,955.
