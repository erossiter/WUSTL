SEN. DAYTON: OVER $225,000 IN FEDERAL GRANTS FOR WARROAD, WINDOM, STEWART, BENSON FIRE DEPARTMENTS
  Sen. Mark Dayton announced today that four Minnesota fire departments have been awarded over $225,000 in federal grants from the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.  
 The funding, provided through the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program, will be used to purchase new equipment and firefighting vehicles, improve fire station facilities, and support firefighter training and wellness programs. 
   "Minnesota's first responders risk their lives every day to make sure our communities are safe," said Dayton. "This grant funding is a significant advance in making sure these brave men and women have the necessary resources and equipment they need to do respond to any emergency, improve safety, and save lives." 
   Administered through the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's Office of Domestic Preparedness, the Assistance to Firefighters Grant Program along with the U.S. Fire Administration provide direct assistance to local fire departments and emergency responders for the improvement of general firefighter and first-responder operations. 
   For more information on these grants please visit: www.firegrantsupport.com or www.usfa.fema.gov/fire-service/grants/afgp/grants. 
   Minnesota communities receiving funding include: 
   * Warroad Fire Department - $56,385 
   * City of Windom Volunteer Fire Department - $71,658 
   * Stewart Fire Department - $42,552 
   * Benson Fire Department - $59,607.
