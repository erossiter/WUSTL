SEN. DAYTON: SENATE APPROVES MORE THAN $4.7 MILLION FOR MINNESOTA AGRICULTURE PROJECTS
  Sen. Mark Dayton announced today more than $4.7 million in federal aid to support five important agriculture projects in the Twin Cities Metropolitan area. The funding, requested by Dayton, was included in the Agricultural Appropriations Committee Report which was filed today. The next step is the bill will go to conference with the House where the final project funding amounts will be decided. 
   "Agriculture is the backbone of our state's economy and America's farmers produce the finest products in the world," said Dayton. "By supporting them with funding these projects, we are investing in our farmers and we are helping to secure for them, a more prosperous future."
   The following is a list of programs included in the appropriations bill:
   * $45,000 for the Cereal Disease Laboratory in St. Paul: Funding will be used to replace the laboratory's retiring research scientist. The position is a key part of a team that investigates the genomics of plant pathogens involving blights of wheat and barley.
   * $298,000 for the Uniform Farm Management Project at the University of Minnesota's Center for Farm Financial Management: Funding will be used to provide farmers with cost-of-production figures to help them make sounds financial and investment decisions.
   * $650,000 for the Agriculture Diversification Project: Funding will be used to establish a region-wide training program for producers and agribusinesses in the use of information technologies and to develop and implement strategies for multiple uses of agricultural land. 
   * $626,000 for the Midwest Poultry Research Program: Funding will support research based on issues critical to Midwest poultry producers, such as food safety, animal health, waste management, and combating poultry diseases. Funds are distributed among nineteen research projects at nine different universities in the Midwest. 
   * $694,000 for the National Farm Animal Identification and Records Program: Funding will be used to expand the voluntary cattle identification program that has been in existence since 1998. The program tracks approximately 1 million animals in 28 states. This funding will expand the program allowing farmers in Minnesota, Michigan, Vermont, and Ohio to participate. 
   * $2,400,000 for the Northern Great Plains Regional Authority: Funding will be used to promote agricultural diversity, helping farmers to develop new crops, information technology and training programs for farmers, and transportation planning for rural areas. The Northern Great Plains Authority focuses on Minnesota, Iowa, North and South Dakota, and Nebraska.
