                                      BUNNING SECURES NEARLY  2 MILLION IN FUNDING FOR KENTUCKY IN THE TRANSPORTATION  TREASURY  HUD  JUDICIARY AND D C  APPROPRIATIONS BILL          WASHINGTON  D C    Monday  November 21  2005                  U S  Senator Jim Bunning today announced that he secured  1 9 million for Kentucky projects in the Conference Report to the Fiscal Year 2006 Transportation  Treasury  Housing and Urban Development  Judiciary and D C  Appropriations Bill that was approved by the Senate   The bill was also approved in the House of Representatives and now goes to President Bush for his signature        These funds will go a long way in helping to improve Kentucky  s transportation safety infrastructure and support economic development around the Commonwealth     said Bunning     I am particularly pleased to have secured funding for the new visitor center at Big Bone Lick State Park that will provide an important cultural attraction in Northern Kentucky and further establish it as a tourist destination        And the new welcome center for Clinton County  s Chamber of Commerce will serve as valuable resource for our communities by promoting new businesses and jobs for working families and helping promote the many businesses in the region        Finally  in order to help our first responders better handle major disasters in the Purchase Area of Kentucky I have secured  200 000 in the bill to help McCracken County build a new Disaster and Emergency Service Building           The following is a list of Kentucky projects which Senator Bunning has directly worked on that are funded in the bill            700 000 KY 52 expansion in the City of Beattyville         600 000 Big Bone Lick State Park Visitor Center          400 000 KY 9 extension in Campbell County         200 000 McCracken County Emergency Services Building         200 000 Clinton County Chamber of Commerce Welcome Center                                                     November 2005
