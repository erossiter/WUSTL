                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Bunning Statement On The Nomination Of Ben Bernanke To Be Chairman Of The Federal Reserve             Monday  October 24  2005                  U S  Senator Jim Bunning today issued the following statement regarding President Bush  s nomination of Dr  Ben Bernanke to replace Alan Greenspan as chairman of the Federal Reserve       It  s a shame that President Bush has decided to select Dr  Ben Bernanke as chairman of the Federal Reserve  Though I believe Dr  Bernanke has the qualifications to be Chairman of the Federal Reserve  I must oppose this nomination        I met with Dr  Bernanke when he was a nominee to the Board of governors of the Federal Reserve  he promised me that he would not be a rubber stamp for Chairman Greenspan but an independent voice who would stand up to the Chairman when he believed he was wrong  Sadly  Dr  Bernanke never once cast a dissenting vote  The Chairman of the Federal Reserve must be an independent voice  Dr  Bernanke had the chance to prove he was an independent voice  he failed to do so        The position of Federal Reserve Chairman is probably the most important economic choice the President has to make   He has chosen poorly                                                         October 2005 News Releases
