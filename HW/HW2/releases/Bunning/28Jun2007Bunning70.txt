                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               Bunning Votes No To Cloture And No To Amnesty               Thursday  June 28  2007                  U S  Senator Jim Bunning today issued the following statement regarding the cloture vote on the Immigration Bill that failed the Senate by a vote of 46 53  Over the last month Bunning helped lead the fight against the amnesty bill that was defeated in the Senate today     I have always opposed amnesty in any shape  size or form  The thousands of calls and e mails my office received over the last month against this amnesty bill only strengthened my resolve  I appreciate those calls  and your voices were heard loud and clear     This amnesty bill was ill conceived from the start  A majority of Senators were not consulted throughout the process  and that strategy doomed the bill  I do believe that we need immigration reform  but we need the right immigration reform that does not include amnesty     I urge my colleagues to start over and go back to the drawing board through the committee process  In 1986  we saw a bad immigration bill pass the Congress that formed the current immigration disaster  I am glad history did not repeat itself today                                                      June 2007 News Releases                                                                       United States Senate
