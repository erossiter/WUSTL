                                      Statement Of Senator Jim Bunning On President Bush  s State Of The Union Address             Tuesday  January 23  2007  U S  Senator Jim Bunning  R KY  offered the following comments regarding tonights address by President Bush to the joint session of Congress   President Bush tonight delivered an important message to Congress that we need to work together to get things done for the American people   One of the most important issues the President addressed in his speech is the need to find alternative sources of energy that will help reduce our dependence on foreign oil   I believe that the Coal To Liquid Fuel Bill I introduced with Senator Obama can play an important part in this effort  With this legislation  we can harness our vast domestic coal resources to break our addiction to foreign oil  improve the environment and keep energy prices lower for all Americans   I also agree with the President that we must work to find a way to provide Americans with access to more affordable health care coverage   And while I will need to take a closer look at the specific plan he unveiled tonight I do believe we must look into options such as expanding Health Savings Accounts and creating Association Health Plans that can help move us towards this goal   On the war front I think we must give the Presidents new plan for Iraq a chance to work   Failure in Iraq is not an option  A safe and secure Iraq is vital to our overall success in the War on Terror and the President is asking us to work with him to get the job done  That is exactly what I intend to do   The Iraqi government has pledged their cooperation in this effort and we will hold them to this pledge   I have my concerns  but I believe this new strategy is the most viable option to turn this war around  Finishing the job in Iraq is essential to our national security   Weve got some big challenges to tackle in the 110th Congress  I am looking forward to working with the President and my colleagues on both sides of the aisle to meet them head on                                                      January 2007 News Releases
