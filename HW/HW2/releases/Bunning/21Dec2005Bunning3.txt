                                      Bunning Statement On The Senate Passage Of The Deficit Reduction Bill             Wednesday  December 21  2005                  U S  Senator Jim Bunning today issued the following statement regarding the Conference Report to S  1932  the Omnibus Deficit Reduction Bill which passed the Senate by a vote of 51 50        The Senate is taking important steps to reduce the deficit by nearly  40 billion over the next five years while at the same time working to make sure we are able to meet some of our most important priorities right now such as fully funding the President  s defense and homeland security requests and spending federal dollars wisely on hurricane relief     said Bunning     The current deficit situation is unsustainable and federal spending is out of control   By curbing spending and reducing the deficit this bill will help ensure that our economy remains strong and growing for future generations to come         Also included in the bill are some commonsense government reforms such as making improvements to the Medicare and Medicaid programs for elderly and low income patients  strengthening education for low income students and offering relief to those low income families who are struggling with high home heating costs this winter          In the Deficit Reduction Bill Senator Bunning secured a provision that would provide a new  one time screening benefit for abdominal aortic aneurysms under Medicare for eligible beneficiaries at risk of these aneurysms  which occur when the aortic walls weaken and rupture         If detected early abdominal aortic aneurysms can be prevented and my provision has the potential to save the lives of many who are at risk of having these aneurysms     Bunning said     The survival rate is less than 15 percent for those who have these aneurysms  and approximately 15 000 people die from ruptured abdominal aortic aneurysms each year   However  when detected before rupturing  AAAs are treatable and curable in 95 percent of cases                                                         December 2005 News Releases
