                                      Bunning McConnell  Department Of Labor Grant To Help Economic Development Along Central Kentucky I 65 Corridor              Wednesday  June 20  2007                  U S  Senators Jim Bunning and Mitch McConnell today announced that the United States Department of Labor is investing up to  5 million in the Central Kentucky I 65 Corridor as part of the Workforce Innovation in Regional Economic Development  WIRED  Initiative     As the central Kentucky region continues to grow  these funds will help integrate both educational and workforce networks to better serve the people of the I 65 corridor  said Bunning  The I 65 corridor is a vital region of the Commonwealth  and these funds will further economic expansion     Investing in the I 65 Corridor makes sense  said Senator McConnell  This region is an economic engine for Kentucky  and todays announcement will help attract employers that need highly skilled workers  This will also help keep our best students in Kentucky to live  work and study  and help grow our states economy     The grant will be spread out over three years and will benefit the I 65 Corridor WIRED Region  which includes 15 counties in central Kentucky  The federal funds will integrate education  economic and workforce development activities and resources around transportation  distribution and logistics  healthcare delivery  research  innovation  commercialization  and entrepreneur networks                                                      June 2007 News Releases
