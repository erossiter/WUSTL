                                      Bunning Announces Federal Funds For Earlington Fire Department             Wednesday  May 24  2006                  U S  Senator Jim Bunning today announced that The Assistance to Firefighters Grant  AFG   Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response  known as SAFER  of the Department of Homeland Security  DHS  has awarded the Earlington Fire Department  84 087 in federal funding  SAFER was implemented by Congress in 2005 as a way to contribute federal funds to firefighters across the country  Out of nearly 3 000 applicants hoping to receive a grant  Earlington was one of 125 to be awarded funding      Earlington will use its grant money to help meet the recruiting needs of the department when trying to lure prospective firefighters  The grant will also aid in placing an emphasis on an integral part of the firefighting profession by ensuring 24 hour staffing for departments and meeting the basic requirements of firefighter standards        The grant awarded to the Earlington Fire Department is well deserved and will allow this great fire department to improve upon its recruiting capabilities  an aspect of the firefighting profession that cannot be emphasized enough     remarked Senator Bunning    These funds  made possible by the SAFER program  are influential in supporting the preservation of our Kentucky fire departments  ensuring that they receive every benefit available                                                         May 2006 News Releases
