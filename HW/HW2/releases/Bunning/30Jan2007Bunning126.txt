                                      Bunning Announces  664 600 In A GNEP Siting Grant For The Paducah Gaseous Diffusion Plant             Tuesday  January 30  2007  U S  Senator Jim Bunning today issued the following statement regarding the announcement by the United States Department of Energy  DOE  that the Paducah Gaseous Diffusion Plant will receive a  664 600 grant for the Global Nuclear Energy Partnership  GNEP  initiative   The funding in this grant is an important step in possibly bringing a nuclear recycling facility to Paducah  said Bunning  Recycling spent nuclear material will help provide a safe and smart way to manage nuclear waste as well as addressing concerns of proliferation  I am confident the good folks at the Paducah Gaseous Diffusion Plant will work hard on the Site Characterization Report   The funds will be used to carry out siting studies to determine the possibility of hosting an advanced nuclear fuel recycling center and or an advanced recycling reactor  Beginning today  recipients will conduct detailed site characterization studies of the sites which were proposed in their Funding Opportunity Announcement  FOA  responses  Recipients will have 90 days to complete these studies and submit a Site Characterization Report to DOE on May 30  2007                                                      January 2007 News Releases
