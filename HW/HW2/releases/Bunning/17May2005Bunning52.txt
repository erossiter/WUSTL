                                      Bunning Statement On Senate Passage Of The Highway Bill             Tuesday  May 17  2005                  U S  Senator Jim Bunning today issued the following statement in support of H R  3  the Highway Act  which passed the Senate by a vote of 89 11   The bill now goes to a Conference Committee where final details of the bill will be ironed out        This is the biggest jobs bill that Congress will work on this year and I am proud to have supported it     said Bunning     It  s going to mean billions of dollars and up to 50 000 new construction jobs for Kentucky         We desperately need to improve our infrastructure  and with the passage of this important Highway Bill  Kentucky will receive its fair share in funding so commerce can move freely and safely throughout the Commonwealth       We don t decide funding for specific transportation projects like replacing the Brent Spence Bridge in Northern Kentucky  completing Louisville  s Ohio Bridges  and upgrading the I 69 corridor through Western Kentucky until the bill is conferenced  but I am going to continue to fight hard to see that Kentucky gets its fair share of the pie        The highway portion of the bill is projected to include  3 17 billion in federal highway formula funds for Kentucky over the next six years    a 9 percent increase over the last six year period   That  s an increase of more than  260 million dollars   In the transit title of the bill  Kentucky is projected to receive a 151 percent increase over TEA 21 in formula grants for transit   Transit formula grants could total  209 million for the bill  a  70 6 million increase in transit funding for the Commonwealth                                                      May 2005 News Releases
