                                      Statement Of Senator Jim Bunning On President Bush  s State Of The Union Address             Wednesday  February 2  2005                  U S  Senator Jim Bunning  R KY  offered the following comments regarding tonight  s address by President Bush to the joint session of Congress         The president tonight laid out a thoughtful agenda for building a stronger America and a safer world       On Sunday we watched history unfold as Iraqi citizens went to the polls to vote for a new democratically elected government       And tonight the president restated his commitment to helping them secure their country and continuing on the path of freedom       We must stay the course in the war against terrorists to ensure that peace prevails in this troubled part of the world        On the domestic side  President Bush addressed issues that should be our top priorities in Congress  including strengthening Social Security and common sense tax reform       On Social Security  the president hit the nail on the head when he said that we must act now to fix a broken system         In 13 years  the program goes into the red  and we can  t afford to keep our heads buried in the sand   The sooner we act the better       One way to make sure that Social Security will remain solvent for our children and grandchildren is through something I have been talking about for almost ten years now    voluntary personal accounts       Personal accounts will help give younger workers an opportunity to start building a nest egg for a stable future       We have a chance now to make some meaningful changes to help future generations   Tonight the President laid those challenges before us   Now it  s time to meet them head on
