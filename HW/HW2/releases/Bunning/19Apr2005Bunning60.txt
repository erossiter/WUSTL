                                      Bunning Statement On Memo Of Assembled Chemical Weapons Alternative Fund Management             Tuesday  April 19  2005                  U S  Senator Jim Bunning today issued the following statement regarding a Department of Defense  DOD  memo by Under Secretary of Defense Michael Wynne that outlines the management of the Assembled Chemical Weapons Alternative  ACWA  funds   The memo directs that the ACWA Program Manager release Fiscal Year 2005 funds and takes the possibility of transporting the chemical weapons at the Blue Grass Army Depot off the table   The memo also directs the ACWA Program Manager to resume the design process to get the facility up and running and destroying the weapons        It  s about time the Department of Defense moved ahead to meet both our treaty obligations and safety obligations to Kentuckians   For too long the DOD has been simply dragging its feet and thwarting the will of the Senate     said Bunning     This memo reveals a real commitment to getting rid of these weapons in a safe and timely manner  and Senator McConnell and I will continue to hold DOD  s feet to the fire on this important matter          None of this would have been possible had it not been for Craig Williams and the Chemical Weapons Working Group   They have stayed ahead of the curve all along and have done a thankless job of keeping us all aware of DoD  s plans to put this program on the back burner     continued Senator Bunning     Last month  during Senate Budget Committee hearings on the FY06 Budget  Senator Bunning questioned then Deputy Secretary of Defense Paul Wolfowitz on the DOD  s delays and budget shortfalls for the process and program to eliminate the chemical weapons at the Blue Grass Army Depot   This decision reflected in today  s memo follows up on the promise Secretary Wolfowitz and the DOD made to Senator Bunning that he would make sure the DOD took a hard look at the ACWA program and help facilitate movement on eliminating the chemical weapons at the Blue Grass Army Depot                                                      April 2005 News Releases
