                                      Bunning Praises Central Baptist Hospital On Earning Top Performer Status In CMS Premier Pay For Performance Project             Friday  January 26  2007  U S  Senator Jim Bunning today announced that Central Baptist Hospital in Lexington  has been awarded an incentive payment for being among the top performing hospitals in the nation for providing high quality care as part of a three year national Medicare pilot demonstration  Lourdes Hospital in Paducah  was also given the distinction   The doctors  nurses  and staff at Central Baptist Hospital should be proud of this great accomplishment  said Senator Bunning  Central Baptist Hospital has provided excellent care for the people of central Kentucky for many years  This award highlights the great work that is being done each and everyday by the good people at Central Baptist   Central Baptist Hospital is among more than 260 hospitals participating in a national pay for performance demonstration project  which was launched in October 2003 by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services  CMS  and the Premier Inc  healthcare alliance  The CMS Premier Hospital Quality Incentive Demonstration  HQID  is designed to determine if economic incentives to hospitals are effective at improving the quality of inpatient care   As part of the HQID project  Central Baptist Hospital volunteered to have its quality data for five specific clinical conditions compared with the performance of other participating hospitals using nationally accepted  standard performance measures  CMS announced today that it is awarding Central Baptist Hospital an incentive payment for scoring in the top twenty percent for one or more of five clinical conditions  which include heart attack  coronary artery bypass graft  heart failure  pneumonia  and hip and knee replacement                                                      January 2007 News Releases
