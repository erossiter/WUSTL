SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  212 000 TO STOP GANG VIOLENCE IN BIRMINGHAM    September 18  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  212 039 to the City of Birmingham for the Gang Resistance and Education Training  G R E A T   Program   The G R E A T  Program works to provide students with the skills they need to avoid gang pressure and violence  said Senator Shelby  By coordinating with federal  regional  state  and local agencies  the Program will provide training to law enforcement officers  who will teach middle school students techniques to keep themselves safe from violence before they are met with gang pressure  Empowering youth at a young age with the knowledge and skills to avoid crime and violence will improve not only their lives  but also public safety in Birmingham  These classes will be available to students in the fourth and sixth grades during both semesters of the school year and one summer session  The G R E A T  Program will teach at risk students about gangs  making mature decisions  dealing with peer pressure  and setting realistic goals                                                          September 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
