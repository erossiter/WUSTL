SHELBY ANNOUNCES  70 000 FOR ALABAMA COALITION AGAINST RAPE     April 3  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Commerce  Justice  and Science Appropriations Subcommittee  today announced that the U S  Department of Justice will award  70 000 to the Alabama Coalition Against Rape  ACAR   The ACAR is a network of domestic violence shelters  organizations  and allied professionals who serve as advocates for the safety and rights of battered and sexually abused women and their children   I am pleased to announce  70 000 for this important coalition to assist women displaced by Hurricane Katrina who have suffered from sexual assault or domestic violence  said Senator Shelby  This funding will also provide training for relief workers to provide trauma counseling for those victims who were displaced in Alabama by the hurricane   This program also specializes in assistance to women affected by Hurricane Katrina by ensuring victims access and transportation to domestic violence services  In addition  the funds may be used to replace equipment or supplies lost during the Hurricane  to provide salary and benefits for new and existing staff responding to the increased workload experienced by centers  and to provide outreach services information to Katrina evacuees in Alabama
