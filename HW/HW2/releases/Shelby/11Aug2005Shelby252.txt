SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  10 000 FOR CRIME CONTROL AND PREVENTION PROGRAMS IN PERRY COUNTY     August 18  2005   WASHINGTON  D  C   U S  Senator Richard C  Shelby  R Ala  announced today that U S  Department of Justice will be releasing  10 603 to Perry County for The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG                      JAG allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime based on the needs of the communities   These funds can be used for technical assistance  training  equipment and information systems for criminal justice for any one or more of the following purpose areas           Law Enforcement Programs   Prosecution and Court Programs   Prevention and Education Programs   Corrections and Community Corrections Programs   Drug Treatment Programs   Planning  Evaluation and Technology Improvements Programs         Perry County and the City of Marion will use these funds to purchase equipment to improve mobile to mobile communication and evidence retrieval from crime scenes   This will increase the integrity of evidence submitted for the prosecution of crimes   These funds will also be used to replace various law enforcement items for the Perry County Sheriffs Department and the Marion Police Department                                                                                                                Senator Shelby said  I am pleased to announce over  10 000 for law enforcement in Perry County   These funds will help improve communication among law enforcement officers and help to ensure the safety and protection of our officers and our citizens                                                      30
