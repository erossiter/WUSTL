SHELBY ANNOUNCES  68 113 FOR MARSHALL SPACE FLIGHT CENTER                          February 27  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Commerce  Justice  Science Subcommittee on Appropriations  announced today that the National Aeronautics and Space Administration  NASA  has released a total of  68 113 to the Marshall Space Flight Center for a geospace science program   Senator Shelby said  I am pleased that Marshall Space Flight Center has received funding for this important program  NASA continues to be on the forefront of emerging scientific research for our space programs  which creates new jobs and helps grow the economy in Huntsville   The geospace science program aims to understand the space that surrounds and is influenced by various solar system bodies  The research supports the Vision for Space Exploration and NASAs long term plan to return astronauts to the moon and extend exploration to Mars and beyond
