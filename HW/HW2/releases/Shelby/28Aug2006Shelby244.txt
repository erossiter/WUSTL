SHELBY ANNOUNCES  48 000 TO THE ALABAMA DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION FOR THE ROOTS OF AMERICAN MUSIC TRAIL    August 28  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Committee on Appropriations  today announced that the Department of Transportations Federal Highway Administration  FHA  will release  48 000 to the Alabama Department of Transportation to develop interpretive materials related to the Roots of American Music Trail  a 33 mile portion of the Natchez Trace Parkway from the Tennessee state line to the Mississippi state line   The Roots of American Music Trail commemorates the areas connection with folk and popular music  said Senator Shelby  Visitors to the Trail will learn about Americas music heritage  and in particular Alabamas contribution to the different varieties of music  The attraction will enhance the experience of visitors by exposing them to the full richness of Americas music heritage  including jazz  blues  Southern gospel  country  and rock and roll  In addition  visitors will learn about historically significant people  places  and events found near the Parkway  Prior to the development Roots of American Music Trail was planned  few pieces of the corridors musical heritage were collected or studied  This project will develop music heritage interpretive materials  including a website  brochures  and maps  Also  the Trail will develop a signing plan for Trail sites along the Natchez Trace Parkway and radio transmitters will be installed at two locations to broadcast information
