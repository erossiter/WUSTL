SHELBY ANNOUNCES  400 000 TO IMPROVE CRIMINAL HISTORY RECORDS PROGRAM    September 1  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard C  Shelby  R Ala   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the U S  Department of Justice will release  400 000 to the Alabama Criminal Justice Information Center  ACJIC  for the National Criminal History Improvement Program  NCHIP   The NCHIP provides law enforcement agencies nationwide with improved quality and accessibility of criminal history record information  said Senator Shelby  The key to reducing the number of repeat criminal offenders in our country is strong communication between all levels of law enforcement  By improving the current system of criminal and non criminal background checks  NCHIP will significantly improve our safety and security   With the funding provided  the ACJIC will eliminate its current backlog of fingerprint cards awaiting entry into the Automated Fingerprint Identification System  allowing Alabama Bureau of Investigation employees to focus on new requests and entries  The ACJIC will also use this funding develop a new arrest to disposition matching routine  which will allow the Alabama Criminal History Repository to match misdemeanor court dispositions to arrest records  In addition  the ACJIC will collect misdemeanor domestic violence convictions and include them in State and national files  collecting information on the estimated 150 000 unreported municipal court dispositions each year and flagging all court dispositions related to domestic violence                                                          September 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
