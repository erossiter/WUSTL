SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  50 000 FOR DRUG ENFORCEMENT IN CLEBURNE COUNTY    August 29  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  49 361 to the Cleburne County Sheriffs Department  The Sheriffs Department will use these funds to hire a full time drug enforcement officer  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing these funds during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   Drug use is a prevalent and growing problem in our communities  particularly the use of methamphetamines  said Senator Shelby  Criminal behavior often results from drug use  both violent and non violent  It is my hope that an officer with a full time focus on fighting drugs will send a clear message that drugs are not acceptable  thereby reducing drug use and criminal activity Cleburne County   The drug enforcement officer will follow up on drug cases made by other officers in the department  produce written reports  investigate  and obtain search and arrest warrants in drug cases  and educate students and teachers on the dangers of drug use at local schools  The officer with also be required to coordinate criminal drug investigations with federal  state  and local law enforcement agencies
