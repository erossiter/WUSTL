SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  1 2 MILLION TO THE ALABAMA CRIMINAL JUSTICE INFORMATION CENTER    August 23  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice will release  1 233 105 to the Alabama Criminal Justice Information Center for its Alliance for the Coordination of Criminal Records Direction  ACCORD  system  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing these funds during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   One of the best ways for us to stop repeat offenders is to ensure information is available throughout all levels of law enforcement  said Senator Shelby  The ACCORD system will give all of our criminal justice agencies  from federal agents to local sheriffs  a comprehensive database detailing a defendants history  Too often  criminals slip through the cracks because critical information is not readily available  I am confident that this system will sideline Alabamas criminal population by ensuring all facets of law enforcement have the same information  thereby keeping all our law enforcement on the same page  The ACCORD system uses both human and automated components to unify and integrate all criminal information and case management systems in Alabama  It will track a perpetrator from arrest through the criminal justice process  giving law enforcement a complete history of each offender  The system can also be accessed by any law enforcement official outside Alabama  including homeland security users
