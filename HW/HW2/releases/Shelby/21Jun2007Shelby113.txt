Shelby Announces  437 075 for Nursing Education at the University of South Alabama   June 26  2007  Washington  D C     U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL  today announced that the U S  Department of Health and Human Services will release  437 075 to the University of South Alabama for advanced nursing education programs  In 2005  the National Commission on Nursing Workforce for Long Term Care determined that there are nearly 100 000 nursing positions available each day in long term care facilities  The Commission also found that the nurse turnover rate exceeds 50 percent  These findings translate into an estimated  4 billion a year in recruitment and training expenses for health care providers  and these costs are certainly apparent not only to long term care consumers  but medical patients across the nation   The United States is in the midst of a nursing shortage  making it essential that we continue to educate  recruit and train nurses  said Shelby  As our nations baby boomers age  their need for quality health care will increase  Nurses are critical to maintaining the best and most effective health care system for this country and we must continue efforts to support nursing related programs   This project is funded through the Health Resources and Services Administrations Division of Grants Management Operations and support advanced nursing education and practice including masters and doctoral programs  combined RN masters degree programs  post nursing masters certificate programs  certificate programs that allow service as nurse practitioners  clinical nurse specialists  nurse midwives  nurse anesthetists  nurse educators  nurse administrators  or public health nurses  among other programs
