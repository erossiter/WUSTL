Shelby Announces Over  4 Million for Port Security in Alabama    May 10  2007  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  today announced that the U S  Department of Homeland Security  DHS  has released  445 23 million for infrastructure and protection projects across the nation  including  4 million for port security and buffer zone protection in Alabama  The Alabama state docks are a vital part of Alabamas transportation infrastructure and economy  said Shelby  As the docks expand and experience increased commercial traffic  we must take the proper precautions to effectively secure our ports and port facilities to ensure that they are not vulnerable to attack  This funding will allow the state docks to continue their efforts to implement and enhance essential security measures to protect the facility and the South Alabama community  The buffer zone protection program identifies and recommends security measures and local law enforcement coordination for the area surrounding a facility  making it more difficult for a potential attacker to conduct surveillance  or to plan or launch an attack   It is now more important than ever that we have a plan in place to protect our critical infrastructure and thwart potential terrorist threats  said Shelby   Projects funded include    3 268 967 for the Alabama State Port Authority in Mobile  Alabama   770 000 for Buffer Zone Protection in the state of Alabama   24 675 for the Ergon Terminaling  Inc  in Mulga  Alabama
