SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  35 000 FOR CRIME CONTROL AND PREVENTION PROGRAMS IN HOUSTON COUNTY       July 13  2005   WASHINGTON  D  C   U S  Senator Richard C  Shelby  R Ala  announced today that U S  Department of Justice will be releasing  35 130 to the City of Dothan for The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG      JAG allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime based on the needs of the communities   These funds can be used for technical assistance  training  equipment and information systems for criminal justice for any one or more of the following purpose areas        Law Enforcement Programs   Prosecution and Court Programs   Prevention and Education Programs                                                     Corrections and Community Corrections Programs   Drug Treatment Programs   Planning  Evaluation and Technology Improvements Programs                       The City of Dothan and Houston County will use these funds to purchase the AFIX Tracker  an automated fingerprint identification system   The AFIX system enables users to quickly search their own databases to compare new fingerprints to those of known offenders   This system will assist the City of Dothan and the Houston County Jail in their efforts to improve their criminal justice systems                      Senator Shelby said  I am pleased to announce over  35 000 for the City of Dothan   These funds will help support Dothans effort to improve the criminal justice system in their community   It is important that our local law enforcement offices are equipped with the proper tools to identify suspects immediately            30                                                          July 2005
