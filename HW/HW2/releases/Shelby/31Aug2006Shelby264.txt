SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  1 MILLION FOR BUILDING GOOD CITIZENS THROUGH CHARACTER AND CIVIC EDUCATION PROGRAM    August 31  2006  Washington  D C       U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice will release  987 228 to the Citizenship Trust at the American Village in Montevallo for their Building Good Citizens through Character and Civic Education program   At risk students participating in this program will learn about the positive character traits associated with civic responsibility  said Senator Shelby  The Building Good Citizens through Character and Civic Education program will help students avoid crime and negative behavior by focusing on ways to become responsible and civically minded adults  Our children need these programs to ensure they are given every chance to develop into contributing members of society  The Building Good Citizens through Character and Civic Education program will target at risk students in Alabama and the southeast  working to increase the knowledge and comprehension of positive character traits  civic roles and skills  and techniques to improve local communities  Students will learn from a curriculum surrounding character and civic education  including the completion of character and civic education activity kits during the school year  and participation in day camp programs during the summer                                                          August 2006 News Releases
