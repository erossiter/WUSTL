SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  600 000 TO ALABAMA LAW ENFORCEMENT FOR JUVENILE JUSTICE PROGRAMS     March 27  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  announced today that the Department of Justice has awarded  637 000 to the Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs  Law Enforcement and Traffic Safety Division   I am pleased to announce  637 000 for Alabamas Juvenile Justice Programs  said Shelby  Programs such as juvenile boot camp and after school accountability programs hold our youth accountable for their offenses  while placing them in a safe and secure environment that has proven to teach invaluable lessons and curb a life of crime  I believe that our children are our future and we should make every effort to keep them on the right track   These funds are given as a Juvenile Accountability Block Grant  JABG   which seeks to reduce juvenile criminal offenses through accountability based programs  JABG funds are allocated to each state based on the states population of youth under the age of 18
