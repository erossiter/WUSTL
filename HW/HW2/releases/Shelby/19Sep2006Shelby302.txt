SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  1 4 MILLION FOR JEFFERSON COUNTY    September 19  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  1 480 482 to Jefferson County for the Sheriffs Integrated Records Management System project  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing these funds during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   Accurate  real time information is vital for law enforcement officers to effectively do their jobs  said Senator Shelby  Communication is the key to ensuring that criminals do not fall through the systems cracks and that no stone is left unturned during an investigation  This project will ensure that the Jefferson County Sheriffs Office has the most up to date equipment available in order to effectively police the community  The Jefferson County Sheriffs Office lacks an integrated records management system and jail management system to share information among agency divisions  patrol and investigative activities  and the jails  With the funding provided  the Sheriffs Office will purchase a new system to collect  analyze and disseminate information  design  procure  implement  and maintain records  implement a reporting tool that provides crime statistic information  and utilize a standard report mandated by the Alabama Criminal Justice Information Center                                                         September 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
