SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  300 000 FOR CITY OF BIRMINGHAM AND JEFFERSON COUNTY      May 5  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice has awarded  323 415 to the City of Birmingham and Jefferson County for local law enforcement   I am pleased to announce  323 415 for the City of Birmingham and Jefferson County law enforcement  said Senator Shelby  I believe we should make every effort to support our first responders  who risk their lives everyday for our safety  It is critical that they have the most technologically advanced equipment to more efficiently do their jobs   Both the City of Birmingham and Jefferson County will use their funding for technological improvements  The Birmingham Police Department will purchase mobile data terminals  providing officers on patrol with accurate data as they need it  Jefferson County will purchase data communication equipment  which will also provide their officers with information from their patrol cars  Both technology upgrades will improve officer and public safety  These funds are released from the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  and allow law enforcement to use the resources to prevent and control crime based on their own needs  In this case  the departments have agreed to share the funding                                                            May 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
