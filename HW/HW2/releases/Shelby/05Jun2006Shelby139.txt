SHELBY ANNOUNCES  500 000 FOR HURRICANE AFFECTED AREAS IN ALABAMA                      June 7  2006   U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  today announced that the U S  Department of Commerce has awarded  500 000 in Economic Development Administration  EDA  funds to the Southeast Tourism Society of Atlanta  Georgia  These funds will allow the Society to develop a tourism marketing campaign for the Gulf Coast states of Alabama  Mississippi  and Louisiana  Senator Shelby said  Tourism is one of the Gulf Coast s most important economic engines  These funds will allow our states to inform the nation that our beaches and coastal communities are open for business  This program will spur economic growth and continue the progress towards complete recovery   President Bush believes one way to create jobs is to grow the economy by encouraging investments in local communities  said U S  Assistant Secretary of Commerce for Economic Development Sandy K  Baruah  This EDA investment will enable the Gulf Coast and New Orleans tourism sector to further its recovery and spur economic recovery in the area impacted by Hurricane Katrina   In 2003  the total impact of the travel and tourism industry in Alabama was estimated at  6 8 billion  while in 2005  that number was estimated at  3 2 billion
