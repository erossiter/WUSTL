SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  500 000 FOR AGING RESEARCH AT UAB    August 31  2006  Washington  D C          U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  today announced that the U S  Department of Health and Human Services National Institute on Aging has released  490 712 to the University of Alabama at Birmingham  UAB      Many elderly citizens experience loss of vision as they age  which can impact their daily lives  including their ability to get to and from important medical appointments  see family and friends  and enjoy their daily activities  said Senator Shelby  UABs research will develop ways for the elderly to maintain their sight  improve their quality of life  and ultimately keep our roads safe from impaired drivers    UABs Center for Research on Applied Gerontology will conduct research on ways to improve sensory and cognitive functions to help older adults maintain everyday abilities  Prior research conducted at UAB in this area led to the development of the Useful Field of View test  which identifies older drivers who are at increased risk for traffic accidents                                                           August
