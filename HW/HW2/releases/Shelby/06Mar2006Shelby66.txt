SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  500 000 FOR THE SELMA INTERPRETIVE CENTER       March 7  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that the Department of Transportation Federal Highway Administration has released  528 693 to the Alabama Department of Transportation for the Selma Interpretive Center   Senator Shelby said  I am pleased to announce that the Selma Interpretive Center will receive over  500 000 from the Federal Highway Administration  I believe that it is important for us to remember the civil rights pioneers who played a pivotal role in helping our state and our country move forward to ensure equal rights for all Americans  The City of Selma lives in the hearts and minds of us all and I believe this center will be an important reminder for generations to come   This funding will help prepare and develop construction plans for the rehabilitation of a historic building across from the Edmond Pettus Bridge  The building will be used as a visitor and interpretive center for the 1965 Voting Rights March  The Selma to Montgomery corridor is designated as one of Americas Byways on the National Historic Trail in recognition of the Selma to Montgomery March and its role in the Civil Rights Movement                                                           March 2006 News Releases
