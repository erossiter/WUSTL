SHELBY ANNOUNCES  17 6 MILLION FOR HURRICANE KATRINA RELIEF     March 29  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that the U S  Department of Transportation  DOT  will release  17 6 million for repairs to the Cochrane Bridge  interstate ramps  traffic signals  and other highway damage caused by Hurricane Katrina  DOT released these funds from the Emergency Highway Aid Package which was approved by Congress in 2005  Senator Shelby voted in favor of this legislation   I am pleased these funds are being made available to the state of Alabama for important repair work  said Shelby  We must continue the rebuilding efforts until the entire Gulf Coast returns to pre hurricane operational levels  A key component of the rebuilding efforts is restoring essential infrastructure to ensure safe travel in Alabama   Damage to the Cochrane Bridge occurred when an offshore oil platform was dislodged by Hurricane Katrinas winds  Funds will reimburse the state for bridge repairs and for new ramps connecting U S  90 and Interstate 10  replacing and repairing damaged traffic signals and highway signs  and clearing trees and other debris from roadways after the storm                                                            March 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
