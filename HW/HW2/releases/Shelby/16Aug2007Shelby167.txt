Shelby Announces  500 000 for Research at the University of Alabama    August 21  2007  Washington  D C     U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R Ala   today announced that the U S  Department of Transportations  DOT  Research and Innovative Technology Administration will release  500 000 to the University of Alabama to assist the Department in solving national and regional transportation challenges through research  education and technology transfer  As our nations workforce increases  our transportation systems will experience an influx of commuters  said Shelby  Unfortunately  with an increase in commuters  our transportation systems will be subject to increased congestion and traffic  The findings from research conducted at UA will assist city planners as they work to manage our transportation systems and ensure the safety of our commuters  The Safe  Accountable  Flexible  Efficient Transportation Equity Act of 2005 authorized up to  76 7 million per year from federal fiscal year 2005 2009 funds for grants to establish and operate up to 60 University Transportation Centers  UTCs   including UA  throughout the United States
