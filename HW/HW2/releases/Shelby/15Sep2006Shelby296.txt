SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  3 MILLION FOR BIRMINGHAM     September 15  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee today announced that the U S  Department of Homeland Securitys Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  has released  1 047 337 50 to the City of Birmingham for Rising West Princeton and Arlington West End and  2 960 062 60 to North East Lake  Collegeville  and Ensley for flood disaster prevention projects in flood prone Birmingham neighborhoods  This project will allow Birmingham to reduce the risk of flood damage and loss of life in the future  said Senator Shelby  These areas of Birmingham have seen tremendous destruction over the past half century from frequent flooding  I support this ongoing project to reduce risk and prevent disasters in the Valley Creek floodplain  With this funding  the City of Birmingham will acquire and demolish 108 repetitively flooded homes in the Valley Creek floodplain  This project will reduce post flood disaster recovery actions and costs spent by residents  city  state and federal governments  as these areas have experienced repetitive flood damages over the past 50 years  The project will also remove existing floodway obstructions and increase the overall floodplain storage and conveyance capacity   The federal funds are part of FEMAs Pre Disaster Mitigation Program  which provides funding assistance to state and local governments for pre disaster planning and projects  The goal of the program is to reduce overall risks to the population and structures  while also reducing the need for actual disaster declarations and relief                                                        September 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
