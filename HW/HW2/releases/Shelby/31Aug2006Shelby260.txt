SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  1 MILLION TO STOP CRIME IN MOBILE    August 31  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice will release  987 228 to the Mobile County Commission to fight crime in the area  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing this funding during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   Gang activity  concealed weapons  and drug trafficking have all increased in Mobile this year  These statistics are unacceptable  said Senator Shelby  The Mobile County Commission will treat juvenile offenders provide juvenile offenders with treatment and aftercare programs  ensuring that they are effectively reintegrated to their neighborhoods  Treating these young people will provide offenders with alternatives to a criminal lifestyle  improving Mobiles public safety  The Mobile County Juvenile Court has operated community based treatment programs for youth aged 13 17 since 1992  With the funding provided  the program will expand to include 80 additional youth  The Mobile County Juvenile  in cooperation with a local non profit agency will operate the program  working to ensure that 65 percent of juvenile offenders who complete the program do not re offend within a year
