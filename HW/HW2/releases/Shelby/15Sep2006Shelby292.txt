SHELBY ANNOUNCES  625 000 FOR UAB DISABILITY AND REHAB RESEARCH PROJECTS    September 15  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  today announced that the Department of Education will release  625 000 to the University of Alabama at Birmingham  UAB  for Disability and Rehabilitation Research Projects   The National Spinal Cord Injury Statistical Center  NSCISC  at UAB is home to the National Spinal Cord Injury Model Systems  SCIMS  Data Center  which collets and warehouses information related to spinal cord injuries  The SCIMS Data Center provides researchers with specific information related to individuals  injuries  and responses to treatment  With the funding provided  the NSCISC will utilize up to date technology to rewrite the database software to a centralized web based system  allowing the NSCISC to manage new data module projects  increase cost efficiency  improve quality control  and enhance security data   There are approximately 11 000 new cases of spinal cord injuries each year  said Senator Shelby  UABs NSCISC  which has been on the forefront of emerging research for over 20 years  will streamline their process by providing researchers with a comprehensive database for all spinal cord injury investigators  Not only will the new database help with individual projects  it will also increase collaboration between scholars  allowing them to easily share the data gleaned from participating patients                                                         September 2006 News Releases
