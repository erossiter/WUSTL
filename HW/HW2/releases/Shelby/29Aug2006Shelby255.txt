SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  1 MILLION FOR FORENSIC SCIENCES IN ALABAMA    August 30  2006  Washington  D C          U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  1 107 269 to the Alabama Department of Forensic Sciences to improve laboratory infrastructure and reduce DNA backlog  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing this funding during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process    DNA analysis is critically important to solving crimes and plays an integral role in criminal investigations  said Senator Shelby  The men and women who work in Alabamas crime labs need the most up to date infrastructure technology available to do their jobs not only effectively  but quickly  With this funding  DNA technology will allow our states judicial system to work to solve outstanding cases    The Alabama Department of Forensic Science will receive  396 000 to reduce backlog through in house analysis and data review and  711 269 to improve crime laboratories infrastructure  allowing them to process DNA evidence efficiently and cost effectively  Both awards will allow the state to utilize the potential of DNA technology                                                          August 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
