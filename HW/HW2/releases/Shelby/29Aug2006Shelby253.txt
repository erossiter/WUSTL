SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  200 000 TO REDUCE GUN CRIME IN ALABAMA    August 29  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release a total of  237 476 to agencies in Alabama for Project Safe Neighborhoods  a nation wide program that aims to reduce gun crime   Project Safe Neighborhoods works to unite existing local gun crime prevention programs and provide them with the additional tools and expertise they need to be successful  said Senator Shelby  I believe that the best way to fight crimes is to increase communication between all levels of law enforcement officers and enforce existing laws  Project Safe Neighborhood will bring many local gun crime prevention programs together through coordination and intelligence sharing  providing the strong offensive needed to stop gun crime   Project Safe Neighborhoods is led by the United States Attorney in each federal judicial district  while the fiscal agent in the community will allocate funding to other programs to further the initiative   Project Safe Neighborhoods Fiscal Agents in Alabama include  The Montgomery Area United Way  funded at  66 052  will fund Project Safe Neighborhood billboards  bus signs  and public services announcement in Middle Alabama  as well as distribute funding to the following programs   Alabama Isolate the Criminal Element   The Dothan Police Departments Street SMART program  a summer long effort to target 150 at risk youth and teach them to resist gang involvement  will be funded for their re entry programs   Crime Stoppers  a toll free telephone number to report tips of illegal firearm crimes      The United Way of Southwest Alabama  funded at  64 920 will initiate programs in coordination with Alabama Isolate the Criminal Element  while focusing on education  prevention  enforcement  prosecution  and technology   The Non Profit Resource Center of Alabama  funded at  96 465  will focus on prevention  control  and reduction of gun crime in high crime areas across the district  particularly in Birmingham
