SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  131 000 FOR BIRMINGHAM CIVIL RIGHTS INSTITUTE     September 26  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Labor  Health and Human Services and Education  today announced that the Institute of Museum and Library Services will award  131 799 to the Birmingham Civil Rights Institute   The Birmingham Civil Rights Institute will use the funding provided to develop and present a leadership initiative for African American museums across the nation  said Senator Shelby  The Institute will use the program to work with upper level managers and junior staff members from other African American museums to build institutional capacity in strategic planning  budget management  audience development and collaboration  and grant writing  The Institute continues to be a leader in presenting African American heritage and culture  This grant will allow the Institute to share its knowledge across the country This funding works to provide opportunities for African American museums to design projects that will enhance the institutional capacity and sustainability through professional training  technical assistance  internships  and outside expertise  The National Museum of African American History and Culture Act authorized the Institute of Museum and Library Services  in consultation with the Council and Director of the Smithsonians National Museum of African American History and Culture  to create a new grant program to strengthen African American museums  This grant announcement marks the first cycle of grants ever awarded in this program                                                          September 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
