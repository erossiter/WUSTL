Shelby Announces  274 250 for Nursing Education at the University of Alabama at Birmingham   June 26  2007  Washington  D C     U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL  today announced that the U S  Department of Health and Human Services will release  274 250 to the University of Alabama at Birmingham for Advanced Nursing Education  In 2005  the National Commission on Nursing Workforce for Long Term Care determined that there are nearly 100 000 nursing positions available each day in long term care facilities  The Commission also found that the nurse turnover rate exceeds 50 percent  These findings translate into an estimated  4 billion a year in recruitment and training expenses for health care providers  and these costs are certainly apparent not only to long term care consumers  but medical patients across the nation   As our nations baby boomers age  their need for health care will increase and our nursing shortage will become even direr than it is today  said Shelby  It is essential that we continue to train  educate and recruit good nurses so that we can maintain our high standards of heath care and lower medical costs across the country  This project is funded through the Health Resources and Services Administrations Division of Grants Management Operations and supports advanced nursing education and practice including masters and doctoral programs  combined RN masters degree programs  post nursing masters certificate programs  certificate programs that allow service as nurse practitioners  clinical nurse specialists  nurse midwives  nurse anesthetists  nurse educators  nurse administrators  or public health nurses  among other programs                                                         June 2007 News Releases                      Year
