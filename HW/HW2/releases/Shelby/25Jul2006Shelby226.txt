SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  200 000 TO FIGHT SEX OFFENDERS IN CULLMAN   July 26  2006  Washington  DC    U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Committee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  149 927 to the City of   Cullman to manage juvenile sex offenders residing in the City    Crimes against children are a plague on our society  said Senator Shelby  The efforts Cullmans study will make will go a long way towards understanding this deviant behavior and determining the best way to catch  treat  and stop sex offenders    The Child Advocacy Center of Cullman will coordinate a team to assess programs and policies in place to manage the Citys juvenile sex offenders  The team will develop new strategies to monitor and evaluate the methods available for management beginning with the investigation of a sexual crime through treatment of the offender  This funding was provided through DOJs Comprehensive Approaches to Sex Offender Management Grant Program                                                                    July 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
