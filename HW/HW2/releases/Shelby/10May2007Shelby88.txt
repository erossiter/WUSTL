Shelby Announces  9 3 Million for Randolph County     May 10  2007  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a senior member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  today announced that the United States Department of Agriculture  USDA  Rural Development has approved a grant of  3 3 million and a loan of  6 million to extend the Randolph County water line   Randolph County is in an ideal geographical position to benefit from the new Kia plant in West Point  Georgia  said Shelby  These upgrades to the countys infrastructure are essential to recruiting Tier 1 distributors in the surrounding area  Other regions in Alabama have seen substantial improvements to their economies through the automotive industry and I think we can expect similar job growth in Randolph County  It is critical that Randolph County prepare for their infrastructure to attract new businesses and possible jobs  The water line extension will provide service to an additional 400 Randolph County citizens  The project will also join the Randolph County water line to the Cleburne County water line  which will improve water service in both counties
