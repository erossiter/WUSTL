SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  80 000 TO CITY OF TUSCALOOSA FOR LAW ENFORCEMENT    June 5  2006   U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  announced today that the Department of Justice has awarded  81 107 to the City of Tuscaloosa to purchase equipment on behalf of the Tuscaloosa Police Department and the Tuscaloosa County Sheriffs Department   I am pleased to announce over  80 000 for the City of Tuscaloosa  I believe it is our responsibility to support those men and women who risk their lives everyday for our safety  said Shelby  It is critical that the Alabama law enforcement has the technology necessary to effectively communicate   The Tuscaloosa Police Department will use their portion of the grant to purchase five Mobile Date Units  MDUs   MDUs allow officers to quickly access information from central databases while in patrol vehicles  The City aims to include a MDU in each patrol unit  The Tuscaloosa County Sheriffs Office will also use 2006 grant funds to purchase an AFIX Tracker 4 5  which will allow easy and quick access to fingerprint matching  personal history  and crime reports   These funds are released from the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  and allow law enforcement to use the resources to prevent and control crime based on their own needs
