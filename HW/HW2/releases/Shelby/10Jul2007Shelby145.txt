Shelby Announces  450 000 for Research at the University of Alabama at Birmingham   July 12  2007  Washington  D C     U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL  today announced that the U S  Department of Transportations  DOT  Research and Innovative Technology Administration will release  450 000 to the University of Alabama at Birmingham  UAB  to assist the Department in solving national and regional transportation challenges through research  education and technology transfer  As our population and workforce continue to increase  cities across the nation will experience an influx of commuters  said Shelby  With additional traffic and congestion  lengthy commutes will have a negative impact on productivity and quality of life  The findings from this research will help city planners attempt to improve commuter traffic patterns  The Safe  Accountable  Flexible  Efficient Transportation Equity Act of 2005 authorized up to  76 7 million per year from federal fiscal year 2005 2009 funds for grants to establish and operate up to 60 University Transportation Centers  UTCs   including UAB  throughout the United States                                                           July 2007 News
