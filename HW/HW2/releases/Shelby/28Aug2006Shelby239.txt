SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  350 000 TO ELIMINATE FORENSIC SCIENCE BACKLOG    August 28  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  357 181 to the Alabama Department of Forensic Sciences to conduct criminal DNA analysis  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing this funding during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   Backlog in Alabamas forensic labs is unacceptable  said Senator Shelby  DNA analysis is critically important to solving crimes and plays an integral role in criminal investigations  With this funding  Alabamas forensic scientists can work to bring justice to the victims of crimes   The Alabama Department of Forensic Science will use this funding for the Forensic Casework DNA Backlog Reduction Program  which allows state and local governments to analyze backlogged forensic DNA casework samples  The samples are from unsolved forcible rape  murder  and non negligent manslaughter and are awaiting results in both government owned and fee for service laboratories
