SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  2 MILLION FOR ALABAMA FORENSIC SCIENCES    September 19  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  1 974 456 to the Alabama Department of Forensic Sciences  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing these funds during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   With the help of the Department of Justices DNA Backlog Reduction Program  Alabama has made great progress in reducing the states DNA backlog from 18 to six months  said Senator Shelby  While great progress has been made  there is still more to do  and this funding will allow Alabamas four crime labs to upgrade their facilities with state of the art equipment and greatly reduce the amount of time needed to process and analyze DNA evidence  It is essential that we continue to work to reduce and eventually eliminate the states DNA backlog and this funding is an important step in that direction  The Alabama Department of Forensic Sciences will use the funding provided to reduce the DNA casework backlog throughout the state  All four regional DNA laboratories in Alabama will benefit from improvements to their infrastructure and analysis capacity  as each DNA laboratory will be provided with equipment and resources in critical areas of the casework process and the implementation of additional resources to prevent future DNA backlogs                                                            September 2006 News Releases
