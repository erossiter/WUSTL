SHELBY ANNOUNCES  200 000 FOR CRIME FIGHTING INITIATIVES IN BIRMINGHAM     September 11  2006  Washington  D C       U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  200 000 to the City of Birmingham to fight drug use and other crimes     Despite the efforts of local law enforcement  crime in the Birmingham area has steadily increased in recent years  said Senator Shelby  We must work together and take the proper precautions to ensure the safety of Birmingham citizens and put an end to continued increases in crime  The Weed and Seed Program has proven effective in other areas  and I am hopeful that the Northside Community will soon benefit from these efforts as well    The Birmingham Weed and Seed Program will work to suppress crime and drug activity through increased enforcement  undercover operations  search warrants  and gun enforcement efforts  The program also works to reduce other various street level crimes  This project will be focused on prevention programs through the involvement of community residents  Local law enforcement agencies will use prevention  intervention  and treatment strategies to improve the area  Northside Community is comprised of the Central City  Druid Hills  and Norwood neighborhoods
