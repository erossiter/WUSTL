Shelby Announces  5 Million for Alabama State University   July 30  2007  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R Ala   today announced that the National Science Foundation will award  4 941 544 to Alabama State University  ASU  for the establishment of a Center for Nanobiotechnology Research  CNBR    The CNBR will provide training and research resources to the Environmental Microbiology Ph D  program at ASU  as well as work in collaboration with the University of Alabama at Birmingham  University of Louisville  University of South Florida and Tulane National Primate Research Center to carry out four research themes in the area of nanotechnology and biotechnology   This funding is great news because it illustrates the strength of the research program at ASU  said Shelby  Nanobiotechnology is a rapidly expanding discipline and the research that will be conducted at the CNBR will enhance ASUs reputation as an emerging premier scientific institution  I look forward to the discoveries made through this center at ASU and the opportunities that it will provide to current and future researchers in this exciting field  The CNBR will also include an international scientist exchange program  provide for travel to scientific meetings  allow for research at collaborating institutions during the summer and provide an annual science symposium
