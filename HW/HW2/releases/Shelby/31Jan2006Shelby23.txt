SHELBY ANNOUNCES  15 MILLION FOR WEST ALABAMA REGIONAL ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT INITIATIVE     February 1  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that the U S  Department of Labor will release  15 million over three years to The Western Alabama and Eastern Mississippi Economic Region  The funding is provided through the Workforce Innovation in Regional Economic Development  WIRED  program  which emphasizes a competitive workforce in the global economy   Senator Shelby said  This funding will bring significant economic development and new opportunities to a region of the state that has not yet reached its potential  In this global economy  it is critical that we constantly explore new ways to promote American products and American jobs  and I am proud that our state of Alabama was selected to participate in this program   The Department of Labor competitively selected 12 economic regions from across the country to receive assistance in the networking of their regional assets  Each region will receive approximately  15 million in funding over a three year period and will be provided ongoing expertise from several of the leading organizations in the field of innovation  The goal in each of these regions is to expand employment and advancement opportunities for American workers and catalyze the creation of high skill and high wage jobs in regional economies  The Western Alabama and Eastern Mississippi region includes 17 counties in Alabama including Tuscaloosa and Selma and 19 counties in Mississippi including Meridian and Starkville                                                            February 2006 News Releases                      Year
