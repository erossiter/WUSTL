SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  1 MILLION FOR TUSCALOOSA SCHOOLS                   June 5  2006   U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that the U S  Department of Education has awarded  999 210 to Tuscaloosa City Schools   I am pleased to announce nearly  1 000 000 for teacher training in Tuscaloosa said Shelby  I believe that a strong knowledge of our nations history is an important part of a solid education  and I am pleased that many of Alabamas teachers are continuing a lifetime of education to better impart their knowledge to our students   The program will allow faculty from the University of Alabama  along with several research institutes  to lead the Tuscaloosa City and County School systems faculty through a summer long intensive institute on methods of history education  The program  Making a Nation  will focus on the founders of the United States  The institute will target students who have not made adequate yearly progress and schools that traditionally have a low percentage of students passing the social studies portion of the Alabama High School Graduation Exam by working with their teachers  The funding is distributed through the Department of Educations Teaching American History Grants Program                                                          June 2006 News Releases
