SHELBY ANNOUNCES  291 000 TO DISEASE RESEARCH AT UAB    September 15  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard C  Shelby  R Ala   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee today announced that the U S  Department of Health and Human Services National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases has released  291 000 for Allergy  Immunology  and Transplantation Research at the University of Alabama at Birmingham  UAB    Infectious diseases affect millions of Americans each day  said Senator Shelby  This study  conducted at UABs nationally renowned medical research centers  could make important strides in fighting and preventing many different types of disease  I am proud to see that once again  UAB is on the forefront of this groundbreaking medical research   Researchers at UAB will study new ways to better control infections by manipulating the immune system  Previous studies have determined that many times the collaboration between two different types of cells can control viral infections  tumors  and parasites  The goal of this study is to understand how these two cell types cooperate to provide protective immunity and may ultimately play a role in building better vaccines to combat existing and emerging infectious diseases                                                         September 2006 News Releases                      Year
