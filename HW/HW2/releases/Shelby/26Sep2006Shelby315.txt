SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  754 000 FOR FAIRFIELD FIRE DEPARTMENT    September 28  2006 U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  today announced that the Department of Homeland Securitys Office of State and Local Government Coordination and Preparedness will release  754 690 to the Fairfield Fire Department  This funding will be provided by the Staffing for Adequate Fire and Emergency Response  SAFER  grant program  As we continue to secure our homeland  communities must have the personnel available to respond to hazards of any nature  whether it is a fire  tornado  or terrorist attack  said Senator Shelby  First responders are on the front lines of our local communities  fighting to protect our citizens  This funding will allow the Fairfield Fire Department to hire new firefighters as they work to reach 24 hour staffing for the station   This funding will help fire departments to increase staffing and deployment capabilities and ultimately attain 24 hour staffing  thus assuring that their communities have adequate protection from fire and fire related hazards  The SAFER Program assists in hiring full time firefighters and supports recruitment and retention of volunteer fire fighters                                                           September 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
