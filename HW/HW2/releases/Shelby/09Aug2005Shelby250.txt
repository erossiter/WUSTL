SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  900 000 FOR CRIME CONTROL AND PREVENTION PROGRAMS THROUGHOUT ALABAMA       August 11  2005   WASHINGTON  D  C   U S  Senator Richard C  Shelby  R Ala  announced today that the U S  Department of Justice will be releasing  924 635 for crime control and prevention programs throughout Alabama   These funds are made available through The Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG                     JAG allows states  tribes  and local governments to support a broad range of activities to prevent and control crime based on the needs of the communities   These funds can be used for technical assistance  training  equipment and information systems for criminal justice for any one or more of the following purpose areas         Law Enforcement Programs   Prosecution and Court Programs   Prevention and Education Programs   Corrections and Community Corrections Programs   Drug Treatment Programs   Planning  Evaluation and Technology Improvements Programs       These funds will be provided to the following communities        The City of Enterprise will receive  11 892 for camera equipment for undercover operations and handheld radar units   Coffee County will also use these funds to purchase a lap top computer to improve crime scene investigating and reporting    The City of Decatur will receive  38 351 for raid jackets and tactical entry vests for the special response team  radar units and in car video cameras     The City of Northport will receive  11 793 for a virtual private network for the Northport Police Departments main computer  a mobile computer for patrol units and a high speed network access to upload completed reports from patrol vehicles    The City of Demopolis will receive  15 856 for two speed detection and reminder trailers to be used in residential areas where vehicles routinely exceed the speed limit   Marengo County will use these funds to purchase software that will enhance surveillance videos which will help investigators to identify potential suspects and witnesses of crimes    The City of Tuscaloosa and Tuscaloosa County will receive  158 656 for the implementation of a crime analysis unit  mobile data terminals  and in vehicle digital video cameras    The City of Anniston will receive  99 544 to upgrade their polygraph equipment and to purchase digital security cameras to be placed both outside and inside of the police department   Calhoun County will use these funds to hire an investigator who will work exclusively on child abuse cases   The City of Oxford will use these funds to address security needs in and around their police building    The City of Guntersville will receive  13 973 to implement their Take Back the Streets in 2005 project targeting the distribution of illegal narcotics and gang activity  These funds will also be used for the purchase of much needed police department equipment      The Baldwin County Commission will receive  11 594 for the implementation of a Behavioral Management Program and improved security at the Baldwin County Juvenile Detention Center    Etowah County and the City of Gadsden will receive  114 012 for mobile computers and data software for patrol vehicles to reduce officer response time and enable officers to share information among law enforcement agencies     Dallas County and City of Selma will receive  69 616 to update the tracking system for sex offenders throughout the county and to implement Project Bulls Eye  a task force of off duty police officers who will patrol targeted areas of high crime    Mobile County  the City of Mobile and City of Prichard will receive  338 321 for law enforcement vehicles  radios  mobile data terminals  automatic vehicle location systems and video surveillance cameras    Cullman County will receive  16 054 for digital cameras to enable law enforcement officers to take crime scene photos to be used in court proceedings    Chambers County and the City of Lanett will receive  11 347 for the purchase of new computers and digital cameras for crime scene investigators    Tallapoosa County will receive  13 626 for radar units  an in car video system  laptop computers and printers   Alexander City will use these funds purchase a new server  computer equipment and laser printers                               Senator Shelby said  I am pleased to announce over  924 000 for crime control and prevention programs throughout Alabama   These funds will provide our law enforcement officers with the necessary tools to combat crime in our communities   We are continuing to improve our criminal justice system by providing the essential funds to upgrade equipment and implement programs that are beneficial to our communities   It is important that we do everything possible to ensure the safety of our law enforcement officers and our citizens             30                                                          August 2005 News Releases
