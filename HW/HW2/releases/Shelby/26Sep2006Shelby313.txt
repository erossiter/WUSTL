SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  263 000 FOR HOUSING COUNSELING PROGRAM IN ALABAMA    September 26  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and Chairman of the Banking  Housing  and Urban Affairs Committee  today announced that the U S  Department of Housing and Urban Development will release  263 226 to housing counseling programs across Alabama   The programs funded through the Department of Housing and Urban Developments Housing Counseling Program will help many Alabamians achieve their dream of home ownership  said Senator Shelby  These educational programs will prepare first time homebuyers for the financial obligations of home ownership  They will also assist with understanding the details of a mortgage and other financing requirements   The Department of Housing and Urban Developments Housing Counseling Program seeks to expand homeownership opportunities and improve access to affordable housing  Through providing housing counseling services to homebuyers  homeowners  low to moderate income renters  and the homeless  the Program advises families and individuals on how to improve their housing conditions and meet the responsibilities of tenancy and homeownership  Counseling efforts also help buyers avoid inflated appraisals  unreasonably high interest rates  unaffordable repayment terms  and other conditions that cause a loss of equity  increased debt  default  and eventually foreclosure   Programs Funded Include   Alabama Council on Human Relations  Inc   23 400   Community Action Agency of 605   Jefferson County Housing Authority   21 202   Mobile Housing Board   44 161   Montgomery Community Action Committee   27 055   Organized Community Action Program  Inc   26 008
