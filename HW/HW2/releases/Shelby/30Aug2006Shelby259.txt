SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  1 MILLION FOR UNIVERSITY OF SOUTH ALABAMA  S VIOLENCE PREVENTION PROGRAM    August 31  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice will release  987 228 for the University of South Alabama  USA  for their Youth Violence Prevention Program  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing this funding during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   Youth violence can lead to a lifetime of crime and imprisonment for many young offenders  said Senator Shelby  By researching the causes of youth crime and determining the best deterrents and preventative measures  USA will empower high risk youth with alternatives to a delinquent lifestyle  Our children are our most valuable resources  and we should make every effort to ensure that they become independent and responsible adults  The USA Youth Violence Prevention Program will work to identify the causes of youth violence  assess current prevention initiatives in the local community  and evaluate intervention programs  USA will also respond to community requests for program evaluation and technical assistance  The program will share their results and findings through evidence based products  presentations  and papers
