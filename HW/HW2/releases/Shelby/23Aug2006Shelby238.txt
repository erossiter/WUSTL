SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  150 000 TO IMPROVE ALABAMA  S FORENSIC SCIENCE    August 28  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  155 450 to the Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs  Law Enforcement and Traffic Safety Division  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing this funding through the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   Forensic evidence is often the deciding factor in determining the guilty party after a crime  said Senator Shelby  Analyzing that evidence should be a process as streamlined and timely as possible  Victims should not have to wait in limbo  nor should a criminal remain on the streets  while evidence waits in a laboratory   The funding is distributed through the fiscal year 2006 Coverdell Forensic Science Improvement Grant Program Solicitation  Coverdell funds are distributed to agencies working to improve the quality and expedience of forensic science and medical examiner services  They can also be used to eliminate backlog in analyzing forensic evidence  including controlled substances  firearms examination  forensic pathology  latent prints  questioned documents  toxicology  and trace evidence
