SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  200 000 FOR YOUTH CRIME PREVENTION     August 29  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  246 807 to the Alabama Center for Law and Civic Education for the development of the national Play by the Rules project  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing this funding during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   Play by the Rules teaches middle school students about the law  thereby providing them with the knowledge to prevent crime  said Senator Shelby  By working with students before they reach their high school years  Play by the Rules equips young people with need to lead a crime free life before they are irreparably influenced by peer pressure  The program also involves parents and teachers  which is critical to reaching adolescents and keeping them on a straight path   Play by the Rules is a joint project between the Alabama Center for Law and Civic Education  the Alabama Department of Youth Services  and the Alabama Attorney Generals Office  The funding provided will evaluate the program  develop a training module to implement the project  develop outreach and promotional materials  and plan and facilitate conferences in preparation of making the program one that reaches young people nationwide                                                           August 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
