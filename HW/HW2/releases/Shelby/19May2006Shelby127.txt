SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  1 MILLION FOR ALABAMA DISTRICT ATTORNEY COMPUTER FORENSICS LABS     May 23  2006  Washington  D C     U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice has released  987 227 to the Alabama District Attorneys Association  ADAA  for forensic computer labs in the state   I am pleased to announce nearly  1 million for computer investigations in Alabama  said Senator Shelby  The Alabama District Attorneys have recognized a need to improve their ability to effectively prosecute crimes in the high tech world we live in today  These funds will ensure the quick and effective prosecution of a variety of crimes and will be specifically helpful in prosecution of child exploitation cases such as child pornography and online solicitation of a minor   The ADAA will implement three regional computer forensic science labs within the state to analyze  store  and hold custody of digital evidence  These labs allow computer forensic examinations and integrated data mining  storage  archival and redundant backup for all digital evidence seized and examined by the ADAA  This technology will allow the ADAA to investigate and prosecute more efficiently by centralizing and streamlining the process   Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing these funds during the federal appropriations process                                                           May 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
