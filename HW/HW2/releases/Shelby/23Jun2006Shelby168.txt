SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  1 2 MILLION FOR NURSING PROJECTS AT THE UNIVERSITY SOUTH ALABAMA     June 27  2006   U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  today announced that the United States Department of Health and Human Services will award a total of  1 285 421 to the University of South Alabama  USA  for advanced nursing education programs   The United States is in the midst of a nursing shortage  making it essential that we continue to educate  recruit and train nurses  said Shelby  This shortage will continue to intensify as baby boomers age and their need for health care grows  Nurses are critical to maintaining the best and most effective health care system for this country and we must continue efforts to support nursing related programs   These projects are funded through the Health Resources and Services Administrations Division of Grants Management Operations                                                           June 2006 News
