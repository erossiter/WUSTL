SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  300 000 FOR NURSING EDUCATION AT THE UNIVERSITY OF ALABAMA     July 18  2006   U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  today announced that the United States Department of Health and Human Services will award  349 067 to the University of Alabama  UA  for advanced education in nursing   As baby boomers age and their need for health care grows  our nursing shortage will become even more dire than it is today  said Senator Shelby  It is essential that we train  educate  and recruit good nurses to maintain our high standards of heath care and lower medical costs across the country   Last year  the National Commission on Nursing Workforce for Long Term Care determined that there are nearly 100 000 nursing positions available each day in long term care facilities  The Commission also found that the nurse turnover rate exceeds 50 percent  These findings translate into an estimated  4 billion a year in recruitment and training expenses for health care providers  and these cost are certainly apparent not only to long term care consumers  but medical patients across the nation   These projects are funded through the Health Resources and Services Administrations Division of Grants Management Operations and support advanced nursing education and practice including masters and doctoral programs  combined RN masters degree programs  post nursing masters certificate programs  certificate programs that allow service as nurse practitioners  clinical nurse specialists  nurse midwives  nurse anesthetists  nurse educators  nurse administrators  or public health nurses  among other programs                                                            July 2006 News Releases
