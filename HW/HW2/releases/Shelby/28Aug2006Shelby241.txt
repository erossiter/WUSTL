SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  200 000 FOR SELMA TO MONTGOMERY MARCH BYWAY    August 28  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Committee on Appropriations  today announced that the Department of Transportations Federal Highway Administration  FHWA  will release  287 800 for the development and installation of interpretive exhibits on the Selma to Montgomery March Byway and National Historic Trail to commemorate the 1965 Voting Rights March   The Selma to Montgomery March Byway and National Historic Trail commemorates an important time in Alabamas history  said Senator Shelby  This funding will be used by the National Park Service to develop and install 55 informational signs along the byway  These signs will provide the historical context necessary to allow generations of Americans to learn about the importance of the 1965 Voting Rights March  FHWA has provided  126 200 for the development of these interpretive exhibits and  161 600 for their installation  The 55 porcelain enamel signs will allow visitors to view objects and features in their own setting  offer information  provide on site interpretation of specific sites and stories  and show features from views visitors are unable to visit  The National Park Service will have lead responsibility for this project
