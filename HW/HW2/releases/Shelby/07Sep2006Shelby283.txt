SHELBY ANNOUNCES MORE THAN  1 1 MILLION FOR UAB BIOTERRORISM RESEARCH    September 8  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard C  Shelby  R Ala   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee today announced that the U S  Department of Health and Human Services National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases has released  1 125 082 to the University of Alabama at Birmingham  UAB  for its Allergy  Immunology  and Transplantation Research   Bioterrorism is a very real and alarming threat to our nation  said Senator Shelby  As we continue to fight the War on Terror  it is critical that our researchers work to find ways to combat bioterror threats  I am proud that these efforts  which could save the lives of millions of Americans  are being conducted at UAB  home to some of the nations best researchers   Researchers at UABs Department of Vision Sciences are working to develop the first antibacterial drugs designed specifically to treat infections caused by exposure to anthrax  plague  and tularemia  Symptoms of exposure to each of the substances are largely indistinguishable  and this project will work to develop one drug that will treat all three  In the event of a bioterrorism attack  a drug created as a result of this research could save the lives of those exposed to the attack                                                           September 2006 News
