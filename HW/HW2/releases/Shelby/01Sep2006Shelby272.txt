SHELBY ANNOUNCES MORE THAN  490 000 FOR UNIVERSITY OF WEST ALABAMA    September 6  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  493 614 to the University of West Alabama  UWA   Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing this funding during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   Education and training programs are necessary to improve the lives of Alabamians struggling to make ends meet  said Senator Shelby  This program will not only help educate parents and students to improve their future job potential  but will also work to enrich relationships  which are the cornerstone of a strong family  I am confident that this program will allow parents and students alike to meet their potential both professionally and within the family  With the funding provided  UWA will implement an outreach and partnership project targeting parents and children in the area  working to keep parents involved in their childrens education  UWA will establish an on campus resource center to provide information  education  training  and support for parents and students  provide technical assistance and training to the Rendering Every Adult and Child Help  REACH  community Education Center in Livingston  including youth violence prevention seminars  training programs  and information to improve parent child relationships and positive behavior  UWA will also use the funding to purchase two finger printing screening machines so that students and faculty can be cleared to work in these programs  The Alabama Child Protection Act of 1999 states that all teacher education and nursing students must be finger printed  These machines will provide for a quicker turnaround on finger printing reporting and personnel clearance                                                        September 2006 News Releases
