SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  246 000 TO STOP GANG VIOLENCE IN RUSSELL COUNTY    September 18  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  52 643 to the Russell County Sherriffs Department for the Gang Resistance and Education Training  G R E A T   Program   The G R E A T  Program will train law enforcement officers to teach middle school students techniques to keep themselves safe from gang violence and pressures  said Senator Shelby  The Program will coordinate with federal  regional  state  and local agencies to equip students with the skills they need to avoid gang pressure  G R E A T  will arm students with the skills necessary become contributing members of society  These classes will be available to students in the fourth and sixth grades during both semesters of the school year and one summer session  The G R E A T  Program will teach at risk students about gangs  making mature decisions  dealing with peer pressure  and setting realistic goals
