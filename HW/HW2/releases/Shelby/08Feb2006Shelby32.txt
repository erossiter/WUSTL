SHELBY ANNOUNCES  2 4 MILLION FOR UNIVERSITY OF SOUTH ALABAMA        February 15  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Commerce  Justice  Science Subcommittee on Appropriations  announced today that the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration  NOAA  will release  2 4 million to the University of South Alabama  USA  to support the Oyster Reef and Fisheries Habitat Enhancement Program   Senator Shelby said  This program will have tremendous benefits for marine habitat and in turn will have a positive impact on Alabamas fisheries and the economy  The University of South Alabama will use the  2 4 million to continue conducting surveys and work on the oyster bed reseeding program in Mobile Bay  The program will attempt to restore productive oyster reefs in areas that have experienced reef loss due to environmental and harvesting factors  A resurgence of the oyster beds will significantly impact the water quality and clarity in Mobile Bay  The funding will also be used to assist the Bay in fostering survival of sea grass meadows and marine species such as blue crab  sea trout  and some snappers and grouper   The funding will allow USA to conduct an inventory and assessment of several habitat types throughout the Mobile Bay area  including shellfish beds  seagrass  and natural hard bottom  USA will also restore oyster reefs and place artificial reefs in Mobile Bay  The research will surround measuring ecological benefits of oyster reefs  fish utilization of artificial reefs  and seagrass habitats as nursery grounds and areas
