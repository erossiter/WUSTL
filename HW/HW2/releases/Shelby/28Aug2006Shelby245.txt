SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  400 000 TO IMPROVE TECHNOLOGY IN THE TRI COUNTY AREA     August 28  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Committee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  394 891 to the Central Alabama Regional Planning and Development Commission  CARPDC   Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing this funding during the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   Americans know all too well how quickly a disaster can strike  said Senator Shelby  Whether it is a terrorist attack  a natural disaster  or a fire  it is critical that our communities have the necessary plans in place should a disaster occur  Technology allows us to protect our communities  and I believe it is vital that local law enforcement agencies are outfitted with the most up to date technology available  The funding provided will promote and improve regional emergency readiness and response  The CARPDC will also work to promote public policy that will protect citizens  facilities  infrastructure  private property and the environment from hazards  The first phase of the project will purchase GIS stations  an encoder  radios  and other equipment  During phase two  the CARPDC will staff GIS training and project oversight  as well as other issues identified in the
