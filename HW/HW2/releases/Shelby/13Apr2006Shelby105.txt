SHELBY ANNOUNCES NEARLY  200 000 FOR MOBILE AREA LAW ENFORCEMENT     April 27  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  announced today that the Department of Justice has awarded  196 544 to the Mobile County Commission  City of Prichard  and the City of Mobile for communication information systems of criminal activity with local  state  and federal law enforcement agencies  to improve technology  and to upgrade equipment   I am pleased to announce nearly  200 000 for the Mobile County Commission  City of Prichard  and the City of Mobile  I believe it is our responsibility to support those men and women who risk their lives everyday for our safety  said Shelby  It is critical that the Alabama law enforcement has the most technologically advanced crime fighting equipment available   The City of Prichard will use its share of the funding to provide high patrol visibility to deter crime  Their funding will purchase vehicles  radios  and safety equipment  among other law enforcement supplies  The City of Mobile will use these funds to update security and surveillance equipment  mobile data terminals  laptop computers and automatic vehicle locator system units for community policing  Mobile County will use their funds to improve the technology used in the Sheriffs Office  and replace computer equipment damaged during Hurricane Katrina  These funds are released from the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  and allow law enforcement to use the resources to prevent and control crime based on their own needs                                                            April 2006 News Releases                      Year
