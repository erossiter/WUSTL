SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  165 000 FOR AUBURN UNIVERSITY                                  June 13  2006   U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  today announced that the U S  Department of Commerce has awarded  165 700 in Economic Development Administration  EDA  funds to the Auburn University Technical Assistance Center  These funds will allow the Center to continue its work in promoting job creation and private economic development in communities throughout Alabama   For 30 years  the Auburn Technical Assistance Center has worked to facilitate economic development through job creation  workforce and management training  and product ideas throughout Alabama  said Senator Shelby  I am pleased that the EDA has provided this funding so that the Center can continue its important work in our state  We  as Alabamians  should continue to make every effort to grow our local economies and create opportunities for the state   The Auburn Technical Assistance Center works in all 67 of Alabamas counties to improve economic standards  Since its inception  the Center has brought together Auburn University researchers with more than 1 400 businesses across the state to solve workplace problems and implement new ideas                                                           June 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
