Shelby Announces Over  4 2 Million for Head Start Projects    November 28  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that the U S  Department of Health and Human Services will release  4 260 074 to the Community Action Partnership of North Alabama  The funding will be used for Head Start Projects in Morgan  Lawrence  Cullman  Franklin  Colbert  Blount  Marion  and Winston counties   This funding will allow 1 102 children to receive childcare  early education  health care  and two meals each day  said Senator Shelby  Head Start is an important program that ensures strong overall development and prepares students for their formal education  All children can succeed if they are given a chance  Head Start provides such a chance for otherwise disadvantaged children   Head Start is a school readiness program for preschool aged children of low income families  The funding was awarded by the Region
