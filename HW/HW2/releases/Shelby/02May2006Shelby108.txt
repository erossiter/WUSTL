SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  100 000 FOR HUNTSVILLE AND MADISON COUNTY LOCAL LAW ENFORCEMENT     May 2  2006   U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice has awarded  104 004 to Madison County and the City of Huntsville for their local law enforcement   I am pleased to announce  104 004 for Huntsville and Madison County law enforcement  said Senator Shelby  I believe it is our responsibility to support our first responders  who risk their lives everyday for our safety  It is critical that they have the appropriate equipment and training before we send them into harms way   The Huntsville Police Department will use the funds to improve officer safety and services to the community  They will also replace emergency light bar systems on patrol cars and provide training services for employees  Madison County will use the funds for an anti gang initiative and law enforcement equipment  In addition  the Madison County District Attorneys office will continue its Gang Intervention and Violence Education  GIVE  Program with these funds   These funds are released from the Edward Byrne Memorial Justice Assistance Grant Program  JAG  and allow law enforcement to use the resources to prevent and control crime based on their own needs  In this case  the departments have agreed to share the funding                                                            May 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
