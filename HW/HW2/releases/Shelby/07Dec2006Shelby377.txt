Shelby Announces More Than  1 9 Million for Bevill State Community College    December 12  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee  announced today that the U S  Department of Labors Presidents Community Based Job Training Initiative has awarded  1 909 973 to the Bevill State Community College   Marion County and the surrounding area have the opportunity to grow their economy by expanding the local mining industry  said Senator Shelby  This funding provided through the Presidents Community Board Job Training Initiative will allow Bevill State to train and educate area students at the high school and associate degree levels  This program will allow Bevill State to become the leading provider of mining industry training throughout the state  preparing their students for better  higher paying jobs  while keeping Alabama on the forefront of emerging energy production   The Presidents Community Based Job Training Initiative is a competitive grant program through which proposals are chosen from a nationwide pool  This year the Initiative received 430 applications for this program and selected only 72 winners  Bevill State is expected to see 120 students enroll in mining training programs and 40 high school students earn certificates with this funding
