SHELBY ANNOUNCES MORE THAN  7 8 MILLION FOR TUSCALOOSA DOWNTOWN REVITILIZATION   September 8  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  CJS   today announced that the National Institute of Standards and Technology  NIST  has released  7 897 824 to the City of Tuscaloosa for its downtown revitalization project  Senator Shelby was instrumental in securing this funding during the 2006 appropriations process  This ongoing project will continue to make a significant impact on Tuscaloosas economy  said Senator Shelby  The development will bring more businesses  activity  and economic investment to the area  making Tuscaloosa a better place to live and work  As the downtown areas economy continues to grow  the City is sure to experience job growth and economic prosperity  This funding will allow the City of Tuscaloosa to continue its ongoing urban renewal plan for downtown Tuscaloosa  The project includes voluntary property acquisition  business and residential relocations  demolition  and utility rehabilitation  With the funding provided  the City of Tuscaloosa will incorporate a new Federal Building  create a town center  and provide opportunities for downtown living and working                                                             September 2006 News
