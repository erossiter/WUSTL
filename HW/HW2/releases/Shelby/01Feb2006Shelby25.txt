SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  400 000 FOR UAH     February 2  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Commerce  Justice  Science Subcommittee on Appropriations  announced today that the National Aeronautics and Space Administration  NASA  has released a total of  454 667 in funding for two projects at the University of Alabama in Huntsville  UAH  to aid the Applied Information and Systems Research  AISR  program   Senator Shelby said  I am pleased that UAH has received funding for these important programs  UAH continues to be on the forefront of emerging scientific research for our space programs  Strong research in these areas not only furthers the education of our students  but also makes important strides for the success of NASA   The projects were funded through a competitive program to apply advances in information science and technology to increase life cycle effectiveness and efficiency of the Science Mission Directorate  SMD  research efforts across all science programs  AISR supports the application of emerging information technologies  concepts  and methodology  among other projects   Funded Projects Include  University of Alabama in Huntsville  Information Technology and Systems  A distributed knowledge extraction framework based on semantic webs services   100 000   University of Alabama in Huntsville  Department of Computer Science  Auroral Phenomenon Localization  classification  and temporal evolution tracking   354 667                                                            February 2006 News Releases
