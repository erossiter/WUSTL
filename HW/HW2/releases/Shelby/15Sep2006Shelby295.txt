SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  2 MILLION FOR SPANISH FORT    September 15  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee today announced that the U S  Department of Homeland Securitys Federal Emergency Management Agency  FEMA  has released  2 073 900 for the Spanish Fort Bluff Stabilization Project  Several homes in Spanish Fort are in imminent danger from the continued erosion of this bluff  said Senator Shelby  This project will help stabilize the bluff and therefore relieve the risk to homeowners as well as passing motorists   The bluff along the west side of Spanish Fort has suffered erosion and threatened areas of U S  Highway 90 during Hurricanes Danny and Georges  The landslides on the bluff threaten 13 homes and a vacant lot along Patrician Drive  Spanish Fort will use the funding provided to stabilize the bank through grading and construction of four parallel reinforced stability benches and install a drainage system to convey storm water and prevent soil saturation                                                          September 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
