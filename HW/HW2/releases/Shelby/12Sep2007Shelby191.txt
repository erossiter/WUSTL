Shelby Announces  294 289 to Marshall County   September 12  2007  Washington  D C     U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R Ala    ranking member of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice and Science  today announced that the Department of Justices Office of Community Oriented Policing Services  COPS  will award  294 289 to the Marshall County Sheriffs Department  This grant is awarded through the COPS Methamphetamine Training and Technical Assistance program  which provides funds for projects that seek to increase community awareness with regard to methamphetamine use and abuse   Unfortunately  methamphetamine usage is on the rise nationwide  said Shelby  Because this drug knows no boundaries of age  gender  class or race  it is critical that our communities are made aware of the dangers and risks associated with using methamphetamine  It is important that we continue to dedicate necessary resources to eliminate dangerous drug  The Methamphetamine Training and Technical Assistance program is one of several COPS initiatives aimed at fighting methamphetamine  Grants were awarded to those projects most capable of having a national impact  These projects will not only be of benefit to the law enforcement agencies awarded funds under the Methamphetamine Initiatives  but also to the thousands of other agencies and communities across the nation working to combat the use and abuse of methamphetamine                                                         September 2007 News Releases                      Year            County
