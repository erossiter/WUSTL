SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  15 000 FOR PETTUS BRIDGE IN SELMA    August 28  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   a member of the Senate Committee on Appropriations  today announced that the Department of Transportations Federal Highway Administration  FWHA  will release  15 614 to for two Travelers Information Stations commemorating the 1965 Voting Rights March  The National Park Service will use the funds to purchase and install two Travelers Information Stations  to be placed at the Edmund Pettus Bridge in Selma to mark the beginning of the Selma to Montgomery March Byway  and the second will be located near the end of the byway  outside Montgomery City limits   The 1965 Voting Rights March was a significant and historical event  not only to our state  but to the nation  said Senator Shelby  The Travelers Information Station will provide important information to visitors traveling the byway and learning about the March  I am confident that these educational stations will augment the already historically rich byway  The Travelers Information Stations will broadcast directional and interpretive information  via a low radio frequency that can be
