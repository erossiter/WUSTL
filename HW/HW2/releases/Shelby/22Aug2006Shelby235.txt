SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  1 2 MILLION FOR EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS IN ALABAMA    August 23  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice  DOJ  will release  1 234 035 to the Alabama Criminal Justice Information Center for the Planned Response Emergency Portal and Accessible Resource Environment Program  PREPARE   The program aims to strengthen the role of first responders in the state of Alabama   Through our experiences on September 11  2001 and last years hurricane season  we learned that planning and preparing is the key to ensure that we can properly protect our citizens  said Senator Shelby  The PREPARE Program will further organize Alabamas emergency response efforts  placing a particular emphasis on the role of first responders  Early preparation for emergencies will improve our states response to any emergency  should one strike  This funding will enhance and further enable the state to access to new integrated modules that will support the intelligence and information needs in Alabama  As Chairman of the Commerce  Justice  and Science Appropriations Subcommittee that oversees funding for the Department of Justice  Senator Shelby was instrumental in obtaining this funding through the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process                                                         August 2006 News Releases                      Year            County
