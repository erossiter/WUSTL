SHELBY ANNOUNCES OVER  740 000 TO FAULKNER UNIVERSITY    August 23  2006  Washington  D C      U S  Senator Richard Shelby  R AL   Chairman of the Senate Appropriations Subcommittee on Commerce  Justice  and Science  today announced that the Department of Justice will release  740 421 to Faulkner University to develop an Elder Law Clinic Program and Resource Center at the Jones School of Law  Senator Shelby was instrumental in obtaining this funding through the fiscal year 2006 appropriations process   The Faulkner University Elder Law Clinic Program and Resource Center is a great example of a community and university working together to advance the interests of both  said Senator Shelby  This program will give professors and students at the Jones School of Law the opportunity to provide legal advice to the local elderly population dealing with a variety of issues  including health and long term care needs  as well as issues of abuse and neglect  Not only will students gain significant experience in the legal arena  but senior citizens in the area will be able to utilize young  energetic law students to assist them with their important legal issues  Faulkner University will use these funds for the design and development stage of the program  including hiring personnel  training  equipment  and supplies  The program will pair law students  who will provide direct legal information  assistance  and representation  with senior citizens in the community who need legal assistance                                                           August 2006 News Releases
